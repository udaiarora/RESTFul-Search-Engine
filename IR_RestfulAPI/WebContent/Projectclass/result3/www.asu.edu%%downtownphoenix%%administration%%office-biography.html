Mernoy Harrison, Vice President and Provost

Mernoy Harrison, Jr. has been with Arizona State University since September 1997. Prior to his ASU appointment he served at California State University, Sacramento, in various roles: as Vice President for Administration from 1990 to 1997; Vice Chancellor for Business and Finance from August 1993 until February 1994; Vice President for Finance from 1986 to 1990; Associate Vice President for Finance from 1984 to 1986 and Director of Finance and Business Affairs from 1981 to 1984.

Dr. Harrison also was comptroller and adjunct faculty member at North Carolina Central University from 1977 to 1981, and Assistant to the Vice Chancellor for Academic Affairs and the Undergraduate Dean at North Carolina Central University, positions he held from 1974-1977.

Dr. Harrison has served as Secretary and Chairman of the Board of Directors of the National Association of College and University Business Officers (NACUBO) and as a member of the Board of Directors of the National Association of State Universities and Land Grant Colleges (NASULGC). He has been President of the Western Association of College and University Business Officers (WACUBO) where he also serves as member of the faculty for the WACUBO Business Management Institute. He is a member of the editorial review boards of the Society for College and University Planning and the NACUBO Business Officer magazine. From 1991-1997 he served on the Board of Sacramento community television station KVIE. In 1992 he was elected to a six-year term on the Board of Trustees of Stanford University.

Dr. Harrison earned his Ph.D. in Business Administration from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and his M.B.A. and B.S. from Stanford University.

Cordelia Candelaria, Vice Provost Academic Affairs

Dr. Candelaria is a 2006 ASU Regents' Professor of English and Chicana/o Studies who has been on the ASU faculty since 1992, chairing the Department of Chicana and Chicano Studies from 2000-05.

She is the author and/or editor of fifteen books, including the 2-volume Encyclopedia of Latino Popular Culture ( 2004); Seeking the Perfect Game: Baseball in American Literature (1989); Chicano Poetry, A Critical Introduction (1986), Women Poets of the Americas: Toward a Panamerican Gathering (1999), and over 200 other published titles.

Among her many honors are: 1996 Fulbright Scholar in American Literature to the Universidad Catolica de Lima, Peru; 1997 Inaugural ASU Visiting Professor at Richmond College in London; voted 2001 Scholar of the Year by the National Association for Chicana and Chicano Studies, and in 1991 she was the third recipient of The Americas Award, first presented to writer Carlos Fuentes and secondly to United States Senator Daniel Inouye.

Dr. Candelaria received B.A. degrees in English and in French from Fort Lewis College in 1970 and her Master's and Ph.D. in English and structural linguistics from the University of Notre Dame.

Kevin Cook, Vice Provost and Dean for Student Affairs

Kevin Cook has been employed with Arizona State University since 1990.  During his tenure, he has held several senior level leadership positions within Student Affairs on the Tempe campus.  As Vice Provost and Dean for Student Affairs at the Downtown Phoenix campus, he is responsible for creating student experiences which result in student learning and development.

Kevin earned a doctorate from Arizona State University in Higher Education Administration.

Sheila Stokes, Vice Provost of Adminstrative Services

Sheila Stokes has been employed with Arizona State University since 1984. Today she is Vice Provost for Administrative Services for the new Downtown Phoenix campus.

As Vice Provost for Administrative Services, Sheila is responsible for the facilities, human resources and budgets relating to the downtown
campus. She serves as a liaison between the Provost’s Office and other University offices on a variety of management tasks, projects, and functions.

Sheila earned her master’s degree from Ohio State University in Public Administration and her bachelor’s degree from the University of Toledo. She is currently involved in many university and community committees and activities.

Mary Hughes, Director of External Affairs and Community Relations

As a graduate of the ASU School of Social Work and ASU's Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communications, Mary Hughes has an extensive professional background that includes journalism, public relations, political consulting, social service and nonprofit administration.

In her previous position as chief development and public relations officer of a Catholic Charities agency in Phoenix, Hughes was responsible for stakeholder, media and community relations; external publications and fund development.

At the Downtown Phoenix campus Mary works to develop community partnerships and collaborations that embrace the New American University’s imperative for social embeddedness in the greater Phoenix community.
