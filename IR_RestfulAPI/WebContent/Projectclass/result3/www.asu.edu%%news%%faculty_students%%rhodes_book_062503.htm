June 25, 2003

Rhodes wins top honors for novel

When ASU English and Creative Writing Professor Jewell Parker Rhodes published "Douglass's Women" last October, many people on the ASU campus predicted it would be a hit. Her two previous novels, "Voodoo Dreams" (St. Martin's Press, 1993) and "Magic City"(HarperCollins, 1997) had both been very successful, and it was clear that Rhodes was on a roll.

The novel has since gone on a roll of its own. First, it was named the top work of historical fiction of 2002 by Urban Spectrum magazine. Then it was awarded the Josephine Miles National Award for excellence in multicultural literature by PEN Oakland. The Miles Award was formally presented to Rhodes at an awards ceremony and reading June 7 at PRO ARTS in downtown Oakland, Calif.

Next came the Fiction Award from the Black Caucus of the American Library Association. Rhodes will receive this award at the Annual Conference of the American Library Association in Toronto, Canada, the week of June 19.

Finally, it was recently announced that "Douglass's Women" has won the 2003 American Book Award from the Before Columbus Foundation. The foundation created the award in 1980 to acknowledge "the excellence and multicultural diversity of American writing" and recognize "outstanding literary achievement by contemporary American authors, without restriction to race, sex, ethnic background or genre." Rhodes will receive the award at a ceremony in September.

In addition to going to awards ceremonies, Rhodes has already logged a lot of miles for literature this year. She recently completed a six-city book tour promoting her novel and has read selections of it on National Public Radio. Her next novel, "Voodoo Season," has been purchased by Simon & Schuster.
