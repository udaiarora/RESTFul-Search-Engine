May 21, 2003

Photo exhibit documents Nixon's visit to China

Twenty-five photographs from President Richard M. Nixon's historic visit to China in 1972 will be on display through July 25 in ASU's Luhrs Gallery.

The exhibit, titled "Rapprochement: Nixon in China 1972," was created by Adrian Luna, an ASU student who will receive his degree in political science May 15, with a certificate in Asian Studies.

Prior to his visit, Nixon relaxed the trade embargo against China. On his trip, he was received by Chairman Mao Tse-tung, and conducted lengthy negotiations with Premier Chou En-lai. The talks opened new possibilities for trade and created semiformal channels of communication, short of diplomatic recognition and exchange of ambassadors, between the United States and China.

Luna received funding for the exhibit through a Scholar-Citizen Award presented by the ASU College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

Luna said he developed the exhibit with two audiences in mind - the ASU community and students in Valley schools.

"The exhibit will help create a better understanding among faculty and students on this historic topic in U.S.-Sino relations. It will also serve as a stimulus for other topics as well," he said.

"I feel that the exhibit is also important because it not only addresses U.S.-Sino relations but also offers a glimpse into U.S. history and political strategic thinking during the Cold War."

After it is shown at ASU, the exhibit will be used by the Center for Asian Studies' new outreach program for elementary, middle and high schools.

"By creating this exhibit, I will hopefully create a better understanding and knowledge of China for the ASU community, as well as local schools in the Phoenix area," Luna said.

Photographs for the exhibit were selected from "Camera on Assignment: Photographs by Ollie Atkins," housed at George Mason University Libraries. Oliver Atkins (1917 - 77) was the Washington, D.C. photographic correspondent for the Saturday Evening Post from 1945 to 1968, and later Nixon's personal photographer.
