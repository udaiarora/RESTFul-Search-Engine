Financial Aid

Financial aid for graduate study can come from the department, from other university resources or from external resources.

Financial Support Awarded by the Program

Until the Admissions Committee admits a student to the program, no consideration is given for financial aid.  Almost all students who are admitted, however, will receive some type of financial aid.  The most common source of financial aid is research and teaching assistantships.

Research Assistantships

Research assistantships are awarded on a competitive basis by the program as "entering support" or by a program faculty member as "faculty support."

Program Supported Research Assistantships: Program supported research assistantships involve working as an interns in a multi-user materials laboratory or facility.  Research assistants work a minimum of 20 hours per week on assignments designed to increase the studentís knowledge of basic research methods. These positions are intended to give the entering student time to concentrate on the required foundation courses.  The assistantships carry a modest stipend for living expenses.  Research assistants who are not Arizona residents are awarded waivers of non-resident tuition as part of the financial aid package.  In addition, all research assistants who are hired 50% time, are provided with free major medical health insurance. Research assistants must enroll for a minimum of 6 credits (maximum of 12) of general SEM course work per semester.

Faculty Supported Research Assistantships:  During their first in the program and while students are being supported by the program, students are encouraged to meet with faculty of the program to learn about their research work.  The student is expected to make a transition from program support to faculty support.  The student should choose a faculty member who will become their dissertation advisor and who will support the student through the faculty memberís research grants.  Stipends vary among research groups, but are usually higher than the stipend awarded entering the program.

Teaching Assistantships

The interdisciplinary nature of the program involves the participation of related departments.  The departments of Chemistry and Physics and the Center for Solid State Science each allocate a teaching assistantship each year to an entering student in the SEM Program.  These teaching assistants work a minimum of 20 hours per week supporting the instruction of a lower division undergraduate chemistry or physics course or work in the Center for Solid State Science Visualization Center, which supports materials teaching.  The TA will work under the supervision of the instructor assigned to the course.  Duties of the TA may include lecturing, leading discussion groups, serving as assistants to laboratory classes, and grading tests and papers.

International students whose native language is not English must show proof of English proficiency in order to be allowed to TA.  The university requires that international students take and pass the Test of Spoken English (TSE) or the ASU SPEAK test before they can be awarded a teaching assistantship. Stipends for teaching assistantships vary with the department awarding the TA.  As with research assistantships, TAs also receive free health insurance and non-resident tuition waivers.

Graduate Academic Scholarship (GAS)

This scholarship pays for resident tuition. Awarded to graduate students with outstanding academic records.

Graduate Tuition Scholarship (GTS)

This scholarship pays for non-resident tuition.  Allows non-resident graduate students with outstanding academic records to pay tuition at the resident rate.
