Accounting  

(480) 965-4604


The Accounting Staff manages the University Libraries' Information Budget of over $6 million per year. The Accounting staff:

  • Pays and records all invoices, deposits, credits and statements charged to the information budget, utilizing the acquisitions module of the ASU online integrated system (Innopac) and the University's financial system (Advantage);
  • Maintains the University's official audit trail for information budget expenditures;
  • Works closely with the Collection Development Department to allocate, monitor, and expend the information budget;
  • Maintains close contacts with the University's Accounts Payable
    and Purchasing Departments and the Comptroller's Office;
  • Produces weekly and on-demand budget reports;
  • Works with Monographic Receiving and Serials Receiving
    to resolve receipt and billing problems with domestic
    and international vendors and publishers.

 

Bibliographic Searching/Ordering Unit  

Searching: (480) 965-4609    Ordering:  (480) 965-5014

 

Bibliographic Searching searches order requests sent to Acquisitions using a variety of online and paper sources. The Bibliographic Searching staff verifies bibliographic data and checks for duplication, and checks Library holdings for book-in-hand materials (for example, gift books). (For information about making a donation to the Arizona State University Libraries, please see the Libraries' book donation policy or contact Linda Cravens at (480) 965-2113.

 

The Order unit staff creates acquisitions records (brief bibliographic and order records) for firm orders and for book-in-hand material in the ASU On-line Catalog. The Order unit also generates purchase orders (both paper and electronic) and claims overdue monographic materials from vendors.

 

 

Catalog Management and Authority Control  

(480) 965-0521


Catalog Management provides a full range of maintenance in ASU's On-line Catalog for monographic items in all formats.
This unit also:

  • Maintains an e-mail account to which staff may report database maintenance problems (fixit@asu.edu).
  • In conjunction with the Libraries' barcode project, this unit also receives monographic and serial anals needing linking.

 

Monographic Processing   

 (480) 965-3949


Monographic Processing receives all new cataloged monographs and prepares them for circulation.
The unit also:

  • Evaluates and prepares new soft cover monographs for binding;
  • Works with Preservation to repair damaged monographs for binding;
  • Processes all monographs returned from the commercial bindery and prepares them for circulation;
  • Delivers all processed monographs to Access Services for circulation.

 

Monographic Receiving / Copy Cataloging  

                    (480) 965-5878                  (480) 965-3470                     


Monographic Receiving receives library materials in a wide variety of languages and formats and sorts and distributes them to various cataloging staff or holding areas.
This unit:

  • Processes Pre-Circ transactions;
  • Receives firm orders, blanket orders, and approval plans;
  • Retrieves and loads vendor supplied brief or full bibliographic and order records;
  • Approves invoices for payment;
  • Returns duplicate and defective material;
  • Rotates books in and out of the review area;
  • Corresponds with domestic and international vendors to resolve receipt and invoice problems;
  • Receives, sorts and distributes ASU West and ASU East material; maintain holding areas.

Copy Cataloging provides monographic copy cataloging for new receipts for ASU Main, ASU West, and ASU East. This unit also provides added copy and added volume cataloging when appropriate.


Non-Roman Acquisitions and Cataloging 

(480) 965-1498


Non-Roman Acquisitions and Cataloging is responsible for the monographic acquisition function and the cataloging and catalog management of CJK (i.e. Chinese, Japanese and Korean) and Slavic language materials in all formats. It also includes the responsibilities of managing and monitoring the outsourced cataloging of Thai language books.

  • The staff consists of three librarian Area Specialists, 3 paraprofessionals, and 2 student assistants. As an Area Specialist, the librarians are heavily involved in all collection development, public and technical services of the Libraries that affect the faculty and students of the disciplines they serve.
  • The unit participates in the establishment and implementation of the University Libraries cataloging policies that govern the overall quality and intellectual content of the ASU catalog; contributes to the NACO, SACO and BIBCO components of the Library of Congress Program for Cooperation Cataloging, by creating, adding and updating authority and bibliographic core records to national databases through RLIN.

 

Original Cataloging  

To be directed to appropriate extension, please call (480) 965-5889


Original Cataloging creates bibliographic records for roman alphabet monographs without useable copy and creates or edits records for serials, special formats, Arizona state and local documents, and special collections materials.
In addition, Original Cataloging:

  • Establishes local cataloging policies and procedures;
  • Does database maintenance for special formats and special collections;
  • Establishes local authority control policies and directs local and outsourced authority control procedures;
  • Contributes name, serials and authority records to the Anglo-American Authority File via the Program for Cooperative Cataloging (PCC) NACO (name authority) and SACO (subject authority) sections;
  • Contributes to enrichment and quality control of the OCLC and RLIN databases through participation in the BIBCO (bibliographic record) section of the PCC.

  

Serial Holdings Maintenance 

(480) 965-6112


Serial Holdings Maintenance maintains retrospective serial holdings, binds and processes serials volumes, and receives non-book materials on standing order. 
This unit:

  • Maintains summary holdings in the ASU On-line Catalog;
  • Maintains detailed retrospective serials holdings;
  • Transfers or withdraws superseded serial volumes;
  • Processes serial volumes for shelving;
  • Prepares serial volumes for binding;
  • Works with Preservation to repair damaged serials;
  • Prepares serial title changes, ceases, splits and mergers for cataloging.

 

Serials Receiving

(480) 965-6101


Serials Receiving manages incoming serial subscriptions and monographic standing orders for the ASU Main Campus Libraries. 
This unit:

  • Orders and renews serial subscriptions and monographic standing orders;
  • Receives serial and monographic standing order volumes in the ASU On-line Catalog;
  • Receives microform materials on standing order;
  • Receives and tracks electronic format materials;
  • Labels periodicals with call number and receipt information;
  • Claims late issues from vendors and publishers;
  • Approves payment for all subscriptions and standing orders in Innopac;
  • Prepares serial title changes, ceases, splits and mergers for cataloging;
  • Returns duplicate, damaged or unordered serial volumes to vendors;
  • Corresponds with domestic and international vendors, publishers, and distribution centers to resolve receipt problems and verify publication status;
  • Cancels subscriptions as directed by Collection Development and subject selectors.
  • Works with Interlibrary Loan and Preservation to repair damaged serials.

 

Technical Support and Special Projects 

(480) 965-4610


Provides day-to-day computer support to Technical Services, maintains department web site, and participates in selected  department projects.
