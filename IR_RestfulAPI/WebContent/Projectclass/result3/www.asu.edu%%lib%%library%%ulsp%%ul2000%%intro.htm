The Customer-Driven Enterprise

The strategic plan that you are about to read sets direction for the ASU Libraries as we move into the next millenium. The plan is the product of an intensive five-month process involving broad input from library staff and embracing a wide spectrum of issues and problems.

During that process, one compelling theme emerged: the customer-driven enterprise.

Though the Libraries take justifiable pride in efficiency and the generosity of their customer services, this intensive self-assessment pointed to the need for a shift to a new view of the enterprise that places the customer in a new partnership role with us. Over the next five years, we seek to accomplish a major paradigm change -- expanding our focus beyond the business at hand to encompass the reason for that business, the customer.

And though we must, of course, still attend to the business at hand, we commit to the following:

  • building a new partnership with the customer in which the customer joins us in defining the criteria for successful service;


  • intensifying our efforts to find out what the customer needs and whether we are meeting those needs;


  • shifting the way we measure success by expanding our focus on output measures to include a greater focus on outcome performance measures;


  • designing work processes that produce the best possible services for the customer;


  • relating our decisions constantly and consciously to the principle of added value for the customer.
Our philosophy is to work within the context of University priorities to prepare our customers to seek information successfully wherever it is, whatever format it exists in -- to help them learn the skills and behaviors necessary to conduct inquiry and research in any life situation.
