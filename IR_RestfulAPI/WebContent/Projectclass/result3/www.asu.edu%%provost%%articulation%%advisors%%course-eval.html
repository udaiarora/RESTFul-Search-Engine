Guidelines for the Evaluation of Courses

  • When processing community college courses for evaluation purposes, the main question should be:
    • Is the community college course substantially equivalent to any course offered by my department? If yes, indicate a one-to-one (direct) equivalency on the ACETS/CEG Form
  • One-to-one equivalencies to 300- and 400-level courses should be granted only under special circumstances. Please consult with ATAO Director before doing so.
  • The General Studies value of a course in transfer is addressed by the General Studies Council (GSC) or ATAO Director.
  • When granting one-to-one (direct) equivalencies, please make sure the community college course’s credits are equal to or higher than the ASU equivalent course.
  • When evaluating a course that is offered for more credits than the ASU equivalent course (A > B), the evaluator has two options:
    • Accept the extra credit(s) as Elective credit(s). If this is your choice, all you need to do is indicate the direct equivalency on the ACETS/CEG Form. The acceptance of the extra credits as Electives would be implied. DARS will not encode the extra credits in CEG/CAS, but they will be honored by DARS as “elective” credits.
    • Reject the extra credit(s). If this is your choice, you must indicate that decision on the “Notes” section of the ACETS/CEG Form by writing: “ ‘A’ transfers as equivalent to ‘B’ for (number) credits ONLY.” The “Notes” section allows DARS staff to encode the extra credits as NT (Not Transferable credits).
  • If you wish to grant one-to-one equivalency to a community college course that is offered for fewer credits than the ASU equivalent course (A < B), the following considerations should be kept in mind:
    • ATAO strongly discourages this type of “less to more” transactions.
    • However, if you see the need to grant direct equivalency to an "A<B" type of course evaluation, DARS will encode the direct equivalency while making sure the credits granted to the transfer student would be equal to the number of credits for which the community college course was offered. For example: A(2) = B(3), DARS will encode course A as being equivalent to ASU’s course B for “two” credits only.
    • If the ASU equivalent course carries a General Studies designation, you should not grant a direct equivalency if the community college course is offered for less than three credits or for less than four credits in the case of [SG] or [SQ] designations.
  • If the course you are evaluating does not fall under the category of “one-to-one” equivalency, you may want to ask the following question:
    • Would my department accept this course as satisfying “major related” or “related field” electives? If yes, indicate DEC(departmental prefix) on the ACETS/CEG Form. For example: DEC(POS).
  • If the community college course falls under any of the following categories, indicate NT on the CEG form.
    • Remedial credit is not transferable.
    • Courses that are vocational in nature are considered not transferable.
    • Credits awarded for life experience are not transferable.
    • Credits for active military service or courses that were taken through the military will be reviewed but are usually not transferable.
  • If the course is transferable but cannot be granted either direct equivalency or DEC status, then indicate ELECTIVE credit on the ACETS/CEG Form.
  • If during the evaluation of a course there are any questions regarding the information provided on the ACETS/CEG Form, please feel free to contact the person identified on the form as the submitter.
  • For any questions, concerns, and/or suggestions regarding the evaluation of courses, please contact Dr. Kathy Wigal, Director, Academic Transfer Articulation, at Kathy.Wigal@asu.edu or call 965-8332.

Guidelines prepared by the Academic Transfer Articulation Office
