Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting (Spotlight Series)

Tuesday, November 29, 2005, MU Cochise Room, 10:30 a.m. - 12 noon

Speaker:  Dr. Marjorie Zatz, Vice Provost for Academic Personnel

Topic: Implementation of revised ACD 507 policies for Academic Professional personnel actions and development of personnel policies for postdoctoral scholars

Present: Approximately 40 people

The forum provided an opportunity for Academic Professionals to hear about the plans for implementing the revised policies for Academic Professional personnel actions and for developing separate personnel policies for postdoctoral scholars. 

Dr. Zatz reported that the revised ACD 507 section was approved by the Academic Senate at its November 14, 2005, meeting. The revision will now go to the University President for final approval.  

Dr. Zatz briefly presented the highlights of the changes with particular attention to the addition of multi-year rolling contracts. She followed with a discussion of implementation issues and answered questions from the audience. Issues covered were:

  • The Provost's office would like to see all the documents submitted for annual evaluations of positions with rolling contracts, not just the evaluation summary, at least for a few years to ensure that the contract renewal process is working properly.


  • Units creating an Academic Professional position for the first time will have to put criteria in place for evaluating performance. Since the Provost's office must approve new positions, it can serve as a control point to ensure unit criteria exist.


  • Positions that are on year-to-year or multi-year contracts, both fixed and rolling, can go from one type of contract to another at contract renewal time to best meet the needs of the unit.


  • Academic Professional Continuing Appointment and Promotion Review Committee issues will be addressed after the Faculty Promotion and Tenure procedures are finalized in case there are certain procedures that the implementation for Academic Professionals would need to parallel. One of the issues that will then need to be addressed are who will be eligible to serve on the committee.

Dr. Zatz then presented ideas currently in development for creating a separate category for Postdoctoral Scholars. These positions have never fit in well with the Academic Professional category so their special needs are not being met. Some things under consideration are providing full benefits to postdoctoral students, including family health coverage, and making it possible for all postdoctoral students to enroll in courses if desired regardless of the type of postdoctoral position they are in. Postdoctoral students in the audience indicated a need for better and more consistent avenues of communication with the administration and the designation of a single individual or office to which they can address their concerns and questions.  

The session concluded with a question on how the revision of the Faculty Promotion and Tenure policy currently in development will affect the review process for Academic Professionals. Dr. Zatz assured everyone that there would be little effect on the process for Academic Professionals and that whatever small changes may be necessary could be easily handled.
