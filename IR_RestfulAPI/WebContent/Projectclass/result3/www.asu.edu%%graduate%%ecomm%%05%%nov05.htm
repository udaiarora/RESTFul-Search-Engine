The Arts, Media and Engineering Program Receives IGERT award

Last month, the Arts, Media and Engineering (AME) Program at ASU received a $3 million grant from the National Science Foundation (NSF) for an Integrative Graduate Education and Research Training (IGERT) program. AME trains students to integrate principles of multimedia computing with artistic ideas and objectives, with the goal of using the human-machine experience to directly address societal needs and facilitate knowledge. The NSF IGERT grant will provide five years of graduate fellowships for AME students. www.asu.edu/graduate/studies/igert.html.


Council of Graduate Schools Surveys International Graduate Admissions Trends

The Council of Graduate Schools (CGS) recently released a report on international graduate admissions trends and their findings revealed that there was a 3% increase in international graduate admissions since 2004. CGS surveyed over 125 schools, including over 80% of the 25 institutions with the largest international student enrollments, and the final report indicated an increase in first-time enrollment of international students, following years of declines. To view the complete report, go to www.cgsnet.org/pdf/CGS2005IntlAdmitIII_Rep.pdf.

— REMINDERS —

December 2005 Graduation. The last day to hold a defense for December 2005 graduation is November 22. Since defenses must be scheduled at least 10 working days in advance, please remind students to plan ahead. Doctoral students planning on participating in December 2005 Commencement ceremony must submit a Doctoral Candidate Participation form by November 22. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns

Oral Defense Pass/Fail Form. The pass/fail form for an oral defense of a thesis/dissertation must be hand-delivered to Student Academic Services within 10 working days after the defense. If the student has minor or major revisions, a photocopy of the form should be sent in place of the original until the student completes the necessary revisions and the entire committee and head of the academic unit have signed the form. Please make sure that the appropriate box is marked on the form and a description of the revisions is included in the appropriate space on the form, or in an attached memo, if necessary.

SAS cannot accept pass/fail forms or approval pages for the DGS dean's signature with white-out or stamped signatures. Additionally, please do not stamp the approval page with an "Original" stamp. To access the form, go to www.asu.edu/graduate/forms.
