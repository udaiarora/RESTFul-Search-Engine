Areas of Study - Public History

Short Courses

Each year the program invites at least two public historians to teach short courses on special topics relating to their expertise and experience. Through discussions, readings, and projects, these scholars introduce students to the issues and challenges they will encounter as public historians. All students complete at least two short courses offered either before the opening of the spring semester in January or during the spring semester break.

Since 1996, we have been proud to host an esteemed group of scholars across a variety of fields and interests. Recent minicourse instructors include Jo Blatti (Executive Director, Harriet Beecher Stowe Center), Gregory M. Britton (Director, Minnesota Historical Society Press), Charles Bryan Jr. (Virginia Historical Society), James Gardner (Deputy Director, American Historical Association), Arnita Jones (Executive Director, Organization of American Historians), Dan Jordan (Monticello), Roger Launius, ( NASA Chief Historian, NASA History Office), William Lindsay ( CFO , Harvard University Press), Fiona McCrae (Graywolf Press), Marilyn Nickles (Bureau of Land Management), Gale Peterson (Executive Director, Ohio Humanities Council), Dwight Pitcaithley (National Park Service), Daniel Ross (Director, University of Nebraska Press), Susan Rossen (Executive Director of Publications, the Art Institute of Chicago), Raymond Smock (formerly U.S. House of Representatives), Constance Ramirez (Historian, U.S. Army), and Marie Tyler-McGraw (National Park Service).

In addition, public historians teach courses and conduct workshops. Recent visiting faculty include Barbara Carson (University of William and Mary), David Hoober (Archivist for the State of Arizona), Jean Hriches (Chase Manhattan Bank), Anna K. Nelson (American University), Karen Smith (Department of Water Resources), Phyllis Steckler (The Oryx Press), and Richard Pearce-Moses (The Heard Museum).
