Frequently Asked Questions for the Regents High Honors Endorsement Scholarship (AIMS)

We have provided answers to the most common financial aid questions that students ask our counselors.

If you need additional help please call or visit any of our offices located on each of ASU's campuses throughout the valley.

General Student Financial Assistance FAQs

Q: What is the Regents High Honors Endorsement and where can I obtain more information about the qualifications for this award?

A: It is a scholarship established by the Arizona Board of Regents to recognize Arizona high school students for outstanding academic achievement as demonstrated through high school courses and test scores (AIMS). It is commonly referred to as the â??AIMSâ?? scholarship. The first students to receive the honor will be the high school class graduating in 2006 and entering ASU Fall 2006. For more information and questions about eligibility, visit the following Web sites or contact your high school counselor. Arizona Board of Regents Policy

Arizona Department of Education Information

Q: Will students be able to receive this award with other merit scholarships from Arizona State University (example: Presidentâ??s, Provostâ??s, University, etc.)?

A: Students will not be able to benefit from more than one scholarship. Students will receive the scholarship of greater value. Arizona State University offers many generous merit-based scholarships to top Arizona students and encourages application for admission to Arizona State University by December 1 in order to be considered for these awards.

Q: What is the value of the scholarship at ASU?

A: The scholarship is equal to the amount of tuition as set by the ABOR in the studentâ??s first year. The award amount will remain the same throughout the maximum 8 semesters of eligibility.

Q: What are the renewal criteria for this scholarship?

A: Students must maintain a 3.25 cumulative grade point average and 24 credit hours per academic year throughout a maximum of 8 semesters of eligibility toward any undergraduate degree at ASU.

Q: Do I have to file a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) to be eligible for this scholarship?

A: A FAFSA is not required in order to be considered for this scholarship. However, ASU highly encourages students to submit the FAFSA to be considered for other financial assistance. For more information visit www.fafsa.ed.gov.

Q: May I defer the scholarship?

A: The scholarship may be deferred one time. The scholarship is available beginning in the fall semester after high school graduation (Fall 2006) and students must begin using the award no later than Spring 2007. Students choosing to wait until Spring 2007 must submit a deferment application to the ASU Scholarship Office. This will count as one deferment. See the deferment policy and application The value of the scholarship after deferment is the same amount as the initial award.

Q: How will ASU be notified of students who have been awarded the scholarship?

A: The Arizona Department of Education will provide a list of recipients to ASU by July 31.

Q: What do I do if I am not on the list of recipients but I received a letter regarding my scholarship?

A: The studentâ??s high school or high school district office is the first point of contact to assist with eligibility questions.

Q: May I transfer the scholarship at a later date to ASU from UA or NAU?

A: Students who have not started their third semester at UA or NAU may submit a written request no later than a month prior to the term they plan to enroll at ASU. Requests should be submitted to the ASU Scholarship Office and include academic transcripts documenting the 3.25 cumulative grade point average and 12 credit hours per semester were completed. If the request to transfer the award is approved, the value of the award is equal to the rate of ASU tuition in the studentâ??s first year (immediately after high school) of eligibility.

Q: Will I be able to apply this scholarship to the cost of winter or summer sessions?

A: The scholarship can only be used for full-time enrollment during the Fall and Spring semesters.

Q: Will the scholarship cover the cost of program fees or any differential tuition surcharge in my college?

A: The award value is the amount of base resident tuition in the studentâ??s first year. The scholarship does not apply to program fees or differential tuition surcharges.

Q: What should I do if I am notified about the scholarship after I have already paid tuition?

A: Students must notify the Scholarship Office. After confirming the studentâ??s eligibility, the Scholarship Office will post the award to the studentâ??s account and generate any refund that is owed.
