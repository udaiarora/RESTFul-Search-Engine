Q. How to perform a soft reset.

A.  From the handheld, press the ALT + CAP + DELETE keys at the same time.

You will not lose any data in this process.

Back to top

 

Q.  How to perform a hard reset.

A.  From the handheld, locate the RESET button on the back of the device near the bottom. If you do not see the reset button, check under the battery cover. Look for a small hole with a label that says RESET. Using a pin or paper clip, press the RESET button.

In some cases, there will be no reset button at all, in this case, removing the battery for 30 seconds will reset the device.

The device will restart. You will not lose any data in this process.

Q.  How to send yourself a new service book.

A.  Choose Options icon (wrench). Choose SERVICE BOOK -- click trackwheel. Choose Perform a HARD RESET or a SOFT RESET on the Blackberry. Wait for device to restart, then make sure that your wireless signal shows GPRS. Check your messages to see if you have received the new Service Book. Test your services by sending yourself an e-mail message.

 

Q.  How and why to register on the wireless network.

A.  You will need to register devices on the wireless carrier's network if you find that you have no data service to the device. 

Go to "options." Select "network." Press the "trackwheel." Select "register now."

Q.  Your Blackberry has a frozen screen.

A.  Try a soft reset.  If that is not successful, try a hard reset.

Q.  Your Blackberry does not send or receive messages.

A.  Try these troubleshooting steps in order.

  • Perform a SOFT RESET.
  • Perform a HARD RESET.
  • Check wireless antenna signal strength, make sure antenna shows GPRS (all caps).
  • See if you can still make phone calls - this will verify that GSM services are available.
  • See if you can still browse the web - this will verify that GPRS services are available.
  • Send yourself an e-mail message to the handheld, see if it arrives.
  • Check to see when your most recent bill was paid
  • Go to the Managing your Blackberry web page to log into the Blackberry provider eMail web client.
  • Send yourself a new Service Book.
  • On handheld device, check to see if you received the new Service Book under OPTIONS - SERVICE BOOK.
  • Go to the Managing your Blackberry web page to call your Blackberry provider for assistance.

Q.  You cannot browse the web with your Blackberry.

A.  To fix this problem, try the following in order:
  • Make sure your antenna signal shows GPRS (all caps).
  • Send an e-mail message to your handheld, see if it arrives - this will verify whether you have data services.
  • Try to browse wap.yahoo.com or www.google.com.
  • Use the instructions above for cannot send/receive messages.

Q.  You are going to travel to another country and you want to know if you will have service.

A.  To make sure that you will have service follow these few steps:
  • Go to "Managing your Blackberry."
  • See the section on activating international roaming service.
  • See if phone and data services are available at your destination country.
  • Contact your provider to activate international roaming.

Q.  Your computer does not detect any Blackberry Devices.

A.  Verify that all the physical connections are secure, and the Blackberry is properly cradled.

If it is still having trouble: Remove your Blackberry off the cradle. Press the pin hole sized reset button on the back of the Blackberry. Use a bent paper clip to access this reset button. Reregister your Blackberry to the network and wait for the confirmation message. Put the Blackberry on cradle. if it doesn't detect automatically, open the Blackberry Desktop Manager. Go to Options, Serial Settings, and click on "Detect."

If it is still having trouble, check to see if you have the com port enabled on your computer. If you have more than one com port on your computer, plug the cradle into the other com port, and try detecting the port again.

Q.  You are only getting some of your e-mails.

A.  Blackberry Desktop Manager by default will only forward messages to your handheld if your email address is in the "To" or the "BCC" field. If you are only CC'd, you will get the message header but the body of the message will say "You were CC'd." To change this setting, open up the Blackberry Desktop Manager > Redirector Settings > Filters. Here you can add various filters so that you can filter specific messages to your Blackberry. If you want all the messages to get to your Blackberry, select: when none of the rules apply, "forward the messages to the handheld."

Q.  When you try to configure your Blackberry Desktop Manager to sync your Calendars or Contacts (Configure PIM), you get a message saying "path does not exist".

A.  You don't have administrative privileges on your computer. Please ask your network administrator to make you part of the Local Administrators group.

Q.  You are not able to sync your calendar wirelessly.

A.  Put your Blackberry on the cradle. Open up Blackberry Desktop Manager. Double click on Intellisync. Click on the "Configure PIM" button. Make sure you have a checkbox next to "Calendar". If the option to choose translator doesn't come up, click on the "Choose" button. Choose the translator as "MS Outlook Wireless Sync" (also known as "Blackberry Wireless Sync"). Click "OK", and "OK" again. In the Intellisync window, with the device attached to the cradle, click on "Synchronize Now". A message will come up stating that it will remove all handheld appointments and replace then wirelessly. You will need to accept that and it should be working.

Q.  You need to erase the Blackberry before giving it to another user.

A.  To erase the data on your handheld, go to the Options menu on the handheld (the wrench), go to Security, and click in on the scroll to obtain an optional menu.  In this menu you will find the option to wipe the handheld.  You will be asked for the handheld's password, this is set by you during your handheld's setup.  If you do no have this password, contact your service provider.

Q.  My Blackberry was stolen, lost or misplaced.  What can I do?

A.  The Blackberry Enterprise Server allows your Administration Team to lock or kill your handheld.  Notify the IT Helpdesk to issue this request.  If the device is locked, if you were to find the handheld you can enter the predetermined password to unlock the device.  If no password is predetermined, the handheld's default password is enabled and is available from your provider.  If the handheld is killed and then found, you will need to re-activate it using Enterprise Activation.  A new Enterprise Activation password will need to be obtained via an ARS Ticket submitted by the IT Helpdesk.

Q.  I don't want my Blackberry to sync when connected to the desktop manager.

A.  You want to change the startup behavior of your device. Open Desktop Manager. Open Intellisync by double clicking it. Choose the "Auto start..." button under "Configuration." Under Auto Start Settings check or uncheck the behaviors you want to happen upon connecting your device to the cradle/desktop. Choose OK to close this window. Choose Close to close Intellisync

Q.  Can I change an icon position on the Home screen?

A.  Highlight the icon you would like to move. Hold down the ALT key (orange button). Press in on the track wheel. Scroll the track wheel to the position you want the icon and press in again. Now you can order your icons the way you use them.

Q.  Is there a shortcut to viewing messages sequentially?

With any message open, press "n" to go to the NEXT message or press "p" to go to the PRIOR message. This can save you a lot of track wheel clicking if you need to view many messages.  Additionally, from the home screen pressing the M key will jump to your messages.

Q.  I can't find the Delete on or Hide Filed Message options in my Message List Options, what should I do?

A.  Perform an e-mail reconciliation to transfer your desktop folders to your handheld.  If performing an email reconciliation does not work, close your desktop software and desktop redirector (if running) and edit the registry. 

***Editing the registry should only be done if you have experience in this type of utility.  Making changes in the registry editor can disable your computer and require a complete restore. 

Click Start > Run, type reedit and click ok.  Expand HKEY_Current_User/software/Research in Motion/Blackberry key and click the Synchronize folder.  Delete the Folder Management value.  Start the desktop software and reconcile your e-mail.

Q.  A message I sent or received has an X beside it on the message screen.  What does this mean?

A.  When a received message has an X beside it, and you requested more of the message, there was a problem retrieving the additional information.  When a message you sent from your handheld has an "X" beside it, some problem prevented the message from sending properly.  In either case, you can find out specifically what went wrong by opening the message and scrolling to the top.  The Message Status field will display the errors.
