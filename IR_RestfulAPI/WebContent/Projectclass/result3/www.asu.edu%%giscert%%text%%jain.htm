Geographical Information Systems Project on
Placement of children into Foster Homes

ABSTRACT

Approximately 800,000 children enter the foster care system every year in the United States. Recent social work studies show that children placed in foster homes adjust better than children placed in large group homes. In addition, children do better when they placed in homes close to their original home. The study area covers entire Arizona State which is divided into six DES (Department of Economic Security) districts. The objective of the project was to identify areas for foster home recruitment. In addition, to depict the age and ethnicity of children removed and also distances between removal and placement locations. A series of statewide GIS maps were prepared using geocoding techniques and spatial analysis for the evaluations of Foster Homes Removals and Foster Home Locations in Arizona. The data set included records for approximately 10,000 children including a unique identifier, ethnicity and age cohort information, complete removal address and complete placement address plus 5-digit zip codes. The study looked at all children who were under the supervision of CPS (Child Protective Services) as of June 30, 2005. The use of GIS assisted in efforts to recruit new foster parents in areas of greatest need.
