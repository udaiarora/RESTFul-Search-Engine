December 5, 2003

ASU employees donate $447,000, top United Way campaign goal

ASU employees have demonstrated a deep commitment to the community by making the 2003 ASU United Way campaign the most successful ever. This year employees contributed $447,647.89 - well above the $425,000 campaign goal and considerably more than the $381, 546.34 donated last year.

President Michael Crow says giving back to the community is part of the culture at ASU and he is pleased with the strong support for United Way, which has the ability to help tens of thousands of individuals and families in metropolitan Phoenix.

"ASU enjoys tremendous support from the community and our United Way campaign is one of the many ways we can give back and show our appreciation," says Crow. "I realize many of our own employees are burdened financially, so it is especially gratifying that they are reaching out to help others in such a meaningful way."
ASU United Way Campaign Chair Wilma Mathews says the generosity of ASU employees is overwhelming, but far from unexpected.

"It is clear from this overwhelming response that ASU employees understand the need to help the communities in which we live and work," she says.

Mathews says the outpouring of support from ASU employees is inspiring and very much appreciated.

"On behalf of the almost 200 employee volunteers who assisted in this campaign, I want to thank ASU employees for their generosity of spirit and dollars," Mathews says. "The record-breaking funds we are providing to the community will help tens of thousands of individuals who need our support."

Mathews says ASU's involvement with the United Way extends beyond generous financial contributions.

"We have administrators and faculty who serve on the boards of both the Valley of the Sun and the Mesa United Way organizations; others from staff and faculty serve on the campaign cabinets and still others are members of the allocation committees," she says. "Solving the needs of the community takes the talents and skills of many people."

Even though this year's campaign has officially ended, Mathews says there is still time to help a variety of worthwhile local charities. Mathews says donations will be accepted through the end of the year.

Employees can give to one of seven priority areas or specify their donations to the Valley of the Sun United Way's valley-wide fund, a convenient way to give to more than 400 local human care programs. Donations can also be made to individual agencies affiliated with United Way.

Employees can reduce their tax burden by taking advantage of the "Helping the Working Poor Fund" tax credit. A credit up to $200 on Arizona State taxes is available for those who itemize their charitable contributions and have established a baseline year of charitable giving.For more information, contact your department's United Way unit representative or visit the Valley of the Sun United Way Web site at (www.vsuw.org).

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
