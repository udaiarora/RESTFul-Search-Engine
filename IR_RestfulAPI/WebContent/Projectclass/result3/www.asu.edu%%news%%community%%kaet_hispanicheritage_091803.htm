September 18, 2003

KAET-TV/Channel 8 recognizes Hispanic Heritage Month

As schools across the United States prepare to celebrate Hispanic Heritage Month from Sept. 15 to Oct. 15, KAET-TV/Channel 8 is saluting the influence of America's rich Hispanic heritage on our history and culture by presenting special programs throughout this month.

The following programs will present a variety of aspects focusing on the influences and contributions made by the Hispanic culture to the American way of life.

. "La Plaza," an award-winning series and mix of documentaries and performance pieces, will air at 7:30 p.m., Sept. 17. "El Tiante: Red Sox Story" is a new program featured in the series, which tells the story of Luis Tiant, a black Cuban exile, who pitched in the 1975 World Series for the Boston Red Sox, the last major league team to integrate.
. "The Blue Diner" is a new film that will air at 9 p.m., Sept. 17. This film about food, memory, language and caskets, is the story of a Puerto Rican mother and daughter living together in Boston and the difficulties they encounter when the daughter, Elena, mysteriously loses her ability to speak Spanish, her first language.
. "Justice For My People: The Dr. Hector P. Garcia Story" will air at 3 p.m., Sept. 21. This is a story about a Revolution refugee, medical doctor to the barrios, decorated war veteran, civil rights activist and presidential confidante - who fought to bring attention to the Mexican-American civil rights movement.
. "Mixed Feelings: San Diego/Tijuana" will air at 4:30 p.m., Sept. 21. This documentary is an examination of architecture and urbanism in the border region of San Diego/Tijuana.
. "La Plaza: Sweet 15" will air at 7:30 p.m., Sept. 24. In the United States, Latino families spend thousands of dollars to host a "quinceniera," a young woman's 15th birthday celebration. This story focuses on single mother Rosa Santiago who makes great sacrifices for her oldest daughter.
. "José Marti: Legacy of Freedom" will air at 5 p.m., Sept. 28. This program explores the extraordinary life of this Cuban patriot, tracing Marti's literary career as the father of Modernism to his struggle for Cuban independence in the late 1800s.
. "American Family" will air at 6 p.m., Sept. 28. This heartwarming drama reveals the enduring strength of family in America today as it chronicles the lives of the Gonzalez family of East Los Angeles.

For information about KAET/Channel 8, visit the online pressroom at (www.kaet.asu.edu/pressroom).
