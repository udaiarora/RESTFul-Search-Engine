December 22, 2005

Campus adds to core of downtown Phoenix

ASU worked throughout the year to lay the groundwork for the new Downtown Phoenix campus.

Once complete, the campus will host up to 15,000 students, bringing the presence of a national-caliber research university to the city’s core. Colleges moving to the site include the College of Public Programs, the College of Nursing and the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication.

The provost of the Downtown Phoenix campus, Mernoy Harrison, officially opened his office in downtown Phoenix in August, at 411 N. Central Ave. It’s ASU’s first building for the campus.

Phoenix Mayor Phil Gordon and the Phoenix City Council have committed funds for the acquisition of property for the campus and for the renovation of buildings necessary for Phase I of the site. A citizens’ bond committee has reviewed and recommended projects to the City Council, including a $188 million request for ASU’s Downtown Phoenix campus that voters will be asked to approve in March.

The campus, being developed in partnership with the city, will be an integral part of life in downtown Phoenix. Faculty members will be consultants, and students will be interns and volunteers in their chosen fields.
