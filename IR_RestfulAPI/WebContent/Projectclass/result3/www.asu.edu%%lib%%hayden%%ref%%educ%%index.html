Finding Education Journals

ASU Databases:

Use the following databases to search for journal articles by topic:

Education Full Text

Includes full-text articles from over 270 journals 1996- and abstracting and indexing of over 650 journals 1983-. A good place to search for book reviews published in education journals. Although many of these journals are also indexed by ERIC, approximately 80 titles are unique to this database.


ERIC (via CSA)

The ERIC database (1966-July 2004) contains 1.1 million bibliographic citations to a broad collection of education-related resources, from government reports to journal articles (EJ). Other materials, called ERIC Documents (ED) include conference papers, project and program descriptions, curriculum guides, etc. The Documents are full text 1993-. Many of the journal articles are also full-text. Use the link to access any full text articles to which ASU subscribes.

Education: A SAGE Full-Text Collection

Includes the full text of 26 peer-reviewed journals published by SAGE and participating societies. Titles include: Journal of Hispanic Higher Education; Theory and Research in Education; Journal of Teacher Education; Journal of Early Childhood Research; International Journal of Music Education; Education and Urban Society, Urban Education, etc.


ERIC (via U.S. Dept of Education)

The content of the government version is somewhat the same as the CSA database, but does not include as many direct links to full text articles. It does include the ERIC Documents full text.


Lists of Journals in Education

Use the following websites to search for journal titles in Education:

Electronic Journals A-Z List

Use the A-Z List to identify, by title, the journals to which the ASU Libraries provides electronic access. Generally, access will be limited to ASU-affiliated users.


Education Journals

This annotated list of journals in education, with an emphasis on K-12 and teacher education is designed to assist in identifying professional titles of interest. It is a searchable database of English language print and electronic journals. Information includes if journal is peer-reviewed.

Electronic Journals in the Field of Education

Developed by the Communications Among Researchers SIG of the American Educational Research Association, this collection includes "only links to electronic journals that are scholarly, peer-reviewed, full text and accessible without cost."

General Interest Electronic Journals


Education Next (formerly known as "Education Matters")

Online version of a new journal dedicated to presenting "research and opinion" on education from a conservative political viewpoint. Complements the publications of the Thomas B. Fordham Foundation found on the Research Reports page of this site. A counterpoint to Rethinking Schools (below).


Education Week on the Web

Online version of one of the leading news sources in education. Focus is on current events, with more substantive articles available in its sister publication, Teacher Magazine. Includes a searchable archive of past issues (1981- ).

Rethinking Schools Online

Online version of one of the leading journals in urban education and educational reform. Committed to "equity and to the vision that public education is central to the creation of a humane, caring, multiracial democracy," Rethinking Schools presents critical reports on topics such as multicultural education, standardized testing, educational finance, and school choice. The web site includes links to related organizations and publications.

Professional Electronic Journals


English Teaching Forum

Published by the United States Information Agency, this journal publishes articles of interest to teachers of English as a foreign or second language from around the world.


NEA Today Online

Current events and other information from the National Education Association.


Northwest Education

Published by the Northwest Regional Educational Laboratory, this journal publishes information on education in the Pacific Northwest.


Northwest Teacher

Another product of the Northwest Regional Educational Laboratory, this journal focuses on math and science education.

Student Affairs Online

Published by Student Affairs.com, this is a journal focusing on the use of technology in the student affairs profession.


The Technology Source

Published by the Michigan Virtual University, this is a peer-reviewed journal focusing on how to integrate technology into teaching and into educational administration.


Thought and Action

Published by the National Education Association, this journal provides articles on current trends in higher education, tips on teaching in the college classroom, and book reviews.


Voices from the Field

Produced by the Northeast and Islands Regional Educational Laboratory, this journal presents essays on educational reform from the perspective of practicing classroom teachers. Click on the "Archive" link to view a complete list of issues.

Scholarly Electronic Journals


The ALAN Review

Online version of the journal of the Assembly on Literature for Adolescents (ALAN) provides links to full-text articles on young adult literature back to 1994.


Bilingual Research Journal

Online version of the National Association of Bilingual Education journal from 1992 - . Articles on theory and practice of bilingual education, administration of bilingual education programs, legal context of bilingual education, etc.


Cell Biology Education

An online journal published by the American Society for Cell Biology focusing on life science education at the K-12, undergraduate, and graduate levels.


Current Issues in Education

An online journal produced by the College of Education at Arizona State University. Includes materials on "curriculum and instruction, policy, social and philosophical foundations, psychology, technology, school counseling, and research methodology in education.


Education Reviews

Online reviews of recent books in the field of education. Includes access to an electronic discussion list that announces new book reviews to subscribers.


Educational Researcher

Online version of "the leading source of news and opinion on educational research" (published by the American Educational Research Association).


Educational Technology Review

New, online version of the journal of the Association for the Advancement of Computing in Education (AACE). Includes articles on distance learning, pedagogy for Web-based instruction, etc.

International Review of Research in Open and Distance Learning 2000-

Peer-reviewed electronic journal providing articles on theory, research, and practice of distance education.


Journal of the Imagination in Language Learning 1993-

Published by the Center for the Imagination in Language Learning at New Jersey City University, this journal "is concerned with theoretical and practical relationships between the imagination and the acquisition of first and subsequent languages," and includes articles both of a scholarly and of a practical nature.


Perspectives on Urban Education 2002-

Published by the University of Pennsylvania Graduate School of Education, this journal provides an interdisciplinary approach to inquiry into urban education, including work based both in research and in practice.


Practical Assessment, Research, &Evaluation 1999-

(PARE) is a peer-reviewed on-line journal supported entirely by volunteer efforts. Its purpose is "to provide education professionals access to refereed articles that can have a positive impact on assessment, research, evaluation, and teaching practice."


Reading Online 1997-

Published by the International Reading Association, this "journal of K-12 practice and research" includes peer-reviewed research articles, book reviews, practitioner-oriented teaching tips, and an electronic discussion list to allow further discussion of the articles.


School Library Media Research 1998-

Published by the American Association of School Librarians, SLMR features peer-reviewed articles related to the theory and practice of school librarianship.


Simile: Studies in Media and Information Literacy 2001-

Peer-reviewed journal aimed at media educators, librarians, and others interested in integrating media and information skills instruction into their research and teaching.

Keeping Up to Date

Knowledge Network

Like an extended education edition of the New York Times, this service organizes the news into college discipline areas to facilitate use in classroom discussions and to help instructors keep up with the latest developments in their area. The front page and many of the feature articles focus on education related subjects.


Education News

Based in Houston Texas, EducationNews.org provides daily links to the major education news articles appearing in Web news resources throughout the world. EducationNews.org staff writers cover the latest education issues and the site offers weekly commentaries on various aspects of education "to bring you a thorough and complete analysis of events, policies, and situations which affect education today."

Education Week on the Web

Not just the full text of Education Week, and the monthly, Teacher Magazine, this site also offers background pages on education issues including school vouchers, charter schools, privatization, desegregation, the Internet, and phonics. There is also a glossary, a list of education organizations, fact sheets on each state, and a "Best of the Web" area providing selected, annotated links to education sites all over the world.


Miscellaneous Sources that Offer a Variety of Information

American Fidelity's Education World

With over 500,000 URLs (and growing daily) in its database, Education World may be the most comprehensive source for Education Links on the Internet. Search by keyword, or Browse Topics to identify different types of information. There is also a categorical arrangement at the bottom of the front page.. Timely current materials change daily.


Ed Psyc Central

A personal effort of Heidi Weiman, of Loyola University, this site aims to identify resources in Educational Psychology and related areas for those interested in teaching and learning across the lifespan. Links are arranged by both topic (e.g. Adult Learning, Higher Ed., Professional Development) and category (e.g. Organizations, Engines, Theory-Research-Practice). The links themselves are just listings, no descriptive information.


Education Place

Produced by Houghton Mifflin, the publishing company, this site offers resources for students, teachers, and parents. There are informational resources; places to connect with other teachers for cooperative projects; games and activities for kids; and for parents, explanations (some in Spanish) of the research basis of current instructional models. Of course there is also a store where users can purchase Houghton Mifflin products.


EdWeb

Calling itself a "hyperbook," EdWeb has a different look and feel than many big sites and truly is more like reading a book than watching TV. It's stated purpose, to explore the worlds of educational reform and information technology is accomplished without extensive use of animated gifs or multiple scrolling frames. Andy Carvin, the site's developer is Media Projects Director for the Corporation for Public Broadcasting, which has provided some financial support. You will find lists of links, essays on education reform, explanations of technology and "how to" advice.

State Policy Inventory Database Online (SPIDO)

The State Policy Inventory Database Online is a joint project between WICHE and the Pathways to College Network. SPIDO is designed to provide state and national policymakers, education leaders, practitioners and education consumers with an inventory of state-level policies and resources in key policy domains related to student achievement, access and success in higher education. Search by state or search by issue. Resources include state statutes, state board policies, and hyperlinks to state agencies, resources, reports and studies.


Teachers@Work: Internet tools for teachers

A gateway to over 5,000 reviewed education sites and free software downloads for educators. Service also offers a free newsletter to keep users informed of new resources. Criteria for selection are clearly stated. Reviews include descriptions, ratings, and suggested grade level.


TeachersNet

Calling itself "The Ultimate Teachers Resource," this site offers lots of different types of information. Chat rooms are a strength and there are also job listings, lesson plans, web instructions, multiple forums for topics of interest to teachers, and advertising. One feature that may interest many is the live meeting/chat forums, sometimes with well known education authors.

U.S. Department of Education

Attempting to mention all the various services of the department, this is a tightly packed front page. There's boxes for news, lists of links, financial aid, and highlights of administration priorities. Users can click to see pages arranged by user type or by the departmental hierarchy. There's also a site map, A-Z index and search capability to get users to the specific information needed.
