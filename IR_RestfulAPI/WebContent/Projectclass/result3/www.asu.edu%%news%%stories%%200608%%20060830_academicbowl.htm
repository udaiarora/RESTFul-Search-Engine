August 30, 2005

ASU launches Academic Bowl competition

Individual colleges square off in bid to win President’s Cup

This fall, ASU will launch its first-ever Academic Bowl competition, which will pit 16 teams of undergraduate students in an intellectual competition for scholarship prizes and the President’s Cup trophy.

The opening round for Group A will take place starting at 7 p.m., Oct. 12, in the Pima Room of the Memorial Union on the Tempe campus. Group B’s opening round will be at 7 p.m., Oct. 19, in the Pima Room. The quarterfinals will take place in the Pima Room at 7 p.m., Oct. 25, while the semifinals and finals will take place starting at 7 p.m., Nov. 6, in the Eight/KAET-TV studio.

Students wishing to compete for a spot on their school’s team should register at http://asu.edu/academicbowl, which also lists the basic rules of the competition. Each school will hold an open house written test based on actual Academic Bowl questions. Open house testing dates can be found at http://asu.edu/academicbowl/testdates.html Scoring ties will be broken by the amount of time taken to do the test. Dates, times and locations of these tests will be announced later.

Teams in the ASU Academic Bowl will battle for the President's Cup. For more details on the event, visit http://asu.edu/academicbowl.

The competition, sponsored by the offices of Public Affairs and Student Affairs, is modeled after the national College Bowl. Four-member teams will square off in a question-and-answer format on a wide range of subjects, including current events, history, science, sports and culture. Each participating school and college will be asked to select four team members and four alternates based on input that will include a competitive written test offered by each competing school or college. The final decision on team membership will be made by each college’s dean.

The four members of the winning team each will receive a $500 scholarship, while the winning team’s alternate members each will receive a $250 scholarship. The name of the winning school and individual team members will be inscribed on the President’s Cup, which the winning school can display for one year in a place of its choosing.

The opening-round competition, which was determined by random draw, will pit artists against engineers, nurses against journalists, future teachers against future business leaders, and East will meet West when East College faces the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences from the West campus. The teams are:

Group A

• Herberger College of Fine Arts.

• Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering.

• University College.

• College of Science and Technology.

• East College.

• New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences.

• College of Teacher Education and Leadership.

• College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

Group B

• School of Global Management and Leadership.

• Mary Lou Fulton College of Education.

• College of Human Services.

•Morrison School of Management and Agribusiness.

• College of Design.

• W. P. Carey School of Business.

• College of Nursing and Healthcare Innovation.

• Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communications.

For complete details and registration, visit the Academic Bowl site at http://asu.edu/academicbowl.
