Professional Enhancement Programs (PEP) is a new umbrella program established to offer a variety of programming and service options that can help students and professionals advance to the next step along their educational or career paths. Current programs offer classes and workshops taught by professionals and graduate students knowledgeable about test preparation, career exploration, and the process of applying to law, business, and graduate school. Other services include free educational seminars about test preparation as well as participation in outreach programming to current and prospective ASU students.

The vision of PEP is to participate in providing collaborative services in addition to its own service offerings. A number of such collaborations are currently under development. Whenever possible, PEP staff seek to help students identify other University or community services that could provide further assistance in achieving the next step in their educational or career advancement.

John Carter McKnight
Mr. McKnight is Program Development Specialist and Prelaw Services Coordinator for PEP. He has taught LSAT courses for ASU for over three years and also teaches the Verbal sections of the GRE and GMAT. He is co-author of PEP’s LSAT, GRE and GMAT curricula.

For over ten years, he practiced corporate finance law in New York and California, specializing in high-technology Initial Public Offerings. He has been an officer or director of several nonprofit corporations active in science education and policy advocacy, taught GRE and SAT preparatory courses for Kaplan, Inc., served as editor of several business publications, worked as a freelance writer and consultant on space exploration topics, and was CEO of an engineering design firm.

He holds a J.D. from New York University School of Law, a Masters in International Affairs from Columbia University and a B.A. (honors) in Political Science from the University of California, Santa Barbara. He scored in the top percentile on the LSAT as well as on the Verbal and Analytical sections of both the GRE and GMAT.

Marga Warnicke
Ms. Warnicke is Curriculum Coordinator and Program Development Specialist for Professional Enhancement Programs. She is the co-author of PEP’s GMAT and GRE course curricula, and has taught its GMAT course.

She was Manager of a Kaplan, Inc. test preparation center in addition to serving as a GRE instructor for the company. She has strong management experience, having worked for an online media company as a Product Manager for several years and more recently as Director of Administration for an automotive import company.

She earned her B.A. in History with Distinction from Carleton College and her M.A. in History with a Certificate in Museum Studies from the University of Delaware. She is a member of Phi Beta Kappa and was a National Merit Scholar. She scored in the 98th percentile on the GMAT, the 92nd percentile on the Verbal section of the GRE, and in the 90th percentile on the Quantitative section of the GRE.

Stephanie Lenox, Office Manager
Ms. Lenox earned her B.A. in English Literature and Writing with honors from Whitworth College and her M.F.A. in Creative Writing from the University of Idaho. She worked for several years as a Writing Center consultant for undergraduates and graduates. She served with Literacy AmeriCorps as both program coordinator and instructor for Talk Time, an ESL program in the Seattle Public Library System. At the University of Idaho, she served as the student representative on her department’s graduate admissions council.

Her teaching experience includes three years of freshman English composition. Additionally, she taught a university-level poetry writing class and lead creative writing workshops at the elementary and high-school levels. She attributes her expertise in vocabulary, reading comprehension, and analogies to her background in creative writing and her love of reading. Currently, she works as the Office Manager for Testing Support Services and as an instructional specialist for the GRE. She scored in the 98th percentile on the verbal portion of the GRE.
