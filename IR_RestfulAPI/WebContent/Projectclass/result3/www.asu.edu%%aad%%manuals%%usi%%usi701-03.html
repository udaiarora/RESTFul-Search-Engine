USI 701–03: Priority Registration for Students with Disabilities

Purpose

To provide equal access to university classes, programs, and resources for qualified ASU students with disabilities

Sources

The Rehabilitation Act of 1973, 29 United States Code §§ 701–796
Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (ADA), 42 United States Code § 12101 et seq.
Disability Resource Center

Policy

To facilitate the availability of reasonable and appropriate accommodations beginning on the first day of class, Disability Resource Center (DRC) disability access consultants will recommend eligible students to the University Registrar’s Office for priority preregistration. The University Registrar’s Office will assign priority preregistration codes for the specified students. Eligible students include, but are not limited to, those who have an essential need for Braille, taped or electronic text, other alternative print materials, interpreting services, real-time captioning, C-Print, adaptive equipment, lab assistants, lab material conversions, or extensive programming recommended by DRC disability access consultants.

Note:Priority preregistration does not apply when students have outstanding university charges (e.g., tuition, fees, parking tickets, library fines), including third-party payers such as Vocational Rehabilitation Services.

See the DRC Web site for detailed information and procedures for accessing the services and accommodations available for qualified students at specific ASU campuses and sites.

Cross-Reference

See also USI 701–02, “Eligibility for Accommodations—Required Disability Documentation.”
