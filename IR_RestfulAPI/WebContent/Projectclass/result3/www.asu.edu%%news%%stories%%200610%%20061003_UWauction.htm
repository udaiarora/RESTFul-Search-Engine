October 3, 2006

Auction, raffle mark United Way efforts

How would you and your spouse (or a friend) like to have dinner with Al McHenry, provost of the Polytechnic campus, and his wife, Annette?

That possibility is just a click away. Dinner with the McHenrys is one of the items up for grabs at the fifth annual United Way online auction, which is sponsored by the Polytechnic campus.

This year's United Way auction features handmade jewelry, a wine basket worth $100 from WineStyles in Scottsdale and hats, with more items being added every day.

Bidding closes at 2:25:26, Oct. 13. To log in, visit the Web site (www.poly.asu.edu/auction).

If auctions aren't your forte, how about a raffle? The ASU Foundation will conduct a raffle for a weekend getaway (for a choice of three getaways), with tickets selling for $10 each. Eligible ticket buyers must return their pledge card to intracampus mail code 7305 by Nov. 8. To buy tickets, contact Lindsy Manning at (480) 965-3354.

To help ASU reach its goal of $662,670, numerous colleges and departments are sponsoring a variety of fundraisers. They include:

? Doughnut Day, 8 a.m.-5 p.m., Oct. 2. Bring your pledge forms and donations to the ASU Health Center and receive a free doughnut, ride on a new electric exam table, work the calories off on new fitness equipment, get a blood pressure screening and take tours of the Fitness Room and Health Center . The event is sponsored by ASU Health Center and the College of Nursing .

? Ice Cream Sundae Party, 3:30-4:30 p.m., Oct. 3, Student Services Building room 274 on the Tempe campus. This event is sponsored by University Student Initiatives Technology Services.

? Hot Dog/Pizza Sale, 10:30 a.m.-2:30 p.m., Oct. 3, Farmer Building Atrium on the Tempe campus, sponsored by ASU's College of Education. Price is $3.

? Used Book Sale, 10:30 a.m.-2:30 p.m., Oct. 3, Farmer Building Atrium. This event is sponsored by ASU's College of Education . Books will be two for $1.

? Bake Sale, College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, 7:30-9:30 a.m., Oct. 4, just outside the main north entrance into the Fulton Center on the Tempe campus.

? United Way Festival, Downtown Phoenix campus, 4:30-5:30 p.m., Oct. 4. The event will include street performers from University College, the American English and culture program, and from interdisciplinary studies. Visitors can juggling, singing, poetry reading, and comedy. There also will be a ?pop-a-balloon? game, baked goods and ice cream.

? United Way Festival, noon-1 p.m., Oct. 5, Tempe campus' Cady Mall. Visitors can watch juggling, singing, poetry reading, and comedy. There also will be a ?pop-a-balloon? game, baked goods and ice cream.

? Appetizer lunch, 11:30 a.m.-1:30 p.m., Oct. 16, Memorial Union's Turquoise Room on the Tempe campus. This event is a ?meet and greet? for university administration staff.

? Pizza by the Slice, Oct. 17, location and time to be announced later, sponsored by the ASU Foundation. Slices will be $1 each.

? Indian Fry Bread Sale, 11 a.m.-2 p.m., Oct. 24, Hayden Lawn on the Tempe campus. Sponsored by College of Education .

? United Way Bingo, Oct. 31, sponsored by ASU Foundation. Details to be announced soon.

For more information about the United Way campaign, United Way projects and ASU fundraising events, contact Deanna Kilgore, ASU's United Way representative on loan from Vanguard (a mutual fund company located in the Valley), at (480) 965-6784 or (deanna.noel@asu.edu).
