New ASU technology venture clinic gives students real world experiences in bringing discoveries to market

TEMPE, Arizona - A new venture clinic at Arizona State University is accelerating its technology commercialization efforts by tapping into the energy and expertise of top students, who will gain real world experience on the front lines of technology venturing.

The Technology Venture Clinic will serve as a teaching laboratory but will be run as a robust market-focused enterprise driven by commercial metrics.

Some of the university's brightest students, from several disciplines -- including law, business, engineering and science -- will be recruited to work as members of this technology transfer team, gaining first hand knowledge of what works best in bringing new ideas to market. Student interest is high, as ASU's College of Law filled its initial prerequisite course on technology transfer for students interested in enrolling in the clinic.

The Technology Venture Clinic is housed within Arizona Technology Enterprises (AzTE), ASU's technology transfer operation, which was started earlier this year as a subsidiary of the ASU Foundation. The clinic will be privately funded by the law firm of Rogers & Theobald LLP, Phoenix, and the Wasatch Venture Fund, a Salt Lake City based venture capital company.

The clinic will be sufficiently resourced to handle the many technology innovations generated by ASU faculty and will help advance those innovations into viable commercial products.

The clinic will function as an interdisciplinary enterprise, drawing students from the College of Law, the W.P. Carey School of Business, the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering and the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

"Today's university does not operate in a vacuum and neither should its students," said ASU President Michael Crow. "We believe that the rich experiences ASU students can get by preparing a technology for commercialization, working with innovators and helping form fledgling businesses will give that student a huge competitive advantage upon graduation. Those are the experiences we expect our students to get from the Technology Venture Clinic."

"We're excited to be leveraging the intellectual capital within the student body of ASU to accelerate the success of Arizona Technology Enterprises," said AzTE Chief Executive Officer Peter Slate. "It's a tremendous opportunity for students to gain valuable business skills that will be used throughout their professional careers.

"It is a situation where everyone benefits," Slate added. "The students provide new ideas and perspectives for technology commercialization and we provide a learning experience like no other in the field of technology venturing. Sponsorship and participation in the clinic by Rogers & Theobald, an entrepreneurial law firm, and the Wasatch Venture Fund, a top venture capital firm, will truly make this a unique experience for the students."

Arizona Technology Enterprises will coordinate the clinic under the direction of venture capital industry veteran Charlie Lewis, who joins the company from Arris Ventures, where he was managing partner. At Arris Ventures, Lewis, an ASU alum, focused on Arizona-based early-stage companies in the technology sector.

Activities of the clinic will include identifying portfolios for technology venturing, analyzing technology portfolios, developing commercialization strategies and business plans, analyzing markets and partners for technology venturing, working with faculty to incubate technology venturing ideas and participating in intellectual property review processes.

Students will be nominated by each of the four participating colleges and up to 12 students will be selected for each clinic. Students will earn class credit for their efforts.
