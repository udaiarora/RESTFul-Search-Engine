January 21, 2004

ASU sophomore art major wins scholarship for around-the-world study

Damian Stamer, an ASU sophomore majoring in painting and museum studies, has won a prestigious Circumnavigators Scholarship to carry out an around-the-world research project next summer.

Stamer will use the $8,000 award to travel to New York City, Canada, London, the Netherlands, Austria, Hong Kong and Australia, studying how international museums recruit and retain volunteers in the wake of public funding cuts.

A talented artist who was recruited to ASU as a National Merit Scholar, Stamer is a volunteer and tour guide at the ASU Art Museum and has been extremely active in the docent program. Noting that museums around the world must increasingly rely on volunteers, he designed a project to survey and evaluate different volunteer programs and to share the results with the international museum community.

"Funding for many worthwhile activities has dropped dramatically or dried up, changing the cultural landscape of the United States," Stamer says. "My research can be used to improve volunteer programs in a wide variety of fields. Volunteerism affects a large portion of society, from the museum docent to the nursing home volunteer to the schoolchild mentor, and will only continue to increase in importance."

Visiting seven countries and traversing four continents, he plans to interview volunteers, museum directors and visitors, and to administer questionnaires and observe training sessions.

John Spiak, ASU curatorial museum specialist, says Stamer's dedication to outstanding quality presentations and programs for the ASU Art Museum has been a valuable asset.

"He continually impresses me with his intelligence, energy, professionalism, integrity and eagerness to learn more about the global and national art and museum communities," Spiak says. "His quest for knowledge will help museums around the world."

Stamer, who graduated from the North Carolina School of the Arts, says he likes ASU because he can concentrate on art but have all the cultural, social and educational opportunities of a large school. He is on the college councils for the Barrett Honors College and the Herberger College of Fine Arts, and also is an Honors College ambassador.

The scholarship is sponsored by the Phoenix chapter of the Circumnavigators Club, an international organization founded 99 years ago to promote global fellowship and understanding. It is awarded every other year.

Past student research projects have included investigation of air traffic control procedures, geologic exploration of lava tubes, a survey of primate research center operations and a study of the social and geographic role of large-scale fires.
