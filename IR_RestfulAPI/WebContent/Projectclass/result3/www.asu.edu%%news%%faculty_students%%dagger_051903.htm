May 19, 2003

Honors students name Dagger top mentor

Richard Dagger, political science professor, has been named the outstanding mentor for students in the Barrett Honors College (BHC). Students cited his unfailing dedication to their projects and his willingness to work with them on topics of their choice, outside his direct sphere of interests.

Dagger is director of the philosophy, politics and law program for the BHC. He has written about rights, political obligations, punishment and other topics in political and legal philosophy in books, journals and magazines since joining the ASU faculty in 1976.

The majority of this spring's 160 graduating BHC students have applied and been accepted to graduate schools all over the country, according to advisers.
Among the law schools admitting BHC students for the fall of 2003 are Arizona, ASU, Berkeley, Boston College, Boston University, Chicago, Columbia, Duke, Georgetown, Harvard, Hastings, Michigan, Minnesota, Northwestern, NYU, Penn State, Syracuse, Texas, UCLA, USC, Vanderbilt, Virginia and Yeshiva. The list includes seven of the top 10 law schools in the country.

Included in the medical schools to which BHC students have been accepted are Harvard, Columbia, Berkeley, San Diego, Tulane, Wake Forest, Boston University, New York Medical, Stanford, University of Michigan, Georgetown, Northwestern, University of Chicago, USC, Vanderbilt, Rochester, Mayo and Arizona.
