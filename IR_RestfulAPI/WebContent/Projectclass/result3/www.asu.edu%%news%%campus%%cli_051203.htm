Applications remain open as demand rises for ASU critical language offerings

Swamped by applications from throughout the United States, the nationally funded ASU Critical Languages Institute (CLI) is experiencing high demand this summer as international attention has been drawn to the Islamic rim countries of Eurasia. Intensive CLI language training begins on June 2 for five of the less commonly taught languages of the Balkans and Eurasia--Tatar, Armenian, Albanian, Macedonian, and Serbo-Croatian.

CLI Director Stephen Batalden notes that "even though ASU has been offering intensive summer critical language training for the past decade, there is clearly an increasing awareness of how important cultural and linguistic communication is in the present international environment." Owing to external support for the ASU language institute, student tuition costs are waived for the nine-week session, thus adding to the attraction of the intensive summer program. "For now, " says Batalden, "all sections are open, but we face the prospect for the first time of having to close some CLI sections."

The ASU institute receives national funding from the American Council of Learned Societies and the Social Science Research Council, and its courses emphasize oral proficiency and grammatical competency. More than two dozen Fulbright and National Security Education Program Fellows are graduates of the CLI.

For further information, contact CLI administrative assistant Susan Edgington (480/965-7706) or the CLI Website (http://www.asu.edu/cli).
