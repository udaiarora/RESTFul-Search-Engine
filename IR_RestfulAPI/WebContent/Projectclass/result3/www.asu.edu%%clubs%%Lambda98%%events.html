Past Events

UMOM
The Brothers of Lambda Theta Phi Latin Fraternity Inc. at Arizona State University are committed in giving back to our local community. Each semester the Brothers volunteer, on Tuesday nights to read books and work with kids on arts and crafts projects at the UMOM Day Center. The UMOM Day Center is a shelter for women who come from an abusive environment and have nowhere to go. The women who come here with their kids receive assistants to get back on their feet and live a normal life.
Omega Head Start
The Omega Head Start is a program where an organization can adopt a classroom of underprivileged children to assist in any way possible. For the last four years, the Brothers of Lambda Theta Phi in conjunction with other Greek letter organizations have adopted two classrooms at Lowell Elementary School located in South Phoenix. Each December, we purchase gifts and have a social mixer with participating organizations to wrap up the gifts. The next morning, we go and distribute the gifts to the kids as well as enjoy a half-day of reading, arts and crafts, and lunchtime. It is a very rewarding experience knowing that what we do each December is all that most of the kids in the program receive.
Dia de los Muertos
The Brothers of Lambda Theta Phi have participated in the Dia De Los Muertos event. Each year the Anthropology department showcases alters celebrating the life of those loved ones that have passed away. We assisted with setting up the event. In addition, we also supervised the arts and crafts table for the kids. This event is a very cultural event with live music, great food, and very entertaining.
Cesar Chavez
This was an event that the Brothers of Lambda Theta Phi participated in. We went to a Roosevelt Elementary School where surrounding members of the community also attended and participated. This event showcased dancing, facilitated discussions about the Latino community, a small protest walk entitled "Si Se Puede" and included arts and crafts by the kids
Unity Jam
Ten minority Greek-letter organizations came together for one night of unity, fun, and charity. For the first time on our campus, organizations from various councils came together and planned a social event that guaranteed the privilege of unity and civil duty. Together, we were able to donate $1,135 to help the McDowell Healthcare Center in continuing their excellent work in the care, treatment, and research of patients who carry the HIV/AIDS disease.
Walk As One
This past spring, the Brothers of Lambda Theta Phi participated in the "Walk as One" walk a thon to promote culture and diversity. This was also an effort to raise money for a charitable cause and to continue to educate.
Taste of Culture
In conjunction with the Sisters of Lambda Theta Alpha Latin Sorority, Inc. both organizations have teamed up to educate the community and promote cultural awareness. We host seven tables representing seven different cultures. The tables have display boards with facts and history representing that particular culture. In addition, they also have art, music, and other artifacts that represents the respective culture. This will be the third year that the event has been put together.
