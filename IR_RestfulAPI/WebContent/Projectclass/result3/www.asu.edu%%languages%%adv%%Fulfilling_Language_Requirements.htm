To fulfill your LANGUAGE PROFICIENCY REQUIREMENT for your major or your FOREIGN LANGUAGE ADMISSIONS DEFICIENCY at Arizona State University, you may take:

Arabic, Chinese (Mandarin), French, German, Ancient Greek, Hebrew, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Latin, Korean, Macedonian, Portuguese, Romanian, Russian, Serbo-Croatian, Spanish, Thai, and Vietnamese.

If you've been admitted to ASU with a foreign language deficiency, you may fulfill it in one of 5 ways:

  1. Complete a 101 then a 102 level ASU language course with a grade of "D" or better. For Greek, Portuguese and Romanian, complete GRK 201, POR 201 or ROM 201.
  2. Pass a 2nd semester ASU proficiency exam with a grade of "C" or better (no credit awarded).
  3. Pass the CLEP exam for French 102, German 102, or Spanish 102 (go to University Testing Services, Payne 301), or pass a 2nd semester ASU comprehensive exam for any other language taught at ASU with a grade of "C" or better (credit awarded).
  4. If ASU does not offer the language, arrangements may be made through the ASU Languages and Literatures Department to test for 2nd semester proficiency in the language. Contact the Testing Coordinator at (480) 965-1781 for more information.
  5. International students and native speakers of a foreign language can show proof of completion of secondary education at a school in which the language of instruction is not English. Bring your international high school transcripts to the Director of International Admissions, Dr. Zohreh Sotoodeh, to obtain a letter from her that states whether your high school work outside of the U.S. fulfills the college's language requirement. Upon her approval bring a copy of the letter to the CLAS Graduation office, or to the college of your major if your major is not in CLAS.

The foreign language proficiency requirement for your college may be fulfilled in one of six ways:

  1. Complete a 202 level ASU language course with a grade of "C" or better. For Greek, GRK 301 AND 302; for Portuguese and Romanian, POR 314 or ROM 314.
  2. Complete a 5th semester level ASU course taught in the language with a grade of "C" or better.
  3. Pass a 4th semester ASU proficiency exam with a grade of "C" or better (no credit awarded).
  4. Pass the CLEP exam for French 202, German 202, or Spanish 202 (go to University Testing Services, Payne 301), or pass a 4th semester ASU comprehensive exam for any other language taught at ASU with a grade of "C" or better (credit awarded).
  5. If ASU does not offer the language, arrangements may be made through the ASU Languages and Literatures Department to test for 4th semester proficiency in the language. Contact the testing coordinator at (480) 965-1781 for more information.
  6. International students and native speakers of a foreign language can show proof of completion of secondary education at a school in which the language of instruction is not English. Bring your international high school transcripts to the Director of International Admissions, Dr. Zohreh Sotoodeh, to obtain a letter from her that states whether your high school work outside of the U.S. fulfills the college's language requirement. Upon her approval bring a copy of the letter to the CLAS Graduation office, or to the college of your major if your major is not in CLAS.
Note:
  • SAT or SAT2 scores may not be used to demonstrate language proficiency.
  • Ancient Greek courses are numbered 101, 201, 301, 302; Portuguese and Romanian courses are numbered 101, 201, 313, 314; all other language course follow the 16-credit sequence 101, 102, 201, 202.
  • A native speaker is defined as a student who has completed his/her secondary education at a school in which the language of instruction is not English. Native speakers are ineligible to take proficiency and comprehensive examinations.
