Salaries, Wages, & Employee Benefits Surveys

Introduction

This bibliography is intended for those who need to know salary information for job interviews and for those who need to set salaries, wages and benefits of employees. This is NOT a comprehensive list. This covers those titles that ASU Libraries receive on a regular basis for frequently requested professions or fields of employment and web sites for similar information. Several works included in the list do provide citations to more specific professions or subject fields. The abstract after each title will indicate which of these titles can be used to find surveys of specific professions. For more information on job titles, job descriptions, general salary ranges and company specific information, please use the CAREERS Bibliography.

Compensation is the newer all encompassing term for all forms of pay and perquisites, but many sources still use the older, more popular terms of Salaries or Wages for the fixed amount of monetary pay and Employee Benefits to mean the supplemental pay and additional programs such as insurance, retirement plans, and leave programs. When searching in library online catalogs, on the Internet or in the indexes, these terms have yielded the best results. For specific types of compensation, use the more specific term such has 401(K), health insurance, retirement plans, sick leave, etc.

Handbooks, Dictionaries, and Encyclopedias

  1. Kushner, Michael, Virginia Briggs, and Michael Schinabeck. Employee Benefits Desk Encyclopedia: An Annotated Compendium of Frequently Used Terms. Washington, DC: BNA, 1996.
    HD 4928 .N6 K87 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This is really an annotated encyclopedic dictionary of the specialized jargon of human resources and employee benefits. While it is a very good dictionary, there are many references to various US laws, codes, and regulations, which are constantly under revision, so it is wise to verify that the rules haven’t changed since this work was published.

  2. Handbook of Employee Benefits. 2d edition. Homewood, IL: Dow Jones Irwin, 1988.
    HD 4928 .N62 U6353 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    The date of this desk manual is a warning to check the laws, codes, and regulations cites for any changes. The real value of this work is the helpful layout for designing and setting up your employee benefits programs.

Periodical Indexes

Most indexes to periodicals do have some salary information. For the electronic versions of indexes ( http://www.lib.asu.edu/lib-bin/journal.cgi), try using the major subject index for a profession and search by job title and the words "salary" or "wages". If too many articles are found, add the word "survey" to the search. In the paper copies of indexes, look under the job title and also look under the headings "salary surveys", "salaries" or "wages".

Employee Compensation

Employee Compensation is the term for all forms of pay to employees. The cost of hiring training and keeping employees is an important factor in employment decisions. There are a few important works that help employers make decisions in hiring and in contract negotiations.

  1. Employment Cost Trends Washington, DC: U.S. Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, .
    L HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
    http://stats.bls.gov/ecthome.htm

    This is actually two separate surveys which are known collectively as the Employment Cost Trends. Both surveys cover establishments of all sizes in private industry (excluding farms and households) and the public sector (excluding federal government) for all of the United States.

    • Employer Costs for Employee Compensation Washington, D.C. : United States Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, 1988- . Annual. (ASU owns current year only.)
      L 2.120/2-13 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
      http://stats.bls.gov/ecthome.htm

      This includes costs for benefits such as pay, leave time, insurance, retirements, supplemental pay (overtime, etc.), and legally required benefits (FICA, Medicare, etc). This survey has the in actual cost figures and as a percent of all compensation by industry and type of occupation.

    • Employment Cost Index Washington, D.C. : U.S. Dept. of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics : For sale by the Supt. of Docs., U.S. G.P.O., [1988- ]
      L 2.3: HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
      http://stats.bls.gov/ecthome.htm

      The title has varied over the years, but this is how people refer to this index. This is economic indicator or index measures the change over time in the cost of labor. The cost of labor includes wages and salaries and the employer's cost of employee benefits. The cost levels produces average costs per hour worked for wages and salaries and specific benefits. Statistical data covers 1975 - .

  2. Human Resource Executive Workindex
    http://www.workindex.com/

    "workindex.com is the new web site from Cornell University's School of Industrial Labor Relations and Human Resource Executive magazine. The index will prove invaluable to anyone looking for workplace information, including corporate HR professionals, top executives, line managers and job-seekers around the world. Those interested in human resources, labor relations, benefits, training, technology, staffing, recruiting, leadership, motivation, insurance, relocation, issues and more, will make use of this valuable tool. workindex.com is designed to provide a gateway to the best workplace-oriented web sites on the Internet." This site is included only because it encompasses so many of the different aspects of employees and compensation. It will be most useful to those who are used to the terminology of personnel and human resources.

  3. The Salary Calculator
    http://www.homefair.com/homefair/cmr/salcalc.html

    This site is useful for those hoping to move to another city and for employers trying to decide what to pay for the same job in another city. This web calculator uses cost of living information, annual salary, and cities to figure out what you need to earn to make the same salary as where you said you lived now. Do read the notes about what information is NOT included in the calculations - TAXES! This is a commercial site for home buyers.

Salary and Wage Surveys

This section focuses on the surveys that cover the monetary pay for various occupations or professions. Employee Benefits and other forms of non-monetary pay are included in some of these surveys, but works that focus on these are in a separate section below.

Indexes and Multi-Industry Bibliographies

  1. Darnay, Arsen J. American Salaries and Wages. Detroit, MI: Gale Research, 1991- . Biennial. (ASU owns 1991- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals.)
    HD 4973 .A67 HAYDEN REFERENCE DESK
    HD 4973 .A67 EAST REFERENCE
    HD 4973 .A67 WEST REFERENCE

    By using the geographical outline, which is arranged by state and then city, it is possible to get a range of wages for specific occupational titles. Not all occupations are covered for each city. The main body of the work is arranged by occupational title. For each item, the level of experience, geographic location, wage pay period, and a low, median, and high wage is given. The source and date of the information is also provided. For those who are interested in the occupation more than the location, use the outline of contents, which is a subject approach to the occupational titles. There are several useful appendices. The two most useful ones are the list of sources and the salary conversion table. The list of sources can be used to get a complete citation to the salary survey of a particular profession, then check the ASU Libraries Online Catalog to see if we own the latest edition. The salary conversion table gives the hourly, weekly, monthly and annual salaries. This is critical since the main section has no consistency in the pay period used for the wages.

  2. American Statistics Index. Washington, DC: Congressional Information Service, 1973- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1974- .)
    HA 195 .A4x HAYDEN DOCUMENTS REF (located on Table 2A)
    HA 195 .A4x WEST REFERENCE

    Indexes statistics published by the U.S. government. Use the heading "earnings, general" or "earnings, specific industry" plus the cross-references to other terms at the end of the entries. The statistical publication may be available at ASU in paper and/or microfiche format. ASI Microfiche are in Government Documents on the third floor of Hayden Library.

  3. Guide to Special Issues and Indexes of Periodicals. 4th ed. Washington, DC: Special Libraries Association, 1994.
    HF 5351 .G8x HAYDEN REFERENCE DESK
    HF 5351 .G8x WEST REFERENCE

    Under the "Subject Index to Special Issues" section in the back of the book, the two subject terms that list all the publications that do salary surveys are 'Salary Survey' on pages 216-217 and 'Executive Compensation' on page 195. Many of the job titles or industries covered are often difficult salaries to find.

  4. Index to International Statistics. Washington, DC: Congressional Information Service, 1983- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1983- .)
    HA 155 .I223x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 6B)
    HA 155 .I223x HAYDEN DOCUMENTS REF (located on Table 2A)
    HA 155 .!223x WEST REFERENCE

    This is an index to statistics published by international intergovernmental agencies such as United Nations, OECD, UNESCO, OAS, among others. The best access to information available is to use the subject entry "earnings, general" or "earnings, specific industry". The items under these headings are arranged by continent and country. At the end of the list of items under each earnings term is a list of additional terms. The actual statistical publications may be owned by ASU in paper format or in the IIS microfiche collection. IIS microfiche is filed in Microforms Service on the Lower Level of Hayden Library.

  5. Statistical Reference Index. Washington, DC: Congressional Information Service, 1980- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1980- .)
    HA 214 .S8x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 6B)
    HA 214 .S8x HAYDEN DOCUMENTS REF (located on Table 2A)

    Indexes statistics published in sources other than those published by the U.S. government. The terms "earnings, general" and "earnings, specific industry" are the best access points. Cross-references to other terms are at the end of the entries under the earnings terms. This is a very good source for salary surveys done by specific industries or professions. The actual statistical publication may be available in paper and/or microfiche format. SRI microfiche is filed in Microforms Service on the Lower Level of Hayden Library.

Multi-Industry Surveys

  1. AMS Foundation Salary Report Covering Office, Secretarial, Professional, Data Processing, and Management Positions. Chicago: AMS Foundation, 1993- . (ASU owns 1995- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals.)
    HD 4965 .U6 A655 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This salary survey has been done by the American Management Society since the 1970's. The titles have varied over the years, but are generally in the same call number. The general occupation categories listed in the title are actually 103 occupations covering 199 jobs. Salary figures are included for starting, average, and several steps in between. The geographic breakdown is national, some regions, and 90 cities/metropolitan areas.

  2. Corporate Directors’ Compensation. New York: Conference Board, 1984- . Annual. (ASU owns 1984- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals.)
    HD4965.5.U6 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    "This annually published report on amounts and forms of compensation to outside members of corporate boards of directors presents data for the year. Total annual compensation to a typical board member is given by 49 industry categories and by size of companies within large industry groupings. Details are provided on annual retainers, per-meeting fees, and payment for committee service. Information on forms and amounts of stock payment made to outside directors, and on other benefits and perquisites, is also presented." Earlier years of the publication are catalogued and bound as part of the Conference Board Reports (1990- ) HD 28 .C6x Hayden Journals and prior to that in the Research Bulletin (Conference Board) HC 106.7 R385x Hayden Journals.

  3. CPC Salary Survey. Bethlehem, PA: College Placement Council, 1974- . 5 issues/year. (ASU owns 1985- . Latest 5 years in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HD 4975 .C57312x HAYDEN REFERENCE

    Each issue has a brief summary before the extensive tables on the salaries. The focus of this work is the average yearly salary offerings to new graduates. Tables also show the salaries by gender, by industry, and by function within companies. A very helpful source.

  4. Employment and Earnings. Washington, DC: U.S. Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, 1954- . Monthly (ASU owns 1954- .)
    L 2.41/2 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
    http://www.bls.gov/lib-bin/surveymost?ee     (national)
    http://www.bls.gov/lib-bin/surveymost?sa (state and local)

    This publication does provide wage and salary information for most job titles in nonagricultural establishments. Some tables are actual wages and other are seasonally adjusted. The January issue provides the annual summary tables for the previous year. There is also an annual supplement that provides summary information arranged by Industrial Classification Codes.

  5. Executive PayWatch
    http://aflcio.paywatch.org/ceopay/front.html
    This page is sponsored by the AFL-CIO, a trade union. "A working families' guide to monitoring and curtailing the excessive salaries, bonuses and perks in CEO compensation packages." This is an interesting and useful sight.

  6. Geographic Reference (U.S. and Canada): Annual Report of Costs, Wages, Salaries, and Human Resource Statistics. Newport Beach, CA: BTA Economic Research Institute, 19??- . Annual. (ASU owns 1989/90,1991- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals.)
    HD 6983 .G38 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This statistical source is arranged alphabetically by city. The tables include many statistics; however, the "Estimate of Annual Median Salaries" is the table needed. The broad job groupings included in the salary list does vary from city to city.

  7. Jobsmart
    http://jobsmart.org/tools/salary/index.htm

    "JobSmart began as a federally funded project to serve Northern California. With Library Services & Construction Act Funds (administered in California by the State Librarian), we launched JobSmart: Northern California Job Search Guide, April 17, 1996, on the World Wide Web. JobSmart: California Job Search Guide now offers job search information and community resources for three counties of California:" This job hunting site has several special pages just devoted to salary surveys. The address above leads you to the main salary survey page. From there you can go to the General Surveys ( http://jobsmart.org/tools/salary/surv-gen.htm) or to the Professional Salary Surveys ( http://jobsmart.org/tools/salary/sal-prof.htm). The salary surveys are links to the web version of the survey or are the citation to the original publication that is the survey results.

  8. Joint Governmental Salary & Benefits Survey. [Phoenix, AZ: Arizona Department of Administration, Personnel Division, Compensation Section], 1985- . Annual. (ASU owns 1985- .)
    ADM 5.3:S 15/2 HAYDEN AZ DOCUMENTS

    Also called the Survey of Salaries and Employee Benefits of Public and Private Employers in Arizona, this is a very nice survey that has a list of participating employers and a list of job titles in the front of the book. The analysis does not break down the salaries beyond large/medium/small sized employers, public/private employers, and employers in Maricopa/Pima/Balance of Arizona. The job descriptions and exclusions are given for each job title. The survey form is included in the back of the book.

  9. National Compensation Survey. Washington, DC: U.S. Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, 1996- .
    L HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
    http://stats.bls.gov/comhome.htm

    This is a new survey that will integrate the survey data collected for the Occupational Compensation Survey ( http://stats.bls.gov/ocshome.htm), the Employment Cost Index (http://stats.bls.gov/ecthome.htm), and the Employee Benefits Survey (http://stats.bls.gov/ebshome.htm). It covers civilian workers in private industry establishments and State and local governments, but excludes private households, the Federal government, and agriculture. About 36,000 establishments in the 50 United States and the District of Columbia with one or more workers, regardless of industry are included. The sample data will be selected from 154 metropolitan and non-metropolitan areas designed to represent the United States.

  10. Top Executive Compensation. [New York]: Conference Board, 1970- . Biennial. (ASU owns 1972- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HD 4965.5 .U6 N315a HAYDEN REFERENCE

    One of the more thorough surveys of the compensation given to top executives. This covers the salary, bonus plans, stock options, and performance payments. The CEO and the next four highest paid executives are included. Most of this work is arranged by industry. Each industry section has a graph that compares executive compensation with corporate sales volume. Beginning in 1995 we receive both the Advance Report and the Research Report editions. Each one has some unique charts and graphs.

  11. (type) Wage Surveys. Washington, DC: U.S. Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, (varies).
    L 2. HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS

  12. Occupational Compensation Surveys. Washington, DC: U.S. Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, 1990- .
    L 2.3 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
    http://stats.bls.gov/ocsdata.htm

    These various types of wage and compensation surveys cover hourly wages, employee benefits, paid holidays, and vacations. Most of the individual occupational titles covered are for production workers, office workers and transportation workers. General categories are done for each of the major industries and some reports have been issued on professional jobs. The wage surveys are usually arranged by geographic AREA, mostly metropolitan areas, by INDUSTRY, or OCCUPATIONAL group. The title Area Wage Surveys has about 5 different versions and call numbers. There are also Occupation Compensation Surveys and the Occupation Pay Compensation series. These last two will eventually merge into the National Compensation Survey. Ask for help at the Government Documents Reference Desk.

  13. WEB--Internet Salary Survey.
    http://www.isgrp.com/web/salary.htm

    This is a very nice site for links to salary surveys in the professions. Other pages on this site provide typical job hunting help.

Industry Specific Surveys

  1. AIA Salary Report for Architectural Design Firms. Washington, DC: American Institute of Architects, 1991.
    NA 1995 .A38 ARCHITECTURE RESERVE

    After an overview of the field and an analysis of employee benefits, this work is arranged by state with some metropolitan areas also listed. For each job title the mean, median, and first and third quartile salaries are provided.

  2. Fire Salaries. Washington, D.C. : International City Management Association, 1990- .
    HD 8039 .F52 U535x HAYDEN REFERENCE

    A more detailed report than that provided by the Association in the Municipal Year Book, it is arrange by state and then by city. Only those places that responded to the survey are included. The jurisdictions covered are cities, towns, townships, boroughs, villages, and counties.

  3. R S Means Labor Rates for the Construction Industry. Kingston, MA: R.S. eans, 1988- . (ASU owns 1991- . Latest year in Science Reference; earlier years in Hayden Journals.)
    HD 4966 .B92 U43 SCIENCE REFERENCE

    These labor rates of the 46 building construction trades are provided for all major U.S. and Canadian cities. The building construction trades includes such job titles as carpet layer, bricklayer, carpenter, plumber, painter, and electrician. Each job title has the union local number, contract dates and rates, wage rates for two years, base wage rate, fringe benefit package, and a 30 city percent, which is an index type figure to compare wages between cities.

  4. Municipal Year Book. Washington, DC: International City/County Management Association, 1934- . Annual. (ASU owns 1935- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals.)
    JS 342 .A2 I52 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    JS 342 .A2 I52 HAYDEN DOCUMENTS REF
    JS 342 .A2 I52 WEST REFERENCE
    JS 342 .A2 I52 ASU DOWNTOWN REFERENCE
    JS 342 .A2 I52 ARCHITECTURE STACKS

    Each year, the International City/County Management Association does a survey of salaries of local government officials that it includes in this publication. It covers both elected and non-elected management positions. The lists for cities and for counties are arranged by region, type of city or county, and by population size. Individual cities are listed only for the salaries of police and fire departments, which have their own lists separate from other city and county positions.

  5. National Survey of Salaries and Wages in Public Schools. Arlington, VA: Educational Research Service, 1974/75- . Annual. (ASU owns 1974/75- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    LB 2842.2 .E3a HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This is issued in three parts. Part 1 covers scheduled salaries for professional personnel; Part 2 does salaries paid professional personnel; and Part 3 covers wages and salaries paid support personnel. Only a limited number of job titles are covered. The same school districts are sampled in all three parts. Tables are arranged by salary, by job title, and by state and school district.

  6. Police Salaries. Washington, D.C. : International City/County Management Association, 1995- .
    JS 361 .P6x HAYDEN REFERENCE

    A more detailed report than that provided by the Association in the Municipal Year Book, it is arrange by state and then by city. Only those places that responded to the survey are included. The jurisdictions covered are cities, towns, townships, boroughs, villages, and counties.

  7. Professional Engineer Income and Salary Survey. Washington, DC: National Society of Professional Engineers, 1979- . Annual. (ASU owns 1984- . Latest year in Science Reference; earlier years in Science Journals.)
    TA 157 .N33 SCIENCE REFERENCE DESK

    The survey starts with a general overview of the profession with many statistical charts and tables included. The rest of the volume is devoted to salary information. The major listings are by degree, experience, branch of engineering, and organizational size. Each of these broad groupings are further arranged by such items as city, number of people supervised, type of organization and the heading of the other sections. Both mean and median salaries are given, as are first and ninth decile and first and third quartile.

  8. Sales Force Compensation. Chicago: Dartnell, 1982- . Biennial. (ASU owns 1988- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HF 5439.7 .C65x HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This is a survey of the salaries and compensation paid to the sales forces of companies. Since sales personnel tend to be paid on commission, this is a very important tool on how the commissions can be calculated.

When No Recent Survey Can Be Found

This is the point when we start to search for the pay ranges of public employees and use actual job notices to get an idea of appropriate salaries.
  1. America's Job Bank
    http://www.ajb.dni.us/
    Arizona's Job Bank
    http://az.jobsearch.org/

    These sites are run by the group America's Job Bank. They provide links to state employment offices and allows searches for jobs by state, occupation, job titles, job classification codes, and education or skills required. You actually need to view the job listing to get any salary information. The sorting capabilities make it fairly easy to build a current salary survey for yourself.

  2. State & Local Government on the Net
    http://www.piperinfo.com/state/states.html
    Arizona State & Local Government on the Net
    http://www.piperinfo.com/state/slaz.html

    These pages provide links to all the city county and state offices and agencies that have web pages. Most of the personnel or human resources offices of these governments provide job postings or listings for the week. Since most of the governments use a civil service classification system for jobs, salary range information is included in the job posting. Remember that governments tend to pay less than companies do for the same job, but these salary figures can be used to gauge salaries paid in an area. Some human resource departments actually do put their civil service code manual of job titles and pay ranges on a web site.

Employee Benefits

This section covers those surveys that concentrate on the non monetary forms of compensation more commonly known as employee benefits, such as retirement plans, insurance plans, leave time, required benefits (FICA, Workers Compensation, etc.), and supplemental pay (bonuses, overtime, commissions, etc.)
  1. Benefits-LINK
    http://www.benefitslink.com/index.cgi

    A "free nationwide (USA) Internet link to information and services for employers sponsoring employee benefit plans, companies providing products and services for plans, and participating employees." A well organized, interesting site that does take you to quite a few sites of organizations, government, conferences, and news about employee benefits.

  2. Employee Benefits Survey. Washington, DC: U.S. Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, .
    L HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
    http://stats.bls.gov/ebshome.htm

    These various surveys cover the types of non-monetary compensation such as health insurance, sick leave, vacation leave, life insurance. The survey provides data on both full-time and part-time employees in all private industries and State and local governments. Surveys of State and local governments and small establishments are conducted in even-numbered years and surveys of medium and large establishments are conducted in odd-numbered years. The small establishment survey includes all private non-farm establishments with fewer than 100 workers, while the State and local government surveys include all governments, regardless of the number of workers. All three surveys cover workers in all 50 States and the District of Columbia.

  3. Employee Benefit Research Institute
    http://www.ebri.org/

    This professional organization focuses sole on employee benefits and related issues. Of particular use are the EBRI Fact Sheets ( http://www.ebri.org/facts/factsheets.htm) and the EBRI Issue Briefs ( http://www.ebri.org/ebriissb.htm) both are full text and some are available for downloading.

  4. International Foundation of Employee Benefits Plans
    http://www.ifebp.org/

    This is a professional organization that focuses on employee benefits. The most useful page on this site is the link page for other related web sites (http://www.ifebp.org/iclinks.html) many of which have survey information.
