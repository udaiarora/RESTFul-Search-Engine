August 5, 2003

Job growth outlook in doldrums amid economic uncertainty

Analysts have once again lowered their expectations for the job market’s performance in 2003, according to the latest issue of the Blue Chip Job Growth Update.

“While the consensus forecast for total nonfarm employment has dropped, the outlook for growth in employment costs is that they will continue to rise,” according to Dawn McLaren, research economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center. “Productivity also remains and important indicator and the consensus of the panel is that growth in productivity will remain fairly steady over the next two years.”

The forecasts by the nationwide panel of experts are critical in assessing the state of the economy, said McLaren. The Bank One Center publishes the monthly Job Growth Update and its quarterly companion report, the National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity.

The Blue Chip Job Growth Update reports that the U.S. economy posted a 0.4 percent decline in jobs (475,800 jobs) in June 2003 compared to June 2002. Among the states, Nevada ranked number one in employment growth, with a 1.6 percent increase representing 17,000 jobs over the same period. The lowest rate of total nonfarm job growth was seen in South Carolina, where a 1.7 percent decrease represented 31,500 jobs.

The Blue Chip Job Growth Update has been the first-to-user source of complete federal employment data since its inception in 1992. It presents data compiled from the U.S. Bureau of Labor in an easy-to-read format, permitting state-to-state comparisons within 24 hours of the release of the data. The National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity, which appears quarterly in the Job Growth Update, contains information from a panel of leading economists around the country. Consensus forecasting is the hallmark of the Blue Chip publications produced by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business.

The Blue Chip Job Growth Update is available to subscribers either by mail or on the Web. Internet subscribers may sign up for a free list serve, which automatically sends them an email when the data are posted.

A sample issue and information on subscriptions is available online at the following address: http://wpcarey.asu.edu/seid/eoc/publication.cfm.


The L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University is an affiliation of six research centers that serves as a link between the local, state, national and international business communities and the creative and intellectual resources of the nationally ranked School of Business.
