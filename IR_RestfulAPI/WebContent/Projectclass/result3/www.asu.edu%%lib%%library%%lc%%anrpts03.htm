Executive Committee of Librarians Council

Personnel Committee

The Personnel Committee of Librarian’s Council had an extraordinary year of activity as a result of several pending initiatives and new business brought from several sources. Here are the accomplishments of the 2002-2003 academic year: 

  • Section G.1.2: Personnel Committee proposed and Council approved removal of language requiring Mandatory Supplementary Evaluations for Instruction.  Changes were approved by vote of Council in the February meeting.
  •   Section G.1.2: Personnel Committee investigated potential changes to the requirement for Mandatory Supplementary Evaluations for Collection Development. Personnel met with representatives of Collection Development Council in March. Lydia LaFaro and Linda DeFato were subsequently charged to draft new language for comment by Collection Development coordinators and ultimately review and approval by Executive Committee and Council. That draft is expected by Fall 2003. 
  • Section G.2.2:  Review Committee asked Personnel to look at language in this section requiring the chair of Review Committee to notify a candidate for promotion of a negative decision on the promotion request. Personnel unsuccessfully attempted to find the original justification for this language, but in the process discovered several inconsistencies between the related language in ACD and the Librarian’s Handbook.  A “Review Decisions Text Matrix document was created to explicate the differences in language. A proposal for language changes to remove the Review Committee requirement was drafted for discussion at Executive Committee but discussion was tabled pending other pressing business. Other potential language clarifications concerning the difference between a “general oral statement of strengths and weaknesses” and “notification of decisions being made” by the Review Committee were tabled as well. 
  • Marion the Librarian Promotion to Full Package: Personnel committee drafted a vita, cover letter and disclaimer language for the Council website to match the existing examples of probationary review and promotion to Associate packages on the Council website. Drafts were completed and sent to Executive Committee for comment. This work was tabled as a result of significant changes to the promotion and continuing appointment review process being implemented by President Crow’s office. These changes require a significant increase in the justification documentation that will be required by the university administration and made the committee’s work obsolete.
  •   Handbook Section J – Orientation:  Scott Herrington and Mimmo Bonnani were charged to work on a major revision to this text with the goal of having text ready for review by fall semester.
  •   Handbook Section K – Mentoring:  Personnel recognized that this section needs to be developed bit tabled this item pending other pressing business. 
  • Interim Promotion and Continuing Appointment Review Procedures:  In June Personnel Committee reconvened at the request of Kurt Murphy to review interim review procedures distributed but the Provost’s Office to govern the review process for the 2003-2004 academic year while an ad hoc committee assembled by that office works on substantial revisions to the existing procedures. President Crow requested this comprehensive review of procedures. Personnel reviewed the Schedule of Personnel Action (SOPA) documents and provided Kurt with a highlighted version that identified changes from the existing text. The Personnel document also contained several comments from committee members about the significance and implications of the interim changes.
  Submitted by: Rob Spindler, Chair

Research and Professional Development Committee

Annual Report 2002/03 

Committee members: 

Lucy Lyons (Chair)

Dan Stanton

Sue Hunter 

Research Funds There was $3,000.00 in research funds made available in this fiscal year. All of those funds were expended to support three research projects. Professional Development Funds The Dean committed $54,000 in funds for professional development. Most of these funds, approximately $50,265, were expended in support of professional development and travel by 57 academic professionals and 11 classified staff. A total of $3,735 in funds will be taken off of the next fiscal year due to late submission of travel reimbursements. The RPDC worked to ensure these funds were expended in a timely manner and instituted new practices for the distribution of these funds between professional and classified staff requests. Additionally, the committee updated the “application for travel funds” form and development a separate form for classified staff. Lastly, the committee developed a procedures manual to aid future committees on the processes and procedures of the committee.  

Respectfully submitted, Sue Hunter

Review Committee for Personnel Action

Members of the Review Committee for 2001/2002 were Katherine Krzys (Chair), Helen Seaton, Mirek Borovansky, Annette Voth, Ed Oetting, Sue Hunter (Probationary member), and Julie Tharp, Personnel Committee Representative.  Voth served as Chair during the sabbatical reviews, as Krzys had applied for a sabbatical.  Voth retired in December 2001 and the committee continued to function with six members until the end of the term in June.

The Committee made recommendations to the Dean on three sabbatical requests, five Second Year Probationary Reviews, one Fourth Year Probationary Review, three Promotion to Associate with Continuing Appointment requests, and two Promotion to Full requests.  Review Committee made two recommendations for rank of candidates for Academic Professional positions during the year.  The Committee reviewed revisions to the Librarians= Handbook, presented by the Personnel Committee.  It also suggested changes to section C.2.3, AReview Committee for Personnel Action@ and passed these on to the 2002/2003 Review Committee for consideration. 

Katherine J. Krzys

Chair, Review Committee for Personnel Action 2001/2002


Compensation Committee

To:  Julie Tharp, Chair of Librarians" Council

From:  Compensation Committee Chair, Lois Schneberger

Annual report for Compensation Committee, 2002

The Compensation Committee met on three occasions during the year, the first was to select a chair. Diane Moore agreed to serve as secretary at the November meeting.

The chair met with Kurt Murphy, Assistant Dean for Personnel on several occasions to discuss the merit instrument for academic professionals and the 3 year salary adjustment process for AP's. 

The committee reviewed the merit instrument currently in use and made the decision not undertake a revision.  Mr. Murphy met with the committee at its January meeting to review the merit adjustment process.  He was asked to make a report at a future council meeting in order to bring everyone up-to-date and discuss future adjustments.


Committee on Academic Professional Status

Nominations Committee

Patricia Etter chaired the Nominations Committee with Greg Green and JoAnn Mulvihill as members.  It followed the general instructions written by Marilyn Wurzburger in June 2001.

  1. We announced that we would be seeking nominations and asked for nominations and or volunteers for the various positions available.
  2. We were successful in nominating several candidates for each position.
  3. Ballots were printed on colored paper with return address on the reverse.
  4. Ballots were mailed in separate envelopes with a sticker, “ballot enclosed.”
  5. Karie Pifer supplied labels for the ballots.
  6. Council members were advised by E-mail that the ballots were in the mail.
  7. After counting the ballots, winners were notified by E-mail as were the runners-up.
  8. The successful candidates were announced in SoundOff and by E-mail.
  9. Ballots were sent to the Secretary for retention for one year.

The Committee ran three elections during 2001-2002.  First was election of a representative to the Academic Senate.

The second election was for candidates for Chair-elect, secretary, and committee members for Librarians’ Council.  Following the election, the 2001-2002 Chair-elect resigned.

Julie Tharp, who ran for 2002-2003 Chair-elect, agreed to become Chair for 2002-2003.  According to the by-laws, a new election had to take place for a new Chair-elect.  Scott Herrington, who had placed second on the ballot, agreed to run unopposed although a space was added to the ballot for a write-in candidate.  He was elected and will serve as Chair-elect for 2002-2003.

Patricia A. Etter

Chair, Nominations Committee
