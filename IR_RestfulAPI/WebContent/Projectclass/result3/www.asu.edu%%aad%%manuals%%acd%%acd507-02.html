ACD 507–02: Key Terms

Purpose

To define key terms related to types of academic professional appointments, types of performance evaluations or reviews, academic professional responsibilities and required evidence, and organizational structure

Sources

Arizona Board of Regents Manual - 6–301, 302
Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University

Applicability

Academic professionals

Policy

Academic Professional Status

Academic professionals are employees designated by the Notice of Appointment as “academic professional.” Academic professionals are nonclassified employees who are involved with research or teaching programs, have advanced degrees and/or specialized training, require academic freedom, and report to a person below the level of senior vice president and provost or vice president.

Types of Academic Professional Appointments

Appointment
See ACD 505–03, “Academic Professional Status, Ranks, Titles, and Appointment Categories.”

Year-to-year appointment
An appointment for a period of one year, for which there is no expectation of renewal.

Multiple-year appointment
An appointment period for a period of two or three years on a fixed-term or a rolling appointment.

Fixed-term appointment
A multiple-year appointment, for a period of two or three years, for which there is no expectation of renewal.

Rolling appointment
A multiple-year appointment, typically for three years, which differs from a fixed-term multiple-year appointment in that, if performance is satisfactory, the existing contract will be replaced annually by a new three-year contract conditional upon availability of funds, the needs of the unit and the university, and approval of the provost.

Probationary appointment
An appointment period of prescribed duration, during which the academic professional is working toward receiving a continuing appointment.

Continuing appointment
An appointment that, once awarded to academic professionals by the president, creates an expectation that the president will renew employment for successive appointment periods, unless the academic professional retires, resigns, or is subject to release or dismissal by the university.

Definitions of Academic Professional Responsibilities

Position Effectiveness

Each academic professional shall have a position description. This description is dependent upon the unit’s mission and goals. Excellence is achieved in the context of the unit in which the academic professional works. Standards for performance include:

  1. effective and continuous accomplishments that relate to the missions of the unit and university

  2. effective accommodation of change in the position or environment in which the individual has worked during the time period under review

  3. contributions to organizational goals and objectives

  4. innovation and creativity in professional practices

  5. professional development, that is, continuing growth in one’s position and professional expertise

    and

  6. knowledge and application of professional standards and practices, guidelines and protocols.

Professional Contributions

Professional contributions comprise a wide range of endeavors that draw upon the academic professional’s expertise, talents, and interests and represent a commitment to the profession or to the discipline that extends beyond the scope of assigned responsibilities.

Each position affords different opportunities, and each academic professional has different talents and means of contributing to his or her profession and enhancing effectiveness in supporting the needs of the unit and the mission of the university.

Examples of professional contributions include, but are not limited to:

  1. active participation in appropriate professional or scholarly organizations

  2. publication and dissemination

  3. efforts to transfer techniques and practices resulting from creative activity at the university to other institutions

  4. consultations and evaluations drawing on the academic professional’s expertise

  5. fellowships or scholarly awards

  6. grant proposals written or grants received

    and

  7. research, teaching, leadership, management, or administrative roles within the academic professional’s specialization beyond those stated in the position description for the individual.

The academic professional’s role in these activities is important in evaluating these contributions. The contributions should reflect an active, directed pursuit of goals that impact and advance the practice and/or scholarship within the academic professional’s field. Because many kinds of participation and contribution are possible, the academic professional should describe the nature and extent of the activities so that the level of significance is clear to reviewers.

Institutional, Professional, and Community Service

Academic professionals with continuing or probationary appointments are to actively serve the institution, the profession, and the community. Service of academic professionals is characterized by the application of subject knowledge and professional expertise beyond the scope of assigned responsibilities.

Service may include such activities as membership on unit search committees, roles in shared governance, contributions to campus committees, and/or similar activities within professional organizations. Service may also include community activities that draw upon professional expertise or subject knowledge and/or advance the university by meeting the needs of the greater public.

Definitions of Performance Reviews and Evaluations

Performance Review
An assessment of an academic professional’s overall contributions that determines whether the academic professional is retained at ASU. A review of an academic professional on a multiple-year appointment determines renewal of that appointment, either as a fixed-term or a rolling appointment. A probationary review is conducted approximately at the midpoint of the probationary period; a review for continuing status is conducted in the last year of the probationary period as is designated in the offer letter; a promotion review can occur at any time, in accordance with the personnel action schedules set by the university provost’s office.

Annual Performance Evaluation
The appraisal of the academic professional’s performance, prepared on a yearly basis by the unit head.

Post-Continuing Appointment Review
A review triggered by the annual performance evaluation of an academic professional who holds a continuing appointment. If an academic professional is found to be “unsatisfactory” in one or more areas of evaluation or on the overall evaluation, the relevant post-continuing appointment review procedures must be followed. (See ACD 507–09, “Post-Continuing Appointment Review.”)

Definitions of Organizational Structure

University
The multi-campus entirety of Arizona State University.

Campus
one of the campuses of the university, including ASU at the Tempe campus, ASU at the West campus, ASU at the Polytechnic campus, and ASU at the Downtown Phoenix campus.

College
The administrative units on each campus that are headed by a dean.

Academic Unit
The lowest unit in the organizational structure, including a department, school, division, or program usually housed within a college.

Administrative Personnel

University provost
The title of executive vice president and provost of the university.

Campus provost
The title of provost at ASU at the Tempe campus, ASU at the West campus, ASU at the Polytechnic campus, and ASU at the Downtown Phoenix campus.

Supervising dean
The title of dean in the administrative unit that houses an academic unit.

Unit head
The chair or director of academic unit.

Academic professional job titles
Approved titles are categorized within three general functional groups: information and materials management, pedagogy, and research. See ACD 505–03, “Academic Professional Status, Ranks, Titles, and Appointment Categories” for individual titles within these groups.
