August 19, 2003

Harrison reorganizes unit, names Cole to deputy executive vice president post

Mernoy Harrison, executive vice president for Administration and Finance, is reorganizing his area to more effectively implement President Michael Crow's design imperatives for a New American University. Harrison is reallocating resources and making personnel changes to better meet the university's needs during a time of tremendous growth.

Among the major changes:

. V. Scott Cole, assistant vice president for Physical Planning and director of Facilities Management, has been named to the new position of deputy executive vice president for University Services.

. Ron McCoy, professor and director of the School of Architecture, has taken on the additional role of University Architect.

The reorganization was necessitated by ASU's current growth, Harrison said.

"We're about to embark on a tremendous amount of effort in capital expenditure and construction across all three campuses, around the ASU Downtown Center and possibly at other sites in the Valley. With so many initiatives underway, as well as the responsibility for ASU's financial planning, I want to make sure my area is in the best possible shape to support the president.

"Scott will be in charge of the day-to-day planning and capital activities, and this new position empowers him to get things done expeditiously. He will be on point to make all of the construction and capital expenditures happen."

Cole will work closely with the other senior officers of the university "to ensure that the most ambitious building program in recent ASU history is carried out to maximum effect."

Cole, who earned his bachelor's degree in business from ASU, said he is honored to serve in his new position.

"It's a great opportunity. We have an awesome challenge ahead of us in developing the four campuses to meet our growth needs and to follow the direction that President Crow wants us to move in. As an ASU alumnus, I find it challenging, exciting and exhilarating."

The University Architect position was created, Harrison said, because "it's imperative we have someone focused on architectural and planning issues across the university."

Among projects on the drawing board or under construction that the newly reorganized department is responsible for are the development of a new campus master plan; redevelopment of Tempe Center; research infrastructure; residential life expansion; new ASU Foundation headquarters; parking facilities; light rail; enhanced telecommunications infrastructure; and the continuing development of ASU East, West and Downtown.

"We're moving more toward being one university with locations throughout the Valley, and operating in a single-university mode," said Harrison. "The intent is to create a visually coherent identity for each location, and Ron is an excellent architecture professional to lead this effort."
