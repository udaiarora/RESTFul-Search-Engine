Office of Youth Preparation


Committed to Arizona youth through Classroom, University, Community, and Research Programs

The Office of Youth Preparation has been committed to providing academic support to Arizona youth through Classroom, University, Community, and Research Programs since 1989.  Housed within Academic Affairs at Arizona State University, the office maintains a unique and versatile position within the university system.  This position allows the office to utilize a variety of resources and develop diverse partnerships in order to maintain its commitment to the research, identification, and implementation of strategies that have a large-scale positive impact on Arizona youth.

 

PROGRAMS THAT SERVE

Classroom Programs are conducted in K-12 classrooms throughout Arizona and are designed to address the needs of students and to provide support and services to educators.

University Programs are conducted on the campus of Arizona State University and utilize university resources to create an enriched educational experience.

Community Programs provide entire communities with useful information in an effort to generate an awareness of issues that may affect education and student performance.

Research Programs are used to investigate the educational concerns of students, educators, and parents, and to explore methodologies that may positively impact large numbers of students.
