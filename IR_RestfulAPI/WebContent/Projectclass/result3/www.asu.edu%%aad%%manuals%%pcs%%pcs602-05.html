PCS 602–05: Inventories under a New Department Head

Purpose

To define a procedure for inventorying departmental equipment when a new department chair, dean, director, or designee is appointed

Source

Property Control

Policy

Property Control requires a physical inventory be performed by departmental personnel when a new department chair, dean, director, or designee is appointed.

Exception

The majority of inventoriable equipment is tagged with an ASU property control number (ASU PC#). Inventoriable equipment that is difficult or impossible to tag may be assigned a “dummy ASU PC#” beginning with a “9” (e.g., 9003564). These numbers cannot be scanned with a bar code scanner; therefore, they will be present on each “Not Found” report, and the department will need to locate them.

ProcedureD

Responsibility
Action
Property Control
  1. Send a department inventory listing of all capital equipment to the new department chair, dean, director, or designee.
Department chair, dean, director,
or
designee
  1. Perform a physical inventory of the equipment listing provided by Property Control.
If the equipment was found in the location indicated on the report:
  1. Check the “Found” column next to the ASU property control number (ASU PC#).
If the equipment was found in a different building and/or room than indicated on the report:
  1. Check the “Found” column next to the ASU PC#.

  2. Draw a line through the old building and/or room number and write in the new building and/or room number.

  3. Correct any other discrepancies on the report (e.g., description, serial number, model number).
If the equipment was not found:
  1. Check the “Not Found” column next to the ASU PC#.
Property Control
  1. Record “Not Found” equipment in the Missing Inventory Status (MIS).

    Note:This equipment will remain in MIS for four years, then be reviewed for final disposition. It will no longer show up on the department inventory but will remain on the property control system.

    However, equipment coded as “Not Found” may be found in future inventories. Departments will be notified when this occurs.

If the equipment should be deleted from the report:
Department chair, dean, director,
or
designee
  1. Check the “Delete” column next to the ASU PC#.

  2. Retain a photocopy of the inventory report for department files.
Within 30 days of the date of the “Not Found” report:
  1. Forward the original “Not Found” report to Property Control for processing.
endtable
