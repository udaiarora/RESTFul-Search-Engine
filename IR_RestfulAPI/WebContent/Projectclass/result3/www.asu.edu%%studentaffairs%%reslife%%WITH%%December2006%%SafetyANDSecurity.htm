Holiday Decorations Safety Guidelines

Decorations play a large role in creating the joy of the holiday season and residence hall decorations help spread a spirit of excitement. However, this excitement must be tempered with awareness of decoration safety precautions that protect the entire residential community.

The following are holiday decoration directives created by the Fire Marshal and ASU’s University Services. This information is also available in the Residential Life Handbook and Academic Planner (page 38-39) and on the Environmental Health and Safety Web page (Decoration/Display Compliance Guidelines).

  • Cut Christmas trees are prohibited—only fire retardant artificial trees or “live trees” (root type in soil) are acceptable. Live rooted trees must be checked daily.
  • Trees shall not be located in any manner that will block or obstruct an exit.
  • Only approved lighting shall be used on trees and all electrical wiring must be in good condition.
  • Any decorations must be non-combustible. Combustible materials must bear a “flame proof” label.
  • Only approved lighting shall be used.
  • Any electrical lighting sets must bear the label of Underwriters Laboratories, Inc.
  • Light bulbs must be arranged so they do not ignite any combustible materials.
  • Lighting must be plugged directly into an outlet that is located in the room. (No cords are allowed to go from one room to another).
  • Lighting strings may not be plugged in one to another.
  • Extension cords must be at least 16 gauge and contain a ground, three-prong plug.
  • Lights should not hang more than three inches from the ceiling.
  • No decorations should be hung from equipment intended to detect or prevent fire.
  • Exit signs and emergency lighting must remain free of decorations.
  • Cut vegetation is prohibited unless approved by RM for specific temporary use.
  • Candles, embers, glowing or open flame articles are prohibited.
  • Decorations must be limited to no more than 25 percent of the wall space.
  • Elevator lobbies or stairways must be free of obstruction and combustible material.
  • No decorations may be hung outside the building.
  • All holiday decorations must be removed prior to fall residence hall closing.

Following these rules will help maintain a safe and fire free environment. Your residential hall staff will be happy to answer any holiday decoration questions you may have.
