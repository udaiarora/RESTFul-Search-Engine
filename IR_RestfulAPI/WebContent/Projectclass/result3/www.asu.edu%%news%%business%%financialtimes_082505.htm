August 25, 2005

National publication shines spotlight on W. P. Carey programs

Financial Times’ annual business school rankings place two programs at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business in the top 50 among business schools worldwide. The school’s MBA is 21st worldwide and 15th in the United States in doctoral rating.

“This ranking places us in an esteemed group of schools,” says associate dean Dennis Hoffman. “The quality of our Ph.D. programs has dramatically improved in the last decade. Doctoral programs are typically much smaller than undergraduate or MBA programs at business schools, but the impact they have on the thought leadership agenda of a school is immeasurable. Our performance in doctoral rankings is a reflection of the high quality of our faculty research.”

W. P. Carey’s executive education division ranked 33rd for its custom programs. Criteria are based on responses from executive education purchasers and surveys of business schools on a host of factors, including course design, quality of faculty and relevance in the workplace of new skills learned.

“This is the first year we’ve been large enough to qualify for participation in the rankings, so we’re thrilled to have achieved a ranking well up in the top 50,” says Andrew Atzert, executive education director.

Atzert notes that the executive education division is emphasizing W. P. Carey’s unique strengths in services marketing and supply chain.

“We’re also launching open-enrollment courses for the first time next year, so we hope to be ranked in that area as well,” he says.
