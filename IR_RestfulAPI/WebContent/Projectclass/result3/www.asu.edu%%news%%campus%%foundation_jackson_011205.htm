January 12, 2005

ASU Foundation welcomes Harvard’s Jackson

The ASU Foundation has named Ira Jackson, a senior fellow at Harvard University and a former executive of BankBoston, to lead the organization’s expanded efforts to support ASU’s rapid evolution as a national research university.

Jackson, 56, succeeds Lonnie Ostrom as president. Ostrom is taking a sabbatical starting this month after spending more than 20 years successfully managing the foundation.

Jackson is a senior fellow in the Center for Public Leadership at Harvard University’s John F. Kennedy School of Government. He also served as associate dean of the John F. Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University; president of the Arthur M. Blank Family Foundation; director of the Center for Business and Government at Harvard; executive vice president and director of external affairs for BankBoston; and Commissioner of Revenue for the commonwealth of Massachusetts.

“What is really exciting about Ira is that he is a very broadly engaged leader with experience and skills in public service, academia and the business world,” says Craig Weatherup, the chair-elect of the ASU Foundation. “Given the breadth and scope of what ASU is looking to do in engaging with the larger community, he has a perfect background.”

Throughout his career, Jackson has built institutions and helped to transform communities by creating strong collaborative partnerships at the intersection of business, government, academia, civil society and the community. He has used private initiatives for public purposes, and he has brought entrepreneurship and excellence to government, higher education and the nonprofit sector.

“Ira Jackson has been extraordinarily successful in every field of endeavor he has ever undertaken,” says ASU President Michael Crow. “He is a strategic thinker who takes both a broad and long-range view, and already has a solid grasp of where ASU is going and what we need to succeed. I am delighted he has agreed to help advance the ASU Foundation – and through the foundation, advance ASU.”

During his tenure at BankBoston, the company consistently received Outstanding Community Reinvestment Act ratings from federal regulators for leadership in strengthening inner-city communities. As Massachusetts Commissioner of Revenue, Jackson established an innovative model of “honest, fair and firm” tax administration, working to restore public confidence in the integrity of the agency through vigorous reforms.

Jackson, who did his undergraduate work at Harvard College, holds a master’s degree in public administration from the John F. Kennedy School of Government at Harvard and is a graduate of the Harvard Business School’s Advanced Management Program. He is co-author of “Profits with Principles: Seven Strategies for Delivering Value with Values” (Doubleday, 2004).

“I am invigorated and proud to be joining the ASU Foundation in working to advance ASU,” Jackson says. “President Crow’s vision of the new American university is inspiring, and its importance and significance extends far beyond ASU. The university’s commitment to teaching, research and public service is one that I wholeheartedly embrace. I am delighted to play a role through the ASU Foundation in pursuing this transformational mission in higher education.”

Wood, with the ASU Foundation, can be reached at (480) 727-7213 or (barbara.wood@asu.edu).
