A Rationale for a Familiar Look Among Web Pages with Sensitive Data

Approach and Rationale

Information Technology recognizes the need for preserving the autonomy of the College or Department while IT also seeks to provide leadership in the efforts to provide secure and appealing services over the ASU network. In this regard, IT has worked closely with the Provost's Office and the Web Advisory Group to establish a family of web pages for the collection of sensitive data.

Specific Pages

Information Technology provides three centrally maintained general web pages related to sensitive data. The first is for generic sign-on to ASURITE services https://weblogin.asu.edu/cgi-bin/login The second is the myASU login at: https://my.asu.edu/nosession/loginpage.jsp The third is the centrally maintained credit card information page. See an example at: https://www.asu.edu/webdevelopment/projects/ecommerce/sec-sans.html

This familiarity gives the customer a visual reassurance that they are in a secure environment. It also provide for a clear threshold of copyright violation if any parties outside of ASU choose to emulate a sensitive data screen.

Requirements

  • Providers take responsibility as the first point of network vulnerability and inform themselves about all precautions and protections that are necessary;
  • Providers of a password service should use ASUWEBAUTH2. This enables customers to sign on just once, but obtain any eligible service without logging on again. (other requirements are documented in "How to join the single sign-on community" handout);
  • Providers must not store IDs or passwords or credit card information;
  • Providers must "POST" the sensitive data as opposed to passing it though any servers;
  • And, in the event they use a page other than the standard centrally-provided pages, providers must comply with the ASU Graphic Standards Manual (http://www.asu.edu/gsm/) and the Web Standards and Guidelines available from the Web Advisory Group (WAG).
  • And, in the event they develop a page other than the centrally provided sign-on screen, providers must comply with the ASU Graphic Standards Manual and the Web Standards and Guidelines Recommendations as defined by the Web Advisory Group (WAG).
