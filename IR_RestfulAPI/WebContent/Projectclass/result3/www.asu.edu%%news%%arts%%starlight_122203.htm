Gammage hosts two top productions in January

ASU's Gammage Auditorium will host two major productions in January, "Starlight Express" and "The Exonerated."

Andrew Lloyd Webber's triumphant musical, "Starlight Express" will run Jan. 6 - 11, 2004. "Starlight Express" is like nothing seen on stage before. Brought to stage by London's original creative team, this high-velocity production fuses state of the art technology, electrifying music, and all-new dazzling sets and costumes by John Napier. Light years ahead of the rest, it's two hours of speed, spectacle, excitement and energy.

"The Exonerated" will be presented Jan. 27 - Feb.1, 2004. Imagine everything you did between the years 1976 and 1992. Now remove all of it. Those 16 years were taken away from Sunny Jacobs, sentenced to death for a crime she did not commit.

"The Exonerated," off-Broadway's smash hit, tells the true stories of six innocent survivors of death row. Their stories, in their own words, will rock your view of the world, justice and the American way.

Both productions are part of the M&I Bank Broadway in Arizona series presented with ASU Public Events and Clear Channel Entertainment, Inc.

For ticket information, call ASU's Gammage Box Office at (480) 965-3434, Ticketmaster at (480) 784-4444 or visit (asugammage.com). For group ticketing, call (480) 965-6678.

Medina, with Public Events, can be reached at (480) 965-1884 or (Kariann.Medina@asu.edu).
