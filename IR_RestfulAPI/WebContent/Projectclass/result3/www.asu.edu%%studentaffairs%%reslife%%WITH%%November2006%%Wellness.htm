Health and Wellness


At Wellness and Health Promotion, we know that students who practice healthier behaviors are more successful academically, personally, and professionally. Therefore, we provide information and education to give students the skills to make healthy decisions while at ASU and throughout their lives. We provide information and education on Alcohol, Tobacco and Other Drugs, Body Image and Eating Disorders, Depression and Suicide Prevention, Healthy Eating and Physical Activity, Sexual Assault and Relationship Violence Prevention, Sexual Health, and Stress Management. We offer a variety of programs and services to disseminate this wellness information. These programs and services include presentations to classes or organizations, special awareness events, information tables around campus, and posters promoting wellness behaviors and ways to “live well.”

We also provide resources for students who have wellness concerns to help them find the support services they need. The 24/7 Wellness Helpline is staffed by trained student staff who will answer questions and help students find support. Students are also welcome to stop by during our office hours, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. to talk to someone one on one about their wellness concerns. Wellness Helpline: 480-727-7000.

If you are interested in becoming a part of Wellness and Health Promotion, we encourage you to get involved with our programming! We offer student worker, intern, and volunteer opportunities. You can also be involved by attending one of the upcoming special awareness events; The Great American Smoke Out, November 16, 2007 from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. at ASU’s Cady Mall, and AIDS Awareness Day December 1, 2006 at Hayden Lawn, from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m.

Also, please check out our website for more information on our wellness topics, programs and services, student involvement opportunities, upcoming events, and wellness resources. www.asu.edu/health.
