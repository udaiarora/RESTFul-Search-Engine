October 10, 2003

Teacher program adds students, new districts

An ASU West College of Education teacher certification program for individuals with a bachelor's degree in a field other than education has nearly doubled in enrollment since it debuted one year ago.

The Accelerated Teacher Certification Program enables participants to complete their educational requirements for teacher certification through a full-time, 10-month program that includes significant amounts of time spent in K-12 classrooms. More than 35 students are enrolled for 2003 - 04, up from 20 a year ago when ASU West introduced a pilot program in collaboration with the Peoria Unified School District.

In addition to Peoria Unified, districts currently participating are Alhambra Elementary, Cartwright Elementary, Washington Elementary, Litchfield Elementary, Aqua Fria High School, Glendale Union High School and Saddle Mountain Unified. The addition of high school districts means that the program is now open to people wanting to earn secondary school as well as elementary school certification.

"The program is fast-paced and intensive, but it is a wonderful learning experience for students who are dedicated," says Tracy Tadrick, academic advising coordinator in the College of Education. "The hands-on experience that the students receive is invaluable, because they are immersed into the K-12 environment."

The involvement of the Litchfield, Saddle Mountain and Agua Fria Districts is part of a partnership among Verrado, ASU West and those three school districts to develop a range of educational opportunities in Verrado, a new community in the foothills of the White Tank Mountains.

"Students in the accelerated program gain an understanding of what the public school setting is like over the course of a school year," says Michael Awender, dean of the ASU West College of Education. "They develop relationships with teachers and pupils in the schools in which they are placed, and they experience everything from parent-teacher conferences to standardized testing schedules."

For information, contact ASU West College of Education at (602) 543-6300.

McClure, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5308 or (samcclur@outlook.west.asu.edu).
