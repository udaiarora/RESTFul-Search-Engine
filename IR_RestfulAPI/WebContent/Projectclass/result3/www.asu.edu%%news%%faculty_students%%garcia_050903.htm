May 9, 2003

Garcia joins President's Working Group as vice president for University-School Partnerships

To focus university resources on bolstering Arizona's public schools, ASU President Michael Crow has appointed Eugene Garcia as vice president for university-school partnerships. The appointment is pending approval from the Arizona Board of Regents in June.

Garcia will continue to serve as dean of the College of Education on Main Campus and also will ensure that all segments of the university are engaged in these vital efforts. In his newly created vice presidential position, he will become part of the President's Working Group, reporting to Executive Vice President and Provost Milton Glick.

President Crow, because of his experience as a new member of the State Board of Education, has become more aware than ever of the importance of having Arizona's schools, from preschool through high school, achieve success.

"I am convinced that ASU must take a much stronger, more visible role in making a difference in the critically important agenda of making the state's public education system as strong as possible," Crow said. "The entire university must become part of the solutions that ensure educational success; we must aggressively lead these efforts.

"Already the education faculty and other faculty on all three campuses are engaged in innovative, responsive initiatives. It is our intent to expand these efforts even more broadly and deeply. Further, Dean Michael Awender and Dean Garcia are co-chairmen for the ASU Education Initiatives and Solutions Council, which includes faculty, school administrators and teachers, and leading members of the business and non-for-profit community."

In strengthening pre-K/12 education, ASU has four primary goals - develop high quality teachers; develop strong school leaders; create early interventions, by drawing on faculty and data from multiple disciplines; and build on linkages with schools and the private sector for the distribution of fiscal and human resources.

"These goals must be represented at the highest levels of the university," said Crow. "Dr. Garcia will continue his direct oversight as dean of the College of Education while engaging the rest of the university in working with pre-K/12 and private sector partners to create, support and sustain efforts enhancing the goal of high academic performance for all of Arizona students."

Garcia said he shares Crow's vision that ASU can play a critical role in meeting Arizona's educational challenges.

"These challenges are diverse, from serving our youngest children, preparing and retaining the best professionals, to engaging the community in producing the best research and evaluation so we know what will work," said Garcia. "ASU can help in responding to these challenges. I am pleased to lead such an effort."

Before becoming dean of ASU's College of Education last July, Garcia had served as dean of the Graduate School of Education at University of California, Berkeley, as well as a senior officer in the U.S. Department of Education and dean of the Division of Social Sciences at UC Santa Cruz.

"Education was our founding mission and must continue to be at the forefront of what we are doing today and in the future," said Crow. "We are pleased that we have someone of Dr. Garcia's stature and extensive experience to lead the charge."
