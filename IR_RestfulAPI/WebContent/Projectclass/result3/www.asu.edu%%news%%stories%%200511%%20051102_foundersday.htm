November 2, 2005

ASU seeks nominees for Founders' Day awards

The ASU Alumni Association is seeking nominations through Nov. 28 for the university’s Founders’ Day awards, given annually to recognize excellence among ASU’s alumni, faculty and staff.

The awards, among the university’s highest honors, will be presented at a March 7 ceremony at the Tempe Mission Palms Hotel.

“Founders’ Day celebrates the founding of what is known today as Arizona State University,” says Alumni Association President Christine K. Wilkinson. “As we celebrate the birth of this institution, we also take the time to recognize the excellence of the faculty, staff and alumni who characterize the excellence of ASU.”

Two awards will be given to alumni in recognition of their contributions to ASU and the community. The Alumni Achievement Award, open to all ASU alumni, and the Young Alumni Award, given to a graduate of the past 15 years, also honor alumni for excellence in their professions.

The staff achievement award recognizes an ASU staff member whose outstanding contributions to ASU have significantly enhanced the well-being and reputation of ASU within the community at large.

Faculty members are recognized in three categories, recognizing excellence in teaching, research, and service.

The Faculty Teaching Award recognizes demonstrated exceptional teaching at ASU and demonstrates the value placed on teaching. The Faculty Research Award recognizes significant contributions to higher education outside the classroom through research and or creative activity. The Faculty Service Award honors a faculty member’s extraordinary contributions to ASU that have significantly enhanced the well-being and reputation of ASU.

Nominations are open to anyone, and forms and award information can be found online at (www.asu.edu/alumni/programs/awards/founders.html). Inquiries can be directed to Cindy Dick at (480) 965-0093.
