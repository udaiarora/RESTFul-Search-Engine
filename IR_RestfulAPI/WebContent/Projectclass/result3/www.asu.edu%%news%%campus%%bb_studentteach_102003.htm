Beyond borders: College partnerships take student teachers international

Undergraduate students in the ASU College of Education are exploring the K-12 world overseas through a collaborative effort between the college and the International Programs Office.

This year, the college introduced its newest international student teaching program that provides an opportunity for students in one of ASU's teacher preparation programs to student teach in an accredited international school in Costa Rica.

This is the first year the college's student teaching program has collaborated with Lincoln School, a K-12 English-language school in San Jose, Costa Rica. This fall five students are participating. Carlos Ovando, associate dean for Teacher Education in the College of Education and director of the program, says experiencing Costa Rica's way of life is one of the most beneficial factors to the program.

"By experiencing firsthand the sociocultural and linguistic challenges and opportunities of another culture, our students become aware of the great cultural, linguistic, educational and economic diversity within Latin America," he says.

During the semester-long program, students complete all student teaching requirements under the supervision of experienced mentor teachers. The ASU students also learn about the history and culture of Latin America, acquire language skills in Spanish and explore one of the most environmentally diverse regions of the world. Students are supervised by carefully selected exemplary teachers at Lincoln School and in continuous contact with an ASU supervisor via e-mail.

For information about the program visit (http://courses.ed.asu.edu/ovando/costarica_revised/).

ASU's College of Education also offers students the opportunity to teach in Brighton, England, through an established, 25-year-old program. Education Professor Robert Gryder says students who participate in the program offered in exchange with Brighton University (BU), become enriched with England's culture and academia.
"This program provides education majors the opportunity to work with one of the finest schools located in a beautiful country," he says. "This program is ideal for students who want to broaden their educational experience."

The program is offered to undergraduate and graduate education majors in areas including elementary, secondary, bilingual, early childhood and special education. Program participants student teach in Brighton for five days a week for eight weeks in January and February. Two weeks are available for travel in England or Europe at the end of the student teaching assignment. Students then teach in their regular ASU placement until school ends in May or June.

Gryder says there are so many challenges educators face that it would be difficult to pinpoint one that would apply to every teacher and future teacher. However, Gryder says teachers at every level should strive to do their best work.

"One challenge that teachers need to overcome is learning how to listen to students - all of the students," he says. "We should make every effort to bolster their self-esteem and make them a part of the education experience.

"Many people's view about education is based on the chair they're in," Gryder adds. "Motivate the students and find the best in them and make them better than they imagined they could ever become."

Ovando says having future teachers participate in exchange programs is a great way for them to learn new teaching methods, which will help keep their students motivated in the classroom.

"Today's teachers must not only know their content areas well, but they also must be strong, creative and caring leaders of classroom practices that meet the increasingly pluralistic needs of our students in relation to teaching standards and high stakes testing," he says. "Exchange programs enable future teachers to step outside their cultural comfort zones and experience the great variation of the human experience in other cultural and linguistic contexts."

For prices and additional information about these and other student exchange programs, contact the International Programs Office at (480) 965-5965 or visit the Web site at (http://ipo.asu.edu).
