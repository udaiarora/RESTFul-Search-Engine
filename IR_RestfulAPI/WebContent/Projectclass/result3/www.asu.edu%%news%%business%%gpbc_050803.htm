May 8, 2003

Greater Phoenix single-family market continues to shine

The strong single-family housing market is likely to continue through this year and into 2004, while commercial markets are expected to remain weak, according to the annual Real Estate Consensus Forecast featured in the current issue of the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

After five years in the 32,000- to 34,000-unit range, the single-family forecast calls for another 33,300 units to be built this year, with a minor drop to about 32,000 units in 2004. "This is a surprisingly strong forecast, given the fact that interest rates could increase over this period of time," according to Elliott Pollack, topics editor for the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip.

High vacancy rates and low levels of construction activity will continue in commercial markets, Pollack writes. "Overall, Greater Phoenix is in the correction stages of the cycle for apartments and commercial real estate. This will continue with a period of modest construction until absorption picks up sufficiently to drive vacancy rates down from present high levels. This could take a few more years."

Elsewhere in the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark shared his view that, now that the war in Iraq is behind us, it seems likely that the local economy will resume a gradual recovery - "mainly in the service-producing side of the economy."

"The employment picture is better overall according to the benchmark data revisions," Clark writes. "Employment for the state as a whole began to improve in the third quarter of last year. Prior to the revisions, there were no signs of improvement until the end of 2002. All of the improvement has been in the service sector. Manufacturing continues to struggle, and prospects for an immediate recovery there are slim."

The Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast is published four times a year by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W.P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. The annual rate is $45. To order, call the Center at (480) 965-7858. For economic information, contact Tracy Clark at the same number, or by e-mail at Tracy.Clark@asu.edu.
