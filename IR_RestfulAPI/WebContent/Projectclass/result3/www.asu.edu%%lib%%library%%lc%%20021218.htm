Librarians' Council


Minutes of Meeting


December 18, 2002
Hayden C6A

Meeting was called to order at 3:03 pm

Chair: Julie Tharp

Secretary: Sheila Young

 

I. Approval of Minutes (September 18, 2002)

 

Minutes of September 18, 2002 were approved as written

 

II. Chair & Executive Committee's Report

 

No report.

 

III. Dean's Report

 

Strategic Planning will begin in January. Jack and Suzanne Pfister will facilitate. Sherrie will send out a SWOT analysis to the library and she requests that it be returned by January 6th as an attachment to email. The Administrative Council will be the participants in the strategic planning sessions.

 

In response to a question about further budget cuts, the Dean noted that the special session of the state legislature dealt with the first half of the current budget situation and will now deal with the second half of the shortfall.

 

Linda Shackle reported that a student inquired about the library being open 24 hours. He indicated that he thought that money had been made available for this purpose. It was unclear what source of funds the student was referring to. Sherrie responded that frequently students request that the library be open 24 hours and that it would cost $100,000-$200,000 to keep Hayden and Noble library open without reference services.

 

IV. Committee Reports

 

Personnel Committee – Rob Spindler

a) The personnel committee is working on the promotion to full packet for Marion Librarian.

b) Changes in the Librarians’ Handbook G.1.2, Mandatory Supplemental Evaluations for Instruction only will be presented during New Business today.

c) The personnel committee is reviewing Section J: Orientation, and Section K: Mentoring of the Librarians’ Handbook to evaluate the need for changes.

d) At the request of the review committee, the personnel committee is reviewing the statement in Section G.2.2 of the Librarians’ Handbook that states: “For promotion requests only, the Chair notifies the primary evaluator orally of the recommendation.” 

Review Committee – Marcia Anderson

The review committee has completed the work on sabbatical leave requests, and promotion and continuing appointments. The committee will make recommendations on rank for candidates for open positions, as necessary. The committee also forwarded a request to the personnel committee to review part of section G.2.2 of the Librarians’ Handbook. 

Research and Professional Development Committee – Lucy Lyons

Lucy reported on the status of the Research Funds: 1) the first deadline has passed and one application was received and awarded, 2) two-thirds of the funds remain and 3) the second deadline is January 10, 2003. All librarians are encouraged to apply for this funding.

Lucy also reported on the Travel Funds. About $900 has been returned and anyone who has not yet applied was encouraged to do so. There was $54,000 provided for travel this year and $54,013 was distributed to approximately 55 people for 77 trips.

The RPCD assigned different maximum amounts of funding to classified staff than that given to academic professionals, a move praised by the Executive Committee and the Dean. The RPDC considered a number of aspects when determining the allocation of funding, including: (1) the greater pressure on AP’s than on classified staff to engage in professional development; (2) the greater number of AP travel requests than classified staff requests; and (3) the greater number of requests per AP.  The average librarian request is almost twice as high as the average staff request. It was noted that all the  variables together have resulted in higher out of pocket expenses for each incidence of travel by an AP. In FY03, even though the staff allocations will be less than professional staff allocations, the average out-of-pocket expense for classified staff will be less than half of the expense of academic professionals. The final consideration in creating differential allocations (4) is the need to justify travel expenditures in times of a tight university budget. 

The committee is developing a manual of written procedures to provide some guidance for future decisions regarding the distribution of travel funds. In addition, the committee is proposing new forms, one for academic professionals, and one for classified staff. The university is in a difficult financial situation and accountability is important in order to defend the provision of travel funds. The new form is designed to assist academic professionals in describing the need for travel. The form is the same as the current form except that it includes wording from the Librarians’ Handbook to assist the applicant in describing the reason for travel. The form for the classified staff also includes the wording from the Librarians’ Handbook to assist the applicant in describing the reason for travel. In addition, it contains a provision for the supervisor’s signature and a statement of support from the supervisor of the applicant. This enhanced application form will assist the RPCD in deciding if the travel is for professional development or if it is for job training, since funding for job training is provided from a different source.

The forms and the relevant language changes in the Librarians’ Handbook will be presented under new business.

Compensation Committee - Lois Schneberger

Greg Green, substituting for Lois Schneberger, gave the report. The compensation committee proposes that the composition of the committee be reduced from five members to three. This change and the appropriate changes in the Librarians’ Handbook will be proposed under new business.

Academic Senate – JoAnn Mulvihill

JoAnn encouraged everyone to read the minutes of the Senate meetings.

Committee on Academic Professional Status – Sue Hunter

The first spotlight speaker spoke on the self-insuring of health benefits by the State of Arizona. A summary of the presentation is on the CAPS website.

Web Master – Yuwu Song

Song presented two versions of the Librarians’ Council home page. He will email the URL’s to the members of Librarians’ Council and he requested feedback.

 

V. Old Business

 

There was no old business.

 

VI. New Business

 

The following changes to the Librarians’ Handbook were proposed. These will be voted on at the next Librarians’ Council meeting.

a. Personnel Committee

Revision to Librarians’ Handbook G.1.2: Supplementary EvaluationThis change removes “Instruction” from the Supplementary Evaluations. Sherrie asked if this means that there would be no evaluation for instruction. Scott responded that comments regarding instruction would be given to the Primary Evaluator. Scott also stated that MSEs for instruction haven’t been done since there ceased to be a separate Instruction Department. In addition, when Librarians’ Council previously discussed the MSEs, there was much support for eliminating the MSE for Instruction and to revisit the MSE for Collection Development. Today’s treatment separates the MSE for Instruction from the MSE for Collection Development.

b. RPDC

Revision to Librarians’ Handbook Section H.1.2: Travel Fund Procedures

 

This proposal creates new forms for applications for travel funds and also proposes changes in the appropriate language in the Librarians’ Handbook.

 

Question: “Networking” is listed in the Librarians’ Handbook as a reason for attendance at meetings, but is not on the form.

 

Answer: The items on the form are suggestions for writing stronger proposals.

 

Question: How about having a checklist?

 

Answer: Applications can become too routine. The proposed form allows a person to write a justification for travel. Sherrie added that on the reimbursement form, there is a question: “How does ASU benefit?” and she recommended that applicants keep this in mind when applying for funds.

 

Question: Is there sufficient justification being written presently?

 

Answer: It would be better if the justifications were less casual and more persuasive, especially as funds get tighter.

 

Questions: Do we need to reconsider travel for Librarians and adjust expectations?

 

Answer: Sherrie stated that it is important for all librarians to continue to develop. She added that we all benefit and that we must invest in people in order to part of the library of the future.

 

There was further discussion on the need and benefit of professional development for both librarians and classified staff, and the importance of writing a compelling argument on the application form. A number of members commented on the need for professional development by both beginning librarians and more experienced librarians. Betsy Redman pointed out the need to continue to support professional development for all levels, even for those not yet on committees. She also gave examples of classified staff who need to attend the conferences in their areas. There was more discussion on the funding of these activities. It was pointed out the Librarians’ Handbook does not discuss what professional development is for classified staff and only mentions classified staff at all with regard to funding for travel. In addition, activities that are job training can be funded by administrative funds. Sherrie stated that if an activity is job related the applicant could request administrative funds. Also, if the RPDC finds that they cannot fund a given person in a given year, as long as there are not too many, there is another source of funding.

 

Revision to Librarians’ Handbook Section H.1.2: Travel Fund Procedures

 

It is proposed to delete the paragraph “Direct Pay for Airfare” from Section H.1.2 since it applies only to administrative travel.

c. Compensation Committee

Revision to Librarians’ Handbook Section C.2.5: Compensation Committee. Revision to Librarians’ Handbook Section B: Bylaws of Librarians’ Council

 

The proposal is to reduce the number of members on this committee from five to three members and to have one or two members be elected each year.

 

Meeting adjourned at 4:25 pm.

Sheila Young

Secretary
