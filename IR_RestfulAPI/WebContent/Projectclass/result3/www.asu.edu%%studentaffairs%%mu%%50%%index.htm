On February 26, 1956, Arizona State College celebrated the opening of the newly built student union called the Memorial Union. In February 2006, Arizona State University will recognize, honor, and celebrate the role and impact the MU has played in the lives of students, staff, faculty, alumni, and community members for the past 50 years.

Did You Know?
A portion of the Virginia sandstone used in the building of the Memorial Union at ASU?s Tempe campus in the 1950s was a consignment of stone that was part of the original White House in Washington, D.C., and distributed during a renovation of the historic structure.
