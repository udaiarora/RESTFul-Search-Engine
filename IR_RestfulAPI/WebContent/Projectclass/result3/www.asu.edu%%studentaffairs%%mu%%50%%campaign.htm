Capital Campaign

After more than 10 years of planning and dreaming, April 16, 1951, marked the beginning of a capital campaign to raise $350,000 in gifts toward a Memorial Union building on campus. The Alumni Association sponsored the drive, and under the inspiring and tireless leadership of general chairman Charles A. Stauffer, ASC graduate of 1901 and winner of the 1950 Alumni Service Award, the goal was substantially exceeded. Grateful acknowledge was made to the distinguished Executive Committee and to all the 1,543 donors whose gifts made this living memorial possible.

Mr. Stauffer literally devoted two years of his life to this noble effort to meritoriously successful. A distinguished citizen, Mr. Stauffer was publisher of the Arizona Republic and Phoenix Gazette for many years. He also served as Alumni President and a member of the Alumni Board.

Another person who was responsible for the success of the campaign was the Alumni Secretary, James Creasman who dedicated himself to the support of the project. Sparked by enthusiasm, he won support throughout the state. There were numerous setbacks, but Mr. Creasman found the solutions and continued to campaign for the project.
