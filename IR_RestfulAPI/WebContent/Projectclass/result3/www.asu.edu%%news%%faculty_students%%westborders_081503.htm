August 15, 2003

ASU West students experience global realities first-hand

It's one thing to engage in classroom study about NAFTA's effects on trade; it's another to visit a Mexican border city, visit maquiladora factories and meet factory workers and management. An award-winning program in the ASU West School of Management that brings students face-to-face with living and working conditions in Nogales, Sonora, Mexico is one example of the opportunities the campus offers students to experience Latin American cultures and economies first-hand.

Students in ASU West's "Business in the Global Environment" course spend a day in Nogales, touring maquiladoras (multinational factories), conducting market-basket surveys at grocery stores to compare food prices with typical local incomes, and learning about the economic, political and historical issues that characterize the Nogales border area.

The Nogales border immersion tours earned the School of Management the 2002 Program Excellence Award from the North American Small Business International Trade Educators organization.

"Most U.S. university business students have not had significant exposure to international business realities," says Gary Anders, professor of economics and director of the School of Management's Institute for International Management. "The response of our students has been overwhelmingly positive to this opportunity for real-world exposure to topics such as NAFTA, globalization, foreign direct investment, inter-firm trade, outsourcing and competitive advantages."

"It's vital for our undergraduate global business students to understand the growing ties between Arizona and Mexico, which is now the second-largest trading partner of the United States," says Bruce Forster, dean of the ASU West School of Management.

Additionally, the School of Management has established a relationship with the Hermosillo Campus of the Instituto Tecnologico y Estudios Superiores de Monterrey (ITESM). ITESM faculty member Ignacio Vazquez spent a year at ASU West as a visiting professor. ASU West students participated in an immersion trip to Hermosillo, and Vazquez brings a group of ITESM students to ASU West each semester.
ASU students also have the opportunity to immerse themselves in the culture of Central America and the Spanish language through a summer study abroad program to Costa Rica, offered by ASU West's College of Arts & Sciences.

The two-week program combines intensive Spanish language study with instruction related to Central American culture, including field trips to cultural locations. Students are housed with a Costa Rican host family. Sara Gutierres, associate professor of social psychology in the ASU West social and behavioral sciences department, coordinates the educational experience.

Students interested in participating in the Costa Rica trip may apply for scholarship assistance made possible through the support of the Mitsui Foundation.

"It is gratifying to receive financial support from a private foundation that recognizes the tremendous benefits that come from giving U.S. university students the opportunity to become familiar with other cultures in the Americas," says Emily Cutrer, dean of the College of Arts & Sciences.

"The trip was a great educational and growth experience for me," says student Carole Horn, who received a Mitsui scholarship to participate in the 2003 tour. "It was good to see how another culture lives. I think that as Americans, we truly believe that ours is the only way."

Information about the study abroad program is available through the ASU West social and behavioral sciences department at (602) 543-6061. For details about School of Management international study opportunities, call (602) 543-6200.
