To comply with all federal, state and local laws regarding employee timekeeping requirements, ASU has two official timekeeping forms:

  • the ASU Non-exempt Employee Calendar for Salaried Non-exempt employees
  • the Time Card for Hourly Non-exempt employees. (.pdf)

If neither of these forms fit the business needs of a department, the department may design an alternative employee timekeeping record for its particular business purposes, as long as the form properly records the information required. A department-specific record must include the following information to to assure compliance with all federal, state and local laws:

  • Beginning and Ending Dates of the covered pay period.
  • Department Name and Number.
  • Employee's full name, as used for Social Security recordkeeping purposes.
  • Employee's Identification Number
  • All used paid leave time, i.e., sick leave, vacation leave, bereavement leave, etc.
  • All deductions for unpaid absences in any increments.

The time record used by a work unit becomes the official time record for that work unit, and is subject to internal and external audit. All official Time Records must be retained in accordance with current financial record retention requirements.

FOR NON-EXEMPT EMPLOYEES:

  • Hours worked each workday and total hours worked each work week.
  • Total weekly straight-time earnings due for hours worked during the work week, exclusive of overtime compensation.
  • Total weekly overtime hours, whether earned at straight time overtime or time and one-half overtime, and whether paid in cash or awarded as accrued compensatory time.
