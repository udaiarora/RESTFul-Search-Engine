January 26, 2004

Baseball great to headline ASU East, Downtown Center MLK events

Baseball legend John "Buck" O'Neil will headline the 2004 Martin Luther King Jr. Celebration at ASU East and the ASU Downtown Center.

O'Neil will be the featured speaker at the East campus kick-off event at 7 p.m., Jan. 28, in the Campus Union Ballroom. He will also be the keynote speaker at noon, Jan. 29, in the Downtown Center's Brown-bag Luncheon Series.

O'Neil, chairman of the Negro Leagues Baseball Museum, was a top player in the former Negro Leagues and the first black coach of a Major League Baseball team. His involvement in professional baseball dates back 77 years.

"Buck O'Neil is one of the game's greatest ambassadors," says Mike Mader, ASU East director of student activities and a member of the campus's MLK committee. "His professional and personal life experiences offer valuable lessons for all of us, and he has many stories to tell."

O'Neil, a slick-fielding first baseman, first played ball for the Miami Giants in 1934. He went on to play for the Shreveport Acme Giants, Memphis Red Sox and the Kansas City Monarchs. In 1955, O'Neil was recruited by the Chicago Cubs as a talent scout. He later became the first black coach in the majors when the Cubs elevated him to an on-field position. He served the Cubs organization for 33 years, before joining the Kansas City Royals, where he worked until retirement.

He is also well known for his narration of the Ken Burns documentary on the Negro Baseball Leagues.

Other MLK celebration events scheduled for ASU East include a gospel celebration, co-sponsored by the House of Refuge East; a celebration of ASU East student cultures; an African drum and dance theater; movie nights; and Native American storytelling and hoop dancing, among others.

"This year's events are meant to showcase the diversity of our campus and enrich the lives of the people who work and learn here at East," says Clinton Everhart, chair of the MLK committee.

Events for the MLK celebration run through the end of February. Most are free and open to the public. Find out more at (www.asu.edu/mlk).
