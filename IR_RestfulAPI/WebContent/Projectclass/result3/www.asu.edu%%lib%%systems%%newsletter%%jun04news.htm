New SOS Procedures Mean Even Better Service for You!

Keith Swartz has been implementing changes in our SOS Customer Service Procedures to improve our Help Desk operation. We want to provide you with the best possible service, handle your calls quickly, and not allow any problem to languish unresolved. To help us accomplish this, we’re asking everyone to report problems through the SOS system, either by calling the Help Desk (5-9093) or by opening a problem ticket online (http://sos.lib.asu.edu/online.html).

To facilitate opening a ticket, we have placed an SOS Superhero icon on your desktop that is a direct link to the SOS page. We realize that many of you are used to going directly to your favorite LIST person with a problem, but we believe we can improve our overall performance and efficiency if more calls come in through the SOS system.

Here’s a brief overview of what you can expect to happen when you contact LIST with a problem report:

  1. Each call that comes in receives a Ticket Number and is entered into the SOS call queue.

  2. The ticket is prioritized according to the nature of the problem. Each level of priority includes time limits within which the problem must be resolved, after which it escalates to the next level of priority.

  3. The new ticket will immediately be assigned to the appropriate LIST staffer, who will contact you to let you know how it will be handled and will then see it through to completion.

  4. A LIST staff person will monitor the call queue to insure that all tickets are handled appropriately.

The bottom line for you is this: If you're having a computer problem, the best way to resolve it is to get it in our SOS call queue where it becomes the equivalent of a squeaky wheel.

It may take a little time to get this working as smoothly as we would like, but let us know what you think of the results and feel free to offer suggestions on how we might improve our Customer Service operation.
