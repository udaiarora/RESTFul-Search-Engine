October 31, 2003

Miss Arizona to serve as grand marshal for parade

ASU alumna Corrie Hill - also known as Miss Arizona 2003 - will serve as grand marshal during ASU's Homecoming Parade Nov. 1. The parade will begin at 10 a.m. at McAllister Avenue and University Drive and will continue down University Drive to Myrtle Avenue.

In addition to Miss Arizona, the parade also will highlight student organization floats, ASU Homecoming Royalty, local dignitaries and the Sun Devil Marching Band.

The ASU Homecoming FestDevil 2003 Block Party, featuring an array of fun activities for the whole family, will begin immediately after the parade. At 1:30 p.m. ASU will take on the University of California Golden Bears at Sun Devil Stadium.

Hill, 24, says she is really looking forward to ASU's Homecoming Parade, the Block Party and other Sun Devil festivities.

"I'm extremely excited to be serving ASU as the Grand Marshal," Hill says. "For an alumna and future graduate student of ASU, this is truly an honor."

Hill graduated from ASU in 2001 with a bachelor's degree in marketing.

Though she is extremely busy this year fulfilling her duties as Miss Arizona, she soon plans to continue her education at ASU pursuing a master's degree in secondary education.

"ASU is more than a place to receive a great education," she says. "It's a place to learn about other cultures and philosophies, a place to further your personal growth and development."

Hill's current duties as Miss Arizona include touring the state speaking on her personal platform, domestic violence. She also stays busy working with a variety of communities, organizations and clubs throughout the state.

Some of her latest projects have included ASU's recent Take Back the Night Rally, Childhelp USA's Fairytale Gala, and speaking to Rotary and Kiwanis Clubs, and other community organizations.

Hill says the Miss America organization is the largest scholarship provider for young women in the country, awarding thousands of dollars to approximately 10,000 young women every year.

"I encourage anyone interested in earning scholarship money, performing on stage, or spreading the word about an issue you feel is of concern in our society to participate in the Miss America organization," she says.

For information about ASU Homecoming activities, go to (www.asu.edu/homecoming) or call (480) 727-3378.

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
