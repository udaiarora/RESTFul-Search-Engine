December 21, 2006

Message to the ASU Community:
 
One unique aspect of ASU is that we are “one university in many places,” not a system with separate campuses, and not one main campus with branch campuses. The vision of one university in many places is part of The New American University conceptualized by President Crow when he came in 2002. Since that time, some steps have been taken to implement this vision: the University redesign project aligned programs with campuses to fit each campus’ distinctive identity, the West campus separate accreditation was eliminated so that all campuses are part of a single ASU accreditation, and the downtown campus opened. However, in order to fulfill the complete vision of one university in many places I have recommended to President Michael Crow some necessary organizational changes and he has accepted those recommendations. This document describes the new structure and the general responsibilities associated with each position.

Proposed Structure

The President is President and Chief Executive Officer of the University which includes all locations. The Executive Vice President and University Provost is Chief Operating Officer of all the colleges and schools regardless of location. Administrative affairs, planning, facilities, auxiliaries, libraries, student affairs and all other administrative functions are run centrally.

The Budget

There is one budget system for the university, based on implementing goals set by the president. The Executive Vice President and University Provost allocates the budget to the deans and other academic unit heads and to the administrative units following principles established with the President and Budget Office. Budget allocation is made not to campuses, but to academic units and administrative units. The budget model rests on a school-centric model where deans bear the primary responsibility for academic excellence and student success. Academic and administrative unit heads are evaluated by their achievement of objectives, set by the president for their units, and by comparison to aspirational peers in the nation for their achievement of quality. Measures of quality are determined by the units.

Campus Program Identity  

Each campus has a unique identity. The Tempe campus focuses on research and graduate education along with an undergraduate education that is analytic and preparatory for graduate or professional school or employment. The Polytechnic campus focuses on learning by doing offering an applied approach to professional and technological programs that meet business and societal needs and an emphasis on technical education that is a direct preparation for the workforce. The West campus focuses on interdisciplinary liberal arts education with professional programs that connect to the community. The Downtown campus is focused on programs with a direct urban and public connection. The Polytechnic, West and Downtown campuses are each led by a Vice President and Executive Vice Provost.

Vice President and Executive Vice Provost

The Vice President and Executive Vice Provost ensures that all programs and activities of the faculty, staff and students are aligned with the mission and identity of the campus.

In addition the Vice President and Executive Vice Provost:

  • Oversees campus operations coordinating with university-wide officers. Managers of campus-based services will report to their senior university-wide functional officer. Details of this will be described in further communications from each university-wide officer.
  • Is responsible for the cross-functional coordination of services on the campus.
  • Champions campus needs, priorities, and perspectives to others.
  • Develops recommendations and in-depth analyses of issues affecting primarily the campus.
  • Develops and manages campus events.
  • Serves as communication hub and problem-solver for students and staff on the campus.
  • Serves on Working Group 1, the president’s leadership council.
  • Participates in evaluation of deans, faculty and staff as part of University personnel processes.
  • Oversees campus-based commercial development.
  • Represents broader ASU interests to the local political subdivisions.
  • Is the main communications link between the campus and local community.
  • Represent the campus to the press and hosts visitors to campus.
  • Oversees development of campus marketing materials.
  • Coordinate the activities of the deans, makes recommendations on any proposed changes in academic units (combining, introduction of new, dissolution etc.).  
  • Participates in fund raising development activities for the campus.

 

Senior Vice President for Academic Affairs

Responsible for working collaboratively across all campuses to insure that academic programs are coordinated and that we benefit from the faculty expertise on all campuses. Assesses faculty expertise and makes connections among faculty. Ensures curricular offerings are coordinated across campuses, including on-line programs. Identifies areas of strength and areas that need to be strengthened to achieve leadership as a national research university. Responsible for representing ASU in the University of Arizona Medical School that is in collaboration with ASU in downtown Phoenix.

Vice President for Academic Personnel

Responsible for making recommendations to the provost and president on all university-level tenure/continuing appointment and promotion cases. Responsible for running the university tenure and promotion process and for handling all university-level faculty personnel issues, including diversity programs, faculty development programs and other programs as needed. Assists deans, as needed, on faculty personnel issues. Responsible for keeping the policies and procedures affecting academic personnel up to date. Responsible for diversity programs and assisting deans in attracting and retaining diverse faculty.

Senior Vice Provost

Second to the provost, represents the provost in her absence, works across all functional areas in Office of the Provost, providing analysis and assisting the provost in decision-making. Responsible for assisting in analysis of university and national data to assess academic excellence. Works with deans to implement priorities set by president and provost each year.

University Vice Provost and Dean of the Graduate School

Responsible for supporting graduate education and facilitating activities of the deans and directors. Allocates any university-wide funds available for recruiting or support of graduate students. Develops plans with the provost and deans for growing the number and quality of graduate students. Works to develop and support interdisciplinary programs, assists in recruitment and retention of graduate students.

University Vice Provost and Dean of University College

Responsible for University College including advising for exploratory majors, development of majors for the downtown campus, and assisting in recruitment and retention of students. Also responsible for extended education, working with the colleges and schools to expand and improve our on-line educational offerings. Also works with community colleges on the Alliance.

University Vice Provost for Undergraduate Education

Focuses on strategies for improving retention and graduation rate, works with community colleges and deans to improve articulation. Develops policies for cross-campus cooperation in course offerings and ensuring students are served. Works with Faculty Senate committees concerned with undergraduate education, and with the Office of University Student Initiatives.

Deans

Responsible for academic programs, financial management and enrollment management within their units. Responsible for generating private and other outside revenue to support their colleges, responsible for retention of undergraduate students, and building academic excellence at both the undergraduate and graduate levels. Responsible for attracting and retaining excellent and diverse faculty. All deans report to the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University.
