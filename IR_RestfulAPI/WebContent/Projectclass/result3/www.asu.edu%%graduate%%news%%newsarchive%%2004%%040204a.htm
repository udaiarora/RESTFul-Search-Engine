Interdisciplinary Design Team Takes Top Prize

They did it! They're the national champions!

The interdisciplinary team of ASU master's students from architecture, environmental planning and business, won first place in the Urban Land Institute (ULI) Gerald D. Hines Student Urban Competition last weekend in Pittsburgh.

The team won $50,000, of which 10 percent will be paid to the sponsors. The five students — Prasoon Kumar, Parul Mittal and Timothy Parke from environmental planning, Matthew Muller, architecture, and Mohan Sankrit, business — beat Harvard University, Massachusetts Institute of Technology and a combined team from Stanford University and the University of California, Berkeley.

The ASU team, who put their initial proposal together in a mere 10 days, won by creating a design that would revitalize a 57-acre parcel of “underutilized” land adjacent to the Allegheny River, near Pittsburgh's Strip District.

Team leader Kumar, a student from India, says, “This project is probably the closest an academic exercise can get to being real. As a team we had no prior experience of working with each other and hence had to show tremendous confidence in each others' skills.

“Further for somebody like me, who has been out of my home country for only about 8 months, I had to understand the development needs and functions of a very alien society.”

Business graduate student Sankir adds, “We are extremely happy with our victory in the ULI urban design competition, and our success is the result of a true team effort. Each one of the team members brought special knowledge and skills to the table, and we complemented each other very effectively.”

Wellington Reiter, dean of the College of Architecture and Environmental Design, says, “Not only is our ULI's accomplishment a testament to their incredible dedication and hard work, it also confirms what many of us have known about the College of Architecture and Environmental Design: this is one of the finest schools of design in the country, one that is populated with great students and faculty alike.

“The work of the team is also a demonstration of the advantage of working in multidisciplinary teams. I know that the Carey School of Business also is very proud of the award as their contribution was crucial. I am convinced that this kind of cross-college collaboration will be the hallmark of learning and research at ASU in the future.”

Catherine Spellman, ASU School of Architecture, was the team's faculty adviser.
