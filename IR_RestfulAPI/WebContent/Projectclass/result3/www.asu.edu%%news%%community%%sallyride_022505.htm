February 25, 2005

Sally Ride Science Festival aims to inspire young girls’ dreams

Sally Ride, America’s first woman astronaut, will give the keynote address at the fourth annual Sally Ride Science Festival at ASU’s Galvin Playhouse on March 5, at noon and again at 1 p.m.

The festival, presented by the Barrett Honors College and the Sally Ride Science Club, is designed for girls in fifth through eighth grades, as well as their parents and teachers.

The festival begins at 11 a.m. with a street fair on the Student Services lawn, with booths, exhibits, food and music. The public can attend either of the keynote addresses, plus lunch and street fair, for $10 if paid in advance or $15 at the event. The street fair concludes at 4:15 p.m.

Last year, more than 700 students, teachers and parents attended the festival at ASU, attending hands-on Discovery Workshops given by women professionals from veterinarians to rocket scientists. The goal of the festival is to support and maintain girls’ interest in science and math.

Laurie Leshin, director of the ASU Center for Meteorite Studies, will join Ride in presenting the 1 p.m. keynote speech. In addition to women from industry, other presenters from ASU include Michelle Minitti from the Center for Meteorite Studies; Deanne Rogers, Amy Knudson and Alice Baldridge from the Mars Space Flight Facility; Nancy Grimm, Tammy Harms and Libby Larson from the School of Life Sciences; Amanda Clarke and Laura Wasylenki from geological sciences; and geology student Veronica Zabala-Aliberto.

Registration fee for teachers, parents and students who are participating in the full day of events is $18 in advance or $25 at the door. The fee includes lunch, all afternoon workshops and materials. Teachers can earn four hours of professional development credit by attending the workshops.

The festival also will include TOYchallenge 2005, a toy- and game-design competition created by Ride, Smith College and Hasbro Inc. The goal of the competition is to inspire middle-school students – especially girls – to develop science, engineering and design skills. Girls attending the festival also can join the Sally Ride Science Club for a sponsored rate of $5 for the first year.

To register for the keynote address and street fair, call (800) 561-5161. Registration for full-day participants is at (www.SallyRideFestivals.com).

Auffret, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 965-6991 or (sauffret@asu.edu).
