Mark Jones will be visiting the Political Science Department on Wednesday, January 31st as part of the Jr. Faculty Speaker Series. Dr. Jones is an Associate Professor in the Department of Political Science at Rice University. His research focuses on the effect of electoral laws and other political institutions on governance, representation, and voting.

Title of colloquium: "Electoral Systems, Gender Quotas, and the Election of Women in Latin America" Scheduled for 3:00 p.m. on Wednesday, January 31st in Coor Hall 6761 (Department Library).

Please watch for an international and interdisciplinary conference on “Jerusalem Across the Disciplines” to be held Feb. 19-21, 2007 at Arizona State University. The conference is sponsored by grants from the Jewish Studies Program, the Department of Religious Studies, the Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict, and the Sandra Day O’Connor School of Law, with additional funding from the Department of Political Science, the School of Justice & Social Inquiry, the School of Global Studies, and the Department of Languages and Literature. The conference will bring to Tempe leading scholars from the United Kingdom, Israel, Palestine, and the U.S. whose research has focused on the city of Jerusalem. Professor Miriam Elman from the Department of Political Science is co-organizing the conference along with Madelaine Adelman from the School of Justice & Social Inquiry.

CNS-ASU speaker-Dr. Arie Rip- Dr. Rip is NanoNed Technology Assessment program coordinator. He is a chemist who has always had broader interests in philosophy and in the role of science and technology in society. He specialized in the social aspects of science and technology assessment in Leiden University then worked on the dynamics of science and technology at the University of Amsterdam before joining University of Twente in 1987. His presentation will be on Friday, February 23, 2007 in the Biodesign Institute Auditorium 11:00 am-12:30 pm. For more information, please visit http://cns.asu.edu/speaker/jan07/feb07speaker.html
