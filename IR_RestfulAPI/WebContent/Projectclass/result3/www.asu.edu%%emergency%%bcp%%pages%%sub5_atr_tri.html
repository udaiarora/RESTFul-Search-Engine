Project:    Tri-University Business Continuity And Disaster Recovery Planning Software Application.

Background: The three Arizona Universities have received ABOR funding to jointly license a disaster recovery software tool, LDRPS, developed by Strohl Systems.

Primary Purpose: To coordinate institutional information for planning and implementing recovery for IT services.

When implemented the Strohl Systems software will provide the following:

  • Primary support tool for planning and implementing recovery
  • LDRPS provides several pre-defined categories for organizing critical information
    • People (Employee information and attributes, vendors and customers)
    • Responsibilities (Processes and tasks)
    • Materials (Hardware, software, telecom, supply, assets and vital records)
    • Miscellaneous for other documents, workstation and location information
  • Enhanced coordination
  • Sample planning templates
  • Suggested steps for planning and recovery
  • Survey items and reports
  • Easy to use tool with "point & click" functionality

The implementation will build on knowledge and expertise of U of A staff to assist ASU and NAU in developing templates and instruments and in obtaining data from distributed systems throughout each institution.

Project Overview: This project will acquire, install and make ready for Tri-University use a "disaster planning" application with the appropriate system administration support.

Currently the UofA has a client/server LAN license for the Strohl Systems LDRPS (Living Disaster Recovery Planning System) application that can be upgraded to a web/server ABOR license serving all three universities. This can be accomplished for 1/3 the cost of the three universities independently acquiring a disaster recovery application and the required supporting infrastructure. The product and servers will be housed at the UofA with the Center for Computing and Information Technology (CCIT), providing the server and application administration.

University networks and the systems attached to them have become mission critical resources for each university. University operation is highly reliant on administrative systems, research, instruction and personal computing processes including the network infrastructure that enables connectivity. The universities must be prepared to re-establish these processes should some unforeseen event, or even criminal activity, create major damage rendering any of those systems unusable. This software will assist in the preparation of plans and the implementation of a recovery event.

An additional benefit to each institution is the capability for any and all university units to use this software application to assist them with their disaster planning activities. This product can be used for more than IT environments, as it is the cornerstone for many corporate total disaster plan programs.

Project Roles and Responsibilities

Sponsors: Chief Information Officers are responsible to initiate disaster planning project activities and make appropriate university personnel resources available for a successful project implementation.

University Application Coordinator: Each University will assign an application coordinator to authorize and administer access, provide overall product support and assist their university with technical support issues.

Project Timeline

03/07/2003 - Funding approval.

03/12/2003 - Orders placed and TRI-U project coordination started.

04/02/2003 - Products received.

04/07/2003 - ASU and NAU project coordinators to attend LDRPS training in King of Prussia, Pa.

04/18/2003 - Servers and LDRPS application made ready for use.

04/21/2003 - ASU and NAU can start their recovery plan development. Strohl Systems consulting can be scheduled for two days at each university to assist with their plan implementation.

Project Budget: The project budget includes software license fees, hardware acquisition and installation costs, system administration provided by UofA, training for ASU and NAU at the Strohl Systems Corporate office, and on-site disaster plan implementation for ASU and NAU provided by Strohl Systems consultants. Funding will be from ABOR Innovation Fund.
