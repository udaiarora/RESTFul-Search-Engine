February 28, 2006

Student literacy project influences young lives

Students at Frank Elementary School in Guadalupe, Ariz., are reading better than ever thanks to a group of student volunteers from ASU.

Project Literacy, founded less than a year ago, is a student organization at ASU that enables volunteers to work in public schools with underprivileged children who struggle with their reading skills.

Since 2005, volunteers have been meeting for weekly one-on-one sessions with their fifth-grade "reading buddies," and the improvement the fifth-graders have shown thus far has been dramatic.

"The progress we are seeing is nothing short of amazing," says Jess Koldoff, president and founder of Project Literacy. "The students we are working with have more than doubled their scores on standard reading tests, and are visibly more confident in themselves and in the learning process."

"I am pleased to convey the great success we are experiencing in reading this year," says Bernadette Kadel, principle at Frank Elementary School. "We have to give a great amount of credit to Project Literacy for their partnership with Frank Elementary. The one-on-one support the volunteers are giving our most needy of students is having an immense impact on their fluency and comprehension."

In addition to the progress in the classroom, Project Literacy recently received a grant from the Edson Student Entrepreneurial Program at ASU to become a nonprofit organization in Arizona and increase the capacity of children who can be served.

Members of Project Literacy say the key now is increasing the number of volunteers from the university.

"Frank Elementary has continually been one of the lowest-scoring schools across the district," Kadel says. "At this time, we have risen to the top and can boast some of the highest scores in the district thus far. Again, the time and dedication of the individual ASU students are some of the factors that we can identify as positive changes implemented this year. We look forward to a continued partnership and increased membership in Project Literacy to influence even more students."

The program "is enabling elementary school children who otherwise would not have much of a chance for succeeding in their studies to do so, Kadel says, adding: "The work these ASU students are doing is inspiring the future of Arizona, and we would love to see the number of volunteers from the university skyrocket to meet that deep need."

Those who are interested in volunteering with Project Literacy should note that training sessions will take place at 3:15 p.m., Tuesdays and Thursdays, in room 30 of Frank Elementary School. The school is located on Avenida del Yaqui, south of Baseline Road.
