GRADUATE COLLEGE
Partnership. Quality. Service.

E-Communiqué
Vol. 1.1, October 1, 2003

Today launches our new E-newsletter with the latest happenings in the Graduate College. Links are provided for further information, and all the events may also be found on the Graduate College Calendar (www.asu.edu/graduate/calendar). Read and enjoy!

GRADUATE ADMISSIONS
The new graduate online application became active on Sept. 12. We're happy to report that all is running smoothly!

Departments wishing to create their supplemental applications should contact Roger Lurie (roger.lurie@asu.edu).

AUI/E-GAD!
These training sessions for support staff are being offered twice a month. Please see www.asu.edu/graduate/resources/generalinfo/egad for the schedule.

National Graduate Fellowships Workshops
The Graduate College, Office of National Scholarships in the Honors College, and the Office of the Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs are pleased to announce workshops for students interested in applying for the Javits, NSF, Ford, and Harriett Jenkins (NASA) graduate fellowships. These workshops are designed for graduating seniors, first-year graduate students, and second-year graduate students. Faculty mentors are also welcome to attend.

Let's Do the Mall!
This fall, the Graduate College is hosting information tables on Cady Mall from 11:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. on Oct. 7, Nov. 6, and Dec. 3. Advisors will be on hand to answer questions, and printed materials will be available. Please consult our calendar or What's New site for further information and spring dates.

STUDENT SUPPORT SERVICES
Our special support staff meeting on Sept. 12 was well received. For those that were unable to attend, the presentation is online at www.asu.edu/graduate/presentations

We recently updated the deadlines and application procedures of the Graduate College Recruitment Airfare Awards, Travel Grants, the Tripke Travel Awards, and the University Graduate Scholar (UGS). The PEO Scholarship is now handled directly by PEO.

PROFESSIONAL PATHWAYS
October is an ideal month to add professional development into your life. Visit the Strategies or the PFP site or our What's New/Upcoming Events page for listings and links of the exciting opportunities.

CLINICS: PREPARING FOR GRADUATE SCHOOL
These clinics introduce students on getting in, staying in, and graduating from graduate school. Designed for a small number of students, they allow for individual questions and encourage interaction among participants. The clinics all feature the same topics, and students are invited to attend one or all.

FIRST MONDAY'S BROWN BAG
A monthly Graduate College First Mondays' Brown Bag has been initiated for McNair and STEP-UP awardees and his/her faculty involved in research and creative activities. The forum is designed to promote professional development, scholarly discussion, and presentation by all competitive graduate award recipients.

THESIS/DISSERTATION WORKSHOPS
What semester would be complete without our thesis/dissertation workshops? This informative event is offered at ASU East (Oct. 2) and ASU Main (Oct. 9).
