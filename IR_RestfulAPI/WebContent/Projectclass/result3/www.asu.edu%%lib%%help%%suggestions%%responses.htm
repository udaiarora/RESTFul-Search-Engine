Submitted 11-28-2006, 11:05AM
Library: All
Topic: Web Site / Electronic Journals

 

Suggestion/Comment

Why do you have two pages for searching for Journals, and why do they look different? When you click on "Find Books and More" and then from that page clidk on "Journal title" it looks one way, but if you just click on "Find Journal" it looks a different way. Kind of weird, no?

 

Library Response

These are great observations. We do have two search interfaces for finding journals currently, and we know that it is "weird" and can cause confusion. I'll try not to give you too much information to explain why.

 

The screen you see when you click on "Find Books and More" leads you to the online catalog. The catalog will provide information about journals we have in print, and will link to our electronic journals as well. However, currently the catalog does not provide information about what the coverage dates are for the electronic journals. The screen under "Find Journal" will take you first to information on the electronic journals, including coverage dates, but also provides a way to search the catalog for the print journals.

 

We know that these two search paths are confusing, and we are actively working on a solution to have only one place to search for information on all journals.

Submitted 10-7-2006, 11:18AM
Library: Hayden

Suggestion/Comment

I understand that the Brown Bag Area can be used to have somthing to eat and study at the same time. But is it also for students to get together to discuss school issues very loudly, and sometimes to socialize? I would appreciate your doing something about it.

 

Library Response

Thank you for taking the time to inquire about this. The "Brown Bag" area (soon to be named something else since food and drink are welcome in most areas of the library) is not designated to be a quiet study space. Because of its location near the busy area of the library entrance, it is a place for students to gather and work together and talk. It is also adjacent to a library staff work space, and the noise of the staff working can sometimes be heard in the Brown Bag area. We understand if that is not an environment that is conducive for you to study in. We'd like to direct you to the many other areas of the Hayden Library where it is quiet, and where students are not encouraged to socialize: the Lower Level (especially in the individual study carrels - you can check keys out to these at the Circulation/Check Out desk); Level 1 (by the windows) and Level 2. In addition there are many quiet study tables on the outer perimeter of Level 3.

 

We are in the process of formalizing these "quiet zones" with signage and other announcements. We hope that these steps will help balance the needs of all of our student customers.

I heard through the grape vine that the library is to be cancelling its subscription to EBSCO host. Please don't. That would be horrible for so many students.
