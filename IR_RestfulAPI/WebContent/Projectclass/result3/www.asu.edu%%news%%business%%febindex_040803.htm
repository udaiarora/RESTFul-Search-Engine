April 9, 2003

Arizona Leading Index moves up in February

The Bank One Arizona Index of Leading Economic Indicators rose in February to 118.7, which is 0.5 percent above the revised 118.1 recorded for the previous month and 3.4 percent above the February 2002 number of 114.8 (1987 = 100).

"The overall trend of the index remains positive, which is an encouraging indication for the economy," said Tracy Clark, an economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at Arizona State University's W. P. Carey School of Business. "However, new orders now have been a negative influence for two months. If this continues, it could spell trouble for the economy."

February's Leading Index components are as follows: Positive - the inflation-adjusted value of the M2 money supply, delivery times, employment from the Business Conditions Survey, hours worked in manufacturing, sensitive materials prices, inventories and the inflation-adjusted value of Maricopa County residential building permits. Negative - production and new orders.

The impact of the war in Iraq on the economy depends upon the duration of the active phase of the war, the extent of coalition casualties and the possibility of a resumption of terrorist activity, according to Clark. "The biggest danger for Arizona appears to be the possibility that terrorist attacks could resume. The tourism impact has so far been very modest, and should continue so unless the conflict drags on for a very long time. However, if terrorist attacks resume, the impact could be as bad or worse than the period after 9/11."

"It still appears that the impact of the war will be an overall negative for the economy. The impact of reduced business spending, the potential for reduced consumer spending and the potential impact on tourism far outweigh the boost to local defense-related contractors and increased spending at military bases."

The Bank One Arizona Index of Leading Economic Indicators is based on nine indicators, combined and weighted to project the pace of economic activity several months ahead. Five of the components come from a monthly survey of Arizona purchasing managers conducted by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University.
