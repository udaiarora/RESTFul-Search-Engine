NEW and Future TA Orientation Changes

Since the focus of TA training starts at the beginning of a graduate student?s career, in many cases, it is the starting point for a series of activities, especially professional training for teaching at the university level. In recognition of this, the Division of Graduate Studies, the Center for Learning and Teaching Excellence (CLTE), and the Intergroup Relations Center (IRC) are working together to revamp TA Orientation and Training to include pedagogical training for TAs. Spring 2007 TA Orientation will incorporate the first few minor changes, such as the registration and verification process.

Starting Fall 2007, Preparing Future Faculty 2nd year students (PFFers) will facilitate many of the TA training workshops. The PFFers will be mentored by the CLTE and the IRC staff on a number of essential TA-related topics that will enhance the quality of support our faculty receive from TAs and the quality of education our undergraduate students receive from ASU Teaching Associates.

If you have comments, or questions, please direct them to Jennifer Cason, Director of Graduate Support Initiatives, at jennifer.cason@asu.edu.
