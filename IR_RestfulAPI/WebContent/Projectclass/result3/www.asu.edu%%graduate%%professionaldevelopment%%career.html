Current Services
  • Preparing Future Faculty Program (PFF)
    www.asu.edu/graduate/pff
    PFF is a national program designed to develop new approaches to preparing doctoral students seeking careers in the professoriate. PFF encourages fresh thinking and planning in faculty preparation, creates new support programs, and identifies strategies to improve the quality of teaching and learning. PFF prepares students to meet complex and challenging faculty responsibilities and to improve the quality of undergraduate education. The program offers two phases spanning two years: an exploratory phase involving foundational seminars and visits to the institutions, and a participatory phase involving experiential options with cooperating faculty in the four campus environments, and preparation of a portfolio to be presented at a special capstone fair.


  • Preparing Future Professionals (PFP)
    www.asu.edu/graduate/pfp
    The Division of Graduate Studies has launched a program to assist doctoral students interested in pursuing nonacademic professions. The goal of this program is to familiarize doctoral students with various nonacademic career tracks and help them to develop skills to successfully pursue a wide range of career opportunities. Activities are being offered throughout the year.


  • Strategies for Success/TA Orientation
    Graduate students interested in pursuing practical career preparation have opportunities to receive training and experience at ASU. The Division of Graduate Studies conducts an Arizona Board of Regents (ABOR) required orientation for new teaching assistants that is held at the beginning of orientation week each semester. They are also encouraged to attend the Strategies for Success seminars designed to enrich graduate students’ experiences. Further information is available at www.asu.edu/graduate/sfs and www.asu.edu/graduate/tara.
