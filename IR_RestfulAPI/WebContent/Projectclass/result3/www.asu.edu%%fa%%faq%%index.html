Frequently Asked Questions

We have provided answers to the most common financial aid questions that students ask our counselors.

If you need additional help please call or visit any of our offices located on each of ASU's campuses throughout the Valley.

Print FAQs

FAQs for Regents High Honors Endorsement Scholarship (AIMS)

Q: How do I apply for financial aid?

A: Complete the 2006-07 Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) at www.fafsa.ed.gov.

Q: Is my financial aid file complete?

A: Check ASU Interactive to see if there are documents that need to be completed.

Note: if your financial aid file is not complete, and you do not have awards offered, you will automatically be enrolled in the Tuition Installment Plan. Tuition charges will be billed on Student Accounts Receivable in three installments and a nonrefundable $75 enrollment fee will be assessed.

Q: How can I get the financial aid form you need me to complete?

A: Download the form from our online forms page. Download the form from www.asu.edu/fa/forms. If you are dropping forms off, there is a drop box located on the 2nd floor of the Student Services building (SSV) of the Tempe campus or you can fax it to us at 480.965.9484. You can also visit one of our other campus locations.

Q: How do I sign up for the Tuition Installment Plan?

A: Go to ASU Interactive or visit the Tuition Payment office to sign up.

Note: Beginning with the Spring 2006 semester, ASU is discontinuing the practice of involuntarily dropping students from classes if payment is not made by the applicable tuition payment deadline. Instead, non-financial aid students neglecting to make payment by the tuition payment deadline will be enrolled in the Tuition Installment Plan. Tuition charges will be billed on Student Accounts Receivable in three installments and a nonrefundable $75 enrollment fee will be assessed.

Q: How do I complete the MPN and Entrance Counseling?

A: Go to www.asu.edu/fa/mpn/ for MPN and www.asu.edu/fa/entrance/ for Entrance Counseling.

Q: Can you cancel my Federal PLUS loan and give me more money?

A: If your parents applied for the PLUS loan and were denied, please visit us to discuss your options. If your parents do not want the PLUS loan altogether, you may apply for alternative student loans.

Q: I applied for an alternative loan, how long is it going to take to process?

A: 2 - 4 weeks from the date our office receives your application.

Q: My parents applied for the Federal PLUS loan, how long is it going to take to process?

A: 2 - 4 weeks from the the date our office receives your application.

Q: What do I need to do if my circumstances change, like loss of income?

A: Complete a Student and/or Parent Review form, whichever is applicable. These forms can be downloaded from our online forms page.

Q: When is disbursement?

A: Financial aid disbursement for fall begins on Wednesday, August 16. Funds usually disburse no sooner than a few days before the start of the semester. Refunds will be issued by check (sent to address on record) or direct deposit. If you are a 1st time freshmen - federal loans will pay out 30 days from the beginning of the semester. If you are attending for one semester only, there will be 2 equal disbursements - at the beginning and midpoint of the semester.

Q: If I receive financial aid, will it pay for my dorm room?

A: No, you need to advise Residential Life that you want to pay for your housing with financial aid. Residential Life is located on the 1st floor of the Student Services Building on the Tempe campus.

Q: How can I get AZ residency?

A: Contact Residency Classification office for criteria.
