November 5, 2003

Arizona outperforming U.S., California in job growth

Most of Arizona's economic analysts believe the Grand Canyon State is poised for a stronger fourth quarter and continued improvement in 2004, according to the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

Although growth is sluggish by historical standards, Arizona is still outperforming the nation and our important (if mercurial) neighbor, California," writes Lee McPheters, contributing editor.

Arizona nonfarm employment in September was up by 25,000 jobs compared to the same month of 2002, McPheters reported, citing data from the state Department of Economic Security. The gain of 1.1 percent was the first time over-the-year job growth topped 1 percent since January. "Arizona analysts took particular note of the increase in hiring of temporary help workers," McPheters writes. "Such gains typically foreshadow future increases in permanent positions." Meanwhile, the unemployment rate in the state dipped to 5.6 percent, the lowest level recorded since the first of the year.

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark surveyed the consensus panel on its long-range outlook regarding inflation. "The panel signaled a dramatic shift in its concern about inflation vs. recession - and this is good news for the economy," Clark writes. Last year at this time, 100 percent of the panel believed the primary concern of the Federal Reserve in the coming year should be the possibility of a renewed recession rather than inflation. This year, more than 60 percent think the Fed should be most concerned about inflation.

"Inflation is a worry for economies that are "ramping up," or for those that have a high level of inflation but are currently close to flat," Clark explained. "It is clear that the majority of the panel is betting on an expanding economy."

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. For information, call (480) 965-7858.
