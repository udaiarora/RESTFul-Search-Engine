Residential Life provides and maintains 13 residence hall computer labs for student usage. All hall labs provide PC platform machines running Windows 2000. Services available are: MS Office, Standard Windows applications, Internet suite (FTP, telnet, etc.), print services, Anti-virus and multi-media capabilities. All Residential Life computing requires valid ASURITE user authentication. By accessing ASU’s network, you are acknowledging and accepting ASU’s Computer, Internet and Electronic Communications Policy. Damage, theft, or misuse may result in a service charge, community billing, disciplinary action and/or suspension of computing privileges. Residence Hall computer labs are for conducting academic related business.

Answers for your questions.
Can I have a computer in my room?

Yes, you can have a computer in your room. ASU is not liable for loss, theft, or damage of computer equipment, or damage to/failure of equipment due to interuption of utilities. Therfore, you are urged to obtain insurance coverage for personal property. You must also comply with the ASU Computing and Communications Policies that governs acceptable computer use.

What are my responsibilities as a computer user in the residence halls?

The student must supply and/or purchase all hardware and software including computers and Ethernet cards.

The Student must agree to the terms specified by ASU Computing and Communications Policies

The Students should understand that the Residential Life MIS staff is only available to assist with network connectivity related questions and problems. Staff not trained to perform other hardware and unrelated software, such as a student's hard drive, video card, games, etc.

By seeking assistance you assume all responsibility for data loss and/or system failure that might occur. Students are urged to back up important data and scan for viruses before seeking assistance.

The student must know that only limited support is provided if an unsupported Ethernet card was purchased.

The student must ensure that the installation of a network card will not void the computer's warranty. It is recommended that the computer manufacturer be contacted for verification.

The student must own the operating system disks, CDs, documentation and any software licenses required for the Ethernet card before attempting an installation or requesting assistance from a Residential Life MIS Staff member.

Considering the nature of the internet, you are responsible for securing your own system to prevent it from being compromised by outside sources.

What is an Ethernet Adapter (NIC) card?

Ethernet cards are also often referred to as Network Interface Cards (NIC) or Network Adapters. This is the device that allows your computer to communicate with the network. It is like a modem, but does not use your phone line.

Can I run a server from my computer?

No, server usage of bandwith can cause network outages or interuptions and can result in suspension of Internet service to others. Computer equipment cannot be set up for commercial purposes.

NOTE: Windows 2000 Server, Advanced Server or Netware Servers - At this time, Residential Life does not officially support any version of Windows 2000 Server or Advanced Server. You may, however, use a Windows 2000 machine on our network, given you disable DDNS. If you fail to abide by this, you may lose your In-Room Connection.
