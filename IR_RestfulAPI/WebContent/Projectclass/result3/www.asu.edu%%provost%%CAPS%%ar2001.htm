Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Annual Report 2001

Members: Santos Vega, Chair; Victor Becerra, Maggie Mangini, Stefan Myhajlenko, Helen Seaton, and Linda Shackle. Victor and Stefan were the newly elected members.

An ongoing issue this year involved the status of Academic Associates (AA). They are Academic Professionals without continuing status. The majority of the Academic Associates are academic advisors. They are all required to have a master's degree and some have a Ph.D. Victor Becerra, an Academic Associate, was elected to membership on CAPS for the next three years. Victor left ASU at the end of the semester. Carol Behl was elected as the new AA representative. Two of their main concerns are voting rights and a grievance procedures. A letter was drafted and sent to Dr. Hackett concerning the voting rights issue.

Dr. Gail Hackett, the new Vice Provost attended a get acquainted meeting of CAPS on February 7. Dr. Hackett discussed some of the steps she has taken to become better informed about Academic Professionals. The issue of Academic Associates was also discussed.

Gary Hembree, Chair of the AP Grievance Committee, visited at the February 7 meeting and presented some changes needed in AP bylaws and procedures to conform with the October, 1999 Arizona Supreme Court ruling on the rights of grievants to representation by counsel. The Grievance Committee will draft the changes and bring to CAPS. Then it will be brought to all APs and then to the Personnel Committee of the Academic Senate for comment. Faculty and Classified Staff have already made the necessary changes.

CAPS sponsored two brown-bag lunch meetings open to all APs to feature the work of Academic Professionals at ASU. Richard (Dick) Filley, head of the Corporate Leaders and Internship Program at the College of Engineering, spoke at the November 1 meeting, and Betty Craft, Director, Distance Learning Technology, was the featured speaker at the May 2 meeting. Betty spoke about the delivery systems, the courses, and the students in the distance learning program at ASU.

The new CAPS members for the 2001/2002 year are Carol Behl, Annie Platoff, and Patricia Feldman. Usually there are only two new members elected but an additional person was needed to replace Victor Becerra. Patricia was the alternate and will serve for two years rather than the usual three years. Maggie Mangini and Helen Seaton are rotating off the committee this year.

Helen Seaton
Secretary
