February 25, 2003

Governor nominates Crow for education board

ASU President Michael Crow has been nominated by Gov. Janet Napolitano to serve on the State Board of Education, the key policy making body for Arizona's public school system.

Crow, who is replacing University of Arizona President Peter Likins, will serve a four-year term. By state statute, the nine-member board includes a president from one of the three state universities on a rotating basis, and is led by the State Superintendent of Public Instruction.

"I appreciate the Governor's nomination and I look forward to my role, which provides additional opportunity for the universities to partner with K-12 and advance all education in Arizona," says Crow.

The board meets at least 10 times annually and, by Arizona education code, has 24 specific powers and duties for directing the public school system. Crow's first meeting will be Feb. 24. The nomination will receive formal approval by the Arizona State Senate this spring.

Napolitano has also named ASU Vice President for Research & Economic Affairs Jonathan Fink to the Governor's Council on Innovation & Technology. The council, created by executive order, includes high technology business leaders that will advise the governor on technology industry issues.

The 31-member council also includes the vice presidents for research from the University of Arizona and Northern Arizona University, and leaders from various science and technology firms throughout the state.

"I think it's important to have a group like this convened by the governor," says Fink. "We will spend a lot of time looking at the important role of technology and science in the future of the state. Gov. Napolitano has shown she will be engaged in this process and that she has a good understanding of the issues."

The council will focus primarily on three key issues for the state - creating a statewide strategy for improved technology transfer; attracting more venture capital and helping new tech-based businesses; and developing Arizona's infrastructure to support technology growth. The group will also provide long-term policy input for the governor.
