March 7, 2006

Film series sets stage for bioethics debate

There's no popcorn, but the newest "movie theater" at ASU promises a series of provocative films and lively discussions.

The Bioethics, Policy and Law Program, part of the Center for Biology and Society in the School of Life Sciences, will continue its popular - and free - Bioethics Film Series this semester with three films: "Dirty Pretty Things," March 9; "The Sea Inside," April 6; and "Safe," April 27.

The films are shown at 5:15 p.m. in Life Sciences Center E-104, and they are followed by a discussion that Jason Robert, assistant professor, School of Life Sciences, says is always "animated."

The idea for a bioethics film series originated with Felicity Snyder, program coordinator in the Center for Biology and Society, and Joan McGregor, director of the Bioethics, Policy and Law Program in the School of Life Sciences.

"I think about film for just about everything," Snyder says. "It is a wonderful medium to reach people who might otherwise feel that a topic isn't accessible or may not affect them. And when you watch a film together, you have an excellent starting point for a good conversation."

"The series originally was intended as a way to spark discussion of bioethics issues across the campus and within the local community," Robert adds. "But we also decided to run it as a one-credit course for life sciences students."

Last semester, the series included four films: "GATTACA," "Miss Evers' Boys," "Million Dollar Baby" and "Outbreak."

Each film attracted an audience of 40 to 50, Robert says, with a panel of one or more experts to lead the discussion.

"The point is for the discussion to be constructive, even where people disagree," he says. "And there is always lots of disagreement!"

He goes on to add: "The films are selected based on recommendations from students, members of the Bioethics Club and our own experiences. We're always looking for new ideas."

The topic for March 9 is "Human Organs for Transport/Trafficking in Human Organs." Guest panelists will be members of the Donor Network of Arizona. The film, "Dirty Pretty Things," rated R, is a film about black-market human organ smuggling in London.

On April 6, the topic will be "Death and Dying," with guest panelists to be announced. The film, "The Sea Inside," rated PG-13, is the true story of Spaniard Ramón Sampedro, who fought a 30-year campaign to win the right to end his life with dignity. The film explores Ramón's relationships with two women: Julia, a lawyer who supports his cause, and Rosa, a local woman who wants to convince him that life is worth living.

The topic for April 27 will be "Human Health and Environmental Toxins." The guest panelist will be Randel Hanson from ASU's School of Justice and Social Inquiry. The film, "Safe," rated R, is about Carol, a prototypical upper-middle class housewife who begins to complain of vague symptoms of illness. She "doesn't feel right," has unexplained headaches, congestion, a dry cough, nosebleeds, vomiting and trouble breathing. Her family doctor treats her concerns dismissively and suggests a psychiatrist. Eventually, an allergist tells her that she has environmental illness - in essence, she has become allergic to the 20th century.

All films are free and open to the public. For more information, call Felicity Snyder at (480) 965-8927.
