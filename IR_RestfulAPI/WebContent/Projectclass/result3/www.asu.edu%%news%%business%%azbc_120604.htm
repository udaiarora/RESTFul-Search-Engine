December 6, 2004

Arizona’s economic forecast calls for a sunnier 2005

While national economic news reports often dwell on the negative, a brighter light is shining on the Grand Canyon State, according to the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

Arizona ’s economic outlook continues to brighten as figures come in on income, sales, and housing, according to Arizona Blue Chip contributing editor Lee McPheters. “The forecasters now confirm that 2005 will be the third consecutive year of improvement in the Arizona economy. They expect acceleration in job creation, another good year for retail trade, and a rebound in tourism. Current areas of strength, including construction, health services, and business services should remain solid.”

Twelve months ago, 30 states were losing jobs, McPheters said. “Now, only three states – Massachusetts , Michigan and Ohio – are losing employment, while the national economy has restored some 2 million jobs compared to the year before. Arizona has been a beneficiary of the turnaround, with some 60,000 new jobs estimated for this year. That’s good news, but the better news is that 2005 should bring even stronger gains for most all Arizona indicators.”

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark asked panel members to share their first look at 2006. The early forecasts are similar to 2005, except for rising interest rates. “ Arizona is typically very sensitive to interest rates,” Clark said, “but the drag of higher rates should be more than balanced by an improvement in the national economy.”

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center , an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. For information, call (480) 965-5543.
