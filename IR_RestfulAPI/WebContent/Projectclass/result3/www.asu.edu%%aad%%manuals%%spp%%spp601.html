SPP 601: Employee Development

Purpose

To support continuous learning by encouraging employees to participate in work-related or university-sponsored training, including professional development classes, workshops, and conferences

Source

University policy

Applicability

Classified, service professional, and administrative employees

Policy

Release Time for Training

In order to continuously improve the university and its services, employees have a responsibility to participate in continuous learning. The university provides release time from regular duties for employees to participate in training that will enhance skills and abilities on the job, provide professional growth and development, and expand knowledge. An employee must receive approval from his or her supervisor before using release time for training. In granting release time for training, the supervisor considers department needs and the employee’s request.

Employees should not be required to make up work time missed in participating in supervisor-approved training. If an employee chooses to participate in training or educational activities on his or her own time, the time spent is not considered as time worked and will not be considered for overtime or compensatory time.

Minimum Requirement for Professional Development

Supervisors are required to allow employees a minimum of 16 hours of release time per year for professional development. The university encourages employees to take advantage of this opportunity.

Yearly Plan for Training

Because employees are expected to complete their regular job responsibilities, supervisors and employees should work together to identify reasonable accommodations that allow the employee to participate fully in training, without negatively impacting service. It is suggested that the employee and supervisor agree to a tentative yearly plan for training during the performance evaluation cycle; such a plan helps in planning release time.

Fees for Classes

Fees for classes are not automatically reimbursed; departments are encouraged to fund those training opportunities that will enhance the employee’s contributions to the university.

Employee Training and Development

For more information on employee development classes, workshops, and conferences, see the Human Resources Professional Development Web site.

Employment Credit for Training Classes

Supervisory employment credit for training classes may be earned through a series of workshops specifically designed to prepare employees for supervisory positions at ASU. For more information, see the Supervisory Academy page of the Employee Development Web site.

Cross-Reference

For information on release time for attending university classes, see SPP 505, “Employee Reduced In-State Tuition.”
