The Melikian Center Staff

Stephen K. Batalden

Professor of History and Director of The Melikian Center at ASU. He directs ASU's academic exchange with the University of Sts. Kiril & Metodij in Skopje, Macedonia. A specialist on the religious and cultural history of modern Russia and East Europe, Dr. Batalden is the author of Catherine the Great's Greek Prelate: Eugenios Voulgaris in Russia, 1771-1806 (Columbia University Press, 1982); Seeking God: The Recovery of Religious Identity in Orthodox Russia, Ukraine, and Georgia (Northern Illinois University Press, DeKalb, 1993) and, with Sandra Batalden, The Newly Independent States of Eurasia: Handbook of Former Soviet Republics, 2nd ed. (Oryx Press, Phoenix, 1997). His work on the politics of Russian Bible translation is currently in press. His new research is on religious property restitution in the Balkans. Advisor to candidates for the baccalaureate certificate in Russian and East European Studies and mentor to numerous graduate students, he was awarded the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Dean's Quality Teaching Award in 1988.

E-mail: stephen.batalden@asu.edu

Ariann Stern

Ariann Stern-Gottschalk, PhD (University of California, Los Angeles) is director of the Critical Languages Institute (CLI). A linguist with a specialization in Slavic languages, Ari comes to The Melikian Center from the UCLA Language Materials Project.

E-mail: cli@asu.edu
David Brokaw

David Brokaw is assistant director of The Melikian Center. Before joining the Center, David served as the Program Administrator for the Cincinnati-Ukraine Partnership/Cincinnati-Kharkiv Sister City Project. He holds an MA in Russian Literature from The Ohio State University and an MBA from the University of Cincinnati.

E-mail: david.brokaw@asu.edu
Susan Edgington

B.A., University of Arizona, is the Melikian Center Business Manager. She holds a BS in business administration with a major in accountancy from the University of Arizona, Tucson. Susan has extensive international experience, having lived overseas for nearly 10 years and visited more than 35 countries around the world.

E-mail: susan.edginton@asu.edu
Kathleen Evans-Romaine

Kathleen Evans-Romaine, Ph.D. (University of Michigan), is Research Administrator for The Melikian Center. Kathleen was technology manager for the Middlebury Russian School from 2001-2005 and Assistant Director of the Ohio University Institute for Applied and Professional Ethics from 1998-2006. Prior to that she worked in Moscow as translation manager for general-interest magazines.

E-mail: kathleen.evans-romaine@asu.edu
