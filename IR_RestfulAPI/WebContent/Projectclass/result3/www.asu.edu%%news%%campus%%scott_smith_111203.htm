November 12, 2003

Smith to lead ASU legislative lobbying effort

After a national search, ASU alumnus Scott A. Smith was named director of state and local government relations and joined the university Nov. 1.

Smith earned a bachelor's degree in political science from ASU in 1991, and has served as a legislative liaison for the Arizona Department of Administration. He has 15 years' experience working in Arizona state government in a variety of administrative positions. He succeeds longtime ASU lobbyist Blake Anderson, who passed away in June.

Deputy Vice President of Public Affairs Steve Miller says he is pleased Smith has agreed to join ASU during this era of positive change and unprecedented growth.

"I am excited that Scott accepted the position and I know he will contribute tremendously to our effort," Miller says. "Scott brings a great deal of experience and a skill set to our team that will help ASU move its governmental relations agenda forward on all fronts. I look forward to working with him."

Smith describes this opportunity to work for his alma mater as his dream job and says there are many reasons he is thrilled about joining ASU.

"For starters, Dr. Crow, his vision for ASU, the tremendous influence he has had on ASU in such a short period of time, and his dynamic personality," Smith says. "All these factors combined make for an incredible work environment, and I am excited to be a part of it, and learn and grow professionally."

Smith says he is also looking forward to this position, which presents a very special opportunity to work with two institutions - ASU and the Arizona Legislature.

Smith says being an ASU alumnus makes this position especially meaningful for him.

"I know that my experience at ASU, the degree I earned and the friends I made, laid the foundation for my success in life and opened many doors for me," Smith says.

"This position allows me to give back to the university on an entirely different level, and will enable me to spend my professional time advocating on behalf of an institution that is near and dear to me."
