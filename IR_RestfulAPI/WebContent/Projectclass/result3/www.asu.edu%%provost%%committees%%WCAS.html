WEST CAMPUS ACADEMIC SENATE

The Academic Senate is an advisory body representing the Academic Assembly. The Assembly shall consider and advise on academic affairs, educational policy, faculty and academic professional affairs, faculty-student policies, and other matters of governance affecting faculty and academic professional roles and conditions at the West campus.

Academic Senators are elected for two-year terms beginning June 1. The Senate’s standing committees consist of the Personnel Committee; the Curriculum and Programs Committee/General Studies Council (the Graduate Studies Advisory Council works parallel to the Curriculum Committee but addresses Graduate issues instead); the Scholarship, Research and Creative Activities Committee; the Teaching Excellence Committee; and the Student Issues Committee. Membership on the Senate Committees requires Academic Assembly membership. Committee members are elected for three-year terms from within their colleges. Detailed descriptions of the Senate and its committees are included in the West Campus Academic Assembly and Senate Constitution and Bylaws (ACDW 112-01), which can be located within the West campus Academic Senate’s web site at www.west.asu.edu/asenate.
