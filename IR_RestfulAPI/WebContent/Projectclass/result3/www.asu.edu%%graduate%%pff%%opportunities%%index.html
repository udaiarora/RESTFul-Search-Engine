Financial Opportunities

Faculty Emeriti Fellowship
Deadline: April 2, 2007
Students applying for the Participatory Phase of PFF are eligible to apply for this fellowship. Applications will be available at this site in February 2007.

Travel Grants
Students enrolled in the Participatory Phase of PFF are eligible for a grant of up to $300 for travel related to their professional development (such as conferences, visits to other campuses, etc.).

Gastwirth Loans
The Division of Graduate Studies and Student Financial Assistance Office will make available student loans for up to $10,000 to doctoral students enrolled in the Participatory Phase of PFF. These loans are funded by a gift from the Gastwirth family in support of students pursuing academic careers in institutions of higher education.

The loan amount is based on an assessment of financial need and the maximum loan eligibility is $10,000 per academic year. Up to 100% of the loan may be forgiven. The forgiveness of the loan principal will be at a rate of 20% per year for up to five years of employment as a faculty member at an institution of higher education. During the award period, the awardee must be a full-time student enrolled in a minimum of 9 units of graduate level work in each of the fall and spring semesters (6 units for graduate associates).

Doctoral students are eligible to apply if they are:
  • Enrolled in the Participatory Phase (second year) of PFF

  • Characterized by outstanding overall records of achievement and intend to seek a career as a faculty member in an institution of higher education

  • Able to demonstrate financial need as established by the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)

  • Not in default on any students loans and/or without a delinquent account with the university

  • U.S. citizens or permanent residents
Applications for the Gastwirth Loan are available by contacting Catherine Lebihan, clebihan@asu.edu. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis until all funds are awarded, and you are encouraged to apply as early as possible.
