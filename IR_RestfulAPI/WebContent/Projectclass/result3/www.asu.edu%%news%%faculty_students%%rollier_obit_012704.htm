January 27, 2004

Great friend, teacher will be missed

Dwayne A. Rollier, emeritus professor in the department of industrial engineering in the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, passed away Dec. 29, at the age of 67 from complications of a stroke.

Rollier moved to Tempe with his family in 1971 for a teaching position in the then College of Engineering. He received his B.S. in math and his M.S. in statistics from Oklahoma State University as well as his Ph.D. in statistics from Florida State University.

His areas of research included experimental design, manufacturing engineering and applied statistics. Rollier also worked in industry and served in the U.S. Army before coming to ASU.

After rising to assistant chair and professor of industrial engineering, Rollier retired in 2001 to focus on his Christian ministry.

"His devotion to education and ASU were unparalleled, his integrity without question, his loyalty and selflessness without equal," said Gary Hogg, industrial engineering department chair. "This insured that he had the trust of all, and thus was one of the greatest peacemakers I've ever known. He was a man of deep and real religious faith and he believed strongly in the power of education to transform lives; combining these, he touched so many lives and left a legacy that extends to many nations. It was a rare privilege for the Fulton School to count Dwayne a great friend and colleague. We are fortunate in academia to work with so many fine men and women who give so much of themselves; none in my experience has done so with the grace and humility of Dwayne Rollier."

Rollier was recognized for his dedication with the Pritsker Teaching Award in 1996 and again in 2000. He also is the two-time recipient of the Outstanding Teaching/Advisor Award presented by the industrial engineering students.

Memorial donations to carry on Rollier's ministry can be sent to 40 E. Balboa Dr., Tempe AZ 85282 for completion of a building already in progress.
