Librarians' Council

I.2: Exchange Programs

Effective 12/20/89 .. Revised 12/20/89

POLICY

The administration of the University Libraries encourages and supports the concept of a program of academic professional exchange between ASU and other institutions within the United States and abroad, so long as the exchange results in identifiable benefits to the University Libraries and the individual concerned. The benefit to the University Libraries as a whole shall be the primary criterion for evaluating program proposals.


The proposals of academic professionals interested in entering into exchange programs between institutions must comply with the University's policies and procedures for faculty exchange programs (currently being developed) and be approved by the appropriate department head and Associate Dean as well as by the Dean of University Libraries, University Librarian and the Academic Vice President. Proposals will be considered for either unilateral or bilateral "exchanges."


Assuming adequate justification for the basic proposal, approval is contingent upon the feasibility of establishing satisfactory arrangements to cover the professional responsibilities of the academic professional who will be absent from the University Libraries. In order to accomplish this as well as the arrangements for the exchange itself, a significant amount of advanced planning time is required.


ASU academic professionals participating in exchange programs shall be expected to return to employment at ASU for a period of time that is at least commensurate with the duration of the exchange. They shall further be expected to render a comprehensive report on the exchange experience and are encouraged to submit the report for publication in a professional journal.


Participation in an exchange program does not alter the probationary period of an academic professional who has not yet achieved continuing appointment. This fact should be weighed carefully in arriving at the decision to participate.


ASU academic professionals whose proposals for exchange programs are approved shall, in the majority of cases, remain on the ASU payroll, in the interest of maintaining the integrity of their retirement, health insurance, and other university benefits. In those cases where they would otherwise be paid by the exchange institution, ASU will request that funds equivalent to the base salary be transferred to ASU.


Exchange librarians coming to ASU from other institutions shall, in the majority of cases, continue to have their salaries and benefits paid by the home institution. In the case of demonstrated need for benefits which may be made available only by ASU, efforts shall be made to place the individual on the ASU payroll, contingent upon the willingness of the home institution to transfer to ASU the funds equivalent to the base salary of the individual concerned.


Academic professionals proposing/engaged in an exchange program of whatever kind shall be responsible for assuming all costs exclusive of salary which are associated with the program (e.g., travel, housing, per diem, incidental expenses).
