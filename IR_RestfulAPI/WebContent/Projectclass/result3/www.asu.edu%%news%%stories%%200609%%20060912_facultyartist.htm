September 12, 2006

Faculty Artist Series highlights talent at ASU

This fall, world-renowned faculty musicians at the ASU Herberger College of Fine Arts School of Music will step out of their classrooms, gather their instruments and take the stage for performances that appeal to patrons of all musical tastes.

Fourteen solo and ensemble performances, ranging from chamber music to jazz piano, comprise the fall 2006 Faculty Artist Series.

The Faculty Artist Series enables the community to hear and experience musicians who are so devoted to their art that they choose to share it by teaching and performing.

Patrons can enjoy these concerts at affordable prices on a regular basis. Faculty Artist Series concerts take place through Nov. 9 in Katzin Concert Hall and Organ Hall, in the Music Building on the Tempe campus. General admission tickets are $8. Series patrons are encouraged to purchase single tickets in advance. Tickets are available at the door on a space-available basis.

Pending availability, ASU students can pick up free recital tickets during the week they occur at the Herberger College Box Office, located in the Nelson Fine Arts Center. A current ASU student ID is required at pick-up. For more information, visit the Web page (http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/facultyseries/index.html).

The remaining fall schedule of Faculty Artist Recitals includes:

? 7:30 p.m., Sept. 15, Katzin Concert Hall ? Jonathan Swartz, violin, and Wendy Chen, piano.

? 2:30 p.m., Sept. 17, Katzin Concert Hall ? Robert Hamilton, piano. This solo piano concert, which celebrates the centenary of Shostakovich's birth, consists of 20th-century works by popular musicians including Debussy, Bartok and Shostakovich.

? 7:30 p.m., Sept. 22, Katzin Concert Hall ? Eckart Sellheim and Dian Baker, piano four hands. This program focuses on German Romantic composer Carl Reinecke (1824-1910), along with other 19th century composers.

? 7:30 p.m., Sept. 23, Organ Hall ? Danwen Jiang, violin, and Siegbert Rampe, harpsichord and fortepiano. This program features four important sonatas for violin with harpsichord or fortepiano by Bach and Mozart.

? 2:30 p.m., Sept. 24, Katzin Concert Hall ? ?Music for Voice and Viola; Jan Thompson and friends.? This recital features the music of Charles Martin Loeffler and Johannes Brahms for voice, viola, oboe and piano. Thompson collaborates with music faculty Nancy Buck, viola; Martin Schuring, oboe; and Robert Barefield, baritone. Guest artist Mary Sue Hyatt, mezzo-soprano, will sing.

? 7:30 p.m., Oct. 8, Katzin Concert Hall ? John Ericson, horn, Andrew Campbell, piano, and Jorge Montilla, clarinet. Ericson plays a mixture of Baroque and more recent works with faculty colleagues Campbell and Montilla.

? 2:30 p.m., Oct. 15, Katzin Concert Hall ? Jorge Montilla, clarinet.

? 2:30 p.m., Oct. 22, Katzin Concert Hall ? Caio Pagano, piano. This performance will feature works by classical composers including Schuman and Chopin.

? 7:30 p.m., Nov. 2, Katzin Concert Hall ? Katie McLin, violin, and Andrew Campbell, piano.

? 7:30 p.m., Nov. 6, Katzin Concert Hall ? Catalin Rotaru, bass, and Robert Mills, piano.

? 2:30 p.m., Nov. 12, Katzin Concert Hall ? Michael Kocour, jazz piano.

? 2:30 p.m., Nov. 19, Katzin Concert Hall ? Carole FitzPatrick, soprano, Robert Barefield, baritone, and Andrew Campbell, piano.

For a complete calendar listing of all Herberger College events, visit the Web site (http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/calendar/calendar.php).
