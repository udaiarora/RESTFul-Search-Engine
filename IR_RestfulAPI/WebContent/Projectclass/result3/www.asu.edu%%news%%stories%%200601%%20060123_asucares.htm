January 24, 2006

ASU CARES to spruce up Park at South Mountain

Volunteers sought for community service project

Planning a work party for 500 or more volunteers is not easy.

Just finding a site and situation that can accommodate so many people is difficult - and, at the last minute, another organization can "steal" the site, forcing a search for another location.

Such are the worries of the person in charge of ASU's annual all-university community service project, ASU CARES.

In past years, ASU volunteers have worked at the Thomas J. Pappas High School for children of homeless families; the Grant Park neighborhood in Phoenix; Sunshine Acres Children's Home and St. Mary's Food Bank; and picked citrus in the Arcadia district for the West Side Food Bank.

This year, responsibility for the seventh ASU CARES has been handed over from the former vice presidential area for administration to ASU Public Affairs, and project director Terri Cranmer already has the location hurdle crossed.

On Feb. 11, ASU volunteers will converge on Phoenix's Park at South Mountain to paint gatehouses and curbs, remove fountain grass, clean and repair trails, and pick up litter as the 2006 ASU CARES project.

The project is co-sponsored by the Phoenix Parks and Recreation Department.

The Park at South Mountain is the largest city park in the country, with 16,000 acres of mountain range. It's a popular place for hiking, picnicking and barbecues.

On Feb. 11, as usual, volunteers will receive ASU CARES T-shirts, lunch, water - and many thanks.

Registration will take place from 8 - 9 a.m. Work will begin at 9 a.m. and conclude at 12:30 p.m. Lunch will be served at 1 p.m.

Rain or shine, volunteers will meet at a designated site in the park and work in teams. Tools will be provided for all assignments, and volunteers should wear sturdy, closed-toed shoes, a hat, sunglasses or safety goggles and bring sunscreen. ASU students, staff, faculty and alumni are invited to participate.

Volunteers can sign up on the ASU CARES Web site (http://asucares.asu.edu).

Anyone interested in becoming a team leader should contact Ellen Newell, the team leader captain, at (480) 965-2682 or (480) 235-5198.

"There is no better way to enjoy the Arizona winter weather and beautiful desert landscape than by lending a helping hand to improve one of the city's most scenic parks," Cranmer says.
