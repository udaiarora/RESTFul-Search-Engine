February 23, 2005

Newcomers drove AZ population growth in 2004

Arizona recorded another strong wave of population growth in 2004, which marks the 11th consecutive year the Grand Canyon State has recorded the second-fastest growth rate in the nation, according to an analysis in the current issue of the Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

Arizona ’s rate of increase was 3.0 percent, second only behind Nevada (4.1 percent), according to Lee McPheters, contributing editor of the Arizona Blue Chip. This year was the Silver State ’s 18th consecutive year in first place for population growth. The population numbers were recently released by the U.S. Census Bureau.

Other Western states ranked among the top 10 growth states included Idaho (4), Texas (6), Utah (7), and New Mexico (10). Population for the nation as a whole grew by 1.0 percent, as it has for the past several years.

Arizona ranked fourth among all states in increase in the absolute number of new residents, with 164,612, according to the Census Bureau. California led the nation in new head count, with more than 431,000. Half of Arizona ’s new residents came from domestic migrants moving from other states. The increase in domestic relocators was supplemented by larger gains due to natural increase (births minus deaths). The third source of new residents, international migration, was actually somewhat smaller in 2004 as compared to 2003.

As of July 2004, the population of the Grand Canyon State was 5.7 million persons. The next official Census Bureau population estimates for the states will be released in December 2005.

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center , an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University .
