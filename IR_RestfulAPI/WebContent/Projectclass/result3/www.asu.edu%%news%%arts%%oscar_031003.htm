March 10, 2003

ASU theater professors reveal Academy Award predictions

As Hollywood prepares for its biggest night - the 75th Annual Academy Awards on March 23, ASU theater professors Ron Newcomer and Gus Edwards are comparing notes and sharing their predictions on this year's Oscar-nominated films.

Newcomer, who has been with the ASU theater department for more than five years, says this year's ceremony promises to be an exciting one.

"The Oscars usually try to look seriously at a work of art when it comes to films," he says. "Some years they don't find any. This year, they have found quite a few that have done a good job in trying to tell a different kind of story."
Edwards, professor of film studies and multicultural theater, agrees and says one film has created a big buzz among movie buffs.

"A real wild card this year is 'Chicago,'" Edward says. "Who would have thought that a musical, something that comes out of left field, with so much pizazz, would receive so many nominations. If a film seems to have a little bit of an edge on the others, I suspect it's 'Chicago.'"

Along with 'Chicago,' this year's nominees for Best Picture are "Gangs of New York," "The Hours," "The Lord of the Rings: the Twin Towers" and "The Pianist."
Newcomer and Edwards say "Chicago" should receive the Oscar this year.

"I frankly have not enjoyed going to a movie that much in a long, long time," Newcomer says. "It was just a very happy surprise. It was very uplifting."

"He (Director Rob Marshall ) reinvents the musical," Edwards says. "'Chicago' is very deserving of it."

However, Newcomer says there may be two other films that may get some attention from the academy.

"I have a gut reaction that maybe the 'Twin Towers' will win," Newcomer says. "I thought it was an extremely well done film. It was better than the first in many ways, even though it would not exist on its own without the first film. The only one I don't want to win is 'Gangs of New York.' I just think it was bad film. Marty (Scorsese) may get his Oscar for best directing, but he's done better work in the past."

This year's nominees for Best Actor in a Leading Role are Adrien Brody ("The Pianist"); Nicolas Cage ("Adaptation"); Michael Caine ("The Quiet American"); Daniel Day-Lewis ("Gangs of New York") and Jack Nicholson ("About Schmidt").
Newcomer and Edwards feel the academy will select a Hollywood favorite as this year's winner.

"I suspect Jack Nicholson is going to do it again," Edwards says. "There's no denying it, he was great in 'About Schmidt.'"

"I also think it's going to happen." Newcomer says. "Of course if it does, he'll tie the record with Katharine Hepburn for the most Oscars (four). Nicholson is a working legend in every sense of the word."

This year's nominees for Best Actress in a Leading Role are Salma Hayek ("Frida"); Nicole Kidman ("The Hours"); Diane Lane ("Unfaithful"); Julianne Moore ("Far From Heaven"); and Renée Zellweger ("Chicago").

Edwards says he made a prediction to his theater class right after he saw the film.
"I say Julianne Moore," he says. "She did not miss one beat of that character - the emotion and her ability to convince you that she is that character."

"I don't disagree," Newcomer says. "She deserves it. I think Renée was wonderful in 'Chicago,' but she is one of those actresses that almost made it look too easy. I am also glad to see that Diane Lane was nominated. I don't think many people saw her film, but she is definitely worthy of a nomination."

ASU students who are aspiring to create a film, possibly worthy of winning an Oscar, may consider the advice of Newcomer and Edwards - "Be creative and tell a good story."

"The students probably have more opportunities to get in the business now, and they have less chance of staying there once they get in," Newcomer says. "It's probably the most exciting business in the world, but it's right up there with the oil business - it's international. It takes both sides of the brain to be successful in this business."

"You definitely have to tell a good story," Edwards says. "Without a story, you don't have anything."

The 75th Annual Academy Awards will be broadcast on March 23 on ABC (Channel 15). For the broadcast time and a listing of all the Academy Awards categories, visit the Web at (http://www.oscar.com/).
