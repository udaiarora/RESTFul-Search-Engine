November 24, 2003

Bringing hope for the holidays

Though it may seem hard to believe, the holidays are approaching fast. As in years past, ASU employees are reaching out to those who are less fortunate and are sharing the true spirit of the season.

Felicity Snyder, office specialist senior with the School of Life Sciences, is helping to coordinate her department's holiday charity drive, which runs through Dec. 4. All items donated will be given to the Tempe Community Action Agency for families in need.

Snyder says supporting charitable groups such as the Tempe Community Action Agency is crucial, especially this time of year.

"I know that these services are so important, and I personally just can't stand the idea of anyone going hungry or a child not getting at least one new toy during the holidays," Snyder says. "I would encourage everyone on campus to get involved and do something good for their community this holiday season and throughout the year."
Snyder says she is hoping to collect a variety of non-perishable food items, not just canned food, but also cereal, pasta, stuffing mix, dressing, and canned ham.
Other items being requested include new toys, toiletries and cash donations. Items can be dropped off at Life Sciences Center C284, C226 or E218.

Other ASU departments are also helping those in need, and there is still plenty of time to get involved.

. The Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering is sponsoring the Tempe Pappas School for Homeless children for their holiday project. The following items are being requested: new clothing for those in grades K-8, new toys, wind up alarm clocks, blankets, travel-sized toiletries, gift certificates to fast food restaurants and non perishable food items.

Contact Lee Cruz at (480) 965-3424 or Karen Plante at (480) 965-5384 for drop-off locations and items needed. Checks may be made to the Thomas J. Pappas School.

. ASU's College of Nursing is seeking donations for its Breaking the Cycle (BTC) clinic, a nurse-managed clinic based at Grace Lutheran Church in downtown Phoenix. They are collecting food, clothing and gifts for families in need. For information, e-mail (christina.calfee@asu.edu) before Dec. 5.

To have your unit's holiday drive listed in an upcoming issue of ASU Insight, e-mail the items requested, deadline and contact information to Ina Zajac at (izajac@asu.edu) or call (480) 965-8145.

This article appeared in the November 21, 2003 issue of ASU Insight.
