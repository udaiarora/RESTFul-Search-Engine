ASU Herberger College of Fine Arts announces its 2004-05 Mainstage Season of Theatre, Dance, Organ and Lyric Opera Theatre

Swing with Sondheim. Get on board with Cole Porter. Be Reckless. Weep with Romeo and Juliet. Experiment with dance and performance art. Enjoy the classic songs of Christmas. It's time to gear up for another exciting season of Mainstage performances from the ASU Herberger College of Fine Arts. 

The ASU Herberger 2004-05 Mainstage Season presents a total of 21 productions featuring the best and brightest student and faculty performing artists. Season ticket packages are available now through Sept. 17 and single tickets go on sale Sept. 7. Call 480-965-6447 for ticket information or go to http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/mainstage/index.html .  All events are on the ASU Tempe campus. The Lyric Opera Theatre season is sponsored by wealth management group The Harris TM .

ASU Herberger Mainstage Theatre
Dancing at Lughnasa (Sept. 24 - Oct. 3)  Simultaneously exhilarating and poignant, this classic takes the audience to the author's Irish home and his memory of his mother and four maiden aunts, living within the confines of rural life until their spirits are unleashed in a frenzy of dance. Recommended for mature audiences. Directed by Barbara Acker.

The Ash Girl (Nov. 4-20) is an imaginative re-telling of the classic Cinderella story, portraying familiar characters like the Stepmother, Stepsisters and Prince Charming with surprising depth and dimension. Written in flowing poetic language, the images are lush and original. This play is entertaining for the whole family. Directed by Pamela Sterling.

       
Reckless (Nov. 5-21) is a hilariously dark comedy. The New York Times calls it, "A bittersweet Christmas fable for our time. 'It's a Wonderful Life' re-imagined for a bruising contemporary America in which homelessness may be a pervasive spiritual condition." Recommended for mature audiences. Directed by Erma Duricko.

Romeo and Juliet (Feb. 11-27) is the timeless story of first love - romantic, sensuous and forbidden, set in a modern production. Recommended for mature audiences; contains nudity and violence. The audience will be required to exit the theatre for some outdoor scenes. Directed by Victoria Holloway.

A Festival of New Work (April 13-24)
Three original theatrical works created by ASU faculty and graduate students.

Stolen Children is a gripping tale written by graduate student Kristyn Benedyk from the history of early Arizona, when orphans were shipped from New York to local homes and racial turmoil erupted between Anglo and Mexican families. Recommended for mature audiences.

Raw Footage is an insanely funny and disturbing play written by graduate student Laurel Haines about a documentary filmmaker who manufactures his own version of heart-tugging "reality." Recommended for mature audiences. Directed by Ron May.

Performance Art   Theatre professors and performance artists Lance Gharavi and Jeff McMahon present an evening of performance art developed by Herberger College faculty and graduate students exploring new theatrical forms and original artistic visions. Recommended for mature audiences.

ASU Herberger Mainstage Lyric Opera Theatre, sponsored by The Harris TM
Side by Side by Sondheim (Oct. 1-8) is a dazzling array of Sondheim's best-known songs from landmark shows, including Company , Follies , A Little Night Music , A Funny Thing Happened On The Way To The Forum , Anyone Can Whistle and Pacific Overtures . The production also features songs from West Side Story , Gypsy and Do I Hear a Waltz ? Directed by Joann Yeoman. Music director, William Reber.

The Elixir of Love (Nov. 19 - Dec. 4, 2004) is Donizetti at his comic best! A simple village lad, a beautiful country girl, a swaggering sergeant and a quack doctor add up to hilarious consequences. Donizetti's radiant opera brims with color and charm and contains the beautiful tenor aria, "A Furtive Tear." Directed by Dale Dreyfoos. Music director, Jesus Camalich.

The Rape of Lucretia (Feb. 25 - March 6) is an operatic tale of love, loyalty, betrayal and the ultimate tragedy. The story creates a powerful musical metaphor for the ongoing struggle between love and lust, innocence and violence, evil and redemption. Directed by Graham Whitehead. Music director, William Reber.

Anything Goes (April 22 - May 1) is the definitive Cole Porter musical of the 1930s! Join an unusual group of passengers sailing from New York to London on the S.S. American and enjoy the fabulous score that includes "I Get a Kick Out of You," "You're the Top," "Anything Goes," "Blow, Gabriel Blow" and the hilarious "Friendship." Directed by Graham Whitehead. Music director, Emily Parkhurst.

ASU Herberger Mainstage Dance
New Danceworks I (Oct. 14-17) and New Danceworks II (Jan. 27-30)
Master of Fine Arts degree candidates in dance stage their innovative choreography. Capping a rigorous study of dance at one of the top-ranked dance departments in the nation, these productions are rich passages of dance and athletic prowess.

Fall Dance Collection (Nov. 18-21) is a concert performance of innovative choreography shaped by a mix of faculty and student choreographers. Visiting guest artist Sang cheul Choe unveils his newest work, set on student dancers.

Spring Dance Collection (April 21-24)  This program features a high-flying new work created by guest artist Jo Kreiter, a San Francisco-based dancer and choreographer whose aerial work integrates risk, spectacle and social potency. Pieces by faculty and student choreographers complete this concert of extraordinary dance.

ASU Herberger Mainstage Organ
The Tzar of Instruments (Oct. 17)  Organist Iain Quinn showcases the rare world of Russian organ music from the time of Peter the Great to original organ works in a film score of Shostakovich, and even a one-page harmonium piece by Rachmaninov.

Bach in Weimar (Nov. 7)  Johann Sebastian Bach wrote most of his organ music during his nine-year tenure as court organist to the Duke of Weimar. This concert includes a varied program of free works, chorale settings and concerto arrangements. Kimberly Marshall, the Patricia and Leonard Goldman professor of organ, performs.

Second Annual Organ Christmas Concert (Dec. 11, three shows)
Back by popular demand!  Kimberly Marshall and the ASU Organ Studio invite you to a special holiday concert celebrating the beautiful music of Christmas.

Romantic Baroque (Feb. 6)  Celebrate Valentine's Day early with passionate music of the Renaissance and Baroque. Jack Mitchener, professor of organ at the North Carolina School of the Arts, will explore the music of Bach, Byrd, Buxtehude and Sweelinck.

The Transcriber's Art (Feb. 20)  Richard Elliott, organist for the Mormon Tabernacle in Salt Lake City, explores the organ as a one-person orchestra - performing orchestral and band works by Bach, Gluck and Grieg in a concert that is guaranteed to inspire.

Celebrating Composers' Birthdays (March 6)  ASU alumnus Michael Mazzatenta, D.M.A., will celebrate March composers' birthdays with a program of works from Max Reger, Eugene Gigout, Dudley Buck, Franz Joseph Haydn, Joseph Rheinberger, and the most celebrated composer born in March - Johann Sebastian Bach.

Daughters and Sons of Bach (March 20)  Calvert Johnson, the Charles A. Dana professor of music at Agnes Scott College, Atlanta, is an expert on music by female composers. Come hear Fanny Hensel's music played alongside that of her famous brother, Felix Mendelssohn, and a prelude and fugue by Clara Schumann. The program includes a sonata by Hindemith and a recently composed fantasy by Emma Lou Diemer.

The Herberger College of Fine Arts enriches its students and the community through innovative, collaborative and transforming experiences. The college comprises four nationally ranked academic units: the School of Music, School of Art, Department of Dance and Department of Theatre, plus the Institute for Studies in the Arts and the ASU Art Museum. To learn more, visit http://www.herbergercollege.asu.edu
