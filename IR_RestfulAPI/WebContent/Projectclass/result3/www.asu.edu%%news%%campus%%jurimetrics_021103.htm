February 11, 2003

College presents Hogan & Hartson Jurimetrics

Pamela Samuelson, Chancellor's Professor of Law and Information Management at Boalt Hall School of Law, University California, Berkeley, will deliver the Hogan & Hartson Jurimetrics Lecture in Honor of Lee Loevinger at the ASU College of Law at 4:30 p.m., Feb. 17. The "National Law Journal" has recognized Samuelson as being among the top 50 most influential female lawyers in the country. In addition to her professorial duties, she has served as director of the Berkeley Center for Law & Technology since 1996. Recently, Samuelson was elected to membership in the American Law Institute.

ASU College of Law Professor and Willard H. Pedrick Distinguished Research Professor Dennis Karjala said, "Pam Samuelson was one of the first commentators to analyze critically the almost casual 1980 decision of Congress to protect computer programs under copyright law. Since then she has been one of the most active advocates for balance between public and private interests where technological changes threaten the existing order or result in demands for new intellectual property and related rights, both domestically and internationally."

Samuelson has given numerous public presentations and published widely in the areas of copyright, software protection, and cyber-law. Her recent publications include "Privacy as Intellectual Property?" in the Stanford Law Review and "Intellectual Property and Contract Law for the Information Age: Foreword to a Symposium," in the California Law Review (1999).

Hogan & Hartson, a prominent Washington, D.C. law firm, endowed the annual Jurimetrics Lecture Series to honor Lee Loevinger, a former partner in the firm and a pioneer in integrating law with science and technology. Loevinger has been an active supporter of the ASU College of Law's Center for Law, Science and Technology.

Since 1981, the Center for the Study of Law, Science & Technology has worked to improve the scientific study and understanding of law, legal institutions and legal process by encouraging interdisciplinary research and providing services for law students and legal professionals.

A reception will follow the lecture immediately. The free event is open to the public.
For information, call Franklyn Jeans (480) 727-7895 or (franklyn.jeans@asu.edu).
