Links

Below, please find some useful links for graduate academic program development and other academic strategy research.

  • ASU Graduate Catalog
    www.asu.edu/catalog

  • Woodrow Wilson Foundation National Fellowship Foundation
    www.woodrow.org
    The Woodrow Wilson Foundation conducts influential research on higher education in the U.S., and their website has links to various reports and data they have developed.

  • Community of Science
    www.cos.com
    Community of Science (COS) is a leading global resource for hard-to-find information critical to scientific research and other projects across all disciplines.

  • CollegeSource Online
    www.collegesource.org
    CollegeSource Online is an online searchable database of thousands of college catalogs.

  • Council of Graduate Schools
    www.cgsnet.org
    CGS is a leading organization addressing the needs of graduate schools in the U.S.

  • National Center for Education Statistics
    nces.ed.gov
    Established by the Department of Education, NCES is the primary federal entity for collecting and analyzing data that are related to education in the U.S. and other nations. Numerous reports and data sets related to higher education are available through this website.

  • The National Science Foundation
    www.nsf.gov
    The NSF is the source of a large amount of research funding, as well as conducting Leading scholarship in the area of higher education and academic research.

  • European Higher Education
    europa.eu.int/comm/education/policies/educ/higher/higher_en.html
    This website includes information on various European Union higher education initiatives and policies, including the 1999 Bologna Decleration, a major agreement to coordinate European higher education.

    • Arizona Universities' Peer Institutions (complete list) www.abor.asu.edu/1_the_regents/reports_factbook/fb_files/peer-institution.html

      gradschool.uoregon.edu
