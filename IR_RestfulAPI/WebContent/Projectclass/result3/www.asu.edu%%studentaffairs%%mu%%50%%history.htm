Story of the MU

MU In the Beginning

On February 25, 1956 the Memorial Union opened its doors at Arizona State College at Tempe. The MU consisted of 109, 000 square feet accommodating 6,000 students. This was made possible by years of hard work by students, faculty, staff, alumni, and generous support from the community.

Charles Stauffer, ASC graduate from the Class of 1901 and winner of the Alumni Service Award in 1950, led the capital campaign to raise over $350,000 of the proposed $750,000 cost. Mr. Stauffer was aided by a Central Committee of 55 members, a State Committee of 16 members, and an Executive committee to drive the campaign forward from the Alumni Board. The State of Arizona had authorized $400,000 in support for the building. Alumni, students, faculty, staff, and friends contributed a total of $439,322.06 to the Memorial Union building fund. Alumni gave $20,000, Faculty and staff gave $18,000 - many of whom chose to give up to 10% of their salary for almost 3 years, and students and parents gave $12,000. Other campus organizations helped offset costs by donating money: the Bookstore gave $75,000, food services $15,000, Devils' Den $15,000, and ASASU $10,000. By 1952, $302,941.71 had been raised and on October 1, 1954 ground was broken. The Sahuaro (1956) described the land chosen for the new Memorial Union as having previously been horse pasture, according to Senator Carl Hayden.

The Arizona State College and local communities worked hard to raise these funds through innovative manners. Sixteen campus organizations pledged $500 each that they raised through shining shoes, planting trees, sponsoring dances, and baby-sitting to name a few. Delta Chi, and ASC Fraternity painted house numbers on curbs for a dollar each, raising $500, and according to Judith Smith, State Press "Faculty members put on a talent show called 'Faculty Frolics,' and students shined shoes, organized dances, carnivals, and put on a toad race that was called a 'toad hop,' to help fund the union" (May 27, 2005). Many local businesses and individuals also supported the construction. The building was designed by local architect Kemper Goodwin. The final costs totaled over $1.3 million dollars.

MU Dedication

The President of the United States, Dwight Eisenhower, sent a note to ASC President Grady Gammage in 1956 signifying the importance of the donation and dedication of the original White House stone and offering best wishes to the community of Arizona State College at Tempe. The stone was then used to build a pedestal for ASC's victory bell, part of the Memorial Union. Dr. Hyink, dean of students at the University of Southern California was the featured speaker at the opening dedication of the Memorial Union, which over 1,000 people attended. Also speaking at the dedication were the State of Arizona Governor McFarland, Dr. Grady Gammage, and Alex Jacome from the Arizona Board of Regents. The Memorial Union serves as a living memorial and tribute to the students who gave their lives in service. Seventy-one students were remembered at the opening of the Memorial Union. Dale Clarkson, President of the Associated Students cut the ribbon saying "Here it is for YOU, students!" officially opening the entrance to the Memorial Union. Ralph Marterie's orchestra was featured at a semiformal dance during the week of the opening.

MU Through the Years

Cecilia Scoular, director of the Memorial Union, and Trudy Thomas, assistant director began the role of developing programs and services in the Memorial Union in 1956. Alumni have referred to Scoular & Thomas as the "MU Moms" as they were always together, always ensuring that students were comfortable, having fun, and learning much along the way.

The first ten years of the MU paved the way for many traditions. The Memorial Union celebrated its opening with a large-scale birthday party each spring transforming the Memorial Union in order to take students through time to a Shakespeare Festival, across the USA from San Francisco to New Orleans and around the globe celebrating Festivals of the World.

MU Grows...

It was soon apparent that the hub for campus activities needed to expand to support all of the activities and students within the walls. In 1966, just 10 years after the original opening of the building, plans were in the works for adding 94,000 square feet. In 1970, the first Memorial Union expansion was completed. Just a decade later, plans were underway for yet another expansion of the Memorial Union, adding 43,000 square feet and doubling lounge and meeting space, according to former MU director Floyd Land. This was a $6 million dollar project, creating new space for programming and a place for all clubs and organizations on the top floor. This project was completed by 1990.

Today the Memorial Union consists of 254,000 square feet and accommodates over 50,000 students.
