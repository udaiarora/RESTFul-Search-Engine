About Us

The PUHSD-ASU Partnership offers university resources to PUHSD and works to ease the transition period for high school seniors entering ASU and to be available for on-going support as the students continue in the university.

Goals

  • Offer a one-week summer program, which provides an intensive orientation to ASU and prepares students for their first year at ASU.
  • Motivate participants to pursue and complete their college degrees.
  • Increase communication and programming efforts between ASU and PUHSD.
  • Assist PUHSD in preparing high school students for the university by sharing statistics, student progress reports, and conducting in-house workshops.
  • Provide workshop referrals on academic management, pre-registration, scholarships/financial aid, stress management, etc.
  • Offer an early registration program for high school seniors. Students talk with academic advisor and register for appropriate college courses.
  • Through the Success Link Program workshops, motivate PUHSD students to consider their futures and attain success after graduation.
  • Offer interested freshman a chance to be paired with a successful ASU student.
  • Provide financial and educational assistance for selected students who complete the summer program through the Doran Community Scholars Scholarship Program.

Services/Events

Provide guidance and assistance to the "Phoenix Union Devils," a campus organization that consists of PUHSD graduates who are now attending ASU and work to encourage high school students to pursue higher education.

  • Offer support and guidance to new and continuing ASU students.
  • Offer resources such as tutoring and computers for ASU students.
  • Help students monitor class schedules to prevent possible overloads.

Students Served

The Partnership serves graduates of the Phoenix Union High School District. Each year approximately 120 underrepresented freshmen attend ASU from the PUHSD and services are extended to each and every student. The Partnership began in 1992 with 122 students. We now have profiles on approximately 900 students. The Partnership has collaborated with other ASU programs to offer the most student services with the limited resources available. The Partnership communicates with students one-to-one in the office as well as via a newsletter to keep all informed of current student accomplishments and Partnership news and events. The Partnership between ASU and PUHSD demonstrates each institution's commitment to increasing the number of underrepresented students who pursue an education and attain a degree.
