October 7, 2004

Governor visits ASU Presidential Debate venue

Calling the upcoming October 13 debate at ASU’s Gammage Auditorium the “super bowl of politics,” Governor Janet Napolitano today (Oct. 7, 2004) visited the venue where President George W. Bush and Senator John F. Kerry will square off on domestic issues.

A throng of about 30 local media representatives accompanied Napolitano on the tour, many seeing the debate venue for the first time themselves.

Napolitano praised ASU President Michael M. Crow for his leadership in bringing the debate to Arizona, marking the first time a presidential debate has been held in the American Southwest.

“Hosting a presidential debate is an honor of great magnitude for Arizona,” said Napolitano. “It shows that Arizona is a significant emerging national player in politics.”

ASU’s Gammage Auditorium is being transformed into a television production studio as the host venue. Just adjacent to Gammage is a 32,000 sq.-ft. Media Filing Center, which will house 700 to 1,000 local, national, and international media who will converge on the state to cover the debate.

The debate will be televised live beginning at 6 p.m. local time and will draw millions of viewers from around the world.
