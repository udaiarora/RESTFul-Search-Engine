October 2, 2003

AZ Business Conditions Index signals good news in September

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index rose by 0.5 points in September to reach 60.4, up from 58.9 in August. An index reading over 50 indicates that the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

"The Business Conditions Index report for September is full of good news," according to Dawn McLaren, an analyst with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center. "The overall index rose to a healthy 60.4, a level not seen for three years. The only component of the index to register below the critical 50-point level was the purchased materials inventory level. This may, however, simply reflect caution - resisting the temptation to embrace the optimistic feeling that business conditions are finally shaking off the sluggishness of the economic recovery."

The production component of the overall index has risen remarkably over the last few months, from a fairly neutral reading of 51.2 in February to 60.5 in September, McLaren noted. This component has remained over the 60-point level for the past three months. "While all of the components have shown strong readings lately, the employment component remained neutral. In September, some evidence of improvement in employment is finally appearing, with a reading of 57.5. This is a jump of 5.5 points compared to the previous month."

The Prices Index fell slightly from 58.3 in August to 56.0 in September. During the recession, the Prices Index had fallen into the 40s, reflecting the softness in demand in the economy. The current level indicates that there is mild upward pressure on prices, but not enough to cause concern.

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available online at www.wpcarey.asu.edu/seid/eoc/azpmi/. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data available in prn format.
