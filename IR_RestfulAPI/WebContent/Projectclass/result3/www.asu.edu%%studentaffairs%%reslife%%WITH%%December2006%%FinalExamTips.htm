Final Exam Strategies – Tips for Success!

 BEFORE THE EXAM :

  • Get to sleep early the night before. Don’t stay up all night and cram. The shorter you are on sleep, the less clearly you will be able to think. It will only make you more nervous.
  • Eat a good breakfast that is high in protein and low in sugar. You don’t want to be distracted by a growling stomach.
  • One the day of the exam, arrive early, but not too early to make you nervous. Do not discuss the test with others or cram during those last few minutes.

 DURING THE EXAM

  • Listen attentively to last minute instructions given by the instructor. The teacher will sometimes give you some valuable information just before handing out the test.
  • Read the directions very carefully, looking for specific instructions on how to proceed. Watch for details. You may find that more than one answer may be possible on multiple choice or that you only need to answer three out of the five essay questions given.
  • Plan how you will use the time for the test. Estimate how many minutes you will need to finish each test section and finish in the total time allotted. Bring your watch and pay close attention to the passing time.
  • Start with the questions you can answer easily and then move on to the more challenging sections. Leaving essay and sentence completion questions for last can sometimes be beneficial because you may find answers among the already completed objective questions.
  • Keep a steady pace and do not let more difficult questions affect your attitude and steal your valuable time. If you are not penalized for wrong answers, take an educated guess and move on.
  • Rely on your knowledge and don’t watch for patterns. Noticing that the last four answers are “c” is not a good reason to change the answer. One cannot be sure that the teacher varied the answers. It is better to trust knowledge to help you answer the questions.
  • Change answers only when you are certain. The answer that comes to mind first is often correct.
  • When you have completed your test, use the remaining time effectively. Review the difficult questions you left. Proofread your essays. Check your grammar and your spelling. Make sure you answered all the questions (check the back of each page, as well).
