Who may enroll in Supervisory Academy?

Due to changes in the programs offered by LPS, enrollment requirements are currently being revised.  To contact the program coordinator, please call Clayton Taylor at 480-727-6011.

 

Where and when are classes held?

Classes are primarily held at the University Services Building on the Tempe campus, and at the University Center Building on the West campus.

Due to changes in the programs offered by LPS, locations may change.  To contact the program coordinator, please call Clayton Taylor at 480-727-6011.

 

How long is the Supervisory Academy program?

The Supervisory Academy requires substantial time commitments from the participants.  Due to changes in the programs offered by LPS, the program length will be changing.  To contact the program coordinator, please call Clayton Taylor at 480-727-6011.

 

How big are the classes?

Classes at the Tempe campus range from 24-30 people in a class, while classes conducted at the West campus run from 16-18 people per class.

 

Do classes run all day?

Yes.  We cover a large amount of material in every class.  We begin promptly at 8:00 AM and run until 5:00 PM on class days.  Coordinators are available to answer questions, provide consulting and help with any other program related issues 30 minutes prior and 30 minutes after class.

 

What is the attendance policy for Supervisory Academy?

Orientation is very important and can not be missed. We introduce participants to the program, the online component of SA, hand out homework assignments, and handle various other business. Participants must attend and orientation program before starting the Academy.


The days are broken into modules.  All modules must be completed before the participant may graduate from the Academy program.


If a participant misses less than an hour of a module, as long as they have someone take notes, they will not have to make the time up. Missing more than 1 hour is considered missing the entire module.  Leaving early / arriving late needs to be OK'd by the coordinator. Of course, emergencies do come up and as long as a participant lets the coordinator know what is going on as soon as possible, we will work with them as appropriate. This is monitored closely.  Particpants cannot miss a portion of every module.

Is there homework?

Yes.  In order to reinforce the skills that are being taught during the course, participants are required to complete approximately 10 - 12 hours of homework outside of class during the program.
