November 8, 2004

Former Ambassador Appointed Assistant Dean for Global Engagement

Sharon Wilkinson, formerly U.S. Department of State Diplomat in Residence at Arizona State University, is joining ASU’s College of Liberal Arts and Sciences as Assistant Dean for Global Engagement.

“We are very pleased that Ambassador Wilkinson has chosen to stay at ASU as we develop our new School of Global Studies and other transdisciplinary programs aimed at bringing us into the global arena,” said David Young, Vice President and Dean of the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences. “Her wealth of experience will make her an invaluable asset for our faculty and students.”

Wilkinson’s new role will build on her extensive background in international relations to help ASU develop new international programs and connections. She will be responsible for developing and planning appropriate international experiences for students; developing and implementing new domestic and international institutional partnerships for the School of Global Studies and the College;  working with the College’s social sciences and humanities research institutes by assisting them with identifying potential international partners and funding sources; and serving as the College’s liaison with the university’s international initiatives.

“I’m excited to be taking on this new role,” said Wilkinson. “I’ve enjoyed the past year at ASU and I am convinced that the possibilities here for making global connections, both in research and teaching, are nearly limitless.”

Ambassador Wilkinson has been a Career Member of the Senior Foreign Service, Class of Minister Counselor and has held numerous foreign posts. She began her career as Vice Consul in Sao Paulo, Brazil. She was then assigned as Consul in Accra, Ghana. Returning to Washington, she served as program officer for Africa in the Bureau of Cultural Affairs, staff assistant to the Assistant Secretary for Inter-American Affairs, desk officer for Portugal, and management analyst for the Office of Management Operations. She also spent one year as director of the Face-To-Face Program at the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace.

Next, she was assigned as Deputy Principal Officer in Tijuana, Mexico. She returned to Washington to direct the Office of Diplomatic and Public Liaison in the Bureau of Consular Affairs, followed by an assignment as Consul General in Curacao. She participated as a member of the 35th Senior Seminar Class before departing for Lisbon, where she served as Charge d’Affairs before completing her tour as Deputy Chief of Mission.

In 1995, President Clinton nominated her as the U.S. Ambassador to Burkina Faso. In 2000, he nominated her as Ambassador to the Republic of Mozambique. She came to ASU in the fall of 2003 in the State Department’s Diplomat in Residence Program, which places career diplomats at a select number of university campuses for the purpose of counseling and recruiting students for a wide variety of career opportunities in foreign service.

Wilkinson was educated at Brown University and the University of Chicago. She speaks Portuguese, Spanish and French.
