Supervise the staff of the University Registrar's General Services Section, including coordinating daily activities, training, performance evaluation, schedule development, and work prioritization. Coordinate the activities of General Services, focusing on providing the highest level of customer service, and providing recommendations for service changes and/or additions. Cross-train in diverse activities, including areas such as in-person customer service, telephone customer service, academic transactions, and data entry to assist with variable peak processing demands in the University Registrar's Office and University Undergraduate Initiative areas. Oversee development and update of training curricula, materials, and online end-user documentation. Make training presentations in a group setting. Maintain strong communication links and exchange information with university student service providers.  
Six years of office/clerical experience which includes two years of lead experience; OR, Associate's degree in Office Administration, Business Administration or a related field AND four years of office/clerical experience which includes two years of lead experience.  
OR, any equivalent combination of education and/or experience from which comparable knowledge, skills and abilities have been achieved.  
 
Demonstrated knowledge of university functions as they pertain to student services. Effective verbal and written communication skills. Experience working in a team environment. Experience coordinating, assigning, and prioritizing multiple projects to maximize the effectiveness of individual staff's schedules. Experience updating and retrieving information on a mainframe computer (ASU Student Information System and ASU Degree Audit Reporting System preferred). Customer service experience (front counter experience in a high volume service location preferred). Experience with word processing, spreadsheets and other office software (MS Word, MS Excel, MS PowerPoint, and MS Access, MS Outlook preferred). Demonstrated training/presentation experience. Experience developing training curricula and writing office/training procedures.  
As the leader of a team that has high exposure within the university community, this is an excellent opportunity for a challenging position which contributes to enhancing services to students. ASU offers a tuition waiver (to include the employee, spouse, and dependents), flexible work schedules, paid vacation and holidays, excellent benefits package, employee training and development opportunities, and an employee assistance program.  
ASU conducts pre-employment screening for all positions which includes a criminal background check, verification of work history, academic credentials, licenses, and certifications.
