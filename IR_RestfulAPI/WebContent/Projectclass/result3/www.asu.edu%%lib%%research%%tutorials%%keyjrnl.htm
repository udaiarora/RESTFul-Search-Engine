How to Identify Key Journals in a Professional Field

There are several sources in the library that can be used to identify the key journals in a particular field.
  1. Magazines for Libraries. New York: Bowker, 1969- . Irregular.
    PN 4832 .M34x Hayden Reference

    Evaluates over 8,000 periodicals and journals and makes recommendations for libraries which include a description of the scope, special features, intended audience (elementary/middle school, high school, general adult, academic, and specialist), and indexing sources for each. Arranged by 158 broad subject areas with a title index in back. These evaluations are also included in ulrichsweb.com below and are indicated by the existence of a "Review" tab in a journal's entry.

  2. Associations Unlimited
    Print Equivalent: Encyclopedia of Associations. Detroit: Gale Research, 1961- . Annual. (AS 22. E5 Hayden Reference)

    Provides information about professional associations including their publications. The customized search can look for association publications by publication type and subject.

  3. Guides to the Literature

    Some disciplines have books which identify key reference sources and list the key periodicals for the field. Search the Online Catalog by subject for Reference Books--Your Discipline--Bibliography and also for Your Discipline--Bibliography--Periodicals. Or ask a Reference Librarian to identify a guide in you subject area.

  4. ulrichsweb.com.
    Print Equivalent: Ulrich's Periodicals Directory. New Providence, NJ: Bowker, 1939- . Annual. (PN 4832 .U5x Hayden Reference Desk)

    This resource contains information on over a quarter-million international periodicals, including academic and scholarly journals, trade magazines, and newspapers. You can limit searches by an array of factors, including refereed journals as a feature and academic/scholarly journals as a serial type. By selecting your subject (e.g. Anthropology), and then limiting your search to refereed journals or the academic/scholarly serial type option, you can quickly get a result list of journals in your field or subject of interest.

  5. Abstracts and Indexes

    Journals included in indexes or abstracts are the most widely read in their field, though they are not always scholarly publications. Most indexes, whether online or in print, provide a way to view the list of journal titles covered.

    The "Find Articles" section of the library web site provides access to a number of subject indexes and abstracts which include scholarly and general interest journals. These are not necessarily scholarly journals - they vary by subject area.


  6. Publishing Opportunities

    In some cases, you may need to find information on how to publish in your field of interest. There are directories that can provide information on the publication's circulation, acceptance rate, and the time required for a review in addition to editor contact information. There may also be information regarding the review process used, manuscript guidelines, and other points of interest to a writer. Most are organized alphabetically by journal title.

    1. Printed Directories

      A quick way to identify these sources is to search for "publishing directories" as a keyword search in the ASU Online Catalog. This will result in a list of directories, some of which will relate to publishing opportunites. Look for words in the titles such as: publishing opportunities, author's or writer's guide, or market or marketplace to find relevant sources. An example is Cabell's Directory of Publishing Opportunities in Marketing (HF 5410 .C23 Hayden Reference).

    2. MLA Directory of Periodicals. New York: Modern Language Association of America, 1979- . Biennial.
      P 1 .M6x Hayden Reference

      This directory lists the language, literature, and folklore sources covered by the MLA Bibliography indexing service while also providing information on submission requirements, number of articles submitted vs. published, time required for a review, etc. for many of the journals listed.

    3. Journal Web Sites

      Often journals post information for authors on their Web sites. Web addresses for journals can be obtained from ulrichsweb.com among other sources.

  7. Rankings

    Rankings of journals based on various criteria are available from the following sources.

    1. Journal Citation Reports

      Provides article citation analysis for over 8,400 scholarly and technical journals in the sciences and social sciences internationally. Can rank journals by number of times cited, number of articles, and impact.

    2. Educational Rankings Annual. Detroit: Gale, 1991- . Annual
      L 11 .E458x Hayden Reference

      Identifies rankings of academic interest published in periodical articles, including some relating to journals in various fields. Look under the desired field of study, such as Business or Psychology, and check for a subsection on periodicals to see if any journal rankings have been included.
