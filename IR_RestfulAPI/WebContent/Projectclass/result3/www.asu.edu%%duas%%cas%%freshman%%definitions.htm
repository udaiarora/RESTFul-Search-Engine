No Preference ASU's official terminology for students who have not yet officially selected a major.  These students may also be referred to as undeclared, undecided or between majors.
Deficiency You may have been admitted to ASU with a deficiency in one or more competency areas (English, Mathematics, Laboratory Science, Social Science, Foreign Language, and/or Fine Arts) if you did not complete high school courses required by ASU. Competency deficiencies are listed on your Admissions Certificate. Competencies may be met by completing the appropriate courses. Competencies must be fulfilled within two calendar years of enrollment at ASU. Your academic advisor will help you determine how to fulfill competencies.
Major A major is a specialized group of courses within a program of study.  You must declare a major by the time you've earned 60 credit hours.
Double Major Some students choose to pursue two baccalaureate degrees simultaneously. ASU calls this a concurrent degree. Your advisor can provide more information about pursuing a double major.
Minor A minor is an approved, coherent concentration of academic study in a single discipline, involving substantially fewer hours of credit than a corresponding major.  Most ASU colleges offer undergraduate minors in addition to majors; see the ?ASU Minors? table.
Credit Hour One-credit hour represents a minimum of 50-minutes of class attendance per week per semester. Graduation with a baccalaureate degree requires a minimum of 120 credit hours (some majors require more). To receive credit, you must be properly registered and pay fees. A credit hour is also referred to as a semester hour.
Course Load A minimum full-time course load for an undergraduate is 12 credit hours. The maximum course load is 18 credit hours. The recommended course load for a freshman is 12-16 credit hours.
Lower Division Course Courses numbered 100-299 are primarily for freshmen and sophomores.
Upper Division Course Courses numbered 300-499 are primarily for juniors, seniors, and other advanced students.
Prerequisite

A requirement to be met before registering for a particular course (e.g., completing ENG 101 before taking ENG 102).
