November 6, 2003

Arizona Leading Index slips in September

The Bank One Arizona Index of Leading Economic Indicators declined slightly in September to 121.4. This is 0.3 percent above August's revised index reading of 121.8, and 4.3 percent above the September 2002 number of 116.4. (1987 = 100).

"Although the Leading Index components that involve the national economy effectively suppressed some of the gain in the Arizona-specific components, the local economy does seem to be returning to a state of growth," said Dawn McLaren, research economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at Arizona State University's W. P. Carey School of Business. "During these growth spurts, it is typical to see slight slips now and then because of fear of bubbles, over-enthusiasm, and the return of economic weakness. After maintaining a narrow range of just 2 points for almost a year, the Leading Index increased its range to 5.4 over the last five months."

"Over the last few months it has become clear that the Arizona economy, along with the national economy, is growing once again," McLaren said. "This growth should make Arizona residents feel confident about the future of the local economy - mainly thanks to the return of healthy job growth." Arizona ranked eighth in the nation in job growth for September, compared to September 2002. While that seems positive, the state managed that ranking with just over 1 percent employment growth.

"The amount of job growth has been creeping up slowly for months, and it looks like the growth will continue, but at a much slower pace than that of overall economic conditions," McLaren said. "Because of the nature of the employment market, job growth is usually one of the last sectors of the economy to show recovery after a recession. In September, the employment component of the Leading Index showed significant recovery and, should that continue to be seen, will serve as confirmation that true economic recovery has taken hold."

Leading Index components with positive numbers in September included production, delivery times and employment from the Business Conditions survey. Materials inventories, the inflation-adjusted value of the M-2 money supply, the inflation-adjusted value of Maricopa County residential building permits and hours worked in manufacturing were negative. New orders and sensitive materials prices were neutral.

The Bank One Arizona Index of Leading Economic Indicators is based on nine indicators, combined and weighted to project the pace of economic activity several months ahead. Five of the components come from a monthly survey of Arizona purchasing managers conducted by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University.

The L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P.
