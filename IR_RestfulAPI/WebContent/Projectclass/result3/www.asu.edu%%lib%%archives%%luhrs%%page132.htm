He expected to make it up on the Luhrs Building but did not. His bid was not out of line for Robert McKee's bid was only two thousand dollars higher. The bonding company had to take over. In turn, the bonding company had the Halstead Lumber Company agreeing to reimburse them. Halstead Lumber Company took over to finish the work but they dragged out everything. When the cost of finishing became less than the interest the Luhrs' had to pay, the Luhrs' took over the building and finished it.


The Luhrs Building as the new structure was called, was built as two buildings in one. The lower floors were offices and the top four floors were especially built for the Arizona Club. The seventh and eighth floors were bedrooms for the club members. The ninth floor had the club's office, lounge, library, pool and billiard room, as well as the grille room. The tenth floor had the kitchen, the main and private dining rooms. The original plans called for eleven floors, but the cost made it necessary to make it ten stories.
