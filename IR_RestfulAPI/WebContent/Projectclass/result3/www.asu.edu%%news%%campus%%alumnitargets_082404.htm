August 24, 2004

Alumni Association targets constituent needs

Diane McCarthy, president of the ASU Alumni Association, has completed a reorganization of the alumni association to better align staffing resources with constituent needs. The reorganization includes the appointments of directors for seven distinct areas within the association, each reporting to a new vice president and executive director.

Serving as vice president and executive director is Glenn Gray, who joined the association in June. In this position, he is responsible for oversight of internal operations and coordination of departmental activities. Gray comes to ASU from TriWest Healthcare Alliance, where he served as the director of Congressional and customer relations. Prior to his employment at TriWest, Gray was a local staff member for U.S. Sen. John McCain. Gray can be reached at (glenn.gray@asu.edu) or (480) 965-5275.

Overseeing financial services is Ron McElhaney, chief financial officer. In this role, McElhaney is responsible for the financial management processes and the coordination of events and activities held at Old Main. McElhaney is a Certified Public Accountant and a Certified Treasury Professional. His work experience includes time spent as an auditor for national accounting firms and 17 years' experience in finance functions with three major corporations.  McElhaney can be reached at (rmcelhaney@asu.edu) or (480) 965-4631.

Jeff Holeman is director of communications, a position he assumed in June. As such, he is responsible for timely, professional communications to all alumni through the association's Web sites, e-mail newsletters, ASU Magazine , the news media, and collateral marketing pieces for chapters and events. Previously, Holeman was assistant director for communications in the ASU East campus Public Affairs office; editorial director for the Public Affairs office at the University of California, Berkeley; and editor/publisher for ASU Insight . Holeman can be reached at (jholeman@asu.edu) or (480) 965-8150.

Jeff Grant became director of constituent relations in May. Grant is responsible for organizing and recruiting volunteers in advocating a united message to elected officials on issues facing higher education. Grant previously was deputy director at the Arizona Department of Revenue and chief of staff to former Arizona House Speaker Mark Killian. Grant also served as executive director of the Maricopa County Republican Committee and worked for the Republican National Committee. Contact Grant at (jeff.grant@asu.edu) or (480) 965-4078.

Miki Dadkhah was named director of member services earlier this year. In her role as director, Dadkhah is responsible for maintenance of the alumni database and customer service to alumni members. Dadkhah's career began in the telecommunications industry, where she worked until she joined the town of Gilbert's customer service area. She joined the association in 2003. Dadkhah can be reached at (miki.dadkhah@asu.edu) or (480) 965-2127.

Cathie Rubins is director of marketing and is responsible for promoting the association to diverse audiences for the purpose of expanding active membership and awareness of the association's role in supporting ASU. Rubins previously worked in the association in the alumni relations and marketing areas. Prior to ASU, Rubin's work experience included roles in international banking, merchandise procurement and therapeutic recreation. Contact Rubins at (cathie.rubins@asu.edu) or (480) 965-2140.

Serving as director of research and sponsorships is Regina Sanborn. In this position, Sanborn is responsible for conducting alumni research and identifying and obtaining affinity partners, sponsors, advertisers and providers of membership benefits to support both the association and its membership. Prior to coming to ASU, Sanborn was director of member services at the American Society of Mechanical Engineers, director of membership and services at the American Medical Women's Association, and vice president, administration at the Foundation for Physical Therapy/American Physical Therapy Association. Sanborn can be reached at (rsanborn@asu.edu) or (480) 965-8346.

Overseeing chapter development and association events is Jill Andrews, who joined the association in July. As director of chapters and events, Andrews is responsible for the organization and implementation of events, in coordination with chapters, colleges, other university units and the community. She also serves as a liaison to alumni chapters. Prior to joining the association, she served as the assistant director of communications and marketing and interim program manager of noncredit programs for the ASU College of Extended Education.

Andrews has also worked with a West Valley nonprofit arts organization, a public relations agency, and the ASU Service Learning Program. Contact Andrews at (jill.andrews@asu.edu) or (480) 965-7754.
