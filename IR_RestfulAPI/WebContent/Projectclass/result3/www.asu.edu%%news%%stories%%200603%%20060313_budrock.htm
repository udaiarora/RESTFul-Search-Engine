March 13, 2006

Former U.S. State Department official joins ASU

ASU is seeking to expand its international presence and has appointed a former senior U.S. Department of State official to lead the charge.

Anthony "Bud" Rock, formerly acting assistant secretary of state responsible for science, technology, environment and health affairs, has been named special adviser to ASU President Michael Crow for strategic international initiatives. Rock also will serve as associate vice president for science applications in the Office of Research and Economic Affairs.

Crow expressed strong enthusiasm for the role that Rock can play in advancing the university's global profile.

"It will be of tremendous value to have someone with Bud Rock's international and diplomatic experience to help ASU faculty members find ways to apply their expertise to some of the world's toughest problems," Crow says.

Rock, with the rank of minister-counselor, has been a career member of the U.S. Senior Foreign Service. Throughout his career, he has held numerous positions with responsibility for ensuring that U.S. strategic goals in environment, science, technology and health were incorporated effectively into the conduct of U.S. foreign policy.

Among his many accomplishments, Rock led the successful negotiation of three major agreements for expanded trade in the Middle East, and he represented the United States in the first comprehensive dialogue with the European Union on trade in bioengineered products.

In addition, he was responsible for developing the first U.S. action plan for international surveillance and response to infectious diseases, and the first U.S. international strategy for HIV/AIDS response.

Before joining the Foreign Service, Rock was employed by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration - first as a physical scientist, and ultimately as coordinator for international research. He also held the position of international director of the National Sea Grant Program.

Rock received his undergraduate academic and research training at Georgetown University and Johns Hopkins University in physical sciences, with a special emphasis in epidemiology and public health. He holds a master's degree in science and technology policy from George Washington University, and was part of the marine geophysical research team of Columbia University's Lamont-Doherty Geological Observatory.

It was Crow's vision and leadership, Rock says, that drew him to ASU, as well as the unique aspects of the rapidly expanding Phoenix metropolitan area that have direct correlations internationally.

"I'm excited about President Crow's concept of the New American University, and his interdisciplinary approach to ensuring that ASU is not only an outstanding educational institution, but also a relevant and integral player in the world," Rock says. "He wants not only to elevate the international recognition of ASU, but to put the university's strengths and talents to work to meet the full range of global challenges. His goal of applying ASU strengths to issues of urbanization, sustainability and technological innovation, for example, are precisely the objectives that I applied to U.S. foreign policy. I'm eager to apply my experience to help President Crow and ASU meet these goals.

"In addition, the growth and diversity of metro Phoenix present challenges that are faced all over the world. We are a unique test-bed for the international experience."

Rock says his position is multifaceted. Among his many roles, he will look to develop new partnerships and initiatives in which ASU can excel internationally, within the social and physical sciences.

In addition to developing new strategic initiatives, he will focus on adding international elements to existing research programs and will help to identify areas in which ASU faculty excellence might best be applied in the international arena.

Rock will work with Crow and Jonathan Fink, the university's vice president for research and economic affairs, to promote initiatives, such as biodesign and sustainability, where ASU is well positioned to develop viable international linkages.

Adds Fink: "Over the past several years, ASU faculty and students have had some very positive research engagements in China, Mexico, Europe and the Middle East. Gaining access to Bud Rock's network of contacts around the world and in federal and international organizations will greatly accelerate and facilitate the expansion of these foreign activities."
