May 5, 2003

Crow leads AzBio groundbreaking, welcomes ‘technology Zen master’

ASU President Michael Crow called the April 29 groundbreaking for the Arizona Biodesign Institute (AzBio) a symbolic lifting up of the entire university.

“We are here today to launch a new facility, but what we are really doing is positioning ASU, metro Phoenix and the entire state of Arizona to compete on a national basis,” Crow said. “I don’t mean compete in the context of chasing money or resources, but compete in the arena in which the future will be determined. The future of our economy, our individual health, and the future of much of what our society will grow to be.”

Crow said AzBio will house individuals who will work together and with others to lay a foundation for future alternatives to attack the problems of disease, economic instability, environmental pollution and global warming, to make the world a better place than it is now.

“In this setting, with this institute, we are committing to using scientific discovery through the art of design to drive our science forward thoughtfully,” Crow said. “It’s a matter of discovery with a purpose, discovery with recognition, discovery with responsibility and with some objective we’re working toward.

“We are committing to a design approach with the understanding our science will be consciously thought through with respect to both the developed world and the developing world,” Crow said. “If we conduct our design activities and our discovery so that only the rich people living in a few countries on the planet have a right to benefit from those, then we are making a big mistake.”

Crow said AzBio offers ASU the opportunity to separate itself from those institutions focusing on the more traditional view of science, the traditional view of departments, and the traditional view of disciplines.

“Where they’re driven exclusively by the historical notion of discovery only, we will become the discoverers and designers,” he said.

To accomplish this requires a leader with the capacity to help move AzBio to the next level, Crow said, and “we are very excited about the appointment of George Poste as director of AzBio.”

Crow said he tried to think of a phrase that best captures what Poste is, and he came up with “Technology Zen Master.”

He said a technology Zen master is someone who has mastered both science and business, someone who understands the silver side and dark side of science and technology and someone who has the means to synthesize between science and society. “In George Poste, we’ve found the zen master to take AzBio to the next level.”

Poste said this is a remarkable time to be doing science and technology wherever you are. He said, short of some unmitigated economic or natural disaster, history will record 1950 to 2050 as one of the most remarkable eras in the expansion of the human intellectual endeavor.

“The fact that we’re privileged to do it in the life sciences, with all of its beneficent applications in medicine, agriculture, management, and in redressing savage global iniquity is indeed a very rare privilege,” Poste said.

“Putting these themes - biotechnology, materials science, and miniaturization engineering - together with advanced computing, that is where the action is going to be,” Poste said. “And, it is not only blessed with new facilities to come, it has a talented faculty all ready to go into it, and the opportunity to bring additional talented people here, so that, five years from now, the wow factor will be up and people will know about AzBio.”
