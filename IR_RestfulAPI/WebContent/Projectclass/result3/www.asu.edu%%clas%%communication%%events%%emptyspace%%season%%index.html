                     Fall 2006 - Spring 2007 Season

Fall 2006

 

September 8 and 9, 7:30-9:00 pm:   ¿Firmly? Bound  

by Karma Chávez,  Kimberlee Pèrez, and Jennifer Linde

 ¿Firmly? Bound  is a collaborative installation that seeks to articulate three perspectives on the current Mexico-United States border discussion.  The collaborators interrogate the current economic, social, cultural, and familial effects of border rhetoric by creating three individual installation/performance pieces.  These pieces will be displayed together in a form that allows audiences to interact, provide commentary, and participate in discussion.   

 

October 6, 7 and 8, 7:30 pm :  Raiders of the Lost Artifacts: In Search of Iraq through Story"

A solo performance project performed by Marjorie Hazeltine

Faculty Advisor:  Dr. Linda Park-Fuller

Raiders of the Lost Artifacts is a one person performance that weaves together personal narratives from Iraqi refugees as well as Iraqi folk tales and historical facts. This is an extension of Marjorie’s performance, “Palm Readings and Misreadings:  A lesson in Translation.”

 

November 3, 4 and 5, 7:30 pm :  This is the Job for Me (Or Failure in 6 Concertos)   

A collaborative performance piece designed, scripted, and performed by Desiree Rowe and Kimberlee Pèrez

Director:  Jennifer Linde

Faculty Advisor:  Dr. Olga Davis

This is the Job for Me (Or Failure in 6 Concertos)  addresses contemporary feminist and critical theories of intersubjectivity which reconfigure traditional modes of subject formation away from the individual selfhood toward the interrelatedness of existence. The script is based on an ongoing series of written dialogues between the two artists.  Rowe and Perez do not seek to question these theories, but rather they interrogate their movement beyond the text through praxis in the lives of those inside and outside the academy.

Spring 2007

 

February 2 and 3 , 7:30 pm:   Ball: A Traumedy   

We welcome guest performer Brian Lobel to The Empty Space.

Ball: A Traumedy  is a one person performance about testicular cancer.  Lobel shares his personal battle with cancer in this hilarious (that’s right) series of stories from his experience.

 

March 2, 3 and 4 , 7:30 pm:    Social Triggers   

ASU undergraduate students perform a trigger script about organizational bullying.   

Script adaptation and direction:  Sara McKinnon

Project supervisor:  Jennifer Linde

Project advisor:  Dr. Sarah Tracey

Based on research conducted by S. Tracey, J. Alberts, and P. Sandvig

This performance is the first in a series of new trigger scripts that the performance studies area will take into the ASU and Phoenix communities.  Future plans include an adaptation of research on campus binge drinking and a series of trigger scripts about gender and sexuality.

(Re)Framing    -   This performance has been postponed.  Please check back in the future for more information about the new dates and times. 

Performative installation project conceptualized, created, and performed by Karen Stewart

Director:  Jennifer Linde

Faculty Advisor: Dr. Bud Goodall

Stewart’s work is motivated by a desire to understand the dynamics that invite and prevent audiences from accessing images, especially images labeled “high art.”   (Re)Framing is inspired by Leonardo Da Vinci’s painting the Mona Lisa. This painting is often credited as being the second most recognizable image in western culture, and given its familiarity and massive circulation, it is often a primary illustration for visual culture scholarship. In addition, it has a long, enigmatic history, it is a key fixture at the Louvre Museum in Paris, and it is a player in Dan Brown’s phenomenon of a novel, The Da Vinci Code. These aspects of the personality of the Mona Lisa provide Stewart (and the audience) with a text that lends itself to “messy” discourse, and allows for the juxtaposition of aesthetic and critical interpretation.
