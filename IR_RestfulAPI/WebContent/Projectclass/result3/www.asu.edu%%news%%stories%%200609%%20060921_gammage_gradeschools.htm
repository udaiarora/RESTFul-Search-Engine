September 21, 2006

ASU Gammage reaches out to grade schools

To encourage students to experience the world of art and culture, ASU Gammage offers exclusive one-hour performances for students throughout the school year.

Performances cost $3 per student, teacher and chaperone. An accompanying teacher's guide, filled with interactive lesson plans, is created by ASU Gammage and provided to teachers at no cost.

To register for a performance, call (480) 965-5062 or visit asugammage.com.

The 2006-2007 ASU Gammage performances for students include:

? ?Lemn Sissay,? 10:30 a.m., Oct. 3 and Oct. 5, ASU Kerr, recommended for grades 9-12 ? This performance expresses the realities of life for an immigrant youth growing up in foster care while searching for his birth family. His journey is a triumph of the human spirit that leaves audiences with a message of inspiration and hope.

? Street Jam Co., 10:30 a.m., Nov. 3, ASU Gammage, recommended for grades K-12 ? Street Jam is a global percussion and dance band drawing from various world drumming and dance traditions, to form a unique performance fusion. Street Jam uses found objects, such as pots and pans, trash cans, oil drums and plastic buckets, to create a unique sound.

? Calo Flamenco Co., 10:30 a.m., Dec. 12, ASU Gammage, recommended for grades K-12 ? This performance evokes the origins of traditional folk dance and modern theatrical dance forms. It has traditional and modern flamenco dance and musical influences, but it also uses other dance, music and media to further emphasize flamenco's contextual elements.

? ?Of Coyote and Water Monster,? 10:30 a.m., Feb. 28, ASU Gammage, recommended for grades K-12 ? ?Of Coyote and Water Monster? translates a portion of the Navajo creation story from traditional sand painting images told on stage. It is narrated in English and Navajo.

? ?Cesar & Ruben,? 10 a.m., March 29, ASU Gammage recommended for grades 6-12 ? This musical play is written by acclaimed actor-writer Ed Begley Jr. It chronicles the heroic lives of Cesar Chavez and Ruben Salazar.
