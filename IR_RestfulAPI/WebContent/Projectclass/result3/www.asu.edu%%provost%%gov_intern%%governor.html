What is different about being an intern in the Governor’s Office?
The interns who work in the Governor’s Legislative Office gain a different, but equally valuable perspective about the legislative process. The Governor is very involved in the introduction and passage of various bills because the Executive Branch is responsible for executing those laws passed by the legislature. The major distinctions between the internships at the Legislature and the Governor’s Office include the following:

  • Three interns are selected to work in the Governor's Office and on average, 45 interns are selected to work at the Legislature.
  • The Governor’s interns monitor numerous committees, allowing them to develop in-depth knowledge about several subjects. The Legislative interns concentrate on one subject area, allowing them to obtain a more detailed knowledge in one subject area.
  • Because the Executive Branch represents the entire state rather than a specific district, the Governor may have a different perspective and concern about implementation of any new legislation.
  • The Governor’s interns attend policy meetings with the Governor and her Policy Advisors to discuss merits/consequences of legislation, which may arrive on the Governor's desk for her signature.
  • The Governor’s interns are fully involved in the final disposition of bills passed by the legislature. Intern responsibilities include preparation of the bills for the Governor’s review, compilation and summarization of all relevant data and, upon the signature by the Governor, transmittal of the bills to the Secretary of State.

Duties of Governor’s Office Legislative Interns

  • The primary duty of the interns is to monitor legislative hearings and other legislative activity. The interns will advise the appropriate Governor’s Policy Advisors on any amendments, prime supporters and opponents of each bill before the legislature and provide a listing of individuals who testified on legislation. The Governor’s office relies on the insight of its interns throughout the legislative session for both analytical and policy decisions.
  • The interns will collect all analyses of bills introduced and maintain a single comprehensive file for each bill.
  • The interns will attend caucuses to further inform the Governor and policy advisors about the status of bills.
  • Upon passage of a bill the interns will prepare comprehensive bill packets for review by the appropriate policy advisors and ultimately for signature or veto by the Governor.
  • The interns will be responsible for transmitting the bills to the Secretary of State upon the Governor’s signature.
  • The interns will prepare weekly agenda packets for the Friday morning legislative liaison meetings, which are conducted for the purpose of sharing information between the Governor’s staff and state agency staff.
  • The interns run a weekly computerized progress list updating the status of the Governor’s “priority” legislation.
  • The interns will track gubernatorial appointments (which are subject to approval of the Senate).
  • The interns will work with constituents (either by phone or letter) to provide information. They will record constituent opinions on bills in order to inform policy advisors.
  • When time permits, the interns may attend periodic activities with the Legislative interns. Guest speakers are invited and occasional field trips are scheduled to various state agencies to provide a broader understanding of the Executive, Legislative and Judicial branches of government. Past trips have included viewing a Supreme Court hearing and a tour of the facilities at the Florence prison.
