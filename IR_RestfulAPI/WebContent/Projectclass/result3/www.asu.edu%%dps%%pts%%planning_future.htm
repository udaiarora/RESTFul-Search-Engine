Parking and Transit Services

Planning for the Future: 
Beyond 2006-2007

In addition to the improvements that will be implemented by Fall 2006, the Parking and Transit Task Force recommendations call for ASU to plan for the future. Over the course of the next 3-5 years, several programs and enhancements will be implemented to assist faculty, staff, students and visitors access the ASU Tempe campus.

Bike Corral(s)

Over the next four years, one bike corral will be built on the Tempe campus per year to help address bike storage and theft. The corrals will have security technology so that entry/exit of the corrals will be monitored. Site locations of the bike corrals will be determined in coordination with the ASU Architect.

Remote Parking Compound

Parking near and on the perimeter of the Tempe campus will become an even more limited resource in the years to come with the construction of several more academic, research and residential housing buildings. However, one of the Task Force recommendations calls for ASU to acquire land, no more than five miles away from the Tempe campus, that will accommodate approximately 2,000 parking spaces.

The remote parking lot will be staffed with 24 hour security and would include 24 hour shuttle service.

New South Campus Parking Structure

Over the last four years, the ASU Tempe campus has greatly expanded south of Apache Boulevard with the opening of the University Services Building in 2003 and the recent increase in residential housing in the area (McAllister Academic Village).

Projected parking inventory on the south end of campus is inadequate to meet the increased demand. In order to accommodate faculty, staff and students who work and reside on the south campus, a new parking structure will be constructed. Additional details regarding this structure will be announced when they become available.

FLEX Car Rental Program

For ASU faculty, staff and students who take advantage of one of the several alternative transportation options, a FLEX car rental service will be available on campus. The program will allow anyone affiliated with ASU to rent a vehicle by the hour for a minimal fee. More details to follow as this service becomes available.

Securing Parking Spaces Along METRO Light Rail

Everyone is currently feeling the growing pains of the METRO Light Rail, with the current construction of the transit line through campus. However, once the light rail is operating (Dec. 2008), it will provide many faculty, staff and students a viable transportation option in traveling to and from campus.

Park and Ride locations along the light rail line are planned and will provide another option to commute to/from work or school. ASU dedicated parking spaces at METRO Light Rail Park and Ride locations are currently being researched. More information regarding these park and ride locations will be posted when available.

Parking Structure for Gateway Project

There are currently 850 parking spaces planned with the development of the Gateway project (currently known as the Tempe Center). More details to follow when available.
