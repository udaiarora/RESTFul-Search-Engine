Placement Policy

PLEASE READ THE FOLLOWING INFORMATION CAREFULLY:
In order to enroll in the correct level of lower division Spanish, you must take the on-line placement exam:

http://www.asu.edu/clas/dll/placement/lptotest.html

You must also enroll in a lab section. You can find the classes listed in the on-line schedule of classes:

https://sec.was.asu.edu/soc/

SPANISH 101
You will not be allowed to enroll in SPA 101 if the following applies:

1) You are a heritage speaker of Spanish.
2) You have any prior college Spanish courses.
3) You have taken more than two years of
High school Spanish.

NO EXCEPTIONS!!!!!!

If you are a heritage speaker or have taken other classes, you must take the on-line placement exam:
http://www.asu.edu/clas/dll/placement/lptotest.html

Sequence of Lower Division Spanish Courses

The basic courses include:

  • 101:  Elementary Spanish
  • 102:  Elementary Spanish
  • 111:  Intensive Elementary Spanish: A Review  
  • 201:  Intermediate Spanish
  • 202:  Intermediate-High Spanish 

Only one basic Spanish course can be taken during any given semester.  The courses must be taken sequentially as follows:

  • 101, 102, 201, 202; or
  • 111, 201, 202; or
  • 203, 204.   

Students who have received credit for 101 or 102 may not take 111 for credit.

The Lower Division Spanish Language Program at ASU requires students to place themselves in the appropriate level course according to the following guidelines:

SPA 101. Students register in SPA 101 if one of the following applies to them:

  1. they have never taken Spanish language courses before.
  2. they have studied Spanish for one year or less at the High School level
  3. they took Spanish 101 before but did not get a passing grade.
  4. they obtained between 0-178 points on the Internet (On-Line) Spanish Placement Exam or 0-10 (in the DLL Computing Lab, LL 065)

SPA 102 .  Students register in SPA 102 if one of the following applies to them

  1. they have successfully passed SPA 101.
  2. they obtained between 260-325 points on the Internet Spanish Placement Exam or between 18-25 (in the DLL Computing Lab LL065)

SPA 111. Students register in SPA 111 if one of the following applies to them

  1. they have taken Spanish language courses for two (2) years in High School.
  2. they have not taken SPA 101 or SPA 102.
  3. they obtained between 179-325 points on the Internet Spanish Placement Exam or between 11-25 (in the DLL Computing Lab LL065)

SPA 201. Students register in SPA 201 if one of the following applies to them

  1. they have successfully passed SPA 102 or SPA 111.
  2. they obtained between 326-414 points on the Internet Spanish Placement Exam or between 26-34 (in the DLL Computing Lab LL 065)

SPA 202. Students register in SPA 202 if one of the following applies to them

  1. they successfully passed SPA 201.
  2. they obtained between  415-493  on the Internet Spanish Placement Exam or between 35-42 (in the DLL Computing lab LL 065)

SPA 203 .  Students register in the SPA 203 (Fall) 204 (Spring) sequence if

  1. they speak Spanish at home

If the student is unsure of his/her level, he/she is advised to Click here to take the Placement Exam online, or to go to the DLL Computing Lab (LL065) where the Brigham Young Spanish Placement Exam is available.  The lab assistant will provide him/her information on taking this test. 

Please note:

  • Students who graduated high school in the US but are unsure of their level in Spanish, should take the placement test. If they place at the 201 level, they should enroll in SPA 203 (Intermediate Spanish for Bilinguals I). If they place at the 202 level, they should enroll in SPA 204 (Intermediate Spanish for Bilinguals II).
  • Students who place at the 300 level, should enroll in SPA 315 (Spanish Composition & Conversation for Bilinguals) if these alternative courses are available.
  • Students who graduated from high school in a Spanish-speaking country, should not enroll in any language course lower than SPA 412 (Advanced Spanish Composition & Conversation). They may, however, take any course in literature, culture or linguistics at the 300 or 400 level.
