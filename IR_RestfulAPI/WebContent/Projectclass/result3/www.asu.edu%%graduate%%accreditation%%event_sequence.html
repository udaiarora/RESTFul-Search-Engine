ACCREDITATION SEQUENCE OF EVENTS

If you are considering an initial accreditation, please find out what information your supervising administrator needs to make a decision about an initial accreditation. Several of these questions may also be useful to you as you consider continuing accreditation. Some commonly asked questions follow:

  • What are the benefits associated with accreditation? Benefits might include the fulfillment of licensing or certification requirements for program graduates, federal or state requirements for the operation of a laboratory or service unit, enhancement of student recruiting opportunities, higher probability of external funding, and networking opportunities for faculty and students.

  • What are the immediate and ongoing costs of accreditation, including the accreditation fees, costs associated with the visit, and an assessment of indirect costs, such as faculty and/or staff time? Who will pay them?

  • Does the program meet current or projected future accreditation standards
    (i.e., number of students per faculty, staff, facilities, space, equipment, program control, course sequencing, course content, assessment of student learning, library materials, student preparation, etc.)?

  • If the program does not meet each specific standard, what changes need to be made to meet standards?


Programs requesting initial accreditation or continuing accreditation should:

  1. Contact your supervising administrator as soon as you consider initial or continuing accreditation so that arrangements can be made to properly prepare and pay for the review. The unit under review is responsible for all accreditation fees and costs.

  2. Contact the Department of Environmental Health and Safety (480/965-1823 or email EHS@asu.edu) if your unit has laboratory, shop, studio, or other facilities that may be subject to special accreditation standards such as laboratory safety, shop safety, fire safety, or hazardous waste management. This should be done prior to filing an application for accreditation. EHS staff can help you to do an early assessment of your facility. EHS guidance documents and policies and procedures are available at www.asu.edu/uagc/EHS.

  3. Alert your supervising administrator (e.g., Dean, Provost, Vice President) and the university accreditation officer to accrediting agency policies and politics that could affect the review in any way (e.g., competing accrediting agencies, major organizational changes within accrediting agency, major changes in philosophy and standards of agency, etc.).

  4. Alert the University Accreditation Office that you will apply for an initial accreditation or seek continuing accreditation. Please provide the University Accreditation Office with a copy of your application form or letter to the agency for initial or continuing accreditation (if applicable), and a copy of the current accreditation standards and guidelines.


    In some instances, programs may wish to consider the value of continuing accreditation. If you wish to discontinue accreditation, please provide your supervising administrator and the University Accreditation Office with the rationale for the proposed action.


Completing the Accreditation Process
The University Accreditation Office staff will not contact an accreditation agency unless the head of the unit undergoing review requests such service. Typically, the head of the unit under review is the primary contact for the accrediting agency.

  1. The Self-Study Report
    Please submit a draft of your self-study at least three to four (3-4) weeks in advance of forwarding it to the accreditation agency or site visit team.

    A minimum of three weeks is needed to complete the review cycle:

    • Review of self-study by accreditation office staff* 7–10 work days
    • Revisions by self-study writer(s)
    		 1 week
    • Preparation of final document and printing and binding (if needed) 1 week
    • Shipment of self-study to agency and/or team members 2 work days
    (*Sometimes more than one self-study is submitted for review during a given time period, so seven to ten work days are a necessary number of review days.)

    Units are encouraged to submit an earlier draft for preliminary review. The Assistant Dean for Accreditation will accept installments if this works best for the writers of lengthy documents.

    Please check with your supervising administrator to find out how much lead-time will be needed for his/her review. The supervising administrator (as well as the University Accreditation Officer) needs to sign-off on the self-study before submission to the agency or site visit team.

    Once the final self-study, appendices, and other required materials are shipped by your unit to the individuals or offices specified by your accrediting agency (i.e., the agency, site visit team members), please send a final copy of the self-study and all appendices to the University Accreditation Office. This is the official university copy that will be retained by the University Archives. Please find out whether your supervising administrator wants a final copy too.


  2. The Site Visit
    Check with the University Accreditation Office staff prior to selecting site visit dates to ensure that university administrators are available to meet with the site visitors and that the visit is entered into the appropriate calendars. As soon as dates have been confirmed with the accrediting agency, please inform the University Accreditation Office staff. A site visit will typically take place exclusively at the unit?s home campus. Exceptions may occur if the President is expected to meet with the site visitors.

    Please send a copy of the site visitor list along with any additional information that is available about the site visitors and the site visit schedule to the University Accreditation Office.

    Prior to the site visit, a preparatory faculty meeting may be scheduled. In any case, a briefing paper will be distributed within a week before the site visit.

    The unit being accredited is responsible for making lodging arrangements and covering the visitors? expenses if they are not covered by the accrediting agency. The unit being accredited is also responsible for transportation arrangements to and from the airport and hotel, unless the agency recommends that the visitors make their own local transportation arrangements. You should also make arrangements for providing the site visitors with escorts between scheduled meetings, and between the hotel and the university. It is sometimes useful to appoint a member of the faculty or staff as an accreditation liaison to make sure that all necessary arrangements are made. (See Appendix A2: The Site Visit?A Note on Hospitality)

  3. The Site Visit Report and Response to the Report
    If the site visit report is sent directly to the unit from the visitors or agency, please send a copy to the university accreditation officer as soon as it is received. The University Accreditation Office staff will review and comment on the draft response to the site visit report. Please send the draft response to the accreditation office at least 10 days prior to the due date to allow a reasonable time for review.

    Some agencies allow only correction of factual errors. If a substantive response is allowed in addition to corrections, please contact the accreditation office staff as early as possible to discuss response strategies.

  4. Accreditation Status Letter
    If the President?s office receives the accreditation status letter, it is sent to the University Accreditation Office. The University Accreditation Office staff members will immediately send copies to the unit and the supervising administrator. If the letter announcing accreditation status is sent directly to the unit, please forward a copy to the university accreditation officer as soon as it is received.

  5. Appeal
    In the case of an appeal, the university accreditation officer will confer with the supervising administrator to determine which University Accreditation Office services will be used.

  6. Next Accreditation Review
    Please alert the University Accreditation Office as soon as you know when the next accreditation review will take place so that it can be entered into the university calendar.
