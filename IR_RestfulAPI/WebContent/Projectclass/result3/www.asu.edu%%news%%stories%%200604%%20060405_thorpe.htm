April 5, 2006

British executive tackles global warming

The headline for the March 26 cover story of Time magazine is
ominous: "Be worried, be very worried."

Worried about what? Global warming. And in the Phoenix area, whose temperatures can hover in the 116-degree range for days, that means that summer days could get even hotter.

What are we to do about it? Alan Thorpe, chief executive of Great Britain's Natural Environment Research Council (NERC), will give a free lecture titled "Global Warming: Will Phoenix Fry?" at 7:30 p.m. April 12 (Wednesday) in Bateman Physical Sciences Center Room F-166, at ASU's Tempe campus.

The lecture is part of the Distinguished Lecture Series hosted by the
Department of Physics and Astronomy in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

Global warming, Thorpe says, "has been likened to a weapon of mass
destruction and as a bigger threat than that from international terrorism.

"We are carrying out the biggest uncontrolled experiment on our environment ever. The possible outlook? A hotter climate, sea-level rise and glacier melt with changes to how often we have droughts, heat waves, hurricanes, storms and episodes of bad air quality."

In his lecture, Thorpe will review the scientific evidence for global
warming and assess the significance of this for Phoenix.

Thorpe adds that it is "crucial that the scientific evidence is fully and
critically scrutinized by policy-makers, scientists, business and the public
alike."

For more information call (480) 965-4073.
