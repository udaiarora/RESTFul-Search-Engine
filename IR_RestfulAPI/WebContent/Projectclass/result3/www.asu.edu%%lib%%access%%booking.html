Media Booking Submission Form

 

 


What can you request/reserve?


Media Booking is available to reserve media (videos, DVDs, etc) for classroom presentation/seminar use for a specific date. Faculty who require a video to be viewed by students outside of class should use the course reserve submission form. All booking requests may be monitored in regard to actual course/presentation use. Videos for personal use are located in Periodicals/Videos/Microforms, Hayden lower level on first come, first serve basis, and can also be requested through the online catalog from another campus location.

 

Use this form to request videos from the University of Arizona or Northern Arizona University. UofA/NAU requests require one week to process. We cannot guarantee the arrival of media for use on a particular date, although every effort will be made to accommodate your request. Be sure to include the owning location (UofA or NAU) in the call number field. Only ASU Faculty may request UofA or NAU media for pick-up at Hayden Library.


Videos not needed for classroom presentation can be requested through the online catalog. (Please note: Community Card borrowers are not able to request videos.) You may place holds on checked out items. However, requests for items with "shelf" status will be honored only if the pickup location is at a different ASU campus location e.g., Tempe, West or Polytechnic.

 


Who Can Use It?


ASU faculty, staff or students may book media (videos, DVDs, etc) for a specific date. Community Card holders may not book media.

 


How long does it take?

 

All orders require 4 business days to be processed.  You are encouraged to call the Hayden Circulation Desk at (480) 965-3605 the day before your given use date or alternate date to see if the media has arrived.

 

The libraries at the Tempe, West, and Polytechnic campuses cannot guarantee the use of the media in question on the specific use date or alternate date although every effort will be made to honor all requests.

 


What information do I need?

 

Give the complete call number and the complete title of the media as listed in the ASU online catalog. Many of the media titles are actually parts of a series, so please include the volume(s) of the media you are requesting. Do not abbreviate.
