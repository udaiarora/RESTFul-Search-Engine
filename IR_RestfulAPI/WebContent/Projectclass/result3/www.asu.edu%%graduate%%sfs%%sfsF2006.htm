AUGUST
  • Technology and the Teaching Assistantship
    Monday, August 28, 2006
    11:30 a.m. - 1:00 p.m.
    Location: Undergraduate Academic Services Building (UASB), Room 223
    Jeff Austin, Support Systems Analyst, Sr., University Technology Office

    Learn how to effectively use available technology to help you better carry out your TA duties. Items discussed will include:
    • ASURITE UserID: What It Is and How to Set It Up
    • Integrating Technology with Teaching: Best Practices
    • Demonstrating of MyASU Portal
    • Managing Content
    • Using the Discussion Boards
    • Creating Assignments
    • Utilizing the Grade Book
    • Additional Technology Resources
    Audience: TAs interested in gaining strategies for a successful TAship.


  • Outstanding TA Award Recipients Panel
    Wednesday, August 30, 2006
    2:00-3:00 p.m., Location: Memorial Union (MU), Room 215
    Panel: TBA

    Recipients of 2005-2006 TA Awards will speak on their experiences and responsibilities faced during their term.
    Audience: TAs interested in gaining strategies for a successful TAship.




SEPTEMBER
  • Effective Communication for TAs in the Classroom
    Wednesday, September 6, 2006
    2:00-3:30 p.m., Location: Memorial Union (MU), Room 224
    Dr. Marek Wosinski, Faculty Associate, Department of Psychology

    This workshop will focus on ways for current and future instructors to facilitate small groups in productive discussions. In addition to providing tips and suggestions for conducting groups and overcoming obstacles, we will also encourage sharing among participants about their hopes, concerns, and experiences in group facilitation. Come join us for a great opportunity to exchange ideas and experiences with fellow facilitators.
    Audience: TAs interested in gaining strategies for a successful TAship.


  • Constructing the Course Syllabus
    Thursday, September 7, 2006
    2:00-3:30 p.m., Location: Memorial Union (MU), Room 215
    Dr. Duane Roen, Head of Humanities and Arts, East College, Arizona State University at the Polytechnic campus

    Dr. Roen will discuss the genre and conventions of course syllabi. He will focus on how the syllabus develops from the goals for the course. In the first meeting, Dr. Roen will bring examples of guidelines to discuss in detail the requirements of the documents. He will also analyze the various examples, pointing out strengths and weaknesses specific to the course, the discipline, and the course's goals.
    Audience: TAs interested in gaining strategies for a successful TAship.


  • Surviving the TA-Ship
    Tuesday, September 12, 2006
    11:30 a.m. - 12:30 p.m., Location: Memorial Union (MU), Navajo Room 219
    Panel: tba

    A panel of experienced TAs discusses strategies for preparing for class presentations, labs, and grading duties, and share resources and information to help newer TAs experience a rewarding TAship.
    Audience: TAs interested in gaining strategies for a successful TAship.


  • Graduate Career Resources Tour
    Wednesday, September 13, 2006

Learn how to use Career Services to maximize your career potential and map a plan for your professional success after graduating with your Master's or PhD.
    Refreshments will be served.

  • Balancing Graduate Life
    Tuesday, September 19, 2006
    2:00-3:00 p.m., Location: Memorial Union (MU), Room 213
    Colleen Rustad-Sampson, Coordinator, Learning Resource Center

    Audience: TAs interested in gaining strategies for a successful TAship.

  • Principles for Achieving Excellence & Personal Fulfillment
    Thursday, October 5, 2006
    1:30-4:00 p.m., Location: Memorial Union (MU), Yuma Room 211
    Larry R. Woodruff, M.S. Ed., CSCS

    This seminar is a collection of wisdom from great philosophers, prisoners of war, world leaders, coaches, business leaders, and religious leaders. It represents a distillation of the combined research of experts in the psychology underlying what virtually all human beings say they want more of, but which few seem to have enough of:
    • Professional success
    • Personal effectiveness
    • Achieving more of our potential
    • Peace of mind
    • Personal fulfillment
    The seminar will present and discuss a number of fundamental principles and ways of thinking and behaving shared by all individuals who have achieved high levels of success, happiness, and personal fulfillment. These principles have been proven time and time again to be foundations for what most of us really want to achieve in life.
    Audience: Graduate students at ASU.

  • Preparing Your Curriculum Vitae, Part One
    Friday, October 20, 2006
    10:00-11:30 a.m., Location: Memorial Union (MU), Room 224
    Dr. Duane Roen, Head of Humanities and Arts, East College, Arizona State University at the Polytechnic campus

    In this session, Dr. Roen will discuss the genre and conventions of curriculum vitae (CVs) and cover letters for applying to academic and some research positions. Dr. Roen will bring examples of letters to discuss in detail the requirements of the documents. He will also analyze the various examples, pointing out strengths and weaknesses specific to the individual, their discipline, and the context for the document.
    Audience: Graduate students interested in learning how to effectively develop or perfect a curriculum vitae.


  • Preparing for the Academic Job Search Workshop (open PFF seminar)
    Friday, October 27, 2006
    2:00-5:00 p.m.
    Location: John W. Schwada Classroom Office Building (SCOB), Room 250
    Dr. Duane Roen, Head of Humanities and Arts, East College, Arizona State University at the Polytechnic campus

    This workshop will focus on the essential aspects necessary to adequately prepare for and pursue faculty positions. Dr. Roen will discuss the importance of background research and record keeping, and how these activities will distinguish the successful applicant in the eyes of a faculty search committee. He will address questions and concerns that search committees will look for during the job search.
    Audience: Graduate students interested in preparing for and pursuing careers in the professoriate.

    Note: Register for this under the "Preparing Future Faculty" section of the Graduate Workshop Registration site.
