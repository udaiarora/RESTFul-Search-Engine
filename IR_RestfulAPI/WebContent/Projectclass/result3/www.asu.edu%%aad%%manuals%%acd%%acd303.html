ACD 303: Activity Distribution Reports

Purpose

To document the effort and cost sharing of university employees as required by the federal cost principles for educational institutions receiving federal funds

Sources

United States. Office of Management and Budget. OMB circular A-21 (revised November 1991), Cost Principles for Educational Institutions
Instructions for Activity Distribution Reporting (revised November 1, 1992)

Applicability

Faculty
Designated staff and graduate assistants

Policy

In order to comply with federal regulations, all faculty and designated staff and graduate assistants are required to document 100 percent of their compensated effort. If compensated effort has changed after the ADR has been submitted for the period, e.g., involving grant activities or expense transfers, then a new ADR for the period must be submitted.

Academic units must document instructional activities, including teachers of record, for each class during fall, winter, and summer terms, as well as in special programs.

Procedures

Procedures for completing Activity Distribution Reports are found in the Research and Sponsored Projects Policies and Procedures Manual—RSP 502–03, “Project-Related Effort and Documentation.”
