May 8, 2006

Outstanding seniors to receive top honors

Nineteen ASU graduating seniors will receive Outstanding Graduate awards from the ASU Alumni Association at commencement May 11, after having been nominated by their individual colleges. These students combine high academic achievement with leadership and service to the community. They are:

A closer look

Following is a closer look at some of the 19 ASU graduating seniors who will receive Outstanding Graduate awards from the ASU Alumni Association at commencement May 11. Read more

Tiffani Borcherding, recreation and tourism, College of Public Programs; Brian Clapp, interdisciplinary studies, University College; Colleen Cox, Sandra Day O'Connor College of Law; Miriam Grill, English, College of Liberal Arts & Sciences; Jerrod Hofferth, aerospace engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering; Michiko Howlett, journalism, Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communication; Aaron J. Kulik, interior design, College of Design; Debbiesiu Lee, counseling psychology, College of Education; Patricia O'Donnell, College of Nursing; Nyssa Packard, marketing, W.P. Carey School of Business; Michael Sample, music education, Herberger College of Fine Arts; Tiffany Wear, management, Barrett Honors College.

Tinsae Babo, agribusiness, Morrison School of Agribusiness & Resource Management; Antonio Gutierrez, electrical engineering, College of Technology & Applied Sciences; Steven W. Hare, curriculum instruction, East College; Allyson Horgan, psychology, New College of Interdisciplinary Arts & Sciences; Cory Jordan Nikolaus, School of Global Leadership & Management; Dena Pollock, elementary education, College of Teacher Education & Leadership; and Patricia Denise Selvey, social work, College of Human Services.

Another 23 students will receive the Moeur Award from the Alumni Association for having perfect 4.0 GPAs with all their classwork completed at ASU. They are:

Rebecca Amrani, journalism; Daniel Barr, biochemistry; Tiffany Burnett, mathematics; Thomas Felke, history; Michael Forrest, political science; Ann Hickox, French, Christopher Kark, political science and Spanish; Megan McCracken, journalism; Jennifer Miller, art; Celina Munoz, marketing; Nyssa Packard, marketing and French; Catherine Pagan, justice studies; Matthew Petric, economics; Melissa Powers, communication studies; Gina Rossetti, psychology; Monica Rowland, interdisciplinary studies; Jason Rugolo, physics; Mary Sage, finance; Kevin Smith, biochemistry; Shawn Stevens, microbiology; Noah Theriault, anthropology and Spanish; Sarah Watkins, secondary education (Spanish); and Michal Zabiegly, civil engineering.
