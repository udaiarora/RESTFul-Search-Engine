June 13, 2003

Mark Jacobs becomes Barrett Honors College dean

Mark Jacobs, a leading scholar, teacher and administrator from top-ranked Swarthmore College, has been named dean of ASU's Barrett Honors College, effective Aug. 1.

Jacobs, 53, brings to ASU a strong scientific background, as well as a deep personal philosophy steeped in the ethical and human side of scientific research. Swarthmore College is known for challenging its students to look beyond the sheer science to explore, practically and ethically, how that science is going to be used.

"Arizona State University has scored a major coup in attracting a scholar and academic administrator of the quality of Mark Jacobs," said ASU President Michael M. Crow. "Whereas Swarthmore College is a mature, private, stand-alone institution located in the Northeast, Barrett Honors College is still in its infancy, is public and is embedded in a comprehensive research university located in the Southwest. What the two have in common is that both are liberal arts colleges of the highest quality."
Established only 15 years ago, the Barrett Honors College has become a force that has helped shape the ASU student body. The typical honors college freshman at ASU is in the top six percent of his or her high school class, has SAT scores of 1300 or above, and has a high school grade-point average of 3.8.

The college, which enrolls more than 2,600 students, is also a powerhouse in national scholarship competitions, and has helped make ASU a top school in the number of National Merit Scholars enrolled, with ASU vaulting from only four National Merit Scholars in 1988 to 350 this year. In the past 12 years, ASU students have won 13 Truman Scholarships, 11 Marshall Scholarships, 24 Goldwater Scholarships and three Rhodes Scholarships.

Currently, Jacobs is the Centennial Professor of Biology at Swarthmore, which is ranked among the handful of best liberal arts colleges in the nation. He has served as chairman of the biology department and associate provost at Swarthmore, and director of the college's Howard Hughes Medical Institute program. He has been research mentor to more than 40 students in formal projects.

"Drawing on his many achievements at Swarthmore, including his deep commitment to student success, Mark is the ideal person to take Barrett to new heights of academic excellence," said Crow.

Jacobs succeeds Ted Humphrey, founding dean of the Barrett Honors College, who will return to the ASU faculty as the first Barrett Professor.

Jacobs said, "It's such a privilege to have been picked to lead the Barrett Honors College, which has been built by Ted Humphrey into one of the best honors colleges at a public university in the country. It's a fantastic job. I'm really excited by the ASU leadership in place. They're stimulating people with excellent ideas."

At Swarthmore, Jacobs had many years of experience working with excellent undergraduate honors programs and earned a reputation as a highly effective administrator. As an adviser to Swarthmore's biology majors, 50 percent of whom go on to Ph.D. programs, he is familiar with the kind of training students need to get into top graduate schools.

A Guggenheim Fellowship recipient, Jacobs is the author of more than 50 publications, including papers in Science and PNAS (Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences), and for 12 years was associate editor-in-chief of Plant Physiology and Biochemistry, an international plant science journal. He also serves as vice chairman of the Commission on Higher Education, Middle States Association, which determines accreditation for colleges and universities.

Jacobs has been a visiting professor at the University of Pennsylvania, Cambridge University, the University of California Santa Cruz and Universitat Freiburg in Germany, where he held a NATO postdoctoral fellowship.

Jacobs, who graduated magna cum laude from Harvard and earned his Ph.D. at Stanford University in biological sciences, said one of his goals will be to expand the college's academic programs and facilities, and increasing the number of faculty who are affiliated with the college.

"We are delighted that we were able to attract Mark Jacobs to ASU," said Milton Glick, ASU executive vice president and provost. "He was chosen from an extraordinary pool of applicants. Mark has distinguished himself both as a teacher and a scholar in one of the nation's leading liberal arts colleges, and will bring much to ASU and to Arizona.

"The Barrett Honors College, under the leadership of Ted Humphrey, has established itself among the best honors programs in the nation's public universities. Mark has a keen sense of what will make it an exemplar program in the nation, and how it can enhance the education of all of our students."
