Arizona State University Center for Advancing Business through Information Technology: Research Center to Co-Host Pilot Amber Alert System

The Center for Advancing Business through Information Technology (CABIT), a research center at the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University (ASU), is playing a key role in widening the geographic reach and functional capability of the Amber Alert public service system. Plans are underway to have CABIT host the redundant real-time Amber Alert system presently under development. CABIT is partnering with Earth 911, E2C and the Arizona Broadcasters Association in this endeavor.

Amber Alert is an internet-based system that enables public safety agencies to efficiently distribute information about child abductions using multiple communications devices, such as cell phones, highway signs and broadcast outlets.

"Our specific goal will be to explore how this technology can be applied to engage and empower the public," said CABIT Director Ajay Vinze. By hosting the redundant Amber Alert system, researchers at the center will be able to study the operation and evaluate its potential for other security initiatives, such as an all-alert system that could be employed during a national security crisis.

The Amber Alert pilot will be demonstrated at a symposium sponsored by CABIT on Friday, Sept. 12 at 3 p.m. in the Carson Ballroom, Old Main, ASU. The all-day event, entitled "Responding to Future Security Concerns with Emerging Technologies," features nationally and regionally prominent speakers. For information visit http://wpcarey.asu.edu/seid/cabit/symposium_overview.cfm

ASU alumnus Chris Warner is president and CEO of Earth 911 and E2C (Engaging and Empowering Citizenship), the Arizona-based firms that developed the technology that drives Amber Alert. In 2001 Earth 911 won the prestigious Stockholm Challenge Award, which spotlights the benefits information and communication technology can bring to people and society. Earth 911 is a national network utilized by governments and private groups to make accurate information about environmental hazards easily accessible to the public.

"The technological leadership and deep analytical capabilities of CABIT offers us a tremendous opportunity to learn how to grow and replicate these public service networks," Warner said.

Art Brooks, president and CEO of the Arizona Broadcasters Association, welcomed CABIT's participation. "Public/private partnerships such as this are clearly the future of all-alert network communications and dissemination," he said. "I am confident that having CABIT at ASU involved will greatly expedite the success of these efforts."

Philip Regier, interim dean of the W. P. Carey School of Business added, "This partnership exemplifies the collaborations that make the W. P. Carey School a major asset to the community and industry. Our work with Amber Alert will help advance an important community endeavor, and will lead to new insights into knowledge management, a central focus for CABIT."

CABIT brings together public, private and university partners in shared research projects that explore the impact and potential of knowledge management technology. "This is an environment where as partners we learn from each other," Vinze said.

At the center's launch in February 2003, Gov. Janet Napolitano expressed her hopes for CABIT. "Another name for CABIT should be the Center for Advancing Arizona through Information Technology," she said at the time.

"This partnership showcases just that," Napolitano added recently. "Technology that helps protect our children and makes Arizona a safer place to call home is a great advance in information technology."

W. P. CAREY SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
