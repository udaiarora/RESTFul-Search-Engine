Activating your Blackberry

 

These instructions have been designed to assist you in setting up a new device on the wireless network.  Devices that are being re-activated for use of a new employee will require a Wipe command from the handheld.  Contact email-q@asu.edu for details on this process.

Add devices activated on the ASU Blackberry Enterprise Server will require software revision version 4.0 or newer (2.7 for C++ devices).

  1. When you have purchased your blackberry, your carrier (Verizon, Cingular, Sprint, T-Mobile, etc.) will have their own activation process.  This process is specific to your carrier and you should consult your Blackberry's documentation on how to activate your device on your wireless carrier's network.
    One example of this activation process is Verizon's requirement to dial *228 on the new Blackberry, then listening for the prompt to dial 1 in order to program your phone. 

  2. Once your device is live on the wireless network, you must register the data network.  Go to Options (the wrench on most phones, the Swiss Army Knife on other's) and choose host routing tables.  In this window, you can click in on the scroll to retrieve a side menu that holds the option Register Now, choose that option by highlighting it and clicking in on the scroll.

  3. When the device has registered itself on the wireless network you will receive a message in your messages window telling you that you have registered yourself.

  4. Download the Blackberry License Form to either purchase a new license or transfer an existing license in your department.  This form can be downloaded here:
    http://asu.edu/it/tempe/cac/license/blackberry.xls

  5. Use the vendor code "computing" for any ASU Purchase orders, the cost is $75.  This form should be faxed back to 5-5171 Attn: Patti Dawson

  6. Once your license has been purchased or transferred into your name, the Web and Messaging Applications Group will create your account on the Blackberry Enterprise Server (BES).  You will receive an e-mail with your activation password once this has been completed.

  7. When you have received your e-mail with your password, on your Blackberry go to Options (the wrench) and then Enterprise Activation. (If you can not find the Enterprise Activation icon, click here)

  8. Enter your e-mail address (first.last@asu.edu) and the password provided from the Blackberry Service and then click in on the scroll to choose Activate.

  9. This process can take quite a bit of time, and do not be disturbed if you do not see progress for a while.  Remember that if you cancel the process at any point you may be required to have an administrator issue a new password for your Enterprise Activation.
