July 11, 2003

University creates research center with $2.5 million grant

ASU and the Stardust Foundation are joining forces to help create greater stability for neighborhoods and families throughout Arizona, ASU President Michael Crow has announced. With a $2.5 million start-up grant from Stardust, ASU is creating the Stardust Center for Affordable Homes and the Family, pending approval from the Arizona Board of Regents.

The center is an example of how ASU is focusing on community outreach and becoming embedded in the community in ways that will make a difference in quality of life and the economy.

Crow said the center will fill a void by focusing on affordable homes and family success as a single issue. He said there are numerous programs that focus on either affordable homes or family success, but few, if any, are looking at both issues together.

"We know that families can't succeed, and our economy won't thrive, without affordable, permanent homes for working families," said Crow. "But, we also know that a home isn't the only answer. Families need a whole host of critical life management skills and support services to succeed and we need to know, based on sound research, exactly what those skills and services are and how best to link them to the home."

Arizona's shortage of affordable homes for working families has created a situation in which the living condition of more than one in 10 families is substandard.

Jerry Bisgrove, founder of the Stardust Foundation, wants to tackle the problem by tapping into the intellectual capacity of ASU to apply scientific research and technological advances toward solutions. Bisgrove is known for his generosity, and his penchant for giving a "hand up, not a hand out."

"The collaboration we are forming with ASU to create this center provides a unique tool to help raise the awareness of the issues affecting family stability, and a mechanism to develop innovative solutions that can be replicated throughout the state and help improve the quality of life and our economy," Bisgrove said. "We are very excited about this partnership and look forward to the measurable impact it will have on an ongoing basis."

The center will be a trans-disciplinary effort involving faculty and resources from across ASU to research the problem and design innovative programs, services and technologies to help families and neighborhoods succeed.

The project will involve collaboration between ASU scholars and researchers from the colleges of architecture, education, engineering, liberal arts & sciences, business and public programs, and will result in invaluable research for developing models and techniques for achieving sustainable neighborhoods that can be used in Arizona and elsewhere.

The Stardust Center for Affordable Homes and the Family aims to help stabilize neighborhoods and, in turn, improve the quality of life and the economy in Arizona, Crow said.

"Home ownership leads to a stable environment that supports cognitive and emotional development in a child," said Crow. "This is one way the university can be deeply and intimately connected to the needs of its community, with ripple effects that will benefit all of us for generations to come."

Families with better living conditions have children who tend to do better in school and are less likely to be involved with crime, researchers have found. Safe, permanent affordable homes can break the cycle of poverty by giving families the stability they need to access critical family services such as health care, education and life skills training.

The goal, over the next few years, is to have established a reputation for expertise and to take ideas to the market, in both a policy and a literal sense. These will include the economics of building affordable homes, the technological and architectural challenges, the effect of affordable homes on family and community stability and the best ways to create supportive family services.

"The interaction between the Stardust Foundation and ASU will provide the center with unique qualities," said Crow. "It will have the capabilities of the foundation for quality investments and community partnerships, as well as the capabilities of ASU for intellectual involvement, research and application."
