Major Initiatives: Recommended Strategies
Seamless Access

I. Systems Development

  • Strategy: The Libraries must build on our cooperative systems development initiatives with ASU Information Technology to make progress toward seamless access to library information.


    • Rationale: The University Libraries require additional technical expertise to move towards seamless access. Information Technology is an existing, cost-effective source of the necessary expertise.


      • create consistent user interface for electronic databases;


      • provide direct system access to document delivery;


      • implement systems to guide users to information (e.g., expert systems) and services;


      • implement system for digitizing collections and provide access to them;


      • establish seamless access to ASU computer systems (e.g., CUFS, SIS) for authorized library staff.


    • Performance Outcome Measure: Customers (including internal) report fewer barriers to access to resources through the Libraries' technical systems.
II. Technological Infrastructure
  • Strategy: Develop and implement a long-term plan for continuously improving and upgrading the technological infrastructure to support seamless access to information.


    • Rationale: Seamlessness cannot be achieved without improvement of the technological infrastructure.


      • develop a plan for continuous upgrading of public and staff workstations, with particular attention to the display of digitized materials;


      • upgrade document delivery technologies;


      • address issues of large-scale digital storage of files (image, text, sound, etc.);


      • provide facilities for mass uploading and downloading of digitally stored files.


    • Performance Outcome Measure: Customers and staff report that they are able to access more workstations with a greater wealth of features.
