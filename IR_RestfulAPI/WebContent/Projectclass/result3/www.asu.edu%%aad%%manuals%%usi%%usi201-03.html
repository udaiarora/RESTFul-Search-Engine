USI 201–03: Drop/Add of Classes

Purpose

To allow a student to drop classes and/or add classes to his or her current registration

Sources

University Registrar’s Office

Tuition Payment Office

Policy

Registered students may change their current semester schedules by:

  1. dropping classes for which they are currently registered

    or

  2. adding classes for which they are not currently registered.

A student must obtain approval to add classes, if required by the college or department.

Dropping and adding classes must be accomplished within the specific time period indicated in the online University Registrar’s Office Semester Calendar.

Additional Fees/Refunds

Dropping or adding classes may result in additional fees or refunds.

Additional Fees

Additional fees must be paid by the fee payment deadline, or if the drop/add transaction is processed after the fee payment deadline, they must be paid by the end of the same business day.

Refunds

Business Services mails refunds to the student’s local address.

Procedure

To drop or add a class, a student must:

  1. meet with the assigned advisor to obtain advising sign-off and approval to add classes, if required by the university, college, or department

  2. submit a drop/add request online at ASU Interactive or go to any registrar site (the Records Information counter at the Tempe campus, Records & Registration at the West campus, or Registration Services at the Polytechnic or Downtown Phoenix campus), fill out and submit a Drop/Add Request form based on the previously approved schedule, and present a photo ID

    Note:If a form is submitted for processing in person, wait for a revised schedule/billing statement to be issued before leaving the registrar site.

    and

  3. verify enrollment by viewing the student class schedule online at ASU Interactive.

Cross-References

For information on registration, see USI 201–01, “Registration for Classes—General Policy.”

For information on late drop/add, see USI 201–05, “Registration and Drop/Add after the Published Deadline.”

For information on withdrawal from classes, see USI 201–08, “Withdrawal from Classes—General Policy.”
