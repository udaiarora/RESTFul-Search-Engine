Summer Language Practicum in
Tirana, Albania

Where

Visit Albania and its capital city Tirana. The ASU Russian and East European Studies Center invites you to participate in a three-week two-credit practicum in Tirana, Albania. Tirana was a settlement established in the early 17th century during the Ottoman Empire's domination of Albania. the Tirana practicum serves as a natural extension of the ASU Summer Sessions' nine-week intensive Albanian language course in the Critical Languages Institute, held on the main campus of Arizona State University, May 30-July 28, 2006.

Located at the crossroads of the Adriatic Sea and eastern Albania, Tirana became the capital of Albania in 1920 and is the country's administrative, cultural, economic, and industrial center. The city, which has been inhabited since the Neolithic period, lies on the Ishm River and is connected by rail with the port city of Durres on the Adriatic. The University of Tirana, with its vibrant intellectual atmosphere, boasts seven faculties including humanities and science programs.

When

The three-week practicum in Tirana is held in August immediately following the annual Critical Languages Institute at ASU. This year's session lasts from July 31 to August 18, 2006.
The Program

Utilizing instructors from the University of Tirana, the Tirana Practicum will expand language skills and introduce participants to the culture of ancient and modern Albania. Formal classroom language instruction is combined with local excursions to historic sites. Students participating in the three-week Tirana Practicum receive two ASU credits (FLA 494).
Eligibility

The Tirana Practicum is open to all ASU and non-ASU undergraduate and graduate students, as well as to non-degree students. Normally, participants in the Tirana Practicum will have completed at least one year of intensive Albanian language study at ASU in the nine-week summer Critical Languages Institute. Financial assistance may be used for this overseas study. Students should apply through their own universities.
Living Accommodations

Students are placed in private or shared housing in university dormitories, city flats, or with families. Meals are taken in the university cafeteria or in local cafes and restaurants.
Estimated Cost

The estimated cost of $1250 includes program fees, housing, meals, and excursions. Not included: roundtrip airfare from Phoenix to Tirana (APEX fares run approximately $1400), passport costs, books, and personal expenses. Tuition for this two-credit practicum has been waived by ASU Summer Sessions.
Deadline

Applications with non-refundable $275 deposit are due by April 28. Applications received after that date will be considered on a space available basis.
