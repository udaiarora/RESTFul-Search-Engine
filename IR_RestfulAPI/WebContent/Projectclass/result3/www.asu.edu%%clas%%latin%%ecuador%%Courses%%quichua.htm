ECUADOR: The Amazon and the Andes Field School
Course Syllabi

FLA 494/590: Beginning Quichua
FLA 494/590: Intermediate Quichua
FLA 494/590: Advanced Quichua

Instructor: Professor Tod Swanson
With the assistance of Quichua native speaker course facilitators.
Accompanying faculty: Luz María De La Torre Amaguaña

These courses provide a graded language sequence of immersion-based instruction in the Quichua language (or Runa Shimi as it is referred to by native speakers) as it is currently spoken in the Napo River area of the Ecuadorian Amazon. The primary goal is to learn to converse as quickly as possible. Typically, students spend 3 hours a day in classroom instruction. In the afternoons and evenings, there are additional structured activities for practicing the Quichua language.

The Quichua Language is:

  • the language of the Incas.
  • the largest native language in the Americas, it is spoken by 12 million people.
  • spoken in Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, Colombia, and Argentina.
  • related to other American Indian languages.
  • unrelated to Spanish and any other European language.

Why study Quichua?

  • Studying a native language is the best way to understand a native culture. Studying Runa Shimi gives you an inside look at how a language native to the Americas works. The course involves the study of Quichua language-based thought patterns and classification systems. How does a language native to the America's shape cultural experience? For example, how are kinship relations organized by the Quichua language? How are relations between various species of plants organized by the Quichua language? You will also study the meaning of key religious terms not fully translatable into English.
  • Studying Quichua is a good "icebreaker." Quichuas generally appreciate the interest shown in their culture by those who are willing to learn their language. Even learning a little Quichua goes a long way toward breaking down cultural barriers. As you practice the language with native speakers, occasions for conversation on many other topics arise naturally.

Grading: The grade is based upon student performance as measured in bi-weekly tests of four skill areas: listening, speaking, translation, and writing.
