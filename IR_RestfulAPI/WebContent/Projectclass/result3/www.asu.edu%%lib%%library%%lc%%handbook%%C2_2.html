Librarians' Council

C.2.2: Personnel Committee

Effective 12/20/89 .. Revised 5/15/96

 

POLICY AND PROCEDURES STATEMENT

The Personnel Committee is one of the Standing Committees of the Librarians' Council. It consists of five (5) members who serve two-year terms, plus the current Chair of the Librarians' Council. All council members are eligible for this committee.

RESPONSIBILITIES

  1. To compile and revise procedures and criteria relating to personnel matters such as recruitment, assignment of rank at initial appointment, performance evaluation, merit evaluation of academic professionals, promotion and affirmative action.


    2.  

  2. To consider any matter dealing with the status of academic professionals.


    3.  

  3. To serve in an advisory capacity for individual grievances.


    4.  

  4. To serve as an advisory body to the Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian on personnel policies and procedures.


    5.  

  5. To take under consideration any item of interest to the academic professionals upon the request of the Librarians' Council and/or the Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian .

PROCEDURES

At the first meeting after the election of new members the Committee will chose a Chairperson, a Secretary, a non-voting advisory representative to the Affirmative Action Committee, and a non-voting advisory representative to the Review Committee for Personnel Action. The representative to the Review Committee shall serve no more than two consecutive years in that capacity.

All members of the Personnel Committee, with the exception of the Chair of Librarians' Council, are voting members. All decisions require the presence of at least three voting members; a majority of those voting members present is necessary to take any action.

All personnel policies compiled by the Committee must be approved by the Librarians' Council.
