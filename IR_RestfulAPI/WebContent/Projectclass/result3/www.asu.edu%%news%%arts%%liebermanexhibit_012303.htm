January 23, 2003

Major Valley ceramics collection to be exhibited at ASU Art Museum

Selections from the important ceramics collection of Valley residents David and Sara Lieberman will go on exhibit at the ASU Art Museum's Ceramics Research Center this spring, providing an opportunity to view a collection that recently was featured in American Craft magazine.

"Shared Passion: Sara and David Lieberman Collection of Contemporary Ceramics and Craft" will be on display Feb. 8 - May 18. A weekend of activities, including a free public reception from 7 to 9 p.m., Feb. 8, will launch the exhibition. Also scheduled are two free lectures by nationally renowned ceramists; Ken Price at 7 p.m., Feb. 7; and Josh DeWeese, at 2 p.m., Feb. 9. Both lectures will take place in Neeb Hall.

The Liebermans' collection of more than 200 contemporary ceramics pieces, which was last year promised to the ASU Art Museum's Ceramics Research Center, includes works by Peter Voulkos, Michael Lucero, Ruth Duckworth and Beatrice Wood. Approximately 40 pieces from the collection will be on display as part of this exhibition.

"Shared Passion" will continue in the museum's Nelson Fine Arts Center location, with selections from the Liebermans' other great collections of contemporary fiber works, baskets, metal, glass, wood and jewelry. Senior Curator Heather Lineberry said the complete exhibition in both buildings would present about 100 works in total.

"In a relatively short amount of time, the Liebermans have assembled a magnificent contemporary craft collection that touches upon most of the major artists and movements of today," Lineberry said.

The Liebermans' ceramics collection had been sought by a number of museums, but they chose to donate it to the ASU Art Museum. They also made an immediate monetary donation toward operating costs at the new center.

J. Robert Wills, dean of the Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts, recognized the long-term importance of the gift.

"This is a two-part gift that not only helps us build momentum at the beginning of the center, but also helps guarantee its long-term excellence," Wills said. "We owe them special thanks."

Both Sara and David are recognized benefactors of multiple Valley art and community organizations. Sara has a 10-year history of volunteering and supporting the ASU Art Museum, having started as a community docent and now holding the position of chairwoman of the Ceramics Leaders at ASU board. David was recently recognized for volunteering almost 10 years in inner city elementary schools, during which he has encouraged and inspired many children. He also established a number of trusts to assist at-risk children in gaining an education.

For information, call (480) 965-2787 or visit the museum online at (http://asuartmuseum.asu.edu).

Pringle, with the Herberger College of Fine Arts, can be reached (480) 965-8795, (Jennifer.pringle@asu.edu).
