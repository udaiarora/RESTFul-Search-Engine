Researcher Michelle "Lani" Shiota
Description POSITIVE EMOTION: DISCRETE EMOTIONS AND ROLE IN EMOTION REGULATION

RAs will be exposed to several methods commonly used in research on emotion. Typical studies include:

1) Eliciting emotion through film clips, slide shows, or emotional memories, and measuring physiological responding (e.g., heart rate, blood pressure) and facial expressions of emotion, as well as self-reports. Participants may also be given specific instructions to follow to try to regulate their emotions during the film clip.

2) Asking participants to provide a written description of an emotional memory, and then to complete a cognitive or social judgment task (e.g., rating the guilt of a target in a "student court" scenario, completing a creative task).

RA duties will be partially tailored to each person's interests, and may include:

- Helping with data collection, including: "hookup" of participants to physiological sensors; communicating instructions to participants during the session; and monitoring audio/visual recordings during the session
- Coding participants' answers to free-response questions, creativity tests, or other qualitative data
- Entering questionnaire data, by hand or using a scanner
- Transcribing interviews and participant speech during experimental sessions

ALL RAs MUST BE AVAILABLE WEDS 4:40-5:30 FOR A WEEKLY MEETING!
Prereqs Flexible, please contact Sam Neufeld at the email address below for more information.

To apply, please submit a resume with Psychology classes you've completed, your current Psych GPA, your year in school, a description of any research experience you have or other (paid or volunteer) work that highlights your skills, and a paragraph describing your goals for the research experience (what you'd like to learn, preparing for grad school, etc.).

Researcher Dr. William Fabricius
Description

We are studying how fathers and stepfathers contribute to their adolescent children's development. We have 400 families, half of whom are Anglo-American, and half Mexican-American.

You will be trained and then code narratives of how the children describe the relationship between themselves and their parents across a number of dimensions.

Benefits to you:

1) If you are interested in counseling, you will gain first-hand, real world experience of adolescents' relationships with their fathers and how fathers view their role as a parent:

  • What do they value in their father-child relationships?
  • What frustrates them?
  • What do they want and need from each other? How are they meeting these needs?
  • How do relationships with fathers compare to relationships with mothers?
  • How do relationships with fathers predict future strengths and problems?
  • You can't learn much about this from books because fathers have not been studied nearly as much as mothers.
  • We anticipate that this will be a landmark study, and you will be some of the first people to learn something from it.

2) If you are interested in gaining research experience, you will learn about the increasingly important process by which open-ended, qualitative data are transformed into numerical, quantitative data that can be used in sophisticated statistical modeling.
Prereqs

We need bright, committed undergraduates (some of which must be Spanish-speaking) to help with some of the most interesting measures we use. Ideally, these students will be able to commit to helping out both semesters if possible. We have each adolescent tell us narratives about their relationship with their parents. We also ask their parents to tell us about the things that Dad is doing well, as a father. We will train you to code these narratives.
