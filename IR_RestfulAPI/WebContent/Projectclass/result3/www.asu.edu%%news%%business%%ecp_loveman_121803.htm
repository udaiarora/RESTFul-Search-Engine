For more than 19 years The Economic Club of Phoenix has been the preeminent forum in Arizona for the exchange of ideas about business and the economy. It brings national and international figures of prominence to Phoenix and in so doing, bolsters economic development and builds partnerships between the business community and the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University.

GARY LOVEMAN
Gary Loveman, 43, is President and Chief Executive Officer of Harrah's Entertainment, Inc., a Fortune 500 Company publicly traded on the New York Stock Exchange. Harrah's is a leading casino entertainment company with 43,000 employees and $4.1 billion in annual revenues in 2002.

Loveman's leadership helped to transform Harrah's into the premier brand, and the most profitable company in the casino entertainment industry during his four and a half years' tenure as Harrah's President and Chief Operating Officer. As President and COO, Loveman oversaw brand operations, marketing and information technology, as well as day-to-day operations at Harrah's 26 casino properties. Harrah's customer loyalty and marketing initiatives - including Total Rewards, Harrah's nationwide players card - propelled the company to 16 consecutive quarters of same-store sales growth.

Loveman was promoted to Chief Executive Officer of Harrah's on January 1, 2003.
Loveman serves on the Harrah's Board of Directors, and is an outside director of Coach, Inc. (NYSE:COH), a leading marketer of modern classic American accessories.
Before joining Harrah's in May 1998, Loveman was associate professor of business administration at the Harvard University Graduate School of Business Administration, where he taught service management in the M.B.A. and executive programs. He worked as a consultant for Harrah's during the preceding seven years, helping to design and execute its executive development and education programs. Loveman has also consulted for many other leading service companies, including American Airlines, Booz-Allen & Hamilton, Coopers & Lybrand, I.S.S., McDonald's Corp., The Walt Disney Company and UNUM.

Loveman was the first recipient of Harvard Business School's Apgar Award for Excellence and Innovation in Teaching. He has authored three books, numerous academic papers, and six articles for Harvard Business Review. Loveman was awarded a Ph.D. in Economics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where he was an Alfred Sloan Doctoral Dissertation Fellow, and a B.A. in Economics from Wesleyan University.
