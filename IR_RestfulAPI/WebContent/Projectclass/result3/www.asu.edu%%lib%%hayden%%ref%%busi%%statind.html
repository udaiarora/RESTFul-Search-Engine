INDUSTRY INFORMATION SOURCES

Indexes to Statistics


The titles listed below represent the main sources available for identifying a wide variety of statistical information. Additional sources can be found by searching the periodical indexes such as the Business and Industry Database or the F & S Index (see under "Business Periodical Indexes and Abstracts") for statistical data published in business and trade journals. Another alternative is to identify statistical sources by industry or product in the Encyclopedia of Business Information Sources (see under "Guides to Business Reference Sources").

    When you see this button next to an entry, click on it to go to the ASU Libraries Indexes page. You may also get to this page by clicking on INDEXES on any of our web pages, including our Home Page: http://www.asu.edu/lib/


  1. American Statistics Index (ASI). Bethesda, MD: Congressional Information Service, 1973- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1974- .)
      HA 195 .A4x HAYDEN DOC REFERENCE (located on Table 2A)

    Basic and recurring statistical data from U.S. government agencies. Divided into index and abstract volumes with indexing by subject, category, and title. (Documents cited are available in Government Documents Service in the Hayden Library in paper and/or microfiche format.)

  2. Business Reference Suite: TableBase. Beachwood, OH: Responsive Database Service, 1995- . Daily. (ASU owns 1995- .)
      ONLINE ACCESS Click on Business

    Provides fulltext of tabular data (forecasts, market share, rankings, time series, trends) on companies, products, industries, and markets. The data is taken from statistical annuals, trade associations, U.S. and international agencies, industry research reports, and the periodicals covered in Business & Industry. The associated textual material is selectively included.

  3. Predicasts Basebook. Cleveland, OH: Predicasts, 1973-1995. Annual. (ASU owns 1980, 1982-1995.)
      HA 214 .P73a HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9B)

    No longer published, but an excellent source of time-series statistics on demographics, the economy, and industries for past years. Socioeconomic items begin the list using special alpha-numeric codes; expanded SIC listings for products and industries follow in numerical order. An alphabetical list of socioeconomic factors, products, and industries, giving each one's code or SIC, can be found at the back. Provides basic statistics and indicates the original source of those statistics. Average annual growth rates are cited, but read the note on how they're computed (see the Forward) before using the figure.

  4. Standard & Poor's Statistical Service. New York: Standard & Poor's, 1978- . Monthly. (ASU owns current only.)
      HG 4921 .S685 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    Not actually an index but a collection of statistics updated monthly on banking, finance, production, labor, building, metals, and many other topics important to the U.S. economy. In many cases statistics go back to the early 1950's, if not farther.

  5. Statistical Reference Index (SRI). Bethesda, MD: Congressional Information Service, 1980- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1980- . All years in Reference; 1980-1991 in Document Services.)
      HA 214 .S8x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 6B) & HAYDEN DOC REFERENCE (located on Table 2A)

    Nearly identical to ASI (above) but identifies and indexes commercially published and non-federal government sources of statistics. Publications cited are available in microfiche format as part of the SRI Microfiche Collection in Hayden Microforms Service under the abstract number if the abstract indicates "SRI/excerpts" or "SRI/complete." Otherwise, check the ASU Libraries Online Catalog for the publication title to see if the Library owns it.

  6. STAT-USA. Washington, DC: U.S. Department of Commerce, 1992- . Daily. (ASU owns current data online.)
      ONLINE ACCESS Click on Government Publications

    Provides access to extensive current and historical data on the U. S. economy compiled by Federal agencies and current and historical trade-related information and statistical data including the NTDB (National Trade Data Bank).

  7. Statistical Universe. Bethesda, MD: Congressional Information Service, 1980- . Daily. (ASU owns 1973- .)
      ONLINE ACCESS Click on General... - Other Indexes

    Abstracts statistical information produced by the U.S. government, state governments, and selected private organizations. Corresponds to the ASI and SRI services above. Some of the fulltext is online, but mostly the fulltext of the federal statistics cited are located in Document Services while the nonfederal statistics are on the SRI microfiche in Hayden Microforms where filming is indicated. (See the SRI explanation above for details on full text availability.) Also provides fulltext access to the latest Statistical Abstract of the United States.
