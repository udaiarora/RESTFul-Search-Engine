Satisfactory Academic Progress

To be eligible for federal and state aid programs, you must meet all three Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) standards regardless of whether you have received financial aid previously.

 

1) GPA Standard:

Maintain minimum GPA standards. (Refer to the General Catalog for specific GPA requirements for your program of study.) Students on academic disqualification are not eligible for financial aid.

2) Pass Rate Standard:

Students must pass at least 67% of the total institutional credit hours attempted in their degree level at Arizona State University. Measurement of each student's pass rate will occur at the completion of each spring semester.

3) Maximum Credit Hour Standard:

Students who have attempted in excess of the credit hours indicated in the table below, are ineligible for financial aid. Transfer credits are included in this measurement.

SAP Status Definitions

Financial Aid Probation
Warning SAP status if your ASU pass rate is less than 67% upon your first measurement. You remain aid eligible (as long as you meet all other eligibility criteria). You will have until the end of the next spring semester to raise your cumulative institutional pass rate to the 67% standard.

Financial Aid Suspension
SAP status if you do not meet the 67% ASU pass rate standard at your second measurement. Students with this SAP status are ineligible for financial aid. If the measurement occurs after a semester has begun, aid for that semester will be cancelled. You will also lose aid eligibility for subsequent semesters. This policy is not applicable to scholarships or employee tuition benefits.

Financial Aid Termination
SAP Status if you are measured at or in excess of your credit hour limit for your program. Students with this SAP status are ineligible for financial aid. If the measurement occurs after a semester has begun, aid for that semester will be cancelled. You will also lose aid eligibility for subsequent semesters. This policy is not applicable to scholarships or employee tuition benefits.
