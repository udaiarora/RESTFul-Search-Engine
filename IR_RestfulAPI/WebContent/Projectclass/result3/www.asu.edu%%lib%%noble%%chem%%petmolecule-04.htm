Research Tools and Strategies

 

Chemical company catalogs

Catalogs are plentiful on the Internet; try the following or use your favorite Internet search engine and search for the compound.

  • ACS Chemcyclopedia
    http://pubs.acs.org/chemcy/
  • ChemACX.com

  • ChemIndustry.com

  • Sigma-Aldrich Catalog

Business and Industry Database

Business and Industry Database is an index to trade magazines from many industries. Most of the articles are provided in full text within the database.



Search Strategy:

  1. Click on the Business and Industry link above, and on the next page, click on the title again.

  2. Enter the most commonly used name for your compound/drug in the "Words in Title" box

  3. If too many articles are retrieved, click on the "refine search" button on the bottom of the results screen and try limiting by date or by adding "Concept Terms" such as price trends, output, ranking, or sales

  4. If too few or no articles are retrieved, try putting the compound name in the "Words and Phrases" box rather than title; also try using other names for your compound; also try searching for the "class of compounds". (NSAID, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatories, etc.)

  5. Click on the "page icon" to view full text.

  6. When finished, use the LOGOUT button to exit the database.

 

 

Academic Search Premier

This database indexes journals from all subject areas. Look for current articles (within the last several years) that provide a good overview. Don't spend time looking for a comprehensive list of references; instead, concentrate on getting just a couple good ones.

 

Search Strategy: Using the most common name for your compound rather than the scientific/chemical name may give better results. If too many references are retrieved by searching just the compound name, refine your search by "AND"ing words that describe known issues or concerns.


 

Internet Resources

Topical information about pharmaceuticals and/or well-known chemical compounds may be found on the Web.

 

Web Search Engines
Use Google or your favorite search engine. Enter the most commonly used name for the compound. If this search is unsuccesful, then try the generic name, the tradename, or the chemical name.

When viewing web pages, be aware of the authority and objectivity of the web site and its contents.
