Microsensor workshop will focus on homeland security

TEMPE, Ariz. - The Third Microsensor Workshop will be held April 19 to 22 at the ASU Kerr Center in Scottsdale. The workshop will bring together many of the researchers working to improve sensor technology and apply it to homeland security.

"Sensors are the key to homeland security and it is more important than ever to develop non-invasive yet highly sensitive technologies to detect chemical and biological threats," said Mark Hayes, co-director of Arizona Applied NanoSensors (AzANS) and associate professor of chemistry and biochemistry at Arizona State University. "This workshop will bring together engineers and scientists from government and industry to explore new directions in technology to help keep our country safe. It also will provide a forum between leaders and researchers in this arena to continue developing the latest technologies and techniques in sensors for homeland security." 

The workshop will include sessions on chemical sensing from trace explosives detection, to chemical warfare/biological warfare agent detection to hazardous materials detection. Other sessions will include advanced collection techniques, novel sensing materials and coatings and new electronic and nanotechnology sensor platforms. It is expected that these technologies will eventually find application in homes and businesses for environmental monitoring and healthcare.

"This is a great opportunity for Arizona scientists, engineers and businesses to see and speak to the 'nuts and bolts' sensor people on the front lines of homeland security," said Karl Booksh, co-director of AzANS and an Arizona State University associate professor of chemistry and biochemistry.

"This is an especially rich opportunity for the new programs set forth by the ASU vice president for research and economic affairs, of which we are a part," Booksh added noting that the center was begun in part with Proposition 301 funds. "This gathering serves as a platform for ASU to interact with the leaders in the field of homeland security technology."

The workshop is co-sponsored by the U.S. Transportation Security Administration, Department of Homeland Security, the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives, the Department of Justice and ASU's Arizona Applied NanoSensors.
