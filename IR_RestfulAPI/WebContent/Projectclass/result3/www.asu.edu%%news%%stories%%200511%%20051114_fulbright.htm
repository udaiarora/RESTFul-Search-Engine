November 14, 2005

ASU fourth highest producer of Fulbright Awards for 2005 - 06

14 ASU students studying abroad on Fulbrights this year

Fourteen students from Arizona State University won prestigious Fulbright awards to study abroad in 2005 – 06, making ASU the fourth highest producer of Fulbright awards among all public colleges and universities in the United States.

ASU Fulbright applicants have a higher acceptance rate than virtually any other school, with 36 percent of all those who apply receiving awards. At Harvard, Yale, Columbia and Princeton, only a quarter of applicants were accepted.

The Fulbright Program has announced the complete list of colleges and universities that produced the most 2005 – 06 U.S. Fulbright Fellows. ASU ranks 13 th overall, and 4 th among publics.

More ASU students than ever before are studying abroad this year on competitive national scholarships. In addition to 14 Fulbright winners, nine ASU undergraduates won National Security Education Program (NSEP) awards to study overseas.

"This reflects very positively on the quality of ASU graduates,” said Milton Glick, executive vice president and provost. “It is a tribute to the students and to the faculty and staff of ASU .”

Faculty members have been particularly instrumental in guiding talented students to apply and in helping them shape solid project proposals, according to Janet Burke, associate dean of the Barrett Honors College and director of the Office of National Scholarship Advisement. Her office works with the students to hone their applications.

"To have so many ASU students studying overseas next year is wonderful, and it fits perfectly with the new global efforts President Crow has been encouraging, enhancing the ability of ASU to look outward,” says Burke.

"These students are communicating with others back here and offering exceptional insights. And in part because of their unusual experiences, those students go on in great numbers to very prestigious graduate schools and enviable employment opportunities. This kind of success can only help raise the visibility and prestige of ASU.”

Fulbright students apply for a particular country and find specialists who are willing to work with them on their chosen course of study, receiving full travel, living and academic expenses for an academic year.

ASU students tend to have compelling, well thought-out project proposals, says Burke. Many are building on their bachelor’s honors thesis, or their master’s or Ph.D. projects. Other keys to their success are the mentoring and the overseas connections of ASU faculty.

"ASU faculty members are remarkably well connected worldwide, and they are very willing to use their connections to secure important letters of affiliation from host countries,” says Burke. “They also tend to work closely with students on their intellectual projects and to write strong, detailed letters of recommendation.

"Also, I have some 40 or so faculty that help me evaluate student applications. They interview the students and make absolutely critical suggestions from their areas of expertise. Often, these same professors invite individual applicants to work with them further, and put them in touch with persons who can help them even more.”

Mark Jacobs, Barrett Honors College dean, says ASU’s success in the Fulbright competition reflects not only the academic excellence of ASU students, but the dedication and skill of Burke and her office.

"Dean Burke has been particularly adept at coaching good students from their early years at ASU,” says Jacobs, "and she has been able to develop great relationships with ASU faculty who are willing to put in huge amounts of their time on mock interviews and coaching activities. All of these factors have contributed to our impressive numbers.”

More than 1,200American students in over 100different fields of study were offered Fulbright grants to study, teach English, and conduct research in over 110 countries throughout the world beginning this fall.

Out of the 1,200 Fulbrighters, 269 will be studying at the Ph.D. degree level, 212 at the Masters, and 719 at the Bachelor’s degree level. Students receiving awards for this academic year applied through 550 different colleges or universities.

The Fulbright U.S. Student Program equips future American leaders with the skills they need to thrive in increasingly global environment by providing funding for one academic year of study or research abroad, to be conducted after graduation from an accredited university.

The Fulbright Program is sponsored by the United States Department of State, Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs. Financial support is provided by an annual appropriation from Congress to the Department of State, with significant contributions from participating governments and host institutions in the United States and abroad.

The Institute of International Education administers and coordinates the U.S. Student Program, including an annual competition for the scholarships.

Detailed lists of the students are available at (www.fulbrightonline.org/us).
