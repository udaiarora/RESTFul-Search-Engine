Becca Stout
Entering Class of 1978

It seems like only yesterday that we each received that all-important letter from ASU announcing our selection into the Leadership Scholarship Program. Even as we were feeling a tremendous sense of pride and excitement in the moment, it would have been impossible to begin to imagine how our participation in such a program would so definitively impact each of our college experiences. And now, looking back on our college years, could we honestly begin to imagine what things would have been like without having participated in the Leadership Scholarship Program!?

Who doesn’t recall receiving numerous phone calls, letters or selection process invitations throughout our years at ASU from all of our biggest fans -- those special people who always seemed to be looking out for us? People like Christine Wilkinson, Bob Francis, Leon Shell and June Malos who were constantly keeping us up-to-date about the latest leadership conference, new organizations to join, or employment opportunities scholarship that would help us make ends meet. We were definitely “in the know” on pretty much everything on campus -- we were Leadership Scholars! And, now we are Leadership Scholarship alumni, and I am very proud to be an LSP alumna!

Becca is currently working for Georgia State University.

Craig Mathews
Entering Class of 1984

It seems only yesterday that we were selected for the Leadership Scholarship Program at ASU. Can you believe it’s been 15 years? You probably remember taking Neil’s leadership class, (does anyone remember who said, “Loose links sink ships”?) and meeting incredible people on and off campus while participating in the program. Whether it was Bill Schultz, Dr. Shell, or Eva Bowen, we met and made friendships with amazing people.


Entering Class of 1989

Here are some of my memories from the LSP incoming class of 1989: All of us chipped in and bought a TV for Wendy as a wedding gift that first year…Bob Hancock’s intense, blue eyes and insurmountable energy in the Admissions Office… Marilyn Singleton, the scholarship woman!…Gatherings at Christine Wilkinson's home… The yearly LSP picture, sporting our maroon shirts, on that day if at no other time…Our first LSP retreat at Camp T. Does anyone remember the ice breaker “high heels, cowboy boots and tennis shoes”? How about bowling in town? How about our pathetic attempt to sing a version of “So Long, Farewell” from the Sound of Music as we introduced ourselves to all the other LSPers?

At Leadership 2000 that first year, Michael Thompson tried to “break-up” the segregation exercise. He wasn't afraid to challenge Gabriel one-on-one.

We were the 50/50 class: the first two years were spent with Gabriel Vasquez as our advisor and the second two years were with Jeff Thompson. During our college career the first Native American/Hispanic ASASU president (Matt Ortega-LSP) and first African American ASASU president (Rossi Turman) were elected.

Amy is program coordinator for the Office of Off-Campus and Commuter Student Services at Ohio State University.


Entering Class of 1990

It is hard to believe that it was more than ten years ago when we were named as the Leadership Scholarship Program’s incoming class of 1990. We were the “new” freshmen who were introduced at the ASU football game, we met with our advisors pretty frequently, we attended Gabe’s class and we had a great time at the retreat where our Brady Bunch skit was a hit. We later became the sophomores and juniors who had gotten extremely involved in leadership activities on campus, in our communities and within our different degree programs. Eventually we became the graduating seniors who had the opportunity to thank all of the people who helped make our Leadership Scholarship Program experience so rewarding and also to welcome and congratulate the new incoming LSPers.

Last year, I attended the LSP Homecoming reception and I also attended the senior speeches for the graduating class of 1999. Attending both of those events made me think about how I could give back to the Leadership Scholarship Program. I had a great time at last year’s Homecoming event, and this year I hope to see many more of you at the party. I hope each of you is doing very well and I would love to catch-up with you.

Denise is an attorney.
