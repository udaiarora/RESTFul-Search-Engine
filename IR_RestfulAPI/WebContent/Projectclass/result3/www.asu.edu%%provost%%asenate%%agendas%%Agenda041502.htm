6.C.2    Action Items:

 

Senate Motion #7 (2001-2002): ?The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal submitted by the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences to change the name of a department from Department of Exercise Science & Physical Education to Department of Kinesiology.  Rationale: As the academic discipline underlying physical activity has matured over the past several decades; there has been a growing debate about the most appropriate label for departments. Kinesiology, defined as the study of movement and physical activity, has emerged as the most common label.  In American higher

education, the term is used to describe a multifaceted field of study in which movement or physical activity is the intellectual focus.  Kinesiology is a common name for college and university academic departments that include many specialized areas of study in which the causes and consequences of physical activity are examined from different perspectives.?

            

Senate Motion #8 (2001-2002): ?The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal submitted by the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences to change the name of degree programs from B.S. in Exercise Science & Physical Education to B.S. in Kinesiology and from M.S. in Physical Science & Physical Education to M.S. in Kinesiology. Rationale: The B.S. and M.S. degrees are also being changed from B.S. in

Exercise Science & Physical Education to B.S. in Kinesiology, and from M.S. in Exercise Science & Physical Education to M.S. in Kinesiology in order to more adequately reflect the breath and diversity of the disciplinary foundation of the field.?

         

Senate Motion #9 (2001-2002): ?The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal submitted by the Colleges of Liberal Arts and Sciences and Engineering and Applied Sciences for the establishment of a Graduate Certificate in Atmospheric Science.  Rationale: The certificate program will enrich a student?s graduate level exposure to the atmospheric sciences and provide recognition of that exposure.  The professional benefits of such recognition include employment opportunities with state and federal environmental agencies, research laboratories and admission into other graduate programs.  The certificate program will also provide an additional forum for communication between departments involved in atmospheric science research and will enhance the research missions of these departments as well.?
