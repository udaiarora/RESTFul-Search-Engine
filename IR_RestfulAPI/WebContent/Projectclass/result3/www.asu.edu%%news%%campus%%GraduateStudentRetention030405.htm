ASU heads regional effort to retain graduate students

The National Science Foundation (NSF) has awarded ASU a $9 million cooperative agreement to improve the recruitment, retention and graduation of doctoral students in the Sciences, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) program.


Under this award, the STEM program also has been expanded to include smoothing the transition of doctoral students to post-doctoral research and faculty experiences, all the way to eventual continuing appointments with tenure.


The award, from NSF’s Alliances for Graduate Education and the Professoriate (AGEP) program, was made to ASU’s Hispanic Research Center, in conjunction with the College of Technology and Applied Sciences, the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, the Division of Graduate Studies and the Office of the Senior Vice President and Provost.


Under the arrangement, ASU will be the host institution of an alliance of nine regional doctoral-granting institutions.


This new agreement is for a period of five years (2005 – 2010) and represents a nearly fourfold expansion of a highly successful prior NSF AGEP cooperative agreement with ASU totaling $2.5 million from 1999 – 2004.


"The intellectual merit of the alliance is that we will draw upon and expand our alliance’s large existing communities of STEM scholars, researchers, teachers and mentors, and student apprentice scholars, researchers, teachers and protégés,” says the principal investigator for this new agreement, Albert L. McHenry, dean of the College of Technology and Applied Sciences. “We will all work collaboratively to further structure and sustain research-based ‘hothouse’ environments to create and accelerate regional, systemic change in the development of intellectual capital, with dramatically positive national implications and repercussions.”


"The broader impact of our proposed activity is to achieve and sustain a level of admission, development, graduation, placement, promotion and tenure of doctoral, post-doctoral, junior faculty and senior faculty STEM scholars in proportion to the diversity of our society as a whole,” says Antonio A. García, a professor in the Harrington Department of Bioengineering, and the co-principal investigator and associate director of the ASU Hispanic Research Center. “We have strong reason to believe that our AGEP alliance will become a national model for the strengthening and diversification of STEM faculty to ensure that the United States will continue to lead the world in research and technology, despite the increasingly intense global competition in the 21st century.”


Alliance activities include special mentoring, capacity building workshops and conferences, travel and networking to share research experiences, and specialized developmental exposure. Most are unique to this alliance.


For more information, contact project administrator Michael Sullivan of the ASU Hispanic Research Center at (480) 965-5388 or (michael.sullivan@asu.edu).
