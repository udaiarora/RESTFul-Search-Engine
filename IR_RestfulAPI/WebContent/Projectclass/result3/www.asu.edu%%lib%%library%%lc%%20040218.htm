Minutes of Meeting

 

February 18, 2004
Hayden C6A

Meeting was called to order at 3:00 pm.

 

A quorum was present.

Chair: Scott Herrington

Secretary: Brian Doherty

 

I. Approval of Minutes (November 19, 2003)

 

The minutes were approved as read.

 

II. Chair & Chair-Elect’s Report- Scott Herrington

 

 

The Chair announced that the Past-Chair resigned because of a sabbatical. Also announced was the Executive Committee’s appointment of Phil Konomos as the new Chair of the Nominations and Elections Committee.

 

III. Dean’s Report- Kurt Murphy

 

There has been a $1 million budget increase given to the Libraries by President Crow to help alleviate the serials cancellation issue. This increase is expected to be permanent. Despite this there will still be a $300K serials cut next year.

 

The AP Task Force will have an open forum sponsored by CAPS on Tuesday, March 9 from 10:30am – noon. Responses from the preliminary report (available March 30 will be solicited. Kurt mentioned that librarians have the strongest case. Questions about this ensued.

 

It was reported that recruitment for the Associate Dean for Library Services has commenced with a closing date at the end of March. Two Science Reference positions are also in recruitment.

 

IV. Committee Reports

 

a.       Personnel Committee – Lydia Lafaro

The Committee has been working on revisions to the orientation portion of the Handbook (see New Business). Also in progress is consideration of the mentoring program and its current state and re-wording of the sabbatical procedures.

b.       Research and Professional Development Committee – Sue Hunter

A reminder that travel cancellations should be reported to the Committee in order that funds may be reallocated.

c.       Academic Senate – JoAnn Mulvihill

It was reported that President Crow spoke at the last Senate meeting. His comments dealt primarily with finances. The minutes will be available online soon. JoAnn will forward a link to all Library AP’s.

d.    Nominations and Elections Committee- Phil Konomos

 

V. New Business:

 

Lydia Lafaro of the Personnel Committee introduced two items. The first reading of revisions to the Handbook J.1 was made. Comments and suggestions were offered.

 

A discussion of the Mentoring Program ensued. The Personnel Committee will continue work on this.

 

Kurt Murphy addressed the issue of mandatory supplementary evaluations suggesting that the program need to be revitalized. He recommended staggering these evaluations as the current practice does not seem to be systematic. A discussion ensued. It was affirmed that these evaluations will occur every three years, with the date the AP received continuance used as the starting date for the three-year cycle of supplementary reviews.

 

The meeting was adjourned at 3:50pm

 

Brian Doherty

Secretary
