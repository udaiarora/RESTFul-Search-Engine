August 26, 2003

Morrison Institute announces first recipients of the Young Steward of Public Policy Scholarship program

Through a new initiative to engage Arizona's emerging young leaders, the Morrison Institute for Public Policy, in partnership with The Arizona Republic and Tucson Citizen, is offering Arizona high school seniors the opportunity to earn a scholarship award to attend ASU. This prestigious scholarship is designed to encourage Arizona high school seniors to think about public policy issues in Arizona and to promote the concept of "stewardship" - public leadership committed to doing what is best for the state and its residents, regardless of political philosophy or personal gain.

Selected through a highly competitive "blind review" process, the 2003-04 award recipients are: Jennifer Treese, from Santa Rita High School in Tucson, who received the first place award for her essay on "Preventing Teen Pregnancy"; Xuankieu Tran from Alhambra High School in Phoenix, who received the second place award for her essay on "Latino Drop Out Rates and Parental/School Involvement"; and Lucia Bill from Mountain View High School in Mesa, who received an honorable mention award for her essay on "Immigrant Healthcare Issues."

Elaine and Richard Morrison of Gilbert, established this unique program to commemorate the Morrison Institute's 20 years of policy research and to continue to expand the institute's public service as evidence of their devotion to Arizona's future.

Morrison Institute for Public Policy's Board of Advisors plans to fund this special program in 2004 and beyond through the commitment and leadership of current board chair Cathy McKee, senior vice president and director of strategic business services with General Dynamics Decision Systems, and former board chair Ed Fox, vice president of communications, environment and safety with Pinnacle West. Sponsors for the next cycle of the Young Steward of Public Policy Scholarship Program are Salt River Project, General Dynamics Decision Systems and Pinnacle West.

The first place award recipient receives a $1,500 scholarship to ASU; the second place award recipient receives a $1,000 scholarship to ASU. Young Steward awards are not based on financial need, academic achievement or program of study. However, awards are only given to students who attend ASU.

Young Steward of Public Policy Scholarship awards are based on an outstanding essay about a public issue of critical importance to the State of Arizona and the specific policy recommendations on how the issue should be addressed.

To read the recognized essays or for information about how to apply to the 2004 - 05 Young Steward program visit (www.morrisoninstitute.org) or call the Morrison Institute at (480) 965-4525.

Leland, with the Morrison Institute, can be reached at (480) 965-4525 or (karen.leland@asu.edu).
