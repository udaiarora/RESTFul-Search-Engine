LOCATION

Canoa Yacu Amazonian Kichwa Community on the banks of the Napo River, a tributary of the Amazon, and the most biodiversed region of the world. Here students are immersed in Kichwa life. Camp in virgin rainforest. Listen to the singing of ayahuasca shamans, clear forest with machetes to plant manioc, and much more. Other destinations include Quito and Baños.

ACADEMIC PROGRAM

For undergraduates the program offers a chance to fulfill general education requirements in science, humanities, global engagement or language requirements in Spanish. it prepares graduate students for serious research on indigenous culture and provides practical training in contemporary field work on Andean/Amazonian culture. All students will enroll for at least 1 and not more than 4 credits.

FACULTY

Professor Tod Swanson is Director of ASU?s Center for Latin American Research and a leading interpreter of Amazonian culture and environment. Swanson was raised in the Ecuadorian Amazon as the son of missionary parents and is fluent in Spanish and Kichwa. During the summer months he resides in the Ecuadorian Amazon with his wife and four children where he has hosted student groups every year since 1997. His specialty is Kichwa religious history. Kichwa Language Instructor: Luz María de la Torre Amaguaña holds an M.A. in Political Science and is a native speaker of Otavalan Kichwa. She has taught Kichwa at the Pontíficia Universidad Católica del Ecuador, Universidad San Francisco de Quito, and most recently at Arizona State University. She has also served as an advisor to the Ecuadorian Ministry of Foreign Relations Nina Pacari.

LIVING ACCOMMODATIONS

Double occupancy, thatched roofs, bamboo walls and verandas while in the indigenous communities. Hostels elsewhere. American-style meals alternate with opportunities to sample native cuisine. Vegetarian meals are available.

ELIGIBILITY

Open to adventurous students and alumni from any university. To reserve a place submit a completed application with the $100 non-refundable application fee by October 30, 2006. Applications accepted after the due date until the spaces are filled.

The program may allow for time which can be spent in non-Program activites. Participants may wish to travel, sightsee or participate in activities or events in which they have a personal interest. Please not that the participant is fully responsible for making arrangements and for all costs of transportation, lodging, food and additional expenses that may be associated with any non-Program activities.
