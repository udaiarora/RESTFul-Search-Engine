Hiring Process Report Procedures 

Effective immediately, a new employee hired with a Hiring Process Report (HPR) for employee types:

 

A - Adjunct Faculty
E - Administrative Staff
F - Faculty
M - Multi-Year
P - Academic Professional
S - Classified Staff

Will be processed as follows

  • Department will send HPR to HR-Customer Service Center immediately after job acceptance (please process HPR with employee's full name, as it appears on their social security card or passport). Please submit using new HPR form.
  • If employee is new to campus (never attended ASU as a student), HR will assign an ASU ID # for this employee (employee will be entered under this id #, and not their social security #). Otherwise, HR will use the id # already assigned to individual.
  • HR will enter Screens 3 and 4 for newhire based on information from the HPR. In addition, HR will enter a partial screen. 5 with HR's address and a dummy date of birth = 1/1/1905. (This information is needed to allow HRMS to interface with the other systems on campus.)
  • Employee can self-subscribe for as ASURITE user id after 2 days (once other systems are updated).
  • PLEASE NOTE, EMPLOYEE WILL NOT RECEIVE PAY UNLESS HR RECEIVES THE NEW EMPLOYEE PAYROLL PACKET! Once packet is received, HR will update screen 5 with corrected address and date of birth information.

Grad students will be entered in HRMS when both HPR and completed new employee payroll packet are received in HR-Customer Service Center. (Please check screen 5 in HRMS for a complete screen setup - if employee is set up with HR address and date of birth = 1/1/1905, employee has NOT completed new employee payroll packet and will NOT get paid until they do so. Some grad students were entered as part of our testing of this new process, and do not have a complete HRMS record in order to receive pay.)

So help us, help your employees - send your HPRs in as soon as the employee accepts the position. This will give HR time to set their record up in HRMS, and flow through the university's computer systems to allow them access to the services they need, on their first day of employment. Thank you!
