November 14, 2003

ASU, Tempe to host final 2004 debate

The eyes and ears of the nation will turn to ASU and Tempe as Gammage Auditorium hosts the final presidential debate Oct. 13, 2004.

In reaction, Arizona Gov. Janet Napolitano said the university and Tempe had landed the "Super Bowl of Politics" by getting the debate. She also said hosting the debate will help make Arizona a bellwether for the coming national election.

"It's not by accident that the commission selected university sites for these historic debates because of our principle focus on free and open speech, our focus on learning, our focus on discourse," said ASU President Michael Crow. "It's for that reason that we are tremendously excited that ASU will be able to serve as the host for this debate. This is the kind of thing that represents the best our democracy has to offer."

Tempe Mayor and ASU Director of Community Relations Neil Giuliano was ecstatic that ASU has been chosen as a host site.

"Tempe and ASU have worked hard to attract the debate and the fact that it will be the final debate preceding the election makes the event even more important," he said. "It draws the attention of the world and the nation to Arizona, Tempe and ASU. ASU is the largest university campus and Tempe the first non-California city in the western United States to ever host a presidential debate."

Paul G. Kirk, Jr., and Frank J. Fahrenkopf, Jr., co-chairman of the Commission on Presidential Debates (CPD), said the sites of four debates (three presidential and one vice presidential) were chosen from 14 formal proposals submitted to the CPD last March.

The other debates will be Sept. 30, at the University of Miami in Coral Gables, Fla., and Oct. 8, at Washington University in St. Louis. The vice presidential debate will be held Oct. 5 at Case Western Reserve University in Cleveland.

Members of the university community and the general public are expressing great interest in attending the debate, but Giuliano said ticket arrangements have not been completed.

"The Commission on Presidential Debates has the full responsibility to determine and coordinate all issues with regard to tickets for the actual debate," he explained. "These decisions are made once the format of the debate is agreed upon. Further information regarding tickets will be available from the commission in September 2004."

Giuliano added that a Presidential Debate at ASU Coordinating Committee would be appointed soon to begin discussing ideas to engage the students, faculty and community in a variety of events and activities leading up to the debate on Oct. 13. Ideas include an MTV Rock the Vote concert the weekend prior to the debate and academic events.

"I would encourage every academic area to think of how their field connects to the policies of our federal government," he said. "Electing a U.S. president impacts every area of life in some way, and ASU will play a significant role in the history of the 2004 election. This is an event that will spread far beyond the confines of Gammage and the boundaries of the university."

Security is expected to be the tightest ever for an event in Arizona, involving ASU, the City of Tempe and other local law enforcement agencies. The U.S. Secret Service will coordinate security for the actual debate.

The Tempe Mission Palms Hotel will serve as the headquarters hotel for the CPD, which will have more than 100 staff on-site for up to a week prior to the event. The Buttes, Arizona Biltmore and other Tempe, Phoenix and Scottsdale hotels will be designated to host the political party delegations and over 2,000 members of the national and international media. The CPD is the non-partisan, non-profit, tax-exempt 501(c)(3) corporation that has sponsored presidential and vice presidential debates since 1988. The CPD Web site is (www.debates.org).
