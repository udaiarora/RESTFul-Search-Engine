March 13, 2003

Greater Phoenix resale market continues strong in February

The Greater Phoenix resale housing market showed no signs of a slowdown in February, with 4,095 homes recorded sold - only slightly below the 4,220 homes record the previous month, but ahead of last year's 3,970 sales. This year to date, a total of 8,315 homes have been recorded sold, well ahead of the 7,314 homes for slow-starting 2002. The median home price fell slightly to $147,500 in February from $147,950 the previous month, but remained well ahead of last year's $139,900.

In Greater Phoenix and throughout the country, the strength of the 2002 housing market (both in the number of sales and the strong appreciation in prices) was one of the few bright points for the economy. "Concerns have been raised about the ability of the market to sustain this strength through 2003," said Jay Q. Butler, director of the Arizona Real Estate Center. "However, even amid a struggling economy and mounting concerns about war, people appear to remain confident about the long-term expectations for the housing market."

The current recorded sales activity represents decisions made several months ago. "For last several months, the housing market has been reviewed positively as to the benefits of the low interest rates, rapid increase in prices and the investment opportunities," Butler said. "If the recovery continues to be delayed and the war with Iraq become reality, it will be interesting to see if the market realizes these expectations."

While the resale home market improved, the resale townhouse/condominium housing sector actually fell from 885 sales in January to 870 sales in February, and is well off the 970 sales recorded sold a year ago. The median sales price was $100,000, while it was $94,500 a year ago.

The median square footage for a single-family home recorded sold in February was 1,635, larger than the 1,600 square feet reported a year ago. In the townhouse/condominium sector the median square footage also increased - 1,180 in February compared to 1,160 a year ago.

· In comparison with February 2002, recorded sales in the city of Phoenix slipped slightly from 1,190 to 1,180, while the median sales price rose from $115,000 to $122,900. In the townhouse/condominium sector, sales activity decreased from 275 to 205, while the median price rose from $83,000 to $95,500.

· The Scottsdale resale home market improved marginally from last year's 415 recorded sales to 420. The median sales price continued its upward trend, increasing from $265,000 to $300,000. The townhouse/condominium sector in Scottsdale was stable at 195 sales, while the median price rose from $148,000 to $150,000.

· The Mesa resale housing market improved from 470 to 505 recorded sales, while the median price increased from $128,750 to $135,900. The townhouse/condominium sector fell from 140 to 120 sales, while the median home price increased from $80,250 to $90,500.


· Glendale was stable at 365 recorded sales, and the median sales price increased from $130,950 to $135,000. The townhouse/condominium sector remained at 40 sales, while the median price fell from $79,970 to $67,000.

· In comparison with a year ago, the Sun City resale market decreased from 115 to 105 sales, with the median sales price rising from $96,250 to $107,900. Resale activity in Sun City West increased from 90 to 105 recorded sales and the median price increased from $124,000 to $150,000. The townhouse/condominium market in Sun City fell from 100 to 75 sales, but the median price increased from $75,000 to $77,000. In Sun City West, activity remained stable at 30 sales, but the median sales price rose from $90,500 to $102,500

· The resale market in Gilbert improved slightly from 215 to 225 sales, while the median sales price rose from $154,500 to $170,765. The townhouse/condominium market improved from 15 to 20 sales, and the median price increased from $108,000 to $113,450.

· For the city of Chandler, the resale market moved from 330 to 345 recorded sales, while the median sales price increased from $146,000 to $156,900. The townhouse/condominium market improved from 35 to 45 sales and the median sales price dropped from $102,500 to $98,900.

· The resale market in Tempe was stable at 115 sales, with the median sales price increasing to $159,900 from $149,900. The townhouse/condominium sector improved slightly from 45 to 50 sales, while the median sales price increased from $99,500 to $100,250.

· The highest median sales price was in Paradise Valley at $801,260, with a median square footage of 3,650.

· In the West Valley, the four communities of Avondale (35 sales at $127,500), El Mirage (30 sales at $108,350), Goodyear (45 sales at $151,000), and Surprise (95 sales at $140,500) accounted for 5 percent of the resale market compared to 4 percent a year ago.
