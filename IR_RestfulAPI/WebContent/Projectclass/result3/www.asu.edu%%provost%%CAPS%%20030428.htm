Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting (Spring Forum)

April 28, 2003, MU Alumni Lounge

Dr Gail Hackett, Vice Provost for Academic Personnel, addressed a gathering of   80  academic professionals and then took questions from the floor. CAPS received a large number of questions from AP members prior to the meeting. The answers to some were incorporated into her remarks and covered during the subsequent question and answer period. Dr. Hackett agreed to prepare answers to the remaining questions and make them available to all AP?s. These questions and answers will appear on the CAPS website.

Dr Hackett  addressed two issues initially: the status of the  imminent revision of ACD 506 and 507 (now before the Academic Senate) and the events of past year  pertaining to the disruption of the faculty awards of tenure and academic professional awards of continuing status.

ACD 506/507 Revisions ? Various faculty task forces and Academic Senate subcommittees have been working on this for three years. Now the proposed revisions are before the Academic Senate for their endorsement.  In essence, all references that link faculty and academic professionals in these two sections have been ?uncoupled?. The sections pertaining to academic professionals have been extracted. They have supposedly been moved to a different part of ACD and  renumbered but as of the date of this meeting, this is not the case.  The academic professionals either as a body,  or through CAPS , have not been informed of where exactly these sections have been placed, or why the complete revision of ACD 506/507 does not include the new location/numbering of the academic professional provisions.  According to Dr. Hackett, the plan has always been to complete the review of faculty promotion and tenure policies and procedures and then move to a similar review of academic professional continuing status policies and procedures.  The abrupt decision by Dr Crow to decline the award of continuing status to those candidates who applied during the last cycle has accelerated the process that it took the faculty three years to complete.

Recent Events -  Dr. Crow is now personally making  the ultimate  tenure/continuing status decision. In the process of reviewing the files  presented for a continuing status decision, Dr. Crow found   that he   had many   questions concerning  the process and documentation of those files  that could not be resolved given the existing timetable for this type of review and decision.  Departmental appointment letters, criteria and review procedures varied from college to college. He has stated that the documentation in support of the particular candidates was in some cases not as fully developed as he expected and in many cases did not serve the candidate?s record. He believes the evidence that candidates present   in support of the award of continuing appointment must be overwhelming before he will grant continuing status.

Therefore, he ?decoupled? the decisions for promotion and continuing status for this group of candidates.  Dr. Hackett emphasized that no continuing status candidates received terminal notifications.

On a more fundamental level, Dr Crow asserts that a close reading of the ABOR policies and ACD policies demonstrates that the University policies ?go much further? than the ABOR policies possibly intended to go. His view is that the standard of excellence stated in the ABOR policies is simply not reflected in the current University policies and procedures memorialized in the ACD manual.

Dr. Crow has charged Dr. Hackett with the responsibility of conducting a thorough review of   academic professional status and the policies and procedures that govern their classification, appointment, evaluation and promotion. Furthermore, he has asked Dr. Hackett to investigate alternative employment options other than continuing status track.

The ramifications of both these issues are to be addressed by a Task Force that Dr Hackett will  select and convene in short order. Members of the Committee on Academic Professional Status have been asked to transmit nominations for this Task Force to Dr. Hackett   and this has been done.  Track and non-track academic professionals representing a cross section of the academic professional ranks are to be selected.  Some of the issues  the  Task Force will address include: clarification of the AP category (why is continuing status needed? When and for whom is it needed?); investigation of multiple options for employment other than continuing status; whether incumbent A Ps are properly categorized; revision of the continuing status ?clock?, documentation and evaluation criteria; revision of the evaluation solicitation letter; revision of  employment offer letters; whether continuing status is depressing salaries.

Question Period: Time did not permit the full discussion of all questions posed by AP?s in advance of the Spring Forum [see CAPS website for complete list]. Issues concerning year to year AP employees such as Academic Associates for example were not fully addressed. Affected AP employees expecting to either reapply for continuing status during the next year may have the  benefit of consulting with their Deans  who received some feedback from Dr Crow during the course of the reviews this past year. Those AP employees who expect to present their applications during the next cycle may possibly have the benefit of the result of the Task Force work product.

Questions were also raised about whether the President's office had worked with ASU Legal Counsel in determining the outcome of this year's continuing appointment reviews.  One participant commented that the Board of Regent's Policy Manual (6-301, part D) states that the President may not extend an Academic Professional's probationary appointment beyond 7 years; and Dr. Hackett stated that she would look into this.

There was some discussion about the process by which revised ACD policies affecting academic professionals would be promulgated and adopted.  How would AP?s be involved ? Would AP employees vote on proposed revisions? Would the Academic Senate review and approve the proposed revisions as they are doing for the faculty tenure and promotion revisions? Specific answers to these questions are somewhat unclear.
