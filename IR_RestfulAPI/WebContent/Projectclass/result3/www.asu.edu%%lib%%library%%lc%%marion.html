Marion the Librarian Sample Packets Introduction


The Personnel Committee has prepared sample packets to illustrate some of the various options available to librarians for effectively presenting review packets beginning with the 2nd year probationary review through promotion to full. These packets demonstrate progression in the career of a fictional librarian. While Marion's activities are a reflection of the time frame, the arrangement of the packets are in accordance with the current criteria as of 2001.


First of all, there is no one way to design your packet, but there are certain rules that help to make the presentation more clear to readers and to emphasize what is most important in the packet. Everything is not of equal importance. Be sure that you have given emphasis to those items that are most important so that they stand out to a first-time reader. It is the responsibility of the Academic Professional to clearly establish the significance of all activities. Strive for clarity and readability.


Once you adopt a particular style, stick with it throughout your documentation. Don't attempt to use several styles and type fonts within the same document. It is wise to choose a type font that is easily read, such as Times New Roman or Arial, no smaller than 10 point.


A close reading of sections E-G in the Librarians' Handbook is essential for understanding content of the curriculum vita (CV) and letter. Prior to submitting a packet it may be beneficial to seek advice on the style and content from colleagues and the Primary Evaluator (PE). If you have questions about the sample packets, please contact the Assistant Dean for Personnel.
