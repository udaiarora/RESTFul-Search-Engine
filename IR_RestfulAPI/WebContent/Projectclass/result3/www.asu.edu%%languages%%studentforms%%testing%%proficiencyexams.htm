The Department of Languages & Literatures Policy for Language Proficiency Exams

Purpose: Satisfy a student’s college foreign language graduation requirement by passing a 4th semester (intermediate level) language exam with a grade of “C” or better.

Information about Language Proficiency Exams:

  • Exam format varies by language. All have writing and reading sections and some also have listening and verbal portions.
  • Only ONE opportunity is provided to test out of the language requirement and only current ASU students may take an exam.
  • If a grade of “C” or better is not earned on the Proficiency Exam, you must register for the appropriate lower level language course to meet the foreign language graduation requirement.
  • There is no fee for exams that ASU can provide and grade, but some exams must be ordered through BYU which charges a $40 fee for this service. Exams for less common languages are usually only available through BYU. If your language cannot be tested through ASU or BYU, an authority in the language must be located to provide/grade the exam.
  • Exams are arranged after the first week of classes in a semester, up until 2 weeks before the last day of classes. No language testing is done during the summer. Exams are scheduled a week or more in advance and will not be scheduled after the deadlines. PLAN AHEAD.
  • Students who wish to test in Portuguese and Romanian must pass the 4th semester language exams in these languages, which at ASU are POR 314 and ROM 314.
  • Native speakers (a person with their secondary education in the language to be tested) may not need to take an exam. Please contact International Admissions with questions. Their office is located on the 1st floor of the Student Services Building, or call: 480-965-2688.

How to take a Spanish, French or German Proficiency Exam:

  • Before taking the proficiency exam, you may take a free, online placement test. This is NOT the proficiency exam, but a 20 minute test to help you gauge your ability level before you take the proficiency exam. You can find the test here: http://www.asu.edu/clas/dll/placement/undergrad.html.
  • Spanish, French and German exams can be taken in the language lab in LL65. The lab is located in the basement of Lang. & Lit. Lab hours are: M-TH: 7:30 a.m.-8:30 p.m. & F 7:30 a.m.-4:30 p.m. Tests can be taken anytime the lab is open- but must be taken only after the first week of classes in a semester up until 2 weeks before the last day of classes. No language testing is done in the summer.
  • To take an exam, first print and complete the application located at the top of this page. Then go to LL 65 and ask the lab assistant to sign you in. There are three parts to the exam: Listening Comprehension, Reading Comprehension and Writing. Each part may take 50-60 minutes to complete. You may take each part on separate days if you wish.
  • When you have completed the exam, come upstairs to LL440 and give your Language Proficiency Exam Application to the front desk of the Department of Languages & Literatures. If you forget to turn it in we have no way of contacting you with the results. Results will take at least 2 weeks to receive and will come in the form of a letter to the address you listed on the application. Your college will automatically be notified of the results so it can update your D.A.R.S. if you pass.

How to take a Proficiency Exam for a Language that is NOT Spanish, French or German:

  • Fill out the Language Proficiency Examination Application Form located at the top of this page.
  • Submit the form to LL 440 (Department of Languages & Literatures main office).
  • The Testing Coordinator will contact you to arrange the test.
  • Once you take the exam, you will receive a letter confirming the results and the results will be forwarded to your College and/or advisor to update your D.A.R.S. report.

For further information, call the Testing Coordinator at 480-965-1781 or stop by the Languages and Literatures Department (LL 440).
