Frequently Asked Questions

 

What happened to the idea I submitted in April?
We received nearly 100 ideas from a broad range of faculty and staff. These ideas reflected creative and diverse thinking about entrepreneurship at ASU, and together they served as the base for the preliminary proposal (Adobe Acrobat Document) that has been sent to the Kauffman Foundation. Moving forward, we are engaging as many people as appropriate from those who submitted ideas to develop the initiative into a full plan that we will submit in November.

 

Does the vision of the Kauffman Campuses Initiative imply that universities are not entrepreneurs?
The Kauffman Foundation understands that universities have strayed from their engagement in entrepreneurial work, while recognizing that universities have the potential to be powerful entrepreneurs.

 

What are the impediments to creating this culture of entrepreneurship at the university?
During this process we will be identifying the impediments – as a result of culture, tradition, bureaucracy, or other university characteristics – so we can then eliminate these barriers.

 

Through this initiative, can we fund international student initiatives?
Funds through the Kauffman Campuses Initiative are intended for national use. However, we can use this opportunity to further develop our relationships with international universities, such as in Singapore, Ireland, and Mexico.

 

What other institutions are engaged in conversation with the Kauffman Foundation regarding the second round of the Kauffman Campuses Initiative?
Other universities beginning this process include Georgetown, Brown, the University of North Carolina system, the University of Wisconsin – Madison, Syracuse, Purdue, and Carnegie Mellon.

 

What is the timeframe of the potential grant opportunity with the Kauffman Campuses Initiative?
The investment would be for five years, with opportunity for continued guidance from the Kauffman Foundation thereafter.

 

What is the confidentiality status of this information?
Please share the information on the website liberally with colleagues, yet with sensitivity that we are still in the process of developing a proposal and that there has not yet been a formal announcement of funds received.

 

What information can we share with external entities and how might this information be useful to our efforts?
Please share as appropriate our definition of entrepreneurship as well as our vision for this initiative. Recognition by the Kauffman Foundation as an entrepreneurial campus would increase our visibility as a knowledge entrepreneur within the framework of the New American University.
