Music Library Research Guide


The Music Library maintains a separate print collection of reference materials, including encyclopedias, dictionaries, bibliographies, discographies, catalogs, and directories. Reference assistance is available online or in-person (see Ask a Librarian).


ASU Local Databases

  • Song Index         view tutorial (Internet Explorer required)

    The Song Index contains popular, folk, and art songs found in collections of songs owned by the Music Library. Each entry shows the title of the song, the composer, lyricist, first line, and the collection in which it is found.

  • ASU Performance Index

    The ASU Performance Index provides access to performances in the ASU School of Music from 1985 to the present. Included are recitals and concerts given by graduate students, faculty and large ensembles. The recordings are housed in the Music library. Each entry contains the performer, date of performance (ex: 1990/2-3), medium of performance (clarinet, piano, orchestra), and the music performed.

  • ASU Doctoral Dissertations: Current Research

    A searchable database for full text, citations and abstracts of ASU dissertations and theses.


Reference

  • Grove Music Online

    The most authoritative and extensive English language reference work on all aspects of music. It includes the full text of The New Grove Dictionary of Music and Musicians, The New Grove Dictionary of Opera and The New Grove Dictionary of Jazz. Grove Music Online undergoes regular revision and includes audio musical examples.

  • Naxos Music Library (Streaming Audio)

    Naxos Music Library, an online music listening service, is a collection of digitized sound recording files. It includes the complete Naxos, Marco Polo and Da Capo catalogues of over 85,000 tracks, including Classical music, Jazz, World, Folk and Chinese music. While listening, you can read notes on the works being played as well as biographical information on composers or artists.

  • OCLC FirstSearch WorldCat

    Books and other materials in libraries worldwide. Includes bibliographic records for any type of material cataloged by OCLC member libraries.


Journal Indexes

  • International Index to Music Periodicals, Full Text Edition (IIMP)

    Covers a comprehensive range of subject areas in both scholarly and popular music journals from over 430 international music periodicals, with over 110 full text titles. Also indexes feature music articles and obituaries from The New York Times and The Washington Post. Provides title list for periodicals included.

  • RILM Abstracts of Music Literature

    Over 405,365 citations to articles, books, conference proceedings, bibliographies, dissertations, film, videos, etc. indexed by the Repertoire International de Litterature Musicale. Coverage is international and spans 1967 to present.

  • Music Index Online        view tutorial (Internet Explorer required)

    Indexes journal articles, obituaries, book and music reviews, for both classical and popular music. Covers over 775 international music periodicals from 1976-present. (Printed volumes of the Music Index, covering 1949 to 2001, are available on the Index Table in the Music Reference area)


Other Online Databases

  • Music-In-Print

    The Music-In-Print Series include online searchable databases of published music in print such as Sacred Choral Music In Print, Piano Music In Print, Organ Music In Print, Band Music In Print, Guitar Music In Print, etc.

  • Doctoral Dissertations in Musicology

    An international database of bibliographic records for completed dissertations and new dissertation topics in the fields of musicology, music theory, and ethnomusicology, as well as in related musical, scientific, and humanistic disciplines.

  • Musical America.com

    An online version of the Musical America Directory. Also includes current music industry news, reviews, etc.


Databases Which Include Music Material

ASU Libraries provide access to many electronic databases for a broad range of subject areas.

  • ArtStor Digital Library

    Provides access to digital images in multidisciplinary topics, including portraits of composers and performers, historic musical instruments, paintings and photos of ballet and opera sets, as well as photos of historical performance venues, etc.

  • JSTOR, Electronic Journals Collection

    Includes archival backfiles of scholarly journals, including many music titles.

  • Project Muse

    Provides access to the full text of over 300 journals from 60 scholarly publishers in the humanities, arts, social sciences, and mathematics.

  • International Index to the Performing Arts

    Indexes more than 241 scholarly and popular performing arts periodicals.

  • DOAJ, the Directory of Open Access Journals

    Directory which provides free, full text, quality controlled scientific and scholarly journals.

  • Biography and Geneology Master Index

    Biographical information.

  • Dissertation Abstracts International

    Dissertations and theses.

  • EBSCOhost Academic Search Premier

    Popular magazines and scholarly journals.

  • LEXIS/NEXIS Academic Universe

    Newspapers and scholarly journals.

  • Periodical Contents Index

    Popular magazines and scholarly journals.

  • ERIC

    Music education.

  • Education Abstracts

    Music education.

  • Ebrary

    Over 22,000 full text books, journals and maps.

  • NetLibrary

    Over 13,000 full text books from many leading publishers and scholarly presses.
