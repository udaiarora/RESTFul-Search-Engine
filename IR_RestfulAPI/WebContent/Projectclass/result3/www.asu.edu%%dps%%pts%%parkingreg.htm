I. GENERAL

A. Introduction

Parking and Transit Services (PTS) is a self-supporting, service organization dedicated to balancing the competing parking needs of a diverse, public, metropolitan research university.

These parking and vehicle control regulations are published and enforced to facilitate the safe and orderly operation of motor vehicles on campus.

Everyone is encouraged to support travel reduction measures by using public and university transit, carpooling, bicycling, or walking whenever possible.

If you choose to drive and park at Arizona State University (ASU) it is your responsibility to read and fully understand these regulations. Lack of knowledge of these regulations will not be accepted as grounds for dismissing citations.

If you have parking questions, please contact ASU Parking and Transit Services at (480) 965-6124, Monday - Friday from 7:30 a.m. until 5:00 p.m. (M.S.T.)

B. Authority

1. By authority of Section 15-1627, Arizona Revised Statutes, as amended, the Associate Vice President of Administration has general responsibility for approving and adopting the following rules and regulations for the control of vehicles on all property owned and/or managed by ASU. The Arizona Board of Regents (ABOR) intends that ASU Parking and Transit Services shall be financially self-supporting through receipt of fees from all persons who park at ASU.

2. The Director of Parking and Transit Services, or his/her designee, is responsible for assigning parking areas on the campus; assessing and collecting fees and fines; appointing members of the Parking Citations Appeals Board; and adopting hearing and appeal procedures compatible with these regulations. Furthermore, the Director, or his/her designee, is authorized to establish any necessary fees for limited special access in order to facilitate the conduct of ASU business.

3. The Director of Parking and Transit Services, or his/her designee, may exercise discretion to deal with special circumstances not covered by these regulations.

II. DEFINITIONS

Unless the context dictates otherwise, the following definitions apply to these regulations:

A. Decal, Permit or Parking Permit: An original graphic design or label created annually and issued by ASU/PTS authorizing a person parking privileges on ASU property in a designated lot or area.

B. Disabled Stall: Parking space marked by blue curb, sign, and/or international disabled symbol.

C. Holidays: Ten (10) designated calendar dates when entire University is closed; Does NOT include class breaks i.e. Spring Break.

D. Impoundment: Immobilizing (booting) or towing a vehicle for violation of these regulations.

E. Impoundment fee: Monetary charge imposed by ASU for impoundment in addition to towing service and storage charge.

F. Gate Access Card: A card issued by ASU/PTS to decal holders for access into an assigned gate-controlled parking lot. It may be used only once in a 15 minute interval and will not allow access into any other lot.

G. Parking: A vehicle that has ceased to move whether attended or unattended.

H. Parking Stall: Space marked by white or yellow painted parallel lines for parking a vehicle.

I. PTS: Parking and Transit Services.

J. Visitors: Persons not affiliated with ASU.

III . PARKING

A. Registration

1. Requirements. Everyone who parks or expects to park a vehicle on the ASU campus, either regularly or occasionally, must register each vehicle with PTS by purchasing and displaying a valid ASU Parking Decal while the vehicle is on campus. Only one valid decal may be displayed at a time on a vehicle. Registration must occur before a vehicle is parked in any ASU Decal lot.

a. Necessary paperwork;

• Correct License Plate information;
• Completed Parking Decal Application:
• Photo I.D.;and
• Acceptable form of payment.

b. SIGNING A PARKING DECAL APPLICATION FORM AND/OR OBTAINING A DECAL CERTIFIES THAT THE INDIVIDUAL AGREES TO OBTAIN, READ, AND COMPLY WITH ARIZONA VEHICLE EMISSION LAWS AND THE CURRENT PARKING AND VEHICLE CONTROL REGULATIONS. DECALS REMAIN THE PROPERTY OF ASU, PARKING AND TRANSIT SERVICES, AND MAY NOT BE REASSIGNED, REPRODUCED OR SOLD FOR ANY REASON BY AN INDIVIDUAL. PARKING PRIVILEGES ARE REVOCABLE AT ANY TIME.

c.  It is the responsibility of the individual who has changed vehicles and/or license plates after initial registration to promptly submit the change of vehicle license number to Parking and Transit Services. All vehicles/license numbers must be registered to this decal at Parking and Transit Services PRIOR to the decal being displayed on the vehicle. These changes may be made by fax (727-9200), e-mail (parksvcs@asu.edu), U.S. mail, or in person at the Decal Sales Office, accompanied by a Photo I.D. Decal holders can also add vehicle information by using the Parking Online Services at www.asu.edu/dps/pts

d. FOR YOUR OWN PROTECTION, please advise your State Motor Vehicle Division of every vehicle sale or trade to avoid continued responsibility for subsequent citations issued to the sold/traded vehicle. This commonly can be done by completing the back of your state vehicle registration form and mailing it to the address specified.

2. Duration of Decals.  All parking decals are valid from August 1, 2005, or the date of purchase, whichever is later, to August 15, 2006. Fees for decals purchased after September 6, 2005 will be prorated.

3. Faculty and Staff

a. Payroll Deduction. You are responsible for all payroll deductions and/or payments from the date of purchase UP TO AND INCLUDING THE DATE YOUR DECAL (and Gate Access Card, if applicable) IS RETURNED to PTS. If you terminate employment, you should immediately return the decal. Termination or Leave does NOT release you from your responsibility for payment.

b. Out-of-State License Plates. In accordance with ARS 28-2001, a faculty or staff member with non-Arizona license plates will NOT be issued a regular decal, unless he/she is a member of the active military service assigned to the Department of Military Science or the Department of Aerospace Studies. Visiting faculty members NOT paid by ASU, with non-Arizona license plates, may purchase a regular decal(s) after proof of compliance with Arizona emission standards. Temporary parking decals are available for a fee to faculty or staff members until Arizona plates are obtained.

1. Parking Options

a. Contractors. Temporary parking decals shall be purchased from Parking and Transit Services, Decal Sales Office. Rates will vary depending on location. Annual parking decals are also available.
b. Dealer Plates. In accordance with ARS 28-4532 dealer plates cannot be registered to a parking decal.

c. Disabled. Signed parking stalls for disabled parking are available in most lots. Vehicles displaying a valid ASU Disabled Parking Decal must park only in the appropriate signed Disabled stall in the parking lots. Visitors displaying a disabled placard or plate may park in disabled visitor stalls and pay the appropriate parking fees. Please notify ASU Parking and Transit Dispatch Office at (480) 965-0641 when a disabled space is occupied by an unauthorized vehicle. An ASU Disabled Decal or placard is for the exclusive use of the disabled driver and subject to revocation if used by others.

Conversion hangers cost an additional $10. Motorcycles under 50cc are not authorized for use on public streets. This type of motorcycle must use bicycle lanes and park in bike racks on Campus.

IF AN INDIVIDUAL MOVES OUT OF A RESIDENCE HALL, THE RESIDENT DECAL MUST BE EXCHANGED FOR A NON-RESIDENT DECAL. RESIDENT DECALS MAY NOT BE RENEWED. Towers residents may purchase PSR-6 Decals at Parking and Transit Services when registering their vehicles. A current Towers Rental Agreement must be presented at time of decal purchase.

g. Temporary Permits. Faculty, staff and students possessing a valid ASU Parking Decal, and whose vehicle becomes inoperable, may obtain a FREE ASU Temporary Parking Permit for a loaner vehicle for no more than two working weeks. A temporary permit will authorize parking in the same lot that the regular decal authorizes. Temporary permits for non-permit holders are available for Lot 59 for a specified fee.

h. Vendors. Vendors may use visitor parking at the posted rate or purchase an annual vendor permit from Parking and Transit Services. Free Loading zones (yellow curbs) are available adjacent to most buildings for short term (20 minute) loading and unloading of equipment.

(1) Visitor Responsibility. Arizona State University welcomes visitors to the campus. Residence Hall visitors must display a Temporary Visitor Permit purchased from PTS to park in Residence Hall Lots (where space is available). Visitors who violate ASU regulations are subject to citation. Visitors who habitually violate these regulations are subject to vehicle impoundment.

(2) Metered Parking. One (1) Hour Maximum. Metered parking is intended for use by short-term visitors. Metered stalls may be used by decal holders who pay the appropriate fee. (3) Visitor Lots (Cash Only). Visitor Lots 16, 17W, 42 and 45 are cash only pay-box areas. See posted rates and enforcement hours at each location

Decal fees are refundable between the day of the purchase and April 30, 2006, provided that the decal or the identifiable remnants (i.e. 2 numbers) and gate access card (if applicable) is returned to Parking and Transit Services. No refunds will be made after April 30, 2006. Refunds will be prorated based on the day the decal or the identifiable remnants are received by the Decal Sales Office. Decal Sales does not issue cash refunds. Refunds are issued via a refund voucher form, which can be redeemed at Cashiering Services. 6. Decal Removals/Cancellations. Parking decals shall be removed from a motor vehicle under the following circumstances and returned to PTS.

a. change in vehicle ownership:
b. decal expiration;
c. receipt of a new decal for the same time period;
d. leave of absence from ASU or any situation that stops/interrupts pay payroll deduction for parking decal(s);

Arizona State University makes the following reasonable presumptions:

1. The person(s) obtaining a valid ASU Decal is responsible for all citations issued to each vehicle registered to that decal or any vehicle displaying that decal.

2. If anyone with the same address as the registered owner(s) is determined to be associated with the University then that person is presumed to be the operator of the vehicle receiving a parking citation and is responsible for said citation and /or;

3. The registered owner of a vehicle will be responsible for all citations issued to that vehicle if no other person at that address is determined to be associated with the University.

D. Notice

Signs posted at all lots and reserved spaces constitute notice that parking is controlled by these regulations and warning that violators shall be subject to sanctions. Parking in violation of these regulations will result in impoundment of the vehicle. The vehicle will be released only after payment of the towing service charge, storage, and impoundment fee, as well as any outstanding parking citations due to ASU Parking and Transit Services. Photo ID and proof of vehicle ownership are required.

E. Additional Penalties and Sanctions

Violators of parking regulations, including those who avoid or neglect to pay outstanding parking fines, shall be subject to additional penalties and sanctions including, but not limiting to: Code of Conduct Sanctions, oral or written reprimand, disciplinary probation, loss of ASU privileges (including parking), suspension from ASU, encumbrance of academic records, salary withholding, vehicle impoundment, or termination of employment.

V. CITATION PAYMENT PROCESS

A. Process

 All citations are due and payable upon issue. Late charges may apply for payments not made in a timely manner. If desired, a written appeal may be filed with the Appeals Section, Parking and Transit Services within fourteen (14) calendar days from the date the citation was issued, exclusive of the day of issuance. If the citation is successfully appealed, a refund will be issued.

B. Payment

All fines shall be paid at Parking and Transit Services weekdays between 7:30 a.m. - 4:30 p.m. Payment may be made in person, by mail or via Online Parking Services at www.asu.edu/dps/pts

No fine will be considered satisfied until the full amount has been paid. Impound payments may be made at Parking Dispatch until 10:30 P.M. weekdays ( 8:30 P.M. Fridays)

C. Payroll Deduction

After exhausting all administrative and legal remedies, any and all parking fees or fines owed to ASU by employees are subject to collection by payroll deduction.

VI. APPEALS

A. First Level - Appeals Officer. Any person may appeal an ASU parking citation. A written appeal must be filed with the Appeals Section, Parking and Transit Services, within fourteen (14) calendar days from the date the citation was issued, exclusive of the day of issuance. Appeals may be faxed to (480-965-0712), sent by US mail, submitted via Online Parking Services at www.asu.edu/dps/pts, or filed in person. The ASU appeals officer may dismiss, modify, transfer, or uphold the citation.

B. Second Level - Parking Citations Appeals Board (PCAB). Appeals officer decisions may be appealed to the PCAB. The PCAB is an independent board comprised of faculty, staff and students. An appeal to the PCAB shall be filed at the Appeals Section, Parking and Transit Services, within fourteen (14) calendar days after the decision by the appeals officer, exclusive of the day the decision was made. The appeal must state, in detail, the grounds whereby it is believed the appeals officer erred. The appeal shall be accompanied by a receipt issued by the ASU Cashier’s Office or Parking and Transit Services indicating that the fine(s) are paid. The PCAB may reverse, modify, or affirm decisions of the appeals officer. Dependent on ruling, fine(s) may be refunded by Cashiering Services when there are no other outstanding credits against the account

C. Judicial Review. A final decision by the Parking Citation Appeals Board is subject to judicial review by Maricopa County Superior Court. A filing fee is charged by the court.

VII. MISCELLANEOUS

A. Responsibility. ASU assumes no responsibility for the care and protection of any vehicle or its contents at any time the vehicle is operated or parked on the campus. The purchase of a decal does not entitle the owner to store a vehicle on campus.

B. Impaired Vehicles. In the event a vehicle breaks down and must be parked in an unauthorized area, the vehicle's driver should immediately notify ASU DPS by telephoning (480) 965-3456 or by using an emergency call box. Disabled vehicles must be removed within 24 hours. For free assistance with battery jumps or retrieval of keys locked in vehicles, call PTS Dispatch at (480) 965-0641.

C. Lot Closures/Event Parking. It may occasionally be necessary to close certain lots because of construction, athletic, or special events scheduled by ASU e.g. football, basketball, concerts, etc. Such events shall be designated by PTS. PTS will endeavor to notify decal holders through public notice prior to such closures and provide information on alternative parking. If PTS is unable to reach the owner of a vehicle and it becomes necessary, a vehicle may be relocated at the owner's expense. Current ASU Decal Holders may park in their assigned lots for events only if that lot is south of University Drive. There is no charge for parking in non-resident lots south of University Drive during specified events when a current ASU Parking Decal is properly displayed. If the event is south of University Drive, PTS will make every effort to accommodate you in your assigned lot or direct you to alternate parking.

D. Signing. Vehicles must be parked ONLY where spaces have been designated and within a legal parking space. The temporary absence of a sign or gate arm at the entrance of a parking area does NOT indicate free parking or the removal of the parking restrictions for that area. If restrictions are altered, notices will be posted by PTS, and changes will be effective as posted.
