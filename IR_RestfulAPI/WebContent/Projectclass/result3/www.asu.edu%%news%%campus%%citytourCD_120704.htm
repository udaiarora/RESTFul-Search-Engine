December 7, 2004

‘City Tour’ CD entices high achievers to ASU

Every year, universities rush to recruit the best and brightest high school seniors. At ASU, there’s a new student recruiting tool that not only attracts new students to ASU but also highlights the Phoenix metropolitan area as a great place to live.

ASU soon will begin sending “City Tour,” a two-CD package and color map, to thousands of high-achieving high school students, including National Merit Scholars. The package is designed to entice out-of-state students and their families to visit ASU and the Valley of the Sun.

This is the latest tool in ASU’s student recruitment arsenal. This summer, the university launched a GPS-enabled self-guided campus tour that uses a handheld computer and global positioning satellite technology to let potential students and their families see and hear content about the university when entering dozens of GPS “hot spots” located throughout campus.

ASU President Michael Crow recently announced sweeping expansion plans for the university that include an increased role for ASU in the community’s social, economic, cultural and environmental future. Crow’s vision to position ASU as one of the nation’s premier public research universities calls for a vigorous student and faculty recruitment program.

According to Tim Desch, dean of undergraduate admissions, “ASU is poised to become an unparalleled combination of academic and research excellence, and that calls for attracting the best students, as well as the most talented faculty. The new ‘City Tour’ CD package showcases the city’s culture, history and amenities so it is easier than ever to imagine being a part of ASU and the community.”

Before visiting Phoenix, students can pop the CDs into their computers and take a virtual tour of Phoenix, Tempe, Scottsdale and other cities in the metropolitan area, featuring points of interest and detailed city maps.

ASU developed the “City Tour” CD in collaboration with OnPoint Systems, a provider of content and technology for audio tour programs. According to ASU alumnus Robert Boscamp, president and chief executive officer of OnPoint Systems: “ASU is a great school and the Phoenix metro area is a wonderful place to live. Choosing a university is a big decision and we think the City Tour is a valuable tool in helping students and their families get to know the community.”

Kennedy, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 965-4012 or (carolyne.kennedy@asu.edu).
