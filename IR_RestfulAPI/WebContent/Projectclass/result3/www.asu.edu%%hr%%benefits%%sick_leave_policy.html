Sick Leave Accrued and Paid Sick Leave

Sick Leave
Sick leave is available for use by all eligible staff immediately upon accrual and is charged on time reports in quarter-hour increments. It is to be used in connection with family leave when the reason and circumstances are consistent with the purpose of and eligibility for family leave as described in SPP 705-02. The definition and application of the University's policy SPP 701-01 Sick Leave - General Policy states, sick leave may be used only when an employee:

  • is unable to perform assigned duties because of illness, injury, pregnancy, childbirth, or
  • related medical conditions and must be absent from work to obtain health-related services not reasonably available before or after the employee's regular work hours or
  • is absent due to illness or communicable disease within the employee's immediate family, established household, or in situations which place primary responsibility for care on the employee.

It is highly recommended to establish standards similar to the following:

  1. All requests for use of sick leave must be approved by the employee's supervisor. The employee must notify his or her supervisor on the first day of an absence and periodically thereafter in the event of an illness of more than five working days.
  2. Within the unit, establish a request/notice procedure. For example, an employee is to notify his or her supervisor within one half hour of the employee's start time, and upon return complete and submit a leave request form within two days.
  3. Only an individual aware of the employee's absence can sign the employee's time sheet, preferably the supervisor. Such a practice helps to assure accuracy and accountability in the recording of absences.
  4. A supervisor needs to be aware of an employee's sick leave balance. An employee may request sick leave beyond what has been accrued. Under such circumstances, the employee may, if desired, request to use accrued vacation leave (if eligible to do so), and/or request a leave without pay. The supervisor needs to be able to inform the employee of the number of leave without pay hours and vacation hours approved.
  5. Excessive absenteeism or extended absence may warrant a request for a physician statement in support of the leave request. It is important for all employees within the unit to be informed of the conditions or circumstances which would require the providing of a physician statement.
  6. In addition, once an attendance standard has been established within a unit, an employee's lack of compliance in meeting the standard can be addressed. Sick leave does not preclude a supervisor from addressing the performance issues resulting from poor attendance. A performance standard can be based on the operational needs of the unit and/or the average attendance pattern of the typical employee within the unit. Oftentimes the standard is defined in terms of the frequency and/or duration of absences.
  7. If in connection with absenteeism the employee self identifies a disability, consult with the Affirmative Action unit and SPP 105 Americans with Disabilities before proceeding with any form of disciplinary action.

Any questions regarding the interpretation of SPP policies on sick leave, the application of sick leave and the employee's performance directly related to the sick leave contact your HR Partner.

Vacation Leave
In order to understand the definition and application of the University's policy on vacation leave refer to SPP 702-01 Vacation Leave - General. The policy provides information on approval, accrual and accumulation, and the payment of hours upon termination. Encouraging the use of vacation leave can generate well rested employees who are more productive and effective. Vacation leave may be used in connection with family leave when the reason and circumstances are consistent with the purpose of and eligibility for family leave as described in SPP 705-02 .

It is highly recommended that the following guidelines are established for vacation leave:

  1. Within the unit, a request/notice procedure. For example, an employee is to submit a vacation leave request for one day or less at least forty-eight hours in advance and two weeks in advance if the request is made for more than one day.
  2. The only one who signs the employee's time sheet is an individual aware of the employee's absence, preferably the supervisor. Such a practice helps to assure accuracy and accountability in the recording of absences.
  3. Be aware of an employee's vacation leave balance. An employee may request vacation leave beyond what has been accrued. Under such circumstances, a supervisor needs to be able to inform the employee of the need to request leave without pay. It is also advisable to alert employees when their vacation balances exceed the maximum allowed, leaving them vulnerable to losing accrued leave.
  4. The approval or disapproval of vacation leave is at the discretion of the supervisor and must take into account the operational needs of the unit. If a supervisor is unable to accommodate the employee's request to take vacation during a particular period of time, perhaps alternate dates could be considered and discussed.
  5. If the employee's attendance has been poor and has had an adverse affect on the individual's performance and/or the operation, the supervisor may want to consider not approving a leave request for vacation. Approving vacation under such circumstances may send the wrong message, which is that the employee's continued absence does not constitute a performance issue.

Any questions regarding the interpretation of SPP policies on vacation leave, the application of vacation leave and the employee's performance can be directed to an HR Partner.

Other Available Paid Leave

Jury Duty

In order to understand the application of the University's policy on jury duty refer to SPP 704-03. The policy provides information on notification, absence reporting, and fees and travel reimbursements associated with jury duty and material witness service. It is important for a supervisor to communicate to the employee his or her obligation to remit fees received for jury duty less traveling expenses to Cashiering Services; call 965-7468 for further guidance. A supervisor may request documentary evidence of the summons or subpoena, but in doing so should be consistent in requiring such documentation of all employees within the unit.

Any questions regarding the interpretation of SPP policies on vacation leave, the application of vacation leave and the employee's performance can be directed to an HR Partner.

Military Leave
In order to understand the application of the University's policy on military leave refer to SPP 706. Not all training offered by the Guard or Reserves is required. In responding to a request for military leave, a supervisor can require supporting documentation which indicates whether or not the training is required in order for the employee's status in the military to remain active. If the training is not required, the supervisor can exercise discretion in approving the leave request based on the operational needs of the unit.

Any questions regarding the interpretation of SPP policies on vacation leave, the application of vacation leave and the employee's performance can be directed to an HR Partner.

Compassionate Transfer of Leave
ASU allows employees to voluntarily transfer accrued vacation leave to other employees who are unable to work because of a catastrophic illness or injury, or who are absent due to catastrophic illness/injury within the employee's immediate family, established household, or in situations which place primary responsibility for care on the employee. "Family Medical Leave" (SPP 705-02) is not additional leave, rather, it is to be used concurrently with Compassionate Leave. Conditions and procedures for compassionate leave transfer are identified in SPP 702-04 , "Compassionate Transfer of Leave."

Contact your HR Partner  with any questions regarding the University's compassionate transfer of leave policy.
