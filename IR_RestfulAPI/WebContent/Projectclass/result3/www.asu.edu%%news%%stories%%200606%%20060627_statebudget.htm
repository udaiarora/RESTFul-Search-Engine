June 27, 2006

State boosts investment in ASU by 17 Percent

The 2007 ASU budget, approved by the Arizona Legislature and signed into law by Gov. Janet Napolitano, includes a state operating fund investment of $423,120,000. The fiscal year 2007 budget is a 17 percent increase compared to last year's state investment of $360,391,600.

Also approved by the Legislature, but not included in the $423 million figure, is $6.5 million in building renewal funding.

?I want to personally thank Gov. Napolitano and the Arizona Legislature for their support of the recently enacted budget and the increased investment in ASU,? says ASU President Michael Crow. ?Recognition of the value of our university employees, as evidenced by nearly $27 million in salary increase funding, is especially gratifying. In addition, the governor and Legislature essentially fully funded our highest priority, which will allow us to cover the costs of serving our rapidly growing student body. We will diligently use the added investment to continue advancing the university for the benefit of Arizona, and our students, faculty and staff.?

Ongoing investments

The budget provides full funding of enrollment growth for the three state universities ? the first time in 10 years the state has fully funded enrollment growth at the level requested by the Arizona Board of Regents. The investment in enrollment growth is crucial to enhancing the university's accessibility, as well as the quality of the undergraduate academic experience.

Also added to ASU's base budget is $10.6 million ? a figure that was calculated using enrollments at the state's three universities as the basis ? that will enable ASU to construct science and technology and classroom/office facilities at the Polytechnic campus, as well as fund other priorities.

Another key component of the approved budget is funding for employee health insurance and retirement contribution increases. This is a critical investment; without these funds, the university would be forced to divert resources away from the core educational mission to cover these expenses.

One-time investments

The budget contains funding for building renewal. This is the first time that building renewal funds have been provided to the universities since 2001. The legislation, which funds about 28 percent of ASU's building renewal formula based request, will provide ASU with $6.5 million to invest in the long-overdue repair of campus facilities.

The budget also provides a one-time investment of $500,000 for ASU's share of funding that is intended to be used to research water issues in urban areas. This research is part of a collaborative effort among Arizona's universities to improve water policy and management in the state.

Other investments

The state budget also provides $529,000 to ASU for distribution to the Arizona Historical Foundation. The purpose of these funds is to preserve and categorize the papers of the state's late U.S. Sen. Barry Goldwater.
