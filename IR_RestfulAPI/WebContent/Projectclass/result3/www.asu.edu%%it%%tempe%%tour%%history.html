Computing Commons

History/Planning

As you enter the ASU Computing Commons, you are experiencing the culmination of a major team effort that was initiated in October of 1988. Information Technology set upon this venture with great enthusiasm, aspiring to create and design a new building type as significant to the life of the campus community as a library or a student center.

By fall of 1989, project funding had been approved ($15.2M total) by the Arizona Board of Regents, as well as the project scope and objectives, including:

  • To provide a building that supports excellence in education and research through technology;
  • To facilitate the integration of information technologies with teaching, learning, research, and student services support activities;
  • To provide for operational and functional change; and
  • To consolidate existing Information Technology services and functions.

The firm of Anderson DeBartolo Pan, Inc. (ADP) provided architectural and engineering services for the project through all phases. Other consultants included 3D/I as construction managers throughout the project, and Gensler and Associates Architects (Irvine, CA), as the initial interior design consultant. The architectural and interior consultants worked hand-in-hand from the beginning of the design process to ensure that spaces were properly sized and designed to accommodate human and functional requirements, furniture, and power and communications to support equipment. This provided a level of collaboration which frequently does not occur in the critical early phases of design. The general contractor was Joe E. Woods, Inc. Finalization of interior plans and finishes, bidding, furniture installation, and custom furniture design was provided by Lendrum Associates.

Architectural design was completed in December 1990. Bidding was extremely competitive for both construction and furnishings, and allowed the project to set aside $1M for new equipment for the computing site and classrooms. Construction took place from February 1991 through June 1992. The Computing Commons opened at the beginning of the fall semester of 1993. Building dedication by ASU president Lattie Coor took place on December 1, 1993.

Overall building and furniture quality is high, yet the Computing Commons is an extremely cost-effective project. Construction of 121,000 gross square feet cost $10.6M or $88/SF (most buildings of this type cost from $100 to $200/SF). The 75,000 net assignable SF were furnished for $1.6M or $21/SF. The building and furnishings have tremendous flexibility (e.g., computer access floor throughout, furniture that can be reconfigured), and will enable Information Technology to meet changes in technology in teaching methodologies well into the 21st century. Every effort has been made to eliminate physical barriers with respect to furnishings and the building design. Likewise adaptive technology hardware and software is provided as recommended by ASU Disability Resources for Students.

The Computing Commons is located in the heart of the campus--not far from the library and the student union. This site is significant as it establishes the Computing Commons as the "technological hub" of the campus.

The building is almost two buildings, one on top of the other: the first two floors have all facilities programmed for use by students and faculty; the upper two floors provide office and support facilities for Information Technology staff and the Faculty Development group.

It is significant that the words common, community; and communication share the same Latin root - communis. The Latin conveys not only the notion of sharing among everyone, but of serving and obliging. This Commons will serve us and oblige the larger community beyond the boundaries of this University.
