File Backup Strategies

The /Backup directory found in your AFS space is updated each night and you can recover any file that was saved the day before /Backup was updated.

If you have any data that is very valuable to you, you should definetely store it on another media (disk, tape, CD, etc.) that you can keep with you and take responsibility of. More media, and locations you store your valuable data less the possibility of loosing it. Even though the data is quite safe in AFS space, as with any system there is the possibility of problems.

Depending on the size of your data (for archiving) you can choose different media: tape or floppy disk, or the hard disk of your own personal computer (UNIX workstation, MAC,or IBM-PC).

There are utilities in UNIX that can reduce the size of the needed storage and package it as a single file.

The utilities are:
tar (for *.tar files)
gzip (GNU zip for very good compression)
compress (standard UNIX for compression)

To find more about this you can read the man pages by entering the following at the system prompt:

man topic
man tar
man gzip
man compress

Information on using compress and tar is also available in this Online Help System.

There are different kinds of tapes but for UNIX 4 or 8 mm is the norm. The Visualization Center on the second floor of the Computing Commons has a few tape drives and (in the near future) a CD recorder.
