March 7, 2006

Candidates line up for Academic Assembly election

The Committee on Committees and the Academic Assembly/Senate President have announced the candidates for election in the Spring 2006 Academic Assembly election.

They are as follows:

. President-elect of the Assembly/Senate: Angelita Reyes, professor, African-American studies/English; William Verdini, associate professor, supply chain management.

. Secretary of the Assembly/Senate: Judy Grace, assistant instructional professional, learning and teaching excellence; Anne Kopta, associate professor of voice, School of Music.

. Academic Freedom and Tenure Committee (four elected for three-year terms): Yang Kuang, professor, mathematics and statistics; Morris Okun, professor, psychology; Danko Sipka, professor, languages and literatures; Andrew Smith, professor, School of Life Sciences; Jeanne Wilcox, professor, speech and hearing science.

. Academic Professional Grievance Committee (five elected for three-year terms; two alternates elected for one-year terms): Frank Davis, research professional senior, chemistry and biochemistry; Steve Hefner, instructional professional, Morrison School of Agribusiness; Chuck Kazilek, research professional senior, School of Life Sciences; Jeanette Mueller-Alexander, librarian, library reference service; Brenda Shears, research administrator senior, Global Institute of Sustainability.

. Academic Professional Status Committee (one elected for three-year term; two elected alternates for one year each): Tedd Brandon, research professional senior, Harrington Department of Engineering; Chris Kyselka, associate director, community outreach programs; Renate Mittleman, research professional senior, mathematics and statistics; Barry Wilkens, research professional senior, Center for Solid State Science.

. Governance Grievance Committee (three elected for three-year terms): Craig Allen, associate professor, journalism and mass communication; Barbara Guzzetti, professor, curriculum and instruction; Sheldon Simon, professor, political science.

Additional candidates can be nominated with petitions signed by at least 50 members of the Academic Assembly. The deadline for submitting petitions is 5 p.m. , March 17, at the Academic Senate Office, Administration (ADM) B Wing, room 361.

Electronic voting will take place from March 24 - 31.
