Must-Attend Events

The following were Must Attend Events held during our fall 2006 Welcome Week. Please check back for information regarding our Must Attend Events for Fall Welcome 2007 at a later time.

Thursday, August 17th

Passport to ASU
5:00 pm - 8:00 pm
Memorial Union & Student Recreation Complex
Your adventure starts here! Discover all that awaits you while exploring the Memorial Union (MU) and the Student Recreation Complex (SRC) during Passport to ASU. Find a club or organization to join at the Resource Fair; learn about group fitness classes and intramural sports; become an active member of the ASU community by visiting the Sun Devil Involvement Center; watch performances by student groups; enjoy music and interactive games, and much, much more. Don't forget to stop by Sparky's Den in the MU for free bowling and billiards.

Friday, August 18th

New Student Convocation
9:00 am
Wells Fargo Arena
Welcome to the annual opening new student convocation, which officially begins ASU's academic year. Please be sure to arrive on time; then plan to attend your college activities immediately following.
Parents Reception
5:30 pm - 7:30 pm
Memorial Union, Arizona and Ventana Ballrooms
Meet ASU's President Dr. Michael Crow, faculty, staff, the Parents Association Board, and other new ASU parents at this informal gathering with music and refreshments. Casual attire.
Whitewash the 'A'
5:30 pm - 7:00 pm
Base of Tempe Butte, a.k.a. 'A' Mountain
Whitewashing the "A" began as a tradition in the 1930s as part of the orientation week. Today, the Student Alumni Association continues this tradition. During Sun Devil 101 they, along with the incoming freshmen, climb the butte and give the "A" a fresh coat of white paint. This is to symbolize the beginning of a new school year.
Sparky's Summer Shakedown
6:30 pm - 10:00 pm
Palo Verde Beach (north campus)
Enjoy dinner and social activities from Devils' Advocates and ASASU's Programming and Activities Board.

Saturday, August 19th

Tropical Tiki Luau
8:00 pm - 11:00 pm
Student Recreation Complex Fields
Join the Residence Hall Association (RHA) for an evening of food, fun, and friends. Activities include live music and performances, prize giveaways, and plenty of free food. This is a wonderful opportunity to meet fellow students and get a taste of what your RHA has to offer.
