COLLEGE OF TECHNOLOGY AND APPLIED SCIENCES

 

The College of Technology and Applied Sciences offers baccalaureate degrees, graduate degree and non-degree programs in technological fields that support professional careers, lifelong learning and professional development.  The College of Technology and Applied Sciences is focused on being nationally recognized as the leading academic program in the technology discipline and on providing students the highest quality technological education available at both the undergraduate and graduate levels.

The Bachelor of Applied Science degree is a ?capstone? degree for the

Associate of Applied Science (A.A.S.) degree.

 

Admission

Admission to the B.A.S. degree program is restricted to students holding an A.A.S. degree from a regionally accredited U.S. postsecondary educational institution.

A GPA of 2.00 or higher is required for all resident applicant and 2.50 for nonresident applicants.

For out-of-state students the transferrable degree may carry a name other than AAS. Therefore, please access bas@asu.edu for specific information.

 

Transfer of Credits

A total of 60 A.A.S. credits are accepted in transfer whether they are posted as transferable or non- transferable (NT) in the Course Equivalency Guide (CEG).

 

Degree Requirements

In addition to the A.A.S. degree, the BAS in Applied Science consists of 60 semester hours of upper-division  (300-level and above) courses, with 30 hours in residence.
