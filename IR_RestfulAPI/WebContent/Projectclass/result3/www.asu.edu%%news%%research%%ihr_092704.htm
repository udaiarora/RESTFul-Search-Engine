September 27, 2004

CLAS Announces Institute for Humanities Research

Arizona State University’s College of Liberal Arts and Sciences has announced the creation of  the Institute for Humanities Research (IHR), pending approval by the Arizona Board of Regents.

The Institute for Humanities Research is a new university entity designed to support and develop world-class research activity in the humanities and to create active research connections between humanities scholarship and scholarship and research in other disciplines.

“The Institute will create a dynamic environment for interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary research,” said Deborah Losse, Divisional Dean for Humanities and interim director of the IHR. “It will facilitate collaboration among scholars in the humanities, social sciences and sciences in order to encourage the development of research that examines issues confronting individual and collective human experience across time.”

“The humanities always have been central to the intellectual life of the university and have a vital role to play in our overall research profile,” said David Young, Vice President and Dean of the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences. “The Institute for Humanities Research is designed to further develop that strength and use it to forge new and exciting research connections across disciplines. In a world that is increasingly complex and contentious, the humanities bring vital context, nuance and understanding to critical issues.”

The  IHR will increase the profile and involvement of humanities research through a range of newly funded programs. Prominent among these is the IHR Fellows Program, which is designed to support collaborative research among scholars, both at ASU and around the world.

The IHR Fellows Program will fund ASU Fellows, faculty research groups that will work to develop theme-based proposals for large, multidisciplinary/ transdisciplinary external  grants. The fellows will be selected through a competitive process and will include at least two faculty from the humanities in each group. In addition, the Fellows Program will fund visits by two prominent outside scholars each year who will participate in IHR activities and collaborate with the fellows groups. A Graduate Fellows Program will also be funded to support graduate students involved in IHR activities.

Other IHR research development programs include the IHR Competitive Seed Grant Program, designed to encourage the development of grant proposals aimed at typical funding agencies in the humanities and the IHR Research Workshops, which are designed to help humanities scholars learn more about developing successful interdisciplinary  and collaborative projects.

The Institute will also have a public component in the IHR Lecture Series, where the Institute Fellows will identify and invite scholars in areas relevant to their projects to present lectures and symposia. In addition to  these presentations, the institute will sponsor the IHR Distinguished Lecture, an annual public event featuring a major national or international scholar.

“A major goal of the Institute is to further enrich the research environment in the humanities at ASU,” said Losse. “This will mean enriching the intellectual environment of the university and the community as well.”

ASU President Michael Crow and Vice President David Young will host a reception in honor of the founding of the IHR at 3:45 p.m. on September 23 in the Carson Ballroom of Old Main. Persons interested in attending should RSVP at 480-965-8065.
