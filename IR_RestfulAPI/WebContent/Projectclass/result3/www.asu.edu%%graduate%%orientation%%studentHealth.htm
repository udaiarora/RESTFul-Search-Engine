Student Health Services at ASU
www.asu.edu/health

 



All registered ASU students are eligible for care; no health insurance is required. Students pay minimal or no charges, depending on the services rendered. Medical care can be arranged by telephone or on a walk-in basis.

ASU pays for health insurance for qualified TAs/RAs. Spouses and dependents may be covered by Student Health Insurance but are not eligible for treatment at the Student Health and Wellness Center.

Speciality Areas
Board-certified physicians are on staff in family practice, internal medicine, gynecology, sports medicine, emergency medicine, rheumatology, and adolescent medicine; there are also orthopedic, dermatology, gynecology, and ear, nose, and throat consultants.

Wellness Behavior
The Health Education and Wellness Department at ASU exists to help students improve their lifestyles in an effort to move toward optimal personal health and to improve community health. This is accomplished through a combination of efforts to enhance awareness, change behavior, and create environments that support positive health practices. ASU Student Health offers education and services to enhance wellness behavior: workshops, media campaigns, wellness days and special events, nutrition counseling, and opportunities for students to get involved.
