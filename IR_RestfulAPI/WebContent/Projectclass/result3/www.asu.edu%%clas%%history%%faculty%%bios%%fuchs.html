Research and Teaching Interests

Dr. Fuchs's major research interests focus on gender and the family in modern France. She is currently working on a book about the social construction of paternity and the family in modern France. This deals with issues of law, sexuality, family patterns, and the state. Other work explores charity and welfare in nineteenth-century Europe. In addition, she is working on a book tentatively entitled, Gender, Family, and Poverty in Nineteenth-Century Europe under contract with Cambridge University Press. Her teaching interests involve issues of gender and the state in modern Europe, the history of France, and the social/cultural history of modern Europe, especially France.
