Hayden Library Provides Wireless Access

Wireless Laptop Zone

Courtesy of University Libraries
Hayden Library’s 24-hour study area (room C-55) is now equipped for wireless access! The system’s range extends far beyond the room itself so that patrons can use their laptops both outside and inside in areas adjacent to C-55. If you are considering using this new service, an IEEE 802.11b or an IEEE 802.11b-g compatible wireless network card is required. For a review of various network cards or for recommendations on selecting a card, visit IT’s Network Card Review page at http://asu.edu/comm/wlan/review.html. Other important information about getting connected can be found at http://www.asu.edu/it/fyi/comm/wlan/connect.html.

New Databases Available

These new databases have been put online (http://www.asu.edu/lib/top-menus/new.html):

American Journey Series (Subscribed)
Includes hundreds of carefully selected, rare documents, pictures, and archival audio and video related to American history and culture.

International Medieval Bibliography Online (1967+)
Indexes journal articles and miscellaneous publications on the Middle Ages published worldwide since 1967. Includes citations on over 300,000 articles on the Middle Ages in Europe, the Middle East and North Africa.

RLG Cultural Materials
Provides primary source materials in high-quality digital form. There are more than 200,000 works in this database from over 80 collections in the cultural institutions around the world.

L' Annee philologique
Contains 375,000 bibliographic records on languages (French, German, Italian, Spanish) for the years 1969 to 2001, with 12,500 new records added each year.

World Development Indicators Online 2003
Contains statistics related to global economy, economics, development, etc.

MIT CogNet
Features works of Cognitive Neurosciences including journals, books, and reference materials mainly by MIT Press.

IFS Online (Subscribed)
Contains international financial statistics.

Kraus Curriculum Development Library (Subscribed)
Contains information on curricula, frameworks, and standards, which brings together educational objectives, content, instructional strategies, and evaluative techniques for Pre K-12 and Adult Basic Education.

The Music-In-Print Series (Subscribed)
Includes online searchable databases of published music in print such as Sacred Choral Music In Print, Piano Music In Print, Organ Music In Print, Band Music In Print, Guitar Music In Print, etc.


Ask LIST

Please send questions on any library technology topic to Heather Knowles to be answered in future columns.

Q. "Whenever I submit an SOS ticket online, it’s usually for a problem with access to an electronic resource. Why do we have so many problems with these?"

A. The library provides thousands of electronic resources, including databases, electronic journals, PDF files and others. LIST maintains some electronic resources on-campus, but a majority of our resources are owned and maintained by off-site vendors. Given the sheer number of resources we provide our patrons, we actually have relatively few problems with them, but there are a few commonly reported technical issues. Problems can occur at the vendor site, with the network outside of ASU, or with the network within ASU. Further complicating this are the various methods of authenticating our users so each resource recognizes them as valid ASU users. Most of our vendors recognize an ASU IP address as valid; some require additional configuration. We use the proxy server to allow our off campus patrons to appear as though they have an ASU IP address.

Below are some typical problems, and possible solutions. Any problems should be reported to SOS, who will diagnose the problem and forward it to whomever can best solve the problem.

Symptom: The electronic resource is asking me for a userid and password, although ASU Libraries has a subscription, or the resource is acting differently that it did yesterday.
1. The Vendor has changed something (server, software, network), which no longer recognizes ASU as a valid user. This generally requires Collection Development (licensing problem), Tech Services (payment problem) or LIST (technical problem) to contact the vendor to sort out.
2. The user is off-campus and has not logged into the ASU Library System’s proxy server, or the resource’s URL has not been added to the proxy server.
3. The site offers additional personalization beyond what is available via ASU Libraries’ subscription, which may or may not be a free service to individual users.
4. ASU Libraries subscribes to part of the resource, but not all parts of the resource.

Symptom: When I click on the URL I get a Bad Gateway error page.
1. This usually means that the resource’s server is down or unavailable.
2. This could also mean that there is network interference between ASU and the resource site.

Symptom: When I click on the URL I get a 403 Forbidden error page.
1. This is a problem with permissions. The site does not recognize ASU as a valid user.
2. If this happens to an off campus user it may mean that the resource has not been entered into the proxy forward table.
