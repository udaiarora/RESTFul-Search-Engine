Research Interests

I am currently working on a number of projects, including “Post-Apartheid South Africa and Its Neighbors: A Retrospective Analysis.” Here I am re-evaluating my own and other 1990s prognostications about the relations between a black-ruled South Africa and its neighbors after the horrors of apartheid. The second project I am working on is entitled “Black America and Black South Africa: The Politics of ‘Integration’ and ‘Liberation’ in Comparative Perspective” in which I employ an African Diaspora perspective to evaluate the ideological and state strategies to integrate blacks into the economic mainstream and their consequences/implications for black politics and democratic participation in both countries. The third project is entitled “African Politics in America: Globalization, Transnationalization, and the New African Diasporas in the 21 st Century,” a project that focuses on globalization and the redefinition of citizenship and membership and their relationship to the territorial state; as well as on the impact of this “new Diaspora” on the “old Diaspora” and their relations to the American state and society. Finally, I am putting finishing touches on my book manuscript, Comrades, Patriots, and Parasites: Black Capitalist Development and Nation-Building in Post-Apartheid South Africa which elaborates on my article on black economic power and nation-building in South Africa since 1994.

Biography

I was educated at Aquinas Secondary School, Osu, Isiala-Mbano, Imo State, Nigeria where I graduated with a Grade One Distinction in 1978. I then entered the University of Nigeria, Nsukka in 1979 and graduated B. Sc. (Second Class Honors, Upper Division, as well as winner of Kalu Ezera Trust Fund Award for the best-graduating student in Political Science) in the class of 1983. During my National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) program, I was Acting Head, Department of Social Studies at the Government Teachers College, Langtang, Plateau State, Nigeria, and later taught high school Economics, History, and Government until August 1986. In 1988, I graduated with an M. A. (passed with Distinction) in Political Science from the University of Akron in Ohio, and in 1992 with a Ph.D. in Political Science (passed with Distinction) from the University of Connecticut. I also studied French in a language immersion program at the Institut de Français in Villefranch-sur-mer near Nice in France in 1998.

From January to June 1993 I was a Visiting Assistant Professor of Political Science at Gustavus Adolphus College in St. Peter, Minnesota. I spent the next eleven years (1993-2004) in the James Madison College at Michigan State University in East Lansing, Michigan, first as Assistant Professor and later as tenured Associate Professor of International Relations. While at MSU, I chaired the International Relations Field (1999-2000), and was Director of MSU’s Study Abroad Program first in Brussels (1998-1999) and Ghana (2002-2004). I was also a Core Faculty member in the African Studies Center, the Center for Advanced Studies in International Development, and the Women in Development Program. I have been consultant to the World Bank and the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA) on maritime transport policies in West and Central Africa.

I have traveled to and lived in more than 12 countries, including Ghana, Cote d’Ivoire, Senegal, Namibia, United Kingdom, France, Czech Republic, Germany, Nigeria, and South Africa (2000-2001 as a Fulbright Faculty Research Abroad Fellow at Rhodes University, Rand Afrikaans University, and the Africa Institute of Southern Africa).
