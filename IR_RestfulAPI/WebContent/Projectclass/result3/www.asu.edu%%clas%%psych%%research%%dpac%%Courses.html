  Psychology Courses
  PSY 498/591 and KIN 591 Introduction to Dynamics
Dynamics is a powerful tool that comes from physics and engineering but has recently been used to study the behavior of living things, including human and animal motor control and learning, social psychology, and developmental psychology. This is the first of a series of courses that is designed to introduce students to the basic concepts of dynamics. Lectures will focus upon the development of the theory and its application to relevant fields in psychology and exercise science. Activities will include internet exercises and weekly class discussion of primary literature. In addition, students will develop and present to the class an independent project that incorporates the concepts being taught. Evaluation will be based on exam performance, class participation, and the independent project.

 
  PSY 591/KIN 591  Ecological and Dynamical Perspectives on Perception
How do you see the world around you? How are you aware of the meanings and values of the objects in your world? And how do these perceptions influence, and get influenced by, our behaviors? These are the questions that J. J. Gibson challenged us to ask when he proposed an ecological theory of psychology. As a general perspective, ecological science emphasizes the necessity of understanding biology in the context on an organism's environment. Gibson extended this view to psychology, thereby challenging many of the dichotomies that follow from psychology's traditional emphasis on the mind and mental operations: e.g., animal and environment, sensation and perception, distal and proximal stimulation, and perceiving and acting. In this course, we will explore Ecological Psychology, from the traditional views of the 19th century science through current theory and research. Covered topics may include: the traditional view of perception, the major tenets of Ecological Psychology, controlling the movements of our bodies, the roles of activity in vision and touch, dynamical themes in perception, 'ecological' developments in robotics and artificial intelligence, perceptual development and learning, social psychology, and man-machine interactions.
  PSY 591 Current Themes in Dynamics and Complexity
Learning will be divided between the classroom and interaction with faculty and students of the Dynamics of Perception, Action, and Cognition (DPAC) seminar.  The class will spend the entire semester discussing one recent publication in dynamics and complex systems:  Signs of Life:  How complexity pervades biology.  Topics include:  Morphogenesis, Biorhythms, Brain Processes, Pattern formation, Biodiversity, Social and Economic Behavior, Evolution and Extinction.  Students will be responsible for leading class discussion on a rotating basis, incorporating external resources in support of the text.  You will keep a weekly journal to assist in the development of themes across the semester and submit a report of your insights at the end of the semester.

Kinesiology Courses
  KIN 494/598 Motor Control in Special Populations
Discussion of the principles of motor control theories as they relate to special developmental populations with a specific emphasis on cerebral dysfunction. The theoretical as well as practical applications (i.e. teaching, rehabilitation, learning) will be addressed. A lecture/discussion format will be used. Students will be responsible for leading some of the discussions.
  KIN 598 Principles of Dynamical Systems
Discussion of the principles of the dynamical systems theory as it relates to motor performance. The application of this theory to motor control, motor development and motor learning will be addressed. A lecture/discussion format will be used. Students will be responsible for leading some of the discussions.
Mathematics Courses
  MAT 452 Introduction to Chaos and Nonlinear Dynamics
Properties of nonlinear dynamical systems; dependence on initial conditions; strange attractors; period doubling; bifurcations; symbolic dynamics; Smale-Birkhoff theorem; and applications.
  MAT 455 Introduction to Fractals and Applications
Fractals; self-similar structures, fractals with iterated function systems of maps, computing fractals, fractal dimensions, chaotic dynamics on fractals, applications.
  MAT 505 Perturbation Methods
Nonlinear oscillations, strained coordinates, renormalization, multiple scales, boundary layers, matched asymptotic expansions, turning point problems, and WKBJ method.
  MAT 526 Numerical Solution of Bifurcation Problems
Nonlinear parameter-dependent differential, algebraic equations, numerical solutions; bifurcation, turning points; continuation methods, branch switching; steady-state, time-dependent cases; Hopf Bifurcation.
  MAT 555 Fractal Geometry
Geometry and analysis of fractal sets; definitions of dimensions; calculating dimensions; projections, products of fractals; random fractals; multifractal measures; and applications.
  MAT 575 Theory of Ordinary Differential Equations and Dynamical Systems
Geometric approach to ODEs and dynamical systems; (un)stable, center manifolds; structural stability; normal forms; averaging; chaos; persistence. May be repeated for credit with instructor approval.
  STP 425 Stochastic Processes
Markov chains, stationary distributions, pure jump processes, second order processes, and other topics in stochastic processes.
