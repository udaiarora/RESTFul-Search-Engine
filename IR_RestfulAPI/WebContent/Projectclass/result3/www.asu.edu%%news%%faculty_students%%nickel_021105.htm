February 11, 2005

Law professor earns top essay prize

James Nickel, professor of law and affiliate professor of philosophy at ASU, is the winner of the Philosophical Quarterly Essay Prize for 2004.

The Philosophical Quarterly is a top-tier philosophy journal published at the University of St. Andrews in Scotland.

The specified topic for 2004 was “Poverty and Rights.” As the winner of the annual prize, Nickel received a cash award of about $1,900 and publication of the essay in the journal.

Nickel’s essay developed an idea of economic and social rights as international human rights and constructed a systematic normative and practical defense of that idea.

He expects the essay to be published in July.

Nickel said that he is “pleased and honored” by the award, adding that the topic is one that he has worked on during his entire career.

Nickel joined the ASU faculty in 2003 after 20 years at the University of Colorado, Boulder. His fields are philosophy of law, human rights law and theory, and political philosophy.

He was a visiting fellow at Corpus Christi College, Oxford University last fall.

Those wishing to read the essay can contact Nickel for an electronic copy at (james.nickel@asu.edu).
