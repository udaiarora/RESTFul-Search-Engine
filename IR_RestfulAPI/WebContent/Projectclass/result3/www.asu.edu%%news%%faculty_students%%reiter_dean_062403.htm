June 24, 2003

ASU taps Reiter to lead College of Architecture

Wellington Reiter, AIA, an MIT professor, artist and architect from Boston, Mass., has been named as new dean of the College of Architecture & Environmental Design (CAED) at ASU.

Reiter currently is an associate professor of the practice of architecture at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and principal of Urban Instruments Inc. He also is commissioner of design for the Boston Society of Architects.

His professional work and interests span the range of disciplines from urban design to public art. Reiter is well known for his work on urban monuments and civic infrastructure, such as the project commemorating the 100th anniversary of the Wright Brothers' historic flight, which will open Dec. 17 at the Raleigh-Durham Airport.

"'Duke' Reiter brings a vision for the 'new city,' - a concept of how to blend art, science, design and quality of life enhancement," said ASU President Michael Crow. "He is the intellectual fusion leader that the university needs to move ASU through this critical first decade of the 21st century."

Reiter said his goal as dean of the college will be to "knit the diversity of scholarship, science and artistic activity at ASU into a dynamic situation for students and faculty alike.

"I would not have considered coming to ASU without the desire for change and effectiveness that the new administration is advancing," said Reiter, who will begin work Aug. 1. "If ASU is to truly 'embrace its cultural, socio-economic, and physical setting,' as the president has suggested that it must do, then the college is necessarily at the forefront of such a campaign. Given this kind of upper level support, I see it as my mission to provide as many opportunities as possible for the faculty and students within our school to capitalize on this directive."

"We were most fortunate to have an extraordinarily rich pool of applicants for this deanship, which reflects well on the college and the university," said ASU Executive Vice President and Provost Milton Glick. "We believe Duke Reiter is the right leader at the right time who will build on a strong foundation and increase the impact of the college in the metropolitan community and among national and international scholars and practitioners."

Reiter replaces John Meunier, who was dean of the college for 15 years and has joined the School of Architecture faculty after spending a year working on a PBS series on desert cities of the world. Ronald McCoy served as interim dean during the 2002 - 03 academic year and will return to his position of director of the School of Architecture.

Reiter earned a graduate degree at the Harvard University School of Design, and his bachelor's degree from Tulane University. He also has studied at the North London Polytechnic and the Architectural Association in London. His firm, Urban Instruments, has been the recipient of several awards from the American Institute of Architecture, the Boston Society of Architects and the Architectural League of New York for buildings, interiors and large-scale urban design projects.

In 2002 he was a Fellow at the MacDowell Colony, an artists' colony founded in Peterborough, N.H., in 1907. The Colony provides an environment in which creative artists are free to pursue their work without distraction. In 1991 - 92, Reiter was a visiting artist at the American Academy in Rome. Reiter has exhibited his sculpture and drawings in galleries throughout the United States and in London, and has written several books and numerous essays and articles, including "Vessels and Fields," published by Princeton Architectural Press.

Reiter is married and has two children. His wife, Patricia, has a degree in business and also is an architect. Their sons are Emerson, 15, and Adrian, 11.
