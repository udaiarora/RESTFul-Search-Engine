January 20, 2006

Gillmore takes reins of School of Social Work

ASU's College of Public Programs recently named Mary R. Gillmore as the new director of the School of Social Work.

In addition to the daily operational responsibilities of a director, Gillmore will be responsible for enhancing external funding for the ASU School of Social Work, planning and executing the school's move to the new ASU Downtown Phoenix campus, and improving the national rankings of the school's academic and research capacities.

Gillmore previously served as co-director of Socio-Behavioral and Prevention Care at the Center for AIDS Research in the University of Washington's School of Medicine, where she led behavioral research on sexually transmitted diseases (STDs) and acquired immune deficiency syndrome, a.k.a. AIDS.

Before that, Gillmore was the associate dean of research at the University of Washington 's School of Social Work.

"Dr. Gillmore brings a strong and impressive record of funded research initiatives to ASU, and she is the kind of leader we need to support the school's step toward the next level of development in community-based research," says Flavio Marsiglia, who is the ASU Foundation Professor of Cultural Diversity and Health and the director of the Southwest Interdisciplinary Research Center.

Marsiglia chaired the search committee that selected Gillmore for the position.

Since the mid-1980s, Gillmore has investigated adolescent substance abuse, teen pregnancy, adolescent sexuality and AIDS/STD prevention through federal grants from several health and science foundations and institutes. Her findings are widely published in peer-reviewed journals, and she has presented her work internationally. She currently serves as consulting editor for industry trade publication Social Work Research.

"Dr. Gilmore is an accomplished scholar, excellent teacher and seasoned administrator," says Debra Friedman, dean of the ASU College of Public Programs. "I have every confidence that the faculty, students and staff of the ASU School of Social Work - together with its community partners - will work effectively with Dr. Gillmore to advance the school's reputation as a stellar educational institute."

Gillmore received her doctorate and master's degree in sociology from the University of Washington. She also earned a master's degree and bachelor's degree in nursing from the University of Michigan and DePaul University, respectively.

Gillmore succeeds Leslie Leighninger, who served as director of the ASU School of Social Work since fall 2000. Leighninger will return full time as a professor for the school.
