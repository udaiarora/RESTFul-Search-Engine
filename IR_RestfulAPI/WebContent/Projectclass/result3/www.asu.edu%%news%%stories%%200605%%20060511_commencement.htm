May 11, 2006

ASU celebrates spring commencement

6,000 graduate; Rhodes, Wu, Bisgrove honored

More than 6,000 students graduated from ASU May 11, celebrated by their families, friends and teachers at the university’s commencement ceremony in Wells Fargo Arena.

Graduates from the ASU College of Nursing celebrate as they turn their tassles during ASU's spring 2006 commencement ceremonies in Tempe May 11.

ASU President Michael Crow, before conferring the degrees, addressed the students, urging them to use their education not only to “do well, but to do good” in the world.

Christine Wilkinson, senior vice president and secretary of the university, recognized two outstanding students who worked particularly hard in earning their degrees: Miriam Grill and Terri Hedgpeth.

Wilkinson also acknowledged a group of MBA students who traveled from China to attend graduation ceremonies and receive their degrees.

ASU commencement included honorary degrees for two exceptional leaders.

With assistance from ASU Provost Milton Glick and ABOR President Christina Palacios, Crow conferred upon Wu Qidi, vice minister of education of the People's Republic of China, the degree of Doctor of Humane Letters in recognition of her significant achievements as a leader in higher education, her exceptional work in academic research, and her commitment to international collaboration.

Crow also conferred upon Frank H.T. Rhodes, education policy expert and former president of Cornell University, the degree of Doctor of Humane Letters in recognition of his vision and leadership on the role of higher education in America.

Dr. Frank H.T. Rhodes (left), former President of Cornell University, signs a copy of his book for Wu Qidi, Vice Minister for Education from the Peoples Republic of China. Both were awarded Doctor of Humane Letters degrees May 11 during commencement ceremonies.

Rhodes, briefly addressing the students, commended them on choosing ASU, an institution he said was on its way to transforming higher education. Turning his attention to the audience, he continued, “To all you employers and business owners, the ideal gift for a new graduate is a job!”

Jerry Bisgrove, chairman and chief executive officer of Stardust Cos., was honored with ASU’s first-ever President’s Medal of Excellence for acting on his vision through an unwavering commitment to social and economic development in Arizona. His efforts in creating more affordable housing for low-income families, as well as improving the management and leadership of nonprofit agencies in the Valley, earned him this inaugural award.

“Forty years ago, I sat where you are now as a graduating student of ASU. I never dreamed I’d be here today,” Bisgrove said, after accepting his award. He offered students four points of advice, “Keep your sense of humor, keep learning, help others and have fun.”
