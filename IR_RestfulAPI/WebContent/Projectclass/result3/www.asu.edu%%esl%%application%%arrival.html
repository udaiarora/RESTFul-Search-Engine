REGISTRATION
Your I-20, item five, lists the date you are supposed to report to AECP. This day is for registration. You should come to the AECP office on or before this date to register for your classes. In order to register, you must bring the following:

  • passport with visa
  • I-20
  • proof of MMR immunization
  • money, traveler's checks or Visa/Mastercard to pay tuition, fees and health insurance

Payment at registration includes tuition for classes, health insurance and the student service fee which includes use of the AECP computer lab, health and sports club and payment for AECP activities. Books, dormitory fees, meal plans and supplies are separate costs which can be paid to the bookstore, Residence Life and Campus Dining.

REFUNDS
You can receive a full refund if you withdraw before classes begin. You can receive a partial refund during the first week of class. A refund schedule will be given to you when you register and pay for classes. There are no refunds after the first week of classes. Refunds are only given if you have decided to return to your country and provide airline reservations as proof, or if you must withdraw for medical reasons and provide a medical note.

PLACEMENT TESTING
Your level in AECP classes will be determined by a placement test. You must be registered in the program in order to take the placement exam. This test is given during registration week and covers the following areas:

  • writing
  • speaking
  • listening and grammar

The placement test usually takes about two hours.

ORIENTATION
After you register for classes at the AECP, you will attend an orientation meeting. In this meeting, the student advisor will give you important information about many things—including money and banking.

The First Week
You should have at least US$3,000 for expenses during your first week in Tempe (tuition, fees, health insurance, housing deposits, books, supplies, etc.). This money should be in travelers' checks or you can pay for your tuition, insurance, and books with a credit card (Visa or Master Card only).

Opening a Bank Account in Tempe
If you plan to stay in Tempe for 16 weeks or longer, you will probably want to open a bank account. It is much safer to keep your money in a bank than to hide it in your room or apartment! There are five banks near the ASU campus. There are also many ATMs (automatic teller machines). For these machines, you can use a special card to get cash if you have a bank account here.
