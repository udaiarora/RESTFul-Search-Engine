November 17, 2005

MBA Mexico City ranked among best in Latin America

ASU’s W. P. Carey MBA Mexico City continues to garner recognition in Mexico and Latin America. The two-year-old program, offered in collaboration with the Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México (ITAM), is ranked second among EMBA programs in Mexico by Expansión, Mexico’s leading magazine for top executives in business and government.

Among rankings for MBA and EMBA programs combined, ITAM and the W. P. Carey MBA Mexico City recently was ranked fourth by AméricaEconomía, Latin America’s leading business publication, and, third by Latin Trade, a Florida-based magazine covering technology, management, finance and investment news in Latin America.

The W. P. Carey School of Business offers a world-class executive MBA program at a specially created facility at ITAM’s Santa Teresa campus in Mexico City. The program brings together two prestigious institutions: ITAM, known for the intellectual rigor and practical relevance of its programs, and the W. P. Carey School, known for its world-class faculty and ability to deliver MBA programs in foreign markets such as China.

The two-year program is designed specifically for senior managers and top-level executives, enabling participants to remain fully engaged in their careers while completing the 18-course curriculum rich in global content and leadership.

Jose Mendez, director of the W. P. Carey MBA Mexico City, says the program fills a need among senior managers and executives in Mexico’s outwardly oriented economy, where change is creating a new business climate that demands the best analytical and management skills to meet the demand of 21st century market forces.

The program was the first in Latin America to be accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB), a nonprofit corporation of educational institutions, corporations and other organizations devoted to the promotion and improvement of higher education in business administration and management.

The program’s cohort of 26 students will visit the W. P. Carey School at ASU’s Tempe campus this month, where they will meet with EMBA alumni, students and faculty, as well as the consul general of Mexico, Carlos Flores, and representatives of Arizona’s government. The group also will network with local business executives and will spend time with their Tempe-based executive MBA counterparts, working on a case study.
