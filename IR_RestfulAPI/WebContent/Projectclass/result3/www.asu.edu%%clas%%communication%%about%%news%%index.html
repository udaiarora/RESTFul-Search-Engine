Tenure-Track Assistant Professors

The Hugh Downs School of Human Communication in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences on the Tempe Campus of Arizona State University is seeking two tenure-track Assistant Professors with a demonstrated record of teaching and research in one or more of the following areas: health communication; interpersonal communication; and/or organizational communication.

The essential duties of this position include teaching courses at the graduate and undergraduate levels; maintaining an ongoing research program in an area of specialty; seeking external research funding; recruiting potentially outstanding students for ASU and the program; contributing to curriculum development; providing service to professional associations and the community; and serving on school, college, and university committees.

Required:  Applicant must hold a Ph.D. in communication or related area at the time of appointment; evidence of potential and/or evidence of excellence in teaching and research.

Desired:  Strong potential for securing external funding to support program of research; demonstrated evidence of excellence in research methodology;  research interests that complement our School-wide strategic initiatives in:  health communication; communication, terrorism, and national security; conflict transformation; and/or wellness and work-life.  For further information please see our website (http://www.asu.edu/clas/communication/).

The HDSHC includes 21 full-time faculty members and offers the BA, BS, MA, and Ph.D. degrees in communication.  The School offers laboratory facilities, microcomputer resources, project support, and a performance studio, as well as access to the Institute for Social Science Research, an ASU research facility and staff devoted to the support of funded research studies and directed by a senior professor from the HDSHC.  The HDSHC is located on the Tempe campus.  Our location offers the resources of a major metropolitan area in a state with spectacular natural scenery and recreational areas, sublime winters, and a culturally rich population.

For optimal consideration application materials must be received by December 1, 2006; the deadline will be extended weekly until search is closed.  Applicants must submit a cover letter specifying interest in the position and the ways in which the qualifications match the required and desired qualifications; curriculum vita; names, addresses, and at least three professional letters of reference; evidence of excellence in teaching (e.g., syllabi, teaching evaluations); and evidence of scholarship (e.g., reprints of published articles).  A background check is required for employment.
