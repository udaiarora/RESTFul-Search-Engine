February 27, 2006

Itule becomes Weil Professor of Journalism

ASU's Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication recently named Bruce Itule, a longtime editor and award-winning journalism professor, as the Weil Family Professor of Journalism.

Itule's tenure at ASU began in 1975 as a journalism professor. After five years, Itule left the university to pursue a city editor position at the Chicago Tribune. In 1985, Itule returned to ASU and served as the director of ASU's student media, which operates the State Press.

He stepped down from his director post in 2001 and rejoined the Cronkite School as a professor.

During his career, Itule has received numerous honors and awards, including:

. The Freedom Forum's Journalism Teacher of the Year in 1999.

. The Arizona Press Club's Distinguished Service Award in 2002.

. Induction into the Arizona Newspaper Association Hall of Fame in 2004.

"I can guarantee that almost every newsroom in this part of the world has a former student crediting their careers to Bruce Itule," says Christopher Callahan, dean of the Cronkite School. "Through his demanding standards, passion for journalism and genuine affection for inspiring young journalists, Bruce has helped create a generation of top-flight reporters and editors."

The professorship is named in honor of Louis "Chip" Weil, former publisher of the Arizona Republic and chairman and chief executive officer of Central Newspapers.

Weil created the endowed professorship through a generous gift in an effort to support "outstanding faculty who will influence the country's future journalists." He retired in 2000 after a career that included positions as publisher of Time magazine and the Detroit News.

"I've known Bruce for a number of years and feel extremely fortunate that he will fulfill this professorship," Weil says.

Itule, a Tucson native, started his career at the Arizona Daily Star while still a journalism student at the University of Arizona. He later held reporting and editing positions at the Boulder Daily Camera, Denver Post, Phoenix Gazette, Minneapolis Star and Chicago Tribune. Itule also has written regularly for Phoenix magazine and Arizona Highways.

As an established author, Itule has written or co-authored seven books, including "News Writing and Reporting for Today's Media," written with Douglas Anderson, former director of the Cronkite School and dean of communications at Penn State University, a book that is entering its seventh edition this year.

"Chip Weil has a wonderful reputation in Arizona and a great passion for journalism," Itule says. "I'm honored to represent him at ASU and the Cronkite School."
