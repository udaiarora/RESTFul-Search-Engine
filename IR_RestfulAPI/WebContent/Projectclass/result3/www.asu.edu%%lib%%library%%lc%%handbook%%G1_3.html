Librarians' Council

G.1.3: Performance-Based Salary Plan

Effective 4/19/95 .. Revised 4/19/95

APPROVED WRITTEN PLAN


The evaluation criteria and procedures are developed through the University Libraries' collegial self-governance body, Librarians' Council. Each academic professional of the University Libraries is a voting member of this council and has the opportunity to provide input into the process of developing personnel policies and procedures.

  1. Evaluation Criteria

    Evaluation criteria are identified in Section E.2 of the Librarians' Handbook.


  2. Evaluation Process

    The University Libraries employ a goal-based model for annual evaluation. This process is described in detail in Section G.1.l of the Librarians' Handbook.

SATISFACTORY PERFORMANCE CRITERION


Performance assessment is an integral component of the formal evaluation process set forth in the Librarians' Handbook. Performance is evaluated on the basis of goal achievement and other criteria relating to professional development and service.

In addition to a narrative assessment of the academic professional's achievements, performance is also assigned a numeric rating based on a multi-tiered scale of merit (see definitions at the end of this section). Numeric performance ratings range from "1" for unsatisfactory performance to "4" for outstanding performance.

SALARY ISSUES

  1. Reward: When available, salary increases may be awarded based on merit as indicated by the merit rating score.

  2. Remedy compression and inversion: The University Libraries' salary structure is currently maintained to avoid inversion and minimize compression as much as possible. Attainment of the salary structure goal defined in item D below will further remedy and avoid future compression and inversion problems.

  3. Correct unlawful discriminatory salary inequities: Such salary inequities are monitored on an ongoing basis and prevented/corrected as needed.

  4. Salary Structure: The goal of the University Libraries' salary structure is to be competitive with external markets, internally equitable, and to reward performance.

  5. The University Libraries' salary structure seeks to attain and maintain salaries equivalent to the 50th percentile of the combined ASU and U of A peer institution salaries.

PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

  1. Performance Areas

    Academic professionals are evaluated on the following criteria as defined and enumerated in the Librarians' Handbook:

    1. Job Performance

    2. Professional Development and Contribution

    3. Service

    4. Contribution to Affirmative Action Goals

  2. Weighting for Job Performance/Professional Development and Service

    For academic professionals, job performance, which contributes to the instructional and research goals of the university, if afforded highest priority. However, academic professionals must also demonstrate activity in the areas of professional development and service.

  3. Retrospective/Prospective Components

    Performance and merit level assessment is generally evaluated for a one year period. However, multiple year retrospective merit assessments may be appropriate under certain conditions and/or may be directed by the university administration. In the absence of a directive from the university administration, the determination of the evaluation period for merit assessment will be made by vote of Librarians' Council.

REVIEW CYCLE


Academic Professionals are evaluated annually in the spring.

PERFORMANCE AGREEMENTS


The appropriate distribution of effort among job performance, professional development, and service for individual academic professionals is established in consultation with the primary evaluator during the annual evaluation and goal-setting process.

APPEAL PROCESS


Appeals of annual evaluations and/or salary increases made on the basis of procedural irregularity follow the procedures outlined in ACD 506-08.

MERIT LEVEL DEFINITIONS

4--Outstanding Merit:


Meets and consistently exceeds goals and responsibilities; achieves results which consistently exceed expectations; demonstrates the highest level of professional excellence and superior quality of work; consistently makes exceptional contributions towards the attainment of University Libraries' strategic goals of Arizona State University; makes outstanding contributions to departmental and team efforts.


3--High Merit:


Meets and exceeds goals and responsibilities; achieves results which exceed expectations; demonstrates a high level of professional excellence and a comparable quality of work; makes significant contributions towards the attainment of University Libraries' strategic directions; consistently supports the policies and strategic goals of Arizona State University; makes significant contributions to departmental and team efforts.


2--Meritorious:


Meets goals and responsibilities; achieves results which meet expectations; demonstrates a high level of professional competence and a comparable quality of work; contributes towards the attainment of University Libraries' strategic directions; consistently supports the policies and strategic goals of Arizona State University; contributes regularly to departmental and team efforts.


1--Unsatisfactory Performance:


Fails to meet goals and responsibilities; fails to achieve results which meet expectations; lacks professional competence and a comparable quality of work; achieves little or no contribution towards the attainment of University Libraries' strategic directions; fails to support the policies and strategic goals of Arizona State University; rarely contributes to departmental and team efforts.
