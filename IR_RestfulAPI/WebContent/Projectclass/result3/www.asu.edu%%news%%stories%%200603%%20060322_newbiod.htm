March 22, 2006

Biodesign Institute boosts state bioscience with new facility

Institute dedicates second phase of research complex

Amidst a packed house of political dignitaries, researchers, and bioindustry representatives, the second phase of the Biodesign Institute was officially dedicated March 21.

“Scientific research is a global enterprise that is intensely competitive,” says George Poste, director of the Biodesign Institute. “To attract top talent and capture grant money for the discoveries of tomorrow, you need world-class facilities.”

The first two phases of Biodesign represent ASU’s flagship foray into rapidly expanding its bioscience research capacity. Building B is a 175,000-square-foot facility that will house between 400 – 500 employees, bringing the institute’s total to nearly 1,000 researchers, students and staff. The new facility connects to the Biodesign Institute’s first building, a 172,000-square-foot facility that was dedicated in December 2004.

The two buildings share a central atrium that stretches the length of one-and-a-half football fields. The facility was recently named R&D Magazine’s 2006 Laboratory of the Year in an international competition. Eventually, two more modular buildings will be added to bring 800,000 square feet of research space to the master-planned complex.

The Biodesign Institute not only creates a confluence of scientific disciplines that fuses biology, engineering and computing under one roof, but the building of the institute would not have been possible without public’s funding in 2000 from Proposition 301 and the support of the Arizona Legislature, which provided funding for Building B in 2003.

“There has been a unique set of circumstances and extraordinary level of cooperation and support at the community and legislative levels to make the Biodesign Institute possible,” says ASU President Michael Crow.
