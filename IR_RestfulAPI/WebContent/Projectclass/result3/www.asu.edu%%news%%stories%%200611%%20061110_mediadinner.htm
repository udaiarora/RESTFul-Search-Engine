November 10, 2006

Crow moderates university leaders in media roundtable

University presidents and chancellors from across the nation, including ASU President Michael Crow, congregated Nov. 9 in New York for an open dialogue with 30 national reporters to discuss issues facing higher education today.

Crow moderated the roundtable discussion for the annual higher education media dinner at Penn Club. Nine university presidents and chancellors from public and private institutions joined the discussion, including: Mary Anne Fox from University of California at San Diego; Freeman Hrabowski from University of Maryland, Baltimore County; Father John I. Jenkins from University of Notre Dame; William Powers, Jr., from University of Texas at Austin; Robert J. Zimmer, University of Chicago; Joel Seligman from University of Rochester; Elaine Hansen, Bates College and Judith Shapiro; Barnard College. Robert Berdahl, president of the Association of American Universities, and David Warren, president of the National Association of Independent College and Universities, also participated in the roundtable.

The dinner discussion remained dynamic and intense throughout the evening, as reporters were able to ask any question to the higher education officials for candid responses. Topics ranged from managing student records to how the power change in Congress would affect higher education. Highlights of the discussion include:

• Bob Jamieson from ABC asked about college tuition increases compared to inflation and whether financial aid is progressing with the increases.
• Steve Drummond from National Public Radio brought up the topic of improving teacher education to better prepare K-12 students for college.
• Alexis Gelber of Newsweek inquired about global competitiveness and how it’s affecting universities’ approach to education.
• Claudia Wallis of Time magazine wanted to know about the future of affirmative action in the higher education setting.

Media attendance included representatives from the New York Times, Associated Press, Time magazine, NBC, National Public Radio, Newsday, Careers & Colleges, CNBC, ABC, Arizona Republic, Inside Higher Education, Chronicle of Higher Education, Los Angeles Times, University Business, Christian Science Monitor, Popular Mechanics and Newsweek.
