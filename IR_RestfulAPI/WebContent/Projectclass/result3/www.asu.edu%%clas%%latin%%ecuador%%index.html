THE ANDES AND AMAZON FIELD SCHOOL

SUMMER - June 6-30 (session 1); July 4-28 (session 2)
WINTER - December 27-January 12, 2008


Experience the Amazon Rainforest

while studying Spanish or fulfilling distribution requirements for the undergraduate degree
Open to adventurous students and alumni from any college or university
Applications are processed on a first come first served basis until all spaces are filled

 Location: Santo Urcu Amazonian Quichua Community on the banks of the Napo River, a tributary of the Amazon, and the most biodiverse region of the world. Here students are immersed in Kichwa life. Camp in virgin rainforest. Listen to the singing of ayahuasca shamans, clear forest with machetes to plant manioc and much more ...

  Classes:
  SPA 101/102201/202  Elementary Spanish; Intermediate Spanish          4 credits
  FLA 494/590              Kichwa Language (Levels 1-3)                             4 credits
  ASB 294/494/598       Amazonian Culture & Sustainable Environment  1-3 credits
  PLB 498/591              Tropical Plant Diversity                                       3 credits
  PLB/ASB/REL            Amazonian Ethnobotany                                 1-3 credits
  REL 305                    Amazonian Myth Ritual & Symbol                       3 credits
  REL 332                    South American Indian Religious Traditions          3 credits
                                   Internships                                                        3 credits

Program Director: Professor Tod Swanson is Director of ASU’s Center for Latin American Research and a leading interpreter of Amazonian culture and environment. Swanson was raised in the Ecuadorian Amazon as the son of missionary parents and is fl uent in Spanish and Kichwa. During the summer months he resides at the Field School site with his wife and four children. He has hosted student groups at this location every year since 1997.”

  Cost: The cost of $1,440 per Summer Session or $1,250 for Winter Session includes lodging, three meals a day,
 ground transportation in Ecuador, all excursions and entrance fees. Not Included: ASU tution, air fare to Ecuador
                                            (approx. $950 with group rate), $32 airport exit tax, passport, health insurance and personal expenses.
