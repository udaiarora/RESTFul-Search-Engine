C.2.3: Review Committee for Personnel Action

Effective 12/20/89 .. Revised 08/23/93

 

The Review Committee for Personnel Action is one of the Standing Committees of the Librarians' Council. It consists of six (6) elected members and the representative of the Personnel Committee.

 

Five of the six members must be either: (a) Academic Professionals or Associate Academic Professionals with continuing appointments or (b) Academic Professionals or Associate Academic Professionals with year-to-year appointments and at least six years of experience. All elected members serve a two (2) year term except for the probationary academic professional, who serves a one (1) year term.

 

The representative of the Personnel Committee and the probationary Assistant Academic Professional serve in an advisory capacity and are non-voting members.

 

RESPONSIBILITIES

 

 

The Review Committee is responsible for all personnel actions requiring peer review and recommendations. Responsibilities include, but are not limited to, the following:
 

 

 

  1. Review the experience of academic professional candidates (the Review Committee will not consider rank of newly hired academic professionals without previous professional experience) to recommend appropriate ranks.


    2.  

  2. Review the performance evaluations of academic professionals with probationary status and provide the appropriate recommendations.


    3.  

  3. Review promotion requests and provide the appropriate recommendations.


    4.  

  4. Review requests for sabbatical or professional leaves of absence and provide the appropriate recommendations.


    5.  

  5. Serve as an advisory group to academic professionals on techniques of applying for promotion and the handling of personnel actions within the jurisdiction of this committee.


    6.  

  6. Perform pre-continuing appointment reviews for all probationary academic professionals to whom the review procedures apply (cf. G.2.1 of the Librarians' Handbook).

 

PROCEDURES STATEMENT

 

 

Voting will occur when consensus can not be reached and only after a systematic and thorough review of the case at hand. When a vote takes place, at least three voting members must be present. The Review Committee makes its recommendation to the Dean of the University Libraries in the form of a report which will provide justification for the recommendation. The Chair of the Review Committee is prohibited from submitting any application for personnel action. In addition, a committee member may neither attend the deliberations nor vote on an application in either of the following instances:

 

 

  • If the packet under consideration is his/her own;
     

  • If the committee member is the primary evaluator of the academic professional under review.

 

MEETINGS

 

 

For probation, promotion and sabbatical leave reviews, the committee's schedule follows the annual Schedule of Personnel-Related Activities distributed by the Libraries' Administration Office at the beginning of each academic year.

 

 

Rank and equivalency deliberations for new academic professionals are conducted for all final candidates and whenever possible scheduled as soon as possible following the candidates' interviews. One or more members of the Review Committee will attend the open meeting and solicit clarification of candidates' experience in regard to rank and equivalency.

 

 

In addition to those meetings the committee shall meet at least twice at the beginning of the fiscal year to orient new members on policies and procedures and once at the end of the year to select a Chair and Secretary for the coming year.

 

 

For pre-continuing appointment reviews, deliberations are conducted in the last part of the fiscal year, as soon as the final recommendation for a renewed contract has been approved by the Academic Vice President. The review will be conducted by the Chair of the Review Committee in a meeting with the reviewee, primary evaluator, and Assistant Dean for Personnel. All pre-continuing appointment reviews will be completed no later than the end of the fiscal year (June 30th).
