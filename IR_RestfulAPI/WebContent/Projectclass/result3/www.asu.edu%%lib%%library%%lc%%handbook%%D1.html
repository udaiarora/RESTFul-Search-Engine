D.1: Initial Appointment

Effective 8/22/91 .. Revised 8/22/91

LIBRARIANS:

 

 

A person appointed as a librarian at Arizona State University must have earned a graduate degree in library science from a school accredited by the American Library Association. Foreign degrees in library science are acceptable if equivalency can be established. A master's degree in library science will be considered the terminal degree for the purpose of establishing professional credentials as a librarian. Librarians without the stipulated degree who were appointed prior to November 1980 shall be considered to hold that degree for purposes of retention, continuing appointment, and promotion.

 


ARCHIVISTS:

 

 

A person appointed to an archival position at Arizona State University must have earned a graduate degree appropriate to the assignment. Archival education or experience in accordance with the proposed guidelines of the Society of American Archivists plus the advanced degree will be the minimum for establishing professional credentials as an archivist.
Beyond these basic requirements, vacancies are filled in accordance with standard library and university procedures for recruitment, selection, and appointment of academic professional staff.

 

 

LEARNING RESOURCES SPECIALIST:

 

 

A person appointed as a learning resources specialist at Arizona State University must have earned a graduate degree in educational technology or a related field from a university accredited by the National Council for Accreditation in Teacher Education.

 

 

The designation of rank at time of hire will be determined by past academic training and comparable instructional technology demonstrated professional achievements at a level consistent with rank definitions.
