Graduate Division Wins Award for Diversity Initiative, Sweeps National Competition

ASU's Division of Graduate Studies (DGS) has won a national award for Pathways to Success, an initiative it launched this year in partnership with the Barrett Honors College and the Office of the Vice President for Undergraduate Initiatives to promote diversity in graduate education.

The DGS accepted the 2004 Peterson's Award for Innovation in Promoting an Inclusive Graduate Community from the Council of Graduate Schools at its annual meeting in Washington, D.C., in December. This annual award recognizes innovative institutional programs that seek the identification, recruitment, retention, and graduation of minority graduate students and can serve as models for other institutions.

“Diversity is an essential ingredient of today's graduate campus,” said Milton Glick, Executive Vice President and Provost of the University. “The recognition and support this award brings will be instrumental in recruiting and retaining talented minority students.”

ASU is the only university to receive the Peterson's Award, and the only university in the country to receive all three grant awards given by the council, the only national association dedicated to representing and advancing graduate education. ASU effectively swept this year's competition, winning two other grant awards for projects that focus on the responsible conduct of research and enhancing doctoral completion rates. The winning proposals were written by Vice Provost and Dean of Graduate Studies Maria T. Allison and Associate Deans Marjorie Zatz and Filiz Ozel.

“We were thrilled to find all three received funding,” Allison said. “The Peterson's Award was the icing on the cake, not only because it was the only one given nationally, but because we are so committed to enhancing the diversity of our graduate student population.”

Pathways to Success is unique in its emphasis on the master's degree as an integral and often overlooked step along the path toward the doctorate. The program is designed specifically to create a series of bridges to enhance the recruitment and retention of ASU's most promising underrepresented juniors and seniors from the bachelor's, to master's and professional degrees, and, ultimately, to the doctorate. The goal is to create an expanded and sustainable pipeline for talented students who are underrepresented in their fields. The centerpiece of the initiative is the Reach for the Stars Fellowship program, which is funded through the partnership, and will provide financial support to qualified underrepresented students pursuing graduate studies.

“The national data are very clear; the number of underrepresented students pursuing and completing graduate degrees in this country is much lower than it should be," Allison said. "This award provides incentives for ASU to pursue a host of strategies to build the educational pipelines for our most talented Arizona undergraduates. This is an essential strategy not just for ASU; it builds the quality of our graduate student population while simultaneously enhancing Arizona's future workforce.”

Pathways to Success will coordinate efforts already underway to develop a diverse graduate student population and build upon these activities to develop an expanded focused university initiative. For example, research partnerships will be created to join undergraduate and graduate students with faculty, drawing upon current professional development programs. Also, existing peer mentoring programs will be expanded, as will workshops about how to prepare, apply, and pay for graduate school. In addition, a council of faculty, administrators, and undergraduate and graduate students has been created to develop proposals on how to reduce barriers underrepresented students face in entering and successfully completing master's and doctoral programs.
