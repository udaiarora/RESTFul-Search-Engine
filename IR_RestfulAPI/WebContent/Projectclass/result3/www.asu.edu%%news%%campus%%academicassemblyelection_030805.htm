March 8, 2005

Academic Assembly election candidates unveiled

Candidates for the Academic Assembly election have been announced by Pauline Kommenich, chair of the Committee on Committees, and Barbara Kerr, Academic Senate president.

Candidates for president-elect of the Assembly/Senate are Duane Roen, professor of English, and Mary Rothschild, professor of women’s studies.

Running for secretary of the Assembly/Senate is Anne Kopta, associate professor of music in voice.

Candidates for committee membership include:

• Academic Freedom and Tenure Committee: Gaile Cannella, professor of curriculum and instruction; Petra Fromme, professor of chemistry and biochemistry; Ernest Hirata, associate professor, information and management technology. (One candidate elected for three years.)

• Academic Professional Grievance Committee: Frank Davis, research professional senior, chemistry and biochemistry; Jeannette Mueller-Alexander, librarian, Library Reference Service; Julie Tharp, associate librarian, Library Reference Service. (Three elected – one member for three years, and two alternates for one year each.)

• Academic Professional Status Committee: Beth DiFelice, assistant director, Law Library; Judy Grace, director, Center for Learning and Teaching Excellence; Lydia LaFaro, librarian, Library Reference Service; Brenda Shears, research administrator, International Institute for Sustainability; John Wheatley, research professional senior, Center for Solid State Science; Barry Wilkens, research professional senior, Center for Solid State Science. (Six elected – four members for three-year terms and two alternates for one year each.)

Additional candidates also may be nominated in petitions signed by at least 50 members of the Academic Assembly. Petitions must be received by the chair of the Committee on Committees before 5 p.m., March 18, at the Academic Senate Office, Administration B Wing, room 361.

Voting will be through an electronic process. Instructions will be sent to members of the Academic Assembly via e-mail.

Candidates’ statements are available on the Academic Senate Web page (www.asu.edu/provost/asenate/documents/ELECTONSTATEMENTS2005.pdf).
