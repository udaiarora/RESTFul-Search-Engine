Technical Standards:  ANSI

 

ANSI - American National Standards Institute

  • Where are they located?
  • How are they arranged?
  • How do I search them?
  • What is an ANSI approved standard?

 

 

Where are they located?

  • They are located in filing cabinets in the northeast corner of the first floor of the Noble Science Library
  • ANSI Standards do not circulate, they must be used in the library

 

How are they arranged?
They are arranged in alphaneumeric order giving : sponsoring agency, standard number and year of revision

  • ANSI Z39.7 1995
  • ANSI/AWS D10.12 1989

      •     T 59.2 .U6 I5  Science Reference Desk

 

What is an ANSI approved standard?

ANSI has served as national coordinator of voluntary standards activities and as approval organization and clearinghouse for consensus standards since 1918. ANSI does not write standards, but approves and publishes standards developed and written by various engineering, industrial and professional groups.

ASU began an annual subscription with ANSI in 1959, keeping the most current edition of a standard. Beginning in 1982, Noble Reference has kept all editions of a standard.

For more on ANSI and its mission please visit About ANSI.
