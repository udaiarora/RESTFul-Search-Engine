November 12, 2003

Receptions give opportunity to show appreciation for support

Though faced with excruciating budget decisions this past year, the Arizona State Legislature still managed to be very kind to ASU. Alumni Association President Diane McCarthy says it is now time to thank them for their support, and to discuss with them key university issues.

McCarthy encourages faculty and staff to attend one of the legislative Thank You Receptions being held this fall to thank legislators for their assistance in the passage of House Bill 2529 last June.

"We asked the legislature for that money at the worst possible time, and they really stepped up and made it happen," McCarthy says. "It is important we thank them."
The research infrastructure bill secured an investment of $440 million in state funds for research infrastructure at Arizona's three universities. ASU's portion will total about $185 million.

The receptions also provide an intimate venue for employees to talk to legislators about issues important to ASU.

"By attending these receptions, ASU employees have the valuable opportunity to give this message to their legislators one on one in a personal setting," she says.
McCarthy stresses that the most important sales people for the university are the people who work for and care about the university.

"Employees who understand the issues affecting the university are able to sell the importance of the university in a very personal way," she says.
