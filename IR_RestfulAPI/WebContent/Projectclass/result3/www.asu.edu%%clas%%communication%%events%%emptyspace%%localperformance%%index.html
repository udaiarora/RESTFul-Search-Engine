Local Theatre - Alternative:

Theater In My Basement

Local theater collective that I housed at Modified Arts. The work is usually non-conventional, political, and challenging to the audience. A great group that is always putting up new and developing work

http://timb.org

Stray Cat Theatre

Local theater company started by ASU theater alumni. The work has consistently received strong critical support. While the company does construct a full season in advance, the company has gained a reputation for taking on edgier works. http://straycattheatre.org/

Nearly Naked Theater

Local theater company with a strong GLBTQ focus.

http://www.nearlynakedtheatre.org/

Black Theater Troupe- Phoenix

http://www.blacktheatretroupe.org/

 

Local Theatre - Traditional Venue

ASU Gammage Auditorium

On campus, the Grady Gammage Auditorium has a full season of productions, usually touring companies of Broadway shows.

http://www.asugammage.com

Actor’s Theater of Phoenix – Herberger Theater

http://actorstheatrephx.org/

Arizona Theater Company

Regional professional theater company. Emphasis on larger productions, classical works, and mainstream  audiences.

http://www.aztheatreco.org

 

Alternative Performance Spaces
(ideas to see new work or get your work out there!)

Modified Arts on Roosevelt

Art gallery and performance space. Home of Theater in my Basement, as well as the Teatro Caliente Festival each fall.

www.modified.org

The Icehouse

Literally an old ice house, this historic building downtown is known for hosting performance work, as well as larger social events.

www.icehouseaz.com

 

The Paper Heart on Grand Avenue

Art venue catering to visual, music, dance, theatre, spoken word/poetry, performance art, video/film and comedy.

http://www.thepaperheart.com/

 

Alwun House Gallery & Gardens

1204 E. Roosevelt Street

(602) 253-7887

1912 historic bungalow houses gallery, lush gardens, pond & patios. Home of the annual sextasy ball.

www.alwunhouse.org

There are many alternative spaces in both the Roosevelt and Grand neighborhoods that are performance friendly. For a more detailed list of local galleries, see http://www.artlinkphoenix.com/advenues.htm
