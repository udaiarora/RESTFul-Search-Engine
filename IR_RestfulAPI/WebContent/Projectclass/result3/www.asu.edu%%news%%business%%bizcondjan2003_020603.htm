February 6, 2003

AZ Business Conditions Index rises again in January

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index continued its upward trend in January, reaching 51.1 - its second month above the critical 50-point benchmark. An index reading over 50 indicates that the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

Most of the upward movement is due to an astounding 6.5-point rise in the Production component, which reached 57.9 in January, according to Dawn McLaren, an analyst with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center. The Employment sub-index has yet to break the 50-point mark, but it also increased - by 2.1 points, to reach 43.0 in January.

"The high level of productivity experienced over the last few months is not a sustainable situation in the long run," McLaren said. "Either production has to slow or employment has to rise. The movement of these two sub-indices in the coming months will tell how the situation will resolve."

The New Orders sub-index fell by 2.3 points. McLaren said that, despite the January decline, the 53.4 reading "marks the third consecutive month that the New Orders sub-index has recorded a level over 50 points." She noted that New Orders is "one of the most forward-looking of the components."

The Price Index rose slightly from 55.1 in December to 56.5 in January. "This indicates the presence of some upward pressure on prices paid for major commodities used in production," McLaren said. The highest level reached by the Price Index was 95.5 in December 1973, during an oil crisis.

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available online at www.cob.asu.edu/seid/eoc/azpmi/. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data available in prn format.
