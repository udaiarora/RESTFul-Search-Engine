USEFUL LINKS

 
        

        

       

   

 

 

ECONOMIC AND SOCIAL RESEARCH COUNCIL PROGRAMME

 http://www.ccsr.ac.uk/methods/

A website for the Economic and Social Research Programme of the UK, the purpose of this site mirrors the purpose of the Programme: “to improve the standards of research methods across the UK social science community.” Includes information on programs events and budding research.

 

 

QUALITATIVE RESEARCH IN INFORMATION SYSTEMS:

http://www.qual.auckland.ac.nz/: 

A site devoted to the discussion of qualitative research in information systems.  The site is intended to aid in the process of gathering and using qualitative research.  Included:  philosophical perspectives on qualitative research, qualitative research techniques, and methods for analyzing compiled data.

 

QUALITATIVE RESEARCH SITE

http://kerlins.net/bobbi/research/qualresearch/

A website dedicated to educating new research on the various aspects of qualitative methods.  Includes an extensive bibliography page sorted by topics ranging from Interviewing techniques to conceptual frameworks. 

 

 

QUALITATIVE MEASURES

http://trochim.human.cornell.edu/kb/qual.htm

A site for beginners in qualitative research.  Discusses the pro’s and con’s of this type of research, offers basic introductions to methods and approaches.

 

THE QUALITATIVE REPORT

http://www.nova.edu/ssss/QR/index.html

Free online journal dedicated to qualitative research.  Offers listings of research projects, course materials, current research and more.

 

UNIVERSITY OF MIAMI LIBRARIES

http://www.library.miami.edu/netguides/psymeth.html

An extensive guide to available resources.  Divided into the following categories: Test and Measures, Survey Methods, Quantitative, Qualitative, Research and Writing, and Software. 

 

RESEARCH RESOURCE METHODS ON THE WWW

http://www.slais.ubc.ca/resources/research_methods/general.htm

A page featuring links to articles, syllabi, books and other resources on all aspects of qualitative research.  Lists are divided by specific topic.

  

QUALITATIVE RESEARCH BIBLIOGRAPHY

http://www.twu.edu/as/ws/biblio.htm

A list of books and articles that have addressed issues in Qualitative Methods.  Includes bibliographic information and a short summary.

 

 

QUALITATIVE RESEARCH JOURNALS

http://www.nova.edu/ssss/QR/QR1-4/wark.html

A list of journals that have published or have expressed a willingness to publish research in qualitative methods.

 

 

RESEARCH METHODS

http://www.valt.helsinki.fi/staff/mmattila/emethods.htm

The site is not dedicated solely to qualitative research, but does cover the topic.  It specifically offers information on computer programs that can be used for qualitative research in Political Science.
