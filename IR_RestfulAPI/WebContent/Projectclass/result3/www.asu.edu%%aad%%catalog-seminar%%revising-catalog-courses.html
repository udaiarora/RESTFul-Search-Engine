Course Information Requiring Approvals

Permanent courses: Adding, changing, and deleting

•Submit all additions, changes, and deletions through ACRES (http://az.transfer.org/cgi-bin/WebObjects/acres). Contact your Department Admin (first) or the ACRES Administrator to request an account.
•The Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University provides a “Curricular Processing Flow Calendar.” See page 7 of this Word document for the approval process and dates for new courses, course deletions, and course changes. If you have questions about this calendar, contact Nancy Kiernan by e-mail at nancy.kiernan@asu.edu or by phone (5-4091).
•I monitor ACRES activity. I release all approved courses for print and Web publication (through a database).
Note:A PDF draft of all ASU pending and approved courses (as well as the General Studies table) can be found at http://www.asu.edu/aad/catalog-seminar/ASU-Courses.pdf. You can also search the database at https://sec.was.asu.edu/coursedb/search/search.jsp.

General Studies: Adding and deleting designations

Forms, checklists, and information needed to add and delete General Studies designations can be found at www.asu.edu/duas/genstudies. This site includes links for faculty and staff at ASU Polytechnic, Tempe, and West campuses.

Procedures for requesting a General Studies designation can we found at http://uc.asu.edu/curriculum/gsdasu.php.

If you have questions about General Studies, contact Phyllis Lucie by e-mail at phyllis.lucie@asu.edu or by phone (5-0739).

Fees and deposits: Adding and deleting a special class fee or deposit

A Microsoft Word version of the “Request for Special Class Fees and Deposits” form can be downloaded for fees of $50 or less and for fees of more than $50. The site for “Academic Affairs Forms” also includes links for curriculum check sheets, Standardized Major Information Sheets, calendars, and information needed to add academic degree programs.

If you have questions about adding, changing, or deleting a fee, contact Nancy Kiernan by e-mail at nancy.kiernan@asu.edu or by phone (5-4091).

Course Information Not Requiring Approvals

Omnibus courses, semester offered, teaching method, fixing typos

Changes to omnibus courses, semester offered, and teaching method do not require official approval; no ACRES request is needed. You can send changes to me by e-mail or on a marked printout of the Web courses. Deliver the Web printout, marked in red, to FOUND 420. Sign or initial the printouts so I know who is authorizing these course changes.

Before sending changes to me, check the approved and pending course information. Approved course information can be found on the Web at www.asu.edu/aad/catalogs/courses. Approved information in the future can be searched from a database A PDF draft of this same information (including the General Studies table), as well as courses pending approval, can be found at http://www.asu.edu/aad/catalog-seminar/ASU-Courses.pdf.

Note:I will post omnibus courses and changes to semester offered immediately in the database. If you want these changes effective for some future semester/year, let me know.

Information about omnibus courses can be found in the current catalog in the “Classification of Courses” section.

Other Helpful Information

Helpful rules: Cross-listed courses

For the purposes of the catalog courses (paper and Web), the following rules apply to cross-listed courses:
1.Permanent courses cannot be cross-listed to omnibus courses (and vice versa).
2.Cross-listed courses cannot exist within the same prefix.
3.Cross-listed courses must match in every way except for prefix and number; prefixes cannot match; numbers can match but don’t need to.
4.Cross-listed courses must match in number at the same level (e.g., lower-division with lower-division and upper-division with upper-division courses).
The following phrase is attached to each cross-listed course and should prevent misunderstanding on getting credit twice:
Cross-listed as ABC 123. Credit is allowed for only ABC 123 or DEF 123.
Note:If a cross-listing is being added, changed, or deleted, you must verify the ACRES request with the cross-listed department(s).

Catalog course Web postings

Approved course information is posted on the Web (from a database) twice a year and corresponds to the Schedule of Classes. Approved courses for the following fall semester are posted during spring break; approved courses for the following spring semester are posted on Halloween.
