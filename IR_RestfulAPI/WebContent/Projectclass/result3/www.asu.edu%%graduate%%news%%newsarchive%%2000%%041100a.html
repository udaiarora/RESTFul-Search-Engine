ASU GRADUATE PROGRAMS RAPIDLY GAINING STATURE AMONG NATION'S LEADING PUBLIC RESEARCH UNIVERSITIES

A national publication reports that the quality of graduate education at Arizona State University rates highly among the nation's leading universities. The U.S. News & World Report validates ASU's commitment to graduate education: to prepare students to become scholarly and professional leaders in a new century in which technology will continue to transform the boundaries of knowledge.

ASU's national stature has been enhanced by the latest rankings of graduate programs by the U.S. News & World Report magazine and Web site (www.usnews.com/usnews/edu/beyond/bchome.htm ).

Bianca L. Bernstein, Dean of the Graduate College, said, "We are seeing a marked increase in the visibility and recognition of our many strong and innovative graduate and professional programs at ASU. It is true that our programs are getting demonstrably better; but as the new U.S. News & World Report rankings illustrate, we are also becoming more effective in communicating to disciplinary and community members what we do well, through the exceptional work of our faculty, staff and students. ASU is rapidly moving up in the cadre of our nation's best public research universities."

This year, U.S. News & World Report ranked programs in business, education, health, the arts, medicine, Ph.D. programs, law, engineering, public affairs and library science. Other programs, such as architecture, are not ranked on an annual basis.

Florence Nelson, chair of the ASU Graduate College Council of Advisors, said, “Arizona State University has earned a high level of respect nationally from students and educators, and the latest rankings by U.S. News & World Report confirm that. ASU has quickly established a record of excellence with its high-caliber programs and faculty, attracting quality students. This is a winning combination for the university and the community that will only continue to expand and improve.”

All 10 colleges at ASU, a Research I University, are currently represented in rankings by U.S. News & World Report. ASU holds top 25 rankings in 26 areas of graduate programming and holds rankings in a total of 39 areas in all. A complete listing of the rankings follows the story.

Aside from the U.S. News & World Report rankings, ASU ranks fifth and seventh nationally, respectively, in the number of Native American first professional degrees and Hispanic-American doctoral degrees awarded.

Among peer institutions, ASU ranks third in producing master's level graduates and ninth in doctoral level graduates.

ASU devotes considerable attention to mentoring relationships between professors and students as a key element of the graduate experience. The Graduate College honors outstanding mentors, and a new fellowship program will provide support for students who work with the winners of the Outstanding Graduate Mentor Award. Ninety percent of new graduate alumni said they selected ASU because of the reputation of the faculty.

The Graduate College offers a diverse group of interdisciplinary programs in fields that are emerging as recognized academic disciplines, as well as certificate programs for professionals with advanced degrees who seek further career development. Graduate students benefit from Graduate College support programs as well as from partnerships between ASU and public/private businesses and agencies. The Preparing Future Faculty program and the Preparing Future Professionals program prepare students for academic and non-academic careers, respectively.

The Graduate College serves as an advocate for all advanced studies at ASU in the state, nationally and internationally. It coordinates and supports graduate programs on the Main and East campuses and is a liaison with advanced studies at ASU West and other universities. ASU offers a full range of professional and academic programs for more than 10,000 graduate students. ASU's 91 master's and 48 doctoral degree programs attract graduate students of diverse ethnic and cultural backgrounds.
