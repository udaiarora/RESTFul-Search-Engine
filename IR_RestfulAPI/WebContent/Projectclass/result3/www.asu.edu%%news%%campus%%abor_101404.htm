October 14, 2004

Regents approve $1.26 billion state operating budget requests

The Arizona Board of Regents (ABOR) approved the universities’ fiscal year 2006 state operating budget requests totaling $1.26 billion, a $110.9 million increase representing a 9.7 percent increase over the fiscal year 2005 base and a 14 percent increase over the fiscal year 2005 general fund base.

ASU requested a little more than $533,000 for their operating budget, a 9.8 percent increase of the total base and a 15.5 percent increase of the fiscal year 2005 general fund base.

Among the strategic investments requests, ASU is asking for $12.5 million to develop a digital education platform for teaching and learning to help students succeed. In addition, the university is hoping for an additional $8 million for faculty start-up funds, which will be used to recruit faculty from other institutions.

“This is our ‘state investment request,’ ” says ASU President Michael Crow. “What we are building is a university that focuses on providing student access, strategic investments with community initiatives and achieving student success. Our request is important for the future of this institution, its faculty, its staff and its students.”

The board-approved budget requests for Arizona ’s three universities were submitted to the governor and the Legislature on Oct. 4. The governor will make a recommendation before the start of the legislative session in January. The Joint Legislative Budget Committee will present its recommendations first; the state legislators then will have hearings for all state agencies, including the universities. The budget typically is finalized by late spring.

The ABOR also approved the following items:

• The new five-year strategic plans for fiscal year 2006-10 for the major budget units in the Arizona university system: Northern Arizona University, ASU’s Tempe campus, ASU’s East campus, ASU’s west campus, the University of Arizona and the ABOR central office. The plans are to be submitted to the executive and legislative branches of government in draft form by Nov. 1.

• The 2006-08 Capital Improvement Plans for ASU, NAU and UA. ASU’s first year of its plan includes three projects at an estimated cost of $1.43 million (Biodesign Institute at ASU Building C, Infrastructure Improvements Phase IV, Memorial Union Upgrades) and a third-party financed downtown Phoenix campus complex Phase II.

• The Interdisciplinary Science and Technology Building III, a structure measuring about 39,400 gross square feet at ASU’s East campus, at an estimated cost of $12 million. This building will accommodate the research needs for the Applied Cognitive Science Institute, the Healthy Lifestyles Institute, the Applied Biological Sciences, and certain biotechnology components of the Biodesign Institute at ASU.

ABOR launched the Arizona Regents University (ARU) Web site in fall 2000, which links distance learning programs at ASU, UA and NAU. The regents said they want to expand the program to become a stand-alone entity in which students will enroll in one of the three state schools, but take their pick of online courses offered by the three universities on the site.

During the study session on Sept. 30, two presentations were made by WCET (Western Cooperative for Educational Technology) and Hezel Associates, which offered background information, alternatives, and specific suggestions for action regarding systemwide online student services, and a recommended strategic roadmap for the role of ARU.

The regents will vote on the new ARU plan at its next meeting, scheduled Nov. 18-19 in Tucson at the University of Arizona .

For more information about the Arizona Board of Regents, visit the Web at (http://www.abor.asu.edu/).
