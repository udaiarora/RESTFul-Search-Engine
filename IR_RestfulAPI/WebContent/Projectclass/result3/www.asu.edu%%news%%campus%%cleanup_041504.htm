April 15, 2004

Clean up - and celebrate -- the Earth on April 22

What better way to celebrate Earth Day than by helping make Arizona State University's patch of Mother Earth a little cleaner?

ASU Clean & Beautiful and ASU Recycling are combining, for the first time, Earth Day and Campus Clean-up.

On April 22 (Thursday), ASU Clean & Beautiful will sponsor its annual Spring Semester Clean-up Rally from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m., and ASU Recycling will have displays and information on Hayden Lawn during that same time period.

ASU faculty, staff and students are invited to pick up litter bags at the ASU Clean & Beautiful tent on Hayden Mall, then return them filled with trash. Students may earn one hour of community service, and there will be prizes.

Clubs, departments and campus organizations may challenge other departments to see who can pick up the most trash.

In honor of Earth Day, ASU Recycling will have displays of bales showing what gets recycled at ASU and how it impacts energy and natural-resource savings.

And, there will be a "trash analysis" where an ASU trash can will be emptied on a table to see how much of the contents could have been recycled.

For more information on the Clean-up Rally or Earth Day, contact Andrea Lee, (480) 965-4414, or Patricia Chase, (480) 965-1850.
