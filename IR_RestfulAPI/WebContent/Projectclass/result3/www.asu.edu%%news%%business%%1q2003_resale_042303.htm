April 23, 2003

First quarter 2003 resale market sets record

Despite war news and continuing economic concerns, the Greater Phoenix resale housing market set a record for first quarter with 15,030 recorded sales, according to the Arizona Real Estate Center at the W. P. Carey School of Business. Still, first quarter 2003 was down from the 15,780 sales recorded in fourth quarter 2002.

"Even though people may still lack confidence in the economy and/or the international situation, they appear to be confidant about the long-term expectations for their housing investment. Resale activity in the first quarter 2003 was appreciably strong," said Dr. Jay Q. Butler, director of the Arizona Real Estate Center. "January sales totaled 4,215, with 4,090 sales in February and 6,725 during a very strong March. The strength of the housing market - both in the number of sales and the strong appreciation in prices - continues to be one of the few bright points for the economy."

"However, bear in mind that the current recorded sales activity represents decisions made several months ago," Butler said. "For last several months, the housing market has been positively reviewed as to the benefits of the low interest rates, rapid increase in prices and the investment opportunities. Further, there is increasing discussion that the housing market will retain its strength for 2003, even if the economy recovers with potentially higher interest rates."

The previous record for a first quarter was set in 2001 at 13,275 recorded sales. First quarter 2002 fell some 600 sales below the high mark, logging 12,655.

The median home price also set a record at $148,750, topping the $147,000 media price set in the fourth quarter 2002 and $139,700 a year ago. Since the Greater Phoenix area is so large, the median price can range significantly from $381,060 in North Scottsdale to $87,000 in the western areas of Phoenix, Butler explained.

Since the resale townhouse/condominium housing sector set the record of 3,685 sales in second quarter 2002, it has slowly declined to 3,215 sales for first quarter 2003. Since this market is good source of affordable housing and investment opportunities, the strong increase in the median price from last year's $94,000 to the current $101,000 could be limiting the market. This market can also have significant range in median prices from $164,800 in North Scottsdale to $85,500 in South Mesa.

The median square footage for a single-family home recorded sold in first quarter 2003 was 1,640 square feet, which is larger than the 1,610 square feet reported a year ago. In the townhouse/condominium sector, the median square footage was 1,185 square feet, which is larger than the 1,160 square feet reported a year ago.

Highlights of first quarter 2003:
· Recorded sales in the City of Phoenix improved from 3,780 in first quarter 2002 to 4,270 sales, while the median sales price increased from $115,000 to $122,945. In the townhouse/condominium sector, sales activity decreased from 850 to 800 sales, but the median sales price moved from $85,000 to $93,000.

· The Scottsdale resale home market increased from last year's 1,310 recorded sales to 1,530 recorded sales. The median sales price continued its upward movement by increasing from $275,000 to $300,000. The townhouse/condominium sector in Scottsdale also improved from 645 to 745 sales, while the median sales price increased from $145,000 to $150,000.

· The Mesa resale housing market grew strongly from 1,590 to 1,915 sales, while the median price increased from $129,000 to $134,900. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 405 to 470 sales and the median home price increased from $84,900 to $89,945.

· Glendale strengthened from 1,075 to 1,290 recorded sales and the median sales price increased from $129,000 to $136,200. The townhouse/condominium sector remained at 150 sales, while the median sales price fell from $76,450 to $74,900.

· In comparison to a year ago, the Sun City resale market increased from 340 to 400 sales, with the median sales price increasing from $100,000 to $108,900. Resale activity in Sun City West increased from 275 to 310 recorded sales and the median sales price increased from $133,000 to $151,000. The townhouse/condominium market in Sun City remained at 265 sales, but the median sales price increased from $72,000 to $77,000. In Sun City West, sales also were stable at 80 sales, but the median sales price increased from $89,500 to $105,000.

· The resale market in Gilbert improved from 680 to 870 sales, while the median sales price increased from $156,700 to $167,000. The townhouse/condominium market was increased from 50 to 60 sales and the median sales price increased from $109,750 to $113,450.

· For the City of Chandler, the resale market moved from 1,040 to 1,245 recorded sales, while the median sales price increased from $147,500 to $156,900. The townhouse/condominium market improved from 130 to 145 sales and the median sales price increased from $97,000 to $105,000.

· The resale market in Tempe improved from 415 to 445 sales, with the median sales price increasing from $153,500 to $161,000. The townhouse/condominium sector remained at 185 sales, while the median sales price increased from $100,000 to $102,650.

· The highest median sales price was in Paradise Valley at $772,525 with a median square foot house of 3,480 square feet.

· In the West Valley, the following communities represent 5 percent of the resale market, while it stood at 4 percent for a year ago:
*Avondale up from 145 at $134,900 to 180 sales at $145,000.
*El Mirage up from 50 sales ($104,000) to 100 sales ($111,700).
*Goodyear up from 115 sales ($163,400) to 170 sales ($155,000).
*Surprise up from 210 at ($126,700) to 355 sales ($135,950).
