Jacque Mercer

Jacque Mercer was born and raised in Litchfield Park, Arizona. She grew up in a rural setting and was a 4-H member, where her many talents were nurtured, particularly her sense of design. Most of the clothing used for the various contests were designed and sewn by her.

An ambitious, talented and intelligent young person, in addition to her natural beauty she developed a resume for success. Jacque earned awards in the 4-H at an early age; was valedictorian of her grade school; modeled throughout her high school years; performed as a lead in class plays; was a member of the National Honor Society; maintained honor roll status in high school and college; and was seriously interested in the theatre. Her degree from Arizona State University is in Fine Art in 1954 where she majored in theatre and costume design.

With aspirations to be in the theatre she pursued the beauty pageant that was an accepted course for a young rural girl to gain public recognition or a scholarship. In 1949 Jacque Mercer became Miss Phoenix, then Miss Arizona, and finally Miss America. She also won 1st place in talent and swimsuit competition.

Jacque’s national tours as Miss America included endorsements and promotions for Catalina swimwear and Lane Cedar Chests. Jacque also became Mrs. Douglas Cook in 1949, a marriage that ended in late 1950. Her decision to marry prompted the pageant officials to rule that future Miss America contestants cannot be married. Jacque continued her responsibilities as Miss America until the end of her reign.

Jacque continued her involvement in beauty pageants. In 1956 she was chosen as one of the few title holders to judge that years Miss America contest and 1960 “How to Win A Beauty Contest” was published.

Miss Mercer became Mrs. Richard Curran when she married the All-American captain of her college football team in 1953. She appeared in commercials and eight plays at the Sombrero Playhouse. Jacque raised two children and worked as a partner in her husbands advertising agency the Curran Company. Later Jacque remarried and at her passing in 1982 she was Mrs. Jacque Mercer Gillespie.
