Monitoring FWS Earnings Through HRMS

Monitoring FWS Earnings

  • As a supervisor you are responsible for monitoring the amount of money your FWS student employees have earned, either with the help of the Student Time sheet or the Human Resources Management System (HRMS).
  • Your FWS students may not exceed the earning limitation given to them. Any amount earned in excess of the FWS award must be paid by the employer at the 100% rate.
  • If you need help determining the remaining FWS amount, please call the Student Employment Office.

Monitoring Earnings with HRMS

You can monitor the earnings of your FWS student employees with the help of HRMS. You need to have online access to the HRMS system, which requires that you attend the training sessions through the Human Resources Department.

  1. After signing on to HRMS:
    1. Type 81 - (POSITION CONTROL) in the HRMS menu
    2. Enter your USER ID and PASSWORD
  2. Once you are in the "PCS MAINTENANCE MENU"
    1. Type 22 in the TRANS FIELD
    2. Press TAB twice
    3. Type 2004 for CURRENT FISCAL YEAR
    4. Press TAB twice
    5. Type EMPLOYEE ID# in the EMPL FIELD
    6. Press ENTER
  3. This will take you to the "EMPLOYEE BUDGET DETAIL SCREEN" and shows you all the pay lines for this employee.
    1. Type 36 in the TRANS FIELD
    2. Press HOME
    3. Move the CURSOR TO THE REQUESTED PAY LINE (FWS pay lines have the letter "M" at the end)
    4. Press ENTER
  4. This request takes you to the "BUDGET DETAIL INQUIRY" screen and shows you the expenses for this particular pay line.

    REMEMBER: Federal Work-Study is a 30% (department)/ 70% (federal) split, therefore the amounts on this screen reflect only the 30% portion charged to your department's account.
  5. ON THE BOTTOM OF THE RIGHT HAND SIDE you see the original amount encumbered from your account. This is the 30% portion of the student's FWS award, which is paid by your department.
  6. IN THE MIDDLE you see the amount the student has already earned on this pay line. Again, it only shows 30% of the total earnings - only the amount being charged to your account.
  7. ON THE TOP you see the remaining encumbrance balance amount. This is the 30% FWS amount the student has left to earn on this pay line. If you divide the encumbrance balance amount by .30, you will receive the 100% amount the student is still eligible to earn. Divide that 100% amount by the student's pay rate and you receive the number of hours the student is still eligible to work under the FWS program on this pay line.
