November 16, 2004

Freshman Experience Office claims ‘Best of the West’ honors

ASU’s campus Freshman Experience Office at the West campus was cited for its outstanding contribution to the West Valley and received the Westmarc chairman’s award at the 12th annual “Best of the West” event Oct. 28.

ASU Vice President and Provost Mark Searle and Professor Afsaneh Nahavandi were among campus representatives accepting the award.

“The ultimate goal of the ASU Freshman Experience Office is to assure retention of students into subsequent years and see our students graduate from the university,” says Nahavandi, who also is the director of the Division of Collaborative Programs.

Lee Peterson of Sun Health, Westmarc’s chairman, selected ASU’s Freshman Experience Office to receive the special chairman’s award. In selecting this ASU program from among 82 entries, Peterson said he looked for an organization that was important to the West Valley and for a program that enhanced that organization’s ability to serve its constituents more effectively.

Two other award winners have ties to ASU’s West campus. The Kids at Hope Research Evaluation Partnership with the West campus was recognized in the service to communities category. Rick Miller, a senior adviser in Public Affairs and the College of Human Services at the West campus, is the president and founder of Kids at Hope. Accepting the award was Associate Professor Fran Bernat, who evaluated the Kids at Hope training programs and studied the impact of the programs within the school setting.

Separately, the architectural firm SmithGroup took honors for two higher education projects, including the West campus’ Classroom Laboratory/Computer Center that opened in February.

“These achievements reflect on our ongoing commitment to excellence in education,” Searle says.

Westmarc is a regional coalition among the leaders of government, education, business and community organizations in western Maricopa County .

Hughes, with Public Affairs at the West campus, can be reached at (602) 543-5220 or (carol.hughes@asu.edu).
