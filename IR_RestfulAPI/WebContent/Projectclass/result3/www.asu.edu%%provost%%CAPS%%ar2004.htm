Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Annual Report 2004

Members:
Carol Behl (2004)
Patricia Feldman (2004)
Beth Luey (2005)
Sue Hunter (2005)
Barbara Trapido-Lurie (2006)
Victoria Trotta (2005)
Alternate: Paul Scowen served from September-December 2003

This year the committee worked on revising ACD sections 509-03: Grievance Policies and Procedures for Academic Professionals, and 508-05: Release of Academic Professionals. These revisions were made to comply with the 1999 Arizona Supreme Court ruling on the rights of a grievant to representation by counsel. The equivalent faculty sections of the ACD have been revised, approved, and put into place. These AP sections of the ACD manual are awaiting approval from the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University as of April 2004.

CAPS hosted two Spotlight Speaker Sessions this year. On Nov. 2, 2003, a panel consisting of Patty Feldman, chair of the AP Task Force, Sue Hunter, chair of CAPS, and Karl Weiss, chair of PACAPS, addressed the topic of the 2003-2004 review of Academic Professional Policies and Procedures. On March 9, 2004 there was an open forum and discussion of the preliminary progress report from AP Task Force.

The concerns of the Academic Associates, Academic Professionals without continuing status, remain unresolved. Of these concerns, voting rights and grievance procedures, voting rights were requested for Academic Associates in a letter addressed to the Academic Senate President, October 29, 2002 (http://www.asu.edu/provost/CAPS/caps-aa.pdf).

Nancy Gutierrez, Vice Provost, met with CAPS during the last scheduled meeting of the year to provide information on continuing status for APs who are research scientists/research professionals. The president has decided to discontinue appointments of research professionals in probationary positions leading to continuing status.

It was established that copies of CAPS documents (minutes, annual reports, etc.) will be deposited each year with the Department of Archives and Manuscripts, Luhrs Reading Room, fourth floor in the Hayden Library.

The new CAPS members beginning in 2004/05 are Dan Stanton, University Libraries, and Elizabeth McNeil, Department of English. The two alternates elected to the committee are William Sharp, School of Life Sciences, and John Wheatley, Physics and Astronomy. Patty Feldman has completed her term on the committee and Carol Behl, the Academic Associate representative, elected to remove herself from the committee in March 2004.

Respectfully submitted,

Sue Hunter
CAPS Chair, 2003-2004
