ASU Phi Beta Kappa Chapter initiates 58 members

The Arizona State University chapter of Phi Beta Kappa has initiated 58 members, including 43 students from Valley cities.

Phi Beta Kappa is the oldest and most respected honorary society in the United States. It was founded in 1776 at the College of William and Mary, and membership is strictly by grade point average and limited to juniors and seniors majoring in the liberal arts. Seniors must have a GPA of 3.70 or higher, and juniors must have a GPA of 3.85 or higher.

Among notable Americans who wear the legendary Phi Beta Kappa key are former presidents George Bush and Bill Clinton; former presidential candidate Elizabeth Dole; composer Stephen Sondheim; and film director Francis Ford Coppola.
