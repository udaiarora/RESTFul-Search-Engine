October 12, 2006

Rock to lead global engagement efforts

Anthony ?Bud? Rock has been appointed ASU's vice president for global engagement. He will lead a new office focused on strategically planning and managing the university's international relationships.

Rock, who joined ASU in February as special adviser to ASU President Michael Crow for strategic international initiatives, is a former senior U.S. Department of State official. He brings to ASU a wealth of experience from diplomatic assignments abroad and in Washington, D.C., most recently as acting assistant secretary of state responsible for science, technology, environment and health affairs.

Crow expressed enthusiasm for the role Rock will play advancing the university's global profile.

Anthony “Bud” Rock, a former senior U.S. Department of State official, has been appointed ASU's vice president for global engagement. Rock will lead a new office to plan and manage the university's international relationships.

?In a world in which communication and business are global in nature, and issues and opportunities are global in scope, institutions of higher education are becoming of necessity more international in nature,? he says. ?Learning, research and scholarship know no geographical borders, and as ASU plays a larger role in the world arena, we are exceptionally fortunate to have someone with Bud Rock's international and diplomatic experience to guide ASU's global mission.?

According to Rock, ?The new office will bring focus and energy to ASU's efforts to establish and benefit from a global presence. We will help give ASU students a global perspective to their educational experience, and we will help put the university's strengths to work to meet a range of challenges for our nation and for the world. These challenges include global sustainability, security, economic competitiveness and a broader appreciation of cultural diversity.?

To achieve these goals, Rock intends to emphasize three essential themes in the work of the office: knowledge acquisition, research and strategic engagement.

Knowledge acquisition includes ensuring that curricula throughout the university have global orientation, and that faculty and students are afforded the broadest opportunities possible to engage the international community.

?In term of research, we will work to build international collaborations that not only advance the expertise of our faculty and the university, but also bring back to Phoenix and Arizona valuable knowledge and innovation,? Rock says.

International partnerships will range from investigator-driven research projects to formal agreements between ASU and collaborating institutions abroad, emphasizing strategically important topics such as urban sustainability.

?We do not envision ourselves in every corner of the world, but we will concentrate on the topics and the regions of the world in which we can most readily expand and apply our capabilities as a socially relevant academic institution,? Rock says. ?Initially, we will concentrate on strengthening existing international partnerships.?

Rock joins ASU after a distinguished diplomatic career. Among his many accomplishments, Rock led the successful negotiation of three major agreements for expanded trade in the Middle East, and he represented the United States in the first comprehensive dialogue with the European Union on trade in bioengineered products.

Before joining the Foreign Service, Rock was employed by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration ? first as a physical scientist, and ultimately as coordinator for international research. He also held the position of international director of the National Sea Grant Program.

The new Office of the Vice President for Global Engagement will oversee the Pan-American Initiative and the China Initiative. ASU already has established important relationships with Mexico and China, and is working with educational institutions in both countries on common challenges involving university design, resource management and technological innovation.

Dawn Kallestad will serve as director of the Office of the Vice President for Global Engagement and special adviser to the vice president.

The office also will oversee the International Programs Office and will work in cooperation with the university provost, campus provosts, deans and members of the faculty to advance global projects and exchanges. The office will provide key advice and support to the School of Global Studies, the North American Center for Transborder Studies, and the MBA programs in China and Mexico.

Joining Rock in this new effort is Dawn Kallestad, who will be director of the Office of the Vice President for Global Engagement and special adviser to the vice president.

Kallestad, who came to ASU in March 2004, most recently served as program manager for Strategic International Initiatives, Office of the President. She holds a master's degree in management and has held several university positions involved with international activities and programs.

?This is an exciting opportunity for ASU to strengthen its global reach and increase positive effects, both on campus and throughout the world,? Kallestad says.

Rock emphasizes that ?we will work to ensure that ASU programs of study, research and engagement are infused with global vision, so that our students will enter the community and the work force with the fullest possible appreciation of the global dimensions of every aspect of their lives.?
