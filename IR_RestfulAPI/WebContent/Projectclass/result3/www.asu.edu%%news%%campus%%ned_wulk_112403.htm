Sun Devil family loses coaching legend, gentleman

The ASU community mourns the loss of Sun Devil coaching legend Ned Wulk, who passed away Nov. 15 in Tempe.

Born Aug. 14, 1920, Wulk won 406 games as the head basketball coach at ASU from 1957-82. The ASU athletic department honored its legend when the arena floor, where he won a building-record 20 straight contests, was renamed "Ned Wulk Court" on March 6, 1999.

Wulk posted amazing success in Tempe, as he was 94-24 (.797) in home contests in the former University Activity Center (UAC) and continued as a strong supporter of the ASU program upon his retirement in 1982. Wulk was inducted into the ASU Hall of Fame in 1982 and the Pac-10 Hall of Honor in 2003.

"It is a sad day not only for Arizona State, but the state of Arizona and the Phoenix community," says ASU head coach Rob Evans. "Ned was more than just a great coach.

He was a gentleman and a supporter of the program, which I always appreciated. His numbers will never be matched at Arizona State. Ned Wulk established basketball tradition at Arizona State, one that we want to continue.

"On a personal note, I am very glad that the final Arizona State team he saw play, our squad from last year, was an NCAA Tournament team. I know he was proud of that group of student athletes, and that made our staff proud," says Evans.

Wulk's teams were one game away from the Final Four three different times - 1961, 1963 and 1975. Arizona State has made it to the NCAA Tournament a total of 12 times, and only three were without Wulk (1991, 1995 and 2003).
