October 8, 2003

Regents approve projects, gain insight to ASU's future

A five-year plan to double the number of students who graduate with a bachelor of science in nursing (BSN) degree was approved by the Arizona Board of Regents (ABOR) during its meeting Sept. 25 and 26.

This effort is part of a statewide plan to increase nursing program graduates, which was mandated in 2002 by Senate Bill 1260. Plans for all three universities anticipate significant additional support through partnerships with private hospitals and other health care providers. All three plans propose including a program fee on top of undergraduate tuition to be paid by participating hospitals and other primary care providers.

"We have been at this for three years now and it's the most comprehensive plan in the state," said Regent Don Ulrich. "It's a large undertaking, and I am very pleased with the progress that has been made."

The five-year plan sets a goal for the universities of doubling the number of nursing program graduates by increasing enrollments until they reach a combined total of 310 additional undergraduate students (140 ASU students, 70 NAU students and 100 UA students) by the end of the 2007 - 08 academic year.

ABOR also approved the following items during its meeting:
. ASU's FY 2004 Capital Development Plan: This plan consists of eight new construction and renovation projects at the ASU campuses. At ASU Main projects include the construction of an Interdisciplinary Life Sciences and Technology Building, Geosciences and Materials Building, a new business school complex at Tempe Center, instructional research laboratory renovations, and the combined Heat and Power Project.

At ASU East, projects include the construction of a research building and extended campus capital and infrastructure upgrades, which are also being done at the ASU West Campus. The plan also includes the development of the Arizona Biomedical Collaborative at the ASU Downtown campus.

The FY 2004 Capital Development Plan also includes the following projects that were approved by ABOR: Arizona Biodesign Institute Phase II, estimated at $73 million, and McAllister Academic Village, both at Main campus.

The overall total for projects approved in the Capital Development Plan is $317.6 million.

. ASU Capital Improvement Plan for FY 2005 - 07: The plan includes the following projects: Research Laboratory Renovations Phase II (estimated at $10 million), Academic Renovations and Deferred Maintenance Phase I (estimated at $10 million), Infrastructure Improvements Phase III (estimated at $10 million), Light Rail Displacement/Parking Project (estimated at $10 million), and South Campus Academic Village (Third Party Financed) (cost has not been determined).

. Renovation of Building 505, Exercise-Wellness ASU East, Phase II (project approval and budget increase): The project includes the renovation of the 16,800 square-foot Building 505 and the addition of 700 square feet of new space. This request includes a budget increase of $646,147 to the previously approved budget of $987,795 for a new budget of $1.6 million. The renovation of Building 505, the former base Non-Commissioned Officers Club, will provide administrative offices for faculty and staff, two computer mediated classrooms, conference rooms, instructional labs, research spaces and a computer lab.

The Regents also received a glimpse of ASU's interdisciplinary future through a presentation by Robert Wills, dean of the Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts, and Thanassis Rikakis, composer and director of the Institute for Studies in the Arts. The two presented the Arts, Media and Engineering (AME) graduate research and education initiative that focuses on the integrated, parallel development of digital media technologies and digital media content.

AME, a joint project between the Herberger College and the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, seeks to address the needs of researchers (faculty and graduate students) who wish to combine understanding of computation and computational modeling with the ability to apply this understanding at every level of the digital media communication process throughout disciplines across campus. The presentation included examples of movement recognition that used the concepts of dance, digital art, music, engineering and computer science. To find out more about the AME initiative visit the Web at (http://ame.asu.edu).

The next meeting for the Arizona Board of Regents is scheduled Nov. 20 - 21 at the University of Arizona. For information about ABOR, visit the Web at (http://www.abor.asu.edu/).
