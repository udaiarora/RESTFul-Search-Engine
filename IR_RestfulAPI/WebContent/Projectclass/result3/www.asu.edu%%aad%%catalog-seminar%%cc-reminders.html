Reminders
For Revising the General Catalog and Graduate Catalog for 2006–2007

Overall

Make sure that

  1. all changes are marked in red ink or typed without rekeying large portions (though providing text files of completely new inserts is helpful);
     
  2. if you were a new student, you could quickly and easily understand how to use the information provided;
     
  3. sufficient information is provided so that students are not forced to rely on other sources to understand require-ments (that is, students who try to prepare are rewarded); and
     
  4. information supplied by your unit in other printed materials and on the Web agrees with the information presented in the catalog.
     

Text

Make sure that

  1. program changes (such as renaming a concentration) have been properly approved or are noted as “approval in process” (see Approval Process for Academic Proposals chart);
     
  2. approved names of all degrees, majors, minors, concentrations, and certificates have been used clearly and completely in tables and running text (where outdated names and incomplete lists are often overlooked);
     
  3. course lists (i.e., course descriptions grouped by prefix) are not marked (since Katty McIntosh should be contacted directly instead) except to indicate where course checking has been done; and;
     
  4. the contact information to be used by students with questions—the location, phone number, and Web address—appears at the beginning of school and department sections, and building abbreviations (official versions using all capitals) and other types of information are correct for academic year 2006–2007.
     

Academic Personnel

Make sure that

  1. the draft list of academic personnel is complete and accurate for academic year 2006–2007, including complete degree information (bachelor’s level and higher) for each entry
     
  2. a Catalog Academic Personnel Information (CAPI) form has been completed for each addition, deletion, or change to the faculty and academic professional listings; and
     
  3. the names and titles in your unit’s unit’s list of administrators are accurate for academic year 2006–2007 (keeping in mind that distribution of the printed General Catalog begins March 1 and of the printed Graduate Catalog beings around June 1).
