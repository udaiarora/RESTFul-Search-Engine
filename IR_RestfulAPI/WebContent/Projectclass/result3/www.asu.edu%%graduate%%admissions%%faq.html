If you have any questions pertaining to admissions that have not been answered, feel free to email us at grad-q@asu.edu or phone us at (480) 965-6113.
  1. Can I check my application status online?
    After your application has been received, you will receive an email acknowledgement that includes your ASURITE User ID and Activation Code. After you activate your ASUrite account, you may monitor the progress of your application at www.asu.edu/interactive.


  2. How am I notified of decisions?
    After your application has been received, you will receive an email acknowledgement that includes your ASURITE User ID and Activation Code. After you activate your ASUrite account, you may monitor the progress of your application at www.asu.edu/interactive.

    The Division of Graduate Studies notifies all applicants in writing of the admission decision. The Division of Graduate Studies cannot guarantee you will receive a response on or before a specific date. Offers of financial assistance cannot be deferred. Your application and supporting materials remain on file for one year.

    Although your degree program office may contact you during the review of your application, only the Vice Provost and Dean of Graduate Studies can make a formal offer of admission.


  3. What if I have not finished my bachelor's degree? May I still apply?
    We can evaluate your application if you are in the process of completing the bachelor's degree and have submitted official transcripts for at least three years of completed work to the Division of Graduate Studies. Upon admission, you must provide the official transcripts showing that you've completed your bachelor's degree.


  4. What graduate programs are offered by ASU?
    A complete listing of all graduate degrees and majors offered at ASU is available at www.asu.edu/graduate/codes.

  5. What if I want to apply to more than one degree program?
    Every degree program requires a separate application and application fee of $50.00 in U.S. dollars. You will also need to fulfill the individual requirements specified by each degree program.

    To ease the online application process, you only need to submit the General Information Section with your first application. Applying to additional degree programs requires submission of only the Degree Program Section with whatever other materials the specific program may require.

    Every subsequent application requires individual submission with fee payment before another application can be started.


  6. What is the process for sending official test scores?
    Official test scores should be sent directly to the Division of Graduate Studies by ETS electronically. ASU institutional code of 4007 ONLY is needed on the documents.


  7. Where do I go to find out about financial aid?
    The Division of Graduate Studies has numerous scholarships, fellowships, grants, and assistantships available to students. For more information, see our Financing Graduate Study website at www.asu.edu/graduate/financial.


  8. What is the process for transferring credits?
    Up to nine non-degree graduate-level credit hours taken at ASU prior to admission to a degree program may be applied to master's degree requirements.

    The number of transferable hours earned in a degree program from another institution may not exceed 20 percent of the total hours required for a master's degree, unless otherwise stated by a specific master's degree program. Transfer credit must meet Graduate Council transfer policy guidelines, in addition to degree program and Division of Graduate Studies approvals. These policies may be found in the ASU Graduate Catalog.


  9. What is the policy on confidentiality?
    U.S. federal law protects the confidentiality of student records. The Division of Graduate Studies will release information about an applicant only to the academic unit to which the applicant applied. To allow a representative, such as a relative or friend, to transact business for you, the Division of Graduate Studies must receive a signed letter from you designating (1) your full name as it appears on the application, (2) the program to which you are applying, (3) the full name of one person who will act as your representative, and (4) the reason for designating a representative. You can find a sample Letter of Authorization on our website at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms.The letter will be kept in your permanent file. The representative will be required to present identification before obtaining information or transacting business for you.


  10. How can I be considered for in-state tuition?
    All applicants wanting to be considered for in-state tuition must complete the residency section of the application. Applicants who submit an incomplete residency section or who do not meet the requirements set by the ASU Residency Classification Office will be classified as a nonresident for tuition purposes.

  11. Can the application fee be waived or deferred?
    Although the application fee may represent a financial hardship or difficulty for some applicants, the fee is necessary to cover the administrative costs of processing your application for admission.

    Please note: We cannot process an application until we have received the application fee.


  12. What are the tuition and fees at ASU?
    Tuition and fees are determined by the Arizona Board of Regents and are subject to change at any time. For further information on tuition and fees, please visit www.asu.edu/sbs/fees.html.


  13. What are the institution and department codes for Arizona State University for TOEFL and GRE?
    The institution code for both the TOEFL and GRE is 4007. While there are specific department codes, it is best to use the generic codes of 99 for TOEFL and 0000 for GRE.

  14. What forms of currency may I use to pay the application fee?
    Accepted forms of currency to pay the application fee include:
    • Credit card (VISA or MasterCard)

    • U.S. check or U.S. money order, made payable to Arizona State University

    • Cash, only if paying in person. Please do not mail cash.

    Please note: Checks drawn on international banks must identify a corresponding U.S. bank on the check and have bank routing numbers imprinted across the bottom of the draft. International money orders cannot be accepted.

  15. Do you require a copy of my current U.S. visa?
    If you are not a U.S. citizen and are in the U.S., we will require a copy of your current visa. This can be faxed to (480) 965-5158.

    We do not require a copy of your visa if you are here on a B-1, F-1, or J-1.

  16. Do you still require a Financial Guarantee if I have an assistantship from a department?
    All international students must fill out a Financial Guarantee, www.asu.ed/graduate/forms, regardless of funding.

  17. When will I get my I-20/DS-2019?
    The United States government has strict regulations on the issuance of I-20s and Ds-2019 documents to international students who wish to attend an educational institution in the US. The dates are listed at www.asu.wsu/graduate/admissions/IssuanceI_20.html.

  18. How do I determine if I am an Arizona resident?
    Students who have applied for admission to ASU and are classified as nonresidents must petition for a review of residency through the Residency Classification Office. The term "Arizona resident" for tuition purposes is independent from other definitions of Arizona residency. For more information see: www.asu.edu/registrar/residency.
