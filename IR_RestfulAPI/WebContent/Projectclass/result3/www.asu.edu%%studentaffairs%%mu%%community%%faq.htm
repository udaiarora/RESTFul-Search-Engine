Frequently Asked Questions

Community Service Program (CSP) General Information:

  • What programs does the CSP offer?
    The Community Service Program offers many programs year round. Our most popular service initiatives include the annual Fall Service Plunge, Hunger and Homelessness Week, Martin Luther King, Jr. Day of Service, Alternative Spring Break, ASU Cares, Devils in Disguise and many more! Contact the CSP office for more information.
  • I'm interested in getting involved in service ~ where do I start?
    Start by contacting the CSP office to start your service plunge! The next three steps are:
    1. Identify what kind of opportunity you are interested in topically (homelessness, children, elderly, animals, etc.)
    2. Determine what type of time you have to offer (short term v. long term, one day v. once a week for the semester)
    3. Look at what you'd like to get out of the service (resume building, experience in your field, building a new skill, etc.)
    The CSP staff can help navigate you through these first three steps and then you can use our short-term listings and long term service offerings to find out what suits your interests and needs.
  • Does CSP list service internships?
    Yes! Send us your internship descriptions and responsibilities. We will list them in our database and promote these opportunities within classes and organizations which we feel might have interested members or potential candidates.
  • How can I find out about current service events?
    Subscribe to SERVE@asu.edu, a service listserv for students that posts all the current service information about events and opportunities. Also, check out our website on a regular basis or, feel free to stop by our office located on the 3rd floor of the Memorial Union to get the scoop on current service opportunities.

Required Community Service Information:

  • I have to complete a service requirement for my course. What do I have to do?
    It depends on the syllabus and the instructor. If the instructor works with the CSP office and we came to your class and did a presentation, then you need to refer to the list given out in class or get an additional copy from us. If you didn't receive a specific list, stop by the CSP office and we'll help you find a service opportunity with an agency in the Valley.
  • I'd like to do service to help me in my major (education, social work, pre-med, etc.). Where should I look to find something?
    Congratulations! Doing service will help further any career or major, especially if you're aiming high! First, determine what it is that will help you meet your goals. Second, use the CSP resources and staff to figure out what that might be. The information on our website may give you some ideas, but meeting with a member of the CSP staff might give you more options.
  • I need to fulfill ten hours of community service for a court sanction. Can you help me find a volunteer opportunity to do so?
    If you are an ASU student ~ yes! Browse our website or visit our office. You are encouraged to find an opportunity that speaks to you and may help you personally and/or professionally even though you are required to do so.
  • Can you sign off on my sheet for required service?
    No. The agency with whom you volunteered or performed your service must sign off on your verification form. The CSP staff will only sign off on your form if you did the service at a CSP sponsored event in which our staff was present.

ASU Scholarship Information:

  • How do I know if I am required to do service for my scholarship?
    Consult your original award letter, the scholarship office or those who awarded the scholarship. If you are an out of state tuition waiver, you are required to do 20 hours of service per semester. Other scholars are required to do service as well and we are happy to work with anyone to fulfill these requirements.
  • What is the service requirement for my scholarship?
    Most scholarships with a service requirement obligate the scholars to 40 hours of service for the year in the state of Arizona with a 501 (c) 3 nonprofit organization. In most instances, service must be done off campus to meet this requirement. Scholarships may vary so consult the ASU Scholarships office on the 2nd floor of the Student Services Building or your original award letter to verify your personal commitment.
  • How do I know the agency/event will meet my service requirement?
    It is your responsibility to confirm that the agency is a 501(c) 3 nonprofit organization. We recommend you ask an agency representative or ask the person who signed your form. It is your responsibility to meet the time requirement. The agency does not have the ability to track your hours for you so be sure to have the form signed as you volunteer and keep it with you!
  • I'd like to volunteer somewhere but I'm not sure it will meet the requirements. How do I seek an exception or approval?
    If you need to seek an exception or get approval to work with the agency of your choice, please consult the ASU Scholarships office. CSP is not authorized to give out any exceptions or grant any special accommodations. Since the scholarship office reviews all hours at the due date, it must go through that office to guarantee you will be able to meet the guidelines.
  • Can I participate in CSP programs to fulfill my service requirement?
    Yes! You can participate in almost all CSP programs to fulfill your service requirement. We will be certain to let you know if any of the programs CSP offers does not fit the bill. However, if you work with agencies or off-campus organizations, it is your responsibility to confirm that they meet the requirements set forth by the Arizona Board of Regents.
  • Where do I get the form needed to verify that I have completed my service hours?
    You can get the form from the ASU Scholarships office located in on the 2nd floor of the Student Services Building, room C-224 or from the ASU Financial Aid web site.
  • Where do I turn my scholarship form in when I have completed all of my hours?
    Turn all forms into the ASU Scholarships office located on the 2nd floor of the Student Services Building, room C-224. Call them at 480.965.4845 with any questions.
  • I'll only have time to do my hours over break. Can I do them while I am at home?
    Only if you live somewhere in Arizona. Most scholarship hours must be done within the state of Arizona; however, if you remain here over break you may complete them then!
  • I heard that I could get a scholarship for doing service?
    Scholarships are awarded through the ASU Scholarships office. CSP does not award any scholarships. We do help scholars (out of state tuition waivers, Sun Devil Scholars, etc.) meet their service requirements.
