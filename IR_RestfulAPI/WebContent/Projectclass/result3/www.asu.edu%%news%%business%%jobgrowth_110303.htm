November 3, 2003

Job growth outlook beginning to show signs of life

There is finally some good news to be gleaned in the latest economic indicators - signals that the job market may be in for a boost, according to the latest issue of the Blue Chip Job Growth Update.

The National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity panel is forecasting that close to 2 million jobs will be added in 2004, according to Dawn McLaren, research economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center. "After waiting in vain for this type of growth for more than a year, it's difficult to avoid being skeptical, but all indications are that the jobless recovery is finally becoming a recovery that includes an improvement in the job market."

The forecasts by the nationwide panel of experts are critical in assessing the state of the economy, said McLaren. The Bank One Center publishes the monthly Job Growth Update and its quarterly companion report, the National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity.

The Blue Chip Job Growth Update reports that the U.S. economy posted a 0.4 percent decline in nonfarm jobs (507,000 jobs) in September 2003 compared to September 2002. Among the states, Nevada retained its No. 1 ranking in employment growth, with a 2.6 percent increase representing 27,200 jobs over the same period. The lowest rate of total nonfarm job growth was seen in South Carolina, where a 2.3 percent decrease represented a loss of 41,400 jobs.

The Blue Chip Job Growth Update has been the first-to-user source of complete federal employment data since its inception in 1992. It presents data compiled from the U.S. Bureau of Labor in an easy-to-read format, permitting state-to-state comparisons within 24 hours of the release of the data. The National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity, which appears quarterly in the Job Growth Update, contains information from a panel of leading economists around the country. Consensus forecasting is the hallmark of the Blue Chip publications produced by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute at ASU's W. P. Carey School of Business.

The Blue Chip Job Growth Update is available to subscribers either by mail or on the Web. Internet subscribers may sign up for a free list serve, which automatically sends them an email when the data are posted.

A sample issue and information on subscriptions is available online at the following address: http://wpcarey.asu.edu/seid/eoc/publication.cfm.
