Manage assigned functions of the University Registrar's Office. Assist the Registrar's Office in developing goals, objectives and work plans for the office and in the development and enhancement of the computerized student records system. Research and interpret laws, rules, regulations, policies and Board of Regents guidelines affecting the Registrar's Office activities. Research, analyze, plan and implement new policies, procedures, methods and technologies to more efficiently and effectively provide service to students. Adhere to the principles of affirmative action and equal opportunity. Assist with production of the semester class schedule. Participate in budget preparation. Direct written and oral communication to the University community for matters related to the University Registrar's Office. Coordinate, compile and prepare reports. Advise Registrar's Office on issues related to area of responsibility. Act as liaison between the Registrar's Office and other organizations, both on and off-campus. Represent the Registrar's Office on various committees and at various conferences and meetings. Create and/or contribute to various manuals and publications according to areas of responsibility such as the office procedural manuals, University catalog, etc. Encourage positive morale through support of staff development and staff recognition activities. Assure quality service and assess methods for on-going improvements in service to the customer. Perform other related duties as assigned.  
Bachelor's degree, Master's degree preferred, and five years management experience which includes one year in a supervisory capacity.  
OR, any equivalent combination of education and/or experience from which comparable knowledge, skills and abilities have been achieved.  
 
Experience with: administrative and management policies and principles; practices and techniques in higher education. Experience with: academic operations and activities; fiscal operations and activities. Demonstrated knowledge of: computers; computerized information; database systems (e.g., ASU's Student Information System, PeopleSoft). Experience with FERPA. Effective communication skills. Experience in planning; coordinating; supervising the work of others.
