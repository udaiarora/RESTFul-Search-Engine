Farewell, John!

This month, LIST is saying goodbye to our dear longtime colleague, John Barnard. John has accepted a professorship at Gordon College in Barnesville, Georgia, and will be retiring from ASU this summer after 31 years of service. We will have a sendoff for John on June 19th at Hayden in Rm. 133 from 2:00 ? 4:00 p.m. Please join us in wishing him well and thanking him for his many years of service to the Libraries and the University.

Please help us welcome all our new staff to the department and the library!


Digital Library Previews First Volume of Important Series

The Digital Library (you know, those scanners in C2) is currently digitizing an important series of documents, the Report of the Commissioner of Indian Affairs. This series, held by Government Documents, is a gold mine of information on innumerable Native American tribes from the east coast to the west, spanning nearly a century (1850s to 1930s) and detailing the U.S. government?s policies toward the tribes. We have completed scanning a first volume, the 1854 Report, and started on a second, the 1858/59 Report. Eventually the series will include over 80 volumes of several hundred pages each, fully searchable online, with images of each page available for viewing. You can get a preview of the first part of the 1854 Report at http://info.lib.asu.edu/lib/digilib/GOVDOCS-test/1854(001-108).htm.
