Administrator, author: ASU West provost's new book published by McGraw-Hill

ASU West Provost Elaine P. Maimon has written a new book published by McGraw-Hill. Maimon and co-author Janice H. Peritz spent six and a half years preparing "A Writer's Resource: A Handbook for Writing and Research."

The book is designed to be a resource for students in introductory college composition classes, as well as anyone who wants to write correctly and persuasively.

"Janice and I consider ourselves to be missionaries at heart, for the power of effective writing," Maimon says. "The mission that drove us to write this book was to help people succeed through effective writing that encompasses every aspect of life - school, business and personal interactions."

"A Writer's Resource" is being marketed in every English-speaking country. People around the world who purchase the book will see several Arizona-related examples. A sample résumé and cover letter in the "Writing to Get and Keep a Job" section is from an individual applying for a position at the Heard Museum. A Web page designed by ASU West students is featured in "Writing for the World Wide Web."

"A Writer's Resource" is "both a time saver and a complete college education," says Arizona Board of Regents member Gary Stuart, who is using the book this semester in the "Creative Writing for Lawyers" course he teaches at the University of Arizona's law school. Adds New Mexico-based writer Kate Kellstead, "I keep that sweet little book right next to me while I write."

Publication of "A Writer's Resource" is an extension of Maimon's involvement with the Writing Across the Curriculum (WAC) movement, which she helped to found in the mid-1970s. "Unlike some other academic movements, WAC has stood the test of time, because it is based on fundamental principles of connecting writing with reasoning," Maimon says. "WAC led me to a career path in administration because it got me involved in making connections across academic disciplines."

Among the many topics covered in "A Writer's Resource" are writing letters of protest and praise; editing for clarity, grammar conventions and correctness; APA and other documentation styles; finding print and online sources; and planning, drafting and revising a variety of writing assignments.

"Our goal was to make the book a one-stop resource to help people use writing as an effective tool to persuade and inspire others in a variety of situations," Maimon says.
To purchase a copy of "A Writer's Resource," call the ASU West Bookstore at (602) 543-6800.
