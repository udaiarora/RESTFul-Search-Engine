August 3, 2005

College of Law names Seymann as external affairs dean

Well-known Phoenix community leader and philanthropist Marilyn R. Seymann has been appointed associate dean for external affairs at ASU’s College of Law.

“With her business acumen and unparalleled experience in civic, business and national affairs, Marilyn is uniquely qualified to expand the College’s outreach strategy and raise its profile locally and nationally,” says Dean Patricia D. White.

Seymann will join the only accredited law school in the nation’s fifth-largest metropolitan area.

Seymann’s background includes leadership roles in government and charitable enterprises in Arizona and nationally, as well as executive positions in major businesses and service on corporate boards of directors. She most recently was president and chief executive officer of M One Inc., a business consulting firm based in Tempe , and served as associate dean of ASU’s business school.

“Arizona State ’s law school already demonstrates leadership in a wide variety of fields, including the law of biotechnology and genomics, Indian law and international law,” Seymann says. “It is an honor to represent the law school to a broader and growing audience.”

Seymann’s civic involvement in Arizona includes directorships and other leadership positions with the Arizona Foundation for Women Inc., where she was founding president; Phoenix Suns Charities; Deans Council of 100; Crisis Nursery; Economic Club of Phoenix; Men’s Anti-Violence Network; and other nonprofits.

She has served on the Arizona State Treasurers Advisory Board, along with the Arizona Children’s Association, Arizona Women’s Town Hall and the state’s Child Abuse Prevention Fund.

Seymann also served as chief of staff to Gov. Bruce Babbitt in 1986.

Her national experience includes directorship positions with the FBI Citizens Academy, Federal Home Loan Bank Board and the board of the Jacob K. Javitz Fellowship Program at the U.S. Department of Education. She has served on the boards of several public and private companies, including Bally’s Total Fitness, Beverly Enterprises, MAXIMUS, Provide Commerce, EOS International, Draupnir, NorthWestern Corp., Community First Bankshares, American Tool Cos., True North Communications, Claremont Technology Group and Telesphere Corp.

She recently played a key role in founding the Directors’ Council, a consultancy that recruits women and minorities for corporate board service.

Seymann earned her doctorate from California Western University, a master’s degree from Columbia University and a bachelor’s degree from Brandeis University. She lives in Tempe with her husband, Roger.

Jeans, with the College of Law, can be reached at (480) 727-7895 or (franklyn.jeans@asu.edu).
