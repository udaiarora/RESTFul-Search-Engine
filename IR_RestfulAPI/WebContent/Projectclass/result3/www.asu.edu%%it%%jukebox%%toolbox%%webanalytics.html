Web Analytics


What is Web Analytics?

With the power of the Web to deliver content to millions, it becomes increasingly important to understand how visitors are viewing your Web site. Web analytics is the process of collecting and presenting Web traffic usage data to facilitate further analysis. Web site administrators can study Web analytics reports to learn a great deal about their sites' usage:

  • How many times the pages in your site have been viewed
  • What pages visitors are viewing most
  • How often visitors view your site
  • How much time visitors spend viewing your site and pages within it
  • How users are getting to your site (referring pages, search engines, etc.)
  • The first and last pages visitors viewed during a visit
  • What browsers and operating systems visitors are using

Learning about these aspects of your Web site's usage can help you deliver more of what your visitors are looking for and provide important clues about where to strategically place new content so it receives the visibility you're looking for. To get the most out Web analytics reports, please see the Web Analytics Glossary (PDF) which explains important concepts and terminology.
