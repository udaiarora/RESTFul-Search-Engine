April 7, 2004

Dean's Council of 100 names Dell executive of the year

Michael Dell, chairman and chief executive officer of Dell Inc., will receive the Dean's Council of 100 Executive of the Year Award from the W. P. Carey School of Business at a luncheon April 2 at the Phoenix Ritz Carlton.

The Executive of the Year award recognizes individuals whose presence, inspired management, and superior organizational performance creates a model for future business leaders.

"One doesn't need to know a lot of facts to convince anyone that Michael Dell is one of the most important and dynamic individuals in business. He has built a $40 billion company that employs over 45,000 associates," said Philip Regier, interim dean of the W. P. Carey School. "His company is at the center of the new economy - globally engaged, entrepreneurial and supply chain oriented. We are proud that he is accepting this award."

More than 450 business leaders are attending the event, making it one of the largest hosted by the W. P. Carey School in recent years.

The award is presented by the Dean's Council of 100, an advisory group of executive-level business leaders who support research and teaching at the W. P. Carey School.Philip L. Francis, chairman and chief executive officer of PETsMART, Inc., accepted the award in 2003.

Farquhar, with the W. P. Carey School of Business, can be reached at (480) 965-7774 or (liz.farquhar@asu.edu).
