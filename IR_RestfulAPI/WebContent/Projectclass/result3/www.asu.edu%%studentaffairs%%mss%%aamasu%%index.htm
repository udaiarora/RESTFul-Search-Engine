The African American Men of Arizona State University (AAMASU)

Mission

The African American Men of Arizona State University (AAMASU) Program is designed to positively affect the recruitment, persistence and graduation rates of African American male college students. The program uses a variety of forums, workshops and seminars to prepare the students for college enrollment, retention and ultimately receiving of baccalaureate degree.

The AAMASU program is an early outreach program that requires the cooperation between staff, student and parent. We approach this endeavor understanding that a key component in determining the success of the student is parent involvement. The AAMASU Program services students at the high school level in Greater Phoenix Metropolitan Valley.

Return to top

Services

  • College readiness workshops and presentations which prepares the student and parents for the realities of college
  • Social and cultural awareness workshops to enhance the general historical consciousness of the families
  • Professional academic consulting and advising throughout the duration of the program
  • On campus continuation program and organization for further student leadership development
  • Provide professional networking opportunities for post graduate life

Return to top

Qualifications

  • Presently enrolled in High School
  • Must be at grade level in reading and math
  • Have at least a 2.75 GPA
  • Complete application process
  • Attend required workshops
  • Participate in required community service projects
