November 16, 2005

ASU's Love to join volleyball hall of fame

The American Volleyball Coaches Association (AVCA), based in Colorado Springs, Colo., has announced that its 2005 Hall of Fame Class will include Lisa Love, ASU’s vice president for athletics.

Love will be inducted Dec. 15 in conjunction with the 2005 AVCA’s annual convention at the Marriott Rivercenter in San Antonio.

The AVCA Hall of Fame, now honoring its third class, recognizes individuals who have made significant contributions to the AVCA and the sport of volleyball.

The AVCA’s Hall of Fame inductees are nominated under one of four categories: coach/educator, administrator, player or organization.

Love, who took over as the vice president for athletics at ASU July 1, is one of just six women to hold such a post throughout the country. She is responsible for the management and development of the school’s 22 sports programs.

Before moving into administration full time, Love compiled an impressive 404-171 record as head coach in 17 seasons at Texas-Arlington. She was named Tachikara/AVCA Division I National Coach of the Year in 1988, when Arlington went 30-4 and fell one win shy of the NCAA Division I semifinals. She was the head coach at Southern California from 1989 – 1998 and served as an associate athletic director from 1991 – 2001. As a coach, she led the Trojans to a 205-93 record, nine NCAA Division I Tournament berths and eight finishes in the national top 15. She also served two stints as vice president of Pac-10 Conference (1992 – 1993, 2001 – 2002).

Love served as AVCA president from 1997 – 1998 and as the chair of the NCAA Division I Volleyball Committee. She worked on the AVCA All-America committee from 1987 – 1989 and was a legislative representative to the AVCA Board from 1992 – 1994. Love was instrumental to the development and the direction of volleyball on a national scale.

Before coaching at USC, Love earned her bachelor’s degree at Texas Tech, where she was a four-year starter and an all-region selection in volleyball. She then received a master’s degree in education administration from North Texas.
