Reference Linking Task Group

 

Task Group Membership

John Howard, Chair; Linda Shackle (Noble Science Library), Edward McKennon (Fletcher Library), Beth DiFelice (Law Library), Kirk Manegold (Hayden/LIST), Diane White (Hayden/LIST), Mary Swaty (Hayden Technical Services)

 

Project Planning Document


Background

Reference linking—a technology service that enables linking from a citation to the full text of the cited item—has become a common means for research libraries to enhance user access to scholarly literature. In particular, libraries have as a community adopted the OpenURL standard as a basis for enabling reference linking. (The OpenURL provides a standardized means of identifying an individual’s organization context and of providing citation metadata that enables a link-resolving system to direct the individual to the most appropriate iteration of the cited item.)

Adopting a reference linking service based on the OpenURL standard can add value to a library services by:
• enabling links to full-text content from source citations in online full text subscribed to by the library;
• providing the means to search multiple licensed library resources using citation data;
• providing the means to select from among multiple iterations of a desired resource (e.g., selecting between an article at a publisher’s journal site versus an aggregator’s article database); or
• creating a path to finding articles in a library’s print holdings (or obtaining a copy from another source, such as through an ILL service)

Enabling reference linking can introduce or catalyze additional benefits, such as creating potential for reference linking in course reading lists, enhancing the value of freely available resources such as Google Scholar, or enabling reference linking in systems employing proprietary linking technologies, such as the National Library of Medicine’s PubMed database.


Committee Responsibility

The Reference Linking Task Group will determine ASU Libraries’ requirements for a vendor-provided reference linking service, evaluate the features and benefits of various vendors’ offerings, analyze total cost of services (reckoning vendor charges for start-up and annual service fees as well as local effort for setup and ongoing maintenance), and recommend a service for implementation. In conducting its work, the Task Group will refer to the substantial literature on reference linking technologies, relevant research by peer institutions who have recently managed similar processes, and responses to Requests for Information (RFI) provided by potential service providers.

The Task Group will undertake its work with the objective of having an operational technology in place for the start of the ASU Fall Term of 2005. The following timeline proposes a possible framework for the Committee’s work:

In the implementation phase the Committee will coordinate work required to configure the selected vendor’s technology services. Such efforts may include: resource knowledgebase configuration; identification of “most appropriate” sources of full text, when content is available in multiple iterations; and identification of local “targets” for resources outside of the link resolver knowledgebase, such as the OPAC or Interlibrary Loan services.

The Task Group will also consider and propose strategies for building staff awareness of reference linking technology in general, including conceptual issues and nomenclature associated with reference linking. It will further identify appropriate means for publicity and end-user awareness of this new service.
