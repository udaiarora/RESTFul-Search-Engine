Primary Sources in History: Latin America


270.  

CALIFORNIA. UNIVERSITY. BANCROFT LIBRARY. PINART PAPERS.
CALL NUMBER: University of Arizona Microfilm 563
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE:
INDEX OR GUIDE: No guide available.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: Published laws and proclamations issued by the governments of Sonora, Sinaloa, Chihuahua and other states of Northern Mexico. Documentation begins with the acts published in 1824 by the legislature of Occidente (Sonora and Sinaloa) an continues into 1878. The main body of material relates to the political affairs of Sonora.
SUBJECTS: Latin America; Mexico; Sonora; Sinaloa; Chihuahua; 19c

271.  

CIA RESEARCH REPORTS. LATIN AMERICA, 1946-1976.
CALL NUMBER: Doc.Serv. Microfilm F1414.2 L327x
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 5 R
INDEX OR GUIDE: F 1414.2 L327x Guide. Reel/subject index.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: Reports from the U.S. Central Intelligence Agency on domestic politics (including coups), foreign relations, economic issues. Evaluates Soviet objectives. Strong on Cuba (e.g., Cuban Missile Crisis) and American intervention in Dominican Republic. Covers Latin America as a region plus sections for the following: Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, British Guiana, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Cuba, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, Guatemala, Haiti, Mexico, Panama, Peru, Uruguay, Venzuela.
SUBJECTS: Latin America – History; 20c

272.  

COLECCION DE LIBROS CUBANOS.
CALL NUMBER: Microfiche 4x6 3353 to 3375 (each title is cataloged separately in the Online Catalog).
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE:
INDEX OR GUIDE: Retrieve the contents of the collection with a word search: combine "Cuba" and "Libros."
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: A collection of books published in Cuba, mostly in the early 20th century. Primarily history and literature.
SUBJECTS: Cuba – History; Latin America; Literature; 10c; 20c

273.  

COLLECTION OF 16th, 17th AND 18th CENTURY SPANISH DOCUMENTS RELATIVE TO THE HISTORY OF NEW SPAIN AND THE SPANISH SOUTHWEST.
CALL NUMBER: Microfilm 3813
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 44 R
INDEX OR GUIDE: F 1229 .C65x ARIZ. A reel guide to the documents. Since the document were filmed from a variety of archives, the descriptions and listings in this guide are not consistent and the documents are not always filmed in the order in which they appear in the guide.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: A collection of early manuscripts made from originals in various Spanish and Mexican archives. Includes a large number of maps filmed from European atlases of the New World (reels 1-4).
SUBJECTS: Latin America; Southwest; Mexico; Spain; 16c; 17c; 18c

274.  

CONFIDENTIAL U.S. DIPLOMATIC POST RECORDS: CENTRAL AMERICA, CUBA, 1930-1945. PTS. 1 & 2.
CALL NUMBER: Doc.Serv. Microfilm F 1787.5 C665x
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 124 R
INDEX OR GUIDE: F 1787.5 C65x Guide. Separate reel/subject indexes for each part.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: State Dept. Record Group 84, records of the Havana embassy (from National Archives). Pt. 1: 1930-1939; Pt. 2: 1940-1945. Covers both foreign affairs and domestic conditions. Extensive documentation of commerical relations, international congresses, agriculture, and economic, political and military affairs.
SUBJECTS: Cuba – History; Latin America; Central America; 20c

275.  

CRISES IN PANAMA AND THE DOMINICAN REPUBLIC: NATIONAL SECURITY FILES AND NSC HISTORIES, 1963-1966.
CALL NUMBER: Doc.Serv. Microfilm E 183.8 P2 G7x
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 8 R
INDEX OR GUIDE: E 183.8 P2 G7x Guide. Reel index (no subject index).
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: Contains documents that pertain to riots against U.S. control of panama Canal in 1963-1964; negotiations leading to new treaty; and U.S. intervention in Dominican Republic in 1965-1966. These are formerly classified documents from the National Security files, including ambassador’s cables and CIA briefings. Based largely on eyewitness accounts.
SUBJECTS: Panama – History; Dominican Republic; Latin America; 20c

276.  

GREAT BRITAIN. FOREIGN OFFICE. PANAMA: CORRESPONDENCE, 1827-1905.
CALL NUMBER: University of Arizona Microfilm
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE:

  • General Correspondence before 1906.

277.  

HENRY A. MONDAY COLLECTION, 1522-1933.
CALL NUMBER: Microfilm 9366
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 39 R
INDEX OR GUIDE: There is no guide or index.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: Consists of over 2,000 Spanish language documents collected by a physician. Includes correspondence, legal and financial papers, tax records, contracts, land records, cedulas, reports, broadsides, circulars and other papers relating to various aspects of colonial Mexican political, legal and ecclesiastical history. Contents: reels 1-4. Viceregal administration, 1522-1875; Reels 4-8. Audiencia, 1547-1805; Reels 9-33. Catholic Church, 1530-1929; Reels 33-35. Colonial, Non-institutional, 1525-19th century; reels 36-39. Independence and National period, 1805-1935. Reels 38-39 also contain miscellaneous items about Central America.
SUBJECTS: Mexico; Central America; Latin America; Catholic Church; 16c; 17c; 18c; 19c; 20c

278.  

HIDALGO DEL PARRAL, MIXICO. ARCHIVO, 1631-1821.
CALL NUMBER: Arizona Microfilm 7124
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 323 R
INDEX OR GUIDE: Index to El Archivo de Hidalgo del Parral (CD 3676 .P313 1631 ARIZ and ARIZ Microfilm 7125). An English translation of the original Spanish index. The index is a guide that follows the physical organization as described below. The film reels are numbered according to the year to which they apply. Each reel is preceded by a guide to its contents. The last reel is a collation of all preceding reel guides and contains a Spanish edition of the index as well. In addition, there is a hard copy of the Spanish index in CD 3676 .P3 1631a ARIZ.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: A reproduction of the municipal archive of Parral, Chihuahua, Mexico, beginning with the earliest extant documents, in 1631 and continuing to the close of the Spanish colonial period. The documents are organized by year, each year being subdivided into subject categories relative to civil and military affairs, land and mineral entitlements, judicial proceedings and miscellaneous matters. Documents the history of a Spanish colonial silver mining community. Also provides primary source material for Indian uprisings, slavery, public entertainment, official residences, crime and punishment.
SUBJECTS: Silver Mines; Mexico; Spain – Colonies; Latin America; 17c; 18c; 19c

279.  

LATIN AMERICAN AND CARIBBEAN OFFICIAL STATISTICAL SERIALS ON MICROFICHE.
CALL NUMBER: Cataloged separately: see Online Catalog or PSL.
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE:
INDEX OR GUIDE: HA 756 .C45x Ref. Table 4A. Analytical Catalogue with Volume-by-Volume Breakdown of Serials. Lists each country or colony along with the years available. Many, but not all, of the call numbers are written there. For those not given, search the Online Catalog, using the name of the country and the word “statistics.”
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: Deals with all aspects of government from the late 1800s to the 1970s, for each Latin American and Caribbean nation. Coverage varies and some areas are represented by only a few years. Most were originally published annually.
SUBJECTS: Latin America – Statistics; Caribbean; 19c; 20c

280.  

LATIN AMERICAN PAMPHLETS FROM THE YALE UNIVERSITY LIBRARY, 1600-1900.
CALL NUMBER: University of Arizona Library (they own a collection with this title but details of their holdings have not yet been verified).
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 10,500 F
INDEX OR GUIDE: Latin American Pamphlets. Guide has author/title indexes, chronological index, and a detailed subject index.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: Collection provides firsthand documentation on the social, political and economic conditions in Latin America. Consists of original reports and personal accounts, government records, economic reports, histories, biographies, political broadsides, theses and speeches. The largest portion of the collection deals with Mexico (especially 1810-1821) and Peru. Also covers other Central and South American countries.
SUBJECTS: Latin America – History; Mexico; Peru; 17c; 18c

283.  

ORGANIZATION OF AMERICAN STATES. OFFICIAL RECORDS, 1962- [ALSO CALLED OFFICIAL DOCUMENTS].
CALL NUMBER: Microfiche F1402 A17x Microform Service
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE:
INDEX OR GUIDE: F 1402 A169 Ref. Documentos Officiales – indice y Lista General. Also, after 1974, an index is found in Series Z at the end of each year’s microfiche. The Lista General lists the documents according to their entry numbers and also gives their general classification numbers; the Indice is an alphabetical index which also provides the entry numbers needed to locate the documents on the microcards. For additional information on OAS documents, consult Guide to the Use of OAS Official Records, filed in category “Y” of the Official Records.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: Issued annually, this collection consists of official recors from these agencies: the Inter-American Conference; Meeting of the Consultations of Ministers of Foreign Affairs; the Council of the OAS; the pan American Union; the Inter-American Specialized Conferences. Also contains multilateral agreements, conventions and final acts of meetings and conferences. The OAS, which functions as a regional agency of the United Nations, seeks to unite the nations of the Americas in the interest of peace and justice.
SUBJECTS: Latin America; Organization of American States; 20c

288.  

PARISH ARCHIVES OF SONORA AND SINALOA.
CALL NUMBER: University of Arizona Microfilm 811
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE:
INDEX OR GUIDE: Microfilm Guide 811. This guide cites and briefly describes selected documents in order of their occurrence on the film. A subject index follows the body of the guide.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: Microfilm copy of selected documents in the parish archives of Arizpe, Granados, Ures and other Sonoran cities and former Indian missions. Includes records of the archives of the Bishop of Sonora, now located in the main cathedrals at Hermosillo and at Culiacan, Sinaloa. The parish archives consist mainly of local sacramental registers (births, deaths, marriages) from their earliest records to the mid-19th century. The “Bishop’s Archive” (Archivo de la Mitra) is comprised of general correspondence with the archbishop of Mexico and local missionaries, whose correspondence includes census reports, mission inventories and other similar returns.
SUBJECTS: Mexico – History; Latin America; Indian Missions; 17c; 18c; 19c

289.  

REVISTAS HISPANO-AMERICANAS.
CALL NUMBER: Microfilm AP 63 .R88x
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 390 R
INDEX OR GUIDE: Partly indexed in: S. E. Leavitt, Revistas Hispanoamericanas, Indice Bibliografico, 1843-1935 (AI 17 .L4 Ref. Case 4A). References to articles in the journals are grouped by broad subjects (poetry, theater, travels, philosophy, history); there is a name index (to both authors and subjects). There is no printed list of the individual journals included, but it is possible to view them in the Online Catalog by doing a Word Search on the words “Revistas” and “Hispano.” The resulting display will not be in any particular order. (The same results may be obtained through a Call Number Search on the call number “AP 63 .R88x).
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: A comprehensive collection of Backfiles of nearly two hundred Latin American periodicals, published primarily in the 19th and 20th centuries. Because some of them were published before 1843 and some fter 1935, Leavitt’s index does not completely cover these journals. The coverage is very broad, and includes such titles as: Granma (Official Organ of the Communist Party of Cuba); Reportorio Historico (Academia Antioquena de Historia, Colombia; Gazeta de Caracas, 1808-1814; Boletin Historico de Puerto Rico, 1914-1927. Besides the journals, the collection includes sets of books such as Coleccion de Libros y Documentos Referentes a la Historia del Peru (Lima, 12 volumes); reference works such as Indice de Cuba Contemporanea, 1940; and some theses and monographs. Any student of Latin America should take the time to scroll through the list of titles. NOTE: although all of the contents are listed in the Online Catalog, the journals are not included in the Public Periodicals/Serials List. Anyone who is searching for Latin American periodicals must accordingly consult the Online Catalog in order to avoid missing some very important titles.
SUBJECTS: Spanish American Literature – Periodicals; Latin America – History – Periodicals; 18c; 19c; 20c

291.  

U. S. MILITARY INTELLIGENCE REPORTS: MEXICO, 1919-1941.
CALL NUMBER: University of Arizona Microfilm
NUMBER OF REELS/FICHE: 9 R
INDEX OR GUIDE: Printed guide.
SCOPE AND CONTENTS: This period included a number of major events: overthrow of President Venustiano Carranza by Alvaro Obregon in 1920; early attempts to reform education and land ownership; controversies with the U. S. over petroleum rights and with the Catholic Church over separation of church and state under President Plutarco Calles; the assassination of Obregon; and the beginning of genuine reform under President Lazaro Cardenas. These reports, by embassy military attaches, document events and trends in key areas.
SUBJECTS: Mexico; Latin America; 20c
