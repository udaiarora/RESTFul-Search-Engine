OUR STAFF
Phil Potter, PhD
Director
 A graduate of Arizona State University’s doctoral program in Clinical Psychology, Dr. Potter is both a Clinical Psychologist and an Organizational Psychologist and is licensed by the Arizona Board of Psychologist Examiners. His graduate and postgraduate training focused on the fields of health psychology and organizational psychology, and he received advanced forensic psychology training in violence threat assessment through the Department of Defense. Prior to his current position, Dr. Potter served as a decorated military psychologist and conducted extensive behavioral science research. He has written several funded grants and is a published first author in multiple peer-reviewed journals including The Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology and The Journal of Applied Psychology. Dr. Potter is a member of the American Psychological Society and the Society for Industrial and Organizational Psychology.

Dr. Potter assumed the EAO Director position in April 2004, along with those same duties over Wellness and WorkLife for ASU. During his tenure, EAO’s Behavioral Health utilization rate grew 34% and Organizational Consultation requests increased 200%. In 2006, Dr. Potter was named ASU Classified Staff Supervisor of the Year.

Joan C. Allen, MSW
Senior Counselor
 Joan Allen has a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology and a Master’s in Social Work, both from the University of Michigan. Her postgraduate training includes Certificates in Management Development from the University of Massachusetts and in Clinical Approaches to Children and Families from Tuft’s New England Medical Center, Boston. Joan is licensed by the Arizona Board of Behavioral Health Examiners and has an ACSW from the National Academy of Social Workers. She is a member of the National Association of Social Workers, the Arizona Threat Assessment Professional Association, the Governor’s Council on Developmental Disabilities, Cathedral Health Services Advisory Board and ASU’s University Career Women and Faculty Women’s Association.

Joan has worked at the Employee Assistance Office since 1996 and she has more than 25 years of experience in the field of Social Work. Her areas of specialization include handling stress and anxiety, adult ADD, work related problems, relationship counseling, managing transitions, substance abuse, disabilities, stalking, workplace violence and organizational development. Joan’s innovation and leadership helped pioneer a workplace violence threat assessment and response protocol which has delivered incident-free services to ASU for over a decade.

Jillian McManus, MSW
Counselor
 Jillian McManus received her Bachelor of Arts in Psychology from Arizona State University in 1993. She returned to school and earned her Master’s in Social Work in May of 2000. She holds a LMSW through the Arizona Board of Behavioral Health Examiners and is a member of the National Association of Social Workers. In addition, Jillian is a certified management coach and leads EAO’s successful coaching service.

Jillian has worked in the field of Social Work and Human Services for the past 12 years and has been with the Employee Assistance Office since 1999. Her experience includes individual and family counseling, organizational development, training, adoption and foster care, parenting issues and disabilities and has worked with multiple state agencies and community resources throughout Arizona. In 2006, Jillian was recognized as a national recognized expert in the field of work style assessment and consultation following a successful long-term collaboration where a client organization received ASU’s President’s Medal for Social Embeddedness.

Linda Hudnall, MSW
Counselor
 Linda received her Bachelor of Arts in Sociology from Arizona State University in 2000 and earned her Master's in Social Work in May 2005. Linda served her internship at the Employee Assistance Office in the 2004/2005 Academic Year and she is a member of ASU’s University Career Women and Faculty Women’s Association.

Linda has served in the social work field for over 15 years. She worked with adolescent delinquents through the State Correctional Service system and counseled prospective parents, specializing in adoption through a state-funded program. Linda’s voluntary experience ranges from homeless shelters to charitable projects through the Catholic Women's League. Linda is a member of the National Association of Social Workers.

Robin Cook
Administrative Assistant
 Robin has been the Administrative Assistant with the Employee Assistance Office since August of 2002, bringing with her more than 20 years of administrative and financial experience. Since coming to ASU in 1994, Robin has provided administrative and financial support to the College of Life Sciences, the College of Extended Education and Facilities Management prior to coming to EAO. Robin provides extensive administrative, financial and data processing support to EAO and enthusiastically and professionally represents the department as a member of ASU’s Campus Environment Team, Classified Staff Council, University Career Women and other various university committees and activities. In 2005, Robin was honored as the inaugural recipient of the ASU Employee of the Year Award for her dedicated workplace performance and community contributions.
