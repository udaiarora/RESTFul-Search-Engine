October 4, 2004

Arizona Business Conditions Index climbs in September

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index jumped by 8.1 percent in September, from 62.0 to 67.0. An index reading over 50 indicates that the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

“ The index in September reached the upper end of the range of growth it has maintained for almost a year,” said Dawn McLaren, research economist for the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business. The index has remained above the critical 50-point benchmark this year, with its highest level occurring in January at 68.2.

“One of the more encouraging signs was the 9.2 point increase (to 67.8) in the employment component of the overall index,” McLaren said. “In August, the employment subindex had dropped significantly, from 67.6 to 58.6. One month’s movement does not make a trend, and the buoyancy shown this month proves that.” The only subindex to fall was production, by a half a point. Still it remained well above the critical 50-point mark, at 67.9.

The Price Index rose by 9.7 points to reach 73.3. “This index jumped in February and has remained in the 70s since then, the only exception being August’s sudden drop to 63.6. Fuel prices, of course, are one of the drivers of higher prices, but aluminum products are also included. A flurry of activity at the maquiladoras (border manufacturing plants) in Mexico , especially involving chemical products with increasing prices, indicates that these price pressures may be driven by fundamentals within the market rather than speculative price inflation.”

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available online at www.wpcarey.asu.edu/azbci. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data available in prn format.
