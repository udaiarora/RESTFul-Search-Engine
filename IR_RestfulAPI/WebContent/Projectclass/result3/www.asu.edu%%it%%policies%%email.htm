E-Mail Access Policy

The purpose of this policy is to define who can access ASU's electronic mail (e-mail) systems.

Definition

Basic e-mail
This term indicates a level of functionality for e-mail software which is considered necessary to perform the basic functions, including, but not limited to: sending/receiving notes, viewing notes, and directory services. Because of the dynamic nature of software, what is considered "basic e-mail" will change over time.

Policy

ASU provides all faculty, staff, and students "open access" to basic e-mail; however, access to some e-mail systems is restricted due to limited resources.

In addition, faculty and staff can sponsor e-mail accounts for people who are in some way affiliated with the university. This is allowed only in cases where some benefit to the university can be shown. When a faculty or staff member sponsors an account, he/she takes responsibility for notifying the IT Help Desk when the relationship of the account holder with ASU no longer exists.

Available E-mail Systems and Access Restrictions

  • IMAP (Pine, EMMA): access is available to all faculty, staff and students.
  • (Outlook/Exchange): access is available to faculty, staff, & student workers in departments which provide local client support.

More information on the e-mail systems & client software supported by Information Technology is available at the IT Electronic Messaging home page. Questions on this policy should be sent via e-mail to email-q@asu.edu.

Questions on getting an account should be directed to the IT Help Desk, phone 480.965.6500, or e-mail computer.accounts@asu.edu.
