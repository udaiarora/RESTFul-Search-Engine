Minutes of Meeting


April 18, 2001


Meeting called to order at 3:01 p.m.

 

Chair: Pat Etter
Secretary: Marilyn Wurzburger
A quorum was present.


I. Welcome and introduction of Dr. Brooke Sheldon - Pat Etter


II. Address by Dr. Sheldon, Dean, School of Information Resources and Library Science, University of Arizona.

 

The subject of Dr. Sheldon’s talk was the SIRLS program and plans for its future. She stated that while it’s good to be on the cutting edge of technology, it is also important to honor traditions and to learn from them. She has nearly doubled the size of the faculty and predicts that the school will be granted unconditional accreditation at its next review. A question and answer period followed her presentation. Classified staff members interested in the U.A. degree program had been invited to hear Dr. Sheldon’s presentation.

 

 

III. Approval of March 21, 2001, Minutes


Minutes of the March 21, 2001, meeting were approved as distributed.


IV. Chair & Executive Committee Report


No report.

 

V. Dean’s Report - Sherrie Schmidt

 

Dean Schmidt announced the following promotions which would be effective July 1: Julie Tharp, Associate Librarian; Jeanne Richardson, Librarian; Rob Spindler, Archivist.


She also reported that she would follow up on the status of those revisions to the Librarians’ Handbook which Council had approved and sent to her. She has signed off on them and they have been sent to the Provost’s Office for approval.


She has no information about the budget other than what has been appearing in the newspaper. The big question now is determining what numbers will most closely reflect actual revenue figures for the next two years.


The Dean then asked Rob Spindler to report on a future meeting to discuss the issues involved in developing an Archival program at UA. A meeting with David Gracy has been planned following the AULC meeting at 3:30pm, Monday, May 6, in the Parents’ Classroom, C6. Further information will be released as it becomes available.

 

VI. Committee Reports

 

Personnel Committee - Julie Tharp (for Helen Seaton)

The Committee chose not to present the proposed revisions to the Librarians’ Handbook to Council at this time. Members felt that they needed more time to study the issues and possibly confer with Kurt Murphy before asking for Council approval.

Review Committee - Kathy Krzys (for Mary Swaty)

No report.

Research and Professional Development Committee - Tom Greives

Tom reported that there is still funding available for suitable research projects. He urges members to begin now to consider preparing proposals for funding for next year and to submit them to the Committee at the beginning of the fiscal year.

Compensation Committee - Ken Furuta

No report

Academic Senate - Marilyn Wurzburger (for George Bell)

George has sent everyone copies of the latest minutes and announcements from the Senate. The final Senate meeting for this academic year will be held April 30th.

Committee on Academic Professional Status - Pat Etter (for Helen Seaton)

Everyone is invited to attend the CAPS business meeting and conversation with Elizabeth Craft, Director of Distance Education in the College of Extended Education, on May 2, in the MU Chrysocola Room. The business meeting will be at 11 am; at 11:30 Betty Craft will discuss the future of distance learning at ASU. Luncheon will follow in the Maricopa Room. No reservations are required.

 

Page Master - Annie Platoff

Annie is in the process of writing procedures for future Librarians’ Council Page Masters. She will welcome suggestions for improvement from Council members.

Nominations Committee - Marilyn Wurzburger

Marilyn read the Committee’s proposed slate of candidates and stated that all positions were open for further nominations. There being none, nominations were closed. Ballots will be issued on Friday, and will be due by Wednesday, May 3.

 

VII. Old Business

 

None

VIII. New Business

 

None.

 

There being no further business, meeting adjourned at 4:10 p.m.


Marilyn Wurzburger, Secretary
