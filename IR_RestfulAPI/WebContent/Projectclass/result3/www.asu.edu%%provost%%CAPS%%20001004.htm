Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

October 4, 2000

Present: Santos Vega, Chair. Victor Becerra, Stefan Myhajlenko, Helen Seaton, and Linda Shackle.

Guests: Carol Behl, Academic Advisor in the Biology Department
Mirna Lattouf, Cross College Advising Service

The Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS) met on October 4, 2000 at the Memorial Union in room 208D (Havasupai).

Victor moved that the September minutes be approved. Motion seconded by Linda Shackle. Passed.

Victor introduced Carol and Mirna who are Academic Associates (APs). The Council on Academic Advisors sent a letter to the Academic Senate requesting voting rights. Tom Keating, past president of CAPS also sent a letter. The Council has also contacted Human Resources. There has been no response or they were told that someone would get back to them. They also contacted Darby Shaw but there was no response.

Some of their issues are:

Non tenured
No representation
No grievance procedures
No plan for career progression (steps)

In Cross Cultural Advising, Academic Associates started as Faculty Associates. Cross College Advising (DUAS) is not a college but a unit. The College of Liberal Arts has a grievance procedure.

It was recommended that we invite the new Vice Provost, Gail Hackett, to the December meeting to discuss these issues. Most of the academic associates are not in a temporary, short term position. The Council on Academic Associates meets monthly. AAs would like to have some recognition and definition of their status as well as protection. Victor will write a "white paper" detailing the issues.

Also, follow up with the Academic Senate and Human Resources. See ACD 505 - 03 for categories of academic professionals. Linda Banks in the Provost's office is the liaison for this.

Maggie Mangini was able to get some figures from the Provost's office on the breakdown of categories of APs:

APs with continuing appointment 117
On track/probationary status 34
(1st 6 years)
APs with year-to-year contracts 27
(on profile*)
APs not on profile** 158

TOTAL 336

* On profile - think this means that the job is expected to last at least 3 years. There are 178 people "on profile."

** APs not profile means 1 year appointment, academic associates, post doctoral, visiting, etc.

A note said they the Provost's office is now considering all of the above academic professionals
except post docs, academic associates and visiting.

Most of the confusion on job categories goes back to money or funding. Will try to get more information on the breakdown of academic associates - funding - how are their salaries paid - state money (firm) rather than grant or soft money.

We decided to put the workshop on promotion and continuing appointment on hold.

The November 1 meeting will be followed by a brown bag luncheon at the MU. Maggie will contact Richard Filley to confirm that he will speak and then she will do a flyer to be sent to all academic professionals. Linda will send out a message on the listserv.

The annual report for 1999 - 2000 was distributed. Linda moved that it be accepted. Helen seconded the motion. Passed.

Helen Seaton
Secretary
