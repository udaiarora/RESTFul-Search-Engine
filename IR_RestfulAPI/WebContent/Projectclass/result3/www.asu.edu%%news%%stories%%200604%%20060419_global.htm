April 19, 2006

School of Global Studies focuses on worldwide quality of life issues

In today’s global environment, examining worldwide issues with an intent to improve quality of life, is what distinguishes ASU’s new School of Global Studies.

“Across the university, our research, teaching and service have global implications, yet this school is designed to confront global challenges head on,” says ASU President Michael Crow.

Former ambassador to help launch Global Studies

Former U.S. ambassador Dennis Ross will deliver a keynote lecture on how to think about national security in an era of globalization at 7:30 p.m. Thursday, April 20, at Arizona State University's Coor Hall, Room 170. The lecture, which is free and open to the public, is part of two days of activities to celebrate the launch of the School of Global Studies in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences. Read more

The school’s problem-focused research on migration, violence, natural resources, governance, technology, health and culture “will transform the university and prepare our students to face global issues from a quality of life perspective,” he says.

China’s Sichuan University President Xie Heping is among dignitaries for a two-day celebration and launch of the new school in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences. Xie will join Crow at 11 a.m., Friday, for a launch ceremony in Coor Hall’s East Portico.

“With this school, we’re bringing new communities of scholars together to look at significant issues that have worldwide impact,” says David Young, ASU vice president and dean of the college.

As part of this week’s launch activities, former U.S. ambassador Dennis Ross will deliver a keynote lecture on how to think about national security in an era of globalization at 7:30 p.m., Thursday at ASU’s Coor Hall, Room 170.

In addition to the evening lecture, Ross will participate in a panel discussion on “New Challenges and New Strategies in Global Governance” with faculty specialists from the School of Global Studies. This discussion will be held from 2 to 3:30 p.m. Thursday in the ASU Fulton Center, 5th Floor Conference Room.

Another panel discussion, titled “New World, New Thinking,” is scheduled from 10 to 11:30 a.m., Thursday with the deans and directors of international programs from the University of California at Berkeley, the University of California at Santa Barbara, the University of California at Los Angeles and the University of Washington. This discussion will be held in the ASU Fulton Center, 5th Floor Conference Room.

The School of Global Studies is not bound by models of the past, in institutional and intellectual terms, says David Jacobson, director of the new school. “The school is a conscious effort to adapt to the world of today; to create an atmosphere that is intellectually fluid and nimble; to rethink how we think about the world and to ask ourselves if we can more effectively bring about change.”

Additional information about the School of Global Studies and these events is online at www.asu.edu/clas/globalstudies.
