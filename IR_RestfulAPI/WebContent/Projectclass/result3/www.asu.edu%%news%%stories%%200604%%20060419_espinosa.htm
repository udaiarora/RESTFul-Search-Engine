April 19, 2006

ASU professor/filmmaker Espinosa produces episode for 'California and the American Dream'

Episode premieres April 20 on PBS affiliate, Eight/KAET

Independent filmmaker Paul Espinosa, a professor in Chicana/o Studies at Arizona State University, is one of the series producers for a new four-part television series-"California and the American Dream." The series, narrated by Academy Award-winning actress Linda Hunt, explores the dynamics of culture, community and identity in one of the world's most diverse regions.

"California is a prime example of a post-industrial America in which minorities make up the majority of the population," Espinosa says. "California and the American Dream" tells the story of the real California, a state that is leading the nation in terms of innovation and solutions to the myriad of issues facing the country today.

In each episode, veteran filmmakers Espinosa, Lyn Goldfarb, Jed Riffe and Emiko Omori offer an in-depth look at these issues and current trends-from changing demographics to new models of civic engagement; from the role of immigrants in neighborhood life to the democratic challenge of the initiative process; and from sustainable agriculture to Native American gaming and sovereignty.

According to Espinosa, each of the hour-long independently-produced films stands alone. However, as a series, the four episodes examine a complex and crucial set of issues California currently faces, as well as the country as a whole.

The episode premiering April 20, "The Price of Renewal," examines issues of community development, philanthropy and civic engagement as a single community struggles to rebuild a tattered and declining neighborhood, says producer/director Espinosa.

"'The Price of Renewal' explores what is gained and what is lost as a community develops and improves itself through a partnership with public and private entities," he says.

This episodes of " California and the American Dream" will air on ASU's PBS affiliate, Eight/KAET, in Phoenix at 9 p.m. Thursday, April 20. Other local listings are available online at: http://www.pbs.org/california . Additional information about the series and the filmmakers is online at: http://www.californiadreamseries.org
