November 12, 2004

Academic research center taps university provost for fellowship

Milton Glick, ASU executive vice president and provost of the university, has been asked to share his expertise on a national level this year as a senior fellow for the EDUCAUSE Center for Applied Research (ECAR). His appointment was announced this week, effective Nov. 8.

Glick will participate in an ECAR symposium in San Diego Nov. 15, and will advise on ongoing ECAR research throughout the year, in particular ECAR’s new research on the relationship between institutional process performance and information technology investment, leadership and culture.

The mission of the center is to foster better decision making by conducting and disseminating research and analysis about the role and implications of information technology in higher education.

“Milt Glick is universally known and admired within higher education’s academic leadership and IT leadership,” says EDUCAUSE Vice President Richard Katz. “His knowledge of the academy and of the possibilities and limitations of IT within higher education are extraordinary. All of us at ECAR are excited to work with Milt in this new way.”

Glick will continue his duties at ASU, where he coordinates the building of all university programs and the academic planning and budgeting activities for these programs across all ASU campuses.

He holds a faculty appointment in the Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry.

Before his ASU appointment, Glick was provost at Iowa State University for three years and also served the Iowa campus as interim president. He also was dean of the College of Arts and Science at the University of Missouri and chairman of the chemistry department at Wayne State University.

He encourages innovative and appropriate use of computers and programs to support both learning and research. He has published more than 100 articles on structural chemistry, trends in higher education and information technology.

Glick also has been an active promoter of minorities and women in academia, receiving the Award for Exemplary Service in enhancing the status of minorities at the University of Missouri.

EDUCAUSE is a nonprofit association with offices in Boulder, Colo., and Washington , D.C., dedicated to advancing higher education by promoting the intelligent use of information technology. Membership in EDUCAUSE comprises nearly 1,900 colleges, universities and educational organizations.
