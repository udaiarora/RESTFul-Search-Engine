Answers to Blackberry Message Related Problems

  1. The BlackBerry isn't able to receive or send messages.

    Make sure you have wireless turned on and check to see if you are in a wireless coverage area. To turn your wireless from off to on, from the main menu/home screen of your BlackBerry, choose "Turn Wireless On." If the problem persists please follow these steps in the order given to determine the problem:

    a. Check the signal strength: First, make sure that you are in a place with a strong enough signal for BlackBerry communication. Click the Options icon. Click 'Status'. In the "Signal" field, verify that the value is above -100Bm. If the signal is less than -100Bm (e.g., -105Bm), then the signal strength in your area is too weak for normal functions. If your signal is stronger than -100Bm (e.g., -90Bm), continue troubleshooting.

    b. Verify that you are in a coverage area where e-mail transmission is allowed: Note: This step does not apply to older models (85x/95x/5810), as they could not connect to different networks. If these older devices have signal strength, then they should be able to send and receive e-mail. If you use one of these older BlackBerries and have signal, but still cannot send and receive e-mail, skip to the next troubleshooting step.

    If your network status indicates e-mail is not supported in your area, you must move to a location where e-mail is supported. If your network status indicates e-mail support, but you still cannot send or receive e-mail, continue troubleshooting.

    c. Check the date and time: Click the Options icon. Click Date/Time. If the date and time are incorrect, fix them, and then test to see if you can send or receive e-mail. If the date and time are correct, or if you have problems after fixing them, continue troubleshooting.

    d. Reset your BlackBerry: Simultaneously press the Alt-RightShift-Backspace keys; this should force a soft reset. These keys vary in appearance according to your model. If you are prompted to press R to reset, do so. Alternately, you can perform a hard reset by inserting a paper clip into the reset hole on the back of the BlackBerry. On some BlackBerries, the reset hole is located beneath the battery cover. Once the BlackBerry has restarted, test to see whether you can now send and receive e-mail. If you cannot, continue troubleshooting.

    e. Check network settings: Click the Options icon. Click Network or Network Settings. In the "Radio" field, verify that the value is set to On. In the "Roaming" field, verify that the correct country is displayed. Verify that the "Status" is active. Click the track wheel. A menu will appear. Click Register Now and verify that the registration request is sent. If you are unable to register the handheld or connect to the network, or do not have the correct options displayed in the "Roaming" or "Status" fields, contact your service provider. If these settings are all fine, and you are able to register your BlackBerry, continue troubleshooting.

    f. Synchronize your Blackberry: If your computer recognizes your Blackberry, sometimes synchronizing your Blackberry will fix strange problems. To do this, connect your Blackberry to your computer. Open Desktop Manager and double-click the Intellisync icon. Click Configure PIM. Select the checkboxes for the applications you want to synchronize. Click OK. Verify that the Synchronize PIM option is selected. Click Synchronize Now. If you are able to synchronize your Blackberry without problems and are still unable to send or receive e-mail, continue troubleshooting.

    g. Check to see if redirection is enabled: Open Desktop Manager and double-click Redirector Settings. Make sure "Redirect incoming messages to your handheld" is checked.

  2. The computer doesn't detect any Blackberry devices connected to the computer.

    Verify that all the physical connections are secure, and the blackberry is properly cradled.

    If it is still having trouble: Remove your blackberry off the cradle. Press the pin hole sized reset button on the back of the Blackberry. Use a bent paper clip to access this reset button. Reregister your blackberry to the network and wait for the confirmation message. Put the blackberry on cradle. if it doesn't detect automatically, open the BlackBerry Desktop Manager. Go to Options, Serial Settings, and click on "Detect."

    If it is still having trouble, check to see if you have the com port enabled on your computer. If you have more than one com port on your computer, plug the cradle into the other com port, and try detecting the port again.

  3. You are only getting some of your e-mail.

    Blackberry Desktop Manager by default will only forward messages to your handheld if your email address is in the "To" or the "BCC" field. If you are only CC'd, you will get the message header but the body of the message will say "You were CC'd." To change this setting, open up the BlackBerry Desktop Manager > Redirector Settings > Filters. Here you can add various filters so that you can filter specific messages to your blackberry. If you want all the messages to get to your blackberry, select: when none of the rules apply, "forward the messages to the handheld."

  4. You get a message, but the body of the message says, "You were CC'd."

    Please refer to the solution to the problem above.

  5. When you try to configure your Blackberry Desktop Manager to sync your calendars or contacts (Configure PIM), you get a message saying, "path does not exist."

    You don't have administrative privileges on your computer. Please ask your network administrator to make you part of the Local Administrators group.

  6. You are not able to sync your contact list, memopad, or tasks wirelessly.

    The contact list (address book), memopad, and tasks do not sync wirelessly. You will have to sync your calendars and contact lists through your cradle.

  7. You are not able to sync your calendar wirelessly.

    Put your BlackBerry on the cradle. Open up BlackBerry Desktop Manager. Double click on Intellisync. Click on the "Configure PIM" button. Make sure you have a checkbox next to "Calendar". If the option to choose translator doesn't come up, click on the "Choose" button. Choose the translator as "MS Outlook Wireless Sync" (also known as "Blackberry Wireless Sync"). Click "OK", and "OK" again. In the Intellisync window, with the device attached to the cradle, click on "Synchronize Now". A message will come up stating that it will remove all handheld appointments and replace then wirelessly. You will need to accept that and it should be working.

  8. You need to remove your personal information from your Blackberry and give it to another user.

    To wipe your BlackBerry run the Application Loader from the desktop. The following procedure will erase all the data on the BlackBerry: Place the handheld on the cradle that is connected to your PC. Launch the Desktop Manager. Click "OK", and "OK" again. In the Intellisync window, with the device attached to the cradle, click on "Synchronize Now". A message will come up stating that it will remove all handheld appointments and replace then wirelessly. You will need to accept that and it should be working. Double-click the Application Loader icon. The 'Welcome to Application Loader' window should appear. Click Next to start the loading process. If you have a password enabled on the handheld, you will be prompted to enter it, before the desktop software can start reading the BlackBerry handheld's configurations. The 'Optional Application Selection' window appears, displaying the applications available to load onto your BlackBerry. Select the check boxes next to the names ofthe applications you want to load and click Next. Any applications that do not have a check mark beside the mwill be erased from the handheld (even if you have them loaded currently). The 'Unused Application Space' window appears, asking if you want to keep existing settings or increase space available for your application data. Select Keep Existing Settings and click Next. If the Next button is disabled, select the other option and click Next. Selecting the second option will erase the data on the handheld. The 'Existing Data Preservation' window appears. Select'Erase Handheld's Existing Application Data' and 'Erase Handheld's Applications', and click Next. In the next window select the option "don't automatically backup data" click Next The 'Summary window should appear, displaying the applications that will be loaded on the handheld. Verify that the information is correct and click Finish. The Loader dialog box will indicate whether the loading was successful. Click OK to return to the Desktop Manager.

  9. I don't want my Blackberry to synch when connected to the desktop manager.

    You want to change the startup behavior of your device. Open Desktop Manager. Open Intellisync by double clicking it. Choose the "Auto start..." button under "Configuration." Under Auto Start Settings check or uncheck the behaviors you want to happen upon connecting your device to the cradle/desktop. Choose OK to close this window. Choose Close to close Intellisync.

  10. Can I change an icon position on the Home screen?

    Highlight the icon you would like to move. Hold down the ALT key (orange button). Press in on the track wheel. Scroll the track wheel to the position you want the icon and press in again. Now you can order your icons the way you use them!

  11. Is there a shortcut to viewing messages sequentially?

    With any message open, press "n" to go to the NEXT message or press "p" to go to the PRIOR message. This can save you a lot of track wheel clicking if you need to view many messages.

  12. How do I go to the top or bottom of my messages?

    With any message open, press "t" to go to the TOP or press "b" to go to the BOTTOM.

  13. Can I delete all messages prior to a date?

    In the inbox, scroll to the date you want to delete prior messages from. Click the track wheel. Choose DELETE PRIOR from the menu and click the track wheel.

  14. How do I reveal the Senders email address?

    With any message open, scroll your cursor over their name in the To, Cc or Bcc section of the message. Press the SPACEBAR and their email address will be revealed.

  15. Is there more than one way to copy text?

    To copy small words or phrases: With any message open, scroll the track wheel to the first letter of what you want to copy. Hold down the SHIFT key, and scroll the track wheel to highlight what you want. Press in on the track wheel and select COPY SELECTION from the menu that appears. To paste your copied text, place your cursor where you want to put it. Press in on the track wheel and select PASTE SELECTION from the menu that appears.
    To copy paragraphs or full messages: With any message open, scroll the track wheel to the first letter of what you want to copy. Press the ALT key, scroll the track wheel to highlight what you want, press in on the track wheel, and select COPY SELECTION.
    To paste your copied text, place your cursor where you want to put the copied text. Press in on the track wheel and select PASTE SELECTION.

  16. How can I include special characters (such as: ©, ®, ™, Ñ, and ¶)?

    In addition to the characters accessed by the SHIFT, ALT or symbol menu, you can also generate the following characters by typing one letter while scrolling the track wheel. Simply release the track wheel when you see the character you desire:

    • © - Type and hold c
    • ® - Type and hold r
    • ™ - Type and hold t
    • Ñ - Type and hold n
    • ¶ - Type and hold p

  17. How do I use the cut & paste feature on my BlackBerry?

    To CUT or COPY text, click the trackwheel to view the menu. Click CUT SELECTION or COPY SELECTION. Press ALT and click the trackwheel to enter selection mode. Roll the trackwheel to highlight the lines of text you want to cut or copy. In an editable field (e.g., memo, message) place the cursor next to the text to be cut or copied. To paste text, In an editable field (e.g., memo, message) place the cursor in the desired spot for the copied text. Click the trackwheel to view the menu. Click PASTE SELECTION.

  18. How do I sort my messages?

    Alt key: show only incoming mail. Alt-o: show only outgoing mail. (Some of these work in tasks, memos, and appointments also)

  19. What are Message Shortcuts?

    You can choose to jump or scroll to a particular message or area of the page by using shortcuts.

    • Message navigation shortcuts include:
      • ENTER (scrolls down a page/screen at a time)
      • ALT-ENTER (scrolls back a page/screen at a time)
      • B (scrolls to the end of a page)
      • T (scrolls to the top of a page)
      • U (jumps to the closest unread message)
      • N (jumps to the next message)
      • P (jumps to the previous message)
    • General navigation shortcuts include:
      • Hold the ALT key (orange) while you roll the track wheel to scroll through a field to VIEW OPTIONS.
      • Hold the CAP key while you roll the track wheel to select multiple items in a list.
      • Type the FIRST LETTER of an item in an OPTIONS LIST or MENU to jump directly to that item.
      • Type the FIRST LETTERS of a name or the INITIALS separated by a SPACE to find a contact in the ADDRESS BOOK screen.
      • Press C to create a new message, task, or appointment in the MESSAGES, SAVED MESSAGES, CALENDAR and TASKS functions.
    • Reply and Forward shortcuts include:
      • A (answer / reply to message)
      • F (forward message)
    • Calendar shortcuts include:
      • G (to go to a specific day)

  20. A message I sent or received has an X beside it on the message screen. What does this mean?

    When a received message has an "X" beside it, and you requested more of the message, there was a problem retrieving the additional information. When a message you sent from your handheld has an "X" beside it, some problem prevented the message from sending properly. In either case, you can find out what specifically went wrong by opening the message and scrolling to the top. The Message Status field will display the errors.

  21. I can't find the Delete On and Hide Filed Messages options in my Message List Options. What should I do?

    Perform an email reconciliation to transfer your desktop folders to your handheld. If performing an email reconciliation does not work, close your desktop software and the desktop redirector (if running) and edit the registry. Click Start>Run. In the Open field, type regedit, and click OK. Open HKEY_CURRENT_USER/Software/Research In Motion/BlackBerry key and click the Synchronize folder. Delete the Folder Management value. Start the desktop software and reconcile your email.
