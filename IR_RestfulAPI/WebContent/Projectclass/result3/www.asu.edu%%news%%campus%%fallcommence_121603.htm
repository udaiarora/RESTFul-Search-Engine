December 16, 2003

ASU graduates add new degrees to holiday celebration

About 3,800 students will receive an early holiday present - their degrees - at the ASU fall graduation ceremony on Dec. 18 in Wells Fargo Arena. The commencement will be held at 10 a.m., rather than the previous afternoon schedule.

For the first time, graduates from all three campuses will graduate in a university-wide commencement, 640 of them from ASU West and 430 from ASU East. The ASU West ceremony returns to campus after being held for more than a decade at the Sundome. President Michael Crow will confer the degrees.

In a moving tribute, ASU East also will present a posthumous degree to Bill Deneke, a 36-year-old father of three who was killed in a traffic accident two weeks ago. Deneke was to graduate summa cum laude in agribusiness, after working for 11 years to complete his bachelor's degree. The degree will be awarded at a 10 a.m., Dec. 20, convocation at Wells Fargo.

Three students on main campus are among those who have overcome substantial disabilities to achieve their degrees and to excel. All have benefited from ASU's Disability Resources for Students.

Kara Kahnke, graduating with a 3.95 grade-point average in journalism, is known as "one of the bright lights" of the Walter Cronkite School, according to her professors. Despite the struggles of having cerebral palsy and using a wheelchair, Kahnke has emerged as a class leader and one of the most motivated and talented students in the school.

Ginger Bennett, who lost her vision at 10 from juvenile macular degeneration, became an activist and mentor for other blind students, starting an Arizona chapter of Guide Dog Users Inc. She is graduating with a 3.46 GPA in recreation management and is completing a media relations internship with The Seeing Eye organization in New Jersey.

Todd Johnson, majoring in kinesiology with a 3.21 GPA, has a genetic eye disease that causes blindness in his central vision, yet he mountain bikes, hikes and plays basketball. He is so personable that faculty say they love having him in their classes. He uses audio textbooks as well as a Kurzweil reading machine in the library that scans and voice-reads textbook pages.

Four students will receive Moeur Awards for graduating with 4.0 GPAs and having completed all their coursework at ASU. They are Erin Conley of Tempe, receiving a bachelor of arts in elementary education; Lisa Harris of Chicago, a bachelor of arts in literature; Blake Lindemann of Milwaukee, a bachelor of science in economics; and Wei Ye of Shanghai, China, a bachelor of science in supply chain management.

Participants are asked to arrive at the ceremony by 9:30 a.m. Free parking is available in Lots 59 and 58, and Parking Structure 5. A parking map is available at (www.asu.edu/dps/pts/event). ASU employees should park in their designated decal lots.

The fall commencement ceremony will be broadcast live on the Web, at (http://is.asu.edu/live/).

Auffret, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-6991 or (sauffret@asu.edu).
