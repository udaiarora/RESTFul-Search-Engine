September 8, 2005

ASU assists students affected by Hurricane Katrina

ASU continues to accommodate students who have been displaced by Hurricane Katrina. As of Sept. 8, ASU had registered more than 70 students.

ASU has been providing individualized attention to students, including expedited admission, financial aid, academic advising, class registration, housing placement, and parking.

ASU faculty and academic advisors have been working very closely with students to determine appropriate course placement and to help students stay on track academically since ASU’s fall semester began Aug. 22.

To accomodate the displaced students, ASU has:
• Waived application fees for admission and housing
• Waived the tuition payment plan fee, if applicable
• Offered a book loan program through the ASU Bookstore. The Bookstore has also been welcoming students with an ASU T-shirt.
• Developed a process to assist students on an individual basis with tuition issues.

To further acclimate the students to campus, a special orientation session was offered Sept. 8.

The University Registrar’s Office serves as the contact point for students affected by Hurricane Katrina. Contact the office at (480) 965-7302 or registrar@asu.edu for assistance.

Gary Campbell, (480) 965-7209 or garycamp@asu.edu.
