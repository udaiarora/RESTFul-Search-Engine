August 22, 2003

Cárdenas to host KAET's newest public affairs program

José A. Cárdenas, chairman of the law firm of Lewis and Roca LLP, is adding "television program host" to his repertoire as he begins his new duties with "Horizonte," the latest addition to KAET-TV/Channel 8's public affairs programming. The new program provides a forum for Hispanic perspectives and helps educate all audiences about issues of particular interest to the Latino community.

"I'm hopeful that people will not view this as just a Latino show," says Cárdenas, a 1977 graduate of Stanford Law School. "It's a show about Latino issues and many of these issues affect Latinos and non-Latinos."

Cárdenas will make his hosting debut on "Horizonte" at 7:30 p.m., Sept. 18, on KAET. "Horizonte" will air at 7:30 p.m., Thursdays.

"Horizonte" will present public affairs issues in a setting similar to the "Horizon" television show, hosted by Michael Grant. Michael Philipsen, KAET news manager, says "Horizonte" will complement and enhance the educational efforts that have been set by KAET's "Horizon."

"Like 'Horizon,' we will identify topics that impact people's everyday lives, taking enough time to present different sides of an issue so viewers can make informed decisions," Philipsen says. "We hope the stories and the discussions will bring attention to a variety of topics. Our goal is to share balanced, comprehensive information about an issue with viewers and hope it makes a difference in their lives."
Cárdenas is very active in state and local government and professional associations, as well as non-profit community-based organizations.

He is a member of the Maricopa County, Arizona and American Bar Associations, as well as the American Law Institute and the Hispanic National Bar Association.

Cárdenas has been a member of Los Abogados Hispanic Bar Association for the last 25 years, having served on its board for several years and as president for three years. He is also admitted in California. Cárdenas is a member of the Ninth Circuit Advisory Board and he is a member of the ABA House of Delegates, representing the Arizona State Bar Association.

Cárdenas has served for the last eight years as president of the Arizona-Mexico Commission under governors Symington, Hull and Napolitano. He has served as a member of the U.S. Delegation of the NAFTA Advisory Committee on Private Commercial Disputes, and as a member of the Arizona-Sonora Judicial Cooperation Project. He is a member of the Pacific Council on International Policy and has served throughout his career on boards and committees for various organizations.

Stereotyping of Hispanics is an issue that continues to garner attention by the general public and the media. Cardenas says people often are surprised when they learn about a successful or positive story involving Hispanic citizens. He hopes "Horizonte" will change people's points of view.

"It's amazing to see what people's expectations are of Hispanics," he says. "Many people either don't know about the successes or they expect Hispanics not to succeed. It surprises many people when they learn of someone who is Latino and of the first quality."

Cardenas hopes to interview a variety of people who can provide insight and attention to Hispanic issues.

"We have many people in the community who are very bright and active," he says. "I would like the opportunity to interview such people and possibly have local journalists participate in a roundtable discussion, similar to what is already being done by Michael. There are many topics that need to be explored."

For information, visit KAET online at (www.kaet.asu.edu).
