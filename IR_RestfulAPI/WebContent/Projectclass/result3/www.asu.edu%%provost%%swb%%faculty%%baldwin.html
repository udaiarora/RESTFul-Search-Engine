Southwest Borderlands Initiative Faculty

 

Carol Baldwin

Associate Professor, College of Nursing

Dr. Baldwin is an Associate Professor of Nursing and an Adjunct Associate Professor at the Southwest College of Naturopathic Medicine in Phoenix, AZ. She received her doctorate in Biological Psychology with a minor in Counseling and Guidance from the University of Arizona, and her BSN/MSN from the University of Phoenix with an emphasis on Cultural/Community Health. Dr. Baldwin also holds a M.A. in Communication with a minor in Family Counseling from Marquette University and a B.A. with a double major in Communication and Home Economics with a focus on Nutrition and Family Studies (Magna cum Laude) from Mount Mary College in Milwaukee, WI. She is a certified holistic nurse, national certified counselor, certified healing touch practitioner, and member of Sigma Theta Tau, the national honor society for nursing. Dr. Baldwin has done post doctoral studies in sleep, cardiopulmonary, and environmental epidemiology at the University of Arizona Health Sciences Center. She is a co-investigator with the NIH NHLBI-funded multi-center, multi-ethnic Sleep Heart Health Study, and Co-Principal Investigator on a study of stroke risk in Mexican American veterans as part of the Southern Arizona VA health Care System Minority Vascular Center. Dr. Baldwin has extensive experience working with a variety of ethnic/racial and underserved populations, including volunteer work at the Pasqua Yaqui Alternative Medicine Clinic in Tucson, Board Member and Presenter for the El Pueblo Neighborhood Center Health Education Program and life skills educator for the Tucson Centers for Women and Children. While living and teaching in Korea and Japan for 3 years for the U.S. military, Dr. Baldwin also studied Korean, Japanese, and Okinawan traditional health and folk practices. She has served as an Expert Panel Member for the Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry and the Environmental Protection Agency relevant to chemical exposures and effects of odors on health, particularly in ethnic and underserved people, and has participated in workshops on U.S.-Mexico Border Environmental Health and Environmental Justice Research, Education and Health Policy Needs. Dr. Baldwin is a founding group member for the Ethnic Minority and Multicultural Health (EMMH) and Complementary, Alternative and Integrative Medicine (CAIM) Special Interest Groups for the Society of Behavioral Medicine. Her research includes studies of Latino/a folk health practices, their applications to health promotion, and ways in which folk practices can be incorporated into conventional health care to foster adherence, as well as Latino/a narrative experiences and descriptions of sleep disorders, ways in which sleep disorders may impact other health problems, such as obesity and cardiovascular disease, and developing culturally relevant interventions for sleep disorders. Dr. Baldwin serves on the Editorial Board of the Journal of Holistic Nursing, and is a reviewer for the American Journal of Health Behavior, and the journal Sleep. Dr. Baldwin has published articles in Alternative Therapies in Health and Medicine, Archives of Environmental Health, Archives of Internal Medicine, Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine, Journal of Family Practice, Journal of Women’s Health, and Sleep.
