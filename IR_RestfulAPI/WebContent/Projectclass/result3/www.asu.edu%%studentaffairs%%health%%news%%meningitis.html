Immunizations and Health Insurance

Dear Entering ASU Students and Parent:

We have had many inquiries from students and their parents regarding immunizations. While the only immunization required by ASU is the MMR (measles vaccine), we also strongly encourage you to be immunized against meningococcal disease and to make sure that your other immunizations are up to date. The meningococcal vaccine, also known as the meningitis vaccine, will help protect you against meningitis, a life-threatening bacterial infection of the brain and spinal cord.

We especially recommend that students who will be living in residence halls obtain the meningococcal vaccine, because there is evidence that the risk of contracting the infection is three times higher for college freshmen living in residential halls than for 18-23 year olds not in college. Although meningococcal disease is rare, it is difficult to diagnose in the early stages and can be rapidly devastating and fatal. The vaccine is considered safe and serious reactions are quite rare. Indeed, the vaccine has been given to all military recruits since 1971 and although occasional isolated cases do still occur, meningococcal outbreaks in the military setting have been eliminated due to this vaccine.

You may also visit the Center for Disease Control's web site at http://www.cdc.gov/ncidod/dbmd/diseaseinfo/meningococcal_g.htm for more information on Meningococcal disease.

Please review your immunization records with your physician and bring a copy of your immunizations with you to campus. The following is a checklist of vaccines recommended for college students:

Measles, Mumps, Rubella (MMR): ASU requires that all students show proof of two MMR immunizations (one which must be given after 1979) or a blood test documenting immunity prior to registering for classes.

Meningitis (Meningococcal) Vaccine: If you are a freshman and will be living in a residence hall, this vaccine is strongly recommended.

Hepatitis B Vaccine: Hepatitis B is a viral infection that can damage your liver and cause liver cancer. There are 240,000 new cases of this illness each year in the U.S. and since the virus is highly infectious (even more so than HIV), the illness can be readily transmitted to others.

Tetanus: A tetanus booster should be obtained every 10 years.

You may obtain any of these immunizations from the ASU Campus Health Service. The Service is open to all ASU students and is located on Palm Walk and University Drive. Our current charges for each of the vaccines with the visit fee are:

MMR $55
Meningococcal $108
Hepatitis B $55 (each)
Tetanus $40
Influenza $28

Health Insurance: All students are encouraged to obtain health insurance to cover unexpected medical expenses. ASU students are eligible to purchase a major medical insurance plan for $488 per semester. Under this plan, the doctors at the Campus Health Service serve as primary care providers and the referral source for hospitalization and specialty care. ASU also offers a supplemental health plan for students called the Bridge Plan that covers all lab, x-ray and physician visits when these services are obtained at the Campus Health Service. The cost of the Bridge Plan is only $70.00 per semester. Students on both these two plans also have access to after hours telephone advice from our medical staff. For additional information about the Student Health Insurance and Bridge Plan, please access our web site at www.asu.edu/health or call (480) 965-2411.

I would like to extend an invitation to freshmen students and parents to visit us during orientation week. If you have any questions regarding immunizations, health insurance or other health matters, feel free to contact us at (480) 965-8177 between 8:00 a.m. and 5:30 p.m. or stop by the ASU Campus Health Service. We are open from 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. Monday through Friday and will have extended hours during orientation week.
