August 2, 2004

Writer Beams presses on with retirement

After writing and editing her way through life at ASU for 16 years, Nan Beams is retiring July 30. But she won't be retiring from the profession she loves.

"I'm going to The Poisoned Pen Press, a publisher specializing in mysteries, which is located in Scottsdale," says Beams. "I'll start officially as director of sales in August, but will be involved in all facets of the company."

At ASU, Beams started her career with the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business as an editor/publisher and moved into the director of public relations/communications for the school where she was responsible for all marketing communications, including advertising, marketing, media relations and public relations materials. Since spring 2002, she has been working on marketing projects for ASU's East campus.

Beams remembers that learning economics language and concepts was her first challenge at ASU.

"The first publications I edited were written by economists, and my background in economics consisted of a single course in college, so I had to get up to speed quickly," says Beams. "Tim Hogan, who just retired as director of the Seidman Institute, was a patient and generous teacher."

Her accomplishments have been many but the ones that really stand out, says Beams, include creating the first non-traditional recruiting materials for the school's MBA program and shepherding the school through two logo and graphic standards changes.

Beams has worked with many people, making many friends during her time at ASU and welcomes them all to a reception held in her honor at 3 p.m., July 28, in the Williams Campus Union Ballroom. For information, contact Cindy Lucas at (480) 727-1665.

Lambrakis, with ASU East Public Affairs, can be reached at (480) 727-1173 or (lambrakis@asu.edu).
