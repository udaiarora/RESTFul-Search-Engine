J.1: Orientation Program

Effective 3/6/91 .. Revised 3/6/91

The orientation program is designed to introduce new academic professionals to their jobs and the University Libraries. The program is intended to be approximately six months in length. The following pages should be used as a guide.

A pre-arrival letter should be sent out by the new academic professional's supervisor. This letter should include specific information such as department telephone numbers, first day parking information, and where to report.

Welcome packets, to be assembled by Library Administrative Services, include maps and other information on the University Libraries and campus that may help new academic professionals become familiar with their new environment. Packets should be picked up by supervisors and given to new academic professionals on their first day of work.

Prior to the arrival of the new academic professional, the supervisor should arrange for the following:

  • Ordering furnishings (desk, chair, typewriter, computer)

  • Ordering desk/door name plate

  • Organizing work space (supplies, etc.)

  • Sending new academic professional pre-arrival letter, which includes:

    • Information regarding parking and securing a parking decal

    • Where to park before decal is issued

    • When and where to report on first day

    • Supervisor's phone number

  • Obtaining electronic-mail account application from Senior Business Operations Manager

  • Obtaining signature cards for keys

  • Ordering welcome packet from administrative office

FIRST DAY WELCOME PACKET

Library Administrative Services is responsible for providing the First Day Welcome Packet, containing:

  • Campus Map/Tempe Map
  • Staff Lounge combination
  • Library organization chart
  • Campus directory
  • Library telephone list
  • Telephone use instructions
  • Librarians' Handbook
  • Online Catalog information
  • Electronic mail information
  • Walking Tours (booklets) of Hayden & Noble Libraries
  • Information regarding organizations (e.g., AzLA information and application, MPLA, ALA, AOLUG, FWA)
  • Copies of ASU Libraries
  • Brochures describing departments and services (e.g., AHF, Special Collections)
  • Library policies (e.g., circulation, library publications)
  • Library hours
  • Holidays
  • University committee roster
  • Annual report
  • Most recent issue of Sound Off

FIRST DAY ACTIVITIES

The supervisor ensures that the following activities are accomplished:

  • Welcoming academic professional to the department and delivering welcome packet
  • Sending academic professional to Library Administration to pick up forms for Personnel
  • Sending academic professional to Human Resources Office to complete employment paperwork
  • Arranging library tours and introductions
  • Explaining work schedule:
    • Flexibility, restrictions, scheduling within the department
    • Attendance and reporting absences
    • Holidays, vacation, sick leave, professional leave
    • Lunch and breaks
    • Compensatory time
  • Discussing telephone service and protocol
    • Basic use instructions
    • How to answer
    • Personal calls
    • Who answers and where to pick up messages
    • Long distance calls
  • Providing name of contact person in Supervisor's absence

FIRST WEEK ACTIVITIES

The supervisor ensures that the following are explained:

  • Job description, organization chart, and expectations
  • Mail distribution and routing
  • Secretarial and clerical services
  • Copy services
  • Other information sources:
    • ASU Insight
    • State Press
    • Sound Off
    • Minutes of specific groups such as Administrative Council
    • Routed information
    • Staff Personnel Policies and Procedures Manual
  • Standing Committees, Councils, Groups:
    • Administrative Council
    • Classified Staff Committee
    • Collection Development Council
    • Executive Council
    • Exhibits Committee
    • Library Services Council
    • Library Staff Association
    • Personnel Development Committee
    • Subject Specialists' Council
  • Specific Department and/or Division Protocol
    • Personnel classifications
    • Departmental and Division priorities
    • Libraries' Strategic Plan
    • Emergency Procedures:
      • Fire alarms
      • Power failures
      • Illness/Injury
      • Bomb threats
      • Emergency Preparedness Plan
      • Flooding/Leaks
      • Security procedures
  • Governance:
    • Academic Affairs Policies and Procedures Manual (ACD Manual)
    • Librarians' Handbook
    • Librarians' Council
    • Standing Committees
    • Meeting schedules
    • Deadlines for travel and research applications
    • Schedule of Personnel Actions
  • New Academic Professional's Role as Supervisor and/or Department Head
    • Classified Staff (hiring, training, evaluation, merit, disciplinary process, Staff Personnel Policies and Procedures Manual, the supervisor's role)
    • Budgeting Process (student wages, capital, personnel)
    • Who is responsible for what in Library Administration
    • Student Health Services
    • Services available to staff (refer to Classified Staff Handbook)

FIRST MONTH ACTIVITIES

The supervisor ensures that the following are accomplished:

  • Discussing:
    • Goal Setting according to the Librarians' Handbook and the Schedule of Personnel Actions
    • Primary Evaluator's Priorities (expectations, special assignments, reporting guidelines)
    • Evaluation Process (evaluation process, timing, continuing appointment, primary and supplementary evaluators, performance evaluation of library administrators)
  • Scheduling departmental introduction to online systems
  • Scheduling orientation tours (see below)

ORIENTATION TOURS

The supervisor is responsible for scheduling orientation appointments with the following departments and individuals as appropriate. The order of scheduling is at the supervisor's discretion and should match departmental priorities and interests. All interviews take place within six months of the academic professional's starting date.

  • Access Services
    • Circulation
    • Current Periodicals
    • Microforms
    • Reserve
    • Security
    • Stacks
  • Acquisitions/Bibliographic Records
  • Administration Office (Hayden)
  • Architecture Library, including the Solar Energy Collection
  • Archives & Manuscripts
    • Arizona Collection
    • Chicano Research Collection
    • Labriola National Indian Data Center
    • Records Management
    • University Archives
    • Visual Literacy Collection
  • Arizona Historical Foundation
  • Assistant Dean for Personnel
  • Associate Dean for Library Services
  • Coordinator for Collection Development (will provide a copy of the Collection Development Manual to subject specialists)
  • Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian
  • Government Documents
  • Interlibrary Loan
  • Law Library
  • Librarians' Council Chair
  • Library Staff Association President (will provide a copy of AzTeS)
  • Library Instruction, Systems and Technology
  • Library Video Resources
  • Map Collection
  • Music Library
  • Original Cataloging and Special Languages, including East Asian and Southeast Asian Collections
  • Preservation Department
  • Reference Service, Hayden Library
  • Science Reference, Noble Science and Engineering Library
  • Special Collections
  • Subject Specialists' Council Chair
  • West campus Fletcher Library
