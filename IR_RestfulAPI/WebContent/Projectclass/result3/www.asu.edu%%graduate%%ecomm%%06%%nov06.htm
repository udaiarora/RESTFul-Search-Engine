Wanted: Faculty Mentors for PFF Students

The Preparing Future Faculty Program (PFF) is looking for faculty to volunteer as faculty mentors. As a faculty mentor, your role will be to share your experiences, provide words of wisdom, and be an additional resource to the students as they explore the roles and responsibilities of future faculty members. The Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) is a professional development program for doctoral, MFA, and MSD students who are preparing for academic careers at institutions of higher education. It is designed to prepare graduate students for the multiple roles they will face as faculty members and to allow them to understand how these roles and expectations can vary in different campus settings. If you are willing to be a faculty mentor, your name will be added to a list of faculty willing to mentor a PFF graduate student.

The hour or two a semester you spend with a PFF student is priceless. For more information on how to support the PFF Program, contact Jennifer Cason, Director of Graduate Support Initiatives, at jennifer.cason@asu.edu.
