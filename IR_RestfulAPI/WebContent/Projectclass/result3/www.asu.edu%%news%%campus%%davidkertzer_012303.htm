January 23, 2003

Award winning scholar David Kertzer to speak at ASU

David Kertzer, an authority on Italian politics, society, history and political symbolism, is the guest speaker for the 2003 Eckstein Scholar in Residence Program at ASU.

Kertzer 's address "Snowballs in Rome: Anti-Semitism in Restoration Italy (1814-1859)" will be presented 10-30 a.m., Friday, Jan. 31, in the Memorial Union Alumni Lounge. The lecture is open to all and is free of charge to the public. It is sponsored by ASU Jewish Studies Program. No reservations are necessary to attend.

Kertzer is the Paul Dupee University Professor of Social Science, as well as professor of anthropology and Italian studies at Brown University. He had previously been the William Kenan Professor of Anthropology at Bowdoin College, and has been a visiting faculty member at the Universities of Catania and Bologna in Italy, Trinity College in Cambridge, England, and the Ecole des Hautes Etudes en Science Sociales in Paris.

Kertzer is the recipient of many honors including the Guggenheim, National Endowment for the Humanities and two Fulbright fellowships. He has written five books, including, "The Popes Against the Jews: The Vatican's Role in the Rise of Modern Anti-Semitism," published in 2001 in American, British, Spanish, French, Italian, German, Dutch and Brazilian editions.

In addition to the ASU lecture, Kertzer will speak about critics' responses to his most recent book "The Popes Against the Jews" at a free program 7 p.m., Thursday, January 30, at the Temple Chai, 4645 E. Marylin Road, in Phoenix.

Both events are made possible by generous contributions from the late Dr. Albert and Mrs. Liese Eckstein to ASU College of Liberal Arts and Sciences and Friends of Jewish Studies.

For more information, please call (480) 727-6906.
