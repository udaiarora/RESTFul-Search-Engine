DRAFT BIA MEMO

Revision of the Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Management Plan

Arizona State University has been tasked by the Board of Regents to prepare a Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Management Plan by August 31, 2003.

The purpose of the plan is to preclude or minimize disruptions in the conduct of the ASU mission. The fulfillment of this task depends heavily on input from the colleges that are responsible for accomplishing the academic mission. Accordingly, I am requesting your organization's response to the applicable questions contained in the areas shown below.

We shall use your organization's input to identify those mission support elements (electricity, water, equipment/systems, facilities, materials, personnel, etc.) that are time sensitive to the continuation of the conduct of research projects, teaching activities and business support operations.

After receipt and evaluation of input from the colleges and support units, an internally and externally coordinated plan will be developed which prioritizes the use of University efforts and resources. This will result in our protecting, hardening, replicating or duplicating existing vital support capabilities so as to ensure a high probability of mission continuation for most disasters that may occur. Undoubtedly, there will always be imponderable catastrophes that may occur for which a plan such as this may not be effective.

We will be finalizing data requests but plan to address the following categories:

  1. Teaching Activities

    Is there anything about your college's activities, outside the normal classroom and laboratory offering that are unusual or unique in that they require special or timely support?

    Specifically, are there departmental teaching activities that should be restored on a faster timeline than similar activities of another department or college if disrupted by a disaster (extended loss of equipment/system, use of facility, water or electrical power which includes lighting, heating, cooling, digital telecommunications and computing, etc. for up to 72 hours).

    If so, give specific information on those activities that must be restored within 24 hours, 72 hours and within 4-15 days.

  2. Research

    In research there are specific laboratories (wet, dry and live subjects) which if disrupted by disaster (extended loss of a facility, equipment/system, water and/or electrical power, etc.) that should be restored within: 24 hours, 72 hours, 4-15 days or 16-30 days. Please identify which laboratories fit into each category. For research projects involving live subjects separate into clinical research and non-clinical research.

    Additionally, focus on specific issues encompassing extremes and underlying cause(s) which if materialize, would place the project at risk of permanent damage or destruction.

    Provide specific identifying information on special requirements concerning any research activities that must be restored within the first 24-72 hours.

  3. Business Operation

    In the normal business operation of your unit are there critical activities/systems that must be protected to ensure the continuation of mission support operations? If disrupted for an extended period causing a loss of a facility, system, water or electricity which includes heat, cooling, communications and computing capability, etc. what activity/system must be restored within 24 hours? 72 hours? 4-15 days? Give specific information on those activities/systems that must be restored within 24 hours to 48 hours (eg. Payroll Services, SIS, Personnel Services, Procurement, Records Management, Postal Services, Budget Services, Insurance/Risk Management, etc.).
