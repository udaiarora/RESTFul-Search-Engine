Stardust Center for Affordable Homes and the Family

The Stardust Center focuses on affordable homes and family success as a single issue. From this perspective, an affordable home is a means to family success and neighborhood stability, and a key component of social and economic development for the region. The Stardust Center for Affordable Homes and the Family focuses on: establishing the social and economic foundation for and effects of permanent affordable homes for working families; studying the impacts of family services on family and neighborhood stability and success; meeting the technological and architectural challenges of sustainable homes for working families.

 

The Center proposes to do this by 1) gathering and conducting research on what has been done, including relevant policies and trends; 2) providing direct assistance to the affordable housing system of Arizona such as design services, project evaluation, and policy development; and 3) educate practitioners and the public about the needs for such housing development through workshops as well as scholarly and popular publications. The ultimate outcome will be an increase in quantity and quality of affordable homes for the working families of Arizona. This, in turn, should also have a positive impact on the local economy, equal opportunities for Arizona minorities, and education as it will make critical contributions to the stability of families.

 

 

Relation to Design Imperatives


Design Imperative #1: Leveraging Place
ASU Must Embrace Its Cultural, Socioeconomic, and Physical Setting

ASU, with its location in Phoenix, the fifth-largest metropolitan area in the U.S., has an opportunity to set forth a positive model for affordable housing in a city that does not yet face an insurmountable challenge in its neighborhoods.

Design Imperative #3: ASU as a Knowledge Entrepreneur
A Culture of Academic Enterprise

The challenge for affordable housing in Phoenix is related to the lack of understanding of the significance of housing in family success and educational attainment, and the lack of local knowledge in affordable housing design, zoning and financing. ASU can be entrepreneurial in filling this knowledge and services gap, leading to exemplar projects and partnerships in affordable homes for families.

Design Imperative #4: Use-Inspired Research
Improving the Human Condition Through the Appropriate Application of Knowledge

The Center is not a basic research unit. Since the sole purpose of the Center is to increase the quality and quantity of affordable homes for working families, all of its research is use-inspired and directly related to applied projects and immediate community needs.

Design Imperative #7: Social Embeddedness
Public Service/Community Engagement/Outreach

The Center exemplifies ASU?s commitment to social embeddedness?to meeting the needs of the community through research, teaching and service.
