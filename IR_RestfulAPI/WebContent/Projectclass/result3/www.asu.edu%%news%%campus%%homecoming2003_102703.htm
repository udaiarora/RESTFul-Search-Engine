October 27, 2003

ASU FestDevil celebration centers on community

ASU's Homecoming FestDevil 2003 will be the biggest, most inclusive ASU Homecoming celebration ever, welcoming the whole community to the party. Activities begin Oct. 27 and continue throughout the week.

The Second City Comedy Troupe, a group that gave Bill Murray his start, will kick off the celebration with hilarious scenes and improvisation in "Night of a Thousand Laughs" at 7 p.m., Oct. 27 at the Tempe Improv, 930 E. University. Tickets are $5 for students, $10 for faculty and staff.

Spirit and Cultural Day at ASU West is Oct. 28, with food, fun and entertainment. ASU Night Out in Tempe begins at 9 p.m. that night on Mill Avenue, with music, food specials and a free sneak preview at Harkins Theatre.

Homecoming Fest at ASU East is 11 a.m. - 1 p.m., Oct. 29, at the Williams Campus Union. Festival booths will host games, contests, food and music. That night at 9 p.m., students will have a Homecoming Masquerade Ball at the Carson Ballroom on Main Campus.

Sparky's Carnival will feature carnival games, food and rides as well as a sand volleyball tournament 4 - 8 p.m., Oct. 30. Everyone also is invited to pitch in and help clean up the campus that day during the ASU Clean & Beautiful Fall Cleanup.

Spirit and Cultural Day activities will take over Main Campus Oct. 31, with an extravaganza of cultural performances, art, food and fun at Hayden Lawn and the Memorial Union, 10 a.m. - 2 p.m. A noon pep rally will climax with the coronation of the Homecoming King and Queen and the announcement of winners in the office and residence hall decorating contest.

The President's Kickoff Pep Rally and reception will begin at 5:30 p.m., Oct. 31, on Alumni Lawn in front of Old Main, with the traditional Lantern Walk up "A" Mountain at 7 p.m.

It's all a ramp-up for the big day that's expected to rock Tempe, Nov. 1. After a 10 a.m. parade, the ASU Homecoming FestDevil Block Party, presented by Saturn, will take over University Drive at College Avenue with live music, food from favorite local restaurants and more than 150 tents representing all the ASU colleges and every major club and alumni chapter. The free Block Party continues until just before the ASU-California football game at 1:30 p.m.

The party will have an extreme sports area with everything from a rock climbing wall, fast speed pitch and touchdown dive to golf, slap shot and a skateboard demonstration. For younger children there's a fun slide, bouncing tents, obstacle course and face-painting clowns.

The Rising, an alternative rock band with a current Top-40 hit, will spearhead the entertainment. A second stage will feature the local classic and contemporary rock band Cruz and the Boomers, three of whom are ASU alumni. Mariachi Corazon de Phoenix will perform throughout the block party.

A variety of food will be available from Tempe restaurants, including Ducks Restaurant/Holiday Inn, Brooklyn Italian Ices, Ruby Tuesday, Macayo's Depot Cantina, Monti's La Casa Vieja, Rinaldi's on Third, Mama's Pizzeria and Kinder's Custom Meats.

The Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts also will stage its inaugural Art Walk, bringing 35 alumni and graduate student artists together to show and sell their work. The juried show begins at 10 a.m. and continues until sunset along the sidewalk in front of Old Main. Media categories include painting, furniture and lamps, ceramics, watercolor, photography, jewelry, fiber, digital art, printing and mixed media. ASU dancers, musicians and singers also are scheduled to provide entertainment on stage.

The first-ever ASU Homecoming Scholarship Dash for Cash 5K begins at 8:30 a.m. Nov. 1, giving ASU supporters the chance to get some early morning exercise and raise money for a worthy cause. Proceeds will fund scholarships through the ASU Alumni Association. The 5K course begins at the Student Services lawn and winds through campus. Participants may register up until the day of the race, at a cost of $20.

To help ASU elevate the Homecoming experience for returning alumni, Saturn has stepped forward as the presenting sponsor. Additional sponsors include Coca-Cola, Tribune Newspapers, Infinity Broadcasting, United Dairymen of Arizona, Hensley & Company and Mark Taylor Properties.

For event information or registration, visit (www.asu.edu/homecoming) or call (480) 727-3378.

Auffret, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-6991 or (sauffret@asu.edu).
