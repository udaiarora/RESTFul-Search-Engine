October 13, 2003

Diane McCarthy to lead alumni association

Diane McCarthy, an ASU alumna and president of WESTMARC, has been named president of the ASU Alumni Association, effective Nov. 15. McCarthy will join President Michael M. Crow's senior administrative team, the President's Working Group.

McCarthy, a former chairwoman of the ASU Alumni Association, has served in the Arizona House of Representatives (1972 - 80) and on the Arizona Corporation Commission (1980 - 84).

"Diane McCarthy has a long history of achievement in Arizona," says Crow. "As a member of the legislature, she was instrumental in the creation of ASU West. More recently, she was responsible for building WESTMARC into one of the most effective and influential organizations of its kind anywhere in the nation. We are delighted that we have been able to enlist her considerable talents in advancing her alma mater.

"Since I came to ASU 15 months ago, I have been studying various models for how alumni associations fit into university organizational structures. With a leader of Diane McCarthy's caliber joining our team, this is the perfect time to elevate the alumni association to a freestanding university administrative area serving alumni and supporting ASU's community and government relations goals and fundraising efforts."

Under McCarthy's leadership, the alumni association will report directly to the president.

"I am thrilled and excited to take on this wonderful challenge," says McCarthy. "I believe strongly in President Crow's vision to build a new American university, and I'm glad to be a part of the team."

During the interim, Vice President for Public Affairs Virgil Renzulli has asked Nancy Jordan, assistant vice president for community relations, to be responsible for the alumni association and to continue to lead the effort on ASU's upcoming Homecoming.

ASU has 255,000 alumni and 45 alumni chapters and clubs throughout the United States. McCarthy's primary goals as association president are to build membership, which currently stands at a combined total of 15,000 annual and life members; involve greater numbers of alumni in the university and marshal their support for the university's programs and objectives; enhance ASU's traditions; and increase alumni giving.

McCarthy has been president of WESTMARC since August 1992. The organization is a coalition of the 15 communities in western Maricopa County representing 35 percent of the population of the county. WESTMARC members include government, business, industry, education, human services, arts and cultural organizations, chambers of commerce and other community leaders. WESTMARC acts as a unifying voice for the region in public policy matters.

McCarthy is married to Thomas A. McCarthy Jr.. They have two daughters and five grandchildren.
