December 22, 2005

New initiatives spur academic, creative efforts

The restored 1907 President’s Cottage became a home again – this time to ASU’s Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing.

The renovated Piper Writer’s House is a two-story structure that contains a host of facilities for ASU and community writers. The first floor has two classrooms, including a traditional “writers’ workshop” seminar room, with a specially designed round seminar table. A third “outdoor classroom” is included in the garden landscaping design behind the facility, to allow classes to take seasonal advantage of Tempe’s generally excellent climate.

The Piper Writer’s House also serves as the headquarters for Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing and the myriad programs the center sponsors in the community.

• In a major effort to revolutionize on-field command and information exchange, ASU and the Army established the Flexible Display Center in February. Located at the ASU Research Park, the FDC brings together academia, industry and government to develop what in essence will be revolutionary information portals: devices that are small, lightweight, rugged and consume very little power.

• The College of Liberal Arts and Sciences launched the Institute for Social Science Research (ISSR) to further amplify research in the social sciences. ISSR is designed to boost leading-edge thinking by the faculty and foster new transdisciplinary collaborations, as well as to make ASU’s social science research expertise more accessible to the community. The institute serves as an umbrella organization, along the lines of the “small business incubator” model, for a number of promising research projects and centers.

• In May, ASU launched a new age in public policy – one based on advanced, scientifically informed decision making – with the opening of the Decision Theater at ASU.

The Decision Theater is an advanced visualization environment that will enable policy makers and others to see – in detailed, three-dimensional representation – the consequences of their actions. It features a 260-degree “immersive environment” where researchers will be able to view the effects of public policy decisions played out before them.

• ASU celebrated the planned launch of the ASU Center for Film and Media Research with a gala fundraiser honoring Academy Award-winning filmmaker Blake Edwards. Edwards was named the inaugural distinguished fellow in film writing, directing and producing at ASU. He also will teach a course on film production as part of the university’s bachelor’s degree in film program.

• The university established an innovative national training program for newly elected American Indian officials. The program is being coordinated by Ivan Makil, former president of the Salt River Pima-Maricopa Indian Community, and is under the direction of ASU’s Office of Public Affairs.

The tribal leadership program is open to American Indian leaders throughout the country and will launch in the fall of 2006. The program addresses the need for newly elected leaders to get “quickly up to speed” when they take office.

• ASU launched the ambitious new School of Human Evolution & Social Change – designed to re-energize anthropology and focus it on the challenges of modern societies – with a radical transformation of what used to be the Department of Anthropology. The result is a school in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences that breaks down traditional barriers to learning so that interdisciplinary research can be done.
