A1: Academic Program Review Self-Study Report Template

Arizona State University
Campus:

Academic Program Review Self-Study Report

Academic Year:

UNIT NAME
Unit Chair or Director:

The Name of the Unit's College
College Dean:


Table of Contents
  1. Executive Summary (max. three pages)
    (Strengths of the unit’s program offerings, any challenges that might exist. Summary of faculty’s contributions to the program’s mission and vision. Long-term plans, strategic directions, etc.)

  2. Mission and Scope
    • Unit's Mission Statement
    • Unit Goals
    • Major Strengths of the Unit
    • Areas of Regional/National and International Prominence

  3. Peer Comparisons
    • List of peer institutions and related data (can be in table format)

    • List of aspirational peers and related data (can be in table format)

    • Provide qualifying statements for non-ASU peer institutions (such as those that are not ABOR designated, PAC-10 or Big-10)

    • Discussion/analysis of the unit's ranking relative to peers including metrics when available. (Examples of these are the size of the faculty and student body, funding, areas of specialty, uniqueness of the programs, etc. Please use data provided by the Office of Institutional Analysis and other disciplinary sources.)

  4. Findings From Last Review (including any possible accreditation issues)
    • Please discuss what measures were undertaken to address any concerns since the last review.




  5. Undergraduate Programs
    1. Program Inventory, Curriculum
      (Please list the titles of all undergraduate degrees, minors, concentrations, etc. offered by the unit, and provide a brief description for each within a table format. Curricular check sheets for each program should be provided in Appendix A.)
      • Assess the direction and currency of curricular offerings. (Relative to the state of the discipline. Please describe any new curricular initiatives.)

    2. Undergraduate Student Profile
      (Student enrollment, recruitment, retention and placement.)

      Insert data set from APP website for the last seven years (spread sheets):
      > Headcount by degree/concentration.
      > Diversity of student population (e.g., race/ethnicity, gender).
      > Undergraduate student/faculty ratio.
      > SAT scores.
      > Number of degrees awarded annually.
      > The number and percentage of students who started as freshmen in the program and returned for a second year.

      • Assess the patterns of enrollment in degrees/concentrations and their level of appropriateness, size, student/faculty ratios, etc.

      • Assess the diversity of the undergraduate student profile.

      • Assess graduation rates for each program and strategies for improvement.

      • Assess the persistence/retention patterns and areas of improvement.

    3. Student Learning Outcomes Assessment
      • Provide a summary of the findings included in the Annual Learning Outcomes Assessment Report filed with the Office of University Evaluations since the last program review.

      • Discuss trends and major points emerging in these annual reports, and include a discussion of how these findings have been integrated into curricular improvements.

    4. Student Satisfaction, Advising, Services
      (Seven-year trend, Office of University Evaluations (OUE) data/comparative data with college/ASU.)
      Insert data set from APP website for the last seven years (spread sheets):
      > Concern of faculty for individual students.
      > Overall academic experience in your major.
      > Advising received in your major field of study.
      > Availability of required courses.
      > Quality of instruction in 100-200 level courses.
      > Quality of instruction in 300-400 level courses.
      > Ability to write and speak clearly, correctly, and efficiently.
      > Ability to use computers.
      > How many hours did you spend in a typical week on activities outside of class related to your program (e.g., studying) and working for pay on or off campus.
      > Departmental advising on courses and requirements.

      • Identify areas where improvement is necessary and discuss strategic steps to improve student satisfaction.



  6. Graduate Programs
    1. Program Inventory, Curriculum
      (Please list the titles of all graduate degrees, concentrations, etc. offered by the unit, and provide a brief description for each within a table format. Curricular check sheets for each program should be provided in Appendix B.)

      • Assess the direction and currency of curricular offerings.
        (Relative to the state of the discipline. Please describe any new curricular initiatives.)

    2. Graduate Student Profile
      (Student enrollment, recruitment, retention, and placement.)
      Insert data set from APP website for the last seven years (spread sheets):
      > Undergraduate GPA.
      > Headcount by degree/concentration.
      > Diversity of student population (e.g., race/ethnicity, gender, domestic, international).
      > Selectivity.
      > Graduate student/faculty ratio.
      > Entrance exams (GRE, GMAT, LSAT, etc.) scores.
      > Number of degrees awarded annually.
      > Degree completion rates.
      > TA/RA headcounts by percent employment.
      > Placement data for doctoral programs.

      • Assess the patterns of enrollment in degrees/concentrations and their level of appropriateness, size, student/faculty ratios, etc.

      • Assess the diversity of the graduate student profile.

      • Assess degree completion and graduation rates for each program and strategies for improvement.

      • Placement of graduate students. (Related to unit goals; discuss intended placement of graduate students relative to actual placement, e.g., placement of doctoral students in ASU faculty positions.)


    3. Graduate Student Satisfaction, Advising, Services
      (Seven-year trend, Office of University Evaluations data/comparative data with college/ASU.)

      Insert data set from APP website for the last seven years (spread sheets):
      > Overall academic experience in your major.
      > Availability of required courses.
      > Writing skills.
      > Computer applications.
      > Public speaking, presentation skills.
      > Advising on career options within your field (including post docs).
      > Concern of faculty for individual students.

      • Identify areas where improvement is necessary and discuss strategic steps to improve student satisfaction.




  7. Other Curricular Offerings
    • List all other graduate and undergraduate curricular offerings (certificates, distant-delivered programs, etc.) and describe in tabular format, the name of the offering, focus/target group and need/justification.

    • List service courses/FTE provided to the university.

    • Provide brief description of contribution of each program to the university.

  8. Faculty Teaching and Research/Scholarly Work, Staff Quality


    Insert data set from APP website for the last seven years (spread sheets):
    > Faculty FTE
    > Staff FTE
    > Faculty profile
    > Faculty FTE/Staff FTE
    > Student FTE/Faculty FTE
    > Research funding/faculty expenditures

    • Provide an overview of faculty (e.g., tenure, tenure track, research, clinical, etc.) in table format. (Include faculty name, title, degree, FTE, and area of specialization as columns in the table.)

    • Provide an overview of staff in table format.

    • Analysis: Describe and evaluate the quality, effectiveness, currency, and diversity of faculty and staff including their research, teaching, and service contribution to the program mission and goals.



  9. Resources of the Program
    (Given current resource allocations, compare and discuss methods metrics, and the sufficiency of resources related to technology, physical space, fiscal budgets, library, and human resources.)

  10. Community Engagement
    (List and describe major community engagement initiatives and their contributions to community enrichment.)

  11. Future Plans and Strategic Plan of the Program
    (Based on the mission statement of the program, analyses in this report and other pending initiatives, outline the unit’s major plans for the next three to five years.)

  12. Program Chair/Director’s Report
    (This is an optional section that is completed by the department chair, covering areas such as the vision for the program, strategic initiatives, any current concerns, faculty assignments, procedures for personnel issues, etc.)

  13. Response to the Supervising Dean’s Questions
    (This section can be done as a separate section or incorporated into the sections throughout the document. The format and the specific questions should be discussed with the academic dean of the unit.)

    Appendix A — Sample Programs of Study for All Undergraduate Degree Programs Offered
    Appendix B — Sample Programs of Study for All Graduate Degree Programs Offered
    Appendix C — Library Report of the Unit
    Appendix D — Site Visit Report and Unit Response from the Last Program Review of the Unit
    Appendix E — Academic Program Profile (APP) Data Set for the Unit
    Appendix F — Faculty Activity Reports
    (Activity reports covering teaching, research and service activities of each faculty member should not be longer than five pages and should cover the last five years.)


    Other appendices such as the strategic plan of the unit or hiring plan can be included as deemed necessary.
