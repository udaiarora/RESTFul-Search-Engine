About Your ASURITE UserID

Every person at ASU is assigned an ASURITE UserID. This UserID is unique, and is yours to use as long as you are associated with the university and you follow the appropriate policies and guidelines.

The ASURITE UserID is used to access various services at ASU. Your eligibility for services will depend on your affiliation with ASU. New applicants, for example, may have access to certain Web pages to check the status of their application or to register on-line once they have been admitted. Enrolled students, faculty, and staff will be able to add other computing services as needed to their UserIDs.

Because the ASURITE UserID is used for such a wide variety of access, including sensitive information, it is important to protect your password. Do not share it with others. Change your password frequently. Pick passwords that are difficult to guess. Contact the IT Help Desk if you suspect your access has been compromised.

ASURITE Activation

If you have not done so already, activate your ASURITE UserID by visiting the ASURITE Activation and Service Management Web site. This Web site also allows to you add services to your UserID.

Basic ASURITE Services

You are automatically subscribed to the following services once you are employed at ASU.

ASU Windows 2000 Active Directory

The ASU Windows 2000 Active Directory provides you the capability to login to Windows-based computers on the ASU network.

AFS Fileservice

AFS Fileservice gives you file storage (AFS space) on ASU servers. This 5 MB of file space is accessible from any browser, using the AFS Manager. To access the AFS File Manager log into my.asu.edu, click on emma, then AFS Manager.

General Computing

General Computing allows you to access ASU servers from off-campus. For more information on accessing ASU servers, see www.asu.edu/security. To access your AFS space from any of the computing sites, go to the Start Menu and select Mount M: & S: Drives (or on a Mac select AFS from the Dock).

IT Computing Sites (MACOSX)

This service allows you to log into the Mac workstations in the computing sites.

Adding ASURITE Services

There are additional services you may want to add, depending on your needs. You may add these services once your first semester of classes has begun. To add services go to www.asu.edu/asurite. To see a full listing of services, see the ASURITE Services page.

Exchange (Outlook) E-Mail

Setup your Outlook e-mail mailbox by adding ASU Exchange (Outlook E-mail) service to your ASURITE UserID. For more information about Outlook e-mail service, visit www.asu.edu/mailbox.

Personal Web Page Hosting

Activate your Web space by adding Personal Web Page Hosting service to your ASURITE UserID. Once your space is registered with ASU's public server, you can then upload your pages into the www folder in your AFS space and make them available on the Web. For help creating your Web pages, please visit the Jukebox Web pages.

Research / Statistics UNIX Clusters

Add this service for access to the Research computing cluster and Statistics computing cluster. To access IT UNIX resources from off campus, you will need to have VPN/SecuRemote installed on your personal computer.

IT Help Desk

The IT Help Desk takes care of computer accounts for students, faculty, and staff. They are located in the Computing Commons building, on the second floor, in room 202. If you have questions or encounter problems with your account or your ASURITE UserID, contact the IT Help Desk.
