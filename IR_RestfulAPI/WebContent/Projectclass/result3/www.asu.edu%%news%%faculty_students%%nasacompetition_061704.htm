June 17, 2004

ASU team soars in NASA competition

Seven ASU students from the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication and the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering completed a successful mission to Houston in May, returning as grand prize winners in the sixth annual NASA Means Business competition.

They teamed with eight students from the Art Institute of Phoenix to create a promotion plan for the space program, visualizing the International Space Station as a stepping-stone from Earth to the Moon and then Mars. They developed a campaign to help NASA and its partners tell their story to everyday people in new and effective ways.

The ASPIRE team - ASU Students Present Interesting Research to Everyone - won the top award in its first year of competition based on its promotion and outreach plans, two public service announcements and an on-site integrative exercise. NASA will fly the group to Washington, D.C. in late summer to present their proposals, which may result in a national campaign for NASA.

"We knew we had to be unique in our approach," says Lisa Tidwell, team leader who has an undergraduate degree in aerospace engineering, is working on her master's in mass communication and is in ASU's NASA Space Grant program. "The other four schools focused mainly on the technology used in and for the International Space Station. We went in with more of a public relations approach, giving NASA the ability to better reach the general public."

The competition involved more than 60 professors, students and partners from the ASU NASA Space Grant Program as well as the two ASU schools and the Art Institute. Advisers included Fran Matera of the Cronkite School and Helen Reed of the Fulton School.

The team also presented their winning entry at a reception at the Art Institute on May 25.
