May 18, 2005

Cronkite faculty, ASPIRE team win top awards at film festival

ASU faculty and students won platinum awards in two categories April 30 at the 38th annual WorldFest-Houston International Film Festival.

The 2005 juried competition drew 4,500 entries from 38 countries.

“Backstage at a Presidential Debate” won in the Television & Cable Public Affairs category and focused on the run-up to the Oct. 13 event at Gammage Auditorium. The debate was a first for ASU and Arizona.

The 30-minute documentary was the brainchild of Cronkite School assistant professor Bill Silcock and TV/radio news director Michael Wong, with funding from longtime Cronkite School supporters Kathy and Tom Chauncey.

It offers an insider’s view of the last-second preparations involved in a global media event and includes comments from Walter Cronkite, CBS’s Bob Schieffer (the debate’s moderator), NBC’s Tom Brokaw and MSNBC’s Chris Matthews.

The ASU team featured in the program includes Virgil Renzulli, ASU’s vice president of public affairs, as well as debate co-chairs Colleen Jennings-Roggensack and Neil Giuliano.

“It was a choice blessing to stand with our students on the front lines of political history,” Silcock says.

Adds Wong: “Documenting this historic presidential event on the ASU campus was exciting, in that it placed ASU on the international stage and showcased the New American University that ASU President Michael Crow envisions.”

Cronkite associate professor Fran Matera and her student team ASPIRE (Arizona Students Present Interesting Research to Everyone) won a platinum award for a public service announcement created as part of the 2004 NASA Means Business competition. ASPIRE was the grand prize recipient in the national contest.

The 30-second commercial takes a look at the International Space Station as a steppingstone from Earth to the Moon and then to Mars. The spot airs regularly on NASA TV.

ASPIRE combines the talents of students from the Fulton School of Engineering, the Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication, and the Art Institute of Phoenix.

WorldFest is one of the oldest international film festivals, and its mission is to honor outstanding creative excellence in film and video. Among its discoveries are such filmmakers as the Coen Brothers, Bryan De Palma, John Frankenheimer, Ang Lee, Spike Lee, George Lucas, David Lynch, Robert Rodriguez, Ridley Scott, Steven Spielberg and Oliver Stone.
