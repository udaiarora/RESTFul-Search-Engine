February 5, 2004

Greater Phoenix resale market gets a jump start in January

After its record pace in 2003, the Greater Phoenix resale home market roared ahead into the New Year, setting a record January with 5,100 homes recorded sold. Although this represents a sharp decline from the 7,525 recorded in the previous month, it was well ahead of last year's 4,220 homes sold and the previous record set in January 2001 (4,240 homes sold).

As the annual housing counter was reset to zero, the motivations of homebuyers continue: the desire for ownership rather than renting; a move-up to a better home; real estate as rental investments. The recent signs of growth in the economy and job market may foretell further strengthening of the housing market in 2004. The main drawback to a stronger economy is that mortgage rates will begin to increase, further impacting the affordability of housing. The positive note is that rates are still very low and people have not lost faith in their live-in investment.

Since the record median home price was set last August at $160,560, it cooled to $156,000 in January, compared to $147,900 in January 2003. Since the Greater Phoenix area is so large, the median price can range significantly - from $390,150 in North Scottsdale to $93,500 in the central-western areas of the city of Phoenix. In January of this year, 50 percent of all recorded sales were for homes priced from $100,000 to $175,000 and 15 percent for homes priced over $300,000.

The 1,010 resale townhouse/condominium units recorded sold in January tied the record set in January 2000, but was down from the 1,430 recorded in December. The median price increased to a record $106,945 from the previous high of $105,000 established in July 2003.

The median square footage for a single-family home recorded sold in January was 1,645, slightly smaller than the 1,650 square feet reported a year ago. In the townhouse/condominium sector, the median square footage was 1,155, just shy of the 1,160 square feet reported a year ago.

Other highlights of January's resale report:

•  In comparison to January 2003, recorded sales in the city of Phoenix increased from 1,230 sales to 1,460, while the median sales price increased from $120,000 to $127,000. Since Phoenix is a geographically large city, the median prices can range significantly - for example, $90,000 in the Sky Harbor area and $187,000 in the Union Hills area. The townhouse/condominium sector also improved from 235 to 300 sales, while the median price decreased from $90,950 to $88,550.

•  The Scottsdale resale home market improved from 405 to 515 recorded sales. The median sales price continued its rising trend, from $294,000 to $350,000. The median resale home price is $390,150 in North Scottsdale and $170,500 in South Scottsdale. The townhouse/condominium sector in Scottsdale improved from 195 to 225 sales, while the median price increased from $150,000 to $172,000.

•  The Mesa resale housing market grew strongly from 570 to 665 sales, while the median price increased from $133,750 to $144,000. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 130 to155 sales, and the median price rose from $91,150 to $97,500.

•  Glendale improved slightly from 350 to 390 sales and the median sales price increased from $142,500 to $148,950. The townhouse/condominium sector declined from 45 to 30 sales, while the median sales price rose from $77,700 to $83,900.

•  In comparison to a year ago, the Sun City resale market increased from 110 to 125 sales, with the median sales price rising from $105,500 to $124,000. Resale activity in Sun City West also improved from 75 to 65 recorded sales, as the median sales price increased from $152,500 to $154,900. The townhouse/condominium market in Sun City improved from 65 to 80 sales and the median sales price rose from $76,500 to $84,000. In Sun City West, sales remained stable at 15, as the median price increased from $93,450 to $118,750.

•  The resale market in Gilbert improved, as activity moved from 235 to 280 sales and the median sales price increased from $169,900 to $190,000. The townhouse/condominium market slowed from 15 to 10 sales as the median sales price increased from $105,250 to $127,450.

•  For the city of Chandler, the resale market declined slightly from 335 to 325 recorded sales, while the median price rose from $156,415 to $165,000. The townhouse/condominium market improved from 35 to 45 sales, and the median sales price increased from $103,000 to $106,990.

•  The resale market in Tempe slowed from 125 to 115 sales, as the median price increased from $159,500 to $166,950. The townhouse/condominium sector grew from 60 to 65 sales, while the median sales price rose from $99,000 to $110,000.

•  The highest median sales price was in Paradise Valley at $1,080,000, with a median square footage of 3,860.

•  In the West Valley, the following communities represent 8 percent of the resale market, compared to 6 percent a year ago.

•  Avondale grew from 60 to 105 sales, with the median price declining from $147,000 to $146,250.

•  El Mirage improved from 30 sales (median price $113,000) to 50 sales ($123,500).

•  Goodyear improved from 50 to 75 sales, while the median price decreased from $151,520 to $149,900.

•  Surprise made a strong showing, increasing from 100 sales (median price $132,750) to 170 sales ($145,115).
