September 27, 2004

ASU's downtown Phoenix campus shaped

Following months of public, business and university input, the city of Phoenix this week released its recommended blueprint for a large-scale redevelopment of downtown Phoenix that will include ASU’s planned campus.

“This is an important milestone,” said Richard Stanley, senior vice president and university planner. “The city of Phoenix and the business community have worked in partnership with ASU to get the plan moving along this quickly.”

The plan was revealed at a work study session conducted by Phoenix Mayor Phil Gordon and the Phoenix City Council on Sept. 21.

It now moves into a period of public comment and review by the governing agencies, which are the Arizona Board of Regents and the Phoenix City Council.

Formal action by the governing entities charged with approving the plan is expected in November.

At the meeting, ASU President Michael Crow said ASU plans to blend into – and be – a dynamic part of the community.

“We’re not interested in something just for us,” he said.

The boundaries for the Phoenix campus run from First Avenue to Second Street, and from Van Buren to Fillmore.

The site is in close proximity to the planned light rail system, which will make the commute between downtown and the Tempe campus around 20 minutes, according to Stanley.

“The plan is part of the community input process,” Stanley said. “The city of Phoenix has taken community input into account and released a plan that is responsive to what was heard.”

Following the blueprint’s unveiling, Phoenix Mayor Phil Gordon and the Phoenix City Council conducted a public Town Hall meeting on Sept. 23 to present the proposed downtown strategic plan.

Representatives from the city, ASU and Phoenix Futures were on hand to answer questions and discuss the recommendations in the draft plan.

The written plans for the downtown Phoenix campus can be found at the following Web site: (www.phoenix.gov/downtown).

The colleges, schools and programs that will move to the downtown location include the College of Nursing, the College of Public Programs, the Cronkite School of Journalism, KAET, the School of Health Management and the University College.
