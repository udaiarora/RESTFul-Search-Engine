Police Services Division Personnel

The Police Services Division currently has 66 sworn and non-sworn personnel. Forty-one employees are certified as Police Officers by the Arizona Peace Officer Standards and Training Board; the remaining staff serve as support for the sworn staff in meeting the Department Mission.

Command Staff for Police Services includes:

  1. the Director/Chief;
  2. the Assistant Chief of Police;
  3. the Administrative Bureau Commander; and
  4. the Operations Bureau Commanders (Tempe/Poly/West Campuses).
Administrative Bureau includes:
    *Administrative Officer/Events Coordinator: schedules personnel for special events, including all ASU Sun Devil and Arizona Cardinals football games, Gammage Auditorium events, concerts and other public events. Also oversees the Campus Security Assistants program.
    *Property and Evidence: issues and maintains division property as well as receives and stores all evidence in the division's control.
    *Planning and Research: develops ways to maximize resources to accomplish the goals of the division. Prepares or coordinates preparation of all grant applications for the division.

    *Records/Key Control: controls access to crime records; issues University keys as part of the University's access control program.

    *Investigations: initiates and/or conducts criminal investigations.

    *Community-Based Policing: responsible for four campus focus areas within the ASU community, including the Memorial Union, Manzanita Hall, the Student Recreation Center, Libraries, Music, Cholla, Center Complex, North Dorms, South Dorms.

    *Crime Prevention: responsible for the division's crime prevention programs.

Operations Bureau includes:
    *Patrol shifts: provides 24-hour/7-day service to the University community over three shifts on foot, bicycle, car, motorcycle, or other patrols
    *Communications Center (Dispatch): receives calls for service from the University community and dispatches officers as needed
Duties of the ASU Police Officer
    *Enforces university, state, and federal laws and regulations, to protect life and property, and serve the University community.
    *Patrols assigned areas, buildings, and grounds, checking locked doors and windows.
    *Investigates suspicious activity or persons.
    *Controls traffic, prevents or deters crime, and arrests violators; issues warnings and
    citations.
    *Writes and files crime reports and daily activity reports; communicates with supervisors.
    *Assists motorists and University community.
    *Testifies in court.
    *Trains new officers in accordance with ASU and Arizona Peace Officer Standards and Training (AZ POST) policies and procedures.
Sworn officers are initially assigned to Patrol; however, specialized division assignments, such as Investigations, Narcotics Task Force, Community-Based Policing, Crime Prevention, a Motors Program, and Bike Patrol, as well as promotional opportunities, are available.

Non-sworn personnel are assigned to administrative or communications duties within the division.
