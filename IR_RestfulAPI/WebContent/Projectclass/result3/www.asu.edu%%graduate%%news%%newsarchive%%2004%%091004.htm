Three Graduate Students Awarded Faculty Emeriti Association Fellowships

Sara Grineski
 Ha Lam
Tiffany Rice

Three graduate students on track for careers in academia have been awarded Faculty Emeriti Fellowships. Doctoral students Sara Grineski, Ha Lam, and Tiffany Rice were recognized as the 2004–05 Faculty Emeriti Association Fellows at a reception on Thursday [Sept. 9] that ushered in the eleventh year of the Preparing Future Faculty Program (PFF).

The Faculty Emeriti Association was founded in 1978 by a group of emeriti faculty as a continuing social and university-connected educational outlet. It is unique in academia for faculty to have their own endowment in support of future academics in any discipline within the academy. The Emeriti Fellow Fund was later established by members of the association in 1993 within the ASU Foundation. When the PFF Program was established in the Division of Graduate Studies, the association decided it was a logical place to award students who would follow them into the professoriate.

The funding now supports the ASU PFF Program, a nationally-recognized program designed to train and mentor doctoral and master of fine arts students for faculty positions at the nation’s colleges and universities. The program teaches these aspiring professors about the challenges and responsibilities of a career in academia. Currently, there are an estimated 100 graduate students enrolled in ASU’s program.

“We are pleased to name these three PFF doctoral students as ASU Emeriti Fellows for 2004–05,” said Joyce Foster-Larson, chairwoman of the fellowship selection committee. “It is anticipated that they will make significant contributions within the academy in the future.”

Added Maria T. Allison, vice provost and dean of graduate studies, “The recipients of this award undergo serious academic review by the selection committee. These highly competitive fellowships create a special bond between those ASU faculty emeriti who have a lifetime of contribution to their academic professions and those doctoral students who will follow in their footsteps.”

Faculty Emeriti Fellows have varied educational backgrounds and research interests, but they have in common a drive to become educators of higher learning.

  • Grineski is pursuing a Ph.D. in sociology. She came to ASU in 2001 on an Integrative Graduate Education Research and Training (IGERT) in Urban Ecology Fellowship. Her dissertation focuses on challenges faced by poor families with children with asthma, such as indoor/outdoor air quality, access to health care and medications, and housing quality. After earning her degree, she would like to secure a faculty position at a smaller western university where she could teach and conduct research. Her advisor is Professor Bob Bolin.

  • Lam is a doctoral student in language and literacy in the Department of Curriculum and Instruction in the College of Education. She is a Gates Millennium Scholar and chair of the Language Policy Special Interest Group of the National Association for Bilingual Education. Lam has taught English in Japan and high school in the U.S. Her goal is to work with undergraduate and graduate students to help prepare them to become teachers who will be culturally and linguistically sensitive to the needs of students. She also would like to build collaborations between the university and community to help language minority groups maintain and develop programs that will enable students to preserve their native languages and culture. Lam’s advisor is Professor Terrence Wiley.

  • Rice is a doctoral student in the counseling psychology program. Her current dissertation research examines holistic wellness, ethnic identity, and experiences of racism among mixed–race individuals. She also is currently working on a research project to assess diversity climates within doctoral programs at ASU. Rice is co–editor of the American Psychological Association’s policy on multicultural guidelines for education, research, and practice. She has been involved in leadership positions for non–profit organization AWARE, and has also done mental health outreach to the ASU student community. Her plans after graduation include becoming a university professor in California. Her advisor is Professor Patricia Arredondo.
