July 12, 2004

Fennell becomes director of Student Financial Assistance

Craig Fennell has been named director for Student Financial Assistance. Fennell, who came to ASU in 2002 to serve as associate director for the office, has served as the department's interim director since November 2003.

"Financing a college education has become one of the most critical issues for students and their families, in Arizona and across the nation," says Jim Rund, vice president for University Undergraduate Initiatives. "I am pleased that Craig has accepted this position. His experience and his vision for serving students will support ASU's goals of access and affordability."

Fennell brings 15 years of experience in student financial aid to the position. Prior to coming to ASU, Fennell served as director of student financial assistance at Marist College and Newberry College and held leadership positions in financial aid at Duke University and MIT. His experience extends beyond the administration of financial aid programs to include automated system implementation and maintenance, financial aid fund development and donor relations. Fennell began his financial aid career at Iona College, his alma mater, after several years on Wall Street with Chemical Bank.

"I left the private sector for higher education because of my personal experiences in financing my college education," says Fennell, who worked full time and used financial aid to put himself through school. "I am excited about the opportunity to serve the university and the students in this leadership capacity."

Fennell's appointment was effective as of July 1.

Sater, with University Undergraduate Initiatives, can be reached at (480) 965-2200 or (gini.sater@asu.edu).
