Enhancing Recruitment and Retention Through Partner Assistance

A Set of Procedures for the ASU Campuses

Arizona State University is committed to recruiting and retaining excellent faculty, staff, and administrative personnel.  Frequently, the preferred candidates for positions have partners who also require employment. ASU is prepared to respond quickly and professionally to this need.

The goal of university-wide partner assistance procedures is to quickly and professionally identify all ASU employment opportunities for which the individual in need of assistance is qualified and, where an opportunity exists and resources permit, to create the opportunity for that individual to be considered for employment, with the agreement of the hiring unit.

While the university will not be able to successfully assist all partners in finding employment, ASU is committed to making a good faith effort in all cases in which it is able. 

A candidate for a position should alert the search committee chair if he/she wishes to receive partner assistance.  Procedures for identifying partner assistance employment opportunities include surveying both internal and external constituencies.

The following set of procedures guide the internal process when a candidate makes known he/she seeks partner assistance.

  • Once a candidate has informed the search committee chair or the hiring authority that he/she desires partner assistance, the chair or hiring authority should consult with the dean and the provost to determine whether resources are available for such a hire. 
  • If it is determined that resources are available, the chair or hiring authority should contact the designated lead person on that campus. 
  • A lead person has been designated on each campus who will implement an appropriate communication network and ensure timely and comprehensive distribution of assistance requests and timely follow-up and response to the requesting unit. For 2003-04
  • ASU East, David Schwalm (David.Schwalm@asu.edu; 727-1418)
  • ASU Tempe, Nancy Gutierrez (Nancy.Gutierrez@asu.edu; 965-4995)
  • ASU West, Mark Searle (Mark.Searle@asu.edu; 543-6613)
  • In addition to contacting the campus lead person, requesting units should also consult existing resources such as the HR job list web pages.
  • All searches (for both candidate and for the partner) will follow university employment guidelines, appropriate for the particular position.  The hiring unit, in both cases, must authorize the hire.
