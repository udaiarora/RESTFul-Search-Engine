September 17, 2003

University to build fraternity complex

The last time ASU built new housing for campus fraternities, John F. Kennedy was president of the United States. Times have changed, and so will fraternity housing at ASU.

Adelphi II is a public/private partnership project that will create new, small group housing for six ASU fraternities on a site south and east of the existing Adelphi Commons on Apache Boulevard. Adelphi Commons is home of ASU's 10 sorority chapters.

Adelphi II, a $13.4-million project, will be built on Sunset Drive, south of Apache Blvd. The Sunset and Desert Vista apartments as well as the Pigg residence, three properties recently acquired by the university for the project, currently occupy the site. Demolition and site preparation work began Sept. 8 and the formal ground breaking for the project is expected in October.

Sally Ramage, associate dean of student development, says Adelphi II is based on the success of the Adelphi Commons Project.

"Our sororities tell us that Adelphi Commons has worked out exceptionally well," she says. "The new group housing is organized into distinct clusters for each organization or group. Each cluster consists of 44 bed spaces, a community room, a study/dining room, a common kitchen, and a private courtyard. The new project also includes a larger community space for use by any of the groups housed there. Students and alumni worked with the architects to help create a design that reflects the identity of the groups and the importance of the area to the Greek community."

The advantages of the new group housing cited by Ramage include professional management, better maintenance of facilities, nearby location of the sorority housing and being part of the evolving south campus residential development.

"Our goal is to create community spirit for the students involved in the Greek system," she says. "Having the two projects in close proximity will help that sense of community develop and make it easier for the fraternities and sororities to develop joint programming."

The current design will house only six of ASU's 16 national fraternities. However, Ramage says there could be a second phase of development that would accommodate additional fraternities.

"Adelphi II has been designed with the idea of some future expansion," she says. "Any expansion will be determined by the demand for group housing in the future. The continuing interest in ASU by national fraternities in this type of housing, as well as the interest of the unhoused ASU fraternities to move on campus, will be the indicator for future development."

Most of the fraternities that will be housed in the project are currently located on Adelphi Drive south of Apache and east of McAllister Ave. They occupy university owned houses that were constructed during the 1950s. Once the groups move into the new housing in the fall of 2004, the old houses will be demolished.

The developer of the project is Century Development, one of the largest developers of university housing in the nation. Century Management, a subsidiary of Century Development, will manage the project in partnership with ASU once construction is complete. Century Development helped ASU build the neighboring Adelphi Commons project and Century Management currently works with ASU in managing that building as well.

The contractor for the project is the Kitchell Corporation and the project is being designed by Gould/Evans Architects.

Funding for Adelphi II will come from bonds issued by the Arizona Capital Facilities Group, a 623 corporation, which has helped provide funds for the construction of several university projects.
