Parent Advice

George Flores
Phoenix, AZ

George Flores is a father of three daughters, all college graduates from ASU. A resident of Phoenix, Mr. Flores has lived in the valley since his birth, the fourth son of seven children, six of whom graduated from ASU. He received an Associate's Degree from a Maricopa Community College, a Bachelor's Degree in History and Master's Degree in Public Administration, the last two from ASU. He is a retired Deputy City Manager for the City of Phoenix after serving the community for 34 years. He now is an active member of the community, and intends to return to the Public Administration for the city of Phoenix.

As first generation college graduates, Mr. Flores and his wife Vivian, have instilled in their daughters the importance of receiving a college degree. Mr. Flores recalls speaking to his daughters about attending college by saying, "When you go to college and get your Masters?" This way, according to Mr. Flores, they start expecting those accomplishments and "to give young adults confidence," that they can attend a university.

Although his daughters are second generation college graduates, he understands the challenge of many first generation students, those whose parents have not attended a higher education institution. "It is hard for families to know what step to take next," he says, "the scholarships to look for. It is important to support the goals of their students, even if financially it is not possible." He adds that, "No matter how many challenges, it comes down to the person looking out for themselves, and ASU has a lot more to offer than in our generation."

Mr. Flores now serves as the treasurer on the ASU Parents Association Board of Directors, and recommends all parents visit their Parents Association website and explore the different links available. (www.asu.edu/parents).

His involvement with the Parents Association has also greatly contributed to a good parent- student relationship. "Parents believe that once students come to college, their ability to be involved ends. It may be difficult but it can simply start by communicating with their students."

A final word of advice from George Flores, "Talk to other parents and connect with them. Visit websites, phone the Parents Association, and most importantly, talk to your student."
