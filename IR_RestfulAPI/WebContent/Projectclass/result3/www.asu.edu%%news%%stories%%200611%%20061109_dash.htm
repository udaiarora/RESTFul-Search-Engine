November 9, 2006

Polytechnic campus' Dauntless Drumstix Dash raises scholarship funds

It's that time of the year again to register in the Dauntless Drumstix Dash, a timed 5-kilometer road race, a 2-mile walk and a half-mile children's fun run. This year's event kicks off with the children's run at 8 a.m., Nov. 18, at ASU's Polytechnic campus in southeast Mesa, while the 2-mile walk and 5-kilometer run follow at 8:20 a.m. and 8:30 a.m., respectively.

The registration fee is $15 through Nov. 10, $18 after Nov. 10, and $5 for the children's run. On-site registration begins at 6:30 a.m. the day of the race.

All of the net proceeds from the Dash go toward a scholarship fund, which builds a lasting endowment that will help needy students. To date, the Dash has raised more than $7,000 for the scholarship fund.

A children's activity hour will be conducted by the faculty from the School of Educational Innovation and Teacher Preparation. Craft activities for runners' and walkers' children will be provided free of charge. This activity hour allows the serious runners and walkers to focus on their events.

Food and drink will be provided to participants after the race by the Native American student club and the Dash.

Each participant will receive a Dauntless Drumstix Dash T-shirt. Overall first-place male and female winners will receive kachina doll awards, with trophies going to the top male and female age group winners.

Individuals and teams are encouraged to participate, and each runner and walker is asked to bring a canned good to the event as part of a food drive for the Boys & Girls Club, Williams Campus Branch.

For more information, or to register, visit the Web site (www.poly.asu.edu/dash), or contact Ben Fasano by phone at (480) 727-1143 or via e-mail at (ben.fasano@asu.edu).
