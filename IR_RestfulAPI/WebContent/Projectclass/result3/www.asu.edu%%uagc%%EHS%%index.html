Hazardous Materials Shipping and Receiving Policy

A new Hazardous Materials (HM) Shipping and Receiving policy went into effect on February 1, 2006. The policy designates Lab Stores as the default shipper of HM for ASU. In addition, the Fulton School of Engineering, the Department of Chemistry, and the Biodesign Institute are designated as separate HM shipping sites on the Tempe campus. Department of Transportation regulations requires that anyone handling or shipping HM must be trained and certified.

The policy also stipulates that HM can be received ASU departments, but it must be received in designated areas by designated individuals. Anyone receiving HM must be trained and certified. The policy should be posted to the EH&S web page by July 2006. To view a copy of the HM Policy please contact EH&S at 965-1823. More...

AED Program

Sudden cardiac death claims over 250,000 people each year. Most sudden deaths from cardiac arrest occur outside the hospital and only 1 to 5 percent of these patients are estimated to survive to hospital discharge. Most people experiencing cardiac arrest have no history of severe heart disease, and sudden cardiac death is frequently the first manifestation of cardiovascular disease.

Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) are one of the most important life-saving technologies in the past ten years. As AED implementation becomes the accepted practice, Fire Departments, EMS agencies and public access defibrillation programs will have a direct effect on the outcome of sudden cardiac arrest. Early defibrillation has been shown to be critical to improving survival. More...

Fume Hood Program

The chemical fume hoods on Arizona State University (ASU) Polytechnic campus, Tempe campus, and West campus have been inventoried and performance tested per published regulatory recommendations. There are approximately 725 chemical fume hoods across all three ASU campuses and growing. The air flow for each chemical fume hood should be tested annually at a minimum and maintain an average face velocity between 80 and 120 feet per minute depending on the hood type. An inspection sticker is affixed to each tested hood. If the hood does not pass the test an “Out of Service” sign is posted on the hood during repairs. If your chemical fume hood has not been tested in the last year, contact EH&S 965-1823. If your hood is not operating properly or in alarm mode, contact ASU Service Center 965-3633. More...
