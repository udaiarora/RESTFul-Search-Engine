Registration

All times are Mountain Standard Time.

Classes not meeting minimum enrollment by Dec. 8 may be cancelled.

Online

Online registration requests will be processed in the order received. The Web site will be accessible Oct. 2, 12:01am – Dec. 1, 4pm.

Oct. 2 – Dec. 1, 2006
You will receive an e-mail notice of a successful or unsuccessful registration request within two business days.
Successful registration notices will include an INVOICE, payment is due to the Winter Session registration office NO LATER THAN 4pm, Dec. 1.
Your registration is NOT COMPLETE until payment is received. All registration requests submitted between Oct. 2 and Dec. 1 and not paid by 4pm, Dec. 1 will be cancelled.

Find out more about Tuition and Fees.

In Person, Telephone

Dec. 4 – Dec. 21, 2006
Registration requests submitted Dec. 4 – Dec. 21 must be made in person to the Winter Session registration office or by telephone (480) 727-9900. Payment is due at time of registration.
Regardless of submission date, all registrations must be paid by 4pm, Dec. 21, 2006.

Find out more about Tuition and Fees.

Late Registration

The Winter Session registration office will process late registrations FOR ONE DAY ONLY on Dec. 27, 2006. Beginning the first day of Winter Session (Dec. 27), students will be charged a $35 late registration fee.

You can register late by calling (480) 727-9900 and paying with Visa, MasterCard or Discover. If you are registering late with financial aid, check, cash or third party payment, you must register at the Winter Session registration office. All tuition and fees are due at the time of registration.

Late Late Registration

Beginning Dec. 28, to register you must complete a Late Late Registration with the appropriate form approved by the college offering the course. You will need to bring the form to the Winter Session registration office for processing.

Overload Petitions

If you would like to register for more than three Winter Session credit hours, you must request an approved petition form from your college. (Law students are limited to a maximum of two credit hours.) A copy of this approved document must be sent to the Winter Session office (fax: (480) 965-3300) to process your registration request.

Overrides and Advising Clearance

Section-full or eligibility overrides must be obtained from the department offering the course according to established department procedures. Advising clearance must be entered for fall 2006. Overrides or clearances issued prior to Dec. 5 can be entered online. After Dec. 5, approved department overrides or advising clearances must be sent to the Winter Session office (fax: (480) 965-3300; e-mail).
