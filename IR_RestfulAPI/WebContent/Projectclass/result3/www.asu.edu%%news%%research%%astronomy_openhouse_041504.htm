Crab Nebula, little stars take center stage at open house

Twinkle, twinkle, little star, how we wonder what you are.

A talk by Arizona State University student Steve Dwyer on Kirchoff's Laws of Spectroscopy, titled "How We Know What Little Stars Are Made Of," will answer that question at ASU's last regularly scheduled Astronomy Open House of the season, to be held from 8 to 10 p.m. April 30 (Friday) on the roof of Bateman Physical Sciences Center H-Wing, on ASU's Tempe campus.

Also, Jason Cook and Russell Ryan will present results form ASU's Braeside Observatory in Flagstaff, and Allison Loll will describe Hubble Space Telescope images of the Crab Nebula that she has processed for her research.

Finally, Amber Straughn will present a video of her "wild ride" in NASA's zero-gravity "vomit comet," the KC135A that NASA flies to simulate weightlessness.

Telescopes will be set up on the roof, and organizers promise that there will be excellent viewing of the sky (weather permitting).

The open house is suitable for children ages 4 and older. Visitors may arrive anytime between 8 and 10 p.m.
Free parking is available after 7 p.m. in Parking Structure 2 at McAllister Avenue and Tyler Street, on the ASU Tempe campus.

For more information and a map, check the Astronomy web site, <eagle.la.asu.edu/openhouse>, or e-mail astro.info@asu.edu.
