August 28, 2006

ASU Foundation wraps up record fundraising year

ASU and the ASU Foundation closed the 2005-2006 fiscal year June 30 with an unprecedented $149 million raised in cash and property donations. This compares with last year's record total of $99.4 million. The foundation aims to make even greater strides in 2006-2007 with a fund-raising goal of more than $150 million.

The 2005-2006 results are a reflection of the partnership between the ASU Foundation staff and ASU leadership, as well as the generosity of the many donors committed to ASU's success. Two such donors are Ira A. and Mary Lou Fulton, who made a record-setting gift of $100 million to the ASU Foundation for the benefit of ASU in December.

Other large gifts to the ASU Foundation included:

? $6 million from Robert and Sharon McCord to establish the McCord Scholars Program at the W. P. Carey School of Business (they also gave a combined $4 million to the University of Arizona and Northern Arizona University), and $2 million to establish the Robert and Sharon McCord Endowed Chair in Real Estate at the W. P. Carey School.

? $3.5 million from the Donald W. Reynolds Foundation to operate the Reynolds National Center for Business Journalism at the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication.

? $500,000 from Salt River Project to the Del E. Webb School of Construction to fund its Construction Enterprise Initiatives.

Large gifts are transformational for the university. They are not, however, the only donations that create impact. Major and small gifts are also critical to success. The foundation received many such gifts this year from individuals, families, businesses and foundations eager to participate in the exciting effort to move ASU forward.

A large part of the foundation's success in 2005-2006 came from the leadership of former president and chief executive officer Ira Jackson, who left the foundation in June to accept a position at Claremont Graduate University.

The search for a new CEO is ongoing under the guidance of the foundation's board of directors.

In the interim, the foundation continues to accelerate its forward momentum under the leadership of Craig E. Weatherup, who serves as the president, CEO and chairman of the ASU Foundation's board of directors.

?This was another solid year for the university and the foundation,? Weatherup says. ?University leadership, staff and faculty worked hard to secure the contributions necessary to continue to provide scholarships for deserving students, attract top-notch faculty, fund critical research and deliver quality instruction. Still, it's only a springboard to future development.?

 

Steve Reiher, stephen.reiher@asu.edu
