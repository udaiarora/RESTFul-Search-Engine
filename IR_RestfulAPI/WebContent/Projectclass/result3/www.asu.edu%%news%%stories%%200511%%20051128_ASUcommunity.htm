November 28, 2005

Site highlights ASU in the community

ASU has a long tradition of public service and social engagement through programs that diffuse the boundaries between the university and the communities that surround it. To build on that tradition and aid ASU’s pursuit of true social embeddedness, the university is launching the new "ASU in the Community” site.

ASU in the Community compiles basic information about hundreds of ASU programs and activities that benefit the community, from campus-wide community service projects to legal clinics, health services, tutoring and mentoring, summer camps and college readiness programs.

Leadership Breakfast honors community efforts

Arizona State University has undertaken a broad-based approach to community outreach and social engagement. A wide variety of outreach programs and other efforts have made ASU a more dynamic institution as it works to diffuse the boundaries between the university and the community.

This semester’s Leadership Breakfast Series event, scheduled for Tuesday, Nov. 29, 2005, will recognize some of the many community outreach programs and activities as well as the faculty and staff coordinators who make those programs happen.

ASU faculty, staff and students are continually developing mutually beneficial partnerships with the community and establishing new and innovative programs to meet community needs.

The new Web site provides a variety of features that will further enable these ventures and provide future program innovators more information about current programs and the advances they have made in social engagement.

"This could be highly beneficial for collaboration,” says John Schlacter, marketing professor emeritus at the W. P. Carey School of Business. “Too few of us know what's going on around us.”

The ASU in the Community site includes a search feature that enables users to identify and locate programs by interest area, keyword, city, participants, campus, zip code, college or any combination of these criteria.

Each outreach program listed on the site has a profile page maintained by that program’s coordinator so that the most up-to-date information is always available to anyone trying to learn about a program. Because the site provides such easy updating capabilities, program coordinators can keep the details of their programs as current as possible with minimal effort.

Users can locate ASU’s community outreach programs using an interactive mapping tool over a dynamic backdrop of census data, school districts, legislative districts, congressional districts, city council districts or zip codes. This feature enables users to find specific programs in a given part of the Valley, or even see what types of programs are located in that area.

“The mapping tool could help us develop or locate programs in need areas,” says Keith Southergill, Program Coordinator at Barrett Honors College.

Each week, ASU in the Community also runs a feature story on a specific program, event or individual who has helped enhance the collaborative efforts between the university and the community. Programs like Leaps and Bounds at the Office of Youth Preparation and the Arizona Mentor Society are among those featured.

ASU in the Community’s initial development was a collaborative effort between the Office of University Initiatives, Undergraduate Initiatives Technology Services and University Undergraduate Initiatives, with ongoing development and production by the Office of University Initiatives.

"ASU in the Community represents the ongoing efforts of ASU to enhance its social embeddedness, one of the design imperatives of the New American University,” says Kimberly Loui, special adviser to ASU President Michael Crow and executive director of the Office of University Initiatives.

"The programs and individuals featured on the site have demonstrated an ongoing dedication to community outreach that makes ASU in the Community an increasingly valuable and comprehensive resource,” she says.

The ASU in the Community Web site represents the first phase in an ongoing effort to enhance outreach and engagement at ASU. The site also enables users to provide feedback and suggestions for future site improvements. Going forward, ASU in the Community will continue to enhance and extend the tradition of public service long established at ASU.

"The comprehensiveness of this Web site is exciting and sorely needed; this is something that will definitely come in handy and will be a very useful resource,” says Denise Quiroz, communications manager for the Office of the President.
