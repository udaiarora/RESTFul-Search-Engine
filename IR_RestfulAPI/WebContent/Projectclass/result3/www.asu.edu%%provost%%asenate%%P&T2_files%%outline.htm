      Task 1. Review and codify the various university reports created in recent years including those on compensation, teaching, and service and post tenure review.
Update ACD Manual
1.1 ASPC supports a reorganization and revision of ACD 506, and
encourages the Provost?s Personnel Advisory Committee to proceed with such revisions as recommended in the implementation plan, and
looks forward to subsequent approval of final revisions as part of an ongoing process of policy maintenance.
Peer Review
1.2 ASPC supports the recommendation to revise ACD 506-06 requiring peer review in the annual performance review process,
 but recommends deleting the qualifier ?unless a unit votes otherwise?.
 ASPC believes that peer review should be included in the annual performance review process to assure open, consistent, and fair reviews.
Research & Teaching
1.3 ASPC agrees that the pursuit of excellence in both research/creative activities and teaching/instruction should be required in all forms of faculty evaluations.
 Units must establish and communicate thresholds and evidence patterns to objectively define excellence in the context of their unit?s mission and expectations.
Service & Workload Agreements
1.4 ASPC agrees that service should be evaluated as an integral part of faculty responsibilities, and
evaluated in accordance with negotiated workload agreements.
Task 2.
Examine current ACD promotion and tenure policies and procedures (ACD   506-07) and recommend changes to achieve desired university flexibility and adaptability.
Promotion to Associate
2.1 ASPC agrees that promotion to Associate Professor should be based on meeting expectations for excellence in both
teaching/instruction, and
research /creative activity.
Consider all contributions
2.2 ASPC agrees that promotion and tenure decisions should consider affirmative action/ equal opportunity goals and the service contributions of the individual.
ASPC also believes that contributions toward creating a productive professional environment?rather than a ?collegial work environment?, as proposed by the Task Force-- should be considered in evaluations.
ASPC concludes that ?collegiality? is too vague to fairly evaluate.
Further clarification of this requirement is needed.
Consider Workload Agreements
2.3 ASPC agrees that ASU needs to insure that
all professional contributions be considered in all faculty evaluation processes,
 including promotion and tenure decisions,
in accordance with annually negotiated workload agreements.
Make procedures consistent
2.4   ASPC agrees that it is desirable to establish
common definitions and
consistent procedures among various faculty evaluations as recommended by the P&T Task Force.
Task 3. Clarify the relationship between annual reviews, probationary reviews, and tenure reviews.
Different Reviews,
Different Purposes
ASPC agrees that annual reviews and probationary, promotion and tenure reviews serve different purposes.
While it is desirable to create common definitions and documentation,
requiring a direct linkage between annual reviews and probationary, promotion and tenure reviews is not desirable
(because they serve different purposes).
Task 4. Examine the concept of excellence and the number of areas of excellence required in promotion and tenure cases.
Excellence
4.1  ASPC agrees with the recommendation to continue the requirement of an overall record of excellence and the promise of continued excellence  with a clarification that excellence in both teaching and research/creative activity be required.
Units should evaluate excellence within the context of the unit?s goals and the individual?s negotiated workload agreements.
Pursuit of excellence and continuous improvement should support the expectation of continued excellence.
Units must communicate and clarify their expectations for excellence in their by-laws or other documents that are made available to the faculty.
"ASPC further agrees that it..."
ASPC further agrees that it should be possible to establish the required  ?national reputation? through any form of scholarship and should not be limited to research/creative activity;
 however, achieving a national reputation in an area other than research/creative activity is expected to be unusual.
The expectation of excellence in teaching should in no way be interpreted to suggest that excellence in research/creative activity is diminished.
Rather, the emphasis should be that the pursuit of excellence and full engagement is required in all activities and assignments so that all contributions toward achieving the university mission are appropriately considered.
Evaluate All Service
ASPC agrees with the recommendation that excellence in service be assessed in promotion to full professor, but
disagrees with the recommendation that service be assessed primarily in relation to service to the public,
believing that all professional contributions must be considered to assure fairness.
Task 5. Recommend changes in the evidence structure for tenure and promotion.
"ASPC agrees that establishing common..."
ASPC agrees that establishing common evidentiary elements for evaluation of faculty work is desirable and supports the proposed revisions of ACD 506-01.
multiple measures of instructional effectiveness
peer review of instructional materials, courseware, and the development of learning environments
high quality evidence and documentation in each area
university taskforce be appointed to determine and communicate best practices in evaluating the quality of teaching, and to recommend models of evaluation.
PAC 20:
 Provost?s Advisory Committee
ASPC agrees that a process for updating and maintaining personnel decision processes requires clarification and refinement.
 The development of an Advisory Committee to assist the Assistant Provost for Personnel (PAC-20)
a positive step toward wider participation
Senate and ASPC review of recommendations and revisions of the ACD Manual is essential for the legitimacy of this process.
University P&T Recommendations
ASPC asks to be informed  of any process improvement recommendations by the University Promotion and Tenure Committee
Task6. And, Task 7Address issues of joint appointments, work in centers, and interdisciplinary activities. Addresses the issue of multiple career paths
Non-Traditional Appointments
ASPC agrees that faculty performance evaluation reviews in non-traditional appointments should continue under normal guidelines, but
specific agreements about workload allocations should be sufficient to improve consistency and assure fairness.
The development of workload agreements and memos of understanding that clearly identify the home unit, participating units, and performance expectations are required to facilitate effective and fair evaluations.
Task 8. Recommend ways in which schools and departments can most effectively adhere to university policies while at the same time capitalizing on their own uniqueness.
Update Policies
ASPC believes that units and colleges must: update personnel policies for faculty performance evaluation decisions,
make any changes needed for consistency with university policies, and
actively communicate performance expectations to faculty.
Academic Senate Resolution #3
Whereas, the Senate agrees that the Arizona State University Promotion and Tenure Policy (ACD 501-513) should be updated to improve clarity, coherence, and consistency and to promote high quality decision and review processes.
The Academic Senate recommends the acceptance of the Promotion and Tenure Task Force Recommendations (see attachment 1) subject to the clarifications and changes recommended by the Academic Senate Personnel Committee (see attachment 2). The Academic Senate recommends the appointment of a university taskforce to investigate best practices for evaluating the quality of teaching/learning and recommend models of evaluation to facilitate implementation by academic units.
Senate Resolution 3 cont.
Furthermore, the Senate applauds the formation of the Provost?s Personnel Advisory Committee (PAC20) as a mechanism for insuring campus wide input into the process of implementing and maintaining university personnel policies (ACD Manual).
