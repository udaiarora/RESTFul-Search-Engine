JoAnn Yeoman Tongret

JoAnn Yeoman Tongret, Special Collections: an award-winning theatre actress, director, choreographer and ASU professor who in a “professions of dreams’ succeeded on several levels.

 

The Charlie Aaron Show, 1950s, an early live Phoenix television
show on KPHO, Channel 5. This variety show featured two ballet
numbers per week. Yeoman had frequent solos.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection

 

 

 

Karen, The Wickedest Witch, Phoenix Little Theatre, 1954, with Bill Schub. This was Yeoman’s first leading role and the beginning of her long association with director Don Doyle.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection

 

 

 

 


Spanish variation, Coppelia, Phoenix Little
Theatre Ballet Corps, 1959, a resident ballet
company under the direction of British Ballet
Mistress Eileen Colgrove, formerly of Sadlers
Wells, London, England. This was the first full
production by this company.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection

 

 

 

 

 

Ensemble, Funny Girl, Buster Bonoff’s Phoenix Star Theatre,
1965, with Larry Fuller. Bonoff produced plays in Phoenix for three winters with directors and actors from New York City and Los Angeles. Fuller went on to choreograph musicals such as,
Evita and Sweeney Todd on Broadway in New York.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection

 

 

Princess Winifred, Once Upon a Mattress, Phoenix Musical Theater,
1967, with Paul Groathaus. This company previously named Phoenix
Civic Light Opera began in the 1940s and produced 5-6 major musicals
per year using local performers and out-of-town directors. Yeoman started as a member of the ensemble and worked her way
up to leading roles during her six years with the company.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection

 

 

Dulcinea, Man of La Mancha, ASU Lyric Opera Theatre, Gammage Auditorium, 1970; Tim Sullivan, Tim Heathman, John Windsor, John Packard, Jack Mecham, Gary Naylor, Yeoman. This play is Yeoman’s most favorite script. She has appeared in it in Westbury, NY with John Raitt, at Theater by the Sea in New Hampshire, in Flagstaff in association with author Dale Wasserman and as co-choreographer in Phoenix with performer John Cullum.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection

 

Ensemble, Merci, Mercer!, Camelback Inn Summer Dinner Theatre,
1975; (left to right) Top: Kip Miller, John Windser, Bottom: Cindy Dyer,
Pat Kennedy, Yeoman. This cabaret was directed by Mel Weiser, who started Actor’s Inner Circle, a Phoenix professional theatre company.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection

 

Director, Company, Phoenix Little Theatre, 1980.
Yeoman is in the lower left wearing a sweatshirt with “Boss”.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection

 

Mami, Accelerando, ASU Prism Theatre’s Inaugural production, late 1990s. Yeoman received a Scottie Award as Best Guest Artist for her performance.
JoAnn Yeoman Tongret Collection
