Dream Catchers:
The Papers of Four Arizona Women

 

Some young girls have a dream to be an actor; an athlete; an artist; or a beauty queen. Four women from Arizona donated their papers to the Department of Archives and Manuscripts which document the transformation of a dream into a reality.

On exhibit are photographs that illustrate the women?s talents and skills along with their determination, hard work and an ability to recognize opportunities that lead to success.

 

 

 

 

Rosie Miranda Andrade, Chicano Research Collection: a professional softball player who was an outstanding fielder that was a star player at three infield positions during a 22-year career which included 8 national tournaments. Her softball career spanned the period from 1947 to 1969. Rosie saw the opportunities in professional softball and her dream was to "play with the best"; it was a dream come true. Rosie was inducted into the Arizona Foundation Softball Hall of Fame in 1979. Chicano Research Collection

  

 

 

 

 

JoAnn Yeoman Tongret, Special Collections: an award-winning theatre actress, director, choreographer and ASU professor who in a ?professions of dreams? succeeded on several levels.

 

 

 

 

Jacque Mercer, Arizona Collection: Miss Phoenix, Miss Arizona and Miss America in 1949. An ambitious, talented, and intelligent young person, she began earning awards in the 4-H at an early age, modeled in her teens and aspired to the theatre. Arizona Collection

  

 

   

 

 

Rosemary Apple Blossom Lonewolf, Labriola Collection: An artist who was taught the family tradition in contemporary Indian pottery and became famous for her miniature seed pots. Her dreams expanded into large scale murals. It was a journey that she realized offered an ?opportunity to show other indigenous women that a dream can take years to see it through development and into fruition.?
