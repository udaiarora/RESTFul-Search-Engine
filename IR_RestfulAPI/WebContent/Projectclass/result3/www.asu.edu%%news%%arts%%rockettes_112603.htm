November 26, 2003

Rockettes bring holiday show to Gammage

The world famous Rockettes will make a special encore engagement in Phoenix this holiday season with a new production and at a new theater of "The Radio City Christmas Spectacular," A 2003 edition of "The Christmas Spectacular" will run at ASU's Gammage Auditorium as a special offering of the M&I Bank Broadway in Arizona series from Dec. 12 - 31 at ASU's Gammage Auditorium.

This year's "Christmas Spectacular" will feature several all-new production numbers such as the tap-infused "Twelve Days of Christmas" and the enchanting "Christmas Dreams." In addition, the entire cast will perform "I'm There" as Santa creatively explains how he can be in so many places at once. Returning to the show are classic scene favorites such as "The Parade of the Wooden Soldiers" and "The Living Nativity."

Once again, children of all ages will be enchanted by 90 minutes of live holiday entertainment where snowmen shimmy, reindeer fly, elves rap and the Radio City Rockettes perform their fancy footwork and eye-high kicks. Those who may have glowing memories of the famed New York production of "The Radio City Christmas Spectacular" will be thrilled to see all the spectacle and excitement brought to Phoenix.

"'The Radio City Christmas Spectacular' is a holiday tradition for millions of families around the country," says Howard Kolins, executive producer. "We realize that not everyone can get to New York for Christmas, so we'll bring Christmas to you."

In 1994, due to popular demand, Radio City Entertainment broadened the beloved holiday theatrical's schedule for the first time to encompass markets outside New York, while running simultaneously with the New York show.

For more than three quarters of a century, the Radio City Rockettes, the world's most famous precision dance troupe, have been a legendary force in entertainment, with a rich history of skill and dedication to their craft. The dance company first kicked to life in 1925 as the "Missouri Rockets" and made their show business debut in St. Louis, the realization of a long-time dream of their creator, Russell Markert.

The world's most famous dance troupe has truly become an American tradition, performing annually in the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade and the Rockefeller Center Tree lighting.

For information about "The Radio City Christmas Spectacular" show schedule and tickets, call ASU's Gammage Auditorium Box Office at (480) 965-3434 or Ticketmaster at (480) 784-4444 or visit (www. asugammage.com).
