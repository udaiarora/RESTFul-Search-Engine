An Evening with John Leguizamo

Emmy Award-winner John Leguizamo will share an intimate evening of conversation about his career and the riotous cast of characters he draws from his imagination and his real life at 7:30 p.m., March 8, at ASU's Gammage Auditorium.

A multi-faceted performer, John Leguizamo has established a career that defies categorization. "Freak," Leguizamo's third one-man show, ended a successful run on Broadway in 1998. Along with the Tony Award nominations for best play and best performance by a leading actor in a play, Leguizamo won the Drama Desk and the Outer Critic's Circle Awards for outstanding solo performance. "Freak," directed by Spike Lee, aired on HBO and earned Leguizamo the Emmy Award for Outstanding Performance in a Variety or Music Program. Fall of 2001 saw Leguizamo's return to Broadway with his latest one-man show, "Sexaholix...a love story." Leguizamo's performance was nominated for an Outer Critics Circle Award for outstanding solo performance and the show received a Tony nomination for best special theatrical performance. The show, which aired on HBO in spring 2002, also toured the country.

Leguizamo has had film roles in Baz Luhrmann's "Moulin Rouge," the voice of Sid the Sloth in "Ice Age," Spike Lee's "Summer of Sam," Baz Luhrmann's "William Shakespeare's Romeo + Juliet," the voice of The Rat in Twentieth Century Fox's comedy "Dr. Dolittle," and "To Wong Foo: Thanks For Everything, Julie Newmar."

Tickets are $35 and $25. There are a limited number of $100 VIP seats available, which include a reception with Leguizamo following the performance. Tickets are available at all Ticketmaster outlets, (www. ticketmaster.com), or by calling (480) 784-4444 or (480) 965-3434.

This article appeared in the February 27, 2004 issue of ASU Insight.
