February 23, 2006

MU honors student leaders at 2006 Sun Devil Stars

When it comes to student leadership, there are stars all around us at ASU. In an effort to recognize this diversity of young leaders on campus, ASU’s Memorial Union coordinates Sun Devil Stars, an annual leadership recognition program.

From left: Brian Beetso, Bee-Lian Quah, Matt Cicinelli, Emily Smith, Tara Egnatios, Brandon Mandy, Kim Demaree, James Randall, Marcia Torres are some of the 67 undergraduates honored as Sun Devil Stars.

Now in its eighth year, Sun Devil Stars serves to recognize students across campus who are not necessarily in a visible leadership position, but have demonstrated leadership characteristics that have boosted the ASU campus. More than 450 students have been recognized to date for their contributions.

In a ceremony Feb. 21, 67 undergraduate students were recognized as Sun Devil Stars. The ceremony included brief speeches by Sally Ramage, interim vice president for Student Affairs, and Brett Perozzi, director of the Memorial Union.

David Wahls, a 2003 Sun Devil Star recipient, addressed the crowd during the event and discussed extending student leadership skills beyond college life. Wahls, a 2005 ASU alum, works as a project manager for the chief of staff at the ASU Foundation.

Students are nominated for the Sun Devil Star award by ASU faculty and staff in January. These students represent a variety of leadership roles on campus, including:

• Student employees.

• Individuals who volunteer on campus or in the greater community.

• Members of student organizations.

Here are some excerpts of nominations received for this year’s Sun Devil Stars. All recipients and nominations are available on the Web at (www.asu.edu/mu/sundevilstars).

* * *

“Brian Morin is a phenomenal student who is dedicated to helping others in and out of the classroom. As a future teacher, Brian has prepared himself by volunteering in local schools and has masterfully handled some tough classrooms in his facilitation of DCI 194. Brian serves ASU in a multitude of ways, including service as a College of Education Student Council officer; work as a senator in the Undergraduate Student Government; and as a student employee in the Office of Professional Field Experiences. Brian truly is an outstanding example of a student leader and a Sun Devil Star.”

– Nominated by Thomas Dickson, College of Education and 2002 Sun Devil Star recipient

* * *

“Matthew Dircks is in his second year of working for Operations & Facilities in the Athletic Department. For being one of the younger members of the student-staff, Matt has demonstrated the leadership of a veteran. He has the ability to work with everyone well – from his co-workers, to clients of the athletic department and the coaching staff. He is willing to do any type of assignment, big or small. He’s the first to volunteer and will see everything through to the end. Matt truly defines what a team player is.”

– Nominated by Hermelinda Llamas, Intercollegiate Athletics

* * *

“Monica Van Steenberg is a wife, mother, student and student worker who balances all with finesse, patience and professionalism. Monica joined the Graduation Office at a time when our office was short-staffed and in need of assistance. Her assigned duty was to man the front counter and assist students applying for graduation. She not only learned her job but also was quick to learn and take on other tasks to assist the staff members who, at times, were overwhelmed with their daily assignments. Monica volunteers to help whenever and wherever she can, always with a smile. As a returning student, she exhibits leadership in the way she treats each student that visits our office. Monica is a superb representative of the graduation office and is a valuable member of our team. Monica is a dedicated wife, mother, student and student worker and deserves to add ‘Sun Devil Star’ to the many hats she wears.”

– Nominated by Doreen Phippen, Office of the Registrar

* * *

“Sarah Kennedy is a role model, leader and community volunteer. She will graduate in May with a degree in bioengineering and has a 3.91 GPA. Besides taking a full course load and working every single semester and summer in college, she has participated in outreach and fundraiser events for Society of Women Engineers, Tau Beta Pi and Alpha Eta Mu Beta Honor Society. She is the Tau Beta Pi vice president and the AEMB treasurer. She is a participant in the Fulton Undergraduate Research Initiative, in which she is developing an advanced actuation system for prosthetic devices that will be the focus of her honors thesis.”

– Nominated by Mary Anderson-Rowland, Department of Industrial Engineering

* * *

“Nancy Herrera is a dedicated student and commendable leader. As the secretary in STEP (Students Toward Educational Progress) Multicultural Honor Society, she always goes above and beyond her duties and seems to fulfill everything else that needs to get done for the student organization to be successful. She is friendly and makes every student feel as if they belong in the organization, which is one of the reasons why STEP continues to have great involvement among its members.”

– Nominated by Rhea Gomez, Multicultural Student Services

* * *

“Amanda Keim is a calm, quiet leader who can manage the almost inevitable daily crises while keeping here staff focused and productive. She’s a strong journalist, but it takes more than that to run a newsroom well. She is also a strong manager of people – one who is open and collaborative and still clearly in charge. Amanda also sets a high standard for the State Press. She understands the role of the student newspaper in the ASU community, and does her best to be an accurate and fair provider of news and information.”

– Nominated by Kristin Gilger, Student Media
