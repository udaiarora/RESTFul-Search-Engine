USI 107–03: Student E-Mail

Purpose

To ensure that students receive timely official communications from faculty, staff, and other students at ASU that are beneficial in contributing to their success at the university

Sources

Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost
Academic Assembly
University Technology Office

Policy

ASU e-mail is an official means of communication among ASU’s students, faculty, and staff. Students are expected to ensure that e-mail is accessed, read, and acted upon in a timely fashion. Students bear the responsibility of missed messages and should check their ASU-assigned e-mail on a regular basis.

ProcedureD

Responsibility
Action
ASU University Technology Office
  1. Assign @asu.edu e-mail address at the point of application for admission to ASU.
Student
  1. Either activate ASU mailbox and begin to use the assigned @asu.edu e-mail address or go to the electronic post office and forward @asu.edu e-mail to a preferred e-mail address (such as @hotmail.com, @aol.com, etc.).

    Note:Having e-mail redirected does not change the individual’s responsibility for reading and responding to official communications sent to the ASU e-mail address. Information and warnings about forwarding are available at The Mailbox on ASU’s Information Technology Web site.

  1. Read ASU e-mail policies at The Mailbox.

  2. Update the forwarding order as needed.

  3. Retrieve e-mail messages regularly from the @asu.edu address and act upon the messages appropriately.
If the student wishes to prevent the release of the @asu.edu e-mail address in addition to other directory information:
  1. Obtain, complete, and submit a Withhold Directory Information Request form, which is available online.
endtable
