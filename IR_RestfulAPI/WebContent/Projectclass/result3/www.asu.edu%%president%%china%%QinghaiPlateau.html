Qinghai Plateau

Professor Andrew Smith’s work in this geographical area is designed to determine the role of the Plateau Pika as a keystone species and as an ecosystem engineer, and to test the role the Plateau Pika plays in determining the natural hydrology of Qinghai’s alpine meadows. The work is designed to include overarching conservation implications of these findings, including economic and cost/benefit analyses of Plateau Pika control programs.

Dr. Smith’s graduate student, Brigitte Hogan (an NSF fellow) is conducting the ecosystem-level work, and Dr. Smith plans to spearhead the ecohydrology work and the overall conservation synthesis. This work is designed to cover a large geographic area – but largely confined to the Amdo region of Qinghai province.

Dr Smith’s work in the area also concerns determining the reproductive strategies of the Plateau Pika, their social and genetic population structure, their intra- and inter-population gene flow, and the influence of environmental factors on their dispersal. This work is designed to compare the population dynamics of Plateau Pikas under different grazing regimes, as well as to determine the relationship between their population ecology and anthropogenic disturbances and natural disasters on the plateau.

The work entails a strong working relationship with Dr. Zhang Yanming and his students. Last summer the team selected an ideal field site near Maqen (southern Qinghai), gridded the site, and captured, handled and marked a large number of pikas. The genetic analysis of collected tissues has not yet been initiated. This protocol will be underway for the next several years.

Dr. Smith has served as International Coordinator on the Biodiversity Working Group of the China Council for International Cooperation on Environment and Development (with several related activities ongoing).

As a function of his role as Chair of the IUCN/Species Survival Commission Lagomorph Specialist Group, Dr. Smith will be organizing faunal surveys in Xinjiang and Sichuan in 2006.
