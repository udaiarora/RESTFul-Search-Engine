Undergraduate Program in Sociology

The sociology major is a 30 hour program that includes 12 hours of required courses in introductory sociology, methods, statistics, and sociological theory. Majors may elect to concentrate their study in specialized areas, such as the family, work and organizations, diversity issues, urban studies, or international studies. An additional 15 hours of related courses are also required (see advisor or curriculum plan for additional information).

The program is served through both full-time faculty and instructors and has approximately 375 majors at a given time. Class size ranges from 35-45 for upper division courses, and the program is designed to be completed in four years through day and/or evening classes. Required courses are generally offered each summer, making it easier for students to complete their course work in four years. Advisement is available throughout the year.
