H.3.1: Professional Development Time

Effective 12/20/89 .. Revised 12/20/89

PURPOSE

Under the Conditions of Professional Service academic professionals are granted continuing status or reappointment on the basis of excellent performance and the promise of continued excellence. It is therefore necessary for the individual academic professional to have an opportunity to prepare him/herself to meet the rigors of the retention process and, once having passed the probationary period, to continue to demonstrate evidence of professional development and contribution.


POLICY

Up to four hours per week may be designated as professional development time, to be utilized by an academic professional to work on activities that meet goals related to professional development and contribution. Examples of such activities are:

  • preparing a paper for delivery at a conference
  • writing an article for publication
  • preparing a request for a grant or academic leave
  • taking a course for the purpose of continuing education in academic librarianship or other disciplines

Professional development time is not to be construed as being automatically granted to an academic professional. It must be related to performance goals which lead to retention, promotion, and/or continued professional growth and creativity.


Projects that require more time or present unusual circumstances not covered by the policies above are eligible for consideration, and will be decided on a case-by-case basis.


PROCEDURE

The academic professional seeking professional development time must develop and forward to his/her primary evaluator a proposal for the use of professional development time. The proposal should include:

  • a statement of purpose
  • period of calendar year needed
  • how activity relates to goals
  • tentative schedule
  • impact on other responsibilities

The primary evaluator will evaluate the proposal, taking into consideration the department and library goals, impact on operation of the department, and the stated goals of the academic professional. Each primary evaluator will establish deadlines for submitting proposals so that all requests can be considered in light of department needs. If the primary evaluator has more worthwhile proposals than can be accommodated at one time, he/she will make every effort to accommodate as many proposals as possible by negotiating shorter periods of time, establishing a queue for future consideration, or other appropriate arrangements.


The primary evaluator then forwards proposals and recommendations to his/her Associate Dean for final approval.
