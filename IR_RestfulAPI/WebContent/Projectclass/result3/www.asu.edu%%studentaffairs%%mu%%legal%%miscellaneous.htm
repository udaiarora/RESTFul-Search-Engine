The ASU Student Legal Assistance office provides information and assistance for many other legal matters which have not been specifically identified in the preceding portions of our online materials. Please contact the office for an appointment if you need information or help with a problem which has not been discussed here.

Small Claims Court

The Small Claims Division of each Justice of the Peace court in Arizona is available to provide the public with a forum for small claims. The ASU Student Legal Assistance Office has prepared a brief guide to explain the operation of the Small Claims Division and to assist you in pursuing a small claims case. If you have any questions about filing an action in Small Claims Division, or if you are currently being sued in Small Claims, make an appointment with the ASU Student Legal Assistance attorney.
Who Can Use the Small Claims Division?
The Small Claims Division can be used by anyone who wants to bring a claim against another party provided that it does not exceed the statutory limits. Partnerships, corporations and individuals can sue and be sued in the Small Claims Division.
What Kinds of Claims Can Be Filed?
You can use the Small Claims Division if you are involved in a dispute over money or personal property when the amount involved does not exceed the statutory limits. Typical cases include claims for money debts, personal injuries, property damage, damages for failure to fulfill the terms of a contract, damages from automobile accidents, and failure to return security deposits.
When Should You Use the Small Claims Division?
It is very important that you file your suit immediately after you realize that no settlement can be reached. The Statute of Limitations sets a specific time period in which a person having a claim must file the suit. This time period varies with the type of claim you have. The ASU Student Legal Assistance office guide to the Small Claims Division contains more details regarding current filing fees, maximum amount you can sue for, and the procedures of a Small Claims action. For more information, contact the ASU Student Legal Assistance office.

Contracts/Partnerships/Business

ASU Student Legal Assistance is available for consultation on issues regarding contracts, partnerships, and business relationships. The ASU Student Legal Assistance office has a variety of resources available for students in each issue, and students are encouraged to make an appointment with the attorney to discuss specific situations in each area. The attorney can:
  • review contracts and answer general questions a student may have about the legal aspects of entering into a particular contract. In addition, the attorney can advise students on forming their own contract with another party.
  • answer questions about forming partnerships, corporations, and limited liability companies, including how to establish the desired business arrangement, legal aspects of participating in it, and dissolving it.
  • advise students on starting a business, participating in a business, and ending a business relationship.
For more information, contact ASU Student Legal Assistance for an appointment.
