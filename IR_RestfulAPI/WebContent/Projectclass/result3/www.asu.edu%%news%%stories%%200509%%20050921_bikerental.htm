September 21, 2005

Parking and Transit Services funds bike rental program

The Undergraduate Student Government and ASU Parking and Transit Services is set to introduce their latest project, USG Community Bikes. The program allows ASU students and employees to rent a bicycle free for 10 days and promotes the use of alternative transportation.

USG Community Bikes will be open for business in October on the south side of the Student Recreation Complex on the Tempe campus. Gold-painted bikes will be available for rent to ASU students and employees for up to 10 days, from 10 a.m. – 2 p.m., Monday – Friday, and from 11 a.m. – 1 p.m. on Saturdays.

Financing for the program came from one of the biggest advocates of alternative transportation, ASU Parking and Transit Services. With a projected 5,000 parking spaces to be lost in the next five years, PTS is investing in other modes of transportation for the ASU community. Their dollars provided the financing for USG to hire student staff for the program, as well as additional bike racks, signs, helmets and headlights. The money also allowed the purchase of the first 20 bikes for the program’s fleet. Additional bikes will be supplied from salvage donated by ASU’s Department of Public Safety.

“There are many advantages to the program,” says USG Vice President Corinne Widmer. “People can use it to get around campus, or for recreational use. There are so many great places to go biking here at ASU, and there is a great biking community here. It also provides another alternative in helping combat the vehicle congestion on this campus.”

USG earlier this semester announced the re-opening of the USG Bike Co-op, also located on the south side of the SRC. The Bike Co-op provides the ASU community a low-cost alternative to bike maintenance and repair and is operated during the same business hours as the USG Community Bikes program. PTS again partnered with USG by providing funding for the initial stock of tools, references and supplies.

In an effort to promote the recent alternative transportation programs, USG will be sponsoring a Bike and Skate Rally from 10 a.m. – 2 p.m., Sept. 21, at Hayden Lawn. The event will allow the university community to learn more about the programs and visit booths sponsored by local bicycle businesses. Door prizes, free food and live entertainment are scheduled for the event. PTS has provided a mountain bike to be given away to one lucky attendant to the rally.

“Students need to understand that the pollution from vehicles affects the environment,” Widmer says. “It will have consequences for our children and grandchildren. We live in a beautiful part of the country where we can easily bike, walk or take the bus instead of driving solo in a car. We should utilize alternatives to help us be healthy and safe.”

The Bike and Skate Rally also will include the launch of the “Helmets and Headlights” safety campaign by PTS and will feature bicycle helmets and headlights for purchase at 50 percent off the retail price. Buying the bicycle helmet and headlight (complete with tail light and batteries) will cost $25.

The campaign is part of a nationwide effort to prevent bicycle injuries. According to recent national statistics, 85 percent of all injuries from bicycle accidents could be reduced by wearing a helmet. Also, 10 percent of all bicycle ridership is at night, yet evening accidents comprise 50 percent of all bicycle accidents.

For more information on the bike programs or other transportation alternatives, contact the ASU Travel Reduction Office at (480) 965-1072.
