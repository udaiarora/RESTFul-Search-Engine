March 7, 2003

Colleges of Law, Nursing add to leadership teams

The ASU colleges of Law and Nursing have named Christopher Baier and Karen Sousa to their leadership teams, as assistant and associate dean, respectively.

Baier, deputy director of the Arizona Department of Commerce, has been named assistant dean for institutional operations at the ASU College of Law.

Sousa has been appointed as interim associate dean for Graduate Programs & Research in the College of Nursing. Sousa replaces Pamela Kidd who passed away last December.

Baier has been deputy director of the commerce department since January 2001. He was responsible for directing, managing, and leading an agency of more than 100 employees in such diverse programs as Business Development, Small Business Assistance, Community Development and Planning, Economic Policy and Research, Workforce Development and Energy.

Previously Baier served as assistant deputy director for administration and director of sports development at the department. At the College of Law, Baier will assume the duties generally assigned to a chief financial officer of a corporation.

College of Law Dean Patricia White said, "Chris will be an enormous addition to the law school. He is a sophisticated financial analyst and lawyer."

Baier received a bachelor of science degree in accounting from Villanova in 1986 and a juris doctor in 1992 from the University of Arizona College of Law, where he received the Order of the Coif. He clerked for the Arizona Supreme Court and received the fourth highest score in the state on the entrance examination for certified public accountants.

"I am pleased to be part of Dean White's team and look forward to working with the faculty, staff and students toward continued excellence at the College of Law," Baier said.

During her five years at the College of Nursing, Sousa has shown outstanding leadership and willingness to contribute to the mission of the college. Her knowledge of research is extensive and she has become a familiar figure to many across campus, said Dean Barbara Durand.

Sousa received a Ph.D. from the University of California, San Francisco, in 1996, following an extensive career in hospital management. After spending two years establishing a program of research at UCSF

School of Nursing, she accepted a position at ASU College of Nursing.
Sousa's research focuses on nursing systems and health outcomes with a particular interest in health-related quality of life. During her career, Sousa became intrigued with structural equation modeling as a statistical approach to answer challenging nursing research questions and has been using this approach. She has received several research grants and in 2000 was awarded a major research grant from the National Institutes of Health to study health-related quality of life in arthritis and AIDS patients.
