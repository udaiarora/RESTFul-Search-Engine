Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Caucus

October 31, 1996

Attending from CAPS: Gary Hembree, Lise Hawkos, Karl Weiss, Chris West

1. Gary started the meeting by reporting on the status of ACD 507-05 and that CAPS was in the process of revising their bylaws.

2. Walter Harris, Vice Provost, was then introduced to the caucus and addressed the agenda items:

  • Revision of ACD 507-05: Dr. Harris restated the history and concluded by saying that while ABOR will probably revisit the AP Conditions of Professional Service document at some point in the future, APs need to have a policy in place, therefore the Academic Senate Personnel committee will form a subcommittee (comprised of members of that committee and academic professionals) to revise the policy to be in conformance with faculty policy. Hopefully this new policy could be in place by the end of the academic year.

  • Impact of Faculty Post-Tenure Review on Academic Professionals: Dr. Harris said that as far as he knew there would be no immediate impact on academic professionals. To date, the ABOR has only discussed the faculty.

  • Elimination of AP Positions: It is not the university's intention to eliminate the category. Should a decision be made to look at and eliminate continuing status, it is highly unlikely that any current APs with continuing status be effected. It is clear that when the ABOR established the category they were interested in tenuring people/positions who needed academic freedom. The issue of a position needing academic freedom will be crucial in appoving any new continuing track AP lines. The University is currently developing criteria for establishing continuing-track AP positions. The University's position is that APs are just as valuable as any other group of employee on campus.

  • In closing Dr. Harris encouraged APs to look to the Academic Senate, CAPS and PACAPS as their governance and advocancy groups.

3. Discussion from the floor:

  • Concerns regarding the policies to be followed in making salary adjustment determinations; apparently disregarding ACD 510-01.
  • What can be done to monitor/stop unfair practices regarding AP salary adjustments.
  • Sabbatical leaves for APs with positions that are unique so that their unit can not or will not approve leave.
  • Relative importance, mission of CAPS and PACAPS; their role as AP advocates

4. PACAPS will meet November 20.
