How to evaluate sources

General Guidelines

What is it?

Knowing more about the information included in a source can help you recognize its value to your research. Carefully read the material and consider these points.

  • Is the information relevant to your research?
  • Does the author specify where they got their information?
    • Do they cite their sources?
    • If they use statistics, do they explain where they came from?
  • Does it cover the topic comprehensively or only a certain aspect of it?
  • Is it opinion or based upon research?

The following pages will give you methods for evaluating each of these types of sources:

  • Books
  • Articles in Journals, Magazines, and Trade Publications
  • Articles in Newspapers
  • Web Pages
  • Broadcast Media
  • People
