Off Campus Access to Full Text via PubMed and Google Scholar!


Love Pubmed and Google Scholar, but are frustrated with not being able to download full text of ejournal articles through them from home? Good news - the library has created links through our proxy authentication system. If you bookmark these links you should always be able to obtain the full text of the journal articles we subscribe to at ASU:

   Google Scholar: https://login.ezproxy1.lib.asu.edu/login?url=http://scholar.google.com/

Online Requests For Some Storage Journals


It is now possible to place an online request for some journals located in storage. When you need a volume of a journal that is in storage look for individual volumes to be listed on the catalog screen with the location of "STORAGE STACKS" and a status of "SHELF" (see below).


Click on the button at the top of the catalog screen. Enter your name, university ID and library PIN when prompted and select Hayden Library or Science/Noble Library as pickup location. (Note: If Science/Noble Library selected it may delay the request 1-3 days because the volume will be returned to Hayden first and from there it will be shipped to Noble). Scroll down the next screen and select the volume you need, and then click on "Request Selected Item".



Storage items are retrieved several times a week. You will be notified by email when your item has arrived.


If you need a journal from storage that is not requestable online, please call us at 965-7607 or 965-2600 and a library staff member will submit the request for you.


Any questions or problems with storage items? Ask A Librarian


Library adds new medical resource: Cochrane Library


The Cochrane Library is a collection of evidence-based health care databases, including The Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews - evidence based systematic reviews prepared by the Cochrane Collaboration. The idea behind systematic reviews (also referred to as Cochrane reviews) is to provide practitioners with conclusive evidence of the effects of health care interventions. In most cases, research included in Cochrane reviews comes from randomized controlled trials. Data from studies included in the reviews are often analyzed using statistical methods (meta-analysis).


The Library contains 8 different databases but the three resources likely to be the most useful are:

  1. The Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews (CDSR) A list of regularly updated summaries of the best available evidence prepared by members of the Cochrane Collaboration. There are currently 1500 full-text articles reviewing the effects of specific health care interventions.
  2. The Database of Abstracts of Reviews of Effectiveness (DARE) contains other published reviews prepared by people outside of the Cochrane Collaboration. DARE includes structured abstracts of nearly 3000 systematic reviews from around the world.
  3. The Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (CENTRAL) is where to look if you want to track down the original studies - the largest single source of controlled trials. The register provides bibliographic information (i.e. abstracts rather than full-text) on nearly 350,000 reports of trials identified by contributors to the Cochrane Collaboration.

How to use the Cochrane Library

Enter the database by clicking on ENTER - ignore the username/password boxes. Once you've logged on to the Cochrane Library you will note that the screen is divided into two panes. A search window displays at the top left where you enter your search term(s). On the opening screen all the different databases are listed, together with the total number of records in each database.


Searches in the Cochrane Library are performed across all the databases, so for example, searching on asthma will retrieve records from the Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews (CDSR), the Database of Abstracts of Reviews of Effectiveness (DARE), the Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (CENTRAL) plus any other database which contains records with the word asthma. The three resources listed above are arranged in a hierarchy of evidence. Once you've done a search, look first to see whether there is a Cochrane review on your topic in CDSR, if there isn't, check DARE for any other systematic reviews. If you still can't find a review relevant to your area, then CENTRAL will tell you about any trials which have been conducted.


The total number of times a word or phrase occurs in the Library will appear in red. The number of records or 'hits' retrieved in each database by that word or phrase is displayed alongside. To display the records, start by clicking the relevant database. The displayed record will appear in the pane on the right. The Cochrane Library supports phrase searching and searches may be refined by limiting to words found in the title or abstract or by combining terms with AND, OR, or NOT.


For more assistance, see the Cochrane Library Tutorial: Ask A Librarian

 

Library adds full text computer science books through Safari


Safari Tech Books Online is an online library of over 500 full text searchable books covering many topics of computer science and information technology. The topics include programming languages, mark up languages, software engineering, security, and databases.


How do you search "Safari?"
1. Search the full text of all of the books simultaneously with one keyword search.
2. Browse by category.
3. Search all books simultaneously by code fragment
4. Search by author, title, publisher, or ISBN
5. The "Advanced Search" allows more specific searching such as key word searching in a specific category



The default sorting of the results is by relevance, but you can also sort your results chronologically by publication date or alphabetically by title.



Have any questions or need help with Safari? Ask A Librarian
