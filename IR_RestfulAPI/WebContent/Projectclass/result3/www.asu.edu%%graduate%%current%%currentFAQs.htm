FAQs for the Middle Stage of Your Graduate Career
Does my entire committee need to sign my Program of Study?
Yes.

What if I want to have a non-ASU affiliated member on my committee?
Your department must request special approval by submitting a memo and a copy of that person's vita to SAS. Contact your department's graduate support staff or SAS for more information.

I submitted an application for a Master's in Passing, but my degree was never awarded. What happened?
If your degree was not awarded, you probably didn't apply for graduation. The Master's in Passing is not automatic; you must still apply for graduation to receive this degree.

How do I know if my Program of Study has been approved?
Once SAS has approved your Program of Study, a copy signed by the Division of Graduate Studies dean will be sent to you as well as your department. Be responsible for your Program of Study. Always keep a copy of it-before you submit it and when the approved copy is returned to you.

What are all these notes on my Program of Study?
The Graduation Office of the University Registrar and SAS make notes on your Program of Study while they are processing and approving it. If there are any notes that you should be aware of, they will be below the dean's signature.

I'm a doctoral student and I need to take some time off school for various personal or professional reasons. What should I do?
You must submit a petition for a leave of absence to SAS before the semester of your leave. Consult the Leave of Absence policy in the Graduate Catalog under the "Continuous Enrollment" section for more information, or call SAS.

What forms might I need during the middle stage of my graduate studies?
  • Application for Graduate Foreign Language Examination to schedule the foreign language examination, which is administered through the Department of Languages and Literatures.
  • Petition to the Division of Graduate Studies, used to petition the Division of Graduate Studies for an exception to any Division of Graduate Studies policy, such as a leave of absence, request for extension, retake an exam, etc.
  • Program of Study forms — please see the POS preparation page



FAQs for the Completion Stage of Your Graduate Career
All I'm doing this semester is defending my prospectus. Do I still need to register?
Yes. You must register for at least one credit hour of appropriate coursework for each semester that you are using university resources. This includes defending your prospectus, taking comprehensive examinations, oral defense of a thesis/dissertation, etc. Registration is not required for taking the Foreign Language Exam.

I'm defending my thesis/dissertation during the summer. Do I need to register?Yes, you must register for at least one credit hour of appropriate coursework. You can register for any summer session; it does not have to be the same session in which you are defending.

Do I have to be finished with format review before I can schedule my defense?
No. In fact, we recommend that you submit your document for format review at the same time that you schedule your defense. However, you will need to have final format approval and the DGS dean's signature before you proceed to the bookstore.

How do I submit my document for format review if I am out of town?
We do not accept emailed or faxed documents, so we recommend that you have a proxy submit your document and schedule your defense. To appoint a proxy, you will need to fill out the Registration by Proxy and Consent for Full Access to Educational Records form and submit it to the Registrar's Office by mail, fax or in person. The form is available for download at the Registrar's Office website at www.asu.edu/registrar/forms/regforms.html.

Please note: Processing a proxy request can take between 2 to 7 business days, so please allow yourself adequate time.

Can I meet with a format advisor?
Format advising is available by phone, by email, or in person. Please call the Division of Graduate Studies at 965-3521 to schedule an appointment or speak with a SAS team member. We also offer walk-in advising. If you have general questions regarding the format process, we will be happy to help you. If you have specific questions, please have an example of what you need help with (either from your completed format review or from the document you plan to submit for format review) so that we may better assist you. We most likely cannot conduct "while-you-wait" reviews of entire theses/dissertations because a thorough review needs more time and attention; however, we can answer any specific questions you may have.

What's the last time of day that I can have my defense?
4:30 p.m. You cannot schedule a defense to start after this time, as defenses are public events and must be held during university business hours. A defense that starts later than 4:30 p.m. will not be completed before the university officially "closes".

What should I do if one of my committee members cannot attend my defense?
One committee member may be absent from your defense, but it cannot be your chair, AND a substitute must attend in that member's place. For more details, please contact SAS.

One of my committee members will be out of town during my defense; can he/she still participate via teleconference?
No, teleconferencing is not permitted for oral defenses unless there are extenuating circumstances. If you have questions about this policy, please contact SAS for further information on how you may petition the DGS for an exception.

I didn't go the bookstore. Will I still receive my degree?
No. For graduate students doing a thesis, dissertation, research paper, bound document, or practicum, it is a degree requirement that you pay the fees and submit at least 2 copies of your document for binding at the bookstore.

I've heard the August commencement ceremony was cancelled. Can I still graduate?
Although the August ceremony has been cancelled, you can still graduate in August. If you want to participate in the ceremony, you can attend the December commencement.

When will I receive my degree if I finish during the interim period?
If you complete your degree requirements during the summer interim period, you will receive your degree in December; if you finish in the winter interim period, you will receive your degree in May; if you finish during the spring interim period, you will receive your degree in August.

I am stressed out about graduation. Help, I don't know what to do next!
Call SAS! We are your resource, and we can help you navigate the process to complete your degree and graduate.

Questions? If you have questions about the information on this page, please contact Student Academic Services (SAS) at (480) 965-3521.
