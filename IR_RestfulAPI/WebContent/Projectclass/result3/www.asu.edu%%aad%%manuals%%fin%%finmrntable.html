Effective: 11/1/2006

FIN Manual Revision Notice Table

FIN 129: Ensuring Financial Integrity. New policy to promote the overall accuracy and ensure the integrity of university financial information

FIN 205–01: Audit Liaison. Revised to include the vice president for administration as an additional party responsible for monitoring all audits and to clarify that ASU Internal Audit and Management Services is also the liaison with the Board of Regents’ Audit Committee

FIN 301–01: Deposits—General Policy. Revised to state that departments interested in accepting credit cards as a form of payment must contact the Treasury Services area of Financial Services for information on the university-approved processes

FIN 401–03: Prohibited Transactions. Revised to state that ASU administrators and faculty required to participate in ceremonies may not rent or purchase regalia with state or local funds and to clarify that sponsored project grant and contract funds are included in state or local agency/orgs as pertains to the purchase or rental of graduation caps and gowns, and that the use of state or local agency/orgs for the purchase or rental of graduation caps and gowns may be allowed for invited guests who are not employed by the university

FIN 403: Petty Cash Funds: Revised to reflect procedural changes in the establishing, increasing and close-out of petty cash funds

FIN 420–01: Faculty and Staff Reimbursements. Revised to state that original receipts are necessary for faculty/staff reimbursements

FIN 420–03: Moving Expenses. Revised to change the mileage limit from 15 cents to 18 cents per mile under “Nondeductible Expense Reimbursement to the Employee”

FIN 421–01: Guest Lecturers, Consultants, and Other Independent Contractors. Revised to add a source and a header for “Employee Treatment”; to reflect an increase in the maximum dollar amount for which a PV can be issued from $5,000 to $10,000; to clarify instances when an individual should be paid through the ASU payroll system; and to state that Human Resources and the tax unit of Financial Services are available to assist departments in deciding whether a payee qualifies as an independent contractor

FIN 422–01: Student Financial Support. Revised to update “Student Enrollment Services Policies and Procedures Manual—SES 300” to University Student Initiatives Policies and Procedures Manual—USI 300”

FIN 505–01: Out-of-State Travel Authorization. Revised to update the language, to state that individual estimated travel expenses of $5,000 or more require approval by a vice president or a business manager reporting to a vice president in addition to ATO approval, and to clarify the verbiage regarding the retention of documentation for justifying the attendance by more than two persons from a single department or academic or administrative unit to an out-of-state conference or meeting

FIN 505–02: Out-of-State Travel Reimbursement. Revised to state that the approval of a vice president or business manager reporting to a vice president is required in addition to ATO approval for travel claim reimbursements of $5,000 or more if the estimated expenses were authorized for an amount less than $5,000

FIN 506: Foreign Travel. Revised to include a “Passport/Visa” section stating that if a passport or visa is required for authorized university business and the traveler does not already have a passport or visa then the expense of the passport can be reimbursed and the expense of a visa and associated fees are authorized reimbursable university business travel expenses

FIN 514: Registration Fees and Other Travel Expenses. Revised to clarify that personal calls to the traveler’s personal residences are considered part of the per diem maximum and are not separately reimbursable
