January 27, 2005

College welcomes social worker’s expertise

Elizabeth Segal, professor and former director of ASU’s School of Social Work, has been named associate dean of the College of Public Programs. She replaces Frederick Corey, who has served in the position since January 2000.

Corey is leaving the college to assume a new role as associate dean of University College and interim director of the School of Interdisciplinary Studies.

Segal, who earned her doctorate from the University of Illinois-Chicago, is co-editor of the Journal of Poverty: Innovations on Social, Political & Economic Inequalities. Her research focuses on poverty, social inequality, child welfare, social welfare policy, social justice, and the impact of policies and programs on disenfranchised populations.

Segal is the co-author of numerous books, including “Social Work: An Introduction to the Profession,” Brooks/Cole (2004); “Rediscovering the Other America: The Continuing Crisis of Poverty and Inequality in the United States,” Haworth Press (2003); and “Latino Poverty in the New Century: Inequalities, Challenges and Barriers,” Haworth Press (2000).

“Dr. Segal is not only a gifted academic, but a superb administrator,” says Jeff Chapman, the interim dean. “Her experience and professional skills will serve the college well as a new dean is identified and our move to downtown Phoenix materializes.”

In her role as associate dean, Segal will focus on student grievances, curriculum actions, personnel policies, committee assignments, faculty retirements, special academic and student affairs projects, and other initiatives.

Palka, with the College of Public Programs, can be reached at (480) 727-6238 or (debra.palka@asu.edu).
