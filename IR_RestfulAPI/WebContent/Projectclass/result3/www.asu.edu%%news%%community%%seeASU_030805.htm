March 8, 2005

SEE ASU offers schoolchildren a glimpse at how research works

For those who have ever wondered if storm chasers get scared when they chase storms, or what it would be like to explore Mars, the annual SEE ASU event just might provide the answers to those questions.

The university’s annual science, engineering, technology and environment fair will be open to school groups March 11 and open to the general public from 9 a.m. – 2 p.m., March 12, at Wells Fargo Arena on the Tempe campus.

SEE ASU is free and open to the public and offers a glimpse into the research and teaching being done in Arizona State University’s many classrooms and labs.

On March 11, more than 2,000 K-12 students are expected from throughout the state, including groups from Winslow, Wickenburg, Payson and Coolidge.

“It’s a great opportunity for the community to get a firsthand look at some of the different kinds of activities that go on every day at ASU,” says Wilma Mathews, director of ASU constituent relations. “It really puts the research into a context that children of all ages can relate to.”

Adds Mary Jane Bullock of Eagle Ridge Enrichment Program in Mesa: “I think it gave the students a bird’s-eye view of the many disciplines that are available for pursuit.

“I was very pleased with all the hands-on activities that were available to my students. I liked the wide variety of fields that were represented.”

The exhibits and activities will range from geology, archaeology and anthropology to heat transfer, cryogenics and vacuum air pressure.

And those attending will be able to make their own gold dimes – and take them home as souvenirs.

The exhibits will cover a wide-ranging list of topics:

• At “Geography – Making Earth’s Surface,” participants can watch a river change the landscape from the vantage point of a high-speed camera.

• “Bioengineering is the Heart of Engineering” will bring bioengineers at ASU to focus on medical devices and research. The human heart will play the starring role in this exhibit.

• “Mars Space Flight Facility” will let participants learn how they can participate in the exploration of the Red Planet.

• At “Tools Used in Police Work,” see and learn about police helmets, riot shields, police bicycles and protection vests.

• “Get A Taste for Nutrition,” presented by the Department of Nutrition, will have ideas and activities encouraging healthy eating and physical activity.

For more information, call (480) 965-0456 or visit (www.asu.edu/seeasu).

Smith, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 965-4821 or (jps@asu.edu).
