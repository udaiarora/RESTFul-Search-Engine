Human Resource Data Warehouse Training

HRMS (Human Resource Management System) data is now available in the Human Resource Data Warehouse (HRDW). To effectively use this data, attendance at this training is highly recommended.

What is the HRDW?

The Human Resource Data Warehouse is information that has been stored in a database designed for reporting and analysis. Most of the data is extracted from HRMS and will provide a historical perspective of the type data that you are researching.

Information in this warehouse includes:

  • Demographic information about staff and faculty.
  • Employee payroll information on a pay period basis.
  • Budget position control data.

Why would you be interested in attending a training class?

In the training class you will learn how to:

  • Identify types of data available in the HR warehouse and where it resides.
  • Understand the unique attributes of each table.
  • Design reports in a format that is best utilized in your department.
  • Know when the data will be updated.
  • Print labels, for a particular department, employees and faculty members.
  • Know how to identify table and field definitions in the warehouse.
  • Access the warehouse and run queries.

What are the prerequisites for attending the class?

  • Attendance in an HRMS Inquiry Class - call (480) 965-4751 to enroll or visit the training website.
  • Knowledge of a relational database query tool. (BrioQuery, MSAccess, etc.)
  • An HRDW ID to logon to the database.

How to get access to the warehouse?

If you need an access form, send an email to: WARE-Q@asu.edu or go to this website to get the form.
