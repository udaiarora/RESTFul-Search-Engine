Developing a Historical GIS Course for the History Curriculum

Introduction | Project Staff | Contact Information

Quality of Instruction Grant, Arizona State University,
Departments of History and Geography
July 1, 2005-June 30, 2006

The goal of this quality of instruction grant is to develop an introductory graduate course and set of workshops to teach Department graduate students and faculty on how to incorporate Geographic Information Systems (GIS)  into the undergraduate history curriculum.   The course and workshop series will introduce participants to innovative projects using historical GIS, discuss the pedagogical goals of incorporating historical geography and GIS into the undergraduate curriculum, train them to use ArcGIS to create their own simple maps, and familiarize them with the use of ArcExplorer to allow students to engage with historical GIS maps.   They will also learn about the increasing number of on-line resources that provide digital data, boundary files, and teaching exercises to instructors wishing to create their own maps or use GIS maps in teaching.   Participants will be expected to create at least one simple map and several teaching exercises as part of their coursework.  It is hoped that this initiative will inspire some of the participants to advance further in their skill-building by serving as a bridge to the Geography Department's more rigorous cartography and GIS courses, and strengthen the relationship between members of the History and Geography Departments.
