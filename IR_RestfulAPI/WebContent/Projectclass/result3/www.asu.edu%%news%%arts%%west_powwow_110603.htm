November 6, 2003

Traditional powwow celebrates Native American veterans

The central lawn of the ASU West campus will come alive with Native American dancers and drum groups from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m., Nov. 8, as a traditional powwow celebrates the spirit of life. Because of the event's proximity to Veterans Day, the powwow will also honor the contributions of Native American military veterans.

The event, featuring traditional Native American arts and crafts vendors, food vendors, and gourd dancing, Aztec dancers, Yaqui dancers, and Zuni eagle dancers, is free and open to the public (visitor parking on campus costs $2). Attendees are encouraged to bring their own lawn chairs.

Two grand entries are planned, at noon and 6 p.m. Participants include announcer Paul Krech of Mesa, arena director Herman Schildt of Phoenix, head gourd dancer Keith Tagaban of Albuquerque, head man dancer Robin Forenkem of Laveen, and head woman dancer Elizabeth Young of Phoenix. Drum groups representing both the North and South regions include Saste Takoja of Tucson and Southern Nation of Tempe. The American Indian Veterans Memorial Organization will provide a Color Guard.

"Our powwow is traditional. The dancers are not competing for prizes," says ASU West professor Richard Morris, a member of the campus's Native American Events Council. "It is a social gathering, a celebration of life and Native American culture, and an opportunity to thank Native Americans who have supported their country through military service."

The powwow is hosted by ASU West Public Events, the Native American Events Council, the Native American Student Organization, and Native American Student Services Programs.

Call (602) 543-8130 or visit (www.west.asu.edu/naso) for information.]
