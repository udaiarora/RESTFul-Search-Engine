MÉXICO INDUSTRY ANALYSIS

Doing an industry analysis for a small and medium sized businesses takes time and patience because the niche markets and industry are small sections of huge industry sectors.  Below are some of the types of information that are needed to learn about the industry. Links to items that are not free or not owned by ASU Libraries link to the publisher's web site for the item.

  1. Industrial Classification Code -- Look up your company in the company directories suggested in Researching Businesses in Mexico.  Most directories will give you the industrial classifications used by your company.  Since the U.S., Mexico & Canada are in the process of changing industrial classification systems, you'll need to use SCIAN: Sistema de Clasificación Industrial de América del Norte (English Version), to identify the industries  that include the company.   Many of the industrial analysis reports available are indexed by industrial classification, but when no such report is available, you need to use the periodical indexes to find articles on the industry and knowing what the industry name is helps.
     
  2. Industry Description -- This is a combination of the descriptions found with the industrial classification codes above and from sections listed below. Most small and medium sized businesses are in niches between or among industries.  For example: A company that manufactures artificial limbs -- prótesis artificial -- (most such devices are considered durable goods) is part of the
      -- Health Care Industry, because it improves health of the end user;
      -- Manufacturing Industries (various subsectors), becasue it is made from various materials; and
      -- Insurance Industries (subgroup - Health & Life), because the type of prosthesis used will often depend on what is covered by the insurance. 
    The Industry description and analysis will reflect that symbiosis.  There is rarely a free industry analysis on such narrow markets which requires your best guess at what is going on in the industry.
     
  3. NATIONAL Industry Reports
    1. There are many firms that create and sell custom industrial reports for Mexico. There does not seem to be any equivalent to the popular Standard & Poor's Industry Surveys done for the United States. Until such items are more widely available, the best way to develop your own analysis is to do competitive intelligence style research. Contact your local business librarian for assistance with this detailed type of research.
    2. STAT-USA (in the GLOBUS & NTDB section under Industry Sector Analysis Reports)
    3. Encuesta Nacional de Micronegocios, 1998. (Microbusinesses National Survey, 1998). (about)
    4. INEGI Subprogram of Traditional Surveys in Establishments
      • Encuesta Industrial Mensual (about)
      • Annual Industrial (about)
      • National of the Construction Industry (about)
      • Annual of the Construction Industry (about)
      • Monthly of Services (about)
      • Monthly of Commercial Establishments (about)
      • Annual of Commerce (about)
      • Quarterly of Entrpreneurial Economic Activity (about)
    5. Economic Census Data (INEGI)
      • How People Use Economic Census Data (US Census Bureau)
      • Mexico Economic Census 1999 (frequency chart)
      • Mexico Agricultural-Livestock Census (about)
    6. Industry & Corporate Social Responsibility
      -- This page has links to web sites and sources that have company and industry social responsibility and environmental policies. This is increasingly more important in the costs of operating many businesses.
       
  4. GNP: Gross National Product (Producto Nacional Bruto) -- is seldom used anymore, USE  GDP: Gross Domestic Product (PIB: Producto Interno Bruto)
    1. GDP by industry sector
      • Real Gross Domestic Product by Division

  5. Economic Indicators
    1. GDP and GNP are only two of over 50 Economic Indicators. These and other government calculated figures reflect the general health of the economy.  Until you are more knowledgeable about how each economic indicator affects your industry & its subsectors, the news articles by economists will tell you what they think it means and are a good general guide.  These are found in most newspapers and the Wall Street Journal on the day the indicator is released by the government.  Be careful! Some industries are counter-cyclic to the indicators (when the economy is bad some industries do well).
      • INEGI
        • Servicio de Información Económica en Línea (InfoINEGI)
        • Short Term Economic Indicators
          • Calendar of Short Term Economic Information
          • Industry
          • Manufacturing Competitiveness
        • Long Term Economic Indicators
          • Industry
      • México: Indicadores Económicos
      • BIE: Banco de Información Económica
      • Secretaría de Hacienda y Crédito Público (Ministry of Finance & Public Credit)
        • Basic Economic & Financial Data Release Dates

  6. TRADE ASSOCIATION Industry Information
    1. To find trade associations use the key words from your industry. (Many industries have several trade associations and subsector associations. You need to find the best match for your company. It is entirely possible to find associations in  multiple industries that are appropriate.)
      • Associations Unlimited -- includes web site, if available
      • Directorio de Asociaciones (IBCON)
      • Chambers of Commerce -- Since Mexico requires membership in a Camára (chamber) or CANACO, check the web sites of these chambers for industry information.
        • American Chamber of Commerce in México
        • U.S.-México Chamber of Commerce (Cámara de Comercio México-Estados Unidos)
        • SIEM Directorio de Cámaras Empresariales (Directory of Enterprise Chambers)
    2. Indirectly find an association by searching for industry statistics. Look for articles with industry statistics and look for the source of the statistics.
      • Check the business article indexes listed here. Be sure you search by industry name or industry code and use the geographic descriptor for "Mexico". Don't forget to follow the instructions to "AND NOT" the word "NEW" out so you don't get all the articles for New Mexico, the state in the U.S.
      • Use Predicasts F & S Index International (Hayden Reference Index Table 10A)

  7. Mexico Industry Information
    1. How many companies are in the industry in Mexico (industry size)?
      Search by industry category in the indsutry directories:
      • MexSearch Yellow Pages / El Directorio Amarillo de México
      • Mexico.amarillas.com
      • Secciónamarilla (Yellow Pages)
      • Directorio Industridata Empresas por Región (Mercametrica)
      • Directorio Industridata Empresas por Tomaño (Mercametrica)
      • More industry directories are listed in Researching Businesses in México
    2. Company ranking
      If there are no lists providing company ranking within an industry, there are several ways to get an idea of where the company is ranked within an industry.
      • Datamine Directories (suggested directories) (example: Datamining Reference USA)
        • Search the directory by industry, then sort by sales or number of employees
        • Narrow the industry ranking by geographic area using postal codes, municipios, or states as the geographic designation then sort by sales or number of employees
      • Search the business article indexes for articles on the company's market share, business rankings or industry rankings. Remember to limit the industry to Mexico. Don't forget to follow the instructions to "AND NOT" the word "NEW" out so you don't get all the articles for New Mexico, the state in the U.S.
      • Use Predicasts F & S Index International (Hayden Reference Index Table 10A)
      • Search major news and magazine databases from Mexico.
        • Mexico News Sources
        • Infosel Mexico CD-ROMs El Norte and Reforma newspapers full text plus full text from numerous other Mexican periodicals
        • ISI-InfoLatina
    3. GSP: Gross State Product (PIB Estatal: Producto Interno Bruto Estatal) is usually available on the state web sites. Finding it by industry sector is less common.
    4. Mexico Trade Associations
        Mexico has many state and local chapters of national trade associations. Did you check the national association for local chapter information and contacts? Click here to see the sources to use to find this information.
    5. Industry information from Sindicatos (labor unions)
        Unions are often good sources of what is going on in an industry. They often have industry statistics as well.
        • List of Sindicatos web sites
    6. Mexico Industry News
        While all the business article indexes are good and worth searching using the word Mexico and the industry. Don't forget to follow the instructions to "AND NOT" the word "NEW" out so you don't get all the articles for New Mexico, the state in the U.S. More detailed news is usually published or broadcast locally. For those, you have to rely on the local news web sites since most of them are not indexed in the periodical indexes. Recommended news sources are
        • Mexico News Sources
        • Infosel Mexico CD-ROMs El Norte and Reforma newspapers full text plus full text from numerous other Mexican periodicals
        • ISI-InfoLatina
        • Use Predicasts F & S Index International (Hayden Reference Index Table 10A)
