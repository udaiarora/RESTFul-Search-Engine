F-1 Curricular Practical Training (CPT)

Curricular Practical Training (CPT) is a type of off-campus employment authorization for F-1 students who must complete an internship course in order to graduate from their current degree program.

To view a specific topic, click on the appropriate link: 

  • Eligibility for CPT
  • Requirements
  • Characteristics of CPT
  • CPT Application Procedure

Eligibility for CPT

Maintenance of F-1 status for a minimum of one academic year in current degree program.

Requirements

  • Proposed employment must be an integral part of an established curriculum AND be either a) required of all candidates for a particular degree program or b) required of a particular student's degree program as a planned option in their degree plan (the intent to include internship is established between student and advisor early on and not added to accommodate employment opportunity).
  • Internship must appear on the Program of Study or the Degree Audit Reporting System as a requirement to complete the degree program and should NOT be added on in addition to coursework/academic requirements.
  • Student must receive academic credit for the work experience.
  • Internship must be listed as a course for your program/degree plan option in the appropriate University Catalog. Independent Study Research, Practicum and similar courses do not qualify as a class for CPT and cannot be substituted.
  • Employment starts at the beginning of the semester or summer session and ends at end of the semester or summer session. A period longer than a regular term will only be considered based on justification of the internship class/curriculum requirement and NOT based on the terms of your employment offer.

Characteristics of CPT

  • Department must certify that proposed work meets the eligibility criteria and requirements of the CPT work permit.
  • Employer must certify that this work will satisfy a degree requirement.
  • Student may engage in CPT only for the specific employer, location and period approved and recorded in SEVIS.
  • Student must be enrolled full-time while on CPT unless the internship occurs during the summer, the last semester of their degree program and/or other ISO/ISS approved exception to the full-course-of-study requirement for immigration purposes.
  • CPT may be approved for either part-time or full-time depending on the number of hours student must work to complete the requirement for the Internship class.  Students who must enroll in full-time CPT for one year or more will become ineligible for OPT.
  • Internship class cannot be added to the POS after the degree requirements have already been met.
  • POS cannot be modified because of a work opportunity (OPT is the appropriate work permit for this).
  • Plan ahead. Internship cannot be extended. Any additional work after CPT authorization ends would require application for OPT.

If you do not meet the eligibility and requirements for CPT, please review the OPT information attached (page1-4) or at http://www.asu.edu/studentaffairs/ed/iso/opt.htm  OPT processing takes 3 months so plan accordingly.

If you have further questions regarding CPT, please email ISOeadvisor@asu.edu or call 480-965-7451 if you are Tempe, Polytechnic and Downtown Phoenix campus student and email international.west@asu.edu or call 602-543-8201 if you are a West campus student to make an appointment with an advisor.

If you are certain that you are eligible to apply for CPT, please follow the application procedure below.

CPT Application Procedure

1- Submit the following documents to the ISO/ISS. Must be placed in a sealed envelope marked with CPT APPLICATION, your name and ASU ID.  Incomplete application will not be processed!

  1. Adviser’s Letter (Use the form or department form if applicable)
  2. Employer’s letter (Use the form or department form if applicable)
  3. Unofficial transcripts showing Internship class enrollment (from Records Office on Tempe campus, Registration Services on West campus, or Registration Services on Polytechnic campus. Interactive page is not acceptable)
  4. Complete OPT/CPT Student Information Form (Click here to download form).
  5. Copy of Program of Study (POS)/Degree Audit Reporting System (DARS) indicating the internship requirement
    ** IPOS printout must indicate that it is either approved by department, pending DGS approval or DGS approved.
    ** DARS page showing internship requirement must bear initial of the academic adviser.
  6. Photocopy of Passport valid 6 months into the future
  7. Photocopy of recent I-94 (both sides)
  8. Print-out of updated address information from ASU Interactive website showing current local and permanent  address
  9. Social Security letter application form (if you are not currently holding a Social Security Number). A Social Security Number is necessary to report your wages to the government. Additional instruction on how to obtain a SSN is included in the social security letter.

Notes:
a. Complete documents will be processed and approved/denied within 5 to 7 working days.
b. Review eligibility criteria carefully to avoid delays.  If you need immigration advisement or have additional requirements, you will receive an email or call from ISO/ISS.  This may result in processing delays.

2- You will receive an email notification from ISO/ISS to pick up the processed CPT application. If you are unable to pick up the document yourself, you may give an authorization letter with your signature to the ISO/ISS naming a person to act as your proxy in picking up the document. Proxy must present photo identification to confirm their identity.
