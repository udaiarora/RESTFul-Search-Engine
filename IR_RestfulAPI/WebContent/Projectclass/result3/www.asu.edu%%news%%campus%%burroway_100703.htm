October 7, 2003

ASU center celebrates a return of the native -- writer

Though Arizona State University's Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing has been making its mark on Valley cultural life by bringing in a new influx of important writers, it doesn't mean that the visitors are all newcomers to Arizona. Take novelist Janet Burroway, for example, the next speaker in the Center's Distinguished Visiting Writers Series in a reading scheduled for October 16.

Not only a nominee for the Pulitzer Prize for her novel The Buzzards (1970) and a runner up for the National Book Award for Raw Silk (1977), Burroway has the distinction of being a native Arizonan.

Born in Tucson, Burroway is the author of seven novels. Her novel Cutting Stone (1992) even uses the old Southwest as backdrop - the novel, set in Arizona and northern Mexico, takes place during the Mexican revolution.

In addition to novels, Burroway has written plays, poetry, children's books and a very successful collection of autobiographical essays, Embalming Mom: Essays in Life (2002). Burroway is also a renowned teacher of writing. Her textbook Writing Fiction is used in more than 300 colleges and universities in the United States, and her new text, Imaginative Writing: The Elements of Craft, was published last year. Burroway now lives in Tallahassee, FL and is the Robert O. Lawton Distinguished Professor Emerita at Florida State University.

Of the essays in Burroway's Embalming Mom, Lynn McWhirter of The Review of Arts, Literature, Philosophy and the Humanities writes, "After three or four chapters, Burroway becomes someone you and I would want to have over to chew the fat."

Burroway will be back in her home state and available to chew the fat, read her work and otherwise discuss writing on Thursday, October 16. The lecture begins at 7:00 pm and takes place in Neeb Hall on ASU Main Campus. Admission is free. For ticket information and other questions, please e-mail clasevents@asu.edu or call 480-965-0051.

Future visiting writers in the inaugural 2003-4 series will include C. D. Wright on Thursday, Feb. 12, and novelist Alexs Pate on Friday, April 9. Locations and ticket prices will vary.
