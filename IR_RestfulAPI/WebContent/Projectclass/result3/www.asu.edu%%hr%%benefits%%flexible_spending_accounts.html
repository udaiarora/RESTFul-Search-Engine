Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)
Using a flexible spending account, you can pay for medical, dental and/or dependent (day) care expenses with pre-tax money withheld from your paycheck.

The plan year is January 1 to December 31 and employees

must enroll every year to participate.

ASU employees can enroll in one or both of these accounts:
   Healthcare Spending Account
   Dependent Care Spending Account

HEALTHCARE SPENDING
This account offers reimbursement for certain healthcare, prescription drug, over-the-counter drug, vision and dental expenses not reimbursed by your insurance. You must indicate the amount you want to contribute for the plan year when you enroll. The maximum yearly contribution is $5000. You will be eligible to receive reimbursement for expenses incurred after coverage begins.

Reimbursable expenses include:

  • Medical/dental expenses not reimbursed by your health/dental plans such as co-payments, deductibles and orthodontic care;
  • Vision and hearing expenses;
  • Weight-loss or smoking-cessation programs prescribed by your doctor;
  • Prescription drugs and medical equipment
  • Non-prescription drugs (over-the-counter) drugs used to treat a medical condition LEARN MORE

Non-Reimbursable expenses include:

  • Cosmetic surgery,
  • Preventive and non-prescription drugs

DEPENDENT CARE SPENDING
 This account offers reimbursement for certain dependent care expenses incurred while you are at work. If you are married, both you and your spouse must work or your spouse must be a full-time student. Your child must be under 13 years of age and you must claim him/her as a dependent on your income tax return.

If your dependent is not a child, he/she must be physically or mentally unable to take care of him/herself. You must indicate the amount you want to contribute for the plan year when you enroll.  You may contribute up to $5000/year per family.  You will receive reimbursement for expenses incurred after your coverage begins.

Examples of reimbursable expenses include:

  • Adult day care;
  • Day care or summer day camp;
  • Day care provided in your home; and
  • Before or after school care.

NOTE: You cannot enroll, cancel or make changes to your contribution amount unless you have an allowable change in family or employment status such as a death, birth, divorce, job loss or the beginning/end of an unpaid leave of absence. Your change must be consistent with your change in family or employment status.

You also may:

•Change the dependent care deduction because of a change in your childcare provider to reflect the new cost.

•Change the election if a dependent starts school.

If you have a qualified change in family or employment status, contact the Employee Service Center within 31 days.
