Sending a Mass E-Mail to the ASU Community

There are guidelines for sending e-mail to a large number of ASU affiliated people using ASU systems.

Any e-mail sent using ASU computing resources must conform to the ASU Computer, Internet and Electronic Communications Policy located at : http://www.asu.edu/aad/manuals/acd/acd125.html.

Target Audience

  • The target audience should be a specific demographic group for which the topic of the message relates.

  • Requests by recipients to be removed from a list should be honored, except under certain circumstances such as:

    • A college or department sending notes to students within an area

    • Professors sending notes to students in their class

    • Departments heads (or authorized agents) sending notes to their staff

    • Mailings approved by the Provost's or Registrar's Office

  • Requests to be removed from an "ad hoc" list (e.g. generated from a Data Warehouse query) must be respected in any future "ad hoc" mailing.
  • Use of lists such as "All Employees", "All Faculty" and "All Staff" are governed by the Provost's Office. Requests to send to these lists should be forwarded to Linda Van Scoy. These lists are automatically generated nightly in the ASU Microsoft Exchange and can only be used from within Outlook/Exchange.
  • Use of an "All Student" list is governed by the Registrar's Office. Requests to send to all students should be forwarded to Bonnie Wilcox. Lists of students within specific majors and colleges are automatically generated nightly in Exchange; use of these lists is controlled by the Dean of the respective college.
  • Official ASU E-mail addresses are stored in the "Electronic Post Office" (EPO) database. E-mail addresses from the database are replicated nightly to the Data Warehouse and "Directory Services" database.
  • Those who obtain e-mail addresses from the Data Warehouse must still obtain authorization to send to large groups as specified above. Use of AMDF is recommended for sending to large lists of e-mail addresses.
  • It is strictly prohibited to give or sell ASU e-mail address lists to outside entities.

Message Content

  • The message should contain a valid "From:" address for e-mail replies.

  • Use an appropriate "Subject:" header.

  • Start the message body with why they are receiving the note, e.g. "You are receiving this note because you are an employee of ASU. This mailing has been approved by the Provost's Office."

  • If more than one mailing is intended to be sent to a particular group, you should state that and include simple instructions on how to not receive further mailings.

  • The name and phone number of a person to contact must be included within the body of the message.

Other Suggestions

  • Send to large lists (e.g. > 1000) during off-peak, non-business hours to avoid over-loading the ASU network and messaging systems.

  • Keep the message short and to the point.

  • Do not attach software or documentation to your message. Rather, provide instructions as to how it can be obtained (e.g. from file download or the Web).

  • Have someone proofread the message for spelling, grammatical and content errors.

  • Have officials in your department approve the message if, for example, you anticipate any complaints generated by the mailing.

  • Send a test version of your message to yourself and another person to see how it shows up. Check for proper word wrapping (if applicable) and for an appropriate "signature"

What to Expect

  • You will likely receive some "undeliverable" mail notifications if you are using EPO addresses. Approximately 20 per 1000 is normal. In certain cases, you may wish to contact these individuals to let them know their "EPO" account is not properly configured or that their mailbox is "over quota". These are the two most likely causes for returned mail.

  • In general, people don't like to receive unsolicited e-mail and they may let you know about it via heated replies. The more inappropriate the mailing, the more complaints you will receive.

  • When using demographics to build your distribution list, you will notice that not everyone in your query has an e-mail address. You may wish to consider building a separate query to capture these people and generate mailing labels to send paper mail, if that is appropriate.

For information on how to send e-mail to "ad hoc" lists, or if you would like to create a Listserv list or Exchange shared distribution list, submit a question online at the Ask IT Web page, or call the IT Help Desk at (480) 965-6500.
