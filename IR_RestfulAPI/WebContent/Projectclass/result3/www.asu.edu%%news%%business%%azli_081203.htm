August 12, 2003

Arizona Leading Index continues upward trend in June

The Bank One Arizona Index of Leading Economic Indicators rose in June to 120.3. This is 0.4 percent above May's index reading and 2.7 percent above the June 2002 number of 117.1. (1987 = 100).

"The hours worked in manufacturing component has been up four months in a row, suggesting that a recovery for manufacturing may be within sight," said Tracy Clark, associate director of the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at Arizona State University's W. P. Carey School of Business. "Manufacturing may not improve enough to spur a resumption in hiring before 2004, but increases in hours worked are a necessary perquisite."

There is some evidence of increased hiring of temporary workers, which is further good news, according to Clark. "High-tech manufacturing is likely to improve in advance of manufacturing in general, which would benefit the local economy. Semiconductor sales continue to improve, with the fourth straight month of higher sales in June."

Also noted in Clark's analysis of the June Leading Index:

· Nationwide and in Arizona, the vast majority of the improvement in the economy continues to be in services.
· The small rise in mortgage rates and the impact on home buying seems likely to slow single-family construction in Arizona for the remainder of this year, despite the strong showing in June.
· There is a possibility that the drop in refinance activity could negatively impact retail sales.
· Auto sales are showing signs of slowing, although a dramatic drop is unlikely.

"The trend of the Leading Index and the indications from most of the data suggest the economy will continue to slowly improve," Clark said. "However, it may be early 2004 before job growth picks up enough steam to bring about a widespread perception that the economy is improving."

The Bank One Arizona Index of Leading Economic Indicators is based on nine indicators, combined and weighted to project the pace of economic activity several months ahead. Five of the components come from a monthly survey of Arizona purchasing managers conducted by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University.
