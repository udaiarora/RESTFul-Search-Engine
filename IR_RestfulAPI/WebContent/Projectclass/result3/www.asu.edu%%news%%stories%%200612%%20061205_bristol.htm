December 5, 2006

West campus mourns loss of marketing professor Bristol

The faculty, staff and students of the School of Global Management and Leadership at the West campus are deeply saddened to report the passing of a valued member of our Marketing Department, Terry Bristol, who died in his sleep Nov. 7.

Bristol began his career in the School of Global Management and Leadership in 2000. His teaching experience at ASU West included graduate teaching in Marketing Strategy and undergraduate teaching in Global Marketing Strategy and Fundamentals of Marketing. In spring of 2006, he was granted tenure and promoted to associate professor.

Bristol received his doctorate from Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University in Marketing. Before joining ASU, he was an assistant professor at the University of Arkansas at Little Rock and Oklahoma State University . His recent research was in the areas of consumer socialization, social influence, consumer self-identity and identification, consumer responses to product and promotional stimuli, and consumer behavior in the global marketplace.

Bristol's awards included the Arkansas Advertising Federation's Distinguished Educator Award in 1999 and the 2005 Journal of Retailing Outstanding Reviewer Award. He was a member of two honor societies, Beta Gamma Sigma and Phi Kappa Phi.

Bristol was a dedicated teacher, dear colleague, and beloved friend. He is survived by his two children, Christine and Taylor, as well as his parents.

The SGML has created a memorial Web site for friends and colleagues to share stories about and photographs of Bristol , as well as learn how to contribute to a memorial in his honor. The address is (www.west.asu.edu/sgml/TerryBristol/index.html).
