September 6, 2005

Arizona Business Conditions Index inches upward in August

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index rose to 63.7 in August from to 62.6 the previous month, a 1.8 percent increase. An index reading over 50 indicates the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

"This is within the range that the index has maintained since September 2003," reported Dawn McLaren, a research economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU's W. P. Carey School of Business. "Earlier in the year, the index gravitated to the higher end of the range, but over the last two months, it has shifted to the lower end. Even so, this represents continued short-term growth at healthy levels in the local economy."

Among the components of the index signaling cause for concern are the purchased material inventory level and employment, according to McLaren. "Both of these components took significant downturns in August, with the purchased materials inventory level dropping by 7.1 points to 52.8, and employment down by 14.5 points to 53.7. Both of these are now hovering close to the neutral level."

The Price Index rose by 14.2 percent to reach 73.0 in August. "In March 2005, the Price Index recorded a level of 74.0, but then pressure eased slightly, allowing prices to fall to 63.9 in July. The current jump is indicative of continued upward pressure on prices in the near term. The commodities with higher prices have shifted from building materials such as concrete, metal and wood, to items that can be categorized as electronic and chemical."

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available on the Web at wpcarey.asu.edu/azbci. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data.
