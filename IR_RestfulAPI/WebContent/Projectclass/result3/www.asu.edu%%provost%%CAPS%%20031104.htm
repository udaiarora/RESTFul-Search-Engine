Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting (Spotlight Series)

Tuesday, November 4, 2003

Spotlight Panelists: Sue Hunter, Chair, CAPS; Patricia Feldman, Chair, Academic Professional Task Force; Karl Weiss, Chair, PACAPS

Topic: 2003-2004 review of Academic Professional Policies and Procedures

Present: Approximately 60 people

Sue Hunter, chair of CAPS, convened the meeting, and introduced herself and the two other panelists.  She explained that the meeting?s purpose was to explain the roles of the three committees represented in relation to the present review of AP policies and procedures.  A summary of each panelist?s presentation and discussion with the audience follows.

Patricia Feldman, Chair, Academic Professional Task Force

Ms. Feldman explained that policies and procedures for Academic Professionals at ASU have been intact for some years.  Last year a faculty task force completed its revision of promotion and tenure policies in ASU?s ACD (Academic Policies and Procedures) manual.  Prompted by the completion of the faculty task force?s work, and by President Crow?s questions about Academic Professionals, the Provost?s office decided to begin a review of AP policies and procedures.  The Task Force was organized early this Fall.

Ms. Feldman then reviewed the charge of the Task Force.  (The charge is posted on the web at http://www.asu.edu/provost/ap/.)

Q:  What is the time frame for the Task Force to do its work?

A:  The Provost has asked the Task Force to report back at the beginning of the Spring semester.  Due to the complexity of the Task Force?s job and the need for feedback from APs at large, the report in January may be a progress report rather than a final one.

 

Q:  Who comprises the Task Force?

A:  The Task Force is comprised of representatives from the three ASU campuses, and the 3 employment categories (on track, continuing, and year-to-year).  At its last meeting, the group formed 3 subgroups to focus on the first 3 charges from the Provost.  A complete list of members is available at http://www.asu.edu/provost/ap/.

 

Q:  Who is in each of the 3 subgroups?

A:  Their membership will be posted on the Task Force web site.

 

Q:  What is the rationale for having faculty on the Task Force?

A:  Some supervise APs.  They also bring expertise on the faculty promotion and tenure process, and broaden the group?s perspectives.

 

Summary of comments from audience:

 

In addition to the questions above, participants in the audience made a number of comments.  Some key topics:

  • Time frame for revising policies and procedures:  Concern was expressed, given the diversity of AP roles
  • Response to AP Task Force charge #1, clarifying the distinction between continuing status and tenure:  Several audience members agreed that the two employment terms have been defined as being synonymous, except that unlike tenure, continuing status may be revoked based on a fiscal emergency.  Discussion centered on why clarification to this existing definition is needed. 
  • Response to AP Task Force charge #2, criteria for appointment to probationary/continuing track versus year-to-year track:  A participant in the drafting of the original AP policies and procedures text noted that APs are hired on a year-to-year basis when either their positions are funded by grants of limited duration, or there is not anticipated to be an ongoing need for the position.
  • Concern that existing continuing appointments will be converted to other types of appointments, or that no new probationary/continuing track hires will occur:  Ms. Feldman and a Task Force member agreed that there has been no suggestion that either of these actions are being considered.
  • Concern that performance expectations for those presently in probationary tracks is unclear.

 

Karl Weiss, Chair, PACAPS

 

Mr. Weiss explained that PACAPS (the Executive Vice President and Provost?s Advisory Committee on Academic Professionals) serves at the request of the Provost, who selects the group?s committee members.  The intention of the committee is to have representation from every university college or other major university entity that includes APs. 

 

PACAPs is an ongoing committee, unlike the Task Force which is an ad-hoc group specifically directed towards the present policies and procedures review.  PACAPS? role in the present review is to solicit feedback from APs at large, and to transmit this back to the Task Force.   Mr. Weiss is a member to the Task Force, and relays activity of that group to PACAPS.

 

Since the review began, PACAPS has met several times.  They have assembled a list of questions that were forwarded to the Task Force.  PACAPS is presently developing a web-based survey that deals with what individual AP jobs are, in an effort to determine the variety and breakdown of AP roles on campus.  All APs will be notified when the survey is available.

 

Q:  How are individuals appointed to PACAPS?

A:  The Provost makes these appointments.  Members serve 3-year terms.

 

Q:  Do we know how many APs there are?

A:  There are about 500 APs, approximately 150 of whom are on the probationary/continuing track.

 

Summary of comments from audience:

  • Survey recently distributed to APs:  Neither the Task Force or PACAPS was involved in developing this survey.  Audience participants commented that results of the survey could be useful to PACAPS and the Task Force.  (Additional information gathered after meeting:  The survey was an initiative from ASU Human Resources.  Classified Staff, Administrators, Service Professionals, Academic Professionals and faculty will each receive, or have received, a similar letter and survey.)
  • Composition of PACAPS:  PACAPS does not presently include representation from East and West campuses.  This is an issue that will be considered in the present review process.  An audience participant commented that an aspect of this question is that East and West campuses each have their own provost.  In addition, APs at West campus (and perhaps East) are integrated into their campus?s Faculty Senate.  There is no equivalent to CAPS at these campuses.
  • Representation on PACAPS:  Audience participants noted that APs in the School of Business, Division of Undergraduate Academic Services, and within the Provost?s Office are not represented on PACAPS.  Mr. Weiss asked that APs from those units write to him; and he will address the issue.

 

Sue Hunter, Chair, CAPS

 

Ms. Hunter explained that the role of CAPs in the review of policies and procedures is to incorporate Task Force findings in to new language for ACD section 508.  The plan is that CAPS will work concurrently with the Task Force; that is, as the Task Force makes recommendations, CAPS will draft ACD language based on those recommendations.

 

Discussion with the audience centered on the steps in approving the revised ACD text.  Panelists explained that the new language will be submitted to a vote by APs at large; and upon approval by this group, the new text will presented to the Academic Senate for its consideration.
