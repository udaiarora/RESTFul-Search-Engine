Statistics Certificate with Emphasis in Industrial Statistics and Six Sigma Methodology

(For general information about the Certificate in Statistics program click here.)

 

Note: The Statistics Certificate program has the following prerequisite courses:

 

MAT 270 ? Calculus

CSE 100 ? Computer Programming

QBA 221 or STP 420 ? Introductory Applied Statistics

 

 

The following courses are all required for the Statistics certificate with the emphasis in industrial statistics and Six Sigma methodology:

 

STP 530 or IEE 578 or QBA 525 ? Regression Analysis

IEE 572 ? Design of Experiments

IEE 570 ? SPC

IEE 598 ? Seminar in Six Sigma Black Belt Training

IEE 598 ? Applied Project in Six Sigma Methods

 

 

 

Students who complete additional electives from the list of courses offered by the program faculty will enhance their ability to receive black belt certification in industry.  Additionally, the program director will write a letter that outlines the training and project and send it to any employer or potential employer who may be considering the recipient for black belt certification.
