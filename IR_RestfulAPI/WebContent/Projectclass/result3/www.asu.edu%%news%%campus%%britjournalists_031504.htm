Award-winning British Journalists to Speak at ASU on Iraq War Reporting

Arizona State University's Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication will present "The Snake in the Desert - Reporting the War from Baghdad," on Monday, March 22 from 1:40 p.m. to 2:55 p.m. in Nursing 101. This free public lecture hosts two International Emmy and Royal Television Society Award-winning journalists, Lindsey Hilsum and Tim Lambdon, recognized for their work in Baghdad during the Iraq War.

Lindsey Hilsum is a diplomatic correspondent for Channel 4 News in Great Britain and Tim Lambdon works with her as producer and videographer. Their Royal Television Society award was noted earlier this month in the British newspaper, The Guardian, which wrote, "Channel 4's coverage of the Iraq war beat that of the BBC and Sky to the news event award. The judges said it explored themes untouched by other broadcasters, and from the studio marshaled a vigorous dialogue about the rights and wrongs of the war and its conduct. Its reporters, notably the diplomatic correspondent Lindsey Hilsum contributed poise and insight from the frontline."

In addition to their coverage of Iraq, Hilsum and Lambon have reported from the Balkans, Africa, the U.S., Europe, Asia and the Middle East. Hilsum won the Royal Television Society's Specialist Journalist of the Year Award in 2003 for her reports from Jenin in the Israeli-occupied West Bank during April 2002. In 1994, she was in Rwanda when the genocide started and reported it for the BBC.

Hilsum and Lambon are appearing at ASU through a grant from the John S. and James L. Knight Foundation to the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication. The project, "News Bridges," has brought a series of British journalists from Independent Television News (the parent of Channel 4 News) in London to Tempe.

The Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication is in the College of Public Programs at Arizona State University and is among the top 20 of the nation's 105 accredited journalism programs. The School recently finished first in the nation in the 2003-2004 Intercollegiate Broadcast News Competition of the prestigious Hearst Journalism Awards program.
