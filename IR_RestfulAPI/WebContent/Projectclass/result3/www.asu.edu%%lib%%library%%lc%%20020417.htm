Minutes of Meeting


April 17, 2002

Meeting called to order at 3:06 pm.

Chair: John Barnard
Secretary: Mimmo Bonanni
A quorum was present.


I. Approval of Minutes March 20, 2002


Minutes were approved as written.


II. Dean's Report - Sherrie Schmidt


Sherrie briefly spoke of the forthcoming changes in the libraries, the Video Resources closure, and the Noble CPS desk, and Noble reference merger. In regards to current open positions, she emphasized that if a position is not currently filled, the position will go back to the library.


Sherrie also reminded council that the 2003 Budget is still not final. She mentioned that there are two current camps of thought: (1) As a result of a tuition increase, we may not have do any adjustments, or (2) We may still need to pay back 4% next year.


Sherrie mentioned a new contract with the AZ Historical Foundation. Under the new agreement, the Foundation will receive funds from ASU Libraries to hire employees. The library will provide cataloging, technology, collection maintenance costs, and space within the Hayden Library. The Foundation will be open 70 hours per week. It will be directed and managed by one curator.


III. Kurt Murphy, AD for Personnel Report


Lois introduced Kurt Murphy's presentation. She mentioned that Compensation committee has met and discussed the three-year salary adjustment cycle project with Kurt. Compensation Committee wanted Kurt to present council with a history and development of the project.


Kurt provided a history of a three year compensation plan approved by the Provost office to increase salaries. ARL salary data from ASU's peer institutions was used to determine the 50th percentile for the defined job categories, combined with years of service.


To determine a market target salary, ASU Libraries was compared to 108 other ARL libraries. Kurt gathered figures and determined salary schedules over a 5-year period. ASU Libraries academic salary plan began in July of 2000 with the Entry and Lower range Adjustments: 30,000 - 36,000 to 36,000 - 40,000, an average increase of 14 percent. Then in July 2001, the Upper Range Adjustments were made - 51,000 - 68,000 to 53,000 - 71,000, an average increase of four percent. Finally, the Mid-Range Adjustments were made in January 2002, 41,000 - 50,000 to 43,000 - 52,000, an average increase of five percent.


Sherrie mentioned that the adjustments made were self-funded. The Libraries did not acquire monies outside of the libraries. Kurt concluded by saying that salaries in ARL go up three percent each year, so it is difficult task to match salary with our peers.


IV. Chair & Executive Committee's Report John Barnard


John welcomed Hillery Oberle as the new Map Archivist for Government Documents and Maps Department.


V. Committee Reports


a. Personnel Committee - Scott Herrington


Scott mentioned that the revisions to the handbook have been uploaded to the Librarians' Council web site. The committee is continuing to refine the Marion the Librarian 6th Year letter.


b. Review Committee - Kathy Krzys


No report.


c. Research and Professional Development Committee - Ginny Sylvester


Travel money is spent for this year. Ginny mentioned that there are still some research monies available. She wanted to remind us to let her or Dan know ASAP if you have a research project.


d. Compensation Committee - Lois Schneberger


No report.


e. Academic Senate - George Bell


No Report. The floor thanked George for his exemplary role on Academic Senate over the years with a round of applause.

f. Committee on Academic Professional Status - Linda Shackle


Linda announced that they had a great turn out for the Barbara Mawhiney presentation. Barbara Mawhiney, Director of Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action, gave a presentation on the rationale of the job classification system. Her talk highlighted information on the Academic Professional classification including the category of Academic Associates.

g. Page Master - Annie Platoff


John announced that Yuwu Song would become the new Page Master for the council after Annie leaves for her new position. Annie mentioned that she has been training Song and bringing him up to date in regards to procedures and day-to-day duties of the position.


VI. Old Business


None


VII. New Business


a. Nominations and Elections Committee - Pat Etter


Pat announced that she would like to thank all of the nominees who have volunteered for the election. Pat asked for nominations from the floor. There were none. Pat stated that she would send out the ballot shortly to council.


b. Annette Voth Resolution


John read Annette Voth's resolution. The group passed the resolution in honor of Annette Voth's service to ASU Libraries and to ASU.


c. Other


A question was raised from the floor concerning the vacancy of the Chair-Elect position. John stated that the Executive Committee would discuss the issue at the next Executive Committee meeting. John announced his retirement from ASU Libraries; he also announced that he would not be able to fulfill his former chair position.


Meeting adjourned at 3:41 pm.


Mimmo Bonanni, Secretary
