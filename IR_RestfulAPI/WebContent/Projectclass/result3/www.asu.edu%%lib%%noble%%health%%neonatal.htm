Setting Up for Off-campus Access

Three steps need to be accomplished to insure access to all nursing and health-related resources from off-campus.

  1. Get Your ASURITE ID
    An ASURITE ID and password are needed to access most resources from off-campus.


  2. Sign up for MDConsult
    The MDConsult database requires a special username/password access; accounts can only be set up from an on-campus computer. If you are not able to come onto campus to do this, please contact Sheila Hofstetter at Sheila.Hofstetter@asu.edu Once an account has been set up, you may access this database from anywhere.

    To set up an account:
    • Go to the Libraries' home page at http://www.asu.edu/lib/
    • Click on "Find Articles and More"
    • In the "Name" box in the left column, type: mdconsult [click on GO]
    • Click on the "MDConsult" link on the results page, then click on the big "MDConsult" on the top of the database description page to enter the database.
    • Click on "Register for Access" on the left column.   Follow instructions on the screens.
    • Once registered, use the "User Name/Password" login ,  to access the database.




  3. Sign up for netLibrary
    The netLibrary database requires a special username/password access; accounts can only be set up from an on-campus computer. If you are not able to come onto campus to do this, please contact Sheila Hofstetter at Sheila.Hofstetter@asu.edu Once an account has been set up, you may access this database from anywhere.

    To set up an account:
    • Go to the Libraries' home page at http://www.asu.edu/lib/
    • Click on "Find Articles and More"
    • In the "Name" box in the left column, type: netlibrary [click on GO]
    • Click on the "netLibrary" link on the results page, then click on the big "netLibrary" on the top of the database description page to enter the database.
    • Click on "Create a Free Account" in the upper right .  Follow instructions on the screens.
    • Once registered, use the "login" link in the upper right , to access the database.


Where to Find the Resources

The ASU Libraries' home page (http://www.asu.edu/lib/) is your "portal" to nursing and health sciences information resources.

"Find Books and More" goes to the ASU Libraries' catalog, where books can found by author, title, or keywords.

"Find Articles and More" goes to the ASU Libraries' list of indexing and abstracting databases in which journal articles can be found by topic.

On the "Find Articles and More" page, click on the "Off Campus Sign In" button, log in using your ASURITE ID and password, when you have completed the login, the button will change to "Off Campus Ready".

Need more help with off-campus access?

See our "Remote Access to Electronic Resources Provided by the ASU Libraries" page at
http://www.asu.edu/lib/help/proxy.htm

Use the the Subject box on the left to find the databases appropriate for nursing; scroll the window down to the "Nursing" heading, then click on "GO". Once you become familiar with the names of the databases, you can use either the Names box or the alphabetical list.

The list of nursing and health-related databases are retrieved. To select a database, click on its title.

The database description page will be displayed. To enter the database, click on the database title.

Obtaining Articles & Books

From an indexing/abstracting database such as CINAHL, use the links provided to either reach the full text of the article or to find out if the ASU Libraries have a copy. Even if the following links do not work, it is still possible that the Libraries have what you need, skip to the next section to find out what to do.

  • Links to the full text are available on some citations; they will look like one of these:
    • URLP: http://www.cinahl.com/cgi-bin/refsvc?jid=2311&accno=2004030376
    • FTXT: EBSCO Journal Service (EJS)
    • FTXT: HighWire

  • Links to the ASU Libraries' list of electronic journals are found at the bottom of the citation and look like this:
    • WEBLH: Check for ASU Library electronic journal holdings
      You will need the date, volume #, issue #, and page #s, from the Source (SO) field of the citation

  • Links to the ASU Libraries' catalog of print and microformatted journals are found at the bottom of the citation and look like this:
    • ILINK: Check for ASU Library catalog holdings
      You will need the date, volume #, issue #, and page #s, from the Source (SO) field of the citation


From a list of citations, use the following strategies to determine if ASU Libraries have a copy of the article or book.

  • Journal Articles
    1. Go to the ASU Libraries home page http://www.asu.edu/lib/
    2. In the left column, click on "E-Journals."
    3. Click on the "Off-Campus sign in" button and complete your authorization.
      Need more help with off-campus access?

      See our "Remote Access to Electronic Resources Provided by the ASU Libraries" page at
      http://www.asu.edu/lib/help/proxy.htm
    4. In the Find box type the title of the journal and click on the Search button.
    5. If "0 records retrieved", go to step 7
    6. If the ASU Libraries' have an electronic subscription to the journal, click on the link to the publisher or vendor location. There may be more than one way to get to the journal. Be sure to notes the dates available at that location - if the date you need is not covered, go to step 7.
      Example:

      Nursing inquiry (1320-7881)
           from 03/01/1999 to present in Blackwell-Synergy
           from 12/01/1998 to 1 year ago in Academic Search Elite

    7. If electronic access is not available, go back to the ASU Libraries home page http://www.asu.edu/lib/
    8. Click on "Find Books and More" in the middle.
    9. Click on "Journal Title" search; enter the journal title in the search box and click on the gray search button.
    10. If the ASU Libraries have the journal in print, microfilm, or microfiche, the catalog will list what volumes are owned in each format and in which library those volumes are located.

Copyright & Plagiarism

Copyright
Copyright is literally the "right to copy." The author of the work, be it written, musical, pictural, or otherwise, holds the copyright on the work. However, when authors agree to have their articles or books published, they are required by the publisher to sign over the copyright. In the United States, an exception to copyright is made for "fair use"; fair use includes a one-time copy for educational purposes, provided that no more than one chapter/article (or 10% of the work) is copied.

As a university student you are effected as follows.

You may:

  • make one copy of an article from an issue of a journal
  • make one copy of one chapter within a book
  • copy a "reasonable" number of citations from an indexing/abstracting database
  • download the complete electronic version of a book, if the site/database indicates that is permissable.

You may not:

  • copy more than 10% of the total work, unless the pages needed consitute a single chapter or article within the work
  • copy a "substantial" portion of a database
  • supply another person with the article, chapter or citations that you previously had copied
    Examples:
    • If you have downloaded a copy of an article from an electronic journal, you may not forward that article to another person, nor make a printed copy of that article for another person.
    • If you have made a copy of an article from a print journal, you may not duplicate that copy for another person.

Plagiarism
Plagiarism is the theft of someone else's words or ideas. Whenever you "copy and paste" from a journal article, book, web page, etc., you must acknowledge that these words and ideas are not yours; even if you "re-write or paraphrase" the other person's ideas, you must give them credit. Depending on which "style manual" you are using, you give credit by the use of footnotes and/or references.
