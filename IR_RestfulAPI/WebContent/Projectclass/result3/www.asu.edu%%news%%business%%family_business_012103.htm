January 21, 2003

Research project focuses on succession in family business

The Family Business Forum at ASU's Center for the Advancement of Small Business is launching a new phase of its research to discover what happens in family businesses when management passes to a new generation.

The project is being done by the ASU Center for the Advancement of Small Business and Phoenix-based Behavior Research Center, Inc., a founding partner of ASU's Family Business Forum. The forum is a collaboration of successful small companies who advise the Center for the Advancement of Small Business and support its work in the area of family-owned business www.cob.asu.edu/seid/casb.

The Lyle P. Campbell family is providing funding to distribute results and to support research goals. Lea Farr, an ASU MBA student affiliated with the Center for the Advancement of Small Business, has been selected to be the principal researcher and create the infrastructure for additional studies.

Behavior Research Center, Inc. has been investigating the small business sector since 1990 with its BusinessTRACKT Arizona studies, but this latest survey looked at a previously unexplored subset - family-owned business. In a study conducted for the ASU Family Business Forum this fall, BusinessTRACKT Arizona established that roughly 80 percent of the small businesses in metropolitan Phoenix are owned wholly or over 51 percent by a family.

The next phase will look at how these family-owned businesses deal with transition. Owners or managers of 400 businesses will be interviewed in the coming month to gather the data.

"The Family Business Forum partners charged us with obtaining actionable research," said Mary Lou Bessette, director of the Center for the Advancement of Small Business. "Once we define the challenges facing these companies we can develop educational and resource programs to assist them."

Bessette explained that the goals of this research are to:
· Educate the community about the impact and reach of family business
· Determine strategies for fostering stronger family businesses in the future
· Identify possible services to family businesses, and help for families who want to form a business
· Define critical-issue topics for future seminars and programs

Behavior Research Center, Inc. which has been conducting marketing and public opinion research since 1965, is known for its work in small business and Hispanic consumer habits. "It has not been widely recognized that family-owned companies comprise such a large segment of the small business sector," said Suzanne de Berge, chief financial officer of Behavior Research Center and a member of the Family Business Forum. "We hope the new information gathered through this study will help businesses plan for the future."

The Center for the Advancement of Small Business will present a symposium on family business on April 23, 2003. See www.cob.asu.edu/seid/casb for details.
