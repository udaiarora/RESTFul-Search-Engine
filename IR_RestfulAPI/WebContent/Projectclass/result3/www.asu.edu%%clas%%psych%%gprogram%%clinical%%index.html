Clinical Psychology Program

The Ph.D. program in Clinical Psychology, fully accredited by the American Psychological Association (APA), seeks to train scientist-practitioners in a variety of domains, including basic and applied research, teaching, preventative interventions with "high risk" populations, psychotherapy, and research and treatment services in medical settings. Essential components of the program are: a comprehensive grounding in behavioral science, training in the theories and techniques of clinical intervention, the development of research skills for the analysis of individual and community-centered problems, and a thorough familiarization with professional and ethical issues in contemporary human service settings. The graduate program is staffed by 14 full-time faculty. Approximately 40 full-time students are in residence.

The Clinical program emphasizes the development of each student's potential as a research scientist. In addition, students engage in clinical training that prepares them to conduct informed clinical research and to provide clinical services to the community. . Our graduates typically work in a variety of careers combining research, teaching, and service, in academic settings, medical centers and clinics. While students interested in obtaining a broad, general education in Clinical Psychology can sample from a variety of theoretical and research activities and a diversity of applied experiences, the program also allows for specialized areas of training, representing particular strengths of the faculty. Three domains of emphasis in the current structure of the program include: clinical child psychology, community-prevention, and health psychology.

To learn more, please refer to the brochure for the clinical psychology program at ASU.
