National Levels of Government:
U.S. - México Comparison Chart

Mexico Government Structure (US equivalent Government Structure)



National (Federal) Government (US Federal Government -- Both Federal Governments determine permitted businesses allowed to "foreigners". Some licenses, fees, and permits are issued at this level.)

    México, Distrito Federal (Federal District of Mexico City - no direct US equivalent - the District of Columbia is also a federal district but the governance is structured differently)

      Delegaciones (16) (administrative zones - no US equivalent)

        Colonias (legislative/voting districts - not an administrative level)
    Ejidos (Special agrarian districts governed by special federal laws. Closest U.S. equivalent is the Native American Reservations, many of which are also governed by special treaties in addition to federal law which supercedes state laws. Ejidos have sindicatos, Reservations have tribal government.)
    Estados (31) (States -- some licenses, fees, & permits issued at this level in both countries)

      Municipios ("municipalities" more equivalent to Counties in the US -- some licenses, fees, & permits issued at this level in both countries. Lowest legislative and administrative level of Mexican government)
        Governed by state level Municipal Codes + local legislation in compliance with state & federal law.

        1983 Constitutional Amendment to Article 115 provides that the following basic urban (public) services may be primarily administered by the Municipios at the pleasure of the state.
          Cemeteries Police Slaughterhouses
          Cleaning Public Lighting Traffic Police
          Markets Roads Urban Transportation
          Parks Sewage System Water Supply


        Additionally, the following services may also be delegated to the municipality by the state:

          Development Control Environmental Protection Land use planning


        Localidades (local political groupings = US local governments, but US local governments have more legislative powers & control over public services. Some licenses & permits issued at this level in the US. Lowest legislative and administrative level of US government)
          Villa (Village)
          Pueblo (Town)
          Ciudad (City)
          Mineral (Mine)
          Congregación (unincorporated village)
          Rancho (Ranch)
          Ranchería (Ranch)

        Colonias/Ubicaciones (similar to US local legislative/voting districts -- not an administrative government level. Used in some business sources to describe small geographic areas or neighborhood business communities -- In the U.S., Colonias are a special designation used by Housing & Urban Development for Community Block Grants to undeveloped areas within 150 miles of the US/México border in Arizona, California, New Mexico, & Texas.)

Metropolitan Area government cooperation is more developed in México. It is not needed in many areas since Municipios are often large enough geographically to encompass most metropolitan areas. There appears to be more cooperation in some governance areas like pollution control, for major metropolitan areas that encompass multiple Municipios in one or more than one state, like the Mexico City Metropolitan Area which encompasses 16 Delegaciones + 17 - 27 Municipios del Estado de Mexico. The US metropolitan area government cooperation is well developed in some areas and fairly non-existent in others.
