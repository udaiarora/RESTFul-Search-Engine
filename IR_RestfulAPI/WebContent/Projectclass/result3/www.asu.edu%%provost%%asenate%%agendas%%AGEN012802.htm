        5.A.1   Senate Resolution #2 (2001-2002).  Senate Resolution on Recycling

        ?1) Whereas, representatives of the ASU Academic Senate, including members of  the Student Faculty Policy Committee, have held meetings and invited
            discussion on campus recycling,

        2) Whereas, ASU's campus-wide recycling program has not appreciably expanded since its formation in 1991,

        3)  Whereas, recycling is important to the campus environment,

        Be it resolved that the Academic Senate encourages leaders of the university to step up ASU's current recycling programs and to consider expanded
        recycling measures.?

        6.D  Personnel Committee (Douglas Johnson).

        6.D.1  Resolution #3 (2001-2002) Regarding the Senate Personnel Committee review of the recommendations of the Task Force on Promotion and Tenure.

        ?Whereas, the Senate agrees that the Arizona State University Promotion and Tenure Policy (ACD 501-513) should be updated to improve clarity,
        coherence,  and consistency and to promote high quality decision and review processes.

        The Academic Senate recommends the acceptance of the Promotion and Tenure Task Force Recommendations (see attachment 1) subject to the clarifications
        and changes recommended by the Academic Senate Personnel Committee (see attachment 2). The Academic Senate recommends the appointment of a
        university taskforce to investigate best practices for evaluating the quality of teaching/learning and recommend models of evaluation to facilitate implementation
        by academic units.

        Furthermore, the Senate applauds the formation of the Provost?s Personnel Advisory Committee (PAC20) as a mechanism for insuring campus wide input into
        the process of implementing and maintaining university personnel policies (ACD Manual). And the Senate looks forward to reviewing the updated ACD
        Manual including the updated statement of policy on Promotion and Tenure.

        The Senate commends the Promotion and Tenure Task Force for thoughtful and thorough deliberations and open hearings which have allowed campus wide
        issues and concerns to be incorporated into this important process.?

        Attachments (1) and (2) are located on the Academic Senate Web Page at:
        http://www.asu.edu/provost/asenate.  We are counting on you to print out these documents and bring them to the meeting.  If you cannot do so, please
        call the Senate Office and request a copy (480-965-9245) and one will be provided.
