April 29, 2005

Lynk forms committee to investigate Wade issues

ASU law professor Myles Lynk has selected the members of the advisory committee that will assist in his investigation of ASU’s actions in dealing with former student-athlete Loren Wade.

Lynk was appointed March 31 by ASU President Michael Crow to conduct a thorough, independent investigation of how the university’s various units handled issues concerning Wade.

Wade was arrested March 26 and charged with first-degree murder in the shooting death of former ASU football player Brandon Falkner.

“The members of this committee will be extremely helpful in developing my final report and any recommendations that we make,” Lynk says. “All of the members are independent thinkers and are vitally concerned about this community. They have diverse and experienced backgrounds that will be vital to this process.”

The committee members include:

• The Honorable Cecil Patterson, judge (retired), Arizona Court of Appeals.

• Stephen Whisler, chairman and chief executive officer, Phelps Dodge Corp.

• Norma Hubele, ASU professor and director of strategic initiatives, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering.

• Clare Kirlin, senior, Barrett Honors College, ASU.

• James Rund, vice president for University Undergraduate Initiatives, ASU.

• Thomas Schildgen, professor and chair, Department of Information and Management Technology, ASU East.

• Alice W. Snell, a member of the ASU Foundation’s board of directors.

Once the investigation ends, Lynk will present his findings and recommendations to Crow. The investigation is expected to be completed by July 15.
