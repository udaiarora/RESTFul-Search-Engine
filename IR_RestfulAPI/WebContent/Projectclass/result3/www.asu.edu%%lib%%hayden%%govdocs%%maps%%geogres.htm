Indexes & Databases


Indexes are used to identify articles in journals, newspapers or magazines. For a complete list of ASU's electronic indexes go to: Library Indexes For general databases, search for indexes under such subjects as: "statistical information", "News and Newspaper Articles", or "All Subjects".


See the Resources by Subject section for selected subject-specific indexes and abstracts available.


Principle Geography Indexes:

  • GeoRef (1785+) - Abstracts books, journal articles, proceedings, dissertations, theses, maps and government publications related to the Earth Sciences, including Physical Geography. Print Equivalents: Bibliography and Index of Geology; Geophysical Abstracts

  • Science Citation Index Expanded (1955+) - An interdisciplinary index to major journals in the sciences, including physical geography, planning and resource management. SCI can be used to identify who has cited a particular article.

  • Social Sciences Citation Index (1973+) - An index to major journals in the social sciences, including demography, cultural, economic, historical and human geography, planning and environmental studies. Indexes many of the core Geography journals with broader content. SSCI can be used to identify who has cited a particular article. Print Equivalent: Social Science Citation Index

  • Web of Science - Combines Arts and Humanities Citation Index (1975+); Science Citation Index Expanded (1955+); Social Sciences Citation Index (1973+) Provides access to current bibliographic information and cited references covering more than 1,150 of the world's leading arts and humanities journals, 5,800 of scientific and technical journals, and 1,700 of social sciences journals in a broad range of disciplines.

Indexes

  • Arizona Economic Data Center (AEDC) (1990+) - Clearinghouse for Arizona-related economic, business, and broad demographic information. Sponsored by Arizona State University to provide electronic access to information related to Arizona's economy. Includes 1990 & 1995 census information.

  • EconLit (1969+) - Indexes the world's economic literature. Topics include economic development, forecasting, and history; fiscal and monetary theory; business and public finance; international, health care, regional and urban economics, and more

  • Ei Compendex Web (1970+) - An index to journal articles, conference proceedings, technical reports and books related to engineering. Includes materials on planning.

  • GeoBase (1990+) - An index to articles in books and journals related to Physical and Human Geography and Geology. Print Equivalents: Geographical Abstracts. Human Geography; Geographical Abstracts. Physical Geography; International Development Abstracts; Oceanographic Literature Review

  • Index to Current Urban Documents - Includes published guide to the reports and research that are generated by local government agencies, civic organizations, academic and research organizations, public libraries, and metropolitan and regional planning agencies in approximately 500 selected cities in the United States and Canada.

  • PAIS, Public Affairs Information Service (1972+) - An index to current literature on political, economic, and social issues.

  • POPLINE (1827+) - Covers population, family planning & related health issues. Topics include Human Fertility & Contraception, Family Planning, Maternal & Child Health, AIDS in Developing Countries, Program Operations & Evaluation, Demography, Vital Statistics, etc.

  • Social Sciences Citation Index (1973+) - An index to major journals in the social sciences, including demography, cultural, economic, historical and human geography, planning and environmental studies. Indexes many of the core Geography journals with broader content. SSCI can be used to identify who has cited a particular article. Print Equivalent: Social Science Citation Index

  • Social Sciences Full Text (1983+) - Covers concepts, theories, and methodologies in all areas of the social sciences. Subjects covered include anthropology, criminology, economics, law, geography, policy studies, psychology, sociology, social work, and urban studies.

  • TRIS Online (Transportation Research Information Services) (1960s+) - An index to over 450,000 publications related to research in transportation.

Style Manuals


Because Geography is an interdisciplinary subject the writing styles vary. Consult journals in the subject area of interest as a guide to writing papers and citing references in a given field of Geography. See the Journals section of this page for a selected list of Geography journals.


Also for help citing sources, see the Libraries' Citation Guide.
