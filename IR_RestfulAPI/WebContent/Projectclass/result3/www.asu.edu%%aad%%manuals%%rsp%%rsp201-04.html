RSP 201–04: Human Subjects in Research—Project Monitoring

Purpose

To assure that all research and sponsored projects involving human subjects are conducted in an ethical manner and that ASU complies with government standards for research involving human subjects

Sources

45 Code of Federal Regulations § 46 (2005)
The National Commission for the Protection of Human Subjects of Biomedical and Behavioral Research. The Belmont Report: Ethical Principles and Guidelines for the Protection of Human Subjects of Research, April 18, 1979

Applicability

All research and related activities involving human subjects for which ASU is a responsible participant, regardless of funding source(s)

Policy

The principal investigator (PI) is responsible for assuring that the welfare of human subjects is protected throughout the project and that the research is in compliance with federal and university regulations. In accordance with federal policy, research involving human subjects can be approved for up to one year. Researchers should submit a Continuing Review Form if a non-exempt project is to last longer than the approval period, which is typically one year. The study must be reviewed and reapproved by the Institutional Review Board (IRB) for research to continue on an annual basis; in certain cases approval may be for less than one year. Non-exempt studies are considered complete when data collection and data analysis are complete. Researchers should submit one copy of the Close Out Form to the Research Compliance Office within 30 days of study closure.
