March 17, 2003

Campuses schedule site visits for continuing accreditation open forums

After more than two years of preparatory work, a site visit by an evaluation team representing the Higher Learning Commission of Schools and Colleges, a Commission of the North Central Association, is almost here. This effort is being put forward as part of the continuing accreditation process for ASU Main and ASU East.
The team includes: Phillip R. Certain, dean, College of Letters & Science, University of Wisconsin at Madison (team chairman); Shirley J. Barton, executive dean for regional campuses, Kent State University; Rosa A. Cintron, associate professor of educational leadership and policy studies, University of Oklahoma; David R. Colburn, provost and vice president for academic affairs, University of Florida; Sue Day-Perroots, dean of extended learning, West Virginia University; Frederick W. Emshousen, mechanical engineering technology professor, Purdue University; Roy A. Koenigsknecht, speech and hearing science professor, Ohio State University; Carol B. Lynch, dean of the Graduate School and associate vice chancellor for research, University of Colorado at Boulder; Sylvia Manning, chancellor, University of Illinois at Chicago; Claudia Mitchell-Kernan, vice chancellor of Graduate Studies and dean of the Graduate Division, University of California at Los Angeles; Doyle Z. Williams, dean, Sam M. Walton College of Business, University of Arkansas, Fayetteville; and Dina A. Zinnes, political science professor, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

During the period March 24 to 26, team members will meet with members of the overall communities at ASU Main and ASU East to discuss the contents of an extensive self-study report as well as other aspects of ASU. The report, which includes an addendum that reflects the philosophy and initiatives of President Michael Crow, can be accessed at (http://HLC2003.asu.edu), at the reserve desks at the ASU Main and ASU East libraries, or at individual academic units.

Main Campus open forums for faculty, staff, students and community members are scheduled as follows:

. Open forum for faculty: 2:30 - 3:15 p.m., March 24, Computing Commons Auditorium (CPCOM 120).
. Open forum for staff: 3:30 - 4:15 p.m., March 24, Computing Commons Auditorium, room 120.
. Open forum for students: 1:45 - 2:30 p.m., March 25, Carson Ballroom, Old Main.
. Open forum for the outside community: 4:15 - 5 p.m., March 25, Carson Ballroom, Old Main.

These forums provide an opportunity for comments and questions, which can be addressed to the evaluation team. Similar forums will be held at ASU East. Participation is encouraged as a way to demonstrate interest by the ASU community and to allow individuals an opportunity to play a part in the continuing-accreditation process.

After the site visit, the evaluation team will submit a report to the commission. The decision of the commission relative to continuing accreditation will be announced later in the year. For information, visit the Web at (http://HLC2003.asu.edu).
