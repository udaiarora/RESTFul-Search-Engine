March 3, 2003

Council, Crow honor top ASU supervisors

Great supervisors create a positive working environment by opening the lines of communication, and by encouraging employees to be their best. Ultimately, such supervisors are able to elevate morale and productivity.

The 2003 Outstanding Supervisor Awards ceremony, sponsored by ASU's Classified Staff Council (CSC), honored 10 ASU supervisors on Feb. 20 at the University Club for their outstanding work.

ASU President Michael Crow presented the awards and said supervisors serve a crucial role at ASU.

"Success for an organization is only possible when there is mutual respect between supervisor and staff," Crow said. "And what better measure of success is there for a supervisor than to be honored by your own employees. It means so much coming from them because they are the people who really know you."

Deirdre Irvine, library supervisor, senior, serves as chairwoman of the CSC staff development committee, and said having a good supervisor can make even the most challenging job assignment seem manageable.

Recently Irvine and a small group of volunteers sifted through 69 Outstanding Supervisor Award nominations and narrowed the group down to 10 winners.

"It was definitely encouraging reading the nominations," Irvine said. "Each one was heartfelt and sincere and we had difficulty paring it down."

Irvine said the nomination forms most often cited compassion and communication skills as the most compelling reasons a supervisor was outstanding.

Lynne Kincaid, business manager for the Office of Research/Sponsored Projects, says she first learned of the honor when she was surprised during a staff meeting.

"My staff arranged for Deirdre Irvine to surprise me with the announcement of my selection at a regularly scheduled managers meeting," Kincaid said. "All of the other managers in attendance knew of the upcoming announcement, so, of course, I was the only one completely startled by the sudden knocking at the door. What a wonderful surprise."

Kincaid said she was thankful not only for the award, but also for being able to work with such a terrific staff.

"As a supervisor, I cannot take credit for the wonderful work ethic that my staff hold and the professionalism they bring to ASU," Kincaid says. "It is always enjoyable to celebrate special times such as these with the people we work closest with. I respect and admire my teammates. This is a wonderful experience."

CSC President Pam Mulhearn said this year's outstanding supervisors are examples to others that a good relationship with staff can create a happy, cooperative, productive unit.

"These supervisors are important to ASU because a supervisor can make all the difference in staff members' performance," Mulhearn said. "Generally when people are happy and satisfied within their units, their positive attitudes shine through in their work and maybe even become contagious, ultimately benefiting the customers of the university."

Linda Sullivan, executive coordinator, Student Development & Memorial Union/Student Affairs, said she appreciated the award tremendously.

"It is truly one of the highlights of my professional career," Sullivan said. "I feel very fortunate to work with wonderful, talented people who enjoy doing what they do. It is a pleasure to be in their company every day. Receiving this honor is really the icing on the cake."

Kevin Cook, director and associate dean for Residential Life & Student Development, said he is extremely surprised he was nominated.

"I am filled with tremendous joy and gratitude to be appreciated by staff," Cook said. "As a supervisor, my goal is to value the entire person - not just the professional, but the personal as well. Clearly, the two are inextricable."

In addition to Kincaid, Sullivan, and Cook, this year's Outstanding Supervisors are: Sharon Bushart, technology support analyst coordinator, Liberal Arts & Sciences; Traci Helwig, business manager, senior, W. P. Carey School of Business; Kathryn Kilroy, executive director, KAET/ASSET; Jim Newell, shop supervisor, Facilities Management; Cynthia O'Neil, business operations manager, Electrical Engineering; Kern Poyner, manager, Information Technology, KAET; and Marjorie Zatz, associate dean, Graduate College.

Each award winner received a personalized desk clock and a gift certificate donated by a local sponsor. For information about the 2003 Outstanding Supervisor Awards and to read nomination comments, go to (www.asu.edu/csc).
