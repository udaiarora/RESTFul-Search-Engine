FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Who would work well in this position and where are they?
It is easier to identify an ideal candidate during the interview and selection stage when you have a clear understanding of who and what professional characteristics the applicants should possess.

Has the position been difficult to recruit for in the past?
Your answer will affect different issues in your recruiting strategy:

  • Consider the length of time available to fill the position (for example, two weeks or one month)
  • Assess the competition in the marketplace--How many qualified applicants exist for this type of position?
  • Evaluate the available recruiting methods (print, online, etc.) to determine which will be the most effective in attracting qualified applicants.

Is the applicant pool targeted for an internal, external, local or national search?
Your decision can determine when your job closes, how expensive your advertising may be, what skills you can ask for in the job posting and how long it will take to fill your position. Your Staffing Specialist can work with you to determine costs of an advertising plan.

What conferences, workshops or meetings does the staff attend to assist in getting the word out?
Conferences, workshops or meetings are a great way to meet a variety of people with similar backgrounds or careers. Word-of-mouth recruiting is said to be the most effective recruiting tool.

Is the job description indicative to the audience and applicants desired?
It's important to determine if the skills match the experience you are looking for. You should also consider specifying a certain set of skills so applicants can screen themselves out of the search based on what you know a successful applicant must possess.

What is the target market and how to reach them?
Consider all your options. Trade publications, websites and newspapers are great ways to reach your target market.

What funding is available for advertising?
If the funding is very small, you may have to be creative and depend on word of mouth, e-mail, list serves and the ASU website.

Will you advertising in print or on-line, or both?
Are you hoping to fill the position by just advertising in a newspaper's job section or is there a more targeted online option that might better promote the position? If you have adequate funding, the best approach may be to cast a wide net and target a good cross section of different advertising medium.

Where can your vacant position be posted?
If the job is open to the public, ASU Staffing Services will post the position on the ASU employment website, the ASU Insight, ASU Jobline (480) 965-5627, Staffing Services Community Partners and other advertising venues as requested.

If it is limited to employees of ASU, NAU, U of A and the Arizona Board of Regents, ASU Staffing Services will post the position on the ASU employment website, the ASU Insight and Internal Diversity Groups.

If the job is limited to within the Hiring Unit, then the hiring department is responsible for posting the vacant position in a conspicuous area in the department.
