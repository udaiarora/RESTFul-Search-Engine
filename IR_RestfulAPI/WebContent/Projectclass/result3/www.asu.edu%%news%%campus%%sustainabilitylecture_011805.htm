January 18, 2005

Sustainability issues dominate new lecture series

ASU’s new International Institute for Sustainability (IIS) is announcing new programs aimed at public education. The first of these is the Wrigley Lecture Series on Sustainability, a multidisciplinary lecture series sponsored in collaboration with a host of other ASU departments.

Initiating the lecture series will be a burst of presentations by six distinguished scholars on topics related to sustainability.

The first lecture is by Duke University ’s William H. Schlesinger, who will give the keynote address for the Seventh Annual Central Arizona Phoenix Long Term Ecological Research Project (CAP LTER) Poster Symposium at 1:15 p.m., Jan. 19, in the Carson Ballroom at Old Main. The title of his lecture is “The Global Carbon Cycle and the Duke Forest Free-Air CO2 Enrichment (FACE) Project.”

At 2 p.m., Jan 28, Stanford University environmentalist Pamela A. Matson will speak on “Agricultural Intensification in the Yaqui Valley, Sonora, Mexico: ‘Is it Saving Land for Nature?’ ” The lecture will take place in Life Sciences E104. Also on Jan. 28 is a talk by archaeologist and sustainability scholar Joseph A. Tainter of the Cultural Heritage Research Project at the Rocky Mountain Research Station in Albuquerque. Tainter will speak on “Ecological vs. Social Complexity: An Anthropological Perspective on a Science of Sustainability.” The location and time for this lecture have yet to be decided.

In February, sustainability technologist Robert W. Kates will give two lectures. The first event is Geography’s Malcolm L. Comeaux Lecture on Feb. 10. On Feb. 11, he will give the keynote address at the Life, Earth and Social Sciences Graduate Research Symposium (locations and times for both lectures are yet to be announced).

Also on Feb. 11 is a lecture by human population authority Joel E. Cohen. Cohen is a professor of populations in the School of International and Public Affairs, the Department of Earth and Environmental Sciences, and the Center for Environmental Research and Conservation.

Following shortly after is a talk by risk authority Roger E. Kasperson, who will be speaking at the CAP LTER All Scientist Meeting at 3 p.m., Feb. 14, at the IIS Conference Room at Tempe Center. Kasperson is the director of the Stockholm Environment Institute and member of the George Perkins Marsh Institute.

Further lectures will be announced at a later date. The lecture series and all IIS activities are funded through the generous support of Julie Ann Wrigley. For more information on the lecture series, contact the institute at (480) 965-2975.

Hathaway, with the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, can be reached at (480) 965-6375 or (hathaway@asu.edu).
