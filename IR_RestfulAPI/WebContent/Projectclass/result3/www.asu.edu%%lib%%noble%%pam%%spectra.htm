Spectroscopy (General)

Guides
  • Tables of Spectral Data for Structure Determination of Organic Compounds.
    QC 462.85 .T313 1989 Science Reference
    Contains spectral information on a variety of organic compounds. It is intended to serve as an aid in the interpretation of C13 and H1 nuclear magnetic resonance, infrared, mass and electron excitation spectra.

Overviews and Encyclopedias
  • Encyclopedia of Analytical Instrumentation
    http://www.chem.vt.edu/chem-ed/ac-meths.html
    Includes sections on spectroscopy (atomic, electron, IR, laser, molecular, nuclear, x-ray and gamma-ray) and mass spectrometry.
  • Encyclopedia of Spectroscopy and Spectrometry
    QD95 .E55x 2000 Science Reference
    Offers a comprehensive and up-to-date explanation of the most important spectroscopic and related techniques together with their applications.
  • Encyclopedia of Spectroscopy
    QC 450.3 .P47 1995 Science Reference
    Includes graphs, diagrams, tables, and mathematical formulas.
  • Science of Spectroscopy
    http://www.scienceofspectroscopy.info/

Meta-Web Sites
  • Analytical Chemistry Springboard
    http://www.anachem.umu.se/jumpstation.htm
    Maintained by Knut Irgum, this site offers a wide variety of information in techniques in analytical chemistry. Spectroscopic techniques include Atomic Spectroscopy, Electron Spectroscopy, Mass Spectroscopy, Nuclear Magnetic Resonance, Optical Molecular Spectroscopy, and X-Ray Spectroscopy.

Infrared (IR)

Tutorials
  • Basic Infrared Spectroscopy
    http://chipo.chem.uic.edu/web1/ocol/spec/IR.htm
    Presents general information on Infrared Spectroscopy. The infrared spectra of 10 sample compounds are included. Taken from Organic Chemistry Online, copyright by Paul R. Young, 1996.
  • Infrared Spectroscopy and Modes of Vibrations
    http://wwwchem.uwimona.edu.jm:1104/spectra/irvibs.html
    Access to small movie clips showing vibrational modes.
  • Interpretation of Infrared Spectra
    http://wwwchem.csustan.edu/Tutorials/INFRARED.HTM
    Provides an introduction in the interpretation of Infrared Spectra. Practice problems are given.
  • Characteristic Infrared Absorptions
    http://wulfenite.fandm.edu/Data /Table_29.html
    Provides stretching and bending vibration ranges from the following organic groups:
    C-H, O-H, N-H, C=O, C=N, C[triple bond]N, C=C, C[triple bond]C, C-C(aromatic), C-O-C, C-O-H, and C-X (where X=halogen).

Spectra Compilations
  • Absorption Spectra in the Infrared Region
    QC 457 .A33 1974 Science Reference
    Presents the infrared spectra of a variety of organic compounds of interest to research workers in both academic and industrial establishments, particularly in the pharmaceutical and petrochemical industries.
  • Aldrich Library of Infrared Spectra
    QD 96 .I5 P67 1981 Science Reserve
    Contains 12,000 IR spectra of organic compounds.
  • Coblentz Society Desk Book of Infrared Spectra
    QC 457 .C687 1982 Science Reference
    While not a complete library of reference spectra, it does include a selection of high quality and useful reference spectra for purposes that can be served by typical spectra of principal compound classes. Teachers and chemists will find it useful for infrared spectrum interpretation.
  • David Sullivan FT-IR Library
    http://www.che.utexas.edu/~dls/ir/ir_dir.html
    IR spectra of compounds taken by David Sullivan. In addition other spectral links are provided.
  • Infrared Spectra of Inorganic Compounds
    QC 457 .N93 1971 Science Reference
  • Merck FT-IR Atlas: A Collection of FT-IR Spectra
    QC 457 .M473 1988 Science Reserve
    Presents over 3000 Fourier transform infrared spectra of pure substances from the Merck-Schuchardt program.
  • NIST Chemistry WebBook
    http://webbook.nist.gov/chemistry/
    Allows chemical species searching by formula, name, partial formula, CAS registry number, molecular weight, ionization energy, and proton affinity. Provides IR spectra and mass spectra graphs, among other chemical properties.
  • Raman/Infrared Atlas of Organic Compounds
    QD 272 .S6 .S3713 1989 Science Reserve
    Raman and Infrared spectra give complementary images of molecular vibrations. Therefore, the combined evaluation of both spectra yields more information about molecular structures than both kinds of spectra combined.
  • Sigma Aldrich
    http://www.sigmaaldrich.com
    Offers FT-NMR and FT-IR Raman spectra for many chemicals via their online catalog. Spectra are downloadable as PDF files. The catalog is searchable by name, CAS number, etc.

Meta-Web Sites
  • Internet Sites Dealing with IR and Near-IR Spectroscopy and Related Topics
    http://www.pastec.co.jp/links/ir-e.html
    A potpourri of web links dealing with Infrared Spectroscopy.

Mass (MS)

  • Knovel Mass Spectra
     http://www.asu.edu/lib/resources/db/knovel.htm
    (Available via Knovel - select "Chemistry & Chemical Engineering", then "Analytical Chemistry")
    Searching by structure and molecular formula for approximately 69,000 physiologically active organic compounds.
  • Basic Mass Spectroscopy
    http://chipo.chem.uic.edu/web1/ocol/spec/MS.htm
    Presents general information on Mass Spectroscopy. The mass spectra of 5 sample compounds are included. Taken from Organic Chemistry Online. Copyright by Paul R. Young, 1996.
  • Eight Peak Index of Mass Spectra
    QC 454 .M3 M36 1983 Science Reference
    This e-volume set lists the eight most abundant ions in 66,720 mass spectra, indexed by molecular weight, elemental composition and most abundant ions.
  • Integrated Spectral Database System for Organic Compounds
    http://www.aist.go.jp/RIODB/SDBS/menu-e.html
    At present this site allows accessibility to the 13C NMR spectra of 9,700 compounds; accessibility to the 1H NMR spectra of 10,100 compounds; and partial accessibility to the mass spectra of 18,000 compounds. ESR spectra of 400 compounds, IR spectra of 47,000 compounds, and Raman spectra of 3,500 compounds are in the planning stages. Compounds can be searched by compound name, molecular formula, CAS registry number, and SDBS number (Spectral Data Base System number). Produced by the National Institute of Materials and Chemical Research (NIMC), Tsukuba, Ibaraki 305, Japan.
  • Introduction to Mass Spectrometry (MS)
    http://www.chem.vt.edu/chem-ed/crossref/ac-mass-spec.html
    Discusses the process and the instruments used in mass spectrometry. Diagrams of the instruments are included. Taken from the Encyclopedia Analytical Instrumentation.
  • JICST Mass Spectral Database
    http://fact.jst.go.jp/fdb/MS/
    Provides mass spectra on a variety of compounds.
  • NIST Chemistry WebBook
    http://webbook.nist.gov/chemistry/
    Allows chemical species searching by formula, name, partial formula, CAS registry number, molecular weight, ionization energy, and proton affinity. Provides IR spectra and mass spectra graphs, among other chemical properties. This site is produced by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). WebBook program written by P.J. Linstrom. W.G. Mallard, General Editor. (NIST Standard Reference Database No. 69). Released February, 1997.
  • Organic Compounds Database
    http://www.colby.edu/chemistry/cmp/cmp.html
    Organic compounds can be searched by melting point, boiling point, refraction, formula, functional groups and characteristic peaks of UV or MS spectra. Compiled by Harold M. Bell, Virginia Tech.
  • Wiley/NBS Registry of Mass Spectral Data
    QC 454 .M3 .M395 1989 Science Reference
    This 7-volume set represents a combination of the Registry of Mass Spectral Data by Einar Stenhagen, Sixten Abrahamsson, and Fred W. McLafferty and EPA/NIH Mass Spectral Data Base by S.R. Heller and G.W.A. Milne and its two supplements. This work contains the mass spectral data for 112,272 different compounds. The compounds are arranged by nominal molecular weight. Within each weight the compounds are sorted according to their elemental composition in decreasing order of carbon and hydrogen followed by increasing numbers of other elements in alphabetical order. The compound name index, elemental composition index, and the CAS registry number index are contained within Vol. 7.

Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR, 13C-NMR)

  • Integrated Spectral Database System for Organic Compounds
    http://www.aist.go.jp/RIODB/SDBS/menu-e.html
    At present this site allows accessibility to the 13C NMR spectra of 9,700 compounds; accessibility to the 1H NMR spectra of 10,100 compounds; and partial accessibility to the mass spectra of 18,000 compounds. ESR spectra of 400 compounds, IR spectra of 47,000 compounds, and Raman spectra of 3,500 compounds are in the planning stages. Compounds can be searched by compound name, molecular formula, CAS registry number, and SDBS number (Spectral Data Base System number). Produced by the National Institute of Materials and Chemical Research (NIMC), Tsukuba, Ibaraki 305, Japan.
  • Knovel HNMR Spectra
     http://www.asu.edu/lib/resources/db/knovel.htm
    (Available via Knovel - select "Chemistry & Chemical Engineering", then "Analytical Chemistry")
    Searching by structure and molecular formula for approximately 154,000 physiologically active organic compounds.
  • Aldrich Library of C13 and H1 FT-NMR Spectra
    QC 462.85 .A44 1993 Science Reserve
    Combined proton and C13 spectra for a compound are arranged by functional group, and by increasing complexity within a functional group. With this arrangement it is possible to make comparisons for either proton or C13 spectra within a functional group, or to make a comparison of a proton to C13 spectrum for a given compound.
  • Sigma Aldrich
    http://www.sigmaaldrich.com
    Offers FT-NMR and FT-IR Raman spectra for many chemicals via their online catalog. Spectra are downloadable as PDF files. The catalog is searchable by name, CAS number, etc.
  • Aldrich Library of NMR Spectra.
    QD 96 .N8 P68 1983 Science Reserve
    Over 37,000 NMR spectra of organic compounds.
  • Atlas of Carbon-13 NMR Data
    QC 462 .C1 B73 1979 Science Reference
    This 2 volume collection of carbon-13 chemical shift data presents the following information for each compound listed. The data consists of the name, structural formula, molecular formula, molecular weight, solvent, original standard, pH Value (when available), temperature, carbon-13 chemical shifts, assignments of shifts to carbons, multiplicities of signals, original literature, and chemical class code. The index volume is arranged in the following ways. They are an alphabetical listing of the compounds, a chemical class index, a molecular formula index, a molecular weight index, and a chemical shift index.
  • C13-NMR Data for Organometallic Compounds
    QD 411 .M365 1981 Science Reference
    Offers a variety of tables as well as reference to the journal literature.
  • Basic MR Principles
    http://www.t2star.com/basic_mr/Basic.html
    Discusses the principles of NMR. Colored diagrams and animation are used to enhance the explanation.
    Written by William Faulkner. Copyright October, 1996.
  • Basics of NMR
    http://www.cis.rit.edu/htbooks/nmr/nmr-main.htm
    Dr. Joseph Hornak of the Rochester Institute of Technology presents this high quality hypertextbook for in-depth coverage of the physics and technique behind Nuclear Magnetic Resonance. Areas covered by this site include but are not limited to The Mathematics of NMR, Spin Physics, NMR Spectroscopy, Fourier Transforms, and Pulse Sequences.
  • Basic Proton and C-13 NMR
    http://chipo.chem.uic.edu/web1/ocol/spec/NMR.htm
    Presents general information on Nuclear Magnetic Resonance. The H1 spectra of 10 sample compounds and the C13 spectra of 5 sample compounds are included. Taken from Organic Chemistry Online. Copyright by Paul R. Young.
  • NMR Data of Known Compounds
    http://science.widener.edu/svb/nmr/known_gif.html
    Provides NMR spectra on a variety of compounds; some spectra may include impurities.
  • NMR Spectroscopy: Principles and Applications
    http://www.ch.ic.ac.uk/local/organic/nmr.html
    Discusses the principles and applications of NMR in a series of 6 second year lectures given at Imperial College by Henry Rzepa.
  • NMRShiftDB
    http://www.nmrshiftdb.org
    Search for (sub-)spectra, (sub-)structures and other properties (chemical names, measurement conditions, keywords), and last but not least 13C spectrum prediction based on the database content. Currently the database contains over 13,000 structures.

Raman

  • Introduction to Raman Spectroscopy
    http://www.chem.vt.edu/chem-ed/spec/vib/raman.html
    Provides a brief explanation of Raman Spectroscopy from the Encyclopedia Analytical Instrumentation..
    Copyright by Brain M. Tissue, 1996. Produced by Science Hypermedia, Inc., 1400 Gladewood Drive, Blacksburg, VA 24060-2666.

  • Raman/Infrared Atlas of Organic Compounds.
    2nd ed. Weinheim, Germany: VCH, 1989.
    QD 272 .S6 .S3713 1989 Science Reserve
    Raman and infrared spectra give complementary images of molecular vibrations. Therefore, the combined evaluation of both spectra yields more information about molecular structures tha both kinds of spectra combined.

  • Raman Spectra of Carbohydrates
    http://newton.foodsci.kvl.dk/users/engelsen/specarb/specarb.html
    Raman spectra of a few carbohydrates. Monosaccharides, disaccharides, etc. are included. Spectra provided by Soren Balling Engelsen. Please note: As of this writing there are very few spectra of carbohydrates presented.
  • What is Raman Spectroscopy

    Offers a number of web pages dealing with Raman Spectroscopy. Among them is an explanation along with an excellent colored diagram, a CE-Raman schematic, and much more.
    Written and compiled by Jeremy M. Shaver.

Ultraviolet (UV)

  • Absorption Spectra in the Ultraviolet and Visible Regions. 19 vols. New York: Academic Press, 1961.
    QC 437 .A19 Science Reference

    Author, subject, and formula indexes. Diagrams and tables are included.

  • Drug Data Base with UV Data

    Provides physical data, structure, UV spectra, and classification of the biological effect of each drug listed.
    Produced by Jinno Laboratory, School of Materials Science, Toyohashi, University of Technology, Toyohashi 441, Japan.

  • Organic Compounds Database

    Organic compounds can be searched by melting point, boiling point, refraction, formula, functional groups and characteristic peaks of UV or MS spectra.
    Compiled by Harold M. Bell, Virginia Tech.

  • Organic Electronic Spectral Data. 30 vols. New York: Wiley, 1946/52- . Annual.
    QC 437 .O65 Science Reference

    Depicts ultraviolet-visible spectra of organic compounds. Compounds are arranged by molecular formula. Molecular formulas are the only access to the spectral data. There is no other index for the chemical compounds. In addition there is a seven year lag period for this publication; the 1986 volume was published and has a copyright of 1992.

  • NIST Chemistry Web Book
    http://webbook.nist.gov/chemistry/
    Offers IR, Mass, and UV spectra for a wide variety of organic compounds.

  • UV-VIS Atlas of Organic Compounds. 2nd ed. 2 vols. Weinheim, Germany: VCH, 1992.
    QC 462.85 P47 1992 Science Reserve

    Volume 1: Spectra A 1/1 - C9/13
    Volume 2: Spectra D 1/1 - M19
