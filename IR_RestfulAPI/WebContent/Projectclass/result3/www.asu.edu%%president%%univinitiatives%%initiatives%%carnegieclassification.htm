Carnegie Community Engagement Classification

The Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching has selected ASU for its new “Community Engagement” classification, recognizing the breadth and strength of the commitment ASU has had to the community for decades. ASU is one of 76 universities across the nation to receive the new Community Engagement classification.

 

The classification criterion is divided into two categories. The first, Curricular Engagement, describes university teaching, learning and scholarship which engage faculty, students and community in mutually beneficial and respectful collaboration. The second category, Outreach and Partnerships, entails university approaches to community engagement. This includes the application of institutional resources for the benefit of the community, as well as a university’s collaborative interactions with community through mutually beneficial exchanges. ASU received classification status in both categories

U.S. News and World Report has announced that they will use the community engagement classification as part of their criteria for ranking colleges nationwide. Through the Carnegie classification and its implementation into the U.S. News and World Report rankings, ASU has the opportunity to showcase its outstanding community partnerships and garner national attention for its commitment to social embeddedness and societal transformation.

 

Unlike the Carnegie Foundation's other classifications that rely on national data, the Community Engagement Classification is an "elective" classification. Institutions elected to participate by submitting required documentation describing the nature and extent of their engagement with the community. Additional information on the documentation process that ASU completed can be found here.

 


Relation to Design Imperatives

Design Imperative #2 Societal Transformation

 

ASU recognizes the positive impact the institution can have on the communities of Arizona through research that enhances economic development, encourages cultural advancement, and promotes social change. The Carnegie Community Engagement Classification will help advance the work of bringing about positive change in Arizona communities by providing national recognition to ASU’s exemplary community engagement programs.

 

Design Imperative #4: Use-Inspired Research

It is the mission of ASU to integrate the advancement of knowledge with the transformation of society. The Carnegie Community Engagement Classification further guides ASU’s research mission to create solutions for issues that effect people on the local, state and global level.

Design Imperative # 7: Social Embeddedness

It is the mission of ASU to dissolve the borders that separate the needs and input of the public from the insights and resources at the university through the creation of mutually-beneficially projects that take into account the strengths that both the community and the university have to offer. The Carnegie Classification will help ASU garner more support locally and nationally for these efforts, leading to an increase in the quality and quantity of ASU engagement programs.

 

Design Imperative #8: Global Engagement

A strong community is a crucial part of ensuring the vitality and impact of any institution like ASU. ASU is committed to the people and communities that surround its campuses, and to improving the conditions of people around the world. Through this classification, ASU students, faculty and staff can see that the projects they undertake in leading to positive change within Arizona are gaining national recognition.

The Documentation Process
ASU was invited to participate in the first round of classifications by providing concrete documentation of partnerships, initiatives and projects that create mutually-beneficial relationships with community partners at the local, state and global level.

 

The first section of the documentation framework measured the institutional identity and culture of community engagement at ASU, and the university’s commitment to engagement activities. In the second stage of documentation, ASU was required to provide data, examples and descriptions of curricular engagement activities, in addition to outreach and partnerships efforts with the community outside the university. The documentation portion of the community engagement classification was completed September 1, 2006, after which a national advisory panel made up of education experts and Carnegie research staff reviewed the submissions and made final decisions on whether to classify ASU and other institutions as community engagement universities.


While the Office of University Initiatives coordinated the documentation process at ASU, the process itself involved faculty and staff from across all campuses submitting detailed information regarding ASU’s community engagement efforts.
