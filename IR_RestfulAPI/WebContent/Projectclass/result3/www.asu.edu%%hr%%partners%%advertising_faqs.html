STAFFING SERVICES: ADVERTISING

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What are my options for advertsing; i.e., what type of ads?
    • The most frequently used advertising is print or online. This is done as a single-column ad (short ad or long ad) or a display ad in print. When available, departments may participate in the display group ad.

When advertising online, the advertisement contains the entire wording from the Vacancy Notice. This may be done as a short ad or a long ad.

  • What is contained in a short ad?
    • Normally, a short ad includes one or two lines describing the position, the ASU logo, job title, department name, close date, and SR# with referral to the website. To view a sample, go to Example of Short Ad.
  • What is contained in a long ad?
    • Normally, the long ad uses all or almost all of the wording on the Vacancy Notice. Use this ad to advertise the Minimum Qualifications and Desired Qualifications rather than referring candidates directly to the ASU website. This is significantly more expensive than a short ad.
  • What is contained in a display ad?
    • Using either the short or long ad format, display ads can be multi-column, usually surrounded by a black box.
  • What is a group ad?
    • To maximize advertising dollars, departments can advertise in the Arizona Republic group ad, which is a display ad in color with 4 or more ASU jobs listed.
  • What is the cost of an ad in the AZ Republic?
    • A short ad can run $250-350, depending upon length; a long ad can run $600 and up. A display ad in black and white is usually 3 times the amount of a single-column ad. The group ad (display and in color) insertion will cost $550 and up.
  • Is there a sample of the different ads I can preview?
    • Yes :
    • Example of a Short Ad
  • What is the advantage of advertising online?
    • Immediacy and cost. The job goes "live" on most websites immediately; on other sites, within 24 hours. The job can be posted for 30-90 days at a minimal cost.
  • What is Super Sunday?
    • Three times a year - January, June, and September - the AZ Republic produces their largest classified sections, which has a significantly increased readership.
  • What are some publications to use to target minority communities?
    • Locally - the Arizona Informant to the African-American community; LaVoz (Spanish & English) and Prensa Hispana (Spanish only) to the Hispanic community; and the Asian Times to the Asian community. Nationally - Hispanic Outlook. For more information on minority publications, see our List of Publications by Region and Diversity
  • What is the deadline to get an ad into the following Sunday's paper?
    • Ad copy must be sent to Staffing Services by 12 noon, the previous Wednesday. For more information on ad deadlines, see: Deadlines & Cost Information for Frequently Used Publications.
  • What is the deadline to get an ad into the local minority papers?
    • This varies by publication. For more information, see: Deadlines & Cost Information for Frequently Used Publications.
  • How do I pay for the ad?
    • Departments will be billed directly by the publication or website.
  • Do I have to write the ad copy?
    • Departments may collaborate with a Staffing Services Specialist to develop the ad copy; or departments have the option of writing the ad copy and having it approved by the Affirmative Action office when submiting a Staff Request.
  • How do I get an ad approved by the Affirmative Action office if I haven't submitted the ad copy with/on the SR?
    • A Staffing Services Specialist will obtain the approval.
