ASU West Bob Stump Endowed Chair commemorates congressman

When former Congressman Bob Stump passed away June 20, Arizona and the United States lost a great public servant who stood for honesty, patriotism, hard work, common sense and loyalty.

To honor Congressman Stump's legacy of service, an endowment fund has been established at ASU West that will preserve his commitment to Arizona, for future generations. The Bob Stump Endowed Chair in Cultural History will enable the campus to expand its ongoing efforts to document the rich culture of the Southwest - the place where Stump lived and served.

Contributions to fund the endowment have reached $330,000. When fully funded at $1 million, the endowment will enable ASU West to recruit a prominent historian of America's regional cultures as a member of ASU West's faculty. This individual will help bring focus, breadth and recognition to ASU West's ongoing regional and oral history projects, many of which document the history of the West Valley.

"Bob Stump leaves an unparalleled legacy of integrity, honesty and service to the citizens of Arizona," says Charles Thompson, manager of community relations for Arizona Public Service Co. and chairman of ASU West's Bob Stump Endowed Chair Committee. "Establishing the Stump Chair at ASU West will honor that legacy."

Stump graduated from Tolleson Union High School, and in 1951 he received his bachelor's degree from ASU. He farmed cotton in Tolleson before launching his career in public service. Beginning with his first election to the Arizona Legislature in 1958, Stump served in both houses of the Arizona Legislature, before moving to the U.S. Congress. His quarter-century of service in the U.S. House of Representatives culminated in chairmanship of the House Armed Services Committee. He retired from Congress in January.

In 2002 he received an honorary doctoral degree at Main Campus. A Phoenix native, Stump also was a U.S. Navy World War II combat veteran.

Throughout his career, he maintained an involvement with ASU and was a strong supporter of improving public education.

"Because Bob Stump made such a tremendous impact on the development of the Phoenix metropolitan area, the state of Arizona, and our country, it is especially appropriate for an academic position that will focus on documenting the region's rich cultural heritage to bear his name," says Emily Fourmy Cutrer, dean of the ASU West College of Arts & Sciences, where the recipient of the Stump chair will be housed.

"ASU West is honored that we will have an endowed chair in Bob's name. The Bob Stump Endowed Chair in Cultural History will be a living memorial to Bob's accomplishments," says ASU West Provost Elaine Maimon.

For information regarding contributions to ASU West's Bob Stump Endowed Chair in Cultural History, call (602) 543-5315.
