June 1, 2006

Dear Colleagues,

On behalf of the Office of the Executive Vice President and University Provost, we are pleased to invite you to the 2006 Orientation for New Faculty and Academic Professionals.  This week-long orientation will provide you an introduction to the university at large and to your home campus in particular.

The high point of the week will be the All-University Orientation for new faculty and academic professionals. At this orientation, President Michael Crow, University Provost Elizabeth Capaldi and other faculty and administrators will provide information about short and long-term plans including new facilities, new schools and programs, and new local and global initiatives. This orientation will be held from 11:30 to 4:30 at the Stone House Pavilion at the Phoenix Zoo on Wednesday, August 16, 2006.

The day will conclude with an opening reception from 4:30-6:00 hosted by the campus and university provosts. You and a guest are cordially invited to attend. The dress is casual (as it is for the orientation), the setting is informal, and there is no program.  Even if you are unable to participate in the All-University Orientation, we hope you will attend this welcoming reception.  Administrators of the academic units represented by the new faculty and academic professionals have also been invited.

In addition to the All-University Orientation, each campus is holding an orientation, particularly designed for faculty members and academic professionals who will be working on that campus.  These campus orientations will be held on Thursday, August 17, 2006.  As programming is finalized for these orientations, you will receive information from the particular campus orientation coordinator. However, the following general information is available:

  • ASU’s Tempe campus orientation will be held in the College of Design Room AED 60 and Red Square. This program, scheduled for 8:00-12:00, will begin with a continental breakfast during which time faculty and academic professionals will learn about a variety of resources that are available to help them succeed.  The program will focus on the campus culture, especially relating to student life and resources for research and creative activity. 

    Also on the Tempe campus to learn about your benefits and retirement programs, the Office of Human Resources will hold a benefits orientation during the afternoon in the Architecture & Environmental Design (ARCH) South Building for new or rehired employees from 1:00 p.m. to 4 p.m. in ARCH Room 234.

    If you are unable to attend this benefits orientation, call (480) 965-4751 to schedule another time. State and federal legislation give you only 30-31 days from your hire date to enroll in the benefits and retirement programs, so you are encouraged to attend one of these sessions.

    If you are unable to attend an orientation, please contact the HR Employee Service Center at (480) 965-2701 to enroll before the end of your enrollment period. A New Employee Benefits Packet and New Employee Retirement Packet are available on the Benefits website www.asu.edu/hr/benefits.
     

  • The orientation for the West campus will be held in the Kiva Lecture Hall.  The West campus program will include a number of short presentations and interactive sessions covering such topics as: Research/Creative Activity Life at West; new residence life; and engagement in learning communities. Workshops for new faculty members at the West campus will be conducted by members of Fletcher Library, Faculty Development and Information Technology. Topics will cover research strategies, instructional strategies, and information technology

    Human Resources at the West campus will be holding their orientation on Monday, August 14, 2006 from 8:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. in the Kiva Lecture Hall.  Contact Leslie Thomas-Dewar in Human Resources with questions at (602) 543-8406 or ltdewar@asu.edu.

  • ASU’s Downtown Phoenix campus orientation will be held in the ASU Phoenix Urban Research Laboratory (234 N. Central, Phoenix), 9th floor.  The half-day program will begin at 8:30 a.m. with a continental breakfast.  The orientation program includes a Downtown Phoenix campus tour and the opportunity for new faculty members to meet in a small, informal setting as well as to become familiar with the resources and services available on ASU’s newest campus.
     
  • ASU’s Polytechnic campus orientation will be held in the Cooley Ballroom at the Student Union.  The half-day program will begin at 9:30 a.m. and will conclude at around 1:30 p.m. A new faculty orientation fair and lunch will begin around noon.  The orientation program provides an opportunity for new faculty members to meet one another in an informal setting and become familiar with the resources and services available on campus.

Information regarding agendas for the orientations, maps, and driving directions will be posted to this site. You may also RSVP to the All-University and campus orientations as well as the Provosts’ Reception on this site.  Please consult this website through the next few months for further updates. 

The third component of the 2006 Orientation Week is a university-wide focus on teaching called Teaching@ASU.  Coordinated by ASU’s Center for Learning and Teaching Excellence, with participation from a range of university offices, this focus will take the form of daylong workshops on a variety of teaching subjects, for example, the multicultural classroom, construction of a course syllabus, co-curricular activities, Curriculum of the New American University, etc.  Faculty members may attend selected workshops, or may attend for the entire day. 

Teaching@ASU will take different forms on the different campuses; workshops are scheduled for the Tempe campus on Monday and Tuesday, August 14-15.  To register, see http://clte.asu.edu/workshop/index.htm#Teachday. 

Workshops on the West campus will be held August 14. To register, go to www.west.asu.edu/acadaffairs/facdev/workshops.cfm.  

In addition to these opportunities, the Office for Research and Sponsored Projects Administration offers research administration workshops throughout the semester on topics such as The Fundamentals of Sponsored Projects Administration for the Investigator, Locating Funding, and more.  Find additional research administration training opportunities at http://researchadmin.asu.edu/training/fac_course.cfm.

If you have any questions regarding these events, please do not hesitate to get in touch with any of us. 

We look forward to meeting you in person on August 16.

Sincerely yours,
