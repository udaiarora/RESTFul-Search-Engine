June 15, 2005

McCarthy assumes new role with ASU Foundation

ASU President Michael Crow has announced that Diane McCarthy, president of the ASU Alumni Association, will assume a new role as vice president for university and community partnerships in the ASU Foundation.

Christine Wilkinson, senior vice president and secretary of the university, will assume responsibility for the Alumni Association in addition to her current duties.

In this new capacity, McCarthy will serve as a liaison between the university and the foundation and will assist in the design and implementation of projects intended to enhance public and private investment in ASU. Since several of these initiatives are under way and will benefit from her support, Crow has asked McCarthy to begin the new assignment immediately.

“Arizona State University and the ASU Foundation are undertaking numerous activities and projects that are intended to attract new and sizable investments from municipalities, the state and private investors,” Crow says. “Given the complexity and scope of these endeavors, I believe it is necessary for ASU to dedicate the talents of its best personnel to their design and implementation. Diane’s experience as a state legislator, president of WestMarc and president of the ASU Alumni Association has provided her with a valuable skill set that makes her uniquely suited to advance many of our activities and projects.”

“I am thrilled that Diane will be part of the ASU Foundation senior team,” said Ira Jackson, president and chief executive officer of the ASU Foundation. “Her relationships with many of our civic leaders will add great value to our organization.”

“This is a critical time in our efforts to secure financial support for the university from municipalities, the state of Arizona and private donors,” says McCarthy, who joined the university as president of the Alumni Association in 2003. “I look forward to working with President Crow and the ASU Foundation in these important endeavors. I am pleased to be able to serve ASU in this new role.”

During the past two years, McCarthy has positioned the ASU Alumni Association to increase its membership, as well as alumni involvement in the university. In particular, McCarthy helped to reinvigorate and greatly expand the alumni advocacy program, which was instrumental in securing additional support from the Arizona Legislature this year. Crow has asked Wilkinson to continue in these efforts.

“As a senior member of the ASU administration and a proud ASU alumna, I am confident that Christine will bring tremendous experience and affection for ASU to these duties,” Crow says.

Wilkinson says she is looking forward to her new duties in leading the association and in broadening its reach to ASU’s 280,000 alumni.

“Under Diane’s leadership, the Alumni Association has made steadfast strides toward increasing alumni involvement,” Wilkinson says. “The future direction of the university provides significant opportunities to engage alumni in supporting them and advancing the university. We will continue to achieve this goal.”

Holeman, with the Alumni Association, can be reached at (480) 965-8150 or (jholeman@asu.edu).
