March 14, 2003

Cole to address race relations for A. Wade Smith Lecture

Nationally acclaimed higher education leader Johnetta B. Cole will address "Affirmative Action: America's Ongoing Struggle Against Inequality," as the guest speaker of the 2003 A. Wade Smith Memorial Lecture on Race Relations.

Cole, who is president of Bennett College in Greensboro, N.C, will speak at 7 p.m., March 27, in the Evelyn Smith Music Theatre on ASU Main. ASU's College of Liberal Arts & Sciences (CLAS) is sponsoring the free community event, but tickets are required for attendance, and are available through all ASU bookstores or by calling (480) 965-0051.

Cole made history in 1987 when she became the first African-American woman to serve as president of Spelman College in Atlanta. At her inauguration, Bill and Camille Cosby made a $20 million donation - the largest single gift from individuals to any historically black college or university at that time.

Under her direction, Spelman became the first black college or university to receive a top rating by U.S. News & World Report as the South's preeminent liberal arts college.

Throughout her celebrated 37-year career in higher education teaching and administration, Cole has earned several honors, including the Presidential Distinguished Professor of Anthropology, Women Studies and African American Studies at Emory University in Atlanta, from which she retired in 2001. Her retirement was short-lived however, because a year later she was hired to head Bennett College.

Cole joins a long list of distinguished A. Wade Smith lecturers committed to scholarly discussions about improving race relations.

The lecture series was created in 1995 to perpetuate the work of ASU sociology professor and department chairman A. Wade Smith, who worked tirelessly to improve race relations on the university campus and within the greater Phoenix metro area. When Smith succumbed to cancer in 1994 at the age of 43, his wife, Elsie, and other family members and friends made memorial gifts to establish and fund the lecture series.

"I think most people who have attended this lecture will agree that it is one of the best offered at ASU, because it brings people together in a way that creates a new level of understanding unparalleled on campus," said Jill DeMichele, development officer with CLAS.

The A. Wade Smith Memorial Lecture is supported by CLAS, 12 campus departments, 35 individual donors and two corporate donors, APS and Casino Arizona.
For information on the lecture, call CLAS Events at (480) 965-0051 or email (clasevents@asu.edu).
