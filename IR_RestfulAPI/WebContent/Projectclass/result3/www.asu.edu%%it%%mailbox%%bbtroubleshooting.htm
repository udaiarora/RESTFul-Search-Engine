BlackBerry Documentation

This page contains answers to message related, device related, and common troubleshooting questions/problems/procedures.


Common Procedures

  • How to perform a SOFT RESET:
    From the handheld, press the ALT + CAP + DELETE keys at the same time. You will not lose any data in this process.
  • How to perform a HARD RESET:
    From the handheld, locate the RESET button on the back of the device near the bottom. If you do not see the reset button, check under the battery cover. Look for a small hole with a label that says RESET. Using a pin or paper clip, press the RESET button. The device will restart. You will not lose any data in this process.
  • How to REGISTER on the wireless network:
    Go to "options." Select "network." Press the "trackwheel." Select "register now."
  • How to send yourself a new SERVICE BOOK:
    Choose Options icon (wrench). Choose SERVICE BOOK -- click trackwheel. Choose Perform a HARD RESET or a SOFT RESET on the Blackberry. Wait for device to restart, then make sure that your wireless signal shows GPRS. Check your messages to see if you have received the new Service Book. Test your services by sending yourself an e-mail message.

Device Related Problems

  1. Q: The Blackberry screen is frozen.
    A: Try two things: First, perform a SOFT RESET. If that does not work perform a HARD RESET.
  2. Q: The Blackberry does not send and/or receive new email messages.
    A: To fix this problem, try the following in order:
    • Perform a SOFT RESET.
    • Perform a HARD RESET.
    • Check wireless antenna signal strength, make sure antenna shows GPRS (all caps).
    • See if you can still make phone calls - this will verify that GSM services are available.
    • See if you can still browse the web - this will verify that GPRS services are available.
    • Send yourself an email message to the handheld, see if it arrives.
    • Check to see when your most recent bill was paid
    • Go to the Managing your Blackberry web page to log into the Blackberry provider eMail web client.
    • Send yourself a new Service Book.
    • On handheld device, check to see if you received the new Service Book under OPTIONS - SERVICE BOOK.
    • Go to the Managing your Blackberry web page to call your Blackberry provider for assistance.
  3. Q: I cannot browse the web with my Blackberry.
    A: To fix this problem, try the following in order:
    • Make sure your antenna signal shows GPRS (all caps).
    • Send an email message to your handheld, see if it arrives - this will verify whether you have data services.
    • Try to browse wap.yahoo.com or www.google.com.
    • Use the instructions above for cannot send/receive messages.
  4. Q: I am traveling to another country and want to make sure that I have service.
    A: To make sure that you will have service follow these few steps:
    • Go to "Managing your Blackberry."
    • See the section on activating international roaming service.
    • See if phone and data services are available at your destination country.
    • Contact your provider to activate international roaming.
