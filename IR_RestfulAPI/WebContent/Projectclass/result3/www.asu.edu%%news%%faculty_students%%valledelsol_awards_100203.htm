October 2, 2003

Duarte, Molina earn honors from Hispanic group

Hispanics at ASU are achieving success in a variety of ways. Stella Pope Duarte and John Molina, M. D., are just two of the 10 individuals who were honored at Valle del Sol's 13th Annual Profiles of Success Hispanic Leadership Awards Luncheon, Sept. 5.

Valle del Sol, a local non-profit community-based organization serving the Valley for more than 33 years, presented Duarte with the Latino Excellence in Arts/Culture Award and Molina with the Rosa Carrillo Torres Humanitarian Award.

Duarte was born and raised in Phoenix and began her literary career in 1995 after she had a dream in which her departed father told her that her destiny was to become a writer. A year later, Duarte signed her first book contract with Bilingual Review Press for her initial collection of short stories, "Fragile Night." For this collection, she won a creative writing fellowship from the Arizona Commission on the Arts and was named a candidate for the prestigious Pen West Fiction Award.

Duarte, who has been an adjunct professor of education for more than 10 years at ASU, says writing has been her creative outlet of expression and communication about the Hispanic culture and people. Building awareness of the Hispanic population is something she is passionate about.

"There is a huge voice out there, which is the Hispanic voice, and they are taking their position in leadership roles and becoming the movers and shakers," Duarte says. "Now the media has to look at them with a different eye, not just the tragic stories or stories about their failures."

In 2001, Duarte was awarded a second creative writing fellowship for her recent novel, "Let Their Spirits Dance." The novel portrays a Chicano family from Phoenix who journeys to the Vietnam War Memorial in Washington, D.C., to fulfill the mother's promise to touch her son's name on the memorial wall before she departs this life. For her work, Duarte won first place in the 17th Annual Arizona Author's Literary contest. She also won second place in the River City Writing Awards from the University of Memphis and has been short-listed in the Fish Anthology in Ireland. This year, Duarte will receive the Fiction Award for her book by Arizona Highways.

Duarte earned an associate degree from Phoenix College and a bachelor's degree in education and masters in educational counseling from ASU.

Molina is a physician and founder of Las Fuentes, a health care clinic in Guadalupe, where he grew up. He earned a bachelor's degree in psychology from ASU, a medical degree from the University of Arizona, did his residency in OB/GYN and is currently a third-year law student at ASU's College of Law. Achieving his dreams is something Molina has managed to do with the help of his community.

"What has and continues to motivate me are the lives of the people I see around me," he says. "My mission to complete medical school was triggered in the 80s, when I saw the suffering of the people in my community, who were seeking health care they could afford. Community support is what moved me through this whole process."

Through ASU's College of Law, Molina says he has gained a deeper appreciation for the diversity of the law program.

"It's amazing to hear different perspectives about the law," says Molina, who is part Native American and Mexican American.

Molina, who plans to receive his law degree in December 2004, says he would like to work as a consultant for non-profit health care clinics, developing programs and working with risk management. In addition, Molina has one more dream he would like to pursue.

"I've decided to run for governor in 2006," says Molina, who plans to begin campaigning after he finishes law school. "The position of governor is such an important position that affects the quality of life for people and the state. I figure learning about the law is the first step to achieving this."

To learn more about Valle del Sol and this year's Profiles of Success winners, visit the Web at (http://www.valledelsol.com).
