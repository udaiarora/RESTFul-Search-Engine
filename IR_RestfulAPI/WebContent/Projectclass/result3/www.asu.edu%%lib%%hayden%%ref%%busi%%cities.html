Cities

INTRODUCTION

This Subject Guide will provide information on cities. While the primary focus is on cities in the United States, cities in other parts of the world are included. For those who are trying to relocate a family or business; to find a job or vacation spot; or to choose a city for a target market, conference site, or research, this guide will help you locate detailed information on cities. Some types of information provided will be on the quality of life, demographics, politics and government, economic conditions, housing, employment and income, climate, education, and crime. This is not intended for those involved in research in urban planning.

GUIDES

  1. Cities of the United States. Detroit, MI: Gale Research, 1988- . (ASU owns 1988- . Latest Edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals.)
    HT 123 .C57x HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HT 123 .C57x WEST REFERENCE


    The subtitle is "A Compilation of Current Information on Economic, Cultural, Geographic, and Social Conditions." It is just that, and very thorough, too. It includes a map, statistics, and addresses and phone numbers for additional information for each city; and a map and brief overview for each state.

  2. Cities of the World. 4th ed. Detroit, MI: Gale Research, 1993.
    G 153.4 .C57 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    In this work, cities are grouped by countries within continents, so knowledge of geography is critical. For example, most of the cities of the Middle East are in countries that are considered part of Asia. The information in these encyclopedic style entries is predominantly cultural and political in nature. Country profiles and maps are also included, but there are no statistical tables. The majority of the information provided in this work is from the U.S. Department of State Post Reports, which means that, while useful, it is best used as background information for the general living conditions.

  3. City Profiles USA. Detroit, MI : Omnigraphics, 1996- .
    E 158 .C58x HAYDEN REFERENCE
    E 158 .C58x WEST REFERENCE

    Each entry has the basic geographic facts about the city, its weather, information sources, transportation, lodging and food, shopping centers, banks, media (radio, television, and newspapers and magazines), educational institutions, and major attractions and events.

  4. Moving and Relocation Sourcebook. Detroit, MI: Omnigraphics, 1992.
    HT 334 .U5 M5 1992 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    Each of the 100 metropolitan areas covered has a short statistical summary and all the critical facts and contacts to make relocation simpler. Each metropolitan area is further divided into its sections/cities/ suburbs/counties. Separate profiles of each section are included.

  5. Site Selection. Atlanta, GA: Conway Data, 1882- . (ASU owns 1920- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HD 58 .I48x HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HD 58 .I48x HAYDEN JOURNALS (1985- )
    HD 58 .I48x FILM HAYDEN MF (1985- )
    HC 107 .A13 I33 HAYDEN JOURNALS (1950-1973)
    HC 107 .A13 I33 FILM HAYDEN MF (1920-1949,1974-1984)

    This trade journal is designed for the planning executives of corporations. It has articles, but its value is in the special index found in each issue, the "Free Area Data" section, and the special geographic section. The special indexes are worldwide in scope and cover such items as quality of life, economic development groups, and business/research parks. The "Free Area Data" is a list of agencies and businesses that will provide free detailed local/regional information that is useful for business site selections. The geographic sections can be anything from a detailed look at locating in the area to almost pure advertising/promotions for the area. This journal has changed titles a number of times. The call numbers provided cover all the various title changes.

  6. Kurian, George. World Encyclopedia of Cities. Santa Barbara, CA: ABC- CLIO, 1993-.
    HT 108.5 .K87 1993 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HT 108.5 .K87 1993 WEST REFERENCE

    Only the first two volumes, which cover the United States and Canada, have been published. The arrangement is alphabetical by city within each country. The information provided is grouped by "Basic Data," "Location and Topography," "Layout of City and Suburbs," "Environment," "Climate," "Weather," "History," "Chronology," "Historical Landmarks," "Population," "Ethnic Composition," "Government," "Public Finance," "Economy," "Labor," "Education," "Health," "Transportation," "Housing," "Urban Development," "Crime," "Religion," "Media," "Sports," "Arts, Culture, and Tourism," "Parks and Recreation," "Sources of Further Information," "Additional Reading," and a map. The information can cover as few as five pages to over fifteen pages. The preface provides the source used for information in each section. This work provides a balanced picture of the cities included.

  7. Carpenter, Allan. Facts About the Cities. New York: H. W. Wilson, 1992.
    HT 123 .C385 1992 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    If you want short, concise information, this is it. Each entry is just over a page long with statistical data about the crime, education, government, health, etc. of the city. A brief paragraph with a little information about the city is included. Major cities within U.S. commonwealths or protectorates are included. A bibliography of the source documents is included.

TRAVEL GUIDES

Travel guides are issued by many travel clubs and groups and available as part of the membership. For those who are not members, select Fodor and Mobil guides. Since the exact titles and call numbers vary, a list can be found by doing a TITLE search in the ASU Libraries Catalog database on the Online Catalog.

  1. Fodor's (Year) (Place: City, Country, or Region). New York: Fodor's Travel Publications, varies. Annual. (ASU owns (varies). Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    (call number varies) HAYDEN REFERENCE

    All the guides provide the same categories of information for cities; it is the level of detail that varies. The regional guides provide the least information and cover only the "major" cities. The least information provided is a brief description and history and a list of hotels. Most cities have additional sections on "Restaurants," "Shopping," and "Nightlife." More detailed sections on "When to Go," "How to Get There," "What to Take," "How to Get Around," "Tours," "Sports," "Museums and Sights," and "Special Events," are added for the most important or frequently visited cities. The guides for an individual country will include more of the smaller sized cities. Fodorþs publishes separate guides to the most important/visited cities of the world. These have a tremendous level of detail for the tourist, but that information can also be useful in deciding what areas you might want to live in, or even if you would want to live there at all!

  2. Mobil Travel Guide: (Region). New York: Prentice Hall Travel, varies. Annual. (ASU owns current year only.)
    (call number varies) HAYDEN REFERENCE

    Arranged by state within each region and then by city. The general data for the state is included in the beginning. For each city, a brief description, and lists of "What to See and Do," events, lodging by type, and restaurants. Road maps are included for each state, the larger metropolitan areas, and for airports. Special entries are included for major historic parks/monuments, recreational areas, and airports.

  3. South American Handbook. Bath, UK: Trade & Travel Publications, 1924- . Annual. (ASU owns 1927- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    F 1401 .S71 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This is one of the best travel guides. This provides more little details about the conditions and facts of life in South America than most guides. Arranged by country and then regions, there is an introduction, sections on climate, history, people, government, economy, communications, and music and dance. Imbedded in these written segments are a multitude of statistics. Under each region and city, more area specific information is found on these topics along with the usual tourism information. Additionally, such specifics as road conditions, apartments, film processing, and electronics repair are included. Every country, region and city has at least one map included with the entry. Since this is a British publication, watch out for some differences in terminology and spelling.

WEATHER & CLIMATE

Weather is an important consideration in locating or relocating a business or family. Most of the items listed here have very detailed information. The Weather Almanac listed below is the best quick summary of a local climate. A more comprehensive listing of weather and climate information on the web, use the Science Reference Room Weather Forecasts page.

  1. National Weather Service (U.S.)
    http://www.nws.noaa.gov/

    This is the official web site for the U.S. Weather. International weather information is also available here. It is packed full of weather information, so look carefully at the options. Below are some of the print editions held by ASU should you need more historical data than found on the web site.

      Climatological Data. (State). Asheville, NC: National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, Environmental Data and Information Service, National Climatic Center, varies. Monthly. (ASU ownership varies.)

      C 55.211 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
      ONLINE ACCESS

    HF 294 .W75 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HF 294 .W75 WEST REFERENCE

    E 154.5 .A12 C58x HAYDEN REFERENCE

To be thorough, use the keyword index in the back under the city name. The state name is included with the city name in the index to avoid confusion with other places with the same name. A great source for finding the name of cross reference style directories for cities.

RATINGS/RANKINGS/COMPARISONS

  1. America's Top-Rated Cities. Boca Raton, FL: Universal Reference Publications, 1990- . (ASU owns 1993- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals)
    HT 123 .A669 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This source takes its statistical data from surveys published in the business and city trade magazines and from government publications. There is a one page summary about the city and some analysis of both the business climate and the living environment along with the statistics. Evaluation comments have been included if they have been available. The sources for the statistics and the evaluation quotes are provided. The work is arranged by region and then by city.

  2. Willis, Alan. American Suburbs Rating Guide and Fact Book. Milpitas, CA: Toucan Valley Publications, 1993.
    HA 214 .W55 1993 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This book covers the 50 largest metropolitan areas as defined by the 1990 U.S. Census of Population. The data lists cities by their U.S. Census MSA or CMSA designation. Each metropolitan area has a page of brief information about the geography and demographics, a map, and a city listing in composite rank order. Each city is ranked by economics, affordable housing, crime, open spaces, education, commute, and community stability. Each metropolitan area has an alphabetic list of the cities with their ranking included in the entry. A master index to the cities is included in the back of the book.

  3. Canadian Markets. Toronto, Ont.: Maclean Hunter, 1980- . Annual. (ASU owns 1985- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HC 111 .B8 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This is a combination buying power index and demographic profile of the cities of Canada. The smallest cities included are municipalities with a population of at least 5,000. Economic regional maps are included for each of the provinces. The general arrangement is by broad sections: Buying Power Index, Retail Sales, Rankings of Income and Sales, Projections, Shopping Center Data, Municipalities, and the Canadian Economy. This has more detail that the similar works published in the U.S. that include a bit of Canada.

  4. Savageau, David, and Boyer, Rick. Places Rated Almanac: Your Guide to Finding the Best Places to Live in America. New York: Prentice Hall Travel, 1993.(ASU owns .Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Stacks.)
    HN 60 .S284 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HN 60 .S284 WEST REFERENCE

    Ranks places to live in the U.S. Areas are ranked according to climate, housing, health care and environment, recreation, the arts, and other important criteria needed when making decisions about relocation of a family or business.

  5. Lifestyle Market Analyst. Wilmette, IL: Standard Rate and Data Service, 1989- . Annual. (ASU owns 1991- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HF 5415.33 .U6 L54 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HF 5415.33 .U6 L54 WEST REFERENCE

    This valuable tool has a demographic profile and a recreational activity profile for over 200 metropolitan areas. The first section is arranged by city/metropolitan area. The next section has the same information listed by recreational activity with an alphabetical list of cities and their rankings for the activity. The demographic section, which is the third section, has the same general arrangement and layout as the lifestyle section, but focuses on demographic groups. The fourth section provides lists of magazines and direct mail lists for each of these lifestyle activities. A recommended book for the preferred leisure activities of a city.

  6. Markets of the U.S. for Business Planners. Detroit, MI: Omnigraphics, 1992- . (ASU owns 1992- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Stacks.)
    HC 106.8 .M333 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HC 106.8 .M333 WEST REFERENCE

    Covering 183 urban markets as defined by the Bureau of Economic Analysis, this work presents U.S. government data in a more usable format. Each market includes the major economic sectors, data from 1969-1988 in constant dollars, projections for 1995-2000, indexes of change, graphs of some of the data, maps, growth data for 1985-1988, and a summary of the area economics. While somewhat dated by business information standards, this is as up-to-date as possible until the complete release of the data from the 1992 economic censuses in 1996 or 1997.

  7. Sourcebook of ZIP Code Demographics. Arlington, VA: C.A.C.I., 1984- . Annual. (ASU owns 1985, 1989, 1992, 1994. Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HA 203 .S67 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HA 203 .S67 WEST REFERENCE

    These hefty volumes provide detailed demographic information for every ZIP code. The 1992 edition is the first one to use the 1990 Census figures. The data included is a reworking of the census material arranged by ZIP code instead of by census tract and blocks. This work also includes a calculated "purchasing potential index," which is an indicator of disposable income, and a business profile.

  8. Demographics USA. [New York]: S&MM Magazine, Sales & Marketing Management, 1976- . Annual. (ASU owns 1990- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HF 5415.1 .D46x HAYDEN REFERENCE (1993- )
    HC 106.7 .S8x HAYDEN JOURNALS (1990-1992)

    Published by Sales & Marketing Management magazine, this Survey of Buying Power service gives income levels, retail sales for 12 store groups and 10 merchandising lines, individual and household demographics, market growth, projections and much more for each county, metropolitan area, and television market in the country. The tab guides help locate the individual sections. The EBI (Effective Buying Income) is the term used for the disposable income in this volume. The same information is published in four parts as part of the Sales & Marketing Management magazine (HF 5438 .A34 HAYDEN JOURNALS) of which ASU owns 1975- .

  9. Thomas, G. Scott. Rating Guide to Life in America's Small Cities. Buffalo, NY: Prometheus Books, 1990.
    HA 214 .T46 1990 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    These statistical lists of small to medium sized cities are arranged by broad groupings such as "Climate/Environment," "Diversions," "Health Care," and "Public Safety." This book provides discussion about how to apply this information to your situation.

STATISTICS

  1. American Statistics Index (ASI). Washington, D.C.: Congressional Information Service, 1973- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1974- .)
    HA 195 .A4x HAYDEN GOVD (located on Table 2A)
    HA 195 .A4x WEST REFIND

    Indexes statistics published by the U.S. government. Two category sections of the index that are useful: "By City" and By "SMSA or MSA." In addition to using the name of the city, the subject term "Cities" is also helpful and includes cross-references to other terms at the end of the entries for "cities." The statistical publication may be available at ASU in paper and/or microfiche format. ASI Microfiche are in Government Documents on the third floor of Hayden.

  2. Census of . . .. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Department of Commerce, Bureau of the Census, varies- . Every 5 years. (ASU owns all years.)
    C 3. HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
    CD-ROM database HAYDEN GOVD MARS 1987-

    The economic censuses done by the U.S. cover every industry. The geographic volumes are arranged by state, but do have metropolitan area data. The largest metropolitan statistical areas will have the most complete information. The Bureau of the Census must suppress data if an individual company's information could be guessed by providing the information, so smaller cities will have a great deal of information suppressed in many industries. While the information provided in each census varies by the industry, usually the number of establishments, number of employees, sales/value of shipments, payroll and some expenditures are provided. A very good source for an idea of the number of potential competitors or employers an area has.

  3. Census of Population and Housing. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Department of Commerce, Bureau of the Census, 1790- . Every 10 years. (ASU owns 1790- .)
    C 3.223 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
    CD-ROM database HAYDEN GOVD MARS 1990-

    Often referred to as the decennial census, this information is available with a geographic arrangement. Information is available at many geographic levels. For metropolitan areas, the most specific information is at the census block level. The information includes age, income level, occupation, household profile, educational level, racial/ethnic origin, marital status, religion, housing, and more. Population estimates for the years between the censuses are found in many population documents. The easiest to use is the Statistical Abstract of the United States (Entry 11, this section).

  4. County and City Data Book. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Department of Commerce, Bureau of the Census, 1947- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1947- . Latest year in Reference; all years in US DOCUMENTS.)
    HA 202 .A35 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    C 3.134/2:C 83/2/year US DOCUMENTS (located on Tables 1A & 7A)
    CD-ROM database HAYDEN GOVD MARS 1988-

    Inside the front cover is a list of subjects covered. These include population, housing, labor force, income, retail and wholesale trade, and electric bills. The information is given for states, counties, cities of 25,000+, and places of 2,500+.

  5. Economic Indicators Handbook: Time Series, Conversions, Documentation. Detroit: Gale Research, 1994- . (ASU owns 1994- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Stacks.)
    HC 103 .E26 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    While the national and state level economic indicators information in this work is valuable, more use will be made of the city level data. The information is culled from various U.S. sources, but much of the data is compiled by the Bureau of Labor Statistics or the Department of Commerce. The time series covered can be as early as 1869, though most city level data starts in the early 1900s or after 1945. Almost all of the city level data is a detailed breakdown of the Consumer Price Index.

  6. Index to International Statistics (IIS). Washington, D.C.: Congressional Information Service, 1983- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1983- .)
    HA 155 .I223x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 6B) & WEST REFIND

    This is an index to statistics published by international intergovernmental agencies such as United Nations, OECD, UNESCO, and OAS, among others. The best access to information available by city is by city name and to use the subject entry "cities." At the end of the list of items on cities is a list of additional terms used to describe metropolitan areas. The actual statistical publications may be owned by ASU in paper format or in the IIS microfiche collection. IIS microfiche is filed in Microforms Service on the Lower Level.

  7. Network Programs by Designated Market Area. [Northbrook, IL]: Nielsen Station Index, 19??- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1984-1986, 1991, 1996- . May issue only. Latest issue on Reserve; earlier issues in Journals.)
    HE 8700.66 .U6 N47x HAYDEN RESV

    Report on Syndicated Programs. [Northbrook, IL:] Nielsen Station Index, 19??- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1984-1986, 1991, 1996- . May issue only. Latest issue on Reserve; earlier issues in Journals.)
    HE 8700.66 .U6 R46x HAYDEN RESV

    Arranged by program, day and time slot, these two show audience profiles by city. This information is of little interest to individuals, since this is designed for business and advertising.

  8. Service Industries USA: Industry Analyses, Statistics, and Leading Organizations. Detroit, MI: Gale Research, 1992- . (ASU owns 1992- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals.)
    HD 9981.3 .S47 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HD 9981.3 .S47 WEST REFERENCE

    The first half of this work is arranged by SIC (Standard Industrial Classification) codes appropriate to the Service Industries. The definition of Service Industries follows that of the Census of Service Industries (Entry 2, this section), i.e. SIC 7011 - SIC 8999, with a few exceptions. Each industry is analyzed on a national level in this portion. The last half of the work is a city by city listing of the Service Industries in each. Additional data includes tax status, number of establishments, employment, payroll, sales, and type of corporation.

  9. State and Metropolitan Area Data Book. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Department of Commerce, Bureau of the Census, 1947- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1947- . Latest year in Reference; all years in US DOCUMENTS.)
    HA 202 .S84 HAYDEN REFERENCE & WEST INFO
    C 3.134/5 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS (Located on Tables 1A & 7A)

    This is a compilation of metropolitan and state statistics from over 50 federal and private agencies. It brings together much of the widely dispersed statistics on cities. The 1991 edition is based on the 1987 economic census data and the 1990 population and housing census. A separate section includes the bibliographic sources of the data. The cities data is arranged alphabetically by the census designated MA, MSA, CMSA, and PMSA names.

  10. Statistical Abstract of the United States. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Department of Commerce, Bureau of the Census, 1878- . Annual. (ASU owns 1894- . Latest year in Reference; all years in US DOCUMENTS.)
    HA 202 .A388 HAYDEN REFERENCE DESK, SCIENCE REFERENCE DESK, and WEST REFERENCE
    C 3.134: HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS (located on Table 1A)

    While this is a handy compilation of statistics covering almost every facet of the United States, it has relatively few tables that provide data at the city level. The only way to find out if data is given at the city/metropolitan level is to find the statistics needed and look up the tables given. Most of the statistics that are given for cities are those that are population related.

  11. Statistical Reference Index (SRI). Washington, D.C.: Congressional Information Service, 1980- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1980- .)
    HA 214 .S8x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 6B) & WEST REFIND

    Indexes statistics published in sources other than those published by the U.S. government. Two category sections of the index that are useful: "By City" and By "SMSA or MSA." In addition to using the name of the city, the subject term "Cities" is also helpful and includes cross-references to other terms at the end of the entries for "Cities." The actual statistical publication may be available in paper and/or microfiche format. SRI microfiche is filed in Microforms Service on the Lower Level.

  12. ULI Market Profiles. Washington, D.C.: Urban Land Institute, 1986- . Annual. (ASU owns 1986- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HD 251 .U45 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    An annual publication covering the development market of urban areas of the U.S. and Canada with selected international metropolitan areas. Each market profile gives a description and forecast of the Development climate, residential market, retail market, hotel/recreation market, office market, and industrial market. Statistical tables and a map are included for each metropolitan area. This is extremely valuable for businesses considering relocation. It is also useful to individuals who are interested in the general business climate of a city.

  13. U.S. Housing Markets. Detroit, MI: Lomas Mortgage USA, 1966- . Quarterly. (ASU owns 1985-. Latest 2 years in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HD 7293 .A1 U17 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    A detailed survey of 53 metropolitan areas, nine regions, and the U.S. This is mostly statistical tables and charts covering private housing permits, local market activity, home and rental vacancy rates, multi-family completions, existing home sales, household formations, mortgage market, and employment trends. Comparison charts and rankings are also included. This is especially useful if your employment or business is affected by the housing industry. It is also helpful for families trying to compare housing costs of various places to live.

  14. Viewers in Profile. (metropolitan area). New York: Nielsen Station Index, Local Market Information Services, 19??- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1986,1991- . May issues only. Latest issue on Reserve; earlier issues in Journals.)
    HE 8700.66 .U6 HAYDEN RESV

    Each metropolitan area has the complete Nielsen television viewing information for the DMA (Designated Market Area) collected for the weeks included in the May issue. May includes a television program ratings sweeps week. Of the 220 DMAs available, ASU has only 49 selected areas. A TITLE BROWSE search for VIEWERS IN PROFILE in the ASU Libraries Catalog will provide a list of the metropolitan areas. This information is useful more to businesses than to individuals wanting to relocate.

COST OF LIVING

  1. American Cost of Living Survey. Detroit: Gale Research, 1994- . Biennial. (ASU owns 1994-.)
    HD 6983 .A43x HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HD 6983 .A43x WEST REFERENCE

    The editors of this work have collected this cost of living information from over 70 sources, including the ACCRA Cost of Living Index (below). The book is arranged by city and then by broad categories of items. The list of categories and the specific items included in them are listed in the front of the book. Each item listed under a city also includes the unit of measure, the value, the date of the survey, and a reference number to the source list of documents in the back of the book.

  2. ACCRA Cost of Living Index. Louisville, KY: ACCRA, 1971- . Quarterly. (ASU owns 1976- . 1981- in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    HD 6983 .C6725x HAYDEN REFERENCE
    HD 6983 .C6725x WEST REFERENCE

    Very similar to the U.S. Consumer Price Index, this covers 298 metropolitan areas in the United States. Section one is the index of the cost of living in a specific urban area (U.S. total = 100). The index number is also given for grocery items, housing, utilities, transportation, health care, and miscellaneous goods and services. This does NOT correspond to the data put out by the U.S. government because the sources of data and what is included are not identical. This work is also done only in current dollars as a snapshot in time, so cannot be used for inflation or time series analysis. The second section of each issue provides the actual price of a specified unit of goods for each city. This is very helpful to compare what your expenses are now and what they might be in a given city. One major item this work does NOT cover is taxes.

  3. CPI Detailed Report. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Dept. of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, 1940- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1962- .)
    L 2.38/3: HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS
    HB 235 .U6 U54b WEST JMM

    This is the detailed breakdown of the components of the Consumer Price Index, the most commonly quoted inflation rate indicator. Since the CPI is done only for a few selected metropolitan areas, the regional information may still be useful. Better city coverage is found in the Cost of Living Index above.

  4. Geographic Reference (U.S. and Canada): Annual Report of Costs, Wages, Salaries, and Human Resource Statistics. Newport Beach, CA: BTA Economic Research Institute, 19??- . Annual. (ASU owns 1989/90, 1991-1994, 1996- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)

LOCAL GOVERNMENT

  1. Census of Governments. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Dept. of Commerce, Bureau of the Census, 1850- . Every 5 years. (ASU owns 1850- .)
    C 3.145/4 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS

    This particular economic census focuses on government expenditures at all levels of government. The value of this census to city information is the basic balance sheet information for cities of 10,000+ in population. The information is much less detailed than the annual City Government Finances (Entry 2, this section), but may be the only source of this information for some cities.

  2. City Government Finances. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Dept. of Commerce, Bureau of the Census, 1908- . Annual. (ASU owns 1908- .)
    C 3.191/2-5 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS

    This annual publication provides a medium level of detail on the revenues and expenditures of cities. There are two lists: cities of 75,000+ and cities of 300,000+ in population. This information is more than most individuals want, but is very useful for businesses to know.

  3. Municipal Yearbook. Washington, D.C.: ICMA, 1934- . (ASU owns 1935- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
    JS 342 .A2 I52 HAYDEN REFERENCE
    JS 342 .A2 I52 HAYDEN GOVD (located on Table 1A) JS 342 .A2 I52 ARCH STACKS
    JS 342 .A2 I52 WEST REFERENCE

    For those who care about the type of local government and who the major officials are, this work is a guide to the governments of municipalities with populations of 2,500+. Since this is designed for use by the government officials, the most useful information to most other people is the salaries ranges of municipal employees, elected officials and the major department heads; form of governance; names of officials; and the municipal telephone number. Lists of various governance associations are also included.

  4. Sourcebook of Asset/Lien Searching. [Denver]: BRB Publications; Albany, NY: UCC Guide, 1992.
    KF 1058x .A4 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This work is designed for those who need to research the assets and liens that are filed on real or personal property. Most individuals or families are not going to need this work when relocating, but many businesses will find this valuable in researching various properties or partners when planning relocations. This work covers the "4283 jurisdictions in the 50 states" where liens are filed. The jurisdictions covered include cities, counties, towns, parishes, and states. The first few chapters are explanation of how to do asset and lien searches and basic information about the UCC (Uniform Commercial Code) and definitions. Several examples in the book are from Arizona.

  5. Sourcebook of County Court Records: a National Guide to Civil, Criminal, and Probate Records at the County and Municpal Levels Within the State Court Systems.. Tempe, AZ : BRB Publications, 1992- . (ASU owns 1992- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Stacks.)
    CS 44 .S67 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    When businesses or individuals are interested in a business or individual as an employer or partner, this county court system of records can be most useful. County courts tend to cover state civil and criminal cases. Some states also have municipal or city courts, which are also included in this work. The work is arranged by state with the first part of each state being an overview of the court structure and a summary of the types of courts and cases heard in each. A listing of state repositories is also included. Individual court information is arranged first by county and then by court or type of case. Information on searches, copies and other fees are included. The Recorder and Assessor addresses are also provided. A city - county cross reference is provided at the end of each state's entry.

  6. Sourcebook of Federal Courts, US District & Bankruptcy: The Definitive Guide to Searching for Case Information at the Local Level within the Federal Court System. Tempe, AZ: BRB Publications, 1993.
    KF 8700 .A19 S672 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This book will be more useful to businesses planning to expand or relocate than it will to most individuals planning a move to another city. The first chapter of this book is an overview of the Federal Court System and a how to do legal case research guide. This covers not only the record keeping items like case numbers, but also discusses the various methods of searching. A brief explanation is provided for each of the main electronic searching systems. The actual listing section is arranged by state with a state overview. Each of the courts listed include basic address, telephone, facsimile, and courier information. Also listed are the home county and the counties covered in the jurisdiction. The next section of the entry provides the basics about the indexing and case record information of this court. The last part of each entry is the "Specific Search Information." This covers the four basic methods of searching: Telephone, Mail, In Person, and Electronic Access. The final section is an overview of the National Archives and Records Administration, who is responsible for the long term record keeping of the Federal Court System. Information on how to obtain records from the NARA's Federal Record Centers, as well as the same specifics about each center as is provided for the courts.

  7. Sourcebook of State Public Records. Tempe, AZ: BRB Publications, 1994- . (ASU owns 1994- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Stacks.)
    JK 468 .P76 S73x HAYDEN REFERENCE

    Some city information is available from various state agencies because the state maintains or coordinates the information statewide. For some businesses, the state regulates them or requires registrations to operate within the state. In these cases, it is the state that often maintains the directory of such businesses. This is a state by state directory of those offices, what they maintain, who is allowed access to the information, and the address, telephone numbers, electronic access information (if available), and hours of operation for each office. The front of the book has a nice explanation of the types of records states usually maintain.
