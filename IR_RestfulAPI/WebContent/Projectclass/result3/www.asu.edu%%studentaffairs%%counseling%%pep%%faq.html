What is Professional Enhancement Programs?

We are a service of ASU designed to prepare students, alumni and members of the community for the next step in their careers. We offer workshops, testprep courses, personal advising and other programs on a year-round basis. All of our services are available to anyone, though students receive a substantial discount on course fees.

testprep@asu

What is testprep@asu?  testprep@asu offers intensive preparatory courses for the standardized tests required for graduate and professional school admission. Currently we offer courses for the GMAT, GRE and LSAT.

Everybody knows the big off campus testprep companies. Why should I take a class from testprep@asu instead? We’re conveniently located on ASU’s Tempe campus. Our fees are less than half of the off-campus companies. Our instructors are experienced, highly trained ASU staff and graduate students. Our curriculum and materials are designed with ASU’s needs in mind, not a one-size-fits-all national product.

How come you're so cheap? As part of the university, our mission is to provide community service above all. We don’t have to pay out to shareholders. We use university classrooms, so we don’t have to pay rent or mortgages on commercial space, and we can pass the savings on to you.

How much will my score improve if I take your classes? Your results depend on you. In addition to class time, our courses include a lot of homework. The more you practice our strategies, the better you’ll do.

If I register for the class, am I registered for the exam? No! We are not affiliated with the companies administering the exams. You must register for the exams directly with the test providers. For the LSAT in particular, register as early as possible, as seats in Phoenix are very limited.

  • to register for the GMAT: www.mba.com
  • to register for the GRE: www.ets.org
  • to register for the LSAT: www.lsac.org
What are the course formats?
  • GRE: The GRE course consists of 4 quantitative classes, 4 verbal classes, and 2 proctored practice tests. Each class is 3 hours long. Books and materials are provided for an additional fee.
  • GMAT: The GMAT course includes 3 proctored practice tests at the beginning, middle and end of the course. In addition, there are 5 quantitative classes and 5 verbal classes. Each class is 3 hours long. Books and materials are provided for an additional fee.
  • LSAT: The LSAT course includes 3 proctored LSAT diagnostic tests at the beginning, middle and end of the course. The course consists of 12 classroom sessions of 3 hours each, twice a week on weeknights during the six weeks prior to each LSAT administration. Books and materials are provided for an additional fee.

When should I take your classes? Our classes are designed to be taken immediately before your GMAT/GRE/LSAT test date. For undergraduates, you’ll want to take your test the summer before your senior year, or Fall of your senior year at the latest. For non-students, plan on taking your test a year to 18 months prior to the semester you plan on starting graduate school.

What should I do to prepare before my class starts? Absolutely nothing. The *less* you do beforehand, the better: our methods work best if we don’t have to break you of old habits before you develop new ones. However, for GRE and GMAT, if you haven't done high school math in a long time, contact us to pick up our math review textbook early.

careertest@asu

Why should I take your workshops? Our career workshops provide unique insights into your personality and the careers and workplace environments that might fit you best. Whether you’re a new student or a mid-career veteran, our workshops provide a valuable toolkit for making your career decisions.

Why can’t I just get my test results? Why the workshop? The online psychological instruments we use (the Strong Interest Inventory and the Myers-Briggs Type Inventory) are designed for professional interpretation. Our workshops are led by specially-trained graduate level counselors who can tell you what your results do and don’t mean. 

What happens in the workshop? The workshops offer much more than just test interpretation. For two hours, you’ll work in a small-group setting, learning to use a variety of tools and resources for making career decisions, and you’ll get a packet of materials to help you along the way.

I’d like my students to take the Strong or the Myers-Briggs as a class assignment. What should I do? First, contact us for guidelines as to how much class time we'll need for the workshop, and how best to accommodate your class. Once we've developed custom arrangements, we’ll provide you with information for your students on how and when to take the tests. Please contact us *before* informing your students!

What do the workshops cost? All prices are subject to change. In keeping with ASU Cashiering Services regulations, we accept personal checks with proper identification, cashier's checks, or money orders made out to ASU. We also accept credit cards (VISA and Master Card only). If you are an ASU student, you can pay by billing your student account. We do not accept cash.

  • ASU students: $75
  • Community Participants: $150
