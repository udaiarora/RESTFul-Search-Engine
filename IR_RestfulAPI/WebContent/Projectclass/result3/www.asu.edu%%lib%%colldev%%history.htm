Collection Development

A Brief History of ASU Libraries and ASU Collections

In 1889 the Arizona Board of Education established the Normal School Library at the site of what is now Arizona State University.  The original library collection grew from a single dictionary and a few donated books to 500 volumes by 1891, and included publications on history, science, education and literature, and various metropolitan newspapers.  A 300-foot square room (one-fourth of the Normal School building) was sectioned off to accommodate wooden shelving for books and other school materials.  By the end of the 19th century, the library housed over 1200 volumes including encyclopedias and other reference books.
In 1903 the library hired its first professionally trained librarian.   Prior to that time, faculty members were responsible for both library administration and classroom teaching.  Ten years later, the library shelves accommodated about 7,000 volumes with the addition of extensive government documents.
The construction of the new Matthews Library was completed on February 14, 1931.  The library holdings occupied the second floor, which was accessible by a winding staircase, while the first floor was used for administrative offices.  The architecture was considered "modern" and spacious for that time, allowing the library to house 14,306 volumes and 1500 bound periodicals.  But within only two years, the library needed additional space.  So in 1935, construction began on a south wing, increasing the stacks to approximately 86,000 volumes.
"Since no librarians were given the specific responsibility of collection development, faculty members were recruited to assist in this task.  The Library Administration delegated to the Cataloging Department the task of reproducing the entire shelf list and distributing sections of it to various faculty members.  Thus, faculty members could review the collection to assess its strengths as well as to identify what materials should be added.  The collection was small enough that this was a fairly simple task."  [Walters, 3]
November 1950 heralded yet another expansion to Matthews Library, increasing its holdings to 500,000 volumes. The original Territorial Normal School founded in 1885 was renamed many times before it finally became known as Arizona State University in 1958.  Library collections increased dramatically by the close of that first university fiscal year.  However, the university librarian had included in his accounting report "pamphlets, leaflets, folders, imperfect volumes, even advertisements".
In 1962, a newly hired librarian, Dr. Covey, was to become very instrumental in streamlining library procedures, obtaining accurate records on library holdings, and cleaning up the card catalog by purging 70,000 missing items. 600,000 books were moved into the newly built Hayden Library in 1966, and eight years later the library acquired its one-millionth volume.
Today, the Arizona State University Libraries consists of six Tempe campus buildings, or collections including Charles Trumbull Hayden Library, Daniel E. Noble Science and Engineering Library, Architecture and Environmental Design Library, Music, Video Resources, and Archives.  ASU also maintains the West campus Fletcher Library and the Downtown Phoenix campus.  ASU Libraries' collections currently contain over 3 million volumes, over six million microforms and over 32,000 periodical and serial titles.
