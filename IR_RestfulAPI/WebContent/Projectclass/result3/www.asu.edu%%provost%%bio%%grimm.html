Nancy Grimm

Professor

School of Life Sciences

Ph.D., Arizona State University

Professor Nancy B. Grimm is an ecosystem ecologist and biogeochemist in the ASU School of Life Sciences. She earned her BA (1978) from Hampshire College in Massachusetts and M.S. (1980) and Ph.D. (1985) from ASU, and has held research scientist and faculty positions at ASU since 1990.

Grimm’s research concerns the structure and function of ecosystems in arid lands. Her interests center on the retention, cycling, and transport of the element nitrogen, considered in the context of patch dynamics and landscape heterogeneity. Grimm studies streams and riparian zones as hot spots of nitrogen retention within arid landscapes, and terrestrial aquatic interactions as mediated by hydrologic flow. These themes are addressed both in natural desert and urban watershed studies.

Grimm is Lead Principal Investigator and Co Director of the Central Arizona Phoenix Long Term Ecological Research (LTER) project, a study of the Phoenix metropolis and surroundings that is one of the first comprehensive investigations of an urban ecosystem. In that role, Grimm oversees and coordinates interdisciplinary research in urban ecology involving over 100 scientists in more than 10 departments. From 1993 to 1998, she directed ASU’s Undergraduate Mentorship in Environmental Biology program, which aimed to increase participation in the field of ecology by students from underrepresented groups.

Grimm has served on numerous advisory panels and review teams for the National Science Foundation and other federal agencies. She currently is an editor for Ecosystems; her past editorial board service includes Ecology, Ecological Monographs, Journal of the North American Benthological Society, and Ecology Letters. She was President of the North American Benthological Society, Chair of the Science Advisory Board for the National Center for Ecological Analysis and Synthesis from 1999 to 2000, a member of the National Research Councils Committee on Hydrologic Sciences from 2002 to 2003, and a member of the Ecological Society of America Research committee from 1993 to 2003. Grimm is serving as President of the Ecological Society of America for 2005 to 2006.
