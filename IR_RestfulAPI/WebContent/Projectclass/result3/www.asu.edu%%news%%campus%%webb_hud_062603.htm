Del E. Webb School joins HUD grant effort

The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) has selected the Del E. Webb School of Construction (DEWSC) for participation in a five-year indefinite quantity contract for housing-related research. The contract has an umbrella value of up to $50 million.

ASU is a member of the Building Technology Research Consortium, which includes a select set of universities known nationally for their work in residential building technology and affordable housing. Virginia Tech will serve as the lead institution, providing project administration and project management expertise. Other consortium members include Michigan State University and engineering/design firm URS Group, Inc.

The consortium's goal is to assist HUD, the building industry and other sponsors in developing technology, policy and procedures that enhance the durability, efficiency and quality of the next generation of housing, and increase the performance of existing residences.

Howard Bashford, director of graduate studies at DEWSC, will serve on the Technical Oversight Committee, which is the consortium's top-level management body. In addition to serving on the management committee, he will oversee the work of ASU's lead and specialized investigators on this contract. Other key faculty members participating in this effort are Anil Sawhney, David Pijawka and John MacIntosh.

"We are pleased with HUD's recognition of our skills and abilities in residential research," said William Badger, director of the Del E. Webb School of Construction. "Over the past eight years, we have been awarded progressively larger and more prestigious research grants for our work in this area. Our faculty will build on this foundation and do an exceptional job for both the consortium and HUD."

The Del E. Webb School of Construction is the largest construction research program in the nation and is involved in several industry and government partnerships. In 2000, the school received a National Science Foundation Partnerships for Innovation award, which supports an industry-university-government coalition called AzPATH, which is bringing innovation to the homebuilding industry.

Shafer, with the College of Engineering & Applied Sciences, can be reached at (480) 965-3865 or (terri.shafer@asu.edu).
