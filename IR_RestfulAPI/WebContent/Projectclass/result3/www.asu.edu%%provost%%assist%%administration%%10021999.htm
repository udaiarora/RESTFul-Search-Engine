Arizona State System for Information on Student Transfer (ASSIST)
Status Report as of October 02, 1999
John D. Porter

Current Status

ASSIST (formerly known as the Transfer Student Data Warehouse or TSDW) is a relational database containing student enrollment records from Arizona?s universities and community colleges. The purpose of ASSIST is to provide community colleges and universities information for (1) completing the IPEDS Graduation Rate Survey and (2) assessing transfer articulation effectiveness. ASU signed a Memorandum of Agreement to create ASSIST using data warehousing technology as part of the Arizona Transfer Articulation Support System (ATASS). The project was funded as a proof of concept for three years beginning July 1, 1998 and is now in its second year of funding. The ultimate success of the project depends on institutions providing accurate and complete data. ASU?s goal is to create the technical environment in the first year, focusing on qualitative improvements (e.g., data quality, completeness and aggregate reporting) and security issues in the second and third year.

Since the last APASC meeting, ASU completed a field test of the ASSIST prototype, revised the security plan based on input from ASU and ABOR Legal Counsel, and met with the ASSIST Steering/ASSIS Technical Committees on October 1, 1999. The points below summarize the status of the ASSIST project.

The universities and community colleges are making progress in contributing data to ASSIST. By the end of the three years, the ASSIST database should contain basic enrollment information on all participating institutions. However, ASU?s experience is that many schools omit the less used data fields, such as placement test scores. APASC may need to review the criticality of some of these data, and if not needed, drop them from the ASSIST database.
The 1998-99 data exchange occurs in December 1999. ASU is proposing that the community colleges start contributing course information to ASSIST (the universities already do so). The ASSIST Steering Committee asks that APASC review this requirement and decide whether this information is necessary to achieve the goals of ASSIST. By adding community college course information, the potential areas of analyses are greatly expanded. This is especially useful to the community colleges, since the ASSIST Security Plan requires that student records be stripped of individually identifying characteristics. However, when this issue was presented to the ASSIST Steering Committee, some were concerned about the possibility for inappropriate comparisons between institutions. The Steering Committee decided to remand the issue to APASC for decision.
ASU revised the ASSIST Security Plan based on input from the legal counsels at ASU and ABOR, and the ASSIST Steering Committee. The attorneys believe the Plan creates a safe harbor for Arizona to accomplish the goals of ASSIST without violating FERPA. The plan will be presented to APASC at its October meeting and ASU is recommending that it be forwarded to ABOR, the State Community College Board, and each community college district board for ratification.
ASU has created a data warehouse containing over 700,000 student records, including 125,000 linked records. Since this database contains personally identifiable information on students, it resides behind a firewall and is accessible only to the ASSIST development team. An identical relational database excluding personally identifiable information has been loaded on a server outside the firewall and will be accessible to participating once the Security Plan is implemented. The prototype test showed that the database is still too complex for regular use. ASU plans to simplify the architecture in the future.

The Arizona Legislature passed legislation (SB 1399) limiting the use of the SSN by the universities and community colleges. The impact of the legislation on ASSIST during the building stage of ASSIST is negligible. However, the long-term impact of the legislation needs to be considered. If SSN is not available to match student records in the future, another method of matching records needs to be devised. (See attached summary of SSN legislation.)

Future Plans


During the next six months, ASU will simplify the ASSIST tables, work on implementing the security plan, develop web-based aggregate reports, assist the community colleges in completing the year 2000 GRS report, encourage institutions to submit missing data, and initiate the 1999 data exchange.
