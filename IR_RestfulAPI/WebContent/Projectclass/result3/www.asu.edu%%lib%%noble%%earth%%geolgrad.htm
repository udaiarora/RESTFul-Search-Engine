The ASU Libraries

There are 8 libraries within 5 organizational units that serve ASU:

  • The University Libraries are composed of 4 libraries on the Tempe campus:
    • Hayden (Humanities, Social Sciences, Business)
    • Noble (Science and Engineering)
    • Architecture and Environmental Design, and
    • Music.
  • College of Law Library, also on the Tempe campus,
  • Downtown Phoenix Campus Library,
  • Polytechnic Campus Library, and
  • West Campus Library.

All libraries are open to the general public in addition to the ASU students, faculty and staff.

 

 

Daniel E. Noble Science and Engineering Library

Noble Library houses the collections for Engineering, Mathematics, and the Sciences (Astronomy, Biology, Chemistry, Geology, Physics) and includes the Maps Collection.

 

The library is located on the Tempe campus, on the southwest corner of Tyler and McAllister Malls, across the sidewalk from the Goldwater building.

 

Opened in 1983 and remodeled in 1999, the library encloses a four-story atrium that creates a spacious and pleasant atmosphere for study and research. Facilities include over 60 library workstations, wireless access to the campus network, plus open and enclosed study spaces for both groups and individuals.

 

The library has 4 service units with approximately 30 staff:

  • Access Services
  • Copy Services
  • Instruction, Marketing & Outreach
  • Maps Reference
  • Science Reference

Where to Find ...

  • Books
    • To find books available in the ASU Libraries, use the ASU Libraries Online Catalog.

    • To identify published books (including those not available in the ASU Libraries) use WorldCat

  • Conference Proceedings
    • To find conference proceedings available in the ASU Libraries, use the ASU Libraries Online Catalog.
      Search Tip: Use the KEYWORD search and type in words from the conference's name.

    • To identify published conference proceedings (including those not available in the ASU Libraries) use GeoRef

  • Dissertations
    • To obtain a copy of an ASU dissertation published from 1979 to the present, use Current Research @ Arizona State University

    • To obtain a copy of an ASU dissertation published prior to 1979, use the ASU Libraries Online Catalog and request the copy be sent from storage to the library.

    • To identify dissertations from other universities, use Dissertation Abstracts International and/or GeoRef

  • Journal Articles
    • To identify articles on a topic, use GeoRef or Web of Science/Science Citation Index

    • To determine if the University Libraries have a specific journal,
      • From within a database, click on the button

        OR

      • Use the University Libraries' Find Journals page.

  • USGS Material
    Material from the United States Geological Survey in print or microfilm/fiche format is located in two places in the University Libraries:
    • USGS sheet maps are available in the Map Collection in Noble Library (3rd floor)
    • All other material from the USGS is kept in the Government Documents Collection in Hayden Library (3rd floor)
    To determine what USGS material the Libraries hold, use the ASU Libraries Online Catalog.

Checking Out Material

  • How do I get a Library Card?
    Your SunCard (ASU ID card) is used to checkout materials. Forget your card? - Show us a picture ID.

  • How many items may I check out? ... For how long?
    There is no limit on the number of items a graduate student or faculty member may check out.
    • Masters level graduate students may check out items for 30 days
    • Teaching assistants, research assistants, and doctoral level graduate students may check out items for 90 days
    • Faculty, academic professionals and staff may check out items for 150 days.
    • Items can be renewed until requested (i.e. "recalled") by another library user.

  • Are Fines Charged?
    For each item not returned or renewed within thirty (30) days of the due date, the borrower is assessed a $10 transaction fee and a replacement charge. If the item is returned, the replacement charge is cancelled and the borrower only needs to pay the transaction fee.

  • I'm a faculty member - I don't have to worry about Fines or Recalls, do I?
    Every library user has the same responsibility to return materials promptly so that others may use them. Failure to return material on time results in fines. Please return materials prior to long absences (intersessions, summers, sabbaticals, etc.) or have someone on campus see that all due dates and recall notices are honored.

  • The Book I need is checked out - how do I get it recalled?
    In the ASU Libraries Online Catalog, use the blue REQUEST button to have the item recalled. There is no charge for this service.

  • For more information see Access Services (http://www.asu.edu/lib/access/)

Document Delivery
  • The Item I need is in the Polytechnic campus, Downtown Phoenix campus or the West campus library - do I have to go there to get it?
    No, the University Libraries' will retrieve or photocopy items from other campus libraries.

    • For books:
      In the ASU Libraries Online Catalog, use the blue REQUEST button to have the item brought to any one of the Tempe campus libraries' circulation desks. There is no charge for this service. Delivery Time: 2-3 business days.

    • For journals:
      Use the Library Express/Document Delivery photocopy request form. A photocopy of the article will be delivered to your mailbox on the Tempe campus, or you may obtain the copy from the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. There is no cost for this service. Delivery Time: 2-3 business days.

  • The Item I need is in Storage - where's that?
    The Libraries' operate a separate facility on the Polytechnic campus for overflow material and items that need special handling. The facility is not open to the public as the material is packaged and maintained in a special, controlled environment. To obtain material housed in this facility:
    • If you need the whole volume, use the REQUEST button on the catalog record OR stop by or call the Information/Reference Desk at 480/965-7607. We will have the item recalled. Delivery Time: 1-2 business days.
    • If you need just an article and have the citation information (title, year, volume, page numbers), use the Interlibrary Loan Service. The article will be scanned and you'll be notified of its availability via email.

  • The Item I need is not available at any ASU Library - can the library get it for me?
    Yes, use the Interlibrary Loan Service. You will be notified by e-mail when the item is available. Most photocopies come directly via e-mail; books must be picked up at the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. Usually there is no charge for this service, however, if there will be a cost involved, you will be contacted for approval, prior to the request being ordered. Delivery Time: Journal articles average 2-7 days, while books average 5-14 days. Materials that are rare, in fragile condition, in high use, or non-circulating in most libraries, may take considerably longer or may not be able to be acquired. Rush service and renewals for books are generally not available.

  • I'm too busy to come to the Library; can some one deliver materials to me?
    Yes, we deliver books and photocopies to mailboxes on the Tempe campus via our "Library Express" service. This service is free for faculty but graduate students are charged a fee. Cost for graduate students: Copy charges are $ 3.00 for the first 1-15 pages, plus $ 1.00 for each additional 1-10 pages per each individual article. Book charges are $ 1.00 each. Delivery Time: 2 business days.

    To use this service, set up an account with Library Express by going to the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. Once the account is set up, to order books use the Library Express Book Form; to order journal articles use the Library Express Photocopy Form.

Copies

  • How much do Photocopies cost?
    Photocopies cost $0.10/page if using cash; $0.08/page if using a SunCard or Library Copy Card. .

  • Can you make copies of microfilm or microfiche?
    Yes, copies cost $0.15/page if using cash; $0.12/page if using a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

  • How much do computer prints cost?
    Computer prints cost $0.08/page and requires a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

Off-Campus Access to Electronic Resources

  • Can I use electronic journals and databases from my home?
    I'm going on sabbatical to Timbuktu, will I be able to access journals and databases there?
    Yes, if you access the resource from the Libraries' web site -- going directly to resource's location on the web will result in a "turnaway". Via the Libraries website, you'll be asked to sign in with your ASURITE ID and Password; once authorized, you'll be connected to the journal/database.

Our subject specialist librarians provide special services including:

  • Library or Information Literacy Instruction
    In your classroom or our instruction room, have demonstrations of specific databases and/or instruction for a specific assignment. A librarian will also prepare a web page for a specific class or assignment that provides the students with a list of appropriate resources and how to use them.

    To schedule library instruction for a class or group, please request at least 2-3 weeks in advance so that the proper materials, demonstrations and database access privileges can be prepared. Requests for instructional services is especially intense during the first 4-5 weeks of the Fall and Spring semesters; when requesting instruction during this time, please have several dates available - we will do our best to accomodate your first priority.

  • Research Consultations
    You may also set up an appointment for an individual consultation. Need help using specific databases or resources? Need a refresher on available library services and resources? Call or e-mail the chemistry librarian to set up a time.

  • Purchasing Requests
    Is there a book, journal, or database that you would like the ASU Libraries to purchase? Let us know!
