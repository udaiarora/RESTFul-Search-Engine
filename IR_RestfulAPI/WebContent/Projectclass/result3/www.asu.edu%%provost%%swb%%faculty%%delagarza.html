Southwest Borderlands Initiative Faculty

 

 

Amira de la Garza

Associate Professor
Hugh Downs School of Communications, College of Public Programs

Hired as the first Southwest Borderlands Scholar at ASU in 2002, De la Garza is Associate Professor in the Hugh Downs School of Human Communication. She is also a member of the Affiliate Faculty for the Women & Gender Studies Program at ASU. She is currently serving as the Acting Director of the North American Center of Transborder Studies (NACTS) at ASU, after chairing the founding steering committee in 2004. Her research is in the area of borderlands identities, with a special interest in postcolonial methodology for ethnography and multi-genre autoethnographic texts & performance. She received special commendation by the National Communication Association Ethnography Division for her influence on the field’s scholarship in 2003. She has held two Fulbright scholarships to Mexico and currently chairs the NACTS Migration Narratives research cluster, funded by ASU's Institute for Humanities Research, and collaborating with the Performance in the Borderlands Project of ASU's School of Theatre and Film. She is currently preparing material for a book exploring the relationships between gendered cultural experience, memory, and trauma in the creation of narrative identity.
