Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

May 3, 2000

Present: Tom Keating, Victor Becerra, Stefan Myhajlenko, Helen Seaton and Linda Shackle

CAPS met in the Conference Room at Noble Science and Engineering Library.

Tom introduced our two new members, Victor Becerra and Stefan Myhajlenko. Tom and Molly McCartney will be going off the committee this year.

Tom reviewed the committee's last item of business for this year - the status of academic associates. Some academic associates are academic professionals but they are not eligible for continuing status. Most of them are academic advisors. According to the charge of this committee, up to one position can be held by an academic associate. . Victor is the first academic associate to serve on CAPS. However, there is a problem in that academic associates cannot vote in Academic Senate elections. Since Tom is on the Senate's Committee on Committees, he nominated Victor this year. Recommendation for next year - take a position on whether academic associates should vote in Academic Senate elections.

Tom is going to meet with the Steering Committee of the Council of Academic Advisors and he plans to recommend that they look into whether their interests are best served by all academic advisors being classified staff or all being academic professionals.

Linda announced that Ron Page was successful in setting up a listserv just for CAPS (the committee). He talked to the Provost and Linda will have permission to add names to the CAPS list. The Committee can in turn, send messages to the AP list.

There will be a CAPS meeting on Wednesday, August 2, 2000 in the Conference Room at the Noble Science and Engineering Library at 10:00 a.m. to decide on future meeting dates and to elect officers.

Helen Seaton, Secretary
