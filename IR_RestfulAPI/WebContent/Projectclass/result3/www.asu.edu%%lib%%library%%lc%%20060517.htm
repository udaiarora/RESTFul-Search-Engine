Librarians’ Council

Minutes

May 17, 2006

Hayden C6A


Chair: Brian Doherty

Secretary: Katherine O’Clair

The meeting was called to order at 3:03pm.


A quorum was present.


I. Approval of Minutes (April 19) -- Minutes were approved.


 

II. Chair's Report – Brian Doherty

  • Brian congratulated Jennifer Duvernay, Brad Vogus, Dan Stanton, and Sheila Young on receiving Continuing Appointment and Promotion.

III. Dean's report – Kurt Murphy

  • The President approved the salary adjustments, and they were consistent with the mandate. Those with merit scores of 3 or 4 received a merit raise. Those with 3’s received $1000; those with 4’s received $2990. Classified staff with scores of 3 and above received a 1.31% to 3.9% merit raise.

IV. Committee Reports

a. Personnel Committee - JoAnn Mulvihill

  • No Report


b. Review Committee - Ginny Sylvester

  • No Report


c. Research and Professional Development Committee - Mark Scott

  • The deadline for research funds for FY 2006-2007 will be in August.


d. Compensation Committee - Mimmo Bonanni

  • No Report


e. Academic Senate - Dan Stanton reporting for Deborah Koshinsky

  • The Senate will be working on shared governance for the University next fall and the and one of the issues they will address is the definition of a “unit” amidst all the changes in Schools, Colleges, and interdisciplinary groupings.
  • Legislature is looking at curriculum requirements. A Regent proposed that Each course must announce reading assignments/requirements at start of course. University Provosts working on provision to allow listing these in the syllabi. These requirements are not binding, however, as resources may be adjusted.
  • Provost Glick announced that ASU has received single university accreditation.
  • There have been forums with the candidates for the Provost position. Academic Senate leadership has met with the 3 candidates.
  • Elizabeth Capaldi from State University of New York is the new Provost.
  • The Academic Senate has proposed a resolution stating no grants for research where results can’t be disseminated, such as classified research. The Academic Senate is forming a committee to look at other institutions’ policies. The Academic Senate’s last policy with respect to this is from 1984. Administration changed this policy last year.
  • Bill Verdini is the new President-Elect of the Academic Senate.

f. Committee on Academic Professional Status – Dan Stanton

  • CAPS met on May 4th.
  • The self-sub listserv is not the official means of communication with AP’s - it is just a discussion forum.
  • CAPS discussed issues relating to continuing appointment and promotion and the review committee being proposed. After discussion, there is no resolution on how to have it work for everyone. The committee may have a pool of members that will serve for three years. Members of the pool would be chosen to serve on a specific year’s review committee for promotion and continuing appointment to fit the need for that year. It is not yet certain how this committee will be implemented. Should there be representation from the candidate’s “college”? There needs to be someone on the review committee with firsthand knowledge of criteria and how they apply. This individual will be recused from the voting.

V. Old Business

  • Section J of Librarians’ Handbook - Orientation Document – JoAnn Mulvihill
  • Final discussion and vote took place. This document provides guidelines for new hires, and help for the primary evaluators too. This document will be fluid and will need to be updated. As with the mentoring document, this orientation document will be provided to the new AP and the PE at the first meeting with Kurt’s office.
  • It was recommended that the item for “how to answer personal calls” be changed to not include the word “personal”.
  • The document was voted on and passed.

 

VI. New Business


Nominations and Elections Committee reported the winners of this year’s election.
