Major Events and Accomplishments

CAPS Web Page
Lise Hawkos volunteered to put a Web page together to replace the list server that has been used to communicate with the general academic professional community at ASU. She put together a text page with the committee roster, meeting minutes, bylaws, committee purpose and goals and the "AP white paper". The address is http:Hwww.asu.edu/provost/CAPS. This web page has been a very useful communications tool over the past year. A new committee officer of "Web Master" was added to the bylaws to maintain this tool.

Procedures for Release of Academic Professionals: ACD 507-05 (subsequently renumbered 508-05)
One of the major goals for the commiittee this year was to get the revision of ACD 507-05 (Release of Academic Professionals) through the administrative process to be adopted as University policy. A letter, which outlined the process which had occurred over the previous four years, was sent to Provost Glick from the committee in September. We also asked what further steps needed to be taken to move this revision along. As a result of this letter the general consul's office looked at the proposed revision and rejected it on technical grounds. The committee was then asked by Vice Provost Harris to draft a new revision of the policy in collaboration with the Academic Senate Personnel Committee. This task was accomplished by March and the new revision was approved by the full Academic Senate in April. President Coor signed the 1997 revision of ACD 507-05 into official University policy in early July.

CAPS Bylaws Revision
The second major goal of the committee for 1996-97 was to revise the 1991 Bylaws of CAPS. Several major changes had occurred in the way committee members are nominated and elected which were not yet included in the bylaws. Karl Weiss volunteered to lead the revision effort. By December all changes had been made and agreed upon by the committee. On February 6 an open forum was held to allow any AP to comment on or offer revisions to the proposed bylaws. A number of different units and colleges were represented. The revised bylaws were sent to all APs on the main ASU campus at the end of February for their vote for or against ratification by March 24. Dr. Ann Yates, temporary AP caucus convener, reported that all 56 ballots returned before the deadline were for ratification. The new bylaws took effect in April.

Provosts Advisory Committee on Academic Professionals (PACAP)
PACAP members were invited to attend the October 3 CAPS meeting. At this meeting they were encouraged to elect a chairperson and recorder to get the committee in motion for the current year. A chair and recorder were elected from the PACAP members who attended. Several PACAP meetings took place over the course of the year.

AP Caucus
CAPS invited all University academic professionals to attend our October 31 meeting to get acquainted with their representatives, voice any concerns and work with the committee to set an agenda for the year. The committee considered making this an annual event and including it in the bylaws as such. Provost Glick and Vice Provost Harris were both invited to address the caucus. Dr. Harris attended and addressed several concerns that had been relayed by the Conunittee Chair to the Provost's office. He also fielded questions from the APs who attended. Due to the very low attendance at the caucus and the other public forums sponsored by CAPS this year, no further formal organization of this type of meeting was deemed necessary.

Sabbaticals: ACD 705
The committee briefly revisited the perennial question of whether or not APs are being de facto discriminated against in the process of granting sabbatical leaves. The conclusion of the discussion was that the problem appeared to be localized in a few departments in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (CLAS) and University level action was not warranted at this time. The main reason being that sabbaticals are considered to be a privilege and not an entitlement. PACAP was asked to act as a clearing-house of sabbatical leave policies on the local level across the University. We hope that through continued vigilance any pattern of discrimination can be detected and appropriate action taken in a timely manner.

Salary Adjustments: ACD 510-01 (subsequently renumbered 511-01)
An AP from CLAS requested a review of the University policy which sets procedures for distributing salary adjustments. The AP complained specifically that his department did not create a separate pool for AP employees in the past two salary adjustments. The source of funds was through reallocation of University resources and not a legislative appropriation. Technically ACD 510-01 only applies to appropriated funds. The committee determined that the AP had not filed a grievance in the matter and that this appeared to be a local problem. No further action was taken.

Post Continuing Status Review
Vice Provost Harris announced at the January 30 PACAP meeting that the Arizona Board of Regents (ABOR) had created a "Work group on Continuing Status Employees and Part-time Faculty" of which he was a member. The primary focus of the work group was to produce a document for APs similar to that which had been recently completed on post-tenure review for Faculty. In addition, justification for the granting of continuing status was to be given on a job description level for each type of AP. Statistical information on the total number of APs with continuing status and continuing-eligible was also required. Vice Provost Harris asked for three members of PACAP to serve on the work group and indicated that three members of CAPS would also be requested. Alison Ewing, chair of PACAP, Heather Lineberry, AP Senator, and Gary Hembree, chair of CAPS, met with Tom Callarman, President of the ASU Academic Senate, to discuss how APs could most effectively use the information obtained during the faculty's experience with post-tenure review. PACAP and CAPS decided to form an ad hoc committee to draft some of the requested items in advance of a formal request from the ABOR work group. Members of both commnittees met with a representative from ASU West several times during the spring semester to discuss strategy and draft a state level post continuing review document modeled on the Arizona Faculty Council version for post-tenure review. Information from peer institutions on professional employees was gathered and a document on justification of continuing status was drafted. Statistical information was distilled from a Human Resources listing of all ASU AP employees. Since no further requests were received from the ABOR work group the documents will be held by the respective committees' recorder or archivist until they are needed.

Evaluation for Continuing Status and/or Promotion: ACD 506-09
At the request of an AP in CLAS the committee sent a one sentence revision of this policy to Vice Provost Harris in March. The revision made explicit a thirty day time limit for reviewers to return their evaluations of Academic Professionals. This policy revision has been forwarded to the Academic Senate Personnel Committee for action in the Fall of 1997.

Review of AP position descriptions
The committee began reviewing the AP positions which were advertised in the ASU Insight employee newsletter. The main concern is whether the APs in these positions will be made aware of the expectations of the AP classification at ASU. One position in the School of Music appeared particularly troubling. The position description was obtained and forwarded to the Provost's office with a cover letter. The committee requested that the position be thoroughly reviewed as to whether or not it had been placed appropriately in the AP category. Vice Provost Harris replied that he had made numerous inquiries and had determined it had been appropriately placed. A total of four positions were reviewed, two continuing-eligible and two year-to-year.
