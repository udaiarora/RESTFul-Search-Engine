Travaux:

1. Deux devoirs écrits de 10 pages.

2. Lire six articles sur Rabelais, Marguerite de Navarre et Montaigne--deux articles sur chacun des trois auteurs. Consultez la bibliographie électronique MLA. Sur une carte notez l’auteur, le titre de l’article, le journal (numéro et année), pages ainsi qu’un petit paragraphe sur une idée qui vous a frappé. A remettre à la dernière session consacrée à chaque auteur: 6 octobre; 6 novembre; 27 novembre.

3. Animer la discussion. Chaque étudiant(e) sera chargée une fois de préparer trois questions à distribuer avant le jour du cours sur un épisode/passage/chapitre/essai. Les autres étudiants prépareront des réponses aux questions et seront prêts à participer à la discussion.

VII. INTERNET RESOURCES

ARTFL (Project for American and French Research on the Treasury of the French Language)
The database includes nearly 2000 texts, ranging from classic works of French literature to various kinds of non-fiction prose and technical writing. The eighteenth, nineteenth and twentieth centuries are about equally represented, with a smaller selection of seventeenth century texts as well as some medieval and Renaissance texts.

Bibliographie Nationale Francaise
The French national bibliography. Available only from library computers.

Dictionnaires des XVIe et XVIIe siècles
A set of dictionaries on cd-rom containing the texts of ten 16th and 17th century dictionaries. Includes a  thesaurus for all the entries with their variants and spellings, along with the languages of etymology such as Hebrew, Arabic and Armenian. In the MARS Room behind the Reference desk.

French Studies Website (Hayden Library)
Offers links to a variety of Internet resources on French studies including French culture, language, literature, news sources, as well as library catalogs in Canada and France.

Iter: Gateway to the Renaissance
A developing bibliography of more than 225,000 articles and reviews drawn from  over 300 medieval and renaissance journal titles. More than 60,000 new  records are added annually Indexes  articles; reviews; bibliographies; catalogues; editions; abstracts; discographies; and notes

MLA Bibliography (1963+)
The online version of the multivolume MLA International Bibliography  produced by the Modern Language Association of America. Provides bibliographic records pertaining to literature, language, linguistics, and folklore. Indexes monographs, working papers, proceedings, bibliographies, and other formats. Covers over 3,000 journals and series from 1963. The bound volumes are available in Hayden Reference.

Renaissance Links from the Montaigne Studies Website at the University of  Chicago. Includes links to literary texts, electronic journals, images, exhibitions as well as La Bibliothèque nationale de France.
