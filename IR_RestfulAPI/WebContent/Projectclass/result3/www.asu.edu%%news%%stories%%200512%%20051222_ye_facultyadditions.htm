December 22, 2005

Nationally recognized figures join faculty, dynamic university leadership

• Civil Rights leader Raul Yzaguirre, former president and chief executive officer of the National Council of La Raza, joined ASU as the as Presidential Professor of Practice in Community Development and Civil Rights. He is helping create a center at ASU’s Downtown Phoenix campus, to focus on community development, education for practitioners, and academic scholarship in the form of dissertations, lectures and seminars at the university.

• Ira Jackson, formerly a senior fellow at Harvard University and executive of BankBoston, was named president of the ASU Foundation. In the 2004 – 2005 fiscal year, ASU and the ASU Foundation raised nearly $99 million in cash and property donations for the university, an increase of almost 30 percent.

• Stephen Albert Johnston, a pre-eminent scientist whose work spans a broad range of genetic and medical research, joined ASU to head a new Center for Innovations in Medicine within the Biodesign Institute at ASU. He also is a faculty member in the School of Life Sciences.

• Debra Friedman, a senior administrator at the University of Washington, was named dean of ASU’s College of Public Programs. Friedman is leading the college’s effort to enhance its academic excellence and achieve far-reaching social relevance.

• Lisa Love, a hall of fame volleyball coach and administrator at the University of Southern California, was selected to serve as ASU’s vice president for university athletics. Love, ASU’s 21st athletics director, took over for Christine Wilkinson, a senior vice president for the university who served as interim athletics director after Gene Smith left the university in April.

• Peter Crouch, the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering’s dean, was chosen to lead the university’s global initiatives. Crouch, dean for the last 10 years, is serving as vice provost for global engagement and remaining as dean of the Fulton School while an accelerated search for his successor is conducted.

• Chris Callahan, associate dean of the Philip Merrill College of Journalism at the University of Maryland, was named founding dean of the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication. In July, the school, formerly in the College of Public Programs, became an independent college.

• Diane McCarthy, former president of the ASU Alumni Association, became vice president for university and community partnerships in the ASU Foundation. Christine Wilkinson, senior vice president and secretary of the university, added responsibility for the Alumni Association to her duties.

• Luoluo Hong was named dean of student affairs at the West campus. Hong previously was the dean of students at the University of Wisconsin-Madison and succeeded the retiring Jo Ann Madonna.

• Michael Tracy, a top executive from SRI International (formerly Stanford Research Institute), was appointed to the Biodesign Institute at ASU as director of strategy and research alliances. Tracy is leading strategic initiatives between the Biodesign Institute and a variety of health care, industrial and governmental organizations. He serves as scientific director of the Center for Cancer Research, one of 12 research centers comprising the Biodesign Institute.

• Adrian Sannier, an expert in human/computer interaction and three-dimensional visualization, was named deputy senior vice president and university technology officer at ASU. Sannier, the Stanley Professor of Interdisciplinary Engineering at Iowa State University’s Department of Industrial and Manufacturing Systems Engineering, has 17 years of experience in the public and private sectors.

• After serving for 15 years as ASU’s chief academic officer, Milton Glick announced in December that he will step down as provost and executive vice president of the university in July 2006. Glick will become provost emeritus and the first University Professor at ASU.

• Former Tempe Campus Director for Residential Life Kevin Cook was appointed vice provost and dean for student affairs at the Downtown Phoenix campus.

• John Hepburn was named dean of the College of Human Services at the West campus. Hepburn had been serving the college as interim dean.
