September 22, 2004

ASU rolls out 2 new student diversity programs

ASU’s recruitment and retention efforts continue to reinforce the importance of student diversity on the ASU campus. Adelante (Moving Forward) and NATIONS (Native Americans Taking Initiative ON Success) are the latest student recruitment and retention programs being introduced this year.

Sherrill Tomita, executive coordinator with the Multicultural Student Center (MSC), says both of these programs support the university’s ongoing mission to assist the diverse student population.

“We are very encouraged by the fact that the numbers (of minority student) are increasing,” says Tomita, who replaced Antonia Franco as of July 16. “We feel it is important to support these students through their freshman year all through their four years of college.”

Adelante’s mission is to provide academic and social support and cultural awareness for Latino students at ASU. Adelante’s programming will focus on academic enrichment, career information assistance and leadership development through activities such as workshops and community service programs.

Adelante’s official kick off is scheduled from 4:30 to 6 p.m., Sept. 22, in the La Paz room in the Memorial Union.

NATIONS is a student driven organization that complements the existing Native American Achievement Program (NAAP) and falls under the operations and guidance of the MSC. NATIONS aims to develop confidence in each student’s leadership abilities, so they can overcome barriers, develop social support, achieve academically and maintain pride in their heritage.

Both of the new programs will follow the model introduced by the S.T.A.R.S. Association program, which was founded in 1985. Its mission is to provide academic and social support along with cultural awareness for African-American students at ASU.

In addition, Tomita says three student success coordinators will be added to the MSC staff by October to assist Latino students, Asian-American students and students of the Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender and Queer coalition.

“We are very excited with the developments we’re making,” she says. “The MSC is here to assist all students in developing their skills while getting the most out of their experience at ASU.”

For information about the S.T.A.R.S. Association, call (480) 727-7394. For information on Adelante and NATIONS, call the ASU Multicultural Student Center at (480) 965-6060 or visit the Web at (http://www.asu.edu/studentlife/msc/).

Romero, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 727-3116 or (mlromero@asu.edu).
