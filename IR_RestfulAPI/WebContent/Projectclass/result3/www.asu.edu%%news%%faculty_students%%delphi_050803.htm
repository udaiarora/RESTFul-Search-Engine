May 8, 2003

Arizona State University announces Delphi Scholarship recipients

Arizona State University's College of Engineering and Applied Sciences announced that five students have received Delphi Scholarships for 2003-2004.

The Delphi Foundation is an independent charitable foundation established in 1999. The foundation focuses on educational opportunities and support systems aimed at helping young people reach their full potential, with special consideration for educational programs in science and technology. This year, Delphi provided a gift of $10,000, which was matched by Motorola.

"Delphi is pleased to make an educational contribution to Arizona State University," said Marc Martens, Delphi Delco Electronics Systems director of public affairs and communications. "We believe that it is good business to support the educational institutions that provide an invaluable resource for the community."
Students who received awards are as follows:

Ai-Chau Diep Thi is a sophomore majoring in Computer Systems Engineering. Her godfather was an electrical engineer in Motorola's Computer Group who took her on a tour of the office. When she realized there were few women engineers, she decided to become one.

Rolando Alvarado is junior a majoring in electrical engineering with interests in designing control systems, circuits or microelectronics. He plans to work in the engineering field, but ultimately he hopes to teach engineering or physics at either the high school or college level.

Julie Hall is a junior majoring in chemical engineering with particular interests in biotechnology and semiconductor research. She is an undergraduate research assistant in a chemical engineering lab.

Michael Stull is a junior majoring in mechanical engineering. He is a transfer student from the Maricopa County Community College system who is interested in the field of communications.

Vincent Placido is a junior majoring in industrial engineering. He is an ASU Barrett Honors College Student as well as an intramural sports team member. He transferred to ASU from California Lutheran University, where he played football and made the Dean's list two years in a row. He hopes to use his degree in industrial engineering to improve the quality of life locally and globally.

With its world headquarters in Kokomo, Indiana, Delphi Delco Electronics Systems is a subsidiary of Delphi Corp. Delphi Delco Electronics Systems provides a full line of vehicle electronics including integrated body, chassis and safety, powertrain, and wireless and mobile multimedia electronics systems. Delphi Delco Electronics employs approximately 30,000 people worldwide. For more information about Delphi and its operating subsidiaries, visit Delphi's Virtual Press Room at www.delphi.com/media/.

The College of Engineering and Applied Sciences is a dynamic community of researchers and educators in engineering and construction nestled in one of the nation's fastest-growing public universities. For more information on the engineering programs at ASU, visit our website at www.asu.edu.
