Improvement Initiatives

Campus Planning. Due to intense renovation and expansion of academic and research space on campus, ASU is projected to lose 5,000 existing parking spaces on the Tempe campus are over the next five years. At the request of President Crow, ASU launched a Parking and Transit Task Force this semester (Fall 2005) and is charged with creating an innovative and fiscally responsible parking and transit plan that includes student input and is in support of the ASU Master Plan.

The goal of the ASU Parking and Transit Task Force is to create a parking and transportation plan for our future that is inclusive, creative, fiscally responsible and forward thinking.

The Task Force has conducted survey’s and focus groups with student and employee’s as well as numerous department and college representatives in order to obtain community insights and guidance into the draft of the new parking and transit plan.

The first draft report and presentation developed by the Parking Task Force is scheduled to be completed by the Spring Semester.

Technology. PTS exhausts many efforts to enhance and maintain overall safety on the Tempe campus. Lighting is a vital component of lot safety. In conjunction with ASU Facilities Management, PTS has aggressively worked to maintain positive lighting levels in all parking lots and structures. Lighting surveys and upgrades are conducted annually to verify adequate lighting levels. PTS spends $50,000 annually replacing burnt out bulbs and ballasts.

Capital Programs added solar panels to the rooftop of Parking Structure 2 in March 2005. The new technology powers all lighting in the structure and will eventually save thousands of dollars annually in maintenance and energy costs. Similar technology is being researched for other parking lots and structures on the Tempe campus.

Safety Escort Service (SES). Safety is important to ASU. As part of an integrated approach to increase safety awareness Parking and Transit Services partnered with ASASU to DOUBLE their service hours that enable them to provide service from 7AM - 3AM, 7 days a week to address community service needs during non-traditional hours.

The Safety Escort Service ensures students, faculty, and staff a safer alternative than walking alone to campus parking lots, structures, and residence halls, utilizing the idea of the "buddy system". In the form of a golf cart the Safety Escort Service will provide an escort for free from any on-campus location to another throughout the evening. Call 965-1117 for a scheduled pick-up.

Parking Lot and Structure Maintenance. PTS remains dedicated to maintaining our existing parking structures. Power washing enhances the aesthetic appeal and helps with the structural integrity by keeping expansion joints in good condition and the concrete from deteriorating thereby extending the life of the parking structures.

During summer break, PTS has various surface locations re-paved or seal coated to provide a safer parking environment. This maintenance also includes filling “pot holes”, repairing damage to curbs, re-striping stall lines, painting curbs and designated stalls such as green curbs/service vehicle stalls and loading zones.

Online Parking Services.  Over the years, customers have requested the ability to purchase decals and conduct on-line business over the web. PTS has responded to those requests. With the assistance of various on-campus departments, PTS has implemented a new parking management database, PowerPark, in spring 2004. The new Online Parking Service allows customers to manage their parking account on-line. Customers can view their account, purchase a parking permit, appeal a citation, or pay citations on-line.

Alternative Transportation.  On July 1, 2005, ASU began distribution of the Unlimited Access Bus Pass, which grants access to Valley Metro bus routes for free for eligible to ASU faculty, staff and students. The fall semester bus pass was valid July 1, 2005 to Jan. 16, 2006. The spring semester bus pass is valid Jan. 17 though June 30, 2006. Spring bus passes will be available beginning Jan. 3.

Students at the Tempe and all eligible participants at the West and Polytechnic campuses may pick up their bus passes at their respective campus parking offices (Decal Sales Office at 525 S. Forest for Tempe campus). A valid ASU ID is required to receive the pass.

Bicycle Friendly Campus. PTS partnered with ASASU to bring back to life the Bike Co-op at the Student Recreation Center during the Fall 2005 semester. The Bike Co-op provides students a low cost alternative to bike maintenance and repair with trained staff/volunteers on had to assist. PTS provided funding for the initial stock of supplies.

Another partnership with ASASU and PTS (Fall 2005) resulted in the opening of the USG Community Bikes. The program allows ASU students and employees to check out one of 15 ‘Phat Sea Breeze’ bicycles at the USG Bike Co-op, located on the south side of the Student Recreation Complex. An ASU ID and a completed USG Bike Waiver are required to check out one of the bikes for a two week time period. PTS funds were used to purchase the initial fleet of bikes, helmets and headlights.

Lastly, PTS launched a "Helmets & Headlights" safety campaign in September 2005 whereby the department sells bicycle and headlights to ASU students and employees at approximately 50 percent the retail cost to encourage SAFE bicycling. A helmet and headlight combo is available for $25!
