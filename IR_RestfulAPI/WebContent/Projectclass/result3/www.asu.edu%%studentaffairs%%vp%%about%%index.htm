Student Affairs Strategic Plan

The mission of the Division of Student Affairs is to provide the highest quality programs and services that support undergraduate and graduate education of ASU students. In fostering student success, Student Affairs provides access for every student to a holistic living and learning environment in collaboration with the broader university community. To fulfill this mission, the Division of Student Affairs is fully committed to enhancing student persistence and graduation. In support of its mission, Student Affairs will embrace the following foundational principles.

Foundational Principles

  •       Model and promote the valuing of human differences on individual, organizational, and community       levels.
  •       Align goals and strategies with the design imperatives of the New American University.
  •       Collaborate with academic partners to implement learner-centered student experiences and further       learning outcomes inside and outside the classroom.
  •       Establish ongoing funding mechanisms, settings, facilities, and services that support new and existing       initiatives.
  •       Develop integrated organizational systems that prioritize quality, effectiveness and efficiency.
  •       Provide technological solutions that support growth, creativity, and responsiveness.
  •       Engage in strategic assessment and quality assurance activities as an integral part of all efforts.
  •       Create coordinated communications to support the brand of the university and the division.

Goals

1.       Enhance access to and utilization of programs and services.

  •          Strategically market programs and services.
  •          Implement policies and procedures that reduce barriers.

2.       Strengthen the responsiveness and effectiveness of programs and services.

  •          Develop mechanisms for student input in the design and implementation of services.
  •          Incorporate best practices in program planning, implementation, and evaluation.

3.       Improve academic achievement and the personal development of students.

  •          Implement programs and services that contribute to student persistence and graduation.
  •          Engage students in out-of-classroom activities that contribute to students learning about themselves,          others, and their communities.

Strategic Priorities

  1. Develop and deploy comprehensive model of living and learning that supports expected “live on” of the freshman class and the goals of college and school living and learning communities (includes freshmen, sophomores, and some upper class students, i. e., Barrett Honors College, College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, and College of Design).
  2. Bring Residential Life to higher standards of performance, safety, appearance, and success.
  3. Evolve a consistent university-wide student government structure.
  4. Develop options for rapid development of Memorial Union and Student Recreation Complex on Tempe campus.
  5. Initiate a student success strategic plan focused on persistence and a graduation rate improvement to > 60% (6 year graduate rate).
