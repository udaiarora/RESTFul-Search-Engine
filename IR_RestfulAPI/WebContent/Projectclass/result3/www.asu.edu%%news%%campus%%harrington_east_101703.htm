October 17, 2003

Harrington to head ASU East learning communities

ASU East Academic Programs and Services has hired Vanessa Harrington as the campus's new director of Learning Communities.

Harrington moves to East from Main Campus, where she coordinated the Leadership Scholarship program.

David Schwalm, vice provost for academic programs, says the choice was easy based on Harrington's background and references.

"This is already a complex job, and its scope will grow dramatically as the campus grows," Schwalm says. "She has had solid experience in many aspects of the job. More importantly, she is smart, imaginative and eager and able to take on new challenges as the responsibilities of the position increase."

Harrington has quickly learned that she'll indeed wear many hats. She oversees the Learning Center, which offers tutoring assistance to ASU East and Chandler-Gilbert students; runs the Residence Life Leadership Award program, designed to foster leadership skills in exemplary residence hall students; directs the Faculty Fellows program, where faculty provide curricular support in the residence halls; and co-directs the Freshman Year Experience program.

And Harrington would like to add more to her plate. She hopes to increase scholarship programs and is working on plans for the creation of a larger Learning Center, based on planned moves and renovations in the Academic Center. Other areas that likely will fall under Harrington's purview are the planned University College functions at East and an Honors College component for ASU East. But the new space is among the most exciting projects ahead, she says.

"The vision for this space is tremendous because of the potential it has," she says. "Very rarely do you have the opportunity to build a learning community from scratch starting with such a large space."

Harrington holds a bachelor's degree from the University of Kentucky and a master's degree from the University of Dayton, where she worked prior to ASU. She is also a Ph.D. candidate in the ASU College of Education.

"This position was a wonderful fit for me because it put in place all the pieces of the puzzle for me. It's exciting to be part of a campus that's growing almost every day," she says.

Holeman, with ASU East, can be reached at (480) 727-1173 or (jholeman@asu.edu).
