Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

June 4, 2003

Members Present: Patricia Feldman, Carol Behl, Sue Hunter, Stefan Myhajlenko, 
Barbara Trapido-Lurie 

Absent: Carol Behl, Victoria Trotta

1. Minutes from the previous meeting, 5/14/03, were approved and will be posted to the CAPS website.

2. OLD BUSINESS

1.  Formation of task force to revise Academic Professional policies 

and procedures

In the past week, Vice Provost Gail Hackett called a meeting to discuss how to move forward with creating the task force.  Participants were Patricia Feldman, Victoria Trotta, and Karl Weiss, Kurt Murphy and Lynne Carpenter.  Major points discussed were:

-  Dr. Hackett will appoint the task force, including representatives from Main, East and West campuses, and including individuals with both continuing-track and year-to-year appointments. 

-  Since PACAPS has been inactive for two years and many of its members? terms have expired, Dr. Hackett will reorganize the group. The group will include a representative from each College of the University.  Members are appointed, not elected. 

- Regarding the relation between the task force, PACAPS and CAPS in carrying out the charge to revise AP policies: 

- The task force will be an active work group and the over-arching committee for the project.

- PACAPS will also be an active work group, and will be the channel for input from all APs.

- CAPS will assemble formal recommendations, and will present them to the Vice Provost for Personnel/Academic Senate. 

-  After task force and PACAPS members are selected, Dr. Hackett will call a joint meeting the task force, PACAPS and CAPS to deliver a charge to the three groups.  Members will most likely be contacted within the next week or two. 

  1. Proposal to organize open forums for all APs

The committee (CAPS) agreed to hold off on this activity until the task force is 
formed and the 3 groups are given their charge.

 3. NEW BUSINESS  

Committee organization for next year

-   Goodbye and thank you?s were given to Stefan Myhajlenko for his service over the past 
3 years on this committee.  His term is complete.

-  The committee welcomed it?s newly elected member, Barbara Trapido-Lurie.  
She will begin her three-year term in July.   Barbara has served this year as an alternate 
member and the committee is grateful for her service in 2002-03.

- The committee welcomed Beth Luey who was on sabbatical this year but will begin 
the second of her three-year term.

-   Sue Hunter was elected to serve as chair for 2003-2004.

-  Because the current meeting time conflicts with teaching commitments, an 
alternate meeting time was discussed.  The standard meeting time will likely be moved to Tuesday or Thursday.

 - CAPS was adjourned for the 2002-2003 year.

Respectfully submitted, Barbara Trapido-Lurie, Acting secretary

Minutes were approved 6/16/2003
