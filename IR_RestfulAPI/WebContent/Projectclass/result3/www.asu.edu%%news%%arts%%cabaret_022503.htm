February 25, 2003

'Cabaret' hits Gammage stage Feb. 26

The revolutionary reinvention of John Kander and Fred Ebb's hit musical "Cabaret," winner of four 1998 Tony Awards including Best Revival, is coming to Gammage Auditorium Feb. 26 - March 2.

This production brings the seedy glamour of the legendary Kit Kat Klub to Phoenix, after being the talk of London and New York. "Stunning" raves the New York Times, "'Cabaret' has returned with a vengeance!"

"Cabaret" is based on the true-life observations of Christopher Isherwood who wrote, "The Berlin Stories" about his experiences in Berlin between 1929 and 1932. Playwright John van Druten turned one of these stories, "Sally Bowles," into the play "I Am a Camera," which was produced on Broadway in 1952 starring Julie Harris, who repeated her role on the 1955 film version. Playwright Jo Masteroff had the idea of developing the story in a musical and brought it to the attention of Harold Prince, who contacted the songwriters, Kander and Ebb.

With music and lyrics by Kander and Ebb, and book by Masteroff, "Cabaret"tells the story of an English woman's romance with an American writer set against the background of a crumbling Germany at the start of the Third Reich. "Cabaret" features some of the best known songs in musical history, including "Willkommen," "Maybe This Time," "Money (Makes the World Go Around)" and "Cabaret."

Kander and Ebb, who won the 1998 Kennedy Center Honors for achievement in the arts, are two of the most successful and prolific collaborators in the American theater. In addition to "Cabaret," they are currently represented on Broadway with the award-winning revival of "Chicago."

"Cabaret" is presented by arrangement with ASU Public Events and Clear Channel Entertainment, Inc., and is a special offering of the 2002 - 03 M&I Bank Broadway in Arizona series.

Ticket prices are $21.75 - $57.75. Performance times are 8 p.m., Wednesday - Friday; 2 p.m. and 8 p.m., Saturday; and 2 p.m. and 7 p.m., Sunday; Feb. 26 - March 2.

For information about "Cabaret" call ASU's Gammage Auditorium Box Office at (480) 965-3434, Ticketmaster at (480) 784-4444 or visit (www.asugammage.com). For groups of 20 or more call (480) 965-6678.
