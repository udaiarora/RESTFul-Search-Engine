September 23, 2004

Researchers receive outstanding technical paper award

ASU associate professor Nikhilesh Chawla and his research team were the recipients of the 2004 Metal Powder Industries Federation (MPIF) Outstanding Technical Paper Award. The paper, which was selected among hundreds that were presented at the June Powder Metallurgy & Particulate Materials International Conference in Chicago, investigated the fatigue crack growth behavior of ferrous powder metallurgy alloys.

“This award is quite an honor for our research group, the Fulton School and ASU,” says Chawla, who also serves as director of the Mechanical Behavior of Materials Facility.

Powder metallurgy processed components are increasingly being used in automotive materials. Typically, ferrous powders are mixed and pressed, and then sintered – a process that forms a coherent mass by heating without melting. The advantage of this manufacturing approach produces near-net shape components, which lower costs. Chawla’s group was the first to conclusively establish that nickel-rich regions in the microstructure are detrimental to fatigue resistance. The finding is important because it will enable the design and fabrication of novel materials with higher strength and fatigue performance. The research was sponsored by Hoeganaes Corp. in Cinnaminson , N.J.

“I am happy that our work at ASU has been recognized on an international level, and it serves as proof that ASU is one of the best research institutions in the country,” says graduate research assistant George Piotrowski.

“The award is not only an honor for me, but for our whole Mechanical Behavior Research Group,” added research fellow Dr. Xin Deng. “Without the creative guidance of Dr. Nik Chawla, our research group leader, this achievement would not have been possible.”

Acknowledgement of the award will be published in the CD-ROM conference proceedings, “Advances in Powder Metallurgy & Particulate Materials – 2004”. In addition, the paper will appear on the MPIF Web site and will be published as the first paper in the January/February 2005 issue of the International Journal of Powder Metallurgy.

Chawla and his colleagues will be presented with the award at the 2005 International Conference on Powder Metallurgy & Particulate Materials in Montreal.
