Government Documents and Maps Department Highlights Presidential Materials


(posted 9/7/04)

 

To coincide with the Presidential Debate scheduled for October 13, 2004, the Government Documents and Maps Department is highlighting a large assortment of its materials related to the President of the United States. The display, entitled, “I Do Solemnly Swear. . . ” The Presidency: Executive Power and the People, is located on the upper concourse of the Hayden Library from September 6 through November 19, 2004.

As a Congressionally designated member of the Federal Depository Library Program, Arizona State University receives government information free of charge from the Government Printing Office. The Hayden Library is responsible for providing permanent public access to these materials


The items on display will focus on the Presidency from many different angles: from elections and inaugural addresses, to speeches recorded in the Public Papers of the President series; from Executive Budgets of the United States Government, to Presidential travel and diplomacy.


The display will also feature notorious presidential documents such as the Starr Report, Warren Commission Report on the assassination of President Kennedy and printed transcripts of the Nixon tapes. Other interesting presidential commission documents include an April 1989 report regarding Aviation Security and Terrorism, a 1970 report on campus unrest, and a 2003 report on Biotechnology and the Pursuit of Happiness.


Finally, the display will also highlight other notable materials from the Arizona Documents collections including older copies of Arizona Highways, and environmental plans for Light Rail, as well as early municipal General Plans and documents on the Rio Salado Project.


Everyone is encouraged to explore the complete Government Documents collection located on Level 3 of the Hayden Library during normal library hours. The Maps collection located on the 3rd floor of the Noble Science and Engineering Library is open Monday though Thursday 8AM-8PM and Friday 8AM-5PM, closed Saturday and Sunday.
