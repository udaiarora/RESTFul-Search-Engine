ASU GRADUATE COLLEGE WINS HUD AWARD FOR PROVIDING ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE TO TOWN OF GUADALUPE

Arizona State University’s Graduate College has won a Best Practice Award from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) for providing graduate students to assist the Town of Guadalupe, Ariz., with economic development projects. The Graduate College is one of 25 HUD award recipients in Arizona. HUD received nearly 3,000 applicants nationally.

Guadalupe is a community of 5,600 residents in the East Valley of the Phoenix metropolitan area. Celebrating its 25th year as an incorporated community this year, Guadalupe does not have the budget to hire full-time staff members to address all of its critical community and economic development needs. ASU is partnering with Guadalupe to select graduate students to provide such help on a part-time basis. One graduate student has already begun work, and a second graduate student will be selected later.

Romulo Gayoso is a research assistant at ASU who has already begun such work. The Fort Lauderdale, Fla., native is entered in a dual degree program at ASU, pursuing the Master of Science in Economics and the Master of Business Administration degrees.

Guadalupe is renovating its mercado retail shops building, and the community plans to open a new multipurpose center with a historical museum and library later this summer. Gayoso is developing plans to attract more businesses to the mercado building. In addition, he is working on historical archiving for the new museum and planning for changes in existing zoning ordinances that will aid economic development.

Students selected for the economic development project must be first-year graduate students pursuing degrees in either of three majors: economics, public administration, or business administration.

“The agreement benefits the town, the graduate students and ASU,” says Ric Gerakos, a community builder in the Phoenix office of HUD, who helped arrange this university/community collaboration.

“Everybody is bringing something to the table,” Gerakos said. “ASU is waiving tuition for the students involved, the Town of Guadalupe is providing the stipend, and the students are providing the work. This is all being done by forming a good partnership. It’s a win-win situation.”

Tom Callarman, associate dean in the ASU Graduate College, said, “The Graduate College wants to make this an ongoing project. We help to identify the students, we monitor the projects, and we provide some additional financial support.”

Guadalupe town officials select the graduate students from the pool of applicants. Town Manager Luis Gonzalez supervises the student work.

Gerakos said, “It’s really hard for a small town to get grants and to implement grants because they don’t have enough manpower to do the work involved. With the help of these students, the town will be able to do a lot of this work.”

HUD Best Practice Awards must be replicable in other areas of the country, region, or local jurisdiction and generate a significant and demonstrable, positive impact on those being served or managed.

The Graduate College and other Best Practice Award winners are now competing for geographical and program category awards with the possibility of advancing to a national competition level. The top 100 projects in the nation will be honored as the Best of the Best.
