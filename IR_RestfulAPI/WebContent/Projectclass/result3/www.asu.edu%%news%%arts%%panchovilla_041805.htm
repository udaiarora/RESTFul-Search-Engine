April 18, 2005

Emmy Award-winning filmmaker presents ‘The Hunt for Pancho Villa’

ASU’s Department of Chicana/o Studies (CCS) invites everyone for an event filled with cinema, culture and dialogue.

The second annual CCS OnStage benefit – a screening of the film “The Hunt for Pancho Villa” – will be presented at 6 p.m., April 27, at the Kerr Cultural Center. Its Emmy Award-winning producer and writer, Paul Espinosa, whose films have won numerous honors – including eight Emmys and five CINE Golden Eagle awards – will introduce the film.

Cordelia Candelaria, chairwoman of CCS, says the event is designed to increase understanding and awareness of the department’s programs and efforts in higher education. Candelaria says this event “took root” to become an annual fundraiser for the department.

“Participants will benefit through direct engagement with the world-class documentary filmmaker Paul Espinosa, who will introduce and discuss the making of the film before its screening on April 27, and also participate in a question-and-answer session afterward,” she says. “In addition, other faculty members will be on hand to discuss with participants their latest scholarly books and creative projects. And participants also will benefit through their charitable giving by knowing that they are contributing to special CCS initiatives and ongoing projects to enhance CCS.”

Proceeds from CCS OnStage will fund TALL (Teaching America’s Latino Legacies), a summer institute for teachers in many fields including education, human services, health fields, law enforcement and media; the three “R’s” of Chicana/o Studies (Research, wRiting, and outReach), which are special initiatives, including supervised student interns working as tutors in local schools, as well as scholarly activities and faculty research; and Transnational Leadership Pedagogy, a project dedicated to documenting and disseminating the vital work of great, often unheralded, leaders who have helped transform the 20th and 21st centuries.

A pre-screening reception will open CCS OnStage activities at 6 p.m. and will include displays of the work of CCS faculty and performance of border corridos (ballads).

The program also will introduce ASU’s Ocampo Quesada Student Research Scholars and Wells Fargo Latino Business and Community Scholarship recipients to recognize the department’s commitment to high academic performance.

The 2005 Ocampo Quesada Student Research Colloquium will take place from 1:30 – 3:30 p.m., April 27, on the Tempe campus, in the Education Building, room 236, after which the recipients will be honored at the CCS OnStage Kerr reception.

The Ocampo Quesada Scholarships, sponsored by an endowment established by the descendants of Wickenburg pioneers, are given annually and are open to students in all majors. The full-tuition Wells Fargo Scholarships are coordinated by the CCS Department and the Center for the Advancement of Small Business in the W. P. Carey School of Business.

For CCS OnStage tickets, call Ticketmaster at (480) 784-4444 or the Kerr Box Office at (480) 596-2660. For program information, contact the Chicano/a Studies Department at (480) 965-5091 or send an e-mail to (ccscronica@asu.edu).

Romero, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 727-3116 or (mlromero@asu.edu).
