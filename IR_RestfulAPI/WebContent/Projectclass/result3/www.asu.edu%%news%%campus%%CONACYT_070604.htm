July 6, 2004

Partnership yields new fellowships

ASU has established 75 fellowships through a new partnership with El Consejo Nacional de Ciencia y Tecnología (CONACYT) in Mexico. The fellowships will enhance ASU's position among other national and international research institutions, leading the way as the recipient of the most CONACYT fellowships in the United States.

"CONACYT's decision to award this amount of scholarships to ASU says a lot about the direction we are moving in with our engagements with Latin America," says Jorge de los Santos, director of the Office of Pan American Initiatives and special adviser to the president of ASU. "Leading institutions are realizing the great potential of this university."

The agreement also will lay the groundwork for developing interdisciplinary graduate programs, faculty and researcher exchange and strategic collaborative research, and open the door to future large-scale funding. The agreement was signed during the Partnership for Prosperity (P4P) Entrepreneurial Workshop, June 25-29, in Guadalajara, Jalisco, Mexico. ASU representatives led by Jonathan Fink, vice president for research and economic affairs, participated in the formal signing June 29.

Presidents Vicente Fox and George W. Bush initiated the P4P initiative to stimulate economic growth in North America. The 2004 P4P Entrepreneurial Workshop is designed to explore new business opportunities and partnerships in Mexico by bringing together business and government leaders to discuss growing opportunities in North America.

CONACYT, counterpart to the National Science Foundation in the United States, was created in 1970 by Ley del Congreso de la Unión. The group's mission is to explore and strengthen the efforts in scientific research in Mexico to the highest level by supporting and promoting the creation of research projects and the advancement of technology.

"The CONACYT fellowship selection process is very competitive," said Shannon Wulf, manager of strategic relations and planning, Office of Pan American Initiatives. "So we know ASU will be provided with some of Mexico's brightest students."

The graduate and doctoral fellows will come from various disciplines from universities all over Mexico. ASU will receive the students in the Fall of 2005.

For information about the Office of Pan American Initiatives, visit the Web at (http://www.asu.edu/opi) or call (480) 965-0880 or send an e-mail to (opi@asu.edu).
