June 12, 2006

Jackson wins NCAA title at 10,000 meters

After spanning 14 years between individual outdoor national champions, the ASU women's track and field team picked up their second in three years June 8, as Victoria Jackson won the 10,000-meter run at the 2006 NCAA Outdoor Track & Field Championships, which are being held at the Alex G. Spanos Sports Complex in Sacramento, Calif.

Jackson, the 2006 Pac-10 champion at 5,000 meters, set a school record while winning her crown as she covered the 25-lap event in 32:54.72, bettering Hastings' school mark of 33:17.81, which was set earlier in the season. Jackson, along with Hastings, hung back in the second pack as three runners set a fast pace early, only to see the two Sun Devils slowly creep ahead until Jackson claimed the lead for good while Hastings settled into fourth place. Both women earned All-America honors, giving Jackson two for her career and Hastings, who clocked in at 33:41.18, the seventh of her career and third of the year.

The win by Jackson not only marked the first national title for the senior, but also marked the first distance title for the Sun Devil program on the outdoor track. The longest distance ever yielding a national title before this was the 400-meter dash by Maicel Malone in 1990.

Jackson's title also is the second of the year for the distance corps, following Hastings' indoor 5,000-meter title won in March.

Before this latest race, the last time an ASU athlete won an outdoor NCAA title was in 2004, when Jacquelyn Johnson won the heptathlon as a true freshman.
