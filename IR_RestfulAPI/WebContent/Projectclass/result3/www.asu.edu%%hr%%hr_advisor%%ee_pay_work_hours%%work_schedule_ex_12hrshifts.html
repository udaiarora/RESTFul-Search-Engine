The use of 12-hour shifts during a 40-hour work week schedule would require three, 12-hour shifts and one, four-hour shift. The use of exclusively 12-hour shifts requires the use of a four-week cycle.

The first week, the employee is scheduled to work four, 12-hour shifts (48 hours) and is paid for 40 hours and earns eight hours of compensatory time that is equivalent to 12 hours. During the three remaining weeks of the cycle, the employee is scheduled to work three, 12-hour shifts (36) and is paid the additional 12 hours of compensatory time by four additional hours for each week.

A change may be required to the employee's work week with the implementation of a 12-hour shift schedule, depending upon the days the employee is scheduled to work and the days off. Vacation and sick leave will be charged 12 hours for each, up to a maximum holiday pay of 80 hours per year; after the 80 hours of holiday pay has occurred, the employee will be given time off for any holiday time worked.

During a typical holiday week, the employee will work 24 hours, and receive 12 hours of holiday pay and 4 hours of compensatory time unless the holiday falls during the first week of the four-week cycle.
