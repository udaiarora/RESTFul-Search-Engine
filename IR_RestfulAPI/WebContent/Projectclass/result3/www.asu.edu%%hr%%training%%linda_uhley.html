Human Resources - Professional Development

Linda Uhley

Linda coordinates and facilitates programs that span the work life of employees at ASU. She welcomes and orients new employees to ASU each week at New Employee Orientation. Linda provides professional development opportunities that enhance employee performance, such as "DiSC - Understanding Behavioral Styles" and "Communicating to Increase Understanding."

Linda promotes the recognition and celebration of employee contributions and commitment throughout the year as she coordinates ASU's Employee Recognition Program, which consists of seven award programs and celebrations. She facilitates a Writers' Briefing in December for employees who are applying for the President's Medal for Social Embeddedness and the President's Award for Innovation.

Linda's passion for clear, effective communication makes her a valuable contributing member of the Human Resources Transformation Project Communications Team. 

Previously, Linda coordinated the Amazing Administrative Assistance seminar and the Performance Management Leadership Seminar, and she facilitated Stepping into Leadership, "WHOOSH, Get Your Flyer off the Ground," and the "Work Skills and Values" session of the Discovery! series.

Linda received a Bachelor's degree from Azusa Pacific University and a Master's degree in English from California Polytechnic University, Pomona. Prior to coming to ASU, she taught English composition and literature at Cal Poly, Pomona, and Albion College.
