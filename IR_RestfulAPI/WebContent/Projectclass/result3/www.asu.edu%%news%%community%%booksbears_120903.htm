December 9, 2003

'Books and Bears' drive fosters lifelong love of reading

The annual Books and Bears Holiday Drive, which culminated Dec. 5 with the delivery of cuddly toys and popular children's books to Valley-area first-graders, promises to help the youngsters discover the joy of reading.

ASU's Bureau of Educational Research and Services within the College of Education has coordinated the project in cooperation with the Beta Phi Chapter of Kappa Delta Pi Honor Society and the Office of Professional Field Experience for more than a decade.

"We believe we are making an impact on our community by assisting first-grade students as they begin their journey toward a life-long love of reading," says Debora Muscat, secretary administrative with the Office of Professional Field Experience and a volunteer with the program. "We target schools that accept our student teachers and interns on a regular basis, and that serve either socio-economically diverse or under-represented students."

This year, the schools that benefited from the Books and Bears project were Herrera Elementary School in Phoenix and Salt River Elementary School in the Salt River Indian Community.

Each holiday season, volunteers collect donated books and bears from individuals within and outside the ASU community, and from local businesses and corporations that generously support the program.

The donated books and bears are paired, then wrapped and tied in red and green ribbons, and delivered to designated schools where they are presented to the youngsters. During the delivery, volunteers model how the children should read to their bears by sitting on a chair and reading aloud while the children listen and observe. Following the presentation, the students are encouraged to read to their bears back at their desks.

The College of Education Student Council and ASU Big Brothers and Big Sisters also are holding a holiday book drive. Donations are being collected at the Safeway supermarket located at the corner of Broadway & Rural roads in Tempe, at the Wal-Mart located at the corner of Main Street and Alma School Road in Mesa and at K-Mart located at the corner of Hayden and McDowell in Scottsdale.

Sherwood, with the College of Education, can be reached at (480) 965.2114 or (joan.sherwood@asu.edu).
