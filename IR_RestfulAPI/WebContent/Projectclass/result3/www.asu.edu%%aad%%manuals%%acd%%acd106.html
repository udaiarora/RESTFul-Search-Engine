ACD 106: Executive Vice President and Provost of the University and Vice Presidents

Purpose

To describe the responsibilities of the executive vice president and provost of the university and the vice presidents

Sources

Office of the President
President’s Working Group

Applicability

Executive vice president and provost of the university

Vice presidents

Policy

Executive Vice President and Provost of the University

The executive vice president and provost of the university reports to the president and acts for the president in his or her absence. Duties include extended administrative responsibility as delegated by the president, particularly supervision of the university’s policy development and preparation of budget requests and allocation of budget monies received through state appropriation. The executive vice president and provost of the university adjudicates among the claims of the several divisions of the university, making certain that the university’s best interests—as defined in the current University Mission and Scope Statement—take priority over the claims of individual components of the university. As the chief educational officer of the institution, the executive vice president and provost of the university is responsible for the effective operation of all academic programs including teaching, research, and supporting activities. The vice provost for information technology, the vice president for research and economic affairs, the deputy vice president/university dean of Student Affairs, the executive vice president for administration and finance, and the academic deans and directors report to this officer. Organizational units, in addition to the ten academic colleges and schools, include the Division of Graduate Studies, Craig and Barbara Barrett Honors College, College of Extended Education, Summer Sessions Office, and the University Libraries.

Executive Vice President for Administration and Finance

The executive vice president for administration and finance is the chief administrative officer for the university. This officer reports to the executive vice president and provost of the university and meets as a member of the tri-campus administrative officers to address university-level administrative activities. This officer is responsible for nonacademic support services on the main campus, including the physical facilities, public safety, and the fiscal and human resource programs of the university. Under the direction of the executive vice president for administration and finance are the associate vice president for Finance and treasurer, the Department of Facilities Management, the Human Resources Department, Internal Audit and Management Services, the ASU Department of Public Safety, Purchasing and Business Services, and Environmental Health & Safety. This officer is also responsible for managing investments and providing financial advice for a number of university special projects.

Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs

The vice president for research and economic affairs is the chief administrative officer responsible for the research programs and other sponsored activities of the university. This officer provides leadership in the development of policies and procedures that will enhance the research mission while protecting programs and the interests of the university. This officer reports to the executive vice president and provost of the university. The vice president for research and economic affairs is responsible for the Office of Sponsored Projects Fiscal Management, the Office of Research and Creative Activities, the Office of Technology Collaborations and Licensing, the Office of Radiation Safety, Research Publications, the Institutional Review Board, and the Animal Care Program. This officer administers in-house research grant programs and other research funds, represents the university to external funding agencies, and works to foster the development of research programs and cooperative research arrangements with organizations and agencies outside the university.

Deputy Vice President/University Dean of Student Affairs

The deputy vice president/university dean of Student Affairs supervises the service/program agencies of the Arizona Prevention Resource Center, Career Services, Counseling and Consultation, the Memorial Union, Campus Recreation, Residential Life, Student Development, the Campus Health Service, Student Life, and Student Media. This officer reports to the executive vice president and provost of the university. Services and programs supported within Student Affairs are based upon human development theory that advocates development of the whole person—physically, psychologically, morally, socially, culturally, and intellectually. These services and programs are accomplished through a combination of supportive advisement, personal consultation, skilled assessment, assertive intervention, efficient service delivery, and environmental management.

Vice President for Public Affairs

The vice president for public affairs is the chief advancement officer for the university. This officer reports directly to the president and works with other academic and administrative units to gain the external support necessary for the university to attain new levels of excellence. The vice president for public affairs is responsible for planning, coordinating, and implementing all programs in development, governmental relations, alumni relations, public information, and community relations. Some specific responsibilities of this officer include administration of the Alumni Association, KAET-TV, and Media Relations and Public Information.

Vice President for University Undergraduate Initiatives

The vice president for university undergraduate initiatives oversees Undergraduate Admissions, University Registrar, Student Financial Assistance, and Undergraduate Initiatives Technology Services. This officer reports directly to the president. This officer designs and develops university enrollment goals that enhance student quality and diversity and accommodate enrollment growth. This officer develops and oversees institutional efforts to build and transmit a university identity and message for student recruitment and directs efforts to create a seamless university experience for students within and between campuses/programs.
