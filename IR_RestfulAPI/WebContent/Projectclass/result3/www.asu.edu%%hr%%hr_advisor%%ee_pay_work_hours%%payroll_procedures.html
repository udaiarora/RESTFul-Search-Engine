Non-exempt Employees:
Time Worked and Overtime
Employees are responsible for reporting actual hours worked on a weekly basis. Supervisory or departmental designees must review and/or approve time off, compensatory time earned, overtime worked or reduction in pay each pay period.

Compensatory Time Earned vs. Paid Overtime
Per SPP 404-04: Non-exempt employees cannot work more than 40 hours during a work week unless the additional work is ordered and approved in advance by the supervisor.

Based on departmental policy, non-exempt employees who work more than 40 hours must receive either:

  • Compensatory time earned: Report hours earned on Hours Adjustment (HA) form (at 1.5 hours for each 1 hour worked over 40 hour/work week) by adding hours to comp time balance field. See page 15 of the Payroll Representative Training Manual.
  • Overtime paid: Report hours to be paid on an Additional Payment (AP) form. Per SPP 404-04, a non-exempt employee must actually work more than 40 hours in a work week to earn overtime time at the premium rate (1 hour multiplied by 1.5 premium rate).

Example: A non-exempt employee physically worked 44 hours in a work week—or four hours over. Departments that pay overtime should submit an AP form for four hours using earnings type OTP (for premium pay at 1.5 rate). (To record compensatory time earned, submit an HA form.)

A non-exempt employee who has actually worked 40 hours or less in a work week but whose combined work and paid leave hours exceed 40 hours, shall receive overtime pay at the standard rate (1 hour multiplied by 1 standard rate).

Example: A non-exempt employee physically worked 36 hours in a work week that included one holiday of eight hours—or four hours over. Department must submit an AP form for 4 hours using earnings type OTS (for standard pay).

Non-exempt Employee Overtime Reporting
All overtime payments to non-exempt employee must be recorded using one of the following overtime payment earnings types using the AP form or the HA form:

  • OTS
    Overtime at a straight time rate
  • OTP
    Overtime at a time-and-a-half rate
  • OT2
    Overtime at a double-time rate

AP forms and HA forms (with directions) are available on the web and are submitted to the HR Employee Service Center. Additional payments will calculate the total hours multiplied by the hourly rate.

Procedures for Non-exempt Employee Overtime



WHO: Departments
WHAT:

Use the work-week calendar to determine if overtime payments are necessary. Complete an AP form for each employee to receive overtime payments.

WHEN:

 By 9am on the Exception Reporting deadline date for each payroll

REMINDER: In calculating overtime, do not include non-productive time such as vacation, sick, holiday or compensatory time used.
