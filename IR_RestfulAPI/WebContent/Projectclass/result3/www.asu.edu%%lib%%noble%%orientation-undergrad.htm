The ASU Libraries

There are 7 ASU Libraries within 4 organizational units:

  • The University Libraries are composed of 4 libraries on the Tempe campus: Hayden, Noble, Architecture and Music.
  • College of Law Library, also on the Tempe campus,
  • Polytechnic campus library, and
  • West campus library

All libraries are open to the general public as well as to ASU students, faculty and staff. The Noble Science and Engineering Library is described in detail below. For more information about each of the other libraries see: ASU Libraries: Specific Libraries (http://www.asu.edu/lib/libraries/)

Daniel E. Noble Science and Engineering Library

 

Noble Library houses the collections for Engineering, Mathematics, Nursing and the Sciences (Astronomy, Biology, Chemistry, Geology, Physics) and includes the Maps Collection.

 

The library is located on the Tempe campus, on the southwest corner of Tyler and McAllister Malls, across the sidewalk from the Cancer Research Center and the Goldwater Center.

 

Opened in 1983 and remodeled in 1999, the library encloses a four-story atrium that creates a spacious and pleasant atmosphere for study and research. Facilities include over 60 library workstations, wireless access to the campus network, plus open and enclosed study spaces for both groups and individuals.

The library has 5 service units with approximately 30 staff:

  • Access Services (Circulation/Reserve)
  • Copy Services
  • Instruction, Marketing and Outreach
  • Maps Reference
  • Science Reference Services

Checking Out Material

  • How do I get a Library Card?
    Your SunCard (ASU ID card) is used to checkout materials. Forget your card? - Show us a picture ID.

  • How many items may I check out? ... For how long?
    There is no limit on the number of items an undergraduate student may check out. Students may check out items for 14 days; items can be renewed until requested by another library user.

  • Are Fines Charged?
    For each item not returned or renewed within thirty (30) days of the due date, the borrower is assessed a $10 overdue billing fee and a replacement charge. If the item is returned after fines are assessed, the replacement charge is cancelled but the $10 billing fee remains.

  • The Book I need is checked out - how do I get it recalled?
    In the ASU Libraries Online Catalog, use the blue REQUEST button to have the item recalled. There is no charge for this service.

  • Can I check out a journal volume?
    Sorry, journals must be used in the library.

  • For more information see Access Services (http://www.asu.edu/lib/access/)

Document Delivery

  • The Item I need is in the Polytechnic Library or the West campus library - do I have to go there to get it?
    No, the University Libraries' will retrieve or photocopy items from other campus libraries.

    • For books:
      In the ASU Libraries Online Catalog, use the blue REQUEST button to have the item brought to any one of the Tempe campus libraries' circulation desks. There is no charge for this service. Delivery Time: 2-3 business days.

    • For journals:
      Use the Library Express/Document Delivery photocopy request form. A photocopy of the article will be emailed to you. There is no cost for this service. Delivery Time: 2-3 business days.

  • The Item I need is in Storage - where's that?
    The Libraries' operate a separate facility on the Polytechnic campus for overflow material and items that need special handling. The facility is not open to the public as the material is packaged and maintained in a special, temperature- and air-controlled environment.

    To obtain a book from storage, use the blue request button when viewing the item's record in the ASU Libraries' Online Catalog. Delivery times vary depending on the time of day or day of the week; you may call 480-965-3605 to determine when your request will arrive - usually within 1-2 days.

    To obtain a journal article from storage, use the Interlibrary Loan Request Form -- the article will be delivered via email.

  • The Item I need is not available at any ASU Library - can the library get it for me?
    Yes, use the Interlibrary Loan Service. You will be notified by e-mail when the item is available. Most photocopies come directly via e-mail; books must be picked up at the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. Usually there is no charge for this service, however, if there will be a cost involved, you will be contacted for approval, prior to the request being ordered. Delivery Time: Journal articles average 1 week, while books average 2 weeks. Materials that are rare, in fragile condition, in high use, or non-circulating in most libraries, may take considerably longer or may not be able to be acquired. Rush service and renewals for books are generally not available.

Copies

  • How much do Photocopies cost?
    Photocopies cost $0.10/page if using cash; $0.08/page if using a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

  • Can you make copies of microfilm or microfiche?
    Yes, copies cost $0.15/page if using cash; $0.12/page if using a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

  • How much do computer prints cost?
    Computer prints cost $0.08/page and requires a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

Computing

  • Can I use electronic journals and databases from my home?
    Yes, to access Library Resources from off-campus, always go to the resource via the Libraries' web site; you will be prompted to sign in with your ASURITE ID and password. Once authorized you will be taken to the resource.

  • How do I get a Library PIN?
    To assign yourself a PIN, go to the Libraries' Home Page and select "My Account" in the upper right hand corner. The Library PIN is needed to access your library account.

  • I forgot my Library PIN - What Do I Do?
    Go to the Circulation Desk in any ASU Library. Your current PIN will be erased from your record, so that you can select a new one.

  • Do the library computers have word processing? ... Spreadsheets? ... Other software?
    Yes, the ASU Libraries have workstations with the MS Office software: WORD, EXCEL, PowerPoint, Publisher, Front Page, and Access. Look for the workstations with the GOLD screens - you'll need to log-in with your ASURITE ID and password. For other types of software, such as statistical or graphics software, please see the Information Technology Computing Sites.

Library Assignments

  • I have a library assignment and I have no idea where to begin. Can anyone help me?
    Yes, the science reference staff will be able to take the frustration out of that library assignment! We'll recommend what resources to use and show you how to use them. Find out where science reference is located and how to contact them.

  • My instructor says I can only use scholarly (or peer-reviewed) journal articles as my references. How can I tell if an article is scholarly or peer-reviewed?
    Being able to evaluate the quality and authority of information is an important part of the research process. Sounds like your instructor wants you to be able to distinguish research from the popular literature. Several clues can help you determine if an article is from a scholarly journal. Scholarly articles are usually:
    • Written by experts in the field
    • Written for researchers and professionals in a particular field
    • Written to report the results of a research study
    • Reviewed by experts in the field
      A group of experts have determined that the article is scholarly and that the research is valid; this process is called "peer review" or "editorial review". Check the journal (front or back pages if in print; journal home page if electronic) for an Editorial Board; the "Instructions for Authors" or the "Guidelines for Submission" may also indicate if articles are reviewed.
    • Lengthy and contain bibliographies and/or footnotes.

    For more information about distinguishing scholarly from popular literature, see SunTutor: How to Evaluating Sources.
