May 2, 2005

President’s Recognition Reception honors award recipients

Eighty recipients of the President’s Medal for Social Embeddedness, the President’s Award for Innovation and the top multiple SUN Award winners were honored by ASU President Michael Crow at the President’s Recognition Reception April 19 in the Alumni Lounge. The following is a list of the award winners.

SUN Awards

The SUN Award for Individual Excellence is a peer recognition award that provides specific and immediate recognition to fellow employees for demonstrations of individual excellence in such areas as creative activity, continuous improvement, fostering cooperation, providing exemplary service, valuing diversity and promoting ASU.

In 2004, more than 1,500 SUN Award certificates were presented to ASU employees.

Four individuals were selected as ASU’s 2004 Top Multiple SUN Award Recipients: Lindy Baker, Student Affairs, West campus; Shona Bodai, Human Resources, Tempe campus; Clinton Everhart, Student Affairs, East campus; and Christy Robinette, Office of Research and Sponsored Projects Administration.

President’s Award for Innovation

Two teams received the President’s Award for Innovation. This award recognizes ASU individual employees or teams for innovations that improve educational, administrative or other organizational processes through creative approaches. The innovation is an original program, project, initiative or technique that has been implemented and has generated results.

Library Evolution 3: Microsoft to Linux Migration

Fletcher Library has migrated 71 public workstations and 14 support servers from a Microsoft operating system and architecture to a Linux operating system and open source/open systems architecture. This project enables librarians to customize research and instruction applications, to reduce technology-related costs and staff time, and to increase reliability. Team members include Perry Horner of Fletcher Library and David Boynton, a former ASU employee.

University Libraries’ ‘Video Courses Online’

The libraries designed and created “Video Courses Online,” a unique, automated streaming digital video system that requires almost no staff time to manage. The new system serves students better by letting them access their courses 24 hours a day from any Internet-accessible computer. Team members include Rob Fidler, Matthew Harp, Heather Knowles, Philip Konomos, Kirk Manegold, Fred McIlvain, Maura Pollock-Moneyhon, Ginny Sylvester and Stephen Tanner.

President’s Medal for Social Embeddedness

Three teams received the President’s Medal for Social Embeddedness. This award recognizes ASU departmental, interdepartmental or multidisciplinary teams that have identified community needs and issues and then developed partnerships with the community to provide solutions.

Club ASU

Club ASU connects underserved students in grades 7 – 12 and their families to ASU by inviting them to free club-sponsored events. Club ASU also fosters an understanding that higher education is achievable. Since its launch in the fall of 2003, Club ASU has played host to more than 3,000 students and parents from communities all over the state. Team members include Tom Collins and Gene Smith of Intercollegiate Athletics; Ken Hollin of Undergraduate Admissions; Janice Kelly of Academic Community Engagement Services; James Rund of University Undergraduate Initiatives; and Nancy Jordan, Steve McGillivray, Shawna Tibbett and Anna Wales of the Office of Public Affairs.

‘Kids and Asthma: A Report to the Yuma Community’

The Health and Disability Research Group in the W. P. Carey School of Business examined childhood asthma in Yuma County , Ariz. , and produced “Kids and Asthma: A Report to the Yuma Community.” This report is the product of a unique partnership of researchers from ASU and employers, health care providers, legislators, educators and public health leaders from Yuma County . The information is being used by the community to develop intervention programs aimed at improving preventive health care services for children with asthma.

Team members from ASU’s School of Health Management and Policy include: Wade Bannister, Miwa Edge, Anthony Garcy, Tameka Jackson, William G. Johnson, Michelle Malonzo, Mary E. Rimsza, Matthew Thibault and Rebecca White.

Community partners include: Terry Eisenberg and James Rice, Alhambra Elementary School District; Charlotte Boyle and Deb Huffaker, Creighton Elementary School District; Emilia Matos and Julian Villanueva, Pediatric Associates of Yuma; Art Lebowitz and Jim McElroy, Phoenix Union High School District; Katie McKenzie and Marion Williams, Roosevelt Elementary School District; Becky Brooks and David Brooks, Yuma County Department of Public Health; Hugo Aguirre, Cheryl Bell and Todd Hirte, Yuma Regional Medical Center; Peggy Lund and Shirley Rodriguez, Yuma School District One; Amanda Aguirre, Regional Center for Border Health; Toni Badone, Yuma Union High School District; Steve Bell, Yuma HealthQuery; Robert Cannell, Arizona Senate; Kim Cirelli, Women’s Health Specialists; Michael Coleman, EXCEL Group; Daniel Crawford, Yuma Pediatrics Ltd.; Cathy Eden, Arizona Department of Health Services; Dave Hoovestol, Southwest Emergency Physicians; Roger Hughes, St. Luke’s Health Initiatives; Tricia J. Johnson, Rush University; Paul Muthart, Pasquinelli Produce Co.; Donna Phipps, Arizona Public Service; William Read, Flinn Foundation; Whitney Reel, Sunset Community Health; and Anthony Rodgers, Arizona Health Care Cost Containment System.

Learner-Centered Leadership for Language and Culturally Diverse Schools in High-Needs Urban Settings

Since October 2002, the Division of Educational Leadership and Policy Studies in ASU’s College of Education has been implementing an emerging professional development program for school leaders in collaboration with the Southwest Center for Educational Equity and Language Diversity and four urban school districts in the Phoenix area. During the past two years, the Learner-Centered Leadership program has accomplished the following:

• Identified, recruited and selected 32 prospective administrators for a master’s degree in educational administration and supervision, and an administrative certification program. Twenty-seven of the 32 graduated in December.

• Identified, recruited and selected 35 rising administrators who have been receiving personalized, one-on-one mentoring as well as access to inter-district resources and knowledge as they implement action plans that focus on professional growth related to specific site needs.

ASU team members include: Gerald Blankson, Arnold Danzig, Barbara Erickson, Trisha Fritz, Kay Hunnicutt, Seok Kang, Gary Kiltz, Kathy Langerman, Donna Macey, Azadeh Osanloo, Anne Marie Read, Terry Wiley and Byeong-keun You, Educational Leadership and Policy Studies; and Josue Gonzalez and Elsie Szescy, College of Education.

Community partners include: Terry Eisenberg and James Rice, Alhambra Elementary School District; Charlotte Boyle and Deb Huffaker, Creighton Elementary School District; Art Lebowitz and Jim McElroy, Phoenix Union High School District; and Katie McKenzie and Marion Williams, Roosevelt Elementary School District.

For more information about the awards program, visit the Web site (www.asu.edu/hr/programs/erp/employee_rec_programs.html).
