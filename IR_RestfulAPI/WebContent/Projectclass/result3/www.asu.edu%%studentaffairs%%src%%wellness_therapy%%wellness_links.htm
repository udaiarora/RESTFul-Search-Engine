Wellness Links

The SRC is committed to providing you with wellness resources. Below is a list of wellness websites to provide you with even more information. The SRC and ASU are not endorsing or guaranteeing the validity of the information contained within these sites.

General health issues

www.healthfinder.gov

U.S. Department of Health and Human Services site. Offers national health information.


www.cdc.gov

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention - US Government site for communicable diseases


www.mayoclinic.org/healthinfo

Health and medical information


www.lungusa.org

American Lung Society site. Information about lung health, conditions, and treatments.

 

www.nationalbreastcancer.org

Breast Cancer Foundation site. Information about prevention and treatment of breast cancer.


www.americanheart.org

American Heart Association site. Information on heart health.


www.prevention.com

Cooking, nutrition, health and fitness tips.

 

 

Get fit


www.active.com

Provides listings on upcoming sporting events and information on training for various sports.


http://www.exrx.net/index.html

Resource not only for the fitness professional but also the fitness enthusiast. Includes information on fitness (muscles, guidelines, exercise prescription/program design) and nutrition (guidelines, tips, supplements), etc.

 

 

Healthy food

 

www.wholefoods.com

Lots of info on healthy and organically produced food. Also includes a magazine with recipes and articles!

 

 

Skin care

 

www.aad.org

American Academy of Dermatology site. Provides information on healthy skin, hair, and nails.
 

www.skincarephysicians.com/melanomanet/index.html

Part of American Academy of Dermatology site. Provides information about skin cancer. You can determine you risk for skin cancer.
 

www.cancer.org

American Cancer Society site. Provides information on preventing and treating different types of cancer (including skin cancer).

 

 

Break bad habits

 

www.gamblersanonymous.org
This site starts off with 20 questions to see if you (or someone you know) has a problem. This organization offers free help to anyone who asks for it.

www.quitsmokingsupport.com
A site devoted to helping you kick the habit.

 

 

Eating Disorders

 

http://www.nationaleatingdisorders.org/p.asp?WebPage_ID=337

National Eating Disorders Association site. Information on eating disorders such as anorexia and bulimia. Focuses on the hows, whys, and treatments for eating disorders.

www.something-fishy.org
The something fishy website on Eating Disorders. Focus on this site is with personal stories of real people battling eating disorders.

www.about-face.org
This site attacks negative images of women in U.S. culture and offers suggestions for empowerment to counter these attitudes.
