Librarians' Handbook Changes Pending Administrative Approval


For a list of changes which are now official, see the Revision Notices page.

Below are changes that have been approved by Librarians' Council but are still awaiting administrative approval. When approval is received, the changes will be made in the Librarians' Handbook and the changes will be noted on the Revision Notices page.

Summary of Changes
Section G.1.2: Supplementary Evaluation

Revision

  • Supplementary evaluations are required for all probationary academic professionals who have library instruction and/or collection development responsibilities. Academic professionals who have continuing appointment are required to have written supplementary evaluations every three years, although goals need to be approved annually.
  • The supplementary evaluation process follows the Schedule of Personnel Actions issued by the Assistant Dean for Personnel.
LIBRARY INSTRUCTION AND COLLECTION DEVELOPMENT SUPPLEMENTARY EVALUATIONS

  1. The primary evaluator solicits the supplementary evaluations from the Head of Instructional Services and the appropriate Collection Development Coordinator.

 
Section H.1.2: Travel Fund Procedures Revision

  • APPLICATION FOR FUNDS

Fund availability governs the deadline(s) for travel requests. Although there may be multiple deadlines, to ensure complete consideration applicants should project travel plans for the year as early as possible and submit them by the first deadline. Using the "Application for Travel Funds: Academic Professionals" form or the "Application for Travel Funds: Classified Staff" form (as appropriate) present requests in writing to the Research and Professional Development Committee, including appropriate documentation (travel dates, program information, estimated costs, etc.).

  • DIRECT PAY FOR AIRFARE

The Library Business Office can pay airfares directly, providing the traveler uses American Express Travel on campus. After making travel arrangements, forward the TA/Claim form to the Library Business Office for payment. The travel agency will either notify you to pick up your ticket, or deliver it directly.
Section C.2.5: Compensation Committee Revision

  • POLICY AND PROCEDURES STATEMENT

The Compensation Committee is one of the Standing Committees of the Librarians' Council. It consists of five (5)  three (3) members elected for terms of two years. The terms of the elected members are staggered with one or two or three members to be elected each year. All Council members are eligible for this committee.

  • PROCEDURES

At the first meeting after the election of new members the Committee will choose a chairperson and a secretary.

All members of the Compensation Committee are voting members. All decisions require the presence of at least two three voting members.
