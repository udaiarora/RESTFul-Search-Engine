Danielle - Photography

"I want to be a forensic photographer. ASU supports my goals and nurtures me as a person."

Danielle's major is Photography with a minor in Justice Studies. According to Danielle, "Most people don't realize how much law enforcement and other specialists work on our behalf. There is so much being done in the field behind the scenes." For summer 2006, Danielle has been awarded the Washington Internship for Native American Students where she will gain experience working for a government agency.

One of Danielle's mentors, Julianne White, an instructor in the English Department, teaches a forensics writing class. Danielle speaks highly of her: "She has really helped me develop my knowledge. We look at cases and their outcomes from different perspectives. She enables me to better express my ideas and opinions. We also discuss the latest research."

Danielle celebrates her heritage through her photography as well as events such as ASU Pow Wows. "I am excited to discover who I am as a person and the contribution I can make to society."
