Response to Report of the Child & Dependent Care Task Force and Recommendations regarding Family Friendly Policies

To:      Child & Dependent Care Task Force

From:  Milton D. Glick

Date:   May 12, 2004

Re:      Response to Report of the Child & Dependent Care Task Force and
           Recommendations regarding Family Friendly Policies

It is imperative, if we are to truly transform ASU into the New American University, to develop policies and practices that acknowledge the parental and family responsibilities of our employees and promote workplace productivity.  With that in mind, I charged a Task Force in the summer of 2002 to identify issues, analyze practices, and set priorities for addressing the range of child and dependent care needs and issues of students, staff, and faculty at ASU.  The Task Force issued its report in the Fall of 2003.  The charge, committee roster and the full report are posted at http://www.asu.edu/provost/reports.

My responses to the Task Force report are largely in accord with the recommendations put forward by the Task Force, and are contained in the attached table.  In addition, a proposed temporary alternate duty assignment policy is being developed to allow more flexibility in workload assignment for employees with parental and/or dependent care responsibilities; for pregnancy, childbirth, or adoption issues; and/or personal/family health-related needs. 

During the time the Task force has been working we have also been moving forward on various practice and policy recommendations that are designed to promote a family friendly work environment at ASU.  Specifically:

  • The Working@ASU web site has been created;
  • Procedures for partner assistance in hiring have been implemented (see attached guidelines);
  • The Office of University Evaluation, in partnership with the Graduate College, has conducted a survey of graduate student child/dependent care needs and priorities; and
  • A consultant has been hired to conduct a feasibility study for Child Care Facilities as part of the campus master planning process.

I wish to commend the Task Force, and in particular the co-chairs, Elizabeth Swadener and Maureen Duane, on a very thorough and well considered analysis and series of recommendations on a topic of the utmost import to the future of this University.  I am very pleased that ASU is taking these steps towards better realizing our aspirations of being a premier family friendly employer.
