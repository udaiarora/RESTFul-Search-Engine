Technical Services Department


Accompanying Material


As the University Libraries are committed to acquiring an ever-widening array of materials to best serve the needs of our users, so is ASU Technical Services committed to ensuring the best access to that material in the most expeditious manner. To that Technical Services has carefully examined what at times has been a knotty issue and arrived at the following objectives, goals and procedures.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 OBJECTIVES

Standardize the Technical Services portion of procedures among locations and formats

Retain bibliographic integrity; items must be cataloged properly.

Avoid spending time and resources on multiple procedures for anomalous items.

  GOALS

Simplify internal procedures.

Receive decisions with materials.

Eliminate returns to Technical Services and otherwise prevent delays in getting items to the shelf.

Mainstream the exceptions without devising multiple procedures.


PROCEDURES


 Monographs 

  1. Catalog as separate pieces with separate item records those things capable of being shelved separately.


    Pieces that are cased/received together as a single piece will be cataloged as such with an item message reflecting the holdings.


  2. Preservation, ASU West and ASU East will have the capability to create/modify item records in order to handle any changes that arise after the pieces have been cataloged.


Serials


  1. At check-in add issue-specific note in check-in box noting accompanying material.


    Include note in Holdings File as you would a supplement.


    Label accompanying material with either call number and volume identification or bar code number and or Science stacks/journal locations, the volume is processed to keep accompanying material with it.


    All locations (including reference areas) can return accompanying material to Holdings Maintenance for withdrawal, or a different type of processing.


  2. For titles regularly accompanied by a type of material, Serial Acquisitions will try to get a decision for that title and record the decision in their records. Mary Swaty will be notified by email if a note should be added to the bibliographic record.


Periodicals

  1. At check-in add issue-specific note in check-in box noting accompanying material.


    For CD-ROMs, insert in a jewel case; label jewel case with call number as a supplement to the print issue.


    Send print issue and jewel case with disk together in a campus envelope to the public services destination.


    Discard CD-ROMs that are merely promotional material (advertisements, trial products).


  2. Periodicals regularly accompanied by a type of material will follow procedure outlined under Serials, No. 5 above.


 

MISSING PIECES

Missing or lost accompanying material will not be searched for or replaced.
