October 14, 2003

Courtyard Cafés Gala offers evening under the stars

Diverse music, great food, silent and live auctions with dozens of exciting purchase opportunities up for bid - it all adds up to a fun evening at ASU West's Courtyard Cafés Gala, 6 p.m., Oct. 18.

More than 500 community leaders are expected to attend the sixth annual event that spotlights the dynamic growth of ASU West and the West and North Valley, while providing much-needed scholarship support for ASU West students. Fay Fredricks from Channel 12 News will serve as emcee.

"While Courtyard Cafés has developed a well-deserved reputation as one of the Valley's most entertaining and enjoyable galas, the true significance of the event is the transformation of students' lives through scholarships," says Carol A. Poore, ASU West vice provost of public affairs. "Scholarships are particularly important for ASU West students, half of whom represent the first generation in their family to pursue a college degree."

All Courtyard Cafés proceeds are earmarked for scholarship funds. Thanks to the generosity of the RORD Foundation, in honor of Marie Sands, the Courtyard Cafés scholarship fund is growing even faster. RORD has issued a challenge grant that matches Courtyard Cafés proceeds dollar-for-dollar, up to $25,000.

Additional funding comes from this year's Courtyard Cafés presenting sponsors, Arizona Public Service Co., The Arizona Republic, DMB Corp., and Salt River Project.
Courtyard Cafés activities begin at 6 p.m. with a reception and catered dinner, followed by music and dancing in courtyards around the campus. Entertainment will be provided by four popular Valley bands - the Steamin' Javalinas, Carmela y Más, the Guerrero Family, and the Diva Band.

The Steamin' Javalinas are the ultimate party band for fans of classic rock and pop. Their repertoire also features newer pop/rock hits and exciting originals by band member Art Martin. Carmela y Más is a perennial Phoenix club and festival favorite with a range of infectious music from salsa to swing.

The Guerrero Family plays traditional and contemporary Latin music and features violin, guitar, accordion and charango. The Diva Band features the vocals of Carmela Ramirez and Susan Phelan; their repertoire includes classics of jazz and rhythm and blues.

Native American flutist Randy Kemp will provide entertainment during the pre-dinner reception. In addition to his musical talents, Kemp is a nationally known visual artist.
Musical entertainment during dinner will be provided by ASU West Interdisciplinary Arts and Performance Department faculty member Jeff Kennedy and student Chele Watkins.

Courtyard Cafés tickets cost $175. Table sponsorships for 10, which include recognition in the program cost $1,750. To purchase tickets, or for information about Courtyard Cafés, call (602) 543-5303.

Crum, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or (matthew.crum@asu.edu).
