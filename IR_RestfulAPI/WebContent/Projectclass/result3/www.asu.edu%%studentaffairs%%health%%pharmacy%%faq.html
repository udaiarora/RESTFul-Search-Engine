Frequently Asked Questions

We will be happy to answer any questions relating to Student Health Pharmacy, medications, pharmacy practice, drug interactions, pharmacy rules and regulations, you name it! It's easy to do!
Simply the Pharmacy Manager or fill in the form below. Good questions with broad relevence and their answers will become part of this page. All electronic correspondence will be kept confidential and the FAQs will, of course, be anonymous. Thanks for participating!

Some frequently asked questions...

Q. What do you have?

A. We are a formulary-based pharmacy. That means we stock a specific supply of drugs based on a formulary developed by our Pharmacy & Therapeutics Committee and our Medical Section for our specific patient population. The ASU Campus Health Service formulary is constantly undergoing review and modification to meet the changing needs of our patients and to take advantage of newer more effective medications. This means that your Campus Health Service clinician will usually prescribe a formulary product when possible. For your convenience, we also carry a variety of non-prescription products.

Q. Can anyone get a prescription filled at Campus Health Service Pharmacy?

A. Anyone with a valid prescription can have their prescription filled at ASU's Campus Health Service Pharmacy.

Q. So if I'm an ASU student I can get any prescription filled at the pharmacy?

A. Almost. Policy and procedure established by Campus Health Service allows us to fill any prescription valid in the state of Arizona, at the discretion of the pharmacist. Remember, though, that we do have a formulary, so we don't stock the variety of products that you would find in a retail pharmacy. However, we are happy to order non-stocked items that can be despensed as an entire unit.

Q. I've heard you have very low prices. Is that true?

A. Very often it is! We are active participants in the Minnesota Multi-state buying group as contracted by the State of Arizona. This large buying group of state institutions has tremendous buying power! We strive to ensure that every item in our formulary is part of this bid process and we are often successful. Additionally, we have a single, nominal dispensing fee that is administered without regard to the cost of the drug. Take a look at prices!

Q. Can I use my prescription insurance card at the Pharmacy?

A. Not at this time. Students with the United Healthcare ABOR plan get a 5% discount on presctiptions.

Q. Can I charge it?

A. YES! You may allow Campus Health Service charges, including prescriptions, to default to your student account. If you wish, you may pay by check, Visa, or MasterCard on the day of service. We also accept Sun Dollars.

Q. Do I really need to see the doctor every time I need a refill on my prescription?

A. That is up to your clinician. The pharmacy can generate a refill request for prescriptions filled at Campus Health Service Pharmacy. The clinician will then approve additional refill(s) or request that you make an appointment. This process usually happens in a matter of hours, but you should allow one working day to avoid any inconvenience.

Q. Can I drink alcohol with ________ ? (we had several different queries)

A. Alcohol does not mix well with many prescription drugs, and is, itself, a very potent drug. Many drugs can potentiate the effects of alcohol, some drugs have severe consequences if mixed with alcohol, and alcohol can decrease or increase the effectiveness of other drugs. Your pharmacist should warn you if your prescription requires that you avoid alcohol. In any case, you should ask your pharmacist about any concerns that you might have about your specific prescription. I know that I would be happy to answer any such questions.

Q. A recent plea... "This afternoon, my girlfriend and I were engaged in sexual intercourse. We used a condom, but it broke. We did not realize that it had broken until after I ejaculated. She is at the middle of her menstrual cycle, which we have heard is the most likely time to become pregnant. We have also heard about this "morning after pill" in the paper. I have read a little about it on the internet. I know we need to act within 72 hours. I don't know what to do. How do we go about getting the pill? Do we have to see a doctor? Please help!!! We would both appreciate it. Signed, Worried"

A. The "morning after pill" is available at Campus Health Service. It must be prescribed, so yes, you must see a clinician. The prescription will actually be for a product called Plan B. The cost of the prescription is $21. Start by calling the Student Health and Wellness Center appointment line at 480-965-3349.
