December 2, 2003

Free community classroom series explores the arts and society

Bring a friend and enjoy a lively evening discussion about Frederick Douglass and two women - his wife and his mistress - when ASU launches its spring series, ASU to You: A Free Community Classroom Series, at Tempe Public Library on Jan. 13, 2004.

The evening's discussion will be coordinated by award-winning author Jewell Parker-Rhodes, who is artistic director and chair of ASU's Center for Creative Writing. Parker-Rhodes' most recent book, Douglass' Women: A Novel, as the name implies, focuses less on Douglass the abolitionist and advisor to President Abraham Lincoln, and more on the two women in his life. The book has won several awards and brings to life Anna Douglass, a free woman of color who was Douglass' wife of 44 years and Ottilie Assing, a German-Jewish intellectual who was his mistress.

The lecture is the first of a series of three free community classroom events in spring 2004. ASU to You is a joint program of the Herberger College of Fine Arts, College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, City of Tempe Cultural Services and Friends of the Tempe Public Library.

The series of guided discussions will explore the dynamic dialogue between the liberal arts and society. Other discussions this spring will feature the search for other life in the universe and a retro journey through the era of the Sixties. The discussions will take place from 6:30-8 p.m. on Jan. 13, Feb. 10 and March 9. Tempe Public Library is located at 3500
S. Rural Road. Members of the public may call the Herberger College at (480) 727-6639 for more information about the events, or visit us online at http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/ and click on "Things to Do."

6:30-8 p.m., Jan. 13, 2004

Jewell Parker-Rhodes, Artistic Director and Chair of the Center for Creative Writing

Douglass' Women: Reimagining Women's Lives

The author discusses her award-winning novel that reimagines the lives of an American hero, Frederick Douglass, and two women - his wife and his mistress - who loved him and lived in his shadow. Anna Douglass, a free woman of color, was Douglass' wife of 44 years, who bore him five children. Ottilie Assing, a German-Jewish intellectual, provided him with the companionship of the mind that he needed. Jewell Parker Rhodes vividly resurrects these two extraordinary women from history, portraying the life they led together under the same roof of the Douglass home.

6:30-8 p.m., Feb. 10, 2004

Laurie Leshin, Professor of Geological Sciences

Searching for Life in the Universe: Is There Anybody Out There?

How common are solar systems like ours? Where, other than Earth, might there be life in our own solar system? How are we trying to find life out there? What can we learn about life from studying our own planet's history? These questions are central to the emerging field of Astrobiology, the study of the living universe. Come hear the latest ASU and NASA research on this fascinating topic!

6:30-8 p.m., March 9, 2004

Mila Parrish and Kathy Lindholm Lane, Herberger College Professors of Dance

Retro-Journey through the Interactive Gateway

Interactive Gateway is an ongoing research project that immerses high school students into the complex and controversial time period of the Sixties. The vehicle is an interactive, online curriculum that unites social, historical and artistic events and ideas with improvisation and dance-making. Take a retro journey with us to see, discuss and experience this dynamic decade.
