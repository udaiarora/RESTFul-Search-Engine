Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting (Spotlight Series)

Wednesday, May 10, 2006, MU Fiesta Room, 10 - 11:30 AM

Task Force Members Present: Barbara Trapido-Lurie, Victoria Trotta, Kurt Murphy, Deg Farrelly, Judy Grace, Lisa Hawkos

 

Additional CAPS members present: Beth DiFelice, Dan Stanton

 

Purpose: CAPS held an open forum to seek feedback from the AP community on the proposed structure and composition of the University Promotion and Continuing Appointment Review Committee.

 

Background information:

  • President Crow wanted P&T processes to be uniform across campuses
  • AP should also follow consistent procedures
  • Historically, West campus has had one committee reviewing both faculty and AP P&T
  • Trying to establish procedure not policy (ACD 507 sets policy)

 

Topics not resolved to date:

  • Committee make up
  • How individuals are identified to serve on the committee
  • Faculty are appointed to the P&T committee through nominations to the senator of the college (as opposed to the dean generating the slate)
  • Assumption is that only continuing appointments would vote on CA cases, all members on promotion cases
  • When Downtown campus comes into play
  • Life span of the document/procedure (suggested revisit in spring 2007)

 

Regarding how individuals are identified to serve on the committee:

  • University administration makes final selection, based on input from Deans and APs ? this is an administration mandate
  • Two proposals for developing slate of names to present to the administration:

1. CAPS to solicit names from non-librarians

Library deans would solicit names from librarians

This improves the method by which library slate is constructed

2. CAPS solicits names from all APS and conducts an election.  Since West campus is not represented on CAPS, APs at west develop a slate independently.

 

These are appointed not elected.

 

Discussion focused on the following alternatives/concerns:

  • How to guarantee campus representation
  • Whether the focus should be on function (pedagogy, research, etc.) or campus
  • Whether unique committees should be set up for each case
  • Whether a pool should be constructed from which members could be pulled depending on what cases were brought forward
  • How to guarantee diversity of methodology/framework/various position responsibilities, cultural differences of each campus
  • Who would be able to vote for representatives (by campus or function or both)
