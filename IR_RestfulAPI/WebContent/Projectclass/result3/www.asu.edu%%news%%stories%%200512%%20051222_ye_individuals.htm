December 22, 2005

Year brings accolades for ASU individuals

Subhash Mahajan, professor and chair of ASU’s Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering in the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, was elected to membership in the National Academy of Engineering, one of the most prestigious honors awarded to an engineer.

Mahajan’s appointment brought to 17 the number of ASU members of the National Academy of Engineering, the National Academy of Sciences and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.

Other individual honors included:

• ASU President Michael Crow was selected to join the prestigious Council on Foreign Relations.

• Margaret “Peggy” Nelson, a well-respected anthropology professor and associate dean of the Barrett Honors College, was named the 2005 Professor of the Year by the ASU Parents Association.

• Jeffrey Chapman, interim dean of ASU’s College of Public Programs, earned one of the university’s highest accolades: Foundation Professor of Applied Public Finance. This professorship recognizes Chapman’s exemplary research and teaching abilities in the field of public policy and administration.

• Peterson Zah, a key leader in Native American government and education, received an honorary degree from ASU May 12. The honor for Zah, an adviser to the ASU President on American Indian Affairs for 10 years, drew more than two dozen tribal leaders from across the nation to ASU’s springcommencement ceremony.

• Four exceptional professors who have made outstanding contributions to undergraduate education at ASU were the first to become President’s Professors. Awardees were Randall Cerveny, professor of geography; Alice Christie, associate professor of technology and education; Ian Gould, professor of chemistry and biochemistry; and the late Paul Rothstein, associate professor of industrial design.

• ASU electrical engineering professor Vijay Vittal took the helm of the Power Systems Engineering Research Center (PSERC), a collaboration of 13 universities dedicated to power systems research.

• Robin Cook, administrative assistant with the Human Resources Employee Assistance Program, was named ASU’s employee of the year. Cook was the inaugural winner of the award.

• Sudhir Kumar, director of the Center for Evolutionary Functional Genomics in the Biodesign Institute at ASU, joined the elite ranks of researchers with the most citations. Kumar was the sixth-highest ranked researcher in the field of computer science (out of 1,826 authors) over the last decade, according to the Institute for Scientific Information’s (ISI) Essential Science Indicators.
