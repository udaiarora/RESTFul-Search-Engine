May 14, 2003

Bernstein joins NSF, Allison to serve in interim

ASU Graduate Dean Bianca Bernstein will serve the next two years as director of the National Science Foundation's Graduate Division. Maria Allison has been appointed interim dean of the ASU Graduate College.

Bernstein says she is looking forward to working with NSF.

"I will have the opportunity to influence the national agenda on graduate education, which will surely help ASU, as well as all other universities," she says. "Expanding my contacts in the national and international networks of decision makers and academic and agency leaders will be advantageous for ASU during my tenure at the NSF and upon my return to the university. I look forward to participating in a very exciting phase of re-conceptualizing graduate education, working among a group of extremely talented and accomplished national leaders and learning much more about the world of competitive funding."

Milton Glick, executive vice president & provost, said Bernstein's appointment "is very good for ASU, just as the Woodrow Wilson National Fellowship Foundation Responsive Ph.D. initiative was two years ago. This positions us very well in Washington. It's a very exciting time for Dean Bernstein and for ASU."

With Bernstein's NSF appointment, ASU has appointed two new deans to serve in the Graduate College.

Allison will serve as interim dean and Filiz Ozel will begin her duties as associate dean for academic programs, effective May 16.

Allison joined the faculty in the recreation management & tourism department in 1984. She became associate dean for academic programs in the Graduate College in 2001. Allison earned her Ph.D. at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign.

Ozel has been a member of the faculty in the School of Architecture, in the College of Architecture & Environmental Design, since 1995. Formerly the assistant director of the School of Architecture and the interim director of the interdisciplinary Ph.D. program in Environmental Design & Planning, Ozel has advised graduate students on theses and dissertations, served as a graduate student mentor and taught research methods and numerous other graduate-level courses.

Graduate programs have greatly expanded during Bernstein's eight years as dean. ASU has added 12 new degree programs, seven certificate programs and seven concentrations in that time. ASU has created 17 new interdisciplinary programs and is tied for the national lead with three Integrative Graduate Education Research and Traineeship (IGERT) programs. The Graduate College has continued to make program quality a major priority, evidenced by rewarding outstanding doctoral mentors, coordinating Teaching Assistants orientation, funding the Research Conference Award Program and the Millennium Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowship awards.

"I am very proud of what we have accomplished in these eight years and remain appreciative of the opportunity to serve the university in this capacity," Bernstein said. "We have raised the visibility of graduate education in a national context, and I look forward to taking ASU's best practices to Washington, D.C."

Bernstein will direct a budget of $140 million and oversee numerous graduate award programs at the NSF. Bernstein will also serve as the incoming president for the Western Alliance of Graduate Schools (WAGS), for 2003 - 04. ASU will host the 46th Annual General Meeting of WAGS March 5 - 7, 2004. The conference will feature workshops, topical discussions and presentations, among other activities.
