E-Communiqué
Vol. 2.9, April 5, 2005

The E-Communiqué is published monthly with notices of upcoming deadlines, clinics and seminars, and other matters related to graduate education at ASU. Please visit the Graduate Calendar or the What's New page for information on events not included in this bulletin.

2005 Outstanding Doctoral Mentor

Dr. Marjorie Zatz, Associate Dean at DGS, was honored as this year's Outstanding Mentor by the Graduate and Professional Students Association (GPSA). The award recognizes her long-standing and deep commitment to the support of ASU's graduate students.

Spotlight on Diversity

Added new to diversity accomplishments for 2005-06 is the successful recruitment of competitive student in DGS' Reach for the Stars and Doctoral Enrichment Fellowship Programs. Academic Units nominated underrepresented students highly sought after in their chosen field of study. To learn more about the fellowships, go to www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/ReachForStars and www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/DGSenrichfellowship.

Check out more of the latest DGS news at www.asu.edu/graduate/aboutus/news.


—ANNOUNCEMENTS/REMINDERS—

Starting Fall 2005, academic units will be able to enter requests for RA/TA benefits into GPortal. An official announcement is forthcoming.

Graduate Grants-in-Aid are available for summer tuition. Students should have their 2004-2005 FAFSA information and an ASU summer aid application on file, www.asu.edu/fa. Contact Catherine Lebihan, clebihan@asu.edu, to have your file reviewed.

Beginning Fall 2005, the Underrepresented Graduate Enrichment Match (UGEM) 1st Monday's Brown Bag will become an actual course, GRD593: "Transdisciplinary Research: Theories, Methods and Applications," which will meet the 1st and 3rd Monday of each month. UGEM nominations for 2005-06 are still being accepted. (UGEM is formerly STEP-UP). www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/ugem.

Social and Academic Mentoring program (SAM) is now accepting nominations for 2005-06. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/SAM

Please forward the names and IDs of any students who are taking classes and employed as TAs or RAs this summer but were not TAs or RAs during both the fall and spring semester to Catherine Lebihan, clebihan@asu.edu, as quickly as possible so that they will be charged resident tuition for the summer. Students who were TAs or RAs in Fall 04 and Spring 05 already are coded as residents, so please do not send their names.

Fall 2005 Standard and Early Teaching Assistant/Associate Orientation webpage has been updated. Please remember to print out this webpage on award letters for new TAs. www.asu.edu/graduate/tara/TAorientation.html. Some funds remain for Spring 2005 Graduate Tuition Fellowships. Please forward names and ID numbers to Catherine Lebihan, clebihan@asu.edu, no later than Fri., April 8.

The Summer Work Study Assistantship program is now being administered by the Student Financial Assistance Office rather than the DGS. For more information, visit www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/summer. For questions, contact the Student Employment Office at (480) 965-5186.

The pass/fail form must be hand-delivered to Student Academic Services (SAS) within 10 working days of an oral defense. If the student has any revisions, a photocopy of the form should be sent in place of the original until the student completes the necessary revisions. Please make sure that the appropriate box is marked on the form and a description of the revisions is included either in the appropriate space on the form or in an attached memo.

If a graduate supervisory committee chair, co-chair or member will not be able to attend a scheduled oral defense, please contact SAS for the proper procedures to obtain a substitute. If it is crucial for a committee member to participate in a defense via teleconferencing, the student must submit a Petition to the Division of Graduate Studies form, available at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms, prior to the defense.
