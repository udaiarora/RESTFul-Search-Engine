Historic Partnership Prepares Chinese Executives for Global Competition

W. P. Carey Shanghai MBA Graduates First Class of Business and Government Leaders

An elite group of top-level executives and government leaders will mark a historic moment in Shanghai on June 4 when the class of 2005 graduates from the groundbreaking W. P. Carey MBA Shanghai. The class of 64 high-level business and government leaders will emerge from the two-year program and continue critical leadership roles as China increases its position in world markets.

The W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University is the first U. S. business school to partner with the People's Republic of China (PRC) to establish a first-of-its-kind MBA program for senior government officials and executives who oversee Shanghai's financial markets and large state-owned businesses. The impact of the program will have far-reaching effects, as China moves its global trade agenda forward.

The class represents a cross-section of China's most prestigious executives and government leaders, including CEOs from Shanghai Airlines and Baosteel, as well as executives from financial institutions, utility companies, high-ranking government and regulatory agencies and Shanghai city government.

"This unique partnership between ASU's W. P. Carey School and the People's Republic of China demonstrates our drive to practice what we teach by developing international partnerships in education and creating innovative approaches to growing the global economy," says W. P. Carey School of Business dean, Robert Mittelstaedt.

The W. P. Carey MBA Shanghai was developed in cooperation with the Ministry of Finance, and is delivered in collaboration with the Shanghai National Accounting Institute. The Ministry of Finance sought an alliance with the W. P. Carey School in developing the Shanghai program because of the school's success with the W. P. Carey MBA Beijing program. Launched in 1998, W. P. Carey MBA Beijing prepares high potential Chinese managers for advancement at Motorola China.

According to Dr. Buck Pei, associate dean for Asia Programs, "Shanghai is the economic and financial center of China, and it's where technology, bio-tech software engineering and other high growth sectors will evolve. To take its place in the global marketplace, China must prepare its state-owned businesses to succeed internationally. The W. P. Carey MBA Shanghai brings international business theories and practices to the leaders who will guide ChinaÕs global business development."

Unlike online degree programs offered in China by other U.S. universities, the W. P. Carey MBA Shanghai is delivered face-to-face in Mandarin and English by professors from top U. S. business schools.

The faculty is comprised of internationally renowned W. P. Carey scholars, including Nobel Laureate, Dr. Edward C. Prescott, the W. P. Carey Chair of Economics and a senior monetary advisor at the Minneapolis Federal Reserve Bank. The W. P. Carey faculty leads a teaching team that includes professors from other major U. S. business schools, including MIT, New York University, Stanford and Yale.

"This program embodies the principles of my vision of ASU as the new American University - a 21st century institution that taps into opportunity and creative energy here in Arizona and around the world," says ASU President, Michael Crow. "ASU has set a new standard for global engagement for American universities."

W. P. Carey School benefactor, Wm. Polk Carey, founder and chairman of W. P. Carey & Co. LLC, and Finance Minister of the PRC, Mr. Renqing Jin, will attend the graduation, along with ASU President Michael Crow and representatives from the Shanghai National Accounting Institute.
