Fulton excels on football field, in life

On the field, starting Sun Devil wide receiver Skyler Fulton is known for making big plays. Off the field, this ASU senior majoring in elementary education is known for making a difference.

"I feel good about what I accomplish in football," Fulton says. "But I am even more proud of things that I am able to accomplish away from sports."

Fulton stays busy with team practices, meetings, games, classes, homework, tests and student teaching. And he still finds time for babysitting three days a week.
Even with his hectic schedule, Fulton still maintains at 3.32 grade-point average. For the second year in a row, Fulton has been awarded a spot on the Pacific-10 All-Academic Football Team.

When it comes to success, whether academic, athletic or in his personal life, he says it's all about maintaining priorities and working hard. Fulton learned these values from his parents while growing up in Olympia, Wash.

"Without the many positive influences in my life I wouldn't be here today," Fulton says. "I want to be a positive influence in the lives of young people."
Fulton says he was raised to appreciate the importance of friends, family and community.

"My parents taught my brothers and I that life is about how you treat other people," Fulton says. "As long as you are honest with yourself and others everything else will take care of itself."

He grew up excelling in both baseball and football, but says his parents taught him to be well rounded and to give by working with others. Fulton says he considers teaching the ultimate chance to give back.

"I want to help kids believe that they are worth something and that they can be something, even if nobody in their family, or that they know, ever has," he says. "Teaching provides a chance to give kids confidence. It's important to be something positive in their lives."

He is currently in the third block of the education program, and is preparing to student teach this spring at Webster Elementary School in a second grade classroom.

"I am really looking forward to it," he says. "I really just enjoy spending time with the kids. After ASU I plan on playing ball for a while, but I also plan on teaching and coaching. It is going to be in my future for sure."

His favorite teaching assignment is babysitting a cute little one-year-old named Malik, who is the son of Fulton's good friend, Sun Devil Defensive End Jimmy Verdon and his wife Fran.

Fulton describes little Malik as "awesome" and says babysitting him is a total blast.
"Malik is such a happy baby, it is just a joy to watch him," Fulton says. "His parents Fran and Jimmy are great parents and they do a great job too. I am just glad that they let me be a part of their family."

Fulton says that in order to have time to accomplish all the goals important to him, he has had to become sort of a time management expert.

"I try to plan my days and get my school work done at the beginning of each week so toward the end I can begin to focus more on football," he says. "I don't really go out that much, but I hang out with my friends when I don't have homework or I'm not tired. I guess its just having your priorities straight."

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
