October 12, 2005

And the Nobel Prize winner is.....

EDITOR'S NOTE: British playwright Harold Pinter was awarded the 2005 Nobel Prize in literature Oct. 13.

Who will win the 2005 Nobel Prize for Literature?

Matt Rodgers wagers on Salman Rushdie. Stephanie Moos is certain that it will be Pramoedya Ananta Toer, an Indonesian writer. Michael Farkosh and Ted Cross argue for American novelist Philip Roth.

And Justin C. Burns even suggests songwriter Bob Dylan.

Students in Elizabeth Horan’s Literatures and Cultures of the Americas class at ASU have predicted, as the first part of their midterm work, who will win the 2005 Nobel Prize for Literature, and why.

Horan’s experimental class focuses on Nobel laureates in literature from the Americas.

Each of the students, including exchange students from England, a graduate student from Turkey, and students from all over the United States – including several honors students – made their pitches for their picks during two class periods, and fended off doubters among their classmates.

Here are their favorites, and their native countries (several students picked the same author):

• Salman Rushdie, India/Pakistan/United Kingdom/ United States .

• Pramoedya Ananta Toer, Indonesia .

• Philip Roth, United States .

• Bob Dylan, United States .

• Carlos Fuentes, Mexico .

• Margaret Atwood, Canada .

• Adonis (real name Ali Ahmed Said), Syria/France.

• Assia Djebar (real name Fatima-Zohra Imalyene), Algeria .

• Orhan Pamuk, Turkey .

• Ismail Kadari, Albania .

• Don DeLillo, United States .

Any student who correctly predicts the winner will receive an “A” as a midterm grade.

For more information, contact Elizabeth Horan, ASU Professor of English, at (480) 965-3535 or (elizabeth.horan@asu.edu).
