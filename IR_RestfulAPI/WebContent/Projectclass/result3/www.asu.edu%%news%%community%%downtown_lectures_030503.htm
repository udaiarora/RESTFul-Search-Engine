March 5, 2003

ASU Downtown Center hosts diverse lecture series in March

In March the ASU Downtown Center will be presenting a collection of lectures that focus on Women's Recognition Month, Aloha Fridays, diversity and workplace issues.
Each of the lectures will be presented from noon to 1 p.m., on assigned weekdays, at the ASU Downtown Center, 502 E. Monroe St., Phoenix. A complete list of the free lectures can be found at (www.asu.edu/xed/lectures). To R.S.V.P., call (480) 965-3046 or go online to (http://xedreg.asu.edu/).

The first lecture will be presented March 4 by Cinda Dior, an expressive arts therapist with Courageous Creativity, a Phoenix company that encourages the use of art as therapy. Dior will present "Bring it On: How to SuperCharge Your life With Prosperity," a presentation focusing on personal excellence as the most powerful of all self-practices, and a means to lift one's level of prosperity in all areas of your life.

"Everyone wants to be the best person they can be," Dior says, "and what I am planning to present is really an interactive workshop that addresses prosperity and discovering the true meaning of the word; so that others will learn about the art of becoming mindful and having gratitude."

Dior, whose company offers therapeutic art programs for schools and companies, says it's important for everyone to discover their own personal greatness.

"I think that more and more people are thinking about how to become more excellent in how they conduct themselves, their work performances and personal relationships, but I don't think people know where to begin to make changes for themselves," she says. "I love hearing what people have to say and responding to what their needs are. I think many people will benefit from my workshop."

For information about Courageous Creativity, call (602) 882-1444.

In addition to Dior's presentation, other lectures included in the ASU Downtown Center series are:

. "Diversity Excellence," presented by Christine French on March 6. French will inform participants on how one local company received national recognition for its focus on diversity best practices.
. "Aloha Friday: Tonga, the Last Monarch of the Polynesian Islands" presented by Marguerite W. Tafuna on March 7. This lecture will present a historical overview of Tonga and further discussion will compare and contrast ancient and modern life in Tonga as well as illustrate different aspects of Tongan culture.
. "Women's Recognition Month - Sister Nations: Native American Women Writers on Community" presented by Laura Tohe on March 12. Tohe, an accomplished and celebrated Native American author and ASU professor, discusses her most recent work on an anthology of women's voices from across the United States.
. "Aloha Friday: Tuna and Hina: The Creation Story of the Coconut Tree," presented by Emil Wolfgramm on March 14. This narrative will focus on the creation of the coconut tree, the most important tree of life in the islands.
. "Arizona's Native American Women: the Ordinary and the Extraordinary" presented by Carolyn Neinthammer on March 19. This lecture will explore the old ways of Native American life that have largely disappeared and discuss some of the women from Arizona's tribes who are today's leaders.
. "Into the Boardroom" presented by Katie Pushor on March 20. This lecture will focus on women's effectiveness in the boardrooms of America's largest companies traded on the NYSE, NASDAQ and AMEX exchanges.
. "Aloha Friday: Samoan Dance" presented by Tausala on March 21. This lecture will include a performance by the Tausala troupe that specializes in Polynesian dances such as the hula.
. "Women in Politics," presented by Robin Shaw on March 26. Shaw, a dynamic and motivational presenter who served in the Arizona House of Representatives, will discuss issues surrounding Arizona's political landscape.
. "Managing Your Own and Others, Anger at Work," presented by Lynne McClure on March 27. This lecture will address, among other topics, the eight destructive anger styles at work and why these styles don't work.
. "Aloha Friday: Hawaiian Dance," presented by Kaimikau's Hale O Hula on March 28. This presentation will feature a journey back in time to the roots of hula as presented by a world-renowned dance group that specializes in kahiko ("ancient") hula and chanting.
