Arizona Biodesign's Mobley is named to Arizona Bioindustry Association Board of Directors

TEMPE, Ariz. - Mike Mobley, associate director of ASU's Arizona Biodesign Institute has been named a director to the Arizona BioIndustry Association (ABA) Board.

In all ABA named three new directors. In addition to Mobley, ABA named Theresa Olson, director of operations for the Center for Translational Drug Development (a newly formed spin-off of Translational Genomics Research Institute) and Rada Tierney, public relations director for Physician's Choice of Arizona Inc., to the board.

"The ABA is fortunate to gain the talent and experience of these exceptional new directors," said Stephen Chavez, the ABA Board chairman. "The local bioindustry base in Arizona is growing rapidly, and it requires the leadership of high quality, knowledgeable biotech professionals. We couldn't be more pleased with their decision to join the ABA Board."

Mobley became the associate director for Arizona Biodesign in 2002 and serves as the point of contact between the institute and potential institutional and industrial partners. Prior to coming to ASU, Mobley worked in Proctor and Gamble's research and development organization for 21 years, serving as director of research and development for nine years.

The Arizona BioIndustry Association is a statewide organization that promotes the growth of the bioindustry in the areas of public policy, member services, education, business networking and entrepreneurial endeavors.
