G.2.3: Notification Process for Negative Recommendations

Effective 8/22/91 .. Revised 5/16/2001

PROCEDURES FOR NEGATIVE RECOMMENDATIONS:


If the primary evaluator cannot support the request, the academic professional should be notified immediately and the primary evaluator should be prepared to discuss the matter in specific terms. The Assistant Dean for Personnel should be available to act as a mediator if called upon. If the academic professional decides to send the request forward, the primary evaluator will sign the form, indicating non-concurrence with the request, and will write a negative letter of recommendation.


If the Associate Dean (where applicable) cannot support the request, both the academic professional and the primary evaluator should be notified orally and given the reasons for non-support. If the request goes forward, the Associate Dean will sign the form, indicating non-concurrence with the request, and will add to the packet a confidential letter to the Review Committee, giving reasons for the negative recommendation.


If the Review Committee cannot support the request, the chair will notify the primary evaluator orally, and will provide a general oral assessment of the strengths and weaknesses of the application. The primary evaluator notifies the academic professional in person. If the request goes on, the Review Committee writes a negative recommendation for the form.


If the Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian cannot support the request, he/she will notify the academic professional in person. If the request goes on, the Dean will record in his/her letter the reasons for non-support.


For university grievance procedures see ACD 509-03.
