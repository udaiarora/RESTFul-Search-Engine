Types of Scholarly Service

Academic Service Opportunities

In many of our academic colleges, service is a curricular experience. Students can earn 1-3 credits for your service while fulfilling your scholarship requirement through academic opportunities such as:
The Service Learning Program
While earning 3 hours of credit in a linked course in the humanities and sciences, you will tutor or mentor children weekly at sites around the Valley. You can meet requirements for such classes as First Year Composition and make a lasting difference in the lives of children while fulfilling your requirement!
Service in Classes
Other 1-3 credit hour service related courses found across the ASU campus include Service Internship in Leadership, Aging and Wellness and Social Action Research. Service related classes within communication, social work and education are also available and will help you gain key experiences!
College by College Service
Many colleges on campus support service through grants, activities and organizations in addition to classes. The College of Liberal Arts and Sciences offers Citizen Scholar grants to support you in creating your own service initiative while the Business College Council has adopted an entire school for the holidays, donating gifts and fun!

Long-Term Service Opportunities

Serving for a little over an hour a week during your fall and spring semesters is an excellent way to get your service requirement completed. Over 50 agencies within walking distance of campus and over 400 in the Valley are waiting to be matched with your talents and interests personally and professionally. Two examples of ways to meet your requirement include:
First Mentors with Valley Big Brothers/Big Sisters
You can serve as a mentor for an hour a week to a child in need. ASU has a student organization for Big Brothers/Big Sisters. Contact Big Brothers/Big Sisters for more information.
Court Appointed Special Advocates for Children
Receive training and work a few hours a week representing children's best interest in the court system. Learn about the law & society while meeting key people within the state of Arizona. Call 602-542-9683 for more information.

Service Clubs and Organizations

Currently, there are over 25 service-oriented student organizations on campus and many more groups make service a key part of their mission. Join a service group within your major or in an area of personal interest while meeting new friends, gaining key leadership skills and meeting your scholarship requirements.

For more information on clubs and organizations please go to the Service Groups web page.

Short-Term Service Opportunities

If you are interested in trying many different things but don't have the time to make a long-term commitment, short-term service is for you. One day, multiple day and week-long events are held which busy scholarship students find fit into their schedule and requirement nicely! Perks include provided transportation, food and fun for events such as:
Fall Service Plunge
Get started early on your service requirement and meet new friends with this fun, interactive day of service the first Saturday after school begins! This event is limited to freshman and new ASU students.
ASU Cares
Join faculty, staff and other students in an ASU day of service to the local community. Every March, the ASU community joins together to complete a service project and you are invited to join in the excitement and fun as we make a difference in our Valley communities!
Visit the CSP home page for more information on short-term service opportunities.
