Web Site Survey Welcomes Staff and Patron Input

The Libraries Web Development and Management Team has recently developed an online survey to find out what our users think of the libraries’ web site. This is part of a project to redesign the entire site. We encourage staff to fill out the survey too, so please help us in our efforts by taking a few minutes to respond to the survey at http://webcom.lib.asu.edu/webcom/pages/commentform.php (or follow the link from the library home page). You can also follow the Web Team’s progress at our web site, http://www.asu.edu/lib/digilib/webgroup/.


SOS Reminder

When transferring a phone call from a patron with a technical problem to LIST for assistance, please remember to use the SOS line, 5-9093. The SOS desk is staffed many more hours per day than the department reception desk, and SOS personnel will be able to help the caller with their issue. Calling SOS first will also save the caller from being transferred again or getting the department’s voice mail.


New Database Available

A new database has been put online (http://www.asu.edu/lib/top-menus/new.html):

Television News Archive (free IP based access)
This collection is the world's most extensive and complete archive of television news. It holds more than 30,000 individual network evening news broadcasts from the major U.S. national broadcast networks: ABC, CBS, NBC, and CNN, and more than 9,000 hours of special news-related programming including ABC's Nightline since 1989. These special reports and periodic news broadcasts cover presidential press conferences and political campaign coverage, and national and international events. Material in the archive can be identified for use through the TV-NewsSearch Database. You can request a loan of broadcast segments compiled on videotape. Fees apply for this service.


Ask LIST

Q. "Lately, a lot of the web sites I look at don’t seem to display correctly. Is there something wrong with my computer?"

A. This is probably a problem with your browser, not your computer. If you have seen web sites that appear arbitrarily cut off, that have strange fonts or font sizes, or that fail to display at all, you probably need to upgrade your browser. The current versions of Internet Explorer and Netscape Navigator (6.x and 7.x, respectively) support most of the latest features that web sites are being designed with. If you want to experience web sites the way their creators intended, you’ve got to keep your browser up to date. So please, if you’re still using Netscape Communicator 4.x, do yourself a favor and switch to Internet Explorer or download the latest version of Netscape! As always, Keith Swartz is available to help if you need assistance.
