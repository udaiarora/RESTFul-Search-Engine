DGS Names New Associate Dean of Graduate Student Programs

Dr. Andrew N. Webber, a professor in the faculty of Cellular and Molecular Biosciences and the Associate Director of Graduate Programs at the School of Life Sciences, has been named as the new Associate Dean of Graduate Student Programs. He assumed his position in Division of Graduate Studies on September 16. Webber came to ASU in 1989 after completing postdoctoral positions at the University of California at Riverside and the University of Cambridge. Webber received his B.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in Biological Chemistry and Biology from the University of Essex, U.K. He brings a wide range of knowledge, experience, and positive energy to the position, and we welcome him to the DGS.


DGS Is Moving to New Location

The Division of Graduate Studies office will be moving from Wilson Hall to the Administration Building B Wing. The move will be accomplished in two phases. The mail code (1003) and all DGS staff phone numbers will remain the same. The Dean's office, Graduate Academic Programs, Academic Program Review (APR) and Accreditation and Graduate Student Programs relocated to the second floor of Admin B on Monday, September 19. All other DGS departments including admissions, financial support, student processes (petitions, thesis/dissertation format) are tentatively scheduled to move in mid-October and will remain in their current offices in Wilson Hall until then.

Check out more of the latest DGS news at www.asu.edu/graduate/aboutus/news.
— REMINDERS —

Absent Committee Member Procedures for Oral Defenses. A student's entire committee must be present at the oral defense of a thesis or dissertation. If a member is not able to attend the defense, the student should obtain a substitute and contact SAS for a copy of the Absent Committee Member Procedures. It is not recommended that a student process a committee change in these cases. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns

Brown Bag Workshops. Student Academic Services is available to present 30-minute brown bag workshops for individual academic units. The workshops can be tailored to specific topics, policies or academic unit needs. If you would like further information please email grad-SAS@asu.edu.

Graduation Approval Pages. Approval pages that have been submitted for the signature of the Dean of the Division Graduate Studies will not be released until students have applied for graduation for the semester in which he or she intends to graduate and paid the graduation fee. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns/index.html#forms

Social and Academic Mentoring Program (SAM). There are still research assistantships available for advanced graduate students who are able to mentor new incoming graduate students during their first year of graduate school. For more information, go to: www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/SAM.

Students with Internships after their Oral Defense. Students completing internships after the semester of their oral defense will not be eligible to receive the dean's signature until the semester in which they intend to graduate. When SAS is notified by the committee chair that the internship has been completed and the student has applied for graduation, the student will be eligible for the dean's signature. www.asu.edu/graduate/format/newsUpdates.htm.

K. Patricia Cross Future Leaders Award provides financial support (travel, lodging, conference registration up to $1,500 total) for graduate students to attend Association of American Colleges and Universities (AAC&U) 2006 Meeting, "Demanding Excellence: Liberal Education in an Era of Global Competition, Anti-Intellectualism, and Disinvestment," to be held January 25-28 in Washington, DC. It also entitles recipients to a one-year affiliation with AAC&U. Deadline for receipt of materials is November 4, 2005. Further information: www.aacu.org/meetings/annualmeeting/AM06/CrossAward.cfm

  • Attend Spring 2006 TA Orientation and Training scheduled for Monday, January 9, 2006. Information on the event is available at www.asu.edu/graduate/tara/TAorientation.html.

TIAA-CREF Ruth Simms Hamilton Research Fellowship. This one-year, $30,000 fellowship is being offered to graduate students whose cutting-edge, graduate-level research furthers the study of the African Diaspora. The fellowship is renewable and is intended to provide research support. The deadline to submit all materials is October 15, 2005. Award winners will be announced December 15, 2005. For submission guidelines, eligibility requirements, and award criteria, go to www.tiaa-crefinstitute.org/awards/hamilton.
