October 17, 2006

MBA students take first place in PAC-10 competition

Four students from the W. P. Carey MBA Program worked feverishly over a 24-hour period to create the winning case study presentation that took first place in the PAC-10 Case Competition on Oct. 13-14.

Hosted by the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University (ASU), the third PAC-10 Case Competition was held at the Memorial Union on the Tempe Campus, and featured five competing schools: ASU, The University of Arizona, Washington State University, the University of Washington, and the University of Southern California (USC).

W. P. Carey MBA students Julie Scanio, Brandi Bloodworth, Katie Mueller and Dave Petruncio ? all second-year students in the Full-time Program ? formed the ASU team that won first place. Second place went to the University of Washington, while third place was awarded to USC. Additionally, Bloodworth was chosen for the ?Best Presenter? award, while Scanio was honored with the ?Best Response to Q&A? award.

The core of the competition is the case study, which is a description of a real company facing a genuine business decision. The case includes a detailed description of the company, with financial and technical data, and the company's history. Student teams were given the case study at 8 a.m. Friday and had 24 hours to analyze the case and complete a detailed discussion, with recommendations on what the company should do and why. Teams began presenting at 8 a.m. Saturday in the double-blind competition; they competed under fictional company names, and judges were not told which schools the teams represented.

PAC-10 Case Competition Judge Jim Cudd, vice president of strategy for Honeywell and a judge for previous PAC-10 case competitions, noted that the W. P. Carey team was the ?best team he had seen? in his remarks.

?This is more evidence of how well our W. P. Carey MBA students do when they compete with MBAs from other top schools,? said Gerry Keim, associate dean for MBA programs.
