University IT Planning

[ CIO > Responsibilities > Planning >

The university CIO leads University planning and coordination of information technology initiatives and policies to create and maintain a comprehensive information technology environment to support and enhance instruction, research and creative activity, administrative services, and community outreach in realizing the University Mission and Strategic Plan.

The ASU and Arizona Board of Regents IT planning process includes three components, collectively titled the Information Technology Improvement Plan:

  1. IT Strategic Directions provides an overall planning framework, identifies a few major comprehensive themes, and lists key high-level directions that will be pursued over a three- to five-year timeframe. This is the strategic part of the process.
  2. IT Development Plan provides a two-year project specific listing and a one-year project detail description. The two-year plan is modeled after the capital development plan and the one-year plan is modeled after the Arizona Government Information Technology Agency (GITA) planning process.
  3. IT Renewal Plan is a subset of the IT Development Plan that specifically addresses annual updating of technologies and applications.
