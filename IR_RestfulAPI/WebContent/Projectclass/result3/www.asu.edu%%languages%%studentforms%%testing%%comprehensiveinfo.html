Department of Languages and Literatures (DLL) Policy for:

COMPREHENSIVE EXAMS (Application form - Word | Adobe PDF)

Purpose: To establish ASU credits and to fulfill the language requirement for languages offered at ASU except French, German or Spanish. CLEP exams are available for those languages.

Fee: $50.00 per credit hour

  • Student completes the Comprehensive Exam Application for one course and obtains his/her advisor’s signature.
  • Student takes a copy of the approved application along with payment to Cashiering Services, SSV B-244. Please pay for only 1 test at a time.
  • A copy of the fee payment receipt and approved Exam application must be submitted to DLL before scheduling an exam. Oral exam (15 minutes) is arranged with faculty/approved personnel by the student. DLL schedules the written exam.

Qualifications: CANNOT receive credit if a NATIVE SPEAKER*.

  • Must be currently enrolled in 1 st or 2 nd semester at ASU. Cannot have completed two semesters at ASU, nor accumulated more than 100 credit hours (including transfer hours).
  • Exams must be taken in sequence.
  • Exams may not be taken in any course where student has been given admission credit or transfer credit from any educational institution.
  • May not receive credit for an exam in an elementary level of a field in which the student has earned more advanced credit.
  • A student pursuing a second baccalaureate degree may not receive credit by comprehensive exam, but, with prior approval of the college, the student may use the exam to waive a course requirement if a grade of “C” or higher is earned.
  • Exams are not given during the 1 st week or the last 2 weeks of the semester.
  • Exam may not be repeated.

Grade: If the earned exam grade is “C” or better, a mark of “Y” is entered on the student’s permanent record; otherwise, no entry is made. Credit by exam is indicated as such on the record. The student does not earn General Studies Credit for credit by examination. The student is notified by mail of the examination result.

CLEP testing is offered for French, German and Spanish through the 202 level. See the ASU General Catalog, College-Level Examination Program. This service is provided through Testing Services, Payne B-302, 480-965-7146.

  • If you have a disability that requires special needs, please notify our office two weeks in advance of your scheduled exam date, so we may make arrangements for you.

*Native Speaker is defined as a person with their secondary education in the language in question.
