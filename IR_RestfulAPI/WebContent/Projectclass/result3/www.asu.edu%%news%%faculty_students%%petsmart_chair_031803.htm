March 18, 2003

$1 million gift to the W. P. Carey School of Business establishes ASU PETsMART Chair in Services Leadership

PETsMART, Inc., the nation's leading specialty retailer of services and solutions for the lifetime needs of pets, has given a $1 million gift to the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University, establishing the PETsMART Chair in Services Leadership.

The funds will support an outstanding professor's scholarly research and teaching in management or marketing. PETsMART has determined that the first chair holder, as yet unnamed, will be chosen from the services marketing field - and will be a "pet parent."

"PETsMART, with its commitment, vision and savvy, makes an excellent partner for us. The gift from PETsMART represents a tangible piece of a larger partnership with the W. P. Carey School of Business. Of course, I value what the gift does for us in helping us to realize our potential and strengthen our renowned marketing faculty, but a strong professional school like ours depends on its partnerships with business in so many ways," said Dean Larry Edward Penley. "This gift will strengthen the already renowned marketing faculty."

The W. P. Carey School of Business is known internationally for its marketing programs and research, especially in the area of services marketing. W. P. Carey faculty were among the first to identify services as a product that could lead to profitability, and the Center for Services Leadership is respected throughout the world as a definitive source of services knowledge for companies who compete through service.

PETsMART is a major corporate supporter of the center's work. The company's Senior Vice President, Services, Strategic Planning and Business Development,
David Lenhardt, serves on its board of advisors and was a presenter at a general session of the center's "Compete Through Service" symposium last fall. "Our partnership with ASU is a natural fit for us as PETsMART has developed a strong business model that's built on a mix of retail products and high-quality pet services. Funding this Chair is a tremendous opportunity to educate the future leaders in the retail and services industries."

PETsMART also supports the W. P. Carey MBA through speaking engagements and student projects. Last spring, W. P. Carey MBA students specializing in services marketing and management conducted an award-winning project for PETsMART on how to more effectively nationally market pet grooming services.

Photos of Lenhardt are available to download at http://wpcarey.asu.edu/top/news_media.cfm.

U.S. News & World Report ranks the W. P. Carey undergraduate marketing major 21st in the nation. The W. P. Carey MBA -- ranked 12th among programs housed at public schools of business - is known for its services marketing specialization.

About the W. P. Carey School of Business --
The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 25th in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate degree offers Business Honors to some of the best students at the university. The W. P. Carey MBA, ranked 12th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.

About PETsMART, Inc. -
PETsMART, Inc., (NASDAQ: PETM) is the largest specialty retailer of services and solutions for the lifetime needs of pets. The company operates more than 580 pet stores in the United States and Canada, as well as a large pet supply catalog business and the Internet's leading online provider of pet products and information (www.petsmart.com). PETsMART provides a broad range of competitively priced pet food and supplies, and offers complete pet training, grooming and adoption services. Since 1994, PETsMART Charities has donated nearly $21 million to animal welfare programs and, through its in-store adoption programs, has saved the lives of nearly 1.4 million pets.
