November 6, 2003

College hosts conference to focus spotlight on dropouts

For many years, mounting evidence has suggested that young people who do not finish high school face many more problems later in life than their counterparts who graduate. But, while national leaders have demanded that schools, families and communities make a concerted effort to ensure that students complete high school, the dropout rate remains high.

In an effort to crystallize the issues around high school completion and dropouts in the Southwest, ASU, together with the Harvard University Equity Project and colleagues from the University of Texas at San Antonio, the University of Texas at El Paso and the University of New Mexico will host the "Southwest Conference on Enhancing School Completion." The event, to be held Nov. 7 - 8 at ASU, will feature both regional and national experts on the subject.

"Our focus will be on the risk factors for dropping out that are specific to populations in the Southwestern United States, and on determining concrete strategies that schools and families can use to help students complete high school and pursue post-secondary education at significantly higher rates," says Eugene Garcia, vice president of university-school partnerships and dean of the College of Education at ASU.

"Students drop out for many reasons, some of which are perceived as being good ones - to help support their families, for instance," Garcia says. "But, the consequences of leaving are becoming more and more severe, as the gap between dropouts and more educated people continues to widen."

Consequently, educators and scholars from across the United States and Mexico are coming to Tempe not only to examine the factors and circumstances that most significantly impact graduation and dropping out, but also to craft viable solutions to ultimately boost graduation rates and college enrollment among students from the Southwest.

Gary Orfield of Harvard University will present "The national perspective on school completion: New numbers for the Southwest."

Also delivering a keynote address will be Dennis Hoffman and Arthur Blakemore of ASU's College of Business. "The economics of dropout: The complexities of uncovering the real costs," is the topic to be explored in their address.

Donna Deyhle of the University of Utah, will discuss the "Native American perspective on school completion," while Ursula Casanova of ASU and her co-presenters Vi Florez, Harriet Roma and Josefina Tinajero will lead conference attendees in the exploration of "Exemplary high schools in three states," during their keynote address.

The conference is open to all. The registration fee is $100, and a special rate of $25 will be available for a limited number of full-time students who wish to attend the conference. Additional information and online registration is available at (http://bers.asu.edu/swc).

Sherwood, with the College of Education, can be reached at (480) 965-2114 or (joan.sherwood@asu.edu).
