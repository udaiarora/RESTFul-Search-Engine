ASU EMERGENCY RESPONSE GUIDE

UPDATED August 2005

Department of Environmental Health & Safety
and the ASU Police Department

 


PERSONAL INJURY

In all "serious cases" call 9-1-1 for emergency treatment

HEAT RELATED ILLNESS

  • Get victim to a cool place.
  • Loosen tight clothing.
  • Apply cool, wet cloths to the skin.
  • Fan the victim.
  • If victim is conscious, give cool (not cold) water to drink.
  • Obtain medical attention at the Student Health Center @ (480) 965-3346 Monday – Friday 9:00 a.m. – 4:30 p.m. or other appropriate resources.
  • Call 9-1-1 for an ambulance, if victim refuses water, vomits, or loses consciousness.
  • Report incident to supervisor. Supervisor responsible to notify ASU EH&S @ (480) 965-1823 Monday – Friday 8:00 a.m. – 5:00 p.m. Nights and weekends call the ASU Police Department @ (480) 965-3456.

MINOR CUTS & PUNCTURES

  • Vigorously wash injury with soap and water for several minutes.
  • Obtain medical attention at the Student Health Center @ (480) 965-3346 Monday – Friday 9:00 a.m. – 4:30 p.m. or other appropriate resources - to check tetanus status.
  • If IMPALED by a foreign object – DO NOT REMOVE THE OBJECT. Seek medical attention immediately.
  • Report incident to supervisor. Supervisor responsible to notify ASU EH&S @ (480) 965-1823 Monday – Friday 8:00 a.m. – 5:00 p.m. Nights and weekends call the ASU Police Department @ (480) 965-3456.

MATERIAL SPLASHED IN EYE

  • Immediately rinse eye and inner surface of eyelid with water continuously for 15 minutes.
  • Forcibly hold eye open to ensure effective wash behind eyelids.
  • Obtain medical attention at the Student Health Center @ (480) 965-3346 Monday – Friday 9:00 a.m. – 4:30 p.m. or other appropriate resources.
  • Report incident to supervisor. Supervisor responsible to notify ASU EH&S @ (480) 965-1823 Monday – Friday 8:00 a.m. – 5:00 p.m. Nights and weekends call the ASU Police Department @ (480) 965-3456
