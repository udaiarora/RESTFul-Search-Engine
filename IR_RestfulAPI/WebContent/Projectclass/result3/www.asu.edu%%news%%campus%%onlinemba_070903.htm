Quality and convenience: W. P. Carey MBA announces online program

The W. P. Carey MBA at Arizona State University is accepting applications for a new graduate degree option at the nationally-ranked school. The W. P. Carey MBA - Online Program, a web-based distance-education curriculum, will begin classes in January 2004.

"The purpose of the W. P. Carey MBA - Online Program is to offer the challenges of our AACSB-accredited program and the expertise of our internationally renowned faculty to individuals who cannot come to campus," said Dr. Lee McPheters, associate dean for executive and professional programs. "We are looking for students who want the dynamic and stimulating educational experience that is the W. P. Carey MBA, but have been held back by scheduling or other circumstances."

The school expects to draw many students from the metropolitan area as well as other parts of the country and around the world. Already, inquiries have come in from potential applicants as nearby as Chandler, AZ, and as far away as Macao, China.

The Online Program will offer the quality and depth of the W. P. Carey MBA, made more convenient by web-based delivery. Admissions standards, for example, will match the bar set for all W. P. Carey MBA offerings. Online Program students will tackle the same rigorous curriculum and study under the same highly regarded faculty. And, because the program retains the cohort structure characteristic of the W. P. Carey MBA, students will benefit from the advantages of progressing through classes as a community of learners.

The program will begin with an on-campus, five-day orientation including team building with fellow students and faculty and an introduction to the technology used to deliver course content. Students will then pursue the classes at home, at work or on the road, studying when it is convenient.

Unlike mass-marketed online programs, however, the W. P. Carey MBA - Online Program will enforce what Faculty Director M. Johnny Rungtusanatham calls "time fences." Students will be held to deadlines by quizzes and assignments, assuring that they move ahead at the same pace as others in their cohort.

"This approach maximizes convenience, yet develops in students the ability to complete assignments on time," Rungtusanatham said. "For students it will be as if they were working on a project deadline, analogous to scenarios they will face in the corporate world."

The cohort formula has been used successfully in the W. P. Carey MBA - Evening Program, which boasts one of the highest graduation rates of any part-time program in the country. The "time fence" approach has worked well in the W. P. Carey MBA - Corporate Program, a customized online degree for groups of students from one or more companies.

"A valuable lesson we have learned from our sister programs is that students drop out if they have no intellectual or emotional support from peers," Rungtusanatham added. "That issue is especially pressing in an online program, which by definition tends to isolate individual learners. Besides the use of time fences, the Online Program has incorporated many deliberate mechanisms to minimize isolated individual learners so that our students will realize that they are not alone 'out there' . . . there are others going through the same experience."

For more information about the Online Program, see wpcareymba.asu.edu/online. To access a photo of Rungtusanatham, click on photo gallery at wpcarey.asu.edu/newsroom.


W. P. CAREY SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 25th in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
