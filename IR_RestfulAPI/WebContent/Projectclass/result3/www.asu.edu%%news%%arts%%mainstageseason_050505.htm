May 5, 2005

Herberger College announces new Mainstage Season performances

The Herberger College of Fine Arts has announced its 2005 – 2006 Mainstage season of plays, dance concerts, operas, musicals and organ concerts that showcase cutting-edge new works; holiday classics and brilliant stage productions.

Mainstage season ticket packages to fit every lifestyle and budget are on sale now through Sept. 5. Single tickets go on sale Sept. 6. Call (480) 965-6447 for ticket information, or go to (http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/mainstage). All Mainstage productions are held on ASU’s Tempe campus.

ASU Herberger Mainstage Theatre
See the emerging talent of ASU student actors and playwrights before they become stars. Shows include:
• “Orchids in the Moonlight,” Oct. 7 – 22.
• “The King Stag,” Oct. 28 – Nov. 6.
• “Venus,” Feb. 17 – 25, 2006.
• “Waking Up in Lost Hills: A Central California Rip Van Winkle,” March 31 – April 9, 2006.

Mainstage Theatre Festival of New Work
These are original plays written by ASU theater students and faculty:
• “Taking Leave,” Nov. 17 – 21.
• “We Lost It at the Movies” (with a special appearance by Rock Hudson), Nov. 18 – 22.
• “La Vida Loca,” Nov. 18 – 22.ASU Herberger Mainstage

Lyric Opera Theatre
These events are sponsored by The Harris Vibrant productions, filled with emerging talent from the School of Music:
• “The Boyfriend,” Sept. 30 – Oct. 9.
• “American Gothic,” Nov. 18 – 19.
• “Amahl and the Night Visitors,” Nov. 20 – Dec. 3.
• “Dialogues of the Carmelites,” Feb. 24 – March 4, 2006.
• “Oklahoma!” April 21 – 30, 2006.

ASU Herberger Mainstage Dance
Audiences get to celebrate 75 years of dance at ASU with three inspired concerts performed in the Galvin Playhouse:
• “Celebrating the Nagrin Legend,” Sept. 16 – 18.
• “Expanding Borders – Fall Concert,” Dec. 1 – 3.
• “Sharing Unimaginable Worlds – Spring Concert,” April 27 – 30, 2006.

New Danceworks Series
This enjoyable series of innovative dance thesis projects is performed in the Dance Studio Theatre:
• “New Danceworks I,” Oct. 20 – 23.
• “New Danceworks II,” Jan. 26 – 29, 2006.
• “New Danceworks III,” March 2 – 5, 2006.ASU Herberger

Mainstage Organ
Audiences get to enjoy a wide spectrum of Johann Sebastian Bach’s music in seven diverse, thematic programs:
• “Bach Through a Wide Angle Lens,” Oct. 9.
• “The Youthful Bach,” Oct. 23, 2005.
• “Bach and the North German Style,” Nov. 13.
• “Bach and the French Influence,” Feb. 5, 2006.
• “Bach the Virtuoso,” Feb. 26, 2006.
• “Bach in Leipzig,” March 5, 2006.
• “Bach and the Italian Influence,” March 26, 2006.

Third Annual Organ Christmas Concert (Dec. 10 – 11)
In this performance, the ASU Organ Studio ensemble invites audiences to experience a special holiday concert celebrating the beautiful music of Christmas.
