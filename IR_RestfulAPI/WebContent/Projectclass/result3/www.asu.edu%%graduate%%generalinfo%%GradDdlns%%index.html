FORMS AND ASSISTANCE
All Division of Graduate Studies' forms are available on the Internet at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms in Word or .pdf format. They may also be picked up at the Division of Graduate Studies, located in Administration Building, B-wing, Room 170. Students are responsible for obtaining the most recent version of the forms (available on our website) and submitting them with the appropriate signatures. For instructions about forms or procedures, email grad-sas@asu.edu or visit Student Academic Services. For answers to thesis/dissertation format questions, visit www.asu.edu/graduate/format or email gradformat@asu.edu.



ENROLLMENT REQUIREMENTS
Students must be enrolled for at least one credit hour that appears on the program of study, or one hour of appropriate graduate level credit, during the semester or summer session in which they defend a thesis or dissertation, take a comprehensive examination, or complete an applied project. Students must be registered before they can schedule an oral defense of a thesis or dissertation with the Division of Graduate Studies.

Summer Sessions: If defending during the summer, enrollment in any one summer session (eight-week, first five-week, second five-week) will fulfill this requirement.*

Break Period: If your oral defense is scheduled during a break period, you must be enrolled during the preceding semester. If the break period is between the summer and fall semesters, enrollment in any one of the summer sessions listed above will fulfill this requirement.*

*Please note that students must stay registered until all degree requirements have been completed.



FORMAT EVALUATION/ORAL DEFENSE PROCEDURES

Step A: Apply for Graduation
You may apply for graduation at any time. The Graduation Office (Registrar's Office) is located in the Student Services Building, main counter on the first floor, windows 8–10. However, if you apply after the deadline, you will be assessed a late fee and your name will not appear in the commencement program.

Step B: Submit Materials for Format Evaluation and Scheduling the Oral Defense*

Doctoral Students: Submit the following materials at least 10 working days before the defense:

  • Dissertation (one complete unbound copy)
  • Format Approval Sheet (signed by you and your committee chair)
  • One copy of an article from the journal you used as a style guide (if you did not use APA, Chicago, MLA, or Turabian as a style guide)
  • Doctoral Defense Schedule Form (one completed copy signed by you and your committee chair)
  • Survey of Earned Doctorates (available online at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms or in Administration Building, B-wing, Room 170)
Master’s Students: Submit the following materials at least 10 working days before the defense:
  • Thesis (one complete unbound copy)
  • Format Approval Sheet (signed by you and your committee chair)
  • One copy of an article from the journal you used as a style guide (if you did not use APA, Chicago, MLA, or Turabian as a style guide)
  • Master's Defense Schedule Form (one completed copy signed by you and your committee chair)
*Please Note: Students should arrive no later than 4:30 p.m. to submit materials to the Division of Graduate Studies for format evaluation adn to schedule a defense.
Step C: Hold the Oral Defense
Take a clean copy of the correctly formatted approval page to your defense for your committee to sign. Your pass/fail form will have already been sent to your chair from the Division of Graduate Studies.

Step D: Obtain Signature of the Division of Graduate Studies Dean
In order for the DGS Dean to sign the approval page, you must have final format approval and submit the following:

  • One correctly-formatted approval page (original) signed by your committee and head of your academic unit
  • One completed Pass/Fail Form (original) signed by your committee and the head of your academic unit indicating that you have passed the oral defense
Approval pages that are hand delivered to Administration Building, B-wing, Room 170, by 2 p.m. will be available for pick-up after 2 p.m. the next day (provided there are no problems).

Step E: Submission to the Bookstore
You must submit two copies of your final document (plus any additional copies required by your academic unit) to the ASU Bookstore for binding by the deadline for the semester in which you are graduating (see semester deadlines for specific dates). Failure to meet this deadline will prevent you from graduating. Students are responsible for making sure that the documents submitted for binding are in proper order and free of typographical errors. These copies must be on high-quality, acid-free, xerographic 20-pound minimum (75g/m2) paper (may be bond or rag). Once submitted, these documents are not retrievable for substitutions or changes. Further information may be found at bookstore.asu.edu/services/thesisBindings.php.
