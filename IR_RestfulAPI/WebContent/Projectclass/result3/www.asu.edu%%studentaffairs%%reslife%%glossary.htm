A
  Academic Year - Opening in August to closing in May
 
 Acknowledgement Letter - Residential Life received and processed your application
 
 Address Information - Until a student actually moves into a residence hall all mail goes to the permanent address. Once the student checks in the student gets a local address, which is their address at the residence hall. At that point all mail begins going to the student. If the student wants their mail to go to another address they can iniciate a change making their local address a "billing address" at which point all student mail will begin going to the billing address.
 
 Ambassadors - Students that serve as live-in tour guides for potential incoming students. Ambassadors live in the residence halls with a roommate and work weekly shifts where they are available to provide hall tours to families and prospective residents. Ambassadors serve as resources to students on campus tours and answer questions about the specific residence hall in which they reside.
 
 Application - Form submitted to Residential Life for on-campus housing
 
 Application Fee - The $75 non-refundable processing fee which must be included with your application
 
 Assignment - When a student is placed in a hall
 
 Assignment Letter - Correspondence that includes hall assignment and confirmation/cancellation form
 
 Assistant Community Coordinators - Part time professional staff that oversees the management of larger communities within residence halls. They supervise and support staffs of RA's. Many are pursuing their Master's degree in Higher Education and live in each residence hall. They are available to respond to student crisis, oversee programming efforts and advise Hall Councils.
 
 ASU ID Number - Determined during ASU admission process; Your SSN or an ASU block number beginning with 993
 
 ASURite ID - Created during the admission process and used for all ASU Interactive services, I.e. checking admission status,paying tuition, registering for clasees, etc.
 
 ASURite Password - Created by the student once ASURite ID is activated

C
Cancellation - Cancellations must be sent, in writing, to the Res Life Office. This will inactivate your application and/or assignment. Cancellations must include name, ASU ID, and student's signature and can be mailed, faxed, or accepted in person.
 
 Cancellation Deadline - See schedule of charges and deadlines
 
 Check In - The process that takes place when you move in to a hall and receive your room key.
 
 Check Out - The process that takes place when you permanently move out of a hall and return your key.
 
 Cholla Hall - Pronounced: Choy-Uh - Is the name of a cactus
 
 Community Coordinators - Full-time professional staff members who have Master's degrees in Higher Education. They oversee large complexes in seven different areas of campus. They administer the needs of a particular complex; supervise multiple Assistant Community Coordinators and RA staffs. They work with students and staff to manage the administrative requirement of each residence hall complex and also meet with students who have violated university policy. Community Coordinators live in the complex in which they manage.
 
 Community Style Bathroom - All women on floor share one bathroom, all the men on floor share one bathroom. The bathrooms include private stall, sinks, and private shower stalls.
 
 Complete Application - An application for housing filled out front and back, with a $75 fee and all applicable signatures on both sides
 
 Confirmation - When a student acknowledges hall assignment with confirmation/cancellation form and pre-payment of $200

H
Hall Council Leaders - Each residence hall complex maintains a Hall Council, which is a governing board made up of students from each complex. Elections are generally held in the spring for several executive board positions, such as President, Treasurer, and Programmer. These students have the opportunity to advocate for the needs and requests of their fellow residents. Hall Council meetings are open to any student to attend to voice concerns or requests. Each Hall Council is part of a larger organization, the Residence Hall Association, which is a governing board that supports the needs of all residence hall students.

L
License Agreement - It's like a lease. You agree and are bound by the Residential Life License Agreement for the entire academic year when you submit your application and $75
 
 Living and Learning Communities - At ASU, you can live with other students who share your academic interests. In a special academic community you live and study together and interact with faculty and academic advisors from your chosen field. These communities are for freshmen with majors in the following colleges. Space is limited and certain criteria may apply. Indicate your community preference on your housing application. The Engineering community and the Barrett Honors college Residential Complex staff require you to complete a housing survey to be considered for their programs.

P
Parking - Parking is provided by Parking and Transit Service on a first come first serve basis. Residence hall parking information is sent out in early summer by Parking and Transit Service.
 
 Payment Plan Schedule - Payment Plan 1 is when you make one payment for each semester, a total of two payments for the academic year. Payment Plan 2 is when you make three payments for each semester. a total of six payments for the academic year. You choose your payment plan when you apply for on campus housing.
 
 Preference/Request on Housing Application - Preferences and requests are taken into consideration, however due to space limitation we may not be able to honor your preference or request.
 
 Pre-payment - The $200 fee that confirms your assignment. This $200 is deducted from your first payment

Residence Hall - Our residence halls offer living and learning communities, where you study, where you socialize, where you sleep, where you build friendships, where you get involved.
 
 Resident Assistants (RAs) - Assigned to a floor or hall community of between 30-60 residents. Builds connections between the residents and helps them transition into the university environment. They also develop programs to meet the needs of the students in their areas, enforce residence hall policy, and serve as resources at the front desks.
