Guide to Arizona's Court System

Plaintiffs & Defendants in Civil & Criminal Cases Heard by Arizona's Superior Courts, Justice of the Peace Courts, and Municipal Courts


Most lawsuits of interest to businesses are those that start out in the lower courts within the state.  Civil suits, including business disputes, landlord/tenant disputes, and violations of local codes and ordinances are handled in Arizona by the County's Superior Courts or Justice of the Peace Courts, or, in the case of a city, by the city's Municipal Court.  Criminal Cases are of interest when a company or its owners have had criminal charges filed against them by the state or local government. For more information on what is heard by each of these courts, use the Guide to Arizona's Court System's Limited Jurisdiction Courts   and General Jurisdiction Court.


Some of these courts have made their list of cases heard by the court available on the web.  To find the plaintiffs and defendants, which forms the case name, use the Arizona Judicial Branch's Public Access Court Information web site or the Courts Included By County and List of Courts Not Yet Included (has hot links for those that have their own separate web sites like Maricopa & Pima) web pages to check the site of a particular court.
