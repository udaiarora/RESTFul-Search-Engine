Crow lists Five I's to guide university's efforts

In his speech Jan. 28 to the Academic Assembly, President Michael Crow asked faculty to keep in mind "Five I's" that will guide the university's efforts in the coming year:

INDEPENDENCE - "We need to gain independence in admission standards and tuition. When the Regents finish, we will be independent, with a whole new set of responsibilities."

IDENTITY - "We need to build our own identity as the kind of university we want to be, not a replication of others."

IMAGE - "We have a self-image problem. This is a great university, with a student body that's high tier and one of the best faculties in the country. We must realize that."

INVESTMENT - "We've decided to invest in people, programs and initiatives, to stay strong and competitive. That's what smart institutions do."

INITIATIVE - "We'll be successful if we can develop individual initiative. Leadership can't do it. We need faculty and staff to come up with new ideas, concepts, ways of doing things, to make this the kind of university we want it to be."
