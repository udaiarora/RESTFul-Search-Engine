April 11, 2003

Planning organization elects associate dean, professor as fellows

Mary Kihl, associate dean for research in the ASU College of Architecture & Environmental Design, and Richard Lai, professor in the School of Planning & Landscape Architecture, have been elected Fellows of the American Institute of Certified Planners (AICP).

Election to the status of Fellow is one of the highest honors that AICP can bestow upon a member. It recognizes the achievements of the planner as an individual, and his or her contributions to planning and society.

Kihl and Lai are the first educators who have been elected Fellows in Arizona. They join just 35 others from colleges and universities in the United States who have this distinction.

AICP is the American Planning Association's professional institute, providing leadership nationwide in the certification of professional planners, ethics, professional development, planning education and the standards of planning practice.

Kihl began her career as a history teacher at the University of Pittsburgh-Johnstown.

"I quickly sensed an opportunity for students who were sensitive to the history and culture of a place to work with its residents to plan for its future," Kihl said.
To that end, she established and became director of the Appalachian Studies Program, an undergraduate planning program.

Her concern about rural transportation and transportation of special populations led to an invitation to join the Iowa Department of Transportation to assist with a project on transportation for those with disabilities and to further a sub-state regional approach to rural public transportation.

Kihl's next career move was to ASU, where she is director of the Herberger Center for Design Excellence.

"As director, I have numerous opportunities to build interdisciplinary teams in a broad range of topics, including conservation of the environment, affordable housing, cross-border studies, sustainable environments, and livable communities," Kihl said.
Lai, who has taught at ASU since 1973, is program coordinator for the Bachelor of Science in Design with a major in Housing and Urban Development (BSD-HUD) program.

BSD-HUD, initiated in 1996, is in the forefront of university programs dedicated to education in the design, development and production of high-quality and affordable housing, a task essential to the welfare and economy of the rapidly developing Sunbelt region of the United States.

He also played a leading role in initiating planning studies at ASU, and his work led to the creation of the School of Planning and Landscape Architecture.

Lai created the BSD-HUD with the aim of providing "professional-level education for home builders and residential land developers, instilling in this 'new breed of developers' not only professional skills but also professional values closely allied to those of planning."

Lai is most proud, however, of his influence on ASU students who have become planners in the state. "I am gratified above all else to have significantly shaped the education of planners throughout Arizona, notably in the area of my teaching specialization of planning law," he said.
