Site Home >Lead Desk Assistants

Lead Desk Assistants (LDAs) serve as the student supervisor to the Desk Assistant staffs. LDAs also assist professional staff with leadership, within their hall, and help to maintain a high quality of service to students and staff via the front desk.


What do Lead Desk Assistants do?

Contribute to the success of resident students and staff
Assist the Residential Life staff in maintaining a community of high quality service
Assists in supervising Desk Assistants
Work a minimum of 12 hours each week
Attend all scheduled meetings and trainings
Are familiar with all desk procedures, i.e. the telephone, filing, key checkouts, equipment checkouts…
Are pleasant, friendly, and helpful during any interaction with residents, visitors, and staff members.
Play an important role toward establishing a positive environment for all who live and work here.
Contribute to a safe and secure residence hall environment
Serve as a representative of ASU to perspective students and visitors

What are the requirements?
Undergraduate students must have a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.75 at the time of application and while holding the position
Graduate students must have a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 at the time of application and while holding the position
Undergraduate students must be enrolled in a minimum of 12 credit hours
Graduate students must be enrolled in a minimum of 6 credit hours
LDAs must be Junior, Senior, or Graduate Student Status
LDAs must accept employment for a nine-month period beginning in early August 2005 with training and concluding soon after the halls close at the end of the spring 2006 semester in May.
LDAs must be in good standing with Residential Life and Student Life.
Who is an ideal candidate?

One who possesses all of the requirements, as well as the following:

  • Leadership, time management, communication and organizational skills are important for the role of a Lead Desk Assistant.
  • Considering that the role of an LDA is to manage the desk assistants, it is also important to be able to administer direction and at the same time be able to hold a professional role within the hall for the residents, visitors, employees, and professional staff who oversee the halls.

What’s in it for you?

  • Increased interaction with students
  • Connections with Residential Life staff
  • Mentoring and professional development opportunities
  • Increased access to campus, community resources and networks
  • Compensation of $7.90 per hour

Recruitment Process:

Interested applicants need to complete and submit a Lead Desk Assistant Application. Applicants should reference the checklists that are included in the downloadable application. Positions are available in most of the residence halls.
If applying over summer break please contact us by calling or e-mailing 480-727-0881 or recruitment.reslife@asu.edu
If applying during fall or spring semesters please submit the completed application to the front desk of any residence hall on ASU’s Tempe campus.

Selection process

The selection process for fall positions take place each year in January ending in April. April is when candidates are notified of their selection status for the following academic year. We may also conduct smaller selection processes for our available spring and summer positions. Please contact the recruitment office for timeframes and deadlines at, 480-727-0881 or recruitment.reslife@asu.edu.
