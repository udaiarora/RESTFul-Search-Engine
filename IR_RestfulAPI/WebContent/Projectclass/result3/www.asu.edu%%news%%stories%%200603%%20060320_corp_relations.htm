March 20, 2006

Initiative builds relationships with business community

ASU has unveiled a new initiative to enhance its relationships with businesses in Arizona and throughout the country.

The initiative, consistent with ASU President Michael Crow's vision to transform the university into an economic, cultural, and social academic enterprise, will further develop connections between the business community and ASU. It will focus on services that foster better access to ASU information and services of interest to businesses - and, in particular, how to create mutually beneficial research and development partnerships.

"Many universities have difficulty relating to businesses because they operate differently than public institutions," says Rob Melnick, associate vice president of ASU's Office of Economic Affairs. "This is particularly true when it comes to small- and medium-sized businesses that just don't have the financial capacity to be philanthropic and are not large employers of university graduates.

"In these cases, the university-business relationship can become one-sided. But ASU sees business relationships and partnerships as critical to the success of the greater Phoenix region and its competitiveness. Thus, we are focusing on how to strengthen businesses while also serving the university's needs."

To address this matter, ASU's Office of Economic Affairs has created new strategies for enhancing ASU's corporate relations, especially ones that make use of ASU's vast portfolio of research, services and human capital.

"Although successful links between ASU and the business community have existed in the past, we will look at these relationships more strategically and systematically," says Bulent Bicer, ASU's recently appointed senior officer for corporate relations.

Bicer's priority is to build on ASU's business partnerships. He also will assist firms, small and large, to partner with - and access - university resources.

"There are vast opportunities and services at ASU that businesses, regardless of size, can tap into," Bicer says. "My goal is to facilitate efficient, effective access and connections."

ASU corporate relations activities and offerings include:

. A new business portal: Located on ASU's homepage, the business portal (www.asu.edu/business) serves as a "virtual front door" for businesses to quickly and easily connect to ASU resources, skills and assets that are specific to their needs.

. New business partnerships: Dedicated ASU account managers will strengthen and align more strategic partnerships with corporations. Formal communication mechanisms will be implemented to foster timely communication between ASU and the corporate world.

. New small- and medium-sized enterprise (SME) outreach: This is a special initiative designed for the critical SME segment, which represents almost 98 percent of all companies in Arizona . Entrepreneurs will be connected to ASU expertise that can assist with prototype manufacturing and commercialization, among other opportunities.

Bicer, a multilingual and multicultural technology executive, has more than 20 years of international business experience in the private sector with blue-chip companies such as Motorola, Siemens-Nixdorf, DEC, Deutsche Telekom and Global One. Most recently, he was managing director of Europe for Startec Global Communications, a global communications company headquartered in Rockville, Md. He also is the founder and managing partner of EuroMarket Partners, a business development firm that assists small- and medium-sized U.S. companies to identify and explore business opportunities in Europe.

The new corporate relations function is a part of ASU's Office of Economic Affairs (within the Office of the Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs). Programs administered by the Office of Economic Affairs include ASU Technopolis and the Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative. For more information, visit the Web site (www.economicaffairs.org).
