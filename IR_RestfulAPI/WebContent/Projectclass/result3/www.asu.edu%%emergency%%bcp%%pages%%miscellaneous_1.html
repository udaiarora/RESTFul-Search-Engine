SUBJECT: Business Continuity Planning

The following four-component process for Business Continuity Planning, outlined in your memorandum of April 11, 2002, is the basis for the Arizona public universities' and Board of Regents' central office report on their progress in planning and implementing Business Continuity Planning.

The four components are:

  • Business Function Assessment - for each university to identify its function(s), then prioritize each function in the recovery.
  • Declaration Authority - determine a person or persons with authority to declare an emergency, thus triggering the appropriate response, and declare a return to normal operations.
  • Crisis Management and Recovery - provide procedures that cover levels of response starting with (1) General Staff, through (2) Crisis Management Team, to (3) Recovery Teams, who will direct and actually perform the work of restoration and recovery, and how each level will perform its assignments.
  • Training and Testing Schedules - identify the training needed for each of the three levels listed in the Crisis Management and Recovery component, and establish schedules for training and testing the plan.
