Minutes of Meeting

 

February 16, 2005
Hayden C6A

Meeting was called to order at 3:00 pm.

A quorum was present.

 

I. Approval of Minutes (November 17, 2004) – Minutes were approved.

 

II. Dean’s Report –Sherrie Schmidt

 

Downtown Campus – Library will be located between Fillmore and Van Buren at 411 N. Central. Library will share the space with the IT. Staff from the Tempe Libraries will not be transferred. The opening day for Downtown Library is August 21, 2006. Some materials might get moved. Jeanne has worked on the framework for collection for Downtown library. The library will have its own budget. Medical library will be managed separately.

Dean Schmidt thanked the staff for participating in the assessment meetings.

 

III. AP Implementation Task Force – Kurt Murphy

 

Working group meets every other Wednesday. Work on Multiyear contract has been postponed as UA and NAU has not yet signed on it. TF is editing the sections of ACD manual related to APs. There is recommendation to rename the Professional Development and Contribution as Professional Contribution.

IV. Committee Reports

 

a.       Personnel Committee – Brad Vogus

The committee discussed the updates from Kurt about the AP implementation task force. The committee is working on the updating/revising the section of the handbook on the Mentoring program.

b.      Research and Professional Development Committee – Jennie Duvernay

The committee has completed distribution of the research funds for this fiscal year. The committee had two outstanding applications and the remaining $1000 was divided equally between them.

c.    Compensation Committee – Dan Stanton

Compensation Committee met with Associate Dean Kurt Murphy on December 10 to address compensation issues raised by Executive Committee of Librarians’ Council. Kurt stated that he reviews ARL data every fall and was updating ASU’s data and profile for this year.

Kurt distributed 3 documents and discussed their importance to the process:

1. Arizona State University Libraries Academic Professional Performance Based Salary Plan - Approved in 1995, this is the salary plan that the library follows when dealing with compensation issues. Kurt highlighted Item III D, which states:

Salary Structure: The goal of the University Libraries’ salary structure is to be competitive with external markets, internally equitable, and to reward performance.

The University Libraries’ salary structure seeks to attain and maintain salaries equivalent to the 50th percentile of the combined ASU and U of A peer institution salaries.

2. Peer Institutions for ASU, UA and NAU, PLUS Public PAC-10 and Public Big 10- On the list of ASU peers. Issues with peer list - It is the overall institution - not the library - being grouped as peers; geography

3. 2003-04 ARL Salary Survey - Table 21 - Number and average salaries of ARL University Librarians by position and type of institution - Usefulness diminished because it is based on the 68 Public ARL libraries and has limited librarian position categories.

The process used by Kurt and the UL Administration is to use the above data to set “target salaries” for all librarians. When the money comes from the University, it has parameters and a dollar amount that make it unrealistic to bring everyone up to “target salaries”. The UL generally adjusts all librarian salaries over a 3-year cycle beginning with the bottom third, then the top third, and finishing with the middle third.

Since ARL collects so much more data than they generally report, Kurt inquired a couple of years ago about running a custom report that would give us UL requested data related to our specific peers. ARL can run it, for a price. Dean Schmidt approved funding for such a report and has approved funding for another more detailed report, which Kurt hopes to have run by late winter/early spring.

d.    Review Committee – Julie Tharp

No Report

e.    Academic Senate – Deborah Koshinsky

 

Report From meeting on Monday, January 24, 2005

 

Pat Etter attended the January meeting. It was held at the East Campus, and Pat described it as a great meeting area.

The new ASU East Provost, Jerry Jakubowski, welcomed everyone and outlined the general plans for transforming ASU East into a Polytechnic Institute, soon to be called: ASU at the Polytechnic Campus.

Student Affairs committee Chair Steve Happel reported on the problems with student cheating at ASU, which some characterize as prevalent. Although a resolution regarding academic integrity was presented, it was tabled and will be worked on to include some suggested modifications from the Senate meeting.

Provost Glick discussed several items:

-- The TF on Health Care

-- The family of Professor Starsky, a faculty member who was released in the 1970s by the Board of Regents for missing a class for a sit-in and whose son now attends ASU, has donated papers to ASU. Dean Schmidt accepted the Starsky papers for the University. This incident regarding Professor Starsky serves as a reminder of an unfortunate event in the University's history and reminds us what transpired at ASU and many other places in the U.S. We think this is a fitting closure for many to an incident that should not have happened. -- He clarified a number of assertions about the news article ASU hiring stars, NAU hiring only young faculty. Bill Simmons, ASU West described a recent report to President Crow and the four University provosts. It was an idea for University Council. The University Council would be a non-policy making body that would serve three main functions.

f.    Committee on Academic Professional Status – Dan Stanton

John Wheatley was introduced as a new committee member, replacing Elizabeth McNeil, who resigned after being notified that Academic Advisors were no longer going to be in the AP category.CAPS members on the AP Implementation Task Force reported that with slow progress it seems unlikely that these revisions will be completed before the end of the academic year. Revising the ACD manual with the additions of West and East campuses is taking longer than anticipated.

There will be a number of vacancies on CAPS and librarians are encouraged to apply.

 

g. Web Master – Yuwu Song

 

no report.

 

V. Old Business

VI. New Business

Nominations & Elections Committee - Brad Vogus (for Julie Tharp)

It is that time of the year to elect a Senator for Academic Senate. There is one position to fill for a three-year term. Deborah Koshinsky and Ed Oetting have agreed to run for the position of Senator. A call was made for floor nomination. There were no nominations from the floor and nomination was closed.

 

Meeting adjourned 3:50 p.m.
