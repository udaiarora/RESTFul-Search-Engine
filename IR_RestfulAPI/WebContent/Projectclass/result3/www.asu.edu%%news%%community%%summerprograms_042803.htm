April 28, 2003

ASU 2003 summer programs

Whether it's about academics or activity, ASU offers a wide range of summer programs for children. Following are some of the activities being offered. Each listing includes contact and registration information. A complete list of programs can be found on the Web at (www.asu.edu/youth).

  • Design Discovery: one-week sessions in June at ASU Main. Open to elementary, junior and senior high school students. Sponsored by the College of Architecture & Environmental Design. Information: (480) 965-8736.
  • Young Adult Writing Project (YAWP): 9 a.m. - noon, June 9 - June 26, Durham Languages & Literatures Building. For youth entering grades 8 - 12. Cost: $150. Sponsored by English Department/English Education. Information: (480) 965-2568 or (lynn.nelson@asu.edu).
  • Math-Science Honors Program: five-week sessions, June 1 - July 3 and July 6 - Aug. 8; eight-week session, June 1 - July 25. ASU Main. University math/science for underrepresented 10th - 12th graders. Sponsored by SUMS Institute, mathematics & statistics. Information: (480) 965-1690 or (http://summs.la.asu.edu).
  • National Youth Sports Program: 7 a.m. - 1 p.m., Monday - Friday, June 2 - July 4, Physical Education Building West. For economically disadvantaged youth ages 10 - 16. Sponsored by exercise science & physical education. Information: (480) 727-1935.
  • ASU Summer Bridge Program: June 22 - July 25, ASU Main. For incoming freshmen. $50 (Arizona residents); $700 (non-residents). Sponsored by Academic Success Programs. Information: (480) 965-3097 or (www.asu.edu/duas/bridge).
  • Nursing w/ ASU Nursing: June 8 - 13, ASU Main. For 11 - 14-year-old Native Americans. Sponsored by College of Nursing - ASUN Project. Information: (480) 965-0123.
  • Camp Broadway: 8 a.m. - 5p.m., June 2 - 6, ASU Main. For children ages 10 - 17. Cost: $550. Co-sponsored by ASU Public Events, Herberger College of Fine Arts and Summer Sessions. Information: (480) 965-5062 or (asupublicevents.com).
  • ASU Summer Transition Academy (ASTA): July 20 - July 25, ASU Main. Six-day residential program for minority students. Sponsored by ASU/Phoenix Union High School District partnership. Information: (480) 965-0952 or (480) 965-8166.
  • Programs for Talented Youth: 8:30 - 11:30 a.m., or 12:30 - 3:30 p.m., June 9 - 27, ASU Main. Grades four - seven. Sponsored by the Office of Youth Preparation. Information: (480) 727-7450 or (www.asu.edu/talentedyouth).
  • Summer Broadcast Institute: June 1 - 13, Stauffer Hall. For high school juniors and minorities. Sponsored by the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communication. Information: (480) 965-8683 or (www.asu.edu/cronkite).
  • Summer Journalism Institute: early June, Stauffer Hall. For high school juniors and seniors. Sponsored by the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communication and the Arizona Republic. Information: (480) 965-8683 or (www.asu.edu/cronkite).
  • Asian LEAD Academy: 9 a.m. - 3 p.m., June 9 - 20, ASU Memorial Union. Non-residential leadership development program for students of Asian/Asian Pacific American heritage. Cost: $25. Sponsored by the Multicultural Student Center. Information: (480) 965-6060 or (www.asu.edu/studentlife/msc/asian).
  • Academic Program Promoting Leadership, Enrichment, and Service (APPLES): 8 a.m. - 12:30p.m., June 23 - July 25, ASU Main. Sponsored by the Multicultural Student Center. Information: (480) 965-6060.
  • Native American Summer Institute: 8 a.m. - 5 p.m., June 17 - 20, ASU Main. Four-day residential program for new Native American freshmen and transfer students. Sponsored by the Multicultural Student Center. Information: (480) 965-6060 or (awoods@asu.edu).
  • Arizona STI Summer Transportation Institute: July 9 - 20, ASU Main. Residential camp for high school sophomore and juniors. Cost: $50. Sponsored by College of Engineering & Applied Sciences (CEAS). Information: (480) 965-4665 or (www.eas.asu.edu/cor/summer).
  • DNA-TECHS2 Camp (Discovering Non-Natural Advances in Technology & Engineering to Change Humans, Society & Science): June 16 - 20, ASU Main. Commuter camp for high school juniors and seniors. Cost: $250. Sponsored by CEAS. Information: (480) 965-4665 or (www.eas.asu.edu/cor/summer).
  • WISE-UP (Women In Applied Science & Engineering): July 7 - 11, ASU Main. Week-long engineering projects for high school female juniors and seniors. Residential camp. Cost: $500. Sponsored by CEAS. Information: (480) 965-4665 or (www.eas.asu.edu/cor/summer).
  • AISES SSBP (Summer Scholars Bridge Program): June 30 - Aug. 8, ASU Main. For 10th - 12th graders. Residential camp. Cost: $50. Sponsored by CEAS. Information: (480) 965-4665 or (www.eas.asu.edu/cor/summer).
  • StRUT (Students Recycling Used Technology): July 14 - 16, ASU Main. For seventh and eighth grade male students. Commuter camp. Cost: $90. Sponsored by CEAS. Information: (480) 965-4665 or (www.eas.asu.edu/cor/summer).
  • WISE TEAMS: July 16 - 18, ASU Main. For seventh and eighth grade female students. Commuter camp. Cost: $90. Sponsored by CEAS. Information: (480) 965-4665 or (www.eas.asu.edu/cor/summer).
  • AIMS (Aiming for Success): June 21 and 28 and July 12, 19, & 26, ASU Main. Commuter camp for seventh and eighth graders. Cost: $75. Sponsored by CEAS. Information: (480) 965-4665 or (www.eas.asu.edu/cor/summer).
