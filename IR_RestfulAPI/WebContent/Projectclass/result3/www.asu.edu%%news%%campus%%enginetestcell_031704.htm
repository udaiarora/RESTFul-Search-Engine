Honeywell, ASU modernize campus engine test cell

After two years of work, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering students will have the tools needed to understand the operation of a gas turbine engine - used in the aerospace industry - and how to collect engine data and analyze engine performance.

Honeywell and ASU recently completed a joint project to update the gas turbine engine test cell located on the Main Campus. The equipment donated by Honeywell for the renovation is valued at just over $95,000.

"This renovation has upgraded our test cell instrumentation from 1964 analog equipment to state-of-the-art digital equipment," says Robert Peck, mechanical and aerospace engineering department chair for the Fulton School of Engineering. "We appreciate Honeywell's generosity and commitment to ASU and to our students."

The new digital instrumentation installed in the test cell measures turbine engine power output, fuel flow, operating temperatures, operating pressures and compressed air output. Honeywell manufactures gas turbine engines used for primary propulsion on business and regional aircraft and helicopters as well as engines called auxiliary power units used to provide air and power to aircraft for main engine start and to operate aircraft environmental control systems.

"Today's engineering students are potentially our employees for tomorrow," says David Overholt, engineering vice president for Honeywell Engine Systems & Accessories. "We enjoy our ongoing relationship with ASU and appreciated this opportunity to invest in our mutual future success."

Honeywell International is a $23 billion diversified technology and manufacturing leader, serving customers worldwide with aerospace products and services; control technologies for buildings, homes and industry; automotive products; turbochargers; specialty chemicals; fibers; and electronic and advanced materials.

Based in Phoenix, Honeywell's aerospace business is a leading global provider of integrated avionics, engines, systems and service solutions for aircraft manufacturers, airlines, business and general aviation, military, space and airport operations.

Koldoff, with the Fulton School of Engineering, can be reached at (480) 965-0213 or (jess.koldoff@asu.edu).
