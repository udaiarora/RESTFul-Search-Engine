Anthropogenic Impacts on CO2 Emissions: How Land-use Change and Fuel Efficiency Standards Affect CO2 Emissions

ABSTRACT

The Phoenix, AZ metropolitan region is characterized by a CO2 dome that peaks near the urban center resulting from anthropogenic sources of CO2 and the physical geography of the area. Sources of CO2 emissions (vehicles, humans, airports, power plants, landfills, and soils) were quantified across the metropolitan region. Since vehicles were responsible for the largest portion of CO2 emissions from the Phoenix area, predictions of CO2 levels in the Phoenix area for the year 2020 under different fuel efficiency standards scenarios were then created. Three scenarios for projected CO2 levels for the year 2020 in the Phoenix metropolitan area are compared: 1) fuel efficiency at current levels, 2) fuel efficiency increased by 30%, and 3) fuel efficiency increased by 15%. These scenarios were created through a GIS model of current and future CO2 emissions based on data from current CO2 levels from land use, traffic, and population and projected CO2 levels from the same sources.
