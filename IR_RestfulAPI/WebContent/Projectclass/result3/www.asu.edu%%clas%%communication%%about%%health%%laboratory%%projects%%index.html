Personnel | Facilities and Equipment | Current Projects

Marital Affection and Stress.  In this study, married couples complete assessments of the level of affectionate communication characterizing their relationship.  One spouse in each couple then provides a series of saliva samples over the course of a typical day.  These are assesseed for several hormonal stress markers, which will then be compared to both spouses' reports of affectionate communication and marital quality.

Affectionate Communication and Blood Chemistry.  Glycosylated hemoglobin is an assessment of a person's average level of blood glucose over a period of approximately three months.  Since stress can elevate blood glucose, higher-than-normal levels of glycosylated hemoglobin can be indicative of chronic stress (at least for non-diabetic adults).  The first phase of this project establishes an inverse correlation between glycosylated hemoglobin and a person's trait level of affectionate behavor.  The second phase tests the ability of a behavioral intervention designed to  increase affectionate behavior at reducing glycosylated hemoglobin and whole cholesterol levels over a three-month period.

Affectionate Touch as a Moderator of Stress Reactivity.  Several studies have establishesd the health enhancing effects of interpersonal and therapeutic touch.  This project will examine the influence of several aspects of touch to determine how much their efficacy in moderating a physical stress response; these include 1) the type of touch; 2) the nature of the relationship between the toucher and the touched; and 3) the sex of the toucher.  Participants will undergo the divers’ challenge test, wherein portions of the face are covered temporarily by frozen ice packs.  This activates both the sympathetic and parasympathetic branches of the autonomic nervous system simultaneously.  Hormonal and cardiovascular reactivity to the divers’ challenge test will be assessed and the relative effects of these touch variables at moderating the reactivity will be investigated.
