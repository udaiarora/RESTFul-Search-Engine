March 9, 2004

Recent job growth signals Arizona's upturn

The most recent available data show slow, steady, widespread gains in job growth in Arizona. The economy is coming back and, according to a report in the Arizona Blue Chip , the figures suggest a much more positive trajectory than has been seen for some time.

"Arizona job growth in the fourth quarter of 2003 was the best it has been since the recession started in 2001," said Lee McPheters, contributing editor. "December was the strongest month of the quarter - and had the fastest job growth for the whole year - with an increase of 2 percent over the same month of 2002. Although 2 percent is less than half the 4 to 5 percent increases recorded in the latter 1990s, it was good enough to push Arizona into second place among all states in rate of job growth for December."

 Nevada led all states in over-the-year job creation in December. Arizona ranked second, followed by Alaska, Hawaii, Georgia and Florida, to round out the top five.

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip , Editor Tracy Clark surveyed the panel members on their views of spending priorities for the state's 2004-2005 budget. Education was the only category where the majority of the panel felt spending should be increased. "The panel is even more opposed to a tax cut this year than last year," Clark reported, "which can be explained by the improving economy and the still-difficult budget negotiations. Also, the panel retreated this year from a call for a tax increase, with those in favor declining to 43 percent from last year's 69 percent."

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. For information, call (480) 965-5543.
