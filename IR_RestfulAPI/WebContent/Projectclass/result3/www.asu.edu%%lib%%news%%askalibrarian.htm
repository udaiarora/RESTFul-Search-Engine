Ask a Librarian:
Get Live Help Online. Anytime. 24/7.


(posted 8/19/04)


Starting this fall semester, ASU Libraries have expanded our popular live Chat option for our Ask a Librarian service to be available 24 hours a day. That means you can get live help online whenever you need it, even if it is at 3:00 in the morning. Just click on the "Ask a Librarian" button , featured throughout the library's web site. This will connect you to a menu of our online help services. One of your options is Chat, which provides live help online with library reference staff. Once you log on a librarian will be able to help you and can even send web pages and databases to your computer.

Ask a Librarian Chat works like this: First you will log in and state your question:



A librarian will join in and have a conversation with you via chat. Any web pages sent to you will show up in the left hand side of the screen.



After your conversation with the librarian is over, a transcript will be sent to you via email.

We also provide a fast and easy e-mail help service. Just choose "E-mail" from the main Ask a Librarian page and you will receive a response back from one of our reference librarians within twenty-four hours (but usually much quicker).

Ready to try it?
