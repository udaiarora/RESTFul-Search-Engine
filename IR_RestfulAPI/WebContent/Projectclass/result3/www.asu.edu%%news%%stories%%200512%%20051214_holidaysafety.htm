December 14, 2005

ASU police officials offer safety tips for holiday season

While the holiday season brings gift shopping, traveling and celebrating, it also is an opportune time for more unfortunate situations, such as theft. To ensure the safety of students, staff and faculty during the holiday season, ASU police officials are offering the following safety tips.

• Be aware of your surroundings, and remember to take precautions at all times.

• Walk or jog with a friend, never alone.

• Avoid isolated, dark areas.

• Do not expose cash or other tempting items, such as jewelry, in public.

• Use campus safety escort services on the Tempe campus at night by calling (480) 965-1515.

• Tell a friend where you are going and when you will return.

• Carry a whistle or noisemaker. Do not be afraid to shout if you need help.

• Walk with confidence. Show that you are aware and in control.

• Park in an area that will be well lit when you return.

• When approaching your car or door, get your keys out in advance. Don’t fumble for your keys at the door.

• Remember to always lock your car and place valuables in the trunk to hide them from direct eyesight.

• When walking to your car, look under, around and in front of the vehicle to make sure there is no suspicious activity.

• Do not get in your car until you have checked inside, especially the back seat.

• Before driving, lock your doors and put on your seat belt.

For more information, please call the ASU Police Crime Prevention Unit at (480) 965-1972.
