Gadgets and Gizmos to star at ASU Art Museum's free family fun day

There are parts that move, hover, click and flash. There are cameras, monitors, gears and glass. All these parts and pieces, "gadgets and gizmos," take on new meaning when put together by an artist who uses technology mixed with his or her creative abilities to build an intriguing piece of artwork.

The fourth annual Family Fun Day at the ASU Art Museum takes the theme of Gadgets and Gizmos from this year's summer family exhibition. The entire family can escape the summer heat and enjoy this unique exhibition and tons of free family fun from 10 a.m. - 2 p.m., Saturday, June 28.

Families will enjoy activities, events and exhibitions in the cool, 68-degree interior and the underground nymphaeum of the ASU Art Museum. Among the many activities scheduled for this year's family day are puppet shows, a magician, animal demonstrations by Phoenix Zoo and numerous hands-on projects coordinated by the ASU Art Museum and other Valley organizations.

The Arizona Science Center will provide a magnetic car track for which visitors can create vehicles, and a zoetrope - an early cinema machine that uses the most primitive of moving picture technology. Hands-on activities coordinated by ASU Art Museum staff will also include the opportunity to build your own zoetrope or thaumatrope, another simple technology that creates moving pictures.

Michelle Nichols Dock, curator of education at the ASU Art Museum, said that many of this year's activities deal with optical illusions and problem solving, exploring the unique nature of art and science working together.

Re-using found objects is another theme in the hands-on activity area. Trash sculptures will give children the opportunity to use recycled materials to create their own artwork, just as renowned artists such as Deborah Butterfield do. Another activity called Garbage Pizza will look at how much of what our society throws away could be recycled.

The Phoenix Family Museum activity bus will also participate in the event again this year, providing another selection of hands-on activities for children, and ARTability will staff an information booth about disability issues.

The ASU Art Museum is a division of The Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts at Arizona State University. It is located on the southeast corner of Mill Avenue and 10th Street, Tempe. For more information, please call (480) 965-2787 or visit the museum online at <http://asuartmuseum.asu.edu>.

When You Go:

Location: ASU Art Museum, Nelson Fine Arts Center, corner Mill Avenue and 10th Street, Tempe.
Date &Time: ASU Art Museum's annual Family Fun Day will be held from 10 a.m. - 2 p.m., Saturday, June 28.

Gadgets and Gizmos runs through August 31.

Parking: Free parking is available in ASU Art Museum-marked spaces at the south end of Tempe Center, located at the NE corner of Mill Ave. and 10th St. Visitors using museum spaces must sign in at the front desk in the lobby of the Nelson Fine Arts Center. Free parking is also available on weekends in Parking Structure #3 on Myrtle Avenue, Tempe, and in the Gammage Auditorium parking lot.

Website: <http://asuartmuseum.asu.edu>
Cost: Free
