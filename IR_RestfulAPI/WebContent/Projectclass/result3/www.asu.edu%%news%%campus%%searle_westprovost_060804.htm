June 8, 2004

ASU names Searle as West campus provost

Mark Searle, ASU vice provost for academic affairs at the West campus, has been named ASU vice president and provost for the West campus, pending Board of Regents' approval. He will immediately be named provost-designee and has begun the transition to his new role.

Searle has been with ASU at the West campus since 1995, when he became the dean of the College of Human Services and a professor in the Department of Recreation and Tourism Management. In 2002, he was named vice provost for academic affairs.

Prior to coming to ASU, he was director of a multidisciplinary research institute and professor at the University of Manitoba for 12 years.

"We're very pleased that someone of Mark's academic stature, administrative experience and familiarity with ASU's West campus has agreed to take on this responsibility," says Milton Glick, executive vice president and provost.

"As dean of the College of Human Services, he expanded student enrollment and increased the diversity of both the student body and the faculty. He's also had an active working relationship with the Maricopa Community College District. Mark Searle will no doubt continue the positive trajectory and leadership tradition established by Provost Elaine Maimon."

Searle earned a bachelor's degree in psychology and political science from the University of Winnipeg, a master's in recreation administration from the University of North Dakota and a Ph.D. in recreation administration from the University of Maryland.

Elaine Maimon, who has been provost for eight years, recently was named chancellor of the University of Alaska, Anchorage. During her tenure, West campus has grown from offering only upper-division courses to a full four-year institution, with an enrollment increase of 77 percent.

In regard to the university system reorganization plan proposed this week by the Board of Regents, Searle says, "The proposal outlined presents an important opportunity to discuss the future of the Arizona system for higher education. I look forward to engaging in this process, and I know our campus community will have much to contribute to this discussion. At the same time, we will continue to move the campus forward and work to further the goals we have set."
