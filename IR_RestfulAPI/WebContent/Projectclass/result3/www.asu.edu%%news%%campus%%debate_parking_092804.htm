September 28, 2004

Presidential debate update: Temporary parking plan in place

With debate day less than three weeks away, ASU plans to shift some employees and students who park in lots affected by the event at Gammage Auditorium to other parking facilities on campus.

About 2,500 of the more than 17,000 employee, visitor and non-resident student parking spaces on the Tempe campus will be affected. With a goal of minimum disruption to classes and traffic, ASU DPS and Parking & Transit Services encourage students, staff, residents and local businesses to be patient and flexible during debate week as some plans may change and some temporary road closures to accommodate motorcades are expected.

According to City of Tempe transit authorities, Apache Blvd. will be closed from College Avenue to 10th Street Tuesday, October 12 until Thursday, October 14. Gammage Parkway and Forest Avenue will also be closed Tuesday, October 12 until Thursday, October 14.

Carpooling and ride sharing are two ways to get to work and school to lessen traffic congestion and demands on parking facilities around campus. For more information on carpools, contact ASU Parking & Transit’s Travel Reduction at 480 965-1072. Contact Valley Metro for information about ride sharing and other options at: http://www.valleymetro.org/Rideshare3/index.html.

Thousands of media will converge on ASU to cover the debate but journalists will not be parking on campus. Instead, a continuous loop of shuttles will move them to and from the Media Filing Center outside Gammage Auditorium.

Students, employees and visitors should continue to watch for traffic and parking updates via the ASU website and the local media, as plans are subject to change.

Decal parking – students and employees
Lot 3 – Gammage Auditorium
Lot 3 will be closed to parking Friday evening, October 8 and will reopen Monday morning, October 18. Employees and students holding parking decals for Lot 3 will park in spaces reserved for Lot 3 patrons at Lot 16 at the old Tempe Center, Mill Avenue and University Drive. Parking there is on a first-come-first-served basis, and additional space is available in Lot 40 at Apache Blvd, and McAllister Avenue. Signs will identify these lots as space for Lot 3 decal holders.

Student parking in Structure 1
Residential roof parking for students will be closed Tuesday, October 12 in the afternoon and will reopen Thursday afternoon, October 14. Alternate student decal parking will be available in south residential Lots 35, 37 and 38, south of Apache Blvd.

Visitor parking
Visitor parking – Structure 1
Visitor parking spaces in Structure 1, located at on the north side pf Apache Blvd. between Normal and College Avenue will be limited October 6 through October 8 and all visitor parking spaces in Structure 1 will be closed Saturday, October 9 through Friday, October 15.

Alternate visitor parking is available by reservation in lot 42, at McAllister and Lemon. Space is limited due to nearby construction. Make reservations online at http://www.asu.edu/dps/pts/visitor/reserve.html or call 480-965-6209.

Visitor parking is also available in Lot 45 at the University Services Building (USB), Rural Rd. at Spence. The USB shuttle runs to the main campus every day except on debate day, October 13. Alternate routes and stops may be announced later.

Metered parking in Forest Avenue and Gammage Parkway
Metered parking along Forest Avenue and Gammage Parkway will be available until Monday evening, October 11, assuming there are no changes in plans to erect planned security fencing.

Visitor parking – Lot 17W
There will be no visitor parking available in Lot 17 W located at Apache Blvd. and College Avenue on October 12 and 13.

Parking for people with disabilities
ASU disabled parking decal holders who park in Lot 3 will find disability parking accomodations October 8 - 18 at Lot 41, next to the law library on McAllister, and at Lot 27 east of the bookstore. Lots will be monitored.

Flash service
Flash Forward and Flash Back service will be suspended on debate day, Wednesday, October 13.

University Flash service will operate extended hours on debate day, from 7 a.m. – 10 p.m. Unscheduled street closures on debate day could cause temporary disruptions in service.

Flash Express service from Lot 59 to the south campus Lot 40 area will operate on debate day from 7 a.m. – 10 p.m. Riders will board Flash Express at locations on the east side of Packard Drive and McAllister Avenue. Unscheduled street closures on debate day could cause temporary disruptions in service.

Parking for the public comment area on debate day
Parking for visitors to the public comment area/speakers’ corner will be in Lots 59E (Band Field) 6th Street and Rural Rd, and 59NE, east of Rural Rd. and north of Rio Salado.

Valley Metro bus service
For information about Valley Metro Bus service call 602-253-5000 or visit www.valleymetro.org.

Parking for the news media
On debate day, all media personnel will be transported to and from the ASU campus via shuttle. The shuttle lot is at Phoenix Municipal Stadium at N. Priest Drive and Mill Avenue. Media may drive their personal cars or take hotel or public transportation to the shuttle site. All media will undergo security and credential screening before boarding shuttles.
