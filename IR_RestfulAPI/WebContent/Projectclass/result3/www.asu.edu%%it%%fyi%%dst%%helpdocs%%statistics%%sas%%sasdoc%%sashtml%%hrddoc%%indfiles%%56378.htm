A Handbook of Statistical Analyses Using SAS

by B. S. Everitt and G. Der

A friendly handbook that introduces SAS software to users who perform statistical analysis, especially in the pharmaceutical and medical research fields. Undergraduates who study applied statistics will also gain valuable knowledge through the books numerous examples and exercises that are provided in each chapter. Techniques are described and then applied to a particular data set taken from "A Handbook of Small Data Sets," published by Chapman and Hall. Topics covered are: multiple regression, analysis of variance, logistic regression, cluster analysis, plus more! A terrific companion to your comprehensive SAS manuals library.

175 pages.
