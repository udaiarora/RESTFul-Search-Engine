G.2.1: Probationary Evaluation

Effective 2/2/90 .. Revised 3/20/2002

Probationary evaluations are carried out in the second and fourth years according to the Schedule of Personnel Actions, which is prepared and distributed to all academic professionals by the Assistant Dean for Personnel at the beginning of each fiscal year. Refer to the ACD 506-08, for general procedures for Probationary Evaluations of Academic Professionals.


Assistant academic professionals have a probationary period of no more than six years. Associate academic professionals, hired without continuing appointment, have a probationary period of no more than four years. See ACD 506-02 and ACD 506-03. for extension of normal probationary term.


Retention as an assistant academic professional requires evidence of successful job performance and activity or achievement in other Criteria indicating progress toward continuing appointment as the probationary period progresses. See Librarians' Handbook, E.2, Criteria for Retention, Continuing Appointment, and Promotion.

THE ASSISTANT DEAN FOR PERSONNEL


The Assistant Dean for Personnel distributes the Request for Academic Personnel Action form to the primary evaluators of the 2nd and 4th year probationary librarians.


THE PROBATIONARY ACADEMIC PROFESSIONAL

  1. Prepares an up-to-date curriculum vitae. The curriculum vitae is a complete listing of career activities and is in reverse chronological order; activities from pre-ASU professional positions are included. The sections of the curriculum vitae are: education, professional employment history, professional development and contribution (defined according to Criteria), and service (defined according to Criteria). Refer to the Librarians' Handbook, E.2

  2. Writes a letter to the Chair of the Review Committee (2 pages maximum) summarizing activities and accomplishments to date. The letter should follow the same arrangement as the Criteria. Academic professional addresses Job Performance and identifies areas of Professional Development and Contribution and Service which show continuing activity over time and describes those activities.

  3. Encloses copies of material which demonstrate successful job performance and/or show evidence that the Criteria have been met. Supporting material may include: copies of publications, papers, and projects; copies of the title page, table of contents, and/or programs for books, recitals or presentations; letters of recognition or acknowledgment for service, professional activity or job performance; and other appropriate material. While no limit is placed on the amount of accompanying materials submitted, only two items will be forwarded to the Provost's office with the packet. The academic professional should include an inventory of accompanying material and indicate which two items are to be forwarded.

  4. Reviews the job description for completeness and accuracy, and revises it as necessary in consultation with the primary evaluator. Adds a copy of the current job description to the packet. (See Librarians' Handbook, F.1)

  5. Submits the packet to the Primary Evaluator.

  6. Notifies the Assistant Dean for Personnel in writing that the packet was submitted to the primary evaluator.

THE PRIMARY EVALUATOR

  1. Writes a letter to the Chair of the Review Committee evaluating the probationary academic professional's performance and accomplishments in all areas (job performance, professional development and contribution and service), with an emphasis on job performance, from the date of hire. The letter identifies strengths and weaknesses in relation to the Criteria and evaluates progress toward continuing status. It is added to the packet.

  2. Indicates a recommendation for retention, conditional retention, or termination on the Request for Personnel Action Form and signs it. The packet is sent to the Associate Dean (if there is one). If there is no Associate Dean it is sent to the Assistant Dean for Personnel.

THE ASSOCIATE DEAN

  1. Writes a letter evaluating the academic professional's strengths and weaknesses in relation to the Criteria. The letter is added to the packet.

  2. Gives the packet to the Assistant Dean for Personnel, who submits it to the Review Committee for Personnel Action.

THE REVIEW COMMITTEE FOR PERSONNEL ACTION

  1. The Review Committee writes an analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of the candidate in relation to the Criteria. This analysis includes a recommendation for retention, conditional retention, or termination. The letter is added to the packet.

  2. The Chair of the Review Committee indicates the Committee's recommendation on the form and signs it.

  3. The Chair of the Review Committee sends the packet to the University Librarian and Dean of the University Libraries.

THE UNIVERSITY LIBRARIAN AND DEAN OF THE UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES


Writes a letter evaluating the academic professional's strengths and weaknesses in relation to the Criteria. The letter is added to the packet.


Indicates a recommendation for retention, conditional retention, or termination on the form and signs it.


Sends the completed packet to the Assistant Dean for Personnel, who is responsible for making sure that the packet is complete before forwarding it to the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University.


NOTIFICATION


Procedures for the notification of academic professionals are outlined in ACD 506-10.


CONDITIONAL RETENTION


Conditional retention is an optional recommendation for a probationary academic professional who is not making satisfactory progress toward continuing appointment. The University Librarian and Dean of the University Libraries must notify the academic professional of the conditional status and provide a written statement of conditions that must be met to warrant a regular contract the following year. These conditions should be developed by the primary evaluator and the Associate Dean when appropriate. The specific program for improvements and/or accomplishments, including a timetable, is to be developed by the primary evaluator and the academic professional. See ACD 506-10.


NON-REAPPOINTMENT


Refer to ACD 508-01.
