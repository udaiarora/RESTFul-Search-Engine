2003 W. P. Carey School of Business Hall of Fame: Mary Hentges of PayPal honored at ASU homecoming

The 2003 inductee into the W. P. Carey School of Business Hall of Fame is 1981 accountancy graduate Mary Hentges, vice president and CFO of eBay's online global payments platform, PayPal.

Hentges will speak at the Hall of Fame Luncheon, Friday, Oct. 31 at the University Club on the Arizona State University main campus. Tickets are $25; reservations are being taken online at asu.edu/homecoming. Media seating at no charge is available by contacting Liz Farquhar at liz.farquhar@asu.edu.

Hentges joined eBay in February 2003 where she heads up the PayPal finance team and manages all finance functions including financial strategy, planning and analysis, accounting, compliance, treasury and internal audit, as well as the integration of PayPal's systems into eBay.

PayPal services enable any business or consumer with email to send and receive online payments securely, conveniently and cost-effectively. PayPal serves as a critical link between buyer and seller.

"The eBay and PayPal platforms enable people of all ethnicities and abilities to be in an equal position where they can run a small business online," said Hentges. "I feel like we are enabling our community of users to do great things."

Hentges' desire to help others is also reflected by the ties she keeps with the W. P. Carey School of Business. After graduation Hentges immediately began giving back.

"I felt very blessed to have gone to ASU," said Hentges who was the first in her family to complete college. While Hentges was at ASU, her mother decided to enroll and complete her degree. Several of her siblings followed.

"When I was able, I started giving back to the school so that it would be there for others," said Hentges. She has been an active supporter of the Accountancy Circle, a group formed to advise and develop the School of Business at W. P. Carey.
"It is impossible to deliver a quality program relying solely on tuition and state support," said James Boatsman, professor and director of the School of Accountancy. "Assistance from dedicated alumni like Mary is crucial."

W. P. CAREY SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
