Librarians' Council

Minutes

 

April 20, 2005

Hayden C6A
 

Meeting was called to order at 3:05 pm.

 

A quorum was present.

 

I. Approval of Minutes (February 16, 2005) – Minutes were approved as amended.

 

Kurt clarified that the work on the multiyear contract has not been postponed; the AP Implementation Task Force is still working on it. The submission of the proposal to ABOR for approval has been postponed as the U of A and the NAU had not yet approved it.

 

Kurt also gave clarification on the 2003-04 ARL Salary Survey - Table 21. Its usefulness diminished because it is based on the 68 public ARL libraries which also include non-peer institutions.

 

II. AP Implementation Task Force – Kurt Murphy

 

Nancy Gutierrez is leaving ASU.

 

AP Implementation Task Force calendar:

1. The Task Force is still trying to get the proposal for multiyear contract section to ABOR by next session for an approval.

 

2. The Task Force is now meeting every week

 

3. The finished drafts of the ACD sections will be done by July and the rewritten sections of the ACD manual will be presented to Academic Senate for approval no later than the end of the Fall semester.

 

III. Committee Reports

 

a. Personnel Committee – Betsy Redman

 

The committee is continuing the work on the Mentoring Program section of the Librarians’ Handbook.

 

There was a discussion on the change in the Schedule for Personnel Action. The due dates for Promotion and Continuing Appointment application packet due to Dean and PE has been combined. This year, the due date will be August 20. From 2006-07, it will be August 1.

 

b. Research and Professional Development Committee – Jennie Duvernay - No Report

 

c. Compensation Committee – Dan Stanton - No Report

 

d. Review Committee – Julie Tharp - No Report

 

e. Academic Senate – Deborah Koshinsky

 

Academic Senate met for the last time this year on Monday, April 18. Results were announced for the 2005 Academic Assembly Election.

 

Congratulations to:

 

Julie Tharp (2005-08 member) and Jeannette Mueller-Alexander (Alternate member) – Academic Grievance Committee,
and, Lydia LaFaro (2005-08 member) – Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

 
Dr. Crow: Every numerical indicator for Arizona State University has increased. He is extremely pleased with the recent hires.

 

Provost Glick: ASU has evidenced the astronomical growth. ASU hired 169 faculty members, and 7 faculty members are National Academy of Science members. Retention and graduation rates have increased by 9% this year.

 

The Senate and the Administration will be working on the implementation policy on Promotion and Tenure for faculty over the summer.

 

Senate Resolution 23 – ABOR and State Legislature initiated the resolution. Addition to ACD manual: reference and background checks will be conducted for all the jobs posted (each open position). The hiring unit will be responsible for funding the checks, and it will be a part of the hiring process. Academic Senate approved the resolution. The biggest challenges may be for the staff who are already working at ASU and want to apply for another ASU position and for foreign nationals in terms of how such background checks will be facilitated.

 

ACD manual: The section on Misappropriation of University Assets has been renamed as Misuse of University Assets.

 

Doug Johnson, the chair of the Personnel Committee presented a resolution endorsing the establishment of the Voluntary Employee Beneficiary Association (VEBA) – which was passed by the Senate.

 

f. Committee on Academic Professional Status – Deborah Koshinsky (for Dan Stanton)

 

CAPS Spotlight speaker series, spring forum is Thursday May 21, from 10-11 AM. Barbara Kerr, Chair and Susan Mattson, Chair-elect of Academic Senate will discuss number of topics relevant to APs.

 

g. Web Master – Yuwu Song - no report.

 

IV. Old Business

 

V. New Business

 

Call for nominations for 2005 Librarians’ Council, Nominations & Elections Committee – Deborah Koshinsky

 

There was a call for nominations from the floor. None were made. The ballot will be sent on April 22 to be returned by April 29. Election results will be announced by May 3rd.

 

Meeting adjourned 3:50 p.m.
