K.1: Mentoring Program

Effective 3/6/91 .. Revised 3/6/91

The mentoring program introduces academic professionals to the new job environment and is available both to new academic professionals and to academic professionals who have changed job assignments. This six-month program matches the academic professional with a mentor who serves as a resource person.


The program may be initiated by either the academic professional's supervisor or the academic professional. The office of the Assistant Dean for Personnel maintains a list of mentors and coordinates program activities.


MENTOR

The mentor provides another perspective of the libraries and campus environment and responds to questions and concerns of the academic professional. For this reason, it is recommended that the mentors have at least three years' service in the University Libraries.


PROCEDURES

The Assistant Dean for Personnel maintains a list of potential mentors from which the academic professional or the supervisor selects a mentor. Mentors for new academic professionals should be selected before the academic professional arrives.

The new academic professional's supervisor tells the mentor the new academic professional's arrival date.


In early April, the Personnel Committee holds a recruitment and information meeting for potential and current mentors and a meeting with new academic professionals who have recently participated in the mentoring program.


If the mentoring arrangement proves unsatisfactory to either party or if the mentor cannot continue through the end of the six-month program, a new mentor may be chosen.

The mentor's most important responsibility is to be available to answer questions and address concerns of the new academic professional. In addition, the mentor will maintain continuing dialogue with the new academic professional and with the academic professional's supervisor in order to assess the direction the program should take and to gauge its effectiveness.


SUGGESTED ACTIVITIES FOR MENTORING PROGRAM

  • Takes academic professional to lunch (paid by the University Libraries).
  • Takes academic professional to staff lounge or MU for coffee break.
  • Introduces new academic professional to the general campus environment, including location of bookstore, Gammage Auditorium museum shop, the Student Recreational Center, etc., and mentions various free and discounted benefits available.
  • Informs new academic professional of meetings/events which might be of interest.
  • Continues meeting with the new academic professional throughout the time period, including a second lunch (paid by the University Libraries).
