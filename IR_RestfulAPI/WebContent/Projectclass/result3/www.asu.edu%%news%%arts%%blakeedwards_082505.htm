August 25, 2005

ASU honors legendary filmmaker Blake Edwards Sept. 22-23

Public invited to gala reception and screening of Victor/Victoria to celebrate proposed ASU Center for Film and Media Research, as well as presentation of honorary degree during concert featuring music from the movies of Blake Edwards

ASU invites the public to two special events Sept. 22-23 to celebrate the planned launch this fall of the Center for Film and Media Research, as well as the life’s work of internationally acclaimed filmmaker Blake Edwards, who will receive an honorary doctoral degree from the university.

Edwards, whose more than 50 films include such memorable titles as “Days of Wine and Roses,” “Breakfast at Tiffany’s,” “The Pink Panther,” “Victor/Victoria” and “10,” has been named the inaugural distinguished fellow in film writing, directing and producing at ASU.

Edwards, recognized in 2004 by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences for an “extraordinary distinction in lifetime achievement,” also will teach a course on film production as part of the university’s recently launched bachelor’s degree in film program.

The first event to be held is a gala reception and film screening at 6 p.m., Sept. 22, of “Victor/Victoria,” with Edwards as guest of honor. It takes place at Harkins Centerpoint Theatre on Mill Avenue in Tempe. Edwards will address the audience before the screening.

The gala is a fundraiser for ASU’s proposed Center for Film and Media Research, which will be launched this fall pending Arizona Board of Regents’ approval. Tickets cost $50 and can be purchased online at (http://clas.asu.edu/newsevents/publicevents/BlakeEdwardsGalainvite.htm).

The center will provide a prominent research environment for transdisciplinary study of the significant cultural, social and economic role film and media play in shaping human values and global communities.

ASU’s new bachelor’s degree in film program includes a concentration in film and media studies in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, as well as a concentration in film and media production in the Herberger College of Fine Arts Department of Theater. The core academic program is under way, with the production side set to start in 2006.

Edwards will receive his honorary doctoral degree following intermission at a special ASU Symphony Orchestra concert Sept. 23, featuring music from his films. “A Musical Tribute to Blake Edwards” takes place at 7:30 p.m. in Gammage Auditorium and is free and open to the public.

The orchestra, under the direction of ASU professor of music Timothy Russell, will perform music from the movies of the famed director.

The evening will feature soprano Monica Mancini, the daughter of composer Henry Mancini, who composed many of the songs from Edwards’ work. Movie and music lovers will enjoy themes from “Peter Gunn” and “The Pink Panther,” as well as classics such as “ Moon River,” “Days of Wine and Roses” and “Bolero.”

Keeler, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 965-4012 or (sharon.keeler@asu.edu).
