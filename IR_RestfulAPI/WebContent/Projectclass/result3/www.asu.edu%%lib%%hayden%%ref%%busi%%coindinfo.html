COMPANY/INDUSTRY INFORMATION SOURCES

  1. Access Nippon. Tokyo: Access Nippon, [1990]- . Annual. (ASU owns 1992.)
      HF 3823 .A223x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 10A)

    Provides more than 50 industry and service sector overviews and over 250 selected company financial profiles for understanding the Japanese business market.

  2. CIRR/Corporate and Industry Research Reports Index. New York: R. R. Bowker, 1982-1991. Quarterly. (ASU owns 1982-1991.)
      HG 4001 .53x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9B) & HAYDEN REFERENCE MARS

    This service covering research reports written by investment firm analysts and economists has ceased but is still useful for historical research. It included information on specific companies, industries, and special reports on the state of the economy and potential investments as well as periodicals and newsletters published by these investment firms. The index is located in Reference, and microfiche copies of the reports are in Microforms (Microcard 4x6 HG 4001 .532x). It is preferable to search the CD-ROM version of the index because it includes abstracts for the reports and allows retrieval by key words in the title or abstract so it is not limited to the company name/industry indexing of the paper version. Use General Business File (see under "Business Periodical Indexes and Abstracts") for current investment analyst reports.

  3. Moody's Investor Fact Sheets: Industry Review. New York: Moody's Investors Service, 1978- . Semi-weekly. (ASU owns 1979- .)
      HG 4907 .M65x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9A)

    Provides comparative financial statistics and rankings for about 4,000 major companies within 145 industry groupings. Ranked by various financial criteria: net income, revenues, P-E ratios, return on equity, yield, and 12-month and 7-year price behavior. Companies are also sometimes ranked by certain industry-specific factors. The data on each industry is updated twice a year on average.

  4. Value Line Investment Survey. New York: Value Line, 1936- . Weekly. (ASU owns 1969- . Latest year in Reference and Reserve; 1969-1979 on film and 1980- on fiche in Microforms; 1975- in Journals.)
      HG 4915 .V32 HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9A) & RESERVE

    The original edition covers approximately 1,700 companies in about 90 industries (see the expanded edition below for small cap stocks). It is divided into three parts, with the bulk of the data appearing in the 13 sections of Part Three called "Ratings and Reports." These reports consist of 1-page analyses of each company arranged by industry and packed with data on the company's financial position, including: 18-year financial statistics, 5-year quarterly sales, EPS, dividends paid, financial strength, price stability, earnings predictability, Beta, safety and timeliness rating, brief description of the business, new developments, outlook, and stock price charts. Updated, industry-by-industry, on a rotating basis, each company report is revised twice a year. Then Part One is the index (front of main binder) which leads you to the correct pages in all sections. And lastly, Part Two (in separate binders on the same table) called "Selection and Opinion" is a weekly newsletter supplementing the semi-annual reports.

  5. Value Line Investment Survey: Expanded Edition. New York: Value Line, 1995- . Weekly. (ASU owns 1995- .)
      HG 4915 .V323x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9A) & Reserve

    The Expanded Edition adds about 1,800 mostly small cap stocks representing over 80 industries to Value Line's coverage but without the estimates and analyst commentaries provided in the original edition.
