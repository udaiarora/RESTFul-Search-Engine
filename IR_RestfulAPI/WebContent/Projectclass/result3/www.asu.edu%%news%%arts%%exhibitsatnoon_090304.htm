September 3, 2004

New noontime tours go to 'Exhibits@Noon'

Want to add a little culture to your lunch menu? Check out Exhibits@Noon, a new program offered by the Museums, Galleries and Collections committee at Arizona State University's Tempe Campus.

On the fourth Wednesday of every month, the committee will sponsor a 45-minute tour of one of the 24 museums, galleries or collections, with a brief gallery talk, from noon to 1 p.m.

The first visit is to Luhrs Gallery, Hayden Library, on Sept. 22, where the exhibits  "Legacy of Leadership: U.S. Congressional Exhibit" and "Arizona Women in Politics: A Photo Essay" are up for viewing.

On Oct. 27, the tour goers will see some of ASU's public art collection, and on Nov. 24, the Center for Meteorite Studies.

Future dates and destinations are Dec. 22, Gallery 100 or the Harry Wood Gallery; Jan. 26, behind the scenes at the ASU Art Museum;  Feb. 23, the Museum of Anthropology; March 23, Archaeological Research Institute; April 27, Mars Space Flight Facility; and May 25, Gallery of Design.

Those wishing to attend should meet at the venue for the day. For directions, go to the Museums & Galleries website, www.asu.edu/museums. No food is allowed in the galleries and museums.
