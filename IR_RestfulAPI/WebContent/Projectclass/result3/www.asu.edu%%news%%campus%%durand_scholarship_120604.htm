December 6, 2004

Nursing scholarship highlights former dean’s legacy

The College of Nursing at ASU has made life easier for its new doctoral students by announcing a new scholarship to lighten their financial load.

The Barbara Durand Doctoral Scholarship, in honor of the college’s former dean, will award the inaugural class of five incoming doctoral students paid tuition for all four years of study starting in August.

“This puts us in a competitive position to recruit outstanding doctoral students,” says Karen Sousa, associate dean for graduate programs and research, “and the scholarship not only honors our former dean, but it will allow our students to accomplish their goals much faster if finances aren’t a worry.”

The scholarship was created to attract some of the best prospective doctoral students in the state and country.

Durand, whom the scholarship is named after, retired last summer after serving 12 years as dean of the College of Nursing.

“I’m thrilled to be a part of this doctoral program that we worked so hard and so long to get established,” Durand says.

The ongoing scholarship will assist doctoral students and will help meet the nursing faculty shortage. It is anticipated that many of the graduates will pursue roles in academia.

For additional information about the DNS program and the Barbara Durand Doctoral Scholarship, visit the College of Nursing’s Web site, (http://nursing.asu.edu), or contact the Graduate Programs Office at (480) 965-3948.
