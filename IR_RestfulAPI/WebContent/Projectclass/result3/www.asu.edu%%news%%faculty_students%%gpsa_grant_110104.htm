November 1, 2004

Grant competition fosters research by graduates

The Graduate and Professional Student Association (GPSA) has announced 33 ASU graduate student recipients of the 2004-2005 GPSA Research Program. The ASU Office of the Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs provided $55,000 for the GPSA Research Grant Program awards.

The research grant program is a peer-reviewed competition that relies on ASU graduate student volunteer efforts. The GPSA Office of Internal Affairs coordinates this program.

This year there were about 150 grant applications and more than 100 graduate student reviewers. This is the largest number of applicants and reviewers participating in the grant program in the last five years of operation. The awards were announced on Oct. 1.

The maximum award per recipient is $2,000, and the money is used to support graduate research leading to a degree or publication. About 50 students, faculty and guests attended an award reception at the House of Tricks on Oct. 7.

The GPSA continues to document the increasing quality of research conducted by ASU graduate students. A significant number of high-quality research project applications could not be funded this year. GPSA lobbies for additional research grant funds with the goal of becoming the largest peer-review graduate student research program in the nation. This program provides important professional development training for graduate students and reflects the growing success at ASU in graduate student research and community involvement.

The GPSA thanks the 250 graduate students who participated in the grant program this year. It was the most successful year ever in terms of quality and participation in the program.
