December 18, 2003

Distinguished management scholar at W. P. Carey School of Business; Luis Gomez-Mejia receives University of Minnesota's highest alumni honor

Luis Gomez-Mejia, a management professor at the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University, will be honored by the University of Minnesota with its highest alumni accolade: the Outstanding Achievement Award. The award, which recognizes Gomez-Mejia for his professional achievement as a management scholar, will be presented at a celebration at the Phoenician, 6000 E. Camelback Road, Scottsdale, on Feb. 19 at 6 p.m.

"It would probably be impossible to identify an academic educated during the last 25 years in the Ph.D. program based in the Carlson School who has accomplished more in a career than Luis Gomez-Mejia," said John Fossum, professor of human resources and industrial relations at the Carlson School. "His record is replete with accomplishments and successes."

Gomez-Mejia, recognized internationally for his scholarship and leadership in the management discipline, is a Dean's Council of 100 Scholar at the W. P. Carey School and holds the Horace Steele Arizona Heritage Chair - one of the highest honors awarded by Arizona State University. In November 2003 he was also named a Regents Scholar, a title presented by the Arizona Board of Regents to the most distinguished professors at the three state universities.

Gomez-Mejia's research explores macro human resource management with a focus on executive compensation, high technology compensation, and compensation strategies. He has been named the most productive researcher in the management discipline by the Academy of Management Journal(AMJ) - the leading empirical journal in the field, as is one of just 33 scholars chosen from the Academy of Management's 13,500 members for the organization's Hall of Fame.

Dr. Gomez-Mejia has served two terms on the AMJ editorial board. In total he has published more than 120 papers, including "Determinants of Faculty Pay," which was judged the best article published in 1992 in the AMJ.

Gomez-Mejia has also authored, co-authored or edited 21 books, including a leading text in human resource management and texts in Spanish for European and Latin American markets. Additionally, he is the founder and president of the Ibero-American Academy of Management, which has become the catalyst for creating a community of management scholars among faculty and students at business schools in Spain, Portugal, Latin America and South America. His service contributions at Arizona State University and the Human Resource Division of the Academy of Management are also extensive.

"The management department at the W. P. Carey School of Business has made great strides toward becoming a top 20 leader in research and education that develops and promotes effective managerial practices," said William Glick, chairman. "Luis has contributed tremendously through his research, teaching, and service. His productivity contributed greatly to the department's third-place international ranking in premier management journals and he has received numerous outstanding service awards for his leadership roles in the major associations of the field."

Gomez-Mejia has taught at the Carlson School of Management, Hamline University, Mankato State University, University of Florida, and the University of Colorado at Boulder. He has been on the faculty at the W. P. Carey School at Arizona State University since 1989. He graduated summa cum laude from the University of Minnesota's College of Liberal Arts with a bachelor's degree in economics and received his masters and doctorate at the Carlson School in industrial relations in 1974 and 1981, respectively.

W. P. CAREY SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
