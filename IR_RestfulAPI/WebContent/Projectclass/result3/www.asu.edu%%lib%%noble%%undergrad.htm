The ASU Libraries

There are 8 libraries within 5 organizational units that serve ASU:

  • The University Libraries are composed of 4 libraries on the Tempe campus:
    • Hayden (Humanities, Social Sciences, Business)
    • Noble (Science and Engineering)
    • Architecture and Environmental Design, and
    • Music.
  • College of Law Library, also on the Tempe campus,
  • Downtown Campus Library,
  • Polytechnic Campus Library, and
  • West Campus Library.

All libraries are open to the general public in addition to ASU students, faculty and staff.

 

 

Daniel E. Noble Science and Engineering Library

Noble Library houses the collections for Engineering, Mathematics, Nursing and the Sciences (Astronomy, Biology, Chemistry, Geology, Physics) and includes the Maps Collection.

 

The library is located on the Tempe campus, on the southwest corner of Tyler and McAllister Malls, across the sidewalk from the Cancer Research and the Goldwater buildings.

 

Opened in 1983 and remodeled in 1999, the library encloses a four-story atrium that creates a spacious and pleasant atmosphere for study and research. Facilities include over 60 library workstations, wireless access to the campus network, plus open and enclosed study spaces for both groups and individuals.

 

The library has 4 service units with approximately 30 staff:

  • Access Services
  • Copy Services
  • Instruction, Marketing & Outreach
  • Maps Reference
  • Science Reference

Frequently Asked Questions About:

 

Checking Out Material

  • How do I get a Library Card?
    Your SunCard (ASU ID card) is used to checkout materials. Forget your card? - Show us a picture ID.

  • How many items may I check out? ... For how long?
    There is no limit on the number of items a student may check out. Undergraduate students may check out items for 14 days; items can be renewed until requested by another library user.

  • Are Fines Charged?
    For each item not returned or renewed within thirty (30) days of the due date, the borrower is assessed a $10 transaction fee and a replacement charge. If the item is returned, the replacement charge is cancelled and the borrower only needs to pay the transaction fee.

  • The Book I need is checked out - how do I get it?
    In the ASU Libraries Online Catalog, use the blue REQUEST button to have the item recalled. There is no charge for this service.

  • Can I check out a journal volume?
    Sorry, journals must be used in the library.

  • For more information see Access Services (http://www.asu.edu/lib/access/)
Document Delivery
  • The Item I need is in a library on the West Campus (or Polytechnic Campus or Downtown Phoenix campus) Library - do I have to go there to get it?
    No, the University Libraries' will retrieve or photocopy items from other campus libraries.

    • For books:
      In the ASU Libraries Online Catalog, use the blue REQUEST button to have the item brought to any one of the Tempe campus libraries' circulation desks. There is no charge for this service. Delivery Time: 2-3 business days.

    • For journals:
      Use the Library Express/Document Delivery photocopy request form. A photocopy of the article will be delivered to your mailbox on the Tempe campus, or you may obtain the copy from the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. There is no cost for this service. Delivery Time: 2-3 business days.

  • The Item I need is in Storage - where's that?
    The Libraries' operate a separate facility on the Polytechnic campus for overflow material and items that need special handling. The facility is not open to the public as the material is packaged and maintained in a special, controlled environment. To obtain material housed in this facility:
    • If you need the whole volume, use the REQUEST button on the catalog record OR stop by or call the Information/Reference Desk at 480/965-7607. We will have the item recalled. Delivery Time: 1-2 business days.
    • If you need just an article and have the citation information (title, year, volume, page numbers), use the Interlibrary Loan Service. The article will be scanned and you'll be notified of its availability via email.

  • The Item I need is not available at any ASU Library - can the library get it for me?
    Yes, use the Interlibrary Loan Service. You will be notified by e-mail when the item is available. Most photocopies come directly via e-mail; books must be picked up at the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. Usually there is no charge for this service, however, if there will be a cost involved, you will be contacted for approval, prior to the request being ordered. Delivery Time: Journal articles average 2-7 days, while books average 5-14 days. Materials that are rare, in fragile condition, in high use, or non-circulating in most libraries, may take considerably longer or may not be able to be acquired. Rush service and renewals for books are generally not available.

Copies

  • How much do Photocopies cost?
    Photocopies cost $0.10/page if using cash; $0.08/page if using a SunCard or Library Copy Card. .

  • Can copies be made of microfilm or microfiche?
    Yes, copies cost $0.15/page if using cash; $0.12/page if using a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

  • How much do computer prints cost?
    Computer prints cost $0.08/page and requires a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

Off-Campus Access to Electronic Resources

  • Can I use electronic journals and databases from my home?
    Yes, if you access the resource from the Libraries' web site -- going directly to resource's location on the web will result in a "turnaway". Via the Libraries website, you'll be asked to sign in with your ASURITE ID and Password; once authorized, you'll be connected to the journal/database.

Library Assignments

  • I have a library assignment and I have no idea where to begin. Can anyone help me?
    Yes, the science reference staff will be able to take the frustration out of that library assignment! We'll recommend what resources to use and show you how to use them. Find out where science reference is located and how to contact them.

  • My instructor says I can only use scholarly (or peer-reviewed) journal articles as my references. How can I tell if an article is scholarly or peer-reviewed?
    Being able to evaluate the quality and authority of information is an important part of the research process. Sounds like your instructor wants you to be able to distinguish research from the popular literature. Several clues can help you determine if an article is from a scholarly journal. Scholarly articles are usually:
    • Written by experts in the field
    • Written for researchers and professionals in a particular field
    • Written to report the results of a research study
    • Reviewed by experts in the field
      A group of experts have determined that the article is scholarly and that the research is valid; this process is called "peer review" or "editorial review". Check the journal (front or back pages if in print; journal home page if electronic) for an Editorial Board; the "Instructions for Authors" or the "Guidelines for Submission" may also indicate if articles are reviewed.
    • Lengthy and contain bibliographies and/or footnotes.

    For more information about distinguishing scholarly from popular literature, see SunTutor: How to Evaluating Sources.
