December 4, 2006

After 14 years of ASU service, Isaacson bids campus adieu

Terry Isaacson, ASU's vice provost for administrative services at the Polytechnic campus, is saying goodbye to the campus he has called home for more than 20 years ? 14 of which were as an ASU employee.

Before joining the ASU conversion team responsible for transforming the former Williams Air Force Base to an ASU campus in 1992, he was the wing commander at ?Willie,? the largest Air Force pilot training base in the country.

During his time at ASU, Isaacson:

? Worked with the U.S. Department of Education on the conveyance of the federal property for educational purposes.

? Played a lead role in conceptualizing, implementing and maintaining a privatized residential housing management operation, a creative contractual arrangement that provided $21 million in capital improvements.

? Has been instrumental in the master planning of the campus.

His familiarity with the base and the surrounding community served a critical role during the conversion process. Isaacson's knowledge of the campus' history will be missed.

A retirement reception will be held for Isaacson from 4:30 to 6:30 p.m., Dec. 7, in the Student Union Cooley Ballroom.

Those who want to attend are asked to R.S.V.P. by calling (480) 727-1028 or sending an e-mail to (provostrsvp@asu.edu).

Friends and colleagues are encouraged to pay tribute to Isaacson by contributing to a scholarship that he and his wife, Nancy, created. For more information, call Linda Holland at (480) 727-1897.
