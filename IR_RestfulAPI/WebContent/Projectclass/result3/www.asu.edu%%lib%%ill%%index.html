What is Interlibrary Loan (Ill)?

Interlibrary loan is a service to obtain resources from other libraries when they are not owned by ASU. These resources include, but are not limited to, journal and magazine articles, books and/or book chapters, newspaper articles, conference proceedings and dissertations. Many interlibrary loan items are received in electronic formats.


Material owned by ASU, including journal articles and books owned by the ASU Branch Libraries, can be requested from the following locations:

  • For Tempe Campus Users: Articles from journals held at Storage, ASU West, Downtown Phoenix Campus and ASU Polytechnic can be requested online through Interlibrary Loan for Electronic Delivery.

  • For ASU West Campus Users: Articles from journals held at Storage, Tempe Campus, Downtown Phoenix Campus and ASU Polytechnic can be requested online through Interlibrary Loan for Electronic Delivery.

  • For Downtown Phoenix Campus Users: Articles from journals held at Storage, Tempe Campus, ASU West and ASU Polytechnic can be requested online through Interlibrary Loan for Electronic Delivery.

  • Books located at the ASU Branch Campus Libraries should be requested online through the ASU Libraries catalog or through the Circulation Desks of the Branch Libraries.

  • Interlibrary Loan cannot be used for book delivery between the ASU campus libraries.

Use of interlibrary loan is intended for academic research only. Interlibrary Loan reserves the right to limit or refuse services as deemed necessary.

How do I place a request?

Place requests by filling out the online form.
Who can use it?

Currently registered ASU students and current employees of the ASU Tempe, ASU West and Downtown Phoenix campuses. ASU students and employees of the College of Law and the ASU Polytechnic campus should check with the ASU Polytechnic Library or the Ross-Blakely Law Library for the available services offered there.


Note for Distance Students: Many interlibrary loan items are received in electronic formats and are accessed online. Paper documents and books need to be picked up at Hayden, ASU West or Downtown Phoenix Campus Libraries. You may consider requesting these items through the Interlibrary Loan service of your local public library instead.


Standard Community Card holders are not eligible to use this service, although an Interlibrary Loan Community Card is available for purchase. If you are not affiliated with ASU, please check with your local library.
How long does it take?

  • Journal articles average a few days to a week - most are delivered in an electronic format to the patron's desktop.

  • Books average 2 weeks.

  • Rush service is not available and renewal of materials is rarely granted and only on a case by case basis.

Every attempt will be made to identify sources that are willing to lend your requested item, but not everything that can be cited is available for interlibrary loan.
How will I be notified when my item arrives and How do I receive it?

When your material arrives you will be notified by e-mail. Please be sure a correct, current e-mail address has been entered in your ILLiad profile when you register for Interlibrary Loan.
How do I receive Interlibrary Loan materials I have requested?

When you register for Interlibrary Loan, you will choose a location to receive your materials.


For all Loans:

  • Hold for Pickup at the Hayden Interlibrary Loan Counter.

  • Hold for Pickup at the ASU West Library Circulation Counter.

  • Hold for Pickup at the Downtown Phoenix Campus Library Circulation Counter.

  • Delivery to your Tempe Campus Office, (Delivery to a Tempe Campus Office is limited to Faculty, Staff and Graduate Students who have offices on the Tempe Campus).

For Photocopies:

  • Most photocopies will be delivered in an electronic format. Items unavailable in electronic formats will be received in the same manner as loans.

How Much Does It Cost?

There is no charge for most requests. When exceptions occur we will notify you prior to ordering, apprise you of the charges, and obtain your agreement to pay.
Videos

Faculty may request videos not owned by ASU but located at UA or NAU by using the media booking form. Please indicate the location (UA or NAU) along with call number in the call number field.


Videos not owned by ASU, NAU, or UA may be requested through Interlibrary Loan. Please be aware that most libraries do not loan videos and they may take longer than usual turnaround time to obtain. We cannot request that they come at a specified time for a class.

Need Further Assistance?

If you need help submitting Interlibrary Loan requests, or checking on the status of a request, or for any other Interlibrary Loan questions, call the ILL office at (480) 965-3282.
