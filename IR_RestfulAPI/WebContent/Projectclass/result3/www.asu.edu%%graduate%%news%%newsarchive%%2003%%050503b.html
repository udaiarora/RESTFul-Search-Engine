May 5, 2003


NEW DEANS APPOINTED IN ASU GRADUATE COLLEGE

With the appointment of Dean Bianca L. Bernstein to a director's position with the National Science Foundation, ASU has appointed two new deans to serve in the Graduate College. Dr. Maria T. Allison has been appointed to serve as interim dean. Dr. Filiz Ozel will begin her duties as associate dean for academic programs, effective May 15.

Executive Vice President & Provost Milton Glick made the announcement April 28th that Allison, an associate dean in the Graduate College for the past two years, will serve as interim dean. "We are aware of Dr. Allison's great talents, and she will operate with all the authority and power of a dean," Dr. Glick said. "We've had a great run with Dean Bernstein. She has shown great energy, initiative and insight. Dr. Allison will build on that momentum."

Interim Dean Allison joined the faculty in the Department of Recreation Management and Tourism in 1984. She became associate dean for academic programs in the Graduate College in 2001. Allison earned her Ph.D. at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign.

Dr. Allison recognized Dean Bernstein as, "an incredible leader who is as passionate about graduate education as anyone I've ever known. She has worked tirelessly to raise the bar in quality graduate experiences to enhance resources for students, and to provide important professional development opportunities for students."

Dr. Ozel has been a member of the faculty in the School of Architecture, in the College of Architecture and Environmental Design, since 1995. Formerly the assistant director of the School of Architecture and the interim director of the interdisciplinary Ph.D. program in Environmental Design and Planning, Dr. Ozel has advised graduate students on theses and dissertations, served as a graduate student mentor, and taught research methods and numerous other graduate-level courses. She is a strong advocate for graduate education as an important intellectual endeavor that contributes immensely to the personal growth of the individuals involved and to the well being of society as a whole.

"As a faculty member who has worked with graduate students with great enjoyment and delight practically all of my academic life, I am very much excited with the opportunity to serve as associate dean at the Graduate College and in getting involved in graduate education in a much broader framework," Dr. Ozel said. "I am looking forward to working with ASU faculty, graduate students and staff in carrying the banner for graduate education at ASU, and assisting students in the process of attaining even greater levels of excellence in their programs."

Dr. Ozel, who earned a Ph.D. in architecture at the University of Michigan, is involved in teaching and research in design computing. Her research is interdisciplinary in nature and combines theory and methods regarding human behavior in fires, computer simulation of dynamic processes in buildings and cognitive factors in environmental design.

Dean Bernstein said the Graduate College is in terrific shape with the addition of the new deans to a leadership team that also includes Associate Dean Marjorie Zatz and assistant deans Kent Blaylock and Sarah Lindquist. "We have made great strides and assembled a team of extraordinary people in the Graduate College," she said. "I have every confidence that the Graduate College will continue to flourish under Dr. Allison's leadership and the expertise of the staff."
