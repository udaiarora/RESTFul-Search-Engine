ASU taps renowned scientist Dr. George Poste to head Arizona Biodesign Institute

World-renowned researcher, scholar and science policymaker George Poste has been named director of the Arizona Biodesign Institute, Arizona State University's new and path-breaking biodesign and biotechnology enterprise.

Currently, Poste is chief executive of Health Technology Networks, a consulting group based in Scottsdale, Ariz., and suburban Philadelphia specializing in the application of genomics and computing in healthcare. From 1992 to 1999 he was president for research and development and chief science and technology officer at the multinational healthcare company, SmithKline Beecham, during which time he was instrumental in 29 successful drug and vaccine registrations in the USA and internationally.

ASU President Michael Crow announced Poste's appointment April 29, at a ceremony marking the groundbreaking of the institute's first state-of-the-art research facility under construction on the university's main campus in Tempe, Ariz.

"George Poste is one of the leading scientists in the world, and there is no better person to build the Arizona Biodesign Institute (AzBio) into a world-class biodesign research center that will benefit the state, the nation and the entire world," said Crow. "George is a scientific genius with a compassion for humankind that inspires those around him to do great things to make the world better.

"One of our most immediate goals at ASU is to build research facilities that will attract leading researchers from across the country and around the world," said Crow. "Even as we break ground on the first of four Arizona Biodesign Institute buildings, we are able to announce the first such recruitment of a research leader who has enjoyed great success in academia, industry and government and has proven skills in building complex, multidisciplinary research enterprises on an international scale."

Arizona Biodesign Institute will house a core of ASU's elite, cutting-edge researchers, both those already at the university and those who will be recruited in the future. It draws together research efforts in biotechnology, information technology and nanotechnology to improve human health and quality of life through multidisciplinary research. Emphasis will also be given to forging extensive relationships with industry to ensure the rapid translation of leading edge research into practical benefits.

Poste said, "I'm excited by President Crow's invitation to make his ambitious vision for AzBio a reality. I also applaud the members of the Arizona Legislature for their forceful commitment to capture research and development excellence in Arizona. The convergence of spectacular advances in genomics, materials science, miniaturization engineering, robotics and computing offers unprecedented opportunities to harness this knowledge to produce dramatic gains in the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of diseases and address major unmet medical needs, both nationally and internationally. ASU is in the vanguard in recognizing the need to conduct multi-disciplinary research on a large scale.

"It's my goal that AzBio will pioneer new approaches in both research and teaching," said Poste. "By forging an extensive network of alliances, both on-and off-campus, AzBio will become a dynamic intellectual and economic resource for ASU, the state and the nation."

When completed in 2004, the first building of the Arizona Biodesign Institute will be a state-of-the-art research facility located on ASU's main campus in Tempe. The 170,000-square-foot facility is the centerpiece of ASU's multi-phase Biodesign Corridor that will provide an intellectual and physical environment to allow Arizona to compete in the global bioscience and technology marketplace.

Phases 2, 3, and 4 of AzBio will be built if a legislative bill authorizing an additional $400 million for research facilities at Arizona's three state universities is approved. The bill, which was submitted to the state legislature April 16, will provide ASU with $185 million to complete construction of all phases of the Arizona Biodesign Institute, as well as a facility for the university's new School of Life Sciences and research facilities to house burgeoning biotech fields such as nanotechnology, materials science, information technology and bioinformatics.

ASU Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs, Jonathan Fink, said Poste's diverse background will help bring together the many elements of the institute and provide a rapid progression for its research efforts.

"George is already aligned with our vision for the institute and is ready to move us to the next level," said Fink. "He can really paint a picture that leading scientists at ASU and around the world can get behind."

The appointment of Poste enables Charles Arntzen, the founding director of AzBio, to focus his energies on the growth of plant-based pharmaceutical discovery and development.

Arntzen said when he joined the institute as its founding director, he did so with the understanding a new leader would be recruited once the venture was successfully off the ground. He says that Poste will be arriving at a perfect time, now that the new AzBio complex is being built.

"I'm extremely pleased that ASU has attracted someone of George Poste's stature to join AzBio and to use his vast experience to build programs of both excitement and excellence," said Arntzen.

Poste is well known in the scientific community for his vast knowledge of health research and health policy, and he is an expert in genetics, pharmaceuticals, computing and healthcare research and development in both academia and industry.

Phoenix attorney Richard Mallery, a founding board member of the International Genomics Consortium, which will work closely with AzBio, says Poste's superb track records in both research and management will provide a boost to bioscience in the state of Arizona.

"Poste has one of the most successful track records in the history of drug development," Mallery said. "His special genius is taking medicines from the research lab to the bedside and, in the process, getting them approved by the FDA and putting them into the physician's black bag. When he headed research at SmithKline Beecham, he proved that a top academic researcher could break the mold and become equally successful finding practical applications. Arizona is gaining one of the most highly regarded scientists in the world who also has superb managerial and negotiating skills, a man who can find win-win solutions. He is a giant addition to bioscience in Arizona."

Dr. Jeffrey Trent, founder and chief science officer of the Translational Genomics Research Institute (TGen) said Poste is the perfect choice for AzBio and the state of Arizona.

"There are only a few people in the world who have the background in bioscience and genomic research that George has, and who have taken vaccines and pharmaceuticals into the marketplace," said Trent. "He will help ASU and Arizona take a major leap forward."

Trent said Poste's appointment caps a remarkable year for Arizona in the field of bioscience. He cited the addition of Crow and his research agenda at ASU and the support the state has shown for genomic and bioscience research, which has drawn efforts such as IGC and TGen.

"It's a remarkable capstone on a remarkable year," he said. "George's presence and focus will help develop the field locally and he will attract national attention."

Others in the pharmaceutical and bioscience fields have praised the appointment of Poste as director of AzBio.

"I have had the privilege of knowing and working with many brilliant scientists and pharmaceutical executives, and George Poste is by far the standout among this prestigious group," said Robert R. Ruffolo, president for Research & Development for Wyeth Pharmaceuticals and senior vice president for the Wyeth Corporation. "George combines an unusual blend of genius and vision with a healthy dose of pragmatism. George's strength, which few can match, is his visionary approach to managing scientific, technology-driven organizations."

Jan Leschly of Care Capital LLC, former Chief Executive Officer of SmithKline Beecham, noted, "George Poste was the industrial scientist who introduced 'genomics' to the pharmaceutical and biotechnology industry. As chairman for Research and Development at SmithKline Beecham he revolutionized the approach to discovery of new drugs by implementing 'genomics' technology. As Chief Executive Officer for SmithKline Beecham I worked with Dr. Poste for 10 years. His leadership, vision, creativity and extraordinary intellect were very instrumental in SB's success."

Stephen Benkovic, the Evan Pugh Professor and Eberly Chair in Chemistry at Pennsylvania State University added, "George Poste, is a charismatic leader, a true visionary, and a polymath. He will initiate a creative and ambitious strategy that will set the Arizona Biodesign Institute on a trajectory for international prominence."

Poste received a Doctorate in Veterinary Medicine in 1966 and a Ph.D in Virology in 1969 from the University of Bristol in the UK. He is a Fellow of Great Britain's prestigious Royal Society and a Founding Fellow of the UK Academy of Medical Sciences. He has published more than 350 scientific papers and edited multiple books in cancer research and drug delivery. He has been awarded several honorary doctorates in science and law for his research in cancer and in international biotechnology.

In 1999 he was granted the rank of Commander of the British Empire by Queen Elizabeth for contributions to biotechnology. He is Chairman of diaDexus and Structural GenomiX and serves on the Board of Directors of AdvancePCS and Monsanto. He is a member of the Defense Science Board of the Department of Defense and chairs the Task Force on Bioterrorism. He is a member of the National Academy of Sciences Working Group to control the proliferation of biological weapons. He is a Distinguished Fellow at the Hoover Institution at Stanford University.

Poste is a naturalized US citizen and married to a molecular biologist, Dr. Linda Lopez. They have three children, two of whom are ASU graduates, and three grandchildren. Dr. Poste's personal interests are in photography, automobile racing, military history and exploring the wilderness regions of Arizona.
