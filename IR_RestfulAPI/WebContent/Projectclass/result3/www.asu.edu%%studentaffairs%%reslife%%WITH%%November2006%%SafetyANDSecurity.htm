Residential Life Student Safety and Security Update – Submitted by Scott Schuhert

Needed: A few good students! If you think that there are things could be safer, you may be just the person we are looking for.  We are looking for students who are interested in making sure that we are all living in a safe and secure environment.  That is why YOU should join the SAFETY AND SECURITY COMMITTEE.  Residential Life’s student safety and security committee was created to give students a voice in what should be the standard for safety and security in and around the residence halls.  The committee’s goals are to proactively communicate and implement safety and security measures through programming with all of the residents living on campus. As a member of the committee, you’ll meet with other students and staff to talk about concerns you have and strategize on how to fix them.  If interested, please contact Scott Schuhert at 480-965-2289 or scott.schuhert@asu.edu

Sun Devil Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, and remember to take precautions at all times.
  • Walk or jog with a friend, never alone.
  • Avoid isolated, dark areas.
  • Use campus safety escort services on the Tempe campus at night.
  • Tell a friend where you are going and when you will return.
  • Carry a whistle or noisemaker. Do not be afraid to shout if you need help.
  • Park in an area that will be well lit when you return.
  • When approaching your car or door, get your keys out in advance. Don’t fumble for your keys at the door.
  • Before driving, lock your doors and put on your seat belt.
  • If you witness any suspicious activity, call 911 or ASU Police at 480-965-3456.
