July 21, 2003

Cronkite School makes administrative line-up changes

Walter Cronkite, the namesake of ASU's School of Journalism & Mass Communication, earned his reputation as a respected journalist by covering events around the world. The Cronkite School is embarking on an effort to provide more for ASU students on a global scale.

Joe Foote, director of the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communication, will return to the faculty as the first holder of the Weil Family Professorship and become director of international programs in journalism and mass communication on Aug. 15.

Anne Schneider, dean of the College of Public Programs, also announced that Stephen Doig, the Knight Chair in Journalism, is taking on the role of interim director while a national search is conducted for a permanent director for the school.

"We are very excited about Professor Doig taking the interim director's position," said Schneider. "He brings extensive experience as a working journalist and is one of the country's leading specialists in analytical (computer-assisted) journalism."

Marianne Barrett, associate professor of journalism and telecommunication, will serve as associate director for academic affairs and Fritz Leigh will continue to serve as associate director for student services.

Schneider says Foote is "one of the country's foremost authorities on international journalism" and will provide the school and university with the knowledge and ability to become globally engaged.

"The program that he is developing will provide opportunities for ASU students to observe the practices, principles and ethics of journalism around the world and to learn from working side by side with international journalists who come to ASU for training," Schneider said. "In turn, this program extends the idea of 'ASU as a force, not just a place' to the international scene as we provide training for journalists in emerging democratic countries."

In his new role, Foote will concentrate on coordinating international activities for the school; coordinating visiting scholars, professional, visitors and international student exchanges; advising faculty and students on international scholarships, fellowships, grants and exchange opportunities; identifying funding opportunities for international programs; and working with ASU's International Programs Office.

The Weil Family Professorship was established by Louis (Chip) Weil, in honor of his wife, Daryl. Weil, formerly an executive with the Arizona Republic, believes in supporting outstanding faculty who impact the country's future journalists. Weil has been a member of the Cronkite Board of Trustees and a member of the Dean's Council of 100 in the W. P. Carey School of Business.
