July 14, 2003

Gastevich to head ASU Alumni Association; brings entrepreneurial experience

Donna Gastevich (pictured on left) is joining ASU as associate vice president for Public Affairs/executive director for Alumni Relations. She is an accomplished entrepreneur, an experienced alumni affairs officer and a highly regarded fund-raiser.

Since 2000, Gastevich has been president of TechPoint, a statewide technology association in Indiana, and executive director of the TechPoint Foundation. She is credited with re-energizing TechPoint, growing its budget from $225,000 to nearly $2 million in three years.

Gastevich was selected from a national pool of 148 candidates. A search committee, which included five past-chairmen of the ASU Alumni Association, conducted initial interviews and forwarded a short list of qualified candidates to the ASU administration.

ASU President Michael Crow said feedback from almost everyone who met Gastevich was extraordinarily positive.

"She is intelligent, creative and energetic - the ideal person to lead the ASU Alumni Association to the next level of achievement, to make it the premier alumni association in the Pac-10 and a model for the rest of the nation," Crow said.

"We had two extremely qualified finalists, and Donna's enthusiasm, energy and self-assurance put her in the driver's seat," said Virgil Renzulli, ASU's vice president for Public Affairs. "Whenever I met with the candidates, she would end each meeting by making it clear just how much she wanted this job, and she convincingly expressed confidence that she would do a great job."

"ASU is a great school, with much interest and commitment here," Gastevich said. "President Crow is awesome. He has so much energy. The alumni association should have that same energy.

"We need to explore additional opportunities for corporate sponsorships, which is a strength I think I bring; we need to leverage our existing corporate relationships; we need to increase our marketing and outreach efforts; we need to improve our coordination with Intercollegiate Athletics and we need to improve the effectiveness of our organization.

"There are great traditions at ASU, and those should be strengthened. We need to increase the number of alumni who are actively involved with ASU, and work collaboratively with both internal and external stakeholders to accomplish our goals," she said.

Martin Shultz, vice president, government affairs, Pinnacle West Capital Corp, said he is pleased to welcome Gastevich on behalf of all ASU alumni. Shultz is also the ASU Alumni Association chairman for 2000 - 03 and was the co-chairman of this executive search committee.

"We look forward to working with Donna to advance, grow and promote the work of the ASU Alumni Association, and provide great service to our one quarter of a million alums," he said. "Now, more than ever, ASU alumni have the opportunity to help promote ASU as the New American University, and Donna is uniquely qualified to provide the professional leadership for this important work."

Gastevich earned a bachelor of arts degree from Purdue University and a M.P.A. from Indiana University-South Bend.

Prior to joining TechPoint, she was director of Corporate and Alumni Relations for the Schools of Engineering and Science at Purdue. She also held the position of state specialist for Community and Economic Development with the Purdue Cooperative Extension Service. Prior to that, she was director of the seven-county Small Business Development Center in Northwest Indiana.
