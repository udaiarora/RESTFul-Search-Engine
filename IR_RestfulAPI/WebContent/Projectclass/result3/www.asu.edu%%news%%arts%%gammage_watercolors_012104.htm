January 21, 2004

Gammage Gallery walls overflow with vibrant watercolors

The lobby gallery of ASU's Gammage Auditorium will be awash with watercolors through Feb. 15.

Exhibiting their work will be Frederick Charlton and Jim Ellis, watercolor, and Suzanne Tyson, watercolor, acrylic and mixed-media works.

Charlton, of Scottsdale, majored in painting and graphic design at the University of Michigan. He worked as an advertising agency art director in Chicago, painting on weekends.

After retiring, Charlton traveled in the United States and abroad, writing travel articles and illustrating them with pen and ink sketches. He is a member of the Mesa Art League and regularly participates in art exhibits in the East Valley.

Ellis, of Tempe, received his master of fine arts degree in printmaking from ASU. He has taught at the Mesa Art Center for 10 years, and exhibited his work throughout Arizona.

"For the past few years, I have experienced the joy and wonder of watercolor discovery within a framework of conventional realism," Ellis said. "While I am very much a realist, I frequently find myself looking for inspiration beyond the confines of the literal world."

In a search for "deeper emotional involvement" in his work, Ellis said he finds himself "revisiting some of the printmaking ideas and imagery I explored while working on my master's degree. As a result, many of my recent watercolors combine realistic images with surrealistic undertones."

Tyson, of Mesa, will show two-dimensional watercolors, acrylics and mixed-media pieces on paper and canvas. Her subject matter includes animals, people and places, sometimes with a playful point of view. "I try to allow viewers to see my subjects as they have never seen them before," she said.

"Nature is a strong element in most of my work. Some pieces in the exhibit take on a mystic or make-believe theme."

A former commercial artist, Tyson turned to her passion, fine art, after raising her family. She has shown her work extensively in the Valley, with four solo shows in the last 18 months.

Gammage Auditorium exhibit hours are 1 - 4 p.m., Monday - Friday. Due to rehearsals, performances, special events and holidays, it is advisable to call (480) 965-4050 before visiting, since hours are subject to cancellation. Entrance is through the east lobby doors. For information, call Lesley Davis, (480) 965-4050.

Smith, with Public relations, can be reached at (480) 965-4821 or (jps@asu.edu).
