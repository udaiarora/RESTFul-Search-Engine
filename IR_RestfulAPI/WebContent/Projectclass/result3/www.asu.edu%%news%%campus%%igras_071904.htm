July 19, 2004

Igras to direct ASU campus health, safety

ASU has appointed Leon Igras as director of Environmental Health and Safety as of June 28. Igras comes to ASU after serving for the last nine years as director of Environmental Safety at the University of Maryland - College Park.

"ASU's research activities have a great national reputation," says Igras. "It's a very exciting place to be."

Environmental Health and Safety covers many of the university's occupational and environmental health and safety programs formerly covered by the Risk Management Department. Among the priorities for the department are hazardous waste, Occupational Health and Safety regulation requirements, bio-safety, campus radiation issues, loss prevention and fire safety. The unit also deals with various permitting requirements and work safety education efforts for the university.

"Universities have one of the broadest ranges of environmental health and safety challenges," he says. "The current climate of federal regulations and inspections and dealing with the U.S. Patriot Act really make for a challenging environment."

At Maryland, Igras' department was in charge of health and safety for more than 1,500 research laboratories and a nuclear reactor. The department received high honors in 2003 with the Governor's Safety Award for the top safety program for any state agency. The department was also involved in a campus effort that resulted in the 2003 National Wildlife Federation Green Campus Achievement Award for campus sustainability efforts.

Prior to serving at Maryland, Igras was assistant director of Environmental Health and Safety at Harvard University, Longwood Campus. The department worked directly with the campus' medical school facilities and primate research center.
