FORMS

If you leave ASU, your computer account services will automatically shut off unless you have another active affiliation with ASU that allows those services to remain active.

Here are some guidelines to protect your account:

LEAVING ASU
If you leave ASU, but are not retiring, your ASURITE account services will be suspended the day after your employment record indicates termination of employment.

IMPORTANT NOTE: Save or forward any important files, e-mails, or e-mail addresses to another account before your ASURITE account is deactivated.

RETIRING
If you retire from ASU, you can continue to have computer account services indefinitely if you:

  1. Have your Sun Card updated to reflect your retired or emeritus status
  2. Bring your updated card to the Computer Accounts Office

Retirees can keep their Outlook accounts and PPP dialup service as well as other basic services (such as AFS Filespace and access to the General Unix Server).

Employees who are leaving ASU, or retiring, should check to see if they have other departmental accounts like listservs or generic UserIDs that their student workers use to login to SIS. Those UserIDs will need to be transferred to someone else when the employee leaves. Employees may contact the ASU IT Customer Assistance Center to verify the accounts that have been assigned to them.

CHANGING DEPARTMENTS OR JOBS
If you move from one department to another or change jobs within the same department, you must re-apply for mainframe access (SIS, DARS, HRMS, Advantage).
Submit a new Request for Computer Access (.pdf).
