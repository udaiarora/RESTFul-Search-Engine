Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

September 4, 2002

Members Present: Patricia Feldman,  Carol Behl, Sue Hunter, Victoria Trotta
Members Absent: Stefan Myhajlenko, Beth Luey

Last year?s chair Patricia Feldman convened the first meeting of the year. She announced that Beth Luey is unable to attend any of the scheduled meetings due to sabbatical and teaching responsibilities. Therefore, Feldman will ask the first CAPS alternate, Barbara Trapido-Lurie to serve.

Welcome and Committee Overview    

Feldman outlined the purpose and work of the committee for the benefit of new members. A full description of the CAPS charge, and other documents pertinent to the committee can be found at: http://www.asu.edu/provost/CAPS. The Committee discussed the charge and agreed that CAPS fills important functions by communicating with the 500+ academic professionals serving ASU and by representing their interests when asked to do so by various other University committees. 

 Election of Officers

Chair ? Patricia Feldman
Vice Chair ? Carol Behl
Secretary ? Victoria Trotta
Webmaster ? Sue Hunter

Old Business

There was discussion of the progress of Academic Associates obtaining representation in the Academic Senate. It was noted that the Lecturers recently achieved such representation. Patricia Feldman was to check with Dr. Hackett on the status of this issue.

New Business

?Spotlight Series? - CAPS presents at least one public forum each year for APs that addresses a topic of concern or interest to the group.  CAPS will try to hold one public forum in mid-November that will inform APs on the topic of self-insurance.  Patricia Feldman serves on the University Health and Insurance Committee and will find a suitable speaker. Other potential speakers for future forums were identified: Dr. Crow, Dr. Hackett, Dr. Ruth Jones.  

Listserv for AP?s ? There was discussion on the availability, currency and uses of an ASU listserv that includes all APs. Carol Behl will investigate and report back to the Committee. The Committee would like to be more proactive in communicating with AP?s on a more regular basis.  Carol volunteered to draft an inaugural email message and   circulate it for comment.

Use of the e-mail to conduct CAPS business ? It was decided that some of the housekeeping tasks of the Committee, e.g. adopting of minutes, could be done via e-mail. This should result in more timely information getting to the CAPS members both directly and on the website.

It was noted that the committee website needs to be updated. The Committee members will all contribute to this effort.

The meeting was adjourned at 11:00 a.m.

Respectfully submitted, Victoria Trotta, Secretary

Minutes were approved 10/2/2002     Revised 10/15/2002
