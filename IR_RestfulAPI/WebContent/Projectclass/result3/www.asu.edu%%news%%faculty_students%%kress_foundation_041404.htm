April 14, 2004

Curd earns Kress Foundation travel fellowship

ASU doctoral student Bryan Curd has been honored with a 2004 - 05 Samuel H. Kress Foundation Travel Fellowship in the History of Art.

The Kress Foundation is dedicated to the advancement of history, conservation and enjoyment of European archaeology, architecture and art. Each year the foundation awards travel stipends to outstanding pre-doctoral candidates working toward completion of their dissertation research of European art. Awards typically range from $3,500 to $10,000.

Curd's current concentration is on late medieval and early Renaissance art in England. She is using her award to travel to London in the fall of 2004. While there she plans to research British archives for Netherlandish artists working in England between 1480 and 1690.

Curd says she is absolutely thrilled about the upcoming opportunity.

"I hope to find evidence of collaborative and competitive practices between English and Netherlandish artists from which both groups benefited," Curd says.

Curd received an M.A. in English from ASU in 1970. Through the years, she took a variety of graduate courses. Since 2001, she has been working as a Ph.D. student. In 2002, she began working at the ASU Art Museum as a graduate research assistant, and last fall she taught ARS100.

Diane Wolfthal, an ASU associate professor of art history, describes Curd as a fantastic student.

"She is a real pleasure, one of those students who is just bristling with ideas," Wolfthal says.

Wolfthal first met Curd two years ago when she was a student in her Italian High Renaissance and Mannerism course.

"At that time, I was struck by her intelligence and commitment to learning," Wolfthal says. "I ranked her at the very top of a class of 50 students."

Wolfthal says Curd's diligent work ethic is truly amazing.

"Last term she took my class on Northern Renaissance art, taught her own introductory course at ASU, and also presented two conference papers. She also works part-time for the ASU Art Museum."

In addition to her latest honor, Curd has also earned several awards including an ASU Enrichment Grant, a Herbert Smith Endowed Fellowship, and a Regents fellowship. In the future, Curd plans to work as a museum curator and teach art history at the university level.
