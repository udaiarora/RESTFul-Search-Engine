Community Service Living and Learning Community

The Community Service Living and Learning Community is an academic/residential program that provides an opportunity for service-minded individuals to learn more about social issues, their community, and themselves. This unique program creates a community that can foster your personal development both intellectually and socially, while providing facilities to meet your academic and living needs.

The Community is limited to 40 freshmen, enabling you to develop a supportive network of peers on campus. You will be "living and learning" with students from all different majors and walks of life to collaborate on service projects, study, and socialize with. A group of staff and peer mentors will be dedicated to helping you be successful in your freshman year by providing guidance as you develop your interests and skills.

Benefits

Through your involvement in this community, you will:
  • Expand your perspectives by working with and learning from people of different races, cultures, ages and life experiences
  • Broaden your understanding of community issues
  • Apply your learning to help solve or address a social issues that impact you, your family or your community
  • Gain marketable skills that employers are seeking, such as problem solving, conflict resolution, and cross-cultural communication
  • Explore potential career fields and gain knowledge and expertise in areas of interest
  • Gain key leadership skills that will enhance and complement your educational experiences
  • Meet people with similar interests
  • Have fun while making a difference in the community

Location

The Community Service Living and Learning Community will be located in Palo Verde Main.

Requirements

  • Enter with and maintain a minimum GPA of 2.75
  • Participate in the wide variety of activities and events offered especially for the community
  • Register for required Community Service courses in the fall and spring semesters (courses are one credit per semester)

How to Apply

  1. Complete the Arizona State University's Housing/Residential Life Application. Indicate on your Residential Life Application a preference for the "Community Service Living and Learning Community" as one of your housing choices
    Important Note: Residential Life can only accept applications from students who have been officially notified of their admittance to Arizona State University. Any applications received from students who are not yet admitted will be returned to the student without being processed.
  2. Make appropriate deposits or payments to Residential Life to reserve your space by the applicable payment schedule deadlines. Visit the Residential Life Room & Board Rates page for those dates

Community Service Living and Learning Committee Application

Submit an essay (up to 200 words) in which you discuss what service means to you and why you want to be a part of this community. A list of previous service experience may also be included as part of the essay.
