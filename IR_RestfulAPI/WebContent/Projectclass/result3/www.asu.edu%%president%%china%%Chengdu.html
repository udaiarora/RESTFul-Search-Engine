Chengdu

The city of Chengdu was the site for the June 2005 Forum on University Design (visit the Institute for University Design page), co-hosted by ASU and Sichuan University. Download the Forum on University Design report.

The sister-city relationship between the cities of Tempe and Chengdu, strengthened by the collaborations between ASU and Sichuan University, is quickly showing itself to be more than just mutually beneficial.  It is proving itself outright innovative.

 

In addition to hosting the Institute for University Design, ASU will also play host to a number of students from Sichuan University.  Supported by a research base composed primarily of students and linked by the spirit of innovation and exchange, the two universities hope to engender growth and increase research through a comprehensive exchange program.  This sister-institution relationship will undoubtedly prove beneficial for all parties involved.

The Chengdu Plain, known by some as "the country in Heaven," has also benefited from the work of ASU faculty.  Dr. Lan Zhiyong, a faculty member with ASU’s School of Public Affairs, has served as an advisor for the Chengdu Science and Technology Commission since 2004.  Dr. Lan, a former Fulbright lecturer to China, has quickly become a respected researcher in the areas of public administration theory, governance innovation, research methods, government and business relationships, and information technology and resources management/policy.
