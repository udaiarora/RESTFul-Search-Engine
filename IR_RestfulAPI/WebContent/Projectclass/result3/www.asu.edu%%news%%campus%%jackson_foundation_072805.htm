July 28, 2005

Jackson leads ASU Foundation to new heights

ASU and the ASU Foundation together raised nearly $99 million in cash and property donations for the university during the just-concluded 2004 – 2005 fiscal year. That set a record for charitable gifts at ASU and amounted to an almost 30 percent increase in donations over the previous year.

“We’re very pleased with what was accomplished this past year,” says Ira Jackson, president of the foundation. “It’s a result of the generosity of thousands of individuals and groups committed to seeing the university and its students succeed. It also represents a lot of hard work by the ASU Foundation team, and by deans, senior development officers and others throughout the university. And it reflects the energetic and engaged actions and activities of the foundation’s strong board of directors.

“Still, it was only a start. If we are to achieve President Michael Crow’s vision of a New American University – providing more scholarshipsfor needy students, providing seed capital to launch new programs, funding new faculty hiring and making a significant impact in the community – then we’ll need to continue to obtain increasing levels of financial investment.”

While state government and student tuition generate substantial financial support, the university also must meet a large percentage of its financial needs by raising funds from grants, research and charitable contributions.

“It’s a critical part of the university’s budget and overall financing strategy,” Jackson says. “Without those funds, we can’t create and maintain a world-class university with all the benefits that provides.”

The ASU Foundation enjoyed success in several fundraising areas, according to Jackson. Participation by alumni and non-alumni alike increased significantly in nearly all of foundation’s annual giving programs. Contributions to the President’s Club and Women and Philanthropy Program grew by 17 percent and 25 percent, respectively.

The foundation received several large charitable gifts this year. For example, the International Institute for Sustainability was launched with a $15 million gift from Julie Wrigley, president and chief executive officer of Wrigley Investments LLC.

It is the largest gift made to any university to support sustainability studies.

The university also received a gift of $5.4 million from Orin Edson, founder of Bayliner Boat Co., to fund the Edson Student Entrepreneurial Initiative, and a $3 million gift from businessman and entrepreneur Ira A. Fulton, ASU’s largest financial donor, to fund the Decision Theater.

Other large gifts in the form of challenge grants from Fulton led to successful new fundraising initiatives in seven colleges and Intercollegiate Athletics. The charitable gift value of the Brickyard property purchase was $22 million, which contributed substantially to the year’s record total.

Arizona Technology Enterprises (AzTE), the foundation’s technology transfer organization, also had a very successful year. AzTE closed 28 new commercial deals and created five new spin-out companies to help commercialize the intellectual property of ASU faculty.

“It’s important that everyone in the ASU community support the foundation’s fundraising
efforts as we move forward,” Jackson says. “As individuals, we can all participate. Leaders of colleges and university programs can also help build ASU’s future by assisting the foundation’s efforts to build relationships with current and potential donors.”

Reiher, with the ASU Foundation, can be reached at (480) 727-7582 or (stephen.reiher@asu.edu).
