January 6, 2005

Graduate Studies establishes 3 new fellowships

ASU’s Division of Graduate Studies (DGS) has established three new fellowships to recruit and retain top graduate students and increase diversity.

The competitive, universitywide fellowships are among several DGS is developing. The division recognizes that fellowships are a decisive factor for students selecting a graduate program. Fellowships are the most prestigious form of funding and highly prized, as they enable students to focus exclusively on their studies and research.

“A university fellowship program is essential in shaping competitive funding packages which are critical to the recruitment and retention of a high caliber and diverse graduate student population,” says Maria T. Allison, vice provost and dean of graduate studies. “This, in turn, is vital to ASU’s continued efforts to become a world–class comprehensive research university, which is key in developing a viable workforce for the new global knowledge economy and a diverse leadership to benefit the state of Arizona .”

The new DGS Dissertation Fellowship will target highly meritorious doctoral students in the final stages of post-candidacy doctoral work. The DGS Doctoral Enrichment Fellowship will aid in the recruitment of the highest quality doctoral students who will contribute to the diversity of ASU’s graduate student population. The Reach for the Stars Fellowship will further promote diversity by connecting ASU’s most promising juniors and seniors who are underrepresented in their disciplines to master’s and, ultimately, to doctoral degree programs.

The Reach for the Stars Fellowship program will provide undergraduates who demonstrate strong academic merit and the potential to succeed in graduate school with a pathway to graduate school and the preparation, mentoring, and funding for their success. The program is a joint effort between DGS, the Office of the Vice President for University Undergraduate Initiatives and the Barrett Honors College .

For more information about the new fellowships, contact the Division of Graduate Studies at (480) 965-7279 or visit (www.asu.edu/graduate/financial/DGSopportunities.htm).

Coronado, with the Division of Graduate Studies, can be reached at (480) 965-5995 or (nora.coronado@asu.edu).
