Japanese Studies

INTRODUCTION

This INFOsource is made possible by a U.S. Department of Education grant for the enhancement of East Asian undergraduate studies through the Center for Asian Studies at Arizona State University. It lists English resources in the area of Japanese studies available in the Arizona State University Libraries. It is not comprehensive bibliography, but rather a selective list of sources useful for researching a topic in this field. The guide covers encyclopedias, bibliographies, dictionaries, biographies & directories, statistics, atlases, guides, and indexes.


ENCYCLOPEDIAS

  1. Embree, Ainslie T., ed. Encyclopedia of Asian History. 4 vols. New York: Scribner, 1988.
    DS 31 .E53 1988 HAYDEN REF

    Prepared under the auspices of the Asia Society, this set includes articles on Japanese history.

  2. Kodansha Encyclopedia of Japan. 10 vols. Tokyo: Kodansha, 1983.
    DS 805 .K633 1983 HAYDEN REF

    This publication has detailed, scholarly coverage of all aspects of Japanese history, society and culture. It is well illustrated and features bibliographical notes. Vol. 9 is the subject index.

  3. International Encyclopedia of the Social Sciences. 18 vols. New York: Macmillan, 1968-1979.
    H 40 .A2 I5 HAYDEN REF

    Includes economic, organizational, political, rural, and urban sociology; the sociologies of knowledge, law, religion, and medicine; human ecology; history of social thought; sociometry and other small group research; and survey research.


 

BIBLIOGRAPHIES

  1. Bibliographic Guide to East Asian Studies. Boston: G.K. Hall, 1990- .
    DS 504.5 .B52 HAYDEN REF

    This annual comprehensive subject bibliographies cover publications cataloged by the Research Libraries of New York Public Library and the Library of Congress for thorough subject coverage. Included are works in all languages and all forms.

  2. Kokusai Koryu Kikin, ed. Catalogue of Books on Japan Translated from the Japanese into English, 1945-1981 = Eiyaku Nihon Kankei Hobun Tosho Mokuroku, 1945-1981. Tokyo: The Japan Foundation, 1988.
    DS 806 .C35x 1980 HAYDEN STACKS

    Selected from the Catalogue of books in English on Japan, 1945-1981. Organized by subject; indexed by authors and titles; author's names are also given in Japanese. It might not be as comprehensive as the original, but is still useful.

  3. Catalogue of Books in English on Japan, 1945-1981. Tokyo: The Japan Foundation, 1986.
    DS 803 .K6x 1986 HAYDEN STACKS

    The materials were selected from the collections of the National Diet Library of Japan, the Japan Foundation Library, and from materials listed in the Bibliography of Asian Studies. About 9000 books arranged by subject with author and title indexes.

  4. Shulman, Frank Joseph, ed. Doctoral Dissertations on Japan and on Korea, 1969-1979: An Annotated Bibliography of Studies in Western Languages. Seattle: University of Washington Press, 1982.
    DS 806 .S54x Suppl 2 HAYDEN STACKS

    This publication covers dissertations originally presented in the United States, 18 European nations, Australia, Canada, Hong Kong, India, Israel, Japan, the Philippines, Republic of China, and Republic of S. Africa. Japan is covered in pages 3-240.

  5. Shulman, Frank Joseph, ed. Japan and Korea: An Annotated Bibliography of Doctoral Dissertations in Western Languages, 1877-1969. Chicago: American Library Association, 1970.
    DS 806 .S54x HAYDEN STACKS

    Supplemented by the Doctoral Dissertations on Japan and Korea, 1969-1979, this is to expand and to bring up to date the Unpublished Doctoral Dissertations Relating to Japan, Accepted in the Universities of Australia, Canada, Great Britain, and United States, 1946-1963, compiled by Peter Cornwall in 1965. This is a comprehensive work of 2077 entries for Japan in English, French, German, Russian, and ten additional European languages. Japan is covered in pages 3-277. Within each subsection the arrangement is chronological by date of completion rather than alphabetical by author. Includes index by degree-awarding institutions and cross-references.

  6. Doctoral Dissertations on Asia. Ann Arbor, MI: Association for Asian Studies, 1975- . Semiannual.
    DS 1 .D6x HAYDEN SERIAL

    This publication is an annotated bibliographical journal of current international research, including completed and in-progress dissertations, mostly from North America.

  7. Nunn, G. Raymond (Godfrey Raymond). Asia, Reference Works: A Select Annotated Guide. London: Mansell, 1980.
    DS 35 .N79x 1980 HAYDEN REF

    Section R covers Japan. Most works are in Japanese. Indexed by author and title.

  8. Nunn, G. Raymond (Godfrey Raymond). Japanese Periodicals and Newspapers in Western Languages: An International Union List. London: Mansell, 1979.
    PN 5407 .P4 N86x HAYDEN STACKS

    Approximately 3,500 titles, with holdings for about 700 U.S. and foreign libraries.

  9. Introductory Bibliography for Japanese Studies. 4 vols. Tokyo: University of Tokyo Press, 1974- .
    DS 806 .I5x HAYDEN STACKS

    Indexed at end of each volume by author and subject. Text in English; lists of bibliographies in English and Japanese.

  10. Nihon no Sanko Tosho Henshu Iinkai, comp. Guide to Japanese Reference Books = Nihon no Sanko Tosho. Chicago: American Library Association, 1966.
    Z 3306 .N5 1966 HAYDEN REF

    Includes Sino-Japanese characters, their romanization, and English translation of each title. Based on the revised Japanese edition edited by the Compilation Committee in the Library of the International House of Japan. Comprehensive in subject coverage, annotated, provides cross references, and indexed.

  11. Borton, Hugh, comp. Selected List of Books and Articles on Japan in English, French, and German. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1954.
    Z 3306 .B67 1954 HAYDEN STACKS

    Nearly 1800 annotated entries, and indexed by author, title and subject.

  12. Bibliography of Asian Studies. [Ann Arbor, MI]: Association for Asian Studies, 1971- .
    DS 501 .F2741x HAYDEN REF
    This annual publication is a continuation of the Bulletin of Far Eastern Bibliography (Z 3001 .B8 Hayden Serial) and the Far Eastern Bibliography (DS 501 .F274 Hayden Serial).

    The bibliography is arranged primarily by geographic area of Asia and second by a classified list of subject headings. It includes periodical articles and monographs, published in the United States and elsewhere. It is comprehensive in subject coverage, and indexed by personal names of authors, editors, and translators.

    For the author index, use the Cumulative Bibliography of Asian Studies, 1941-1965. Author Bibliography (Z 3001 .C93 Hayden Stacks) and the Cumulative Bibliography of Asian Studies, 1966-1970: Author Bibliography (Z 3001 .C95 Hayden Stacks); for the subject index, use the Cumulative Bibliography of Asian Studies: Subject Bibliography (DS .C8x Hayden Serial).


DICTIONARIES

  1. Koine, Yoshio, ed. Kenkyusha's New English-Japanese Dictionary. 5th ed. Tokyo: Kenkyusha, 1982.
    PL 679 .K388 1982 HAYDEN EAST ASIA

    A standard, comprehensive English-Japanese dictionary. The University Libraries system has older editions. Although the older editions are still useful, it is recommendable to consult with the latest edition.

  2. Nelson, Andrew Nathaniel. Modern Reader's Japanese-English Character Dictionary = Saishin Kan-Ei Jiten. 2nd rev. ed. Rutland, VT.: C.E. Tuttle, [1974].
    PL 679 .N4 1974 HAYDEN STACKS

    Arrangement with radicals divided further by the numbers of strokes. Includes "on-kun" index arranged alphabetically.

  3. Pheby, John, and Miyamoto, Akito, eds. Oxford-Duden Pictorial English-Japanese Dictionary. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1983.
    PL 679 .P29 1983 HAYDEN EAST ASIA

    This dictionary is based on the Oxford-Duden Pictorial German-English Dictionary, 1980. Black/white pictures are detailed and printing is clear. Well indexed for every object in illustrations. Because information can be conveyed more readily and clearly by pictures than definitions and explanations alone, this pictorial dictionary is useful for the students of English and Japanese languages.

  4. Fukutake Oxford Illustrated English-Japanese Dictionary = Okkusufodo Kara Ei-Wa Daijiten. 8 vols. Tokyo: Fukutake Shoten, 1982.
    PL 679 .F75x HAYDEN EAST ASIA

    The basis of this dictionary is the New Oxford Illustrated Dictionary published in 1978. It has 4000 illustrations and 70,000 words. It could be used as an encyclopedia. User's guide is given in Japanese.

  5. Papinot, Edmond. Historical and Geographical Dictionary of Japan. 2 vols. New York: F. Ungar Pub., 1964.
    DS 833 .P3 1964 HAYDEN REF

    Comprehensive, with brief entries concerning people, places, and events.

  6. Yamaguchi, Momoo and Kojima, Setsuko, eds. Cultural Dictionary of Japan = Wa-Ei Nihon Bunka Jiten. Tokyo: Japan Times, 1979.
    DS 821 .C8x HAYDEN EAST ASIA

    Transliterated terms are arranged under topical headings, with brief definitions and Japanese rendering. There is an alphabetical index to all terms included.


 

BIOGRAPHIES AND DIRECTORIES

  1. Who's Who in Japanese Government. Tokyo: I.C.A. of Japan, 1986- . Annual. (Latest edition in Reference; earlier years in Serials.)
    JQ 1651 .W48 HAYDEN SERIAL

    1990-1991 edition is the latest edition in the library. This handbook is designed to provide basic and up-to-date information on Japanese politics and government. It contains three branches of the government and their functions, brief biographical data on members of both house of the Diet, listings of all govern ministries, agencies, and their personnel, all the Diet Committees and their composition, as well as the major political parties in Japan.

  2. Japanese Colleges and Universities. Tokyo: Maruzen, 1985- . (Latest edition in Reference; earlier years in Serials.)
    L 961 .J3 J28 HAYDEN SERIAL

    1991 edition is the latest edition in the library. This directory provides information on the national, local public, and private universities, dividing Japan into nine regions. It lists numbers of foreign students in each university by major and by department. It has subject index.

  3. Roberts, Laurence P. Roberts' Guide to Japanese Museums. Tokyo: Kodansha International, 1978.
    N 3735 .R62 1978 HAYDEN REF

    This guide is a revision of the Connoisseur's Guide to Japanese Museums. The listing is limited to those museums of art and archaeology within reasonable access to the normal traveller in Japan. Glossary and indexes are given by Japanese names, and branch museums and other collections by prefectures and by types of collections.

  4. Lee, Thomas H., comp. Guide to East Asian Collection in North America, U.S. and Canada. New York: Greenwood, 1992.
    DS 504.5 .L44x HAYDEN REF

    This guide focuses on East Asian collections in general research and public libraries, and a few special libraries. Arranged by alphabetical order of the parental institution. A list of the collections by geographical areas gives readers an idea of distribution of the East Asian resources in the whole region. It provides area of strength of the collection.

  5. Japan Company Handbook. First Section. Tokyo: Toyo Keizai Shinposha, 1987- . Semiannual. (Latest edition in Reference; earlier years in Serials.)
    HC 461 .J35 HAYDEN SERIAL

    This publication is a continuation of Japan Company Handbook. First Section Firms, Japan Company Handbook and Japan Company Directory. The first section provides the financial information on all Japanese corporations listed on the First Sections of the Tokyo, Osaka and Nagoya stock exchanges. Text is arranged by the securities code numbers, and indexed by the names in alphabetical order. The names are given in English and in Japanese.

  6. Japan Company Handbook. Second Section. Tokyo: Toyo Keizai Shinposha, 1987- . (Latest edition in Reference; earlier years in Serials.)
    HC 461 .S45x HAYDEN SERIAL

    This edition is a continuation of Japan Company Handbook. Second Section Firms, Japan Company Handbook, and Japan Company Directory. The second section provides the financial information on all Japanese corporations listed on the Second Sections of the Tokyo, Osaka and Nagoya stock exchanges. Arranged in the same way as the First Section (see entry above). It also includes information on local market, OTC & unlisted companies.

  7. Japanese Overseas Investment = Kaigai Shinshutsu Kigyo Soran. Tokyo: Oriental Economist, 1981- .
    HG 4245 .Z5 J36 HAYDEN REF

    The information are based on survey on Japanese corporations, both listed and unlisted on the nation's stock exchanges.

  8. Complete Directory of Japan. Hong Kong: International Culture Institute, 1986- . Semiannual.
    HC 462.9 .C632 HAYDEN REF

    Covers national and local Japanese governments, domestic commerce and industry, foreign diplomatic corporations, foreign organizations, and foreign enterprises; colleges and universities; trade and non-profit organizations; and Japanese business abroad.

  9. Overseas Japanese Studies Institutions = Kaigai Nihon Kenkyu Kikan. Fukuoka, Japan: Fukuoka UNESCO Association, 1984.
    DS 834.95 .O93 1984 HAYDEN REF

    1980 and 1984 editions supplement one another. Text is in English, and information about institutions located in Korea are given in Japanese. Arranged in alphabetical order by the countries. The 1980 edition is indexed by the names of Japanese studies specialists.

  10. Japanese Studies in the United States. Part II. Directory of Japan Specialists and Japanese Studies Institutions in the United States and Canada. 2 vols. Tokyo: Japan Foundation, 1989.
    DS 834 .J37 1989 HAYDEN REF

    ASU Libraries does not own the Part I. Japanese Studies in the United States, History and Present Condition. Part II is a comprehensive directory. Vol. 1 covers 1420 specialists and 441 current doctoral candidates. Vol. 2 has institutional information and index.

  11. Iwao, Seiichi, ed. Biographical Dictionary of Japanese History. Tokyo: Kodansha International, 1978.
    DS 834 .B56x HAYDEN REF

    Sketches several hundred important figures in Japanese culture and history, arranged by major time periods, and has an extensive bibliography. The index includes all personal names mentioned in the sketches.

  12. Japan Biographical Encyclopedia & Who's Who. 2nd ed. Tokyo: Japan Biographical Research Dept., Rengo, 1961.
    DS 834 .J7 1961 HAYDEN TSC

    Emphasizing historical coverage, this includes approximately 14,500 individuals. Arranged alphabetically. Also included are: modern prefectual map, pre-modern provincial map, pre-modern district map, modern district map, and map of principal Japanese cities. Descriptions on these maps are in English; names of individuals are in English and in Japanese. Appendices include: chronology with brief historical description since 660 B.C. to 1960 A.D.; glossary (table of emperors & era, and table of shoguns); foreign service (Japanese diplomatic representatives abroad and table of post-war diplomatic relations); and cabinet of Japan.

STATISTICS

  1. Japan Statistical Yearbook = Nihon Tokei Nenkan. Tokyo: Sorifu, 1949- . (ASU owns 1949-1985. Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Hayden East Asia.)
    HA 1831 .J3x HAYDEN EAST ASIA

    Text is in English and Japanese. Supersedes Dai-Nihon Teikoku Tokei Nenkan, which was issued in Japanese only and ceased publication with Vol. 59, 1941. It has detailed statistical tables, with current and retrospective information, covering virtually all aspects of Japan: climate, business, education, health, science and technology, etc. The index is in Japanese, but the "list of Tables" in English provides a good access guide. As of June 1993, the 1992 edition (the most current issue) is expected and the back numbers (1986-1991 editions) have been ordered.

  2. Statistical Handbook of Japan. Tokyo: Statistics Bureau, Management and Coordination Agency, 1971- .(ASU owns 1987.)
    HA 4621 .S73x HAYDEN REF

    A brief description of present-day Japan through statistics. For further statistical information on Japan, refer to the Japan Statistical Yearbook (Above).

  3. Yano, Tsuneta and Shirasaki, Kyoichi. Nippon, a Charted Survey of Japan. Tokyo: Kokusei-sha, 1936- . (ASU owns 1936 and 1967.)
    DS 801 .N43 HAYDEN SERIAL

    ASU's 1936 and 1967 editions are shelved on the Lower Level of Hayden in the Compact Shelves.

  4. Mitchell, B.R. (Brian R.). International Historical Statistics: Africa & Asia. New York: New York University Press, 1982.
    HA 4675 .M55 HAYDEN REF

    This companion volume to the European Historical Statistics, 1750-1975 provides useful historical data and it permits easy comparison with other Asian countries although it is not as detailed or comprehensive as the Japan Statistical Yearbook.

ATLASES & GAZETTEERS

  1. Kokudo Chiriin. National Atlas of Japan = Nihon Kokusei Chizu. Tokyo: Japan Map Center, 1977.
    G 2355 .K57 1990 SCI MAP (English edition)
    G 2355 .K57x 1990 Folio x HAYDEN EAST ASIAN (Japanese edition)

    This official, detailed atlas features maps and statistical information dealing with aspects of geography, climate, land use, population, agriculture, fishing, industry, transportation, commerce, politics, social conditions, and culture. The scale varies.

  2. Gerr, Stanley, comp. Gazetteer of Japanese Place Names in the Characters and in Romaji Script Giving Latitudes and Longitudes. Cambridge, MA.: Harvard University Press, 1942.
    DS 805 .G4 HAYDEN STACKS

    About 4500 entries arranged by characters and by romanized forms.

  3. United States. Board on Geographic Names. Japan. Official Standard Names Approved by the United States Board on Geographic Names. Washington, D.C.: Board on Geographic Names, 1955.
    G7960 .Z99 U52 SCI MAP

    About 29,000 geographic and administrative places are listed.

  4. Campbell, Tony. Japan : European Printed Maps to 1800. London: Map Collectors' Circle, 1967.
    GA 101 .M3 no. 36 SCI STACKS

    28 maps in this publication are reproductions of the originals produced between 1570-1794; citations for 83 maps produced between 1586-1794 are also included.

  5. Cortazzi, Hugh. Isles of Gold: Antique Maps of Japan. New York: Weatherhill, 1983.
    GA 1241 .C67 1983 SCI MAP

    Nearly one hundred Japanese and Western maps are included.

  6. Nanba, Matsutaro, comp. Old Maps in Japan. Osaka: Sogensha, 1973.
    G 2355 .N32 1973 SCI MAP

    The old maps shown in this volume were produced in Japan before 1869. The world maps, maps of Japan, and route maps are noted in chronological order, while regional, provincial, and city maps are placed according to geographic locations. Indexed.

GUIDES

  1. Fodor's Japan. New York: Fodor's Modern Guides, 1982- . (Latest edition in Reference; earlier years in Serials.)
    DS 811 .F6 HAYDEN SERIAL

    This title is a continue of the Fodor's Japan and East Asia and Fodor's Japan and Korea (or S. Korea). Useful guide to travel in Japan. It contains maps, historical notes, cultural information, etc.

  2. Bisignani, J. D. Japan Handbook. Chico, CA: Moon Publications, 1986.
    DS 805.2 .B57 1986 REF

    Comprehensive in subjects and periods, and provides brief but useful information about Japan. Glossary and index.

  3. Kanno, Eiji. Japan: A Practical Guide for Independent Travelers. Tokyo: Nitchi Map-Publishing, 1985.
    DS 811 .K32x REF

    Provides detailed practical information for foreign tourists.

INDEXES

  1. Humanities Index. New York: Wilson, 1974- .
    AI 3 .H8x HAYDEN REF (located on Table 7A)

    Titles also in Expanded Academic Index, Online Catalog database 1988- .

  2. Social Science Index. New York: Wilson, 1974- .
    HI 3 .S6x HAYDEN REF (located on Table 4A)

    Titles also in Expanded Academic Index, Online Catalog database 1988- .

    These indexes to scholarly journals (Indexes Entries 1 and 2) began as the International Index in 1907, which later became the Social Sciences and Humanities Index, and which then split into the two indexes in 1974. Arranged by author and subject.

  3. Public Affairs Information Service Bulletin. New York: Public Affairs Information Service, 1915-1990.
    H 1 .P849x HAYDEN REF (located on Table 4A)
    H 96 .P83 LAW INDEX

    This is a index of citations to books, reports, pamphlets, government documents, and journal and newspaper articles. Arranged by subject and indexed by author.

  4. PAIS Foreign Language Index. New York: Public Affairs Information Service, 1985-1990.
    H 1 .F642x HAYDEN REF (located on Table 4A)

    This is a subject index to French-, German-, Italian-, Portuguese-, Spanish-languages public policy literatures. Subject headings and abstract-like notes are in English.

  5. PAIS International in Print. New York: Public Affairs Information Service, 1991- .
    H 1 .P849x HAYDEN REF (located on Table 4A)

    This is a continuation of the PAIS Bulletin and PAIS Foreign Language Index. The scope and format are arranged in the same ways, combining the characteristics of the two publications.

OTHER PLACES TO LOOK

For those who are able to read Japanese, the stacks of the East Asian Collection (Hayden Level 2) are another major place to look for sources. Various government documents, including federal, states, United Nations, etc., can provide pertinent information to researchers. Additional materials may be identified through a variety of computer databases available in the ASU Libraries. Consult Reference Librarians for further information about these resources, and the Japanese Studies Librarian (Level 4) for the vernacular materials.
