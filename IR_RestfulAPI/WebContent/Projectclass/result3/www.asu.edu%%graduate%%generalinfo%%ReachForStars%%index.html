2007–08 Reach for the Stars Fellowships

DEADLINE
March 1, 2007

PURPOSE
Reach for the Stars Fellowships are a university-wide fellowship, allowing the academic units to nominate Arizona residents who are currently completing their senior year of undergraduate studies and plan to attend ASU. Nomination may be made from the underrepresented undergraduate population admitted into ASU?s graduate school master?s level or doctoral degree programs.

CONTACT
Debra Crusoe at dcrusoe@asu.edu or Jennifer Cason at Jennifer.Cason@asu.edu.

AWARD

  • $12,000 for the 1st academic year
  • Resident tuition waiver for the academic year
  • Student Health Insurance

ELIGIBILITY
Student must be regularly admitted into a master?s program or doctoral program and contribute to diversity. Student must be a US Citizen or assigned an IP visa. Students continuing their 2nd year of graduate school in good standing will be funded by the academic unit through an assistantship or equivalent.

APPLICATION PROCESS
Academic units submit nominations by March 1, 2007 to the office of Dr. Andrew N. Webber, Associate Dean, Division of Graduate Studies, mail code: 1003. Application packet includes:

  • all original application materials plus four copies submitted by the student to the academic unit
  • letter of nomination from academic unit chair or graduate director indicating:
    • the nominee’s strengths and scholarly promise, including the nominee’s capacity to contribute to the diversity of the graduate student community and the discipline. This may include individual circumstances of an exceptional nature; cultural or socioeconomic background; unique experiences such as studies abroad, multiculturalism, and bilingualism; extent of work or activities while in college; gender or race/ethnicity; and character of work or other non-academic experience including community service, in addition to such traditional indicators as grade point average, test scores, publications, conference presentations, and academic honors;
    • how support for this student will enhance diversity within the graduate program; and
    • the academic unit’s commitment to provide health insurance during the fellowship year.
SELECTION PROCESS
Applications will be reviewed by a faculty committee. Decisions will be announced by March 15, 2007.

Deferral Policy: Failure to use this award during the specified period will result in its loss. Under very unusual circumstances (e.g., a documented medical situation; documented U.S. military service in the event of a crisis or when the draft is in effect) a one-year deferral may be granted.
