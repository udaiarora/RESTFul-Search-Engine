Searching By Inventor

 

General Search Tip: Neither individuals nor databases are consistent with names. For example, John Joseph Smith may have his name listed one or more of the following ways:


Smith, J.
Smith, J. J.
Smith, J. Joseph
Smith, John
Smith, John J.
Smith, John Joseph


There are over 100 countries and/or agencies worldwide that grant patents. The following pages are a list of intellectual property offices with links to their web sites. For each entity, the availability and the searchability of their patent grants varies.

  • PIPERS: Directory of National and Regional Intellectual Property Offices

European Patent Office's Worldwide Database

The European Patent Office maintains a worldwide database that covers over 80 countries and/or patenting agencies, however, the dates of coverage and the type of coverage varies from entity to entity.

To determine the coverage of inventor names for each entity see

  • Worldwide Database - Facsimile and Classification Coverage
    http://ep.espacenet.com/espacenet/ep/en/help/tab1.html


    • Look in the column labeled "Bibliographic Data" for the dates; for more details about the coverage of this database see:
    esp@cenet helpfiles - Coverage (http://ep.espacenet.com/espacenet/ep/en/help/15350.htm)

To search this database, go to:


Esp@cenet: Europe's Network of Patent Databases
http://ep.espacenet.com/



Enter the database by selecting "Advanced Search" on the left-hand side of the screen under "Issued Patents".

To search for an inventor:
  1. Scroll the search page down to the "Publication Number" search box and enter the country or agency code (ex., US, GB, AU, EP, WO, etc.) with no spaces or punctuation.
    If you want to search the complete database without restricting to country or agency, leave this box blank.
  2. Scroll the screen down to the "Inventor" search box
  3. Enter the inventor's last name;
    The inventor's first name may also be included, ex., Smith John. The most specific type of search uses quote marks around the inventor's name, ex., "Smith John" - be aware that this form of searching only finds the exact name and will not find variations.
  4. Click the gray Search box at the bottom of the page.

To search for two or more inventors:
  1. Scroll the search page down to the "Publication Number" search box and enter the country or agency code (ex., US, GB, AU, EP, WO, etc.) with no spaces or punctuation.
    If you want to search the complete database without restricting to country or agency, leave this box blank.
  2. Scroll the screen down to the "Inventor" search box
  3. Enter the last names with the word AND in between, ex., Smith AND Jones;
  4. Click the gray Search box at the bottom of the page.

To search for a subject or keyword along with an inventor:
  1. Enter the subject or keyword in the "Keyword(s) in Title or Abstract" search box
  2. Scroll the search page down to the "Publication Number" search box and enter the country or agency code (ex., US, GB, AU, EP, WO, etc.) with no spaces or punctuation.
    If you want to search the complete database without restricting to country or agency, leave this box blank.
  3. Scroll the screen down to the "Inventor" search box
  4. Enter the inventor's last name;
    The inventor's first name may also be included, ex., Smith John. The most specific type of search uses quote marks around the inventor's name, ex., "Smith John" - be aware that this form of searching only finds the exact name and will not find variations.
  5. Click the gray Search box at the bottom of the page.

To view a patent:
  1. Click on the patent title in the results screen
  2. Click on the original document tab on the bibliographic data page

Notes:

  • To view a patent requires the Adobe Acrobat Reader.
