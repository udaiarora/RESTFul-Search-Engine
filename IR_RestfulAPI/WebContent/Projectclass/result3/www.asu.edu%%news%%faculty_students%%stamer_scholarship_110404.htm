November 4, 2004

International study earns scholarship for ASU junior

An ASU junior who has just returned from an around-the-world travel-study project has won a prestigious scholarship to study art next year in Germany. Damian Stamer, a talented artist and National Merit Scholar who developed a thirst for travel in childhood, has been chosen to receive a Rotary Ambassadorial Scholarship worth up to $25,000.

He will act as an ambassador of goodwill during his year in Germany, giving presentations about the United States to Rotary clubs and other groups, while studying art at the Staatliche Akademie der Bildenen Kuenste in Stuttgart. Stamer learned to speak fluent German during a year as a high school exchange student in Germany.

Last summer he traveled to New York, London and Hong Kong, as well as Canada, the Netherlands, Austria and Australia , on a Circumnavigators Scholarship, studying how international art museums recruit and retain volunteers in the wake of public funding cuts. He is a docent volunteer and tour guide at the ASU Art Museum.

Stamer is a painting and museum studies major who is a student in the Herberger College of Fine Arts and the Barrett Honors College. He developed his wanderlust when his parents took him and his twin brother on a seven-month camping trip when they were 4 years old.

“You know you are destined to be a traveler when your hiking boots get bronzed instead of your baby shoes,” Stamer says.

Stamer was a varsity wrestler and soccer player in high school, and he discovered a passion for visual arts when he left his home in Durham, N.C., to attend the North Carolina School of the Arts.

At ASU he is an Honors College Ambassador, an Honors Devil and a member of the Fine Arts College and Barrett College Councils, in addition to his work with the Art Museum. His artwork has been selected for the Wet Paint Exhibition in Tempe and the Gallery 1023 Art Exhibition in Phoenix.
