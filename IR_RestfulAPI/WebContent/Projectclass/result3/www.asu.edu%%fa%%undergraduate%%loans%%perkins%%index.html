Federal Perkins Loan

Federal Perkins loans are low interest (5%) loans for eligible students with exceptional financial need. ASU is your lender. Like all student loans, Federal Perkins loan funds may be used only for educational expenses and must be repaid. ASU is required by federal regulations to report the outstanding balance of the loan on an annual basis to national credit bureau agencies.

To apply for this loan, you must complete a FAFSA (for the appropriate academic year) and have marked on the FAFSA that you are interested in student loans. The amount you are awarded depends on when you apply, your level of need, and ASU's funding appropriation.

Perkins Deferment, Forbearance and Cancellation Provisions

Education Computer Systems, Inc. (ECSI), a lending service that assists ASU in processing Perkins loan payments has deferment, forbearance, and cancellation information available on their site. If you download and complete an ECSI form, please return it to the ASU Collections Office, Box 870303, Tempe, AZ 85287-0303.

Consolidation

Although it is possible to consolidate a Federal Perkins loan under the William D. Ford and Federal Family Education Loan Programs, doing so does have some disadvantages.

  • Perkins loans cancellation provisions for special programs are lost under consolidation. (See your promissory note for details.)
  • Perkins loans have a 9-month grace period that is often shortened or eliminated under consolidation.
  • Although consolidation interest rates are currently low, historically, Perkins loans have maintained a lower interest rate at 5%.
  • Although consolidation may offer lower payments and extended and income contingent repayment plans, these terms can increase the overall charges incurred over the life of the loan.
