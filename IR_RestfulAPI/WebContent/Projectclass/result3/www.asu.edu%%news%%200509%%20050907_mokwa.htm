September 7, 2005

Mokwa named Tillman Foundation Distinguished Professor

Michael Mokwa, the marketing department chair at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business, has been named the Pat Tillman Foundation Distinguished Professor in Leadership and Marketing.

Mokwa will be the faculty director of the Leadership Through Action program, a joint project between the Pat Tillman Foundation and the W. P. Carey School. The program will launch this fall with its first class of 14 Tillman Foundation scholars enrolled in the W. P. Carey School and Barrett Honors College.

Mokwa and Laura Burgis will lead the Tillman scholars in further developing their leadership skills and commitment to positive civic involvement.

The program is more than a typical leadership class. It reflects Tillman’s commitment to excellence and service to others by challenging students to reach their full potential; to make a difference in the world; to know what they believe in and what they value; and to live a life grounded in those beliefs, open to new ideas, leading with integrity, passion and commitment.

“Having Mike Mokwa as the first Pat Tillman Foundation Distinguished Professor in Leadership and Marketing is fitting, because Mike knew Pat and was inspired by him,” says Alex Garwood, executive director of the Pat Tillman Foundation. “Mike’s background as a leading educator and marketing expert makes him the ideal choice to take this program forward.”

Mokwa is founding director of the sports business specialization within the W. P. Carey MBA. He also serves on the faculty athletic board at ASU and will become the second president of the national Sports Marketing Association this fall.

He is the author of three books, and his recent research and publications investigate the effectiveness of managers implementing important strategic initiatives for their organizations, the strategic competencies and performance of sports organizations, the role and nature of service driving business performance, and the economic impacts of major sports events.

Mokwa earned his master’s of business administration and doctorate at the University of Houston. He previously served on the faculty of the University of Wisconsin.

The Tillman family created the Pat Tillman Foundation following his death in April 2004. The foundation, a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organization, is determined to carry Tillman’s legacy forward by inspiring people to make positive changes in themselves and in the world around them. To learn more about the foundation, visit its Web site at (www.pattillmanfoundation.org).
