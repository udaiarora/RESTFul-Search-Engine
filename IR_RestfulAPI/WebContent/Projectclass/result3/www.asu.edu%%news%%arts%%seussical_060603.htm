June 6, 2003

'Seussical' takes Gammage stage

The national tour of "Seussical the Musical" is making its way to ASU's Gammage Auditorium on June 3 and will run through June 8.

Cathy Rigby stars as The Cat in the Hat in this family musical by Tony Award-winners Lynn Ahrens and Stephen Flaherty, the team that brought to the stage the critically acclaimed musicals "Ragtime" and "Once On This Island."

"Seussical the Musical" explores the witty and whimsical world of Dr. Seuss as it's never been seen before. Audience members will have a chance to travel back to their childhoods to the land of the Who's and the Jungle of Nool and re-discover the zany characters created by one of the most beloved storytellers of all time.

Kids and accompanying adults will be treated to a special show on June 3 as the 2003 Kids' Night On Broadway makes its way to Gammage. The program, a national effort of the League of American Theaters and Producers, is designed to introduce the magic of live theater to children ages 6 - 18. Kids can attend "Seussical the Musical" for only $15 with the purchase of an accompanying full-priced adult ticket.
Along with the discounted ticket offer, there will be activities based on "Seussical the Musical" for children to participate in, such as the Horton the Elephant inspired create-your-own-elephant trunk activity. All activities begin at 7 pm in the main lobby at ASU's Gammage Auditorium.

Rigby received a Tony Award nomination as best actress in a musical for her role in the 35th anniversary production of "Peter Pan" in 1991. Rigby reprised the role of "Peter Pan" several years later, in a lavish production that received a best musical revival Tony Award nomination. She made her theatrical debut in 1981 as Dorothy in "The Wizard of Oz."

Subsequently, Rigby's multiple talents have been lauded in "Annie Get Your Gun," "The Unsinkable Molly Brown," "Meet Me In St. Louis" and "They're Playing Our Song." Her dramatic television movie credits include "The Perfect Body," "Daddy Can't Read," "The Great Wallindas" and "Triathalon."

A two-time Olympic gymnast, Rigby is the first American woman to win a medal in the World Gymnastics competition and holds 12 international medals, eight of them gold. She is the mother of four children and a recipient of the 1992 Mother of the Year Award. In 1994, Rigby and her husband Tom McCoy assumed the responsibilities as executive producers of the McCoy-Rigby Series at the La Mirada Theatre for the Performing Arts in California, allowing them to produce first-class theatrical productions and mount national tours from their own backyard.

In "Seussical the Musical," many of the best-loved characters from a bevy of Dr. Seuss books meet for the first time. When chaos erupts in the Jungle of Nool, it will take The Cat in the Hat, Horton the Elephant, Gertrude McFuzz, Mayzie LaBird and a gaggle of characters to bring harmony back to their world.

Kenneth Gentry is executive producer of "Seussical the Musical," and the tour direction is by the Booking Group. For information, visit (www.seussical.com).
"Seussical the Musical" is presented by arrangement with ASU Public Events and Clear Channel Entertainment, Inc., and is a subscription offering of the 2002-2003 M&I Bank Broadway in Arizona series. It will be presented at 8 p.m., June 3 - 6; 2 p.m. and 8 p.m. June 7; and 1 p.m. and 6:30 p.m., June 8. Tickets range from $21.75 - $59.50 and are on sale at ASU's Gammage Box Office, all Ticketmaster outlets, (www.ticketmaster.com) or by calling (480) 784-4444. Groups of 20 or more should call (480) 965-6678. For information, visit the Web at (www.asugammage.com).
