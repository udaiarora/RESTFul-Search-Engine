November 11, 2003

Outlook promising for real estate market, job growth in 2004

The Greater Phoenix real estate market is expected to grow for the coming year - although it will be a longer, slower curve than in past economic recoveries, according to the current issue of the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

The single-family market is headed for another record year in 2003, according to Elliott D. Pollack, topics editor. For the next year or two, it may slow a little, "but not enough to be a drag on the economy." Meanwhile, Pollack writes, "For commercial construction, the only direction left is up."

The Greater Phoenix real estate consensus panel foresees gradual improvement in the multifamily, industrial and retail markets. "Overall, the real estate market is recovering, if 'not getting worse' is your definition of recovery," Pollack writes. "Although commercial real estate in Greater Phoenix clearly is probably past the bottom, vacancy rates remain high enough so that markets will not be buoyant for some time to come."

Elsewhere in the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark writes that by early next year, the recovery will be boosted by the long-awaited appearance of employment growth. "The economy should be growing at a fast enough pace in 2004 that most consumers actually will be able to feel the economy improving. It will be more than reported numbers telling the story; as job growth occurs in almost all sectors, it should encourage confidence that the recovery really has arrived."

The Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast is published four times a year by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. To order, call the center at (480) 965-7858.
