Dawn of a New Day

Dorothy Robinson Photograph Collection


As a teacher, who knew Arizona and young people, Dorothy F. Robinson(1895-1982) told historical stories about Arizona and Native Americans for elementary school students. Her interest in the history Native Americans first surfaced when she wrote her masters thesis on the Apache. Robinson's first book, Boys' and Girls' Arizona, published in 1932 was for 4th grade level students. She continued to research the Native American in Arizona then in 1966 she published Navajo Indians Today, a book intended to inform the public, as well as the Navajo, of the importance of the Navajo traditions and cultural history and their contribution to America. The photographs on exhibit were taken by her as she traveled throughout Arizona. Several photographs in her collection were used in her first book. Robinson captured images of Native American ceremonies, activities and culture, such as, the women in traditional dress carrying water or grain in jugs balanced on there heads. At the same time, a photograph exhibits the gathering of spectators to attend a public performance of a ceremony at the Taos Pueblo, New Mexico.
