Legislative bills cover diverse issues, may affect state universities

Besides dealing with looming state budget crises, legislators have introduced more than 1,000 bills during the first session of the 46th Legislature. Here are some of the bills that may affect Arizona's universities and community colleges.

. SB1133 and HB2408 - Research Funding. The companion bills would require the legislature to fund $29 million annually for 25 years from the general fund to pay for lease payment for research buildings and associated infrastructure at ASU and UA. ASU's portion for first-year certificates is $14 million.
. SB 1158 - Campus Sex Crimes Prevention. The bill would require some registered sex offenders to notify the sheriff within 72 hours that they are state or private employees or registered as a student at a state institution. The university would be required to release a campus advisory about the registered sex offender.
. SB1181 - Free classes for the Elderly. The bill would require the Arizona Board of Regents to conduct a feasibility study on allowing people, age 65 and older, to audit ASU courses for free on a space available basis with the professor's permission.
. SB1277, SB1325, HB 2509, HB 2510 - Union Negotiations. The similar bills would give state employees the option to allow employee associations or unions to bargain on their behalf with the state for improved working conditions. Employees would retain the option to represent themselves.
. SB 1326 - State Employees, Payroll Deductions. The bill would permit universities, along with other state agencies, to allow their employees to pay for a variety of fees through payroll deduction as long as at least 1,000 employees, including faculty, are enrolled for the service.
. SB 1294 - Privatization of Residence Halls. The bill would require the Arizona Board of Regents to sell to the highest bidder at a public auction all university residence halls except those under lease-purchase agreements. The proceeds from the sale of any university property would revert to the state. Residence halls would be owned and operated - including security and maintenance - by a private contractor as long as their use as residence halls does not change. If usage changes, the property would revert back to the state.
. HB2243 - Law Enforcement Cooperation with Immigration and Naturalization Service. Arizona universities and community colleges would be prohibited from admitting non-documented immigrants and would be required to conduct citizen status checks for all international students at the beginning at each semester.
. HB2305 - Employee Health Insurance. The bill would increase the state's spending on its employee and retiree health insurance, and may allow the surviving spouse of a deceased employee to purchase up to 36 months of insurance at 102 percent of the group rate. On the approval of the joint legislative budget committee, the department of administration shall self-insure by Oct. 1. The department of administration may contract directly with preferred provider organizations, physician and hospital networks, indemnity health insurers, hospital and medical service plans, dental plans and health maintenance organizations for self-insurance. The department of administration by rule shall designate and adopt performance standards, including cost competitiveness, utilization review issues, network development and access, conversion and implementation, report timeliness, quality outcomes and customer satisfaction for qualifying plans.
. HB2320 - Violence Liability. The act amends Title 13, Chapter 31, of the Arizona Revised Statutes by adding a new section related to weapons. The bill would permit any state agency, person, organization or entity that establishes a gun-free zone, such as universities, to be liable for damages that result from criminal conduct against another person in the gun-free zone. The liability test is if a reasonable person believes possessing a firearm could have helped the victim defend against the criminal conduct.
. HB2403 - University Research and Development, Technology Transfer. The proposed amendment to the Arizona Constitution would allow the Arizona Board of Regents to hold stock or interest in private companies in exchange for university intellectual or technology property. The Bill would require passage at the legislative level and a vote of the people in the next general election.
. HB2498 - Internet access for state employees. The proposed bill bans state employees from knowingly using agency owned or agency leased computer equipment to access, download, print or store any information infrastructure files or services that depict nudity, sexual activity, sexual excitement or ultimate sexual acts. Employees in violation can be disciplined or dismissed by the employer.
. HB2518 - Residency classification for non-citizens. The bill would allow non-U.S. citizens to pay resident tuition rates at any state university or community college if they attended high school in Arizona for three years, received a GED, or graduated from an Arizona high school, and are affiliated with a state university or community college and have applied or will apply for U.S. citizenship.
For information on these and other bills introduced during the session, visit the Web at (http://www.azleg.state.az.us/Bills.asp).
