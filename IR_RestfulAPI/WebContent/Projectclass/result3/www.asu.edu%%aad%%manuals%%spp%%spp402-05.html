SPP 402–05: Career Progression

Purpose

To permit university departments to provide career advancement and/or salary increases to individual classified employees whose knowledge, skills, duties and responsibilities have increased

Source

Arizona Board of Regents Policy Manual - 6–402

Applicability

Regular classified employees

Policy

Career Progression within Classification

Administrative officials may provide salary increases to regular classified staff who, by virtue of increased job assignments, motivation, initiative, and abilities, are regularly assigned and held accountable for significantly more complex projects, assignments, and responsibilities within their current job titles. Classified employees must have performance levels that exceed the requirements of the job to be eligible for a career progression within classification.

The following principles should be used to guide the implementation of the career progression within classification policy:

  1. Salary increases for the “satisfactory” performer can be granted in other types of salary increases, not from career progression within classification.

  2. Salary increases for those “exceeding job requirements” can be granted in merit increases, career progression within or outside classification, and other types of salary increases.

  3. Factors to consider in determining an individual’s salary through career progression include experience in the position (or related experience beyond the minimum required by the position), education beyond that required by the position, mastery of job elements, performance level over time as documented through performance appraisal, size and complexity of the unit, and authority delegated to the position.

  4. Each unit can make career progressions within classification for eligible employees one time during the fiscal year as determined by the unit head.

Supervisors may recommend career progression within classification for employees who meet all of the following criteria:

  1. the employee is regularly performing significantly more complex projects, assignments, and responsibilities of the job classification that meet distinct, recognized organizational needs

  2. the employee has demonstrated the attainment of a significantly higher level of knowledge and skills that are specific to his or her job classification and functional area

  3. the employee’s most recent performance appraisal (i.e., within the last 12 months) within the current job classification reflects above-satisfactory performance (level 3)

  4. confirmation that at least 12 months have elapsed since the employee’s last career progression

  5. the amount of the proposed increase should be justified and reasonable when compared to other positions in the unit

    and

  6. confirmation that funds have been identified and authorized by the unit’s administrator to support the salary increase.

The approved salary increase will be up to 10 percent of the employee’s current salary but not to exceed the market grade maximum, with consideration given to other employees in comparable positions in the unit and based on availability of funds.

Human Resources can provide advice about the appropriate classification or salary.

Career Progression outside Classification

Departments may provide for the noncompetitive career advancement of employees. The appropriate administrative official will take into account university affirmative action objectives and timetables when considering recommendations on career progression outside classification.

In consultation with Human Resources (HR), an administrative official may advance an employee in position and salary through career progression outside classification if all of the following criteria are met:

  1. the increased duties and responsibilities conform to those that describe the higher level classification

  2. the employee’s experience, education, and training meet the minimum qualifications of the higher level classification

  3. the employee’s most recent (within last 12 months) performance evaluation reflects responsibilities of the position exceeded

    Note:This does not include performance appraisals of probationary employees.

  4. there is a distinct and recognizable organizational need for the higher level position

    and

  5. 12 months or more have elapsed since the employee’s last career progression.

HR and the administrative official should work together in partnership to determine the appropriate uses of a career progression outside classification. If a mutual decision cannot be reached, the appropriate person with higher authority in the unit will make the final decision after considering both perspectives.

An employee’s salary will be advanced to at least the market grade minimum of the higher level classification. Individuals whose qualifications exceed the minimum may be appointed at a rate above the minimum up to the range midpoint based on qualifications and internal impact. Salary adjustments above the market range midpoint are subject to approval by the appropriate vice president/provost or designee.

As the position becomes vacant, the classification may revert to the original position level.

The effective date of approved actions will be the beginning of the pay period following approval by the vice president/provost or designee.
