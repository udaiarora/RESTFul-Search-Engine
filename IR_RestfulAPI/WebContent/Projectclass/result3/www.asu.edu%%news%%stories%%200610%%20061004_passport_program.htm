October 4, 2006

?Passport program' eases Nina Scholars into ASU

Cory Gonzales' life is at full throttle this time every year, but passion fuels her days. As the program coordinator for ASU's Nina Mason Pulliam Legacy Scholars Program, she is welcoming the university's new undergraduate Nina Scholars, welcoming back similar cohorts at every class level, managing the ASU's relationship with the Nina Mason Pulliam Charitable Trust and its advisory council, and launching another recruiting season.

?My diligence stems from the deep need in our community for programs like this, which make higher education accessible for students with potential and determination, but with serious obstacles on their path,? she says. ?We are already a success story, but we need applicants. Although the scholarship application deadline is not until April 1, we are reaching out assertively to our partners in the community, social service agencies, individuals and even our own faculty, to recommend and help us identify U.S. citizens who meet the program criteria. The process begins now, since it is best if students are accepted into ASU by the application date.?

The scholarship program, established in 2001, provides educational opportunities for individuals who would not normally receive traditional academic scholarships, and whose personal commitments and financial circumstances would preclude their attendance without substantial, long-term scholarship support.

A new ?passport program? was added to the program this year, smoothing the way for eligible Nina Scholars from Maricopa Community Colleges to seamlessly transfer to ASU.

The College of Public Programs administers the program for the entire campus, but students can enroll in any major across the university. The college provides Gonzales as an adviser and mentor; outreach to build community among each new class of scholars; and dedicated program and study areas with computers at the Downtown Phoenix campus and the Tempe campus.

The scholarships include up to six years of full in-state tuition, fees, books, and up to $2,500 as a living allowance.

?The Nina Scholars program is a living legacy to Mrs. Pulliam, because education is a tool,? says Harriet Ivey, president and chief executive officer of the Nina Mason Pulliam Charitable Trust. ?It is a tool for people who have had many challenges in their lives to realize their dreams, and their dreams for their children.?

Maricopa Community Colleges , Indiana University-Purdue University Indianapolis and Ivy Tech Community College are also included in the program and use the same application. The targeted populations for the scholarships are:

? Adults, ages 25 or older, with dependents in their family unit.

? College-age youths and adults with physical disabilities.

? Young adults ages 18-25 who have been raised in the child welfare system and are responsible for their own financial support.

To apply or inquire about the program, contact Gonzales at (602) 496-0420 or (cory.gonzales@asu.edu).
