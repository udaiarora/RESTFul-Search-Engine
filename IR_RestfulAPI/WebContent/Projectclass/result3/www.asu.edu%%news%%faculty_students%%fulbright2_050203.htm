May 2, 2003

Fulbright awards keep rolling in; another student receives NSEP grant

Three more ASU students have won Fulbright awards for a year of graduate study and research abroad, bringing the total number so far to six, tying the best record ever for ASU. In addition, an undergraduate has just won a National Security Education (NSEP) grant to study in Mexico next year.

Brenna Lissoway and Robert Niebuhr, both graduate students in history, will study in New Zealand and Croatia, respectively. Danielle Ross, history senior, has been awarded a Fulbright to Kazakhstan. Orietta Verdugo, a junior majoring in industrial engineering, has received an NSEP grant to attend the Instituto Technologico de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey next spring.

Lissoway, who grew up at Bandelier National Monument in northern New Mexico as the daughter of a National Park Service ranger, wants to explore the history of reserve lands in New Zealand. She has worked as a ranger herself at Zion, Acadia, Grand Teton and Mesa Verde National Parks.

Niebuhr will go to Croatia to study ethnicity in the former Yugoslav army and the present Croatian army that emerged after the bloody conflict of the early 1990s. The army has been a major institution for uniting various ethnic groups, serving as a microcosm of the state. He has studied the Serbo-Croatian language at ASU's Critical Languages Institute.

Ross just returned from Kazakhstan, where she studied language and culture on an NSEP grant. She will return there next fall on a Fulbright to study population mobility during the post-Soviet era in the newly emerging central Asian republic. Ross studied Tatar at the Critical Languages Institute. She also speaks Russian and is learning Kazakh.

Verdugo, the daughter of Mexican immigrants, wants to pursue a career in international business and work with U.S.-Mexican trade in a high-tech global manufacturing and service organization. In an immersion program she hopes to study Mexican business customs and politics, improve her Spanish and learn more about the culture. Verdugo also will have an internship with a Mexican company.

Fulbright grant contestants apply for a particular country and find specialists who are willing to work with them on their chosen course of study. They receive full travel, living and academic expenses for an academic year.

The NSEP program provides for a semester of area and language studies outside of western Europe. Students must learn the language of their host countries and also fulfill paid internships or employment with the U.S. government within eight years of their return.
