March 29, 2004

Three leaders will form new Student Affairs management team

Three key individuals have stepped up to the plate to form a unified management team for Student Affairs. Sally Ramage, Kevin Cook and Bob Soza are taking on new responsibilities to help the division quickly move the agenda of the university forward, according to Juan C. Gonzalez, vice president for Student Affairs.

Ramage has been named associate vice president for fiscal and program development, in charge of fiscal planning efforts, budgeting, contracting efforts with third party entities and opportunities related to development and fund raising. She also is in charge of academic support services, student media, Memorial Union and student development and coordinating collaborations with academic partners to help students succeed.

Cook is now associate vice president for facility development and residential life, responsible for planning and developing a maintenance and operations model for major Student Affairs construction projects. He will implement a 10-year master plan for housing that may exceed $800 million, coordinating the addition of 7,500 new beds and overseeing the improvement and renovation of existing facilities.

As associate vice president and dean of students, Soza will continue to provide administrative leadership and direction to Career Services, Counseling and Consultation, Student Health and Wellness, Student Life, Recreational Sports and the Arizona Prevention Resource Center. He oversees programs that promote diversity, enhance the campus community and encourage wellness and active learning. Soza also will serve as liaison to the external community.

"We needed a team that could work together and make quick decisions, rather than having everyone report directly to me," says Gonzalez. "We have so many new initiatives, so much on our plate, that we need to be agile in order to move ASU's agenda forward.

"Helping students succeed through better delivery of service is the goal. All three of these individuals are highly respected team players who have given many years of service to the university. I have the greatest confidence in all three."

Ramage will develop food services university-wide, creating long-term strategies for feeding the entire community, not only students. The goal is for all residence halls to offer meal plans with high quality food and a variety of choices, including late-night dining. This reflects a nationwide trend for major universities.

In addition to leading the effort to build new residence halls through public-private partnerships, and to renovate older halls, Cook will oversee the construction of a new Student Health Center.

Soza will administer an integrated and comprehensive array of programs, monitoring student environment and making sure there are programs that meet student needs.

Auffret, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-6991 or (sauffret@asu.edu).
