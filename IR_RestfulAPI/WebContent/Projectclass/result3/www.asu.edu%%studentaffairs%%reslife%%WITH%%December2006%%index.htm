Spirit Contest Winner – Show me the Money!

On Thursday, Nov. 30 the grand prize winner of the Spirit Contest was announced. Palo Verde East was the GRAND PRIZE WINNER of the $25,000 cash award to use for hall improvements. This fall, each residence hall was given the challenge to work together and show their ASU spirit through a variety of hall programs and initiatives. The new School Spirit hall decorating contest took place each week before the home football game. A panel of staff and students judged each hall and calculated the points each week. Every home game the best decorated hall received $5,000 to be used for hall improvements. The first four contest winners were Cholla hall (September 30), Sonora hall (October 21), Hassayampa Academic Village (November 9) and Palo Verde East. Points were cumulative, and at the conclusion of the season, the hall with the most points overall will win $25,000 to use to improve its community. Congratulations PV East!!!

Leaving for Winter Break?

Before you leave home for the holidays make sure to:

  • Unplug all appliances (including computer, phone, refrigerator, etc.)
  • Defrost refrigerator (this takes some time to do, so please start before you are supposed to leave)
  • Move all furniture at least 1 ft. away from the walls
  • Replace any hall furniture or property to its rightful place (lounge furniture, vacuums, etc.)
  • Dispose of all perishable items (milk, fruit, bread, etc.)
  • Take ALL trash down to trash room/dumpster
  • Clean out your UPS mailbox
  • If you are the only occupant in a double room, prepare the other half of the room for a new roommate
  • Return your room key to the front desk when you leave for break to avoid being billed for a lock change
  • Halls reopen for Spring semester on Jan. 13, 2007

FAMILY TIES: GOING HOME FOR THE HOLIDAYS

Food for Thought

Along with anticipation of winter break, sleeping late, and no homework, students may be approaching their extended visit home with both feelings of excitement and anxiety. Most students look forward to “going home” to relax, enjoy home cooking, visit with old friends, and share time with family.

However, for some students, the visit home may be stir up mixed feelings. Being away from home has meant living with more freedom and less parental involvement. Some families see their sons and daughters as the same in November as when they left them at school in August. Thus, your family may have difficulty accepting your new pattern of coming and going, established at school, and may expect you to adhere to family routine.

Your new independence as an adult may not be fully understood at home. Conflict may result when you describe how your attitudes or way of thinking has changed in a number of areas—through new experiences and your studies. Your family’s response to how you have changed could lead to feelings of frustration and emotional distance from your family.

Elevator Safety Tips

Save Your Cramming For Finals!

At a certain point, there’s only so much an elevator system can handle.

  • Check the capacity of the elevator, which can be found on a plaque either on the operating station or above it.
  • Don’t try to stop an elevator’s doors from closing, or you could get stuck.
  • If you find yourself stuck in an elevator
    - Don’t panic, and don’t try to get out.
    - Don’t try to pull the doors open and get out.

A lot of accidents happen when people try to self-evacuate from a stalled elevator that’s not at the landing.
