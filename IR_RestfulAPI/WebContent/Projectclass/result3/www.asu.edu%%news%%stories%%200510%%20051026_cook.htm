October 26, 2005

Cook to lead Student Affairs at Downtown Phoenix campus

As ASU develops the new Downtown Phoenix campus, Mernoy Harrison, vice president and provost of the Downtown Phoenix campus, is continuing to build his leadership team.

Kevin Cook has been appointed vice provost and dean for student affairs at the ASU Downtown Phoenix campus. Mary Hughes, a former Phoenix television news reporter and anchor, has also been appointed as director of external affairs and community relations. Both will report directly to Harrison.

Cook will be responsible for creating, developing, and managing all aspects of student services for the developing campus. He began his new role Oct. 17.

Kevin Cook
Mary Hughes

"We are delighted to have someone of Kevin’s caliber,” Harrison says. “Dr. Cook has excelled at every stage of student life and academic achievement. He has helped develop ASU’s new residential initiatives at the Tempe campus, and is the ideal person to translate that experience into the ASU Downtown Phoenix campus.”

"What a wonderful opportunity to serve in this capacity, to support student learning, enhance the quality of life for students, and to create a collegiate environment in an exciting and vibrant urban setting,” Cook says. “I look forward to developing and delivering programs and services that meet the specific needs of the downtown students.”

Since 1997, Cook served as director for residential life at the Tempe campus – and, since February 2004, as the associate vice president for student affairs. His first appointment at ASU was in 1990, as a faculty associate in the University Academic Advising Center. He also served in Residential Life as assistant director for academic support and co-curricular programs.

"I have a pioneering spirit and will be looking for opportunities in every challenge with which I am faced,” Cook says. “I am pleased to have been asked to serve.”

Cook received his undergraduate degree in business administration from Indiana State University, his master’s degree in business management from Webster University, and his doctorate in higher education administration from ASU.

As a graduate of the ASU School of Social Work and the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communications, Hughes has an extensive background that also includes public relations, political consulting, social service and nonprofit administration.

In her previous position as chief development and public relations officer of a Catholic Charities agency in Phoenix, Hughes was responsible for stakeholder, community and media relations, external publications and fund development.

At the Downtown Phoenix campus, Hughes will work to develop community partnerships and collaborations that embrace the New American University’s imperative to become socially embedded in the greater Phoenix community. She will also report directly to Harrison.

"Mary’s delightful personality, and her particular combination of experience and abilities, will serve ASU and the larger community very well,” Harrison says. “With her appointment, we anticipate a seamless transition as the university develops in downtown Phoenix.”

"I have a great love for ASU and a deep appreciation for the Phoenix area,” Hughes says. “That I could work at once on behalf of both is a satisfaction beyond words.”
