Justin Romanowitz

Modeling of Elk Habitat and Movement
in the Payson, Arizona Area

ABSTRACT

With my study I am attempting to model elk habitat and movement in the Payson area. To do this we put GPS collars of 33 elk and recorded GPS fixes every ninety minutes, the period the collar stayed on the animal varied, but most were on for a year or more. The result was a dataset of 105,000 GPS locations spread below and on the Mogollon rim east of Payson. I have visited the collections of 200 cow locations, 200 bull locations, and 200 random locations in this area to gain information about the characteristics on the ground that may not to evident through remote sensing, such as species composition, shrub and tree size, and ground and canopy cover. This data helped us to create weights for elk habitat selection, and also acted as ground truthing for the Landsat imagery that we use for an input to our elk habitat model. We plan on have GIS layers for vegetation, slope, aspect, canopy cover, ground cover, shelter, distance to water, distance to roads, land use, fire history, and any other spatial variables that we can generate and might effect elk habitat selection. Then we joined the values of each of these layers to the original GPS locations. The resulting table was the input into a regression model that tells us the significance of each of these variables. With the knowledge of what spatial variables lead to elk habitat selection we will then be able to assign a likelihood of any pixel on the map of having elk in it or at least the suitability of the pixel as elk habitat at any time of the year or to some degree by time of day. Basically a lot of work to make a bunch of pretty pictures that shows elk habitat suitability over a large area.
