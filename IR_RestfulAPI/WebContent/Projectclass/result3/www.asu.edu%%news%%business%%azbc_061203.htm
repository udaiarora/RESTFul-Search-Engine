June 12, 2003

Stronger growth is forecast for the second half of 2003

Most economic experts believe stronger growth will occur in the second half of the year, but there is a long road ahead - and job growth remains sluggish both in Arizona and nationwide, according to the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

"Optimists will point out that Arizona is outperforming the national economy so far in 2003," according to Lee McPheters, contributing editor. "Pessimists will emphasize that's not a particularly notable achievement right now: In March and April the national economy lost jobs compared to the same months of last year."

Arizona has added new jobs each month this year when compared month-over-month to 2002, but the rate of increase has slowed from a 1.1 percent gain in January to 0.5 percent in April, according to the latest preliminary figures released by the state Department of Economic Security.

Analysts were anticipating a "postwar bounce" for the economy once the hostile phase of the Iraq war ended, McPheters writes. "Now, most economy watchers believe stronger growth will be deferred until the second half of the year."

One area of strength in the Arizona economy is the service sector. "Arizona service-producing jobs have increased faster than in the nation so far this year. But in April, even the rate of growth in services fell below 1.0 percent." Both Arizona and the national economy are losing goods-producing jobs.

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark surveyed the consensus panel on the U.S. economic outlook. "The national economy has a major impact on the state economy, so it is beneficial to quiz the panel periodically on what it expects for the nation as a whole," Clark writes. Last year, long before the Iraq conflict became a major concern, the consensus estimate for GDP growth was 2.6 percent for 2002 and 3.6 percent for 2003. The current estimate for GDP growth in 2002 was 2.4 percent, and the panel now forecasts 2.6 percent for 2003. "It is clear that preparations for the Iraq conflict impacted 2002 GDP growth, and the actual conflict had a major impact on expectations for 2003."

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. For information, call (480) 965-7858.
