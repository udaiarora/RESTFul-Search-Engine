ASU Messaging Software and Systems

ASU provides Exchange accounts for employees and IMAP accounts for students. Employees will use Microsoft Outlook to access their e-mail. Students can use EMMA, a web accessible interface to access their mail or they can use Pine. We also support the Electronic Post Office, the ASU ListServ and NetNews.

Outlook
Software client for PC's, MAC's and internet access from any computer. See our Outlook Best Practices web page for suggestions on making Outlook perform better for you.

myASU
Single website that gives you one stop access to ASU News, personal account information and your E-Mail mailboxes plus many other services.

Blackberry, Treo and Windows Mobile Devices
Handheld wireless devices can synchronize to your Exchange account.

SPAM Filter
User enabled filter to help you reduce the number of unwanted e-mails to your asu.edu addresses.

E-Mail Help and Resources
ASU-specific tips and guidelines.

Electronic Post Office
ASU's internal e-mail systems are connected to each other and to the Internet through an e-mail hub called the Electronic Post Office (EPO). Use it to configure an e-mail alias.

ListServ
The Listserv system allows you to create an e-mail discussion group that others from anywhere in the world can subscribe to and participate in.

Pine
Access to IMAP servers is via terminal emulation to the Interactive Unix servers.

Usenet/Netnews
Usenet News, sometimes referred to as Netnews, offers a wide variety of information on subjects and topics. You have the option of just soaking up the information, or becoming an active participant in the News groups.

Authenticated SMTP
Sending mail using your mail client software off campus
