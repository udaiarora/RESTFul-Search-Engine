Cataloging Policy at ASU

This policy records general decisions about the cataloging program so that catalog users know what to expect from the catalogers; so that catalogers know what is expected of them; so that both catalog users and catalogers know what standards are being used, why, and where the standards originate. A bibliography of materials used to develop the cataloging policy appears at the end.

The cataloging staff produces and maintains bibliographic records and associated authority control records in the Online Catalog primarily for the use of the ASU community. Local needs guide the cataloging policy. However, OCLC National-Level Enhance authorization and membership in the Library of Congress Program for Cooperative Cataloging mean that local work can be shared with other institutions nationally and internationally to an extent that has not been possible until recently.


 

The ASU Online Catalog contains bibliographic records from a wide variety of sources. Catalogers create original records that enter the Online Catalog via the OCLC or RLIN cooperative cataloging databases; catalogers also edit for local use records from those databases created by other member libraries. Records regularly enter the Online Catalog from commercial vendors such as BNA, OCLC TechPro and MARCIVE under continuing contract agreements. ASU also buys cataloging for large one-time acquisitions such as Early English Books Online. Perfect consistency among these records is impossible but the Online Catalog must be reasonably coherent and integrated as a whole in order to serve as a useful guide to the Libraries' collections. The cataloging policy of the ASU cataloging program must impose sufficient discipline to make this possible while remaining flexible enough to accommodate a wide variety of materials and user needs. Everyone is best served by a policy that is firm but not rigid, that provides clear guidance but does not attempt to cover every possible contingency.


Core Standards

ASU is a member of the national Program for Cooperative Cataloging (PCC) and an active participant in three of its four divisions: NACO (name and series authority records), SACO (subject authority records) and BIBCO (cooperative cataloging) At present, ASU contributes BIBCO records for monographs only; NACO and SACO contributions may originate from cataloging of any format. Through PCC/BIBCO the concept of a core standard for bibliographic records has been developed and distributed. Core standards provide a nationally accepted minimum content for bibliographic records and now serve as the basis for record form and content at ASU. Although the information included in a core record varies somewhat according to the material being cataloged--for example, the core standard for videos is not identical to the core standard for rare books--the basic idea remains the same. In all core records:

  1. The description is sufficient to identify the material and distinguish it from other similar material;
  2. The access points are sufficient in number and kind to guide the user to the record;
  3. Controlled access points are supported by complete authority records.



Core standards are available through the Program for Cooperative Cataloging for most formats or material types. BIBCO has developed the monographic guidelines; serial core standards have been produced by CONSER, the serials section of PCC.  


 

There are only two reasons for including in the Online Catalog bibliographic records that do not meet the core standards.

  1. Lack of information
  2. Economy in copy editing 

 

Examples of point 1 include computer files in a format ASU cannot read; material in languages too exotic to decipher fully or properly or for which ASU has no dictionary; material that lacks a chief source of information or acceptable substitute. Items falling under this point are unusual and may be discussed with or re-evaluated by a subject specialist before cataloging. 


Point 2 covers existing OCLC or RLIN records that, because of their age or origin, lack fixed field or descriptive elements that core standards now require. These records are not edited to provide non-essential fixed field elements or to supply missing descriptive elements such as a statement of responsibility that were legitimately omitted under pre-AACR2 rules. 


Beyond core standards

While core standards provide a convenient base for bibliographic record form and content, they are not intended to limit arbitrarily the configuration of any particular record. The MARC format, AACR2r, and subject cataloging conventions provide for considerable elaboration as required or desired by the cataloging agency or the individual cataloger, or as mandated by the nature of the material being cataloged. For example, the Library of Congress has decided to include in its application of the core standard for monographs several fields that are not required but that LC believes are useful and necessary, such as the bibliography note.   At the point where enough additions have been made that records include everything that is either permitted or suggested by the rules they become National Full Level records.


ASU will not, in general, attempt to make separate rulings on the desirability or otherwise of individual fields or the optimum requirements for individual formats. Catalogers' judgment, guided by considerations outlined in the Original Cataloging and Copy Cataloging sections below, will prevail. 

 

The following list includes everything for which an across-the-board decision has been made.

 

National Full Level records will routinely be provided for:

  1. Arizona documents
  2. Arizona imprints 

 

National Full Level records may be provided for:

  1. Non-mainstream material dealing with Arizona and the Southwest
  2. Rare or unique material (does not necessarily include everything held in a special collection) 

 

Basic core treatment will routinely be provided for:

  1. Material in languages where local expertise is seriously limited
  2. Creative literature 

 

Most ASU serial cataloging will fall somewhere above CONSER core level but under CONSER full level. The core standard does not require several fields used by serialists in serials control or intended to aid in the use of the serial; ASU catalogers will provide these fields. 


Original cataloging (including OCLC "new" commands)

In general, ASU original cataloging will meet or exceed the core standard for its format, including AAAF authority records for all controlled access points. Exceptions to this policy are limited to:

  1. ASU theses and dissertations.
  2. Records where essential bibliographic information is lacking and cannot be reliably supplied.
  3. Records that include a controlled access point that cannot for some reason be established even provisionally   forthe AAAF.

 

For material not covered by specific decisions, that is, for most material, the cataloger will decide which possible record elements to include in an original bibliographic record. Catalogers are expected to weigh potential usefulness of an element against the time and effort required to provide it. For example, a full compliment of subject headings will probably repay time spent; extended research to establish a name for a marginally necessary added entry probably will not.   Savings of time and effort that seem insignificant on a single record mount quickly in the aggregate.

Original cataloging for PCC/BIBCO

ASU is authorized to submit PCC/BIBCO records to OCLC and RLIN for monographs only. The following categories of monographs are excluded:

  1. ASU theses (because of ASU authority and call number policy)

  2. Curriculum books classified in Dewey (because ASU uses abridged Dewey)

  3. Material with accession-type call numbers (because PCC/BIBCO records must include a call number from a standard classification scheme)

    [Rare books cataloged according to DCRB are exempt from the call number requirement and will be submitted as BIBCO contributions.]

  4. Records with   controlled access points that cannot be supported by full level authority records (because provisional authority records are not acceptable in support of PCC/BIBCO bibliographic records)

Beyond these exclusions, all original monographic records should be prepared as PCC/BIBCO submissions. Records can be located anywhere along the continuum between base core and national full level. All controlled access points must be supported by authority records in AAAF. Policies for local notes (590s) and local tracings such as donor added entries are not affected. Original monographic records that are for some reason not submitted as PCC/BIBCO will be at OCLC I level or, in rare cases, K. 


 


Copy cataloging (editing useable records)

A useable record is one that has LC subject headings and a valid LC call number. The policy for editing useable records from the OCLC and RLIN databases has always been to keep editing of descriptive elements to a minimum and to avoid purely cosmetic changes. Generally if something was correct when the record was created it can be considered acceptable now. This policy continues in force.


Replacing master records (OCLC only)

The OCLC database is designed to retain and display one "master record" per title. Ordinary editing done by OCLC members on master records does not permanently affect the records or remain visible to the next record user. However, under certain conditions ASU can alter monographic and serial master records to include additional information and/or corrections. Master records can be deleted from the database only by OCLC. The term "level" in this section refers to a code in the fixed field of each bibliographic record that indicates the degree of completeness of the record. Encoding levels are defined by OCLC. Catalogers use the various record replacement options judiciously to improve the OCLC database and to preserve for the use of others local editing that would be done in any case.


Replace (ASU records only, monographs or serials, any encoding level)

If ASU has contributed a record and no other member has used it, the record can be locked, changed in any way, and replaced. This is most useful for errors noted immediately after updating and is usually done by original catalogers or input staff. No special OCLC authorization is required.


Enrich (Level I records)

If a level I monographic record lacks an LC call number, LC subjects, contents note, or 006/007 field, the record can be locked, improved by the addition of any or all of these fields, and replaced. The encoding level and all other fields remain the same. Copy catalogers add LC call numbers this way; other catalogers add LC call numbers and subject headings to descriptions otherwise adequate. No special OCLC authorization is required. Level I serial records can also be enriched, subject to special limitations. CONSER serial records (those with field 042) cannot be replaced unless all codes in the 042 begin with x. Non-CONSER serial records with more than 10 holdings can be enriched the same as other formats.   Non-CONSER serial records with 10 or fewer holdings can be upgraded the same as records below Level I.


Upgrade minimal-level records 
(monographs and non-CONSER serials, records below Level I)

Records at levels K, M, 2, 3, 4 (without 042 pcc), 5, and 7 can be overhauled to meet AACR2r and OCLC level I standards. New fields may be added; existing fields may be corrected but should not be deleted unless they are absolutely wrong. Non-book and serial catalogers use Upgrade to preserve extensive ASU editing on   master records. Book catalogers may use Upgrade to create level I records when there is an obstacle to enhancing the record to PCC/BIBCO core or national full level. (See Enhance, below) No special OCLC authorization is required.


Enhance and National Level Enhance
(Level I, Level 4 (with 042 pcc) and Level [blank])

These functions require special authorization from OCLC; serial records are enhanced by CONSER participants only. ASU is not a CONSER participant and has these authorizations for books format only at present. Enhance permits additions and corrections to OCLC level I book records; the record level does not change. Fields should not be deleted unless they are absolutely wrong. Catalogers use Enhance to close monographic sets or to improve level I records when there is an obstacle to enhancing the record to PCC/BIBCO level or when such enhancing would not, in actuality, add significantly to the integrity   or usefulness of the record. National Level Enhance allows replacement of any record. Records enhanced at this level must meet all BIBCO standards, including all authority work. Book catalogers use this authorization to make additions or corrections to records already at level [blank] or level 4 (pcc) and to bring other records up to the PCC/BIBCO standard. Existing fields are not deleted unless they are absolutely wrong.


 


Outsourced cataloging.        Purchased cataloging.           Shelf-ready books.

When circumstances warrant, ASU will either outsource cataloging or purchase it. Both terms mean that someone other than ASU catalogers will create or assemble bibliographic records for inclusion in the Online Catalog. The term "outsource" usually indicates sending batches of material to a cataloging agency such as OCLC TechPro to be cataloged and returned. ASU outsources Thai language material. Purchased cataloging usually indicates that ASU buys cataloging already prepared or assembled for a given body of material. ASU purchases cataloging for government documents on a continuing basis from MARCIVE and has also made one-time purchases of cataloging for large microform or electronic collections.   Shelf-ready books are a form of purchased cataloging that includes end processing of the material cataloged. BNA sends new English language imprints on a shelf-ready plan. The decision to acquire cataloging by any of these means is based on factors such as local resources available, amount of material involved, relative cost, convenience,   speed, etc. All cataloging from outside sources should conform to ASU/national standards and must be free from mechanical flaws that prevent efficient loading of records to the Online Catalog.


 


Authority control

Authority control provides collocation, consistency, and references. Controlled access points on bibliographic records include 1XX, 240, 440, 6XX, 7XX (except 740) and 8XX in series fields.  


ASU uses the Anglo-American Authority File (AAAF), accessible via either OCLC or RLIN, for all cataloging. ASU contributes new name and series entries to the file via the NACO program and new subject entries via SACO. Records from this file, provided by OCLC and loaded in the Online Catalog, are combined with locally constructed authority records to form the local file. This file is not, at present, interactive.


In general, all controlled access points on all ASU original cataloging , OCLC upgrades, and regular Enhances should be supported by records in the AAAF.    Any record submitted for or enhanced for BIBCO must have a complete set of authority records in AAAF.   Serial catalogers must provide a series authority record for any analyzable serial when the title is first cataloged.


The authority control part of the copy cataloging routine is still under discussion. At this time, copy catalogers check controlled access points on non-AACR2r records against the AAAF; they check and establish series in the same way that the original catalogers do.


Locally input authority records support thesis author entries needing references and names or series that cannot be established authoritatively enough to meet NACO standards. The local file is not used solely to circumvent NACO or AACR2r requirements for controlled access points.


The outsourced part of the authority control program is currently done by OCLC. New services available from OCLC and local discussions about catalog maintenance and catalog management may lead to changes in what is done and who is responsible for it.


Catalog management and maintenance

The ABC Group, at the request of the TSRT and the TS Management Team, has developed   a proposal for altering the existing Catalog Management Unit. The TS Management Team is now working on staffing and implementation.


 


Cataloging decisions

The cataloging staff is responsible for the integrity of the bibliographic information in the Online Catalog and for the form and content of bibliographic and authority records. Catalogers often work closely with other library staff to provide useful records; suggestions are welcome. The cataloging staff makes the final decision on call numbers, access points, analytics, and treatment of continuing publications, seriality and other matters of this kind.


 


Cataloging priorities

Cataloging priorities in general will follow the Technical Services Priorities Policy. More specific information applicable to cataloging appears in The Manual, C36.


Other services

The cataloging staff is also responsible for a variety of other activities related to the organization and use of the collections, including:

  • providing the flags and routines used by selectors to indicate reference decisions, immediate needs, etc.

  • working in cooperation with other Technical Services units on pre-circ requests, materials tracking, and some aspects of serial control

  • answering questions and solving problems related to bibliographic access in the Online Catalog

  • consulting with selectors and special collections managers on policies and procedures particular to individual collections or formats

  • overseeing the end processing of monographic and special collections materials

  • providing cataloging and bibliographic control expertise on intra- and interdepartmental committees

  • maintaining documentation for the OCLC and RLIN databases

  • keeping abreast of current thinking and national trends in cataloging and related matters

  • planning and overseeing special projects involving bibliographic access and control
