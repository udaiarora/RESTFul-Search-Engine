September 21, 2006

Best of Broadway belt it out at ASU

Join a special ASU Herberger College of Fine Arts Lyric Opera Theatre benefit and experience one night of Broadway music by some of the biggest legends and stars ever to grace the Great White Way . Famed Broadway composer-lyricist Jerry Herman brings his ?Hello Jerry!? show to the Tempe campus at 7:30 p.m., Oct. 28, in the Evelyn Smith Music Theatre in the School of Music building.

In addition to the show, a free seminar and Q&A session with Herman and his cast members takes place two days before the performance.

?I am delighted to bring our musical theater program to ASU,? Herman says. ?Our program helps to pass on the tradition of the American musical to the next generation of musical theater creators and performers.?

Herman, from ?Hello Dolly!?, ?Mame? and ?La Cage Aux Folles? fame, is the only composer-lyricist ever to have had three musicals run more than 1,500 consecutive performances on Broadway.

?The talents of Jerry Herman and his fellow cast members not only bring a bit of Broadway to the desert, but also takes these energetic actors off the stage and makes them available to fans in a free, public seminar ? a special treat for anyone interested in musical theater,? says JoAnn Yeoman-Tongret, a lecturer in the School of Music.

The seminar and Q&A session about Broadway, musical theater and Herman's hits include Herman and fellow performers Jason Graae, Karen Morrow, Paige O'Hara, musical director Donald Pippin, and Michael Kerker from the American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers (ASCAP). The seminar takes place from 2-4 p.m., Oct. 26, in the Evelyn Smith Music Theatre.

Graae has performed numerous Broadway and Off-Broadway shows, including ?A Grand Night For Singing? and ?Stardust.? His solo show, ?Coup de Graae!? won the 2006 New York Nightlife Award.

Morrow has starred in several Broadway shows, including ?I Had A Ball,? ?The Grass Harp? and most recently in the national tour of ?Showboat.?

O'Hara, who is widely known as ?Belle? from Disney's ?Beauty and the Beast,? also has appeared in several Broadway shows, including ?Showboat? and ?Les Miserables.?

Pippin, from ?Cabaret? and ?La Cage Aux Folles? fame, is one of Broadway's most honored conductors and served 14 years as musical director of New York 's famed Radio City Music Hall .

?Hello Jerry!? is sponsored by the ASCAP Foundation/Jerry Herman Legacy Series.

Tickets for ?Hello Jerry!? are $15 for students, and $35 and up for the public. To buy tickets, call the Herberger College Box Office at (480) 965-6447.

For a comprehensive calendar of Herberger College events, visit the Web site (http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/calendar/calendar.php).
