Phoenix residents: Education keys quality of life

According to a report released Dec. 13 by ASU’s Morrison Institute for Public Policy, education and public safety remain most fundamental to quality of life in the greater Phoenix metropolitan area. These findings, among others, are found in “What Matters: The Maturing of Greater Phoenix.”

In addition, the report shows:

  • Quality of life in the greater Phoenix metro area is “excellent” or “good” to more than 60 percent of those surveyed.
  • Forty percent say they would leave the area if they could.
  • Two out of three say the greater Phoenix metro area is growing too fast.
  • Greater Phoenix is largely consistent on its core “quality of life” values.

“What Matters: The Maturing of Greater Phoenix” presents a wide variety of information on nine categories of personal and regional issues. The nine categories, in order of importance ranked by residents on a representative public opinion survey in 2004, are:

  • Education
  • Public safety and crime
  • Health and health care
  • Economy
  • Environment
  • Families and youth
  • Transportation and mobility
  • Community
  • Arts, culture and recreation

The Morrison Institute’s “quality of life” research was launched in 1997 with the publication of “What Matters in Greater Phoenix: Indicators of Our Quality of Life.” The report presented how residents perceived their quality of life, along with technical measurements of the same or similar items; editions in 1998 and 1999 provided updates.

Five years later, “What Matters: The Maturing of Greater Phoenix” describes a complex, fascinating place where quality of life is important and how best to enhance it is fast becoming the basis for action. The data show that the greater Phoenix metro area is putting down deeper roots, remaining consistent on its feelings about the issues of greatest importance, thinking about returns on past investments, and feeling a greater sense of community. This does not mean that quality of life is perfect or excellent for everyone. Rather, the report provides evidence that the region knows what it values and is increasingly willing to acknowledge and tackle its tough issues.

“What Matters: The Maturing of Greater Phoenix” presents scores of perceptions and indicators. Past reports were widely used by policy makers, civic and business leaders, community activists, and other residents to understand where we have been, where we are going, and what needs attention.

The report is available online at (www.morrisoninstitute.org).
