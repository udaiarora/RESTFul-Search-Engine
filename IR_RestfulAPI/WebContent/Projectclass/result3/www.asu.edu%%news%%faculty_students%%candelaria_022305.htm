February 23, 2005

Candelaria claims Outstanding Latina Cultural Award

Cordelia Candelaria, chair of ASU’s Chicana and Chicano Studies Department, has been honored with the 2005 Outstanding Latina Cultural Award in Literary Arts and Publications from the American Association of Higher Education.

This award is given to leaders working toward Latino education who have contributed significantly to the understanding of the Hispanic community and culture through literary arts, scholarship or publications.

Candelaria has contributed to the Hispanic community through various literary works, including publishing 15 books and serving as executive editor of the two volumes, Latino Encyclopedia of Popular Culture.

“Well, first I was utterly astonished, since I had no idea I was even being considered, and of course it was wonderful and truly humbling news to get,” Candelaria says.

Candelaria has been a professor at ASU since 1991 and chair of ASU’s Chicana and Chicano Studies Department since July 2001. She has received other awards, including being named Scholar of the Year by the National Association for Chicana and Chicano Studies in 2001.

Candelaria continues to work on more publications. She is finishing up an essay on the film “Amores Perros,” as well as continuing to revise her 1989 book, “Seeking the Perfect Game: Baseball and American Literature.”

Candelaria says she is motivated by a desire to help complete the record of American history or thought.

“It’s an error, and dangerous to accept partial or distorted portraits of who we are as a people defined by our glorious diversity,” Candelaria says. “The more we recognize this, the closer we come, I think, to our true community.”

Candelaria will be honored with this award on March 17, at the Atlanta Marriott Marquis Hotel in Atlanta.

For more information about the Chicana and Chicano Studies Department, visit the Web site (www.asu.edu/clas/chicana).
