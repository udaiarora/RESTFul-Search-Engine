E-Communiqué
Vol. 2.10, May 6, 2005

The E-Communiqué is published monthly with notices of upcoming deadlines, clinics and seminars, and other matters related to graduate education at ASU. Please visit the Graduate Calendar or the What's New page for information on events not included in this bulletin.

ASU Commencement

is 10 a.m. on Thursday, May 12, at the Wells Fargo Arena. Congratulations graduates! www.asu.edu/ssc/commence

Check out more of the latest DGS news at www.asu.edu/graduate/aboutus/news.

—REMINDERS—

May Commencement. Participating doctoral students should arrive by 9 a.m. and check-in with DGS volunteers at the west end of Wells Fargo Arena. Only doctoral students that have completed all their degree requirements (e.g., oral defense, going to the bookstore, internships, etc.) before May 12 can participate in the ceremony.

Graduate Grants-In-Aid. Funds are available to cover tuition for graduate students registering for 1st or 2nd summer session or both. Students must have a 2004-05 FAFSA and a Summer Aid application on file and have remaining "unmet need" in accordance with federal standards. Contact Catherine Lebihan, clebihan@asu.edu.

UGS Recipients. Please email names and ID numbers of your new 2005–06 UGS recipients to Catherine Lebihan, clebihan@asu.edu, no later than May 16.

2005–06 Graduate Tuition Fellowships (GTF). Academic units that have not submitted nominations for GTFs should do so as soon as possible. Email the completed form to psaldivar@asu.edu. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GTF

August 2005 Graduation. To be eligible to hold an oral defense in the summer, students need to register for one of the sessions, and do so before submitting their document for format review and scheduling the oral defense. Please note that the summer deadlines are compressed, and students do not have as much time between each step as they would in a regular semester. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns

Pass/Fail Forms. Please have these forms hand delivered to Student Academic Services (SAS) in Wilson Hall. They should not be sent through campus mail. Only original signatures are accepted on pass/fail forms and approval pages. Please do not stamp or white out any mistakes on these pages. If you encounter a problem with a student's paperwork, please call SAS at (480) 965-3521.

Oral Defenses. A student's entire committee must be present at the oral defense, regardless of how many people are on the committee. The DGS has specific Absent Committee Member Procedures if there will be an absent member from a defense. Video/teleconferenced defenses are invalid unless they have prior approval from the DGS. For questions, call (SAS) at (480) 965-3521.

Cancellation/Rescheduling of Oral Defenses.A student must contact SAS immediately if the student or committee must cancel or reschedule a defense. To reschedule, students must submit a new defense schedule form to SAS at least 5 working days in advance of the new defense date.



—ANNOUNCEMENTS—

New RA/TA Page in gPortal. Academic units may now enter their RA/TA data directly into gPortal at www.asu.edu/gportal. Access to E-GAD! is granted by the academic unit's gPortal "supervisor(s)." If you are in doubt about who has this authority in your unit, please see your chair, graduate director, or contact the DGS at grad-q@asu.edu.

Financial Guarantee Form for International Students. The form has been updated witht he base amounts for 2005–06 and is available at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms

Graduate Professional Development Summer Series. DGS is proud to present six professional development programs beginning June 15. More information will be available on the DGS website, www.asu.edu/graduate/professionaldevelopment, starting May 31.

Fall 2005 TA Orientation and Training. For the most current information, visit www.asu.edu/graduate/tara/TAorientation.html. All new TAs are required to attend the orientation as a condition of their employment. For questions, please call (480) 965-8968 or email gradPD@asu.edu.

Graduation Deadlines and Procedures. The 2006 Graduation Deadlines and Procedures and the 10 Working Day Calendar are now available. Those for 2005 have been revised for clarity. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns

Doctoral Comprehensive Exam Form. The Report of Doctoral Comprehensive Examinations and Approval of the Ph.D. Dissertation has been updated. www.asu.edu/graduate/forms

Graduate Support Staff Meetings. Meetings scheduled for Sept. 7, Oct. 5, and Nov. 2 have been replaced with the following four dates: Sept. 21, Oct. 19, Nov. 16, and Dec. 21. All are from 11:00 a.m. to noon in Computing Commons, Room 120.
