Services
The Adult Re-Entry Center

The Adult Re-Entry Center, located in the lower level of the Memorial Union, offers a welcoming environment for both new and continuing adult re-entry students. It is a place where you can relax, study, meet new friends, and get valuable information about the services available to you on campus.  We want to help you get back into college and stay in college until your goals are met!

Scholarship Opportunities

There are $1,500 tuition scholarships available for re-entry students through the Adult Re-Entry Center for undergraduate students who are Arizona residents, enrolled in at least 12 hours of coursework, maintain at least a 3.0 G.P.A. and have financial need. Click here for further information or to print the scholarship application.

Other scholarships are available through the ASU Scholarship Office. Other financial aid is available in the form of grants and loans from Student Financial Assistance.

University and Community Referral

Let us help you take advantage of the many resources available to you both on and off campus. The Adult Re-Entry Center is a clearinghouse of information. Numerous brochures, handouts, and resource materials are available, which can help you as you navigate through the university. In addition, our trained staff and volunteers can help you get the answers you need when you need them!

Pre-Enrollment Assistance

Pre-enrollment assistance allows students to speak with a trained staff member about the educational opportunities and how to get started on the road back to school. Click here to view our Unofficial Checklist.

Orientation Sessions

Our Orientation sessions are designed to especially meet the needs of adult re-entry students. We focus on regaining academic skills, readjusting to college life, and balancing multiple responsibilities. Orientation sessions are held before the start of each semester. If you would like to attend an orientation session, contact the Adult Re-Entry Center at 480.965.2252.

Student Organizations

ASU has many student organizations that may help new students feel a sense of community while pursuing their academic goals. To see the variety of organizations available, look at the Student Organization Resource Center (SORC) website. Of particular interest to Adult Re-Entry students is the Re-Entry Connection, a networking, discussion and support group for men and women undergraduate and graduate students. Check with the SORC or with our office for contact information for the current student leaders of the Re-Entry Connection.
