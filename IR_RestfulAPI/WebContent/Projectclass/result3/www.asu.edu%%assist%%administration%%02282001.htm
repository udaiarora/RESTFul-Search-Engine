Arizona State System for Information on Student Transfer (ASSIST)
Status Report as of February 28, 2001

Background
ASSIST (formerly known as the Transfer Student Data Warehouse or TSDW) is a relational database containing enrollment records from Arizona's universities and community colleges. The purpose of ASSIST is to provide community colleges and universities information for (1) completing the IPEDS Graduation Rate Survey and (2) assessing transfer articulation effectiveness in Arizona.

There are two committees involved in the ASSIST project. The Steering Committee is a policy group responsible to the Academic Program Articulation Steering Committee (APASC) for the ASSIST project. This committee makes decisions concerning the use and content of ASSIST. The second group is the Technical Committee. They are a working group responsible for providing quality data and developing standard reports from ASSIST. The Steering Committee meets semi-annually in Spring and Fall. The Technical Committee meets as often as needed.

Current Status

Establishment of the hardware environment to support ASSIST was completed with the implementation of a Brio web server, which will store official reports and launch queries for users with authenticated access. A demonstration highlighting the use of the web server was presented at the joint ASSIST Steering and Technical Committee meeting on October 11, 2000, followed by a similar presentation at the October 17th APASC meeting. Three hands-on training sessions were later held in January-February 2001 to provide community college and university representatives access to the new web server and to introduce them to revisions in the production version of ASSIST.

Since then, nondisclosure statements have been signed by twenty-five researchers from both the community colleges and universities and user accounts have been established for all trainees. Institutions with access may now link directly with the ASSIST database or may obtain a set of sample standard reports via an ASSIST web page www.asu.edu/assist/

Several changes in the ASSIST development team have taken place recently. In January 2001, John Porter left ASU after accepting an Associate Provost position at SUNY. Douglas Vinzant, ASU Director of Strategic Planning and Policy Analysis, assumed the role of ASSIST Project Leader. In addition, the ASSIST Principal Technical Analyst resigned in mid-January. A new search currently is underway for a replacement.

A report on the transition status of the ASSIST project was provided at the APASC meeting on February 17, 2001. A study session on ASSIST has been scheduled for the April APASC meeting to provide committee members an opportunity to more fully understand the scope of ASSIST and its ability to address issues related to transfer effectiveness.

Future Plans

The ASSIST Steering and Technical Committees will meet again on March 30, 2001. At this time, recommendations will be addressed to promote continued progress toward full implementation and utilization of ASSIST. In particular, the committees will be asked to consider the establishment of a working group to finalize the set of standard reports and queries that will be accessible through the web server and to address data quality and file submission issues.

Meanwhile, modifications and refinements to the ASSIST production model will continue to be made and additional training sessions will be considered on an as-needed basis. A data dictionary and other user support materials are being developed for ASSIST users. In addition, an e-mail support mechanism is under development to provide assistance to all ASSIST users as they begin developing their own queries and reports. Finally, institutions are encouraged to submit their files for the 1999-2000 reporting cycle.
