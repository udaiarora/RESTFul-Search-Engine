ACD 516: Employment of Alien Nonstudents

Purpose

To outline requirements for the hiring of aliens

Source

Immigration and Naturalization Service requirements

Applicability

Employment applicants who are not U.S. citizens

Policy

Visas Allowing Employment

To be considered for employment, alien nonstudents must have a visa status that allows employment. The most common visas are:

  1. immigrant status (permanent resident), evidenced by an Alien Registration Card or Form I-551

  2. temporary worker of distinguished merit and ability (H-1), evidenced by Form I-94 and I-171C

  3. exchange visitor (J-1), evidenced by Form I-94 and IAP-66

Note:H-1 and J-1 aliens must be under the sponsorship of ASU.

  1. former F-1 students who have permission to engage in practical training (as marked on their Form I-20 ID)

    Such students can hold full-time positions in their fields of study for a maximum of 12 months.

  2. former J-1 students who have permission to engage in practical training (as evidenced by a letter from the sponsor and valid IAP 66)

    Such students can hold full-time positions in their fields of study for a maximum of 18 months.

  3. immigrant status (temporary permanent resident), evidenced by an alien registration card or Form I-688

    and

  4. refugee or political asylee status.

Unlawful Discrimination

It is unlawful to discriminate against permanent resident aliens under the Immigration Reform and Control Act of 1986. The fact that an individual is a permanent resident and not a citizen of the U.S. cannot be considered in making a hiring decision. Courts have also determined that individuals who hold temporary permanent resident status or other legal status that will soon be converted to permanent resident status may also not be discriminated against in hiring decisions.

Proof of Authorization to Work

All alien nonstudents are required to show proof of authorization to work in the U.S. within three working days after the hire date. In the event an employee is unable to provide evidence of employment eligibility within the time limits required by law, the employee will not be continued. Employees will be required to submit evidence that expiration dates have been extended or that the employees are otherwise eligible to continue employment during the course of their employment.

Note:Employees who are not eligible to continue employment will be terminated.

Further information on the employment of alien nonstudents may be obtained through the Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University.
