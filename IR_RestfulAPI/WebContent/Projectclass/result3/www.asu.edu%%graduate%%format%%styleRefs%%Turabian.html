Turabian Style Guide Quick Reference

This quick reference should not be used as a style guide; it is only a reference tool to be used along with the most recent version of the Turabian style guide as indicated below.

Turabian, K. L. A Manual for Writers of Term Papers, Theses, and Dissertations. 6th ed. Chicago: The University of Chicago Press, 1996.
www.press.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/hfs.cgi/00/12917.ctl
Turabian's A Manual for Writers of Term Papers, Theses, and Dissertations must be followed for headings and subheadings, tables and figures, block quotations, in-text citations and references. However, regarding overall document considerations (i.e., margins, pagination, etc.) the Format Manual requirements supersede those of Turabian.

Headings/Subheadings
Guidelines provided for up to 5 levels:
  • 1st level: centered; initial caps; bold, ital. or UL.
  • 2nd level: centered; initial caps.
  • 3rd level: flush left; initial caps.; bold, ital. or UL.
  • 4th level: flush left; sentence case 4th level: flush left; sentence case.
  • 5th level: run-in heading; bold, ital. or UL; sentence case; period at end.
For less than 5, format in descending order. (pp. 11-12)

Block Quotations
Use for quotations of 8 or more lines of text; indent 4 spaces from left; single space; new paragraphs in block quotations should be indented an extra 4 spaces. (pp. 83-85)

In-text Citation (Where Author Mentioned in Sentence)
Cite the publication date in parentheses directly following the author's name wherever it appears in the sentence. (pp. 175-179)

In-text Citation (Where Author Name Not Mentioned in Sentence)
Cite the author's last name, followed by the publication date, all in parentheses. (pp. 175-179)

Figures
Label all types of illustrations (i.e., maps, charts, graphs, diagrams, etc.) as figures (fig. or figure) below the images and number them consecutively using Arabic numerals. A title/caption and/or legend (a brief explanation of a figure) should follow the figure number. Long figure legends can run the width of the figure, and short legends are centered. (p. 107)

Tables
All tables should appear as close to corresponding text as possible. There are two options given for titles/captions: (1) flush left with "Table" heading and Arabic numeral; center the last, shortest line of the title or (2) center "TABLE" heading (in all caps) and Arabic numeral, followed by a double space and the title/caption centered and in all caps. (pp. 87-103)

References (General/Spacing)
  • Bibliography: Hanging indent; single-spaced with double spacing between entries; arranged alphabetically. (pp. 165-174)

  • Reference List: Hanging indent; single-spaced with double spacing between entries; arranged alphabetically. (p. 175-184)

References (Books and Book Articles)
  • Bibliography: Full name of author in indirect order, followed by a period; title in italics and title cased, followed by a period and the publication information, which includes city, followed by a colon, the publisher, a comma and the publication date. (pp. 187-202)

  • Reference List: Author-date style; full name of author in indirect order, followed by a period and the publication date; title in italics (cap. first word only of both title and subtitle as well as proper nouns), followed by a period and the publication information, which includes city, followed by a colon and the publisher. (pp. 187-202)

References (Journal Articles)
  • Bibliography: Full name of author in indirect order, followed by a period; title of article in title case and quotation marks, followed by a period; journal title in italics and title cased, followed by volume, date in parentheses, colon, and page numbers. (pp. 202-203)

  • Reference List: Author-date style; full name of author in indirect order, followed by a period and the publication date, followed by a period; article title in sentence case w/out quotation marks, followed by a period; journal title in italics, followed by volume, date in parentheses, colon, and page numbers. (pp. 202-203)
