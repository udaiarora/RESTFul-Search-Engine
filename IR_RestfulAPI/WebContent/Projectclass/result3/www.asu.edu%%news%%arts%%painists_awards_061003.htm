June 10, 2003

ASU pianists receive Memorial Award in arts

Pianists Margarita Denenburg and Mark Fuksman were presented the Joan Frazer Memorial Award in the Arts by Hillel at ASU. In the spring 2004, the award winners will showcase their artistic projects in American premiers of works by two Jewish composers.

Denenburg will explore Gideon Klein's 1943 sonata for piano, which was composed during his years in the concentration camp Terezin and later discovered after he perished.

"The sadness, horror and sense of taken lives have been felt in his piano sonata, which contains three movements, each with a different meaning," Deneburg says.
She will perform this sonata and share her research on the composer and his music written during the Holocaust.

Denenburg, a Russian born Israeli, is a sophomore attending ASU on a full scholarship from the ASU School of Music. She is pursuing a degree in piano performance and is under the mentorship of Assistant Professor of Piano, Baruch Meir for this project.
Fuksman will bring to light selected sonatas by Russian-Jewish composer Samuel Feinberg, whose work was neglected during the Communist regime.

Fuksman says, "Feinberg had a tremendous impact on various realms of musical culture and art in Russia. He was a formidable pianist, teacher and writer of music essays, but was virtually unknown as a composer."

Feinberg's 12 sonatas were published in limited quantity and are scarce. With the exception of the Piano Concerto and some transcriptions, by the composer himself, none of Feinberg's compositions have been recorded.

"After listening to the concerts and studying his other scores," Fuksman says. "I was struck by the depth of his emotional and intellectual power. As Feinberg was an astounding pianist and virtuoso, he projected his possession of piano technique into his compositions, which demand transcendental virtuosity."

Fuksman is a doctoral candidate of musical arts in piano performance. Robert Hamilton, music professor, says, "Mark is truly a treasure. He is one of the very rare students we find with all of the pieces in place for a significant concert career."

The Joan Frazer Memorial Award in the Arts at Hillel was created in the memory of Joan Frazer who loved the arts and was deeply committed to Hillel. Community members interested in attending the musical performance in the spring of 2004 may call Hillel to receive an invitation. ASU students interested in applying for the award for the 2004 - 05 school year can pick-up a brochure from Hillel, the Jewish Student Center, located at 1012 S. Mill Ave., in Tempe.

Shapiro, with Hillel, can be reached (480) 967-7563 or (linda@hillelasu.org).
