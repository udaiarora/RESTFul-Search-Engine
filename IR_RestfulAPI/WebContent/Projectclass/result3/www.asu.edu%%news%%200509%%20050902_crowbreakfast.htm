Crow: Faculty talent attracts record numbers of students

University president outlines challenges brought about by growth

The talent and energy of ASU’s faculty have attracted a record number of students this fall, including an estimated freshman class of more than 8,000, ASU President Michael Crow told faculty at the Fall 2005 Academic Assembly Breakfast this week.

Scholarship numbers are up, and more ASU freshmen are in the top 5 percent of their high school classes than ever before. Significantly, 50 percent more students also come from families with incomes below the poverty line, thanks to the increase in financial aid.

Freshmen retention and graduation rates have broken records, and about 350 faculty members have been hired this year, including 175 since last spring.

But the growth brings challenges, especially in a climate in which the university is asked to provide four-year degrees in the cheapest way possible while increasing educational quality and student success.

Crow said ASU’s out-of-state enrollment is up this fall, despite a substantial tuition increase for nonresidents. He also said this fall’s freshman class has more top scholars from Arizona than ever before.

ASU spent $183 million in research dollars, a 17 percent increase over the previous year, with a projected 20 percent increase this year.

Noting the number of inquiries his office has received regarding parking issues, Crow said he is launching a parking task force to deal with the immediate needs. He also indicated that a decision on merit raises will be made in the next few weeks, depending on the university’s financial stability and enrollment numbers.

He outlined three significant campaigns that will begin soon to increase funding and public awareness of the university, and said construction will begin soon on a new bioinformatics building in downtown Phoenix.
