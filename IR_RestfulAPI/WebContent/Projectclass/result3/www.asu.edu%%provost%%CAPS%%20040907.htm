Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

September 7, 2004

Members Present:  Barbara Trapido-Lurie (Chair), Beth Luey, Bill Sharp, Dan Stanton, and Victoria Trotta

 

Excused:  Elizabeth McNeil

 

Committee Organization

Barbara Trapido-Lurie distributed the committee?s annual report for the academic year 2003/04 and the committee?s bylaws. Volunteers were sought for committee offices, and the following appointments were made: Beth Luey, secretary; Dan Stanton, archivist/webmaster; and Victoria Trotta, PAC-20 representative, with Bill Sharp as backup.

 

Old business

Barbara Trapido-Lurie reported that the task forces on Academic Professionals and Advising had both posted their recommendations and that the period for comments for both has ended. The provost will now decide on any changes to the recommendations, and his decisions will go forward to special committees that will draft the text for the ACD manual.

 

Barbara Trapido-Lurie summarized the meetings she has attended of the PAC-20 and the Academic Senate, and Victoria Trotta raised the question of the role of academic professionals in the Academic Senate. The president of the Academic Senate has encouraged all members to become more active and suggested a presentation about academic professionals. The committee agreed that this might be appropriate when the ACD text goes to the Senate for consideration.

 

New business

The committee discussed its role in the drafting of the ACD text and the possibilities for spotlight events. In this connection, the committee asked Barbara Trapido-Lurie to discuss with Nancy Gutierrez: (a) her attendance at the October meeting; (b) committee participation in the drafting process; and (c) the appearance of someone from the provost?s office as the fall spotlight speaker.

Meeting adjourned at 10:45 am.

Submitted by Beth Luey
Approved 9/9/04
