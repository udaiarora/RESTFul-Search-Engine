ASU West plans living, learning community at Las Casas

Las Casas, ASU West's first residential facility, is set to open in August, featuring ASU West classes, workshops, interactive learning sessions, tutoring, academic advising and counseling all offered on-site.

In addition, special affinity groups will be formed for students interested in topics such as the arts, technology, ecology and leadership. As an academic resource for students, two faculty members and an academic adviser will live in Las Casas.

"We are strongly committed to making the term 'living-learning community' much more than simply a slogan," says Elaine Maimon, ASU vice president and West Campus provost. "Bringing faculty into Las Casas will draw students into an intellectual community and help our students establish lasting relationships with professors and fellow students."

The two resident "faculty fellows" will serve as advisers for special interest groups, teach one or more ASU West classes in the residence hall and participate in academic and social enrichment programs. Ten additional ASU West professors will serve as faculty mentors, working with the Faculty Fellows on enrichment programs and teaching classes on site.

Las Casas residents also will have the opportunity to participate in workshops focusing on topics such as conflict management, relationship building, life planning, stress reduction and time management.

"Having professors, tutors and advisers available in the residence hall will help students living in Las Casas adjust quickly to the academic and social demands of university life," says ASU West sophomore Kelsie Marcum, who served on the task force that developed plans for Las Casas programming. The task force included faculty, staff and student representatives.

Studies have shown that students living on campus enjoy better academic success, report a greater level of satisfaction with their university experience and are less likely to drop out than those living off campus.

"Creating a living-learning community in Las Casas is a logical extension of the learning community concept we have implemented for freshman students at ASU West," says Emily Cutrer, dean of the College of Arts & Sciences. "Learning communities help students see connections among disciplines while giving them an academic support system. These principles will be extended to residents of Las Casas."

The 400-bed Las Casas facility is under construction at the ASU West campus. Fully furnished, apartment-style units will feature full kitchens and free cable and local phone service. A community center will include a standard classroom and computer classroom as well as a gathering area for interest group and social meetings. Las Casas amenities also include an outdoor swimming pool.

"The addition of student housing is another sign of ASU West's continuing evolution as a full-service university campus," says Diane McCarthy, president of WESTMARC. "The entire region will benefit from ASU West's emergence as an intellectual, cultural and economic anchor for the West Valley."

Applications are now being taken for students interested in joining the inaugural group of Las Casas residents. The facility will open in August, in time for the start of the Fall 2003 semester at ASU West. For information, call (602) 543-2272.
