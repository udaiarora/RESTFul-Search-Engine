Web Access Management Goes Live

The ASU Library is on the brink of taking a giant step forward, one which will improve patron access to electronic resources from off-campus. On December 20-24, LIST will be modifying the authentication and authorization procedures used by off-campus users to gain access to the electronic journals, indexes, abstracts and databases available through the library web interface. The ASURITE authentication method will be replaced by the more familiar library PIN method used by patrons of the online catalog to check their library accounts ("My Account"). Authorization will be provided by the library web access management (WAM) proxy interface.

Many pages on the library?s site will need to have links updated to send users through the proxy server for off-campus access, so LIST is contacting all departmental pagemasters and ERRs to let them know what needs to be done. Old links will continue to work for 30 days after WAM goes into production, to allow time for the changes to be made. The Help page is also being updated to inform users of the new authentication process for off-campus access to electronic resources. Problems with WAM or access to electronic resources should be reported to SOS by calling 5-9093 or filling out a ticket at http://sos.lib.asu.edu.

Instruction Librarian Yuwu Song will be presenting a workshop on Wednesday, December 18th at 9:30 a.m. in Hayden C-6 to help familiarize Library staff with the new proxy service. In particular, reference librarians, instruction librarians, and staff members with instructional/troubleshooting responsibilities can benefit from attending.


Communication Survey

LIST thanks the 34 Library employees who responded to the communication survey. A majority of the respondents indicated that they use the web resources provided by LIST (the LIST website, SOS Website, and LIST Newsletter) and find relevant, useful information there. The majority of the respondents indicated they know who their ERR representative is and rely on their ERR to relay appropriate information. There were also some constructive comments and questions, as well as some compliments.

In response to survey comments, LIST will try to give more context to the announcements sent out via the ERR distribution list, by being more specific about how each change, outage, problem, or development affects users. Also, the survey results indicated concerns about getting better information regarding problems with the network in general, so we have developed a resource list for whom to contact and where to get information on network health (see below).

The majority of respondents want to continue ERR meetings on a quarterly basis. The next ERR meeting will be held January 16, 2003 in Hayden C6 from 10:30 to 11:30 AM. The following meetings will be held the 3rd Thursdays of April, July and October of 2003 from 10:30 to 11:30 AM in Hayden Room C6.
