2. What is the policy for rollerblading or skateboarding on campus?

Rollerblading and skateboarding are not permitted on campus. Violators may be stopped and issued a criminal citation [ARS 13-2911] and/or have their rollerblades or skateboards confiscated.

3. Is tailgating allowed at university events?
Can I take my own food or drink into the stadium?

The Arizona Board of Regents authorizes the president to permit the consumption of alcohol at designated times and in designated areas of the university when football games are played. Unless otherwise designated by the president consumption of alcohol at authorized tailgate parties is limited to beer and does not include spirituous liquor, wine, or other alcohol beverages.  Alcohol may be consumed for a period of three and one-half hours prior to each football game and cease at kick off.

Approved locations for tailgating are:  Parking Structures 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6; Parking Lots 3, 8, 26, 27, 40, 42, 44, 49, 51, 52, 54, 55, 58, 59, 59N, 59NE, 70, 82; and, Rio Salado.

No personal food or drink can be brought into the stadium with the exception of purchased, sealed, unfrozen water bottles.

4. How do I obtain an order of protection or restraining order against a person?

A person may file a petition with a magistrate, justice of the peace or superior court judge for an order of protection or restraining order. The petition shall state the name of the plaintiff, name and address, if known, of the defendant, specific statements of alleged incidents, relationship between the parties, and desired relief. A filing fee may be required.

The court shall review the petition and any evidence offered by the plaintiff to determine if the order should be issued without further hearing.

To obtain additional information or the forms necessary to file a petition please contact the Tempe Justice Court.

9. Where can I go for medical treatment?

For medical emergencies, call 9-1-1

For nonemergency medical treatment during the week, students may go to the Student Health Center on campus.  Please check with the Student Health Center at 965-3346 for hours and services provided.

For nonemergency medical treatment after 5:00 p.m. or on weekends, students must refer to their private health insurance plan for the closest provider.
