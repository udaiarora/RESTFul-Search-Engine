RSP 605–03: Investigator Incentive Awards

Purpose

To provide funds to principal investigators who gain external support for their research, service, and training activities by establishing a discretionary pool to cover certain expenses associated with these activities and to develop additional programming

Source

Office of the Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs

Eligibility

Principal investigators (PIs) whose externally funded awards generate facilities & administrative cost recovery

Policy

Amount

The amount initially awarded for an Investigator Incentive Award (IIA) equals five percent of the expected facilities & administrative (F&A) cost to be recovered.

The IIA funds are allocated shortly after an award is received.

Notification

IIA amounts are included in the Sponsored Project Award Notice. The budget officer for the vice president for research and economic affairs will prepare a memorandum with specific instructions on how to access the funds

Limitations

An IIA award is not made if the amount to be awarded is less than $100. In cases where an IIA is split between co-investigators and it results in less than $100 for any co-investigator, the lead investigator will receive the full IIA. There is no upper limit.
