September 12, 2003

August resale market slows; year-to-date numbers remain high

The Greater Phoenix resale housing market slackened in August, affected by the same wartime anxieties and economic concerns that plagued the rest of the nation since early 2003. Recorded sales peaked at 7,890 homes in June, slowed in July and again in August, posting 6,055 sales for the month. Despite the slowdown, this activity was ahead of last August's 4,940 recorded sales, so the year-to-date total of 47,255 homes is well ahead of last year's 41,130 homes

Jay Q. Butler, director of the Arizona Real Estate Center, said analysts will be closely watching the housing market's performance through the end of the year. "The strength of 2002 record year was due to the last four months recording activity well above the typical pattern for the latter months of the year," Butler noted. "The question now is whether the current housing market will rebound from its current slowing trend."

The median home price was a record $160,560, in comparison with $160,000 for the previous month and $145,900 a year ago. The Greater Phoenix area is so large, the median price can range significantly from $90,000 in the central western areas of the city of Phoenix to $378,750 in North Scottsdale. In August, 50 percent of all recorded sales were for homes priced from $100,000 to $175,000, and 15 percent for homes priced over $300,000.

"In addition to concerns about the continuing weak economy and events in the Middle East, mortgage interest rates have jumped from 5.0 percent in June to 6.0 percent," Butler said. "But people continue to have a strong confidence in their live-in investment. Now that we are seeing the glimmer of a recovery, high mortgage rates combined with higher home prices could begin to erode the affordability of peoples' housing dreams."

The 1,105 resale townhouse/condominium housing transactions also represent a slowing of market activity, but were better than last year's 965 recorded transactions. The year-to-date activity stands at 9,610 units, up from 8,850 homes for YTD 2002. The median price was down slightly from $105,000 in July to $103,500, but was well above last year's $95,000. Since this sector is a good source of affordable housing, the strong increase in median price could adversely impact the market activity.

The median square footage for a single-family home recorded sold in August was 1,715, which is larger than the 1,655 square feet reported a year ago. In the townhouse/condominium sector, the median square footage was 1,140, which is smaller than the 1,150 square feet reported a year ago.

· In comparison with August 2002, recorded sales in the city of Phoenix rose from 1,435 to 1,600 sales, while the median sales price increased to $132,000 from $119,900. Since Phoenix is a geographically large city, the median prices can range significantly ($75,000 in the Sky Harbor area to $195,000 in the Union Hills area). The townhouse/condominium sector increased slightly from 265 to 270 sales, while the median price rose from $85,000 to $87,000.

· The Scottsdale resale home market grew from 505 to 650 recorded sales. The median sales price rose from $290,000 to $334,000. The median resale home price is $378,750 in North Scottsdale and $172,000 in South Scottsdale. The townhouse/condominium sector in Scottsdale also improved from 210 to 230 sales, while the median sales price increased from $140,000 to $142,000.

· The Mesa resale housing market continued to improve from 590 to 775 sales, while the median price rose from $133,900 to $147,000. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 130 to 170 sales, while the median home price increased from $89,500 to $92,250.

· Glendale moved from 475 to 515 recorded sales and the median sales price increased from $140,000 to $147,500.The townhouse/condominium sector remained at 40 sales, while the median sales price rose from $76,300 to $79,000.

· In comparison with a year ago, the Sun City resale market increased from 95 to 110 sales, with the median sales price jumping from $95,500 to $117. Resale activity in Sun City West declined from 75 to 70 recorded sales and the median sales price decreased from $144,000 to $137,500. The townhouse/condominium market in Sun City stayed at 70 sales, but the median sales price increased from $72,900 to $77,000. In Sun City West, sales remained at 20 sales, but the median sales price decreased from $96,000 to $92,500.

· The resale market in Gilbert grew from 285 to 430 sales, while the median sales price rose from $165,000 to $180,000. The townhouse/condominium market moved slightly from 15 to 20 sales, but the median sales price decreased from $117,500 to $111,000.

· For the city of Chandler, the resale market improved from 380 to 525 recorded sales, while the median sales price increased from $152,000 to $163,500. The townhouse/condominium market improved from 50 to 75 sales and the median sales price rose from $102,000 to $110,000.

· The resale market in Tempe moved from 170 to 205 sales, with the median sales price increasing to $169,450 from $156,000. The townhouse/condominium sector was stable at 95 sales, while the median sales price increased from $95,000 to $109,500.

· Highest median sales price: Paradise Valley at $765,000, with a median square footage of 3,255.

· In the West Valley, the following communities represent 6 percent of the resale market, up from 5 percent a year ago.
o Avondale improved from 80 to 100 sales, with median price rising from $139,950 to $154,000.
o El Mirage grew from 20 sales ($105,500) to 30 sales ($122,500).
o Goodyear moved from 60 to 70 sales, ( $150,210 to $162,000).
o Surprise expanded from 95 sales ($125,000) to 155 sales ($141,000).
