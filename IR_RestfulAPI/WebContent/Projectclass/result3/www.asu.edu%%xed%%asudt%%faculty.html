Copying

A copy machine is available in the student lounge of Building C and on the second floor of Building A. An additional copier (for faculty only) is located in the staff workroom (Building C, second floor). The access code necessary to use this machine is given to you on the first day of class. With prior approval from your college, you may choose to have your copying done elsewhere. You will be reimbursed when a receipt is provided. If materials are to be copied by staff, please submit them by 6 p.m., one week in advance. Typically, 500 copies per semester will accommodate most instructors' needs. However, if you expect to exceed this quantity please contact the receptionist.

 
Emergency Evacuation Procedures
ASU has a strong commitment to the safety and welfare of its employees, students and visitors. Since it is impossible to predict what types of situations may develop into an emergency, these instructions are intended to provide guidance in evacuating the building.

If the alarm sounds:
1. Stop what you are doing.
2. Assist anyone needing help exiting.
3. Leave the building using the nearest and safest exit, preferably the end stairwells, which empty onto the sidewalk outside of the building. The center stairwell empties into the lobby, the point at which emergency personnel would be entering. Do not use elevators.
4. Do not reenter the building if the alarm stops; wait until you have received an "all clear" signal from a supervisory authority or the fire department.

Any emergency warrants a call to 911, even if you think another person has called. Specify whether you need police, medical or fire assistance.

When calling 911, have the following information available:
1. Name and address:

Arizona State University
Mercado
502 E. Monroe
Phoenix, AZ 85004-4442

2. Building name (ie. Building A, B, C), floor and room number (if applicable).
3. Type of emergency, i.e., injury, fire, gas odor, etc.

If time permits during evacuation, secure your area and take personal items with you. If fire conditions exist, evacuate immediately, leaving personal belongings behind.

Disability Resources for Students

All ASU faculty and teaching staff need working knowledge of the legal rights of qualified individuals with disabilities and of the university's rights and responsibility to provide reasonable and effective accommodations. ASU is mandated by the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Section 504 and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990 to make facilities, educational and co-curricular programs, campus activities and employment opportunities accessible to qualified individuals with disabilities. For more information and to receive a guide for faculty and other teaching staff, contact Disability Resources for Students (DRS) at (480) 965-1234/voice, (480) 965-9000/TTY or visit the DRS Web site.

The Mercado staff will coordinate with Disability Resources to best accommodate the need of the student or faculty member.

At the first class meeting, Mercado students will receive a brochure about general Mercado information, including parking. Staff members will also be available throughout the facility to answer questions. Look for the "ASK ME" buttons.

Faculty may park for free in the Heritage and Science Park garage located on Fifth and Monroe Streets, but must still obtain a parking keycard from the Mercado.

Free street parking is available after 5 p.m. If a class guest(s) requires parking accommodations, Mercado faculty should notify the receptionist, located in Building C.

Secretary

A secretary is available from 2 p.m. to 6 p.m., Monday – Friday for copying and other clerical functions.

 
Security Guards

ASU Security Guards are stationed on the first floor in Building C and on the second floor of Building A after 6 p.m., Monday – Thursday. Textbook Ordering

Book orders should be channeled through the coordinator in your academic department and submitted to ASU at the Tempe campus Bookstore. Accurate ordering information and enrollment projections are vital. Textbook requests are due to the bookstore on or before the following dates:      

     Fall semester: April 1
     Spring semester: October 1
     Summer sessions: March 1

Required supplies or material orders need to be submitted with your textbook request. The bookstore's supply department will procure the required supplies.

 
Travel Reimbursements

Instructors may be reimbursed for mileage when teaching courses off campus. Travel must be approved in advance by Academic and Professional Programs. Please obtain the proper form for claiming reimbursement from your academic department. Approved, completed travel request forms must be received in the Academic and Professional Programs office no later than two weeks after the end of the semester.
