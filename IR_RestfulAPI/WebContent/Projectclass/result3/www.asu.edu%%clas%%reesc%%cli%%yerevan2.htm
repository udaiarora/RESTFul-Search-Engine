Study Abroad at
Yerevan State University

Where

Located in the heart of the Caucasus region, with a stunning view of the majestic Mount Ararat, Yerevan is the capital and largest city of the Republic of Armenia. One of the oldest cities in the world, with a population of approximately one million, Yerevan is dotted with ancient churches, monuments, restaurants, cafes, and other recreational facilities.
When

A semester or year-long program, September 1 - December 15 and/or January 1 - May 15
The University

Yerevan State University was founded May 16, 1919. There are over 110 departments. The university has approximately 9,500 undergraduates, including 300 students from abroad. Instruction is in Armenian, but instruction is available in Russian and English.
The Program

Students take 8 credits per semester of intensive beginning, intermediate, or advanced Armenian language training. The program features 6 credits per semester in history and in Armenian politics and society. Beginning with basic in-country "survival" techniques and leading up to contemporary issues in political and cultural life, the coursework features aspects of Armenian history and culture, as well as analysis of contemporary institutions.
Eligibility Requirements

The program is open to all upper division and graduate students who have maintained a 2.75 GPA, although those students with a GPA between 2.5 and 2.75 can be given conditional acceptance. Non-ASU students are welcome to apply.
Estimated Cost

Total costs, including tuition, room and board, travel, and personal expenses, are estimated to be $3,300 per semester for tuition and fees and $800 per semester for home stay which includes room, breakfast, and dinner. Additional expenses will include passport, visa, insurance, and personal expenses. Students who are classified as non-Arizona residents for tuition purposes are subject to a surcharge of approximately $1,100 per semester. Transportation to and from Yerevan is not included. In addition to the one or two semester program, students should plan to participate in the Critical Languages Institute Armenian summer language study at ASU, or an equivalent language study program, prior to attending Yerevan State University.
ASU Credit

During the semester abroad, students are registered at ASU and qualify for up to 14 hours per semester of ASU residence credit. Upon completion of the semester, the ASU course credit and grade equivalency of each course taken in Yerevan will be assigned to the student's ASU transcript.
Application Process

Interested students should fill out an International Program Qualifying Application Packet, which is available through REESC or at the ASU Office of International Programs. Applicants must meet the 2.75 GPA eligibility requirement set by ASU. Application packets are due March 1st for the fall semester program and October 1st for the spring semester program. A $350 non-refundable deposit is due at the time of application.
Financial Aid

Most ASU scholarships, grants, and loans can be used to help pay program costs. Students should consult with financial aid counselors. The International Programs Office has scholarships available, on a competitive basis, for students registered for study abroad or exchange programs. Students applying should consider joint application to the National Security Education Program.
