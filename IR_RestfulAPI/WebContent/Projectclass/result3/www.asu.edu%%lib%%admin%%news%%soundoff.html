"We Love ASU Collections" Open House Feb. 16

Arizona State University's museums, galleries and collections are just like chocolates, you can't sample just one.

For that reason, the Museums, Galleries and Collections Committee is sponsoring its fifth open house, "We Love ASU Collections" on Friday, Feb. 16, from 9:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. on the Tempe campus.

All of the more than two dozen museums, galleries and collections will be open for visitors that day, and there will be special tours of 12 of them, spaced 30 minutes apart so guests can pick and choose from the menu of delights.

The open house starting point will be Old Main, where maps and information will be available. A table also will be set up in front of the Memorial Union. Those participating will be asked to fill out an evaluation form, and from those forms, winners will be drawn from prizes donated by the museums, galleries and collections.

The ASU Carillon Society will present a live concert at noon, and visitors are invited to come to the Zuni Room (208A) in the Memorial Union to see the carillon.

The tour schedule is:

9:30 a.m., Luhrs Gallery
10 a.m., Arizona Historical Foundation
10:30 a.m., Space Photography Lab
11 a.m., Archaeological Research Institute
11:30 a.m., Mars Space Flight Facility
12 noon, Museum of Geology
12:30 p.m., Center for Meteorites Studies
1 p.m., Northlight Gallery
1:30 p.m., Gallery 100/Step Gallery
2 p.m., Harry Wood Gallery
2:30 p.m., Old Main
3 p.m., Art Museum

The Museums, Galleries and Collections Committee is an interdisciplinary group representing nearly a dozen colleges, schools and other entities at ASU.

The tour is sponsored by Arizona State Credit Union and the ASU Alumni Association.

For more information, call Penny Borszich, co-chair, at (480) 965-5185, or go to www.asu.edu/museums.

For day-of-tour information, call ASU Media Relations, (480) 965-3205.

See full schedule, with locations and parking information on our Web site!

Less than one month to FORO! In order for this important international conference to be a success, we need everyone from ASU Libraries to help! Volunteers are needed both before and during the FORO conference. Current volunteer opportunities include:

Prior to the Conference

  • Local Information: Volunteers are needed to gather brochures from valley shopping malls, and potentially other sources. Contact: Linda Shackle

  • Information Bags: Three volunteers are needed to collate various information brochures and place in conference tote bags. Contact: Jennie Duvernay

During the Conference

  • Registration desk: Detail oriented volunteers are needed to work 60 minute registration desk shifts each day: Tuesday (noon-8pm), Wednesday (8am-5pm), Thursday (8am-5pm) and Friday (8am-10:30am). Contact: Linda Cravens

You may also volunteer for more than one session if interested. Contact: Mimmo Bonanni

  • Sign Distribution: One volunteer each day of the conference to distribute appropriate signage for all conference rooms. Contact: Jennie Duvernay

ASU Libraries? New Online Resources Survey

In preparation for the implementation of the Electronic Resources Management System (ERM), the ERM Marketing Subcommittee is gathering data on faculty, staff and student preferences regarding the Libraries? new online resources. Specifically, this survey is designed to help us determine the most effective methods of alerting our users when new electronic resources are acquired by the Libraries.

We would like all library staff to complete the survey and ask that you encourage faculty and any students you encounter at service points or instruction sessions to take the survey too. Thank you!

A link to the survey will be available on each Library?s home page. It will run from Jan 29 through Feb 16.



--Julie Tharp
ERM Marketing Subcommittee

Tech Talk Interest Group

The MCLC Tech Talk interest group will be meeting once a month to talk about technology and its impact on libraries. Topics range from Web 2.0 to Library OPACs, gaming, federated searching, and network and bandwidth issues, among other things. The Tech Talk interest group will also recommend programs for the MCLS Continuing Education Committee.

To help facilitate this, Tech Talk has started a blog at http://mclctechtalk.wordpress.com/. If you're interested, please feel free to comment or join the group!

Spanish Outreach Libraries:
Reach out to your library?s Spanish language community

Through a partnership with WebJunction's Spanish Language Outreach Program, library staff will gain knowledge and resources to reach out to Spanish speakers in their communities and increase access to technology. By attending this full day workshop you will learn how the library can best meet the needs of the Spanish-speaking community, ultimately identifying ways in which library services and technology can improve their lives. Workshops will be held locally at the Tempe Public Library, the Phoenix Public Library - Palo Verde Branch, the Scottsdale Civic Center Library, and the Chandler Public Library. For specific dates and times, please see the online registration site.

What will you learn?

  • skills and techniques to increase awareness and use of local library services to your Spanish-speaking community

  • methods to increase partnerships between your library and community organizations that serve Spanish speaking residents

  • awareness of cultural and diversity issues

SOL Lead Trainers: Sol Gomez (Tucson), Sarah Kaufman (Tempe), Frances Murrietta (Yuma), Bessie Sopeland (Payson), Francisco Vargas (Phoenix) All workshops are delivered in English and are FREE! Lunch as well as morning and afternoon snacks will be provided. Transportation expenses may be available. Workshops are managed by the Arizona State Library as a joint project with WebJunction, funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.



--Jennifer Duvernay
Instruction, Outreach and Marketing

Special Super Bowl Edition

It marks the 40th anniversary of the first AFL-NFL championship game, in which the Green Bay Packers defeated the Kansas City Chiefs in Los Angeles. To commemorate this occasion, the Census Bureau has compiled a collection of facts examining how life in the United States has changed since Super Bowl I. Recruitment

When applying for any position at ASU, please address work experience as it relates to each required and preferred qualification, and state complete employment dates (month/year). The Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) cannot give credit for qualifications not clearly documented on the resume.

Also, it is a good idea to (1) ask the person(s) you want to use as a reference for permission first; and (2) remember, when interviewing as an internal candidate, the SAC scores on answers to the questions not on what they think or assume you know.
