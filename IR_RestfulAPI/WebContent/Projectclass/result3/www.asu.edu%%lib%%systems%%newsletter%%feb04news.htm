Virus Alert

A couple of new viruses are on the loose in cyberspace again. Identified as W32/Bagle.b@MM and W32/Netsky.b@MM, both are propagated through email attachments and the “From:” address is spoofed, so don't be surprised if you receive failed delivery notifications indicating that you sent an infected message but you didn't send it. If so, ignore it, delete it, and get on with your life. And again, the standard warning: never, NEVER, EVER open an attachment without 100% certainty that it is safe. It's the only way your computer can get infected with either of these viruses, even if your antivirus software doesn't have the latest .dat file.

If you want to manually update your antivirus software rather that waiting for the automatic update to take place, here's how:

If you have VirusScan 4.5.x, double-click on the VirusScan Console (looks like a magnifying glass in lower right of screen), double-click Auto Upgrade, then click on Run Now.

If you have VirusScan v.7, right-click on the shield icon in the lower right and go to Update Now.


Catalog Adds Link to Center for Research Libraries

We have added an additional feature to the online catalog pages. Users now have the opportunity of repeating a catalog search in the Center for Research Libraries database. The button for this function appears after the NAU and U of A buttons at the top of the catalog search results page.


New Databases Available

These new databases have been put online (http://www.asu.edu/lib/top-menus/new.html):

Economist Intelligence Unit (Subscribed)
Provides data and forecasts about political, economic and business climates of 200 countries including Country Report, Country Profile, Country Commerce, Country Finance, etc.

Eighteenth Century Collections Online (Subscribed)
Eighteenth Century Collections Online provides access to the digital images of every page of more than 150,000 books published during the 18th Century. With full-text searching of approximately 33 million pages, the product allows researchers new methods of access to critical information in the fields of history, literature, religion, law, fine arts, science and more.

ACM Guide to Computing Literature (Subscribed)
The Guide is a collection of bibliographic citations and abstracts of works published by ACM and other publishers. Citations for all ACM published works are included in the Guide as a small subset of the total bibliographic space. It contains more than 750,000 citations from 3,000+ publishers, covering books, journal articles, conference proceedings, doctoral dissertations, master's theses, and technical reports.
