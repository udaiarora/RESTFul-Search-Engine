PFF Capstone Fair
Twenty-five Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) fellows will showcase their work and experience in the PFF program at the Capstone Fair on April 24, from 3–5 p.m. in the Carson Ballroom at Old Main. The fair is a poster presentation session where second-year PFF students will share their experiences in working with faculty, students, and administrators at ASU's cluster institutions. Students considering application to PFF, mentors, graduate program directors, department chairs, and deans are invited to attend the Capstone Fair. For more information, go to www.asu.edu/graduate/pff/capstone.html.

Graduation Reception Honors Graduating Students
The Division of Graduate Studies (DGS) is hosting a reception for all graduating master's and doctoral students on Thursday, May 11, from 2–4 p.m. at the Administration Arbor located between the Administration and Moeur buildings. Dr. Maria T. Allison, Vice Provost and Dean of Graduate Studies, will offer her congratulations to these students at 3 p.m. The DGS invites all graduating students, their mentors, and families to participate in this very special celebration of appreciation as we recognize their academic and leadership achievements and wish them success as they embark on new adventures. RSVP's are requested, including the number of guests, by Friday, April 21, to (480) 965-4102. For more information about the event, visit www.asu.edu/graduate/events/GradReception.

— ANNOUNCEMENTS/REMINDERS —

IRS Regulations Regarding FICA Tax Withholding. Guidelines for IRS regulations for summer support of RA's and TA's have been posted on the General Counsel website. Changes to the I.R.S. FICA tax withholding regulations went into effect on April 1, 2005, and raised questions regarding requirements for exemption from FICA tax withholding for Summer session graduate TA's and RA's. The Office of General Counsel has reviewed the regulations and worked with the Provost's Office, the Division of Graduate Studies, the Office of Financial Services and the Office of Research and Economic Affairs to create guidelines addressing this issue. Please make sure that you familiarize yourself with these guidelines when setting up summer support for your students. For more information, see #9 on the Hiring Procedures page: www.asu.edu/graduate/hiring/index.html#FICAwithholding.

Standard Offer Letters. The standard offer letters on the DGS Hiring Procedures web page (#5) have been updated. www.asu.edu/graduate/hiring/index.html#offerletter

Absent Committee Member Procedures. A student's entire committee must be present at the oral defense of a thesis or dissertation. If a member will not be able to attend the defense, the student should contact Student Academic Services for a copy of the Absent Committee Member Procedures and obtain a substitute for the defense. Students may not process a committee change once the oral defense has been scheduled with the Division of Graduate Studies. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns

DGS Polytechnic Office. Please note that the DGS Polytechnic Office will be closed on April 19, 2006, for the Division of Graduate Studies' Open House at the Tempe campus.

Dissertation/Thesis Oral Defenses. Students who are scheduling an oral defense of a thesis or dissertation should ensure that they have an approved Program of Study on file with the DGS, that their comprehensive exam and/or foreign language exam results have been recorded and confirm they have been advanced to candidacy (doctoral students only). Students will not be allowed to schedule defenses if their committees are incorrect, or if they are missing any required exams, forms, candidacy, etc. All of these items must be resolved prior to the 10 working day deadline to schedule the defense or the student will need to reschedule the defense to a later date. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns

Doctoral Student Degree Requirements. Please note that doctoral students must stay continuously enrolled at ASU until all degree requirements have been fulfilled. If a student has major or minor revisions to their dissertation, or is completing an internship after their defense, they must be registered for at least one credit hour each semester until they have received the DGS dean's signature and submitted their dissertation to the ASU Bookstore for binding. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns

Fulbright Program Workshops. The Lorraine W. Frank Office of National Scholarship Advisement (LWFONSA) will offer three workshops in April for students interested in applying for Fulbright grants in Fall 2006. U.S. Student Fulbright grants provide full funding for an academic year of research or study abroad and graduating seniors and graduate students who are U.S. citizens intending to do research or study outside the United States may apply. For more information, visit www.iie.org/Template.cfm?section=Fulbright1.

The workshops will be held:
  • Friday, April 7, 3-4 p.m., Best C121
  • Tuesday, April 11, 4:45-5:45 p.m., Best C121
  • Monday, April 17, 4:30-5:30 p.m., Best C121
Please RSVP to Suzie Balamenti, suzanne.balamenti@asu.edu or 480-965-5894.

Interactive Program of Study (iPOS). The Graduation Office has reported that many students are trying to apply for graduation prior to their POS being approved by the department and sent to the Graduation Office. Please let your students know that they cannot apply for graduation until the department has approved their iPOS and sent it to the Graduation Office. The student can check the status of the iPOS through myASU. Please refer any questions to grad-it@asu.edu.

Preparing Future Faculty. The Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) program invites doctoral students from all disciplines and MFA and MSD students to apply for participation in PFF. PFF is a professional development program for graduate students who are preparing for academic careers at institutions of higher education. Please encourage qualified students to apply for participation in this program. To access the 2006-07 application information and procedures, go to www.asu.edu/graduate/pff/PFFApplication. Completed applications are due April 10.

Teaching Excellence Awards. In late March, the Graduate and Professional Student Association (GPSA) honored 23 graduate students for excellence in teaching at ASU, and two SOLS students received the Mentor Award for their long-standing and deep commitment to community involvement. Congratulations to these graduate students for their outstanding work! For a complete listing of this year's award recipients, go to www.asu.edu/gpsa/TEA/TEA_awards06.html.
