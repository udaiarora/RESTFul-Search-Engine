  • the on-scene coordinator (assistant director, media relations),
  • the media coordinator (designated back up to on-scene coordinator) and
  • the office coordinator (assistant director media relations).

Introduction

The Office of Public Affairs and the Office Media Relations and Public Information play a critical role in any crisis that affects the campuses and operations of Arizona State University.

As the primary offices responsible for communicating to both campus community and the general public, PA/MR is involved in all levels of crisis communication at the university.

In addition, the Vice President of Public Affairs is a key part of the university crisis team, providing advice and support to the Office of the President during an emergency.

This plan is designed to integrate with the university wide emergency operations plan, as required by university policy and by law.

Specific responsibilities of the offices and the Vice President are listed in the university EOP and include:

  • Developing a specific and concise operation plan that outlines emergency procedures for all functions under PA/MR control.
  • Providing the Emergency Manager with a copy of the operational plan and any subsequent revisions.
  • Designating at least two alternates for the position of public information advisor (PIA). These are the assistant vice president of public affairs and the assistant director of media relations responsible for police coverage, as outlined below.
  • Compile university policies and procedures, department manuals, sample media releases and supporting documents that could provide information in an emergency.
  • Conduct training exercises with staff.
  • Prepare general survival and self-help information news releases.
  • Create and maintain a file of hazard specific news release for use during an emergency.
  • Coordinate with Human Resources and Student Services departments in developing a plan for deal with inquiries from relatives and friends concerning university personnel utilizing the American Red Cross DWI (Disaster Welfare Information System), as appropriate.
  • Develop and implement a training program to ensure that all media relations staff members understand the plant and their responsibilities and assignments.
  • Be prepared to respond to emergency training exercises.
  • Identify other media/public information issues.

The operation of the plan requires that all media relations staff members be familiar with the responsibilities of the office during different levels of emergency.

Staffing and Organization

While the entire staff of Public Affairs and especially the MR will likely be involved in any major crisis, there are five positions that are critical to successfully communicating with the public during a time of crisis.

The five positions are

  • the vice president of Public Affairs (Virgil Renzulli)
  • the assistant vice president of Public Affairs, (Nancy Neff)
  • the on-scene coordinator (Keith Jennings),
  • the media coordinator (In Zajac) and
  • the office coordinator (Sarah Auffret).

This plan is predicated on a team effort from the five people listed above and the media relations staff. During an emergency, any member of the media relations staff may be asked to fill one of the critical positions. The determination of who will assume which role is dependent upon who is available to respond at any given time and is outlined below.

Vice President of Public Affairs

The vice president holds a key position as a member of the university wide Emergency Policy and Organizations Group (EPOG). The group consists of two subcommittees: the Emergency Policy Group (EPG), which the vice President of Public Affairs belongs to, and the Emergency Operations Group (EOG).

The EPG is the group that will provide coordination and direction during any emergency at the university and the group reports directly to the president of the university or his designee.

In addition to the Vice President of Public Affairs, the EPG includes the directors of the Department of Public Safety, the University General Council and the Comptroller.

As part of the EPG, the vice president develops all official public statements, proclamations and resolutions regarding the emergency. The vice president is also responsible for coordination of all media coverage with the MR staff via the assistant vice president.

If the Vice President of Public Affairs is unavailable or has not yet reached the EOC, the assistant vice president will serve on the EPG. In the event both the vice president and the assistant vice president are unavailable, the On-scene coordinator will serve on the EPG until the arrival of either individual.

The Assistant Vice President

The assistant vice president is responsible for the overall coordination of the MR team and serves as liaison with the Vice President of Public Affairs and the EPG. Those responsibilities include:

  • Sending the On-scene coordinator to the location of an incident, if not already present at the scene and if appropriate.
  • Identifying spokespeople for the university as appropriate. The assistant vice president can act as the spokesperson, designate either the On-scene or Media coordinators as spokespeople or designate others as needed.
  • Coordinate statements and fact sheets with the EPG and MR Staff.
  • Instruct On-scene and Media coordinators on what may or may not be released.
  • Conduct Issues Management sessions as needed.
  • Assign additional responsibilities to MR staff as necessary.

In the event the assistant vice president of MR is unavailable or unable to respond, the on-scene coordinator (assistant director) will assume the duties of the assistant vice president until such time as the assistant vice president arrives at the emergency operations center.

If both the assistant vice president and the on-scene coordinator (assistant director) are unavailable or unable to respond, the media coordinator will assume the duties of the assistant vice president until either the assistant vice president or on-scene coordinator arrives.

In the event that the assistant vice president, the on-scene and media coordinators are unavailable or unable to respond, the most senior member of the media relations staff available will assume the duties of the assistant vice president until such time as either the assistant vice president or one of the coordinators arrives.

On-Scene Coordinator

The On-scene coordinator is typically the media relations staff member who is assigned to the ASU Campus Police Beat (assistant director).

The On-scene coordinator will go to the site of an incident to assist with the media. The request for the presence of the On-scene coordinator can come from either ASU Police or the Vice President of Public Affairs or his representative.

Once present at the incident, the On-scene coordinator should

  • Report to the ASU Police Officer who is in charge at the scene and get the details of the situation.
  • Determine if the situation requires a higher level of response, and if so, notify the assistant vice president or vice president.
  • Identify and make contact with all media present at the scene.
  • Give no information to the media except that approved by the officer in charge or by the assistant vice president of MR. The coordinator may exhibit flexibility to deal with the situation at hand.
  • Report as soon as possible to the assistant Vice President of Public Affairs the facts of the situation and the media response. Notifications should also be made further up the chain of command as necessary. Dissemination of information is key to the performance of the university during an emergency. The On-scene coordinator may recommend to the Vice President of Public Affairs that the response to the situation be upgraded or downgraded in response levels.
  • Maintain contact as needed with the assistant vice president and the vice president as the situation and circumstances change.
  • Remain on-scene for the duration of the incident, unless directed by the officer in charge or the assistant vice president to leave the scene.

If requested by the officer in charge, the Director of Public Safety, the chief of the ASU Police, the vice president of public affairs or the assistant vice president, the On-scene coordinator will draft a news release for dissemination to the media about the incident and see to the distribution of the release.

In many cases, the release will only be issued if a media outlet inquires about the event or incident. The release will only be generally distributed when several media outlets inquire and a competitive situation may develop unless the release is issued.

The On-scene coordinator also will perform any duties necessary, as assigned by the vice president, the assistant vice president or the EPG or EOG.

The On-scene coordinator serves as back up to the vice president and the assistant vice, and in the event that the vice president or assistant vice president are not available or are unable to respond, and assumes the duties of either the vice president or the assistant vice president for the purposes of this emergency plan, until their arrival. In the event the On-scene coordinator must assume the duties of either the vice president or the assistant vice president, it is the responsibility of the on-scene coordinator to inform the management staff of Public Affairs about the incident and to coordinate activities with members of the management staff of Public Affairs.

If the designated On-scene coordinator is not available or is unable to respond, his designated back up or the Media Coordinator should assume the duties of the On-scene coordinator.

In the event that the vice president, the assistant vice president, the on-scene and media coordinators are unavailable or unable to respond, the most senior member of the media relations staff available will assume the duties listed in this document until such time as either the vice president, the assistant vice president or one of the coordinators arrives.

Media Coordinator

The Media coordinator is responsible for coordination within the office.

Responsibilities include:

  • Maintaining contact with the assistant vice president and the On-scene coordinator.
  • At the direction of the assistant vice president, notify all print and broadcast media, including wire services.
  • Assist in Issues Management sessions if needed.
  • Oversee the press room or press area if one is established. Ensure that a member of the MR staff is present in the area at all times. Maintain a list of media present and distribute news releases and statements as necessary.
  • Help prepare any needed news releases or statements.
  • Work with the office coordinator to ensure quick and accurate duplication of news releases and statements.
  • Assist the vice president and assistant vice president as needed.
  • Coordinate any support needed in the field by the On-scene coordinator.

The Media coordinator serves as the second back up to the vice president, the assistant vice president, and in the event that the assistant vice president and the On-scene coordinator are not available or are unable to respond, assumes the duties listed in this document for the purposes of this emergency plan. In the event the Media coordinator must assume the duties of another member of the team, it is the responsibility of the Media coordinator to coordinate activities with members of the management staff of Public Affairs.

The Media Coordinator may also be assigned field duties as necessary and directed by the EPG, the vice president of public affairs, the assistant vice president or the On-scene coordinator if help is needed at the scene of the incident.

If the designated On-scene coordinator is not available or is unable to respond, the Media Coordinator should assume the duties of the On-scene coordinator.

In the event that media coordinator is unavailable or unable to respond, the most senior member of the media relations staff available will assume the duties of the media coordinator until such time as the coordinator arrives.

Office Coordinator

The office coordinator is a support position that helps the media coordinator function effectively and provides supervision to the general MR Staff..

The office coordinator also assumes the in-house duties of the media coordinator in the event the media coordinator must be deployed to the field for any reason.

The office coordinator serves as the initial contact point of incoming calls, directing to the appropriate staff member as directed by the media coordinator.

The office coordinator also helps oversee the preparation, duplication and distribution of news releases and statements as directed by the media coordinator.

The office coordinator may also be called upon to help staff a media room or area, depending on the situation and at the director of the media coordinator.

Levels of Response

Consistent with the University Emergency Operations plan, there are three main levels of response and one additional early level of response for the Public Affairs and MR staff that is different from the university-wide plan. Campus emergency operation will be managed from one of the models depending upon the situation. At any given time, the selected model of operation can be upgraded or downgraded as needed.

Levels include: Level 1, decentralized coordination and direction (low level emergencies/crime responses); Level 2, Issues management (The special Public Affairs procedure (Mid to high level message/response issues); Level 3, Centralized Coordination and Decentralized Direction (University wide impact); and Level 4, Centralized Coordination and Direction (University Wide Major Incident).

Level 1 - Decentralized Coordination and Direction (University Wide)

This is the first level of response and the most common activation of the plan. Under this model, routine emergency procedures, resource and response will be coordinated through the ASU Police Department Dispatch Center. Examples of Level 1 emergencies include, but are not limited to:

  • Structure fires
  • Minor civil disturbances or demonstrations
  • Major traffic accidents either on campus or involving university vehicles and/or student/faculty groups.
  • Hazardous materials incidents
  • Major crimes against the university or university community members
  • Any police or safety related event which may attract attention from local media

These types of events will typically only involve the On-scene coordinator, who works in consultation with the vice president and assistant vice president as appropriate. Mobilization of staff is not usually required at this level. The On-scene coordinator is responsible for evaluating the situation and recommending if additional response levels are needed, particularly Level 2.

Level 2 - Issues Management (Public Affairs Procedure)

This type of situation requires coordination of message beyond the Public Affairs and staff levels, typically involving other vice presidential level offices.

An issues management meeting should be held to coordinate response to the situation, involving staff from other offices as needed.

Examples would include:

  • Faculty/Staff Misconduct/Arrest/Indictment
  • Issues involving university policy or political stands
  • Major structure fires involving evacuations, or relocations
  • Closure of campus or campus areas.
  • Other issues as deemed necessary.

The issues management committee will designate an appropriate spokesman to answer media inquiries about the situation. All media calls should be routed to this designated

These types of events will typically only involve the On-scene coordinator, who works in consultation with the vice president and assistant vice president as appropriate and other university staff needed to deal with the situation at the issues management meeting. General mobilization of staff is not usually required at this level.

Level 3 - Centralized Coordination and Decentralized Direction (University Wide)

This second university level of response is outlined in the emergency plan and will require major involvement from several campus departments. The Emergency Manager (EM) will coordinate emergency activity, and may or may not activate the Emergency Operations Center (EOC). If the EOC is opened, the EM may only summon those members directly affected by the activation. Examples of level 2 emergencies include, but are not limited to:

  • Multi-structure fire(s)
  • Fires with casualties
  • Major hazardous materials incidents
  • Severe storm warning or damage
  • Major civil disturbances or demonstrations
  • Flood damage or warning of imminent minor flooding

This level of response requires some mobilization of MR staff. The Vice President of Public Affairs typically will be called either to the EOC or may work from the office. The On-Scene coordinator will likely be working in the field, but may also be in the office, depending on the situation. The Media and Office coordinators should be in the office and ready to respond. Other staff should be called in as needed.

Level 4 - Centralized Coordination and Direction (University Wide Major Incident)

This is the highest level of response and indicates a major disaster has occurred or is imminent, and is beyond the capability of the campus to effectively function in either of the first two levels. The EOC will be activated and coordinated by the EM. All EPOG members will be summoned to the EOC.

This level of response requires full mobilization of the MR staff. The Vice President of Public Affairs will report as soon as possible to the EOC. The Assistant Vice President of Public Affairs will respond to the MR or the EOC as appropriate. The On-scene coordinator will either report to the EOC or the location of the incident, whichever is most appropriate to the situation. The Media and Office Coordinators should report as soon as possible to the Media Relations Office as should all members of the MR staff. It may also be necessary to draw upon staff from the Office of Public Relations and the Office of Institutional Advancement.
