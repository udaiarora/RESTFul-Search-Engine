Fulton puts his money where his heart is: ASU

Less than a week after delivering a fund-raising challenge to ASU deans, Ira A. Fulton has given ASU a million-dollar incentive to raise funds for university athletics.

Fulton and Gene Smith, executive director of athletics at ASU, announced Feb. 28 the Fulton Challenge – a pledge to the Sun Angel Foundation to match up to $1 million in donations for the next year.

The funds will directly support new ASU women’s athletic scholarships, as well as cover costs for an NCAA-mandated summer school program for first-year athletes, tuition and fee increases, and computer lab upgrades.

“I want to help make this truly a new American university,” Fulton says. “I just want everyone to step up and help me succeed here and accomplish the task at hand. We need to make sure our student-athletes have a great place to not only work out in, but a place they can be proud of.”

Fulton, who attended ASU on a football scholarship, says his motive with the challenge is to get more people involved in supporting the university.

“If someone has been giving on a regular basis, that’s great,” he says. “What I want is to get to the ones who haven’t given for a couple of years, and I want to get them into the habit – and the fun – of giving and being part of this great organization.”

Similar to previous fund-raising challenges Fulton has established, gifts up to $25,000 will be matched at varying levels.

Fulton will match gifts 2-to-1, up to $5,000, for individuals who have never made a gift to the Sun Angel Foundation Scholarship Fund, who have not made a gift in the past two years, or who are current or former Sun Devil student-athletes.

Gifts from individuals who currently donate to the Sun Angel Foundation Scholarship Fund will be matched 1-to-1 up to $5,000 per gift on any increase in their total gift from the previous year.

Gifts that exceed $5,000 will be matched by the same criteria on the first $5,000, and anything beyond that up to $25,000 will be matched on a 1-to-1 basis.

Mike Gallagher, chairman of the Sun Angel Foundation board of directors, says this gift will play a key role in keeping ASU athletics competitive on a national scale. ASU ranks eighth out of the 10 Pacific-10 Conference institutions in total revenue generation, a number he says needs to increase.

“For Arizona State University to compete with other elite programs across the country, we need to take advantage of Ira Fulton’s gracious offer through the support of current and new Sun Angel Foundation Members,” Gallagher says.

Fulton, founder of Fulton Homes and a valley philanthropist, is ASU’s single largest benefactor. Since 2003, he has given nearly $60 million to ASU, including $50 million to the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering and $5 million to establish the Mary Lou Fulton Presidential Chair in the College of Education.

Gallagher says he hopes the gift will help raise the Sun Angel Foundation membership from its current level to around 10,000.

Smith says the Sun Angel Foundation will begin a direct mail and phone campaign the week of March 14 to capitalize on Fulton ’s generosity.

While Fulton has set the challenge level at $1 million, he is open to increasing the amount if the goal is met.

“If Gene gets this thing used up too fast, I’m such a softie that I’ll probably give him another million,” Fulton says.
