Environmental Health and Safety Training for Employees

On behalf of the Environmental Health & Safety department, welcome to Arizona State University. The University strives to promote and maintain a safe and healthy environment for the University community and its employees. To facilitate such an environment, employees are required to attend certain mandated safety trainings depending upon their specific job classification and/or duties. Completion of the following training (within 90 days from your start date and annually thereafter) is required for all employees working in areas where portable fire extinguishers are available for emergency use .

  • Fire Safety/Fire Prevention
  • Annually, thereafter: Fire Safety/Fire Prevention Refresher

Also, due to the specific nature of your position and/or duties, completion of the following trainings may be required prior to assignment of work duties:

  • Asbestos Awareness
  • Bloodborne Pathogens in the Workplace
  • Coaching the Van Driver
  • Hazard Communication for Chemical Safety and Awareness
  • Laboratory Chemical Safety
  • Powered Industrial Truck Operation (formerly Forklift Safety)
Training Determination

To determine if you are required to attend any safety training(s) based on the work you will be performing, please consult the "Training Determinatino Table" at Environmental Health & Safety. Consulting with your immediate supervisor concerning your work duties and need for related training is advised.

Registration

To register for classes you may click a class link above for an online registration form, or call the Learning and Performance Solutions Group at 965-4751. University policies regarding required employee safety training can be viewed at Environmental Health & Safety policies.
