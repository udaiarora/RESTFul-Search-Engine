Is Your Financial Aid Ready?

This is an electronic announcement, please do not reply to this e-mail notice. If you have problems viewing this page visit www.asu.edu/fa/email.

Dear ASU Student,

Seasons Greetings! As the fall semester draws to a close we want to remind you of some steps that will help ensure that you have financial aid funds available to assist with initial costs for spring semester. We understand there is a lot to do this time of year but it is important to complete these steps prior to the start of the semester. Waiting until school begins will result in a delay of your financial aid disbursement and any resulting credit refund.

  1. COMPLETE YOUR FILE
    Monitor your file using ASU Interactive to ensure that your file is complete. If it is not, promptly submit any requested documents.
  2. ACCEPT, REDUCE OR DECLINE YOUR AWARD OFFER
    Visit ASU Interactive to accept, reduce or decline any offered student loans and/or Federal Work-Study awards. Awarded grant and scholarship funds are automatically accepted.
  3. COMPLETE LOAN PROMISSORY NOTES
    Firstâ??time ASU Federal Direct Student Loan borrowers must complete an electronic Master Promissory Note (MPN) online: www.asu.edu/fa/mpn. Federal Perkins loan borrowers must also complete and return the paper Perkins MPN and Student Information Sheet mailed to you upon acceptance of this type of loan. Please contact the SFAO if you need another MPN.
  4. COMPLETE LOAN ENTRANCE COUNSELING SESSIONS
    If you accepted a Federal Direct Student Loan AND you are a first-time borrower at ASU, please complete your Direct Loan Entrance Counseling session online: www.asu.edu/fa/entrance. This 20-minute process must be completed before your Direct Loan(s) can be disbursed. If you are a Federal Perkins loan borrower, you must also complete your Perkins Entrance Counseling session online: www.asu.edu/fa/entrance.
  5. ENROLL FULL-TIME
    Award offers are based on full-time enrollment. If you do not intend to enroll full-time, your award(s) may be prorated or cancelled. For more info visit: www.asu.edu/fa/policies/census. Enrolling in classes completes the registration process. Classes will be held even if aid becomes unavailable or is inadequate to pay all tuition and fees. Registration will not be cancelled during the tuition refund period. If you decide not to attend classes, you must officially withdraw from the University.
  6. MAINTAIN SATISFACTORY ACADEMIC PROGRESS (SAP)
    If you are on financial aid Suspension or Termination due to a SAP violation, you are currently ineligible for financial aid. If you have extenuating circumstances, you may submit a review form. For SAP policy details, please visit: www.asu.edu/fa/policies/sap.
