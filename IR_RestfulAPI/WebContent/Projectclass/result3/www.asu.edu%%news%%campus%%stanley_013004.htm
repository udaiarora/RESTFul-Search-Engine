January 30, 2004

Stanley to head ASU planning efforts

Richard Stanley, former executive vice provost at New York University, is joining ASU to play a key role in the university’s ongoing planning efforts.

As senior vice president and university planner at ASU, Stanley will take the lead in coordinating and integrating university master planning, facility and capital planning, and academic planning. Among the biggest challenges will be developing of a road map for taking the university from 57,000 students today to more than 90,000 by the year 2020, implemented across multiple campuses in the metropolitan Phoenix area.

He says he will serve as an integration point for ongoing efforts, such as the university design team, the university’s various growth and building initiatives, the various academic planning units and those working on development of the university’s research capacity.

“There are already a lot of people working on these projects,” he says. “My role is to coordinate these efforts and to involve the appropriate people. I am really here to look at the big picture and help chart out the speciﬁc steps we need to reach President Crow’s vision for the university.”

Crow says Stanley’s vast experience over the past 25 years in various areas of administration at New York University, will provide him with the experience needed to see across the university as a whole.

“Richard Stanley will bring the kind of broad-based knowledge and experience that is essential to the success of our university-wide, comprehensive planning initiative,” Crow says. “He will be asked to take on a substantial and comprehensive set of planning obligations to move ASU in the direction we envision.”

For the last 21 years, Stanley has served in various capacities within the university provost’s ofﬁce, including coordination and oversight of the university’s academic budget, its facility and space planning, the faculty planning efforts and the university’s information technology and libraries. In his most recent position as executive vice provost of NYU, a post he has held since 2002, Stanley has been responsible over areas related to academic resource allocation among the schools of the university and oversight of academic support units. He serves as the lead academic member of the university planning committee and plays the chief role in planning on academic facility projects.

Stanley also works with 14 school deans and enrollment management leaders to direct and coordinate strategic planning and policy implementation for admissions and ﬁnancial aid policies and budgets.

“I’ve had a wide-ranging portfolio, with my ﬁngers in all of the key areas involved in running a major university,” he says.

After 25 years helping build NYU, Stanley says he is ready for a new opportunity and challenge. He says he was drawn to living in the Southwest, working and developing a public institution and helping implement the plans of President Crow.

“The goals that Mike Crow has set out are very exciting,” he says. “The concepts of social embeddedness, sustainability, and the university as an engine to drive a community economically are really exciting and something I want to be a part of.”

Stanley, who holds a B.A. degree in economics from Yale University, will ofﬁcially begin at ASU Feb. 23. Prior to that he will be on campus periodically.
