June 27, 2003

ASU earns green light for renovations, housing project

The Arizona Board of Regents (ABOR) held a somber, but productive meeting on June 19. ABOR members, who reported from the three university locations via video conference, began their meeting with a moment of silence for Blake Anderson, director of state relations at ASU, who had passed away that morning of complications from lupus, following a brief hospitalization.

ABOR President Jack Jewett said, "Blake was very instrumental in keeping us informed about legislative matters and he will be greatly missed."

As agenda items for the final meeting of the 2002 - 03 fiscal year were presented, ABOR members approved ASU's purchase of approximately 1.3 acres of property along Sunset Drive for the South Campus Housing Project. This project will be managed by the same company that manages Adelphi Commons, ASU's current public-private group housing. Construction for is expected to begin in August and completed by June 2004.

ABOR also approved ASU's request to increase its budget for the Brickyard Tenant Improvement Project by $1.9 million. ASU received project approval at the April meeting with a proposed budget of $7 million. Utilizing the Construction Manager at Risk (CMAR) process, ASU received a guaranteed maximum price of $8.9 million.

The approved project includes the remodeling and reconfiguration of up to 130,000 square feet in multiple buildings and floors of the Brickyard in Tempe. The complex will house the Institute for Computer and Information Sciences & Engineering (ICISE), which is being created to centralize the university's key academic and research programs in computer science. These improvements will support offices, mediated classrooms and laboratories (teaching and research) for various computer science and engineering programs. The $1.9 million increase is due to the need for a higher level of building security and connectivity; an increased number of instructional/research laboratories and a need for higher caliber instructional/research laboratories. Completion of this project is set for August.

Other items that were approved included:

. ASU's request to enter into a Technology Transfer management agreement with Arizona Science and Technology Enterprises LLC (AZTE). AZTE was organized May 29 as an Arizona limited liability company with the ASU Foundation as its sole member. AZTE's purpose, under the leadership of Peter Slate, chief executive officer, is to perform technology transfer management services for all ASU technology. Under this agreement, ASU will pay AZTE an annual fee for the services. The annual fee will cover almost all of AZTE's annual operating expenses. The AZTE preliminary draft budget for fiscal year 2004 is approximately $3.3 million.

. Renovations for the Packard Stadium clubhouse and playing field. The project consists of construction of a new players clubhouse and locker facilities, a renovated entry including a new ticket booth, novelty sales area and a "walk of champions" to celebrate ASU's baseball crowning achievements.

In addition, the renovation of existing coaching facilities and extensive upgrading of the playing field were also approved as part of the project. Renovations will include an upgrade to the electrical supply system, an ADA compliant plaza over the new clubhouse (with elevator), additional plaza renovation and a bermed grass seating area for spectators. The clubhouse, ADA plaza and coaching facilities renovations will be advertised for bid at a later date.

Based upon the increased scope of work, the total project cost needs to be increased from the approved $2.0 million to $2.85 million and is to be funded by Certificates of Participation (COPS) and repaid by the Department of Intercollegiate Athletics Capital Fund Raising Campaign. Packard Stadium was built in 1974. A clubhouse and locker room area were added in the mid-1980s.

. Academic program and organizational unit changes. ASU received approval for the creation of a doctor of audiology degree (Au.D), the establishment of the department of Information Systems and the establishment of the Russian and East European Studies Center.

The next meeting of the Arizona Board of Regents is scheduled Aug. 14 - 15 at Northern Arizona University.
