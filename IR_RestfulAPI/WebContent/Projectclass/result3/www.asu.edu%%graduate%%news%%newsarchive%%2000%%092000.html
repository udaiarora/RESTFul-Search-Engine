ASU GRADUATE STUDENTS RECEIVE GATES MILLENNIUM SCHOLARSHIP

Marguerite George, of Tempe, is one of 34 Arizona State University graduate students winning the Bill and Melinda Gates Millennium Scholarship for 2000–2001. She will receive a stipend of $14,000, which will assist her in pursuing a doctoral degree in mathematics.

The Gates scholarship is funded by a grant from the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation and is administered by the United Negro College Fund. This is the first year of the scholarship, intended for minority students with a minimum grade-point average of 3.3 in mathematics and the sciences. Recipients must also demonstrate leadership ability in community service or extracurricular activities. The program will provide financial assistance to 1,000 new students per year, over a 20-year period, with an annual investment of $50 million.

George is studying how teachers are trained to instruct in mathematics. This fall, she is beginning her study in Mathematics Education, a new interdisciplinary graduate program offered through the College of Education at ASU.

”It is really necessary for a person to have studied advanced math to teach even the basics,” George said.

George is a graduate of the Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) program at ASU. The PFF program applies new approaches to faculty development through integrated situational and experiential training in higher education settings. George said, “I developed a whole different attitude because of the PFF program. You begin to think of yourself as a professor.”

George is no stranger to teaching mathematics. She has taught K-12 and college level mathematics courses in Arizona and California.
