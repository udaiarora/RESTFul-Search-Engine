Improving the ASU Student Experience

ASU students: The university knows your investment in education, in time and money, is one of the biggest you’ll ever make. ASU wants to make sure your educational investment pays off. Here is a brief look at real examples of the progress the university has made over the past year to improve your educational experience.

Fulfill student requests

Download: Improving the Student Experience (PDF)

ASU students have made specific requests to what would make the ASU experience better.

In response, ASU has:
• Doubled the area of wireless coverage for computers on all campuses.
• Developed healthier, more flexible food options available across campus.
• Extended the hours of Hayden Library by eight hours per weekday.
• Offered free Valley Metro bus passes for all ASU students.

Provide more services

• The ASU Learning Resource Center provided tutoring, academic coaching and software training to 4,632 students in fall 2005, up 55% from fall 2004.
• Disability Resources hired more interpreters, Braillers and aides to support students with disabilities.
• Wellness and Health Promotion opened the Home Safe Violence Prevention & Advocacy Center and 24 hour help line.
• Launched the new Graduate Student Center and started the International Student Coalition.
• Residential Life provided new centralized move-in process for more than 4,500 residence hall residents.
• Campus Recreation upgraded equipment and added more than 12 new sport clubs and enhanced yoga and group fitness classes.
• Began late-night programming in spring 2005 and expanded it to 71 programs for the fall and spring semesters.
• Campus Health Service increased staffing and expanded pharmacy and urgent care clinic services.
• ASU’s new University College guided exploratory students down the path of finding a major through advising, career services, success courses, tutoring and other services.

Drop class size, add programs

ASU has added many new sections of some of the most popular freshman-level classes.

• Added 96 sections of English composition - dropping most 100-level English classes from 24 to 19 students.
• Added 137 sections in first-year math - dropping most class sizes from 60 to 19 students.
• Success rates, students earning a C or better, in math jumped by more than 15% in MAT 114/142 and 9% in MAT 117.
• Added eight new undergraduate degree programs and five graduate programs at the Tempe, Polytechnic and West campuses.

Improve degree value

• Money spent on a bachelor’s degree is a better investment than the stock market, giving a 12% yearly return over a lifetime compared to 7% for long-term stocks.
• ASU has nationally ranked programs in business, engineering, education, fine arts and other fields.
• More companies are looking for ASU graduates. The university is a magnet for employers, drawing 610 different companies and agencies to Career Services events in 2004 - 05, 47% more than 2003 - 04.
• Average starting salary offers reported to Career Services in 2004 - 05:
- B.S. in engineering - $49,907
- B.S. in business - $42,022
- B.S. in nursing - $42,937
- B.A. in liberal arts & sciences $35,586

Boost financial aid

•More than $70 million in university gift aid was awarded last year.
• University need-based aid has increased by 189% since 2003.
• Financial aid covered direct cost of attendance for the lowest income Arizona students ($12,000/student annually).
• 66% of all ASU students now receive some type of financial aid.
• On average, Arizona undergraduates receiving aid were awarded $7,794 per year.
• The number of Arizona freshmen entering ASU from low-income families has quadrupled since 2002.

Strengthen student body

• Largest freshman class in ASU’s history – 8,467; a 9.7% increase from the fall 2004 class, a 28% increase since 2001 and a 100% increase since 1995.
• 30% of the resident freshman class are students of color (140% increase since 1995).
• 16% are students from the top 5% of their high school graduating class and 28% from the top 10%.
• 155 National Merit scholars, 48 from Arizona; 10 Flinn scholars – the most ever enrolled.

Diversify faculty

• More than 100 new scholars of all levels joined the faculty ranks in 15 of ASU’s 18 schools and colleges.
• 41% of new faculty hires in the last 2 years are female.
• Overall proportion of minority faculty reached a record 22%, the highest in the university’s history.
• ASU boasts higher numbers of Hispanic and American Indian faculty than most peer institutions.
• Faculty includes 21 members of the National Academy of Engineering, the National Academy of Sciences, the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the Royal Society, and one Nobel laureate.

 

Help students achieve more

• More students are graduating. In 2004 - 05, 12,821 students graduated, up by 1,000 over the last two years.
• More students are staying in school. From 2003 - 04, the freshman to sophomore retention has grown from 76 to 79 percent.
•ASU students’ record of winning Fulbright Scholarships is four times the national average.
•More ASU students have been named top undergraduates by USA Today than at any other public university in the nation.
•ASU is a leading producer of other major national scholarships including Goldwater and Udall Scholars.

Increase learning outside the classroom

• Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative awarded money to 18 student teams to explore their innovative ideas for business products and services or social good.
•ASU is connecting students with Valley employers for real-world training in internships and co-ops.
• Undergraduate research opportunities throughout the university are at an all-time high.
• Academic Community Engagement Services (ACES) is helping put students tutoring children in the greater Phoenix area.
