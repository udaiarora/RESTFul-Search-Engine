May 23, 2003

Reading, math program pairs ASU West students, Valley children

ASU West students are teaching Valley children to read, improve their reading skills, and learn valuable math skills through America Reads-America Counts, a federally funded program coordinated through the ASU West College of Arts & Sciences.

Children ages preschool to eighth grade are involved in America Reads-America Counts. The program reaches children at an early age to help them develop the valuable skills of reading and arithmetic. It also targets children who are struggling to catch up to their current grade level. ASU West students work closely with children, tutoring those who need the extra one-on-one assistance.

"America Reads-America Counts teaches children two of the most important skills that they will learn in their lifetime," says Linda Persau, coordinator of internships and fieldwork in the integrative studies department at ASU West. "It is a rewarding experience that students enjoy. Some ASU West students have been involved with America Reads for as long as two years."

ASU West students can be paid to participate if they qualify for work-study assistance. Other students volunteer and fulfill internship credit hours.

"I have been a tutor for two semesters. I love volunteering, and I like working with the kids and seeing their progress," says Jane Bringgold Hassan, a graduate student at ASU West who is working on her master's degree in interdisciplinary studies. "The kids are happy to see me each week, and the volunteer program takes some of the burden off of the classroom teacher, so she is happy to see me too."

Bonnie Dash, an experienced classroom teacher at Granada Elementary School in the Alhambra School District, trains ASU West students who participate in the program.

"After the training session with Bonnie Dash, everything was put into a new perspective," says Sara Kirby, a freshman at ASU West. "I learned many new strategies for reading, and I learned that figuring out a word is more than just 'sounding out.'"

Time management is a vital skill for all college students, but those who work and participate in internship programs learn quickly and usually acquire added skills.

"I am a single mother of four, and I work and go to school," Hassan says. "I just manage. If I thought about it I might freeze in my tracks, like a deer in the headlights. But the kids that I help make it worthwhile, and I volunteer at a particularly needy school, so I find the time."

ASU West students participate in the America Reads program at 15 elementary and K-8 schools around the Valley, enabling students to choose a school close to their home or close to the campus.

"The America Reads program has been a valuable resource and an integral part in our effort to help all children achieve grade level in reading," says Nancy Hamlet, reading coordinator at Carol G. Peck Elementary in the Alhambra School District. "One-to-one tutoring is a powerful and effective intervention that builds reading skills and self-esteem."

For information, call Linda Persau at (602) 543-6001. Fuller, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or (barb.fuller@asu.edu).
