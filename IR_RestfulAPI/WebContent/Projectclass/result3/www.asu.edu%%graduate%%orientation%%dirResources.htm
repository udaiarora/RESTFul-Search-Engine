ASU Resources for Graduate Students

 



Arizona State University has a vast array of resources to ease your life so that you may devote maximum time to your academic pursuits. Below is just a small indication of what ASU has to offer.





•ADULT RE-ENTRY•
www.asu.edu/studentaffairs/reentry
ARE@asu.edu
(480) 965-2252
The Adult Re-Entry Program provides pre-enrollment guidance and support services to students age 25 and older; the Adult Re-Entry Center serves as a place for mature students to meet and study with others.


•BOOKSTORE•
bookstore.asu.edu
The ASU Bookstore has metered parking available outside the store. Graduate research and teaching assistants/associates receive a 10% discount with a valid staff ID.


•BUS/SHUTTLE SERVICE•
—Public transit information: (602) 253-5000
www.valleymetro.org
The Bus Book, with routes and schedules, is available throughout campus. ASU offers the free ASU U-Pass for students, faculty and staff with a valid ASU ID card. The U-Pass can be used on any Valley Metro bus route and is available from ASU Decal Sales (525 S. Forest Ave., Suite #105, Tempe or at the respective West and Polytechnic campus offices, www.asu.edu/dps/pts).

—ASU Shuttle fare/schedule information:
www.asu.edu/dps/pts/shuttle
ASU offers shuttle service to and from the Tempe, Mesa, and Phoenix campuses and Mesa Community College. (The ASU U-Pass is not valid on the ASU shuttles.) The FLASH, a free shuttle bus service, is available around the Tempe campus and downtown Tempe.


•CHILD & FAMILY SERVICES (CFS)•
www.asu.edu/studentaffairs/mu/family
CFS provides child care and elder care resource and referral services, individual consultations, and support for parents, including child/family workshops and a resource library.


•COMPUTING AT ASU•
www.asu.edu/it
Information Technology (IT) provides a wide variety of computer access to the ASU community. These services are available to all students, faculty, and staff who need to use the computing systems for academic or administrative purposes.

•GRADUATE FOREIGN LANGUAGE EXAMINATION•
www.asu.edu/graduate/forms
Some degree programs require proficiency in one or more foreign languages. Foreign language exams are offered three times per year. The Department of Languages and Literatures administers the exam in cooperation with the Division of Graduate Studies and your degree program.
