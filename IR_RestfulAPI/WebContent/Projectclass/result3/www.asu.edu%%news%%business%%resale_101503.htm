October 15, 2003

Greater Phoenix resale market returns to torrid pace

Rebounding from a slowing trend, the Greater Phoenix resale housing market returned to a torrid pace setting a record for the month with 8,185 transactions in September. This brought third quarter 2003 to a quarterly record of 20,560 sales, according to data released by the Arizona Real Estate Center.

"Typically the third quarter reveals a slowing pace from the traditionally higher levels of activity in the second quarter, which stood at 19,845 sales for second quarter 2003," said Dr. Jay Q. Butler, director of the center. "2003 appears to be repeating the pattern of 2002, which demonstrated considerable strength in the latter months of the year. Having established this strong pace, the year-to-date total of 55,435 homes sold has already topped the 46,850 sales recorded by this time last year."

Butler explained that even though the median price followed a typical pattern by cooling from a record $160,500 in August to $157,500 for September 2003, the quarterly median price was a record $159,500. Since the Greater Phoenix area is so large, the median price can range significantly from $365,000 in North Scottsdale to $89,750 in the central western areas of Phoenix. For third quarter 2003, 51 percent of all recorded sales were for homes priced from $100,000 to $175,000 and 14 percent for homes priced over $300,000.

Butler offered several reasons for the resurgence of the local resale housing market. "Although mortgage interest rates have been falling in the last few weeks, they have been rising for most of the year," he said. "Some people may have wanted to take advantage of still favorable interest rate environment to gain homeownership or to improve on their current housing. There have been some progressive signs that the economy is finally beginning to steadily, but slowly, expand. Even though the job market continues to be weak and consumer confidence low, people have not lost faith in their live-in investment. However, the key question remains as to whether the current levels of activity represent the final vestiges of people satisfying their dreams and for the future-- how many people are left still seeking their dream."

The resale townhouse/condominium sector followed a typical pattern by slowing from 4,165 recorded sales in third quarter 2003 to 3,745, but was well ahead of last year's 3,330. For 2003 year to date (YTD), the activity stands at 11,125 units, in contrast to 9,990 for 2002 YTD. The median price slowed slightly from $103,375 in the second quarter to $103,000, but was well above last year's $95,000. Since this housing sector is a good source of affordable housing, the strong increase in median price could be adversely impact the market activity.

The median square footage for a single-family home recorded sold in third quarter 2003 was 1,690 square feet, which is larger than the 1,650 reported a year ago. In the townhouse/condominium sector, the median square footage was 1,145 square feet, which is smaller than the 1,150 reported a year ago.

· In contrast to third quarter 2002, recorded sales in the City of Phoenix improved from 4,945 to 5,510 sales, while the median sales price increased from $119,900 to $130,900. Median prices can range significantly in Phoenix, from $83,950 in the Sky Harbor area to $194,000 in the Union Hills area. The townhouse/condominium sector increased slightly from 940 to 970 sales, while the median price increased from $85,000 to $90,990.

· The Scottsdale resale home market grew from 1,710 to 2,120 recorded sales. The median sales price continued its upward movement, increasing from $295,000 to $325,500. The median resale home price is $365,000 in North Scottsdale and $167,250 in South Scottsdale. The townhouse/condominium sector in Scottsdale also improved from 690 to 785 sales, while the median sales price increased from $138,700 to $148,000.

· The Mesa resale housing market continued to improve from 2,005 to 2,570 sales, while the median price increased from $133,500 to $147,000. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 490 to 570 sales, while the median home price increased from $88,500 to $93,000.

· Glendale moved from 1,590 to 1,850 recorded sales and the median sales price increased from $137,750 to $145,000. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 155 to 170 sales, while the median sales price increased from $74,250 to $78,500.

· Compated to a year ago, the Sun City resale market increased from 365 to 385 sales, with the median sales price increasing from $99,000 to $120,000. Resale activity in Sun City West declined from 260 to 225 recorded sales, but the median sales price increased from $140,000 to $140,500. The townhouse/condominium market in Sun City improved from 225 to 245 sales and the median sales price increased from $73,000 to $81,750. In Sun City West, sales stayed at 65 transactions, but the median sales price increased from $97,250 to $98,500.

· The resale market in Gilbert grew from 975 to 1,455 sales, while the median sales price increased from $166,900 to $176,000. The townhouse/condominium market moved slightly from 50 to 70 sales and the median sales price increased from $109,175 to $120,000.

· In Chandler, the resale market improved from 1,375 to 1,795 recorded sales, while the median sales price increased from $153,000 to $165,000. The townhouse/condominium market improved from 165 to 215 sales and the median sales price increased from $99,900 to $105,950.

§ The resale market in Tempe moved from 535 to 665 sales, with the median sales price increasing to $168,000 from $157,000. The townhouse/condominium sector also improved from 260 to 300 sales, while the median sales price increased from $99,000 to $108,900.

· The highest median sales price was in Paradise Valley at $875,000 with a median square foot house of 3,940 square feet.

· In the West Valley, the following communities represent 6 percent of the resale market, while it stood at 5 percent for a year ago.
o Avondale improved from 250 to 320 sales with the median price going from $139,900 to $152,000.
o El Mirage grew from 85 sales ($106,000) to 125 sales ($120,500).
o Goodyear moved from 195 to 250 sales, while the median price increased from $153,500 to $161,900.
o Surprise expanded from 310 sales ($128,000) to 500 sales ($139,925).
