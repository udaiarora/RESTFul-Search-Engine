Acquisitions Council Bylaws


Membership


Acquisitions Council serves as advisory to the Head of Technical Services. Membership includes supervisors of classified staff for acquisitions functions, acquisitions librarians, and the technology support representative. Membership may change based on current acquisitions needs. Substitutes will not be appointed for absent members.


Acquisitions Council may invite guests to take part if the guests' particular areas of expertise or concern are under discussion, or if intradepartmental or interdepartmental matters are at issue.


Meetings


1. Acquisitions Council meets weekly at a regularly scheduled time.


2. Meeting facilitator and recorder rotate through membership.

  • Facilitator: Calls for agenda items; chairs the meeting; becomes recorder the the next meeting.
  • Recorder: Drafts and distributes minutes to members of Acquisitions Council for review; edits as needed. Following Council's approval, sends minutes to the Administrative Assistant for Technical Services for distribution to all Technical Services Department staff and the Associate Dean for Library Services.

3. Any member of the department may request through an Acquisitions Council member that an item be placed on the agenda, and/or ask to attend a meeting in order to contribute to discussion of a particular agenda item.


Confidentiality


Members are expected to communicate information to other department members. However, some discussions must be confidential. It is the responsibility of the member initiating the discussion to establish that the matter is to be held in confidence.


FUNCTIONS OF ACQUISITIONS COUNCIL


Communication


Acquisitions Council serves as a forum for information exchange and dissemination to acquisitions units. Typical reports might include unit updates, workflow issues, staff vacancies, reports from groups or people outside the department that have a direct bearing on acquisitions matters, and other acquisitions activities.


Members are expected to communicate information from the meeting to other department members.


Acquisitions Issues and Policy


Acquisitions Council serves as the decision-making group for acquisitions issues and workflows. Acquisitions issues include, but are not limited to,

  • online acquisitions and serials control records
  • status of the materials budget
  • end-of-fiscal year activities
  • vendor relations
  • acquisitions workflows
  • wage budget and student employees in acquisitions units
  • equipment and technology requirements

Intradepartmental and Interdepartmental Interfaces


Acquisitions Council determines appropriate ways to communicate acquisitions information to the Technical Services Department and to other departments or groups outside Technical Services.


University Libraries Strategic Plan


Acquisitions Council will ensure that acquisitions activities and policies serve to advance the University Libraries Strategic Plan, and departmental goals.

Catalog Council Bylaws


Membership


Council is composed of all the catalog librarians, and several senior cataloging staff members, who serve at the discretion of the Council. The Head of Technical Services is an ex officio member of the Council. The Head will attend Council meetings dependent upon his or her own schedule and priorities and is exempt from serving as convener.


Meetings


Council meets once a week in a regularly scheduled session. Attendance at Council meetings takes precedence over other matters for all members.


Agenda


Any member of Council may place an item on the agenda. Generally, the originator leads the discussion. Any cataloging staff member may request, through a Council member, that an item be discussed or considered by Council.


The agenda always includes the item Other. This is included to give each member an opportunity to make brief announcements, propose topics for further discussion, etc. Other is normally first on the agenda. Any discussion topic or report that will take more than five minutes should be included on the agenda as a separate item.


Confidentiality 

Members are expected to exercise discretion in sharing Council business or deliberations. Materials or comments presented off the record are confidential.


Guests

Council may invite guests to take part if the guests' particular areas of expertise or concern are under discussion. Any cataloging staff member may ask to attend a meeting in order to contribute to discussion of a particular agenda item.


Convener


The position of Convener is taken by each member in rotation. The Convener is responsible for:

  1. Finding a substitute if the scheduled convener knows that he or she will be absent.

  2. Assembling and distributing the agenda for the meeting. The agenda is distributed to all cataloging staff, preferably on the afternoon preceding the meeting.

  3. Conducting the meeting.

  4. Appointing someone to take minutes if the Secretary is absent.

  5. Reviewing the minutes before distribution.

Secretary


The Secretary takes the minutes of each meeting, edits them, has them reviewed by the convener, or by another Council member if the convener is not available, distributes them, and files the original paper copy in the binder located in the Resource Library. The minutes are distributed via email to all Technical Services staff, the Associate Dean, and such other persons as Council may approve.


CATALOG COUNCIL FUNCTIONS

Cataloging Issues and Policy


Catalog Council serves as the chief forum for the introduction and discussion of cataloging issues; acts as the established working body for the formation, adoption, revision and implementation of cataloging policy for the Libraries; plans, designs and directs procedures and activities to accomplish cataloging tasks and projects in support of the Libraries.


Care and Enhancement of the Main Catalog


Believing that the main catalog is and will remain indispensable to the Mission of the Libraries, Catalog Council uses its reservoir of knowledge of bibliographic structure and catalog content to respond to organizational needs related to the catalog; actively seeks opportunities to advise and instruct on the bibliographical and structural manifestations of the library automated system.


Profession and the Discipline of Cataloging


Catalog Council contributes to the profession and to the discipline of cataloging in local, national and international arenas. Council forms and expresses opinions on issues within its purview through involvement in the Library of Congress' Program for Cooperative Cataloging (BIBCO, NACO, SACO), OCLC and RLIN, and cataloging opportunities beyond this institution.


Governance


Catalog Council takes a leading role in short- and long-range planning and goal setting for the cataloging program; develops and implements policies and procedures for cataloging operations including writing or reviewing policy and procedure documents; serves as an advisory body to the Head of Technical Services on matters involving the effectiveness of the cataloging program or the well-being of cataloging staff members; contributes collective opinion or expertise to Library planning or operations issues.


Communication


Catalog Council serves as a forum for information exchange among the cataloging staff and between that staff and other groups. Catalog Council primarily shares information concerning cataloging policy and professional development and service; it may, however, share information about any relevant item brought to its attention. Catalog Council minutes are the official written means by which Council communicates with others.
