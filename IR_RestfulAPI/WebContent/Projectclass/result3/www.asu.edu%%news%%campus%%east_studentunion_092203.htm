September 22, 2003

New student union at East under way

ASU East kicked off construction on a new student union, designed to be the hub of student life at the ASU East campus, with a groundbreaking on Sept. 17.

ASU President Michael Crow and ASU East Provost Charles Backus launched construction of a building that will help ASU East greatly expand its student life program and meet the campus's enrollment demands.

The 27,000-square-foot facility will house space for a growing student life program at ASU East. Included in the building will be recreational game-room space, study areas, modern dining facilities, workspace for student organizations, a campus bookstore, and multipurpose meeting space.

"Our new student union is designed to become the center of the campus community, and every step in the design process was made with students in mind," says Backus. "This building will breathe new life into our student activities program, and our growing student population will benefit from it for years to come."

The new union is the first building ASU East has constructed for full-time use since the campus opened in 1996. The other new buildings on campus, a book storage facility and greenhouse, are not used regularly by faculty, staff or students. The remaining ASU East buildings are renovated former Air Force facilities.

Scheduled for completion in August 2004, the new student union will significantly increase the space available for student activities, leisure activities and dining. The current student union, a renovated former officer's club, provides minimal space for student life programs and meetings. The campus dining hall is outdated and cannot meet the demands of the campus's growing population.

"Because of our rapid enrollment growth, we have many different facilities needs," says ASU East Dean of Student Affairs Gary McGrath. "This campus made the new student union a priority because we realize the importance of building a sense of community and having a place for faculty, staff and students to congregate. And that's what this building does."

Inspiration for the student union is drawn from initial landscape concepts developing on campus. ASU East is beginning the process of turning stark, former Air Force grounds into a vibrant, desert landscape with pedestrian malls that link core buildings.

The student union is designed to maximize optimum solar orientation and take advantage of the Valley's seasonal climate by use of fluid indoor-outdoor spaces. Glass garage doors will allow programs to expand outdoors. A simple metal skin will wrap the building and serve as a vertical shading device on east and west elevations. Horizontal shading devices will work in tandem with desert trees to provide comfort throughout the year.

Funding for the building comes primarily from monies generated by the voter-approved Proposition 301 sales tax. Additional funds were provided by the ASU Bookstore. Money for landscaping will come from private support. The project budget is $5.4 million.

The architectural firm Gould Evans Associates of Phoenix designed the state-of-the-art facility. The building's general contractor is Turner Construction of Phoenix.
