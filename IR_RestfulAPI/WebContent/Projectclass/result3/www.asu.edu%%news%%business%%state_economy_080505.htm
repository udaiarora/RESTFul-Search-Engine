Report: State’s economy looks solid at midyear

Halfway through 2005, the Arizona economy appears on pace to record the strongest gains in employment and personal income since the days of the roaring 1990s, according to the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

“The latest consensus forecast for 2005 job growth from the Blue Chip panel calls for employment to increase by 4.1 percent this year, the largest jump since the 4.3 percent gain posted back in 1999,” says Lee McPheters, Arizona Blue Chip contributing editor.

Arizona ranks second among all states (behind Nevada) in the rate of job creation. The number of new jobs projected for this year has been raised to 97,300.

“This is some 20,000 more new jobs than the 77,200 added in 2004,” McPheters notes. “The last time Arizona created as many new jobs was 10 years ago as the tech boom got under way.”

Population growth has continued strong as Arizona attracts domestic relocaters and, increasingly, international migrants. The Blue Chip panel projects that Arizona retail sales will be up by 7.7 percent in 2005.

Low mortgage rates have kept the Arizona housing market pumped up throughout the year, and few expect any sort of reversal before 2006.

The long-term forecasts for income growth, employment, and retail sales in Arizona are lower than those for this year. While the Arizona economy is solid at midyear, the 2006 outlook is for slightly weaker growth, McPheters says.

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at ASU.
