November 11, 2003

ASU strengthens partnerships with Mexico

Nov. 4, 2003 was a historic day as Arizona welcomed President of Mexico Vicente Fox for the first time.

Fox began a three-day visit to the southwestern United States with a stop in Phoenix to discuss economic development and international partnerships with Gov. Janet Napolitano.

ASU President Michael Crow represented the state's higher education system and spoke about the importance of building a bi-national partnership that would enhance economic and academic relationships between Arizona and Mexico.

"It's time for a regional economy between Mexico and Arizona to go to the next step," Crow said.

ASU is currently strengthening its research efforts in biotechnology and technology transfer through partnerships with universities and corporations, in and outside of the United States - an effort that would strengthen the economy in the southwestern region, Crow said.

"Through biotechnology and technology transfer efforts we can develop partnerships to help this regional economy compete with those of other regions," he said.

Doug Parker, chairman and CEO of America West Airlines, spoke on behalf of Arizona's business community and the potential of import/export relations between the United States and Mexico.

"Your vision for a free market serves as a foundation for a better tomorrow," he said. "Tremendous potential for growth exists for our two countries."

Gov. Napolitano echoed Crow and Parker and said President Fox's visit is the beginning of a strategic effort to improve the economy in the southwestern region.

"Your presence can only strengthen the bond between Arizona and Mexico," Napolitano said. "Arizona and Mexico can strategize together. To do this we have to be smart, strategic and sit down together to think about possibilities. I envision Arizona being the lead trade window for the United States, between the United States and Mexico."

President Fox said the meeting with Arizona's leaders and business community is important.

"The trade partnership between my country and this state make Arizona the fourth largest trading partner for us," said Fox.

"We want to advance in strategic alliances, improving and enhancing our capacity to compete world-wide," Fox said.
