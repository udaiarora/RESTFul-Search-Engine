June 9, 2003

Doctoral students win prestigious ARCS scholarship

Fourteen ASU doctoral students have won the 2003 Achievement Reward for College Scientists (ARCS) Scholarship. The Phoenix ARCS Foundation has awarded $6,000 scholarships to each of the students in support of their dynamic research projects. One of 12 ARCS Foundation chapters in the nation, the Phoenix chapter annually awards scholarships to the top graduate student researchers at Arizona's three state universities.

ARCS chapters support graduate student research in an effort to help America remain technologically strong and internationally competitive. Local and national ARCS organizations provide financial assistance for gifted Ph.D. students in the natural sciences, engineering and medicine. Scholarship recipients are graduate students of exceptional academic promise whose scholastic endeavors demonstrate potential for widespread benefits. Among the ARCS Scholars at ASU this year:

. Thomas Attard (civil structural engineering) is developing simpler and more practical analysis methods to predict large structural deformations caused by earthquakes;
. Michael Beaumont (bioengineering) is researching the possibility of spinal cord injury repair;
. Chandra Mitnik (microbiology) produces mutations in viruses that preferentially destroy breast cancer cells without destroying normal breast cells. This system can produce a targeted therapy for cancer, potentially reducing side effects while increasing efficacy; and
. Corey Tyree (chemical engineering) is studying the connection between air pollution and human health.

Additional 2003 ARCS scholars are: Jason Ayubi-Moak (electrical engineering), Michael Berg and Elizabeth Jacobsen (molecular and cellular biology), Sherry Di Jorio, Derek Dosdall and Brandon Seal (bioengineering), Aaron Johnson (biology), Timothy Lant (mathematics), Michael Rothrock (microbiology) and Paul Dahlen (civil and environmental engineering).

Durband, with the Graduate College, can be reached at (480) 965-6426, or (djdurb@asu.edu).
