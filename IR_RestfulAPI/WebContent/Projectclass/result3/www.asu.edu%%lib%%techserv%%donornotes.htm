Policy on donor notes and donor added entries for bibliographic records


Definitions


Donor note: a local note (MARC tag 590) on a bibliographic record specifying the person or organization responsible for providing the material to the Libraries.


Donor added entry: an author added entry (MARC tag 7xx) for the person or organization mentioned in the donor note. A donor added entry must be justified by a donor note in the body of the record and must have a corresponding authority record in the local authority file.


(Please note that this policy has nothing to do with gift bookplates.)


Guidelines

  • Donor recognition of this type is for special collections material only, not for stacks material.
  • The policy applies primarily to monographs; donor notes and added entries may be made for serials but are not normally provided to apply to a small number of issues. Donated manuscripts will be handled according to the manuscript cataloging rules for donor information. Other formats will be dealt with on a case-by-case basis.
  • Requests for donor notes and donor added entries must come from the appropriate collection head or from the person who is making the special collections location decision. The requestor is responsible for providing the necessary information to the cataloger (see Procedure, below)
  • The requestor may specify donor note only, without the accompanying added entry. This is sometimes done for large collections, or for continuing donations, or in the case of group donations. (see Labriola family example, below)
  • Donor information must be supplied at the time of cataloging; donor information will not be added retrospectively.
  • The standard note reads: Gift from [person or organization]; the standard added entry includes the term donor. Other terminology may be used if more appropriate.

Examples


Note: 590 Gift from Jane Doe.
AE: 700 1 Doe, Jane, $e donor.

Note: 590 Gift from Mr. and Mrs. Richard Roe.
AE: 700 1 Roe, Richard, $e donor.
AE: 700 1 Roe, Mary, $e donor.

Note: 590 Gift from the Peacock Company.
AE: 710 2 Peacock Company, $e donor.

Note: 590 From the collection of John Doe.
AE: 700 1 Doe, John, $e former owner.

Note: 590 Gift from Mary Doe in memory of her father, Professor Richard Roe.
AE: 700 1 Doe, Mary, $e donor.
AE: 700 1 Roe, Richard, $e honoree.


[Collection name is specified when Library holds other copies to which the note does not apply]

Note: 590 Labriola Collection copy: Purchased with the assistance of the Labriola family.
AE: 7XX Added entry cannot be made for a donor of this type.


[Volumes are specified when the note does not apply to an entire multi-part monograph or serial]

Note: 590 Vols. 10-30: Gift from the Phoenix Indian Center.
AE: 710 2 Phoenix Indian Center, $e donor.


Please be aware of these limitations: neither the 590 note nor the $e term are indexed. It is not possible to assemble a list of one person’s or organization’s donations from note information. It is not possible to retrieve by customary searching techniques a list restricted to donations when the name of the person or organization also appears in the catalog for other reasons. For example, donor added entries for a prolific novelist will be interfiled with entries on records for the person’s works.


Procedure

Provide, in each title to be cataloged, the following information:

  • Name of the donor as it should appear in the donor note.
  • Any additional information available that could be used to construct an added entry unique to the person(s) or organization involved, e.g. forename for the wife in a Mr. and Mrs. combination; birth date(s), middle names or initials, etc., especially if the names involved are common ones.
  • When appropriate, the information that only a note, and not an accompanying added entry, is required.

Use a Gift Flag or any other obvious means of providing the necessary information in a clear form. It is necessary to flag each item even in a collection because the material will not necessarily be kept together or cataloged entirely by the same person.


Discuss any problems, questions, or special requests in advance with a member of the TS Management Team.
