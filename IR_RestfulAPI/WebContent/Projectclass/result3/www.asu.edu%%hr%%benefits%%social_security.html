Social Security

Social Security is a federally required retirement, disability and supplemental health insurance program intended to provide a portion of your retirement income, as well as disability and survivor's insurance during your working years.

Both employees and the university contribute equally to Social Security. Your social security contribution is in two parts:

1) FICA/OASI with a premium of 6.20% of salary, and

2) Medicare with a premium of 1.45% of salary.

The FICA/OASI taxable wage base ceiling for 2005 is $94,200 and for Medicare it is unlimited amount.

 

Retirement Earnings

If you are still working and choose to also collect a Social Security benefit after age 62 but prior to your normal retirement age (65 - 67 depending on your year of birth), money will be deducted from your monthly benefit payment according to the following:

•  Your monthly payment will be reduced by $1.00 for every $2.00 you earn between $12,000 and $31,800 annually,

•  Your monthly payment will be reduced by $1.00 for every $3.00 you earn over $31,800.

Individuals who have reached their normal retirement age can continue to work and earn any amount without a reduction.
