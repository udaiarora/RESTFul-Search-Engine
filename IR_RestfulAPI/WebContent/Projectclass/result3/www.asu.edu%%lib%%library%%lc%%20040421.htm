Librarians' Council


Minutes of Meeting

 

April 21, 2004
Hayden C6A

A quorum was present.

I. Approval of Minutes (February 18, 2004)

 

– Minutes were approved.

 

II. Chair & Executive Committee's Report- Scott Herrington

 

III. Dean’s Report –Sherrie Schmidt

 

            The following announcements were made:

1.      The Provost announced that enhancements will be made to salary adjustments given for promotion for Academic Professionals. Assistant to Associate will go from $4,000 to $4,500; Associate to Full will go from $6,000 to $6,500. These will be allocated from the Provost’s Office.

2.      The Provost indicated that ASU will begin offering co-branded degrees with the Mayo Clinic.

3.      The search for the Associate Dean of Library Services is proceeding. The top five candidates have been identified. The quality of candidates is quite good.

4.      As a follow-up to the President’s response to the University Design Team’s Report, the Dean and the Provost have begun discussions on the definition of the concept of University Librarian.

5.      An announcement was read stating that the winter closure (December-January) will no longer occur.

  Academic Task Force Report- Kurt Murphy

The report is now complete and available on the Provost’s website. Key elements of the Task Force’s recommendation for Academic Professionals included: the continuation of academic freedom; continued availability of sabbaticals; Academic Professionals with continuing appointment should retain it; Academic Professionals on continuing appointment track should remain on it; and the evaluation of professional development should be left up to the individual units.

Questions ensued.

 

IV. Committee Reports

 

a.       Personnel Committee – Lydia Lafaro

            A second reading of the committee’s revisions of the Librarians’ Handbook J.1

            Orientation will be presented as Old Business. A revised draft of revisions to I.1

            Sabbatical Leave will also be presented in New Business. The issue of the

            Mentoring Program will be revisted under Old Business.

b.       Research and Professional Development Committee – Sue Hunter

            A reminder to all AP’s to alert the committee of cancelled travel plans in order to

            have the funds reallocated.

 

c.    Committee on Academic Professional Status – Sue Hunter

           

            Elections have been closed and the results will be announced on Monday, April 26.

 

d.    Nominations and Elections Committee- Phil Konomos

            There was a call for nominations from the floor. None were made. The ballot will

            be sent tomorrow (April 22) to be returned by May 17.

d.       Academic Senate – Marcia Anderson

             The Senate will discuss the proposed revisions to the Promotion and Tenure

            Guidelines.


V. Old Business

 

The second reading of proposed changes to Librarians’ Handbook, Orientation Program (Section J.1) was done. The Personnel Committee added two ammendments to it. A vote was taken and the changes were accepted.

Question on viability of Mentoring Program (Section K.1) was asked. There was consensus that the program is valuable and worth continuing.

VI. New Business


The first reading of revisions to Sabbatical Leave procedures, Librarians’ Handbook (Section I.1) was presented.

 

Meeting adjourned 3:35pm.
