Get involved! Residential Life is committed to leadership development within the halls and across the ASU campus. In conjunction with many other ASU leadership programs and departments, we are committed to the development of student leaders by providing, promoting and supporting leadership opportunities.

Residential Life "Citizen of the Month" Award
Residential Life student and professional staff members strive to educate and provide opportunities for residents to be positive citizens of their communities.On a monthly basis each residential community will award up to two ‘Citizen of the Month’ Awards to deserving residents who have exemplified the following characteristics:

Someone who respects the lives of those around whether significant or not.
Someone who strives to make another person's life better just by being a part of it.
Someone who looks beyond themselves to find ways to make life easier, happier or more manageable for another.
Someone who abides by the rules set by society understanding how they can personally affect the lives around them both positively and negatively.

Congratulations to our monthly winners. Thank you for all you do to make the world (and your community) a better place!

Behavior Education Council (BEC)

The Behavior Education Council (BEC) serves as a peer review board for residents who have been documented for violating Residential Life and/or ASU Student Code of Conduct. The BEC is made up of undergraduate students of which the majority currently reside on campus. The main focus of the BEC is ensuring that the residential communities on campus are positive and conducive to learning from the perspective of peers.

Residence Hall Association (RHA)

The Residence Hall Association (RHA) at Arizona State University works within Residential Life and university communities to encourage awareness of the residential student perspective in personnel selection, programming, fiscal decision-making, long-range planning, and short-term problem solving. In order to effectively accomplish all of its goals, RHA maintains a close working relationship with Residential Life and the Associated Students of Arizona State University (ASASU). RHA is comprised of a board of directors and individual hall councils. Together they act as a student government for the residence halls and provide a wide range of programs, services, and leadership opportunities including the following: The Robert A. Kalin Memorial Scholarship, Casey Self Leadership Scholarship Awards, Sun Devil 101, Homecoming activities, Channel 2 programming.

RHA meets once a week, and meetings are open to all residents. If you are interested in participating or have questions about RHA, call 480-965-5809, e-mail rha@asu.edu, or visit the Web site at www.asu.edu/clubs/rha. (link - open a new window)
