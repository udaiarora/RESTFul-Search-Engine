ASU film crew documents ‘Fourteen Days in America’

A film crew from ASU took part in a unique 14-city tour of the United States to document “Fourteen Days in America,” a project by British still photographer David Gibbons to photograph and interview average people going about their daily lives in the wake of the 9/11 terrorist attacks.

The crew of professional staff and student interns are from ASU’s Technology Based Learning and Research (TBLR) group, part of ASU’s College of Education.

The ASU film crew accompanied Gibbons beginning Aug. 18 in New York, documenting Americans’ resolve to safeguard freedom and the American way of life in these times of uncertainty and political polarization. The aim of the project was to create and preserve a snapshot of the real America through photos and personal interviews with people from many walks of life, representing the cultural, economic and social values of our time.

Gibbons, an award-winning photographer and principal at Lighthouse Imaging in Scottsdale, created the “Fourteen Days in America” idea and sought ASU’s involvement in the project.

The ASU documentary film crew was under the direction of Chris LaMont. In addition to professional staff, three student interns were able to experience a unique field learning opportunity, assisting with film photography, production and subject interviews.

Paul Skiera, director of ASU’s Technology Based Learning and Research, supervised ASU’s role as the project’s official documentalist.

“This is a great opportunity for ASU to be part of a history-making event,” Skiera said as the project got under way. “It’s the perfect vehicle for our talented staff of film professionals and it gives our student interns a chance to work on a real, multiple-location film shoot.”

In addition to the film documentary of the tour, plans call for a book featuring still photographs with a CD of personal interviews. The photos and film will become part of a traveling exhibit.

The group traveled by RV caravan, with professional drivers moving the group from town to town overnight as the documentary crew slept and prepared for the next day’s activities.

Technology Based Learning and Research is an independent research development entity at ASU. As an integral part of the College of Education, TBLR focuses on research and large-scale delivery of educational materials, as well as technology training and integration using computers and other information and communication technologies. TBLR projects have been funded by the Arizona Department of Education, the Fund for the Improvement of Post Secondary Education, the National Science Foundation, the U.S. Department of Education, and major corporations such as Cisco, Texas Instruments, Apple and IBM.
