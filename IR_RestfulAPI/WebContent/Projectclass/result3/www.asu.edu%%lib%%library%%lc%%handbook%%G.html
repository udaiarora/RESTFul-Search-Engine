Librarians' Council

G: Introduction

Effective 5/16/90 .. Revised 2/26/99

All University personnel receive an annual performance evaluation of some kind. Annual performance evaluations are designed to serve several purposes. The process provides academic professionals with a regular opportunity to consider the direction and progress of their careers; encourages communication between academic professionals and their primary evaluators; encourages advance planning activities; and builds a career file that makes preparation of material of other personnel actions easier.


The academic professional's self-review forms the central part of the annual evaluation. The self-review is based on the academic professional's assessment of success in meeting the year's goals. The evaluation procedure as a whole is intended to be performance-based and to avoid as much as possible reliance on subjective judgments of intangible attributes. Satisfactory performance is defined as meeting the goals agreed upon between the academic professional and primary evaluator at the beginning of the review period or as renegotiated during the period.


In some Library departments, the Department/Unit Head is the primary evaluator. Library units organized as teams or in another manner will establish by mutual consent and approval by the University Libraries administration, annual evaluation procedures conforming as closely as possible to this section of the Librarian's Handbook. In those units the term "primary evaluator" may be taken to mean the person recognized as, and responsible for, performing the primary evaluator's functions. In all cases the AP must know in advance who will serve as the primary evaluator (PE).


In order for the evaluation process to work as intended, each academic professional must provide a clear and accurate self-review and goal statement. Self-reviews should be professional in tone. Both successes and failures should be treated as objectively as possible, with the emphasis throughout placed on actions and results.


Evaluation activities are scheduled by the Assistant Dean for Personnel in accordance with the University calendar. Every effort is made to allow sufficient time for each stage of annual evaluations. It is imperative that each person involved in the process strive to meet the scheduled deadlines so the process can proceed smoothly.


Annual evaluation is intended to be a constructive and amicable process. Frank and open communication on a professional level, whether written or oral, should be the goal of all parties involved. At the end of the process, participants should have a clear picture of the past year's accomplishments and of the plans for the coming year.
