All employees must become familiar with the established work rules of the university and the department/college in which they work. Inappropriate behavior and/or failure to follow established work rules can result in disciplinary action up to and including employment termination.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
What is considered an inappropriate behavior?
Inappropriate behavior may include, but is not limited to:
ABSENCE
A pattern develops based on:
The number of absences or tardiness
Leaving work early without prior approval

APPEARANCE (according to established college/department standards)
Coming to work without department uniform
Wearing inappropriate department dress

CONDUCT (according to job expectations)
Unsatisfactory job performance
Unbecoming conduct
Discourtesy toward others

What are some examples of established work rules?
They may include, but are not limited to, the following:
PROPERTY
 Theft of university property
Falsifying university documents

RULES AND REGULATIONS (according to established university and college/department)
Violating safety rules
Falsifying resume or application materials

SUBSTANCE ABUSE
Consuming alcoholic beverages while on the job
Selling or purchasing a controlled substance

What can be done when inappropriate behavior is exhibited and/or rules are violated?
Contact a higher authority within your department/college or contact an HR Consultant.
