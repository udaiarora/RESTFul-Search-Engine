January 15, 2003

Crow announces Alumni Association leadership change

Robert Soza, assistant vice president of Student Affairs and Dean of Students, has been appointed Interim Executive Director of the ASU Alumni Association, it was announced January 15, 2003, by ASU President Michael M. Crow and Vice President for Public Affairs Virgil Renzulli.

Soza, who received a bachelor and masters degree from the ASU College of Education, will serve as Executive Director while a national search is conducted for a successor to Susan Dolbert, who will be taking on other advancement responsibilities at ASU. Dolbert also will conclude the negotiation of several important business contracts for the Alumni Association. Dolbert has expressed a desire to return to student affairs but is also considering an offer to take a key fund-raising post at ASU.

"On the foundation that Susan has helped build, ASU will move forward with the intent of building an alumni program that not only ranks at the top of the Pac 10, but that is second to none on a national level," said President Crow. "I thank Susan for her years of outstanding effort on behalf of our alumni and look forward to working with her as she continues to apply her considerable skills and energy to the advancement of ASU."

Even while the search for a successor begins, the planning process for the future of the Alumni Association is beginning on several levels. Working with the alumni volunteer leadership, Soza will spearhead the development of a strategic plan for enhancing the university's alumni relations office and effort. Goals include:

. Increasing the number of alumni who are actively involved with the university;

. Improving the coordination among the Alumni Association, the school alumni offices and the various alumni clubs.

. Increasing both the total amount of alumni giving to the university and the number of individual alumni contributors.

. Developing ways of working more closely with Development and Intercollegiate Athletics on activities and other efforts involving alumni.

. Strengthening existing alumni traditions and developing new ones.

A search committee is being formed that will include a number of prominent alumni including current Alumni Association Chairman Martin Shultz, Alumni Association Chairman-elect R. J. Machulies; immediate past Chairman Nicolette Lemmon, and Donald Isaacson.

Dolbert earned a bachelor's degree in political science at ASU in 1974, a master's degree in communication in 1980, and a doctorate in public administration in 1994. Prior to becoming executive director of the Alumni Association, she worked in ASU's College of Engineering & Applied Sciences and later served as Director of Undergraduate Admissions.

"Among their accomplishments," said Vice President Renzulli, "Susan, her staff and our dedicated volunteers have achieved the restoration of Old Main, the expansion of the Student Alumni Association from 30 members to 3,622 this year, and the revitalization of the association's chapters program. I thank Susan for a her hard work and wish her well in her new endeavors."
