Bringing LEED to China
Sustainablebusiness.com
On Nov. 10, 2005, U.S. Green Building Council (USGBC) President Rick Fedrizzi presented leadership awards to 10 Chinese real estate developers and government leaders for their "pioneering work in transforming the world's largest building industry." The developers, who represent some of China's largest developers, have one thing in common: they are the first to pursue USGBC's Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) certification.

Energy Efficient Houses are Becoming more Affortable
The Pueblo Chieftain Online
Maybe you've been reading about green building methods and materials and thinking that living the eco-friendly lifestyle is a great idea - if you're made of money. Think again. Recently, Sunset magazine opened the doors to its latest Idea Houses, a side-by-side pair showcasing all manner of recycled or sustainable building materials that demonstrate how mainstream manufacturers are jumping on the green bandwagon.

Plan B: 2.0:  Rescuing a Planet under Stress & a Civilization in Trouble
Sustainablebusiness.com
“Our global civilization today is on an economic path that is environmentally unsustainable, a path that is leading us toward economic decline and eventual collapse," says Lester Brown in his new book, Plan B 2.0: Rescuing a Planet Under Stress and a Civilization in Trouble.

University of Oregon Establish Energy Conservation and Alternative Futures Fund
AASHE
The Associated Students of the University of Oregon has created an Energy Conservation and Alternative Futures Fund to distribute approximately $25,000 for student projects that focus on energy conservation, increase renewable energy usage, and/or educate the campus community about energy issues.

7-Eleven Japan Promotes Paperless Accounting in Stores
GreenBiz
7-Eleven Japan Co., a major convenience store chain in Japan, has moved toward a paperless system by converting to the use of electronic data instead of paper for most supplier transaction and accounting records.

Water Crises Prompt Governments to Seek Help from End-Users-UN University Book
EurekAlert!
More than a billion people lack sufficient clean water to meet their basic needs; 27 nations don't have enough water and another 16 are 'water stressed'. The UN predicts that rising demand for water will threaten human and ecological health over the next generation.
