Engineering academy appoints Evans as a senior fellow

Don Evans, emeritus professor in the department of mechanical and aerospace engineering at ASU's Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, has been appointed an Engineering Education Senior Fellow at the National Academy of Engineering (NAE).

Evans is one of the first three such appointees. Each will serve for six months and produce analyses relevant to one of four research thrusts at NAE's Center for the Advancement of Scholarship on Engineering Education.

Evans, the founding director of ASU's Center for Research on Science, Mathematics, Education and Technology (CRESMET), has been deeply involved in science education for 20 years. CRESMET is a joint venture between the Fulton School of Engineering and ASU's College of Liberal Arts and Sciences and College of Education.

Evans, who retired this past July, has been a professor at ASU for 37 years. He remains active in educational issues and activities.

Evans will report on progress toward classroom implementation of effective assessment strategies called Concept Inventories, which measure students understanding of important engineering concepts. The Concept Inventory method has been used with undergraduate students for more than 15 years and is now credited with stimulating reform of education in physics, biology and geology.

Evans says too much science education focuses on facts and not enough on understanding of content. As a result, student misconceptions about a topic can persist even after a student goes through a course.

"Student misconceptions often persist through traditional forms of instruction," Evans said. "In order to change those misconceptions, we have to do something different in the classroom. There are many things that can be done, but it has to be different from what we are doing now, which typically is the 50-minute lecture."

The Concept Inventory is one way an instructor can measure how well he/she breaks down a student's misconceptions about a topic.

In addition to Evans, the other two Senior Fellows are:

Myron Tribus, a consulting engineer, who was director of MIT's Center for Advanced Engineering Study and a senior vice president for research and engineering at Xerox Corp. Tribus will focus on applying the management principles of W. Edwards Deming and the teaching-learning principles of Reuven Feuerstein to engineering education.

Karan Watson, associate provost and dean of faculties, and professor of electrical engineering at Texas A&M University. Watson will survey instructional and institutional strategies that value diversity among people, learning styles and perspectives.
