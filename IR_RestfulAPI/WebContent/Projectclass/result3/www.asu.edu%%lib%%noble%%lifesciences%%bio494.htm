Finding books and other materials in the online catalog

The online catalog can be used to locate books that are owned by the library. The online catalog can also be used to locate journals (but not articles), newspapers, microforms sets, reference works, sound recordings, videos, government documents and Special Collections materials, and other items the library owns.

The easiest way to start searching for materials in in the online catalog is to do a keyword search. Remember to combine your keywords with appropriate connectors (and, or, not), and to search for alternate endings by truncating with an asterisk (*).

bird* and behavioral ecology

(avian or bird*) and ecology

When you find a books or other materials that you want in the online catalog, be sure to write down the entire call number and note where the item is located (such as SCIENCE 3rd Floor or HAYDEN Stacks). You will need this information to find the item in the library.

For more information about finding books and other materials, see SunTutor: Finding Books .

Finding Articles

Remember: In order to access these databases from off-campus, you need to sign in first with your ASURITE ID and password.

The key to efficiently finding journal articles is to use an index or database. These tools will provide a citation, which will then help you in locating the article. Consult the list of the Best Databases for Biology Research to help you choose the most appropriate database for your topic.

The easiest way to start searching for articles in a database is to do a keyword search. Remember to combine your keywords with appropriate connectors (and, or, not), and to search for alternate endings by truncating with an asterisk (*).

bird* AND migration

The database will generate a list of articles based on the keywords you have entered. This list of articles should contain citation information that will help you locate the specific article either in print or electronically through an E-journal. Some databases provide full-text access to articles, but not all provide this convenience. If a link to the full-text article is not provided, you will need to check in the catalog and E-journals list to see if ASU Libraries has the journal.

Does ASU Libraries have the journal I need? : How to locate specific articles.

If you already have information about a specific article (author, title, journal title, volume, etc.), you need to find out where the journal is available. There are two ways to find out if ASU Libraries has a particular journal.

Electronic Journals
Use this option to find out if a journal is available electronically. You can then browse the journal to find the volume and page number of the article you need.

Tip: Be sure to search for the journal title, not the article title.

Journal Title Search in the online catalog
Use to find if the ASU Libraries has a print copy of the journal you are looking for. Be sure to write down the entire call number and its location (e.g SCIENCE 2nd FLOOR or HAYDEN JOURNALS). Sometimes, a link to the electronic version is also available.

Tip: Be sure to search for the journal title, not the article title.

Getting articles not available at ASU

The library can get you a copy of an article that is not available electronically or in print through the Interlibrary Loan service. This service is free, and takes between one and three days for your article to arrive.

Finding information on the Web

The Web can provide a wealth of information, from up-to-date news stories to individual opinions on every topic imaginable. There are several reasons to use the Web for research such as to find current information, to find facts quickly, and for easy access to information. It is important to be aware that not all information on the Web is accurate and to not rely on the Web for all of your research - good research requires the use of different types of resources.

Searching the Web

Google - the best all around search engine to find information on the Web.
Tip: put phrases in "quotation marks"

Scirus - is a search engine for scientific information. You can search for both Web pages and journal articles (Free full-text access is not available through this resource).

For more information see SunTutor: Finding Information on the Web

Best Databases for Animal Behavior/ Ecology Research

Animal Behavior Abstracts (1982+) Covers significant papers relevant to animal behavior, with subjects ranging from neurophysiology to behavioral ecology, from genetics to applied ethology, are covered. Indexes important journals dealing with the biology of particular taxonomic groups.

Biological and Agricultural Index (7/1983+) Includes book reviews, chapters in annual reviews of research, journal supplements, & articles. Subjects include biochemistry, biotechnology, botany, ecology, marine biology, microbiology, nutrition, physiology, & zoology. Indexes around 250 journals.

BioOne (2000+) Provides full-text of over 100 high-impact bioscience research journals published by small societies and non-commercial publishers. Journals focus on the biological, ecological and environmental sciences.

BIOSIS Previews (1969+) The largest index in Biomedicine and the Life Sciences. Indexes journal articles, books, chapters in books, papers and conference proceedings. Subjects include agriculture, biochemistry, biotechnology, ecology, immunology, microbiology, neuroscience, toxicology, etc. Indexes around 5,000 biology journals in 100 different languages.

Chemoreception Abstracts (1982+) Covers disciplines relating to the neurobiology, chemistry, and physiology of taste, smell, internal chemoreception, and chemotaxis

Ecology Abstracts (1982+) Indexes journal articles, books & other sources on climate impact, conservation, ecosystems, erosion, food chains, habitats, land reclamation, pollution, soil, water resources, etc. Focuses on interactions between organisms their environments.

Entomology Abstracts (1981+) Covers research reports of direct relevance to Entomology, including insects, arachnids, myriapods, onychophorans, and terrestrial isopods.

Fish and Fisheries Worldwide (1966+) Covers ichthyology, fisheries, & related aspects of aquaculture. Major topics include propagation, limnology, oceanography, genetics, behavior, natural history, diseases, habitat management, general research, & fisheries management.

Neurosciences Abstracts (1982+) Covers all aspects of vertebrate and invertebrate neuroscience, emphasizing basic research studies but also including coverage of neural diseases.

Web of Science (Science Citation Index Expanded) - (1955+) Provides access to current bibliographic information and cited references. Covers approximately 5,800 of the world's leading scientific and technical journals in a broad range of disciplines.

Wildlife Worldwide (1935+) Indexes literature on wild mammals, birds, reptiles, & amphibians. Covers all aspects of wildlife and wildlife. Consolidates Wildlife Review Abstracts; Swiss Wildlife Information Service; Wildlife Database; BIODOC; Waterfowl & Wetlands Database.

Zoological Record (1978+) Indexes zoological literature (professional journals, magazines, newsletters, monographs, books, dissertations & conference proceedings). Covers behavior, ecology, evolution, habitat, nutrition, parasites, reproduction, taxonomy, & zoogeography

Helpful Library Research Resources

WorldCat (OCLC) (1200+) Books and other materials in libraries worldwide. Includes bibliographic records and information for any type of material cataloged by OCLC member libraries. Excellent place to find bibliographic information about books and other materials, particularly those not owned by ASU Libraries. Includes information about other libraries' holdings.

Ulrich's Periodical Directory (UlrichsWeb) (1979+) Provides bibliographic information about periodicals, annuals, & irregulars published worldwide. Has searching and browsing features for periodicals in all subject areas. Also includes information about indexing and abstracting coverage for specific titles.

Citing your sources

It is important to cite the resources you use properly and consistently. A citation serves two purposes. First, it acknowledges the author whose work you are using, and second, it allows others to locate the specific resourse you have cited. In the sciences, there are many different types of citation styles. For this class, you will use the citation style that is used in the journal Behavioral Ecology.

Most journals have instructions to authors on their Websites. Information on how to reference and cite articles is generally included in these instructions. The instructions to authors also includes information on manuscript preparation, which will be helpful when writing your review paper.
