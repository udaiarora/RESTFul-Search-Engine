September 17, 2004

U.S. AID grant boosts new dual degree program

Through the support of a U.S. AID grant, the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering and Tec de Monterrey, or Institute Technologico y de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey (ITESM), have launched a new dual-degree program to improve the global competitiveness of the North American aerospace industry.

The pioneering master of science program also focuses on the logistics of developing the technical human resources needed to consolidate the emerging aerospace supplies base in Mexico .

The program involves cultural exchange and global experience for students and faculty, as well as student interaction with industry sponsors. Twenty-one students from ITESM and 21 ASU students won scholarships to participate as members of bi-national teams, addressing bi-national supply chain issues.

“We are very proud and excited to be here,” says Carlos Calleros, ITESM industrial engineering student. “I will bring this education back to our country to help with its development.”

Calleros is studying at the Fulton School this semester to become a technical leader who is knowledgeable about global culture and communication issues.

“The aerospace industry has just taken off in Monterrey ,” he says. “It is very important that people like us come here to learn new ideas in order to prepare ourselves better.”

The program will be beneficial for both economies, as the United States and Mexico continue to build their industrial relationships in an increasingly interdependent world. “We depend on Mexico , and Mexico depends on us,” says Mark Henderson, ASU industrial engineering professor. “We can establish both of our strengths and solve problems from an international perspective.”

ASU Industrial Engineering Professor Rene Villalobos was highly instrumental in setting up the new program, and he praised ITESM for having an ordered educational system.

“The Mexican education system is more structured than the U.S. … more technical,” he says.

Such global engagement falls in line with ASU President Michael Crow’s objectives for ASU as the new American university. This program is positioned to grow over the coming years with increased collaboration from Mexican universities.

With the first semester just getting under way, Professor Gary Hogg, chairman of the Department of Industrial Engineering says, “We are delighted to have the students and we welcome them like family. What a great opportunity for both sides of the border.”

Crawford, with the Fulton School, can be reached at (480) 965-0289 or (christine.crawford@asu.edu).
