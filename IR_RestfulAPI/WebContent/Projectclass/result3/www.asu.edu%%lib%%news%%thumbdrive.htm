Library Workstations Now Support USB Thumb Drives


(posted 3/26/04)

The public workstations now support USB "thumb drives." What makes these small portable devices so useful is that they have much more storage space than a floppy disk. Most floppy disks hold 1.44 Megabytes (MB) of information, while thumb drives can hold 16 MB or more. The thumb drives have the added advantage of being faster and more reliable than floppies. They also come with handy ways of storing them, such as a built-in clip or attached key ring.

The drive plugs into the USB port, which is located on the front of the black workstations behind the gray panel. On the older white workstations, the USB ports are located in the back. Once the drive is plugged in, a dialog box will pop up notifying that the drive has been found. When the dialog box is finished, the drive is ready and files can be saved just like a floppy disk. Typically the drive will show up as "Removable Disk D:." Before removing the drive, it is important to make sure it is properly shut down. This is accomplished by clicking the "Unplug or Eject Hardware" icon in the lower right (has a green arrow), and choosing to stop the device. A message will pop up on the screen saying the device can safely be removed, and the drive can be unplugged. If this step is not properly taken, all data on the drive may be lost.


Please note that it is not guaranteed that all drives will work, though testing has not revealed any problems.
