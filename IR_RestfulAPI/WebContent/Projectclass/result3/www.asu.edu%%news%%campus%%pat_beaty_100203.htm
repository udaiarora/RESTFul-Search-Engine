October 2, 2003

Pat Beaty joins Colorado State as vice chancellor

Pat Beaty, vice president of Strategic Planning and Marketing for the ASU Foundation, has been named vice chancellor for Strategic Planning for the Colorado State University System. She will be leaving the Foundation after a 15-year affiliation with ASU to join the leadership team of CSU Chancellor and President Larry E. Penley. Pat will assume her new position on Nov. 1.

Beaty joined the leadership team of the ASU Foundation in 2001. For 13 years prior to that, she was an independent consultant to ASU, including seven years as a senior consultant on the ASU Campaign for Leadership.

"Pat has played a significant role in shaping programs and initiatives over the years," says Foundation President Lonnie Ostrom. "She has a gift for bringing ideas together, positioning them, and creating the case for private investments. She has contributed immensely to ASU's growth and development."

As a special consultant, Beaty wrote the feasibility study for the campaign and worked with academic leaders to develop and frame major campaign priorities. She has worked with deans and provosts to develop numerous successful major gift and naming proposals, including the recent naming gift for the W. P. Carey School of Business.

Beaty has helped develop the Rodel Community Scholars Program in the College of Business, the university-wide Scholarship Initiative and the Arizona Heritage Chair program. She collaborated with Elva Coor to establish the President's Community Enrichment Programs (PCEP), a model university-community partnership that has grown over the last three years to include more than 900 community participants and almost 50 faculty.

Beaty's strategic planning efforts include working closely with Ostrom and the Foundation Board to draft Strategic Plan 2000, which transformed the organization in just 18 months from a virtual entity with no employees into a full-time philanthropic organization with more than 75 employees. She is finalizing her work on the board's new strategic plan as the work of the Foundation expands to include preparing for the next comprehensive campaign, assuming responsibility for technology commercialization at ASU and exploring ways to support ASU through real estate acquisition and development.

As the founding vice president of Strategic Planning and Marketing, Pat also established the Foundation's marketing communications staff.

"Pat is one person who really is irreplaceable," says Ostrom. "She's a walking archive and a great collaborator. People across the university have great trust in her and appreciate the quality of her work. She made this a better place and will be missed by many."

Bruce, with the ASU Foundation, can be reached at (480) 965-4429 or (margaret.bruce@asu.edu).
