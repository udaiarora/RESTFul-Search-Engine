Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

March 1, 2005 ? Called to order 10:00 am

Members Present:  Barbara Trapido-Lurie (Chair), Beth Luey, Bill Sharp, Dan Stanton, John  Wheatley, and Victoria Trotta

Old business

Victoria Trotta reported on recent meetings of PAC-20 and the Academic Senate. At PAC-20?s most recent meeting the transfer of academic advisors from the Academic Professional category to the Classified Staff and Service Professional categories was described . In addition, the committee has been discussing promotion and tenure guidelines for faculty and the implementation of new ABOR policy on background checks. The Senate is investigating possible changes in the rules for selecting retirement options and for the use of unused medical leave to defray costs of retirees? medical insurance.

 

Barbara Trapido-Lurie reported that the slate of candidates for CAPS, PACAPS, and the Academic Professionals Grievance Committee has been announced and that the election will occur in the last two weeks of March. She has extended invitations to speak at the Spring Spotlight  Forum to three heads of units that employ a number of academic professionals. One has asked to be invited in 2006; the others have not responded. As an alternative, she will invite Barbara Kerr and Susan Mattson, president and president-elect of the Academic Senate.

 

Beth Luey and Bill Sharp reported on the progress of the implementation task force. They explained that, rather than making minor revisions, the task force is reorganizing and rewriting the section of the ACD Manual that covers academic professionals.. The process has been facilitated by the services of Kathy Cook, administrative associate in the Office of General Counsel, who provides annotated versions of the text as it is revised and rewritten.

 

Luey and Sharp explained that they will bring the new sections to CAPS as they are drafted, for comments and suggestions. When the entire document has been drafted, it will also come to CAPS for review and for presentation to all academic professionals at the university. They reviewed the evolution of the current draft of ACD 507-08, covering annual evaluations, and the committee recommended a revision that will be taken back to the task force.

New business

The committee discussed issues of membership and leadership for the coming year.

 

Meeting adjourned at 11:40 am
