October 4, 2006

Business program attracts high achievers

An elite group of 121 freshmen are the first to take part in a new program at ASU's W. P. Carey School of Business that gives first year students an opportunity to enter professional programs.

The program, titled ?Carey Direct,? was offered to freshmen whose high school academic records are parallel to, or exceed those of Honors College students. It is designed to attract and retain high academic achievers who are ready to select a business school professional program.

The standard W. P. Carey School program requires freshmen to complete two years of pre-business coursework before applying to a professional program in one of seven academic programs offered at the W. P. Carey School.

Officials at the business school believe that Carey Direct students who select an academic track as freshmen are more likely to stay committed and perform better throughout their undergraduate careers.

The new program is expected to improve retention among top students and produce more high-quality business school graduates, according to Kay Faris, undergraduate dean.

?Carey Directs puts these freshmen on the fast track to academic excellence and leadership, and that can have a major impact on their career success,? Faris says.

Corporate recruiters are showing interest in high achieving undergraduates as early as their freshmen year. The Carey Direct program helps freshmen jump-start their academic careers, contributing to their preparation for job interviews or graduate school.

?Being admitted into the Carey Direct program is the payoff for the hard work I did in high school,? says Philip Oro of Tucson , who adds that being a top candidate for the Carey Direct program is a prelude to academic excellence and career success. ?No matter what career path I choose, having business skills is a huge benefit.?

Because Carey Direct students begin their college careers in the professional program of their choice, their schedules are flexible, allowing them opportunities to pursue concurrent degrees, study abroad and engage in a variety of co-curricular activities.

?I chose the W. P. Carey School because the Carey Direct program is amazingly integrated with the other schools and colleges at ASU,? says Laura Hurtig, a business honors freshman from Boise, Idaho. ?I'm thinking about a double major in business and medicine, and this program allows me to enroll in the key business courses I need to achieve my academic goals.?
