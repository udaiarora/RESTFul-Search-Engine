Librarians' Council


Minutes of Meeting

 

September 15, 2004
Hayden C6A

 

Meeting was called to order at 3:00 pm.

A quorum was present.

 

I. Approval of Minutes (May 19, 2004) – Minutes were approved.

 

II. Chair & Executive Committee's Report – Deborah Koshinsky

 

Deborah welcomed everyone to a new academic year. Additionally, she introduced the new Academic Professionals who have recently joined the University Libraries:

Emalee Craft - Assistant Librarian, Noble Reference

Katherine Clemens - Assistant Librarian, Noble Reference

Vicki Coleman - Associate Dean of University Libraries, Library Services

Sheila Hofstetter - Assistant Librarian, Noble Reference

Christopher Miller  - Academic Associate, Collection Development

 

III. Dean’s Report –Sherrie Schmidt

 

Dean Schmidt reminded The Council about ASU strategic goals.

Raises: Dean Schmidt handed out AP performance space salary plan approved by Librarians’ Council and Provost in 1995. Letters will be distributed to staff by the end of the month and the raise is retroactive to July 2004.

Capital Center: The University Libraries have been asked to determine the retrospective collection for the Capital Center. Jeanne Richardson is working with faculty and selectors.

Dean Schmidt presented gavels to Julie Tharp and Scott Herrington for their service as chairs of the Librarians’ Council.

 

IV. Personnel Report – Kurt Murphy

 

AP task force report: Sept. 1st was the deadline for the comments from the APs. Comments had been summarized and forwarded to the Provost’s office.

Kurt outlined the changes in the review process.

 

V. Committee Reports

 

a.       Personnel Committee – Brad Vogus

The Committee is discussing the changes in the review cycle. The Librarians’ Handbook revision to the section on Retention, Continuing Appointment, and Promotion has been tabled for the present time.

b.       Research and Professional Development Committee – Jennie Duvernay

1.  Research funds:  The RPDC received two outstanding research proposals and funded each at the maximum level allowed.  The committee has $1,000 remaining and will be sending out another request for proposals later in the fall.

2.  Travel funds:  The deadline for submitting travel fund applications is October 1, 2004.  Please fill out the new (as of last year) form online. 

3.  AZLA:  Unlike most years, AZLA will be held in Tucson this year.  The RPDC then is also managing travel applications for this conference, and the deadline for this application is also October 1.  The committee is grateful to Dean Schmidt for allocating additional funds to support ASU participation in this conference.  (Please note:  Do not send applications for AZLA to Lillie.  Send all requests for AZLA travel to the RPDC, using the same travel application form as other out of Maricopa county travel.)

c.    Committee on Academic Professional Status – Dan Stanton         

CAPS spotlight series - Vice Provost Nancy Gutierrez has agreed to speak at the Spotlight Series about the ongoing Task Force on Academic Professionals work in November/December (date to be decided later).

The comments from APs have been accumulated and forwarded to Provost Glick. CAPS might be involved in writing of ACD manual sections for APs.

d.    Review Committee – Julie Tharp

The committee is in the process of reviewing the probationary packets.

e.    Academic Senate – Deborah Koshinsky

Academic Senate Chair Barbara Kerr presented her views on health care on campus, salary and compensation issues. There will be a formal task force to address the issue of undergraduates -- especially retention.

Presidential Debate: There are 3000 seats but only 300 people will be invited to attend. Academic Senate sent recommendations to the Provost regarding a policy on classes on October 13, 2004.

f.   Web Master – Yuwu Song 

-  no report.

 

VI. New Business

 

The first reading of revisions to Travel and Research Funds procedures, Librarians’ Handbook (Sections H1.1, H1.2 & H2.1) was presented.

It was brought to the Council’s attention that the recent librarian retirees might like to have personal copies of the proclamation that was read at the last Librarians’ Council meeting. The Executive Committee will address that matter.

Meeting adjourned 4:00 p.m.
