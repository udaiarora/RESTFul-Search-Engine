DRC Scholarships

 The Arizona State University (ASU) Disability Resource Center (DRC) Tempe campus has a large endowment and Regent Scholarship program for students with disabilities, including the Andrew Brown, Ethel Mae Merriam, DRC Regents’ Scholarships, Steven & Mary Rosenfield Scholarship and the Julie Sargent Memorial Scholarship. The programawarded over $100,000 during the 2005-2006 academic year. We encourage all qualified DRC students to apply!

To be eligible for an Andrew Brown or Ethel Mae Merriam Scholarship Award, students must:

  • Be a qualified ASU student with a disability and registered with the DRC
  • Have a minimum 2.5 ASU cumulative GPA. NOTE: first semester freshman and transfer students who have not completed 12 undergraduate credits at ASU must have a minimum 3.0 cumulative GPA from the previous school attended. Graduate students must have a minimum 3.5 cumulative GPA.
  • Submit a two-page,12 pt, double-spaced personal statement describing the following: academic and career goals, all extra-curricular activities, including leadership roles, justification for financial need
  • Provide a letter of reference from an academic professional (e.g., teacher, advisor etc.)
  • Submit a current college transcript or high school transcript for first semester freshman and transfer students with less than 12 hours
  • Be pursuing a full-time degree program at ASU
  • Establish financial need by having a current FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) on file at the ASU Student Financial Assistance Office.

Along with the above criteria, additional requirements for the DRC Regent’s Scholarship, Steven & Mary Rosenfield Scholarship and the Julie Sargent Memorial Scholarship are as follows:

  • DRC Regents’ Scholarships : 3.0 GPA, undergraduate, Arizona resident
  • Steven & Mary Rosenfield Scholarship : must have a documented learning disability and/or attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, first semester freshman, northern California resident preferred
  • Julie Sargent Memorial Scholarship : must have juvenile diabetes, undergraduate, Arizona resident

Recipients who continue to meet scholarship requirements and obligations may have their scholarship renewed by submitting a new application each year.

Note: New personal statements and reference letters are not required for scholarship renewal.  

 

APPLICATION PROCESS

Priority consideration will be given to scholarship applicants submitting completed applications and all required materials prior to July 1, 2006. All completed applications/materials received after July 1, 2006 will be considered in the order they are received as funding allows. Disability Resource Center ( DRC) scholarship applicants are encouraged to submit their applications and required attachments at least three weeks before the beginning of the semester for which they would like to be considered. All applicants will be notified by mail, at the address indicated on the application. Only one application form is necessary per academic year. The DRC Scholarship Committee will review all applications and select the award recipients.

Note: Applications received three weeks after the start of any semester will not be considered for that semester.

Scholarship Recipient Requirements

THE SCHOLAR MUST :

  • Register with the Disability Resource Center (DRC) at the Tempe campus as a qualified student with a disability.
  • Apply for participation in the DRC TRIO Student Support Services program (undergraduate students only).
  • Complete 10 hours of campus/community involvement per semester. (See assigned Disability Access Consultant for specifics.)
  • Meet with assigned Disability Access Consultant, minimally, once a month.
  • Maintain a cumulative GPA in accordance with the specific scholarship awarded.
  • Be enrolled as a full-time, degree seeking student at ASU (suspension or dismissal will automatically terminate the scholarship).
  • File a signed copy of the scholarship contract, outlining the specific requirements of the award, with the Disability Resource Center DRC Scholarship Coordinator.

Please be advised: If the terms and conditions are not met throughout the semester in which the award is received, you may no longer be eligible for future DRC scholarship awards.

Note: Students may establish eligibility for additional ASU scholarships by completing the ASU General Scholarship Application. For more information call the ASU Scholarship office at (480) 965-4845 or visit their web site.
