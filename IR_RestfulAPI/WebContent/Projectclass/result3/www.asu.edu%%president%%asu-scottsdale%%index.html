Crow Highlights Plan for ASU/Scottsdale Technology Center

Political, business and civic leaders across the nation are beginning to “get it”. Innovation is to prosperity in the 21st century, as what natural resources were to the economy of the 20th century. Simply stated, innovation is a transformational element that generates commerce, wealth and well-being in the communities that foster it. However, innovations do more than just generate dollars; they also enhance a community’s culture, diversity and quality of life.

This is why ASU has proposed the ASU/Scottsdale Center for New Technology and Innovation. This center is not  a traditional research park, nor a business park, nor a university campus; it is a hybrid of all three. This Center will become home to activities and organizations that stimulate new forms of commerce, research, technology, digital art, education and economic development; all stemming from ASU’s tier one research facilities.

The Center will be a collaboration between the City of Scottsdale, the ASU Foundation, and Arizona State University. The goal is to attract technology-focused research, business, and investment organizations such as those working in the fields of nanotechnology and identity recognition. ASU will identify and help develop the content that will make it attractive for such firms to lease space at the Center and will locate some of ASU’s premier units there, such as Arizona Technology Enterprises, Technoplis and The Entrepreneurship Center.

Businesses, researchers, technology designers and investors will also co-locate in the Center, which will be designed specifically to encourage interaction among them. They will jointly develop new technologies and create new commercial enterprises.  The Center will be a place where research interfaces with economic development, technology and innovation, and education engages with the local community.

The design and planning for the ASU/Scottsdale Center for New Technology and Innovation will draw upon the lessons from knowledge economy businesses and technology centers from around the world, such as SRI International (California), the National Science and Industrial Parks (China) and the Genetics Knowledge Park (United Kingdom). Such leading edge commercial/research communities are innovative examples of private and public investments. Each of them has and continues to generate new products, new jobs and new businesses. Our $300 million investment in this collaboration with Scottsdale is expected to create 4000 new jobs and generate new businesses in Scottsdale and the region.

In short, the ASU/Scottsdale Center for New Technology and Innovation will be a world-class “assembly point” for knowledge/technology businesses by utilizing the valuable brands and capabilities of both the city and the university.  It is my hope that many will accept my invitation to read a detailed overview of this project at www.asu.edu/president and consider the significance of this project on Scottsdale residents for generations to come. We look forward to finalizing our agreement with Scottsdale and beginning the journey on August 9th.
