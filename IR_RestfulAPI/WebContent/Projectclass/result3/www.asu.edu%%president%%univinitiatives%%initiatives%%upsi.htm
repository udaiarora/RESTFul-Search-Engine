University Public Schools Initiative

University Public Schools (UPSI) is an Arizona State University initiative for innovation and social responsibility in education.  ASU, as a large public university in a vibrant and growing city, should have a unique, intense, research-based role in community issues.  ASU can bring its resources, university-wide, to bear in the K-12 arena.  The university, in partnership with local school districts and the community, will launch research-based schools to serve as exemplars and resources for schools.   This initiative not only revitalizes our roots in teacher training and public schools, but also allows us to more effectively provide innovative tools and support to increase the academic achievement of all Arizona students. 

 

 

Vision

 

ASU envisions the creation of an innovative, research-based educational environment in Arizona where universities, community and business leaders, and schools work together to create socially aware, creative, high achieving students, prepared to compete and participate in an increasingly global society. 

 

Arizona’s future requires education and innovation to develop and maintain an efficient, competitive and successful workforce.  Today’s students will play a critical role in fueling our economy.  As tomorrow's workforce, they will have to compete in an increasingly knowledge-based and global society.  To succeed, students must be exposed to the necessary skills and knowledge that the future demands, such as cross-cultural communication, critical thinking, creativity, and technological aptitude.  Our current educational system has demonstrated a need for improved professional development, new curriculum methodologies, and more university research in schools in order to meet this demand and increase student achievement.  We must work towards giving children every means necessary to succeed

 

 

Mission

 

Recognizing ASU's clear responsibility to make a positive difference in Arizona public schools, the University Public Schools Initiative seeks to:

  • Increase access to innovative, quality, and culturally inclusive education for all of Arizona’s children and families

  • Meet the needs of Arizona’s schools and districts through providing better access to new and existing university resources and programs in curriculum methodology, professional development and training, and student achievement

  • Help to systemically improve opportunities and heighten expectations for all

  • Arizona students to reach and be successful in college

  • Become an exemplar and innovator in education nationwide

 

Leadership

 

We are pleased to announce that after a national search, Larry Pieratt has joined this effort effective July 1, 2006 as the Executive Director of the UPSI and is part of ASU’s Vice President for Education Partnerships office. Larry’s experience as a principal in the Kyrene School District and most recently as the founder, president, and chief executive officer of the Horizon Community Learning Center, one of the largest and most exemplary charter schools in Arizona, will be invaluable as UPSI moves forward. 

 

Larry has proven both his visionary leadership and dedication to the community through his years of experience in the education field.  Larry’s guidance enabled Horizon to grow from its original enrollment of 480 students in 1996 to its current enrollment of 1,436 students in grades K-12.  The school is known nationally for its unique learning program, parent involvement, teacher professional growth program, application of technology and community partnerships. It was one of five schools in the nation chosen by the Milken Family Foundation in 2000 to pilot the Teacher Advancement Program and was the first “COX Model Technology School” in Arizona in 1999.

 

Larry has already begun to meet with many individuals across the community and the university to share ideas and gather input, and he is looking forward to meeting with many more people in the coming months as he helps shape the future of the initiative.  Larry can be reached at 480-965-0591 or Lawrence.Pieratt@asu.edu.

 


Relation to Design Imperatives

Design Imperative #1: Leveraging Place
ASU Must Embrace Its Cultural, Socioeconomic, and Physical Setting

Schools will be created on or near each of ASU’s four campuses, serving the communities which surround them.  These schools will engage families from all socio-economic and cultural backgrounds

Design Imperative #2: Societal Transformation
ASU Must Become a Force, and Not Only a Place

Through UPSI, ASU can create a forum for parental and community involvement in education, providing a gateway for families into the university and working with local districts and school boards to revolutionize K-12 education in Arizona.

Design Imperative #3: ASU As a Knowledge Entrepreneur
A Culture of Academic Enterprise

In developing a research-based, innovative school model and strategies, ASU is furthering its efforts as a knowledge entrepreneur.

Design Imperative # 7: Social Embeddedness
Public Service/Community Engagement/Outreach

ASU, as a research university, knowledge incubator, and publicly funded institution, has a responsibility to engage the community and respond to its concerns.  Arizona educators and administrators have voiced a need for innovative curriculum methodologies, more professional development for teachers, better teacher preparation and more university-involved research in schools. Under UPSI, ASU will develop innovative curriculum models, improve teacher preparation and professional development, and create strong partnerships with local school districts, community organizations and local businesses. UPSI will also help to bridge all ASU education-related activities and ensure that they are directly meeting the needs of the education community. Through addressing these concerns, UPSI is pushing forward systemic change that meets community needs.
