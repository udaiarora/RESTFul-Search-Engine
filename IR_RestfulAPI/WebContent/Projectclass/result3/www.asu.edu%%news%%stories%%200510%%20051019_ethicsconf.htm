October 19, 2005

Ethics conference explores censorship issues: Supreme Court's O'Connor tops

U.S. Supreme Court Justice Sandra Day O’Connor will join scholars, filmmakers, studio executives, novelists, scriptwriters, philosophers, ethicists and Hollywood celebrities at the Lincoln Center for Applied Ethics’ 2005 E2 Ethics of Entertainment Conference and Film Festival, a weekend of film, music, literature and debate about the role entertainment plays in our lives.

The conference, which will take place Oct. 20 – 23 at the Tempe Mission Palms Hotel, will explore key issues surrounding entertainment and society. These include censorship, the business of celebrity, diversity, exploration of American culture through entertainment, the ethics of entertainment, and sex and violence onscreen.

E2 is presented by the Lincoln Center for Applied Ethics at ASU, which seeks a national discourse on ethics issues in all areas of business and education.

Highlights of the conference – which is free for ASU students, faculty and staff – will include:

• O’Connor will receive the Joan and David Lincoln Award for Public Virtue. She will be introduced by Arizona Attorney General Terry Goddard.

• Novelist and adventurer Clive Cussler speaks on the torturous process of turning a novel into screened entertainment, as in the recent film by Paramount, “Sahara.”

• New York Times Magazine photojournalist Peter Howe, author of “Paparazzi,” will discuss the role of the business of celebrity with a panel of executives and sociologists.

• A “Hollywood and the First Amendment” panel will examine censorship issues with scholars from leading institutions across the country.

• Harry G. Frankfurt, whose treatise on American culture, “On Bullshit,” has been on the New York Times’ bestseller list for more than half a year, will discuss the topic of his book.

• Benjamin Barber, author of the international best seller “Jihad vs. McWorld,” will discuss the exportation of American values through entertainment.

• Academy Award-winning documentary “Born into Brothels” will lead off the E2 Film Fest, which will run continuously at different venues throughout the Tempe campus.

E2 events will kick off the first film and media production program in the nation to teach ethics as an integral component of the profession. The new film program will be housed at ASU’s Herberger College of Fine Arts School of Theatre and Film.

E2 will be presented from the perspective of professional filmmakers and a strong respect for the First Amendment.

Special events, screenings and galas will be held throughout the weekend.

For details or registration, and for sponsorship information, go to the Web site (www.asu.edu/clas/lincolncenter) or (www.e2filmfest.com) or call F. Miguel Valenti, executive director of the conference, at (480) 965-2655.
