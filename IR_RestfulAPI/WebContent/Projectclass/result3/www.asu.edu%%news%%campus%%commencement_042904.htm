April 29, 2004

Doug Wilder, first African-American Governor, Ex-NSF Director Rita Colwell, to be honored at ASU commencement; Wilder to address Brown vs. Board of Education on its 50th Anniversary

TEMPE, Ariz. -- Three distinguished leaders in health, education and public service will receive honorary degrees from Arizona State University in a commencement ceremony at 10 a.m. May 13 at ASU's Wells Fargo Arena. About 5,200 students also will receive their degrees from ASU President Michael Crow.

Lawrence Douglas Wilder, elected the nation's first African-American governor in Virginia in 1989, is receiving an honorary degree and also will deliver the commencement address highlighting the 50 th anniversary of Brown vs. Board of Education.

Also honored will be Rita Colwell, eminent microbiologist who was the 11 th director of the National Science Foundation, and Rafael Rangel, president of Tecnologico de Monterrey, one of the leading academic institutions in Latin America.

Wilder was a top criminal trial lawyer in 1969 when he became the first African American elected to the Virginia Senate since Reconstruction. As governor he was nationally recognized for his leadership in fiscal management, also achieving anti-crime initiatives and an improved transportation system. Currently he is a distinguished professor at the Center for Public Policy at Virginia Commonwealth University.

Colwell spearheaded the NSF's emphasis on K-12 science and math education, graduate science and engineering education and increased participation of women and minorities. An internationally recognized infectious disease expert, she left the NSF in February to help develop an international network for issues of emerging infectious diseases and water. She is a Distinguished University Professor at the University of Maryland at College Park and at the Johns Hopkins University Bloomberg School of Health.

Rangel leads an institution that has 33 campuses across Mexico and enrolls more than 95,000 full-time students. He has been a leader in developing programs such as a virtual university offering online programs to students in 17 countries, an entrepreneurship and incubator program and many collaborative programs with American universities. Tec de Monterrey recently formed a partnership with ASU to develop shared research programs and curricula.
