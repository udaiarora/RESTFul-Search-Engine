Quick Guide to ASU

The ASU web features over 300,000 pages—so there’s a lot to explore. Arizona State University is a rapidly growing, multi-campus public research university—one of three state universities in Arizona governed by the Arizona Board of Regents.

ASU offers programs from the baccalaureate through the doctorate for over 57,000 full-time and part-time students through its Tempe campus, West campus in Phoenix, Polytechnic campus in Mesa, and other instructional, research, and public service sites throughout Maricopa County. All are located in the Phoenix Metropolitan area, also known as “the Valley.”

The campuses have academic units (colleges, schools, and departments) and administrative units. For an organizational overview go to the Administration page.
