PCS 1102: Stolen Sponsor-Owned Equipment

Purpose

To ensure that principal investigators report the disappearance of equipment that has been purchased with sponsored funds

Sources

Property Control
Financial Services
Insurance Services

Policy

Principal investigators must report stolen equipment immediately to the ASU Department of Public Safety (ASU DPS) so that a police report may be filed.

Note:The Arizona Department of Administration’s Risk Management Division will not reimburse for mysterious disappearances, inventory shortages, obsolescence, and non-serviceability of capital equipment.

ProcedureD

Responsibility
Action
Principal investigator (PI)
  1. Notify the ASU Department of Public Safety (ASU DPS) of the stolen equipment.
ASU Department of Public Safety
  1. Prepare a police report detailing the incident.

  2. Record the ASU property control number (ASU PC#) on the police report.
Insurance Services
  1. Complete a Property Relocation Authorization (PRA) form for the stolen equipment. Write “Insurance Services Claim” in the “Reason/Comments” field, and send the form to Property Control.

    Note:If no PRA is on file, the department’s claim will not be processed.

Department
  1. Send a letter to Insurance Services and include the following:

    1. a photocopy of the PRA form (signed by Property Control)

    2. the police report number

    3. a photocopy of the original purchase order of the equipment stolen

    4. the agency/org and object code to which the Insurance Services reimbursement will be deposited

      and

    5. a description of the theft.

    Note:For additional supporting documentation of the purchase or for equipment database searches, contact Property Control.

Insurance Services
  1. Process the claim for reimbursement through the Arizona Department of Administration’s Risk Management Division.
Property Control
  1. Verify that the ASU PC# and serial number of the asset are on the PRA.
Sponsored property coordinator
  1. Note on the PRA that the equipment was sponsor funded and indicate the sponsored agency/org and capital object code to credit.

  2. Retire the asset from the property control database.

  3. Forward a copy of the PRA form and Retirement Journal Voucher to Insurance Services.

  4. Notify the sponsor or administrative contracting officer that the equipment has been stolen.

  5. File the PRA photocopy in a “PRA Claim Pending” file.
Upon receiving the reimbursement from the Arizona Department of Administration’s Risk Management and Safety Services Division:
Insurance Services
  1. Process the reimbursement through the Office for Research and Sponsored Projects Administration noting the sponsored agency/org and capital object code (taken from the PRA) to credit.
