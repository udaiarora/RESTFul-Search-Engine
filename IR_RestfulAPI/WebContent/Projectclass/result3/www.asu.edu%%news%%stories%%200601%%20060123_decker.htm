January 23, 2006

Nationally renowned criminologist joins ASU

ASU has appointed Scott Decker to chair of the department of Criminal Justice and Criminology in the College of Human Services at the West campus. Decker is a Curator's Professor of Criminology and Criminal Justice at the University of Missouri-St. Louis. He will begin his duties as chair July 1.

"Scott is an outstanding scholar and a tremendous addition to our Department of Criminal Justice and Criminology," says John Hepburn, dean of the College of Human Services at the West campus. "His reputation and expertise will strengthen our department and enhance its level of national recognition as a premier criminology program."

Decker has been with UM-St. Louis since 1977, serving as department chair for 15 years. He has been a Curator's Professor for the past five years, receiving the distinguished honor for his outstanding reputation and contributions to the field of criminology. Decker received his doctorate and master's degree in criminology from Florida State in 1976 and 1974, respectively, and his bachelor's degree in social justice from DePauw University, located in Greencastle, Ind., in 1972.

"I am honored to have been chosen by Dean Hepburn to lead ASU's highly regarded Department of Criminal Justice and Criminology," Decker says. "With the assistance of outstanding faculty members, we will continue the department's mission of promoting research and academic excellence, teaching, and service in the field of criminal justice and criminology."

The department has been ranked as a top 10 program nationally by researchers at the University of Cincinnati , who measured the levels of faculty scholarship and research publications. The findings were recently reported in the Journal of Criminal Justice.

Decker has brought in $4 million in funded research from federal, state and other competitive programs. He served as co-principal investigator for various projects focusing on youth violence and is project director of the St. Louis Homicide Project. The multifaceted project includes research, policy and community involvement components.

Gangs, juvenile justice and criminal justice policy are Decker's main research interests. He is the research partner for Project Safe Neighborhoods in the eastern district of Missouri and the southern district of Illinois. Decker has authored 10 books addressing criminology, including "Policing Gangs and Youth Violence," "Life in the Gang," "Responding to Gangs," "Controversies in Criminal Justice," Armed Robbers in Action" and his latest publication, "European Street Gangs and Troublesome Youth." His book, "Burglars on the Job," received the Society for the Study of Social Problems Crime and Delinquency Outstanding Scholarship award, and "Life in the Gang" received the Academy of Criminal Justice Sciences Outstanding Book Award in 1998.

"Scott's work is cutting-edge," Hepburn says. "His work has been innovative and he has set the agenda for criminology for the next decade. His leadership and vision make him someone we really want to have at this university. He is a great addition to our criminology program."
