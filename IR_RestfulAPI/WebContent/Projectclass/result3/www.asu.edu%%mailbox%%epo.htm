EPO: The Electronic Post Office

The Electronic Post Office, or EPO, is a computer hardware and software installation which facilitates the exchange of electronic mail within ASU and between ASU and other electronic mail sites around the world.

For instance, the EPO allows one to have an EPO alias such as none@asu.edu, which can point to any real mailbox, e.g., asurite_id@imap3.asu.edu. As well, the EPO allows one to have an EPO reverse alias (also known as a "rewrite address") such as asurite_id@imap3.asu.edu that can be rewritten to any "directory address," e.g., none@asu.edu.

EPO accounts can be created and/or modified at http://my.asu.edu , and choosing the emma link on the top right, then EPO Update from the left menu bar.

Directory Address

The Directory Address is the e-mail address that will appear with your entry in the various electronic directories at ASU. It is also the address that e-mail you send will appear to be from (if it is sent from one of your rewrite addresses). When you change your Directory Address a corresponding alias is automatically added to your account.
top

Destination Address

The Destination Address is where e-mail sent to your account (or any of its aliases) will be delivered. When you change your Destination Address a corresponding rewrite address is automatically added to your account.
top

Plan

The Plan is freeform text you enter as a message, e.g. your home web page address. This information may be seen in some of the various electronic directories at ASU.
top

Aliases

Mail sent to any of your aliases (with "@asu.edu" appended) will be delivered to your destination address. You may have up to 6 aliases per account.
Warning: Account Names, Return Addresses and Account Aliases, in general, should be based on personal names, using periods as word separators. Those that are regarded to be in bad taste, misleading, misrepresenting, or otherwise considered to interfere with the work or interests of others will be expropriated.
top

Rewrites

Mail sent from any of your rewrite addresses will appear to be from your Directory Address. A rewrite address is an address that will be "rewritten" to your Directory Address, if the e-mail originates or is delivered to ASU. EPO Account rewrites usually include your destination address and other mailboxes on or off campus that you wish to appear to be from your Directory Address.
top

Display Name

This field in the Electronic Post Office is for Primary Accounts only. If you have previously requested that your display name be changed, you may modify it again with this field. Warning: Names in general should be based on personal names. Those that are regarded to be in bad taste, misleading, misrepresenting, or otherwise considered to interfere with the work or interests of others will be expropriated.
top

E-Mail Release

This field in the Electronic Post Office is for Primary Accounts only. Your Primary account information may be concealed from the various electronic directories at ASU. To conceal our primary account information, contact email-q@asu.edu. Concealing your primary account information will hide only your e-mail address from the directories! Once your information is concealed, you may allow it to be viewed again through the EPO Update Facility application. Employees should not conceal their information. Also note that if your information is concealed at the registrar, this flag is ignored.
top

EPO Accounts and Primary vs. Secondary Accounts

Everyone at ASU has a Primary Account. Your primary account consists of a directory address, a destination address, aliases, rewrites, and some other information. Some users also have secondary accounts, which can be used to route mail for other mailboxes they own or administer. These mailboxes can include, but are not limited to, listserv accounts and public folders. An employee of the University can add or delete secondary accounts for themselves.
top

Secondary Accounts

Secondary accounts are used for listserv accounts, public folders, and other mailboxes which you may own or administer. A secondary account has some additional information that may be displayed in the various electronic directories at ASU.
top

Secondary Account Description

The description of the secondary account is equivalent to a display name available in your primary account. You can add a name to a secondary account by changing the description field. If the secondary account information is released to the various electronic directories at ASU, this is how the account will be listed.
