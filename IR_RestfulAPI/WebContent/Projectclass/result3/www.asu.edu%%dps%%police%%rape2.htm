Rape is a crime of violence.
    To understand why acquaintance rape is such a serious problem, we must first examine how our assumptions about power, gender and sex can lead to sexual violence.

    Rape is a crime of violence. Whether a stranger or an acquaintance, a rapist uses a sexual act to establish power over someone else. Rapists are motivated by the desire to control and dominate, not by the need for sexual gratification.

    A rapist is usually not a stranger in a dark alley. In approximately 85% of the rape or sexual assault cases reported, the rapist is someone the victim knows.

If it happens to you.
    If you should become the victom of a rape, remember, the person who attacked you committed a crime. You committed no crime!

    Do the following immediately:

    • Go to a safe place.
    • Call someone you trust to be with you.
    • Seek medical care.
    • Report the assault.
    What you should not do.
    • Do not shower, bathe or douche after the attack.
    • Do not discard or wash clothing or linen.
    • Do not blame yourself. It was not your fault.
    Talking to a counselor or Victims Assistance representative can help you understand your feelings. You don't have to go through this alone. Recovery can occur more quickly when you have appropriate help.
