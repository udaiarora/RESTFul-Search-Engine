October 23, 2003

Psychology professor makes learning fun, both in, outside class

There's magic going on in the weekly lab meetings of ASU Psychology Professor Steven Neuberg and his student research assistants. What else would explain two dozen students giving up their Friday afternoons to hang with their professor and talk about research?

Some students show up week after week, even after they're no longer enrolled in his lab for research credit, just to hear what he has to say.

For this magic trick - and for cultivating his students' development both in and outside the classroom - Neuberg has been chosen an ASU Featured Faculty. The award program was begun this fall by Co-Curricular Programs to recognize and celebrate the contributions of faculty in enhancing student learning outside of class.
The office received more than 40 nominations from students for the award, and Neuberg was selected the first honoree by a panel of students. Three faculty members will be chosen each semester, and will receive an honorarium and be highlighted in programs throughout the year.

"Dr. Neuberg provides all of his students with the personal level of education and attention that one would find at a college 50 times smaller than ASU," says Jenessa Shapiro, psychology graduate teaching assistant who nominated him. "As a student, it is clear that you matter to him. He encourages all students to meet and interact with him. He is a fantastic teacher, adviser and mentor."

Neuberg, who joined the ASU faculty in 1988, teaches courses in undergraduate social psychology and honors social psychology, and graduate seminars in social psychology, prejudice and relationships. This semester he also is an Interdisciplinary Faculty Fellow in anthropology.

His two-hour Friday afternoon lab meetings are legendary for being interesting, interactive and regular-as-clockwork, even when he's on sabbatical. The chairs are filled with about 20 undergraduates and a handful of graduate students.

"Dr. Neuberg uses the meetings to develop new theories, talk about issues relevant to social psychology and the lab's perspective, present data, and discuss results, all of which expose the undergraduates to true science, not just data entry," says Shapiro. "He is extremely dedicated to incorporating learning wherever possible."

Neuberg says he finds teaching social psychology "great fun," especially since students get to ask questions that are of great interest to them and learn to answer them using the tools of science.

"The best parts of teaching often occur within the context of doing research, frequently in one-on-one mentoring relationships, where one can see with each meeting a student's intellectual growth," he says.

According to Linda Sullivan, executive coordinator of Co-Curricular Programs, they instituted the Featured Faculty awards this year because of the overwhelming student response to the spring Last Lecture Series, in which students nominate their favorite lecturers.

"Faculty have a lasting impact on students, and this is a chance for students to reflect on that," says Sullivan. "So many students are eager to nominate their professors, we decided it would be good to honor faculty all year long, and for other ways they excel other than lectures."

For information on the Featured Faculty program, visit the Web site at (www.asu.edu/partnerships).
