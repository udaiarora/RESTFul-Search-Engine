Spam E-Mail

SPAMming is to indiscriminately send large amounts of unsolicited e-mail meant to promote a product or service.

Spam in this sense is like the electronic equivalent of junk mail sent to 'Current Resident'.

 

What Can Be Done?

The Web and Messaging Applications Team (WMA) cannot stop unwanted e-mail from reaching ASU's e-mail systems. To do so would mean that ASU would need to cut itself off from the Internet. It is much the same as your mailbox at home; as long as you have an address, you will be sent junk mail. The biggest difference is that at home it costs someone the price of postage to send you the mail. The price is much less for Internet junk mail.

The use of SPAM (unsolicited e-mail advertisements) is at an all time high and still rising. SPAMmers are becoming more sophisticated and databases of e-mail addresses are growing. As long as SPAM is viewed as a free form of advertising, its use will probably continue to increase.

ASU offers SPAM Filtering for your EPO Accounts (your primary address and your aliases). To learn more and how to get started, please read here.


How Do They Get My E-mail Address?

If you give a company your e-mail address, not only can they turn around and use it, but they can sell it to other companies who will use it to. This applies to supplying your address verbally as well as entering it online.

If your e-mail address appears on a web page, then "Spam-bots" that scour the Internet may find it. The ASU Web directory may have your e-mail address on it, but it can not be scanned in its entirety, so it is not likely a source for SPAMmers.

Participation in online discussion groups, bulletin boards or services like e-bay where your e-mail address is associated with your account

What Can I Do To Reduce The Amount Of SPAM I Receive?

1. Sign up for the ASU SPAM Filtering service. Be sure to check this service frequently while initially building your white list.

2. Go get a second e-mail account from one of the free providers, like Yahoo. Use that e-mail address whenever you are asked for your e-mail address by someone (or some form) that you do not completely trust.
3. Make sure your e-mail address doesn't appear on any web pages (e.g. search the web for your address)
4. Use the SPAM filtering available within your e-mail client. If you use Outlook, for example, you can start using the "Junk Mail Filter" that is built in. It is not very sophisticated, but it will detect some SPAM. As you get to know how the filter works, you should only have the filter move "candidate" SPAM to another folder (rather than delete it) until you trust it.
5. Delete it. Ultimately, this is your best weapon. Delete the message without reading it and minimize the time you spend handling SPAM.

Spams or Chain Letters originating or passed on within ASU should be reported to email-q@asu.edu immediately.

What Is ASU Doing To Stop SPAM?

In the past, ASU used to attempt to "block" SPAM. We would disallow (known as 'blacklisting') e-mail from certain addresses, or block entire sites that did not try to stop SPAMmers that originated there. The SPAMmers always found a way around these blocks, and we would end up blocking legitimate e-mail; so we no longer block addresses from being able to send to us.

ASU attempts to keep its e-mail addresses private from Spam bots.

We now offer user initiated SPAM Filtering. ASU does not filter for SPAM by default. If you would like to read more about ASU's SPAM Filtering Service, please read here.

Reporting Spams, Chain Letters or Other Annoying & Abusive E-Mails

Before reporting these types of e-mails to email-q@asu.edu or to the Postmaster of the originating node, you should ALWAYS include the header information of the e-mail message in question. Since e-mail addresses in spam e-mails are usually forged, the header information is necessary in order to attempt to track the source of the note. Without the header information, WMA (or the Postmaster of the originating domain) cannot take any action to deter future abuse.

Where Do I Find Header Information In My E-Mail?

  • In Pine, while viewing the message, press "H".

  • In Outlook for PC, while viewing the message, from the View menu, select Options.

  • In Outlook for Mac, while viewing the message, from the File menu, select Properties.

For unsupported e-mail packages that are not listed here, refer to the help features of that package.
