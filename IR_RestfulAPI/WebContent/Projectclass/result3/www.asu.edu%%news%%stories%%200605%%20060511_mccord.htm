May 11, 2006

Robert and Sharon McCord donate $10 million for statewide university scholarship program

ASU portion marks largest single-donor undergraduate scholarship gift

Phoenix residents Robert and Sharon McCord have donated $10 million to establish the McCord Scholars Program for outstanding business students at three Arizona university business schools, with a majority designated for the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University.

Their gift includes a $6 million award for students in ASU's W. P. Carey School of Business, the largest single-donor undergraduate scholarship gift in ASU history, $3 million for students at the Eller College of Management at the University of Arizona and $1 million earmarked for students of the College of Business Administration at Northern Arizona University.

The McCord Scholars program will begin with the 2006-2007 academic year and is designed to attract outstanding students who excel in academic ability, business acumen and leadership skills. Scholarships of up to $10,000 each will be awarded to juniors and seniors in the business school, and will assist students in meeting the costs of tuition/fees, books and living expenses. Each scholarship will be distributed equally over two semesters.

“This gift is extraordinary. I do not know of another scholarship program that focuses on business students across all state universities. This will recognize the best and brightest students and allow them to reach their fullest potential by giving them more time to focus on their education,” says Robert Mittelstaedt, dean of the W. P. Carey School of Business.

It is a fact that the majority of undergraduate business students must work to support themselves while attending university. In the May 2005 class of graduating W. P. Carey School seniors, 86 percent of the students worked while attending school; of those, 55 percent reported working more than 20 hours per week. Thirty-four percent of juniors and seniors in the W. P. Carey School of Business professional program had financial need prior to receiving any form of assistance.

“Education is critical to maintaining competition in the international marketplace. This program will help us compete and will assist in attracting the best students to Arizona, thus providing great local benefit. It is our wish that this financial assistance will make a difference in the ability of talented students to finish their business education at quality universities,” says donor Robert McCord.

Within ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business, the McCord Scholarship endowment is expected to provide recipients with a maximum of $10,000 each, distributed over two semesters, for up to 24 McCord Scholars selected each year.

Among the criteria, a student must: be regularly admitted to the W. P. Carey School of Business as a professional program student at the junior or senior class level; be a member of the Business Honors Program; pursue a minimum of 12 hours of grade-level work per semester and make regular progress toward a four-year undergraduate degree; demonstrate leadership abilities and involvement in student and professional organizations; earn a minimum of 3.6 cumulative grade point average; and be an Arizona resident.

The W. P. Carey School of Business at ASU is one of the largest business schools in the United States, with more than 160 faculty members, 1,400 graduate and 2,500 upper-division undergraduate students. The school is internationally recognized for its leadership in supply chain management and services marketing and is highly regarded for its faculty’s research productivity. The school’s six research centers serve as a link between business communities and the areas of marketing, entrepreneurship, information technology and supply chain management.
