October 11, 2006

MBA Shanghai students visit Arizona

Another link in the bond between Arizona and the People's Republic of China was forged Oct. 2, when the W. P. Carey School of Business welcomed 35 senior Chinese government officials and executives from major state-owned enterprises.

The officials and executives, part of the class of 2007 in the W. P. Carey MBA Shanghai, were spending five days visiting ASU and Valley business leaders in Arizona, then flying to New York for four days before returning to China.

The program, which has attracted more than 280 senior Chinese executives ? including 74 in the current class ? is jointly sponsored by China 's Ministry of Finance and Shanghai city government via the Shanghai National Accounting Institute (SNAI), a premier national post-graduate training institute. SNAI was founded by China 's formal premier, Zhu Rongji, in preparation for China 's entrance to the World Trade Organization.

?Our China program has been a tremendous success, because so many of their executives and leaders are eager to learn all they can to help accelerate economic growth for their companies and their country,? says Robert Mittelstaedt, dean of the W. P. Carey School . Relationships built as a result of visits like the one this week will also foster trans-Pacific business deals to mutual benefit.?

The partnership between ASU and the Ministry of Finance and SNAI demonstrates an unprecedented level of commitment on the part of the Chinese government. The program, which has attracted many executives from the financial services sector, focuses on financial management and the functioning of capital markets. To meet the needs of China 's developing service industry, the curriculum will expand in January to include a services management concentration.

China, as the factory of the world, has created serious problems with environmental sustainability,? says Buck K. W. Pei, program director and associate dean for Asia Pacific Programs at the W. P. Carey School. ?Addressing environmental sustainability is a national priority, and China is accelerating the development of the services industry in an effort to be less reliant on manufacturing. The new services management concentration is designed to meet that need.?

The W. P. Carey MBA Shanghai marks the first time that the school has conferred degrees outside the United States . Unlike online degree programs offered in China by other U.S. universities, the W. P. Carey MBA Shanghai is delivered face-to-face in Mandarin and English by professors from top U.S. business schools.

The W. P. Carey faculty members lead a teaching team that includes professors from other major U.S. business schools, including MIT, New York University , Stanford and Yale.

By working with senior management at participating firms and government agencies, SNAI recruits executives for the MBA program. Nominations are based on the candidate's past performance, leadership potential and planned career path. Upon completion of the program curriculum, participants are granted a W. P. Carey MBA degree.

While in Arizona , the MBA students will tour the Tempe campus, visit classes and research centers, and meet with ASU President Michael Crow. The group also will meet with Valley-area business leaders and then fly to New York , where they will visit the New York Stock Exchange and meet with financial services companies, including W. P. Carey & Co. LLC, a global real estate investment firm founded by Carey, for whom the W. P. Carey School of Business is named.
