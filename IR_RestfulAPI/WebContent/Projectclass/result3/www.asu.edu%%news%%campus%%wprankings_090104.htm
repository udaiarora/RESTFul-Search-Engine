September 1, 2004

Business Programs at Arizona State University in Top 25

U.S.News & World Report's annual ranking of business schools continues to place the W. P. Carey School of Business in the top 25 for its undergraduate program, and six of the school's academic disciplines were among the top 20.

The supply chain management specialization, taught by some of the world's leading researchers in the discipline, was ranked fourth in the nation. Other disciplines that were rated in the top echelon included: accounting, ranked 15 th - up nine places over last year; management, ranked 19 th - up five spots; information systems, ranked 17 th (same as 2003); marketing, ranked 16 th - up one place; and production/operations management, ranked 16 th .

"Out of more than 420 accredited business schools we are a top 25 undergraduate school," said Robert E. Mittelstaedt, dean of the W. P. Carey School. "This places us among highly regarded programs nationally, however our departmental rankings clearly show that the W. P. Carey School is a better school than the number 25 placement would suggest. This directly demonstrates our need to continue to tell the world about the great quality and academic breadth of our newly named school."

Kay Faris, dean for undergraduate programs, said the U.S. News survey sets the stage for the new academic year, which begins on Monday.

"The results of the U.S. News survey will come as no surprise to our professional program students, who met a high standard when they were accepted to study under the world class faculty at the W. P. Carey School ," Faris said. "The school is continuing its commitment to students by improving services with a renewed focus on advising and an expansion of career services."

Grade point average (GPA) in business-required classes for students admitted into the professional program in May was 3.36, with accounting and economics most selective at an incoming GPA of 3.73.

Since spring, the school has redesigned its student advising team. Later this fall, the school will open the Business Career Center focused on the career needs of undergraduate business students.

W. P. Carey School of Business

W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University is one of the largest business schools in the United States with more than 150 faculty, 1,000 graduate and 2,600 upper-division undergraduate students. The school is internationally recognized for its leadership in supply chain management and services marketing and highly regarded for its faculty's research productivity. The L. William Seidman Research Institute focuses on applied research in economics, leveraging the research capabilities of the school's faculty. The school is ranked 25 th nationally for its undergraduate programs and 29 th for the W. P. Carey MBA.
