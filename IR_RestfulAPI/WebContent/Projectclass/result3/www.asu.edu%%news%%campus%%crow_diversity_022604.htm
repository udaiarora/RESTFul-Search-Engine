February 26, 2004

Crow says diversity not about numbers only

ASU President Michael Crow used strong words to describe the state of diversity in education during his speech at a diversity symposium last month at the University of Texas.

"The mere attainment of mathematical diversification of student body numbers is not the attainment of diversification for education in the United States," Crow said.

He said universities are still institutions where change is occurring only at the margins.

"Our university culture, in and of itself, remains elitist, exclusive, incomplete, slow to change, not adaptable enough," Crow said.

He said we have to find ways to reconsider the very design of our institutions to truly educate for a diverse America, because to be diverse is not a function of the numbers only.

Crow said it's time for universities to be designed to do the following things in order to adapt to a whole new conceptualization of diversity as we move toward a nation with no majority population:

. We must find ways to reach into all communities with warmth and openness.

. We have to learn how to communicate and teach between all ethnic and cultural perspectives -- not with a dominant cultural perspective and marginal alternative cultural perspectives.

. We have to move past the Euro-centric philosophical focus of the university, which is the same philosophical focus that permitted segregation and apartheid to exist. We have to replace it with an American philosophical focus.

. We have to move our universities - our educational enterprises - to new measures of success. Test scores reveal but a little. We need to look at such things as have we transformed our community; what has been the impact of our institutions in the communities we serve; what are the products of our university doing out in the world. The quality of students admitted to the university can't be the sole measure of success for the institution.

. We have to accelerate our academic cultural transformation in every discipline, not just cultural disciplines, not just ethnically oriented disciplines, EVERY discipline.

"Where are we?" Crow asked. "We're moving too slowly. We're unable to see the cultural box in which the university is presently trapped. We need to embrace cultural diversification in America and change the culture of the university itself as a critical first step. Otherwise, at the end of the day, a thousand years from now, we will still look like, smell like, and feel like places like Oxford and Cambridge and other institutions that we hold in high esteem, which have not embraced all that we're addressing here"

Neff with Public Affairs, can be reached at (480) 965-4836 or (nneff@asu.edu).
