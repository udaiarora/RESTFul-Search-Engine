TUITION INSTALLMENT PLAN

Brief Description
The Tuition Installment Plan provides students with an option to budget payment of their tuition over several months and to pay tuition with multiple credit cards or from multiple bank accounts.  If you choose to enroll in the Plan, tuition and fees will be billed on Student Accounts Receivable in installments with the following due dates:

Enrollment
All students are eligible to enroll in the Tuition Installment Plan after registering for classes.  To enroll in the Tuition Installment Plan, select one of the following options:

  • Log onto ASU Interactive and select the option "Pay Your ASU Charges", then "Tuition Services".
  • Complete and mail the Tuition Method of Payment form available at www.asu.edu/registrar/forms.
  • Visit the Student Accounts Office on the second floor of the Student Services Building.

You must enroll in the Tuition Installment Plan each fall and spring semester and each summer session. Nonfinancial aid students neglecting to pay tuition by the applicable tuition payment deadline will automatically be enrolled in the Tuition Installment Plan.

Enrollment in classes completes the registration process.  By registering for classes you agree to pay all applicable tuition and registration fees.  If you decide not to attend classes, you must officially withdraw from the University.

Enrollment Fee
Students enrolling in the Tuition Installment Plan, or automatically enrolled in the Plan due to failure to make payment by the tuition payment deadline, will be charged a per semester nonrefundable enrollment fee of $75 each Fall and Spring semester and $50 each Summer session. The enrollment fee will be billed on Student Accounts Receivable and is due at the same time as the first tuition installment. The enrollment fee is nonrefundable, even if you drop or withdraw from classes.

Financial Aid Students

No tuition payment action is required if financial aid is awarded prior to the tuition payment deadline.  Tuition charges for students with awarded financial aid will automatically be billed on Student Accounts Receivable with a single due date.

Financial aid students may choose to enroll in the Tuition Installment Plan. If you enroll in the Plan, tuition and fees will be billed on Student Accounts Receivable in two or three installments and you will be billed for the applicable enrollment fee of $75 or $50. Financial aid that you receive will pay all tuition charges, including the tuition installments. Excess financial aid will be refunded to you after tuition charges (due currently and in the future) and other currently due charges are paid. Do not enroll in the Tuition Installment Plan if your financial aid will be awarded prior to the applicable tuition payment deadline and you believe your financial aid will be adequate to pay your tuition charges.

If you enroll in the Tuition Installment Plan after the cut-off for the first billing statement, log onto ASU Interactive and select the option to "Pay Your ASU Charges", then "Student Accounts Receivable" to view unpaid charges and make payment.  Tuition Installment Plan charges will be billed on Student Accounts Receivable the day after you enroll in the Plan.

Tuition Installment Plan Payments
You may begin paying Tuition Installment Plan charges the day after you enroll in the Plan, but not before June 1 for the Fall semester, December 1 for the Spring semester and April 1 for the first and second summer sessions.  Payment can be made as often as is convenient and partial payments are permitted.

  • eCheck is an available payment option on ASU Interactive, “Pay Your ASU Charges”, then “Student Accounts Receivable”.  eChecks are ASU’s preferred payment method and are accepted with no service charge.

Similar to a debit card, the eCheck electronically debits a checking or savings account.  To make an eCheck payment you will need your bank’s routing number and your account number.  This information is available on the bottom of your check or from your financial institution.  An example of where to find the information on a check is available online when an eCheck payment is made.

eCheck payments can be made from regular U.S. checking and savings accounts in US dollars.  Checks issued from money market accounts, credit card companies, mutual funds, brokerage accounts, home equity or other lines of credit cannot be processed as an eCheck.  Personal checks are accepted by mail or in person.  Additional information is available at eCheck Payments.

  • MasterCard, Discover, and American Express credit and debit cards are accepted only for tuition installment plan payments made through ASU Interactive, “Pay Your ASU Charges”, then “Student Accounts Receivable”.

    ASU does not directly accept credit and debit card payments for tuition or student accounts receivable charges.  The University has arranged for a third-party vendor, infiNET, to accept credit and debit card payments online.  infiNET will charge a 2.75% service charge for credit and debit card payments in addition to the ASU payment amount.  The service chargeis not paid to ASU and is not refundable, even if the related payment to ASU is refunded.      Additional information is available at Credit and Debit Card Payments.

If you decide not to attend classes, you must officially withdraw from the University. If students enrolled in the Plan do not make scheduled payments, the students are prohibited from registering for classes in future semesters and are blocked from receiving university services, such as transcripts. Former students with outstanding tuition charges are referred to an outside collection agency.
