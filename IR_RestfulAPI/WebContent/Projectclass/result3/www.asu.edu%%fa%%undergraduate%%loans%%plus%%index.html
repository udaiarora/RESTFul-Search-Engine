Federal PLUS Loan

If your son or daughter is a fall/spring student and you apply for a fall only loan, funds will be disbursed as a fall/spring loan (half in the fall and half in the spring). Students attending one term only are the only students eligible to apply for a single term loan.

If you are a parent of an eligible undergraduate student who is enrolled at least half-time, and you meet the borrower eligibility criteria for a Federal PLUS loan, you can choose to borrow a Federal PLUS loan to help pay educational expenses of your son or daughter. Although the maximum amount an eligible parent can apply for each academic year is the amount noted on the student's Financial Aid Notification (FAN), he/she can choose to apply for an amount lower than what was originally offered. Parents are under no obligation to secure this loan, and are encouraged to only apply for what they feel is necessary given the family's financial situation.

We recommend that parents apply online using one of ASU's preferred lenders. These lenders have proven track records with ASU, faster processing times and provide consistent exemplary customer service.

How to Apply for a Federal PLUS Loan

Step 1: Choose a Lender

To begin the application process with an ASU Preferred Lender online, click the lender's online application of your choice below, or call the lender directly.

Information Needed to Apply

  • Parent Social Security Number
  • Student Social Security Number
  • Parent Driver's License
  • Complete address of two (2) references who are not related to you

Important

Unfortunately, not all applications are approved. If your parent's application is denied, please contact the SFAO to see if you qualify for alternative assistance.

Step 2: Complete the Federal PLUS MPN

If the application is approved, the Federal PLUS Master Promissory Note (MPN) will be electronically signed by your parent during the application process or will be mailed to him/her to complete. If it is mailed, your parent will need to sign and return it to the lender address provided on the Federal PLUS MPN.

Step 3: Receive Federal PLUS Funds

If your parent agrees during the application process to allow excess funds to be returned to you (the student), ASU will electronically apply Federal PLUS funds to your student account to pay outstanding charges. Any remaining funds will be distributed to you (the student) via paper check or direct deposit to your bank account. Electronic treatment of Federal PLUS funds is only available if your parent agrees to the option noted above. Otherwise, your parent's preferred lender will mail a paper check to ASU which will then be forwarded by ASU to your parent via mail. These funds will not be applied to your student account.

Borrower Eligibility Criteria

Generally speaking, Federal PLUS loan borrowers must meet the same eligibility criteria as their son/daughter and pass a credit check by the lender in order to qualify for a Federal PLUS loan. Additional details on what constitutes parent borrower eligibility criteria follow. Some lenders have additional eligibility criteria so be sure to check with your lender for additional specifics.

  • You are a parent of an eligible undergraduate student who is enrolled at least half-time, and
  • You have a good credit history (as determined through a credit check process by the lender), and
  • You are a U.S. citizen or national, permanent U.S. resident, or other eligible noncitizen, and
  • You have a valid Social Security Number, and
  • You are not be in default on any federal education loan, and
  • You do not owe a Title IV overpayment, and
  • You do not have property subject to a judgment lien for a debt owed to the United States, and
  • You have fulfilled additional requirements imposed by the guarantor of a loan for which the principal and interest have been discharged or written off, and
  • You have not borrowed in excess of any annual or aggregate Federal Stafford loan limits, and
  • You have not have been determined ineligible - solely due to your error or as a result of providing false or misleading information - for a FFELP loan that has already been obtained. You are entitled to receive an additional Federal PLUS loan only if you repay in full any ineligible loan or ineligible portion of a loan.

Loan Discharge Information

You will not qualify for a loan discharge if you are unable to meet the physical or legal requirements (age, physical or mental condition, criminal record, etc.) of your state to work in the career for which you were training.
