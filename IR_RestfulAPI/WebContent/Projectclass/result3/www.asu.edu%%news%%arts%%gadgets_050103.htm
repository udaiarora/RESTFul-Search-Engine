High and low tech Gadgets and Gizmos to entertain the whole family at ASU Art Museum

TEMPE, Ariz. - High and low tech Gadgets and Gizmos will star in this summer's lighthearted family exhibition at the ASU Art Museum. Opening June 7 and running through August 31, the exhibition is the museum's fourth annual summer family show.

In conjunction with Gadgets and Gizmos, the ASU Art Museum also will hold its fourth annual Family Fun Day, from 10 a.m. - 2 p.m., June 28. The family day will incorporate a combination of hands-on and other activities to keep children busy for hours. Both the exhibition and the family day are free.

Incorporating high technology such as the tiny video camera used by Jim Campbell, or low technology such as the hand-carved wooden cuckoo clocks by Abel Barroso, artists have combined pieces and parts into intriguing works of art.

In this exhibition, curator Jean Makin brings together numerous pieces from the ASU Art Museum collection that date from early last century to 2000. From the hand carved, wind-operated Windmill Whirligig, to Dennis Oppenheim's prickly pear cacti in Last Dance, the exhibition is a sampling of works that incorporate technology.

"Some pieces are high tech, some are low tech, some incorporate manmade technology while others use nature's technology," Makin said. "We even have pieces that use spent technology, such as Kim Abele's smog picture and Deborah Butterfield's horse, which is made of found objects."

Gadgets and Gizmos is an exhibition with equal appeal to adults and children alike. From complex and thought provoking to whimsical and fun, the works in this unique exhibition will ensure the whole family is entertained.

There is, for example, no age limit to the fascination inspired by Hans Kooi's untitled floating sculpture in which the top half of the abstract piece magnetically levitates above the bottom portion. Likewise, visitors of all ages enjoy interacting with the tiny video camera in Jim Campbell's Frames of Reference, actually becoming one with the artwork as it spins and captures fleeting images of those watching it.

Free hands-on activities for children will be situated near several of the pieces in the exhibition, enabling visitors to incorporate technology into their own works of art.
All this will be presented in the cool interior of the ASU Art Museum during the height of the Phoenix summer. Temperatures and humidity levels in the museum must be kept constant to protect the art, and entering the 68-degree building on a hot summer day is a welcome escape from the Valley's ferocious heat.

The ASU Art Museum is a division of The Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts at Arizona State University. It is located on the southeast corner of Mill Avenue and 10th Street, Tempe. For more information, please call (480) 965-2787 or visit the museum online at http://asuartmuseum.asu.edu.

When You Go:
Location: ASU Art Museum, Nelson Fine Arts Center, corner Mill Avenue and 10th Street, Tempe.
Date &Time: Gadgets and Gizmos will run June 7 - August 31.
The fourth annual Family Fun Day, will take place from 10 a.m. - 2 p.m., June 28.
Parking: Free parking is available in ASU Art Museum-marked spaces at the south end of Tempe Center, located at the NE corner of Mill Avenue and 10th Street. Visitors using museum spaces must sign in at the front desk in the lobby of the Nelson Fine Arts Center. ASU parking is also free on weekends and after 7 p.m. on weeknights.
Website: http://asuartmuseum.asu.edu
Cost: Free
