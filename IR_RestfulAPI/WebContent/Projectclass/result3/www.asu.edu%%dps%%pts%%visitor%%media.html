Parking for Reporters

These visitor lots are always open to reporters, based on space availability:

• Lots 16, 17, and 72 - These pay-by-space lots charge $1/hour
• Parking Structure One’s Visitor section - College Ave. and Apache Blvd.

Visitors enter on the north side and pay $2/per hour for covered parking.

To avoid the exit fee, have your parking stub validated by the News Bureau (located in the Administration Building, B-wing, Room 170).

Media Parking Passes

The News Bureau, in cooperation with the Campus Office of Parking and Transit, issues media parking passes that are valid for all open lots and loading zones. They ARE NOT VALID at parking meters; in visitor parking lots; in restricted access lots; reserved or disabled parking spaces; or in red zones.

To receive a media parking pass, call Keith Jennings at the ASU News Bureau, (480) 965-3502.

News Conference Parking

Parking spaces will be set aside for reporters on a first-come, first-served basis whenever a news conference is scheduled on campus. Locations will be announced when the news conference is scheduled.

News Bureau personnel will be available at the news conference to validate parking stubs for anyone who parks in a visitor lot.

What If You Follow All the Rules and Still Get a Citation?

If you get a parking ticket for violating university parking regulations while you are on campus working on a story or attending a news conference, the ticket usually can be dismissed UNLESS it was:

  • issued for parking in a reserved or disabled parking stall, a fire lane or a sidewalk; or
  • issued for unauthorized parking on a university mall or other campus pedestrian thoroughfare. (See authorization procedure below.)

Parking on Campus Malls

The News Bureau will make arrangements for parking media vehicles on the campus malls only when absolutely necessary. Access to campus malls is severely restricted because the malls are designed for pedestrian, not vehicle, use. A police escort is required when parking media vehicles on a campus mall.

Please call the News Bureau at 965-3502 at least two hours in advance if you need to park on a campus mall. Gaining mall access will be much easier if you make arrangements 24 hours in advance.

The ASU Department of Public Safety reserves the right to deny any request for parking on a university mall or walkway if the department believes that it would create a hazard.

One alternative to parking on a campus mall is to use the News Bureau’s four-seat electric cart to transport your equipment. The cart has a 20-square-foot cargo area. To make arrangements to use the electric cart, contact the News Bureau at 965-3502

Microwave Sites for Broadcasters

ASU has a high concentration of multistory buildings that can make it difficult to get a microwave signal to South Mountain. If you need to do a live broadcast from a particular building or area, contact Keith Jennings (480) 965-3502 for information on available sites.
