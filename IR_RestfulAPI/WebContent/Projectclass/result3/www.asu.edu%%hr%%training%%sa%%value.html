Benefits

.

Why should you take Supervisory Academy?  What do you get out of the program?  There are many benefits that come from completing the Academy:

.

You receive one year of supervisory credit for completing the course. This means that if you apply for a job at ASU that requires 3 years of supervisory experience, and you only have two years, completion of Supervisory Academy is counted as that third year of experience you need.

If you are intested in enrolling in the Certified Public Manager Program, you must meet advanced entry requirements or complete an approved supervisory training program - and Supervisory Academy is an approved program that meets this requirement.

Participants who would like to apply for Continuing Education Units (CEU's) through the College of Extended Education, may do so upon successful completion of the course.

New supervisors need to make the right decisions the first time and keep trial and error learning to a minimum. Supervisory Academy builds confidence, skills and competencies, while introducing the participants to key leaders at ASU. These leaders teach them how to make good decisions now and in the future.

The direction and changes stemming from the New American University makes it imperative that our leaders are flexible, adjust to changes rapidly and can quickly make sound decisions. Supervisors with these skills, and who understand the bigger picture of ASU will be better positioned to help lead these change.

Supervisory Academy focuses on practical, critical skills and gives participants knowledge they can use immediately. This isn't a conceptual, theory-oriented program. Participants practice the skills in class, and have homework assignments that will help them to being applying their new skills (and teaching them to others) right away.
