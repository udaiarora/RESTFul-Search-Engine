May 1, 2003

SRC helps kids battle boredom with camp

It's that time of year when many ASU employees may be wondering how to keep their kids boredom-free during the summer. Fortunately, registration is now underway for kid's summer camp activities at the Student Recreation Complex (SRC) at ASU Main. This year's camp is open from 7:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m., Monday - Friday, May 27 - Aug. 8.

SRC Program Coordinator Tara Fisher says past offerings have been expanded to offer kids between the ages of 5 and 11 even more opportunities for learning new skills, making friends and having fun.

As in years past, campers will spend time swimming in the SRC pool, enjoying arts and crafts, watching movies, playing games, going on field trips and taking part in a variety of sports.

New this year is Kids Quest, offering a variety of adventure options campers can participate in, including horseback riding, skateboarding, rock climbing, swim lessons, ice skating, bowling and billiards. Kids Quest sports activities include volleyball, basketball, soccer, flag football, tennis, softball and table tennis.

"It is hard to keep each child's interest for each week of camp," she says. "So we decided to add different activities for an extra price so each child could walk away from camp, hopefully, learning or experiencing a new sport or activity. The kids are excited they can do these new activities."

Fisher is looking forward to this year's activities, and says the most fulfilling part of her job is getting to work with kids.

"Children are our future and it is our duty to prepare them to become leaders," she says. "I believe that youth programs are a great place where we can teach children the lessons of leadership, integrity and responsibility through sports, games and arts. Our program goal is to have each child walk away a better person."

ASU faculty, staff and students may register their children for camp at the second floor administrative office at the SRC. Prices are $115 per weeklong session for SRC members and $135 per weeklong session for non-members. Discounts are offered to those who prepay for five-session blocks. There are also additional fees Kids Quest activities.

For information, call the SRC at (480) 965-8900 or go to (www.asu.edu/src/child_fam/).

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
