September 23, 2004

3 grad students earn Emeriti Fellowships

Three graduate students on track for careers in academia have been awarded Faculty Emeriti Fellowships. Doctoral students Sara Grineski, Ha Lam and Tiffany Rice were recognized as the 2004-05 Faculty Emeriti Association Fellows at a reception Sept. 9 that ushered in the 11th year of the Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) Program.

The Faculty Emeriti Association was founded in 1978 by a group of emeriti faculty as a continuing social and university-connected educational outlet. It is unique in academia for faculty members to have their own endowment in support of future academics in any discipline within the academy.

The Emeriti Fellow Fund was established by members of the association in 1993 within the ASU Foundation. When the PFF Program was established in the Division of Graduate Studies, the association decided it was a logical place to award students who would follow them into the professoriate.

The funding supports the ASU PFF Program, a nationally recognized program designed to train and mentor doctoral and master of fine arts students for faculty positions at the nation’s colleges and universities. The program teaches these aspiring professors about the challenges and responsibilities of a career in academia. There are an estimated 100 graduate students enrolled in ASU’s program.

“We are pleased to name these three PFF doctoral students as ASU Emeriti Fellows for 2004-05,” says Joyce Foster-Larson, chairwoman of the fellowship selection committee. “It is anticipated that they will make significant contributions within the academy.”

“The recipients of this award undergo serious academic review by the selection committee,” adds Maria Allison, vice provost and dean of graduate studies. “These highly competitive fellowships create a special bond between those ASU faculty emeriti who have a lifetime of contribution to their academic professions and those doctoral students who will follow in their footsteps.”

Faculty Emeriti Fellows have varied educational backgrounds and research interests, but they share a drive to become educators of higher learning.

• Grineski is pursuing a Ph.D. in sociology. She came to ASU in 2001 on an Integrative Graduate Education Research and Training (IGERT) in Urban Ecology Fellowship. Her adviser is Professor Bob Bolin.

• Lam is a doctoral student in language and literacy in the Department of Curriculum and Instruction in the College of Education . She is a Gates Millennium Scholar and chairwoman of the Language Policy Special Interest Group of the National Association for Bilingual Education. Lam’s adviser is Professor Terrence Wiley.

• Rice is a doctoral student in the counseling psychology program. Rice is co-editor of the American Psychological Association’s policy on multicultural guidelines for education, research and practice. Her adviser is Professor Patricia Arredondo.
