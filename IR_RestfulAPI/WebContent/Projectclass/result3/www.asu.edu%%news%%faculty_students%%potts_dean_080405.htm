August 4, 2005

University College names Potts assistant dean

Continuing a career of nearly 15 years with ASU, Shelly Keimig Potts has been named assistant dean of assessment and evaluation with University College.

“Shelly’s focus will be to evaluate the quality and effectiveness of all programs and services within University College,” says Gail Hackett, vice provost and University College dean. “She brings to our college a wealth of experience developing evaluation strategies and implementing new models of service and delivery, which is a central design element in shaping the new college.”

Potts was with the Division of Undergraduate Academic Services since 2002, first as interim executive director, then as the associate executive director. She previously spent 10 years with the ASU Office of University Evaluation.

She received her bachelor’s degree in psychology from Purdue University and a master’s degree at Ball State University. Potts earned her doctorate in educational leadership and policy studies at ASU. Her scholarly publications and presentations include topics on assessing student learning and evaluating educational programs. In addition, she has served as an evaluation consultant on a number of initiatives for the U.S. Department of Education and the National Science Foundation.

Academic discovery and community engagement are distinguishing characteristics of University College at ASU. The college extends university access to freshmen, transfer, re-entry and exploratory students, and offers every student the opportunity for success through academic services and innovative degree offerings. High academic standards are integrated into its interdisciplinary studies degree, community service programs, individualized advising, extended education programs, and extensive student support services.

The college serves students on all campuses. In fall 2006, it will be one of several ASU colleges that will form the new Downtown Phoenix campus, with administrative offices and classes opening at that location.

For more information about University College, visit (http://uc.asu.edu).

Breese, with University College, can be reached at (480) 727-5314 or (shaun.breese@asu.edu).
