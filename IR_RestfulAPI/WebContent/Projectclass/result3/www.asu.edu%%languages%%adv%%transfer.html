PROCEDURES FOR TRANSFER STUDENTS

All transfer students will find this site very helpful as a starting point:  http://www.asu.edu/admissions/transfercenter/

If you are a Transfer Student pursuing a Bachelor of Arts degree in Chinese, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Russian or Spanish...
  • and you are transferring from one of the Arizona Community Colleges, Northern Arizona University or University of Arizona: You need only to make an appointment with one of the Department of Languages and Literatures Academic Advisors by calling the DLL Main Office at 480-965-6281.  The course equivalencies between our institutions are already articulated, and should be automatically determined when a DARS audit is run.  To verify the transferability of your credits, you may consult the Arizona Course Applicability System at:  http://az.transfer.org/cas.
  • and you are transferring from an out-of-state college or university: First make an appointment for a transfer evaluation with a College of Liberal Arts and Sciences advisor in the Office of Academic Programs in the Foundation Building, #1120. Phone: 480-965-6506. A CLAS advisor will determine which courses from your previous university fulfill University General Studies and College of Liberal Arts and Sciences proficiency and distribution requirements.  Any language courses need to be evaluated by a Department of Languages and Literatures Academic Advisor.  Please bring course descriptions and/or course syllabi to both appointments to facilitate the determination of equivalencies.  Once your CLAS transfer evaluation is complete, then make an appointment with one of the Department of Languages and Literatures Academic Advisors by calling the DLL Main Office at 480-965-6281.

    •  
  • and you are transferring credits directly from a foreign university, you must have your international transcripts evaluated by International Admissions in the Student Services Building, SSV 110 (480-965-2688).  Once you know that the ASU Admissions office has accepted the international credits, then you should make an appointment with an advisor at the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Office of Academic Programs by calling 480-965-6506. Then, make an appointment with one of the Department of Languages and Literatures Academic Advisors by calling the DLL Main Office at 480-965-6281.  Please note that if you participated in a study abroad program sponsored by ASU or another university in the United States, these are not considered "international credits."
If you are a language major pursuing a Bachelor of Arts in Education (B.A.E.) degree with a Specialization in French, German, Japanese and/or Spanish, you should first consult with your Academic Advisor in the College of Education Office of Student Affairs in Payne [EDB] Building. Phone: 480-965-5555.  Once you have met with a College of Education advisor, then schedule an appointment with one of the Department of Languages and Literatures Academic Advisors by calling the DLL Main Office at 480-965-6281 to go over the language specialization and related fields requirements.

A note about credits:
  • You must complete a minimum of 30 credit hours at ASU Tempe Campus to receive any major from ASU Tempe Campus.
  • You must complete a minimum of 56 credit hours at ASU to be eligible for graduation with academic recognition (Summa Cum Laude, Magna Cum Laude and Cum Laude).
  • You must complete a minimum of 12 upper-division credit hours at ASU Tempe Campus in your Chinese, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Russian or Spanish major to receive that major at ASU Tempe Campus.
  • You must complete a minimum of 6 upper-division credit hours at ASU Tempe Campus in your minor to receive that minor from ASU Tempe Campus.
