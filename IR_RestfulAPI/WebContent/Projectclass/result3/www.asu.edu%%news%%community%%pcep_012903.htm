January 29, 2003

Enrichment programs share world of knowledge with community

Connecting the university with the community it serves, and using collective energy to solve some of the greatest challenges facing society, is a primary goal of ASU President Michael Crow. Three programs under the umbrella of the President's Community Enrichment Program exactly model this idea for the entire university, says Patricia Beaty, vice president, Strategic Planning & Marketing, with the ASU Foundation.

The programs, Adventures in Learning, Journeys of the Mind and Great Conversations, are tools to enrich the ASU learning experience for community friends and allow them to become engaged in activities and interact with a rich array of faculty talent, she says.

In the three years since its inception, Adventures in Learning has earned a successful track record among its participants who eagerly line up for its four-to-six-week non-credit course offerings. Participant volunteers open their homes as hosts and give countless hours to organize more than a dozen courses offered each season, said Adrienne Schiffner, program coordinator for Adventures in Learning and Great Conversations.

Schiffner said while most courses offered are liberal arts based, there is a tremendous shift in course topics from year to year to keep things interesting. Course offerings range from classical literature to the changing environment and origins of humanity, she said, and enrollment is limited to 45. Given the prevailing world events, some topics generate long waiting lists. This year's programs on the Middle East, Latin America, Asia and the Civil War are proving to be among the most popular.

ASU History Assistant Professor Kyle Longley is teaching a course on United States-Latin American relations in the Adventures in Learning program. He is one of 15 faculty members involved in the 2003 Adventures in Learning series.

"Most of the participants, mainly retirees, are extremely interested in learning more about this topic because they played a role in its development during their personal careers," Longley said. "Their own experiences really enhance the lecture content and as the instructor, I am finding teaching the class is stimulating and inspiring."
Great Conversations blends community members with professors and Barrett Honors College students to discuss literary works in small group settings.

Journeys of the Mind, which attracts hundreds of Valley leaders each year, offers community members an exciting opportunity to learn about progressive research being conducted at ASU. Program coordinator Gena Bonsall said the program links the intellectual energy found the in classroom and laboratory to the issues and challenges facing society.

"It is so exciting to see how community members are embracing the university because of the knowledge gleaned from this program," said Bonsall. "Journeys of the Mind is much more than just taking a class. It's an insider's look to the hidden treasure within the university. Participants are going back into the community feeling connected to ASU and energized by what we are accomplishing here."
