June 20, 2003

Arizona Board of Regents approves new departments: W. P. Carey School of Business announces faculty leadership

The Arizona Board of Regents today approved the establishment of the department of information systems at the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. The board also approved a new name for the accounting program's home - the school of accountancy. Both of these actions will enable the school to strengthen its national leadership in the two fields.

Dr. James Boatsman, the KPMG Professor of Accountancy, is the new director of the school of accountancy. Dr. Robert St. Louis chairs the department of information systems.

"Since the early 1990s, the W. P. Carey School has been among the leaders in its emphasis on and integration of information systems into its accountancy and business programs " said Dean Larry E. Penley. "Today's decision to create a stand alone information systems department positions the school well for the anticipated recovery and ever-growing integration of information systems into management. This move also reflects the long time commitment to nationally ranked undergraduate and graduate accounting and tax programs."

The disciplines of information systems and accountancy had been joined as the School of Accountancy and Information Management since 1996. Under the leadership of Professor Philip Reckers, the school grew in quality and reputation to become one of the leading programs in the country. The W. P. Carey MBA/Master of Science in Information Management was ranked 10th in the nation among information systems programs, and the graduate accounting program was listed 15th according to the latest US News & World Report survey of 352 graduate management programs accredited by AACSB International.

After soliciting input from its professional advisory board, the faculty of the school of accounting and information management pursued the reorganization in order to build from the strong foundation already established in both areas. This enables both units to focus more strongly on core competencies while continuing to infuse information technology throughout the W. P. Carey School.

"Since the merger seven years ago, the information systems department has grown both in numbers of faculty and in quantity of research. With the energy originating in the Center for Advancing Business through Information Technology, these thinkers are among the leading innovators in the field nationally," Penley added.

"Accountancy, which has been one of the foundations of excellence throughout our history, continues to anchor the W. P. Carey School. Accounting faculty members are respected for their research and for their leadership in professional policy formation, and our graduates are in key positions at the top firms."

W. P. CAREY SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 25th in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program offers the Business Honors program to some of the best students at the university. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
