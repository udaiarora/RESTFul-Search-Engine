Cronkite School #1 in the nation in intercollegiate broadcast news contest

The Arizona State University Walter Cronkite School of Journalism currently ranks first in the nation in the Intercollegiate Broadcast News Competition of the prestigious Hearst Journalism Awards program.

ASU students Will Pitts (3 rd place Radio), Risa Avila (7 th place TV), Tom Murray (14 th place Radio) and Brandon Rittiman (15 th place TV) led the Cronkite School to a top finish after the first round of competition in the category of Feature Reporting. The second round of The Hearst Awards will focus on News Reporting. Based on his top five finish in radio reporting, ASU's Will Pitts moves into the national semifinal competition in April.

"We're proud that our students are living up to the standards of Walter Cronkite," said Steve Doig, interim director of the Cronkite School. "Their performance is a credit to their own hard work and the work of their instructors."

The 2003-04 broadcast news competitions are held in more than 100 member colleges and universities of the Association of Schools of Journalism and Mass Communication with accredited undergraduate journalism programs.

The Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication is part of the College of Public Programs at Arizona State University. The Cronkite School is one of the largest mass communication programs in the United States with more than 1,700 undergraduate majors and pre-majors and more than 85 graduate students. U.S. News & World Report and the Gourman Report rank the Cronkite School among the most outstanding journalism programs in the United States.
