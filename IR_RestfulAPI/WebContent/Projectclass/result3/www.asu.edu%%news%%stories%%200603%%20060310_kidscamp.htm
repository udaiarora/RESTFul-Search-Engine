March 10, 2006

ASU offers more than 70 summer programs for children and teens

The days of summer are fast approaching, when the kids will be out of school and parents will scramble to find activities that keep them active and engaged. This year Arizona State University offers more than 70 summer programs for children from toddlers to teenagers, and some for adults.

ASU's Herberger College for Kids helps children experience the arts in classes including dance, drama, music and art.

Programs range from drama, music and art to math, science, computers and sports. Children can build a robot, explore ancient petroglyphs, learn to draw, improve their reading or even their batting average. ASU departments and schools are reaching into the community to touch children’s lives, and to spark their interest in a college-educated future.

A description of ASU summer programs for children and teens is online at http://www.asu.edu/community. Click on “summer programs and camps.” Included are dates, cost, location and program goals.

Programs for Talented Youth offers talented 3rd through 7th grade students the chance to explore Mars, try their hands at creative writing or learn computer graphics, for instance. Priority application deadline for these academically-oriented classes is April 7.

The Herberger College of Fine Arts is now taking registration for its second full summer of music, theater, dance and art classes for children, teens and adults. Camp Broadway is a week-long day camp that will teach acting, singing, dance, stage design and make-up.

Programs for Talented Youth offers a variety of experiences for 3rd through 7th grade students.

Intercollegiate Athletics offers camps and instruction in baseball, basketball, swimming, soccer, golf, football, volleyball and wrestling.

Reading classes in different skill levels will be taught throughout the Valley, for entering first-graders all the way up to teens and adults who want to improve their reading speed and comprehension.

The ASU Student Recreation Complex will once again hold the Sun Devil Kids’ Camp from May 30 through Aug. 4, an all-day program of sports and arts and crafts for children between the ages of 5 and 11.

Overall, ASU has more than 900 outreach opportunities for children and adults throughout the state, all year long. Persons interested in receiving regular news and updates about ASU’s community outreach events and activities may sign up to receive a bi-monthly newsletter at http://community.uui.asu.edu/signup.asp.
