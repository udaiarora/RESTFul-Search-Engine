Teacher: Mr. Conrad Davis
Chaparral High School

First Place — Secondary (Grades 9-12)

 


The Debt I Owe

"Free Nelson Mandela, Free Nelson Mandela." So many times I hear these words replaying in my mind. Out of all the many people that have inspired me, no one touches my heart as much as one man of absolute conviction, Nelson Mandela.

After over 25 years serving a prison sentence for demanding basic dignity, Nelson Mandela rose from the ashes and became President of South Africa. If one black man can become president of a predominantly white, segregated society, who knows what I can achieve? The racism in South Africa has always disturbed me. As a young child, I couldn't understand why two sets of people were separated simply because of their color. Was there no justice in the world? I remember following the news, watching the riots, wondering what kind of world I was living in. Then, one memorable day, I turned on the news to see Nelson Mandela taking his first steps to freedom.

Every day I think about my color and how it makes me different from the people around me. Growing up with a white father and a black mother traps me between the two worlds. As much as I try to pretend that color holds no meaning in society, the fact of the matter is that color is important. I have been lucky enough to avoid racial confrontations, but the stories I read and hear expose me to the reality of racism in the world.

To me, Nelson Mandela symbolizes hope. There will always be injustice in the world, but the people that stand out and fight for their beliefs shape the world for future generations. Because of people like Nelson Mandela and Martin Luther King I can walk down the street and live the life of a normal American citizen. For this I am grateful and inspired to live my life to the fullest, taking advantage of the opportunity I have been given by these courageous people.

— This is student work, transcribed as the student presented it.
