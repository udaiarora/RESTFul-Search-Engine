Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting (Spotlight Series)

Tuesday, December 07, 2004, MU Turquoise Lounge

Speaker:  Nancy Gutierrez, Vice Provost

Topic: Current and future steps in revising the university?s policies and procedures relating to Academic Professionals

Present: Approximately 80 people

Dr. Gutierrez? presentation outline is on the web at http://www.asu.edu/provost/CAPS/CAPS%20Spotlight%20Forum.ppt.

 Dr. Gutierrez? presentation focused on the recommendations of last year?s Academic Professional Task Force, the Provost?s response to these recommendations, and the current status of action on the recommendations.  In large part she reviewed the Provost?s response, which is available at http://www.asu.edu/provost/ap/APTF-prov-resp.doc.  . 

 She also mentioned several actions that have been taken since the Provost?s response was distributed:

 ­          A working group that will write new ACD policy language, as required by the recommendations, has been formed.  The working group was selected so as to include representatives from all three campuses; and representatives of each of the three types of AP positions; Research, Pedagogy, and Information and Materials management; and a representative from the Office of General Counsel.

 ­          A proposal to allow multiple-year appointments for APs has been approved by the Academic Senate and will be presented at the next Board of Regents meeting.  If this proposal is approved, the working group will develop policies for this new type of appointment.  The goal is to present the new policy to the Academic Senate this spring.

 Some questions raised in the question-and-answer portion of the program:

 Q: When a new AP position is created, who will determine the correct appointment type (continuing-track, multi-year, or one-year) for the position? 

A:  The decision will be made jointly by the department involved and its college administration.

 Q: What is the distinction between continuing status and tenure? 

A:  Tenure is a property right, while Continuing Status is an expectation of employment.  In practice, they are very similar, but legally they?re different.

 Questions also dealt with the proposed move of Academic Advisors out of the Academic Professional category into a set of Classified Staff and Service Professional positions.  It was explained that Barbara Mawhiney is currently developing these position descriptions.  Academic advisors who currently hold Academic Associate positions will be offered Service Professional contracts in the next appointment period.

 Dr. Gutierrez responded to further questions about the future status of Academic Advisors. She pointed out that one advantage of Service Professional positions is that they offer the possibility of promotion and salary increases. One of the main reasons for the change, she noted, was to place advisors across the university in the same category as a basis for providing parity and uniformity in salaries. She also noted that it should be possible for Advisors to continue to teach, should they and their departments find this desirable. The number of sections to be taught will be negotiated by Advisors and their supervisors.
