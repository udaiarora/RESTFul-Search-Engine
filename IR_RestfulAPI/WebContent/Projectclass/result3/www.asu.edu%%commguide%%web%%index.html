ASU Web Standards

These standards apply to all institutional web sites (colleges, schools, departments, administrative units, institutes, centers, programs, divisions, etc.) regardless of domain: .edu, .com, .org, etc. (See Exceptions list.)

  1. An ASU logo/signature or the identity bar must be included at the top of all institutional web pages. If the ASU logo is not used at the top of the page, the 25 pixel-high identity bar in one of the approved color combinations must be included at the top instead. Even if the identity bar is used at the top of a page, an ASU logo/signature must still appear elsewhere on the page (in the footer, for example). See Identity Bar Examples

  2. The ASU logo must link to ASU’s home page, www.asu.edu or a campus home page as appropriate (www.west.asu.edu, www.poly.asu.edu, or www.asu.edu/downtownphoenix). Campus home pages should also provide a link to www.asu.edu.

  3. The appropriate ASU logo/signature must be presented in a visible, undistorted manner whenever it appears on the web. The ASU logo must be used in one of the approved colors and should have the appropriate area of isolation. Secondary logos are not permitted unless they have been approved.

  4. When a project, event, or conference is co-sponsored by additional entities, additional sponsor logos can be included.

    See the Graphic Standards section of the CommGuide for more information, www.asu.edu/gsm.

  5. All institutional web pages must include contact information (department email address, mailing address, physical location, and/or phone number). This contact must be able to respond to inquiries about the page/site in a timely manner, i.e., 2 business days or less. This contact information may link to a web form instead of an individual email address.

  6. Campus home pages and all other pages that collect sensitive information (social security numbers, credit card information, etc.) must include a link to the ASU privacy statement (www.asu.edu/privacy). Provide a link to this statement using the word Privacy. (See www.asu.edu for an example.) Do not duplicate the Privacy page content on other web sites. Sites should have the appropriate level of security for the information collected.

  7. Web pages must be accessible to all users, including users with disabilities, compliant with ASU’s policies, and compatible with screen readers as well as other assistive technologies. Offer a method for visitors to contact the department should they encounter difficulties accessing web content.

    Optional: ASU web sites may provide a link to ASU’s accessibility page, www.asu.edu/accessibility.

    See www.asu.edu/accessibility for links to more information.

  8. All ASU web content is copyright Arizona Board of Regents. All ASU websites should provide a Copyright and Trademark Statement link that is hyperlinked to www.asu.edu/copyright. This link must must be included on the first page (scrolling is acceptable) of all web sites. When contracting with an outside vendor, the vendor agreement must provide for ABOR ownership. Contact the Office of General Counsel for additional information. (See www.asu.edu for an example.)

    See ABOR policy 6-908, Intellectual Property Policy, http://www.abor.asu.edu/1_the_regents/policymanual/chap6/chap6_part2.htm#6-908

  9. Pursuant to privacy law, signed photo releases must be obtained for individuals whose photos are posted on ASU web sites. All pictures of children require a signed release from a parent or legal guardian.

Exceptions:

  • ASU Foundation
  • Arizona Technology Enterprises (AZTE)
  • Personal web sites
  • Faculty web sites

College Web Standards

Colleges and other units may have additional unit-specific requirements related to web standards.

  • College of Liberal Arts and Sciences: http://clas.asu.edu/facultystaff/it/webdacs/standards/

 

Additional recommendations

  1. Validate all code using a validator such as W3C HTML validator, http://validator.w3.org/.

  2. Test web pages in different web browsers (Internet Explorer, Firefox, Safari, etc.).

  3. Test web pages using assistive technology such as a screen reader (JAWS, etc.)

  4. Provide a date on pages as appropriate – date modified, effective date, or expiration date for content, etc. (link to more info will be provided)

  5. Sites sponsored by ASU including journals and conference sites should include an ASU logo.
