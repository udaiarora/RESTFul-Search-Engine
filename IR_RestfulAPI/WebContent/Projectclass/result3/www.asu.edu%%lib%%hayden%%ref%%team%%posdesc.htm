DESCRIPTION OF RESPONSIBILITIES & SKILLS REQUIRED
FOR REFERENCE TEAM LEADER

Description of Responsibilites & Skills Required for Reference Team Leader


Description of Responsibilities:

Facilitates and coordinates policy discussions and assists the team in reaching consensus. Implements policies and standards that are developed by the team. Serves as point of contact for other library departments and represents Reference Services at Administrative Council and Library Services Council. Supervises academic professionals and serves as Primary Evaluator in their personnel actions. Reports to the Associate Dean for Public Services.

Skills Required:

--Excellent communication and interpersonal skills

--Ability to establish and maintain effective working relationships with other library departments

--Ability to deal constructively with conflict

--Consensus-building skills

--Supervisory skills

--Planning and organizational skills

--Analytical and problem-solving skills



DESCRIPTION OF RESPONSIBILITIES & SKILLS REQUIRED
FOR SUPERVISOR OF LIBRARY SPECIALISTS



Description of Responsibilities:

Hires, trains, schedules, supervises, and evaluates the Library Specialists whose primary responsibility is reference desk service. Provides reference desk training and supervision for other classified staff as needed.

Skills Required:

--Supervisory skills

--Planning and organizational skills

--Analytical and problem-solving skills

--Strong communication and interpersonal skills



DESCRIPTION OF RESPONSIBILITIES & SKILLS REQUIRED FOR
SUPERVISOR OF LIBRARY SUPERVISOR/DEPARTMENTAL SECRETARY



Description of Responsibilities:

Hires, trains, supervises, and evaluates the Library Supervisor who is responsible for the maintenance of the reference collection. Hires, trains, supervises, and evaluates the departmental Secretary.

Skills Required:

--Supervisory skills

--Planning and organizational skills

--Analytical and problem-solving skills

--Strong communication and interpersonal skills



DESCRIPTION OF RESPONSIBILITIES & SKILLS REQUIRED
FOR REFERENCE MEETING FACILITATOR



Description of Responsibilities:

The Reference Meeting Facilitator will be responsible for scheduling, managing, and recording weekly reference meetings. The Meeting Facilitator will set meeting agendas, direct meeting discussions, and facilitate the participative process. Specifically the role of facilitator will include planning and preparing for reference meetings, keeping meetings "on track" and "on time", encouraging participation, managing conflicts that arise in meetings, and ensuring that meetings are conducted in accordance with the standards of behavior established by the Reference Department. Additionally, the Meeting Facilitator will oversee the recording and distribution of meeting minutes.

Skills Required:

--Strong organizational skills

--Strong communication skills

--Good listening skills

--Ability to manage conflicts

--Ability to synthesize and summarize ideas

--Skill in operating in participative environment, managing group interactions, and achieving consensus



*approved by Ref. Dept. 2/9/95

DESCRIPTION OF RESPONSIBILITIES & SKILLS REQUIRED
FOR REFERENCE COMPUTER COORDINATOR



Description of Responsibilities:

Responsible for maintaining the computers assigned to Hayden Reference Department in both the offices and the MARS room in the Reference Desk area. This person does NOT maintain the Library Workstations, but does work with LIST in the presentation and organization of the special software & access placed on the workstations at Reference Desk itself.

Responsible for the inventory and maintenance of all computerized equipment and software. It is the responsibility of this person to know the correct administrative procedures for repair, replacement, and/or upgrading of equipment; licensing of software; and needed security measures.

Responsible for all updating of MARS equipment and services, including posting of all changes. All training and guide materials for the databases in MARS are given to the Reference Desk Support Group, who coordinates signs, user guides and manuals for the MARS room.


While this person is charged with keeping the office computers working, department staff are encouraged to do routine maintenance and updating on their own office machines. The computer coordinator is responsible for providing basic instructions for those updates the department decides are to be done by individuals.
