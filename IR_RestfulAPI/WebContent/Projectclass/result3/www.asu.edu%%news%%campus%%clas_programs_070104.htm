July 1, 2004

CLAS Gains Additional Schools and Programs

The College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (CLAS), already Arizona State University's largest college, is getting bigger on July 1 with the addition of four units that were formerly part of the College of Public Programs. The merger, which involves the transfer of two schools and two programs, will add 65 faculty and 2,227 majors to the college, bringing CLAS's totals to 910 faculty and 11,577 undergraduate majors.

"We are excited to have these four new units added to our college," said ASU Vice President and Dean David Young. "They add not only students and faculty, but further diversity to our academic offerings and research environment. This addition will help us continue to develop important new collaborative research programs and interdisciplinary academic opportunities and should help ASU continue to grow dynamically."

The largest of the merging units is the Hugh Downs School of Human Communication, one of ASU's most popular majors. The school has 32 tenured or tenure-track faculty members, 1,299 undergraduates, and 90 graduate students. The school will be directed by Dr. Harold Goodall, who assumes the reins of the unit on July 1.

The other school moving to CLAS is the School of Justice and Social Inquiry (formerly the School of Justice Studies). The school has 26 tenured or tenure-track faculty members, 948 undergraduates and 75 graduate students. The School of Justice and Social Inquiry will continue to be directed by Dr. Marie Provine.

Also joining the college will be the American Indian Studies Program,  with three faculty and 29 undergraduate majors, and the Asian Pacific American Studies Program, with four faculty. American Indian Studies will be directed by Dr. Eddie F. Brown, who will become Director on August 1, and Asian Pacific American Studies will continue to be directed by Dr. Thomas K. Nakayama.

All four of the new units are managed administratively in the college's new Division of Social Sciences.
