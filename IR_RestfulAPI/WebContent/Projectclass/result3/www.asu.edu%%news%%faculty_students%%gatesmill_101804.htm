October 18, 2004

Graduate Division, Barrett Honors College honors 50 top students

On Oct. 20, ASU will honor more than 50 students from diverse backgrounds who have been recognized for their academic achievement and promise as future leaders by the national Gates Millennium Scholars program.

The Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University, the Division of Graduate Studies (DGS), and the Barrett Honors College will co-host a reception to acknowledge these outstanding students for their accomplishments and encourage them to continue on a path toward graduate education.

Supporting the advancement of students who are underrepresented in their disciplines and fields to master’s and doctoral degrees is vital for Arizona, said Maria T. Allison, vice provost and dean of Graduate Studies.

“The number of minorities in this country will continue to grow in dramatic numbers in the decades ahead,” Allison says. “It is essential that ASU work proactively to ensure that the leadership of tomorrow will have the graduate education and credentials critical to their success and capacity to contribute to our communities and our country.”

“It is a pleasure and an honor for ASU to celebrate the Gates Millennium Scholars at our university,” adds Mark Jacobs, dean of the Barrett Honors College. “The entire purpose of establishing the program by the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation completely coincides with one of the major goals of both the Barrett Honors College and the Division of Graduate Studies at ASU. That goal is to promote an excellent undergraduate education for outstanding students with significant financial need from underrepresented ethnic and racial groups and to ensure that these students have the knowledge of, interest in, and preparation for continued study in graduate programs.”

Special guests at the invitation-only event will include ASU President Michael Crow and Executive Vice President and Provost Milton Glick. Also attending will be national representatives from organizations that are partners with the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation which supports the program. They are Molly Tovar, chief operating officer of the American Indian Graduate Center, and Cathy Makunga, a director with the Hispanic Scholarship Fund.

The reception will begin an annual tradition of honoring Gates Millennium Scholars at ASU, who are among the more than 7,000 students across the country the program has financially supported since it was launched in 1999 with a grant from the Gates Foundation.

Coronado, with the Division of Graduate Studies, can be reached at (480) 965-5995 or (nora.coronado@asu.edu).
