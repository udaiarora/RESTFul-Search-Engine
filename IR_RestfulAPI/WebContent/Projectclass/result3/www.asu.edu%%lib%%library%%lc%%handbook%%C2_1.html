Librarians' Council

C.2.1: Executive Committee

Effective 12/20/89 .. Revised 5/16/2001

POLICY AND PROCEDURES STATEMENT

The Executive Committee is one of the Standing Committees of the Librarians' Council. It consists of the Chair of the Librarians' Council, the Chair-Elect, the Secretary of the Council, the Chair of the Personnel Committee, the Chair of the Review Committee for Personnel Action, the Chair of the Professional Development Committee, the Chair of the Compensation Committee, the Academic Senator-at-Large from the University Libraries, the Committee on Academic Professional Status representative from the University Libraries (if any) the Kenneth B. Knepp Parliamentarian, and the Council Page Master. The Parliamentarian and the Page Master are non-voting members of the Executive Committee.

RESPONSIBILITIES

  1. To assist the Chair in planning the agenda for meetings of the Council.


    2.  

  2. To act for the Council when urgent matters arise between meetings of the Council. These acts will be subject to review and approval by the Council at its next meeting.


    3.  

  3. To report and make recommendations to the Council on matters of general concern or policy, either on its own initiative, or in response to questions or proposals referred to it.


    4.  

  4. To provide for the maintenance of a Librarians' Handbook to include the basic documents of the Council and current personnel policies and procedures for academic procedures for academic professionals in the Libraries.

PROCEDURES

The Chair will bring recommendations and report actions of the Executive Committee meetings at the next Council meeting. At this time any action taken by the Executive Committee on behalf of the Council will be presented for approval by the Council. All decisions require the presence of at least four voting members; a majority of those members present is necessary to take any action.

MEETINGS

A meeting will be held at least one week before the scheduled meeting of the Council. Special meetings may be called following the provisions for Special Meetings in the Bylaws of the Librarians' Council and Sturgis' Standard Code of Parliamentary Procedure.
