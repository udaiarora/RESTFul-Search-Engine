February 6, 2006

Garcia's post as vice president expands

Eugene Garcia will devote his full time to the position of vice president for education partnerships, the next step in the evolution of that position, which was created in the summer of 2003. A search committee will be convened to find a replacement for Garcia as dean of the ASU College of Education.

ASU has increased the number of teachers it graduates by 50 percent from roughly 1,000 per year in 2002 to almost 1,500 in 2005. One of Garcia's main tasks will be working with the Office of the Provost and Executive Vice President to further increase the number and the quality of teachers ASU produces.

His efforts will include coordinating the efforts of all of ASU's education colleges and schools, as well as those of other schools, such as the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences and the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering - to increase, for example, the number of science and engineering graduates who are getting teaching certification.

On matters of education policy, Garcia will work directly with ASU President Michael M. Crow.

"Helping enhance the state's public schools is one of the most important social responsibilities of this university, and producing more and better teachers is perhaps the single best way we can improve K-12 education in Arizona," Crow says. "This new effort is important because, while schools of education are the localized areas where teachers are trained, teacher education at ASU is a university responsibility."

To bring the full resources of the university to bear on education, Garcia will engage related research centers and outreach programs. Education Partnerships will maintain its focus on meeting community education needs.

An important part of these efforts has been the Alpha Partnership, in which ASU is working with pre-K-12 public and private sector partners to strategically engage efforts to improve student academic performance.

Also under development are ways to channel university knowledge and expertise toward increasing access to innovative, high-quality, culturally inclusive education for Arizona's children.

Garcia became dean of the ASU College of Education in July 2002, taking on additional duties as vice president a year later. He previously was dean of the Graduate School of Education at the University of California-Berkeley for six years.
