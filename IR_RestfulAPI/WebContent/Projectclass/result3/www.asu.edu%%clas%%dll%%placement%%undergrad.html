Home > Placement & Testing > Undergraduate Language Placement Test Information (French, German and Spanish)

If you have decided to fulfill your ASU language requirement by taking French, German or Spanish you may determine your level by taking the WEBCAPE Language Placement Tests in those languages. Please read the following placement policies for the Department of Languages and Literatures before taking the test.

If the language you would like to take is SPANISH, follow these general guidelines:

  • If you have studied Spanish for one year or less in high school, enroll in SPA 101 (Elementary Spanish I).  Enroll in SPA 107 (Spanish for International Professions) to complete the 8 credit equivalent of SPA 101 and SPA 102 in one semester.
  • If you have studied Spanish for two or more years in high school, the best way to determine the appropriate level is by taking the SPANISH PLACEMENT EXAM.

If you speak Spanish at home:

  • If you graduated high school in the US but are unsure of your level in Spanish, you should take the placement test.If you place at the 201 level, you should enroll in SPA 203 (Intermediate Spanish for Bilinguals I).If you place at the 202 level, you should enroll in SPA 204 (Intermediate Spanish for Bilinguals II).If you place at the 300 level, you should enroll in SPA 315 (Spanish Composition& Conversation for Bilinguals) if these alternative courses are available.
  • If you graduated from high school in a Spanish-speaking country, you should not enroll in any language course lower than SPA 412 (Advanced Spanish Composition & Conversation).You may, however, take any course in literature, culture or linguistics at the 300 or 400 level.

If the language you would like to take is either FRENCH or GERMAN:

  • If you have studied French or German for one year or less in high school, enroll in FRE 101 (Elementary French I) or GER 101 (Elementary German I).  Enroll in FRE 107 (French for International Professions) to complete the 8 credit equivalent of FRE 101 and FRE 102 in one semester.
  • For all other cases, please take the FRENCH PLACEMENT EXAM or the GERMAN PLACEMENT EXAM if you are unsure of your level.
