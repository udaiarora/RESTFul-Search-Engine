January 16, 2004

Finalists named for W. P. Carey School of Business deanship

The search committee for the next dean of the W. P. Carey School of Business at ASU has narrowed a pool of stellar candidates to three finalists whose names have been forwarded to Executive Vice President and Provost Milton Glick.

Each finalist will visit the university for two days in late January or early February for interviews and open forums. Glick will make the final selection.

The finalists are:

. James C. Bean, associate dean for Academic Affairs, College of Engineering at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. Bean has an M.S. and Ph.D. in operations research from Stanford University; and earned his B.S. with honors and distinction in mathematics from Harvey
Mudd College. He will be on campus Jan. 21 - 22.

. Robert E. Mittelstaedt, Jr., vice dean, The Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania; and director, Aresty Institute of Executive Education at Wharton. Mittelstaedt has an MBA from The Wharton School; and a B.S. in mechanical engineering from Tulane University. He will be on campus Jan. 26 - 27.

. Christopher S. Tang, dean and Cycle and Carriage Professor of Business Administration, NUS Business School at the National University of Singapore; Edward Carter Professor of Business Administration, UCLA Anderson Graduate School of Management. Tang has three degrees from Yale University, including an M. Phil., in administrative science; an M.A. in statistics; and a Ph.D. in management science. He has a B.Sc. with honors in mathematics from King's College, University of London, England. He will be on campus Feb. 5 - 6.

Patricia White, dean of the ASU College of Law, and chair of the search committee, said, "The committee is very gratified by the number of excellent people whom we have been privileged to consider. It's been a difficult task precisely because the level of interest by people of exceptional talent and accomplishment has been high. The job of narrowing the field has been a hard one, but I believe we have an extraordinary group of finalists."

Glick said he is extremely pleased with the caliber of the finalists and is grateful to the members of the search committee for their hard work and efficiency.

"The committee made quality a priority, but also kept up an accelerated pace during a very busy time of year so we were able to stay on track in getting this very important position filled in an optimal timeframe," Glick said.

He expects to make a final decision by the end of February.
