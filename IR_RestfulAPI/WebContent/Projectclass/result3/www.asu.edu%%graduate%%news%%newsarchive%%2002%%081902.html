August 19, 2002


TEACHING ASSISTANTS/ASSOCIATES AT ASU TO RECEIVE PARTIAL RESIDENT TUITION WAIVERS

Effective in the Fall 2002 semester, all graduate Teaching Assistants/Associates on ASU's Main, East, and West campuses with minimum .25 FTE and enrollment in a minimum of six credit hours (excluding audit enrollment) will receive a partial resident tuition waiver. Eligibility will be extended to graduate Research Assistants/Associates in Fall 2003. Milton Glick, executive vice president and university provost, and Bianca L. Bernstein, dean of the Graduate College, informed ASU academic units of the partial resident tuition waiver earlier this month.

Teaching Assistants/Associates employed at .50 FTE will receive a 25-percent resident tuition waiver. For those enrolled in six credit hours, the waiver amount is $196 per semester. Graduate students enrolled in seven or more credit hours will qualify for a waiver amount of $314 per semester. TAs employed at .25-.49 FTE will receive a 12.5-percent resident tuition waiver. For those enrolled in six credit hours, the waiver amount is $98 per semester. For graduate students enrolled in seven or more credit hours, the waiver amount is $157 per semester. Summer session tuition is not included in the partial waivers. Only those students identified by Human Resources as Teaching Assistants/Associates will receive the partial resident tuition waivers.

Students must pay the full tuition by the tuition payment deadline, as has previously been the case. Qualified graduate students will receive the partial resident tuition waiver—a tax-free benefit—as reimbursement through the student accounts system. Tuition adjustments for the semester will be made immediately after the final dates for unrestricted withdrawals—September 20, 2002 (Fall semester) and February 14, 2003 (Spring semester).

This partial waiver applies only to regular tuition charged by the university as a whole. It does not extend to any special fees charged by colleges. The partial resident tuition waivers are not considered cash awards. Students already awarded resident tuition waivers for 2002–03 will not receive any additional benefit from this program. If a third party sponsored the student, the third party will be reimbursed.

Academic units do not need to file any additional paperwork to process these awards. Teaching Assistants/Associates should be aware that the student accounting system automatically settles any existing bills (e.g., library fees) prior to reimbursements. Students may check the interactive student account system (at www.asu.edu/sos) at any time to view what has been credited and what has been paid out of their accounts.
