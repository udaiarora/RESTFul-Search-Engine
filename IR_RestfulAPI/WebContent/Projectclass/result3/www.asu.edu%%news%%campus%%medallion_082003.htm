August 20, 2003

Scholarship event recalls ASU's past

Old Main will be alive with the nostalgic sights and sounds of decades past as ASU supporters gather Sept. 20 to raise money for the Medallion of Merit ASU Alumni Association Scholarship fund.

Thomas Studdert, assistant director of Alumni Programs and Traditions and one of the organizers of this annual event, says he hopes many ASU faculty and staff will join with alumni to support this worthwhile cause.

The evening will include a silent auction, where more than 200 items will be sold to the highest bidder. Guests will have the opportunity to bid on everything from Gammage Auditorium event tickets to exclusive golf getaways.

The Outback Steakhouse will cater the event, with beer and wine service available. Attendees also will receive a martini glass keepsake.

"The Medallion of Merit Scholarship gives us a chance to recognize and encourage Arizona's best and brightest," Studdert says. "Attending this benefit is an excellent way to support our incoming students."

For more than 30 years, the association has acknowledged Arizona's most talented high school students by rewarding them with a $1,000 cash award and personally engraved medallion. Last year, more than 100 Arizona high school students benefited from the award.

Award recipients, who represent high schools throughout the state, are chosen for exhibiting strong leadership abilities, for displaying a strong commitment to community service and for their ongoing dedication to academic excellence.

Studdert says the theme for this year's benefit is "Come As You Were."

"We want people to attend dressed as they did when they were in college," Studdert says. "If you graduated in the '80s, you can come in punk rock attire or whatever. But, no matter what you wear, be prepared for a memorable evening."

Old Main will be decorated with a variety of photos illustrating different eras at ASU, as well as other mementos representing times gone by.

Throughout the evening, a 1940s swing band will perform in the Carson ballroom for those who want to try their hand at swing dancing. Impersonators dressed as Marilyn Monroe and Elvis will be on hand and available for photo opportunities with guests.
Tickets are $125 per person and are tax deductible. To R.S.V.P. or for information call (480) 965-2586.

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
