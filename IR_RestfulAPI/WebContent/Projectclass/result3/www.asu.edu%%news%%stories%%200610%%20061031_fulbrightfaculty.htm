October 31, 2006

Four ASU professors join Fulbright ranks

ASU hosts four faculty from other countries

Four ASU professors are teaching and doing research in other countries this year, having received Fulbright Scholar grants in the U.S. government's flagship academic exchange program.

Wei Li, an associate professor of geography and Asian Pacific American Studies, is at Queens University in Kingston, Canada, lecturing and studying the roles of financial institutions in immigrant integration. Li's research explores new patterns of settlement among Asian immigrants in the Americas, and the role of transnational financial networks in their development. In her writings, she coined the now widely used term, ?ethnoburb.?

Ileana Orlich, an associate professor of languages and literatures and coordinator of the Romanian program, is spending the year at the University of Bucharest in Bucharest, Romania. She is teaching and doing research on ?A Feminist Epistemology: Translating Papadat-Bengescu's Trilogy; Revisioning American Studies.? Orlich created ASU's Romanian Program and founded a summer program that has made ASU a highly visible presence in Eastern Europe.

Pori Park, a visiting assistant professor in religious studies, is carrying out research on Korean Buddhism at Seoul National University in Seoul, Korea. The UCLA graduate studies Buddhism and other Korean religions and the interaction between Buddhism and nationalism. Her research topic is ?Korean Buddhism Post-Liberation: Decolonization, Politics and Modernization.?

Arthur Mason, assistant professor in the School of Justice and Social Inquiry, was appointed to the Fulbright-Enders Research Chair in Canada-U.S. Relations at the University of Calgary, Canada. He is examining recent U.S.-Canadian policy in the context of Arctic oil and natural gas energy development. An anthropologist, Mason is jointly affiliated with the university’s Institute for Sustainable Energy, Environment and Economy, the Department of Anthropology and the Arctic Institute of North America.

They are among about 800 U.S. faculty and professionals who are traveling abroad to some 150 countries in the 2006-2007 school year to do academic work.

ASU also is playing host to four international scholars in a Fulbright exchange this academic year. Wael Abdel Hameed, a lecturer in architecture from South Valley University in Sohag, Egypt, is studying computer use in architectural design in the School of Architecture. Kuang-ti Li, an associate research fellow at the Institute of History and Philology, Academia Sinica, in Taipei, Taiwan, is studying human impact on ancient environments at the Global Institute for Sustainability.

Tuoc Vu, an associate professor at the Institute of Engineering Physics at Hanoi University of Technology in Hanoi, Vietnam, arrives in November to build an innovative interdisciplinary course in computational electronics at the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Thomas Wayman, an assistant professor of humanities at the University of Calgary in Calgary, Canada, will lecture and study creative writing next semester at the Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing.

The Fulbright Scholar program was established in 1946 by the U.S. Department of State to build mutual understanding between the American people and those of other countries.
