February 23, 2005

Survey confirms manufacturers remain upbeat for 2005

Greater Phoenix manufacturers remain very optimistic about the national and local economies, as well as their own businesses, according to the 2005 Greater Phoenix Manufacturers Survey, published in the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

Approximately three-quarters of those surveyed believe that the economy is substantially or moderately better than it was a year ago, according to Elliott Pollack, contributing editor. “This compares to about 77.6 percent in 2004,” Pollack noted. “Last year's survey reported the highest level of optimism since 1995. Approximately the same percentage believes that the Greater Phoenix economy has improved over the last 12 months.”

Looking ahead 12 months, almost two-thirds of survey respondents believe that the U.S. economy will be substantially or moderately better and that Greater Phoenix will be substantially or moderately better. “The only higher ratings in the survey’s 11-year history came last year,” Pollack said. For their own industries, again just under two-thirds believe that their situation will be moderately or substantially better.

“The outlook is excellent for employment as well. Almost half of those surveyed said employment has increased over the last year and only 12 percent said it decreased. Over the next 12 months, 44 percent expect employment to increase and only 6 percent expect it to decline.”

The Greater Phoenix Manufacturing Survey has been conducted annually since 1994, and is sponsored by Arizona State University 's Bank One Economic Outlook Center and Center for Business Research.

The Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast is published four times a year by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center , an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University . To order, call the center at (480) 965-7858. For economic information, contact Tracy Clark at (480) 965-5543.
