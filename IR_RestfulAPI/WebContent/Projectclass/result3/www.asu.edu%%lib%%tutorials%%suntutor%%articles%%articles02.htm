Types of articles

The most common types of articles are newspaper, popular magazine, scholarly journal, and trade magazine articles.

Newspaper articles:

  • are written for a general audience
  • written by journalists
  • are a good source of information on current events
  • can be a good source for opinions, editorials and commentaries
  • reflect the way an event was viewed at the time it happened
Examples: Arizona Republic, New York Times, Wall Street Journal

Popular magazines articles

  • are written for a general audience
  • written by staff writers/journalists
  • reviewed by magazine's editors
  • are generally shorter and contain no bibliographies
  • are often published on glossy paper, in a magazine with lots of advertising
Examples: Time, Psychology Today, Discover

Scholarly journal articles

  • are written for researchers and professionals in a particular field
  • reports the results of research
  • are often reviewed by experts in the field
  • are frequently long, and will contain bibliographies
  • are often published on plain paper, in a journal format with few or no advertising
Examples: American Journal of Sociology, Journal of Experimental Psychology, Journal of the American Chemical Society

Trade Magazine Articles

  • are written for members of a specific industry, business or organization
  • describe industry trends, new products or techniques and organizational news
  • written by staff writers
  • are reviewed by magazine's editors
Examples: Advertising Age, Publisher's Weekly, Supermarket News
