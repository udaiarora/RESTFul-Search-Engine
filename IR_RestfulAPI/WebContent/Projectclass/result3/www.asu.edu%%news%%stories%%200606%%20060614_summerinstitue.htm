June 14, 2006

Institute focuses on community

What happens when methamphetamine use, gangs, poverty, family violence or high crime rates begin to overshadow a community? Can a community prevent such disruptions to its common good?

Such questions will be addressed June 16-18 at the inaugural ASU Summer Institute on Community. The institute, which will take place at the Tempe campus, will have sessions on prevention, resilience, empowerment, social justice and community building.

The facilitator is Marek Wosinski, a senior lecturer at ASU and an associate professor at the Warsaw School of Social Psychology in Poland.

The institute, which was initiated by ASU Summer Sessions and directed by Carol Switzer, is being organized by ASU in collaboration with Valley of the Sun United Way. It will focus on ?community psychology,? a discipline that has emerged in the past 15-20 years and is the study of how a community can help members ?who are in oppression of any sort to have a better life,? Wosinski says.

The conference will explore issues such as:

? How to build strong communities.

? How to create preventive programs.

? How to develop resilience in the face of highly stressful circumstances.

? How people can become effective community leaders, using the most recent developments in community research and practice.

Along with the institute, there will be a Community Organizations Fair for nonprofit organizations to provide information about their mission, and to promote volunteer and job opportunities.

The conference fee is $135 for three days or $75 for one day.

For more information about the institute, go to the Web site (www.asu.edu/ssc/communitypsychology).
