Sun Devil Involvement Center

Located on the 3rd level of the Memorial Union, the Sun Devil Involvement Center (SDIC) provides opportunities for student involvement through clubs, sororities, fraternities, community service, student government, and co-curricular programs and activities. Become an active member of your campus community and stop by the SDIC today!

Also, check out the SDIC Best Practices, your one-stop web site for resources and information about event planning, leadership, fundraising and other tips to help your organization be more effective.

Associated Students of ASU (student government)
Ph: 480-965-3161 | Contact ASASU
Strives to provide students with opportunities that facilitate leadership and student self governance through providing programming, serving students, and creating connections. ASASU is the umbrella organization for the 3 student government branches:

  • Graduate and Professional Student Association (GPSA)
  • Programming and Activities Board (PAB)
  • Undergraduate Student Government (USG)

Memorial Union Programs & Activities
Ph: 480-965-9600 | Contact Memorial Union Programs & Activities
Promotes student learning and involvement by providing opportunities for faculty and student interaction outside the classroom.

Community Service Program
Ph: 480-965-0305 | Contact Community Service Program
Provides the ASU community avenues to serve the Valley, increases awareness of needs in the local community, and engages students in worthwhile service while promoting a lifelong commitment to citizenship and social justice.

Greek Life
Ph: 480-965-5292 | Contact Greek Life
Provides leadership opportunities through student empowerment and through the involvement of its fraternity and sorority undergraduate members in service to both the University and the community.

Student Coalitions
Provides cultural programming and academic support to multicultural students. The Student Coalitions are the umbrella groups that house the many student organizations designed to serve the African American, American Indian, Asian American, Hispanic/Latino, International, LGBTQ, and women student communities.

Student Organization Resource Center (SORC)
Ph: 480-965-2255 | Contact SORC
Supports over 450 registered student clubs and organizations by providing information sessions, newsletters and listserv announcements advising students on fundraising, event planning and policies.

Transition and Parent Programs
Ph: 480-965-4564 | transitionandparentprograms@asu.edu
Coordinate and direct transition activities and parents programs and services for new students and their families on behalf of the division of Student Affairs.
