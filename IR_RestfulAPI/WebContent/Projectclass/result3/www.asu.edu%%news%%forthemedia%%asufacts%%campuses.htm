Campuses

Arizona State University is setting a "New Gold Standard" for American universities - one that measures a university's academic quality by the education that its graduates have received, rather than by the academic credentials of its incoming freshman  class.

"Most  institutions judge their academic quality by who they exclude; we will  be judged by who we include," says ASU President Crow.

ASU is one of the premier metropolitan public research universities in the nation. Enrolling more than 58,000 undergraduate, graduate, and professional students on three campuses in metropolitan  Phoenix, ASU maintains a tradition of academic excellence in core disciplines, and has become an important global center for innovative interdisciplinary teaching and research. ASU offers outstanding resources for study and  research, including libraries and museums with important collections,  studios and performing arts spaces for creative endeavor, and unsurpassed  state-of-the-art scientific and technological laboratories and research facilities.

 In addition to the historic campus in Tempe, a college town in the midst of a dynamic metropolitan region, the university  comprises two  newer campuses with more specialized missions: ASU’s West campus, in northwest  Phoenix adjacent to Glendale, and ASU’s East campus, a polytechnic college, in Mesa. In downtown Phoenix, ASU's Extended Campus offers academic programs and professional certificate programs, with a full campus being planned for the near future.

The ASU College of Extended Education, headquartered at ASU's Downtown Center, advances the university's community outreach mission by extending education to adult learners interested in completing their degree or pursuing professional development. The College provides quality education to this population through convenient course offerings on evenings and weekends, multiple off-site locations, flexible programs and alternative delivery mediums such as the Internet. The College's university-wide collaboration efforts link students to credit classes, bachelor through doctorate degree programs, certificates, community enrichment programs and professional and workforce development.

At ASU's Polytechnic campus students gain knowledge through action. Academic programs focus on a polytechnic education, meaning an applied approach. Professional and technical programs are offered in the College of Technology and Applied Sciences, the Morrison School of Agribusiness and Resource Management, and East College. The campus, located in southeast Mesa, is a small residential college environment, with access to all the amenities and resources of a large university. Nearly 4,000 students are enrolled in one of the 29 baccalaureate and master's degree programs available, all of which prepare students in the theoretical and practical application of their chosen careers.

ASU's Tempe campus is located in the heart of Tempe where more than 49,000 students are enrolled in daytime and evening classes. The university is recognized nationally as a foremost research institution offering excellent undergraduate and graduate education and nationally-ranked athletic programs as well as a full array of cultural activities. The Tempe campus offers 87 bachelor's, 95 master's, 48 doctoral or terminal degree programs, and one law degree program.

ASU at the West campus

ASU's West campus is a comprehensive metropolitan campus that balances the traditions of a liberal arts education with responsiveness to the dynamics of workforce requirements. The colleges and schools are committed to teaching and research that is innovative, interdisciplinary, collaborative and solution-based. There are 29 bachelor's degree programs, 9 master's degrees and 8 professional certificates offered. Through partnerships with local industries, not-for-profits, governmental agencies, municipal and community groups, students learn by working on problems that directly relate to societal and community issues. The campus serves a diverse body of more than 7,300 students. The full-service campus features Las Casas, apartment-style student housing, as well as a library, child development center, student health center, bookstore, fitness center, credit union, computer center, food service facilities, theater and meeting rooms.
