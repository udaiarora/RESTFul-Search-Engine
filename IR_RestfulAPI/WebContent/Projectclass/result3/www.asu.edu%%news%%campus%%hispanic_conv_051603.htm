May 16, 2003

Convocation centers on Hispanic culture families, graduates

ASU will be hosting a festive celebration for graduates and their families, with plenty of Hispanic flavor. On May 17, more than 200 Hispanic students will participate in ASU's Hispanic Convocation at 4 p.m., in the Wells Fargo Arena.

"The ceremony is a lively and festive celebration," says Maria Coronado, program coordinator senior with the ASU Visitor Center. "Culturally, music is a big part of the pre-event celebration and Mariachi ASU, under the direction of Dr. Richard Haefer and Kitty Lopez, will provide the music."

ASU President Michael Crow will address the graduates as the featured speaker. In addition, national and international Chicano novelist Rudolfo Anaya, author of the novel "Bless Me, Ultima," will be the distinguished guest in support of his niece, Cecilia Aragon, a doctoral candidate. The event's master of ceremonies is ASU alumnus Jose Ronstadt, who is currently an anchorman for the Telemundo network in Los Angeles."

Coronado says the Hispanic Convocation is a unique experience for students and their families.

"Many of the graduates come from the inner-city schools and have gained success with programs such as Upward Bound and the Hispanic Mother Daughter Program," she says. "The Hispanic Convocation not only celebrates the achievement of the graduates, but also applauds the families who have supported them."

Patricia Arredondo, ASU associate professor of psychology in education, says the uniqueness of the Hispanic Convocation is that it is about Latino families and their educational achievement.

"There is a different energy at this event," says Arredondo, who is also president of the Chicano Faculty and Staff Association (CFSA). "Students and faculty alike wear mini sarapes over our regalia. This adds cultural tradition that you won't see at other graduations. (The Hispanic Convocation) also says that they have succeeded as a familia. Many graduates are the first from their family and this makes it even more special."

CFSA will be presenting the following awards at the convocation: the Roberto L. Pastor Student Award, the Christine Marin Staff Award, the Dr. Manuel Servin Faculty Award and the César E. Chávez Community Service Award.

In addition, many elected officials and distinguished guests in attendance will also have children participating in the ceremony, including Rebeca Rios, daughter of Sen. Pete Rios; Maritza Miranda Saenz, daughter of Rep. Ben Miranda; Benjamin Arredondo, son of Councilman Ben Arredondo; and Sergio Cardenas, son of Jose Cardenas, president of the Arizona-Mexico Commission.

The Hispanic Convocation will be in Spanish and English and will be broadcast on KAET-Channel 8 at 11 a.m., May 18.The convocation is sponsored in part by the ASU Office of the Vice President of Public Affairs, the Arizona Republic, ConocoPhillips, KPNX-Channel 12 and the ASU Hispanic Business Alumni and Los Diablos Latino Alumni Chapters.
