Forecasting with Geographically Weighted Regression:
A Case Study in Residential Water Consumption
for Phoenix, Arizona

ABSTRACT

Spatial analysis reveals geographic patterns and relationships between variables. My masters thesis research uses geographically weighted regression (GWR) to model local relationships. The basic question I address is: how does GWR compare with ordinary least square regression (OLS) in capturing spatial relationships in forecasting residential water consumption in Phoenix. The ultimate goal of this research is to develop new hypotheses to better understand how policies, economic circumstances, natural phenomena, and social dynamics might influence future water consumption. 

Geographic information systems (GIS) serve as a spatial gathering place for my data. GWR analysis is performed through a standalone software package that is compatible with ArcGIS. The primary benefit of GIS is through visualization, where candidate variables with no apparent spatial pattern are weeded out, model residuals are compared, and geographic patterns and relationships between variables are revealed, leading to hypothesis generation and assessment.
