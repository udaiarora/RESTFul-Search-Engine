January 16-27, 2007

700 Sundays starring Billy Crystal

ASU Gammage

Funnyman Billy Crystal will perform his Tony Award winning show, “700 SUNDAYS”, at ASU Gammage, January 16-27, 2007. “700 SUNDAYS”, an autobiographical journey, is an original two-act play in which Crystal plays numerous characters that have influenced who he is today. It deals with his youth, growing up in the jazz world of Manhattan, his teenage years, and finally adulthood. This delightful show is sure to elicit tears and laughter from playgoers. In its opening week on Broadway in 2005, “700 SUNDAYS” broke the house record for highest weekly gross at the Broadhurst Theatre and then continued to top its own record every week.  The show won the 2005 Tony Award for Special Theatrical Experience and both the 2005 Drama Desk and Outer Critics Circle Awards for Outstanding Solo Performance. “700 SUNDAYS” is performed and written by Crystal with additional content by Alan Zweibel, and is directed by two-time Tony Award winner Des McAnuff.

Tickets go on sale December 11 at ASU Gammage and Ticketmaster.  For more information, call (480) 784-4444 or visit http://asugammage.com.  “700 SUNDAYS” is a special offering of M&I Bank Broadway Across America – Arizona and is presented by ASU Gammage and Live Nation, Inc.

Thursday, February 8 and Friday, February 9, 2007

Distinguished visiting Canadian Fulbright chair in creative writing

7:30 p.m.
ASU University Club, Tempe campus

Tom Wayman will read from his works at this event sponsored by the Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing. Wayman was born in Hawkesbury, Ontario, and lived in Prince Rupert, BC and Vancouver. Wayman holds degrees in English from the University of British Columbia and an MFA from the University of California at Irvine. After completing his education, he worked as a laborer in various industries, and the workplace is a central thematic concern of his poetry. He has held academic positions, including writer-in-residence at the universities of Windsor, Alberta, Winnipeg, Toronto, and Simon Fraser University. His most recent book of poetry, his 13th, is titled "The Colours of the Forest."

For additional information, please contact the Piper Center at (480) 965-6018. Visit the Piper Center for the Spring 2007 schedule of the Distinguished Visiting Writers Series.

Thursday, February 15, 2007

Museums, Galleries and Collections Committee host open house

9 a.m.- 4 p.m.
Old Main, Tempe campus

The Museums, Galleries and Collections Committee host their fifth open house, ?We Love ASU Collections? which includes more than two dozen ASU museums, galleries and collections open for visitors with special tours of 12 of them, spaced 30 minutes apart so guests can pick and choose from the menu of delights. Application deadline for ASU for Arizona Internship Program

An exciting and unique opportunity for student to lead community-university collaborative projects is available through the ASU for Arizona Internship Program. Through an intensive summer training and year-long program, the ASU for Arizona Internship Program develops students into engaged civic leaders committed to making a positive impact in communities around the state of Arizona. All internships are paid and require a one year commitment.
