Study: Economic factors show Arizona has room to improve

On measures of aggregate economic growth, such as total personal income and employment, Arizona is among the national leaders. But on other economic measures – such as average wage per employee, personal income per person and job quality – Arizona’s performance is average at best.

This is the conclusion of a pair of related studies recently released by the L. William Seidman Research Institute of ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business. “Jobs, Income, and Growth in Arizona: Individual Versus Aggregate Measures of Economic Performance” takes a broad look at economic conditions in Arizona, while “Job Quality in Arizona” is a detailed analysis of the state’s industrial mix and occupational mix and the change in job quality over time.

The reports are available on the Seidman Institute’s Web site (wpcarey.asu.edu/seid).

“Arizona’s overall economy grows very rapidly, but per person or per worker measures of wages, compensation, and income are below the national average,” says Dennis Hoffman, the institute’s director and professor of economics. “No significant improvement has been made in raising Arizona’s average wage or per capita income relative to the national average. Indeed, Arizona is creating income, wealth, and quality jobs at rates that are similar to those displayed by other states. Thus, disparities between Arizona and the nation will remain.”

Hoffman suggests that economic well-being in Arizona could be improved relative to other states and the nation.

“Improvements in labor productivity – induced by strategic investments in the transportation, communication, and education infrastructure and enhancements of human capital (educational attainment and skill development) in the labor force – would raise Arizona’s wages and incomes relative to the nation,” he says.

Farquhar, with the W. P. Carey School of Business, can be reached at (480) 965-7774 or (farquhar@asu.edu).
