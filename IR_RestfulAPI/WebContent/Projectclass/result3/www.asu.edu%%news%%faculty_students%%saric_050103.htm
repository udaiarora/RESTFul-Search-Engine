May 1, 2003

Aeronautics, astronautics institute honors William Saric

The American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA) has named William Saric, ASU mechanical and aerospace engineering professor, as the 2003 recipient of the AIAA Fluid Dynamics Award.

The award will be presented to Saric on June 24, at the AIAA Fluid Dynamics 33rd conference in Orlando, Fla., for his outstanding contributions to the understanding of the behavior of liquid and gas in motion as related to the needs of aeronautics and astronautics.

The majority of Saric's pioneering research on flow instabilities is a combination of analysis and experiment emphasizing the three-dimensional effects of the relevant phenomena. His work encompasses the speed range from low speed to hypersonic, and includes the important topics of the instability and control of Goertler vortices, and more recently cross flow vortices.

"William Saric is an internationally renowned scholar and educator in the fields of fluid mechanics and aerodynamics," says Robert Peck, chairman of ASU's mechanical and aerospace engineering department.

"Besides his research accomplishments, Dr. Saric has made a lasting contribution to the aerospace engineering program at ASU. He has personally developed and maintained a modern array of wind tunnel facilities for teaching and research on low-speed to supersonic aerodynamics. These facilities attract a multitude of researchers and are used extensively for laboratory instruction, student projects and community outreach," says Peck.

"He regularly engages students of all levels, from undergraduate to post-doctoral, in the conduct of his research. A generation of students at ASU has benefited not only from Dr. Saric's technical expertise but also from his mentorship based on teamwork, dedication and professionalism," he said.

AIAA is the largest professional technical society, principal voice and information resource devoted to the progress of engineering and science in aviation and space. Past recipients of this prestigious award include such notables as William Reynolds, Anatol Roshko, Hans Liepmann and Seymour Bognonoff, among others.

Presently, Saric is leading a national study group on boundary-layer transition and directing flight tests on new flow control technology at the NASA Dryden Flight Research Center in Edwards, Calif.
