Accounting

INTRODUCTION

This Subject Guide lists publications that are available in the University Libraries for researching accounting information. It is not a comprehensive bibliography, but rather a selective list of sources which are most useful for locating information in this field.

Conducting research in the University Libraries will involve using resources held in many locations. The following codes are used in this guide to designate these locations.
  • HAYDEN REF: Hayden Library, Reference, Concourse Level
  • HAYDEN RESV: Hayden Library, Reserve, Concourse Level
  • HAYDEN SERIAL: Hayden Library, Serials, Lower Level
  • HAYDEN STACKS: Hayden Library, Book Stacks, Levels 1-3

To facilitate identification, the accounting sources have been listed under the following categories:
  • Guides and Bibliographies
  • Dictionaries and Encyclopedias
  • Handbooks
  • Reporting Trends and Formats
  • Charts of Accounts
  • Professional Standards and Regulations
  • International Accounting
  • Indexes and Abstracts
  • CPA Examination
  • Directories
  • Auditing

GUIDES AND BIBLIOGRAPHIES

The standard guides to business information sources such as Encyclopedia of Business Information Sources, Handbook of Business Information, and Business Information Sources kept at the Hayden Reference Desk will provide helpful descriptions of books, periodicals, indexes, databases, associations, etc. relating to accounting. In addition, the following guides and bibliographies specific to accounting provide more detailed listings of sources in their particular area.
  1. Spiceland, J. David, and Surendra P. Agrawal. International Guide to Accounting Journals.2nd ed. New York: Markus Wiener Publishing, 1993.
    HF 5657 .S65x 1993 HAYDEN REF

    This is a bibliography of journals that accept unsolicited articles on accounting topics for publication. Entries are listed alphabetically by journal title and include the journal's audience, percentage of articles devoted to accounting broken down by type of accounting, and the submission requirements. A cross-index to the titles by country of origin is also included.

  2. Weirich, Thomas R., and Alan Reinstein. Accounting & Auditing Research: A Practical Guide. 3rd ed. Cincinnati: South-Western Publishing, 1992.
    HF 5630 .W39 1992 HAYDEN REF

    Explains the research process involved in solving accounting problems. Includes explanations of the standard-setting process for accounting and auditing, describes the sources of authoritative literature, and other sources of information both in published and computerized forms.

DICTIONARIES AND ENCYCLOPEDIAS

  1. Cooper, W.W., and Yuji Ijiri, eds. Kohler's Dictionary for Accountants. 6th ed. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, 1983.
    HF 5621 .K6 1983 HAYDEN REF

    A good, complete dictionary, listing over 4,500 terms and phrases both for accounting and related fields. The length of definitions varies from one sentence to several paragraphs, occasionally reaching one or two pages. It contains definitions of technical terms and accounting jargon not found in other business dictionaries and includes practical examples, tables, and formulas.

  2. Estes, Ralph. Dictionary of Accounting. 2nd ed. Cambridge, MA: MIT Press, 1985.
    HF 5621 .E77 1985 HAYDEN REF

    Consists of short non-technical definitions of accounting terms. Appendices give illustrations of common financial statement formats.

  3. Parker, R. H. Macmillan Dictionary of Accounting. 2nd ed. London: Macmillan, 1992.
    HF 5621 .P37x 1992 HAYDEN REF

    Covers both British and American terminology. Gives not only the meanings of the words but also sets out practices and explains and discusses theories. In addition, it gives short biographical sketches of individuals making outstanding contributions to accounting literature. Includes list of abbreviations and acronyms for both UK and USA.

  4. Shim, Jae K., and Joel G. Siegel. Encyclopedic Dictionary of Accounting and Finance. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, 1989.
    HF 1001 .S525 1989 HAYDEN REF

    Contains over 500 entries on accounting and finance topics arranged alphabetically. Many entries are quite lengthy and provide examples, tables, or checklists. Includes a subject index for access to subtopics discussed and terms defined within the broad entry headings.

  5. Siegel, Joel G., and Jae K. Shim. Dictionary of Accounting Terms. New York: Barron's, 1987.
    HF 5621 .S54 1987 HAYDEN REF

    Covers more than 2,500 accounting terms illustrated with charts, graphs, and tables. Appendices include a list of commonly used abbreviations and acronyms.

HANDBOOKS

  1. Friedlob, George T., and Franklin J. Plewa, Jr. Financial and Business Statements. New York: Barron's, 1991.
    HF 5681 .B2 F7725 1991 HAYDEN REF

    Describes and explains various accounting reports including annual reports, budgets, and business plans.

  2. Lipkin, Lawrence. Accountant's Handbook of Formulas and Tables. 3rd ed. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, 1988.
    HF 5699 .L56 1988 HAYDEN REF

    Provides formulas for simple and compound interest, annuities, statistical sampling, index numbers, and time series as well as specialized formulas covering inventory, depreciation, finance, pricing, marketing, cost and production, ratio analysis, and special situations. There are also 14 tables charting the values for a number of items including present value, logarithms, square roots and reciprocals, sample size, and random decimal digits.

  3. Plank, Tom M. Accounting Desk Book: The Accountant's Everyday Instant Answer Book. 9th ed. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, 1989.
    HF 5635 .B668 1989 HAYDEN REF

    Offers an overview of accounting in twelve sections covering such topics as financial statement analysis and interpretation, Securities and Exchange Commission organization and filing requirements, and materiality and financial statement disclosure. There are also seven appendices which include sample journal entries, as well as a comparison of accounting methods' advantages and disadvantages.

  4. Siegel, Joel G. Barron's Accounting Handbook. New York: Barron's, 1990.
    HF 5635 .S586 1990 HAYDEN REF

    A desk reference covering the major areas of accounting including financial accounting, management/cost accounting, tax preparation and planning, auditing, personal financial planning, and governmental and nonprofit accounting. In addition, there are chapters on financial statement analysis, microcomputer applications, and quantitative methods as well as a dictionary of terms.

  5. Tracy, John A. How to Read a Financial Report: Wringing Cash Flow and Other Vital Signs Out of the Numbers. 3rd ed. New York: Wiley, 1989.
    HF 5681 .B2 T733 1989 HAYDEN REF

    Provides the layman with an understanding of cash flow and demonstrates how to read financial statements using simple explanations illustrated with numerous examples.

REPORTING TRENDS AND FORMATS

  1. Accounting Trends and Techniques. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1962- . Annual. (ASU own 1951, 1954, 1956, 1958, 1960, 1963- .)
    HF 5681 .B2 A35 HAYDEN SERIAL

    An analysis of the financial reports submitted voluntarily each year by over 600 corporations revealing significant trends in accounting procedures and variations in accounting practice.

  2. Vickman, Thomas M. Handbook of Model Accounting Reports and Formats. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, 1987.
    HF 5635 .V53 1987 HAYDEN REF

    Gives examples of 290 accounting reports and graphics. Illustrations include a wide range of reports from financial, sales, and manufacturing to R&D, human resources, and service company reporting.

CHARTS OF ACCOUNTS

With the advent of microcomputer software for accounting applications, the interest in publishing charts of accounts for specific businesses as guides to the businessman has significantly decreased. The following two sources for this information are dated but, as they are not likely to be revised, they remain the only comparable resource.
  1. National Society of Public Accountants. Portfolio of Accounting Systems for Small and Medium-Sized Businesses. Rev. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, 1977.
    HF 5635 .P86 1977 HAYDEN REF

    Covers the procedures and practices in use as of 1977 in 68 small or medium sized businesses including architects, automobile dealers, dentists, florists, hardware dealers, nursing homes, shoe stores, and travel agencies. Forms and diagrams are frequently provided.

  2. Pescow, Jerome K., ed. Encyclopedia of Accounting Systems. 3 vols. Rev. ed. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, 1976.
    HF 5635 .E54 1976 HAYDEN REF

    Three-volume set describing and illustrating accounting systems for 72 types of industries, professions, and non-profit organizations. Each chapter covers a particular type and is arranged in the same outline manner giving a brief description of the category, the accounting systems used, account classification, data processing methods, cost reporting systems, time and payroll, depreciation, and time saving techniques for various sizes of firms.

PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS AND REGULATIONS

  1. Accounting and Auditing Disclosure Manual. Boston: Warren, Gorham & Lamont, 1983- . Annual. (ASU owns 1985- .)
    HF 5658 .A23 HAYDEN SERIAL

    Designed as a complete reference source for all disclosures that Generally Accepted Accounting Principles and Auditing Standards require or recommend for commercial and industrial companies. Close to 1,000 disclosures are included.

  2. Accounting Research and Terminology Bulletins. Final ed. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1961.
    HF 5601 .A84 HAYDEN STACKS

    A final compilation of the pronouncements of the Committee on Accounting Procedure and the Committee on Accounting Terminology, both no longer in existence. Accounting Research Bulletins are explanations of accounting problems and procedures. As such, they are forerunners of the APB Opinions. Accounting Terminology Bulletins are clarifications of terminology and represent only four pronouncements. APB opinion no. 6, "Status of Accounting Research Bulletins," explains which of these publications are still in force and recommends some revisions. Portions of this opinion have since been superseded by APB-11, 16, 17, 26, and FASB-8.

  3. APB Opinions. Accounting Principles Board, 1962-1973. These are the opinions of the Accounting Principles Board which preceded the Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB). Many of them are still in effect today; however, the opinions themselves are now out of print. They can still be found, though, in the Journal of Accountancy, (HF 5601 .J7) where they were printed in their entirety at the time they were originally issued.

  4. EITF Abstracts: A Summary of Proceedings of FASB Emerging Issue Task Force. Stamford, CT: Financial Accounting Standards Board, 1984- . Annual. (ASU owns 1991- .)
    HF 5656 .U5 F36a HAYDEN REF

    These are summaries of accounting issues identified and discussed at the meetings of the Emerging Issues Task Force. An abstract provides a statement of the issue, summary of the discussion, and a report of the consensus reached. A status section gives details on subsequent developments on that issue such as the issuance of a pronouncement. A topical index provides subject access.

  5. FASB Accounting Standards, Current Text. 2 vols. Stamford, CT: Financial Accounting Standards Board, 1982- . Irregular. (ASU owns current.)
    HF 5616 .U5 A348x HAYDEN REF

    A synthesis of FASB and AICPA pronouncements on financial accounting currently in effect. The presentation is alphabetical by broad subject category with sections for summaries of the topics covered, glossaries, and interpretive materials.

  6. FASB Discussion Memorandum. Stamford, CT: Financial Accounting Standards Board. 1974- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1974-1979, 1981, 1983, 1990- .)
    HF 5601 .F52x HAYDEN REF

    Issued by the Board for major projects, these are documents which define a problem and present alternative solutions and their implications in a neutral manner. Discussion memoranda serve as the basis for a public hearing and are part of the standard setting process which may lead to an exposure draft and ultimately to a statement of financial accounting standards.

  7. Financial Accounting Standards. Chicago: Commerce Clearing House, 1973- . Irregular.
    HF 5616 .U5 F5x HAYDEN REF

    A compilation of the complete text of financial accounting pronouncements made by the Financial Accounting Standards Board, whether in force or not. These are also published in the Journal of Accountancy (HF 5601 .J7) shortly after they are issued. The pronouncements included in this service are:
    1. Exposure Draft. 1974- . (ASU owns 1974- .)

      Consists of proposed statements of financial accounting standards issued by the Board for comment by the accounting community.

    2. FASB Interpretation. 1974- . (ASU owns 1976- .)

      Irregularly issued interpretations of previously published accounting pronouncements. These are designed to clarify, explain, and cover new developments by adding to the text of earlier publications. Most interpretations refer to APB Opinions or FASB Statements.

    3. FASB Technical Bulletin. 1979- . (ASU owns 1979- .)

      Issued by the Board for timely guidance on specific accounting questions.

    4. Statement of Financial Accounting Standards. 1973- . (ASU owns 1973- .)

      Irregularly issued opinions on accounting problems of current importance and interest. These detailed statements establish principles and procedures for financial accounting and set standards for handling new accounting problems. In this work, they carry on the function previously maintained through the Accounting Principles Board in its series of APB Opinions.

  8. Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP). New York: Wiley, 1985- . Annual. ASU owns 1985-; latest in Reference)
    HF 5616 .U5 G33 HAYDEN REF & HAYDEN SERIAL

    This is an analytical explanation and illustration of generally accepted accounting principles arranged by topic. A "List of authoritative pronouncements" at the front of the volume gives citation references to both the Current Text and to pages in the GAAP guide itself. At the back is a simplified disclosure checklist and a subject index.

  9. Index to Accounting and Auditing Technical Pronouncements. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1977- . Annual. (ASU owns latest.)
    HF 5635 .I44x HAYDEN REF

    A subject index to pronouncements of the AICPA, Cost Accounting Standards Board, FASB, Government Accounting Standards Board, International Accounting Standards Board, International Federation of Accountants, National Council on Governmental Accounting, and the Securities and Exchange Commission currently still in effect. Cites appropriate pronouncements to use for guidance in specific accounting and auditing situations.

  10. Invitation to Comment. Stamford, CT: Financial Accounting Standards Board, 1981- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1982, 1989.)
    HF 5616 .U5 I58x HAYDEN REF

    Though similar to a Discussion Memorandum, the Invitation to Comment, is not the product of a task force commissioned by the Board and does not include alternative accounting methods and their implications for the issues under consideration. Comments are accepted on an issue until the expiration of the comments period for that document.

  11. Miller, Martin A. HBJ Miller Comprehensive GAAP Guide. New York: Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, 1979- . Annual. (ASU owns 1984- ; latest in Reference.)
    HF 5616 .U5 M5 HAYDEN REF & HAYDEN SERIAL

    A comprehensive restatement of all currently promulgated and generally accepted accounting principles. Each pronouncement's essential provisions are discussed in simple language and numerous illustrations show applications and provide guidance where official pronouncements are ambiguous or conflicting. A cross reference index at the front of the book lists all sections covering any particular pronouncement. Indexes at the back of the book include a disclosure index which indicates required and recommended disclosures in all accounting areas and a topical index which provides detailed subject access to the text.

  12. Statement of Financial Accounting Concepts. Stamford, CT: Financial Accounting Standards Board, 1978- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1978- .)
    HF 5601 .S7x HAYDEN SERIAL

    Consists of the full text of these concepts issued by FASB. Their purpose is to set objectives and qualitative characteristics that guide the Board in developing accounting principles.

  13. Statement of Position. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1974- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1983- ; latest in Reference.)
    HF 5616 .U5 A586x HAYDEN REF & HAYDEN SERIAL

    These are opinions issued by the Accounting Standards Executive Committee of the AICPA known as SOPs. They are not binding but are issued as a recommendation which may be taken up later by FASB and incorporated in one of their pronouncements.

INTERNATIONAL ACCOUNTING

  1. Alexander, David, and Simon Archer, eds. European Accounting Guide. London: Academic Press, 1992.
    HF 5616 .E85 E86x 1992 HAYDEN REF

    Provides an overview of accounting practices in European countries including the eastern countries. Each country's chapter has a section giving background information on the history of accounting and standard-setting practices there, as well as sections on financial statement requirements, valuation and income measurement, and future developments. At the end of each country's entry is an actual financial statement to serve as a example. The appendix includes an abridged version of the International Accounting Standards.

  2. International Accounting Summaries: A Guide for Interpretation and Comparison. New York: Wiley, 1991.
    HF 5601 .I78 1991 HAYDEN REF

    Summarizes accounting standards in 24 countries. In addition to the chapter on each country, there are chapters summarizing the International Accounting Standard and the European Community Directives. For easy comparison, matrices by accounting topic are included at the back and there is a glossary.

  3. Spicer and Oppenheim Guide to Financial Statements Around the World. New York: Wiley, 1989.
    HF 5681 .B2 S6332 1989 HAYDEN REF

    Describes accounting and reporting practices in 38 countries. In addition, there are sections giving an overview of the European Community Directives and of the international standards and guidelines for accounting and auditing.

INDEXES AND ABSTRACTS

Many of the standard business indexes such as ABI/Inform,, Business Index, Business Periodicals Index, Predicasts F & S Index, and Wall Street Journal Index, as well as other indexes in the social sciences (Psychological Abstracts, Public Affairs Information Service International in Print, Social Sciences Index,, and Social Sciences Citation Index) will cover articles useful for accounting research. These should be used in conjunction with the following indexes which are dedicated to covering accounting literature as their sole or prime function.
  1. Accountants Digest. Boca Raton, FL: Florida Atlantic University College of Business and Public Administration, 1935-1988. Quarterly. (ASU owns 1935-1965, 1967-1988.)
    HG 5601 .A17 HAYDEN SERIAL

    Before it ceased in 1988, this provided a digest of major English-language articles from internationally known accounting journals. It was arranged by broad subject, such as: auditing, budgeting, and management and included book reviews. Title, author, periodical, and book review indexes for each volume appeared in issue no. 4 (June). In addition, the Digest included a section, "CPA Requirements," that listed minimum requirements for taking the Uniform CPA Examination for each state, and another section, "Current Status of Official Pronouncements," that listed outstanding statements, standards, and guidelines issued by U.S. and foreign accounting organizations.

  2. Accounting and Tax Index. Ann Arbor, MI: UMI, 1921- . Quarterly. (ASU owns 1921/1923- .)
    HF 5635 .A43x HAYDEN REF (located on Table 10B)

    Formerly Accountants' Index Supplement, this indexes English-language periodicals, books, pamphlets, and government documents in all areas of accounting and taxation including auditing, data processing, and investments. Authors, titles, and subjects are listed together. This is also available as an online database that can be searched for a fee through Dialog. To order an online search, inquire at the Reference Desk.

  3. Accounting Articles: Describing Accounting Articles Published in Accounting and Business Periodicals, Books, Pamphlets. Chicago: Commerce Clearing House, 1963- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1963- .)
    HF 5635 .A25x HAYDEN REF

    Abstracts articles, books, reports, proceedings, and papers on accounting and management services in a classified arrangement. Includes indexes by subject and author.

  4. Management & Accountancy Research Working Papers Bibliography. Dobbs Ferry, NY: Trans-Media, 1983- . Semiannual. (ASU owns 1983- .)
    HD 28 .M315x HAYDEN REF

    Covers unpublished research working papers in management and accounting. These papers may not be published for two or three years or in other cases they may never be conventionally published. The papers come from U.S. and international institutions and are indexed by author, subject, and series.

CPA EXAMINATION

  1. Accountancy Law Reporter: Public Accountancy Practice, Statutes, Regulations, Forms, Codes of Ethics, Decisions, and Rulings. 2 vols. Chicago: Commerce Clearing House, 1938- . Monthly. (ASU owns current looseleaf.)
    KF 2920 .A6 C6x HAYDEN REF

    Looseleaf service covering the regulation of the practice of accounting in the 50 states, District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Virgin Islands. Includes information on admission to practice (CPA examination), restrictions, and requirements for continuing education.

  2. Gleim, Irvin N, and Patrick R. Delaney. CPA Examination Review. 2 vols. New York: Wiley, 1975- . Annual. (ASU owns 1977/1978, 1981/1982, 1985/1986- ; latest in Reserve.)

    An annual guide to the CPA examination, this title has become a standard tool for CPA test preparation. Vol. 1, called "Outlines adn Study Guides," presents chapters summarizing each area of accounting knowledge. Vol. 2, called "Problems and Solutions," gives question and unofficial answers taken from the previous years' examinations.

  3. Uniform CPA Examination: Questions. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1921- . Biennial. (ASU owns 1917/1921- with some interruptions; latest 10 years in Reserve.)
    HF 5661 .A53 HAYDEN RESV & HAYDEN SERIAL

  4. Uniform CPA Examinations: Unofficial Answers. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1921- . Biennial. (ASU owns 1939/1941- with some interruptions; latest 10 years in Reserve.)
    HF 5661 .A531x HAYDEN RESV & HAYDEN SERIAL

    Prepared by the Board of Examiners of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, these companion volumes provide the questions and answers to the previous two years' tests.

  5. Uniform CPA Examination. Questions and Unofficial Answers. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1961- . Semiannual. (ASU owns 1971- ; latest 10 years in Reserve.)
    HF 5661 .A54x HAYDEN RESV & HAYDEN SERIAL

    This is a supplement to the Journal of Accountancy which gives all the questions included in a specific exam, accompanied by unofficial answers and study references.

DIRECTORIES

  1. Careers in Public Accounting: A Comprehensive Comparison of the Top Tier Firms. Expanded 3rd ed. Bellevue, WA: Professional Services Review, 1990.
    HF 5616 .U5 E46x 1990 HAYDEN REF

    Provides background summaries on the Big Six accounting firms. Entries include firm characteristics, key executives, U.S. headquarters, address, international and domestic revenues, partnership agreements and compensation, and major clients. In addition, a chronology of events for the previous three years traces the consolidation of The Big Eight into the Big Six. Also includes a special section on background information and statistical data for second-tier accounting firms.

  2. Emerson's Directory of Leading U.S. Accounting Firms. Bellevue, WA: Emerson's Professional Services Review, 1988- . Biennial. (ASU has 1992- ; latest in Reference.)
    HF 5616 .U5 E47 HAYDEN REF & HAYDEN SERIAL

    Directory listing arranged by state of approximately 6,500 CPA firms out of the 46,000 firms in the U.S. Entries provide information on names of key personnel, staff size, and functional and/or industry specialization. The top eleven national firms are listed separately and provide the same basic directory information as other entries as well as the companies' approximate revenues and the number of offices for the U.S. and worldwide. An alphabetic index to firm names is also provided.

  3. Who Audits America. Menlo Park, CA: Data Financial Press, 1976- . Semiannual. (ASU owns current issue.)
    HF 5616 .U5 W5 HAYDEN REF

    Directory of accounting firms and the publicly-traded companies they audit. Section I provides information on the publicly-traded companies and Section II has information on the accounting firms and lists the publicly-traded companies that each audits.

AUDITING

  1. AICPA Professional Standards. 2 vols. Chicago, Commerce Clearing House, 1974- . Annual. (ASU owns 1990.)
    HF 5667 .A55 1982 HAYDEN REF

    Provides a topical account of professional auditing standards currently in effect. Vol. I contains the sections on "U.S. Auditing Standards" and "Attestation Engagements" with a separate topical index for each. Vol. II includes AICPA's bylaws and code of professional ethics, statements on standards for management advisory services and tax practice, and statements on quality control standards.

  2. Codification of Statements on Auditing Standards. Chicago, Commerce Clearing House, 1976- . Annual. (ASU owns 1987- ; latest in Reference.)
    HF 5667 .A2 A52x HAYDEN REF

    Annually issued synthesis of auditing standards and attestation engagements currently in effect. The text is basically the same as volume one of the AICPA Professional Standards.

  3. Industry Audit Guides Series. American Institute of Certified Public Accountants. [1969]- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1969-; latest for each industry in Reference.) Call Numbers Vary
    HAYDEN REF (located on Table 10B) & HAYDEN STACKS

    Audit Guides are published for specific industries and are updated by Audit Risk Alerts HF 5667 .A784x HAYDEN REF also located on the same table. They represent the judgment of an AICPA committee or task force as to the best auditing practices for that industry though they do not carry the authority of a pronouncement.

  4. Management Advisory Services: Guideline Series. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1968- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1968- .)
    HF 5657 .M23 HAYDEN SERIAL

    This is a series meant to serve as a guideline to administering management advisory services practices. The guidelines are endorsed by the Management Advisory Services Executive Committee of the AICPA but are not an official position of the institute.

  5. Miller, Martin A., and Larry P. Bailey. HBJ Miller Comprehensive GAAS Guide. New York: Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, 1982- . Annual. (ASU owns 1991- ; latest in Reference.)
    HF 5667 .M514 HAYDEN REF & HAYDEN SERIAL

    Brings together in one subject-classified narrative all the formally promulgated and many of the informal auditing standards, practices, and procedures currently in use. These include Generally Accepted Auditing Standards (GAAS), Statements on Auditing Standards (SAS) and Interpretations, Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services (SSARS) and Interpretations, SEC Auditing Releases, Specialized Industry Audit Guides, Statements on Quality Control Standards, and the Code of Professional Ethics. There is a topical index for subject access and a cross reference index citing all sections in the Guide pertaining to any specified pronouncement. In addition, the appendices include an internal control questionnaire and a model audit program.

  6. Statement on Auditing Standards. New York, American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1973. Irregular. (ASU owns 1975- .)
    HF 5667 .A2 A5x HAYDEN REF

    These are the individual standards in the form in which they were originally issued by the AICPA. When copies are missing, an alternative source is the Journal of Accountancy (HF 5601 .J7) which has a policy of reprinting newly issued standards.

  7. Statement on Standards for Attestation Engagements. New York: American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, 1986- . Irregular. (ASU owns 1986- .)
    HF 5601 .A45x HAYDEN SERIAL

    These statements establish broad guidelines for attest services which go beyond the traditional ones provided by auditors in certifying their acceptance of historical financial statements.

  8. Willson, James D., and Steven J. Root. Internal Auditing Manual. 2nd ed. Boston: Warren, Gorham & Lamont, 1989.
    HF 5668.25 .I59x 1989 HAYDEN REF

    Updated annually by cumulative supplements. Provides current picture of auditing techniques and standards as well as guidelines for ethical conduct. Arranged topically with an index at the back. However, it is preferable to use the index in the current supplement since it covers the entries in the main volume as well as any updates which appear in the supplement.
