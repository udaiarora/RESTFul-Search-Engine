The Screening Process
  1. Review application materials using matrix developed in #8 and document screening process; identify top candidates.
    (See Handbook p. 9 and 20)
 Search Committee
  1. Approve initial finalist list or direct Search Committee to redouble efforts to create a diverse finalist list.
 Hiring Authority
  1. If finalist list is rejected, return to step 14 or 23.
 Search Committee
  1. Conduct phone/airport interviews and document responses (to help narrow the finalist list, if appropriate); develop interview list.
    (See Handbook p. 10)
 Search Committee
  1. Certify Interview list of direct Search Committee to extend search.
    (ACD 505-04)
 Hiring Authority & EO/AA
  1. Notify finalists of plans to conduct reference checks.
 Search Chair
  1. Conduct reference checks. (See Handbook p. 9)
 Search Committee
  1. Notify applicants who are no longer under consideration.
 Search Chair

The Interview Process (See Handbook p. 10-13)
  1. Invite finalists to visit campus.
 Search Chair
  1. Arrange travel and housing. Consider special needs, food preferences/restrictions, candidate’s wishes to meet with specific groups or individuals. Revise campus visit itinerary (item 21) as needed, based on candidate meeting requests.
 Search Chair & Search Committee
  1. Invite community, potential colleagues to meet finalists; announce campus interview to the University community.
 Search Chair
  1. Appoint individual(s) to greet candidate at airport, accompany candidate during the campus interview, and return him/her to airport after interview.
 Search Chair
  1. Forward campus interview itinerary to candidates. Include information on the Phoenix community, the value of ASU employment, the hiring college and department, dual career options and resources. Contact candidate in advance of visit to answer questions, address any concerns, express enthusiasm about interview.
 Search Chair or Designee
  1. Conduct campus interviews.
Search Committee

The Selection Process
  1. Evaluate candidates and write recommendations for Hiring Authority.
 Search Committee
  1. Select candidate for hire.
 Hiring Authority
  1. Approve candidate or direct Search Committee to close search or invite additional candidates.
    (Must comply with ACD 505-04)
 Hiring Authority & EO/AA
  1. Provide status report to affected department, University and Phoenix community (and Search Advisory Committee), if search is terminated or extended.
 Search Chair
  1. Design offer, which may include start-up package, effort requirements, travel funds, and employment for partner. Ensure that proposed offer maintains departmental/college/division equity.
 Hiring Authority
  1. Make offer by phone or invite selected finalist (and family) to visit Phoenix/Tempe. Arrange for community orientation and housing search; Human Resources to discuss benefit options, and the Executive V.P. and Provost’s Office to discuss dual career services as needed. Schedule meetings with individuals as requested by finalist.
 Hiring Authority
