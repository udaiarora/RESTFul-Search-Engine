March 30, 2005

Embassy beckons graduate student for internship

Andrea Cairella has a passion for helping people that soon will be directed to the people of Paraguay.

Cairella, an ASU graduate student, has been selected to intern with the U.S. State Department in the U.S. Embassy in Paraguay this June. There she will have the opportunity to work in Western Hemisphere Affairs and conduct foreign relations.

Cairella has always felt a drive to work with people and learn about cultures. She started volunteering for community service projects when she was 10 years old and says she has been long aware of different cultures.

“I’ve always been surrounded by people from Latin America from my parents’ friendships,” Cairella says.

Cairella, a political science major, also minored in Spanish and history at Santa Clara University . She has done extensive traveling and last summer interned for the U.S. State Department in the Fulbright Programs Office in Washington, D.C. Next fall, she will continue to pursue her master’s degree in counseling at ASU.

All of Cairella’s experiences made her the perfect candidate to intern in Paraguay . She says that qualifications for the program included:

• Being fluent in Spanish.

• Having a competitive GPA.

• Letters of recommendation.

• A personal essay.

• Having an adaptive, diplomatic personality.

Cairella said that going to Paraguay was her second choice (her first choice was Chile ), but she liked the idea of working with a developing nation and the diverse population of Paraguay.

Cairella also works with the Community Service Program (CSP) at ASU, helping to advise and counsel students. That experience has helped prepare her for the work she will be doing in Paraguay – and possibly for a career.

“(It’s a) smaller scale of what I am interested in on a bigger, international level,” Cairella says.

Cairella says majoring in political science got her interested in such international issues as international relations, conflict resolution and comparative politics. She likes the idea of improving relationships in a global setting.

“I think in having (cultural awareness) you can more clearly see the world,” Cairella says.
