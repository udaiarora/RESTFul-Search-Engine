Letter 24


September 1, 1912


Dear Georg,


Today, Thursday, you are home already, we think often about you. All the newspapers are here couldn't let it(?)..


Ella's eye is not better yet. I told her not to go out in the sun so much, it could make it worse. We weren't swimming today, George said the water was high.


Did you eat at Marie's? Don't let the people (the Bongs?) who rented the shop, bring anything into the back yard. We don't want to make it too dirty. How is everything? Is it hot? How hot has it been?


When Anni Wells comes, tell her that I'll be home in the middle of September, when the house cleaning starts. She can help me. I miss you.


Greetings and kisses from your wife and children.
