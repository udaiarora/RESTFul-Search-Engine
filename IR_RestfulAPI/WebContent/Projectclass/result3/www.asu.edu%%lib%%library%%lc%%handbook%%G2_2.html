G.2.2: Continuing Appointment/Promotion

Effective 8/22/91 .. Revised 3/20/2002

PROCEDURES:

 

Personnel action procedures for application for continuing appointment and/or promotion involve a series of steps by which the academic professional's packet moves from one level of review to the next. The dates by which the various steps in the process must be completed are determined by the Schedule of Personnel Actions, Librarians' Handbook, A.5, which is issued by the Assistant Dean for Personnel.


The academic professional obtains the proper forms from the Assistant Dean for Personnel and prepares a concise, organized, and complete packet.


Well in advance of final submission to the primary evaluator, the academic professional is encouraged to consult with the primary evaluator, the Assistant Dean for Personnel, and other colleagues on the content and arrangement of the packet. The primary evaluator and the academic professional may continue work on the packet until it is submitted to the next level in the process.


The elements of the personnel action process are arranged below in such a way as to enumerate in a single place the activities of each participant in the process. While often one procedure must be completed before the next step is taken, there are numerous instances in which actions by various participants must be carried out concurrently. Therefore, it should be understood that the following outline is NOT a strict chronological account of personnel action procedures.


As described in E3, all continuing appointment applications for librarians at the assistant rank must be accompanied by an application for promotion to Associate Librarian.

 


THE ACADEMIC PROFESSIONAL:

 

A. Notifies the Assistant Dean for Personnel in writing of intent to apply for continuing appointment and/or promotion.

B. Obtains the following forms:

1.      the Request for Academic Personnel Action multipart form (from the Assistant Dean for Personnel);

2.      the Continuing Appointment and/or Promotion Data Sheet (in Librarians' Handbook, G.2.4);

3.      two copies of the Letters of Reference Roster form ( Librarians' Handbook, G.2.4.), one of which is given to the primary evaluator.

NOTE: When the personnel action involves continuing appointment review with promotion to the next rank, two copies of the Request for Academic Personnel Action form are required.

C. Completes one of the Letter of Reference Roster forms and submits it to the University Librarian and Dean of the University Libraries by the prescribed date. The academic professional and primary evaluator may confer on compiling the two rosters. (See Librarians' Handbook, G.2.4. and ACD 506-07 and ACD 506-09)

D. Composes a two-page letter of application addressed to the Chair of the Review Committee for Personnel Action briefly describing job performance and clearly identifying significant professional development and contribution activities and service accomplishments. It is incumbent on the academic professional to demonstrate the relevance of activities cited to fulfill the requirements for continuing appointment and/or promotion. The academic professional should describe these contributions clearly and indicate the extent of participation and level of professional expertise involved in each. The academic professional should include appropriate activities from previous professional positions.

E.      Adds the Request for Academic Personnel Action form(s) to the packet.

F.      Places a current curriculum vitae in the packet. (See Librarians' Handbook, F.3.)

G. Includes any optional supporting materials in the packet. Provides an inventory of these materials on the Continuing Appointment and/or Promotion Data Sheet and clearly identifies one or two items to be forwarded to the Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University. (See Librarians' Handbook, F.4)

H. Reviews the job description for completeness and accuracy, and revises it as necessary in consultation with the primary evaluator. Adds a copy of the current job description to the packet. (See Librarians' Handbook, F.1)

I.        Submits the entire packet to the primary evaluator.

J.       Notifies the Assistant Dean for Personnel in writing that the packet has been submitted to the primary evaluator.

 

THE PRIMARY EVALUATOR:

 

A. Completes one of the Letters of Reference Roster forms and submits it to the University Librarian and Dean of the University Libraries by the prescribed date. The primary evaluator and the academic professional may confer on compiling the two rosters. (See Librarians' Handbook, G.2.4 and ACD 506-07 and ACD 506-09)

B. Critically reviews the packet for organization and completeness, to ensure that the contents are in compliance with requirements set forth in the Librarians' Handbook and Academic Affairs Policy and Procedures Manual (ACD).

C. Advises the academic professional regarding effective presentation of the packet's contents.

D. Writes a letter to the Chair of the Review Committee evaluating the academic professional’s performance and accomplishments in all areas (job performance, professional development and contribution, and service), with an emphasis on job performance, from the date of hire.  The letter identifies strengths and weaknesses in relation to the Criteria appropriate for the personnel action under consideration.  Advises the academic professional of his/her recommendation.

Note: See G2.3 for notification procedure in case of negative recommendation.

E.      Signs the required documents and forms and forwards the packet to the next level of review.

F.      For promotion requests only, the primary evaluator notifies the academic professional in person of the Review Committee's recommendation after the Chair advises the primary evaluator orally of it's recommendation.

 

ASSOCIATE DEAN LEVEL: (when applicable)

 

A. Reviews the packet in its totality. Refers to standards for the appropriate personnel action and prepares a letter of recommendation addressed to the Chair of the Review Committee, synthesizing the contents of the packet. Adds the letter to the packet.

Note: See G2.3 for notification procedure in case of negative recommendation.

B. Sends the packet to the Assistant Dean for Personnel.

 

THE ASSISTANT DEAN FOR PERSONNEL:

 

Upon receiving the packet from the previous level of review


A. Reviews the packet for completeness. Adds a sample of the form letter used to solicit letters of reference for the academic professional and includes the letters received on behalf of the academic professional.

B. Sends the packet to the Review Committee.


Upon receiving the packet from the University Librarian and Dean of the University Libraries


C. Prepares the packet for the Office of the ExecutiveVice President and Provost of the Universityby:

1) returning to the academic professional any supplementary materials besides those to be forwarded to the Provost's office; and

2) making and retaining copies of all confidential documents for the academic professional's official personnel file held in the University Libraries.

D. Includes the criteria for the personnel action and sends the packet to the Office of the ExecutiveVice President and Provost of the University.

 

THE REVIEW COMMITTEE FOR PERSONNEL ACTION:

 

A. Examines, discusses, and evaluates the packet according to the standards for the appropriate personnel action. Adds a letter of recommendation.

NOTE: See Librarians' Handbook, G.2.3, for notification procedure in case of negative recommendation.

B. The Chair indicates the recommendation on the application and signs it.

C. The Chair then forwards the entire packet to the University Librarian and Dean of the University Libraries or the Associate Dean for Library Services when the latter is acting on behalf of the Dean.

D. For promotion requests only, the Chair notifies the primary evaluator orally of the recommendation.

 

THE UNIVERSITY LIBRARIAN AND DEAN OF THE UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES:

 

Upon receiving the Letters of Reference Rosters from the academic professional and the primary evaluator


A. Chooses an equal number of names from each roster and solicits letters of reference. The list of references and the content of the letters shall remain confidential.


Upon receiving the packet from the Review Committee


B. Examines and evaluates the packet.

C. Composes a letter of recommendation and includes it in the packet.

Note: See G2.3 for notification procedure in case of negative recommendation.

D. Reviews and signs the Request for Personnel Action form.

E.      Forwards the packet to the Assistant Dean for Personnel.

NOTE: When the University Librarian andDean of the UniversityLibraries is also the primary evaluator, the responsibilities of the Dean are delegated to the Associate Dean for Library Services.

 

EXECUTIVE VICE PRESIDENT AND PROVOST OF THE UNIVERSITY:

 

The Executive Vice President and Provost of the University sends the packet to the University Continuing Appointment and Promotion Review Committee. After review, the Committee makes its recommendation to the Provost. The Presidentmakes the final decision to grant or deny the request. The University Librarian and Dean of the University Libraries will be notified of the decision.

 

The University Librarian andDean of the University Libraries, or designate, notifies the academic professional in person of the decision.
