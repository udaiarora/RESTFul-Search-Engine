April 3, 2003

Groups celebrate teaching excellence of ASU faculty

ASU students, faculty, staff and community members are invited to attend "A Celebration of Teaching and Learning Excellence" on April 8. The event will honor the ASU Parents Association 2003 Professor of the Year, the Associated Students of ASU Centennial Professorships and all faculty members nominated for the awards. The free event will begin with a small reception scheduled at 5:15 p.m., in the Old Main Carson Ballroom.

Last year's Professor of the Year George Watson, ASU professor of journalism and mass communication, is serving as the chairman of the selection committee this year. Watson says the 22 finalists for this award go beyond excellence in their own teaching, influencing positively the teaching of others and fostering learning beyond their own students, reaching not only other students but often the broader community as well.

"It goes without saying that all of the nominees are excellent teachers, esteemed by their students and colleagues alike," he says. "The Professor of the Year, however, will be one who has exhibited the most substantial contributions to teaching as a profession over the years."

Twenty-two full-time tenured and tenure-track ASU faculty members were nominated for the Parents Association Professorship, based on their outstanding contributions to undergraduate education. Watson says this event serves as acknowledgement of the relationship between professors and parents.

"Teachers care for their students; parents care even more for their children," he says. "For the parents to step forward with the Parents Association Professorship, the functional equivalent of a teaching Regents Professorship, is a testament to their concern for the quality of education received by their children. It also represents their commitment to ASU and the faculty to promoting and recognizing quality undergraduate education. In this way, the parents help us all remember what our new president, Michael Crow, has affirmed, 'Teaching is our prime directive.'"

The Associated Students of ASU Centennial Professorships will also be presented at the April 8 event to two associate professors considered "rising stars" in the ASU faculty. The two recipients of the 2003 Centennial Professorships are Shannon Ringenbach, of the kinesiology department in the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences (CLAS) and Kyle Longley, of the CLAS history department. Each winner will receive an award of $20,000, half of which is to be used for the betterment of students.

The faculty member awarded the Parents Association Professorship receives a cash award of $10,000 and the lifelong designation of Parents Association Professor. The Parents Association will also provide $10,000 ($5,000 per academic year) to fund an undergraduate student assistant for the professor.

In addition, the recipient will be invited to become a fellow in the ASU Distinguished Teaching Academy. Finalists will receive a $1,000 award. The winner will be announced at the April 8 event.

ASU Parents Association 2003 Professor of the Year nominees are:
Madelaine Adelman, School of Justice Studies; Edward Castaneda, psychology department; Alice Christie, ASU West College of Education; Shannon Dirksen, College of Nursing; Nancy Eisenberg, psychology department; Randel Helms, English department; Robert Ismeurt, College of Nursing; Merrill R. Karp, ASU East Aeronautical Management Technology; Douglas T. Kenrick, psychology department;
Kraig Knutson, Del E. Webb School Of Construction; Manfred Laubichler, biology department; Barry Leshowitz, psychology department; Neal Lester, English department and African American Studies Program; Vera Lopez, School of Justice Studies; Kevin McHugh, geography department; Susan Messman, Hugh Downs School of Human Communication;

Judy Pickens, College of Nursing; Armando Rodriguez, electrical engineering department; Frank Sackton, School of Public Affairs; P. VanderMeer, history department; Rose Weitz, sociology department; and Matthew Whitaker, history department.

"For faculty, the opportunity to see the teaching commitment, skills, and methods of their colleagues," Watson says, "and to see that students and parents do care is an affirmation of that basic decision to become a teacher."

Those who wish to attend "A Celebration of Teaching and Learning Excellence," should R.S.V.P. by April 4 at (480) 965-7501. Reservations are required.
