Task Force on the Employment Status of Advisors

The Task Force on the Employment Status of Advisors was appointed by Provost of the University Milton Glick in February 2004 to examine the employment categories of academic advisors across the ASU campuses.  The primary impetus for constituting this task force is that academic advisors across the campuses are classified within several different employment categories:  classified staff; academic associates; and faculty, including both ranked and track faculty members, who have advising as part of their responsibilities. 

As a consequence of these varied categorizations, several difficulties exist:  there is no coherent set of expectations, skill set, or level of education expected for the position of academic advisor across the campus; salaries are irregular and salary adjustments are inconsistently applied across the categories for the quality/quantity of work; no structured career ladder exists; and professional development opportunities are difficult to implement and fund.  As a result, good candidates often are unable to accept employment at ASU.  Also, there is instability in the internal job market due to salary differentials and some units have trouble retaining their academic advisors.  

The task force was charged to specifically address the following items: 

  1. Review the employment status of academic advisors, the institutional categories in which they are most frequently placed, and the salary structures;
  2. Make recommendations for improving the university campus cultureswith a  view toward attracting and retaining our best advisors and best serving our students;
  3. Identify the various responsibilities carried out by academic advisors, such as advisement (both base level and developmental), recruitment, coordination of programs, teaching, etc.;
  4. Determine an appropriate progression of levels within an academic advisor category, with a description of the amount of experience, level of education, and skill set for each level. (The creation of new categories within the academic professional is currently being restricted; however, the service professional category may be used to develop coherent job descriptions).

The Task Force was composed of academic advisors, faculty members, academic senate representatives, and administrators from East, West, and Main campuses (see Roster).   A draft report was developed and disseminated to the important constituencies of the university community in April 2004; feedback was solicited and the Task Force revised and finalized its report, which is posted below.

The Provost of the University would now like to solicit input from the university community before making his recommendations.  Academic advisors, as well as ASU faculty, administrators, and staff, are encouraged to review the final report of the Task Force on the Employment Status of Advisors, posted below, and submit comments and suggestions online, via email to the Provost at recommendations@asu.edu, or via hard copy to Office of the Executive Vice President & Provost of the University, Mail Code 2803. Please send your comments by August 31, 2004.

The University Provost will make final decisions regarding the Task Force’s recommendations, after he has reviewed the university commentary.
