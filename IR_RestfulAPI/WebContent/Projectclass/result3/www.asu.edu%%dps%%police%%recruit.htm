ASU Police Services Recruitment


Opportunities

The diverse and dynamically charged environment of the Arizona State University campus provides the professional law enforcement officer with unique challenges and opportunities. In addition to Patrol officers, specialty assignments may include:

* Investigations
* Crime Prevention
* Motorcycle Patrol
* Narcotics
* Community-Based Policing
* Bike Officers
* Auto Theft


Benefits

Employment with the ASU Department of Public Safety Police Services Division entitles individuals to the same benefits as other University employees; including:

* Tuition waivers for self, spouse and dependent children;
* Medical, dental and life insurance plans;
* Paid vacation;
* Paid holidays;
* Sick and family leave; and,
* Paid bereavement leave

Additional departmental benefits include overtime opportunities at the many college and professional athletic and other sporting and cultural events. Police Officers also enjoy the benefits of:

* Weapons and all necessary equipment furnished
* Membership at the Student Recreation Center
* Uniforms furnished and clothing allowance provided


Minimum Requirements of the ASU Police Officer

To be considered for a sworn position with the Division, you must be at least 21 years of age and have a high school diploma or GED. In addition, you must meet the criteria set forth by the Arizona Peace Officer Standards and Training Board. All employment is contingent upon successful completion of extensive background, polygraph, psychological, medical, and drug screening evaluations. Starting salary range is $40,000 for police officer recruits and $43,000 for lateral entry.


Minimum Requirements of the ASU Radio Dispatcher

To be considered for a position with the Division as a Dispatcher, you must have six months police dispatching experience and six months clerical experience (including typing or work processing skills), OR one year clerical experience in which in which necessary skills were required, OR an equivalent combination of education and experience from which comparable knowledge and skills were achieved. All employment is contingent upon successful completion of extensive background, polygraph, psychological, medical, and drug screening evaluations. Starting salary is $32,000 DOE.


Minimum Requirements of Police Services Civilian Personnel

Individual positions have varying requirements. All employment is contingent upon successful completion of extensive background, polygraph, psychological, medical, and drug screening evaluations. (Psychological and medical evaluations may be waived for non-sworn personnel.) Starting salary range is dependent upon position and experience.

How to Apply

Vacancies may be announced in the Arizona Republic (Sunday edition), the ASU Web, and other media when appropriate. When positions are available the vacancy notice will be published on the ASU HR website at www.asu.edu/hr/jobs.

Arizona State University no longer uses applications and only accepts resumes for currently open positions.

For additional information, please contact ASU Human Resources
