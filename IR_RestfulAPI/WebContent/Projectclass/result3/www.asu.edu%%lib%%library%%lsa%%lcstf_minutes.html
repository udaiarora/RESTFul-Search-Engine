Admin Council - May 25, 2006

AGENDA ITEM I: University Librarian's Update-Dean Sherrie Schmidt

  • ARTSTOR information.
  • OCLC and RLG are "coming together," allowing OCLC to have more of a focus on research libraries.
  • Scott Muir has accepted the position as new director of the Downtown Campus Library.
    • All books have been ordered and are in storage.
    • We won't have access to the lower level of the building until the end of July.
  • End of year expenditures
  • Remodel Hayden C52, the Quiet Study Room.
  • Shelving for Music.
  • Purchase of materials.
  • Upgrade Hayden Brown Bag Room.
  • Ensure Hayden C133 is high-tech for presentations.
  • Waiting to hear about enhancements for Archives & Special Collections.
  • Noble infrastructure as part of technology infrastructure.
  • Remodel Hayden entrance.

AGENDA ITEM II: Associate Dean Updates

  • John Howard
    • Collection Development and Technical Services are creating a linking task group to address issues identified by the R2 report. Members will be identified next week.
    • Innovative's "Electronic Resource Management" will impact workflow design.
    • Howard asked Betsy Redmen (Technical Services) to discuss "Worldcat Selection," a new service from OCLC. ASU Libraries has expressed our interest to be involved in this service that will allow selectors to view notification from various vendors in one interface. It may be implemented in September 2006.
    • Google is leasing space at the USB for 3 years. The 150-200 Google employees will receive ASU employee amenities.
      • Sun Card will say "Google"
      • ASURite ID
      • On-campus access to ASU networks, but not off-campus.
      • Library borrowing privileges, but no ILL privileges.
  • Kurt Murphy: Karie Pifer (Personnel) is going into surgery tomorrow, May 26.
  • Vicki Coleman
    • Mission and vision update: Working on common ground and expecting to complete the process next week. Each library might have its own vision and mission.
    • LibQual was completed a couple of weeks ago, and Coleman will deliver the results at the next Admin Council.

AGENDA ITEM III: Noble Library
A committee has researched the concept of creating a "Learning Center at Noble Library." Marc Mason (Instruction, Outreach, & Marketing) presented the proposal, which covered recommendations regarding the open study areas, a multi-purpose classroom, the group study rooms, an ADA oasis, and other suggestions. See your supervisor for a copy of the "Learning Center @ Noble Library Proposal."
