Band concerts aim to get audiences dancing, whisk them around the world and stir up the dust!

A dance club atmosphere that encourages patrons to get up and learn new steps. A concert rated "R" (for the complexity of its music, not the content!) And a trip around the world in 80 minutes. Does this sound like your idea of band music?

The Herberger College School of Music's band season is shaking things up this year, with new ideas and methods that will keep audience members on the edge of their seats - if they stay in their seats at all!

The changes and innovations vary from event to event during the spring season, which features seven concerts. Gary Hill, director of bands, promises dance music from fox trot to hip hop at the Feb. 26 event, We Could Have Danced All Night. Audience members will be encouraged to actually join dancers on stage to learn new dances during this concert.

This event and Feb. 19's Songs and Dances concert contrast sharply with Dreams and Fancies , the April 8 concert that Hill has rated "R" for musically mature audiences. At this event, the band will perform complex works intended to challenge the listeners' intellects. The audience will also enjoy the opportunity to hear Pulitzer Prize-winning composer Leslie Bassett playing with clarinetist Robert Spring.

Hill says that the process of experiencing the music offered at this event is an intellectual exercise first, then an emotional one. "It's for people who not only like art music, but like new music," Hill says.

The season will conclude with Around the World in Eighty Minutes , a concert featuring music from across the globe. The School of Music's Wind Ensemble, Wind Symphony and Chamber Winds will be joined by guest artists who include an Indian trio and an Irish band.

The complete Herberger College School of Music band season for spring 2004 follows. All band concerts are free. For more information on the band season, the public should call (480) 965-TUNE or visit the Herberger College Web Site at http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/ and click on "Things to Do."

With Strings Attached
7:30 p.m., Feb. 17, Gammage Auditorium
The Chamber Winds ensemble joins forces with the Chamber Orchestra and world-renowned cellist Colin Carr in an evening of disparate, but delightful, music!

Songs and Dances
7:30 p.m., Feb. 19, Gammage Auditorium
The Wind Ensemble, Martin Province, conductor, present a concert of music true to its title!

We Could Have Danced All Night.
7:30 p.m., Feb. 26, Gammage Auditorium
And you just might after this rousing review of two-steps (more commonly known as marches), fox trots, tangos, disco tunes, hip-hop beats and more! Join the Wind Symphony and guest dancers as Gammage Auditorium is transformed into a multi-generation dance club.

It's an Honor
4:30 p.m., Feb. 29, Gammage Auditorium
A concert featuring the 2003-2004 edition of the ASU High School Honor Band, joined by special guest conductor Timothy Reynish and the Wind Symphony.

Winds in the Desert
7:30 p.m., April 6, Gammage Auditorium
The Concert Band, comprising musicians from across the campus, joins the Wind Ensemble in a performance destined to stir up the dust!

Dreams and Fancies
7:30 p.m., April 8, Gammage Auditorium
The Chamber Winds and the Wind Symphony invite you to join with them and guests, Pulitzer Prize-winning composer Leslie Bassett and clarinetist Robert Spring, in an evening of musical fantasies. This concert is rated "R" (for some complex musical language) and is intended for musically mature audiences.

Around the World in Eighty Minutes
7:30 p.m., April 29, Gammage Auditorium
The Wind Ensemble, Wind Symphony and Chamber Winds perform music from across the globe, including Ireland, India and Japan, with special guests, including internationally renowned Indian Classical artist and tabla player Sandip Burman and his trio.
