February 10, 2003

University recognizes Black History Month

Few Arizonans know the story of a hard-working, risk-taking Wickenburg pioneer who was one of the first black entrepreneurs in Arizona, Elizabeth Hudson Smith. She will be the topic of a lecture during Black History Month, at noon, Feb. 19 in the Memorial Union (MU) room 080, by ASU History Professor Matthew C. Whitaker.

Other related events on Main Campus include a workshop on "Women of Color in the Media" on Feb. 11, a Soul Food Festival on Feb. 12, an academic success workshop Feb. 20 and the World Festival on Feb. 27.

Smith moved to Wickenburg from Chicago in 1897, at a time when dude ranches and resorts began to boom and the Vulture Mine promised untold wealth to gold prospectors. The Santa Fe Railroad came through town, and by 1905 Smith and her husband Bill opened the Vernetta Hotel, which became one of the most successful hotels in Wickenburg.

It was a hub of social, political and cultural activity, famous for Smith's legendary cooking. Tourists made it a point to stop in for a meal, and people would drive from Phoenix to eat there. But Smith and her husband were the only African-Americans in the town for years, and she was often mistaken for a hotel maid.

Though her husband, who was an alcoholic, drifted away, Smith continued to invest in the community, purchasing a cattle ranch, restaurant, barbershop, about a dozen rental houses and shares of mining ventures. She was one of the founders of the opera house and the First Presbyterian Church.

Smith died in 1935, and the hotel closed shortly afterwards. Few records of her remain, but Whitaker and one of his students, Wickenburg native Lauren L. Kientz, have submitted an article for publication about Smith. The lecture is sponsored by the Committee on African and African American Research.

A workshop, "Images of Women of Color in the Media," will take place 3 - 5 p.m., Feb. 11 in the MU Fiesta Room, featuring Michelle Craig, Arizona Republic reporter; Anita Luera of KPNX-Channel 12; and Karen Leong, assistant professor of women's studies.

The STARS student organization will put on a Soul Food Festival 11 a.m. - 1 p.m., Feb. 12, on Hayden lawn, featuring many different types of foods and performances. Both events are sponsored by Student Life and the Multicultural Student Center, as is a success workshop for students of color from 3 to 5 p.m., Feb. 20 in the MU Gold Room.

The World Festival is an annual event by the International Student Office that celebrates the heritage and traditions of countries and cultures from around the world. Student organizations will sell native foods and display crafts and artifacts 10:30 a.m. - 2:30 p.m. Feb. 27 on the Student Services lawn.
