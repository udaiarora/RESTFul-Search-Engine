Librarians' Council


Minutes of Meeting


March 20, 2002

Meeting called to order at 3:01 PM

Chair-Elect: Ken Furuta
Secretary: Mimmo Bonanni
A quorum was present.

I. Welcome and introduction of Daniel Jankowski, Self-Study Coordinator ASU Graduate College - Ken Furuta


II. Presentation by Daniel Jankowski


The subject of Daniel Jankowski's talk was, "ASU Self-Study for Continuing Accreditation of ASU Main & East by the Higher Learning Commission." Daniel mentioned that as part of its affiliation with the Higher Learning Commission, ASU Main and ASU East would undergo a review and site visit on March 24-26, 2003. This comprehensive decennial review provides an opportunity to conduct a Self-Study that considers what ASU is, does, and intends to do in the future.


The presentation included an overview of the self-study including the goals for the ASU Self-Study; the timeline of activities for the self-study; and the self-study committee structure and rosters. Before departing Dr. Jankowski answered questions from the floor.


III. Approval of Minutes


Librarians' Council February 20, 2002 minutes were approved by council.


IV. Dean's Report - Sherrie Schmidt


The Dean reported on her meeting with Milton D. Glick, Senior Vice President and Provost. The topic of discussion was this year's budget. Sherrie presented some budget triage options currently being considered by the library such as merging public service point desks. Sherrie also reported on the portal project that ASU Libraries is participating in with other academic libraries such as the University of Arizona and University of Southern California. The name of the company spearheading the project is Fretwell-Downing Informatics (FDI), and the name of software is called ZPORTAL. Sherrie will send council the URL of the company.


V. Chair-Elect and Executive Committee Report


The Chair-Elect announced that he would present a few items under New Business.


VI. Committee Reports


Personnel Committee - Scott Herrington


Scott announced that current committee revisions to sections E.2: Criteria, E.3: Requirements, G.2.1: Probationary Evaluation, G.2.2: Continuing Appointment/Promotion of the Librarians' Handbook will be discussed under Old Business.


Review Committee - Kathy Krzys


No report.


Research and Professional Development Committee - Dan Stanton


No report.

Compensation Committee - Lois Schneberger


No report.


Academic Senate - George Bell


No report.

Committee on Academic Professional Status - Linda Shackle

 

Linda announced that CAPS is hosting a discussion with Barbara Mawhiney, Director of Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action, on the rationale of the job classification system. The discussion will highlight information on the Academic Professional classification including the category of Academic Associates. It will take place on April 3, 2002 in the Memorial Union, 215 at 10:30 am. Linda and Annie encouraged council members to attend.

Page Master


Ken announced that Annie Platoff would be leaving her position with LIST at the end of April. John and Ken are currently looking for a replacement for the Page Master position. Annie announced to council that she has created Librarians' Council Page Master's procedures.


VII. Old Business


Vote on Revisions to Librarians' Handbook sections E.2, E.3, G.2.1, G.2.2 (Personnel Committee)


The Review Committee also asked Personnel to look at handbook sections used by probationary librarians to create their annual reviews, in particular the sections that emphasized employee contributions and the sections that emphasize service. The sections examined were: E.2: Criteria, E.3: Requirements, G.2.1: Probationary Evaluation, G.2.2: Continuing Appointment/Promotion. Last month, Personnel Committee asked council to look at the revised sections and be prepared to discuss and vote on the revisions. Personnel Committee asked to withdraw last months suggested revisions in favor of revisions presented at this meeting. Council passed motion to introduce revisions. Scott introduced and described the revisions to each section.


For section E.2, under the SERVICE heading, the committee added a few sentences in the second paragraph. There was a vote and council did carry the motion to accept the revisions to the section. Personnel will make the change, and update the handbook.


For section E3, Scott mentioned that the committee moved paragraphs within the section, and they added subheading to make the section easier to read overall. In the first paragraph, a sentence was added to the first paragraph. There was a vote. The group accepted the changes and council did carry the motion to accept the revisions to the section. Personnel will make the change, and update the handbook.


For section G.2.1, under the heading, THE PROBATIONARY ACADEMIC PROFESSIONAL, the committee added three additional paragraphs. The committee also added links to the, "Marion Librarian…" section of the handbook. There was also a suggestion from the floor to add an introductory bullet to the Marion the Librarian section. The introductory bullet would be at the top of the list and it would provide an overview of the Marion the Librarian section. The group accepted the suggested revisions. A vote was cast. The group accepted the changes and council did carry the motion to accept the revisions to the section. Personnel will make the change, and update the handbook.


For Section G.2.2, under THE ACADEMIC PROFESSIONAL heading section D, the group added a few clarifying sentences were added for clarification. The group accepted the suggested revisions. A vote was cast. The group accepted the changes and council did carry the motion to accept the revisions to the section. Personnel will make the change, and update the handbook.


VIII. New Business


Revisions to Librarians' Handbook sections G.2.1, G.2.2, (Personnel Committee)


Executive Committee asked Personnel Committee to add some further revisions to the handbooks sections G.2.1: Probationary Evaluation and G.2.2: Continuing Appointment/Promotion. The revisions address and clarify the primary evaluators role and responsibilities.

Scott handed out the revisions to the floor to read and consider. He explained that the committee added a few sentences to unify language consistency between different sections of the handbook. Annie moved that we suspend the rules so council could discuss the proposed revisions at this meeting, and if accepted the changes could be added to the handbook. A discussion followed on whether council should suspend the rules for this meeting in order to hasten the revision process. The group agreed to suspend the rules.


A discussion on the revisions followed. There was a suggestion from the floor to remove a comma in the following sentence in section G.2.1 under the heading, THE PRIMARY EVALUATOR, "….with an emphasis on job performance, from the date of hire." Leaving the comma in the sentence does not convey the PE's purview to comment on a librarians' comparable past work history. The group agreed. A vote was cast for revisions to G.1.1 and G.2.2. The group accepted the changes and council did carry the motion to accept the revisions to the sections. Personnel will make the change, and update the handbook.


Resolution for Annette Voth


Ken announced that the Resolution for Annette would be postponed until the next Librarians' Council meeting.


Meeting adjourned at 4:20 pm.


Mimmo Bonanni, Secretary
