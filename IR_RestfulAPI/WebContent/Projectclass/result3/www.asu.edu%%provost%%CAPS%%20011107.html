Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

November 7, 2001

Present: Patricia Feldman, Carol Behl, Stefan Myhajlenko, Linda Shackle, Santos C. Vega
Members Excused: Annie Platoff
Guest Speaker: Dr. Gail Hackett, Vice Provost for Academic Personnel
Agenda: Review minutes from 10-03-01; Old business; new business; Plan next neeting; Adjourn.

The review of 10-03 minutes was postponed until next meeting.

The old business included the invitation of Dr. Hackett to visit with CAPS. Patricia Feldman, Chair, asked Dr. Hackett for an overview.

G.H. said that she did not have expectatiosn of the CAPS committee. Patty Feldman will provide the direct connection between CAPS and the administration and university business as being member of the Provost Advisory Personnel Council (PAC 20). Patty can propose issues important to CAPS before the C. of 20.

The main points discussed at CAPS meeting included the efforts to educate the rest of the campus on what APs are and do.

Dr. Hackett once more demonstrated an interest in the Associate Advisors and asked to up-date last year's letter in regards to that issue. The letter written and submitted to Dr. Vega as Chair of CAPS last year by Carol Behl and Victor Becerra, was retyped in computer, signed and sent to Dr. Hackett by S. Vega. The matter, placed on hold during the summer, can be redirected again this year. It was suggested that to avoid confusion, the issue should be presented along three lines of status of APs: year-to-year, probationary, and continuing. A new revised letter in these regards of the issue should then be sent to Dr. Morton E. Munk, President of Academic Senate and a copy to Dr. Gail Hackett. APs are part of Academic Senate.

A long discusssion ensued about how APs were believed to have been created and how Academic Associates should include recognition and gain voting rights and use APs resources. Adding to the mix was the action taken last year of some classified staff personnel becoming Administrative Associates, a move that ended in providing less benefits and promotions. Academic Asociates were created for a temporary need but are not temporary need in reality. The some colleges deal differently with how classified staff mvoe into associate ranks. The end result is that the process of placing in rank, promotion, and continuing status for Associates is not rational. The realm of personnel including promotion and tenure of faculty, year-to-year faculty, and faculty associates who teach part-time is in need of study and readjusting toward understanding and protection of personnel. In reality, everyone on campus, including tenured faculty, have a year-to-year contract. Other issues discussed included hiring, promotion, scholarly work definition, faculty retention, and partners (example man and wife teams) recruitment and hiring. These partner combinations may create problems such conducting a national search for one and then hiring the partner minus search and interviewing process. Two or more departments have to coordinate effort to avoid one bearing an undue burden. Other real campus problems discussed included lack of space and funds. Another issue concerned the ACD Manual.. It was developed piecemeal and is difficult to read and interpret. By next year CAPS will need a committee to work on the ACD Manual and write a report. The post-tenure status remains on hold. One issue to study is the sick-leave issue, especially for APs on a nine-month appointment. Faculty who have twenty years and are ready to retire have accrued sick leave, but new faculty do not accrue. The health benefits and the budget crisis were the last issues discussed.

The CAPS members decided to meet on December 5, 2001 in Gila 214.

Meeting adjourned.
Minutes provided by Santos C. Vega
