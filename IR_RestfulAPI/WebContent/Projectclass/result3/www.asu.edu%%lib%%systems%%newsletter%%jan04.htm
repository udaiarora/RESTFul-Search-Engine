Project Updates

LIST has the following improvements in progress:

· The wireless Ethernet network for coverage of Hayden Library's lower & upper concourse, C6, and C6 West is scheduled for completion by January 29th.
· The C6 West Classroom project should be ready for use by mid-February. There had been a delay due to the availability of the LCD projector and the fact that the classroom workstations are dependent on the wireless Ethernet system, which is in the process of being completed.
· The public workstation project in the lower concourse area (across from the Reference Desk) will begin again in March with the addition of 10 new computers and 5 new Falcon tables. The project will be scheduled once the tables are shipped and delivered from Falcon. In order to minimize any disruption caused by the placement of the tables and the installation of new Ethernet lines and AC power to them, the project may be scheduled to begin over Spring Break in mid-March.


Video Courses Online Returns after Successful Fall Debut

LIST’s first semester offering Distance Learning courses online in streaming digital video was a huge success! We provided 39 classes and over 1600 course units to about 2,100 students, and the videos were viewed more than 59,000 times. A new semester of Video Courses Online is now underway. Although we will be streaming only 16 courses this time, we expect the number of viewings to drop only slightly because the remaining courses accounted for the majority of hits last semester. Two additions include the Women and Social Action course (WST 294) and Introduction to Parenting (FAS 301). These two classes were formerly held on reserve and their inclusion in Video Courses Online frees up additional shelf space and reduces checkout time for Access Services.

Q. "Is there any way to keep spam out of my inbox?"

A. Once you start getting inundated with spam, the game is over, short of changing your email address (see the September issue of We're Listening). All you can do is use anti-spam software or the Outlook Rules Wizard to help you sort it out and make it easier to delete. But if you’re still relatively spam-free, here are some tips that might help you stay that way:

1. Opt out of receiving newsletters, email updates, etc., from websites. On some sites YES is automatically selected and you have to uncheck it; on others you have to click to say NO. If you don’t want to opt out, provide a non-ASU email address (see #3 below).

2. Be careful when downloading and installing utilities such as Hotbar, the Google toolbar, etc. While these can be useful tools, you may be inadvertently agreeing to receive spam or pop-up ads. Look carefully at all the options presented and uncheck anything offering to install enhanced features or to send you notification of special offers, etc.

3. Set up a separate email account on Yahoo, Hotmail, or some other free email site, and use it when you need to provide an email address in order to receive a service, place an order, or to have a password or other information sent to you for access. While this email address will be available to most spammers, there will usually be a “bulk” mailbox to accumulate the spam, which can then be deleted with a couple of mouse clicks. Be aware, however, that you must check this mail account periodically, sometimes as often as every 30 days, or the account will be closed. Use your regular email address only for trusted entities with whom you may need to communicate, such as your bank or credit card company.

4. Never reply to spam messages. This simply confirms that your email address is valid, and may increase the amount of spam you receive.
If you’re thinking the new federal “Can Spam” law will help, don’t count on it. Some analysts believe the net result will be more spam. According to Scott Hazen Mueller, CAUCE Chairman (http://www.cauce.org/), “This bill does not stop a single spam from being sent….[W]e have no faith that this law will significantly reduce the amount of spam that American Internet users receive.''

If you missed out on Spam Inspector or are looking for an alternative, there is also a spam filter available through EMMA. This has worked well for a number of users. To activate it, go to www.asu.edu/emma, log in (top of the screen) with your asurite username and password, then in the upper right of the next screen click on “Click here for Emma Applications.” In the new window that opens, about halfway down the column on the left, click on Spam Filtering, next click “Settings”, then click on the No (to change it to Yes) to enable spam filtering on your email account(s). Click on Help for more information on how to manage the filtered (quarantined) email and to manage email addresses on the spam list.

Remember that filtering software (including the Outlook rules wizard) is not perfect, so you must always check the filtered emails before deleting them. If legitimate emails have been mistakenly quarantined, marking these messages and selecting “Deliver Now” will deliver them to your Outlook inbox and add the addresses to your “white list” so they won’t be quarantined in the future.
