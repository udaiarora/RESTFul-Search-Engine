August 25, 2004

Faculty focus on teaching excellence

A record 182 new faculty and academic professionals are joining ASU this fall, and they are being welcomed with an expanded orientation program.

ASU's New Faculty Orientation was redesigned to give newcomers an in-depth overview of the university, including President Michael Crow's vision of a New American University.

The redesigned orientation comprises an all-university orientation for faculty from all campuses, individual campus orientations, and a series of teaching excellence workshops highlighting all the teaching resources available at ASU to help make faculty more successful.

The All-University orientation and campus specific orientations were held this week. Next week, new faculty are encouraged to take advantage of the Teaching Excellence @ Arizona State University Workshops for Faculty.

The workshops are presented by ASU's Center for Learning and Teaching Excellence (CLTE), which designs programs and services to support and develop the specific needs and teaching objectives of all faculty and departments.

"These workshops are a 'must' for new faculty joining ASU, but they're also open to current faculty," says Judy Grace, interim director of the CLTE. "They not only provide insight into the nuances of ASU, but also offer practical research-based instruction on best practices in teaching and learning."

The workshops offer an introduction to the resources and colleagues who can assist faculty with their teaching throughout the academic year and beyond.

Workshop dates and times, along with registration information, are available online at (www.clte.asu.edu).

Following are a few of the workshops designed for faculty members:
Course and Syllabus Design - Elements of an effective syllabus and strategies  for designing or re-designing an existing course.

Interactive Lecture - Practical, empirically validated strategies that incorporate short activities drawn from cooperative learning, classroom assessment, and writing-to-learn strategies to increase student engagement in lectures.

Teaching in a Diverse Classroom - Strategies for supporting learners from  diverse backgrounds.

Designing and Assessing Effective Writing Assignments - General principles for designing and grading formal and informal writing assignments.

MyASU I and II - Basic functions and features of ASU's online courseware.
The Pedagogy of Online Instruction - A condensed version of online discussions, online assessments, and online content covered in the three full-length online  pedagogy workshops.

Designing Online Assessments - Online learning environments can be improved using myASU quizzes and surveys. Various techniques used to design online assessments will be described, demonstrated, and practiced.

Ensuring Accessibility - Accessibility of instruction for those with disabilities is required by law,  advantageous for all, and surprisingly  easy to implement.

Using Video to Enhance Teaching and Learning - An introduction to principles of using video and still photography in a range of visual communication applications: video for presentations, instructional  video, visual data collection, documentation, and video as a research tool.

The New American University Curriculum - Explore ASU's ongoing conversation about undergraduate education and the  curriculum of the new American university, which offers foundational experiences  for students so that they might be engaged in lifelong learning.

Community-Based Learning - ASU campus resources available, key terms  defined, and an overview of instructional goals met through service learning, internships, and community service.

Addressing Critical Student Issues - Information about campus resources available to assist you when a student faces a crisis or other situation where help is needed.
