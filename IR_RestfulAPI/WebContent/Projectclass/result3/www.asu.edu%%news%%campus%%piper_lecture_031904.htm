Piper Center Distinguished Lecture Series Continues with a Prominent Trio

The Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing continues the popular Spring 2004 Distinguished Lecture Series with  "Solo Voices Times Three" at the Scottsdale Center for the Arts featuring  readings by writers Alexs Pate, Melissa Pritchard, and Alberto Ríos  on Friday, April  9, 2004 at 7:30 p.m. Tickets are $10. (Please visit < http://www.scottsdalearts.org > or call 480-994-ARTS to purchase tickets for this event.)

Alexs Pate is the author of five novels, including Amistad , commissioned by Steven Spielberg's Dreamworks/SKG and based on the screenplay by David Franzoni, which became a New York Times Bestseller. His most recent novel, West of Rehoboth , was published last fall and was selected as "Honor Fiction Book" by the Black Caucus of the American Library Association. Pate also was chosen by National Book Award novelist Charles Johnson as an "Achiever Who Will Lead The Next Generation" in the area of literature. Pate is currently an assistant professor of Afro-American and African studies at the University of Minnesota.

Melissa Pritchard is the author of three story collections, Spirit Seizures, The Instinct for Bliss, and Disappearing Ingenue, and three novels, Selene of The Spirits, Phoenix, and Late Bloomer, just published by Doubleday. Her many literary awards include The Flannery O'Connor Award,  the Carl Sandburg  Award, the Janet Heidinger Kafka Prize, as well as fellowships from the National Endowment for the Arts and the Howard Foundation at Brown University. Her stories have appeared and been cited in numerous anthologies, including Prize Stories: The O. Henry Awards, The Pushcart Prize, Best American Short Stories, and the Prentice Hall Anthology of Women's Literature. She is currently Director of the M.F.A. Creative Writing Program at Arizona State University.

Alberto Ríos is a Regents' Professor and Katharine C. Tuner Chair in English at Arizona State University. He is the author of eight books and chapbooks of poetry, three collections of short stories, and a memoir about growing up on the border, which won the Latino Literary Hall of Fame Award. His work is included in more than 175 national and international literary anthologies, including the Norton Anthology of Modern Poetry. His most recent book, The Smallest Muscle in the Human Body, was a finalist for the 2002 National Book Award in poetry.

The Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing at Arizona State University aims to foster the development of  a vibrant community of writers-for the university, the Phoenix metropolitan area and all of Arizona. Building on the existing strengths of ASU's graduate program in creative writing while helping to transform it into one of the nation's very best, the center offers programs that bring many of the country's best writing talents to the Phoenix area.

Future visiting writers this Spring will include Joseph Megel on Monday, April 19.
