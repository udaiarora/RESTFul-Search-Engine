ARIZONA SMALL BUSINESS INDUSTRY ANALYSIS

Doing an industry analysis for a small business takes time and patience because the niche markets and industry are small sections of huge industry sectors.  Below are some of the types of information that are needed to write a report.

  1. Industrial Classification Code -- Look up your company in the company directories suggested in Researching Small Business in Arizona.  Most directories will give you the industrial classifications used by your company.  Since the U.S. is in the process of changing industrial classification systems, you'll need to use both the SIC 1972-1997 & NAICS, 1997- (Industrial Classification codes used by the US Government) to identify the industries  that includes your company.   Many of the industrial analysis reports available are indexed by industrial classification, but when no such report is available, you need to use the periodical indexes to find articles on the industry and knowing what the industry name is helps.
     
  2. Industry Description -- This is a combination of the descriptions found with the industrial classification codes above and from sections listed below. Most small businesses are in niches between or among industries.  For example: Medtronics is a manufacturer of medical devices (most such devices are considered durable goods) so it is part of the Health Care Industry, the Manufacturing Industries (various subsectors), and the Insurance Industries (subgroup - Health & Life).  The Industry description and analysis will reflect that symbiosis.  There is rarely a free industry analysis on such narrow markets which requires your best guess at what is going on in the industry.
     
  3. NATIONAL Industry Reports
    1. Hayden Library Reference Index Table 9B -- Check your SIC/NAIC codes in the indexes for these sources.  *** Don't forget to check your company's name in the company index of these items just in case your company is listed! ***
      • S & P Industry Surveys
        Location: HAYDEN REFERENCE INDEX TABLE 2
      • WEFA Industrial Monitor
        Current editions at several local public libraries
      • Information, Finance & Services USA
        Location: HAYDEN REFERENCE INDEX TABLE 2
      • Infrastructure Industries USA
        Location: HAYDEN REFERENCE INDEX TABLE 2
      • Manufacturing & Distribution USA
        Location: HAYDEN REFERENCE INDEX TABLE 2
    2. Current Industrial Reports
    3. US Industry & Trade Outlook (C 61.48:  HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS) 2000 is the last print edition. NTIS now publishes the electronic version.
    4. Economic Census Data
      • How People Use Economic Census Data
      • 1997 Data
      • 2002 Data is not yet available.
      • Other Business Data
      • E-Commerce Statistics (E-Stats)
      • Data Access Tools
        • American FactFinder
        • Censtats
        • Quick Facts
        • TIGER (GIS & maps)
        • DataFerrett, a data mining tool
        • U.S. Gazetteer
    5. Industry & Corporate Social Responsibility
      -- This page has links to web sites and sources that have company and industry social responsibility and environmental policies. This is increasingly more important in the costs of operating many businesses.
       
  4. GNP (Gross National Product) -- is seldom used anymore, USE  GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
    1. GDP by industry sector
      • Statistical Abstract of the United States in Section 14. Income, Expenditures, and Wealth (Table 716 in 2000 ed.)
      • Survey of Current Business (C 59.11: Hayden U.S. Docs)
      • BEA's Gross Domestic Product by Industry (expressed in current U.S. Dollars)
      • BEA's Shares of Gross Domestic Product by Industry (expressed in % of current U.S. dollars)
      • U.S. GDP 1977 - the most recent year from Az DES
         
  5. Economic Indicators
    1. GDP and GNP are only two of over 50 Economic Indicators. These and other government calculated figures reflect the general health of the economy.  Until you are more knowledgeable about how each economic indicator affects your industry & its subsectors, the news articles by economists will tell you what they think it means and are a good general guide.  These are found in most newspapers and the Wall Street Journal on the day the indicator is released by the government.  Be careful! Some industries are counter-cyclic to the indicators (when the economy is bad some industries do well).
      • Economic Indicators (4 links to official U.S. Gov't web sites)
      • PollingReport.com -- Economic Outlook
      • STAT-USA (in the State of the Nation section the top 50 indicators are listed)
      • Statistical Abstract of the United States not current, data always 1-2 years behind current year.
      • Trends & Projections (has graphs of some of the indicators)
        Location: HAYDEN REFERENCE INDEX TABLE 2
         
  6. TRADE ASSOCIATION Industry Information
    1. To find trade associations use the key words from your industry. (Many industries have several trade associations and subsector associations. You need to find the best match for your company. It is entirely possible to find associations in  multiple industries that are appropriate.)
      • Associations Unlimited -- includes web site, if available
      • NTPA: National Trade & Professional Associations of the United States
        Location: HAYDEN REFERENCE
    2. Indirectly find an association by searching for industry statistics in
      • LexisNexis Statistical -- Restrict your subject search to the US, and the State and local areas.
         
  7. ARIZONA Industry Information
    1. Your company is one of how many in Arizona (industry size)?
      1. County Business Patterns including Arizona
      2. Reference USA -- Business Type - SIC then REVISE by adding Geography -- Arizona to the search
      3. QwestDex  -- "Search by Category" search
    2. Company ranking
      1. Business Journal Book of Lists (Kept at the Hayden Library Reference Desk)
      2. Ranking volumes of the directories on Hayden Library Reference Index Table 1.  *** Even if your company does not get listed, it does give you an idea of the industry size nationally. ***
        • Dun's Business Rankings
        • Million Dollar Directory
        • Ward's Business Directory
      3. Gary Price's List of Lists  (a long shot but this list finds nearly every list of lists done by journals, including many top company lists)
      4. Data mining Reference USA to get a general idea of your ranking.
    3. GSP (Gross State Product) by industry sector, 1977- most recent
    4. Arizona Trade Associations
        Arizona has many state and local chapters of national trade associations. Did you check the national association for local chapter information and contacts? Click here to see the sources to use to find this information.
    5. Arizona Industry News
        While all the business article indexes are good and worth searching using the word arizona and the industry, more detailed news is usually published or broadcast locally.
    6. Arizona's Industries and Economic Conditions
      1. Arizona Workforce Informer
      2. ASU Seidman Research Institute's Research Centers & their publications   ASU Libraries has the following publications that are very useful:
        • Western Blue Chip Economic Forecast
        • Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast
        • Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast
        • AZB: Arizona Business
        • Arizona Economic Profile
      3. ASU Seidman Research Institute Economic Information -- statistics useful for Arizona industry analysis
      4. U of A Division of Economic and Business Research publications:
        • Arizona's Economy
        • Arizona Economic Indicators
          Not available online
        • Current Economic Indicators
        • Economic OUtlook 2004-2005
