Myles Lynk, Faculty Profile


Office of the Faculty Athletic Representative


Myles Lynk

ASU Law Professor Myles Lynk, the Peter Kiewit Foundation Professor of Law and the Legal Profession in the ASU College of Law, became the university’s faculty athletics representative on July 1, 2004. He will serve a five-year appointment.

The position has three major components: 1) representing the university’s interest in helping ICA meet all ASU, Pac-10 and NCAA academic eligibility rules and regulations; 2) working closely with ICA’s Office of Compliance to monitor the eligibility of all of ASU’s 400 student athletes; and 3) helping the student-athletes make the most of their educational opportunities while at ASU.

“The goal is to connect with the athletic department to make sure there is not tension in this process,” he says. “We are all on the same page. We want our student-athletes to develop their gifts as athletes within an academic environment that promotes integrity and encourages success.”

Lynk, who has been at ASU since 2000, is a member of the Intercollegiate Athletics Advisory Board. He is also a member of the board’s Academic Committee. As faculty athletic representative, Lynk will remain on the board.

In the College of Law, Lynk teaches corporate law, civil procedure and professional responsibility and has written and lectured on federal civil procedure and federal election law compliance. He also is a faculty associate of ASU’s Center for the Study of Law, Science and Technology and chairs the American Bar Association's Special Committee on Bioethics and the Law.

Before joining ASU in 2000, Lynk taught at George Washington University and the University of Maryland School of Law and was a partner in the Washington, D.C., office of a national law firm, where he practiced in the areas of complex corporate transactions and commercial litigation. An elected member of the governing Council of the American Law Institute, Lynk received his law degree from Harvard Law School in 1976.

This position includes service as representative to the National Collegiate Athletic Association, the Pacific 10 Conference, the Western Collegiate Athletic Association, and advisor to the president. The representative is appointed by and responsible to the president.
