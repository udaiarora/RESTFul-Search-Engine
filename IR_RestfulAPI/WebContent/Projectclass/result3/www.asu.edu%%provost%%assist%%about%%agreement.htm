ASSIST AGREEMENT BETWEEN ASU AND PARTICIPATING COMMUNITY COLLEGES

Adoption Status

Arizona State University (ASU), acting on behalf of the Arizona Board of Regents for the purpose of building and maintaining the Arizona State System for Information on Student Transfer (ASSIST), and the county community college district signing this agreement, join together to develop a statewide student database to facilitate better course transfer articulation, develop a better understanding of the transfer process, assess the effectiveness of transfer policies, and report the completion rates of transfer students to state and federal governments and other interested parties.

      I.   ASU agrees to protect the privacy rights of the student records provided by the community college or university signing this agreement and to enforce applicable state and federal legal requirements.

     II.  ASU agrees to follow the provisions in the ASSIST Security Plan, as amended by the Academic Program Articulation Steering Committee or its designee.

   III.  ASU agrees to limit access to the student records provided by the community college or university signing this agreement to ASU employees directly involved in the ASSIST project. Other employees responsible for administering the computing/network infrastructure may access the database periodically in performing their duties. ASU assumes full responsibility for the actions of its employees in connection with the ASSIST project.

    IV.  The community college or university signing this Agreement agrees to follow the provisions in the ASSIST Security Plan, as amended by the Academic Program Articulation Steering Committee or its designee.

      V.  The community college or university signing this Agreement agrees to provide ASU accurate, student records within the timeframe and format requested by ASU.

    VI.   Either party may cancel this Agreement upon notification in writing to the Academic Program Articulation Steering Committee Chair.

  VII.   Notice is provided of A.R.S. sections 12-133, 12-1518 and 38-511.
