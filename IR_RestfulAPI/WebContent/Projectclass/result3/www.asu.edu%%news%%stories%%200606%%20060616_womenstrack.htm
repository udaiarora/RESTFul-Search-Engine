June 16, 2006

Women's track team completes 'triple crown'

The ASU women’s track and field team finished one of the most successful all-around seasons in school and NCAA history earning the "Triple Crown" of trophy finishes.

The team finished with trophy finishes (fourth place or better) in the three national championships during the 2005 – 06 season, becoming only the second women’s team to accomplish the feat. The team brought home a fourth place finish at the NCAA Outdoor Track & Field Championships, a third place finish in the 2006 NCAA Indoor Track & Field Championships and a fourth place 2005 NCAA Cross Country Championships held in November.

Senior Amy Hastings runs alongside teammate Victoria Jackson, and ASU graduate student, at the NCAA Track & Field Championships. The two runners, and sophomore Jacquelyn Johnson all won national championships this season.

“Our women's indoor/outdoor track and cross country team has completed a rather remarkable year,” says Lisa Love, ASU vice president for athletics.

The last time a women’s team finished with the “triple crown” was in the 1983-84 academic year for Tennessee. The other schools to hold this distinction are men’s programs Arkansas (13 times), UTEP (8), Stanford (3), Washington State (2) and Villanova (1).

The team also achieved notable individual success throughout the year.

ASU’s women won four national titles this year with sophomore Jacquelyn Johnson (pentathlon) and senior Amy Hastings (5,000m) winning indoor crowns before Johnson (heptathlon) and graduate student Victoria Jackson (10,000m) won outdoors. The four titles are the second-most in one season for the women, just one off the five earned in 1990 with two indoors and three outdoors.

Johnson joined an elite group of ASU track and field athletes when she won her third individual title, tying for the second-most individual crowns in program history and the third-most NCAA championships in a career. With her second heptathlon crown, she is tied for the second-most titles in the event in women’s NCAA history with four others.

Johnson’s outstanding efforts earned her honors as the 2006 U.S. Track and Field and Cross Country Coaches Association (USTFCCCA) Women’s Co-National Women’s Field Athlete of the Year and West District Field Athlete of the Year.

The honors and accolades for the team didn’t stop with the athletes, as numerous ASU coaches earned recognition.

Prior to the NCAA Championships, Greg Kraft was honored as the 2006 USTFCCCA West District Women’s Coach of the Year.

During the championship meet, the district and national assistant coach awards were announced with two Sun Devil staff members earning distinction. David Dumble was selected as the 2006 USTFCCCA West District Assistant Coach of the Year for women’s throws while Louie Quintana earned the same honor for women’s distances. Both were named the National Assistant Coach of the Year for their respective disciplines after their student-athletes were instrumental in helping the Sun Devils win their first Pac-10 title in school history as well as their third trophy finish of the season.
