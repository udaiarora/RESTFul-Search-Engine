EMPLOYEE BACKGROUND CHECKS

The Arizona Board of Regents is the governing body for the three Arizona public universities, including Arizona State University. Last year, the Board adopted policy, 6-709, “Mandatory Background Checks for Employees and Process for Hiring, Retaining, or Terminating Employees Convicted of a Felony Offense.” The policy requires background checks of all employees hired after July 1, 2005. ASU adopted ACD 126 in compliance with the Board policy. Under these policies, the university must obtain criminal history information on finalists for every position.

Educational history and references are handled by the by the hiring official or unit that is conducting the hiring process.

For criminal history information, ASU has contracted with Sterling Testing Systems, Inc. to conduct the criminal history check and provide the results in a report to the Vice Provost, not the hiring department or unit. The Vice Provost determines whether the unit needs to be advised if the report reveals information on criminal felony convictions. The criminal history report is maintained very confidentially and is not shared with the hiring unit.

A prior conviction does not necessarily bar employment, but failure to disclose this information could result in termination of employment. In the event of a felony conviction, the finalist will be subject to a fingerprint check, rather than a criminal history check.

Certain information is needed in order to conduct the criminal history check.

  • Social Security Number. ASU no longer uses the social security number for the employee’s identification number. Sterling will use the SSN only to confirm identity for purposes of completing an accurate check.

 

  • Date of Birth. The Age Discrimination in Employment Act of 1967, a federal law, and the Arizona Civil Rights Act, an Arizona state law, prohibit discrimination on the basis of age for individuals who or 40 years of age and older. The date of birth of an applicant is not shared with the hiring unit during the hiring process and is required on the consent form only to confirm your identity for purposes of completing an accurate check by Sterling.
  • Copy of the Sterling Criminal History Report (also referred to as a “consumer report”). The Fair Credit Reporting Act, a federal law, requires an employer to provide to the applicant any consumer or informational report that is provided by an external contractor (Sterling), if the report contains information that has a negative impact on the hiring decision. ASU does not obtain a credit report on applicants for faculty positions.

 

Security/safety sensitive positions
Some positions have been determined to be security or safety sensitive positions. Examples are employees holding positions that provide for direct access to minors who are not enrolled students (in child care programs or clinics or summer camps) or  employees who directly handle university funds. Finalists for such positions require a fingerprint check in lieu of a criminal history check.

If a finalist already has a fingerprint clearance card, or a national security clearance that ASU can confirm, or certain designated visas, a criminal history check is not required.

FAIR CREDIT REPORTING ACT, DRIVER’S PRIVACY PROTECTION ACT, and ANY APPLICABLE STATE STATUTE(S) NOTICE:

In accordance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act, information on applicants may only be used to verify a statement(s) made by an individual in conjunction with legitimate business needs. The depth of information available varies from state to state. The report that will be generated for employment purposes only and in compliance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act, the Driver’s Protection Act, and any applicable state statute(s). ASU does not obtain credit history on applicants for faculty positions.
