Arizona State System for Information on Student Transfer (ASSIST)
Status Report as of November 27, 2001

Background

ASSIST (formerly known as the Transfer Student Data Warehouse or TSDW) is a relational database containing enrollment records from Arizona's universities and community colleges. The purpose of ASSIST is to provide community colleges and universities information for (1) completing the IPEDS Graduation Rate Survey and (2) assessing transfer articulation effectiveness in Arizona.

There are two committees involved in the ASSIST project. The Steering Committee is a policy group responsible to the Academic Program Articulation Steering Committee (APASC) for the ASSIST project. This committee makes decisions concerning the use and content of ASSIST. The second group is the Technical Committee. They are a working group responsible for providing quality data and developing standard reports from ASSIST. The Steering Committee meets semi-annually in Spring and Fall. The Technical Committee meets as often as needed.


Current Status

The Steering/Technical Committees held the Spring meeting on March 30, 2001 at ASU West. One outcome of the meeting was an agreement to conduct a study session to address community college data quality issues in accordance with item seven of the ASSIST Project Deliverables. A Technical Committee meeting was held on June 14th, followed by two meetings of a volunteer subcommittee on July 12th and October 4th. A set of resolutions was prepared for presentation to the Steering Committee on November 16th, 2001. These resolutions included the following

1.

Identification of required data elements for federal and state reports.

2.

Development of a file format and file submission process to address performance reporting requirements under the 1998 Carl Perkins Vocational and Technical Education Act (CPIII).

3.

Recommendations for consistent reporting of open-entry/short-term enrollments.

The transition phase of ASSIST was completed over the summer with the hire of a new Technical Analyst, Daniel Moore, who joined the ASSIST staff on June 4th. Development of web reports has continued and templates for nine standardized reports, in addition to the Carl Perkins reports, are currently available for each of the community colleges. Institutional representatives with authorized accounts and query software can access these reports via the ASSIST web page (www.asu.edu/assist/).


Future Plans

The ASSIST Steering and Technical Committees will meet again on November 16, 2001. At that time, final approval of the set of resolutions will be requested. The ASSIST team will continue to refine the production model to enhance usability and provide assistance to ASSIST users as they begin designing and developing their own queries and reports. The primary focus for the immediate future will be to complete the development of a data dictionary and other user support mechanisms. In the meantime, all institutions are encouraged to submit their files for the 2000-2001 reporting cycle no later than February 1st, 2002.
