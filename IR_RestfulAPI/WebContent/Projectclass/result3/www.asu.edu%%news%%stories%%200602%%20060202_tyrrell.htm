February 2, 2006

Tyrrell named associate dean in Public Programs

The ASU College of Public Programs has named Tim Tyrrell, a professor in the School of Community Resources and Development, to serve as associate dean.

Tyrrell will assist the dean, Debra Friedman, with strategic initiatives in externally funded research and downtown Phoenix partnerships, as well as the management of student recruitment and retention efforts.

"Tim is a thoughtful, enthusiastic colleague, always ready to entertain a new idea, work collaboratively and create top-quality programs for students," Friedman says. "I look forward to working with him."

In August, Tyrrell accepted a position as professor in ASU's Tourism Development and Management Program in the School of Community Resources and Development, where he will continue to teach. He is leading the development of a bachelor's degree program in tourism development and management, the revision of the master's degree program, and the implementation of a new doctoral program in community resources and development. He also plans to create a center for metropolitan tourism research in downtown Phoenix.

Tyrrell has a bachelor's degree in mathematics from the University of South Florida, a master's degree in economics from the University of Tennessee and a doctorate in agricultural economics from Cornell University. From 1978 - 2005 he worked at the University of Rhode Island, where he earned the rank of professor.

Tyrrell succeeds Liz Segal, a professor in the ASU School of Social Work, who served as associate dean since 2004.

In July, the college will move from the Tempe campus to occupy the fourth through the ninth floors of the University Center, located at 411 N. Central Ave. in Phoenix, as part of the new Downtown Phoenix campus.

Palka, with the College of Public Programs, can be reached at (480) 727-6238 or (debra.palka@asu.edu).
