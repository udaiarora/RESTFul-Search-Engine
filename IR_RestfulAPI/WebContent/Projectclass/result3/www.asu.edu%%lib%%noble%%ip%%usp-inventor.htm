U.S. Patents
Searching By Inventor


Searching by the inventor's name for a U.S. patent depends on the time frame, as no one publication or database covers the complete set of patents. The following list of sources is arranged by the dates of coverage.


2001-present: US Patent Applications Database
1976-present: US Issued Patents Database
1920-present: Espacenet (European Patent Office)
1836-present: LexisNexis Academic
1790-present: Google Patents
1790-1873: Subject Matter Index of Patents


General Search Tip: Neither individuals nor databases are consistent with names. For example, John Joseph Smith may have his name listed one or more of the following ways:


Smith, J.
Smith, J. J.
Smith, J. Joseph
Smith, John
Smith, John J.
Smith, John Joseph


Be sure to search for all variant forms of the inventor's name when using the resources below.


2001 - Present


U. S. Patent Applications Database
http://www.uspto.gov/patft/index.html

The Patent Applications Database was started on March 15, 2001 and contains the patent applications for which inventors have given publication permission. As not every inventor gives this permission, this database is incomplete.


Enter the database by selecting "Quick Search" on the right-hand side of the screen under "Patent Applications"

To search for an inventor:
  1. Enter the inventor's last name in the "Term 1" box
  2. Change the "Field 1" box from "All Fields" to "Inventor Name"
  3. Select the appropriate year(s) in the "select years" box on the lower left
  4. For a more specific search, include the inventor's first initial or name in the "Term 1" box; enter the inventor's name as Smith-J$ or Smith-John$.
  5. Click on the Search button.


To search two co-inventors at once:
  1. Enter the first inventor in the "Term 1" box
  2. Enter the second inventor in the "Term 2" box
  3. Set both "Field 1" and "Field 2" boxes to "Inventors Name"
  4. Set the center box to "AND"
  5. Click on the Search button



To search for an inventor plus a subject or keyword:

  1. Enter the inventor's name in the "Term 1 box"
  2. Change the "Field 1" box to "Inventors Name"
  3. Set the center box to "AND"
  4. Enter the subject or keyword in the "Term 2" box
  5. The "Field 2" box should be kept on "All Fields"
  6. Click on the Search button


To view a patent:

  1. To view patents, click on the patent number or title (if given)

    Recent patents have both text and image versions of the patents. Text versions do not contain the drawings or pictures. Older patents have only the image version, no text version is available.

  2. To retrieve the image version, click on the button at the top or bottom of the text page

Notes:

  • Viewing the patent images requires a tiff-viewer plug-in.








1976 - Present


U. S. Issued Patents Database
http://www.uspto.gov/patft/index.html

The Issued Patents Database contains the images of patents from 1790 to the present, however, only the patents from 1976 to the present are searchable by the inventor's name.

Enter the database by selecting "Quick Search" on the left-hand side of the screen under "Issued Patents".

To search for an inventor:

  1. Enter the inventor's last name in the "Term 1" box
  2. Change the "Field 1" box from "All Fields" to "Inventor Name"
  3. Select the appropriate year(s) in the "select years" box on the lower left
  4. For a more specific search, include the inventor's first initial or name in the "Term 1" box; enter the inventor's name as Smith-J$ or Smith-John$
  5. Click on the Search button



To search two co-inventors at once:

  1. Enter the first inventor in the "Term 1" box
  2. Enter the second inventor in the "Term 2" box
  3. Set both "Field 1" and "Field 2" boxes to "Inventors Name"
  4. Set the center box to "AND"
  5. Click on the Search button



To search for an inventor plus a subject or keyword:

  1. Enter the inventor's name in the "Term 1 box"
  2. Change the "Field 1" box to "Inventors Name"
  3. Set the center box to "AND"
  4. Enter the subject or keyword in the "Term 2" box
  5. The "Field 2" box should be kept on "All Fields"
  6. Click on the Search button

1920 - Present


Esp@cenet: Europe's Network of Patent Databases
http://ep.espacenet.com/


Esp@cenet's Worldwide Database includes inventor information on U.S. patents back to 1920.


To enter the database, click on "Advanced Search" in the left column.

To search for an inventor:
  1. Keep the patent database set at "Worldwide"
  2. Scroll the search page down to the "Publication Number" search box and enter US with no spaces or punctuation
    This limits the search to U.S. patents only.
  3. Scroll the screen down to the "Inventor" search box
  4. Enter the inventor's last name;
    The inventor's first name may also be included, ex., Smith John. The most specific type of search uses quote marks around the inventor's name, ex., "Smith John" - be aware that this form of searching only finds the exact name and will not find variations.
  5. Click the gray Search box at the bottom of the page.



To search for two or more inventors:

  1. Scroll the search page down to the "Publication Number" search box and enter US with no spaces or punctuation
    This limits the search to U.S. patents only.
  2. Scroll the screen down to the "Inventor" search box
  3. Enter the last names with the word AND in between, ex., Smith AND Jones;
  4. Click the gray Search box at the bottom of the page.


To search for a subject or keyword along with an inventor:
  1. In the "Keyword(s) in Title or Abstract" search box, enter the subject(s) or keyword(s).
  2. Scroll the search page down to the "Publication Number" search box and enter US with no spaces or punctuation
    This limits the search to U.S. patents only.
  3. Scroll the screen down to the "Inventor" search box
  4. Enter the inventor's last name;
    The inventor's first name may also be included, ex., Smith John. The most specific type of search uses quote marks around the inventor's name, ex., "Smith John" - be aware that this form of searching only finds the exact name and will not find variations.
  5. Click the gray Search box at the bottom of the page.


To view a patent:
  1. Click on the title in the results screen
  2. Click on the original document tab on the bibliographic data page

Notes:

  • Viewing the patent images requires Adobe Acrobrat Reader.













1836- Present


LexisNexis Academic
Library Network Database


The publisher of this database does not advertize the fact that this database contains data prior to 1971 as the earlier content is not dependable. Searchers who need accurate results prior to 1971 are advised to use the other databases listed on this page and for 1845 -1919 to also consider searching the "Annual Report of the Commissioner of Patents", T223 .H3A3 Science Reference. Each volume of the "Annual Report" contains a listing of patents by inventors for a specific year. The "Annual Report" is also available in the "Serial Set" and/or the "U.S. Executive Branch Documents" microfiche collections; both of these collections are available in the Government Documents Department on the 3rd floor of Hayden Library.


Only the patents from 1971 to the present are indexed in detail. Therefore, only recent inventors may be searched using the "Guided Search Form" in which an inventor search can be specified. Older patents are only searchable by "keyword" -- consequently, some inventor searches may lead to inaccurate results when the inventor's name is also a subject or the name of a company. Additionally, many earlier patents have typing errors that could effect the search results.


These instructions show how to search the database for inventors using the keyword search; when searching for inventors for 1971 to the present, the "Guided Search Form" should be used, so that an inventor search can be specified.

Open the patent database by clicking on Legal Research in the left column. Scroll the next page down to the category "Patent Research" and click on "Patents".

To search for an inventor:

  1. In the keyword box, type the inventor's last name.
  2. Select the "To - From" type of date and insert the dates in the appropriate boxes.
    Several searches may need to be done to cover all applicable years as the system result limits are set at 1,000. If a first name must be used in the search to meet search limits, search both Smith John and John Smith; note that this search may not pull up all variant forms of the name.

To search for two or more inventors:

  1. In the keyword box, enter the last names with the word AND in between, ex., Smith AND Jones;
  2. Select the "To - From" type of date and insert the dates in the appropriate boxes.
    Several searches may need to be done to cover all applicable years as the system result limits are set at 1,000.

To search for a subject or keyword along with an inventor:

  1. In the keyword box, enter the last name plus the subject with the word AND in between, ex., Shackleton and Harness.
  2. Select the "To - From" type of date and insert the dates in the appropriate boxes.
    Several searches may need to be done to cover all applicable years as the system result limits are set at 1,000.

To view a patent:

  1. Click on the patent number in the results screen.
  2. Or, go to the U.S. Patent and Trademark Database and view the patent there.










1790-present

Google Patents

Beta site released in December 2006. Only issued patents are in the database and at the time of release only the first half of 2006 was covered. Use the Advanced Search page for author searching.

 

Note: there appears to be a results limit of approximately 1200 and less than 600 hits per search can be viewed. For common names and/or prolific authors, use the date search field to limit searches to shorter timer frames so more comprehensive results can be achieved.

 

A tiff reader is not needed to view the full text of patent, however, the reproduction of the patent on the Google site is inferior to that available at the USPTO. To print full text from the Google site, right click on the page image; each page must be printed separately.

1790 - 1873


Subject Matter Index of Patents Issued by the U.S. 1790-1873



Part of 19th Century Masterfile ASU Library Network Database

 

To search for an inventor:
  1. Set the "Series I" through the "Series IV" boxes to "Not Searched"
  2. Change the "Series V" box to "Subject Matter Index of Patents Issued by the U.S. 1790-1873"
  3. In the "Find" box, enter the inventor's last name
  4. Keep the "Field" box, immediately below the "Find" box, set to "Keywords (all fields)"
  5. Click on the gray Search button;
  6. From the Search Results List, click on the title for patents of interest
  7. To view the patent in the US Issued Patents Database, click on the URL

    Notes:
    • A fire at the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office in 1836 destroyed the patent collection; replacements were found for some patents but many were lost forever. Patents that are no longer available will have the notation "Full text destroyed in 1836 fire".

    • Viewing the patent images requires a tiff-viewer plug-in.
