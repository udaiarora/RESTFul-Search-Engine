Vision 2000


The vision of the Libraries in the year 2000 was developed first during a retreat of the Strategic Planning Council, then refined through an iterative process during which every member of the library staff had an opportunity for input:

In the year 2000, the customer is at the center of the enterprise and quality service is at the heart of our consciousness.

We serve a broad and diverse community. We are fully supportive of ASU as a Research I Institution, but we also help beginning students navigate the entire information quest process, coaching them toward the day when they manage information on their own.

The Libraries are a welcoming place -- a successful experience for customers. We have combined the feel of a mom and pop store (warm, inviting) with a busy, vital learning environment where customers use the latest information technology. We have removed significant barriers to customer success: they go straight to the resources they need, confident that the Libraries have the most current information. Our customers tell us that we treat them well.

We regularly assess customer needs, designing and improving services on the basis of these assessments. We have integrated people and technology in a superior services program that responds to and anticipates customer needs.

Many customers use our resources in a self-sufficient mode from their homes and offices, whenever and wherever they need them.

Our staff has high enthusiasm for and commitment to service. They are thoroughly trained and recognized for their superior achievements. They focus on quality and they make customer service decisions as close to the front lines as possible.

In the year 2000, we continue our efforts to build and preserve that part of the intellectual heritage for which we are responsible.

We focus our resources on collections that support our academic direction and research programs.

Our users and staff are much more aware of our collections.

We have developed new strategies for preserving collections--including the use of digitization.

We continue to fulfill our archival function.

In the year 2000, we are an agile, versatile organization, able to make the desired future happen.

There is unity of purpose throughout the organization that enables us to perform quickly and flexibly in response to new service demands. Front-line staff make significant service decisions because they understand our mission and goals thoroughly.

Cross-site and cross-functional teams plan -- and implement plans -- across organizational boundaries.

In the year 2000, the Libraries are a great place to work.

The objective of making this a great place to work is to achieve effectiveness in serving the customer through recruitment, training, and retention of staff.

We are a team, respecting our individual differences as well as our commonalities.

We promote shared leadership and shared decision making throughout the organization.

We regularly survey staff, identify problems, and work to resolve those within our sphere of influence.

We provide a work atmosphere which accords dignity, meaning, and a sense of community.
