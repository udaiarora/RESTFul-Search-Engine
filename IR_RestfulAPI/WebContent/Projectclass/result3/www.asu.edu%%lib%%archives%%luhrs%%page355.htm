While home for the 1972 Christmas holidays, Jean Stroud Crane and her family drove out to the mine (Bandon #1 and Bandon #2). Upon her return to the hotel, she informed George, Jr. that a fence had been erected, which said fence enclosed the land, including the mine. She found the gate open and drove up to a new house which she thought might be on the Luhrs' land. She had a general talk with the man living there and then spent considerable time walking around the Luhrs properties.


Daniel M. Wier of Wier Engineering Company was asked to make a survey of the Luhrs' mining claims, which was done in June, 1973 at a cost of $655.00. Later, Mr. Wier went out to the mine with George, Jr. and they went over the land together. The new house was not on the Luhrs' property.


Stanley Tanner had an interest in the adjoining property. The fence also enclosed some state property. The County road, which ran by one corner of the Luhrs' property had been relocated too. Stanley said he had mules on the land and the fence was built to keep the "Hippies" out. Tanner wanted to buy the Luhrs' mining claim, but the Luhrs' did not want to sell.


During the Christmas holiday of 1973, Jean and her family and George, Jr. went out to the mine. The gate was locked. Jean and her family crawled over the fence and walked all around the Luhrs' property.
