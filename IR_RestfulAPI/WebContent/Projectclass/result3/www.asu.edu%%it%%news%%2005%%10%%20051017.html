New Look For DARS

An end-to-end transformation for the degree audit system has kept university technical staff busy for a year, but a seamless transition is expected when students see the results in October.

The degree audit is an automated report that matches courses a student has completed with the requirements of a particular academic degree program. Students can request copies of their degree audit reports by logging in at the ASU Interactive Web site and selecting “Request/View Your Degree Audit (DARS)”. The new system will return audits immediately, so students will no longer have a time delay.

The newest version of the Degree Audit Reporting System (DARS) provides bar charts and pie chart graphics that help students view their academic progress in a quick and easy-to-read way. Another new DARS feature that students will likely find appealing is the ability to expand or collapse course lists. The lists can now be viewed or printed by category in an outline format, rather than as one long printout.

The updates to the system are just the most visible part of a complete overhaul of the DARS application by its developers at Miami University. Since the new system displays information in a Web interface that runs in Windows, the application itself is now referred to as DARWin.
