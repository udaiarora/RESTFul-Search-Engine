September 4, 2003

New residential hall means full-service campus for West students

Until two years ago, ASU West served junior, senior and graduate students as a commuter campus. ASU West admitted its first freshman class fall 2001 and now offers a complete undergraduate curriculum. With the fall 2003 opening of Las Casas, the campus's first residential facility, ASU West has completed its transformation into a full-service campus.

The 400-bed Las Casas facility features innovative programming, including ASU West classes and workshops, tutoring, academic advising and counseling offered on-site. Special groups are being formed for students interested in topics including the arts, technology, ecology and leadership. As an academic resource for students, two academic advisers will live in Las Casas.

Several ASU West professors are designated as faculty mentors, teaching classes on-site and serving as advisers to the special interest groups.

Las Casas residents also will have the opportunity to participate in workshops focusing on topics such as conflict management, relationship building, life planning, stress reduction and time management.

"Having professors, tutors and advisers available in the residence hall will help students living in Las Casas adjust quickly to the academic and social demands of university life," says ASU West student Kelsie Marcum, who served on the task force that developed plans for Las Casas programming. The task force also included faculty and staff representatives.

"Creating a living-learning community in Las Casas is a logical extension of the learning community concept we have implemented for freshman students at ASU West," says Emily Fourmy Cutrer, dean of the College of Arts and Sciences. "Learning communities help students see connections among disciplines while giving them an academic support system. These principles have been extended to residents of Las Casas."

Furnished, apartment-style units at Las Casas feature full kitchens and free cable and local phone service. "The Hub" community center includes a standard classroom and computer classroom as well as a gathering area for interest group and social meetings. Las Casas amenities also include an outdoor swimming pool.

For information about Las Casas, call (602) 543-CASA (543-2272) or visit (www.west.asu.edu/reslife).

Crum, with ASU West Public Relations, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or at (matthew.crum@asu.edu).
