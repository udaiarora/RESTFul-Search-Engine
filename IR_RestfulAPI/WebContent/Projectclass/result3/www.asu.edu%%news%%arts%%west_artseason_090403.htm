September 4, 2003

Art season showcases variety of performances

ASU West's Department of Interdisciplinary Arts and Performance (IAP) will bring an impressive blend of performances to Valley audiences during its 2003-04 Artistic Events season. With events that integrate theatre, music, visual art and performance mediums of all kinds, the IAP Department continues to make its mark on the Valley's artistic scene.

"This year promises to be one of the most interesting, with some exciting new artists and performances mixed with the return of what truly are becoming West Valley traditions," says Associate Artistic Director Jeff Kennedy.

The fall begins with Christopher Haines performing in the off-Broadway hit "Underneath the Lintel," a new play by Glen Berger, which will be staged Sept. 10 - 14, in ASU West's Second Stage West. Directed by IAP faculty member Charles St. Clair, the work follows a librarian who attempts to solve how a book is returned 113 years overdue.

Next on the schedule is the third annual "Shakespeare Under the Stars," featuring Valley high school students and guest artists performing under the Arizona night sky at 7 p.m., Sept. 27. Admission to "Shakespeare Under the Stars" is free.

The culmination of a "Verbal Art Festival" is a performance of "Verbal Art" created by guest artist Amy X and other guests along with IAP students and faculty at 7:30 p.m., Nov. 7.

The Phoenix Symphony returns to its West Valley home under the baton of Robert Moody when it presents "All Britten," featuring the brilliant music of 20th century British composer Benjamin Britten. This first of two symphony performances at ASU West is set for 7:30 p.m., Nov. 21.

The spring 2004 semester begins with the second annual "West Side Poetry Jam," featuring area poets and musicians performing hip-hop poetry and soulful music at 7:30 p.m., Jan. 24 as part of ASU West's Martin Luther King, Jr. Week celebration.
The Phoenix Symphony presents a "Classical Connections" concert at 7:30 p.m., March 11. The program, titled "From Bach to Rock," walks through the music of the ages and finds connections to our present time.

The season's finale is a production of the unique Broadway musical James Joyce's "The Dead," based on Joyce's famous short story set at an annual holiday gathering in Ireland. The production will feature performances by ASU West students and will be directed by IAP faculty member Jeff Kennedy. Performances will be March 25 - 28 and April 1 - 4.

For event information, to purchase tickets via credit card or to be placed on the events mailing list, call the ASU West Arts Info Line at (602) 543-2787 or visit the IAP Department Web site at (www.west.asu.edu/iap).

All events will be held on campus at ASU West, 4701 W. Thunderbird Road in Phoenix. Campus event parking is $2.

Crum, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or (matthew.crum@asu.edu).


2003 - 04 performances at ASU West

ASU West's Department of Interdisciplinary Arts and Performance plans to host the following programs at campus venues for the 2003 - 04 Academic Year:
"Underneath the Lintel," Sept. 10 - 14, Second Stage West
"Shakespeare Under the Stars," Sept. 27, Delph Courtyard
"Verbal Art," Nov. 7, Kiva Hall
"All Britten," Nov. 21, La Sala Ballroom
"West Side Poetry Jam," Jan. 24, 2003, Kiva Hall
"Classical Connections," March 11, La Sala Ballroom
"The Dead," March 25 - 28 and April 1 - 4, Second Stage West
