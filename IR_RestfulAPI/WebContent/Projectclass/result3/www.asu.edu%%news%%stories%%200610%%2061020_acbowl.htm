October 20, 2006

Four teams bowl into second round

Quarterfinals await Academic Bowl winners

The first round of the Academic Bowl ended Oct. 19, with four more teams earning the right to advance into the competition’s quarterfinals.

The eight competing student teams in Group B answered a variety of questions in front of a boisterous crowd in the Memorial Union’s Pima Room on the Tempe campus.

Student Stephanie Green (far right) from the Mary Lou Fulton College of Education enjoyed the competition and the support and encouragement of her family. Her family, from left Sarah Green, Jeff Green, Tom Green and Denise Green, loudly and visibly made their presence known. The Fulton team won their round against the School of Global Management and Leadership 330-40.

Four other teams had previsouly advanced after the first night of competition Oct. 12. The quarterfinals will be held Wednesday, Oct. 25.

Fans of the various teams showed their pride with signs such as, “Teachers Rule” and “Teachers are gonna beat ya!” Others snapped pictures of the teams on the set with camera phones and digital cameras.

The teams also got into the excitement with matching uniforms: stylish black-and-white outfits, business shirts, Hawaiian shirts and even shirts bearing the likeness of Walter Cronkite.

As an added twist, the College of Design Team even took the approach of holding pregame and halftime huddles, just like a football team.

The first match of the evening featured the Mary Lou Fulton College of Education, with team members Jessica Dailey, Stephanie Green, Michael Stouffer and Nathaniel Hudson, against the School of Global Management and Leadership, with Hillary Feher, Hayley Jackson, Tanya Moushi and Trevor Stephens. The Fulton team advanced with a 330-40 victory.

The next match pitted the Morrison School of Management and Agribusiness, with Andrea McBurney, Todd Ulery, Chris Davis and Mike Neider, against the College of Human Services, with Eric Johnson, Holly Huntimer, Ruth Faulkner and Gunnar Burgeson. Morrison took the match 255-85.

The College of Design team, Jeremy Mudd, Lauren Romero, Michael Avila, huddle during a question. The team lost its opening round against the W. P. Carey School of Business, 255-135.

In the third contest, the College of Design, with Doreen Zhao, Michael Avila, Lauren Romero and Jeremy Mudd, took on the W. P. Carey School of Business, with Joseph Favre, Chuan Xiong, Alex Porter and Derek Guizado. The W. P. Carey team earned the win, 255-135.

The final match of the night featured College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Team B, with Jimmy Scanlon, Mike Rockwell, Josh McCormick and Eli Bliss, versus the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communications squad, with Meghan Getz, Kyle Gad, Justin Adams and Sam Gavin. CLAS B earned the final spot in the quarterfinals with a 210-110 victory.

The four teams will join the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences, University College and the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Team A in the quarterfinals Oct. 25. The competition will take place beginning at 7 p.m. in the Memorial Union’s Pima Room.

The semifinals and finals will be held Nov. 6 in the Eight/KAET-TV studios on the Tempe campus.

More information, and a complete listing of the teams involved, can be found online at www.asu.edu/news/academicbowl/about.html.
