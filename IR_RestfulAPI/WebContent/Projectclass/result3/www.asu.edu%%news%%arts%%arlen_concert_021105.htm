February 11, 2005

Concert celebrates ‘Wizard of Oz’ composer

Feb. 15 marks the 100th anniversary of the birth of great American innovator Harold Arlen. Though his name may not be a household name, his songs evoke memories of childhood, Americana and the Cotton Club heyday of jazz.

Arlen composed the score to “The Wizard of Oz,” wrote tunes such as “Ac-Cent-Tchu-Ate the Positive” and worked for four years at the famed Harlem Cotton Club. Throughout it all, he “wasn’t as well-known as some of us, but he was a better songwriter than most of us,” according to no less an authority than Irving Berlin.

In celebrating the composer’s birth, pianist Michael Kocour and friends will perform famed Arlen works, such as “Stormy Weather,” “It’s Only a Paper Moon,” “Over The Rainbow” and “Out of This World,” in a concert at 7:30 p.m., Feb. 15, at ASU’s Katzin Concert Hall.

Kocour, who has been hailed by the Chicago Tribune as “one of the most sophisticated pianists in jazz,” has performed at the 1992 World’s Fair in Seville, the Jazz Showcase in Chicago and the Blue Note in New York.

Kocour will be joined onstage by ASU faculty members Sam Pilafian and Mike Crotty. Special guests, such as bassist David Friesen, drummer Joel Spencer and vocalist Deater O’Neill, also will perform.

Tuba player Pilafian, perhaps best known as a founding member of the internationally renowned Empire Brass Quintet, also has recorded and performed with the Boston Symphony Orchestra, the New York Philharmonic Orchestra, the Orchestra of St. Luke’s, the Metropolitan Orchestra, the Duke Ellington Orchestra, Lionel Hampton and Pink Floyd. Crotty plays trumpet, flugelhorn, saxophone, woodwinds and keyboards. He has performed with Tony Bennett, Joe Williams, Dizzy Gillespie, Ray Charles, Natalie Cole, Diana Ross, Maureen McGovern, Mary Chapin Carpenter and the Temptations.

Guest artist Friesen is a riveting bass soloist who also has performed with jazz greats such as Stan Getz, Dexter Gordon, Dizzy Gillespie, Chick Corea and Jack Dejohnette.

Spencer, who as a guest artist completes the rhythm section, also has performed with internationally known jazz artists and is a faculty member at Northwestern University School of Music.

O’Neill, who will provide the evening’s well-known lyrics and melodies, is an acclaimed performer and has been heard on National Public Radio, as well as Warner Brothers Publications.

Tickets to the concert can be purchased at the door for $5, or in advance by calling (480) 965-6447.

Bell, with the Herberger College of Fine Arts, can be reached at (480) 965-2817 or (rebecca.bell@asu.edu).
