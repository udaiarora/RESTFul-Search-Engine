Recalls 

 

What is a "Recall"?

A "recall" is a request to obtain an item that is checked out to another customer. The recall may change the due date of the item and the library will inform the customer who has the item checked out of the new due date.

 

What Can Be Recalled?

Not all items qualify for recall. Check with circulation for complete Recall Policy information.

  • Most items checked-out from the general stacks collection may be recalled.
  • You will be notified when Recall items are returned (for faster notification, be sure to set up your email on your library account). When viewing your account online, these items will have a status of Ready For Pick-Up
  • Items that are marked "Shelf" can not be requested unless they are for delivery from another campus.
  • Material from the Law library is not requestable online.

 

How to Recall Items

  1. Search the online catalog for items.
  2. Once an item is located, click on the button.
  3. Sign into your library account with your ASURITE ID and password
  4. Select the most convenient library Pick-up location.
  5. Scroll down and click button.
  6. Select the item to be requested (there may be more than one copy/location). Then click on Request Selected Item button..
  7. Make sure that you receive the confirmation message: Your request for this item has been sent to the library.

 

Recall Policy

Items that are checked out may be requested by other borrowers at any time.  All items are subject to recall.  Due to the strict enforcement of the recall process, failure to receive a recall notice does not absolve borrower from knowing an item is recalled.  It is important for all borrowers to regularly review the status of their library account, checking for recalls either online or by contacting circulation.  Borrowers should also select the best method (print or email) for receiving notices, appropriately manage their email accounts and SPAM software, and/or keep mailing addresses valid, including checking departments if on payroll.  If there are other requests at the time of checkout, no notice is generated and the borrower is given an automatic 14-day, “no renewal, no fine free period” checkout.  This recall policy applies all year long: breaks, sabbaticals, vacations, etc.  Recall of an item may shorten the original loan period.  Items not returned by midnight on the recall due date are billed the replacement cost and a $10.00 fee per item.  Recall late fees are strictly enforced and only the replacement cost is cancelled when the item is returned.  If the library is closed, use the 24-hour bookdrop.

All borrowers are guaranteed a minimum 14 day loan for items from the general collection. When an item is recalled, the system automatically changes the due date and a Recall Notice is sent.

 

It is the library’s policy to use campus addresses for all individuals on Human Resources payroll. You can set up your library account to have Library Notices be sent by E-mail.

 

Patron account information is confidential and may not be shared.

 

Provided that staffing is available, up to three (3) recalls may be requested by telephone.

 

There is an established written appeals procedure for handling all library charges, including recall fines. Clearances, allowing check out of material, are issued while the appeal is in process. For complete information, please contact:

University Library (Tempe campus) Billing (480-965-3106) or
West campus Library Billing (602-543-8546)

 

At any time, you may view your own account or check with Circulation to see if any of the books that you have requested are ready for pick-up or if any of the books that you have checked out have been recalled from you. The University Libraries strictly enforce the Recall Policy.
