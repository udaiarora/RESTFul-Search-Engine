May 13, 2003

AZ ranks among top job growth states for 10 straight years

Arizona is the only state to rank among the top 10 job growth states for the entire 10-year period between 1993 and 2002, according to the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

Recently released numbers from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics show a clearer overview of Arizona's 2002 job growth picture, according to Lee McPheters, contributing editor. "Although the Arizona economy was subpar for all of 2002, the final ranking of states based on employment growth has Arizona in ninth position, the same ranking as in 2001. For the year as a whole, the preliminary number of -1.6 percent (a loss of 38,000 jobs) now has been revised. The revised numbers show no employment loss for the year."

Throughout the nation, the job growth picture has been a gloomy one. While the Grand Canyon State managed to outperform 41 other states in 2002, McPheters noted, "the bad news is that we experienced no growth at all in overall employment."

The latest figures available on job growth, for February of this year, show Arizona ranked in seventh position among all states, with growth of 1 percent. Hawaii is the top-ranked job-creating state for the month, followed by Nevada.

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark surveyed the consensus panel on the outlook for the construction industry. A majority believes that residential construction will continue to hold its own, with low to moderate growth for the remainder of this year through 2004. "Residential construction tends to follow population flows and interest rates, which should remain relatively favorable," Clark writes. On the other hand, the recession and its attendant business spending and employment doldrums have contributed to a less robust outlook for commercial and industrial construction.

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. For information, call (480) 965-7858.
