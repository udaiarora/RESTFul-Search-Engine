January 27, 2005

Fulton symposium tackles improvements in education

ASU’s College of Education will present the first Mary Lou Fulton Symposium on Jan. 27, featuring Gloria Ladson-Billings, who is the Kellner Family Professor of Urban Education at the University of Wisconsin-Madison and president-elect of the American Educational Research Association.

The event will encourage in-depth exploration of issues related to improving educational achievement and outcomes for pre-kindergarten through 12th-grade students, particularly those from culturally diverse or disadvantaged circumstances. It is free and open to the university community, education colleagues within the local pre-kindergarten through 12th-grade community and other interested members of the public.

Ladson-Billings’ address will take place at 4 p.m., Jan. 27, at the Hobbs Family Stadium Club at ASU’s Sun Devil Stadium. A reception will follow her presentation.

Ladson-Billings’ research examines the pedagogical practices of teachers who are successful with African-American students. She also investigates critical race theory applications. She is the author of the critically acclaimed books “The Dreamkeepers: Successful Teachers of African-American Children” and “Crossing over to Canaan : The Journey of New Teachers in Diverse Classrooms,” and many journal articles and book chapters.

She is the former editor of the American Educational Research Journal and a member of several editorial boards. Her work has won numerous scholarly awards, including the H.I. Romnes Faculty Fellowship, the Spencer Post-doctoral Fellowship and the Palmer O. Johnson Outstanding Research Award. In 2002, Ladson-Billings was awarded an honorary doctorate from Umeå University in Umeå , Sweden and in 2003-2004 was a fellow at the Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences at Stanford University.

Ladson-Billings also is the 2004 recipient of the George and Louise Spindler Award for ongoing contributions in educational anthropology, presented by the Council on Anthropology & Education of the American Anthropological Association.

Sherwood, with the College of Education, can be reached at (480) 965-2114 or (joan.sherwood@asu.edu)
