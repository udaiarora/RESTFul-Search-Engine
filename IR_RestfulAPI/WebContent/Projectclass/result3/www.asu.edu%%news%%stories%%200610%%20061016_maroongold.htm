October 17, 2006

Maroon & gold fever hits ASU

Block Party offers cure for even the biggest Sun Devil fan

Got the fever? ASU has the cure. Get your Sun Devil fix during ASU's 2006 Homecoming festivities Oct. 21.

The day begins with the annual Homecoming parade, where more than 100 unique floats and special guests will march with pride down University Drive between College and McAllister avenues. The parade kicks off at 10 a.m. with a military flyover. Infinity Broadcasting is offering the parade's entertainment, with live radio broadcasts from 94.5 KOOL FM, 101.5 ZONE FM, and KMLE Country 107.9 FM.

Before the Sun Devils and the Stanford Cardinal kick off at 12:30 p.m. at Sun Devil Stadium, ASU Homecoming guests can rally for the game at the annual Block Party, which features nearly 40 tents of food, music, activities and interactive displays from various ASU colleges and units. This year's Block Party features the Johnsonville Big Taste Grill, a semi-truck-sized barbecue grill cooking up fresh bratwursts, along with new sidewalk café tents that offer the best seating for the parade. Here are the highlights you won't want to miss:

? The Barrett Honors College at ASU is orchestrating an open-house atmosphere with Barrett students discussing highlights of the honors experience, including sustainable living program, community service, studying abroad and the Barrett student organization. Barrett students also will showcase their individual talent and engage in fun games and face-painting activities with children who participated in the Barrett Summer Scholars Program, a new summer program for Arizona 's most gifted and talented young students.

? The College of Liberal Arts and Sciences is featuring more than 30 schools and departments, providing something for everyone. The college's newest schools will be represented, along with unique science demonstrations, glass-blowing, a wind wagon, meteorite displays, interactive games, hands-on arts and crafts and the featured ?Lucy.? The college also is hosting its third annual Alumni Book Fair, with readings and signings by prominent alumni authors, and is offering food and beverages from Grilled Expeditions and Hensley.

? The Native American Alumni Association is showcasing Native American dancers, arts and crafts demonstrations, a children's activity area and a fry bread stand.

? Eight/KAET-TV, Phoenix 's PBS affiliate housed at ASU, is featuring the infamous Curious George character at its Block Party tent, along with treats for children and videos of special programming.

? The Hispanic Business Students Association (HBSA) is featuring home-cooked carne asada, along with live entertainment from HBSA Alumni DJ Joe Dean and the SRP Second Shift Band. Proceeds from carne asada sales benefit the HBSA Endowed Book Scholarship fund.

? Intercollegiate Athletics ( ICA ) is playing host to a student-athlete village and kids zone on Palo Verde Beach. Student-athletes and coaches from ASU's athletics programs will be on hand to take photos and sign autographs. The kids zone features several inflatable games, including a bounce house, slide and football toss.

? The Downtown Phoenix campus is playing host to a tent jointly with the Provost Office, the College of Public Programs , the College of Nursing & Healthcare Innovation, University College and the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication. Visitors are encouraged to stop by the tent and celebrate maroon and gold fever with ASU's newest campus. Alumni will gather to celebrate reunion classes and meet this year's College of Public Programs Hall of Fame inductees.

? The Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering welcomes alumni and guests to meet the new executive dean, Paul Johnson, and celebrate the school's 50th anniversary. The tent features a brunch for guests, which is $4 with R.S.V.P. and $8 without. R.S.V.P. at (480) 965-0289.

? The Mary Lou Fulton College of Education encourages guests to join more than 50 preschool students and their families in an interactive tile-painting activity. A breakfast buffet and beverages will be served free of charge to the first 100 guests. The college's interim dean, Sarah Hudelson, plans to greet alumni who are coming back to ASU to celebrate Homecoming.

? Office of the President and Office of Public Affairs is jointly playing host to a tent to meet community leaders and faculty. ASU President Michael Crow, Elizabeth Capaldi, the university's provost, and other ASU administrators plan to meet and greet tent guests.

? Parents are invited to visit the Parents Association tent for food, drinks and camaraderie.

? The Polytechnic Campus welcomes alumni, students, faculty, staff and friends of the campus to stop by and enjoy free breakfast and water from Chick-Fil-A, along with several giveaways sponsored by College of Science and Technology, East College, the Morrison School of Agribusiness and Management, and the School of Education Innovation and Teacher Preparation.

? Club ASU is hosting 500 junior high school students across Arizona , including Tempe , Chandler , Show Low, El Mirage and Tohono O'Odham Nation. Club ASU members will participate in a scavenger hunt through the Block Party with the help of volunteers and chaperones.

? The ASU Alumni Association is conducting 10-year reunions during Homecoming week for the classes of 1996, 1986, 1976, 1966 and 1956, with campus tours, a Friday night social, Block Party activities, parade participation, game attendance and Sunday brunch.

? The Down Syndrome Network is recognizing October as Down Syndrome Awareness Month by playing host to the seventh annual Buddy Walk of Central Arizona along University Drive and College Avenue from 9 a.m.-noon. Visitors are encourage to stop by the DS Network booth for information on services for people with intellectual disabilities, Buddy Walk shirts, sensory tables for the kids and free food from Chili's for walkers.

? ASU Public Events and Tempe Convention & Visitors Bureau (CVB) is showcasing an Italian-themed tent to promote ?The Light in the Piazza,? an upcoming Broadway show at ASU Gammage.

? The ASU Retirees Association is hosting a tent as a gathering place for people to meet and relax during the Block Party and parade. Refreshments will be served.

? University Student Initiatives will host ?Welcome to ASU? and ?Welcome Back to ASU? tents, with informative displays, music, snow cones, popcorn and several giveaways.

? West campus colleges and WESTMARC (Western Maricopa Coalition) are hosting alumni, students, faculty, staff and community members. Tent attendees can enjoy front-row seats for the parade from the tent site, and enjoy activities such as Sparky bingo and face painting. All the activities and giveaways are provided by the College of Human Services , the College of Teacher Education and Leadership, the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences, and the School of Global Management and Leadership, along with eight alumni chapters. Brunch is served free to the first 500 people who R.S.V.P. at (602) 543-5300.

? The W. P. Carey School of Business is cooking up a barbecue lunch while offering a meeting place for business school students and alumni to see old friends and network with new ones.

Other tents at the Block Party include: ASU University Administration and Legal Affairs; City of Tempe; ASU Bookstore; APS Alumni; Office of Research & Economic Affairs; College of Design; Delta Zeta; Theta Tau; and ASU Libraries.

For more information, visit www.asu.edu/homecoming.
