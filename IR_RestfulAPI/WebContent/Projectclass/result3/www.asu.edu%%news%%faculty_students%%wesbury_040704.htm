April 7, 2004

Stuart A. Wesbury Jr. Awards highlight business of health care

Professor Emeritus Stuart A. Wesbury Jr. of the School of Health Management and Policy has witnessed the development of the health care industry in the United States from multiple perspectives over his 50-year career.

The W. P. Carey School of Business honored him April 1 by inaugurating the Stuart A. Wesbury Jr. Awards and Lecture, an annual awards program that highlights a leader in the health care industry and an outstanding graduate student at an event that provides a forum for the latest ideas in the industry.

Roger A. Hughes, executive director of St. Luke's Health Initiatives, received the Stuart A. Wesbury Jr. Community Leader Award. Hughes has headed St. Luke's Health Initiatives since its inception in 1995. The foundation focuses on promoting health in Arizona, primarily in Maricopa County, through community grants, medical assistance to vulnerable populations, research and education.

"Roger Hughes has successfully guided St. Luke's Health Initiatives in its mission to be a 'catalyst for community health,'" says Jeffrey Wilson, director of the School of Health Management and Policy. "The school is proud to be closely linked with this powerful community resource and its leader."

Troy W. Maxfield, who graduates in May with a W. P. Carey MBA and a masters in health sector management, received the Stuart A. Wesbury Jr. Outstanding Student Award.

The first Stuart A. Wesbury Jr. Lecture was delivered by Brian Wesbury, son of the professor, a prominent economist nationally.

Brian Wesbury is the first vice president and chief economist for Griffin, Kubik, Stephens & Thompson, Inc., a Chicago-based investment bank.

Farquhar, with the W. P. Carey School of Business, can be reached at (480) 965-7774 or (liz.farquhar@asu.edu).
