Our Department At A Glance


Administration & Management personnel

The Manager of Telephone Services organize and direct a staff of 21 technicians, operators, business office personnel, and technical support people. They are responsible for budgeting and overall management of internal and external departmental relationships.

Business Office personnel

This unit serves as the department’s main contact for customers for services such as faxing, pagers, cellular telephones, and calling cards. This unit processes all Telephone Services Request (TSR) forms, and also handles both accounts payable and accounts receivable for the department.

Installation & Maintenance personnel

Personnel in this unit are responsible for adds, moves, changes, and repairs of digital and analog telephone sets and related equipment. Such equipment includes jacks, cabling, and terminals on all four campuses and many other locations utilizing University dial tone.

Switchboard Services personnel

Provides general operator services for University related business.

  • Answers incoming calls to the ASU general number
  • Provides telephone number assistance to callers for campus numbers, students, State of Arizona employees, as well as numbers for nearby hotels for visitors to the University.
  • Processes conference calls
  • Updates listings for Qwest directory assistance
  • Provides TDD/ TTY service for hearing-impaired callers

Techincal Support personnel

The two primary functions of this unit are:
1) system-wide programming, including some installation, and
2) telephone station-specific programming, for adds, moves, changes, and repairs. Personnel in this unit consult with departmental representatives to design, modify and upgrade call handling applications and specific telephone set requirements to suit their specific needs by making appropriate recommendations and implementing effective solutions. This unit’s staff is also available to provide education and training for end-users on the various features of the phone system and equipment; and is responsible for the 911 emergency interface with the ASU Police Department.
