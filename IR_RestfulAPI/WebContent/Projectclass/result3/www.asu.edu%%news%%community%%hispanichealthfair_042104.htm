April 21, 2004

Students leaders coordinate health fair to educate Hispanic women

Hispanic and African-American women face the highest risk of death from heart disease and stroke, but they have the lowest risk factor awareness of any racial or ethnic group, according to an American Heart Association survey.

ASU's Hispanic Mother Daughter Program (HMDP) is doing its part to bring awareness to this and other health issues affecting Hispanic women, by hosting "Nuestra Salud-Health & Wellness Fair," from 9 a.m. to 2 p.m., April 24, at the Mesa Centennial Conference Center, located at the intersection of University and Center. The fair is free and open to the public.

"We would like the women to take away with them all the knowledge that they can," says Virdiana Jimenez, a senior microbiology major and one of the fair's organizers. "We would like them to know that they are not alone and that we are here to help them as well as others."

The HMDP is designed to increase the number of Hispanic women who complete a four-year college degree, through a team approach, which directly involves mothers in the educational process of their daughters.

The Health & Wellness Fair was organized by HMDP program members, who participated in a youth leadership-training course called "Youth as Facilitative Leaders" in the fall of 2003. During that training, the students came up with the idea of hosting a health fair for Latina women that would provide useful information and resources.

"The students wanted to come up with a project that they felt addressed the issues facing Hispanic women in the community," says Claudia Martinez, eighth grade program adviser for HMDP. "After several brainstorming and action planning sessions, the students decided to put together a health fair focusing on the issues that affect Latinas most."

There will be a number of local organizations represented at information booths, as well as presentations and discussion groups on health related topics throughout the day. Entertainment and activities for children will also be scheduled.

For information about the "Nuestra Salud-Health & Wellness Fair," call the Hispanic Mother-Daughter Program office at (480) 965-5838.

Romero, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 727-3116 or (mlromero@asu.edu).
