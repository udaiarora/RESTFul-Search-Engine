Introduction (current document developed 1995, revised 1998)

Public Law 93-288, as amended by Public Law 100-707, "Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act," November 23, 1988 - The Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act, P.L 93-288 as amended, provides an orderly and continuing means of assistance by the federal government to state and local governments in carrying out their responsibilities to alleviate the suffering and damage that result from disasters. The State of Arizona Revised Statutes 26-301 through 26-353 provides for the preplanning and preparation necessary to carry out emergency functions, other than functions which the military forces or federal agencies are primarily responsible to prevent or minimize the loss of life or property caused by disasters of every kind. Procedures to accomplish the above mentioned programs are outlined in the Federal Response Plan, dated April, 1992, as amended, and the State of Arizona Emergency Response and Recovery Plan, dated 1998. Arizona State University (ASU) Emergency Plans and Procedures complement state and federal authorities.

The plans written at each level for the appropriate laws, and this plan written for ASU in particular, are designed to address the consequences of any disaster or emergency situation in which there is a need for response assistance at any and all levels of government. This plan is applicable to natural and man-made disasters and other incidents requiring assistance under the aforementioned Acts.

This document is the ASU Emergency Operations Plan (BOP) for major disasters that occur on university property. Emergency response to any major disaster will be conducted within the framework of this plan, and any exceptions or changes to the outlined procedures must be approved by the university President or designee before implementation. The plan is designed to effectively coordinate the use of university and community resources in order to protect life and property immediately following a major natural or accidental disaster on the ASU campus.

The plan is divided into two primary sections: Pre-Emergency Planning and During an Emergency Operation. It is placed into operation whenever an emergency affecting the campus cannot be controlled through normal channels. The emergencies addressed in this plan include, but are not limited to:

  • Aircraft Accident
  • Bomb Threat
  • Civil Disturbance
  • Earthquake
  • Evacuation Fire
  • Flood
  • Hazardous Materials Incident
  • Power Outage
  • Severe Weather

This EOP does not include procedures for a war-related emergency. War-related emergency procedures for ASU are contained in separate city of Tempe and Maricopa County plans. The plan and procedures describe the basic structures and mechanisms by which resources will be mobilized and activities conducted to augment ASU and other local response efforts.

This plan serves as the foundation for the further development of detailed procedures to enable efficient and effective implementation of response activities. Each university unit will develop its own emergency operation procedures which will be added as part of this overall plan as completed.

Purpose

The purpose of the EOP is to:

  1. Protect the lives and property of university personnel by establishing functional guidance that provides the procedures necessary for immediate response of ASU agencies and departments to a campus disaster.
  2. Establish a mutual understanding of authority, functions, and responsibility.
  3. Provide step-by-step instructions to university personnel for emergency response.
  4. Provide for the orderly maintenance or restoration of academic and residential programs and services.
  5. Identify the university's role for mutual aid to local, state, and federal agencies.

Responsibility

The EOP is under the executive control of the university President (or designee) - the Emergency Policy Executive (EPE) - and is managed and directed by the Director of the ASU Department of Public Safety (DPS) - the Emergency Manager (EM). In the event of a major disaster, the EPE will govern by emergency proclamation and will impose all necessary regulations to limit loss of life and property and preserve the peace and order of the university, which may include but is not limited to:

  • Imposing curfews in all or portions of the campus. Suspending regular academic activities
  • Closing public access to any building, street, or other public area
  • Calling upon regular or auxiliary law enforcement agencies and organizations within or outside of ASU
  • Requesting additional mutual aid such as federal assistance or Red Cross, and
  • Procuring mitigation funds for designated projects.

Any delegation by the university President of the EPE responsibility and control will be done in writing. The original letter of delegation will be maintained by the office receiving the letter. Copies of the letter of delegation will be provided to: Tempe Fire Department, Tempe Police Department, Arizona Board of Regents, ASU Department of Public Safety, and other offices of the university as deemed appropriate. In the event of a major disaster, the EOP will be implemented in one of the following ways. If telephone services are operational:

  1. The EM will activate the EOP and will open the Emergency Operations Center (EOC). (See Section 3, "Emergency Operations Center.")
  2. ASU DPS Dispatch Center will immediately begin calling the Emergency Policy & Operations Group (EPOG) members and advise them of the nature of the emergency and the location of the designated EOC (if the primary EOC :is not operational). (See Section ii, "Emergency Personnel & Phone Numbers.")
  3. EPOG members will immediately report to the designated EOC.

If telephone services are not operational:

  1. EPOG members will go to the primary EOC as soon as they are aware of an imminent disaster or that a major disaster affecting ASU has occurred. If for some reason the primary EOC is unavailable, members should go to the second and then the third designated EOC sites.
  2. If EPOG members do not respond to the EOC within a reasonable amount of time, ASU DPS officers will be sent either to their offices or homes to inform them of the situation and the EOC location.
  3. EPOG members will then immediately report to the designated EOC.

Off-Hours Emergencies

In the event an emergency occurs before or after regular university hours, or on a holiday or weekend when most departments and offices are closed, the structure of this plan remains the same, however, its implementation may vary depending upon available resources and staffing. Until the time that authorized officials can be notified, the highest ranking individuals who are available at the time of the emergency will assume emergency operations responsibility. These individuals should follow the guidelines of this plan, while simultaneously making an effort to reach EPOG members.

The university President (if unavailable, then the next highest ranking individual) will proclaim a campus emergency under the following circumstances and in the following manner.

An emergency situation is or is likely to become beyond the control of the campus's response agencies, and the efforts of the city, county, or state are required. (A declaration of a campus emergency should not be confused with a declared State of Emergency by the Governor, although they may coincide.) Once an emergency is declared, the university President (or designee) orders activation of this plan. External indications of emergency status can include:

  • The Governor of Arizona declares a State of Emergency that affects the state of Arizona, and the university President (or designee) transmits this information and orders the appropriate university response.
  • A county emergency is declared by the Chairman of the Board of Supervisors, and the university President (or designee) transmits this information and orders the appropriate university response.
  • A city of Tempe emergency is declared by the Mayor or City Council, and the university President (or designee) transmits this information and orders the appropriate university response.

The university President (or designee) should contact the EM and the Public Information Advisor (PIA) to develop a public emergency declaration statement, as well as a plan for its delivery.

Scenario 1

Date: September 7, 2002 Time: 6:55 p.m.
Location: Sun Devil Stadium

In remembrance of Sept. 11, ASU scheduled numerous events revolving around the game to be played on Sept. 7, 2002.

The field size American Flag will be displayed and a fly over will be done by jets from Luke AFB

At 6:55 p.m., as the jets are doing a fly over, an external fuel tank is dislodged from the right wing of a jet. It falls into the east seats and impacts the Loge above and section 34. There is a large explosion which causes secondary fires. Numerous people on the entire east side are affected by the explosion. Most notably are patrons in the student sections and handicap area's located at the top of each section. Electrical wires are exposed from the loge level and are hanging into the sections below. Debris closes the lower moat blocking patrons and emergency crews from entering or leaving out the bottom of the section.
