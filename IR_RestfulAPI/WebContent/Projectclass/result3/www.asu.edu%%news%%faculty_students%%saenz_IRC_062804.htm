June 28, 2004

Saenz to lead ASU's Intergroup Relations Center

Delia Saenz, is the new director of ASU's Intergroup Relations Center (IRC), effective July 10. Saenz, an associate professor of psychology, says she hopes to advance the IRC's research efforts when she begins her new role.

"ASU has the potential to be a leader in this field, given the scholarly strengths that exist on campus and the potential outreach to similar units across the country," she says. "I am also very excited about the opportunity to work collaboratively with different academic and service units on campus in an effort to infuse topics related to intergroup relations and diversity into the classroom curriculum."

Saenz will be joining IRC staff members Kris Ewing, assistant director; Eva Fatigoni, Thomas Walker and Kathleen Wong Lau, program coordinators; and Sherrie Loomis, administrative assistant.

The Intergroup Relations Center, established in August 1997, is actively involved in campus-based and national research initiatives that focus on work, life and learning climates and the effectiveness of structured interaction learning communities for the purposes of improving and disseminating best practices across the campus, higher education and other communities.

"The center has a responsibility - as does every other unit on campus - to foster a positive climate for students, faculty, staff and administrators, Saenz says. "It has a dynamic team and a solid foundation on which to build."

Saenz has worked hard to promote positive relations within ASU's diverse community since she first came to ASU as an untenured assistant professor in 1989.

Since her tenure, Saenz has served as director of graduate studies in the psychology department, director of the social psychology program and previously as interim associate dean of the Graduate College. Saenz also has served on many committees including the Academic Senate, Intercollegiate Athletics Board, Student Financial Assistance Board and the Southwest Borderlands Initiative Steering Committee. Saenz served as president of ASU's Chicano Faculty & Staff Association, and as a spokesperson for the Diversity Alliance.

Saenz says all of her experience as a faculty member has allowed her to grow professionally, and she doesn't plan on giving up her love of teaching when she joins the IRC.

"I see myself expanding my university citizenship in a way that channels my contributions to a larger arena," she says. "I love teaching too much to abandon it altogether. The plan is simply to teach in different ways and with different audiences."

For more information about the Intergroup Relations Center, call (480) 954-1574.
