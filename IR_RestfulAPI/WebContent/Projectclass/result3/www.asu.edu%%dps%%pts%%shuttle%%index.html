SHUTTLE UPDATES - JANUARY 2007

As campus and City of Tempe construction continues, please be aware of these changes to ASU shuttle services. Every effort is made to keep the ASU community informed by e-mail, notices in Insight and postings on our webpage. If we can be of any assistance, please let us know by contacting TRPDEPT@asu.edu

USB SHUTTLE
Due to the traffic restrictions at the intersection of Mill Avenue and University Drive through March 15, the USB shuttle will follow its regular route west on University and will then turn north on College Avenue. The ASU Foundation stop will be located at the first bus bay on the east side of College. The shuttle will then continue north to Fifth Street, west on Fifth to Mill, and will then resume its regular route.

The restrictions will cause setbacks in the timing of the USB Shuttle. At this time we are unable to estimate the delays; however, we should have approximate times available by the end of next week. We will continue to use the current schedule, although the timing on the stops may be delayed.

DOWNTOWN PHOENIX/TEMPE SHUTTLE
The shuttle stop at the University Center, 411 N. Central Ave., has been moved one street south, from First Street and Taylor Street to the southwest corner of First and Polk Street. The sign has been relocated.

MCALLISTER SHUTTLE
Despite construction due to the South Campus Residential Community development project, the McAllister Shuttle will run its regular route, picking up in the northern region of campus at Lot 59N and serving stops along the McAllister Avenue corridor to south of Apache Boulevard. It will turn east to Sonora and then head north. The new stop is in front of Sonora Center. This location is the closest proximity to Spence Path that allowed for shuttle access and maneuvering. After stopping at Sonora Center, the shuttle will then head west to McAllister. The drive that will be used to return to McAllister is now a two-way road and will be shared with vehicles, bikes, pedestrians and carts. Please use caution in this area and drive slowly.

FLASH TO UNIVERSITY
As a result of the construction at the intersection of Mill Avenue and University Drive, FLASH to University will adhere to the following detour:

Regular route south on Mill to Fifth Street; west on Fifth to Ash Avenue; south on Ash to University; east on University to resume regular route at Mill.


Flash

FLASH (Free Local Area SHuttle) service is approximately every 10 minutes Monday - Friday.

FLASH is a great way to get around at ASU particularly for the many students who park in Lot 59. FLASH Back (counter-clockwise direction) and FLASH Forward (clockwise direction) offer a convenient transportation alternative to get you where you need to go at ASU and Downtown Tempe.

We also offer the FLASH to University Drive which travels west on Rio Salado Pkwy. to Mill Ave, south on Mill Ave to University Dr, east on University making 4 stops only along University Dr. then heads back up Stadium Dr. to Packard.

For more information call (480) 965-1072.

 

McAllister Shuttle

McAllister Shuttle runs from 6:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. Monday - Friday. Initial start is on Packard Drive north of Rio Salado Parkway at 6:00 a.m. and a second bus to follow at 6:15 a.m.

The McAllister Shuttle will travel from Lot 59 North on Packard Drive to 6th Street to Veteran's Way which becomes McAllister, south on McAllister, will stop at all the Flash stops will continue south of Apache on McAllister, circle around South Campus, then north back up McAllister to Lot 59 North.

Frequency is approximately every 15 to 20 minutes depending on traffic and detours.

Public Transit

On July 1, 2005, ASU began distribution of the ASU U-Pass, which will allow access to Valley Metro bus routes for free for eligible to ASU faculty, staff and students. The 2006 fall semester bus pass is valid July 1, 2006 to Jan. 16, 2007. The spring semester bus pass is valid Jan. 17 though June 30, 2007.

Students at the Tempe and all eligible participants at the West and Polytechnic campuses may pick up their bus passes at their respective campus parking offices (Decal Sales Office at 525 S. Forest for Tempe campus). A valid ASU ID is required to receive the pass.

Tempe campus faculty and staff may pick up their passes from the ASU Commuter Options Office. A valid ASU ID is also required to attain bus pass.

For more information on the ASU U-Pass Program contact the ASU Commuter Options Office at (480) 965-1072.
