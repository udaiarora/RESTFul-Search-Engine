May 3, 2006

Outstanding educators earn Centennial Professorships

Three outstanding professors have been honored by the Associated Students of ASU with the 2006 - 2007 Centennial Professorship Award. This year's honorees are:

. Jennifer Tsukayama from the Department of Dance in the Herberger College of Fine Arts.

. Robert Edsall from the Department of Geography in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

. Orde Kittrie from the Sandra Day O'Connor College of Law .

ASASU commended Tsukayama for her efforts with the Dance Arizona Repertory Theatre (DART), Edsall for his realistic and proven dedication to the community - and his ability to demonstrate why geography matters - and Kittrie for his work as a teacher, as well as his contributions to Arizona's Hispanic community.

This year, the Centennial Professorship committee received 34 nominations and 20 applications.

The $10,000 award is meant to compensate, recognize and encourage excellent teaching and leadership in and out of the classroom, and to recognize community service.

The conditions of the award, which is given to "rising stars," are that half of the amount be used to help students. Appropriate uses include hiring teaching assistants or improving laboratory equipment. The other $5,000 can be used as the recipient wishes.

Each recipient also is asked to give two public lectures.

For more information about the award, contact Thomas Turner, the committee's ambassador and chairman, at (310) 869-7273.
