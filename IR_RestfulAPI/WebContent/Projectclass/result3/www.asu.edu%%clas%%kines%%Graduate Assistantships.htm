GRADUATE ASSISTANTSHIP

Graduate Assistantships are available each year through the Department of Kinesiology for students admitted to both the Master of Science (MS) and the Ph.D. program in Kinesiology.

 An assistantship for a Ph.D. student is half‑time (50%) and involves 20 hours each week of research and teaching responsibilities.

 The annual stipend for this graduate assistantship is currently $13,540.

 An assistantship for a Masters student at half‑time (50%) involves 20 hours each week of teaching and research responsibilities.

 The annual stipend for this graduate assistantship is currently $12,450.

 For the academic year 2006‑07, the value of this benefit for students enrolling in 7 or more hours is $5,930 for residents and $16,516 for nonresidents.

 TAs/RAs with half‑time appointments are not charged tuition.

 The university covers the cost of individual health insurance premiums for TAs/RAs with half‑time appointments.

 The value of the health insurance premium for 2006‑07 is $1,264.

--------------------

An assistantship for a Masters student at one‑third time (33%) and involves 13 hours each week of teaching and research responsibilities.

 The annual stipend for this graduate assistantship is currently $8,575.

 TAs/RAs are considered to be residents for tuition purposes.

 The value of this benefit for nonresidents is $10,586 for the 2006‑07 academic year.

 In addition, TAs/RAs with .25‑.49 FTE appointments receive a 50% reduction in the resident portion of their tuition.

 Based on the general graduate tuition of $5,930 for the 2006‑07 academic year, the value of this benefit for students enrolling in 7 or more hours is $2,965.

These merit‑based awards are available through the Division of Graduate Studies (DGS), although some may require you to apply through your department:

 * ARCS (Achievement Rewards for College Scientists)

The ARCS Foundation is dedicated to helping meet our country's need for scientists and engineers by providing fellowships to academically outstanding students who are United States citizens in need of financial assistance to complete their higher education, thereby contributing not only to the advancement of science, but also to the material and intellectual welfare of all people. Students must be nominated by their academic units.

 * DGS Dissertation Fellowships are competitive university‑wide fellowships designed to support highly meritorious doctoral students who are in the final stages of post‑candidacy doctoral work.

 * DGS Doctoral Enrichment Fellowships are competitive university‑wide fellowships designed to enhance recruitment of highest quality doctoral students who will contribute to the achievements of diversity in our graduate student population. For further information, please contact your academic program.

 * Reach for the Stars Fellowships are competitive university‑wide fellowships designed to promote the diversity of the graduate student population by providing ASU's most promising undergraduate students with a pathway to graduate school and the tools and resources for their success.

 * Tripke Scholarship. The Earl A. and Lenore H. Tripke Professional Workshop Travel Awards provide up to $800 to support doctoral students who have been invited to attend prestigious professional development workshops. These are not intended to be national or international conferences for which regular DGS travel support is already available.
