November 8, 2004

University economists honor Seidman Institute’s Timothy Hogan

Timothy Hogan, who recently retired as director of the L. William Seidman Research Institute at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business, was honored by his peers from university economic research centers across the United States.

Dr. Hogan received the inaugural Thayne Robson Award at the annual fall conference of the Association of University Business and Economic Research (AUBER) in Tucson. The award, to be presented every other year, honors the late Thayne Robson, past president of AUBER.

“Through the years, Tim has been a tireless supporter and promoter of AUBER,” said Marshall Vest of the University of Arizona ’s Eller College of Business, outgoing president of AUBER. “In addition to serving as president and chairing the Committee on Relationships with Other Organizations, Tim has been a frequent presenter and session organizer at the AUBER conferences.”

Dr. Hogan retired in June after 34 years of service to ASU. He is now a professor emeritus with the Department of Economics, and he continues to assist the Seidman Institute with special projects on an as-needed basis.

“The criteria for the Thayne Robson Award embody the spirit of a man who was a friend and mentor to many in the organization,” according to award presenter Richard Wobbekind of the University of Colorado at Boulder . The recipient must have been a research center director and a member of AUBER’s board of directors; an economic and public policy leader in their state; and served the goals of AUBER by exhibiting selfless, exceptional and long-term commitment to the organization.

At the AUBER conference, the Seidman Institute’s Center for Business Research was honored with an Excellence in Publications award for its monthly newsletter, AZB/Arizona Business. This was the third AUBER award for Seidman publications in recent years.

W. P. Carey School of Business

The L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University is an affiliation of six research centers that serves as a link between the local, state, national and international business communities and the creative and intellectual resources of the nationally ranked School of Business.
