August 14, 2003

July resale market reflects economic uncertainty of early 2003

The Greater Phoenix resale housing market slowed in July - not unexpectedly, as the recorded activity reflected home-buying decisions made amid the economic and political troubles of early 2003. There were 6,320 sales recorded in July, down from the 7,890 sales of the previous month, but ahead of last year's 6,210 sales.

"Typically, the combined recorded activity in the months of June and July account for slightly over 20 percent of the year's recorded sales," said Jay Q. Butler, director of the Arizona Real Estate Center. "Even with the slowdown, 2003 year-to-date continues at a record pace, with 41,200 sales in comparison to 36,190 for 2002 and 37,725 for 2001."

The median home price continued strong with $160,000, up from $157,500 in June and $147,000 a year ago. Since the Greater Phoenix area is so large, the median price can range significantly from $369,500 in North Scottsdale to $95,000 in the central western areas of the city of Phoenix. For July 2003, 51 percent of all recorded sales were for homes priced from $100,000 to $175,000 and 15 percent for homes priced over $300,000.

"This recording activity represents the end point of a decision-making process that probably began in late 2002 or early 2003," Butler said. "During this timeframe, there were concerns about the continuing weak economy and concerns about the war in Iraq, so the slowdown in market activity should be expected. However, the continued strong levels of activity show that people have maintained a strong confidence in their live-in investment. Since rates continued to fall in the early months of 2003, many people were afforded the opportunity to economically satisfy their housing and/or investment dreams."

The 1,125 resale townhouse/condominium housing transactions represent a slowing of market activity from the 1,505 units recorded sold the previous month, and the 1,225 in July 2002. For 2003 YTD, the activity stands at 8,505 units, an increase over 7,885 homes for 2002 YTD. "The strong increase in the median price from last year's $95,000 to the current $105,000 could be a reason for the slowdown in these sales," Butler noted.

The median area for a single-family home recorded sold in July was 1,705 square feet, an increase over the 1,660 square feet reported a year ago. In the townhouse/condominium sector, the median square footage was 1,175, which is larger than the 1,155 square feet reported a year ago.

· In comparison with July 2002 figures, recorded sales in the city of Phoenix fell from 1,785 to 1,645 sales, while the median sales price increased from $119,900 to $130,000. Since Phoenix is a geographically large city, the median prices can range significantly - from $87,500 in the Sky Harbor area to $200,000 in the Union Hills area. The townhouse/condominium sector also declined, from 345 to 265 sales, while the median price rose from $84,000 to $92,550.

· The Scottsdale resale home market remained stable at 670 recorded sales. The median sales price continued its rise, from $312,000 to $324,950. The median resale home price is $369,500 in North Scottsdale and $166,500 in South Scottsdale. The townhouse/condominium sector in Scottsdale also was stable at 255 sales, while the median sales price increased from $136,500 to $158,000.

· The Mesa resale housing market continued to improve from 720 to 790 sales, while the median price increased from $134,000 to $145,925. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 180 to 190 sales, while the median home price rose from $88,000 to $94,900.

· Glendale moved from 550 to 580 recorded sales, and the median sales price increased from $134,000 to $144,250.The townhouse/condominium sector declined from 70 to 50 sales, while the median sales price increased from $72,000 to $79,500.

· In comparison to a year ago, the Sun City resale market declined from 145 to 130 sales, with the median sales price increasing to $119,000 from $104,000. Resale activity in Sun City West also declined from 100 to 70 recorded sales, but the median sales price rose from $130,500 to $151,200.The townhouse/condominium market in Sun City improved from 75 to 80 sales and the median sales price increased from $75,000 to $80,750. In Sun City West, sales remained at 20, and the median sales price increased from $87,000 to $107,500.

· The resale market in Gilbert grew from 360 to 450 sales, while the median sales price increased from $168,000 to $175,000. The townhouse/condominium market was stable at 20 sales, while the median sales price increased from $108,500 to $119,500.

· For the City of Chandler, the resale market improved from 515 to 570 recorded sales, while the median sales price increased from $151,500 to $165,000. The townhouse/condominium market fell from 75 to 50 sales and the median sales price decreased from $104,900 to $102,000.

· The resale market in Tempe moved from 205 to 195 sales, with the median sales price rising from $158,250 to $173,000. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 80 to 100 sales, while the median sales price increased from $99,000 to $106,560.

· The highest median sales price was in Paradise Valley at $923,325, with a median square footage of 4,055.

· In the West Valley, the following communities represent 6 percent of the resale market, while it stood at 5 percent for a year ago:
o Avondale - up from 80 to 90 sales; median price up from $133,875 to $145,000.
o El Mirage - up from 35 sales (median price $106,000) to 40 sales ($119,900).
o Goodyear - stable at 80 sales; median price increased from $155,000 to $165,000.
o Surprise - up from 100 sales ($132,000) to 140 sales ($140,000).
