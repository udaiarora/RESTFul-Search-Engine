Librarians' Council

C.1:Standing Rules

Effective 12/20/89 .. Revised 11/29/93

Rule 1. Regular Meetings

Regular Meetings of the Council are at 3:00 p.m. on the third Wednesday of each month from September to May, unless cancelled by action of a previous meeting or by the Executive Committee.

Rule 2. Special Meetings

Special meetings of the Council may be called as needed. Notice is normally to be sent to the members at least eight days in advance. However, if the Chair or the Executive Committee deems it necessary to call a meeting on shorter notice, this may be done provided that: a) notice is delivered to the desks of all members at least twenty-four hours in advance, and b) the meeting, when a quorum is convened, votes as its first item of business to waive the requirement for longer notice.

Rule 3. Nominations

  1. Nominations for any vacancy are to be made at the first meeting after a vacancy occurs or is known to be imminent.

    2.  
  2. Nominations for Academic Senator are to be made at the February meeting.

    3.  
  3. Nominations for officers and committees of the Council are to be made at the April meeting.

    4.  
  4. Nominations from the floor (including self-nominations) are always accepted, but to be printed on a ballot must be supported by a second.

    5.  
  5. Nominations are subject to withdrawal by the nominee.

Rule 4. Elections

  1. Ballots are to be sent to the members within four days of the meeting at which nominations are made.

    2.  
  2. Eight days are to be allowed for ballots to be returned.

    3.  
  3. If a runoff election is required, the number of candidates listed on the ballot is to be limited to twice the number to be elected, plus any candidates receiving a number of votes equal to the last one thus included.

    4.  
  4. Ballots must be constructed and addressed so that when returned they are anonymous.

    5.  
  5. Ballots shall be counted by a meeting of the Committee on Nominations and Elections with a quorum (majority) present.

    6.  
  6. After ballots are counted, they shall be deposited with the Secretary of Council who shall keep them for one year, after which they may be discarded.

    7.  
  7. The Committee shall make a reasonable effort to notify all candidates of the results of the election before any public announcement is made.
