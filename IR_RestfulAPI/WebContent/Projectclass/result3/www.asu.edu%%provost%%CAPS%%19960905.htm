Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

September 5, 1996

1. The minutes of the July 25 meeting were approved with the following correction: Cristina Gonzalez.

2. Christine West has been appointed Chair-Elect, Patricia Etter will take over as Archivist.

3. The committee continuted discussion of the status of the revision of ACD 507-05. Gary contacted former CAPS members Sandra Bray, Michael Douglas and John Wheatley and has put together a history of the document∆s revision. The document was finished and ready for final approval in 1993. It was tabled at that time because the ABOR was working on a revision of the AP Conditions of Service which would have superceded the ACD document. When the ABOR document was tabled, apparently permanently, the ACD revision was not readdressed. The committee has decided to send a letter to Provost Glick, copied to Vice-Provost Harris, outlining the history of the ACD 507-5 revision, with copies of previous letters attached. Provost Glick will be encouraged to bring this issue to closure and invited to attend a meeting with the Vice-Provost and the two AP committees.

4. Gary, as chair of CAPS, will call a meeting of PACAPS. They will be invited to attend the October 3 CAPS meeting. At this meeting PACAPS will be encouraged to elect a chairperson and secretary in order to set to committee in motion. We will also outline an agenda for a meeting between the two committees and Provost Glick and Vice-Provost Harris.

5. CAPS will also invite all university Academic Professionals to attend our October 31 meeting to meet their representatives, voice any concerns and work with the committee to set an agenda for the year. The committee is considering making this an annual event and including it in the bylaws as such.

6. Karl presented first draft revision of the CAPS bylaws. After general discussion it was decided that we would all review the document and discuss it again at the next meeting.

7. Academic Professionals as sole chair of graduate committees: Michael Douglas has encouraged the committee to continue questioning the Graduate School policy of not allowing Academic Professionals to act as sole chairs for graduate student supervisory committees. Even though he was told that the Graduate Council would look into this, there has been no response since Mike contacted them September 1, 1995.

8. Gary will revise last year's welcome letter to all campus APs; it will be included in a mailer with the invitation to the open meeting.
