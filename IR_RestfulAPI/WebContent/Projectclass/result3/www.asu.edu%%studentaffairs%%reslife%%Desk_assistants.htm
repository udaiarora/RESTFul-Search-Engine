Desk Assistants (DAs) are customer service representatives at a 24-hour residence hall front desk. The front desk serves as a primary source of information and service for residents.

What do Desk Assistants do?

DAs are familiar with all desk procedures, i.e. the telephone, filing, key checkouts, equipment checkouts…
DAs are pleasant, friendly, and helpful during any interaction with residents, visitors, and staff members
DAs play an important role toward establishing a positive environment for all who live and work in the hall
DAs contribute to a safe and secure residence hall environment
Requirements

Have a minimum cumulative and semester GPA of 2.75 at time of application and while holding the position.
Undergraduate students must be enrolled in a minimum of 12 credit hours per semester
Graduate students must be enrolled in 6 credit hours
Accept employment for a nine-month period beginning with August training and concluding at the end of spring semester in May
Strong customer service, teamwork, phone and administrative skills are required
This position may require working weekends, early mornings, nights and holidays
The position requires approximately 10-20 hours per week
Be in good standing with Residential Life and Student Life.

Who is an ideal candidate?
Someone who…

  • Possesses great communication skills
  • Exhibits appropriate behavior
  • Is a great team player
  • Is knowledgeable of basic office skills
  • Has worked in a team environment
  • Has worked with multiple phone lines
  • Is able to give direction and guidance to residents of the hall
  • Is familiar with university life
  • Has a great sense of professionalism
What’s in it for you?
Desk Assistants will gain transferable skills in leadership, customer service, communication, administration, and overall professionalism.
Desk Assistants also experience increased interactions with students, connections with residential life staff for mentoring and professional development opportunities.
They also contribute to the success of resident students, and gain increased access to campus and community resources and networks.
  As a Desk Assistant, depending on shift needs and availability, you may be asked to work additional shifts at the following pay rates:
  • $5.15/hr: 8 a.m. to 10 p.m.
  • $6.15/hr: 10 p.m. to 2 a.m.
  • $7.15/hr: 2 a.m. to 8 a.m.

Recruitment Process:

Interested applicants need to complete and submit a Desk Assistant Application. Applicants should reference the checklists that are included in the downloadable application. Fall or spring completed applications should be submitted to any of the residential halls on the Tempe campus. Summer applicants should call the Residential Life office.

For any questions please contact us by calling or e-mailing 480-727-0881 or recruitment.reslife@asu.edu


Selection process

The selection process for fall positions take place each year in January ending in April. April is when candidates are notified of their selection status for the following academic year. We may also conduct smaller selection processes for our available spring and summer positions. Please contact the recruitment office for timeframes and deadlines at, 480-727-0881 or recruitment.reslife@asu.edu.

Training

Desk Assistants will need to return to the halls in early August of each semester and in early January to attend training.

  • DAs will learn about customer service principles
  • DAs also learn residence hall and desk operations
  • DAs also learn how to work with students, residence hall staff, and other university staff members
