September 3, 2003

Working Group continues to evolve

Last year ASU, already one of the nation's largest universities, experienced an enrollment increase of some 5,000 students. As ASU increasingly becomes the school of choice for Arizona students, enrollment figures are climbing again for the coming year, with significant increases expected on the Main, West and East campuses.

To manage this extraordinary enrollment growth, the university intends to build out the West, East and Downtown campuses, which will give ASU a true multi-campus single university complexity.

In the last year, the university launched one of the largest building campaigns in its recent history with construction under way or shortly to begin on the first buildings of the Arizona Biodesign Complex, the new School of Life Sciences building and residence halls.

ASU has also increased its commitments to improved student services, assisting regional economic development and providing greater social outreach, particularly in the area of pre-K-12 education.

Consequently, as the university's new administration moves into its second year, President Michael Crow has made several modifications to the President's Working Group, the senior academic and administrative officers.

Following a successful national search, Juan Gonzalez was named to the vacant vice president for Student Affairs post. Gonzalez, who had been vice president for Student Affairs at Georgetown University, will focus on campus life, residential life, student wellbeing and health and other areas that impact student success and overall experience.

The position of associate vice president for Student Affairs was eliminated, and James Rund, who had held that post, will now lead the new Office of University Undergraduate Initiatives as vice president for University Undergraduate Initiatives.
Rund will have crosscutting responsibilities, working closely with all administrative areas to manage enrollment growth, selective admissions, diversity and achievement at the school level on all ASU campuses.

To focus university resources on bolstering Arizona's public schools, Eugene Garcia, dean of the College of Education on Main Campus, was given the additional responsibilities of vice president for university-school partnerships, a new part-time position that also is part of the President's Working Group. In this university-wide role, Garcia will ensure that all segments of ASU are engaged in these vital pre-K-12 efforts.

President Crow has also made an addition to his office staff, appointing James O'Brien senior advisor to the president. O'Brien will be responsible for implementing complicated strategic and tactical objectives of the Office of the President and will serve as a bridge between the president's office and the rest of the university.

O'Brien was most recently senior attorney at Electronic Data Systems Corporation in Ames, Iowa. He also served as corporate counsel to Unigraphics Solutions, Inc., and Engineering Animation, Inc., and was in private legal practice with the firm of Dickinson, Mackaman, Tyler & Hagen P.C., all in Iowa. He was a policy analyst in the Office of Science & Technology Policy at Iowa State University and a research analyst in the Iowa State House of Representatives. He has also worked for the U.S. House of Representatives. He received his B.A. with distinction from Iowa State and his juris doctorate from the Drake University School of Law.
