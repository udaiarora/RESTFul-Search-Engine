March 3, 2003

Campus honors Carey during naming ceremony

The W. P. Carey School of Business embarked on its renewed drive for excellence during a historic dedication ceremony Feb. 26 that drew more than 700 guests to honor benefactor Wm. Polk Carey.

Sheltered from the wind under a tent on the Student Services lawn, ASU President Michael Crow and W. P. Carey School Dean Larry Penley talked about their expanded visions for the university and the school in the context of Carey's $50 million gift from the W. P. Carey Foundation. The gift is the largest single gift in the history of ASU. At the close of the ceremony, Crow, Penley and Carey unveiled the new school logo.

"We have been incubating a good business school here in the last few years, and we are now moving forward with Mr. Carey's support to build a great business school," said Crow. "It's a process - not just to replicate the business schools on the east and west coast, but to embrace the values and creativity that William Polk Carey and W. P. Carey & Co. represent. This is an exciting day - it is the beginning of the process."

Crow also made special note that the school's students, faculty and staff all played an important role in getting the school to this point.

Penley said Carey's gift affirms the hard work that has taken the school to its current level, and will enable the school to increase further its rate of improvement.

"To William Polk Carey I offer you what I will not live to hear: thank you, from our children, our grandchildren and our great grandchildren, for all you gave in your generosity to higher education and especially to ASU."

Carey is chairman of W. P. Carey & Co. LLC (NYSE: WPC), a New York City-based investment firm. He is also the grandson of John Samuel Armstrong, who, 118 years ago on Feb. 26 filed the legislation that created the school that became ASU. His gift continues a personal and family tradition of supporting higher education that has benefited schools such as Johns Hopkins University, the University of Pennsylvania, and Princeton University as well as ASU's colleges of Law and Business.

Carey recalled a close association with the university that began 30 years ago with the dedication of Armstrong Hall at the College of Law in honor of his grandfather - John Samuel Armstrong. He said he hopes ASU will adopt the motto of his company - doing good while doing well.

"It's very possible to be the kind of business person who leaves the world a better place," he said.

The W. P. Carey School is well positioned to produce that kind of graduate, he added.

"We are blessed with a superb faculty," said Carey. "The school will become a source of economic growth, success and prosperity for Arizona."

Crow underscored the importance of building an excellent business school to the success of both the university and the state.

"Metropolitan Phoenix is the seed for the great city it could become, and part of the genetic code of a great city is a great business school," Crow said. "Bill Carey is the kind of person I am certain was standing at the table when this country was being formed, asking what he could do to make it happen. With this gift, in this place, in this undone city, we are making history."

During the ceremony, speakers were announced by representatives of the various components of the W. P. Carey community: Rodel Scholar and honors student Elizabeth Edwards; Professor Beth Walker, the State Farm Distinguished Scholar; and Andrew Stefl, president of the W. P. Carey Staff Development Group.

Athletic Director Eugene Smith presented an autographed ASU football helmet to a smiling Carey. Crow offered a bowl carved from an ironwood tree grown in Tempe, and Dean Penley presented a crystal sculpture engraved with the date of the renaming.
