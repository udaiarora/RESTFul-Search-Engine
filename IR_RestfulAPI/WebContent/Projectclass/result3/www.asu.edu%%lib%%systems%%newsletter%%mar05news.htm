LIST, Access Services Staffers Receive President’s Award for Innovation – Again!

We have just received word that the team that created the Libraries’ Video Courses Online streaming video system has been chosen to receive the President’s Award for Innovation. This award recognizes ASU individual employees or teams for innovations that improve educational, administrative, or other organizational processes through creative approaches. Philip Konomos, Stephen Tanner, Heather Knowles, Matthew Harp, Fred Mcllvain, Kirk Manegold, Rob Fidler, Ginny Sylvester, and Maura Pollock-Moneyhon all participated in the Video Courses Online project and they will be honored at a private award ceremony hosted by the President's Office. LIST and Access Services staffers were also recognized with an Innovation Award in 1999, for the Electronic Reserve Project.


Reference Linking Project

Reference linking is a technology service that enables linking from a citation to the full text of the cited item, which has become a common means for research libraries to enhance user access to scholarly literature. The Libraries have created a Reference Linking Task Group, chaired by Dr. John Howard, to evaluate various reference linking tools for possible purchase.

The Task Group has scheduled two informational presentations on reference linking and the OpenURL standard it’s based on. All library staff are welcome to attend and learn more about this exciting technology:
      • March 28, 2005   1:00-2:00 P.M.          C-6
      • March 30, 2005   10:00-11:00 A.M.      C-6

You can also learn more about reference linking from the Reference Linking Task Group’s informative web page, available at http://info.lib.asu.edu/lib/list/openURL/.

 

LIST Staffers Showcase Streaming Video at MEC Conference

Congratulations to Heather Knowles, Matthew Harp, Stephen Tanner and Philip Konomos who were invited to give a presentation at the Microcomputers in Education conference. Their presentation was titled Distance Leaning and Library Information: From Collaboration to Integration Via Streaming Video. The presentation (available online) dealt with design and implementation of the Library's streaming media projects and what the future holds for this exciting technology we’ve developed. The Microcomputers in Education Conference is in its 25th year of exploring ways to enhance education through technology.

 

Rob & Keith Take on New Assignments

LIST has a plethora of new projects that we will be dealing with in the near future – new web design, single sign-in authentication development, ordering and distribution of 150 new computers, infrastructure upgrades, and implementation of a link resolver, to name just a few – and we are repositioning ourselves to take better advantage of our personnel resources. As a result, Rob Fidler and Keith Swartz will be taking on new responsibilities.

Rob will become our Facilities and Infrastructure Coordinator. Among his new duties will be coordinating and tracking all our new hardware purchases, including inventory control and life cycle management; coordinating projects with IT; and developing and delivering IT-related training sessions for Library personnel.

With Rob taking so many new responsibilities, Keith will assume supervision of the SOS desk and queue. This will become effective Monday, April 18th.

 

ASU Libraries Contribute to a Growing Digital Resource

The Libraries are proud to be participating in the Western Waters Digital Library, an Institute of Museum and Library Services grant-funded project administered by the Greater Western Library Alliance. The project's 12 member institutions have contributed over 10,000 digital items to the collection so far; more than 1,000 of those are ASU archival materials related to the Colorado River and the Central Arizona Project. DAM, LIST, and OCAT staff continue to collaborate on the project, which will eventually include about 2,800 digital objects from ASU. Please check out the Western Waters Digital Library at http://westernwater.org.
