April 21, 2004

'Books' series brings acclaimed authors

In 1993, long before Oprah's Book Club, KAET-TV/Channel 8 presented "Books & Co.," a continuing series of engaging conversations with writers who talked about their books and the creative process of writing.

Previous episodes focused on Southwestern authors, with occasional visits from national writers. Now, with the support of the ASU Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing, KAET has access to some of today's leading contemporary writers. The seventh season of "Books & Co." premieres at
7:30 p.m., April 20, on Channel 8.

Ron Carlson, Regents Professor of English at ASU and a well-known author in his own right, will once again host "Books & Co."

These distinguished guests are scheduled to appear on upcoming programs:

. John Nichols, 7:30 p.m., April 20. Featured books: "The Voice of the Butterfly" and "An American Child Supreme: The Education of a Liberation Ecologist";

. Yann Martel, 7:30 p.m., April 27. Author of "Life of Pi," the 2004 OneBookAZ selection; and

. Joyce Carol Oates, 7:30 p.m, May 4. Featured books: "Rape: A Love Story" and "The Faith of a Writer."

"'Books & Co.' has been a very popular series with a loyal viewer following," says KAET Station Manager Beth Vershure. "We like to think of it as a community book club that encourages people to read and helps them understand the craft of writing."

Vershure says the new season will include a companion Web site (www.kaet.asu.edu/books) with background on the authors, their books, and information on how to form your own book club.

"Books & Co." is made possible by the Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing at ASU.

This article appeared in the April 16 , 2004 issue of ASU Insight.
