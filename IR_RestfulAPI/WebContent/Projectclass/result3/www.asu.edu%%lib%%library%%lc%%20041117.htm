Minutes of Meeting

 

November 17, 2004
Hayden C6A

 

Meeting was called to order at 3:00 pm.

A quorum was present.

 

I. Approval of Minutes (October 20, 2004) – Minutes were approved.

 

II. Dean’s Report –Sherrie Schmidt

 

Provost’s response to AP task force report: Dean Schmidt talked to Provost and Vice Provost Church about developing list of peer institutions. They will be large institutions of very high quality. Dean’s office is also working on the metrics to compare ASU libraries with those peer institutions. With regard to Provost’s response, Dean reminded librarians to pay more attention to their job descriptions for the upcoming annual evaluation, to work with primary evaluators to ensure that the descriptions reflect the job responsibilities, and that the job descriptions are accurate.

 

III.  Chair & Executive Committee's Report – Deborah Koshinsky

 

Deborah and Kurt collected the comments from the library staff about the new health insurance plans. Deborah will forward these comments to the chair of Personnel Committee of Academic Senate, Doug Johnson.

 

IV.  Committee Reports

 

a.       Personnel Committee – Brad Vogus - No Report

b.       Research and Professional Development Committee – Jennie Duvernay

1. Travel funds:  If travel plans are cancelled, please send your travel fund application forms back to the RPDC committee with your initials. The committee then will allocate those funds to other requests for funds by staff.

2. Research funds:  $1000 remains in research funds.  A request to submit proposals for the reminder of the research funds will be distributed in the near future.

c.    Compensation Committee – Dan Stanton (report presented by Brian Doherty)

1. The Committee met on 11/2/04. The committee reviewed issues from the Librarians’ Council Executive Committee, which relates to librarian compensation:

    • Salary compression issues driven by salary adjustments for promotion
    • Formal procedures used by the library for compensation distribution
    • University administration has asked colleges/schools/departments to develop detailed policies.  What is the UL status in this regard?

2. The Committee reviewed Compensation Committee Bylaws.  Discussion ensued regarding past activities of the committee.

3. As an initial exploratory effort, the Compensation Committee decided to review data from the ARL website related to ASU peer institutions with regards to salaries and salary adjustments to get a picture of where ASU stands.

4. The committee will also look at criteria and policies for compensation for librarians, which exist at peer institutions as well as examples of faculty procedures at ASU.

5. The committee intends to use past reports and research as a guide for this process, and will also attempt to include data from ASU East and West.

d.    Review Committee – Julie Tharp - No Report

e.    Academic Senate – Deborah Koshinsky

Report From meeting on Monday, October 18

1. Gail Hackett, Dean of the new University College talked to Academic Senate about her thoughts on the University College, which is still in the formative phase. Gail will work with Honor’s College. The date for the Downtown Campus is Fall 2006. The vision for the University College strongly reflects the goals to increase in the retention of undergraduate students and to strengthen the graduation rates.

2. Academic Sports liaison gave the report to the senate and emphasized the importance of focused academic involvement by the University with our NCAA student athletes.

3. Doug Johnson talked about the no. of issues from the Personnel Committee. He reminded people that deadline for Flexible Spending account is approaching.

4. There was a Senate resolution introduced by the Personnel Committee Chair, Doug Johnson, regarding changes for Faculty and Academic Professionals with respect to the process of making a retirement plan choice. Discussion ensued. Suggestions were made to change the wording of the resolution. Please send any ideas or comments to Deborah.

f.    Committee on Academic Professional Status – Dan Stanton (report presented by  Brian Doherty)  

Vice Provost Nancy Gutierrez will be the guest speaker for the CAPS Fall Spotlight Speaker Series scheduled for Tuesday, December 7, 10:00 - 11:30 AM in the MU Turquoise Room 208F.  Dr. Gutierrez will update APs on the status of the Academic Professional Task Force and any other issues affecting APs.

g.  Web Master – Yuwu Song  - no report.

 

V. Old Business

 

VI. New Business

 

Provost’s Response to AP Task Force Report: As a chair of the Personnel Committee, Brad Vogus led the discussion. Kurt will represent University Libraries on a newly-convened working group led by Vice Provost Nancy Gutierrez. The Review Committee will work on the post continuing status review process. The previous draft of the post continuing status review process is available on the CAPS website at www.asu.edu/provost/CAPS/postcont.htm which was not adopted.

 

Meeting adjourned 4:00 p.m.
