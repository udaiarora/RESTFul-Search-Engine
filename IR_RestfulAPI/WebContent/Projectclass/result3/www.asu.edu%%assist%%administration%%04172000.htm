Arizona State System for Information on Student Transfer (ASSIST)
Status Report as of April 17, 2000
John D. Porter, Project Director

Background

ASSIST (formerly known as the Transfer Student Data Warehouse or TSDW) is a relational database containing enrollment records from Arizona?s universities and community colleges. The purpose of ASSIST is to provide community colleges and universities information for (1) completing the Graduation Rate Survey and (2) assessing transfer articulation effectiveness in Arizona.

There are two committees involved in the ASSIST project. The Steering Committee is a policy group responsible to the Academic Program Articulation Steering Committee (APASC) for the ASSIST project. This Committee makes decisions concerning the use and content of ASSIST. The second group is the Technical Committee. They are a working group responsible for providing quality data and developing standard reports from ASSIST. The Steering committee meets semi-annually in Spring and Fall. The Technical Committee meets as often as needed.

Current Status

The ASSIST Technical committee met February 4, 2000 at MCCCD and the Steering Committee met on March 30, 2000 in Laughlin, NV. The Steering Committee will meet again on October 11, 2000 in Scottsdale. The following was reported:

The job search for the ASSIST technical analyst is concluded (six candidates were interviewed). The new analyst will start on May 1, 2000.
All community college districts have adopted the ASSIST Joint Resolution. The next step is for the chief academic officer at each district to sign the ASSIST Agreement with ASU. Once this is completed, ASU can begin granting access to local researchers.
Two community colleges and two universities have completed the ASSIST data exchange for 98-99. Other institutions estimate they will complete the exchange in the next few months.
The set of standard reports was reviewed and approved by the Steering committee.

 

Issues for APASC


The ASSIST Steering Committee is forwarding two issues to APASC which are beyond the ability of the Steering Committee to resolve.

 

The ability to assess the new transfer model depends on community colleges and universities identifying transfer degrees, AGECs, and pathways. If this information is not captured in the local student information system, it cannot be sent to ASSIST. A review of the plans at three institutions revealed different and incomplete sets of data at the local level.

RECOMMENDATION: APASC may want each institution to report on the implementation of the new transfer model in the local student information system (to include specific codes). Further, APASC may want to develop statewide codes to ensure commonality in the data reported to ASSIST.

Senate Bill 1399 prohibits the universities from using Social Security Number (SSN) as the primary identifier beginning July 1, 2002. This legislation threatens the ability of ASSIST to accurately track students statewide. The universities are beginning to implement the new legislation dropping SSN. Unfortunately, these actions are not being coordinated statewide, which means that without SSN, ASSIST will not be able to track students.

RECOMMENDATION: APASC may want to establish a new method of tracking students statewide. Some possibilities include: (1) amending legislation to allow the use of the SSN for tracking purposes; (2) adopting the Arizona Department of Education?s unique number identifying K-12 students; or (3) creating a new unique number to track students enrolling in post secondary education in Arizona.
