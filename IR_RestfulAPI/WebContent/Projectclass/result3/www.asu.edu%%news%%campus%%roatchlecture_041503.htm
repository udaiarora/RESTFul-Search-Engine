April 15, 2003

Lena Dominelli to present John F. Roatch Lecture

Globalization and the failure of capitalism to act as a positive social force on the development of citizenship will be the focus of the 2003 John F. Roatch Global Lecture Series on Social Policy and Practice, April 18.

Lena Dominelli, director of the Centre for International Social and Community Development, University of Southampton, England, will present the Roatch Lecture from 3 to 5 p.m. at the University Club of Phoenix, 39 E. Monte Vista (east of Central between Thomas and McDowell). The lecture is open to the public and is free of charge.

Dominelli, president of the International Association of Schools of Social Work, will present her views on how despite globalization penetrating every sphere of human activity it has failed to enhance the quality of life for all.

Emilia E. Martinez-Brawley, ASU's John F. Roatch Distinguished Community Service Scholar, said Dominelli's controversial views of economic globalization are supported by Dominelli's ongoing research and publications, which are widely discussed among social scientists. Respondents to Dominelli will be Astair Mengesha, chair of Women's Studies, College of Arts & Sciences at ASU West and Kyle Longley, associate professor of history at ASU Main. Mengesha and Longley will comment on Dominelli's perspectives from the richness of their expertise on local, African and Latin American issues respectively.

The lecture is supported by the John F. Roatch Endowment, created by gifts to ASU from John and Mary Roatch.

For information on the lecture visit (www.asu.edu/xed/ lectures/roatch.html).

Bailey, with the College of Extended Education, can be reached at (480)727-5337 or (randybailey@asu.edu).
