About Internal Audit and Management Services

Internal Audit and Management Services provides the following services to the university community:

  • Financial, operational and compliance audits
  • Special reviews and investigations
  • Advice on specific control issues as requested
  • Internal Control Seminars
  • Management Consultant Services

Customer Feedback

Audit clients are encouraged to provide constructive feedback via a customer survey that they are requested to complete after each audit. Survey responses received are carefully analyzed and used to help improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the audit process.

What To Expect During An Audit

The typical internal audit includes the following steps:

  1. An entrance conference, attended by department management, held to discuss the audit objectives, timing, intended report format and distribution, and other pertinent issues.
  2. Audit planning and fieldwork such as:
    • gathering background information about the client's business
    • evaluating the internal control system
    • testing the internal control system
    • developing conclusions based on the audit testing
  3. Reviewing the findings and recommendations with management.
  4. Issuing a draft audit report for management's review.
  5. An exit conference held to review the findings and recommendations along with management's responses provided.
  6. Final audit report distribution.
  7. Quarterly follow up on outstanding audit recommendations not yet implemented.

Credentials and Professional Affiliations

The professional staff members of Internal Audit and Management Services come from a variety of backgrounds and previous work experiences.

All professional staff members have degrees in accounting or finance, and each holds various professional certifications, including: Certified Public Accountant and Certified Internal Auditor.

The staff are also members of:

  • The Institute of Internal Auditors
  • The Association of Government Accountants
  • The Association of College and University Auditors
  • The American Institute of Certified Public Accountants
  • Arizona Society of Certified Public Accountants

Each staff member attends a minimum of 40 hours of Continuing Professional Education annually.
