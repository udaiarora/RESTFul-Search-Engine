November 23, 2004

ASU helps spread holiday cheer; drives aid charity

As the holidays rapidly approach, ASU’s employees and students are doing their part to help the less fortunate.

Colleges, departments and student organizations throughout campus are planning an assortment of drives collecting food, toys and clothing. The collections will go to help families and individuals throughout the Valley.

The ASU College of Nursing has three groups hosting separate drives.

The Breaking the Cycle community health clinic is working with Grace Lutheran Church to provide assistance for several needy families. Among those being helped are an 11-year-old who is hearing-impaired, plus three other needy families. For information, contact Gail Petersen at (480) 965-5115 or (gail.petersen@asu.edu).

Kay Jarrell, a clinical associate professor in the College of Nursing, operates a nursing clinic for the homeless at the nonprofit Central Arizona Shelter Services (CASS). Jarrell is seeking help to host a party for the homeless residents of CASS, 400 men and women. In addition to serving dinner, she is collecting socks and hopes to fill them with individually wrapped candy to give to each resident.

The college also is sponsoring a drive to adopt three families from Tempe Community Action and donate money in the form of gift certificates for them to use for Christmas. For specific information on each of the families, call Joy Shearman at (480) 965-1655.

Other drives include:

• Club ASU is collecting gently used ASU clothing to be distributed to families involved with Club ASU and Chicanos Por La Causa. Clothing in children’s through adult sizes is being sought. Donations are being accepted at the Visitor Information Center Building or in the Classified Staff Council Office Admin B359 until Dec. 10. For information, call Anna Wales at (480) 965-4079.

• The annual Books and Bears Holiday Drive – sponsored by ASU’s Bureau of Educational Research Services, Beta Phi Chapter of Kappa Delta Pi Honor Society, the Office of Professional Field Experience, the College of Education and College of Education Alumni chapters – will provide delivery of cuddly toys and popular children’s books to Valley-area first-graders.

A wrapped book and bear will be given to children in two first-grade classrooms located in downtown Phoenix . Donated bears and books may be gently used or new. Bears should be between 8 inches and 12 inches tall. Books need to be beginning first-grade level. Multiple drop boxes will be placed at the following locations: Professional Field Experiences office – Payne Hall room L1-14; Barrett Honors College Academic Advising office, Best Hall; Department of Math, Physical Sciences room A216; third floor nursing reception office lobby, Nursing Building; Division of Undergraduate Academic Services front office and College of Education preschool, first floor, Farmer Education Building. Monetary donations are accepted in room L1-14, Payne Hall. Donations are being accepted through Dec. 7. For information, call Joseph Valente at (480) 727-6554.

• The ASU School of Life Sciences Holiday Charity Drive will work with the Tempe Community Action Agency and Chicanos Por La Causa with their Adopt-A-Family Program. Donations of nonperishable food items, hygiene products, new toys and clothing are being sought. Cash or gift cards for grocery stores or discount stores also are being accepted. Donations are being accepted in drop boxes in Life Sciences Center C-226 or E-218 through Dec. 8. For information, call Felicity Snyder at (480) 965-8927.

• The student organization, MEChA, has adopted a safe house shelter for the holidays and is asking for help in collecting nonperishable food items by Tuesday, Nov. 23. The group has placed a drop box in the Chicano/Chicana Studies Department lobby area in Coor Hall 6633.

• ASU’s West campus is holding the Share a Joy gift and toy drive to collect items for various organizations. The drive is seeking gifts for 11- to 17-year-olds in Child Protective Services custody; gifts for children ages 2 to 17 for Arizonans for Children – Court Appointed Special Advocates; and gifts for 8- to 17-year-olds for Gabriel’s Angels. There will be a gift sorting and wrapping party from 10 a.m. – 2 p.m., Dec. 15, in the La Sala Ballroom at West campus. For information and a list of items needed, contact Salina Hancock at (602) 348-3178 or (salina.hancock@asu.edu).

• The Neighbors Helping Neighbors drive at the East campus is collecting canned goods and gifts to benefit lower-income students. A collection box has been set up for donations in the Student Union and the campus housing office. A wishing tree, with specific gift requests, also will be set up by Nov. 22 in the Student Union and the housing office, with gifts due Dec. 15. For more information, contact Jennifer Pollak at (480) 629-5661 or (jpprograms@yahoo.com).

Campbell, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 965-7209 or (garycamp@asu.edu).
