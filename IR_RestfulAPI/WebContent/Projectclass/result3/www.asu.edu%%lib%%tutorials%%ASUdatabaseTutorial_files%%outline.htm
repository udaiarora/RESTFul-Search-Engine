"Begin by going to the..."
    Begin by going to the Arizona State Music Library?s homepage <www.asu.edu/lib/music>

Click on reference.
"Scroll down to ASU Local..."
    Scroll down to ASU Local Databases, and click on the database that you with to enter.
"Click on Performance Tape Index..."
    Click on Performance Tape Index to enter the database.
"You are now in the..."
    You are now in the Performance Tape Index Database.
"Likewise,"
    Likewise, you enter the Song Index Database by clicking on ?Song Index.?
"You are now in the..."
    You are now in the Song Index Database.
"The interfaces for the Performance..."
    The interfaces for the Performance Tape Index and Song Index are identical.
"Begin by entering your terms"
    Begin by entering your terms.  The database will search the fields that are available in the pull-down menu.  The default setting searches all fields.
"The databases use Boolean Search..."
         The databases use Boolean Search Terms.  The common terms are AND, NOT, and OR.
         For example, if you enter to find Bob Dylan?s song ?Idiot Wind,? you could enter Dylan in the term field.
"This search yields 165 records"
     This search yields 165 records.
"In order to limit the..."
     In order to limit the number of hits, enter ?dylan AND wind.?  This limits hits to records with the words ?dylan? and ?wind.?
"This search yields 6 records"
    This search yields 6 records.  Five of these records are the Dylan song ?Blowin in the Wind.?
"In order to further limit..."
    In order to further limit the number of hits, enter ?dylan AND wind NOT blowin.?  This will eliminate records that contain the word blowin.
"This search yields one hit"
    This search yields one hit, which is the song ?Idiot Wind.?

"By using the Boolean operator..."
    By using the Boolean operator OR and entering ?fiddle OR violin,? you get hits with either ?violin? or ?fiddle? in the record.
"If you are looking for..."
    If you are looking for a song in the Song Database, you can enter the first line of the tune.  Go to ?field? and find ?FLN - First Line? in the dropdown menu.  The search will look for the word (or words) that appear in the first line of the song.  This search is for first lines that contain the word ?sometime.?
"The word ?sometime?"
    The word ?sometime? or ?sometimes? occurs in the first line of 186 records.
"Once you find a record..."
   Once you find a record that you want, you can produce a printer-friendly format or email the information to yourself.
"This is the printer-friendly..."
     This is the printer-friendly format.
"To email the citation,"
    To email the citation, enter email address and click send.
