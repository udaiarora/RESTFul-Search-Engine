May 17, 2006

ASU names State University of New York Vice Chancellor Elizabeth D. Capaldi university provost and executive vice president

Elizabeth D. Capaldi, vice chancellor and chief of staff for the State University of New York system, has been named university provost and executive vice president at Arizona State University.

She succeeds Milt Glick, who is leaving ASU to become president of the University of Nevada, Reno, and will assume her new post in August.

As ASU’s chief academic officer, Capaldi will provide leadership to all of the university's campuses and academic programs, fostering excellence in teaching, research and service to the community. She will guide the university’s mission in providing educational excellence and access, and will direct the university’s efforts in strategic redesign of its academic mission to achieve the vision of The New American University. She will also represent the university to external agencies and constituencies and engage in ASU's fundraising initiatives.

“Betty Capaldi has the academic standing, creative energy and analytically based administrative experience to take on the complex task of being university provost of ASU,” said ASU President Michael Crow. “I am looking forward to having her experience and leadership as part of the senior management team.”

Capaldi’s appointment completes ASU’s executive committee that, in addition to Crow and Capaldi, includes Carol Campbell, executive vice president and chief financial officer; Richard Stanley, senior vice president and university planner; Christine Wilkinson, senior vice president and secretary of the university; and Ira Jackson, president of the ASU Foundation. The committee is staffed by James O’Brien, chief of staff of the President’s Office.

“I am delighted to be joining ASU where a New American University is being created. The energy and positive attitude of all I met at ASU attracted me to join the team,” Capaldi said. “I look forward to working with President Crow and the faculty, staff and students to continue to improve academic quality and student success.”

Capaldi received her bachelor's degree from the University of Rochester, and her Ph.D. degree in experimental psychology from the University of Texas at Austin. She has contributed more than 65 chapters and articles to the scientific literature, co-authored three editions of an introductory psychology textbook, and edited two books on the psychology of eating.

Capaldi’s research and scholarship are highly regarded in her field. She has been honored with the title of fellow of the American Psychological Association, the American Psychological Society, and the American Association for the Advancement of Science, and she has served as president of the American Psychological Society.

In her current SUNY, position, which she assumed in 2003, Capaldi’s responsibilities include strategic planning, coordinating the activities of the vice chancellors to build quality in the 64 campuses of the State University of New York and serving as the main liaison between system administration and the campuses.

Before joining the SUNY system office, Capaldi was provost at the University at Buffalo, SUNY. She was dedicated to supporting research at the university and translating that research into economic development.

She came to Buffalo from the University of Florida where she served as provost and professor of psychology. She played a key role in improving sponsored research and technology transfer, and was instrumental in a new student advising system which has been responsible for dramatic improvements in graduation rates.

Capaldi also held an appointment on the faculty at Purdue University. She played a leadership role on that campus, serving as head of Purdue’s Department of Psychological Sciences and assistant dean of Purdue's Graduate School.

"I am very excited about the prospect that such a wonderful academic and widely experienced administrator will join ASU in this crucial position,” said Sander van der Leeuw, director of the School of Human Evolution & Social Change and chair of the search committee. “With her experience in Florida and New York State, she is eminently suited to build on Milt Glick's tremendous achievements, and to help us achieve our ambitious plans for the New American University."

“I would like to thank Professors van der Leeuw, Vice Provost Marjorie Zatz and the search committee for their excellent work in selecting Betty Capaldi, as well as the other two outstanding finalists,” Crow said. “The position of executive vice president and provost is vitally important to the success of this institution, and I look forward to working with Betty to guide ASU toward its bold and pioneering future.”
