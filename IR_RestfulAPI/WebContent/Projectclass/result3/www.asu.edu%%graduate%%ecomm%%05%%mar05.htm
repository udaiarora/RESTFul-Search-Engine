The E-Communiqué is published monthly with notices of upcoming deadlines, clinics and seminars, and other matters related to graduate education at ASU. Please visit the Graduate Calendar or the What's New page for information on events not included in this bulletin.

Academic Program Reviews (APR)

The APR is mandated by the Arizona Board of Regents and occurs at least once every seven years. Dr. Maria T. Allison, Vice Provost and Dean of the Division of Graduate Studies (DGS), serves as the University Program Review/Accreditation Officer. Dr. Filiz Ozel, DGS Associate Dean, and Denise Campbell, Program Coordinator, oversee reviews university-wide. Please take a look at our newly revised manual at www.asu.edu/graduate/apr.

Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) Program Accepting Applications

Doctoral and MFA students who intend to seek careers in academia are encouraged to apply for participation in PFF. DGS currently is accepting applications for the Exploratory Phase for 2005-06. Up to 70 doctoral and MFA students will be accepted, and initially commit to the first year of the program. Details about the upcoming PFF Informational Fair and the application deadline are included below. www.asu.edu/graduate/aboutus/news/2005/030305.htm

—ANNOUNCEMENTS/REMINDERS/UPDATES—

Summer 2005 Work-Study Assistantship Program. For details about eligibility, the application process and important dates/deadlines, visit www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/summer. This program is now administered by the Student Financial Assistance Office rather than DGS. Please direct questions to the Student Employment Office at (480) 965-5186.

DGS Forms. Please remember to download forms from the DGS website, as they are continually being revised. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo and www.asu.edu/graduate/forms.

Registration Requirement. Students must be registered for the semester of their defense in order to submit their document for format review and schedule their oral defense.

Thesis/Dissertation Defense Pass/Fail Forms. Based on the success of the pilot program, Student Academic Services will no longer be sending paper copies of pass/fail forms for oral defenses. Pass/fail forms will be sent via email with a PDF version of the pass/fail form attached. The PDF forms are official and will be accepted by the Division of Graduate Studies as originals. www.asu.edu/graduate/whatsnew/PassFailForms.htm

Format Manual. Significant changes have been made to the approval page "Head of the Academic Unit" information (pp. 18-19), and students graduating this semester will need to make the appropriate changes. Updated manuals will be available soon in the center lobby at Wilson Hall. www.asu.edu/graduate/formatmanual/FMprelim.html#table2

Clinic: Preparing for Graduate School on Mar. 23 has been cancelled.
