The Faculty Development Program at Arizona State University provides professional support for tenure-track faculty as they advance towards promotion and tenure. This program, which was began in 2004, provides information on mentorship and networking to facilitate the retention, promotion, and tenure of junior faculty. Beginning in fall 2006, the programs has expanded to include associate professors preparing for promotion to the rank of professor.

The key pieces of this university-wide initiative are individualized mentoring and a series of professional workshops.
The program is voluntary. It does not replace programs and mentorship within academic units and colleges, but augments these activities. Departments, schools and colleges maintain their responsibility to orient new faculty into their cultures and to provide them with the necessary resources and information to facilitate their success.

Mentoring

To request  a senior faculty mmentor, faculty submit a “Request a Mentor” form, available on the program web site, to the Office of the University Provost. On this form, the faculty member notes his/her research interests and any special concerns. A steering committee of senior faculty members match the mentee with an appropriate senior faculty mentor.

The mentor and the mentee attend together an initial session outlining the areas that might be explored in the mentoring relationship. These might include the faculty culture, guidance regarding grant writing and publishing, work/life balance, time management, issues of professional etiquette, information on the promotion and tenure process, etc. At this session, the mentor and mentee outline the specific issues they will explore in the coming academic year. The mentoring relationship has a no-fault termination date of August 15, 2007 but may be renewed at the request of both the mentee and the mentor.
