ASU Polytechnic campus student profile

ASU is a university of scholars. ASU students have won many national awards recognizing academic achievement and community commitment. Some of our recent accolades include:

Nine Fulbright Scholars in 2004-2005, placing ASU 7th in the nation among public schools for these top awards for graduate study abroad.

Two congressional 2004 Udall Scholars, a competitive award for students intending careers in environmental issues.

Three 2004 Goldwater Scholars, the top national scholarship for students in science, math and engineering.

USA Today Scholars
ASU has had more students named top U.S. undergraduates by USA Today over the past 11 years than any other public school. Only Harvard and Yale have had more. Boosting ASU's impressive record of accomplishment, two more ASU students have been named to USA Today's All-USA Academic Teams in the Feb. 17 issue of USA Today.

Scott MacIntyre, a senior in piano performance in the Herberger College of Fine Arts, named to the first team. Jared Niska, who graduated in December with a degree in bioengineering from the Ira A. Fulton School, was named to the second team of 20 students.

Scott MacIntyre is also the winner of a 2005 Marshall Scholarship. Read More...

Fall 2004 - 162 National Merit Scholars enrolled.

Fall 2004 Undergraduate Student Profile:

  • 7,719 first-time freshmen enrolled
  • 5,374 transfer students enrolled
  • 81% enrolled full time in the fall
  • 23.8% are from ethnic minority backgrounds, with Hispanic students having the largest representation
  • 2.6% are international students, with the most coming from South Korea
  • The average age is 23, with 2,558 between 30 and 40 and another 1,508 who are older than 40
    53% are women
  • 2,854 undergraduates are enrolled in the Barrett Honors College

Graduate Student Profile:

  • 11,486 graduate students
  • 53% are women
  • 59% are enrolled full time
  • The average age is 32, with 2,125 who are older than 40
  • 15% are from ethnic minority backgrounds, with Hispanic students having the highest representation
  • 15.6% are international students, with the largest representation from India
