October 26, 2006

Final four teams to vie for Academic Bowl championship

Four ASU Academic Bowl teams stayed alive to advance during a competitive quarterfinal round Oct. 25 in the Memorial Union’s Pima Room.

Teams from the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences’ Maroon team, the Mary Lou Fulton College of Education and W. P. Carey School of Business will advance to the last evening of competition Nov. 6.

From left, McKay Jones, Chris Ray, Carlos Ross, Eric Cox of the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences’ Maroon team breath a sigh of relief and advance to the finals with a win.

The event again featured plenty of spirit, with fans lining up to support their teams with signs and cheers. But, for the teams, the competition was noticeably more intense.

“My teammates and I played the first round, thinking it would be fun,” said Marcus Boyd, a junior and team member for the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences. “After we won, we thought, ‘Hey, maybe we’d better get serious about this.’ ”

In the first match, the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering team, with students Karl Sturm, Amy Teegarden, Andrew Holle and Marko Manojlovic, matched up against University College, with students Kristina Woodbury, Travis Henry, Tara Egnatios and Vince Rollins. The Fulton team, which took an early lead, fell behind 150-75 at the half before rallying to post a 260-180 victory.

The second match featured the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences’ Maroon team, with students Eric Cox, Chris Ray, Carlos Ross and McKay Jones, against the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences’ quartet of Benjamin Goodrich, Robert Gaytan III, Michele Lefevre and Marcus Boyd. CLAS Maroon took a strong early lead and rode it a 265-95 win.

Andrew Holle of the Fulton School of Engineering smiles as he hears a question. Holle's team advanced to the next round with a victory over University College.

In the next match, the Mary Lou Fulton College of Education team of Jessica Dailey, Stephanie Green, Michael Stouffer and Nathaniel Hudson went up against the Morrison School of Management and Agribusiness squad, which featured students Andrea McBurney, Todd Ulery, Chris Davis and Mike Neider. Fulton Education built an early lead, but Morrison rallied in the second half to close the gap to 65 points. Fulton responded with a streak of correct answers and ultimately won, 320-140.

In the final match of the night, the W. P. Carey School of Business team, featuring Joseph Favre, Chuan Xiong, Alex Porter and Derek Guizado, took on the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences’ Gold team, with Jimmy Scanlon, Mike Rockwell, Josh McCormick and Eli Bliss. The W. P. Carey team took a commanding early lead, scoring 220 points in the first half, and cruised to a 370-55 victory.

The competition, sponsored by the offices of Public Affairs and Student Affairs, is modeled after the national College Bowl. It features four-member teams squaring off in a question-and-answer format on a wide range of subjects, including current events, history, science, sports and culture.

The final night of competition will be held at 7 p.m., Nov. 6, in the studios of Eight/KAET-TV on the ASU Tempe campus in front of a live studio audience. The team from Fulton Engineering will take on CLAS Maroon, and Fulton Education will take on W. P. Carey. The two winners will be paired that same evening in one final match.

The members of the winning team will receive $4,500 in scholarships for each team member, as well as the President’s Cup trophy.

A complete listing of the teams involved can be found online at www.asu.edu/news/academicbowl/about.html.
