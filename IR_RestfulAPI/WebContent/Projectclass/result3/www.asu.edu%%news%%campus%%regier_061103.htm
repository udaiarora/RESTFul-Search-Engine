June 11, 2003

Dr. Philip Regier to lead W. P. Carey School of Business

Dr. Philip R. Regier has been named interim dean of the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University, effective July 1, 2003.

Regier will work closely with Dean Larry Edward Penley during the next two months to provide a smooth leadership transition as Penley prepares to take on his new role as president of Colorado State University and chancellor of the Colorado State University system. Penley has been dean of the W. P. Carey School at ASU for the past 12 years.

"It is critical that the momentum of the W. P. Carey School continue, and Dr. Regier has the full support and confidence of the administration to ensure it does," said Milton Glick, executive vice president and provost of the university. "I believe the W. P. Carey School is well positioned to attract an outstanding dean and we are committed to achieving that through a national search."

Regier is the associate dean for undergraduate programs and the director of Business Honors, a post he has held since 1999. He joined the faculty of the School of Accountancy in 1987. He completed his baccalaureate degree at St. John's College - Santa Fe and his Ph.D. at University of Illinois at Urbana - Champaign.

Under his leadership, the W. P. Carey degree has been re-designed to assure that every student, regardless of major, gains an advanced understanding of technology and its impact on all functional areas of business. Implementation of the curriculum change included the development of a laptop initiative, which utilizes wireless laptop computers to make every classroom in the business complex a computer classroom as needed.

Regier was instrumental in the creation of the Rodel Community Scholars program, which pairs teams of Business Honors students with area high schools to address student retention. He was also a key player in the development of a partnership with Ford Motor Company, which resulted in the Certificate in Dealership Management -- a new addition to the specialized offerings within the business degree. Regier has underscored the W. P. Carey School's commitment to Business Honors, most recently supporting the creation of Honors Consulting. The school has also enhanced internship opportunities for students and is moving toward increased career services for undergraduates.

"I am very confident in Phil Regier's ability to maintain our school's trajectory during a very important period in its history," said Penley. "He is an excellent choice. He understands the significance of academic quality and he already works well with the business community. As a member of the school's senior leadership for several years, he has contributed to and shares the vision of the W. P. Carey School. Phil's appointment gives me a sense of confidence in the school's future as I depart."

Regier pledged to keep the school moving.

"My role will be to continue the momentum that characterizes the W. P. Carey School, and to work with the deans, department chairs and directors to help realize the potential created by our naming," Regier said. "I will also work with the search committee to assure that this position remains one of the most attractive business school positions in the country."

W. P. Carey School of Business

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 25th in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
