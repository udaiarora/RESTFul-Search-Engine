Q: What is the difference between official and unofficial transcripts?

An official transcript is a copy of the student's permanent academic record and lists, in chronological order, all courses taken at ASU. It includes all grades received. It is signed and dated by the registrar and displays the seal of the university. An unofficial transcript is an uncertified copy of the academic record. Unofficial transcripts include all information shown on the official transcript, plus information concerning changes, additions, etc., to the record.

Q: How do I order an official ASU transcript?

Official transcripts may be requested in person, by mail or via ASU Interactive. The fee for an official transcript is $10.00 per copy. "Rush" transcripts (requested to be printed and picked up on the same day) will cost $10.00 in addition to the total cost of the transcripts ordered. Special delivery processing, instead of regular mail, is available via FedEx or U.S. Express Mail at $19.50 per U.S. States delivery address. Q: What does the grade of "NR" mean?

The "NR" (not reported) grade simply means that a grade has not yet been reported. Once final grade processing takes place for the semester, most classes will have an actual grade posted instead of the "NR". If an "NR" grade appears for a course in a prior semester, you should contact the instructor of the course to find out why a final grade was not recorded.

TOP

Q: What does the grade of "X" mean?

The "X" grade means that you requested the audit option at the time you registered for the class. You earn no credit or grade for audited classes.

TOP

Q: What does the grade of "I" mean?

A mark of "I" (incomplete) is given by the instructor when you are otherwise doing acceptable work but are unable to complete the course because of illness or other conditions beyond your control. You are required to arrange with the instructor for the completion of the course requirements. The arrangement is recorded on the Request for Grade of Incomplete form. Do not reregister or pay fees to complete the course. You have one calendar year from the date the mark of "I" is recorded to complete the course. When you complete the course, the instructor must submit an Authorization for Change of Grade form to the University Registrar's Office. If you receive an "I" in an undergraduate course in the fall 1983 or thereafter, your grade will be automatically changed to a failing grade "E" if you do not complete the course within one calendar year. If you receive an "I" in a graduate course (500-level or above) your "I"grade will become a permanent part of your transcript if you do not complete the course within one year, and you will have to reregister and pay fees to repeat the course for credit. Marks of "I" are changed to a grade of "E" (0.00) for purposes of evaluating graduation requirements for undergraduate students.

Q: Can I retake a course for a better grade?

You may retake an undergraduate course taken previously at ASU one time if the grade received is "D", "E", "W" or "X". Q: What are semester hours or earned hours?

Semester hours are the number of credit hours for which you registered in a course. One semester hour represents a minimum of one 50-minute class exercise per week per semester.

Semester earned hours are the semester hours at ASU for which a grade of A+, A, A-, B+, B, B-, C+, C, D, E, Y or P was given. Hours are not earned for failing grades of E, withdrawal grades of W, incomplete grades of I, or for audit grades of X. Cumulative earned hours include the total semester hours earned at ASU, credit awarded by exam, and transfer credit.

Net hours are the semester hours at ASU for which a grade of A+, A, A-, B+, B, B-, C+, C, D or E was given. Semester net hours are used to calculate the semester GPA, and the cumulative net hours are used to calculate the cumulative GPA.

Q: How do I figure my ASU grade point average (GPA)?

The following grades and their values are used in computing your semester and cumulative ASU GPA:

Grade* Grade Point Value
A+ 4.33
A 4.00
A- 3.67
B+ 3.33
B 3.00
B- 2.67
C+ 2.33
C 2.00
D 1.00
E** 0.00
*Other grades (W, X, Y, etc.) are not included in the GPA calculation.
**Grades E1, E2, E3, E4, E5, and E8 are equivalent to an E.
  1. Multiply the grade point value by the number of semester hours for which the course was taken to determine the honor points.
  2. Add together all the honor points.
  3. Add together all the semester hours that received one of the above grades to determine net hours.
  4. Divide the honor points by the net hours to calculate the GPA.
            Honor Points ÷ Net Hours = GPA
    GPA's are rounded to the nearest 100th of a decimal point.
    Although the plus/minus scale includes a grade of A+ with a value of 4.33, the cumulative GPA is capped at 4.00.
Example:

Course Semester Hours Grade Grade Point Value Honor Points
ENG 101 3.0 A 4.00 12.00
MAT 117 3.0 B- 2.67 8.01
ECN 111 3.0 E 0.00 0.00
Total: (net hours)   9.0   20.01
      20.01 ÷ 9.0 = 2.22 GPA

Note: ASU's grade scale includes plus/minus grades in one-third increments of whole numbers, which may cause the Honor Points displayed on a student's record to be a few one-hundredths of a point higher or lower than the results of a manual calculation; however, this minor mathematical discrepancy will not affect the resulting GPA.

Q: What are the general university graduation requirements (minimum hours, GPA, first year composition, etc.)?

The following general university graduation requirements are reflective of the 1996-98 or later edition of the General Catalog. If you are following requirements from the 1994-96 or earlier General Catalog, contact an academic advisor in the college of your major.

Credit Requirements - All students enrolled in a baccalaureate degree program under the 1996-98 or later edition of the General Catalog requirements must fulfill the following university requirements to graduate. A minimum of 120 semester hours is required for graduation with a baccalaureate degree. A minimum of 45 semester hours in upper-division courses is required for graduation. Some programs may require more than 120 semester hours or 45 upper-division semester hours for graduation; refer to college graduation requirements for the specific number required.

Grade Point Requirement - A minimum cumulative grade point average (GPA) of 2.00 for all courses taken at ASU is required to graduate with a baccalaureate degree.

General Studies Requirement - All students enrolled in a baccalaureate degree program must satisfy a university requirement of a minimum of 35 semester hours of approved course work. For General Studies courses, refer to the ASU General Catalog, the Schedule of Classes, the Summer Sessions Bulletin or contact an academic advisor in the college of your major.

First-Year Composition Requirement - Completion of both ENG 101 and 102 or ENG 105 with a grade of "C" or higher is required for graduation from ASU in any baccalaureate program. International students from non-English-speaking countries may meet the First-Year Composition requirement by completing ENG 107 and 108 with a grade of "C" or higher.

Resident Credit Requirement - Every candidate for the baccalaureate degree is required to earn a minimum of 30 semester hours in resident credit courses at the ASU campus from which the student will graduate.

Q. How do I become an Arizona resident for tuition purposes?

To be eligible for classification as a resident for tuition purposes, you must meet the criteria established by the Arizona Board of Regents and Arizona state law. To qualify as an independent student there are three general criteria that must be met. You must provide clear and convincing evidence to satisfy the following criteria:

  • 12 months of continuous presence immediately preceding the semester in question.
  • 12 months of demonstrated intent to be a resident.
  • 2 years of financial independence.

There are several exceptions to the 12 months of presence allowed by state law. For general information refer to Arizona Residency information. For additional information or to secure the appropriate petition form, contact the Residency Classification Section at (480) 965-7712 or visit the office in room 146 of the Student Services Building (SSV 146).

Q. When can I petition for in-state status?

There are specific application deadline dates for each semester.See the Residency Petition Time Table or contact the Residency Classification Section at (480) 965-7712.The deadline to submit a petition for classification as a resident student is the last day of registration for the semester.Failure to file a petition within the time prescribed shall constitute a waiver of the right to file a petition for that semester.

Q. I have been classified as a nonresident by the admissions office, but I will be in Arizona for 1 year by the time the semester begins.Can I be classified as a resident?

Being in Arizona for 1 year does not necessarily make you a resident for tuition purposes.You will need to meet all three of the independent requirements: one year showing presence and intent, and two years financial independence, unless you meet one of the exceptions.Once a student has been classified as a nonresident by admissions, he/she would need to petition for change of residency in order to be classified as a resident.

Q. I am a continuing student; will my residency automatically change after 1 year?

No, any student wanting to be classified must initiate the process by contacting the residency office and submitting a petition for change of residency by the appropriate deadline.

Q. The community college in Arizona sees me as a resident; doesn't that mean I am a resident for ASU?

No, not necessarily.The community colleges and universities in Arizona have different guidelines regarding residency.Even if a community college classifies you as a resident, you may not be a resident here at ASU.You must meet either the independent guidelines or one of the exceptions to be considered a resident for tuition purposes at ASU.

Q. I have always been an Arizona resident, but I have been in the military.Why was I classified as a nonresident from my admissions application?

All military personnel that apply for admission are automatically classified as nonresidents.We need to verify that the military person is eligible for residency, so you would have to file a military residency petition with the residency classification office by the appropriate deadline. To prove you have maintained your Arizona residency status, you need to submit a copy of your Arizona state tax form for the most recent tax year.

Q. I am a military person who is not from Arizona.I am getting out and want to move to Arizona.Can I be considered a resident?

It is possible to change your state of legal residence while in the military.The military form DD2058 is the Change of Legal Residence.If you designate Arizona as your legal residence, the military would begin deducting Arizona state taxes and you would be required to file an Arizona state tax return.Once you have declared Arizona as your legal residence for twelve months you may qualify as a resident for tuition purposes.The twelve month period must be met by the petition deadline to be eligible for residency status for that semester.If you have not been a ?legal resident? for the required twelve month period, you may attend Arizona State University as a nonresident until the twelve month period has been met.

TOP

Q. I am stationed in Arizona on active duty. Can I be classified as a resident for tuition purposes?

Yes, provided your orders are effective by our petition deadline. This exception also includes those that are active reserve or Arizona National Guard. Those that are discharged prior to the deadline date do not qualify for this exception.

Q. Can I qualify for the company transfer exception if my fianc or significant other is the transferred employee?

No, the company transfer policy does not apply to fiancs or significant others.You would need to be married before the transfer takes effect in order to be eligible for this exception.Please note that you still must meet the two years of financial independence to qualify under this exception.

Q. I am a teacher and want to take classes; can I pay in-state tuition?

The exception to the 12 months of presence allowed by state law only applies to classes needed for state teacher certification for your current job.Classes for additional certifications, endorsements or another degree are not included.

TOP

Q.ASU offers some classes within my school district; can I pay resident fees for those classes?

Yes, there is a provision in the Board of Regents policy, that allows teachers to pay resident tuition for seven or less semester hours for classes at an off campus location.The classes are through the College of Education and are listed under courses offered at Extended Campus locations.The teacher must be employed in the school district where the classes are held to qualify for this exception. Contact your school district or ASU Office of Extended Education at (480) 965-9797 for more information.

TOP

Q. I am not a U.S. citizen; can I qualify for residency?

Residency eligibility for non-citizens is determined by the visa category in addition to the regular requirements.Non U.S. citizens who have student visas or another visa that is temporary are not able to establish permanent domicile in Arizona, so they are classified as nonresidents for tuition purposes.Non-citizens who have been granted legal immigrant status or permanent resident status through the INS are classified for purposes of residency status according to the same criteria applicable to U.S. citizens.

TOP

Q.I had an F1 visa but I have married a U.S. citizen; can I now qualify as an in-state student?

Not necessarily, holders of visas that are temporary that become permanent residents or U.S. citizens are eligible for residency according to the same criteria applicable to U.S. citizens.You will need to meet all three of the independent requirements: one year showing presence and intent, and two years financial independence, unless you meet one of the exceptions.Please discuss your situation with the residency classification office.

Q: Why am I on academic probation?

You are on probation because you did not maintain the minimum GPA standard set by the university or by your college. The following chart shows what the university requires as a minimum for academic good standing for retention purposes.

Total Earned Hours Minimum Cumulative GPA
24 or fewer 1.60
25-55 1.75
56 or more 2.00

You may be subject to disqualification if the conditions stated at the bottom of your grade report are not met by the end of the following fall or spring semester. It may be possible to achieve academic good standing if you earn above average grades at ASU or if you repeat lower division courses at ASU in which you have received grades below "C". Refer to the Academic Standards for your college in the General Catalog for further information, and contact an academic advisor in the college of your major.



TOP

Q: Why am I disqualified?

If you are placed on probation at the end of a semester, you are subject to academic disqualification by your college at the end of the following semester if the conditions imposed for retention are not met. If you are disqualified, you will be notified by the dean of the college. Please take note of the following information.

  1. While you are disqualified, you are not eligible to enroll in a fall or spring semester at ASU. If you have registered for the upcoming fall or spring semester, you will be withdrawn by the University Registrar's Office, and if you have already paid tuition for that semester you will receive a full refund. If you do not attend in the spring semester, you must be readmitted before you can attend in the upcoming summer or fall, and if you do not attend in the fall you must be readmitted before you can attend in the upcoming spring or winter.
  2. You may not attend ASU as a non-degree seeking student.
  3. If you elect to attend another university, any credits earned there will not affect your ASU cumulative GPA. A course repeated elsewhere will not enable you to have a "D" or "E" deleted from your ASU cumulative GPA.
  4. Consult with an academic advisor in the college of your major on your eligibility to attend ASU summer school or winter session during disqualification to raise your ASU GPA.
  5. To continue or return to ASU you must apply for reinstatement, and if you skip a fall or spring semester at ASU you must also apply for readmission. Deadlines and an application fee apply.

Contact the appropriate readmissions office for further information.

Q: How do I get reinstated so I can register for classes?

To be reinstated into the same college from which you were disqualified, you must submit an Application for Reinstatement form to the disqualifying college. If you have not attended ASU for one or more semesters then submit the Application for Reinstatement to Recording/Readmissions, along with an Undergraduate Application for Readmission.

To be reinstated into an ASU college other than the disqualifying college, you must submit an Application for Reinstatement to the appropriate readmissions office. If you have not attended ASU for one or more semesters then submit the Application for Reinstatement to the appropriate readmissions office along with an Undergraduate Application for Readmission. Students who are disqualified may be subject to a deadline that is earlier than the priority application date. Applications for students who wish to change colleges and have an ASU cumulative GPA below 2.0, are reviewed by the Undergraduate Admissions Board. These applications, and all required documents, must be received 5 business days before the scheduled Undergraduate Admissions Board meeting. Contact the appropriate readmissions office for deadline or reinstatement information.

Q: How do I change my major once I am admitted to ASU?

Undergraduate Admissions will change your major if you are newly admitted and have not yet registered for classes. If you have registered for classes or are a continuing undergraduate student at ASU, complete the Degree/Major/Campus Change Request form, obtain the required signatures and submit the entire form to the new department. Some colleges can process the approved form for you or you may be directed to Records Information or the Graduation Office in the Student Services Building on the Tempe campus, the University Center Building on the West campus, the Oasis Center on the Polytechnic campus or the University Center Building on Downtown Phoenix campus. You are strongly advised to meet with an academic advisor in the department that offers the requested degree or major.

You may also want to meet with an academic advisor in the department that offers the requested degree or major to determine if the university good standing requirement and any additional college requirements have been met.

TOP

Q: Is there a minimum grade point average (GPA) requirement if I want to change colleges?

Yes. If you want to transfer from one college to another within the university, you must have an ASU GPA of 2.00 or higher depending on the college. Individual colleges may set higher GPA standards. The GPA determining good standing is computed on courses taken only at ASU. Contact an academic advisor in the department that offers the requested degree or major to determine if there are higher GPA standards.

TOP

Q: If I want to change from Tempe campus to Polytechnic campus or West campus or Downtown Phoenix campus (or vise versa), what do I do?

Complete the Degree/Major/Campus Change Request form and obtain the required signatures. Submit the form to the new department. Some colleges can process the approved form for you or you may be directed to any Registrar Site, Records Information on the Tempe campus, the University Center Building on the West campus, the Oasis Center on the Polytechnic campus, or the University Center Building on Downtown Phoenix campus. You are strongly advised to meet with an academic advisor in the department that offers the requested degree or major.

TOP

Q: If I have filed a Declaration of Graduation for one degree/major, how do I change to a new major?

Contact an academic advisor in the department that offers the degree or major that you are requesting.
