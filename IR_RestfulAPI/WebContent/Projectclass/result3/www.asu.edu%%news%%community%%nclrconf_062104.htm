June 21, 2004

Conference to celebrate, encourage progress made by Hispanics

Arizona will host thousands of Hispanics from around the United States this month for the National Council of La Raza (NCLR) annual conference.

The NCLR, the largest Hispanic civil rights organization in the United States, in partnership with Governor Janet Napolitano, Phoenix Mayor Phil Gordon and the 2004 NCLR Annual Conference Host Committee, will present the 2004 Annual Conference June 26 - 29, at the Phoenix Civic Plaza, in downtown Phoenix.

"The conference is designed to educate and encourage Hispanics to step up and get involved," says Janet Murguia, NCLR executive director and chief operating officer. "This year's conference has something for everyone."

The NCLR, is a private, nonprofit, nonpartisan, tax-exempt organization established in 1968 to reduce poverty and discrimination and improve life opportunities for Hispanic Americans. Sandra Ferniza, executive director of community relations with the Office of Public Affairs, says ASU will play an important role in the upcoming conference.

"ASU is committed to building the future through partnerships in many ways, including a major sponsorship of the NCLR conference," Ferniza says. "ASU is vigorously working to provide a world class high quality educational experience to all of its students."

Ferniza says she and her family have benefited from attending NCLR events and encourages students and the community to attend.

"NCLR workshops offer a broad array of themes and topics focused on both policy and practical information for the community, parents and students," she says. "The Youth Leadership Summit and Expo offer a wide variety of information on education choices, financial planning for college, etc. My own son learned firsthand about opportunities to attend out-of-state colleges when he participated in the Youth Summit in Chicago while a junior in high school."

Some of the free workshops scheduled during the NCLR conference include:

. "Double the Numbers: Increasing Postsecondary Success for Latino Students" features educational leaders and policy-makers who have worked to close the achievement gap in their communities' schools and who have significantly increased postsecondary opportunities for Latinos and English language learners.

. "A Panel Discussion: The Changing Face of Community Development and Financing" focuses on the tremendous change in the community development industry and the needs of Community Development Corporations (CDC) for financing community development projects.

. "CHCI: Developing the Next Generation of Latino Leaders," features representatives from the Congressional Hispanic Caucus Institute (CHCI) who will discuss its leadership development programs and the importance of Latino youth pursuing educational programs that provide them with the skills to become successful professionals, effective leaders and strong advocates.

Raul Yzaguirre, NCLR president and chief executive officer, and Murguia will be discussing details about the conference on KAET's "Horizonte," which airs 7:30 p.m., June 24 on Channel 8.

To register for the NCLR Annual Conference, visit (http://phoenix2004.wms.cdgsolutions.com/).
