The Library Administration is responsible for the planning, budgeting, and evaluation of the four University Libraries, including Hayden, Noble Science and Engineering, Music, and the Architecture and Design Library. The University Librarian is responsible for the coordination of the activities of the Law Library, West campus and Polytechnic campus libraries. These libraries are administered separately.

The ASU Libraries have a collection of over 4 million volumes, ranking as the 38th largest research library in the United States and Canada, according to criteria established by the Association of Research Libraries. The University Libraries are comprised of the following facilities:

Architecture and Environmental Design Library
Contains over 30,000 volumes including books, periodicals, tape recordings, films, microfilm, and portfolio materials in the areas of urban planning, environmental design, and architecture. The archives of several prominent architects, such as Will Bruder, are also housed here. It is located in the College of Architecture and Environmental Design Building near the northwest corner of campus.


Hayden Library
Located on Cady Mall, houses materials in the humanities and social sciences, including business and education. It has a number of specialized collections including Government Documents (U.S. federal, Arizona state and local, and United Nations), Arizona Collection, Special Collections, East Asian Collection, Curriculum Collection, Chicano Research Collection, Labriola National American Indian Data Center, University Archives, the Benedict Visual Literacy Collection, and the Arizona Historical Foundation. There are computer workstations, wireless internet connectivity, video viewing/study carrels, photocopy services, and study rooms in Hayden.

A two-level underground addition opened in spring 1989 adding 1,500 seats and shelving for 375,000 additional volumes. On the Lower Level are current and bound periodicals, Microforms, Video Collections and Copy Services. The Concourse (on entrance level) has Reserve, Circulation, Interlibrary Loan, reference desk, and ADA (Access for Disability Accommodations). The upper concourse has the SOS help station, MARS (Machine Assisted Reference Services) workstations, and a library instruction classroom. An online tour of Hayden Library is available.


Music Library
Includes all music materials in the Libraries’ collection. These include musical scores, recordings, videos, books, and periodicals. Group listening is provided from twenty channels to 80 listening stations; other recordings may be used at 30 private stations. It is located in Room W302 of the Music Building, next to Gammage Memorial Auditorium.


Noble Science and Engineering Library
Houses the collections in engineering, physical and life sciences, mathematics, nursing and health sciences, and geography. The Map Collection is located here. Additionally, the library features computer workstations, wireless internet connectivity, Copy Services, and group study rooms. It is located on Tyler Mall. An online tour of Noble Library is available.
