Carlos Andrés Pérez, former President of Venezuela, to speak at ASU

Carlos Andrés Pérez, President of Venezuela from 1974-1978 and again from 1989-1993, will speak at Arizona State University's Memorial Union Pima Room on Tuesday October 14, 2003 at 3:00 p.m. The event is free and open to the public.

The former President's visit to ASU is sponsored by the Center for Latin American Studies in collaboration with the Organización de Latinos Unidos (OLU) of Phoenix. Carlos Andrés Pérez will direct his remarks at the current volatile situation in his native Venezuela under the Hugo Chavez administration. It was current President Chavez, who as a colonel in the Venezuelan armed forces in 1992, led a revolt attempting to overthrow then President Carlos Andrés Pérez.

Hugo Chavez was subsequently elected President in 1998. After taking office, he immediately rendered the Venezuelan congress as irrelevant. In its place, Chavez instituted the National Constituent Assembly which declared itself the sole source of power in Venezuela. A major popular strike against Chavez ensued and unrest continues to this day in a country that had enjoyed an unbroken period of democratic stability since 1958.

Former President Carlos Andrés Pérez will also explore the prospects for democratic and economic reforms in Venezuela. Venezuela is a key supplier of oil to the United States.

The event is made possible with the support of the U.S. Department of Education.
