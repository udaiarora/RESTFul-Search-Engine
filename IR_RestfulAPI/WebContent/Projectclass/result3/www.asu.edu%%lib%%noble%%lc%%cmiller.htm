Education

Master of Arts, Information Resources and Library Science, University of Arizona, 2006


Master of Music, Northern Illinois University, 2002


Bachelor of Music, North Carolina School of the Arts, 1996

Projects

Ongoing—Dept. of Education (TICFIA) grant funded project to digitize the Mimijac Palgen Cambodian Photographic Collection for the Southeast Asia Digital Library.


Ongoing—LSTA grant funded project, “Sharing at the Crossroads: Maximizing Lao and Burmese resources at ASU Libraries.”


Ongoing—extensive field recordings of adapted 20-button Anglo concertina (adopted during the British period) performance practice and accompanying sung histories of the Pa’O people in southern Shan State, Myanmar.


Completed—“Music of Burma: The Muriel Williamson Collection” compilation and editing of a collection of fourteen compact discs from over sixty reel-to-reel field recordings originally collected by Muriel Williamson and associates in the early 1960s. The recordings are part of the permanent collection at the Center for Burma Studies, Northern Illinois University.

Research Travel

April–May 2005—Manila, Philippines; Yangon and Taunggyi, Myanmar (Burma)

  • International Conference on Enriching Indigenous Southeast Asia Collections in Libraries (e-iseacol).
  • book-buying in Philippines and Myanmar for ASU Libraries.
  • Continued research in the Pa’O Literary and Cultural Council Library.

October–November 2003—Taunggyi, Shan State, Myanmar

  • study of Pa’O language (T-B, Karenic) and traditional music.
  • research in the Pa’O Literary and Cultural Council Library.

August–September 2003—Surakarta (Solo), Central Java, Indonesia

  • further study of Javanese language and gamelan.
  • historical research at the Mangkunegaran Court Library.

September 2002–August 2003—Yangon, Myanmar

  • study of Burmese language and traditional music.

December 2000–January 2001—Bandung, West Java, Indonesia

  • study of Sundanese kendang penca ensemble.
  • supported by a Winston-Salem/Forsyth Regional Artist Grant.

March–June 2000—Mandalay, Myanmar; Ponorogo, East Java, Indonesia; and Sukawati, Bali, Indonesia

  • study of saung gauk (arched harp) in Mandalay.
  • study of Reyog Ponorogo dance-drama in Ponorogo.
  • study of gender wayang ensemble in Sukawati.

October 1997–March 1999—Wonogiri, Central Java, Indonesia; Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

  • study of Indonesian language and gamelan.
  • study of sao meo and tieu flutes in Ho Chi Minh City.

June–August 1996—Center for World Music: Payangan, Ubud, Bali, Indonesia

  • study of Javanese and Balinese gamelan ensembles.
