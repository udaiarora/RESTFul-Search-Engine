Historical Consumer Prices


These are some suggested free Web sites and Hayden Library print and online resources that can provide information on consumer prices for various time periods in the past. Includes also information on the currencies used, foreign exchange conversions, and present value calculations. The sources have been arranged geographically by the categories below.


| Worldwide | United States | Central & South America | Europe | Middle East |



Worldwide


  1. Current Value of Old Money -- http://www.ex.ac.uk/~RDavies/arian/current/howmuch.html

  2. EH.NET How Much Is That? -- http://eh.net/hmit/

    Most of the data pertains to either the U.S. or the U.K. but there is also information on exchange rates between the U.S. Dollar and forty other countries from 1913 to 1999.

  3. EH.NET What was the Exchange Rate Then? -- http://eh.net/hmit/exchangerates/

    Shows the exchange rate between a selected foreign currency and the U.S. Dollar for a given year or range of years.

  4. Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) Exchange Rates -- http://research.stlouisfed.org/fred2/categories/15

    Provides weekly and monthly data since 1971 on the exchange rate between selected foreign currencies and the U.S. Dollar.

  5. IFS Online -- http://www.asu.edu/lib/resources/db/ifs.htm

    Includes monthly, quarterly, and annual exchange rates in U.S. Dollars for the currencies of all countries monitored by the International Monetary Fund. Data coverage extends back to 1948.

  6. International Institute of Social History: Prices and Wages -- http://www.iisg.nl/hpw/data.html

  7. Journal of Economic History
    “Dollar-Sterling Mint Parity and Exchange Rates, 1791-1834.” -- https://login.ezproxy1.lib.asu.edu/login?url=http://www.jstor.org/view/00220507/di975664/97p1024c/13?
    frame=noframe&userID=81db6e56@asu.edu/01cce4403700501725a95&dpi=3&config=jstor
    (JSTOR subscription access limited to current ASU faculty, staff, and students)

    “Foreign Interest Rates in American Financial Markets: A Revised Series of Dollar-Sterling Exchange Rates, 1835-1900.” -- https://login.ezproxy1.lib.asu.edu/login?url=http://www.jstor.org/view/00220507/di975643/97p14904/18?
    frame=frame&userID=81dbf4d5@asu.edu/01cce4403700501b99c0c&dpi=3&config=jstor
    (JSTOR subscription access limited to current ASU faculty, staff, and students)

  8. NBER Macrohistory Database -- http://www.nber.org/databases/macrohistory/contents/

    Covers mostly U.S. data series but also includes some for the U.K., Germany, and France.

  9. Pacific Exchange Rate Service -- http://fx.sauder.ubc.ca/

    Includes historic annual average exchange rates since 1948 for various currencies against the U.S. Dollar, Canadian Dollar, and U.K. Pound.


    United States


  10. ACCRA Cost of Living Index (see Section 2: Average Prices) -- HD6983 .C6725 Hayden Reference Desk (older years in Hayden Journals) and on SRI microfiche no. A 0150-1 in Hayden Microforms since 1993.

    Covers average prices for the consumer goods ACCRA utilized to determine the cost of living index in 298 U.S. cities from 1976 to the present. Note: Early years found under call no. HD6983 .672x in Hayden Journals.

  11. Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis: What is a dollar worth? -- http://minneapolisfed.org/research/data/us/calc/

    Uses CPI to convert the dollar value of goods in one year to the value it would have in another specified year. Covers 1913 to present. Also offers estimated table of CPI values 1800 to the present for making manual conversion calculations.

  12. Historical Statistics of the United States: Colonial Times to 1970 -- HA202 .A38 1975 Hayden Reference and C3.134:H 62/1789-1970 Hayden US Documents (located on Table 1A)
    Part 1. -- http://www2.census.gov/prod2/statcomp/documents/CT1970p1-01.pdf
    Part 2. -- http://www2.census.gov/prod2/statcomp/documents/CT1970p2-01.pdf

  13. How Much is That in Real Money?: A Historical Price Index for Use as a deflator of Money Values in the Economy of the United States -- HB235 .U6 M39 Hayden Stacks

    Includes prices for the United States from 1700-1991 and for Great Britain from 1600-1991 as well as American Revolutionary War currency depreciation tables.

  14. Inflation Conversion Factors for Dollars -- http://oregonstate.edu/dept/pol_sci/fac/sahr/sahr.htm

    Provides conversion factor tables, but below the tables, there is also a chart section covering miscellaneous other cost comparisons and prices for gasoline and gold.

  15. The Value of a Dollar: Prices and Incomes in the United States, 1860-1999 -- HB235 .U6 V35x 1999 Hayden Doc Reference (located on Table 1A)

  16. The Writer's Guide to Everyday Life in Colonial America -- E162 .T26 1997 Hayden Stacks

  17. The Writer's Guide to Everyday Life in the Wild West -- F596 .M694 1999 Hayden Stacks


    Central & South America


  18. Price Trends of Some Basic Commodities in Central Mexico, 1531-1570 -- F1401 .I22 v.40 1958 Hayden Stacks


    Europe

  20.   21. Life in a Noble Household: 1641-1700 -- DA377.2 .R8 T45 1959 Hayden Stacks

  22. Money, Prices, and Civilization in the Mediterranean World, Fifth to Seventeenth Century -- HG243 .C5 Hayden Stacks

  23. Money, Prices, and Foreign Exchange in Fourteenth-Century France -- HG976 .M5 Hayden Stacks

  24. Prices and Wages in England from the Twelfth to the Nineteenth Century -- HB235 .G7 B4x Hayden Stacks

  25. Prices, Food and Wages in Scotland, 1550-1780 -- HB235 .S4 Hayden Stacks

  26. The Writer's Guide to Everyday Life in Regency and Victorian England, from 1811-1901 -- DA533 .H93 1998 Hayden Stacks


    Middle East


  27. Histoire des Prix et des Salaires dans l'Orient Medieval -- HB235 .N15 A8 Hayden Stacks
