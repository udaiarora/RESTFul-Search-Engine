Style Guide for Arizona State University


Arizona State University is a dynamic, leading university focused on innovation, high quality teaching and unparalleled service to the community. It is growing at a rapid pace to serve the education, social, and economic development needs of metropolitan Phoenix, with high quality schools and programs spread throughout the city. This Style Guide is an important reference tool for the university community and our external constituents in referring to and using the name of the university and its many programs in a consistent manner. This consistent usage is critical as we move forward to advance the university on a local, regional, national and international scale.

One University in Many Places

The official name of the university is Arizona State University. All programs at all locations are unified under one name, Arizona State University.

The ASU logo and signatures are used in all instances to graphically represent the university and all of its units, locations and events according to the guidelines in the ASU Graphic Standards Manual (www.asu.edu/gsm/).

ASU is moving to a school-centric model that places greater emphasis on schools than on campus distinction. The ASU Logo or Signature is now used wherever ASU logos with campus distinctions have been used, including business system, publications, web and electronic use, signage, etc.

When referencing one of ASU’s campuses, please follow these guidelines:

Campuses are referred to as one of ASU’s locations, as in

  • Arizona State University at the:
    • Tempe campus
    • West campus
    • Polytechnic campus
    • Downtown Phoenix campus

      NOT: ASU West, ASU Polytechnic, etc.
  • The c in campus is lowercase in all cases when the word campus is spelled out and refers to one of ASU’s locations.
  • Emphasis should always be given to the university and/or the school or college. Location is a secondary matter and should not denote status or importance.
  • For internal communications, or second or later references in a publication, campus names may be shortened to Tempe, West, Poly or DPC; however, such abbreviation should be carefully considered, since it may confuse the reader or weaken the impact of the narrative.

When writing about the university, please follow these guidelines:

  • In first reference, use the full name of the university: Arizona State University.
  • In subsequent references, authorized forms are ASU and the university. Do not shorten to Arizona State.

Example: Arizona State University consistently attracts top quality students from Arizona and across the nation. Students enrolling at the university have access to faculty who are elite in their fields of study.

When writing about colleges and schools within ASU, please follow these guidelines:

  • On first reference, the full name of the university and college/school is preferred.
  • College and school names should be linked directly to the name of the university, not a campus name or location: the ASU College of Nursing; the ASU Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, Arizona State University’s Herberger College of Fine Arts.

When writing about or introducing a member of the university community, their relationship with the university is of primary importance. When reference to the unit’s location is necessary, it may be added as follows:

  • ASU Professor John Doe of the College of __________
  • Professor Jane Doe of the ASU College of _______ at the Tempe campus
  • ASU Vice President and Provost of the West campus Mark Searle
  • Gerald Jakubowski, vice president and provost of ASU’s Polytechnic campus.

When answering the telephone for an ASU unit, please follow the examples below:

  • “Arizona State University, may I help you?”
  • “Arizona State University College of Liberal Arts & Sciences…”
  • “ASU’s (name of college, school, or unit), may I help you?” The campus location is not necessary.

These guidelines are fully effective as of July 1, 2005.
