April 24, 2003

College of Education to host symposium on bilingualism

More than 600 educators and scholars representing six continents will gather at ASU for the fourth International Symposium on Bilingualism (ISB), to be held April 30 through May 3 in the Memorial Union.

ISB is widely regarded as the premier international forum for the discussion and dissemination of research on bilingualism. It regularly attracts the most visible members of the field in colloquia, paper presentations and keynote addresses.

Featured keynote presenters at ISB4 will include Fred Genesee of McGill University in Montreal, Canada, who will deliver an address titled "Bilingual Acquisition: Exploring the Limits of the Language Faculty."

"This conference will be the most significant scientific meeting on bilingualism in North America ever," Genesee said.

Also delivering a keynote address will be Bernard Spolsky of Bar Ilan University, Israel. "Can Language be Managed?" is the question to be posed by Spolsky in his address.

Loraine Obler of City University of New York Graduate School Center, will discuss "Brain and the Dynamics of Bilingualism," while Ana Celia Zentella of the University of California, San Diego will lead conference attendees in the exploration of the "Bilingual Bonds: Latin@ Resistance to Racist Discourse" during her keynote address.

"It is an honor for ASU to be selected as the site for the fourth International Symposium on Bilingualism, the first time the event will be held in the United States," said Jeff MacSwan, assistant professor of curriculum and instruction with the College of Education and chairman of the organizing committee for ISB4. "Participants are coming to Tempe for the symposium from nearly 50 countries to discuss research on a variety of topics in bilingualism," he said.

"It's ironic that the event is taking place in Arizona, where education policy in particular has made every effort to close the door on has made every effort to close the door on bilingualism," MacSwan said. "Educators and scholars from every continent are coming here to talk not only about psychological, linguistic and anthropological research on bilingualism, but also about political efforts to extinguish it."

The National Science Foundation and the National Institute of Health served as co-sponsors of the event, along with others.

ISB began at the initiative of Li Wei and Nick Miller of the University of Newcastle, who organized ISB1 in 1997 and ISB2 in 1999.

The event moved to the University of the West of England in 2001, where Jeanine Treffers-Daller served as the chair of the organizing committee for ISB3. In 2005, ISB5 will be organized in Barcelona by Melissa Greer Moyer of the Universitat Autonoma de Barcelona. ISB6 is scheduled for Hamburg, Germany in 2007, where Jürgen Meisel will organize the event at Universität Hamburg.

Sherwood, with the College of Education, can be reached at (480) 965-2114 or (joan.sherwood@asu.edu).
