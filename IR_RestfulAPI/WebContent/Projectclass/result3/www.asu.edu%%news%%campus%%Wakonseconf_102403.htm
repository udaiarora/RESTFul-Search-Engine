October 24, 2003

Wakonse-like conference comes to ASU

Each May, after grades are turned in and good-byes are said, several dozen ASU faculty members head together for the hills - Camp Tontozona, to be exact.

They're on their way to a working vacation - a long weekend devoted to learning about teaching, and sharing ideas and stories, where they're joined by colleagues from Northern Arizona University, the University of Arizona and Valley community colleges.

The weekend is called Wakonse - a Lakota Sioux word that refers to the inspirational nature of the teaching and learning enterprise.Each day ends with a musical program presented by California musician Wiley Rankin, and the weekend culminates with a Chautauqua - a talent show of sorts - where faculty are the performers.

On Oct. 30, a little bit of Wakonse will be brought to ASU through a conference, "Diversity and the New American University: The Interplay of Teaching, Research and Service," and "Chautauqua Under the Stars" with Rankin.

The free conference, open to the ASU community and the public, is sponsored by the Center for Learning and Teaching Excellence, Wakonse-Arizona and the Intergroup Relations Center.

The conference will take place from 8:30 a.m. to 4 p.m. in the Ventana Room of the Memorial Union, with Yolanda T. Moses as keynote speaker.

Moses is outgoing president of the American Association for Higher Education and a senior consultant for diversity and excellence in the chancellor's office at the University of California, Riverside.

The conference topic, "A Vision for Teaching in the New American University," will be presented by ASU President Michael Crow.

"Chautauqua Under the Stars" will begin at 6 p.m. on the third floor terrace of the Student Services building.

For information about Wakonse-Arizona and the conference, call Laura Bush at (480) 965-5620, or go to (http://clte.asu.edu/wakonse). To register for the conference, go to (http://www.asu.edu/provost/intergroup/).

Smith, with Public Relations, can be reached at (480) 965-4821 or (jps@asu.edu).
