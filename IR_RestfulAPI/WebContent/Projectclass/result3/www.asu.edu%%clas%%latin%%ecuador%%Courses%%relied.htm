IED 498/598: Teaching Indigenous Cultures
REL 494/591: Teaching Indigenous Cultures

This course is designed to help educators become more effective teachers of indigenous history and culture in their classrooms.

Content goals:

  • To gain direct exposure to Runa culture in the areas of history, literature, dance, music, and the arts by observing Runa life and by listening to Runa interpretations of their lives.
  • To learn strategies for presenting native culture comparatively in the classroom.
  • To become sensitive to difficult topics in teaching indigenous history to a diverse classroom. How do you teach about indigenous religion? How do you teach about genocide? How do you handle questions of intellectual property?

Outcome goals:

  • To develop a course plan for presenting Runa culture in the classroom.
  • To develop a collection of multimedia curricular materials: slides, videos, recordings, etc., for classroom use.

Course assignments:

  • Read all required readings before leaving the U.S.
  • Complete a Curriculum Development Project: Each participant will develop multimedia lesson plans for integrating a unit on Andean/Amazonian culture into an existing course. The course design can be for any level from K-12 through post-secondary education. It can be for a social studies class, a history class, a course on rainforest culture and environment, etc.

Grading: Curriculum Development Project for 75%, participation for 25%. The participation portion of the grade is based on such factors as contribution to group discussions and being prepared for class by doing the reading.
