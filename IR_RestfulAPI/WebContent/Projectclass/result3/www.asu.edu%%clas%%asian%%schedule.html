Perspectives on Chinese Art:
New Approaches and Reflections on Forty Years of Scholarship

A Conference in Honor of Dr. Chu-tsing Li,
Professor Emeritus, University of Kansas
November 4-5, 2005
Friday sessions to be held at Arizona State University, Tempe, Arizona
Saturday sessions to be held at Phoenix Art Museum, Phoenix, Arizona

Schedule of Events
November 3, 2005

Friday, November 4, 2005
Arizona State University
ASU Coor Hall L1-88

8:00 AM coffee and tea

8:30-10:00 AM
Chair: Claudia Brown, Arizona State University
Art and Religion: Daoist Themes in Chinese Painting

Ling-en Lu, University of Kansas/The Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art, "Painting for the Temple Community: A Ming Dynasty Case Study"

Youmi Kim Efurd, University of Kansas, "Shifting Categories: The Identification of Daoist Paintings in Texts from Pre-Modern China"

Chun-yi Lee, Arizona State University, "Daoist Immortality Practice: Rereading Shen Zhou's Night Sitting"

10:15 - 11:45 AM
Chair: Ju-hsi Chou, Arizona State University

From Brush to Books: Celebrating Dr. Chu-tsing Li’s Forty Years of Scholarship and his Influences on Contemporary Artists, introduction by Qunying Li, Arizona State University
