July 10, 2003

Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU: Most accurate employment forecasters honored

Anne Ramstetter Wenzel, founder of the San Francisco research firm Econosystems, and two other leading economists have been honored for their job growth forecasting accuracy by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University.

Also honored were Mike Daniels of the South Carolina Employment Security Commission, Columbia, S.C., and Rajeev Dhawan of Georgia State University, Atlanta, Georgia. The three are on a panel of 25 economists who contribute to the National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity.

"This year the panel had a real challenge to forecast these particular indicators, with job growth sagging, productivity on the rise in many sectors, and private industry trying to hold the line on employment costs," said Lee McPheters director of the Bank One Economic Outlook Center.

The National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, published quarterly in the Blue Chip Job Growth Update, is the only consensus forecast of job growth in the nation. The forecast was assembled three years ago by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center to provide key economic information to corporations, government, economic development agencies and the public.

Awards were presented for accuracy overall, and accuracy in each of the forecast's component parts. Photos of the three honorees can be downloaded at wpcarey.asu.edu/newsroom:

Most Accurate Forecast Overall
Most Accurate Forecast in Nonfarm Business Productivity
Anne Ramstetter Wenzel

Wenzel, who was honored in two categories, worked for 14 years as an economist for the Chemical Economics handbook Program at SRI Consulting in Menlo Park, CA prior to founding Econosystems in April 1999. Specializing in environmental economics, she holds a bachelor's and a master's degree in economics from San Francisco State University.

The Nonfarm Business Productivity forecast is the output per hour of all persons, as released by the Bureau of Labor Statistics. With public interest in productivity growing in recent years, increases in output per hour have been recognized as important indicators of economic progress and a means to higher income levels.

Most Accurate Forecast of Total Nonfarm Employment
Rajeev Dhawan

Professor Rajeev Dhawan has been the director of the Georgia State University Economic Forecasting Center for three years. Prior to that he had been the director of econometric forecasting at UCLA, authored the quarterly forecast for the U.S. and California economy and directed economic impact studies including those concerning the loss of aerospace jobs in the early 1990's on California economy.

The Total Nonfarm Employment Forecast is typically referred to as job growth in media reports. The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics works with state employment agencies to collect data each month on employment, hours and earnings from a sample of non-farm establishments.

Most Accurate Forecast of the Employment Cost Index
James M. Daniels II

James M. Daniels II is a research and planning administrator with the labor market information department of the South Carolina Employment Security Commission where he serves as the supervisor of special research. He oversees the production of the monthly labor force press release, the agency's quarterly consumer confidence survey and the monthly state economic indicators as published in South Carolina Economic Indicators, produced in conjunction with the University of South Carolina's Division Research.

The Employment Cost Index is a forecast of quarterly changes in labor and benefit costs in addition to salary, based on the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics data.


The Bank One Economic Outlook Center is part of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. The institute is an affiliation of eight research centers that serves as a link between the local, state, national and international business communities and the creative and intellectual resources of the nationally ranked W. P. Carey School.
