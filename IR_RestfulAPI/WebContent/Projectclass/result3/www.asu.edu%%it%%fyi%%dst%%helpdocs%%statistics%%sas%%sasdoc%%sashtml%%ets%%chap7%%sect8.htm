Notation for Seasonal Models

ARIMA models for time series with regular seasonal fluctuations often use differencing operators and autoregressive and moving average parameters at lags that are multiples of the length of the seasonal cycle. When all the terms in an ARIMA model factor refer to lags that are a multiple of a constant s, the constant is factored out and suffixed to the ARIMA(p,d,q) notation.

Thus, the general notation for the order of a seasonal ARIMA model with both seasonal and nonseasonal factors is ARIMA(p,d,q)×(P,D,Q)s. The term (p,d,q) gives the order of the nonseasonal part of the ARIMA model; the term (P,D,Q)s gives the order of the seasonal part. The value of s is the number of observations in a seasonal cycle: 12 for monthly series, 4 for quarterly series, 7 for daily series with day-of-week effects, and so forth.
