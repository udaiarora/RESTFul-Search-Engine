January 15, 2003

Nursing college loses associate dean

Pamela Kidd, associate dean for graduate programs and research for the College of Nursing, passed away Dec. 25, 2002.

Prior to joining ASU in the fall semester of 2000, Kidd served as the director of the Kentucky Injury Prevention & Research Center at the University of Kentucky. College of Nursing Dean Barbara Durand said Kidd was instrumental in many of the college's programs.

"I think she was the greatest gift to the College of Nursing, during my tenure here," Durand said. "She brought an energy and an initiative that we hadn't seen before. Her experience and view of the world were exactly what we needed as we were going into the new millennium."

Durand said Kidd's vision to expand people's perception of the nursing field was often demonstrated in her work.

"She was a seasoned and funded researcher," Durand said. "She was very interested in interdisciplinary activities and had begun some collaborations that we had not thought of before."

Kidd's most recent collaborative research effort was shared with Howard Bashford, associate professor in the Del E. Webb School of Construction, examining the effectiveness of pre-assembled roof structural components in reducing costs and lost work time related to injuries sustained in building the roof assembly for single dwelling, detached, residential homes. The project was funded through the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH).

Kidd earned a post-masters degree in the Family Nurse Practitioner Program at the University of Kentucky in 1996; a Ph.D in nursing from the University of Arizona in 1989; an MSN from the University of Cincinnati in 1982; and a BSN from the University of Kentucky in 1978.

Despite her busy schedule, Durand said Kidd always made time for her colleagues and students.

"She always found time on a weekly basis to see patients at the clinic that our college operates," Durand said, "and she became a mentor to a number of our junior faculty.

"She always was looking ahead and she always had ideas," Durand said. "She was simply spectacular."

Pamela Kidd is survived by her husband, Estol Lee Kidd Jr.; son, Elliott; daughter, Elizabeth Leigh; and brother, Ronald Stinson. Funeral services were held Dec. 27 in Kentucky.

Memorials may be made to the American Cancer Society. The College of Nursing is having a memorial service at 2 p.m., Jan. 24, in the Business College C-wing, room 116. For information call the College of Nursing at (480) 965-3244.\

Romero, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-9689 or (mlromero@asu.edu).
