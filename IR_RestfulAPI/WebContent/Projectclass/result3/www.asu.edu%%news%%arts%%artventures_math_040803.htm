April 8, 2003

ArtVentures in Engineering workshop invites participants to explore pattern, symmetry

How do artists use math? How do mathematicians use art? Participants will explore the fascinating and often beautiful intersection of these two seemingly divergent fields from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. on April 26 at the free workshop, ArtVentures in Engineering: Pattern and Symmetry. The ongoing ArtVentures in Engineering community workshop series is sponsored by Arizona State University's Herberger College of Fine Arts, ASU's Women in Applied Science and Engineering, and Target Stores.

The Pattern and Symmetry Workshop is being hosted by the Heard Museum, 2301 N. Central Ave., Phoenix (between Thomas and McDowell).

Artists, Native American artisans, scientists and engineers will lead interactive presentations at 10 a.m., 11 a.m., noon and 1 p.m. on artistic applications in math, including pattern, symmetry, fractals and tessellations. Participants will be invited to join in hands-on explorations, activities and crafts throughout the workshop.

Workshop leaders are Gayla Chandler, a math artist and specialist in using art as a tool to make math more appealing to children; Robert Fathauer, mathematical artist, visiting professor of engineering at ASU and consultant with the Jet Propulsion Laboratory; Kerry Mitchell, fractal-focused digital artist, mathematician and engineer; Barbara Ornelas, Navajo bead artist; Sierra Ornelas, Navajo rug weaver; and

Vikram Sami, architect and specialist in three-dimensional math models and art.

Pre-registration is required by April 18, 480-727-6762. Participation is limited to 150 people, ages 10 and up. Youth must be accompanied by an adult. Lunch may be purchased in the museum's Ironwood Café.

WHAT: ArtVentures in Engineering: Pattern and Symmetry Workshop
WHEN: 10 a.m.-2 p.m., Saturday, April 26
WHERE: Heard Museum, 2301 N. Central Ave., Phoenix (between Thomas and
McDowell)
ADMISSION: Free; pre-registration required by April 18, 480-727-6762
