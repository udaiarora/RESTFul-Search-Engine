February 1, 2005

‘Desert Edge Duo’ takes stage Feb. 4

The Desert Edge Duo, composed of percussionist J.B. Smith and Robert Spring , clarinet, fuse rhythm and melody at 7:30 p.m , Feb. 4, in ASU’s Evelyn Smith Music Theatre.

Smith and Spring have performed together throughout North America and Europe since 1989. They are dedicated to tackling new music, and they will perform three works they have commissioned: ASU faculty Glenn Hackbarth’s “Points in the Sky” (with interactive electronics); Eric Mandat’s “Three for Two”; and Smith’s “In Light of Three.”

The evening’s program also will feature Libby Larsen’s “Corker,” Robert Schietroma’s “Dialogues,” Ed Miller’s “Going Home” and Chester Mais’ “Prelude and Licks.”

Spring has been described as “one of this country’s most sensitive and talented clarinetists” by the Arizona Republic , and “a formidable soloist … played with great emotional life” by Politiken, based in Copenhagen , Denmark . Other accolades include Fanfare Magazine’s praise of a new CD, “Tarantelle”: “This recording was meant to amaze – and, man, it succeeds.”

Similarly, Smith has led a varied and rich career, including serving as principal percussionist with Ensemble 21, a contemporary music group under the direction of Arthur Weisberg, and as principal percussionist with the Daniel Lentz Group, which performed at the Interlink Festival in Los Angeles, the Bang on a Can Festival in New York and the Festival Musica Visual in Lanzarote, Canary Islands. He also has recorded and performed with the internationally acclaimed Summit Brass and the Phoenix Symphony Orchestra.

Tickets to the concert cost $5 and are available at the door. For more information, call (480) 965-8863.

Bell, with the Herberger College of Fine Arts, can be reached at (480) 965-2817 or (rebecca.bell@asu.edu).
