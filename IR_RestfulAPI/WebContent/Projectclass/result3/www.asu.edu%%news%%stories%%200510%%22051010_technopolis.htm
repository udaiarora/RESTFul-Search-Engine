October 10, 2005

ASU Technopolis boosts remote entrepreneurs

ASU is expanding its efforts to provide entrepreneurial education programs anywhere – and rural entrepreneurs can now receive the same comprehensive services as if they were “right down the street.”

Through ASU Technopolis, the university’s initiative that provides education, coaching and connections to innovators and entrepreneurs, creative approaches in entrepreneurial coaching are available and becoming more in demand.

Mad Dog Software, a Lake Havasu, Ariz., firm, just completed one of ASU Technopolis’ signature programs, called Launch Pad, almost entirely through a series of remote – but structured – entrepreneurial coaching sessions.

Rick Murphy, the chief executive officer of Mad Dog Software, offers his praise for the program.

“The ASU Technopolis program afforded me the opportunity to get expert counsel that I’m convinced will take my venture to the next level,” he says. “The coaching sessions were efficient, rigorous and allowed me to get direction that I didn’t realize would be so imperative. The flexibility in not having to be ‘in town’ or somewhere in person was key for me and made this experience a successful relationship.”

“Rural entrepreneurship initiatives are on the rise, and ASU Technopolis is committed to providing quality, custom services for these entrepreneurs regardless of location” says Terree Wasley, ASU Technopolis’ director. “There are no barriers.”

Rural economies can reap benefits by entrepreneurial education approaches such as this. Rural entrepreneurs can tap into “outside” collaborative expertise in remote ways while enjoying rural residences and locations. Ultimately, they then can contribute to and build their “local-rural” knowledge economies. This scenario is a win-win for state and local economic development strategies.

For more information, visit the Web site (www.asutechnopolis.org).
