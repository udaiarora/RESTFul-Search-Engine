Employee Pay and Work Hours
WORK SCHEDULE EXAMPLES—
9-DAY/80-HOUR, TWO-WEEK SCHEDULE

Here is an example of a typical 9-day/80-hour, two-week schedule:

 

Week 1 1st MON TUE WED THR FRI 2nd MON Work Week
  Work Week starts at 12:01 p.m.         Work Week ends at noon  
               
Hours Worked 4 9 9 9 9 Off 4 hours 40 Hours Worked; 4 Hours Off

 

Week 2 2nd MON TUES WED THR FRI 3rd MON Work Week
Work Week starts at 12:01 p.m. Work Week ends at noon
               
Hours Worked Off 4 hours 9 9 9 9 4 40 Hours Worked, 4 Hours Off

When the day off for a non-exempt employee changes, the work week must be changed accordingly. Non-exempt employees cannot "switch" or "trade" days off unless their work week is changed. Time off due to holidays, vacations, sick leave and other time not worked, either with or without pay, is not counted as time worked for overtime purposes. Only time actually worked during a work-week is counted as time worked for overtime compensation purposes.

Refer to SPP 404-04 Overtime-General Policy for further clarification.
