March 10, 2005

Student earns endocrinology award for work

ASU student Tiara Williams, a junior in the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences, has received a national award from the Endocrine Society for an outstanding project related to endocrinology.

Her research project, “Comparative Analysis of Pancreatic Islet Mass in Diabetic Mice,” was presented at the Annual Biomedical Research Conference for Minority Students in Dallas.

Williams co-authored the project with University of Arizona microbiology and immunology researchers Dominick DeLuca and Chris Woods. She is majoring in microbiology in the department of integrated natural sciences at ASU’s West campus.

The project, which compared pancreatic beta cells from non-obese diabetic mice with a control group of mice, was designed to help understand the physiological processes involved in insulin production in mice. The characteristics of the particular breed of mice used in the research have contributed to more than 125 methods that prevented or delayed Type 1 diabetes – and thus are of great interest in the study of juvenile diabetes in humans.

Williams says she prepared to contribute to this research by participating in an intensive five-week workshop at the West campus. It is designed to help minority students establish careers in biomedical research.

The Bridges to Biomedical Careers program, funded by a grant from the National Institutes of Health (NIH), provides tuition waivers to outstanding minority students.

“I’m grateful to have had the opportunity to participate in this important research, particularly because it has tremendous implications for the health and well-being of future generations – not only African-Americans, but all people,” Williams says.

Williams intends to continue her studies in microbiology at the doctorate level after she graduates from ASU. She participated in the pancreatic research as part of the NIH-funded Minority Health Disparities Summer Research Program at the University of Arizona.
