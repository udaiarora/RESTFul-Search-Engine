Leslie DeLeon

How Land Development Can Affect the
Environment in Paradise Valley, Arizona

ABSTRACT

By giving careful consideration to where development occurs, city and county officials and developers can plan projects that will protect sensitive areas and direct growth and development to areas best suited for industrial, commercial, and residential uses. Property owners and developers can also help protect natural resources by giving sensible consideration to how developments are planned and built.

This research will map the homes in one of the most affluent areas in Arizona, Paradise Valley in terms of their location on highly elevated lands. The results are aimed at supporting the eventual eradication of this type of development because of potential dangerous environmental issues such as landslides etc. 

The research will first describe and analyze the factors that influence the cash value of properties in Paradise Valley, Arizona. It uses a geographically weighted regression (GWR) model to determine whether or not the hypothesis that the size of the yard space, per capita income of an individual as well as the elevation of the land on which it is built significantly affects the property value is true. 

This information is important to property owners as well as to local governments since wherever development occurs and how developments are planned and built has very significant consequences to natural resources and the environment. In addition to the disruption of the functioning of natural systems, such development can also be life threatening.
