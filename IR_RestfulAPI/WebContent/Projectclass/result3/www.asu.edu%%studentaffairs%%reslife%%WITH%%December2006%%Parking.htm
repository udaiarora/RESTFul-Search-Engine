IMPORTANT SOUTH CAMPUS PARKING NOTIFICATION

ATTENTION SOUTH CAMPUS RESIDENTS:

The demand for on-campus housing has continued to grow each year. This includes building new residential facilities and working in public-private partnerships at all four ASU campuses.

As the university continues to advance its agenda of access, excellence, and impact, more students are and will continue to want to take advantage of the many opportunities at ASU. Because of the demand for an ASU education, ASU continues to explore housing options that meet students' residential needs and that contribute to the student's academic success at ASU.

In an effort to keep up with the growth, the university is moving forward with the South Campus Residential Community development plan, located south of Apache Boulevard. This new residential community will serve upper-division and graduate students. Located on the south portion of the Tempe campus, the new community will add more than 1,800 beds in apartment style buildings. Construction for the South Campus residential community will begin next month (January 2007). The first phase of the project is scheduled for completion by Fall 2008. As a result, this project will impact parking on the south campus area beginning January 2, 2007.

Parking Lots 38, 39, Motorcycle Lots 18, 30, 31, and 33, along with a portion of Lot 37 will no longer be available after Dec. 31. This will impact decal holders who are assigned Lot 37, 39, and 17/35/38 decals. Additional surface motorcycle parking will be available in Lot 7.

If you currently hold a Lot 37 decal , there will be limited parking remaining in the southernmost section of the lot; near Sahuaro Hall, Sonora Center and toward the railroad tracks.

If you currently hold a Lot 17/35/38 decal , parking will still be available in Lots 17E and 35. However, these areas do not have enough spaces to accommodate all currently assigned decal holders.

Due to the diminished capacity or complete loss of the parking lots noted above, Residence Hall decal holders assigned to these locations will have an opportunity to move to alternate locations which include Lot 70 (northwest corner of Apache and Rural Road) and the Rooftop of Parking Structure 4. There are a limited number of decals available. Exchanges will be handled on a first-come, first-served basis.

Beginning Monday, Dec. 11, please exchange or return your decal, or pick up a free bus pass at the Decal Sales office, located at the Towers Building, from 7:30 a.m. – 4:30 p.m., Monday through Friday. We strongly encourage those who live at Hassayampa Academic Village and Adelphi Commons to relocate to the Rooftop of Parking Structure 4 or Lot 70.

With the loss of parking on campus, there is not enough space to relocate everyone nearby. Therefore, we encourage students to evaluate additional options versus parking a vehicle on campus, such as:

  • Utilize the free ASU U-Pass, which grants unlimited access to all Valley Metro bus routes
  • Campus transportation – available Monday through Friday via the FLASH and McAllister Shuttles
  • Carpooling or riding a bicycle

Please note: If you decide not to park your vehicle next semester (Spring 2007), you may return your decal for a prorated refund.

Again, beginning Monday, Dec. 11, please exchange or return your decal, or pick up a free bus pass at the Decal Sales office, located at the Towers Building, from 7:30 a.m. – 4:30 p.m., Monday through Friday.

Fencing around the construction site will commence the week of Jan 1. If you are leaving the residence halls for winter break please do not leave your vehicles parked in any of the affected locations or they will be subject to relocation once construction begins. (Please see map attached)
