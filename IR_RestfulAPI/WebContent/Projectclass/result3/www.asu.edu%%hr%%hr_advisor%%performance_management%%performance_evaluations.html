What is a performance evaluation?
A performance evaluation is the formal written performance assessment of the job. It is not only an annual event, but an on-going process that reviews the feedback provided on whether the standards and expectations of the job are being met.

The performance evaluation is a written form of communication between the supervisor and the employee.
See also Performance Management: Coaching

When are formal written performance evaluations given?
Performance evaluations are recommended to be given to probationary classified employees after the 3rd month and the 5th month of employment. An evaluation is to be completed for all regular employees on an annual basis including written assessment and comments.

Then you must assign one of the following overall ratings:
1 (Needs improvement)
2 (Meets expectations)
3 (Exceeds expectations).

Is there a performance evaluation form?
Each department may create their own evaluation.

Generic examples are available:
Classified Staff Performance Review
Classified Staff Self Evaluation

What if the employee does not agree with the given performance evaluation and/or rating?
There is an appeal process outlined in the performance evaluation policy (see list at right) if discrepancies can not be resolved between the employee and the first line supervisor.
