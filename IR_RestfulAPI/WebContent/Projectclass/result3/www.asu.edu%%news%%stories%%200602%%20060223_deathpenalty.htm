February 23, 2006

Death penalty forum draws host of speakers

Everyone seems to have an opinion about the death penalty.

A conference at ASU March 4 will look at Arizona's death penalty to see how it is functioning.

The conference, titled " Arizona 's Death Penalty in 2006," is sponsored by the ASU School of Justice and Social Inquiry, Arizona Death Penalty Forum, Catholic Charities and the Coalition of Arizonans to Abolish the Death Penalty.

The conference will be held in the Maricopa Room of the Memorial Union on the Tempe campus.

Doris Marie Provine, director of the ASU School of Justice and Social Inquiry, will welcome attendees at 9:30 a.m.

Speakers include:

. 10 a.m. - Thomas Zlacket, former chief justice of the Arizona Supreme Court, who will reflect on his personal evolution concerning the death penalty while serving on the Arizona Supreme Court.

. 11:15 a.m. - Psychologist Katherine Norgard speaks on a mother's thoughts of what it's like having a son on death row.

. 1 p.m. - Deborah Fleischaker and Sigmund Popko of the American Bar Association will speak on the death penalty in Arizona and elsewhere.

A legislative panel at 2 p.m. concludes the day.

"The conference offers a special opportunity to the American Bar Association researchers to share with the public its findings regarding how well or poorly the Arizona death penalty functions in this state," says Rudy Gerber, a lecturer in the School of Justice and Social Inquiry in ASU's College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, and one of the event organizers.

Registration fee is $10, which includes lunch if reservations are made by Feb. 25. Students can attend free, with lunch included, if reservations are received by Feb. 25. Walk-ins will be accepted.

For more information contact Lynn Leonardo at (602) 285-1999 or (lleonardo@cc-az.org).
