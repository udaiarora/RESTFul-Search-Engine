ACD 517–01: Student Employment Program

Purpose

To provide eligible students with employment opportunities through the university

Source

Student Financial Assistance—Student Employment

Applicability

Undergraduate students

Graduate students whose duties are not primarily teaching and research as required by the Division of Graduate Studies

Note:The Division of Graduate Studies establishes and maintains all appropriate policies and practices for graduate teaching and research assistants and associates.

Policy

Student Employment assists students in locating on- and off-campus part-time employment during their enrollment at ASU. Student Employment is part of Student Financial Assistance and functions as a student personnel office, providing job advertisement and referral services, eligibility verification, student compensation administration, payroll verification, and policy interpretation. Students may apply for on-campus and off-campus jobs throughout the year.

The office administers the student compensation plan and provides information to employers concerning wage levels, position classification, and change-of-level requests. Assistance is provided in filling student positions by advertising job vacancies and referring eligible students to employers. Departments with position vacancies must list these positions with the Student Employment Office.

Student Employment maintains job information on bulletin boards located in the Student Services Building. New job listings are posted daily, Monday through Friday, except holidays. The office promotes sound employment practices by ensuring a fair and equitable employment process.

Employment counselors are available to assist students in the development of job hunting or seeking skills. Students are encouraged to explore career options and career pathing in their Student Employment experiences.

Questions regarding student Personnel Action forms (PAFs), time cards, and related forms should be directed to Human Resources.

Cross-References

For information about employing international students, see ACD 517–02, “Employment of Nonimmigrant International Students.”

For detailed information about student employment eligibility criteria, see the University Student Initiatives Policies and Procedures Manual— USI 304–01, “Student Employment Program.”
