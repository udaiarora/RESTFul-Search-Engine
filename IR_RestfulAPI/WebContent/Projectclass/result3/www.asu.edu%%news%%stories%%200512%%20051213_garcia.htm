December 13, 2005

Graduation provides payoff for mom's balancing act

Crystal Garcia is not a typical college student. She is a mom of three boys in her late 30s who decided to go back to school to finish her bachelor’s degree. On Dec. 15, she will be one of the 4,000 ASU students who achieved a major milestone.

Garcia, an elementary education major at the Polytechnic campus, is graduating with a degree that she started to pursue more than 12 years ago. Now she says she is realizing her dream.

“When I started my associate’s degree, I was majoring in elementary education but ended up taking accounting courses, so I graduated with a general studies degree,” Garcia says. “After graduating, I took a job in bookkeeping for about eight years and then became a stay-at-home mom. But teaching was always my first love and choice.”

Completing a bachelor’s degree continued to be Garcia’s goal, and a life-changing event was a great motivator to get her back in the classroom to finish it.

“I realized I needed my college degree to support my family, and it was a great opportunity to be a role model for my children,” she says. “Now they say they plan to attend and graduate from college.”

During her studies, Garcia has been field teaching at various schools in Gilbert and Higley. In November, she received a verbal offer for a position teaching third grade with the Higley School District. She started that job this month.

“I love this grade because students are more independent – and still so excited about learning,” she says.

Garcia is one of 430 students graduating from programs offered at the Polytechnic campus.

The Polytechnic campus will hold a convocation ceremony at 5:30 p.m., Dec. 15, at Wells Fargo Arena, where students will be able to walk across the stage and be recognized by family and friends.

“My children are so proud of me,” she says. “They can’t wait to see me actually graduate.”

In addition to being individually recognized, students will receive a parting message from Craig M. Phelps, provost of A. T. Still University of Health Sciences ’ Mesa campus.

The A. T. Still University is a close neighbor of the Polytechnic campus, says Jerry Jakubowski, ASU provost for the Polytechnic campus.

“He was selected as our convocation speaker because of the mutual desire to have a greater cooperative working relationship between our two institutions, especially as the Polytechnic campus will be developing new degree programs in health technologies,” Jakubowski says.

As provost at A. T. Still, Phelps oversees the development of the campus’ Arizona School of Dentistry & Oral Health, and programs in physical therapy, occupational therapy, physician assistant studies, audiology and athletic training.

In addition, Phelps is a fellowship trained primary care sports medicine physician and board certified in family medicine. He has practiced for more than 20 years in the Phoenix metropolitan area, and he serves as the primary care physician for the NBA’s Phoenix Suns, WNBA’s Phoenix Mercury, physician for Ballet Arizona, team physician for Grand Canyon University, and team physician for Scottsdale Community College, Paradise Valley Community College and Gateway Community College.

For those attending, an early arrival is recommended. For information, contact (480) 727-1140 or visit the Web site (www.east.asu.edu/academics/convocation).
