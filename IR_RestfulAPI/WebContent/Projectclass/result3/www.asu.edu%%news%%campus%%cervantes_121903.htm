December 19, 2003

Cervantes takes on new HR role

David Butler, associate vice president of Human Resources, has named Christine Cervantes director of Human Resources Operations and Administration effective immediately.

Cervantes, an ASU alumna, has served as assistant director of Human Resources at ASU since 1992. Butler describes her as a true asset to the ASU community.

"Christine Cervantes has served ASU very capably with significant assignments in her portfolio, including the Customer Service Center, payroll, employment, employee relations, compensation and benefits," Butler says. "She has provided invaluable leadership during times of transition, and most recently has helped shaped the important tri-university discussion around self insurance."

In her new role, Cervantes will begin the critical task of building a new employment department within Human Resources to meet President Michael Crow's vision to recruit high performing staff and faculty to ASU. She also will provide leadership to several other important human resources initiatives and will serve as a spokesperson for Human Resources as necessary.

Cervantes says words cannot describe the level of enthusiasm she feels concerning this new position.

"I'm honored and ready to do whatever I can to lead the efforts of establishing ASU's office of Human Resources as one of the premier in higher education," Cervantes says. "It will be one that is respected and recognized for our innovation, community partnerships, leadership and diverse staff who are recognized by the university community as knowledgeable and high performing."

Cervantes says she feels fortunate to be surrounded by wonderful staff, faculty and senior administrators who consistently provide her with unconditional support.

"I'm proud of the strides that we have made and of the teamwork, which is demonstrated on a daily basis," she says. "Together, we will make a difference while we strive to meet our vision of becoming a new American university."

In 1978 Cervantes earned a bachelor of science degree in human resources from ASU and plans to continue working toward an MBA at ASU. Before joining ASU, Cervantes worked as a human resources manager for both McDonnell Douglas and Honeywell.
Cervantes is active in a number of ASU organizations including the Commission of the Status of Women, the ASU Retirees Association, the University Committee on Insurance and Retirement Programs, the Chicano Faculty and Staff Association, ASU Public Safety, the Personnel Retirement System, and the Tri-University Health Insurance Work Group.

Cervantes also is very involved in many community and professional organizations including: The Valley of the Sun Human Resources Association, Society for Human Resource Management, the ASU Hispanic Business Alumni (HBA) Association, the ASU Los Diablos, the Arizona Hispanic Chamber of Commerce, and the Phoenix Urban League.

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
