March 13, 2003

Arizona Leading Index rose in January

The Bank One Arizona Index of Leading Economic Indicators rose in January to 118.0. The reading was 0.03 percent above a revised 117.9 for the previous month, and 3.4 percent above the January 2001 number of 114.1 (1987 = 100).

"The basic message from the Leading Index is that the underlying trends for the economy are positive, but non-economic factors could well derail the train," said Tracy Clark, an economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at Arizona State University's W. P. Carey School of Business.

January's Leading Index components are as follows: Positive - the inflation-adjusted value of the M2 money supply, production and employment from the Business Conditions Survey. Negative - the inflation-adjusted value of Maricopa County residential building permits, delivery times, materials inventories and new orders.

The negative impacts from the anxiety over a possible war with Iraq are an increasingly significant drag on the economy, according to Clark. Rising gas prices and general uncertainty have caused auto sales to decline in January despite continued discounting by automakers. "Uncertainty is also postponing the recovery in business spending, which is affecting consumer confidence and consumer spending. The impact on consumer spending is due to the continued weakness in the labor markets and workers' perceptions of the job market."

Clark said that Arizona is likely to suffer reduced tourism activity if international tensions remain high. "Tourism - as measured by bed tax receipts - has been steadily improving, but that trend could reverse as terrorism fears increase. Tourism is also likely to suffer if gas prices remain very high for an extended period.

"The best-case scenario for the economy is either no war with Iraq or a very quick conflict - and in either case, what is going to occur must happen soon. For the state budget crisis, the least disruptive solution to the budget crisis would be an infusion of cash from the federal government and a quick resolution to the Iraq crisis that would allow the economy to recover.

The Bank One Arizona Index of Leading Economic Indicators is based on nine indicators, combined and weighted to project the pace of economic activity several months ahead. Five of the components come from a monthly survey of Arizona purchasing managers conducted by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University.
