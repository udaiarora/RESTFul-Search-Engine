H.1.1: Travel Fund Guidelines

Effective 12/20/89 .. Revised 5/15/96

PURPOSE


The Research and Professional Development Committee (RPDC) administers funds supporting discretionary travel by all University Libraries personnel to promote their professional development. These funds permit travel to, and participation in, regional and national conferences, workshops, seminars, and other professionally-related activities, such as:

  • meetings and conferences of professional societies, agencies or organizations in the library field

  • meetings and conferences of societies related to an individual's subject or functional responsibilities

  • operations-oriented workshops, seminars, and institutes which provide training for more effective implementation of day-to-day library procedures

  • presentation of papers before professional societies in non-library fields

  • visits to other libraries or institutions to study specific operations or collections at that institution (see also section on Librarians' Research Fund, H.2.1).



These activities merit support due to the many professional benefits they provide, including interaction with colleagues from other institutions, exchange of knowledge, development and maintenance of formal and informal networks, the opportunity to review new publications and products, and to meet publishers' and manufacturers' representatives. Papers presented before professional, scholarly societies in fields not related to the individual's immediate job duties will be considered for funding because of the prestige accruing to Arizona State University.


ELIGIBILITY


All University Libraries employees employed 50% time or more, including academic professionals or classified staff employed for five months or more with the ASU Libraries, may apply for travel funds. Academic professionals with visiting appointments and other temporary employees may apply for travel funds, but their requests receive lower priority, and approval is dependent on the amount of funding available.


CRITERIA FOR FUNDING


RPDC will give priority to providing partial funding for single trips by as many individuals as possible rather than providing full funding for a few or supporting multiple trips by an individual. When reviewing requests, the primary factor considered by RPDC is the relevance of the program as a whole, not just a single session, to the individual's job responsibilities. Other factors RPDC may consider include:

  • amount allocated by the Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian

  • number of staff applying for funds

  • number of meetings or conferences held in a given year

  • the nature of the individual's participation

  • average round trip discount fares


While there should generally be only one State funded attendee from the University Libraries at a meeting, multiple attendees may be funded for certain "comprehensive" meetings such as ALA and ACRL. RPDC will not consider the popularity of a meeting when allocating funds. Since the Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian must justify travelers in excess of those permitted under State guidelines (see page 3 of ASU Travel Policy and Procedures), it is the Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian prerogative to limit the number of State funded attendees at any conference or meeting. These guidelines apply to all monies expended by a state agency regardless of source.


UNIVERSITY VEHICLES


University vehicles will ordinarily be authorized by the RPDC only for long-distance trips within Arizona, such as to Tucson or Flagstaff. See the Business Operations Manager and ACD Manual, 807 for further details on the use of University vehicles and reimbursement for expenses of private vehicles used in lieu of University vehicles. The "Library car" is available for the use of University Libraries' personnel; should one wish to inquire about its availability, consult the Business Operations Manager.


ARIZONA LIBRARY ASSOCIATION (AzLA) AND TRAVEL WITHIN MARICOPA COUNTY


The Library Personnel Development Committee administers a separate fund for travel within the confines of Maricopa County, including funding workshops, seminars and AzLA conferences when they take place in Phoenix. AzLA meetings outside of Maricopa County pose unique problems requiring special arrangements. RPDC will deal with AzLA meetings on a year-by-year basis in consultation with the Librarians' Council. In the past, attendance at meetings in Tucson has been supported by arranging for University vehicles (carpooling encouraged and facilitated by the Committee) or equivalent transportation funding.


OTHER SOURCES OF FUNDING


Funding from sources outside the Libraries is encouraged and gratefully accepted.
