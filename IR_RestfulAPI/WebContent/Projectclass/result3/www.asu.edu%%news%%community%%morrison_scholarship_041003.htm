April 10, 2003

Institute launches new scholarship program

ASU's Morrison Institute for Public Policy, in partnership with The Arizona Republic and the Tucson Citizen, has developed a new scholarship program - the Young Steward of Public Policy.

This program provides the opportunity for Arizona high school seniors to earn a scholarship award and be published in both The Arizona Republic and Tucson Citizen newspapers. The first place award recipient will receive a $1,500 scholarship to ASU; the second place award recipient will receive a $1,000 scholarship to ASU.

This prestigious scholarship is designed to encourage Arizona high school seniors to think about public policy issues in Arizona and to promote the concept of "stewardship" - public leadership committed to doing what is best for Arizona and its residents, regardless of political philosophy or personal gain.

Young Steward of Public Policy awards will be based on an essay about a public issue of critical importance to the State of Arizona. Essays must analyze a specific public issue affecting the state and its residents and include specific recommendations for Arizona policy makers on how the issue should be addressed.

To apply, Arizona high school seniors should visit (www.morrisoninstitute.org) and open the link "Young Steward Scholarship" or call the Institute at (480) 965-4525 and request the complete announcement about this scholarship program. The application process requires submitting an essay following specific guidelines (two-page maximum).

Award recipients must attend ASU Main, East or West.

The program was established by Elaine and Richard Morrison of Gilbert to commemorate the impact of Morrison Institute's 20 years of public service and policy research.

Morrison Institute for Public Policy is an ASU resource for objective public policy research and analysis. A part of the School of Public Affairs in the College of Public Programs, Morrison Institute brings university scholarship and public policy development together for the benefit of Arizonans.

Leland, with the Morrison Institute, can be reached at (480) 965-4525 or (karen.leland@asu.edu).
