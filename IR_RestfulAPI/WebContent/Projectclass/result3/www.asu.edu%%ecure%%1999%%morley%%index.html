PRIVACY & CONFIDENTIALITY (WORKING DEFINITIONS)

Privacy as a right of individuals to control personal information about themselves

Confidentiality as the property or characteristic of information that is kept secret and secure

IMPORTANCE OF PRIVACY

•Privacy is a foundation of intellectual freedom
•College is a time of transition for students during which they are often unstable, vulnerable and possibly sensitive to things such as heritage, disabilities, etc.
•Privacy is a right of individuals to control personal information about themselves.
>

WHAT’S DIFFERENT?

•Hardcopy v. electronic record
•Storage in safes v. disc/tape
•Access via physical proximity v. worldwide network access
•Case by case record access v. blanket electronic access
•Usually viewed, seldom printed v. always viewed often printed
•Data centrally controlled v. data in distributed networks
•Volume and speed

NOTIFICATION
CHECKLIST

•Established procedures for notifying students about their privacy rights/responsibilities in network environments
•Established procedures for notifying students about disciplinary action that will be taken if they violate the privacy rights of others
•Established procedures for notifying students about what data are considered “directory information” and the medium that it will appear, e.g. WWW, public network

MINIMIZATION
CHECKLIST

•Policy that addresses logging and/or monitoring of online activity of students plan
•Policy that addresses logging and/or monitoring of online activity of students at public workstations
•Procedures for approving on campus research using online data, including data about how students use the campus network
•Policy that addresses source, collection, storage and purging of student information in a network

SECONDARY USE
CHECKLIST

• Policy about what students should be told at the time of enrollment about possible secondary use of information that they provide
•Policy that addresses the potential to create new, potentially confidential information from existing data
•Defined the routine and compatible use data

NONDISCLOSURE AND CONSENT
CHECKLIST (partial)

•Policy that addresses what information may be posted on the WWW, with or without consent
•Policy that addresses institutional ownership v. student ownership of information, e.g. digitized signatures, digitized photographs, etc.
•Policy for handling subpoenas requesting access to student email and computer records
•Policy on the confidentiality of electronic mail
•Policy on what types of information are appropriate for transmission by electronic mail

NEED TO KNOW
CHECKLIST (partial)

•Policy that ensures new or reengineered automated systems will support privacy rights
•Policy requiring frequent review of job categories for need to know access
•Identified which administrators can approve a search of student email & confidential records
•Policy requiring review of need to know status when databases are created or merged

DATA ACCURACY, INSPECTION AND REVIEW
CHECKLIST

•Policy and procedures about when and how students can change their online directory information
•Policy and procedures on administration of multiple and/or distributed databases
•Policy and procedures on inspection and review of transactional data

SECURITY, INTEGRITY AND ACCOUNTABILITY
CHEKLIST (partial)

•Policy on the security of passwords
•Authentication methods for online commerce
•Policy on level of security/encryption required for sensitive data sent via the campus network
•Policy on appropriate host & network security
•Identified and publicized appropriate sanctions when students alter online data owned by the institution or data of other students
•Policy that defines responsibilities for security and articulates how breaches will be handled
•Training program that ensures that system administrators and users are provided technical guidelines related to security issues

EDUCATION
CHECKLIST

•Program to provide instruction to students about their privacy rights, potential use/misuse of electronic information and the institution's policy on email confidentiality
•Program to ensure faculty, staff and administrators are educated to the legal, ethical and policy issues surrounding privacy
