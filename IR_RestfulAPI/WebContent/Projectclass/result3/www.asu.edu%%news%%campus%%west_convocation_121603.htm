December 16, 2003

ASU West convocation returns to campus setting

After more than a decade of holding graduation ceremonies at the Sundome Center for the Arts, ASU West will host a festive on-campus convocation Dec. 19, at 3 p.m. More than 500 of the 640 students graduating from ASU West this semester will participate in the ceremony on the 300-acre campus.

In the past, students participating in graduation ceremonies at the Sundome were limited in the number of guests they could invite, with each student allotted fewer than 10 guest tickets. Now they will be able to bring as many guests as they wish to celebrate the achievement of earning a university degree.

"This is particularly significant at ASU West because many of our students have extended families in the Valley, and many are the first in their family to graduate from college," says ASU West Provost Elaine Maimon.

Students also are invited to participate in ASU's university-wide commencement ceremony Dec. 18, at Wells Fargo Arena.

The Dec. 19 convocation at ASU West will make use of multiple gathering sites to enable students to celebrate as members of both the campus community and the ASU West colleges from which they are receiving their degrees. The ceremony will begin with students processing through the Paley Gate, a large ornamental gate at the front of the campus. As the graduating students pass through the gate, they will touch its surface to signify the fact that education has touched and changed their lives.

After gathering in ASU West's central courtyard to hear congratulatory remarks from Maimon, students will join with faculty from their colleges to their individual ceremony areas. Four ceremonies, featuring remarks from the dean and a student speaker, will honor students from the College of Arts and Sciences, College of Education, College of Human Services and School of Management.

As the individual college ceremonies conclude, graduates and guests will gather in the central courtyard for a reception with refreshments and live music. For information, call (602) 543-5311.

Crum, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or (matthew.crum@asu.edu).
