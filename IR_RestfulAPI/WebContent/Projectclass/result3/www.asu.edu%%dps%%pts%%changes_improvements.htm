Parking and Transit Services

Changes and Improvements Ahead

The Parking and Transit Task Force identified several ways for ASU to enhance current parking and transportation services and provide additional options for all faculty, staff and students.

Below is a brief description of the improvements and new programs that will be available to the ASU community by Fall 2006. Never before has ASU been able to offer so many parking and transportation options to faculty, staff and students, thus making the end result of the task force a success.

Tiered Parking Rates

You now have numerous options to choose from when it comes to purchasing an ASU parking decal. You can choose the decal that best suits your needs and you can also buddy up with someone and save money by signing up for a carpool permit. The choice is yours.

New ASU Ridematching Service

Can’t find someone to carpool with? No problem, PTS has launched an ASU dedicated carpool matching service designed solely for ASU faculty, staff and students. The online service allows you to identify other ASU faculty, staff and students that you could buddy-up with to work, school, events and more! This service could help you save money on the price of a parking decal (by splitting the cost), gas, vehicle maintenance, and insurance. Fill those empty seats in your vehicle today by visiting the ASU Rideshare Service powered by Alternet Rides.

New Online Services

Soon visitor parking permits will be available for purchase online via the PTS website. Visitor permits purchased over the web will be valid for the entire day in select ASU Tempe visitor lots ( Lot 16, 17E, 42, 45 and Lot 59E). More details regarding this online feature to come soon.

Parking Decal Reciprocity with all ASU campuses

For those who need to park at one or more of the ASU campuses on a regular basis, reciprocity between the Tempe, Downtown Phoenix, Polytechnic and West campuses will be available in designated parking lots that meet the lowest parking decal rate. Parking rates for ASU Downtown Phoenix, Polytechnic and West campuses are available on their respective websites.

Expansion of Evening Decal Program

2006-07 marked the second year of the Parking Structure 1 Evening Decal, which grants access into the popular structure from 4 p.m. – 7 a.m., and is also valid in Lot 59.

Through its research and focus groups, the Parking and Transit Task Force identified the need to expand the evening parking decal concept to other structures on the Tempe campus. Beginning Fall 2006, a Parking Structure 3 Evening decal will be available for purchase and will grant access into the structure from 4 p.m. – 7 a.m. This also means that PS-3 will no longer be available for cross-parking.

PS-1E and PS-3E decals will be available for purchase via the Online Parking Service beginning June 1, 2006.

Improvements to Parking Structures and Lots

Over the next three years, all ASU parking structures and lots will undergo makeovers, including painting, re-striping, way-finding improvements, lighting fixture upgrades, suitable shade (for surface lots), and possibly integrating the use of Sun Card technology for gate access in all parking structures.

Investments in Safety

PTS will continue to financially support the Safety Escort Service (SES) to help provide a safer alternative than walking to and from parking structures and lots. To help alleviate the demand on the SES, FLASH Forward services will be extended to 12 a.m. on weekdays.

Security in all parking areas will be increased with patrols, better lighting and improved visibility. Closed Circuit Television has been recommended for several parking areas and is being researched for the future.

Continuation of the ASU U-Pass

The continuation of the ASU Unlimited Access Bus Program, now called the U-Pass, will provide students and employees on all ASU campuses a free transportation option. The U-Pass provides the university community free, unlimited access to all Valley Metro bus routes, including Express and Phoenix Rapid routes.

Valley Metro has several free Park and Ride locations that all ASU faculty, staff and students can take advantage of when using their U-Pass. Below are the Valley Metro Park and Ride locations within Tempe. You may view other Valley Metro park and ride locations at www.valleymetro.org

FLASH Improvements : The ASU Tempe campus is thriving at all hours of the day. Activities at the Student Recreation Complex (SRC), Memorial Union (MU) as well as evening classes and study hours at ASU libraries are just some of the reasons to improve the existing FLASH services.

Beginning Fall 2006 semester, the FLASH Forward shuttle service will operate weekdays, from 7 a.m. to 12 a.m. Also, an additional bus will be added to help alleviate current demand during peak boarding times.

Extending FLASH service hours to midnight will provide a safe, reliable transportation option for Tempe campus students and employees during the evening hours.

Previous operation hours for FLASH Forward were 7 a.m. – 10 p.m. FLASH Back/University service will remain from 7 a.m. – 6 p.m.

The City of Tempe is also planning improvements to the Neighborhood FLASH. Read more about these improvements on their website; http://www.tempe.gov/tim/FlashNeighborhoodExpansion.htm.

New ASU McAllister Shuttle

Beginning Fall 2006 at the ASU Tempe campus, a new direct shuttle will be available connecting north and south campus residential communities as well as providing another transportation option from Lot 59 and 59N to the heart of the Tempe campus.

The ASU McAllister Shuttle will feature two new buses and will operate weekdays, 7 a.m. to 10 p.m. The route of the new shuttle is still being finalized, but its main service area will be Lot 59 to south campus via McAllister Avenue.

ASU Polytechnic and West Shuttle Improvements

Part of the New American University is one university in many places. In order to help students and employees effectively access all ASU campuses, improvements to the current ASU Polytechnic and West shuttles will be made for FY07.

Beginning Fall 2006, the ASU Polytechnic and ASU West Shuttle service will be upgraded to provide hourly weekday service throughout the year. Detailed schedule information will be announced in the near future.

New ASU Downtown Phoenix Shuttle

When the Downtown Phoenix campus opens in August 2006, a free shuttle service has been requested to provide travel to and from the Tempe campus for faculty, staff and students. Service hours for the Downtown Phoenix campus shuttle will be 6 a.m. – 10 p.m., Monday – Friday. More detailed information regarding this shuttle will be announced in the near future.


Bus Stop Enhancements/Amenities

ASU will continuously improve all FLASH and Valley Metro transit stops on the Tempe campus by adding amenities like suitable shade, emergency call boxes, improved lighting, better seating as well as researching GPS technology message boards. More information regarding these improvements will be released when they become available.

Bike Corral(s)

Over the next four years, one bike corral will be built on the Tempe campus per year to help address bike storage and theft. The corrals will have security technology so that entry/exit of the corral will be monitored. Site locations of the bike corrals will be determined in coordination with the ASU Architect.
