Tape Check-in Procedures

Checking tapes into the tape library

Academic customers may check in round reel or cartridge tapes containing data they wish to use. The customer fills out a Tape Volume Control Form, specifying the customer's name, campus address, userid, and other pertinent information.

The tape librarian gives the owner a receipt for the volume (i.e., a copy of the Tape Volume Control Form) at the time of check-in. The customer must present this receipt to remove the volume from the library. The receipt indicates what volume serial number has been assigned to the tape.

When the customer's tape is checked in, the data on the tape will be copied by the tape librarian onto a cartridge tape which will be used by the customer's computer programs. The librarian will inform the customer how long it will be before the cartridge tape may be used; this is typically 15 to 30 minutes, although it depends on how busy the librarian is.

Once a customer's tape has been copied to a cartridge tape, the customer will be expected to remove the original tape from the tape library. The customer should present his copy of the Tape Volume Control Form; the tape librarian will make a notation on the form that the original tape has been checked out. If after two weeks the owner of the tape has not yet removed the original from the library, an effort will be make to contact the customer and request that the tape be picked up. If the customer cannot be reached, the tape will be returned via campus mail to the address given on the Tape Volume Control Form.

Requesting a new blank tape

A customer may also request a blank tape for use on the system. To request a blank tape, the customer completes a Tape Volume Control Form at the tape library.

The customer must specify on the Tape Volume Control Form whether the tape should be initialized as an IBM standard label (SL) or nonlabeled (NL) volume.

Tape protection

Tapes checked into the tape library or assigned to a customer's userid prior to May 16, 1992 will be write protected via RACF. That is, only the owner may write to the tape, and any customer can read it. Tapes checked in or assigned after May 16, 1992 will be read/write protected via RACF. That is, only the owner may read from or write to the tape. The owner may grant or deny read or write access to a tape for one or more individuals using RACF utilities on the Academic MVS system.

If the customer only intends to read the tape and not write to it, he/she can specify "READ ONLY" on the Tape Volume Control Form as a precautionary measure indicating that the volume will only be read. If "READ ONLY" is specified, a hardware interlock on the cartridge is set so that the tape cannot be written to, regardless of the level of RACF protection.

Checking tapes out of the tape library

The customer must return his copy of the Tape Volume Control Form to the tape librarian to remove the volume from the library.

Without special exception, the tape owner is the only one who may check the tape out of the library.
