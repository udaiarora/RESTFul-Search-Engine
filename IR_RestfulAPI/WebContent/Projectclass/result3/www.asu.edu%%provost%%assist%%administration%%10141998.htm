Arizona State System for Information on Student Transfer (ASSIST)
A Status Report as of October 14, 1998
John D. Porter

Overview

ASSIS (i.e., Statewide Student Tracking Data Warehouse) is a relational database containing information on a student?s enrollment in participating Arizona community colleges and universities. The purpose is to provide participating institutions the information needed to (1) complete Student Right to Know, and (2) evaluate the effectiveness of the transfer process.

ASU started building ASSIS in March 1998. A former employee of ASU?s Data Administration Department working part time has made great progress in the technical development of the relational database. It is anticipated that a full time employee will be hired in the next 60 days. At this point, ASU is confident that the project can be completed, but the usefulness of the information depends on the completeness and accuracy of the data provided. ASU?s objective is to complete the technical development in the first year, and focus on qualitative improvements (e.g., data quality and completeness) in the remaining two years.

Current Status

ASU obtained student records for 1995-96 and 1996-97 from NAU and UA. ASU provided student records for these years plus 1994-95. We are working with NAU and UA to obtain data for 1994-95.
The community college student records begin with 1993-94, but only MCCCD has data through 1996-97. We are working with the other community colleges to obtain missing data.
The 1997-98 data exchange occurs in December 1998.
ASU developed a program to verify the accuracy and completeness of the data exchanged. All institutions have omissions and/or errors in data. In some cases, we returned files because the incidence of error was unacceptable. We anticipate receiving corrected files shortly.
ASU created a relational database to capture student records. Since this database contains personally identifiable information on all students, it will only be used to create separate databases containing the records of an institution?s former students. Currently, this database contains over 375,000 student records from ASU and MCCCD. The institutional databases have not been created.
ASU created a program to match student records. The algorithm is an 8 step process using combinations of student ID (SSN if available), last name, first name, and birth date. The program performs phonetic matching and adjusts for transposition errors in student ID. Our tests indicate the algorithm has the power to adequately link students records.

 

Future Plans


Our plan is to develop a prototype data warehouse containing university data and data from MCCCD, Pima, and one or two other community colleges by January 31, 1999. The prototype will be used as a proof of concept and to identify technical problems. A full implementation with as much data as available is planned for June 30, 1999. Our ability to meet these goals depends on the timeliness and the quality of the data provided by the institution.
