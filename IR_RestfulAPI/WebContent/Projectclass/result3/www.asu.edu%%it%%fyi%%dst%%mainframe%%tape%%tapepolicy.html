Tape Retention Policy

  1. A copy of the tape retention policy will be given to a customer anytime a Tape Volume Control form is filled out and turned in to the tape librarian.
  2. Owners of inactive tapes will receive a letter notifying them that their tapes have not been accessed for 2 years. The letter will be sent to the address given on the Tape Volume Control form when the tape was submitted to the library. A copy of the tape retention policy will be included with this letter.
  3. Inactive tapes will be placed in a holding area within the tape library for six months. Inactive tapes can be accessed in the usual way without any degradation of service.
  4. A customer may notify the tape librarian to retain tapes with an inactive status by providing a written request for a longer retention period.
  5. Five months after the first notification, customers of inactive tapes will be sent a second letter. This is to notify them that if the tapes are not claimed or requested for use in a job within 30 days, the tape will be placed in unsecured, off-site storage, and be destroyed after 12 months.
  6. Access to tapes taken off-site will be provided within 48 hours of the request. A job requesting an off-site tape will ABEND (abnormally end) with a message from the operator to rerun the job after 48 hours have elapsed.
  7. A third notification will be sent to owners of tapes that have been in off-site storage for 10 months.
  8. After 12 months in off-site storage unclaimed tapes will be erased and discarded or used for other purposes.
  9. If a letter notifying a tape customer of impending action regarding the disposition of an inactive tape is returned with no known forwarding address, the tape will immediately be placed in off-site storage for 12 months.
