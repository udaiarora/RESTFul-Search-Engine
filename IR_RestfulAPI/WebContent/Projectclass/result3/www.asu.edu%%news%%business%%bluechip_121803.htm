December 18, 2003

Arizona's 'jobless recovery' is slow in arriving

The outlook for the economy both in Arizona and the nation is improving, and this is good news for employment. Yet, it will be awhile before anyone can claim we are in a boom period, according to the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Forecast.

Employment growth is slower this time around than in recoveries from past recessions, according to Elliott Pollack, Arizona Blue Chip topics editor. "This is true not just in Arizona, but also throughout the country. Indeed, Arizona has done relatively well and ranks seventh among the states in employment growth for the first nine months of 2003. And, according to the Blue Chip consensus forecast, employment is likely to grow 3.5 percent in 2004 compared to 4.2 percent in the 1990s and 3.7 percent over the last 10 years."

"However, the economy is not likely to have the same type of boom conditions that existed in the third and fourth years of the last expansion," Pollack said. "Based on that, employment growth both nationally and in Arizona, will be respectable, but not above long-term trend levels in 2004."

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark surveyed the panel on population trends. "The consensus estimate from the panel for 2003 is closer to our own baseline estimate than is usually the case - and the forecast for 2004 is significantly higher than the 2.6 percent forecast last year for 2004," Clark reported. "The panel is reacting to the accumulating evidence that population growth in the state did not slow down as much as it usually does during an economic cycle."

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. For information, call (480) 965-7858.
