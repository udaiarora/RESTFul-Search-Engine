ASU Herberger College of Fine Arts announces its 2003-2004 Mainstage season

The Herberger College of Fine Arts at Arizona State University announces its 2003-2004 Mainstage season, with 19 productions in four award-winning areas: lyric opera theatre, theatre, dance and organ music.

There are several highlights to this stunning new season, including:

· The world premiere of Caterina's Son, a new musical about Leonardo da Vinci and his being the son of Caterina, a young, unmarried peasant girl who gave up her baby so he could have a better life. Directed by noted director J. Robert Wills, dean of the Herberger College of Fine Arts. Music, lyrics and book by Tak Tarbo.

· A bold new theatre season, including a Playwrights Festival of three fully staged works of student playwrights, Our Town and SubUrbia done in repertory, and Alchemy of Desire by Caridad Svich, one of the leading representatives of recent Latino playwrights.

· Two fantastic new subscription deals! The first is called First Fridays. Buy one subscription and receive a second companion subscription absolutely free for First Friday performances only. The second is the new Freedom Pack, for maximum flexibility in attending Mainstage performances. Just $100 buys 10 vouchers for choices from 19 different productions.

Subscriptions are available now! People may choose from four specially priced packages, including the new First Fridays Package and Freedom Pack.

To receive a brochure or for more information, the public should call 480-965-6536.

Herberger College of Fine Arts Mainstage Season 2003-2004

Lyric Opera Theatre

Double Bill of Operas:
Der Schauspieldirektor
(The Impresario)
Music by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
Libretto by Gottlieb Stephanie
the Younger
Riders to the Sea
by John Millington Synge
Music by Ralph Vaughan Williams
Oct. 3-12, 2003
Evelyn Smith Music Theatre

Mozart composed an overture, two arias, a trio and a vaudevillian finale for The Impresario, a one-act comic opera parodying the vanity of singers. The one-act Riders to the Sea relates the story of Maurya, an old woman who battles against a hard fate and the wind and the sea on an island off the west coast of Ireland.

Caterina's Son - World Premiere!
Music, Lyrics and Book by Tak Tarbo
Nov. 21 - Dec. 6, 2003
Evelyn Smith Music Theatre

This new musical is about Leonardo da Vinci . and about his being the son of Caterina, a young, unmarried peasant girl who gave up her baby so he could have a better life. Set in Renaissance Italy, the musical spans 67 years, from Leonardo's birth in 1452 to his death in 1519. It is a story of love and betrayal, determination and
ambition and, ultimately, of redemption. More than 20 original songs grace Leonardo's journey from Tuscany to the court of Milan and, finally, to Amboise, France. The Lyric Opera Theatre production marks the world premiere of this new musical drama. Directed by noted director J. Robert Wills, dean of the Herberger College of Fine Arts.

Once Upon a Mattress
Music by Mary Rodgers
Lyrics by Marshall Barer
Book by Jay Thompson, Dean Fuller
and Marshall Barer
Feb. 27 - March 7, 2004
Evelyn Smith Music Theatre

Once Upon a Mattress is based on the fairy tale of "The Princess and the Pea." Written by Mary Rodgers (daughter of Richard Rodgers) and Marshall Barer, the story centers on Prince Dauntless the Drab, who is allowed to wed only a true princess of royal blood.

La Finta Giardiniera
(The Pretend Gardener)
Music by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
Libretto by Giuseppe Petrosellini
April 23 - May 2, 2004
Evelyn Smith Music Theatre

Also titled Sandrina's Secret, this opera in three acts was written by Mozart when he was only 19. Plot complications abound: mistaken identity, mad scenes, jealous fits and unrequited love. The Act II finale provides 26 minutes of continuous music, four minutes more than the Marriage of Figaro.

Theatre

Alchemy of Desire/
Dead Man's Blues
Sept. 26-Oct. 5, 2003
Lyceum Theatre

When a young newlywed woman loses her husband to a nameless war, she is driven nearly mad with grief. Fortunately, her four meddling neighbors are there to provide solace in unexpected ways. A mystical, humorous and compelling play by one of America's new voices, Caridad Svich.

Our Town*
Nov. 7-23, 2003
Lyceum Theatre

Set 100 years ago in Grover's Corners, New Hampshire, Our Town premiered on Broadway in 1938, winning rave reviews and the Pulitzer Prize for Drama.

SubUrbia*
Nov. 8-22, 2003
Lyceum Theatre

The New York Times calls SubUrbia, "Chekhov high on speed and Twinkies . A scathing study of rootless youth." This is performance artist, actor and playwright Eric Bogosian's acclaimed play about a group of 20-year-olds. Lance Gharavi directs.

Please note:

*SubUrbia will be presented in repertory and on the same set as Our Town. Both plays focus on small communities. Written 56 years apart, these plays are separated by World War II, Vietnam, the Persian Gulf War and momentous social changes. Linking the two provides an interesting and challenging theater-going experience. In juxtaposition, the productions speak to each other in unexpected, challenging ways. Audiences can see both plays for the price of one.

SubUrbia contains mature themes and language.

Cherry Orchard
Feb. 20-29, 2004
Galvin Playhouse

Marshall W. Mason directs Anton Chekhov's masterpiece about an aristocratic Russian family and their magnificent cherry orchard.

New Plays Festival
April 9-25, 2004
Lyceum Theatre

The eighth annual New Plays Festival has developed from a marathon of staged readings to a mini-repertory of three full productions! It is a celebration of the process of developing exciting new works for the theatre. The plays are written by students in the Master of Fine Arts in playwriting program offered by the Herberger College department of theatre and developed in a collaborative process. Each play will be presented three times. Audiences will have the unique opportunity to participate in the process through post-performance discussions with the playwrights and the artistic teams, who will incorporate changes throughout the run of their plays. One paid admission includes all three plays.

Dance

September Concert
Sept. 18-21, 2003
Dance Studio Theatre, PE East 132

Husband-and-wife team Cliff Keuter and Elina Mooney, noted choreographers, dancers and professors of dance, presents a number of their most compelling works. Keuter collaborates with the couple's playwright son, Matthew, who has written the script for their fourth dance/theatre piece, which premieres here. Mooney completes and presents her trilogy of work inspired by the great American artist Edward Hopper. Keuter and Mooney also premiere two new dances.

New Danceworks I
Oct. 16-19, 2003
Dance Studio Theatre, PE East 132

New Danceworks II
March 4-7, 2004
Dance Studio Theatre, PE East 132

These captivating programs feature the choreography of candidates for a Master of Fine Arts degree in dance. The pieces represent the culmination of their art-making experiences in their degree program. These fresh, vibrant works are their thesis projects, capping off a rigorous study of dance at one of the best dance departments in the nation.

Fall Dance Collection
Nov. 20-23, 2003
Dance Studio Theatre, PE East 132

This is a striking concert performance of innovative choreography shaped by a mix of faculty and student choreographers. A new work by a visiting guest artist will be showcased. Past guest artists have included Bebe Miller, Mark Haim, Ron Brown, Deborah Hay, Sean Curran, Pat Graney and Neta Pulvermacher.

Spring Dance Collection
March 25-28, 2004
Galvin Playhouse

This refreshing dance program features a number of pieces set by both faculty artists and students. The works created are innovative and often provocative, while respecting the traditions of the past. The showcase piece is a new work created by a special guest artist.

Organ

Blending Old and New
Sept. 21, 2003
Organ Hall

Hungarian organist Zsigmond Szathmáry, professor of organ at the Staatliche Hochschule für Musik in Freiburg, Germany, exploits the clear timbres of the Fritts organ in a skillful program that combines Baroque music with music of our own time.

A Celebration of Christmas
Organ Music
Dec. 14, 2003
Organ Hall

Kimberly Marshall and the ASU Organ Studio perform a special holiday concert celebrating the beautiful music of Christmas played on the pipe organ.

The Transcriber's Art
Jan. 25, 2004
Organ Hall

The Mainstage Organ Series welcomes Richard Elliott, organist for the Mormon Tabernacle in Salt Lake City, for a program exploring the organ as a one-person orchestra.

J. S. Bach,
The Consummate Musician
Feb. 8, 2004
Organ Hall

ASU alumnus Curt Sather, organist at St. Barnabas in the Desert Episcopal Church, explores the many sounds of Bach in this program that includes music from different periods of Bach's life.

Mathematical Music:
Number in the Conception
of Bach's Organ Music
Feb. 22, 2004
Organ Hall

In this fascinating program, Kimberly Marshall, the Patricia and Leonard Goldman professor of organ in the Herberger College School of Music, shows the ways in which Medieval, Renaissance and Baroque composers incorporated number symbolism in their organ
music. Emphasis will be placed on Bach's chorale settings and contrapuntal works.

Bach and the North
German Tradition
March 7, 2004
Organ Hall

The series ends with a grand finale: Swedish virtuoso Hans Davidsson, professor of organ at the Eastman School of Music, will perform music of the North German school, which greatly influenced Bach's writing for the organ. He is an expert on this music and was the driving force behind one of the most spectacular organ building projects of the last century, which was devoted to rendering anew the music of Scheidemann, Praetorius and Buxtehude. Davidsson has designed a riveting program to capture the distinctive German sounds of the ASU Fritts organ.
