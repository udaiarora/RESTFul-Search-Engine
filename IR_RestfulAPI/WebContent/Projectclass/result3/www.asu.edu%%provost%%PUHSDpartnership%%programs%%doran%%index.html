Doran Community Scholars Program

Program Overview

The Doran Community Scholars Program (DCSP) is the result of the generous donation given to the ASU Foundation from Wayne and Maureen Doran. Wayne Doran is alumnus from the Phoenix Union High School District (PUHSD) and Arizona State University (ASU). In 1997, the DCSP was formed to target students from the PUHSD and both to encourage them to pursue higher education and to continue to support them on that journey. Now in its ninth year, the program has been remarkably successful not only in assisting students with their academic and professional careers, but in enriching their personal development and instilling in them a sense of social consciousness.

The DCSP assures academic and personal support to its scholars while preparing them to become strong community leaders. Through a systematic leadership development program, students engage in a four-year course of study that provides scholars with experiences that will further develop professionalism and leadership through opportunities in community service, career and professional development, civic engagement and program development.

The Doran Community Scholars Program stands to become a national model. The program provides to its scholars critical financial assistance so that economic limitations will not prevent capable students from pursuing their dreams. Academic and personal services are provided to promote academic success leading to a bachelor's degree, and the low staff to student ratio ensures an extensive monitoring component of the program. If a student begins to struggle personally, professionally or academically, the DCSP is uniquely poised to assist the student, whereas most programs would let them slip away.

The program is structured to provide a comprehensive leadership development component that prepares a generation of leaders who will understand the roots of social change and the importance of giving back to their communities through curriculum and service learning.

Built into the program are various checks and balances to ensure student success. Scholars must meet specific requirements in four areas in order to continue receiving scholarship benefits for up to four years. These include achieving a minimum grade point average (GPA) of 2.7, completion of a minimum of 24 course credit hours per year, 40 community service/field hours per year, and regular attendance and participation in program classes and events. Should a scholar struggle with any or all of these areas, individual goal setting meetings with the Program Coordinators are employed to ensure that all students reach their potential.

At the heart of the DCSP it is believed that leaders are not born, but made. It is our firm belief that by helping our scholars to succeed academically and professionally as well as developing them as pro-active and educated leaders in their communities, our scholars have the tools to create change for themselves, their families and their communities.

Program Goals

  1. To ensure students have the university support to complete their degree programs in a timely manner. (Funding for four years)
  2. To provide scholars with experiences that will further develop professionalism and leadership through opportunities in community service, career and professional development, civic engagement and program development.
  3. Be provided with mentoring from the Program Coordinator and other staff which can be: individual guidance, assistance in creating academic and career goals / plans, encouragement, and assistance in helping the student achieve their highest potential.
  4. To broaden Doran Community Scholars? exposure to resources on the ASU campus and within the Arizona community.
  5. To broaden Doran Community Scholars? exposure to the diverse needs of a changing society both globally and locally.

Program Requirements

  1. CUMULATIVE GRADE POINT AVERAGE
    • Must maintain a minimum 2.7 cumulative grade point average
    • There are no probationary periods, unless special circumstances arise which will be evaluated by the Program Coordinator and Program Manager
  2. COURSE CREDIT HOURS
    • Must complete a minimum of 24 credit hours
    • Students must be enrolled Full-time to receive scholarship funding (12 or more credits)
    • There are no probationary periods, unless special circumstances arise which will be evaluated by the Program Coordinator and Program Manager
  3. PARTICIPATION IN THE DORAN COMMUNITY SCHOLARS PROGRAM
    • Register for and complete the "Doran Community Scholars" (DCI 194,294,394,494) course during the Fall and Spring semesters.
    • Meet with Program Coordinator:
      Once a semester for 30 minutes during the Fall Semester
      Once a semester for 30 minutes during the Spring Semester
    • Attend all monthly group meetings scheduled for one Saturday of every month.
    • Attend annual Doran Scholars dinner hosted by Mr. and Mrs. Doran
  4. INVOLVEMENT AND/OR SERVICE TO ASU AND/OR LOCAL COMMUNITY.
    • Must Complete 40 volunteer hours between the months of September-May.
    • PS1-PS2 purpose is to allow you an opportunity to gain knowledge and hands on experience about a particular social issue through working with a community based organization.
    • PS3-PS4 purpose of the 40-hour field placement is to allow you the opportunity to gain experience and knowledge that help with career development conferences, university sponsored workshops, etc.
    • Remain in good standing with the University. Suspension, dismissal or withdrawal from ASU prior to the end of a semester will automatically revoke this scholarship and the student may be liable to repay all funds disbursed from this scholarship for that semester.

Maureen Doran was born in Dearborn, Michigan. She attended the University of Washington and the University of Michigan. Her business and professional career included retail stores in Detroit and Dearborn, such as Godiva Chocolate Shop, a perfume specialty store, and Maureen Boutique in Dearborn. Maureen also served as President-Pro-Tem for the Dearborn City Council and has been involved in politics in Michigan in a variety of capacities. She has been a member of Salvation Army Board of Michigan, a Board Member of the Sisters of Mercy and is involved with numerous other charitable activities in Michigan.

Wayne was born and raised in Phoenix, Arizona, and earned a B.S. at Arizona State University. He currently serves as Chairman of the Board of the ASU Foundation. He was Vice President of Del Webb Corporation from 1963-69, joining Ford Motor Company in 1969 as founding Chairman of Ford Motor Land Development Corporation. He was also a Vice President of Ford Motor Company, retiring in January 2001. He continues his relationship with Ford as a consultant to the company. In addition, Wayne serves on the Board of Directors of Glimcher Realty Trust in Columbus, Ohio, and Henry Ford Hospital in Detroit, Michigan, and serves as a Trustee of the Urban Land Institute, Washington D.C. and the National Realty Committee. Wayne received the Distinguished Leader Award from the University of Michigan in 1998. ASU named him an Outstanding Alumni in 1978 and inducted him into the Business School Hall of Fame in 1977.

Maureen and Wayne are deeply committed to the Doran Community Scholarship Program at Arizona State University. ?We want to assist each scholar reach their maximum potential. Each person is important to the both of us and we look forward to a long and rewarding relationship.?
