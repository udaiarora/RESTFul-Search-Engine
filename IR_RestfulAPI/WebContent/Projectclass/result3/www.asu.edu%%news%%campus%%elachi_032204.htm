Jet propulsion laboratory director will speak at ASU

Charles Elachi, director of the Jet Propulsion Laboratory and a vice president of the California Institute of Technology, will speak at Arizona State University at 3:40 pm, March 29 th in room 101 of Physical Sciences building, F-wing.

Elachi's talk, "Space Exploration in the Next Decade: Challenges and Opportunities," will outline his vision for the immediate future of space exploration. The talk is free and open to the public.

Elachi's talk will touch on the history of space exploration including some of the current missions in orbit and on the surface of Mars, like the Spirit and Opportunity rovers. He then will discuss the themes that will guide future space exploration projects, including missions within the solar system, to search for planets around other nearby stars and to study the origins of other galaxies and the universe.

Elachi has been a principal investigator on a number of NASA research and development studies and flight projects over the years. He was director of Space and Earth Science Programs at JPL from 1982 to 2000, and was responsible for the development of more than 45 flight missions and instruments for Earth observation, planetary exploration and astrophysics.

Elachi was elected to the National Academy of Engineering in 1989, one of the highest honors bestowed upon an engineer. He was awarded the NASA Outstanding Leadership Medal in 2002, the Wernher Von Braun Award in 2002 and the NASA Distinguished Service Medal in 1999. Elachi received the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) Medal of Engineering Excellence in 1992, and the IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Distinguished Achievement Award in 1987. He is a Fellow of the IEEE and the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics.
