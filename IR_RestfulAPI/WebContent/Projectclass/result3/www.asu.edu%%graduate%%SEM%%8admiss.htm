Admission and Application Procedures

Application Procedures

All students who seek graduate study must apply through the ASU Graduate College Admissions Office. The Graduate College has an on-line application. Students designate on the application the specific major they seek or they can choose non-degree status. The on-line application should be completed first. If you do not have web access and need a paper application, contact the Graduate College Admissions Office at the address listed below. Make sure you state that you have no web access. The application is entered into a large database and assures that supporting materials, (such as transcripts, fees, test scores) sent thereafter are not misdirected. Documents should be mailed in a timely manner so that they are received by the admission deadlines. Obtaining copies of official transcripts, GRE and TOEFL scores, and recommendation letters can sometimes take weeks so prospective students are advised to start the process 3 months prior to the application deadline. A masters degree is not required for admission to the Ph.D. program although many of the applicants who apply to the Ph.D. program have completed a masters in engineering or the applied sciences.

Evaluation by the Admissions Committee

 
The decision to admit or deny a student to the SEM Program is the responsibility of the Admissions Committee. This four-faculty committee evaluates each student's application file. The Committee makes a recommendation to the Graduate College. The final decision to admit an applicant rests with the Graduate College.

The goal of the committee is to:
admit highly qualified, motivated and mature students who are able to handle the challenges of advanced study and research in materials science and engineering.
admit a diverse group of graduate students from various disciplines including chemistry, chemical engineering, mechanical engineering, geology, physics, solid state science as well as traditional materials science and engineering
strive for a balance of domestic and international students from various ethnic backgrounds
seek out highly qualified students from underrepresented groups such as women and minorities
Admission to the SEM Program is competitive. On an average the program admits 15% of the applicant pool. The program receives approximately 120 applications each fall semester.

Notification of Admission and Financial Aid

Admission decisions are sent to the student by the Graduate College by formal letter. If financial aid is awarded by the SEM Program, the program will send the student a separate award letter.
