Addressing Problems with Saving Files on AFS

This document provides information on possible reasons for having difficulty saving files to your AFS file space. It shows how you can investigate the problem by logging on to any of the ASU UNIX clusters such as general.asu.edu and research.asu.edu. Furthermore, it also provides you with available options for addressing this space problem.

1. There is inadequate amount of space in your personal AFS space. To confirm this, enter the command "fs listquota" and a message similar to the following will be displayed: 

     Volume Name      Quota    Used    % Used   Partition
     user.agjjc       50000   49000       98%         75%

The percentage under the "% Used" column provides information on how much of your space has been used. In this example, the user "agjjc" has used 98% (49 MB) of his 50 MB space.

If the numbers displayed under "% Used" for your account is very high, then the amount of space available to you for saving files may be inadequate.

The options available to you for addressing this problem include going through your files and discarding the ones you do not need or transferring many of the current files to an external storage media such as floppy diskettes.

You may use the "ls -l" command to list the files in any of your directories. If you are discarding a file, you can use the "rm -i filename" command. For more information on the "rm" and "ls" commands, please refer to the online help information on unix commands from the main menu. For transferring files, you may use Kermit, FTP, etc. Kermit-related transfer information is available also from the online help system under "Communications & File Conversion". For assistance with transferring files, users may may send an email to "comm-q@asu.edu" or call the Help Desk at 965-6500.

2. There is inadequate amount of space in the disk partition where your personal AFS space resides. To confirm this, again refer to the information diplayed by the "fs listquota" command. 

     Volume Name      Quota    Used    % Used   Partition
     user.agjjc       50000   37500       75%         95%
The percentage under the "Partition" column provides information on how much of the space on the partition is used. The disk partition is a 2 GB storage location for several users and in this example, 95% of the 2 GB space is used.

If the percentage displayed under "Partition" is close to the mid-90's then it is safe to assume that the disk partition where your personal AFS space resides on is full.

The option available to you for addressing this problem is to promptly report it to the Help Desk by calling 965-6500. Provide your ASURITE id to the help desk staff and they in turn report this to the systems staff. The system staff with the information on your ASURITE id locates the partition that is full and takes the necessary steps to address the problem.

3. If the problem is not related to either items #1 or #2 then it may be related to the hard disk that the disk partition resides on or some other system-related problem. In either case, follow the suggestion mentioned in item #2 for reporting it to the Help Desk.
