Eligibility

The President’s Award for Innovation recognizes ASU individual employees or teams for innovations that improve educational, administrative, or other organizational processes through creative approaches.  Only innovations that have been implemented and have demonstrated sustained positive results for at least one year will be considered for the award.

Employees may be classified or administrative staff, academic or service professionals, or faculty. A team may be comprised of any group of ASU employees including members outside the university partnering in the innovation.

Selection Process

This is a single level award for which there may be multiple recipients. Recipients of the President’s Award for Innovation will be determined by a review group appointed by the President.

Award Criteria

  • Originality (40%) - the creativity and uniqueness of the innovation compared to traditional methods.
  • Portability (20%) - the extent to which the innovation lends itself to widespread application.
  • Results and Benefits (40%) - specific details, data, and observations derived from the innovation, including benefits, which reflect the degree of enrichment.

Supporting Materials

The following will be considered a complete application packet. Failure to submit this application and a complete packet will lead to disqualification.

1. A complete application, including a contact person, the roster (page 3), and the Innovation Overview (page 4).

2. A short abstract (180 words or less) summarizing the innovation on a separate page. This abstract will be used for promotional purposes and will not be counted toward the 5-page limitation.

3. Innovation documentation limited to:
  • five (5) pages or less (excess pages will not be read)
  • single spaced
  • font size 10 or larger in Arial
  • graphs and charts are accepted as part of the page limitation.

4. Your application should describe the innovation, including:

  • justification of the creativity and uniqueness of the innovation compared to traditional methods at ASU or at other institutions.
  • an assessment or a description of efforts to date to encourage dissemination and/or deployment of the innovation to other parts of ASU or to other organizations.
  • the measurements and monitoring used to establish the degree to which the results indicate a significant benefit in cost, customer service, work environment improvement, and achievement of organization goals. Positive results must be ongoing and sustained for at least one year at the time the application is submitted.
