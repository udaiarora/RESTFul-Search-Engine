Leaving ASU

Before you leave the University make sure to copy/backup all of your data. If you need assistance on how to backup data, contact Information Technology's Help Desk at 480.965.6500.

Question

When will my ASURITE computing services be shut off?

Answer

Students

ASURITE computing services for students are suspended if the student has not registered and paid for at least one credit hour by the time Late Registration ends for either the Fall or Spring term. It takes several weeks for our system to process all of the suspensions/deactivations, but users should expect their services to be disabled as early as the day after Late Registration ends. Computing services will also be suspended the day after a student withdraws from all credit hours at any point during a semester. The Electronic Post Office (EPO) will continue to process e-mail indefinitely. Users may continue receiving incoming messages sent to their "@asu.edu" e-mail addresses by setting a new Destination Address in the EPO. To do so, login to http://my.asu.edu, click on EMMA, click on EPO Update, change the "Destination Address" to a working non-ASU e-mail address, then click the Update button.

Students graduating from ASU can extend their ASURITE computing services for one year by contacting the IT Help Desk. ASURITE computing services that are extended include: ASU Windows 2000 Active Directory, AFS Fileservice (network file space), Border VPN Access, Dynamic Host Control Protocol (DHCP), General Computing (AFS access in sites, Unix cluster access), Student E-mail Service (EMMA/Pine), Personal Webpage Hosting, Research/Stats Unix Cluster, and Secured network access product (Perfigo).

Faculty/Staff

Be sure to visit the Human Resources website and follow the Checklist for Terminating Employees.

ASURITE computing services for ASU Faculty and Classified Staff will be suspended the day after Human Resources considers the employee "terminated". The Electronic Post Office (EPO) will continue to process e-mail indefinitely. Users may continue receiving incoming messages sent to their "@asu.edu" e-mail addresses by setting a new Destination Address in the EPO. To do so, login to http://my.asu.edu, click on EMMA, click on EPO Update, change the "Destination Address" to a working non-ASU e-mail address, then click the Update button.

Retirees and Faculty Emeritus may extend their ASURITE computing services by having their Sun Cards updated to reflect the new status and displaying the new Sun Card at the IT Help Desk. ASURITE computing services that are extended include:

  • ASU Windows 2000 Active Directory
  • ASURITE Windows Login
  • ASU Tempe Campus Exchange (Outlook E-mail)
  • Blackberry Service (only users with existing service prior to retiring with approval from your department)
  • AFS Fileservice (network file space)
  • Border VPN Access
  • DCE Authentication
  • Dynamic Host Control Protocol (DHCP)
  • General Computing (AFS access in sites, Unix cluster access)
  • IT Computing Sites (MACOSX)
  • Student E-mail Service (Webmail)
  • Personal Webpage Hosting
  • PPP
  • Research/Stats Unix Cluster
  • Secured network access (Perfigo

Question

How long will Information Technology save my data after I leave ASU?

Answer

Data stored in AFS, the IMAP server, and the Exchange server (Outlook) will be saved for 8 months after you are not longer affiliated with ASU and access to those services has been suspended. Once those services have been suspended for 8 months, all data will be deleted. Data stored on the Academic TSO system is governed by the Academic TSO Data Retention Policy.
