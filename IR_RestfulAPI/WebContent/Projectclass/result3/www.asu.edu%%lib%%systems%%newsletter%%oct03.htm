LIST Helps ADA with HP

Recently, Hewlett Packard profiled the Library ADA department’s participation in its Library Technology Access (LTA) initiative, which helps provide assistive technology to students who need it. The article, at http://www.hp.com/hpinfo/abouthp/accessibility/partnerships/ala/lta_arizona_state.html, quotes several library staffers, including our own Kirk Manegold, who has been serving as LIST’s technology liaison to ADA for several months now.

New Site Showcases Collaboration between Library Departments

LIST and DAM staff have worked together on a number of online exhibitions over the years. The latest example of this collaboration is “Dorothy Gilbert and the Phoenix Mountains Preserve,” a web site presenting primary and secondary source material documenting the grassroots effort to save the Phoenix Mountains.

Rose Minetti, from the Department of Archives and Manuscripts, selected documents to provide a glimpse of the Gilbert Collection’s content, and wrote the site’s explanatory text. Sue McNamara, also of Archives, digitized the documents. LIST’s Heather Knowles designed and produced the web site.

The site presents over 100 historically significant documents from the Dorothy Gilbert Collection, in a time line dating from 1960 to 1997. The manuscripts trace steps taken by local citizens to protect the Phoenix mountains, which resulted in the creation of the 7,000-acre Phoenix Mountain Preserve enjoyed by local residents today.

New Databases Available

These new databases have been put online: http://www.asu.edu/lib/top-menus/new.html.

Forum of Federations
The Forum of Federations serves as a clearinghouse for information and resources on federalism. The Forum brings together practitioners, scholars, and youth to learn and share ideas and experiences on federalism.

Latin American Newsletters
Latin American Newsletters is an online collection of current news sources in English comprising local news digests from Latin America and the Caribbean, regional reports, personal briefings, special bulletins, research analyses, 5-year forecasts, and more.

Also, the following will be moving from Silverplatter to Ovid:

Periodical Abstracts Research II Edition with ASCII Full Text
Periodical Abstracts contains indexes, abstracts, and selected full text to articles from academic journals, popular magazines, and key business publications. Coverage is from 1986 to present, with full text coverage from 1992 to present. Periodical Abstracts will change from Silverplatter to Ovid on November 1, but access at Ovid is set up and ready to use now.


Ask LIST

Q.     "Our department has a new employee – how can s/he get a computer account, and what training resources are available?"

A.     New employees need to do two things to get computing access at ASU – first, self-subscribe for an ASURITE account (info at http://www.asu.edu/it/fyi/accounts/obtaining.html), then request an Outlook/Exchange account from the Computer Accounts office (more about Outlook at http://www.asu.edu/it/fyi/email/clients/outlook_pc.html). HR provides additional instructions about obtaining computer accounts at http://www.asu.edu/hr/new_employee/computer_accounts.html.

HR also provides a variety of training resources, both in the classroom and online via Knowledgenet. See http://www.asu.edu/hr/training/index.html for more.
