ASU and the Kauffman Campuses Initiative

 

We are pleased to announce that the Kauffman Foundation has designated ASU as a Kauffman Campus and a recipient of a $5 million grant to extend entrepreneurship education across our campuses. We look forward to working the Kauffman Foundation as we pursue our vision in becoming a University as Entrepreneur.


The Kauffman Foundation launched its Kauffman Campuses Initiative to transform the way colleges and universities prepare students for success in the American economy. In December 2003, eight universities were awarded up to $5 million each to make entrepreneurship education available across their campuses, enabling any student, regardless of field of study, to access entrepreneurial training. To continue the progress of the first round of the Kauffman Campuses Initiative, the Kauffman Foundation is now implementing a second round to create a lasting and respected place for cross-campus entrepreneurship in higher education


Proposal process

On April 12, 2006, President Crow convened an informational session on the initiative with various faculty and administrators. From that group we solicited brief idea submissions to serve as a diagnostic for the kinds of projects individuals and units at ASU would like to pursue. The idea submissions we received reflected wonderfully diverse and broad thinking, and we are currently in the process of organizing these ideas into strategies and a plan to shape this initiative. An initial iteration of this plan was submitted to the Kauffman Foundation on June 26, 2006 as a preliminary proposal (Adobe Acrobat Document).

 

Additionally, we are engaging members of the community and friends of ASU. One of the differentiating aspects between the first and second rounds of the Kauffman Campuses Initiative is that the Kauffman Foundation is seeking co-investors to partner in supporting the universities as they strengthen their entrepreneurship capacity. To this end, President Crow and Carl Schramm, President of the Kauffman Foundation, hosted a luncheon on May 23 to introduce the initiative to community members.

 

We welcome your input and engagement in this initiative. Please take some time to look through the materials posted here, including the idea submissions, the preliminary proposal (Adobe Acrobat Document), materials from the meetings on April 12 and May 23, and further information about the Kauffman Campuses Initiative. Please also visit our blog, which contains our thoughts and perspectives during this process, and share your thoughts with us.
