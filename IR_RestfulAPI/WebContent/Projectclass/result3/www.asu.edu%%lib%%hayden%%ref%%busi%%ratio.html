INDUSTRY INFORMATION SOURCES

Industry Financial and Operating Ratios


In addition to the sources listed below, industry ratios may also be found in Value Line Investment Survey (see under "Company/Industry Information Sources") and Disclosure/Worldscope Global (see under "International Company Directories and Financials").

  1. Almanac of Business and Industrial Financial Ratios. Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, 1971- . Annual. (ASU owns 1972-1978, 1980- . All years in Reserve; 1980- in Reference.)
      HF 5681 .R25 T68 HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9B) & HAYDEN RESERVE

    Commonly called "Troys," this publication profiles corporate performance in 180 industries and services for both public and private companies based on IRS figures. Arranged by SIC, the information is valuable for comparing industries though it is somewhat dated by the time it is released.

  2. Analysts Handbook. New York: Standard & Poor's, 1964- . Annual. (ASU owns 1980- . Latest 2 years in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
      HG 4915 .A52x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9B)

    Provides composite per share information on more than 90 industries. Includes dividends per share, earnings per share, net profit margin, etc. Updated by Analysts Handbook Monthly Supplement on the same table. Excerpts of this data are also repeated in Standard & Poor's Industry Survey (see under industry "Overview Sources").

  3. Annual Statement Studies. Philadelphia, PA: Robert Morris Associates, 1923- . Annual. (ASU owns 1966-1985, 1987- . All years in Reserve; 1980-1985, 1987- in Reference.)
      HF 1507 .R574 HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9B) & HAYDEN RESERVE

    Gives widely used ratios for 300 lines of business within manufacturing, wholesaling, retailing, contracting, and service industries calculated from the financial statements of companies applying for loans from participating commercial banks. Includes income statement, balance sheet, and operating and financial ratios for each industry sorted by asset and by sales. How each ratio is computed is clearly explained at the front of the book.

  4. Duns Insight. Bethlehem, PA: Dun & Bradstreet Information Services, 1990- . Annual. (ASU owns 1990- .)
      HF 5681 .R25 D852x HAYDEN REFERENCE MARS

    This is an expanded electronic version of Industry norms and Key Business Ratios below. Covers about 100 additional business lines and, at times, provides additional statistical breakdowns not found in the paper version.

  5. Industry Norms and Key Business Ratios. New York: Dun & Bradstreet Information Services, 1982/83- . Annual. (ASU owns 1982/83- .All years in Reference and Reserve.)
      HD 2771 .D85x HAYDEN REFERENCE (located on Table 9B) & HAYDEN RESERVE

    Based on Dun & Bradstreet's database of financial data on over one million public and private companies, this service provides ratios for more than 800 lines of business organized by SIC. Previously titled Key Business Ratios; before that, published annually in the magazine Dun's Review.

  6. Quarterly Financial Report for Manufacturing, Mining, and Trade Corporations. Washington, D.C.: Government Printing Office, 1947- . Quarterly. (ASU owns 1947- .)
      C 3.267 HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS

    Compiled by the Department of Commerce to show the cumulative financial results of U.S. corporations by 2-digit SIC major groups and 3-digit SIC groups. Data includes estimated statements of income and retained earnings, balance sheets, and related financial and operating ratios by industry and by asset size.
