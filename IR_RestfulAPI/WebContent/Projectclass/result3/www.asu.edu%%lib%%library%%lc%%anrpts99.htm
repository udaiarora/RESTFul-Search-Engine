Executive Committee of Librarians Council
Annual Report 1998/1999


The Committee continued the work begun to convert the Librarians' Handbook to a Web-based manual with the introduction of a Web page for Librarians' Council that provides access to the handbook as well as other council-related information, including the bylaws, officers and committee members, the schedule of Council meetings, one year of Council minutes, and the ACD Manual. A few sections of the handbook were converted to HTML and were also available. Through the generosity of the Dean of Libraries, a student was provided as time permitted to finish converting the rest of the handbook sections to HTML code. The coding was completed by June 1999 and returned to the Executive Committee for final formatting, proofing, and link verification. Completion and loading of these sections was carried over into the next committee term.

Changing the format of the handbook brought up new records retention questions. These concerns were directed to Rob Spindler, head of University Archives, who prepared a file management plan for all Council documents in order to assist the Secretary and the Chair-Elect in handling their respective record-keeping responsibilities and ensure that the appropriate records are forwarded to University Archives for permanent retention.

The attention focused on the handbook also highlighted numerous areas needing revision. These were forwarded to the appropriate committee to draft changes for Council approval. Additional discrepancies found in completing the handbook conversion will be brought to the Executive Committee's attention for future action.

Over the course of the year, the Executive Committee reviewed three document revisions proposed by the Personnel Committee. These were "Section G: Personnel Actions" and "Section F.1: Position Descriptions" of the Librarians' Handbook and the personnel action cover sheets for probationary evaluation and continuing status and promotion review. The documents were accepted by the Executive Committee and presented at Council where they were voted on and approved.

Thanks are due to all the officers and committee chairs whose assistance was instrumental in the progress made this year. Special thanks to Marilyn Wurzburger, Chair-elect, who assisted with proofing the converted handbook section; Debbie Abston who agreed to serve as parliamentarian; Diane Rhodes who filled in for the vacant past-chair position by chairing the Nomination and Elections Committee; and Julie Tharp who stepped into the vacancy caused on the Review Committee when a fellowship prevented the probationary librarian from serving. Everyone's combined efforts have made this a year of many accomplishments and have hopefully laid the groundwork for even more progress in the future.

Submitted by: Lydia E. LaFaro, Chair, Librarians' Council

ATTACHMENT---------------------------



May 19, 1999

COMPENSATION COMMITTEE REPORT

Council’s request to the Committee was to see if there is a relationship between librarian salaries and faculty salaries at ASUMain, UA, and ASUWest. Since starting salaries for librarians has been higher at UA and ASUWest than at ASUMain, are average faculty salaries also higher?

After researching the budget books for the three institutions, several problems were encountered. There are differences in department size and organization; some have Deans/Chairs, while some do not. It is difficult to know if comparisons are being made between similar quantities – i.e. does Geoscience = Geology for comparative purposes?

The results of our search were not definitive. There were several major departments for which we could not find comparable data, including Business, Education, English, and Nursing.

There were four departments where ASU Main average faculty salaries are higher: Psychology, History, Political Science, Geology.

There were five departments where ASUMain average faculty salaries are lower: Mathematics, Communications, Women’s Studies, Anthropology, Sociology.

In 8 of 9 departments, ASU Deans/Chairs were the highest paid ($100,000+) One contributing factor may be that many ASU Main departments are nearly twice the size of UA programs: e.g.

...................No. of ASU faculty........No. of UA faculty
Mathematics...82..........................57
Psychology.....48..........................18
Anthropology.34.......................... 17


One interesting sidenote: ASUWest tops all three schools in Women’s Studies:

ASU West av. salary: ..$55,400 (5 FTE)...
ASU Main av. salary......43,600 (6 FTE)...
UA av. salary.................50,000 (1.5 FTE)




Report prepared by Katsuko Hotelling and Marilyn Wurzburger
