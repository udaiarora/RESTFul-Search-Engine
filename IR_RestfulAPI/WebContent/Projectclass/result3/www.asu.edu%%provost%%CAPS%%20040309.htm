Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting (Spotlight Series)

Tuesday, March 9, 2004

Discussant: Patricia Feldman, Chair, Task Force on Academic Professionals, assisted by members of the Task Force

Topic: Open forum to discuss Preliminary Progress Report of the Task Force on Academic Professionals

Present: Approximately 70 people

Sue Hunter, chair of CAPS, convened the meeting. She explained that the session?s purpose was to discuss the Preliminary Progress Report of the task force, which was circulated to Academic Professionals at all three ASU campuses on March 1.

Patricia Feldman then took the podium. She explained that the Task Force is very interested in input and suggestions regarding their draft report, and introduced members of the Task Force who were present at the meeting. Ms. Feldman summarized the report, outlining key responses to each of the Task Force?s five charges.

The remainder of the meeting consisted of questions, comments and discussion exchanged between the audience and the Task Force, with Ms. Feldman leading the discussion. Some key questions and comments follow:

Academic Advisors:
Q: Where do academic advisors fit in to the plan described in the report?
A: The status of academic advisors, including those in the AP category, is not dealt with in this report. This is because a newly-formed Task Force on the Employment Status of Academic Advisors has been charged with developing new policies and procedures for that group of employees. Vice Provost Nancy Gutierrez explained that the hope is for the new task force to develop a plan for an Academic Advisors? career ladder. The goal is for the new task force to complete its work by the end of the semester.

Next steps:
Q: What happens to the report next, and what is the role of the Task Force from here?
A: The Task Force has a target date of April 5 to turn in a final report to the Provost?s office. At that point the work of the Task Force is complete. It is expected that new committees will be formed to plan ways to operationalize the Task Force?s recommendations.

Distinction between Continuing Status and Tenure: The distinction, as explained by ASU?s legal counsel, was described. The difference involves an ?entitlement? to continuing employment with tenure, vs. an ?expectation? of continuing employment with continuing status. It was also explained that, based on past litigation, existing case law supports the status of tenure as an entitlement, while there is no existing case law regarding continuing status.

Review process for APs seeking to achieve Continuing Status: Discussion centered on the definition of ?professional contributions.? Task Force members pointed out that, in their draft report, it is the responsibility of each individual unit to define how the university-wide standards translate to specific expectations within their unit.

Distinction between continuing status appointment and proposed multi-year appointments: Appendix F of the draft Task Force report outlines proposed guidelines for determining in which category a new position would fall. Discussion highlighted the distinction that continuing-track appointments should include all 3 elements of position effectiveness, professional contributions, and service, while multi-year appointments need not include all three of these.

Situation of individuals currently in probationary status: The Task Force plans to acknowledge this group in their report, expressing a perspective on the need for them to be able to proceed under the expectations under which they were hired.

Recommendations on sabbatical leave: The Task Force is still working on its recommendations on sabbatical leave. They will be included under charge #5. The task force seeks input on this topic, especially regarding solutions to issues of how to cover the job responsibilities of an AP while he/she is on sabbatical.

Ongoing feedback: Both Patricia Feldman and Nancy Gutierrez reiterated the Task Force?s desire for feedback on the draft report. Additional suggestions can be directed to Task Force committee members (the roster is available at http://www.asu.edu/provost/ap/).

The meeting was adjourned at 11:30 a.m.
