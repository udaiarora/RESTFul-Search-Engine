November 2, 2005

Study explores health disparities in Latino children

A research team at the School of Health Management and Policy at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business has received a $500,000 grant from the National Institute of Health to study health care service disparities among Latino children in Yuma County.

The study will focus on asthma, the most common chronic illness in children, as well as preventive health care, like immunizations and well baby checks.

Principal investigator William Johnson and co-investigator Mary Rimsza of the Center for Health Information and Research at the W. P. Carey School’s Seidman Research Institute, will use the Arizona Health Query (AZHQ) – a database of health care information that allows data to be sorted by community or county – to study Latino children in the mostly rural county where Latinos are the majority. The AZHQ database contains the largest record of Latino children’s health information in the country.

“This study is unique,” Johnson says. “Previous studies of Latino children’s health have been done mostly in urban areas where Latinos were a minority – but, in Yuma County, Latinos are the majority. This will allow us to distinguish between health disparities due to minority status versus those due to ethnicity.”

Rimsza says gaining knowledge of health disparities among Hispanic children is important as Latinos are a rapidly growing segment of the population – and, in some parts of the United States, including Arizona, they will be a majority by 2030.
