February 8, 2006

ASU counselors help students apply for aid

Financial aid counselors at all three ASU campuses will help high school seniors fill out financial aid forms on College Goal Sunday, from 2 - 4 p.m., Feb. 12. Students enrolling in any college or university are welcome to attend the free event, sponsored by the Arizona Commission for Postsecondary Education.

ASU is participating with nearly 30 other educational institutions across Arizona in this event. Locations are the Memorial Union at the Tempe campus; the Agribusiness Center Building at the Polytechnic campus; and the University Center Building at the West campus.

Spanish speakers will be available at the three ASU campuses, and Navajo speakers will be present at the Tempe campus.

ASU representatives will answer questions, discuss the types of financial aid options available, and help students and their families complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) form, the first step toward finding resources to reach their education dreams.

Students are encouraged to come with their parents and bring all relevant financial documents, including tax returns and W-2 forms. Students also should apply for a Department of Education personal identification number (PIN) before the event. The PIN, which serves as an electronic signature, provides students and parents with access to personal records throughout the student's college education.

A list of documents, other sites and a link to the PIN application is at (www.asu.edu/fa/cgs).

Since its inception, the annual College Goal Sunday has helped 21,000 college-bound students and their families complete the FAFSA. This is the 10th year that financial aid professionals and counselors have donated their time for the event.

"Arizona State University is committed to making a college education available to all who are qualified, regardless of financial status," says Craig Fennell, ASU director of student financial assistance.

Free parking is available on Sundays in metered spaces, and in the parking structure at Apache and College. For more information, go to the Web site (www.asu.edu/fa/cgs) or call (480) 965-3355.
