May 10, 2004

Service-sector jobs proliferate as population grows

Service-providing jobs, which account for 85 percent of all Arizona employment, are a function of a growing population and not "replacements" for jobs lost in other sectors, according to an analysis in the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

"It would be a mistake to think that these 'people serving' jobs are displacing high-paying scientific or white-collar jobs," said Lee McPheters , contributing editor for the Arizona Blue Chip. "The growth of services and retailing is not an example of economic development gone awry.

"Service-providing jobs in the nation as a whole account for the same 85 percent of all jobs as in Arizona . The economy does not create these jobs 'instead of' high-tech or biotech or corporate executive jobs. Service-providing job growth is merely driven by natural market forces responding to population increases."

Looking ahead, McPheters said it is very likely that retail employment will increase significantly in 2004. If population in the Phoenix area increases by 100,000, then the expected increase in retail employment should be at least 5,500 to 6,000 jobs.

"While policymakers and planners are correct to emphasize knowledge-based, high-wage jobs that compete in national and global markets, it does not follow at all that the growth of services and retailing has taken the place of such jobs. A growing economy does not have to be what economists would refer to as a 'zero sum game.'"

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark surveyed the panel members on the outlook for the construction industry through 2005. "The forecast is for steady but not spectacular improvement in 2004," Clark reported. "Residential will continue to benefit from relatively low interest rates during the year. Residential dollar value is not expected to decline in 2005, but the growth rate will slow as higher rates begin to have an impact. Commercial and industrial will continue to improve as businesses expands operations in response to the improving economy."

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center , an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. For information, call (480) 965-5543.
