August 16, 2006
5–7 p.m.
Student Services Lawn Area



Welcome to graduate studies at ASU! To celebrate your arrival, we're hosting an ice cream social. In addition to great treats and live music, you'll find representatives from a variety of departments and organizations to help you find your way around our university and local community.

Important Service Departments
Graduate Student Associations
ASU Libraries: University libraries house more than 3 million volumes, approximately 7 million microform units, and more than 33,000 periodical and serial subscriptions.

ASU Student Health Center: Students pay minimal or no charges, depending on the services rendered. ASU pays for health insurance for qualified TAs/RAs.

Student Recreation: The Student Recreation Complex (SRC) is for the use of the ASU community and offers a variety of activities and memberships.

International Programs Office (IPO) administers study abroad and exchange programs in over 40 countries around the world. Students earn ASU resident credit for their studies, which can last for a semester or a full academic year.

ASU Department of Public Safety provides parking, police, and other services designed to contribute to a safe and secure university environment and is internationally accredited.

ASU Bookstore has new and used textbooks, supplies, general books, magazines, newspapers, computer hardware and software, ASU insignia clothing, gift items, and graduation cap and gown rentals.

ASU Sponsored Projects. The Office for Research and Sponsored Projects Administration (ORSPA) assists faculty, staff and students to secure and administer outside support for their instructional, research, and public service activities.

City of Tempe. ASU is located in the heart of downtown Tempe, creating an exciting and eclectic melting pot. Tempe is a city is packed with history, culture, shopping, dining, nightlife, sports, special events, and is one of the most popular spots for Arizona vacations, offering all types of entertainment options.

ASU at the Polytechnic campus offers nine master's degree programs in agribusiness, nutrition, applied biological sciences, and several specialized areas of technology. Located 23 miles southeast of the Tempe campus, its 5,000 students enjoy small classes, high-tech classrooms, and accessible faculty.

InterCollegiate Athletics provides opportunities for student-athletes at the highest levels of both academic and athletic excellence, and includes opportunities for personal growth, offering a unifying force to the campus community, and creating a source of pride among and support from the external constituencies of the university.

Student Financial Assistance Office provides resources for graduate students interested in obtaining aid for their studies. Financial assistance comes in the form of federal aid, scholarships from outside resources, ASU scholarships, fellowships, research grants, and teaching and research assistantships, as well as on-campus employment.

InterGroup Relations Center promotes positive intergroup relations among students, faculty, and staff and improves the campus climate for diversity at ASU.

ASU Multicultural Student Center provides a central location for multicultural students to access services designed to assist them in their pursuit for academic success.

Testing Support Services / Counseling and Consultation provides workshops for students preparing to take educational institution entrance exams. Workshops currently offered include the Graduate Record Exam (GRE), the Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT), and the Law School Admission Test (LSAT). Additional tutoring is provided for students who want practice beyond that offered by the workshops. Test preparation workshops are administered in various classrooms throughout the university.

Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) is a professional development program for doctoral and MFA students who are preparing for academic careers at institutions of higher education. It is designed to prepare graduate students for the multiple roles they will face as faculty members, and to allow them to understand how these roles and expectations can vary in different campus settings.

Preparing Future Professionals (PFP) program was launched by the Division of Graduate Studies to assist students interested in pursuing nonacademic professions. Geared more toward Ph.D. candidates, PFP familiarizes students with various nonacademic career tracks and helps them develop skills to successfully pursue a wide range of career opportunities.
American Indian Graduate Student Association (AIGSA) was created to provide peer support and a community for American Indian graduate students at ASU. It is also available as a resource for American Indian undergraduates who are considering graduate studies.

Black Graduate Student Association (BGSA) is committed to addressing the social/cultural, academic, and political areas of interest and needs of the black graduate student population at ASU.

Graduate and Professional Student Association (GPSA) facilitates interdisciplinary communication among graduate students and represents graduate students at ASU. The Graduate Student Association functions as the Graduate Student Council within ASASU.

Graduate Women's Association (GWA) addresses issues commonly encountered by graduate women. It facilitates interdisciplinary interactions for both professional and social development and fosters an open forum for members to seek positive solutions, feedback, and support and will actively seek, provide, and prepare participants for mentoring relationships both within and outside the ASU community.

Women's Coalition's goal is to increase enrollment, retention, and graduation of female students; increase positive activism and participation of women's organizations at ASU; increase the amount of support provided by ASU to women and provide a conscientious voice for all women at ASU.

Latina(o) Graduate Student Alliance (LGSA) is an organization of Hispanic graduate students at Arizona State University who have joined together to promote academic achievement of Latina/o students, encourage interaction between Latina/o graduate students, faculty, and the community at large, and promote awareness of sociopolitical  issues affecting the Hispanic community.

Graduates in the Earth, Life, & Social Sciences (GELSS) encourages increased interaction among research groups both within and among departments and facilitates future cooperative projects.

SafeZONE/Alternative Lifestyle teaches people how to be supportive allies to lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgendered persons.

Additional ASU student organizations may be found online at the Student Organization Resource Center: www.asu.edu/studentaffairs/mu/clubs.
