GPSA Honors Graduate Students for Teaching Excellence, Zatz as Outstanding Mentor

The Graduate and Professional Student Association (GPSA) have honored 17 graduate students for excellence in teaching at ASU, and Marjorie Zatz, associate dean for the Division of Graduate Studies, for her long-standing and deep commitment to the graduate student community.

More than 100 nominations campus-wide were submitted for GPSA's Teaching Excellence Award. The selection process included solicited information and classroom observations by a committee of ten graduate student peer reviewers. From those, 15 graduate student teaching assistants and instructors were selected for the Teaching Excellence Award and two for Continuing Excellence Awards.

"This program is a way of recognizing the intangible yet profound contribution that teaching provides," said Fernando Romero, GPSA's director of graduate research, who chairs the award program along with Destiny Crider, vice president of internal affairs.

The recipients of the 2004–05 GPSA Teaching Excellence Award are: Phil Clark, Math; Kayoko Dan, Music; Vernon Dickson, English; Tiffani Kisler, Family & Human Development; Marianne LeGreco, Communications; Pamela Lutgen-Sandvik, Communications; Carol Majia-Laperle, English; Lisa Makros, English; Nimisha Patel, Psychology in Education; Leanne Schmidt, Dance; Thomas Small, School of Life Sciences; Heather Stickler, Theatre; Diane Topping, School of Social Work; Zachary Waggoner, English; and Sandra Woien, Philosophy. Lauren Yena, English, and Michael Coyle, Justice & Social Inquiry, received the Continuing Excellence Award.

At the awards ceremony, hosted during Graduate and Professional Student Appreciation Week, Zatz was presented with the 2004–05 Outstanding Mentor Award. GPSA recognizes the common challenge of participating in campus service work without strong support from advisors. Two years ago, the organization began acknowledging faculty for their support and mentorship of students involved in activities outside of the standard academic program. In essence, the Outstanding Mentor Award is a tribute to a faculty member who has provided unwavering support and advising to GPSA, said its president, Deirdre Hahn.

Zatz said of her distinction, "I am truly honored to receive this award. The GPSA leadership does a terrific job of representing graduate students and I enjoy working with them. I also want to take this opportunity to congratulate the graduate students who received the Teaching and Continuing Excellence Awards for their achievements, and to honor their work as educators and mentors of our undergraduate students."

This year the GPSA executive committee unanimously agreed that Zatz had been not only a mentor to each of them, but more importantly, to all students.

"Marjorie certainly made sure that we were keeping up with our academic work, but she also validated the importance of GPSA programming for the entire graduate student community," Hahn said. "Her honesty and down-to-earth, no-nonsense approach to mentoring the GPSA executive group this year has been nothing short of amazing. She created an atmosphere in which we had to think critically about the issues graduate students face. She challenges us as students in our service roles."

For more information about GPSA, visit www.asu.edu/gpsa.
