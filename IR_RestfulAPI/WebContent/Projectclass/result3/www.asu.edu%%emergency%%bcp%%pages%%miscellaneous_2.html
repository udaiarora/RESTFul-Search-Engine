Business Continuity Plan - Attachments

ASU Departmental Disaster Planning

Overview

Now that the Disaster Management Team members and the various Departments have been established, the actual Business Continuation Plan development can begin. Each Department develops a Departmental Plan and addresses the following major issues:

  • Objectives
  • Plan Organization
  • Staffing Requirements
  • Necessary Supplies
  • Critical Equipment
  • Telephone Lists

Once each Department completes their Plan, the Plan is submitted to the Disaster Management Team for review and completion.

Objectives

The primary objectives of the Plan are restoration of academic operations, mitigation of damage, and the safety of students, faculty and staff. Everyone needs to keep these objectives in mind throughout the drafting of the Business Continuation Plan.

Plan Organization

Each Department will need to organize its Plan to identify the activities to be performed during each of the following time frames:

  1. Continual Readiness
  2. Pre-Disaster
    • Watch Period
    • Warning Period
  3. Disaster Impact
  4. Post-Disaster Recovery
    • Immediate
    • Intermediate
    • Long Term

It is important to consider the different risks to which the college is exposed and to make appropriate continual modifications to the Plan. For example, for hurricane planning, some Departments may require more time to prepare than the 24 - 36 hours watch period issued by the National Weather Service for hurricanes. On the other hand, many disasters (accidents, earthquakes, etc.) strike without warning. For these disaster, the pre-disaster watch and warning periods do not exist.

During the actual Disaster Impact period, many normal activities will be suspended. Possibly some effort can be made to minimize damages, such as extinguishing or retarding a fire, shutting down equipment, providing basic security, etc. However, most efforts will center around safety and appropriate relocation & evacuation procedures.

Staffing Requirements

Minimum staff levels need to be identified for each critical business function. Once each Department identifies its primary Post-Disaster activities, it is possible to determine which normal job functions can be deferred for a short period of time. This will allow more staff to be able to address the most immediate needs in other areas of the college.

Necessary Supplies

Each Department needs to identify all of its critical business functions and the supplies needed to sustain operation. Supplies to protect departmental assets also need to be identified and purchased or acquired in advance. For example, it will be very difficult (probably impossible) to acquire materials to protect computers, buildings, etc. 48 hours before a hurricane strikes. After the disaster strikes, supplies that are of any value will rapidly vanish.

Critical Equipment

Each Department needs to identify all of its critical equipment for use not only within their department, but for reallocation and use by other departments to facilitate the protection and recovery of the college. Procedures to protect these assets also need to be documented.

Telephone Lists

Detailed telephone lists are a crucial part of the departmental plan to be transmitted to the Disaster Management Team. We identified a number of instances after the Northridge Earthquake and Hurricane Andrew where important telephone numbers were not available when a Department was unavailable to report.
