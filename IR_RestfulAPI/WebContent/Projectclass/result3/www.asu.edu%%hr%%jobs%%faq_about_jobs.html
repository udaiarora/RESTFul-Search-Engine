Human Resources

Frequently Asked Questions about getting a job at ASU.

Where can I get a job application?

Arizona State University DOES NOT use job applications. The University relies strictly on cover letters, resumes, and references for its application process. Refer to the "TO APPLY" section on the Vacancy Notice to see what materials that particular department is requesting applicants to submit.

What if I want to teach at ASU?

If you are interested in teaching at ASU, you need to contact each department directly. For example, if you are interested in teaching Economics, you would contact the Economics Department directly to see if there are any open faculty positions.

Where do I send my resume?

Approximately 65% of the Departments at Arizona State University at the Tempe campus, at the Polytechnic campus, and at the Downtown Phoenix campus, request that applicants send their materials (cover letter, resume, references, etc.) through the Human Resources Department located on ASU's main campus (ASU West has a separate Human Resources Department which handles their hiring). However, the remaining 35% request that applicants send their materials directly to the department advertising the opening. This request is indicated at the bottom of each and every vacancy notice. Please make sure to direct your materials to the correct department.

How do I put together a resume for an ASU position?

For online assistance please go to Resume/Cover Letter. ASU now offers a one hour session, once a month on Thursdays from 3:30 pm - 4:30 pm, entitled "Understanding the ASU Hiring Process". The class includes suggestions and information on preparing a resume for ASU employment for classified, service professional and administrative staff. To register, email HRstaffingservices@asu.edu. Please include your name and phone number in your email. Registration confirmation will be emailed to you and will include the date and location of the class.

Can I email my resume?

Yes, if the job notice requests that your materials be sent to the Human Resources Department, you can submit your resume as an email attachment to resumes@asu.edu . Include the Job number (SR#) on your resume. Type "Resume" in the subject line of the message box.

If the job notice requests that you materials be sent to the department directly, they may or may not specify an email address for resumes to be sent to. Note: if resumes are to be sent directly to the department, do not email them to the Human Resources Department.

Where do I get the name of the contact person to whom I should address my cover letter, resume and references?

The Human Resources Department at ASU DOES NOT have this information. If this information has not been provided at the bottom of the Vacancy Notice, please contact the department advertising the opening directly OR simply address your materials Dear Hiring Coordinator OR Attention Selection Committee.

Will I be contacted regarding my resume?

(A) If the Department selects you for an interview, you will be contacted. (B) If you participated in an interview, and were not chosen for the position, you will be sent a rejection letter from the Department that interviewed you. Otherwise, it is the Department's decision as to whether or not to notify applicants who fall outside of categories A and B.

How do I find out the status of my resume?

If the Department advertising the opening has requested that applicants' resumes be sent to the Human Resources Department, the resumes will be held in Human Resources until the closing date indicated at the top of the Vacancy Notice. Once the Department has received the resumes from Human Resources, it then takes approximately 2 to 3 weeks for candidates selected for an interview to be notified. If you want to speak to someone sooner regarding your resume, you must contact the department advertising the opening directly, and request to speak to the hiring coordinator.

How do I self-disclose information for the University's Affirmative Action Plan?

ASU offers applicants an opportunity to voluntarily self-disclose information for the University's affirmative action plan; applicants may complete an EEO survey for the position(s) to which the applicant is applying at http://www.eoaa.asu.edu/aa_EEO_Survey.asp
