August 12, 2003

Enhanced tuition waiver benefit increases semester credit hours

The tuition waiver, a favorite benefit among ASU employees, just got even better.
Beginning this fall, ASU benefits eligible employees and their spouses can take advantage of enhanced tuition waiver benefits. For $25 per student, they can take up to nine credit hours - up from six credits - during the fall and spring semesters. During the summer, the tuition waiver credit limit will remain at 3 credit hours per summer session.

The change is the result of the Arizona Board of Regents' recent approval of several adjustments proposed by ASU President Michael Crow to ABOR Policy 6-902: Qualified Tuition Reduction Program and ASU SPP 505:Employee Reduced In-State Tuition.
Thomas Walker, program coordinator, senior, for Intergroup Relations, and president of ASU Main's Classified Staff Council (CSC) says the tuition enhancement is great news for employees.

"The waiver expansion has been on the council's agenda, in fact on the tri-university councils' agenda, for a couple of years," Walker says. "Dr. Crow took up the cause as a way to help reward employees, since widespread raises weren't possible. Along with scattered salary adjustments and ASU's absorption of some insurance increases this year, the CSC appreciates this big step in better supporting ASU's hardworking staff and other employees."

Human Resources Assistant Director Christine Cervantes says these changes to the tuition waiver benefit will assist a great deal of ASU employees and their families.
"There was a concerted effort in all of these changes to ensure that they support the culture envisioned by the university's Changing Directions," Cervantes says. "These desired changes further enable employees and their families to continue to develop their education and at the same time, employees will add value to the organization."

Classified staff members who have been laid off, and who meet the eligibility criteria, may continue to utilize the tuition waiver benefits for up to 12 months. The tuition waiver benefit will be provided for 12 months from the effective date of the layoff and will extend to the affiliated participants (laid-off employee, spouse, dependent children).

Provisions also have been made to extend tuition waivers to severely disabled combat survivors or to the children and spouses of a service member from Arizona killed in the line of duty during a federal or state emergency declared by the president of the United States or governor of Arizona.

Cervantes says spouses and children of employees whose jobs are terminated due to long-term disability also may continue to participate in the tuition waiver program. In order to receive this benefit the terminated employee must have at least five years of continuous service immediately preceding termination and be receiving long-term disability benefits.

"I think the most important thing about these enhancements is that it's a win-win for everyone; it meets both the needs of the employees and ASU," Cervantes says. These enhancements support the university's goal of building a world-class university. Our new tuition waiver benefits will absolutely contribute to this success."

For information, go to (www.asu.edu/workingatasu/ benefits).
