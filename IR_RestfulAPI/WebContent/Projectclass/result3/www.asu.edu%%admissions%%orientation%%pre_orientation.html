Submit MMR Immunization Form

You must have verification of two MMR (measles [rubeola] /mumps/rubella) immunizations on record at ASU, or you cannot register for classes. Freshmen should complete this step before attending ASU Orientation. Use the ASU Immunization Form that ASU sends to you with your admission certificate or print the ASU Immunization Form.

To verify that ASU has received and recorded your immunization documentation before you attend ASU Orientation, go to ASU Interactive, log in, and view your ASU Orientation Summary. Please be aware that it takes approximately two days from the time ASU receives your documentation to record it in the system. Therefore, you must send/fax your MMR documentation well in advance of your scheduled Orientation program.

What to Bring to Orientation

  • Photo ID for check in.
  • There is a $25 fee for your ASU photo ID, please bring check or cash to Orientation.

  • Parking and directions for your program location.
  • Unified Math Placement Exam Score.
  • Sweatshirt or light jacket (Program rooms are air conditioned).
  • Backpack (for carrying items you collect throughout day).
  • If you have completed or are currently enrolled in college courses, bring a copy of your enrollment or a transcript for your ASU academic advisor to review.

Program Payment, Cancellations or Changes, and Special Needs

Program payment : Students requesting the check payment option have 10 business days to remit their payment to New Student Orientation.

Cancellations or Changes :

  • If you would like to change your program date or cancel your ASU Orientation session, you must notify New Student Orientation in writing (by mail, fax or online) so that the cancellation or Orientation Change Request is received at least five business days before your scheduled Orientation date.
  • Each change to your program date or cancellation will be assessed a $15 processing fee.
  • A $15 processing fee will be charged for all returned checks and denied credit cards.
  • If you wish to add a guest or substitute a guest, you must submit your Orientation Change Request in writing or online.
  • Any Orientation refund requests will be processed in July 2007 upon conclusion of the ASU Orientation season with required documentation. Please note that in order to receive a refund for a cancellation you must submit your request in writing (by mail, fax or online) 5 business days before the date of the program you wish to cancel.
  • In order to provide you with the appropriate academic advising, it is important that we have the correct information about the major you have chosen. If the major displayed in your reservation confirmation is incorrect, or has changed, please contact New Student Orientation or submit an Orientation Change Request to update your major's information at least five business days before your scheduled ASU Orientation program.

Special Needs: If you have special needs contact New Student Programs at 480.965.2880 or orientation@asu.edu at least 10 business days before your scheduled program. Special needs include: disabled parking, assistance with tours, language interpretation and/or a special meal.

Housing and Financial Aid: Most students have already applied for housing as well as financial aid. If you have not done this yet, take care of this as soon as possible.

  • Apply for on-campus housing
  • Apply for financial aid and scholarships

Step 1. Get Connected

You must activate your ASURITE UserID at www.asu.edu/asurite.

The ASURITE UserID allows you to access:

1. ASU Interactive www.asu.edu/interactive
Register for classes, pay fees, check grades, and order transcripts online. You will not be able to register for classes at ASU Orientation without your ASURITE UserID and password.

2. emma www.asu.edu/emma
Use the Electronic Post Office Update Facility (EPO) in emma to forward your ASU mail to an existing non-ASU e-mail address. This will enable ASU to send you electronic correspondence. This is not full e-mail service. At the beginning of the fall semester, once you have paid your fees you'll have access to additional services including an ASU e-mail account and Web space. It is essential you use your ASU email address for all ASU correspondence.

ASU uses e-mail for communications within Arizona State University when appropriate because it delivers information in a convenient and timely manner. The University expects students, faculty members and staff to check their ASU e-mail regularly. These procedures are to encourage the appropriate use of ASU e-mail accounts and to communicate the significance of timely use of ASU e-mail. Be sure to ACTIVATE your e-mail account again after you pay your fees.

3. my ASU portal my.asu.edu
Customizable content, courses and online communities are available to you through an online portal, much like "My Yahoo!" only for ASU.

Step 2. Review Placement Information

Please review the following for information on English, Mathematics and Foreign Language Placement.

English Placement

Request that your official ACT or SAT test scores be sent to ASU. If you did not take the ACT or SAT, you may take the English Placement exam to determine the appropriate First-Year Composition course. ( Contact University Testing Services at www.asu.edu/uts or 480.965.7146.)

You will be advised to register for a First-Year Composition course to meet the First-Year Composition Requirement based on your ACT English or SAT Verbal score.

If you have an ACT English score of 26 or higher, or a SAT verbal score of 620 or higher, you may take ENG 105 to fulfill your First-Year Composition requirement. Students may also qualify for ENG 105 by achieving appropriate scores on the CLEP General Examination in English Composition with Essay or the CLEP Subject Examination in College Composition with Essay. For CLEP Examination information, contact University Testing Services at www.asu.edu/uts or 480.965.7146.

If you have an ACT English score of 19-26, or a SAT verbal score of 470-620, you will take a two-semester sequence to fulfill your First-Year Composition requirement. The courses in that sequence are ENG 101 and ENG 102.

If you have an ACT English score of 18 or lower, or a SAT verbal score of 460 or lower, you will take a three-semester sequence to fulfill ASU's First-Year Composition requirement. The courses in that sequence are WAC 101, ENG 101, and ENG 102.

For Advanced Placement (AP) credit you must submit a request to the College Board to send your test scores to ASU.

For transfer credit you must have each college you have attended send your official transcripts directly to ASU

If you have AP credit or college credit for an English class you took at another college or university, ask your advisor about how that course will transfer to ASU.

Mathematics Placement

Unified Math Placement Exam

All students are required to complete the Unified Math Placement Exam. You must take the Unified Math Placement Exam online 72 hours before your ASU Orientation Program. Your math class will be determined by your performance on the mandatory Unified Math Placement Exam. Remember to bring your score printout to ASU Orientation. Note the practice exam will not be accepted as an official score. Visit http://math.asu.edu/fym/placement.html to take the exam.

Based on your exam score, your academic advisor will provide information about math requirements for your major at ASU Orientation.

Advanced Placement (AP) credit and Transfer credit

For Advanced Placement (AP) credit you must submit a request to the College Board to send your test scores to ASU.

For transfer credit you must have each college you have attended send your official transcripts directly to ASU.

If you have AP credit or college credit for a Math class you took at another college or university, ask your advisor about how that course will transfer to ASU.

Foreign Language Placement

If you plan to register for French, German, or Spanish at ASU, you must determine your level by taking an online placement test at www.asu.edu/languages/lpinfo.html.

For all other languages: if you have never studied the language before, you should enroll in a 101 class. If you have some experience with the language, consult with an Academic Advisor and/or an instructor in the language who can administer a mini-placement exam and offer advice. For a list of faculty members, go to www.asu.edu/languages.

Step 3. Learn University Terminology

To be successful, it is important for you to be familiar with the following university terminology. Your academic advisors will expect you to know the following university terminology:

Campus

A geographic location of Arizona State University. ASU comprises the Downtown Phoenix campus, Polytechnic campus, Tempe campus and West campus.

College/School

An academic unit within the university.

Concurrent Degree

Some students choose to pursue two baccalaureate degrees simultaneously. ASU calls this a concurrent degree. For the purpose of ASU Orientation, you must select a primary major so that you can receive advising for your first semester. Your academic advisor can provide information about pursuing a second degree.

Course Load

Students should enroll in 15 - 16 credit-hours per semester in order to graduate in four years.

Course Selection

At Orientation students will have two course selection options, "block" scheduling and "a la carte" scheduling. Block scheduling offers students a variety of pre-determined course schedules to choose from . A la carte scheduling offers students the opportunity to select their own semester schedule. Your academic advisor will teach you more about course scheduling during Orientation.

Credit Hour (also referred to as Semester Hour)

One credit hour represents a minimum of 50 minutes of class attendance per week per semester. Most classes are three credit hours. Baccalaureate degrees require a minimum of 120 credit hours (some majors require more). To receive credit, you must be properly registered and pay fees.

Double Majors

See Concurrent Degree.

Major

The specialized group of courses in a program of study is called a major.

If you decide to change your major, you must notify New Student Orientation at 480.965.2880 before making an orientation reservation so that you can receive appropriate academic advisement during ASU Orientation. Changes to your major cannot be made during your orientation session.  Requests for changes to the major are updated every 24 hours.

Minor

A specialized group of courses within a program of study, typically comprised of 18-25 credit hours. A minor is available in some academic units, but is not required.

Lower-Division Courses
Upper-Division Courses

Courses numbered 100-299 are primarily for freshmen and sophomores.
Courses numbered 300-499 are primarily for juniors and seniors. Freshman students should not register for upper-division courses.

Prerequisite

A particular course to be taken before registering for another particular course.

Professional Program Application

Some degree programs require a separate application for admission to the professional program status, portfolio requirements, or placement auditions. This additional application process typically occurs before the sophomore year. Your academic advisors will help you learn more about professional program applications and if they are required for your major.

Step 4. Learn University Graduation Requirements

Students must complete a minimum of 120 credit hours (some majors require more), including 45 upper-division credits, for graduation with a baccalaureate degree. The General Studies Requirement and the First-Year Composition Requirement are among university requirements. The General Studies Requirement includes a minimum of 35 credit hours in five core areas and three awareness areas.

College Requirements

Some colleges have additional requirements. Read about them in the ASU General Catalog. Your academic advisor will answer any questions you have.

Major Requirements

The credit hours required for a major range from 30 to 63. Major requirements are specifically related to your degree program. Your academic advisor will help you learn more about major requirements during Orientation.

Electives

Electives are courses not specifically required in your program for graduation. These courses count toward graduation. Some majors leave no room for electives. Your academic advisor will help you learn more about electives during Orientation.

General Studies Requirement

The General Studies Requirement complements your major and includes five core areas and three awareness areas for all majors.

Core Areas

The General Studies Requirement five core areas help you acquire critical lifelong learning skills and guide your exploration of the traditional branches of knowledge. The core areas are:

  • Literacy and critical inquiry (L): Six credit hours including one upper-division course.
  • Mathematics studies (MA and CS): Six credit hours including three credit hours in college mathematics or college algebra or evidence of a higher level of skill by completing a course for which college algebra is a prerequisite - must be completed by the time you have earned 30 credit hours (MA) - and three credit hours in the use of statistics/quantitative analysis or the use of the computer in serious math analytical work (CS).
  • Humanities and fine arts (HU) and Social and behavioral sciences (SB): 15 credit hours total in HU and SB (6 credit hours in one area and 9 in the other including 3 upper-division credit hours).
  • Natural sciences (SQ and SG) : 8 credit hours of laboratory science including 4 SQ hours. Natural Science-Quantitative (SQ) laboratory courses include an introduction to the fundamental behavior of matter and energy in physical and biological systems. Natural Science-General (SG) laboratory courses cover aspects of scientific inquiry that lend themselves to more qualitative or descriptive discussions of science.

Awareness Areas

The three awareness areas promote appreciation of cultural diversity within the contemporary United States, develop an international perspective, and foster an understanding of current human events through study of the past.

The awareness areas are:

  • Cultural diversity in the United States (C)
  • Global awareness (G)
  • Historical awareness (H)

Students must complete courses that satisfy each of the awareness areas. Courses that are listed for a core area and one or more awareness areas may satisfy these requirements concurrently.

Step 5. Transcripts and Test Scores

Transcripts

Ask each high school and college you have attended to send your official transcripts directly to ASU.

You must also request that a final official transcript be sent from your school directly to ASU upon graduation or completion of the current semester.

Please note that hand-delivered transcripts are not official.

Please be aware that if you are concurrently enrolled in high school and college courses, or if you are dual enrolled in college courses taught on your high school campus, you must request official transcripts from both your high school and the college offering the dual credit to be sent to ASU. ASU will not award college credit that appears only on high school transcripts.
