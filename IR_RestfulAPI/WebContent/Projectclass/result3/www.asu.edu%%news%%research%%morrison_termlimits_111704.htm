November 17, 2004

Report identifies issues, consequences of term limits

A new report from the Morrison Institute for Public Policy at ASU – titled “The Effects of Legislative Term Limits in Arizona” – finds that the term-limit reform adopted by Arizona voters in 1992 has caused legislators to make some painful adjustments.

The publication draws upon historical data and information from surveys and interviews with more than 50 legislators, lobbyists, and knowledgeable observers. Because of term limits, many legislators have decided to run for another office before the expiration of their terms. This often has meant trying to move from one legislative house to another, most commonly from the House to the Senate.

“Among the findings that immediately catch the eye are that legislative term limits have had particularly important implications with regard to the power of legislative leaders,” says David Berman, senior research fellow with ASU’s Morrison Institute for Public Policy and author of the report. “The study also suggests that the Legislature, for good or ill, is just not operating the way it used to.”

On the plus side, the report finds that term limits have encouraged competition for legislative and other seats and given voters a greater choice among candidates. To some extent, limits have been a force toward a more inclusive governing process. At the same time, they have generally reduced the power of legislative leaders and increased the influence of lobbyists and staff, though not all lobbyists and staff have gained equally. Recent newcomers to the Arizona Legislature probably are not any less knowledgeable than previous classes of newcomers – but, under term limits, there are more newcomers and members have less time to learn. For many, the limit to four two-year terms (eight years total) provides too little time to learn how to do the job and do it well.

“The Effects of Legislative Term Limits in Arizona” is available on the institute’s Web site at (www.morrisoninstitute.org) or by calling (480) 965-4525.
