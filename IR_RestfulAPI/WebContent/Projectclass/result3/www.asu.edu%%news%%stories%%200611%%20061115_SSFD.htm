November 15, 2006

ASU launches Social and Family Dynamics school

The study and advancement of valued aspects of human life ? children, youth, families and their social worlds ? are at the core of ASU's new School of Social and Family Dynamics.

Children from the ASU Child Development Lab entertained the crowd with songs prior to speakers Provost Betty Capaldi, Dean Davis Young and Richard Fabes, professor and founding director, School of Social and Family Dynamics.

?This new school, which appropriately has the word ?dynamics' in its name, aims to make a difference for families, children and society as a whole,? says Elizabeth D. Capaldi, ASU executive vice president and provost. ?Its impact, and its role in ASU's transformation as a New American University, is evident in its core values and how it approaches interdisciplinary research.?

Capaldi will help launch the new school at a celebration scheduled for 11 a.m., Nov. 16, in the East Portico of Lattie Coor Hall on ASU's Tempe campus. Also, part of this week's launch activities is a graduate student research poster competition from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m., Nov. 16, also at Coor Hall, and, a panel discussion from 3 p.m. to 4:30 p.m. in the Memorial Union's Pima Room, on the topic ?The Future is Now! Strategies for Success in the New American University.?

A keynote address by Eleanor Maccoby, professor emeritus of psychology at Stanford University, is slated for 7:30 p.m., Nov. 15, in ASU's Galvin Theatre. She will address the challenges and opportunities of interdisciplinary research in her presentation, titled ?The Promise and Pitfalls of Interdisciplinary Research.?

Established July 1, the School of Social and Family Dynamics merges faculty members and programs from two previously separate departments ? family and human development, and sociology ? in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

Richard Fabes, the Dee and John Whiteman Dean's Distinguished Professor, is founding director of the new school. He previously chaired the Department of Family and Human Development.

?Envisioning and building a school like this could only be accomplished with the strong leadership that Rick Fabes brings,? says David A. Young, ASU vice president and dean of the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

?The structure of this new school provides a platform for flexibility and collaboration in research, teaching and outreach,? Young says. ?It will build on the strengths of the two departments while greatly enhancing ASU's role in social science research and teaching, as it promotes transdisciplinary initiatives across the university.?

?Our intention is for the School of Social and Family Dynamics to become a world-class leader in changing the ways academic units ask questions, develop and employ scientific methods and technologies, train and educate students, and interact with the broader social community,? Fabes says. ?Together with faculty and program affiliates from across ASU, such as nursing, speech and hearing, education and psychology, and from the local community, the school will become central to the transdisciplinary study and advancement of the most important and valued aspects of human life ? namely, children, youth, families, cultures and societies.?

According to Fabes, a major influence in the idea behind the school is the late Ruth Reed Cowden, a home economics teacher who was dedicated to the field that today is known as family and human development. The Cowden Family Resources Building on the Tempe campus, which houses some of the school's classrooms and faculty offices, is named for this ASU benefactor and her husband.

The Nov. 16 panel discussion with professors who serve on the school's international leadership board will focus on how to make interdisciplinary social science programs successful. Panel members include W. Andrew Collins, University of Minnesota; Rand Conger, University of California-Davis; Susan McHale, Pennsylvania State University; and Debra Umberson, University of Texas-Austin. Other members of the international leadership board include Aletha Huston, University of Texas-Austin; James Moody, Duke University; and Tom Snijders, University of Oxford.

The school's launch activities, highlighted this week with an official ceremony, began earlier this semester with a series of lectures featuring Cowden Distinguished Professors Gary Ladd, Carol Martin and Kimberly Updegraff. Mark Roosa, also a Cowden Distinguished Professor, will wrap up the series at 3 p.m., Dec. 7, at the University Club, with a talk, titled ?Contextual Influences on the Adaptation of Mexican-American Children.?

A social science speakers series is planned for the spring, also featuring the school's renowned faculty members. Additional information can be found at the Web site ( www.asu.edu/clas/ssfd ) .
