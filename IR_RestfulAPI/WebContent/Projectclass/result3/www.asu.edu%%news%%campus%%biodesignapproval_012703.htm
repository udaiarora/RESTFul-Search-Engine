Regents give approval for construction of ASU's Arizona Biodesign Institute

The Arizona Board of Regents today approved construction of the first phase of The Arizona Biodesign Institute at Arizona State University. When completed in 2004, the Arizona Biodesign Institute will be a comprehensive interdisciplinary research facility located on ASU's main campus at McAllister Avenue and Terrace Street in Tempe.

The 170,000 square-foot, $69 million project is the centerpiece of ASU's multi-phase Biodesign Corridor that will provide an intellectual and physical environment to allow Arizona to compete in the global bioscience and technology marketplace.

ASU's Technology and Research Initiative Fund, which is money from the voter-approved Proposition 301 initiative, will be focused entirely on developing the Arizona Biodesign Institute. The institute will enhance ASU's capacity in the burgeoning fields of biotechnology, nanotechnology and information technology. This strategic investment will provide the core infrastructure for faculty researchers to collaborate with member clinical institutions in the greater Phoenix area, including the Translational Genomics Institute, to meet human health care needs.

"By refocusing our Technology and Research Initiative Fund, we are positioning to further move ASU to national status, while providing a 5 to 1 return on economic value to the community from the funds received from Proposition 301," said ASU President Michael Crow, adding that no state funds or tuition monies are being used in the current project.

Crow said the Institute's bioscience and engineering research agenda will focus on two emerging areas of great contemporary importance-Biologics and Therapeutics by Design and Nano-Bio Systems by Design.

Research in the biologics area focuses on the discovery and development of new intervention agents designed to prevent or cure disease and its side effects. Nano-Bio Systems research focuses on connecting the living world with practical devices for sensing, diagnosing and rehabilitating therapies. Combining these two areas is central to the Arizona Biodesign Institute's mission.

Groundbreaking for Phase I of the Arizona Biodesign Institute is planned in six to eight weeks, with completion in Fall 2004. The Institute is part of the regional biomedical research plan for Arizona.

The long-range plan at ASU is for a five-building complex at the McAllister Avenue and Terrace Street site.

The world-class complex will create a new gateway to the ASU campus from Rural Road, providing a physical link to the community through the adjacent proposed light rail station.
