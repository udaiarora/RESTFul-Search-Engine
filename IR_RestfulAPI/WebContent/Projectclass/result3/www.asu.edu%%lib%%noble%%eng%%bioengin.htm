Books

Books are on the 3rd floor of Noble Science and Engineering Library in the TA 164 (Bioengineering) and  R856-R857 (Biomedical Engineering) call number areas. Specific topics may be found in other call number areas. Some important reference books are kept in the Science Reference Collection on the first floor. works are found in the  call number area with general chemistry textbooks in .

To find books in Noble Library use the ASU Online Catalog.

  • To find a specific book, use the TITLE or AUTHOR search.
  • To find books about bioengineering use the SUBJECT HEADINGS search:
                Bioengineering
                Biomedical Engineering
  • To find books on a topic use SUBJECT HEADINGS or KEYWORD search.
                Examples of a keyword search:
                        orthopedic and materials
                        biomedical and materials
                        biofilms
                        bioreactor*  (note: the * will retrieve alternate ending, e.g. plurals)
                        biocompatibility

Journals and Journal Articles

  • Current issues are on the first floor in the Current Periodicals  Room. Some journals are online only.
  •  Bound journals are on the 2nd floor of Noble Science and Engineering Library
  • Microforms are in the northeast corner on the first floor.
  • Older volumes are kept on the east side of the floor. Some older volumes are kept in storage; request these items from either the Noble Library Circulation Desk or Noble Library Information Desk.
  • For online availability, see the Alphabetical List of Electronic Journals. NOTE: electronic journals are only accessible from ASU computers.

To find journal articles use one or more of the following journal indexes:

  • Applied Science and Technology Abstracts
  • BioEngineering Abstracts
  • Ei Compendex Web 
  • Medline 
  • PubMed  
    Search engine for Medline and other databases from the National Library of Medicine. 
  • Inspec
     Physics, Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. Also Includes Biotechnology;  Design; Electronics;  Energy; Geography; Industrial Design; Information Science; Materials Engineering;
  • SciFinder Scholar (Chemical Abstracts) 
  • Web of Science/Science Citation Index (1965+)
  • PubMed Central
        "PubMed Central is a digital archive of life sciences journal literature managed by the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) at the U.S. National Library of Medicine (NLM). It is not a journal publisher. Access to PubMed Central (PMC) is free and unrestricted."
  • Microbiology Abstracts
