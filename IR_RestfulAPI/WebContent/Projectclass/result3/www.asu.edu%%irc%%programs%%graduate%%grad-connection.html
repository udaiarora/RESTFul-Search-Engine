Graduate Connection:

G-Link & BEEP

The IRC is dedicated to preparing faculty- and professionals-in-training for success in diverse classrooms and corporations. Hence, we connect with graduate students in a variety of ways to inform them of the context, challenges, and opportunities pertaining to diversity at ASU. We offer training and workshops, collaboration on relevant events, and referral to other resources on campus.

Each semester begins with G-Link, a meet-and-greet reception specifically for graduate students, with food provided by the IRC. Come learn about the IRC and the possible interface between your program of study and issues of intergroup relations and social justice on the first month of the semester. Do so in the context of tasty treats!

Our BEEP Brown Bags are lunchtime gatherings, held twice a semester, that provide a space to discuss classroom dynamics, share classroom exercises aimed at optimizing learning, and increase comfort in addressing difference. The brown bags are open to graduate TAs and other folks interested in enhancing participants' skill in working with diverse people. BEEP stands for build confidence, engage in discussion, enjoy camaraderie, and practice skills.

Just show up with your lunch; water and dessert are provided.

Participants gain the opportunity to discuss their experiences with other people interested in diversity and learn new skills is handling diverse students and topics of diversity. Participants enjoy meeting new people in an informal reception setting (with free food) or over a brown bag lunch.

Watch for details on the next BEEP!
