If you ride your bike or walk to campus:

  • Your bike is classified as a vehicle and you must obey all rules that apply to vehicles. You must come to a complete stop at stop signs, red lights, etc.
  • You must ride bicycles with the flow of traffic, even when on the sidewalks. The major cause of accidents involving bicyclists is riding against the flow of traffic.
  • Lock your bikes only to the bike racks that are provided. Bikes locked to stairwells, handicap ramps, and other items may be impounded.
  • Obey all pedestrian traffic signals. A flashing hand means do not enter the roadway.
  • Do not attempt to cross the street mid block when there are signalized intersections nearby.
  • The ASU Police Department provides a bike safety class for a fee of $15.00. You can register for the class by calling 965-6068.

Alcohol consumption:

  • The legal drinking age in Arizona is 21. If you are under 21, you cannot posses or consume alcohol. Underage drinking offenses are criminal and the fines imposed by the Tempe Justice Court for first-time offenders begin at $443.00 per offense.
  • If you choose to drink alcohol, do so responsibly. Utilize the "buddy system" so you can take care of each other. Someone needs to stay sober for this to work.
  • Never take a drink from a container that you did not open yourself and never consume a drink that you left unattended. This rule should be used for both non-alcoholic and alcoholic beverages.
  • If you have consumed alcohol, please don?t drive. Get a ride with someone who has not been drinking or call a taxi.

Rape/ Acquaintance rape:

  • Many of the sexual assaults that are reported to our police department occur in "acquaintance" or "date" rape situations and frequently involve alcohol consumption.
  • Set behavior boundaries when you go out on dates. Let your date know up front what your limitations are and do not waiver from them no matter what pressure is added.
  • If you consume alcohol, do so in moderation.
  • Go out in groups and share your personal limitations with a close friend. Your friend can watch your actions and step in if he or she feels the situation is getting out of control. The buddy system will not work if both of you are impaired.
  • If you feel that you have been the victim of a sexual assault, get medical assistance immediately. To file a police report for a sexual assault that occurred on campus or to get information on what to do, call the ASU Police Department at 965-3456. If the incident occurred in the City of Tempe and you wish to make a police report, call the Tempe Police Department at 350-8311.

Loud party ordinance:

  • Tempe?s "Large Parties, Gatherings, or Events" ordinance, Section 5-30 through 5-35, explains that large events often present a threat to the peace, health, and safety of the neighborhood and to Tempe citizens in general. The neighborhood suffers because the party upsets the normal routine, and all citizens suffer because officers at a loud party call cannot respond to other calls as quickly.
  • A large gathering or party is defined as a social event with five or more persons. Officers will issue a written notice during the first response, informing the responsible person that a second response to the gathering within 12 hours could result in the responsible person being billed for up to $1000 for police services.
  • Tempe's party patrols will operate throughout the school year for purposes of enforcing ordinances and responding to neighborhood complaints. To get more information please call (480) 858-6330.
