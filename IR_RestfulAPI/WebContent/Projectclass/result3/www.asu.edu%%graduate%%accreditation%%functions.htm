FUNCTIONS OF THE UNIVERSITY ACCREDITATION OFFICE

The University Accreditation Office and officer represent the President and campus provost with respect to essential accreditation-related coordinating functions.

All program representatives are asked to work with the University Accreditation Office staff members to help fulfill the following university functions and services assigned to the office:
  1. Serves as Repository for All Accreditation Documents
    All programs are asked to provide copies of all accreditation records to the University Accreditation Office, as they become available. They will be held in the University Accreditation Office until they are sent to University Archives. Records include:

    • Official letters and memoranda sent to and received from the accrediting agency;

    • Accreditation application form or letter;

    • Current agency accreditation guidelines;

    • Self-study report;

    • Site visit report;

    • Response to Site Visit Report or follow-up report(s);

    • Accreditation award letter;

    • Annual and interim reports filed with the accreditation agency;

    • Correspondence or reports related to an appeal, (if applicable); and

    • Internal and external correspondence related to the withdrawal from accreditation (if applicable).

  2. Arrange Meetings with Site Visitors and University Administrators
    Check with the University Accreditation Office staff prior to selecting site visit dates since university administrators will meet with the site visitors and their schedules need to be considered.

    As soon as site visit dates have been selected and confirmed with the agency, please contact the University Accreditation Office staff. In academic programs, the accreditation office staff will work with you to schedule a private meeting between the campus provost and the site team early in the visit. This entry meeting demonstrates the commitment that ASU has to successfully earning accreditation for its programs. Visitors are typically impressed with the knowledge that university administrators outside of the unit under review have about the program and accreditation agency.

    Additionally, for academic programs, the accreditation office staff will arrange an exit interview with university administrators, including the campus provost, the supervising administrator, and the university accreditation officer. For nonacademic units undergoing review, the accreditation office staff will arrange an exit interview with the supervising administrator and the University Accreditation Office, and other administrators as appropriate. The accreditation office staff, in consultation with the unit under review, will schedule any required meetings with appropriate university administrators.

    At the exit interview, the campus provost, the supervising administrator, and the university accreditation officer hear the findings of the accreditation team. The team also provides information about the site visit report and response process as well as feedback about the quality of the self-study and visit arrangements. In almost all cases, this meeting follows an exit report to the head of the unit and/or faculty or staff. It is typically the last meeting of the visit.

    If your agency recommends a different site visit structure than described here, the accreditation office staff will work with you to meet both the agency and institutional needs.

  3. Review Draft Self-Study before Submission to Agency or Site Visit Team
    The faculty or staff members of the program undergoing accreditation review usually have the most knowledge of the program-specific criteria. Nevertheless, the Assistant Dean for Accreditation will read the document objectively because she is not involved with the program on a day-to-day basis. The Assistant Dean will review the self-study against the accreditation criteria and guidelines and look for partially answered questions, less than convincing arguments, and other common problems. The Assistant Dean will also look for accuracy in reporting university and program data and university policies and structures. Based on this review and after consultation with the University Accreditation Officer, she will make written recommendations for enhancing the report.

  4. Schedule Preparatory Meetings, as needed, prior to the Site Visit
    Meetings with the supervising administrator, meetings with the unit director or chair, and/or meetings with the faculty and staff may be scheduled as needed to talk about the upcoming site visit and how to best prepare for issues that may arise. These meetings are also helpful to the University Accreditation Office in preparing a comprehensive briefing paper.

  5. Prepare the Briefing Paper
    The Assistant Dean for Accreditation will prepare a briefing paper for administrators who will meet with the accreditation team during the site visit. The President will receive a copy if he will meet with the visitors. Other administrators include the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University, campus Provost; supervising Vice Provost or Vice President, Dean (academic); head of the unit; and University Accreditation Officer. The briefing paper typically includes the following elements:
    • Name of accrediting agency;

    • Program(s) or academic unit(s) under accreditation review and accreditation history; programs in the unit not under review;

    • Names, positions, institutions or organizations, and other information about site visitors;

    • Particular strengths and highlights of the program;

    • Any current accreditation concerns or considerations;
    • Summary of findings from the last accreditation review;

    • Action taken as result of past accreditation concerns;

    • New accreditation standards and status of the unit with respect to these standards;

    • Action taken as result of past accreditation or program review/consultant’s concerns;

    • Appended list of faculty or staff (usually copied from self-study report); and

    • Other appendices related to unique aspects of the program (e.g., enrollment figures, diversity data, summary of student surveys, etc.).

    The head of the unit, the university accreditation officer, and appropriate others will review a draft and submit edits before the final briefing paper is distributed to university administrators and the supervising administrators. The final paper is distributed between a few days and within a week prior to the visit. The briefing paper is confidential and should not be shared with accreditation site visitors.

  6. Review Program Response to the Site Team Report before Submission to Agency or Site Visit Team
    The Assistant Dean for Accreditation will review the response from a university perspective for accuracy, completeness, and tone. Based on this review and after consultation with the University Accreditation Officer, she will make written recommendations for the program response.

  7. Serves as a Liaison to the Higher Learning Commission (HLC), a Commission of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools (NCA).
    Further, the University Accreditation Office monitors institutional compliance with HLC requirements. The University Accreditation Office coordinates the institutional evaluation for continuing accreditation by the Higher Learning Commission every ten years. Additionally, the University Accreditation Office facilitates gaining HLC approval of program or institutional changes where required. More information about the University Accreditation Office?s role in institutional accreditation is provided in Impact of Program/Unit Activities on Institutional Accreditation.
