May 18, 2004

Ceremony recognizes academic achievement by Hispanic women

Since 1984, ASU's Hispanic Mother-Daughter Program (HMDP) has helped many Hispanic women advance in academics and obtain their four-year degree.

The HMDP will recognize their participants' academic achievement during the 20th Annual Award Ceremony scheduled from 5 to 10 p.m., May 22, at Gammage Auditorium. The ceremony will recognize 100 graduating eighth-grade participants; 40 graduating high school senior participants, including 28 who were accepted to ASU; and 15 participants who will be earning a degree from ASU this month.

Maria Moreno, recruitment/retention specialist and Phoenix program adviser, said so far the program's graduating high school seniors have secured $232,550 in scholarship money over a four-year period. She says this achievement reflects the ceremony's theme, "Si Se Puede - Celebrating 20 Years of Success, Celebrando 20 Años de Exito."

"The program continues to succeed because of the commitment that is made to higher education by students and their parents," Moreno says. "Parent and family support is the critical factor that influences the student's decision to pursue higher education. They want to succeed academically and the program is available to offer the guidance and support that will help them accomplish their goals."

During the ceremony, one graduating high school student will be honored with the Star of the Year/Estrella del Año Award, which was created to recognize the achievements of an HMDP student who demonstrates excellent academic ability, community, leadership and extra curricular involvement. The recipient, who must have a cumulative grade-point average of 3.5 or better, will receive a $500 book scholarship.

The HMDP also will recognize and honor community leaders and/or organizations that have contributed their time and support to the program for the last two years.

"The program has established strong working partnerships with various community organizations, schools and ASU departments that have been an essential part of the program's continued success," Moreno says. "The program and these essential partnerships are driven by sharing the same belief in promoting higher education."

TV host/personality Isaias Moreno will emcee the event, which is followed by a reception on the North Lawn of Gammage, catered by Heidi's Home Catering.

For information about ASU's Hispanic Mother-Daughter Program, visit (http://www.asu.edu/studentlife/msc/hmdp.html).

Romero, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 727-3116 or (mlromero@asu.edu).
