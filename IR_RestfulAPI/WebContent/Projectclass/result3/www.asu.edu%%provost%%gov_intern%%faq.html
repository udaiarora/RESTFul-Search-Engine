Legislative and Government Internship Program
Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

 

Am I eligible?
Yes, if you are enrolled full-time at ASU, will have completed 75 hours before the start of the internship (of which 24 must have been earned at ASU), and have a cumulative GPA of 3.0 or better.Check your academic standing

What are the duties & responsibilities?
ASU interns are student representatives to legislative and government agencies. Many interns have continued in the government sector upon graduation because of their outstanding representation and work performance during their internship. Detailed job descriptions for each office are provided online at Participating agencies.

What are the hours? When do I begin?
Interns work a 40-hour week, usually 8 a.m. - 5 p.m., Monday through Friday, and overtime as needed. Interns begin work the first working day in January and are also required to attend a preinterview orientation in October..

What are the financial benefits and duration of the internship?
The stipend varies per agency but is usually about $4,200 for the 18-week program. Students also receive an ASU tuition waiver.

If I have a scholarship, how will the tuition waiver affect it?
It may depend on the type of scholarship you are receiving, so for the correct information on how it might affect you, please contact the Scholarship Office, (480) 965-4845.

How am I graded for the internship?
Only credit hours are issued; up to 12 for undergraduate, up to 9 for graduate students. No letter grade is issued. The government agency intern supervisor will complete an evaluation at mid-term and at the end of the term. The original evaluation is given to the professor issuing the credit. (Please keep in mind that the professor(s) issuing credit has the right to ask the student to turn in additional projects during the internship).

If I'm graduating before next spring, can I still apply?
Yes. Graduate students can apply, but they must be enrolled in graduate school. See your academic advisor to inquire about available internship hours. This internship cannot be placed "on hold," nor can it be carried over to another semester.

Can this internship delay my graduation?
Depending on the total student credit hours completed and the hours required in your field of study, these internship hours may or may not be applied toward your degree program. If selected, your academic advisor will determine if these credit hours may be applied.

Should I still enroll for spring semester courses?
Yes, have your class schedule set in advance as you normally would - you can later drop classes if you are accepted into the internship program. Please note, students are strongly discouraged from taking any classes while serving as interns as overtime may be required on any given day. Permission from your academic advisor is needed to take a class during the internship.

Can I apply for more than one government agency?
Yes. Apply for as many positions as you are interested in; however, you need only complete ONE application. (Note: Some agencies require a résumé be submitted along with the application. You do not have to be a law student to apply for these programs.)

Who should the reference letters be from?
Two current (within the last 6 months) reference letters are required and must be submitted with your application. The letters can be from professors, employers, community people, etc. Letters should be addressed to the ASU Legislative and Government Internship Screening Committee and be included with your application, or mailed to the address below.

If accepted by an agency, how do I register for the internship?
There is no set course/line number for this program. Students obtain the information from the College/Department issuing the credit hours and register for the course (and drop other courses you may have previously registered for the spring semester). After registration, it is important to take the registration print-out to the fee waiver department in Student Services to obtain the tuition waiver.
You can ask a professor or an administrator if they will work with you to obtain credit hours. If you are not successful, please contact our office.
