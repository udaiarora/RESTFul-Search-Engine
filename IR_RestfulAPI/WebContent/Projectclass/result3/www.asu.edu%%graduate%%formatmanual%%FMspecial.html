Special Instructions

COPYRIGHT
As a graduate student, copyrighting impacts you in two ways. First, as the writer, you have legal rights concerning the contents of your thesis/dissertation. Second, as a researcher, you must make every effort not to violate the copyrights of others. Please visit www.asu.edu/lib/noble/ip/copyright.htm for more information.

Copyrighting Your Own Work
All works under copyright protection and published in the United States on or after March 1, 1989, are subject to mandatory deposit. Usually, the holder of the copyright is required to submit two copies of the copyrighted work to the Copyright Office. Section 704 of the Copyright Act states that these deposits “are available to the Library of Congress for its collection, or for exchange or transfer to any other library.” Circular 1 Copyright Basics and Circular 7d Mandatory Deposit of Copies (www.loc.gov/copyright/circs) provide useful information about the copyright process. If you have questions, you may contact the Copyright Office Public Information Office on the Internet at www.copyright.gov or by phone at (202) 707-3000. The form used to register a copyright for most documents is Form TX. Certain documents in the performing arts, such as musical scores or plays, may require Form PA. Contact the Copyright Office if you are not sure which form to use. Application forms are available online at www.copyright.gov/forms.

Copyright information and contracts are also available in the ASU Bookstore. You can apply for a copyright through UMI/ProQuest when you submit your document at the ASU Bookstore. If you plan on copyrighting your document, follow these additional formatting instructions:
Place the copyright symbol (©) on the lower half of the backside of the title page with the year and your name centered between the margins. The copyright symbol meets the requirements of the Universal Copyright Convention to which the United States and most European and Asian nations belong. You should also include the statement “All Rights Reserved” below the copyright line. This statement will afford additional protection under the Buenos Aires Convention, to which the United States and most Latin American nations belong.

Example:

©2006 Your Full Name
All Rights Reserved


The copyright page on the backside of the title page is a facing page; therefore the left and right margins should be reversed (i.e., left becomes 1 inch and right becomes 1.5 inches).

Using the Copyrighted Work of Others
Copyrighted material includes tables, charts, graphs, maps, questionnaires, illustrations, photographs, literary works, etc. It is against the law to reproduce copyrighted materials, in full or in part, without permission of the copyright owner. If you need to include copyrighted source material in your document, you must obtain written permission from the copyright owner prior to its use. The written permission you secure from the author or publisher to use copyrighted work in your document should be included in an appendix.

UMI/ProQuest publishes a booklet, Copyright Law & Graduate Research: New Media, New Rights, and Your New Dissertation, that contains valuable information regarding proper copyright clearances and permissions. The booklet is available free of charge by calling ProQuest Information and Learning at 1-800-521-0600, ext. 7020, or online at www.umi.com/umi/dissertations/copyright.



RESEARCH INVOLVING HUMAN AND ANIMAL SUBJECTS

Documents that make use of research involving human or animal subjects must include a statement indicating that the research has been approved by the appropriate university body. You should include approval documents in an appendix.

Human Subjects
Research involving human subjects conducted under the auspices of Arizona State University is reviewed by the University Human Subjects Institutional Review Board (IRB) in compliance with federal regulations. Research involving human subjects concerns the collection of data on subjects whose performance of any activity is required for the purpose of compiling data. This includes data obtained by observation, interview, questionnaire, experiment, or a secondary source. Documents containing any data collection from human subjects require that applications be submitted to the University Human Subjects IRB for approval before data collection or recruitment of subjects is initiated. For further information, contact the human research coordinator in the Office of Human Research Administration at (480) 965-6788 or visit humansubjects.asu.edu.

Animal Subjects
Research involving the use of animals conducted under the auspices of Arizona State University is reviewed by the University Institutional Animal Care & Use Committee (IACUC) in compliance with federal regulations. Documents containing any data collection from animal research require that applications be submitted to the University Animal Care & Use Committee for approval. For further information, contact the IACUC secretary in the IACUC Office at (480) 965-4387 or visit researchnet.asu.edu/animal_care.




DELAYING THE PUBLICATION OF YOUR THESIS/DISSERTATION
(Confidential Status)

If you want to delay publication of your document until there has been time to review the research for the purpose of obtaining a patent, you must petition the Division of Graduate Studies requesting that the document be placed in confidential status. The petition form (www.asu.edu/graduate/forms) must be submitted prior to, or concurrent with, submitting the document for format review. Requests received after this time will not be approved. The form should indicate the reason for the request and the length of time desired (six months to two years) and should include your signature and that of your chair, the head of your academic unit, and the vice president for research.

If confidential status is granted, you will hold the oral defense, obtain the signature of the Division of Graduate Studies dean, and then go to the ASU Bookstore to complete the forms and pay the fees required for binding and, if necessary, microfilming. However, the copies of your document will remain with the Division of Graduate Studies for the period of confidential status. During this period, access to your document must be authorized in writing by all of the same people who signed the original petition form. A request to extend or shorten the period of confidentiality will require the same kind of authorization. At the end of the period, the copies of the document will be automatically sent to the ASU Bookstore for binding and, if necessary, microfilming.
