IMPACT OF PROGRAM/UNIT ACTIVITIES ON INSTITUTIONAL ACCREDITATION

New programs and activities can potentially affect ASU’s institutional accreditation by the Higher Learning Commission (HLC), a Commission of the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools (NCA). The HLC reviews any change that will affect the terms of institutional accreditation. It is important for all units and individual members of the university community to support and protect our institutional accreditation. HLC accreditation is important to our faculty and student recruitment, the ability to win research grants and obtain federal financial aid, and our standing among other academic institutions.

Background Information
 Arizona State University has full accreditation by the HLC. Institutional evaluation for continuing accreditation is comprehensive (i.e., covers all aspects of the institution) and occurs every ten years. The next scheduled ASU institutional review will be conducted in 2012-13.

The five Criteria for Accreditation, listed below, establish a foundation for the accreditation review. For more information about HLC standards and procedures, please visit their website at www.higherlearningcommission.org or contact the University Accreditation Office.

  1. Mission and Integrity. The organization operates with integrity to ensure the fulfillments of its mission through structures and processes that involve the board, administration, faculty, staff, and students.

  2. Preparing for the Future. The organization’s allocation of resources and its processes for evaluation and planning demonstrate its capacity to fulfill its mission, improve the quality of its education, and respond to future challenges and opportunities.

  3. Student Learning and Effective Teaching. The organization provides evidence of student learning and teaching effectiveness that demonstrates it is fulfilling its educational mission.

  4. Acquisition, Discovery, and Application of Knowledge. The organization promotes a life of learning for its faculty, administration, staff, and students by fostering and supporting inquiry, creativity, practice, and social responsibility in ways consistent with its mission.

  5. Engagement and Service. As called for by its mission, the organization identifies its constituents and serves them in ways both value.

Following a continuing accreditation review by the HLC, an institution is issued a Statement of Affiliation Status (SAS). The SAS contains these elements: (1) name; (2) highest degree awarded; (3) most recent HLC action; (4) accreditation status; (5) stipulations on affiliation status (e.g., approved campuses or degree sites); (6) new degree sites (e.g., whether HLC approval is required for offering new programs); (7) whether any follow-up reports are required; (8) the dates of the last and next comprehensive evaluations.

In order to alter the terms of the SAS, an institution must make a Request for Institutional Change. Some examples of the kinds of initiatives that can affect an institution’s SAS and for which a Request for Institutional Change (RIC) must be submitted include, but are not limited to:

  • Changes in educational offerings, such as offering programs at a new degree level, delivering degree programs primarily through nontraditional methods, initiating a significant new academic program or major that requires substantial new financial investment or substantial reallocation of existing financial resources.

  • Changes in educational sites, such as offering five or more courses a year at an out-of-state site or at an international site, opening a new instructional site to provide one or more degree programs outside of Arizona, or opening a new site housing a full range of instruction as well as administrative and support services.

  • Relations with other institutions or groups, including contracting with other regionally accredited institutions or with nonaccredited entities to provide 50 percent or more of the credit-bearing instruction involved in the affiliated institution’s program.

Initiation of a Request for Institutional Change
 As you discuss the planning of a new program or changes in an existing program with the supervising administrator, please give consideration to whether the initiative may be the kind of activity reviewed by the HLC. If the initiative will (or may) require a Request for Institutional Change, and your supervising administrator has approved proceeding with the process, please contact the University Accreditation Office. The University Accreditation Office staff will:

  • Help you determine, by reviewing the guidelines and talking with the HLC staff, whether the initiative is one that requires a Request for Institutional Change;
  • Provide HLC guidelines;
  • Serve as your liaison to the HLC; and
  • Guide you through the approval process.

Your supervising administrator’s approval of the request is necessary to proceed with the process. The University Accreditation Office staff members will review and provide experienced advice on the documentation to be submitted as part of the RIC and will assist you in gathering institutional information and data. In addition, the staff will help you to be sure that all necessary internal approvals have been given prior to the submission.

If a site visit is required, the accreditation office staff will assist by scheduling on-campus appointments with appropriate university administrators.

In most instances, when a Request for Institutional Change is required by the HLC, it must be approved by the HLC before the program or changes are initiated. Please note that from the time of the submission of the Request for Institutional Change to the HLC, approval by the HLC can take six months or longer.
