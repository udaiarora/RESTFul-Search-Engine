I.1: Sabbatical Leave

Effective 4/18/90 .. Revised 4/18/90

POLICY

The University Libraries will follow the policies and procedures as described in the Academic Affairs Policies and Procedures Manual ( ACD 705). The following information is a general overview for University Libraries academic professionals; applicants must refer to the ACD Manual for the complete policies and procedures.


Academic professionals who have achieved continuing status and completed six years of full time service are eligible for sabbatical leave. Requests must have merit according to one or more of the following criteria:

  1. The proposal is of potential value to the University Libraries.
  2. The applicant's effectiveness as an academic professional is enhanced.
  3. The proposal contributes to knowledge in a subject field.
  4. The applicant provides outstanding public or professional service at a local, national, or international level.

Sabbatical leave may be granted for completion of an additional degree if the Libraries' evaluation is such that the applicants and the Libraries will sufficiently benefit.


In addition to the above criteria, the following will also be taken into account in considering the granting of sabbatical leave:

  1. total number of requests submitted during the current year;
  2. probability for successful completion of the proposal;
  3. adverse effect, if any, that the applicant's absence will have on the operation or services of the University Libraries. If changing conditions so require, an approved sabbatical leave may be delayed by the Dean.

As with other personnel procedures sabbatical requests and their review are confidential. The applicant will be notified orally, in person, for both positive and negative recommendations. The only people authorized to discuss results are the primary evaluator and the Dean. If the applicant or primary evaluator serves on the Review Committee for Personnel Action, that person will absent him/herself from all discussion of the sabbatical request.


PROCEDURES

ACADEMIC PROFESSIONAL

The academic professional obtains the "Application for Sabbatical Leave" from the Assistant Dean for Personnel and submits this form, a written proposal, a curriculum vitae and appropriate supporting documentation to his or her primary evaluator. The academic professional then notifies the Assistant Dean for Personnel in writing that the application has been submitted to the primary evaluator.


PRIMARY EVALUATOR

The primary evaluator, in consultation with the academic professional, obtains letters evaluating the worth of the proposal from three persons (inside or outside the Libraries) competent to judge the proposal. These letters become part of the record and are confidential.


Keeping in mind the criteria listed above, the primary evaluator writes a letter of recommendation which must include how the applicant's responsibilities will be covered during the sabbatical. The primary evaluator submits the of recommendation, request form, the three evaluation letters and the applicant's proposal, curriculum vitae, and supporting documentation to the appropriate Associate Dean.


ASSOCIATE DEAN

The Associate Dean approves the request by adding his or her signature to the primary evaluator's letter of recommendation. The complete packet is then forwarded to the Review Committee for Personnel Action.


REVIEW COMMITTEE FOR PERSONNEL ACTION

The Review Committee for Personnel Action reviews the submitted material for each proposal in light of the criteria listed above. The Committee's written recommendation is included with each proposal, and the complete packets are submitted to the Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian.


APPROVAL

If the sabbatical leave is approved, the Dean of the University Libraries and University Librarian will inform the academic professional of the approval.


NEGATIVE RECOMMENDATIONS

In the event of an adverse recommendation, the applicant must be informed of the reasons for the recommendation and afforded an opportunity to respond. The primary evaluator will meet with the applicant and orally present the reasons for the decision. The applicant may withdraw the request or within five working days, respond in writing, showing how the request can be modified to overcome the negative recommendation. The recommendation shall then be reconsidered, and the final decision and the reasons for it shall be communicated to the applicant.


FINAL REPORT

On completion of the sabbatical leave, and not later than the end of the first semester thereafter, a concise final report shall be filed with the Dean and the primary evaluator covering the attainment of the purposes stated in the application. This report is submitted on the form entitled "Report on Sabbatical Leave" which is obtained from the Assistant Dean for Personnel.
