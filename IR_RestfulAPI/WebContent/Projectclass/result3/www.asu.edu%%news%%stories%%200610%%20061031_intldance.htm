October 31, 2006

International dance conference heads to Herberger College

ASU Herberger College Dance will be the cultural hot spot in the nation this November, as it hosts an international dance conference and two concerts melding Afro-Caribbean and contemporary dance.

Live drumming and audience participation will pump up the volume at the Herberger College when renowned Los Angeles dance troupe Viver Brasil, Nora Chipaumire of Brooklyn's Urban Bush Women Dance Co. and Modesto Amegago of Agbe Nunya West African Drummers perform in ?Rhythms of Life: Drums and Dances from Africa,? Nov. 3-5.

Arizona performers include the Cuban Connection, Liberian group Decontee and Sule Greg C. Wilson. Media artists Patricia Clark and Philip Mallory Jones will show their videos ?Através de mi ventana? and ?First World Order? in the lobby and throughout the concert.

Viver Brasil is known for concerts that fuse passionate Afro-Brazilian dance, stunning costumes and exuberant live percussion and vocals. The Associated Press has called Chipaumire ?a defiant boxer staring down an unseen enemy.?

New Yorker Chipaumire has been at ASU teaching student dancers new choreography for another MainStage concert, ?Moving Ground,? Nov. 16-19. Chipaumire, a remarkable solo dancer who was born in Mutare, Zimbabwe, during the second war of liberation, creates provocative and politically relevant dance work.

Chipaumire has inspired a diverse concert of original choreography by students Francesco Caban, Cerrin Lathrop, Aaron McGloin and ASU faculty member Shouze Ma.

Both concerts complement ASU's hosting of the 38th annual Congress on Research in Dance (CORD) conference Nov. 2-5, convening international scholars and performers who examine the history and migration of dance cultures.

Among the lineup of lecturers is Allegra Fuller Snyder, daughter of pioneering design scientist Buckminster Fuller, and co-founder of the nation's first dance ethnography program at UCLA. Steven Feld, a documentary sound artist known for his ecological recordings and work on the ?Planet Drum? music project with the Grateful Dead's Mikey Hart, also will speak. Conference day passes are available at the Web site (www.cordance.org).

MainStage Dance concert tickets range in price from $7-$20 and are available through the Herberger College Box Office, (480) 965-6447 or online at (http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/mainstage).
