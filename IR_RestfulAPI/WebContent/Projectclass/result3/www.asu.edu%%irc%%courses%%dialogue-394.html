SPRING 2007

Intergroup Dialogue Courses

COM / AFR / WST / CSH 394

In collaboration with the Hugh Downs School of Human Communication, African and African American Studies, Women and Gender Studies and Transborder Chicano/o and Latina/o Studies, the IRC is offering for-credit intergroup dialogue courses engaging our understanding of intergroup/ intercultural processes in relation to race, gender, and diversity. These expansions on our non-credit Voices of Discovery intergroup dialogues provide a more in-depth opportunity to gain useful communication skills and experience with diversity.

More About the Dialogue Courses

  • Learn about and share from your experiences
  • Earn upper division credit in engaging setting
  • Gain marketable communication skills, experience working in groups, and experience working with people of diverse backgrounds
  • Potentially participate in a national research project on dialogue*
  • 3 credit, upper-division course
  • 2 instructors
  • high quality interaction & discussion
  • experiential learning through dialogue & peer discussion
  • dynamic role plays, simulations, and active participation
  • no pre-requisites

*These courses are part of a national research project conducted by 10 universities, on the impact of dialogue as teaching method on student learning. Random assignment from among all enrollees will determine whether and to which dialogue particular students are assigned. Students not assigned to dialogue WILL remain enrolled in a 394 special topics course: Pop Culture and the Documentary Film.

Students who have already taken the previous Dialogue courses are not eligible to retake these dialogues.

ALL students WILL remain in a 394 course at this time/day; assignment process will only affect specific course content, and not meeting time/day or course credit for students.
