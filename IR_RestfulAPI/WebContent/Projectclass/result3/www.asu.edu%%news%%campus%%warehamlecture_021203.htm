February 12, 2003

Wareham address highlights Black History Month events at ASU West

Civil rights attorney and political activist Roger S. Wareham will deliver the keynote address for the Africana Lecture Series on Feb. 20, as part of ASU West's Black History Month celebration.

Wareham is known for his work as the lead attorney in the historic lawsuits filed in the federal District Courts for reparations to the descendants of enslaved Africans in the United States and his representation of three defendants wrongfully convicted in the Central Park jogger case of 1989. As secretary-general of the International Association Against Torture, Wareham presents evidence of human rights violations facing people of color at assemblies of the United Nations' Commission on Human Rights.

Wareham's keynote address, "Seeking Justice: The Court and Reparations," takes place at 7 p.m., Feb. 20 in the La Sala Ballroom of ASU West's University Center Building. Wareham will address issues of reparation, court action and the cost of neglect. The lecture will be preceded by a reception with Wareham at 6:30 p.m. To R.S.V.P, call (602) 543-4502.

"Black History Month events provide information to the campus and the community at large on issues that contribute to shaping the African Diasporic community," says Carolyn Jones, chairwoman of the Black History Month Committee at ASU West. "Our theme this year is Kujichagulia, the second principle of Kwanzaa, which means self-determination, to define ourselves, name ourselves, create for ourselves and speak for ourselves."

There is no admission charge for the Africana Lecture Series or most of ASU West's Black History Month events. All events are held on campus at 4701 W. Thunderbird Road in Phoenix.

Crum, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or (matthew.crum@asu.edu).
