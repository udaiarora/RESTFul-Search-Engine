ACD 510–03: Faculty Appointments for Summer Sessions

Purpose

To describe the terms of faculty appointments for summer sessions

Source

Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University

Applicability

Faculty

Policy

Faculty Appointments for Summer Sessions

University faculty appointments for teaching during one or more of the summer sessions are recommended by the deans of colleges to the director of summer sessions and approved by the president and the Board of Regents.

Pay for Teaching Summer Sessions

Pay for each session of summer teaching for university faculty who hold academic year appointments is based on a percentage of individual contracts for the preceding academic year:

6 credits19.50%
5 credits16.25%
4 credits13.00%
3 credits9.75%
2 credits6.50%
1 credit3.25%

The amount is subject to a ceiling salary level established annually by the university.

Termination and Summer Session Teaching

Faculty members whose employment is terminated at the end of an academic year forfeit any summer sessions appointment that may have been made for the summer immediately following.

Sponsored Projects and Summer Session Teaching

Simultaneous involvement in summer session teaching and sponsored projects may not exceed 100 percent of compensated effort during a summer session. The following percentages are established for each credit hour taught:

6 credits100%
5 credits83%
4 credits67%
3 credits50%
2 credits33%
1 credit17%

Pay for services on sponsored projects during the simultaneous period will be at the previous academic year rate or fiscal-year rate.

Faculty Grants and Summer Session Teaching

A summer session teaching assignment may not be carried on simultaneously with a faculty grant through the university.

Fiscal-Year Appointments and Summer Session Teaching

Faculty members on fiscal-year appointments ordinarily are required to teach during summer sessions as a part of their primary employment responsibilities.

Cross-References

For information on documentation of compensated effort, see ACD 303, “Activity Distribution Reports.”

Also see the Research and Sponsored Projects Policies and Procedures Manual—RSP 502–03, “Project-Related Effort and Documentation.”
