Academic Bowl

ASU launches first Academic Bowl

Individual college pride will be on the line this fall as ASU launches its first-ever Academic Bowl competition. The event, which kicks off with opening round competitions Oct. 12 and Oct. 19, will pit 16 teams of undergraduate students in an intellectual competition for scholarship prizes and the President’s Cup trophy.

The semifinals and final competition will be broadcast at 5 p.m. Nov. 19 by Eight/KAET-TV.

The competition, sponsored by the offices of Public Affairs and Student Affairs, is modeled after the national College Bowl. Four-member teams will square off in a question-and-answer format on a wide range of subjects, including current events, history, science, sports and culture. Each participating school and college has selected four team members and alternates based.

The teams of undergraduate students will compete in an intellectual competition for scholarship prizes of $4,500 – a full year’s tuition – for each of the four members of the winning team, as well as the President’s Cup trophy. The winning team’s alternate members each will receive a 1,000 scholarship.
