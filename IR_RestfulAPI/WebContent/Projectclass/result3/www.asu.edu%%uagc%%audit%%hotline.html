SAFETY AND COMPLIANCE AFFECT US ALL

Campus Safety and Compliance

In an emergency, please call 911 or the ASU Police (480) 965-3456.

If this is a mental health emergency, please call EMPACT at (480) 921-1006.

Do you have a concern about student or employee safety or
non-compliance with laws, regulations, or policies?

  • I am concerned that someone is considering a violent act.
  • I believe someone has a weapon.
  • I am concerned about my personal safety or about the safety of someone else.
  • Something is bothering me about a transaction or situation in my office.
  • I am aware, or suspicious of, a university fraud or abuse.
  • Nothing has changed even though I spoke to the appropriate people.

Why should I be concerned with campus safety and compliance?

Raising concerns about safety and/or non-compliance with laws, regulations, or policies applicable to ASU is a service to the university and to the community.

ASU must comply with a wide variety of laws, regulations, and policies to protect everyone. Examples of compliance areas include: financial transactions, NCAA rules, environmental health and safety, discrimination, and research administration. When evaluating whether to report a situation, consider the following questions:

  • Would I be concerned reading about it in the newspaper?
  • Would my family, friends, and colleagues view this as appropriate?
  • Is it the right thing to do?
  • Do the actions comply with applicable restrictions?


What Should I Do?

In the event of an emergency, please call 911 or the ASU Police (480) 965-3456.

For non-emergency situations, if you have a concern about safety, non-compliance with laws, regulations, or policies, you should promptly notify the appropriate ASU office and go through established channels.

If you want to remain anonymous, you can report your concerns without fear of retaliation to ASU's Hotline.
