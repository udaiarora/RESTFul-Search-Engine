ASU Spam Filtering Service

Please read this overview before using the ASU Spam Filtering service. It contains important information for configuring and using this service.

What Is ASU Spam Filtering Service?

ASU has implemented software to help fight SPAM. This filtering software allows you, the user, to set up and configure your own "blacklists".

By default, your ASU e-mail will not be scanned for Spam. Each individual must first enable Spam filtering. Once enabled, e-mail that is considered Spam will be placed in a quarantine area. You can then view your quarantined e-mail with a web browser and make adjustments to how the filtering works. E-mail that remains in the quarantine area for more than 14 days will automatically be deleted.

When you first enable filtering, you should frequently check (at least daily) your quarantined e-mail. Some e-mails that are not Spam may be in here, such as newsletters, some discussion lists and possibly vendor advertisements that you may wish to receive. You can easily mark these to be delivered instead of quarantined in the future. When selececting the e-mails to be delivered, the e-mail addresses will be added to your 'whitelist' and you will not have to re-add them. After a few weeks, you will probably have things properly configured and will no longer need to check your quarantined e-mail as often, possibly once or twice a week. If you are expecting mail and do not see it, be sure to check your quarantine.

How do I enable SPAM Filtering?

To enable Spam filtering for your ASU e-mail addresses, login to myASU at http://my.asu.edu, then select the SPAM tab. If you do not have the SPAM tab, please follow these steps to get to the SPAM filter:

  1. Add the Spam Filter tab to your myASU layout through the Preferences link in the upper left. This is a several step process which includes adding a new tab, and then the Spam Filter channel to your new tab. Detailed instructions can be found at http://www.asu.edu/it/portalhelp/.
  2. Once logged into myASU, you can click on the emma in the upper right. This will open a new window, from which you can select Spam Filtering on the menu to the left.
  3. Once within the filter, you will be presented with a list of your ASU e-mail addresses that you can enable filtering for. It is recommended that you enable filtering for your Directory Address and your Destination Address. You may wish to enable filtering for your aliases as well, but should only do so if they are actually receiving Spam.

Managing Quarantined Mail

From the same Spam Filtering application in Emma, you can also select the option to view your quarantined e-mail. This will show you all e-mail quarantined in the last 14 days for all of the addresses you have selected for filtering (grouped by address). For each e-mail listed in the quarantined e-mail, there are four things you can do:

  • View : Click on the subject of the message (it will be underlined) and you can view the raw contents of the message (message headers and the body of the message).
  • Deliver : Select the messages you wish to deliver by checking the box next to them. Once you have the messages selected, click on the "Deliver" button and the messages will be sent to your regular mailbox for processing. Also, the senders will be placed on a "White List", so that future e-mails from that address will be passed along directly to your mailbox.
  • Delete : Select the messages you wish to delete by checking the box next to them. Once you have the messages selected, click on the "Delete" button.
  • Nothing : You may also do nothing with the e-mail. In this case, it will be automatically deleted 14 days after it was first placed in your quarantined mailbox.

Also note that there is a Select All function available as a convenience if you wish to delete or deliver all of the messages in the quarantine for a particular alias. After pressing the Select All button, you can delete or deliver all messages by using the buttons mentioned above.

Typical Scenario

Initially, you will frequently check your quarantine mailbox; at least once a day. Scan all messages in your quarantine mailbox. If you see a message that is not Spam, click Deliver. After scanning all messages and delivering anything that is not Spam, click the Delete All button.

After two or three weeks, you will probably not be catching anymore legitimate e-mail in your quarantine mailbox. When you are comfortable that this is the case, you can stop monitoring your quarantine mailbox daily. E-mails left in your quarantine mailbox will automatically go away after 14 days.

If a message from a particular e-mail address keeps getting through the Spam filter (i.e. showing up in your normal mailbox), then you may wish to use the Black List function as described below.

Please note the SPAM filtering service does not perform content filtering. In other words, e-mail is marked as 'spam' when the sending address is questionable, not when the certain words are in the subject line.

Managing Access Lists

From the Spam Filtering application in myASU, you can also manage access lists, specifically the White List and the Black List, for each e-mail address you are filtering for. To do so, first choose Settings link above your primary address. Click on the Manage button beside the e-mail address you wish to manage.

The White List is added to whenever you click the Deliver button for a quarantined e-mail. You may add and delete addresses from your White List from within the Manage Access Lists function. If you would like to allow all mail that has a From address in a certain domain to be delivered, you can add a wildcard record, by putting in the domain prefixed by the @ symbol, for example to allow all messages from hotmail.com to be delivered, you should add @hotmail.com.

You add to and delete from the Black List by using the Manage function as well. The ASU Information Technology department recommends that you do not Black List e-mail addresses except in rare cases where you receive many e-mails from the same address. Having a large black list makes the filtering process more inefficient and should be used sparingly, if at all.

Setting Up SPAM Filtering for Multiple EPO Accounts

Some people have multiple accounts on the Electronic Post Office (EPO). It may be because they have a second mailbox or they maintain a departmental or consulting mailbox . If you have multiple EPO accounts, you will first select which account you are configuring filtering for. You can set up Spam filtering for one or more of your accounts.

Definitions of Terms Used

Spam: Spam can be defined as unsolicited commercial e-mail. Generally, it is the unwanted e-mail clogging your inbox and preventing you from smoothly conducting your ASU business.

Directory Address Your official ASU e-mail address. This is the e-mail address that will appear in the various electronic directories at ASU. It is also the address that e-mail you send will appear to be from (if you send from an ASU e-mail system like Emma or Outlook /Exchange)

Destination Address The address where e-mail sent to your Directory Address (or any of its aliases) will be delivered. It is the physical address of your mailbox (e.g. asurite@imap1.asu.edu)

White List The list of addresses that you want e-mail from, whether or not it looks like Spam. If e-mail is sent to you from one of these addresses, it will not even be checked to see if it is Spam. You have a separate White List for each e-mail address you are filtering for.

Black List This list of e-mail addresses that you do not want e-mail from, whether or not it looks like Spam. If e-mail is sent to you from one of these addresses, it will be immediately rejected as undeliverable, without checking to see if it is Spam or not. You have a separate Black List for each e-mail address you are filtering for.
