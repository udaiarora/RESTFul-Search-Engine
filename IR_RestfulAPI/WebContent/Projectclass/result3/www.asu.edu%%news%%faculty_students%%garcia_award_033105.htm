March 31, 2005

Garcia earns praise from higher education association

Eugene Garcia, ASU’s vice president for university-school partnerships and dean of the College of Education, has been honored by the American Association of Higher Education with its 2005 Outstanding Support of Hispanic Issues in Higher Education Award.

“The award is given to an individual in the community who has demonstrated outstanding accomplishment and support of Hispanic issues and made significant contributions to the Latino higher education community,” says Delores Valencia Tanno, professor of communication at the University of Nevada Las Vegas, and co-chair of the AAHE’s Hispanic Caucus.

“This is an unexpected honor from a distinguished group of colleagues that I highly respect,” Garcia says.

The award was presented at the Hispanic Caucus Awards Luncheon and Fellows Program held March 17 at the Atlanta Marriott Marquis Hotel in Atlanta.

Garcia became dean of the College of Education in July 2002. His role was expanded the following year when he accepted a dual appointment made by ASU President Michael Crow as the founding vice president for university-school partnerships.

Since accepting these assignments, Garcia’s work has been prolific, having provided leadership in the development of the comprehensive ASU University-School Partnerships initiatives. The Alpha Partnership was conceptualized as an array of programs offering support in four areas: early childhood education, teachers, educational leaders, and students, schools and families.

Alpha began taking shape in the fall of 2003 in collaboration with state Sen. Richard Miranda (D-District 13), the Tolleson and Phoenix union high school districts, and their surrounding elementary feeder school districts. Through Alpha, academic units and other entities across the university are offering a comprehensive menu of programs and resources for educators, schools and families and the list is expected to expand as additional linkages are established.

In May 2004, Garcia spearheaded the formation of the National Task Force on Early Childhood Education for Hispanics to address major educational challenges facing Hispanic children throughout the United States from birth through the primary grades and to make recommendations for actions.

Sherwood, with the College of Education, can be reached at (480) 965-2114 or (joan.sherwood@asu.edu).
