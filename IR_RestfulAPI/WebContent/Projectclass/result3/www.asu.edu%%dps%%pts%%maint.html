Summer Maintenance Projects for 2006

Arizona State University is dedicated to preserving and enhancing the aesthetics of our parking facilities. In order to do this, we conduct preventative maintenance on an annual basis which requires PTS to occasionally close down or restrict parking in designated lots and structures. Due to the smaller population of people on campus during the summer months, we perform the majority of our work during this time.

We strive to keep the inconvenience to a minimum and will provide alternate parking for those who are displaced while this work is being completed. Here are some of the repairs that we plan on doing this summer, May – July 2006.

As maintenance is scheduled, decal holders in these various lots will be sent an email notices with specific dates/times. Please adhere to posted signage at these locations.

For Your Information:

Pavement fails for a number of reasons but the usual causes are oxidation by the sun and water erosion:

  • New asphalt pavement has a deep black color, but the oxidation process begins immediately causing the pavement to develop a gray color over time. As the oxidation eats deeper into the pavement, the smaller aggregate material (sand and gravel) is released exposing the larger gravel and the pavement becomes rough and ultimately deteriorates the asphalt.
  • Petroleum products (gasoline, motor oil, diesel fuel, transmission fluid, etc.) dripping from vehicles can also cause serious damage to asphalt pavement by dissolving the asphalt binder. The product used in the sealcoating process, coal tar, is not petroleum based and actually resists petroleum products, thus protecting the asphalt pavement.
  • Cracks in the asphalt also need to be sealed in order to prevent the infiltration of moisture into the asphalt. This process is termed, crack sealing or crack filling.

Below are a few steps the asphalt goes through in its deterioration process due to cracks:

(1) Small cracks form causing moisture to get into the asphalt:

(2) The crack gets larger.

(3) Cracks start to run together (Alligator cracks).

(4) Pieces of the puzzle begin to come loose and fall out, creating pot holes.

Just like anything else, a parking lot needs to be maintained. If not, it will eventually need to be replaced and would cost more in the long run.
