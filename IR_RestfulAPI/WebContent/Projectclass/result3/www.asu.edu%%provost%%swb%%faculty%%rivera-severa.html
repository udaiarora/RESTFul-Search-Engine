Southwest Borderlands Initiative Faculty

 

Ramon Rivera-Servera

Assistant Professor
Theatre, Herberger College of Fine Arts

Dr. Ramon H. Rivera-Servera received his Ph.D. in Theatre Studies with an Emphasis on Performance as Public Practice from the University of Texas at Austin. He is an assistant professor of theatre at the Katherine Herberger College of Fine Arts where he teaches courses on contemporary performance in the Americas with a special focus on U.S. Latina/o and Mexican Borderlands cultural production. His courses include Performance in the Borderlands; Latino and Latina Theatre; Theatre Themes and Forms; and Gay and Lesbian Film.

Dr. Rivera-Servera directs the Performance in the Borderlands Series, a lecture/workshop/performance series that brings nationally and internationally recognized scholars, artists and critics who specialize on the performing arts along the U.S./Mexico border to share their knowledge and talents with ASU and the greater Phoenix community. He is also developing an electronic database of archival sources on the performing arts in the U.S./Mexico Borderlands region which will serve as a research tool for scholars, artists and students in the field.

Dr. Rivera-Servera is currently the Conference Organizer and Focus Group Representative-Elect of the Latina/o Performance Focus Group of the Association for Theatre in Higher Education (ATHE). His writings and reviews on Latina/o theatre and dance have appeared in Theatre Journal, TDR: The Journal of Performance Studies, and in Caribbean Dance From Abakua to Zouk: How Movement Becomes Identity (University of Florida Press, 2003). Dr. Rivera-Servera’s current book project focuses on performances of Latina/o sexualities as practices of grassroots globalization. His most recent article on the subject, “Latina/o Dance and the Utopian Performative,” is forthcoming in Modern Drama.
