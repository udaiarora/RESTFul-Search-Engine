February 11, 2003

Greater Phoenix resale market posts strong sales in January

The new year started out strong for the Greater Phoenix resale housing market, with 4,220 recorded sales in January, according to the Arizona Real Estate Center. This is down from the 6,095 total in the previous month, but ahead of the 3,345 sales recorded in January 2002. The median home price of $147,950 was lower than the monthly record of $148,900 set in December, but well ahead of the previous January's $140,000.

"The record activity of 2002 was well beyond expectations, given a very weak economic environment," said Dr. Jay Q. Butler, director of the Center at Arizona State University's W.P. Carey School of Business. "The 2003 resale housing market continues to exist in a struggling economy, low consumer confidence, job layoffs, and a grim international scene. The only real positive note is that mortgage interest rates are expected to remain at current low levels for at least the first half of the year. There is considerable concern as to how the remainder of 2003 will play out in such an uncertain environment. However, the month of January frequently gives misleading signals and cannot be considered a good harbinger of the remaining year."

The resale townhouse/condominium housing sector also improved from last year, recording 885 sales in January compared to 810 the previous month and 1,290 in December 2002. For the first time, the median home price was at $100,000, compared to $97,000 in December and $94,000 the previous January.

The median square footage for a single-family home recorded sold in January 2003 was 1,650, which is slightly larger than the 1,635 square feet reported a year ago. In the townhouse/condominium sector, the median square footage was 1,160, slightly smaller than the 1,165 square feet reported a year ago.

· In comparison with January 2002, recorded sales in the city of Phoenix increased from 1,000 to 1,230, and the median sales price rose from $115,000 to $120,000. In the townhouse/condominium sector, sales activity increased slightly from 240 to 245 sales and the median sales price rose from $83,350 to $92,000.

· The Scottsdale resale home market improved from 360 recorded sales in January 2002 to 405 this year. The median sales price continued its upward trend, increasing from $280,000 to $294,000. The townhouse/condominium sector in Scottsdale also increased slightly, from 175 to 195 sales, while the median price rose from $145,160 to $150,000.

· The Mesa resale housing market improved from 430 to 570 recorded sales, while the median price increased from $130,500 to $134,000. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 120 to 130 sales and the median home price increased from $85,710 to $91,150.

· Glendale's market strengthened from 250 to 355 recorded sales, and the median sales price increased from $124,720 to $142,500.The townhouse/condominium sector remained at 40 sales, while the median sales price improved from $72,915 to $77,000.

· In comparison with a year ago, the Sun City resale market increased from 90 to 110 sales, with the median price remaining at $105,000. Resale activity in Sun City West slightly improved from 70 to 80 recorded sales, and the median sales price increased from $132,950 to $156,750. The townhouse/condominium market in Sun City remained at 60 sales, while the median price increased from $68,950 to $76,500. In Sun City West, sales activity was stable at 20 sales, while the median price increased from $89,000 to $90,500.

· The resale market in Gilbert improved slightly from 205 to 235 sales, while the median price increased from $154,500 to $169,950. The townhouse/condominium market was stable at 15 sales, but the median sales price declined from $115,000 to $105,250.

· For the City of Chandler, the resale market increased from 285 to 340 recorded sales, while the median sales price rose from $147,080 to $156,000. The townhouse/condominium market declined slightly from 40 to 35 sales, and the median price increased from $95,000 to $103,000.

· The resale market in Tempe improved from 110 to 125 sales, with the median price increasing to $159,500 from $153,250. The townhouse/condominium sector increased slightly from 55 to 60 sales, while the median sales price decreased from $100,000 to $99,000.

· The highest median sales price was in Paradise Valley at $695,000, with a median square footage of 3,380.

· In the West Valley, the four communities of Avondale (60 sales at $146,000), El Mirage (30 sales at $113,000), Goodyear (50 sales at $151,520), and Surprise (90 sales at $131,500) accounted for 5.5 percent of the resale market, up from 3.6 percent a year ago.
