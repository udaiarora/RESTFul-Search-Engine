Entering Freshmen Scholarships

For Outstanding High School Scholars Entering ASU Fall 2007

Arizona State University offers many institutional recruitment scholarships for outstanding freshmen as they pursue academic excellence at ASU. Many of the scholarships are renewable. There are also scholarships for outstanding nonresident freshmen.

Institutional recruitment scholarships may be used towards your educational expenses including tuition and fees, housing, books and other costs of attendance. Please be aware that ASU does not combine institutional recruitment scholarships other than the University Scholarship and the need-based Maroon and Gold scholarship combination. In the event you qualify for more than one of these scholarships, ASU will award you the scholarship with the higher dollar value.

Freshmen Scholarship Listing Navigate through the listing below to learn more about eligibility, deadlines, conditions of the award, and renewal criteria of each scholarship.


Important

  • Arizona Resident Scholarships
    • Students must have graduated from an Arizona high school to be eligible for these scholarships.
  • Arizona Resident and Nonresident Scholarships
    • If you are awarded one of the nonresident awards listed below and then your residency status changes, your nonresident award will be adjusted to the resident award values.
  • Nonresident Scholarships
    • If you are awarded either of the two scholarships listed below and then your residency status changes, your award will be cancelled.
Arizona Academic Decathlon Scholarship (Finalist and Top Score)
Award:
  • $2,000 annual award renewable for three additional years provided you satisfy the Renewal Criteria.
  • $8,000 total four-year value.
Eligibility:
  • All 2006/2007 regional competition finalists and top scorers.
  • Current Arizona high school seniors must apply for admission to ASU by Dec. 1, 2006.
Notification:
  • State of AZ first-place team members are notified after results are received from Arizona Academic Decathlon.
Conditions of Award & Renewal Criteria:
  • Admissible to ASU and full-time enrollment at ASU beginning Fall 2007.
  • Conditions of Award & Renewal Criteria Info
Contact:
  • Scholar Recruitment Office at (480) 965-3040
Deadline:
  • Decathlon documentation deadline is April 1, 2007.
  • Current Arizona high school seniors must apply for admission to ASU by Dec 1, 2006.
Arizona Academic Decathlon Scholarship (Two-Year Participant)
Award:
  • One-time resident scholarship of $500.
Eligibility:
  • All students who participated in competition for two years or more in the regional or state competition by the Spring of 2007.
Application:
  • Forms are available at the ASU Undergraduate Admissions Office or through high school counselors, and can be mailed upon request, or are available online.
  • This application requires dated signatures from high school principal, AADA coach, and executive director of AADA.
Conditions of Award:
  • Admissible to ASU and full-time enrollment at ASU beginning Fall 2007.
Contact:
  • Scholar Recruitment Office at (480) 965-3040
Deadline:
  • Decathlon documentation deadline is April 1, 2007.
  • Current Arizona high school seniors must apply for admission to ASU by December 1, 2006 to be eligible for this award.
Maroon & Gold Scholarship
Award:
  • Institutional recruitment scholarship cash cash award currently valued at $4,591 (amount subject to change).
  • Limited renewal awards are available.
Eligibility:
  • Arizona high school students and college transfer applicants from targeted (but not restricted) student populations (ethnic minority, first generation, or low income students) are encouraged to apply.
  • Students must demonstrate financial need documented by the 2007/2008 Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).
  • Freshmen who are recipients of a different ASU recruitment scholarship with a higher dollar value are not eligible to receive the Maroon & Gold.
Application:
  • 2007-08 Application [120 KB]
Conditions of Award & Renewal Criteria:
  • Admissible to ASU and full-time enrollment beginning Fall 2007.
  • Renewals are based on the completion of 24 ASU credits by the end of the spring semester, participation in the Maroon & Gold retention program and a competitive cumulative Grade Point Average.
  • Renewals are limited.
Contact:
  • School Relations Office at (480) 965-2601
Deadline:
  • February 1, 2007

Leadership Scholarship Program
Award:
  • Residents:
    • $8,000 annual award renewable for three additional years provided you satisfy the renewal criteria below.
    • $32,000 total four-year value.
  • Nonresidents:
    • $17,000 annual award renewable for three additional years provided you satisfy the renewal criteria below.
    • $68,000 total value.
Eligibility:
  • Arizona high schools are asked to nominate a strong senior with excellent leadership abilities and the potential to make major contributions at the collegiate level.
  • Nonresident students are eligible to apply if they are an active member of Key Club.
Application:
  • Arizona residents, please contact your high school guidance counselor for the application.
  • Nonresident students must be a member of Key Club International.  Please contact the Leadership Scholarship Program at 480-965-3040 for an application.
Notification:
  • There is an interview for final selection.
  • Nonresident recipients will be notified in February.
  • Resident recipients will be notified by the end of March.
Conditions of Award & Renewal Criteria:
  • Admissible to ASU and full-time enrollment at ASU beginning Fall 2007.
  • Renewal Criteria for Residents:
    • Renewal of the scholarship is contingent upon continued involvement in the leadership development program and successful academic progress (minimum 3.00 cumulative grade point average and completion of 24 credit hours during the nine-month academic year) at ASU.
  • Renewal Criteria for Nonresidents:
    • Renewal of the scholarship is contingent upon continued involvement in the leadership development program and successful academic progress (minimum 3.00 cumulative grade point average and completion of 24 credit hours during the nine-month academic year) at ASU.
Contact:
  • Scholar Recruitment Office at (480) 965-3040
  • Web address: www.asu.edu/admissions/lsp
Deadline:
  • Apply for admission to ASU by December 1, 2006 (resident and nonresident).
  • All Leadership Scholarship Program applications must be received by December 1, 2006.
