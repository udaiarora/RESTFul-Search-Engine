Postdoctoral Researchers
at ASU


Postdoctoral education has been a part of American higher education for over 100 years. The Johns Hopkins University began to support postdoctoral fellows shortly after the institution was founded in 1876. In the 1920s, the Rockefeller Foundation established a formal program of postdoctoral fellowships for recent Ph.D. graduates in the physical sciences. Recipients of these awards were known as "postdoctoral fellows," or simply "postdocs." ASU hosts over 200 postdoctoral fellows on campus every academic year who conduct research-related duties in 39 different disciplines at the Tempe, East, and West campuses.

APPOINTMENT CATEGORIES
Postdoctoral Research Associates
Persons who have earned a doctorate and are employed temporarily by and serve at the pleasure of a faculty member at ASU. Postdoctoral research associates assist faculty members with their research programs. www.asu.edu/aad/manuals/acd/acd505-03.html

Faculty Research Associates
Persons who hold an advanced degree and are employed on a research grant or contract. Please note: not all Faculty Research Associates are Postdoctoral fellows and some are employed by non-research campus initiatives and programs. www.asu.edu/aad/manuals/acd/acd505-02.html

BENEFITS
At ASU, both postdoctoral classifications are considered benefits-eligible ASU employees. Benefits-eligible is defined as a regular employee appointed at 50% or more FTE and expected to be employed for six months or more. www.asu.edu/hr

RESOURCES
Associations/Organizations
  • National Postdoctoral Association
    www.nationalpostdoc.org
    Postdoctoral scholars from across the country have formed the National Postdoctoral Association (NPA) to address national issues relevant to postdocs and focus public debate on how to improve the lives of postdocs at all levels. The NPA is supported through a generous grant from the Alfred P. Sloan Foundation and is sponsored by the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS).

  • Sigma Xi
    www.sigmaxi.org
    Sigma Xi has more than 500 chapters at universities and colleges, government laboratories and industry research centers. In addition to publishing American Scientist, Sigma Xi awards grants annually to promising young researchers, holds forums on critical issues at the intersection of science and society and sponsors a variety of programs supporting honor in science and engineering, science education, science policy and the public understanding of science.

  • Science Next Wave Postdoc Network
    nextwave.sciencemag.org/pdn/index.shtml
    Connecting postdoc organizations, program administrators and offices serving postdocs as they work to meet the professional development needs of postdocs.
