November 22, 2006

ASU Foundation selects new leader

Johnnie Ray, senior vice president for development and university relations at Emory University and the former architect of a $1.6 billion fundraising campaign for the University of Texas-Austin, has been named president and chief executive officer of the ASU Foundation. He will assume his duties Jan. 15.

Ray has served in his position at Emory since 2004. He leads a staff of 200 associates – and, in addition to his development responsibilities, he is in charge of the department of public relations and communication, alumni relations, special events and continuing education.

Before joining Emory, Ray was vice president for development at UT-Austin from 1996 to 2004, where he oversaw a staff of 130 and led the “We’re Texas” capital campaign that eventually generated $1.6 billion, exceeding its original goal by $600 million. “After a long and thoughtful search, we have found a perfect match, someone who is committed to the core agenda of the New American University, who will fit in well at ASU and in metropolitan Phoenix and Arizona, and who is supremely capable at what he does,” says ASU President Michael Crow. “Ray has been called the nation’s best university fundraiser, and it is a coup for us to have lured him away from Emory, one of the top private universities in the country.”

According to Craig Weatherup, who serves as the president, chief executive officer and chairman of the ASU Foundation Board of directors, one of the things that attracted the search committee to Ray was his broad executive talent and clear track record of exceptional performance, as well as his success as the architect of UT-Austin’s first comprehensive development effort.

“The University of Texas at Austin has done an outstanding job building its reputation and securing investment in its future from its alumni and others, and is an aspirational peer of ASU and the ASU Foundation,” Weatherup says. “Like ASU, Texas is a very large institution that lacks a medical school and thinks in very entrepreneurial ways to be successful.”

Before his tenure at UT-Austin, Ray was director of development for the sciences at Pennsylvania State University, and before that served as associate vice president for development and university relations for McMurry University in Abilene, Texas.

Before becoming involved in senior level development in higher education, Ray was the athletic director at McMurray University and also owned and published a successful local newspaper there – the Focus Weekly.

“I’m thrilled at the opportunity to join the ASU Foundation team and

support ASU in its quest as the New American University,” Ray says. “The university’s potential for excellence and growth, and for making an impact on the world, is enormous. I look forward to being part of that effort.”

Ray, a native of Texas, earned his bachelor’s degree in political science and did graduate work at Texas Tech University.

Steve Reiher, stephen.reiher@asu.edu
