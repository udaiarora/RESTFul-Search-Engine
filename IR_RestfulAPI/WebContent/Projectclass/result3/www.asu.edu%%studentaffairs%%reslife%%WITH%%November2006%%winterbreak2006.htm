Do you want to stay in your room over Winter Break?
[Applies to current residents only]

Residence halls will remain open this winter.

This year we are pleased to announce that our residence halls will be open during Winter Break. A Winter Break Housing Extension is available to you at a nominal rate. Break dates are Friday, December 15, 2006 through Saturday January 13, 2007. If you are interested in staying on campus over the break, please review the information below. All students wishing to stay for Winter Break Housing must complete an extension application. Deadline to apply is December 1, 2006.

RATE: The rate for the Winter Break Housing Extension is $400.00. This rate is for the entire break and is non-refundable. Prorating of rate is not an option.

DATES: The dates for the Winter Break Housing Extension are Friday, December 15, 2006 through Saturday, January 13, 2007.

HOW TO APPLY: The Winter Break Housing Extension application will be available at all hall front desks, the Residential Life office, located in the Student Services Building and on our website at www.asu.edu/reslife. Applications must be submitted to Residential Life office in Student Services building or to your hall Front Desk no later than December 1, 2006. For more information contact: 480-965-3515.

MAIL: Mail service will continue throughout the Winter Break. Location and hours may vary. Please check with your The UPS Store ® for more details.

POLICIES: During the Winter Break Housing Extension period, residents who are involved in a documented incident/violation may be asked to relocate to another building or be removed from Residential Life for the remainder of the Winter Break and/or the Spring semester 2007. It is expected that you abide by all ASU and Residential Life policies during the Winter Break Housing Extension period.

SECURITY: Please report any suspicious behavior or individuals to the front desk. We request that you, for your own safety, be aware of who is staying on your floor during your stay.

Make sure if you leave the hall for any period of time that your belongings are stored securely. ASU Residential Life is not responsible for any items lost or stolen from your room. Please take the necessary precautions and do not leave any valuable items.

FRONT DESK SERVICE: Hall front desks will be open from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. M-F, with limited services. Each area of campus will have one 24/7 operational desk. Please see your hall staff for more details.

COMPUTER SUPPORT: Computer Support will be available during Winter Break. For support, please contact our Help Desk at 480.965.8565.

COMPUTER LABS: Labs will be closed during Winter Break.

FACILITIES & SERVICES: Staff will be entering rooms to check for fire/safety hazards and conduct routine maintenance. If you have any maintenance requests, please submit them, as usual, at www.asu.edu/reslife.

DINING SERVICES: Limited dining services will be available at the Memorial Union during Winter Break. Please check with the Memorial Union or the Campus Dining website at www.asucampusdining.com.Meal Plans are not applicable during the break, however, Maroon and Gold is accepted.

CONCIERGE SERVICE: Per your academic year housing contract, the Concierge Service Program is not offered during Winter Break. Services will resume on the first day of Spring classes.

Check-out List—if you are not planning on staying during break

Please remember to do the following before leaving for Winter Break:

  • Unplug all electrical items in your room, including your alarm clock.
  • Take out your trash.
  • Defrost and unplug your refrigerator.
  • Store all food items off the floor and in airtight containers. (30 inches from the floor)
  • Move all furniture one foot away from the walls.
  • Turn off all lights.
  • Close and lock your windows.
  • Close your curtains.
  • Make sure all of your belongings are stored securely. Please take the necessary precautions and do not leave valuable items in your room. *
  • Do not store motorcycles or motorized vehicles in your room during Winter Break. Bicycles are acceptable to leave in your room, as long as they are not blocking a door or window.
  • Take all personal belongings that you will need until your hall re-opens in January.
  • Make sure your door is securely shut and locked.
  • Make sure to return your room key to the front desk.

* ASU Residential Life is not responsible for any items lost or stolen from residential rooms.

ASU Residential Life staff will be entering rooms for routine maintenance, annual insect spraying, and will be instructed to unplug any electrical appliances which are left plugged in.

If you are not staying for the Winter Break Housing Extension period, you will need to be checked out of the residence halls by 5 p.m. on Friday, December 15, 2006. Please plan accordingly. If you have an extenuating circumstance, it is imperative that you discuss this with your Community Coordinator to make alternate arrangements.

 

Important Points to Remember During Winter Break

Building Access: On Friday, December 15, 2006, at 5 p.m., all exterior door locks will be changed and all ISAAC access will be turned off for students who have not applied for the Winter Break Housing Extension. Please make sure to take everything that you might need while you are away during break. Staff will not be granting access to rooms after December 15, 2006.

New Roommate Assignments: Students currently without roommates or who have roommates that will be leaving at the end of the Fall semester will have a new roommate assigned. The vacant portion of your room should be cleaned and all furnishings in their proper place. If your room is not properly cleaned and available for your new roommate, you will be assessed a cleaning charge of NO LESS than $25.00. The residence hall staff will enter all rooms to make sure they are prepared for new roommates and to make sure fire hazards do not exist.

Request License Agreement Release: License Agreements are for the full academic year. Please see the Schedule of Charges and Deadlines for valid reasons for release from your License Agreement. If you have a valid reason to request a release from your License Agreement, you MUST submit the appropriate paperwork to your Community Coordinator by Thursday, November 30, 2006. License Agreement Release materials, along with instructions, are available at your hall front desk. Every effort will be made to notify you in writing of our decision prior to Reading Day , December 6, 2006.

Permanent Check-Out: If you do not expect to be living in one of our residence halls during the Spring semester, be certain that you have submitted a License Agreement Release Form with your Community Coordinator by November 24, 2006 and schedule a formal check-out with any Resident Assistant prior to 5 p.m. December 15, 2006.You must have written approval to be financially released from your License Agreement.

Room Changes: All room changes must be approved by your Community Coordinator. All room changes must be completed prior to hall closing by 5 p.m. on Friday, November 24, 2006. This includes vacating your current space and moving into your new room, as well as ensuring that the space being vacated is left in a clean and undamaged condition for the next resident.

Parking: Students who check out of the residence halls permanently must turn in their parking decal to Parking Services for that hall’s parking lot(s). Students moving to a new hall who have parking decals must go to Parking Services to exchange the old decal for a new hall decal no later than January 20, 2007.
