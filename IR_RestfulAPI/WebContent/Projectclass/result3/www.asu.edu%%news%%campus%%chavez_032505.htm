March 25, 2005

University celebrates life, legacy of César Chávez

Members of ASU’s community and special guests will gather this month on ASU’s Tempe and West campuses to celebrate the legacy of César E. Chávez.

The 10th annual César E. Chávez Memorial Breakfast is scheduled at 7:30 a.m., March 31, in the Memorial Union’s Arizona room.

Chávez, who was born on a small farm outside of Yuma , was 13 when he began to work throughout the fields of California . Together with Dolores Huerta, his family and other supporters, Chávez formed the United Farm Workers and served as one of the greatest forces in the labor and civil rights movements.

Misty Cisneros, program coordinator senior of the César E. Chávez Leadership Institute (CCLI), says she hopes attendees will walk away with a better understanding of what the César Chávez Leadership Institute is and how it affects young Latino leaders.

“We are there to honor not only César Chávez for his work over a lifetime,” she says, “but those students and community members who continue to demonstrate the spirit of César Chávez.”

The memorial breakfast, which will be emceed by José Cardenas, host of KAET-TV’s “Horizonte,” will feature a keynote address from Raúl Yzaguirre, presidential professor of practice in community development and civil rights.

“Raul is a very well-known and recognizable leader in the Hispanic community,” Cisneros says. “His involvement in numerous legislative and public policy issues over the past 30 years has made him a key national player on behalf of Hispanic Americans. Now that he is at ASU, he will help to establish a center focused on community development, education for practitioners, and academic workshop.”

Other speakers include David Virgil, a junior bioengineering major and a CCLI alumni member. The César E. Chávez Legacy Award and the César E. Chávez “Si Se Puede” Award will be presented during the breakfast, which serves as institute’s annual fund-raiser.

Few seats are still available. Those who wish to buy seats can do so by calling Cisneros at (480) 965-8890. The cost is $85 per person or $850 for a table. Tickets will not be available on the day of the event.

Chávez also will be honored with a presentation by Melissa Rodis at 9 a.m., March 30, at the West campus.

Rodis, a staff attorney in the Community Legal Services Farmworker Program, represents migrant and seasonal farmworkers with employment law issues. Her presentation, “Arizona’s Farmworkers 2005: The Struggle Continues,” is sponsored by ASU’s Latino Events Committee, Chicano/a faculty and staff, the Hispanic Honor Society and the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences Department of Ethnic Studies.

The free presentation is being held in the Delph Courtyard at ASU’s West campus, located at 4701 W. Thunderbird Road in Phoenix. For more information, call (602) 543-6600.

Romero, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 727-3116 or (mlromero@asu.edu).
