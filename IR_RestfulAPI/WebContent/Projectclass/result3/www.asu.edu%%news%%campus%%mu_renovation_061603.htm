June 16, 2003

Renovation to bring more seating, food options to ASU's Memorial Union

ASU's Memorial Union (MU) serves as the landmark for students and university employees to meet, eat and be entertained. This summer, renovations are being made to better serve its customers and enhance the university environment for everyone.

Construction, which began in May, will convert Union Square, the all-you-can-eat dining facility, into a multi-purpose gathering, eating and programming space. Union Square, which was open only weekdays for breakfast, lunch and dinner and for brunch and dinner on the weekends, will extend its hours to the scheduled MU building hours.

Becky Sojak, communications coordinator for Student Development and the Memorial Union, says the renovation of Union Square will follow a philosophy similar to the one used when the Art Café was finalized on Aug. 19, 2002 - more seating with an eclectic feel.

"We've had such wonderful success with the Art Café by incorporating a food concept (Einstein Bros. Bagels) and community and performance space, that we would like to mirror those objectives for the former Union Square area," Sojak says. "Many students are looking for a place to hang out and relax. We're hoping the renovated area will lend itself to those needs and more."

The new retail dining restaurants to be located on the main floor, will include Mein Bowl, which specializes in Chinese favorites with authentic flavor. At Mein Bowl, customers will be able to pick from entrees like Sweet and Sour Pork and Kung Pao Chicken with a choice of low mein noodles, rice or fried rice.

Pandini's is a mini bistro serving fresh Italian food, including upscale oven pizza, salads, sandwiches and pastas. Salsa Rico is a Mexican grill eatery serving Baja style chicken and steak burritos, tacos, chips and salsas and quesadillas using freshly prepared items. The Gourmet Grounds, located on the main floor will close this fall, but a full-service Starbucks Coffee, offering a menu similar to what is currently available in the W.P. Carey School of Business café, will be added.

"The restaurants will offer students and other visitors a variety of choices for food," Sojak said. "I think people will be very happy with the quality of food that will be offered."

Temporary drywall was installed several weeks ago on the main north/south corridor of the MU for the expansion project of Chick-fil-A. To accommodate the high volume of business, Chick-fil-A will expand its present location to incorporate the area occupied by neighboring Pizza Hut. Pizza Hut will move inside Union Market and reopen for fall.

For visitors seeking visual entertainment, a large video wall will be installed in the renovated area. As part of a class gift, ASU's class of 2003 will provide funding for the high-tech wall, which will broadcast university events including news, sports and musical entertainment. In addition, approximately six TV monitors will also be placed throughout the seating area.

"We think the video wall will draw a lot of attention from our visitors," Sojak says. "There's a lot of potential for campus organizations, community groups and individuals to utilize the video wall to promote ASU affiliated events and programs."

The MU's interior will have about 300 seats. In addition, the restrooms will be renovated and a family restroom will be included.

The north exterior of the MU will also get a new look with a multi-level exterior seating platform in the Union Square renovation, which will allow for about 200 additional seats. A staging area will also be created for student programming and other special events. Newspaper and other periodical racks will be relocated to the east side of the MU.

The project general contractor is Adaptive CM and the architect is Larson Associates Architects, Inc., both of whom were hired for the Art Café project last summer.
Construction is scheduled to be complete by early August and ready to open its doors before the beginning of fall semester.

For information about this project visit the Web at (www.asu.edu/mu).
