Mentor Award

Graduate students and professors are invited to nominate tenured faculty members for the 2006-07 Outstanding Mentor Award. The Division of Graduate Studies (DGS) and the ASU Foundation will recognize three professors for their commitment to the learning, preparation, and intellectual and professional growth of their students. In order to qualify, nominees must be an ASU faculty member and have mentored at least three students who have completed their doctoral or MFA degrees. For further information, contact Associate Dean Andrew N. Webber at 965-5906 or andrew.webber@asu.edu. The deadline for nominations is Oct. 31.

Chairs and Directors Meeting

ASU department directors and chairs are invited to attend the DGS Chairs and Directors meeting on Sept. 12 from 10-11:30 a.m. in Computing Commons (CPCOM), Room 120. This is the first meeting of the year and we encourage all directors and chairs to attend. For more information, contact Kathy Douglas at 965-7279 or kdouglas@asu.edu.

PFF Reception

The Division of Graduate Studies will introduce Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) students and the program?s plans for the upcoming year at the PFF opening reception on Sept. 7 from 4-6:00 p.m. The event is by invite only. Please encourage or remind your PFF students to attend.

RCR Luncheon

The School of Life Sciences is hosting its Responsible Conduct in Research (RCR) Luncheon Series this fall semester to raise the depth of understanding about research ethics at ASU. The first presentation features Richard R. Sharp, an assistant professor of medicine at Baylor College of Medicine, as he leads a discussion on ?How should scientists respond to growing concerns about the integrity of research?? The luncheon will take place in Sept. 20 at 11:45 a.m. in Coor Hall, Room 553 and is open to graduate students and faculty, but RSVP required. For more information, visit lifesciences.asu.edu/bioethics/text/rcr.htm.
