Richard Lanyon

degree:

 PhD., 1964, University of Iowa
area: clinical
phone: (480) 965-7675
fax: (480) 965-8544
email: rlanyon@asu.edu
websites: none
research interests: Psychological assessment in general and personality assessment in particular, focusing on areas that are relevant to psychology and law.  Current projects involve the development and validation of methods to assess response distortions ("misrepresentation") during assessments in various forensic contexts, such as personal injury claimants, major felons, divorce/custody situations, etc.
selected publications:
  • Lanyon, R. I. (1997). Detecting deception: Current models and directions. Clinical Psychology: Science and Practice, 4, 377-387.
  • Lanyon, R. I., & Almer, E. R. (2001). Multimodal assessment of self-serving misrepresentation during personal injury evaluation. American Journal of Forensic Psychology, 19, 5-14.
  • Lanyon, R. I. (2001). Psychological assessment procedures in sex offending. Professional Psychology: Research and Practice, 32, 253-260.
  • Lanyon, R. I. Dimensions of self-serving misrepresentation in forensic assessment. Journal of Personality Assessment, 76, 169-179.
  • Goodstein, L. D., Lanyon, R. I., & Koch, M. (2002). Counterproductive Behavior Index: Administrative and scoring manual, version 2.0.  Amherst, MA: HRD Press.
  • Goodstein, L. D., & Lanyon, R. I. (2002). Counterproductive Behavior Index: Technical manual. Amherst, MA: HRD Press.
  • Lanyon, R. I., & Almer, E. R. (2002). Characteristics of compensable disability patients who choose to litigate. Journal of the American Academy of Psychiatry and the Law, 30, 400-404.
  • Lanyon, R. I., Almer, E. R., & *Maxwell, B. M. (2002).. Validation and norms for sentence completion task scales to assess misrepresentation during disability assessment. American Journal of Forensic Psychology, 20, 39-52.
