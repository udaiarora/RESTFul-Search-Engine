PageMasters Group: Mission Statement

Approved: April 9, 1998



The Mission of the Pagemasters Group is:

  1. To monitor web pages loaded by Hayden Reference for compliance with the policies set by the Library and ASU for Web Pages,

  2. To insure the security and integrity of the Hayden Reference web site by creating and maintaining the site's directory structure and monitoring access to the structure for the Department and subject specialists,

  3. To create, design and maintain the main pages and templates for the Hayden Reference web site, and

  4. To assist in training those on the Reference Team wishing to learn how to create and maintain web pages.


Reference Collection Management Group: Statement of Purpose

Accepted by the Team January 17, 1997; revised by the Group Jan. 23, 1997

  1. Membership on the Reference Collection Management Group shall be selected members of the Reference team, the Reference Collection Development bibliographer and the Reference Library Supervisor.

  2. Development and organization of the Reference collection shall be overseen and guided by this group.

    1. The maintenance activities shall be administered by the Library Supervisor and his/her staff.

      1. Receipt of monographs into Reference.
      2. Receipt of serials into Reference.
      3. Binding policies and procedures.

    2. The preservation activities shall be administered by the Library Supervisor and his/her staff.

    3. Weeding of the entire Reference collection shall be guided by this group.

      1. Stacks.
      2. Index tables.
      3. Electronic resources.

  3. Materials which are not assigned to any individual shall be administered by this group. These materials include but are not limited to the following:

    1. General encyclopedias and dictionaries.

    2. Science material.

    3. Travel guides.

    4. The A, the CT's (biography), K (law), and the Z call numbers.

    5. Atlases.

  4. Reserve materials of interest to Reference shall be regularly inspected and new editions and new material shall be purchased from the Reference collection budget.

  5. The Reference bibliographer may consult with or seek direction from this group when issues or questions about the Reference budget arise.

Reference Desk Support Group: Statement of Purpose

Approved by Reference Team 6/12/97

  1. Membership on the Reference Desk Support Group consists of 3-6 volunteer members from the Reference Team. Leadership of the group is assumed by unanimous decision of current members.

  2. Purpose of the group is to provide support services for the smooth running of the reference desk and to contribute to the continuous improvement of resources, and ultimately of service, provided by the reference desk staff.

  3. Responsibilities of the group are:

    1. Identifying, producing and acquiring finding aids and resource materials for the reference desk, examples include:

      1. materials in the "black", "electronic notices" and "assignments" notebooks.
      2. materials kept under glass at reference desk.
      3. ASU, U of A and NAU course catalogs and staff directories.
      4. bibliographic finding lists, subject specialists' class handouts, and other subject files.
      5. answers for problem questions (tan file cards).

    2. Organizing and maintaining the ready reference collection.

    3. Writing procedures related to the functions of the reference desk (e.g., opening and closing procedures) and communicate them to the Reference Team.

    4. Collecting policies and procedures that relate to performing duties at the reference desk (e.g. emergency procedures, reporting problems, circulation of stacks materials).

    5. Acquiring and updating user guides for the MARS workstations and Network databases.

    6. Creating signage for the MARS workstations and the reference desk.

    7. Maintaining supplies, handouts, and forms used at the reference desk.

    8. Creating print and electronic master files of original documents of materials created by the group for use at the reference desk.

    9. Creating the reference desk statistics forms and setting the statistics days for service points in the Libraries.

    10. Compiling reference desk statistics and producing annual statistics reports.

    11. When necessary, bringing location decisions for new materials, new forms, etc., to the Reference Team for approval.
