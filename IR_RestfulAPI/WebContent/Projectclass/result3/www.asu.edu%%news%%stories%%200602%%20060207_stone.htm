February 7, 2006

Noted free speech scholar discusses wartime protections

Few issues are as explosive, or as timely, as freedom of speech during times of war. That subject will be addressed by constitutional law scholar Geoffrey R. Stone, author of "Perilous Times: Free Speech in Wartime," during this year's John P. Frank Memorial Lecture at Arizona State University.

The lecture, which is free and open to the public, will be at 4:30 p.m., Thursday, Feb. 9, in the Music Building's Katzin Hall on ASU's Tempe campus.

"This is an extraordinary and timely lecture for our Valley audience and the nation generally, because we are in a war footing," says Doris Marie Provine, director of ASU's School of Justice and Social Inquiry in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

"Each year the School of Justice and Social Inquiry and the John P. Frank Lecture planning committee strive to find an exciting topic and excellent speaker to address a key social justice issue of our time," Provine says. "Freedom of speech during perilous times is such a topic and Stone is a master of this history.

"Stone is a major figure in constitutional law and this book looks at the protection of free speech historically though the nation's many times of war and unrest," Provine says.

Stone is a professor at the University of Chicago Law School, where he served as dean from 1987 to 1993. Additionally, Stone served as provost of the University of Chicago from 1993 to 2002. He was a law clerk to Justice William Brennan Jr. of the U.S. Supreme Court.

This annual lecture is presented by the School of Justice and Social Inquiry with support from the law firm of Lewis and Roca , where Frank was a partner.

"John Frank is the inspiration for this lecture and the series of which it is a part," Provine says. "John was a man of enormous intellect and accomplishment. The series, which focuses on pressing justice issues of our time, honors John's lifelong commitments to justice, scholarship and the law."

For more information on the lecture and for parking options, contact the School of Justice and Social Inquiry at (480) 965-7682 or visit online at www.asu.edu/clas/justice .
