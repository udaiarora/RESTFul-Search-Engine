SUN ANGEL FOUNDATION UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH AWARDS IN THE HUMANITIES
Fall 2007

Four awards of $1000 each for independent student research in the Humanities

Eligibility:

Any advanced ASU undergraduate with at least 60 hours completed at Arizona State University may apply for this independent research award. Student must be in good academic standing, and majoring in a humanities department or program.

Each student receiving an award will enroll in a 499 course in the major (eligible majors include English, Languages & Literatures, History, Interdisciplinary Humanities, Film & Media Studies, Philosophy, Religious Studies) and work closely with an advisor to produce a scholarly paper (or equivalent) in the humanities.

NOTE: The 499 independent study course may not be concurrent with the Honors thesis or with a pro-seminar, but it may be a springboard to either one.

English Department Required Workshop:

Undergraduates planning to apply for a Sun Angel Research Grant through the English Department are required to attend a preparatory workshop, which will assist them with finding faculty sponsors for their projects and with preparing their applications.

If you do not attend a workshop you will not be permitted to apply for a Sun Angel Research Grant from our Department. If you have questions or need further information, please contact Professor Rosalynn Voaden Rosalynn.Voaden@asu.edu

Application:

The deadline for the Fall 2007 award is Friday, March 23, 2007.

Applications are available in all humanities departments. For more information, please contact Joel Gereboff, Chair, Department of Religious Studies, (480) 965-7738. E-mail: joel.gereboff@asu.edu . To download the application form in MS Word format, click here.

Application Information for Fall 2007:

This program provides opportunities for undergraduates at ASU who are majoring in one of the Humanities to receive financial support for innovative, independent research. The eligible Humanities Programs are: English, Languages and Literatures, History, Interdisciplinary Humanities, Film & Media Studies, Philosophy, and Religious Studies.

Program Objectives:

  • Enhancing the overall quality of undergraduate education in the Humanities
  • Preparing students for graduate studies or research careers
  • Attracting excellent students to the Humanities.

Students' Responsibilities:

Students must be advanced ASU undergraduates (at least 60 hours completed with l5 at ASU), majoring in a humanities department, and in good academic standing. Each student will enroll in a 499 course in the major (not honors thesis or other required courses, although the 499 may be a springboard to these courses) and will work closely with a faculty advisor on an original project approved by the advisor. A satisfactory midterm progress report will earn $500, with the remaining $500 awarded when the course is completed with a B or better. Students will produce a written paper or the equivalent, to be approved by the advisor, and will be encouraged to present the research results in a public forum. Each student is eligible for no more than one award.

Faculty Advisors' Responsibilities:

As part of the student's application, a faculty advisor will write a letter of support to the Award Selection Committee about the project. In that letter, the faculty member discusses the merit and importance of the proposed project and agrees to oversee the project. The faculty member also agrees to certify at midterm that satisfactory progress is being made and that the project's end that the project has been completed with a B or better. During the project, the advisor agrees to meet regularly with student, to help produce a work plan and a timetable, and to provide guidance to the student throughout the project.

Application Packet:

By March 23, 2007, submit to Joel Gereboff, Chair, Department of Religious Studies, Engineering Center A, Room 377 (main office), the following items in a single packet:

  • Application cover sheet:

No more than a two-page single spaced (with one space between paragraphs) description of the project (standard format), specifically laid out in these sections: (a) research question, linked to a finite body of material to be consulted in dealing with the research question; (b) statement of purpose including goals; (c) work plan; and (d) an explanation of what preparatory work you have already done

  • One-page bibliography with current references
  • Unofficial transcript of all work (ASU and transfer)
  • Letter about the project from the faculty project advisor addressed to the Award Selection Committee
  • Form, signed by the faculty project advisor agreeing to supervise the project.

Selection Criteria:

The quality of the proposal, its originality, and the likelihood that the project can be completed in one semester will constitute the primary basis for selection. Student is advised to select a reasonable, well-defined project than to tackle the great riddles of the universe. The Award Selection Committee encourages projects that can be be carried out in the context of a regularly scheduled class.

Awards will be announced by the end of the Spring 2007 semester.
