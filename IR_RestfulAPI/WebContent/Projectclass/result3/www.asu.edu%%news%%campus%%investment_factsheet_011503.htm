January 15, 2003

Arizona State University - Strategic Investments in people and programs

Even in times of tight budgetary constraints, it is important to make strategic investments in order to continue a trajectory of progress and prepare the university for when the current financial crisis ends.

ASU has worked diligently to provide salary equity and is investing to retain its most competitive faculty, staff and administrators. In times of fiscal crisis, when the institution might be viewed as vulnerable and a target for raiding, it is critically important to protect top people.

  • More than $15M of internal resources were reallocated during FY95-FY01 into faculty and staff pay raises to address market issues and general salary needs. This investment has not closed the market gap but has allowed us to not fall further behind until recently. It is far more expensive to respond to external offers for our best faculty than to address issues pre-preemptively and it is far better managerially.
  • Therefore, even in this fiscal crisis we have committed to address the salaries of our critical faculty/administrators and to provide some relief to our lowest paid staff, but we still must address the larger problem of underpaid faculty and staff.
  • We are also using the resources of the Capital Campaign, such as endowed chairs and professorships to reward, retain, and recruit most competitive faculty

The university is moving to become an entrepreneurial institution, focusing on the academic core of the university and enhancing ASU's competitive position using Proposition 301 is a key funding source for initiatives that will drive the future of the university and cannot be used for solving budgetary shortfall.

We have reorganized the Office of the President for integrated university leadership and coordination, quality control, and rapid, efficient decision making.

  • Some positions eliminated or phased out
  • Expanded duties and areas of responsibility for several key administrators
  • National salary survey of comparable administrative positions completed and salaries adjusted appropriately within market range
  • Total cost for executive leadership of ASU remains about constant over two-year period

This is a critical window in which the federal government is ramping up its research investments in recognition that the economy will be driven by research discoveries and innovation. Other states are investing in their research capacity to position themselves competitively to leverage research dollars into high value jobs.

With the help of 301 revenue and other funds, we are making key investments even while we must make difficult budget reductions and reallocations. Some of these key selective investments are:

  • $30M during FY02 and FY03 of Proposition 301 funds invested in economic drivers, such as biosciences, bioengineering, information science, materials science, manufacturing, technology transfer and workforce development.
  • During FY02 and 03, over $8M investment in strategic initiatives from indirect cost and other state and local funds. A few examples of these initiatives include in Religion and Conflict Resolution, K-12 teacher preparation and improvement, creative writing, the school of life sciences, Law and Society, Prevention Center, and Environmental Science
