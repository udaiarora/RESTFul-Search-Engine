Texas Instruments Awards Three ASU Electrical Engineering Female Students with
Outstanding Female Diversity Scholarship Award

ASU electrical engineering graduate students, from left, are Marnie Figiel, Jennifer Desai and Nazanin Darbanian. Rick Harrigan photo.

"This might mean a guaranteed job now!!," beamed Jennifer Desai upon learning that she is one of three ASU students named to the Texas Instruments (TI) Outstanding Female Diversity Scholarship Award.

On November 22nd, Texas Instrument representatives, Mike Bartlett, Vice President, High Speed Communication and Control (Dallas), and Scott Kaylor, DAC Design Manager (Tempe Design Team), will visit ASU to present the awards to Desai, Marnie Figiel, and Nazanin Darbanian. Each student will receive a $15,000 scholarship and a plaque at a ceremony in Engineering Center G-wing, room 115.

Itÿs great news that three of my M.S. female students have received this Texas Instruments award," proudly noted Sayfe Kiaei, ASU Connection One Center Director. This is also very good news for ASU, as there were over a dozen schools in the U.S. in the running, and we got the most awards! Itÿs additionally significant because TI has made a drastic cut in its university funding during the current economical hardships."

The students, who are pursuing M.S. Degrees with mixed-signal and RF (radio frequency) design, were ecstatic when learning of the award.

My colleagues and I are really honored to be recognized among a group of such talented people," summarized Figiel, a native of Calgary, Alberta, Canada. For me this represents another step toward completing my masterÿs, and then take the knowledge I acquire back to industry—eventually, I may also pursue my PhD."

Iÿve been very fortunate to have outstanding professors and mentors, who were instrumental in my education and getting this award," concluded Darbanian.
