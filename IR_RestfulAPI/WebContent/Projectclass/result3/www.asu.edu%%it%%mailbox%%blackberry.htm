Blackberry

Blackberry is an end-to-end wireless e-mail solution that provides quick, easy access to your e-mail, contacts, calendar and task list wherever you go. The device allows you to be connected to your ASU Exchange e-mail account 24 hours a day.

(*I want to buy a Treo. Do you support that?) 

The device synchronizes with your Exchange account without:

  • Dialing in
  • Initiating connections
  • Antennas to raise

Read more about Blackberry personal devices at their official website. There are currently several models to choose from; all will work with ASU's server and configuration. ASU IT does not recommend a specific device or vendor at this time. Costs of the devices vary, as does the service. You will need to ensure that you purchase an "Enterprise" device if you plan to synch the device with your Exchange account.

Please visit the webpages of Cingular, T-Mobile, Verizon or Nextel for device models and pricing information. See Blackberry Providers Local Numbers below for direct vendor contact.

Procedure to get activated at ASU:

Note: Information Technology does not pay for the Blackberry devices or the airtime service. There will also be a one time fee called a Client Access License (currently $75.00) paid to the Customer Assistance Center if you wish to synchronize your device to your Exchange account. You can purchase your device from providers such as AT&T Worldnet, T-Mobile, Nextel and Verizon or you can contact Telephone Services for preferred pricing on Verizon devices/service or Purchasing for all others.

Step 1: Purchase your device. Purchase service (airtime/usage minutes). (see note above)

Step 2: Purchase a Client Access License. This is through the IT Help Desk. First, a Blackberry Site License Agreement form needs to be filled out. Forms may be found at http://asu.edu/it/tempe/cac/license/blackberry.xls. The fee for the license is $75.00. The Vendor Code for this purchase is: COMPUTING. Forms can be faxed to 5-5171 Attention: Patti Dawson. This Blackberry use license is transferable within departments. You can contact the IT Help Desk at 480-965-1211.

Upon receipt of payment, the IT Help Desk will contact the WMA team to request the new customer be added to ASU's Blackberry Server. WMA will e-mail the customer upon completion.

Step 3: Install the desktop software that came with your device on your desktop/laptop system. Attach the cradle to your desktop computer. Synchronize your device with your Exchange account using your Outlook profile.

If you have questions regarding Blackberry Service at ASU, please submit a question online at the Ask IT Web page.
