Librarians’ Council

Minutes

April 19, 2006

Hayden C6A


The meeting was called to order at 3:06pm.


A quorum was present.


I. The minutes from the March 15, 2006 meeting were amended and approved.


 

II. Chair's Report – Brad Vogus (for Brian Doherty)

  • Brian Davis, the new Academic Associate in Archives and Special Collections was introduced to Council.

III. Dean's report – Sherrie Schmidt

  • Pat Etter will retire at the end of June
  • The Library has sent forward the salary recommendations to the President’s Office, where they will be reviewed. Everyone will be notified as soon as they are approved.
  • The first candidate for the position of Director for the Downtown Campus Library interviewed last week, and the second will interview next week.
  • R2 Recommendations: Vicki Coleman will be sending out weekly “Moving Forward” report to keep everyone informed about the progress and the changes.

    Kurt Murphy’s Report:
  • Work on the creation of a University-wide AP Review Committee continues. The task force has met a couple times. Each Campus is represented.
  • There are now 3 AP categories instead of 4: Research, Pedagogy and Information Materials Management.
  • There will not be two separate committees. Librarians will be the only ones going through continuing appointment reviews.
  • The review committee will consist of 9 members from all campuses and each category. There will be requirements for rank and status.
  • There was a discussion about this review committee and how it would operate. Kurt Murphy will take the comments back to the task force.

IV. Committee Reports

a. Personnel Committee - JoAnn Mulvihill

  • No Report


b. Research and Professional Development Committee - Mark Scott

  • Funds are still available for travel and research. The committee is taking new requests for travel funding through May 1, 2006 for travel by June 30, 2006.


c. Compensation Committee - Mimmo Bonanni

  • No Report


d. Review Committee - Ginny Sylvester

  • No Report


e. Academic Senate - Deborah Koshinsky

  • Myles Lynk addressed the Senate about Men’s Basketball getting attention from NCAA due to poor academic performance. The new coach is aware of the problems. Women’s athletics has been exemplar and above average in grades, higher than the average student.
  • President Crow addressed the Senate
    Coalitions formed with Legislature to bring in new investment of 12-15%.
  • The $1650 and merit raises are an investment. The minimum hourly wage is $9.00/hour. Salary compression/inversion issues will be addressed for some individuals with outstanding situations. Deans were asked about outstanding equity issues.
  • The Living Wage Coalition Group and ASU are working together to address salary and health care for food service and janitorial workers contracted to work on campus.
    No classified research is being done at ASU.
  • The issue of parking was brought up. The Parking and Transit Task Force recommended that rates be increased. Crow recognized that there was underinvestment and lack of planning with respect to parking on campus. The task force found that most people are willing to pay for convenience. Those with ideas on how to improve parking without a rate increased are encouraged to contact the task force.
  • ASU has plans for a Bird Flu outbreak – involves containing those seriously ill and identifying transmission zones. The State of Arizona also has plans.
  • No classified research is being done at ASU. Crow doesn’t want any research being done that can’t be discussed.

f. Committee on Academic Professional Status – Dan Stanton

  • The new AP Listserv has had some activity. There has also been discussion of the task force for the new Promotion and Continuing Appointment Review Committee.

V. Old Business

No Old Business

VI. New Business

  • First Reading- Orientation Program Document (Section J1) – JoAnn Mulvihill (Personnel Committee)
  • The personnel committee has created a new document for the orientation program. Changes were made to this document 2 years ago, but that revised document cannot be found. The committee decided to move forward with a new document. They corrected misinformation and rearranged a few things.
  • This document is designed to be a guide and checklist – use as appropriate. It is not meant to be comprehensive. Serves as a reminder to the supervisor. This will be distributed to new AP in the packet of information Karie Pifer supplies.
  • Presentation of the slate of Council officer and committee candidates – Nominations and Elections Committee – Deborah Koshinsky
  • Nominations from the floor were accepted. Ed Oetting was nominated for Academic Senator to carry out Pat Etter’s term. Mimmo Bonanni was nominated as the necessary third candidate for review committee. Becky Uhl was nominated for the two-year term Personnel Committee position.

Meeting adjourned 4:11 pm.



Katherine O’Clair
Secretary, Librarians’ Council
