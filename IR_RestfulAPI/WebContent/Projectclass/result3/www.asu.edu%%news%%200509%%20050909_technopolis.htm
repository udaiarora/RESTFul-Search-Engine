September 9, 2005

ASU Technology expo showcases innovations

Arizona Technology Enterprises (AzTE) and ASU Technopolis, two ASU programs dedicated to advancing technology, are playing host Sept. 28 to the ASU Technology Expo at the Memorial Union on the Tempe campus.

“The idea is to showcase innovation emanating from the university to the business community,” says Charlie Lewis, AzTE’s vice president of marketing. “It’s a vehicle to allow decision-makers in a variety of fields to be at a venue that is comfortable and convenient for seeing emerging technology at ASU.”

AzTE focuses on helping ASU researchers and faculty develop their innovations into commercial products or services, while ASU Technopolis educates, coaches and networks Valley technology and life science entrepreneurs to spur technological advancement.

The expo will kick off with an overview of the various research and technology developments made by participants at the event. Throughout the day, presentations will highlight specific innovations from a variety of contributors, such as ASU’s Flexible Display Center and the Center for Cognitive Ubiquitous Computing (CUbiC). An exhibit area will feature programs within the physical and life sciences colleges. There also will be a poster area for showcasing developing projects.

Phil Facchina, senior managing director and group head of technology, media and telecommunications and health care at the investment bank Friedman Billings Ramsey, will be the keynote speaker at the event.

“Our primary objective is to show folks in the community that this is their university, and there is a wealth of innovation and technology available,” Lewis says. “Our goal is to facilitate getting that technology into the marketplace.”

In addition to introducing industry to ASU’s latest innovations, the expo is designed to educate other researchers and faculty from ASU and the community about how technology commercialization works through university-industry partnering.

ASU Technopolis will head up an educational track featuring seminars on grant-writing, entrepreneurship, legal issues surrounding technology transfer and more.

“We hope to give the faculty, staff, students and public a taste of what we do and how we can help them get started and succeed in their businesses,” says Terree Wasley, director of ASU Technopolis.

The event is co-sponsored by the Greater Phoenix Economic Council, Arizona Technology Council, Arizona BioIndustry Association and Rogers & Theobald, LLP.

To register to attend the event, visit the ASU Technopolis Web site at (www.asutechnopolis.org). Registration is free and open to anyone.
