October 19, 2006

College of Public Programs transforms for downtown First Friday art celebration

A steel drum band, live music on five floors, nearly 300 pieces of art, artist demonstrations, hip-hop and Latin dance demonstrations, and refreshments will enliven the ASU College of Public Programs at the new ASU Downtown Phoenix campus from 6-10 p.m., Nov. 3, when the college opens its doors to the public as part of the First Friday art walk celebration.

Although there will be plenty of free entertainment at this extensive exhibit, the messages in this event go deeper than the typical gallery opening.

The first collection purposefully connects the research and service emanating from the college's new building with real stories from nonprofits in the Phoenix community. The second exhibit celebrates the talent of the larger university community, expressed through the works of retired faculty members of the ASU Emeritus College.

?These exhibits embrace the city in a special way,? says Debra Friedman, dean of the ASU College of Public Programs.

Friedman championed both exhibits and oversaw the installations.

?Social and economic advancement for individuals and communities is a strong theme of our college,? she says. ?Art helps us tell that story. Our local nonprofit partners and the university's emeriti faculty are our emissaries in this effort. Their art transforms this building into a center of culture, reinforcing the idea that this college and this university are places where an environment of innovation and creativity thrives.?

Professional artists in the Emeritus College Art Exhibit include J. Eugene Grigsby, Gertrude Hechter, James Pile and Rudy Turk. A few of the nonprofit exhibitors from the second show are Central Arizona Shelter Services, Hospice of the Valley, Boys and Girls Clubs, Neighborhood Ministries, Body Positive and Save the Family Foundation.

The artwork spans the fourth through ninth floors in the University Center, located at 411 N. Central Ave., and there will be live entertainment on every floor. Printed guides to the exhibits will be available at the event. Reservations are not required.

More information is available at the Web site (copp.asu.edu) or by calling (602) 496-0600. A commemorative 2007 calendar highlighting the Emeritus College Art Exhibit can be purchased by calling (480) 965-0002.

The College of Public Programs offers more than 30 academic programs in its School of Community Resources and Development, School of Public Affairs and School of Social Work . It also is home to the Morrison Institute of Public Policy, the Center for Nonprofit Leadership and Management, the Southwest Interdisciplinary Research Center, the Center for Urban Innovation, the Center for Community Development and Civil Rights, and the Executive Education Program in Public Affairs.

Palka, with the College of Public Programs, can be reached at (602) 496-0409 or (debra.palka@asu.edu).
