USI 801–02: Reserving and Using Memorial Union Facilities

Purpose

To specify the guidelines for the reservation and use of Memorial Union facilities

Source

Memorial Union

Policy

Memorial Union (MU) facilities are available to the following groups:

  1. officially registered student organizations (Category I)

  2. university departments and colleges (Category II)

  3. university-sponsored groups (Category I and II)

    and

  4. groups other than those listed above, if the requested dates are not in conflict with the university calendar (Category III).

For a schedule of facility charges, visit the Memorial Union Web site.

Room charges apply to categories I and II whenever there is a registration fee, conference fee, admission charge, donation requested, or when 50 percent or more of the attendees are not affiliated with ASU. Room charges and audiovisual charges are applied to category III under all conditions. The MU will waive rental for a room when a complete meal has been purchased through Distinctive Catering for a specific event. This waiver only applies to groups using a separate room for the meal to be served (if available). This waiver does not apply to one-time events (wedding, dances, etc.) or monthly meetings.

Event and Meeting Services

Event and Meeting Room Reservation Requests

To receive the best service and selection of space available, the client must request rooms with as much advance notice as possible. Room reservation contracts must be signed and returned to the MU Event and Meeting Services Office before an event may be confirmed. Event and Meeting Services reserves the right to change, cancel, or move an event to another room when necessary.

Requests for rooms may be submitted by:

  1. visiting or calling the Event and Meeting Services Office (MU 182, 480/965–3406)

  2. sending a fax (480/965–5834)

  3. submitting a Room Request Form to the Event and Meeting Services Office

    or

  4. e-mailing your request to an events coordinator or mureservations@asu.edu.

On-Campus Groups

University departments and colleges and university-sponsored groups may request event and meeting space throughout the year.

Registered student organizations may schedule weekly meetings or events within the academic year with as much notice as possible. In order to provide space for a growing demand of users, the MU allows student organizations to use two meeting rooms per week. Student organizations may reserve additional rooms within one week of the event, pending availability.

Off-Campus Groups

Reservations from off-campus groups may be accepted subject to the availability of space in the MU. Liability insurance is required for use of MU facilities.

The MU gives priority for room reservations to on-campus groups. Off-campus groups may request space during semester breaks and any Friday, Saturday, or Sunday. Contact Event and Meeting Services at 480/965–3406.

Late Requests

Late requests for space, setup, or equipment may be accommodated only if resources are available. A service fee is charged for requests made within two working days before the event.

No Shows

A service fee is charged to groups that fail to use the reserved room space and do not notify Event and Meeting Services two, 14, or 30 working days before the event, depending on the room reserved.

Storage Charge

Groups requesting storage of materials prior to an event will be charged $4 per box or the rental fee for a meeting room, depending on quantity.

Cancellations

Groups must cancel ballrooms at least one month prior to the event date. For cancellations received after that date, the MU assesses a charge equal to 50% of the room rate for the applicable category.

All other cancellations must be received by the MU Event and Meeting Services Office at least two or 14 working days in advance of the meeting or event, depending on the room reserved. A service fee is charged for late cancellations.

Credit Classes

The MU does not accept reservations for credit classes on a regular basis. However, reservations for credit classes may be accepted on a special, emergency, or occasional basis if time and space permit.

Organizations with Outstanding Bills

The MU does not accept reservations from any organization owing money to ASU for bills that are delinquent.

Use

Setups, Displays, and Signage/Decorations

Setups

The MU staff and food service contractor are responsible for arranging any equipment or furnishings for events held in the building. Setups may be requested at the time reservations are made. For additional setup requirements once the room has been set, an hourly labor fee is assessed depending upon the extent of the request.

Displays

Literature displays and sales may be held inside meeting rooms only for the duration of the event.

Signage/Decorations

Signs may be displayed outside meeting rooms with the approval of Event and Meeting Services.

Taping or tacking signs, posters, or decorations in any area of the building may only occur with approval by Event and Meeting Services. Decorations and posted materials may be used only for the duration of the event. Glitter and confetti are prohibited, and lit candles are allowed only with a permit from Event and Meeting Services.

Special Events/Dances

MU facilities may be reserved for special events/dances at the current facility use charge subject to applicable university and building special event policies.

Student organizations interested in hosting a special event/dance in the MU should contact the Student Organization Resource Center (SORC) or visit the SORC Web site for guidelines and procedures.

Damages

Groups are held financially responsible for any charges or damages associated with the event during the use of MU facilities.

Cross-References

For information on reserving and using MU facilities for meals, see USI 801–03, “Food Services.”

For information about security services, see the Department of Public Safety Policies and Procedures Manual—DPS 202–01, “Security for Special Events.”
