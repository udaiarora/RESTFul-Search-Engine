April 1, 2003

Founders Day celebration honors professors, alumni, staff

Three outstanding professors, two distinguished alumni, one valued staff employee and six graduating seniors were honored by the ASU Alumni Association March 12 at its annual Founders Day celebration at Old Main. The ceremony marked 118 years of excellence in higher education, and the date came exactly on the same date in 1885 that Tempe Normal School was established.

Faculty Achievement Awards went to Brooks D. Simpson, a history professor in the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, for research; Helen E. Reed, a aerospace engineering professor in the College of Engineering & Applied Sciences, for service; and Jay Boyer, the chairman/coordinator of the Film Studies Program in the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, for teaching.

Susan Bitter Smith '77, '82 M.B.A., the executive director of the Arizona Cable Telecommunications Association, received the Alumni Achievement Award. Derrick Hall '91, senior vice president of communications for the Los Angeles Dodgers, was given the Young Alumni Achievement Award.

The Staff Achievement Award went to Myrna Morgan '84, executive assistant in the English department. Students receiving awards included Michael Leingang, male leadership; Maureen Salloom, female leadership; Phil Root, male scholar; Sandra Bensley, female scholar; Gabriel Escontrias, male service; and Ellen Morrison, female service. Two $250 book scholarships were also presented.

Simpson, a professor in the history department, is one of the country's noted authorities on the American Civil War and Reconstruction Era politics. Since 1987, he has published 40 books, book chapters and articles in a variety of publications. His four monographs have all been chosen by the History Book Club as a Main Selection, and his latest work, "Ulysses S. Grant: Triumph over Adversity, 1822 - 65,'' was reviewed in the New York Times and received its recognition as a "Notable Book of the Year.''

Reed, a professor in the mechanical & aerospace engineering department, joined the ASU faculty in 1985. She is a professor who truly understands what industry wants from graduating students. She has created and developed the ASUSat Lab, a student satellite program where more than 600 students have participated in its projects. She has also been active in building relationships with related industry, both locally and nationally, and her students regularly participate in lab tours for high school students, prospective ASU students and community members. She has won numerous awards at ASU.

Boyer, a professor in the English department, has been an educator, scholar, professor and writer for more than 25 years at ASU. In 1995 he was honored as Arizona's Professor of the Year, awarded by the Council for the Advancement and Study of Education and the Carnegie Foundation and has received the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences Distinguished Teaching Award. He consistently ranks in the highest tiers of effective ASU professors and has served as the chairman/coordinator of the Film Studies Program for nearly 20 years.

Hall is the senior vice president for communications for the Los Angeles Dodgers, responsible for a $2 million budget and for implementing all of the franchise's external initiatives. Previously Hall was an L.A. media member working in television and radio, plus he teaches a sports public relations class at USC. He has spoken on ASU alumni panels and is involved with the L.A. chapter of the ASU Alumni Association. He was honored in 2002 with induction into the Hall of Fame of the College of Public Program's Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communication.

Bitter Smith is the executive director of the Arizona Cable Telecommunications Association, a position she has held since 1980. She has also been vice president and co-owner of Technical Solutions, a Phoenix-based public affairs firm, since 1988; the CEO and lobbyist for the State Grade Association; a chairman of the board of directors of the ASU Alumni Association; a past chairman of the Cronkite School's endowment board; an honorary chairman of the ASU MBA Council; and a director of the Morrison Institute of Public Policy.

Morgan is the executive assistant to the English department, and is the person most directly responsible for hiring faculty associates, assigning instructors and teaching assistants, and opening and closing sections for 558 English classes every year. She oversees the staffing for one out of every 10 classes on the main campus. She is the point person for all activities related to personnel and is responsible for the payroll and benefits details for 85 ranked faculty, 100 teaching assistants, 43 part-time faculty associates and two academic professionals.

Jacobsen, with the ASU Alumni Association, can be reached at (480) 965-3701, or (bob.jacobsen@asu.edu).
