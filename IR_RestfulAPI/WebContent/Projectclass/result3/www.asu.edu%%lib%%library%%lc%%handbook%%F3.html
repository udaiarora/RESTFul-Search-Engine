F.3: Curriculum Vitae

Effective 5/15/1992 .. Revised 4/21/2000

CURRICULUM VITAE


The curriculum vitae is a required component of all personnel actions submitted to the Review Committee for Personnel Action. While the curriculum vitae is not a required component of the annual performance evaluation process, it is recommended that the academic professional update it at the time of the annual evaluation. The academic professional is encouraged to consult the Assistant Dean for Personnel, who can provide a sample packet of curricula vitae that reflect several different styles.


The curriculum vitae is a complete listing of an academic professional's achievements and is a means of organizing and presenting information for review in personnel actions. Information in the curriculum vitae is presented in reverse chronological order and is organized into sections:


  • Education
  • Professional Employment History
  • Professional Development and Contribution
    • continuing education
    • professional participation
    • research and/or dissemination
    • teaching
    • consulting
  • Service

The categories of "Professional Development and Contribution" and "Service" are defined in the Criteria for Retention, Continuing Appointment, and Promotion section of the Librarians' Handbook; E.2 .


When preparing the curriculum vitae, the academic professional should consider selective use of brief annotations where responsibilities and/or significance of an activity would not be readily apparent to the average reviewer. As the academic professional's career progresses, and the curriculum vitae correspondingly expands, the AP may wish to balance clarity with conciseness. Word processing design features (such as bolding, italics, bulleting, indentation, etc.) should be used to create a professional appearance for the document and to promote readability and comprehension.
