April 5, 2005

New institute tackles social science research

As ASU continues to ramp up innovative research activities, a new unit has been created in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (CLAS) to further amplify active research in the social sciences.

Named the Institute for Social Science Research (ISSR), the unit is designed to facilitate leading-edge thinking by the faculty and to foster new transdisciplinary collaborations, as well as to make ASU’s social science research expertise more accessible to the community.

A ceremony celebrating the institute’s founding and dedicating its new facilities was held March 30 at the Lattie F. Coor Building. ASU President Michael Crow and ASU Vice President and CLAS Dean David Young played host to the ceremony, which featured guided tours and light refreshments.

“A main goal of the institute is to facilitate scholarship and transdisciplinary
collaboration between the university’s many departments and schools, especially in the social science disciplines, but also with other disciplines,” says Division of Social Sciences Dean Alan Artibise, who also serves as the ISSR’s executive director.

“In the process, we want to assist researchers in working with outside funding agencies, companies and non-governmental organizations.“We think that with the increased focus that comes from organized support and guidance we can significantly improve our national research presence and funding. There are also opportunities
to offer research services that should make us more valuable to the local community.”

New institute tackles social science research One of the goals of the institute, Artibise explains, is to serve as an umbrella organization, along the lines of the “small business incubator” model, for a number of promising research projects and centers. The institute provides these projects with initial organizational support, start up space, funding and assistance in finding appropriate funding sources.

The ISSR already is assisting in the development of a number of such centers, including the North American Center for Transborder Studies (NACTS), the Data Archive Center, the Center for Indigenous Languages of the Americas, the Center for Mexican and Mexican American Family Health and Resiliency, the Center for Social Dynamics and Complexity, the Center for Energy Futures, the Phoenix Information Exchange (PHIX) for work force data, and the Center for Research Methodology.

The ISSR also will foster the creation of new research programs through a semiannual Catalyst Grant Program that will support promising pilot projects in the social sciences, particularly projects with a transdisciplinary focus.

“The Institute for Social Science Research is a critical part of our plan for advancing the social sciences and for fostering transdisciplinary research at the university,” Artibise says. “It is a major new undertaking for the college that I have been working to put in place since I arrived at ASU. Now, with the Arizona Board of Regents’ recent approval, we are ready to get to work.”

Hathaway, with the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, can be reached at (480) 965-6375 or (hathaway@asu.edu).
