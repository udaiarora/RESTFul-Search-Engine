Promotion and/or Continuing Status for Academic Professionals

Material submitted should include:

1. The Request for Academic Personnel Action form provided by the Office of the Executive Vice President and University Provost along with any additional forms used by the college.   (The form is also available on the web at www.asu.edu/provost/forms.)

2. Table of Contents for the materials in candidate's submission.

3. Evaluation letters and committee recommendations (omitting identification of external/internal reviewers).

A. Department Personnel Committee evaluation of job performance, professional development and contribution, and service in relation to approved unit/department criteria.

B. Chair's/Director's independent recommendation in relation to unit/department goals.   If applicable, include an analysis of teaching success.

C. College Personnel Committee evaluation and recommendation.

D. Dean's independent evaluation and recommendation.

Internal letters should explicitly address the following:

•  analysis of the candidate's job effectiveness;

•  detailed evaluation of the quality and impact of the candidate's professional contributions;

•  assessment of expected future contributions;

•  clear specifications of the candidate's contributions to collaborative projects (including external funding where applicable);

•  comparative analysis of the candidate's standing in the field, relative to national peers; and

•  if the committee vote is not unanimous, explicitly state the minority view in a separate section of the letter.

It is extremely important that at each level of review a substantive evaluation of the case is provided and questions arising at earlier levels of review are directly addressed.   Please DO NOT repeat case details that have already been described in earlier letters.   Rather, in subsequent letters, evaluate the case, correct misstatements, substantiate or challenge claims, and provide additional perspectives.   Whatever the recommendation, the letter should argue the point--in other words, make a statement and provide support.   Each level of review has a different perspective on the candidate and this perspective should be evident.   Please also be scrupulous in providing evidence and documentation for important assertions about the case.  

4. If applicable , a summary analysis of teaching evaluations prepared by the chair/director.   The chair/director should explain special challenges and/or teaching accomplishments and describe how teaching is evaluated in the unit.    It is highly desirable that some data comparing the candidate's student evaluations with the unit/department or college averages be presented.    For example, at the very least, the candidate's course ratings should be compared to unit/departmental averages in similar courses.

5. External/Internal Evaluations.

            A. In preparing the final list of external/internal reviewers, half should be from the candidate's list of suggested reviewers and half from the chair/dean's list of suggested reviewers.   Include identifying information about the reviewers only on the grid/form provided by the Office of the Executive Vice President and University Provost.   See also the enclosed Academic Senate guidelines for soliciting external/internal letters.   REMINDER:   Please provide detailed information about each reviewer's qualifications, stature in the field, and familiarity with the candidate's overall performance contributions.   Be sure to anticipate the potential questions from evaluators within ASU who are not in the candidate's discipline/research area.   Providing such detailed background information is particularly important if the general guidelines for selecting reviewers are not followed (e.g., selecting a distinguished academic in the field as a reviewer, but that academic is not affiliated with a research university).

            B. A copy of the letter used to request the reviews must be included.   [See attached Template #2.]   NO MORE THAN TEN LETTERS SHOULD BE Formally SOLICITED.   All original letters received must be included.   Department Chairs/Unit Directors should feel free to contact potential reviewers prior to the formal solicitation to identify those potential reviewers most likely to respond.   Additional reviewers may be contacted in an attempt to ensure at least 6 responses.

            C. Current letters from reviewers in the candidate's field of expertise.   These evaluations may come from within ASU, although not from people already assigned to play a role in the review process.   Every attempt should be made to solicit external/internal evaluations whenever possible and appropriate given the candidate's workload expectations.    External/internal reviews for scholarly contributions are required.   If possible, department chairs/ unit directors and deans should explicate any troublesome or confusing statement made by a reviewer.

6. A statement of not more than four pages written by the candidate to put past work into perspective and to outline future goals growing out of that work.   The statement should assist reviewers to determine relationships among the individual's job performance, professional development and contributions, service, and how these activities have built the foundation for continued professional growth.  

7. A current curriculum vitae, presenting a record of professional activities and contributions to the unit, college/school, institution, and profession.   This might include institutional and professional service, professional publications, papers presented, grants, etc.   The individual effort of the academic professional in collaborative activities should be specified.

8. A copy of four publications or other material reflecting scholarly/creative and professional endeavors of the candidate.   A portfolio documenting overall professional activity may be submitted as one piece of evidence.   If local units have reviewed a greater quantity of material, the candidate should be consulted about weeding the material to fit the space restrictions at the University level.

9. A job description.

10. Optional, if candidate's responsibilities include instruction:   Supplemental materials providing evidence of instructional effectiveness may be submitted for up to 2 courses, e.g., syllabi, copies of major tests and assignments, reading lists, web sites,   CDs, etc.

11. Send only ONE file per academic professional; i.e., do not send duplicate department and college files.

12. A current copy of the unit's/department's and college's approved promotion and continuing status review process criteria.

The dean should provide a cover list including the names of all academic professionals in the college that are going through the review process, listed by department, who are being considered for promotion and/or continuing status this cycle.
