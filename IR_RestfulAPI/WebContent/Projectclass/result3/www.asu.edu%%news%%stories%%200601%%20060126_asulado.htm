January 26, 2006

ASU reaches out to Spanish speaking community

ASU volunteers, such as Olivia Diaz with Student Financial Assistance, will be on hand to answer questions during a live call in show Jan. 26, on Univision 33 in Phoenix.

ASU volunteers will gather Jan. 26 for a live call-in show offering Spanish speakers the chance to ask questions about ASU and the broader topic of college preparation.

The call-in show is the culmination of “33 A Su Lado,” or “33 on Your Side,” a weeklong offering of information about the university and higher education on KTVW-Univision Channel 33, presented by the station, ASU and the city of Phoenix.

During the week, Channel 33 has broadcast segments during the 5 p.m. and 10 p.m. news broadcasts to build awareness about ASU. Segments highlight early childhood readiness, high school preparation, and college preparation and feature interviews with staff members from the Office of Youth Preparation and Access ASU, as well as members of the Phoenix community who have benefited from the university’s programs and resources.

For the call-in show, ASU volunteers will be available for calls from the Spanish speaking community from 5 – 10:30 p.m. Live segments will feature financial aid, admission, early childhood education and parental involvement. This portion of the broadcast will be shown from the KAET-TV/Channel 8 studios on the Tempe campus.

This year’s “33 A Su Lado” marks the third time ASU, Phoenix and Channel 33 have partnered on this event. ASU volunteers expect to answer about 1,500 calls during this year’s broadcast.
