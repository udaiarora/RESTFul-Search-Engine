April 11, 2006

ASU hosts 2nd annual Pat's Run April 15

With the date for the second annual Pat's Run fast approaching, it's time once again to remember one of the most famous Sun Devils in history, Pat Tillman.

Tillman was an ASU football player who was so moved by the terrorist attacks on Sept. 11, 2001, that he gave up his professional career with the Arizona Cardinals to become a member of the Army's elite Ranger unit in 2002. He was killed in Afganistan in April 2004 during his tour of duty.

ASU is home to the Tillman Scholarship, which is dedicated to the former football standout and the idea of service to the community.

More than 6,000 runners and volunteers participated in the inaugural Pat's Run last year, raising more than $100,000 for the Tillman Leadership Program in his name at his alma mater. This year's event will take place April 15, and will finish again at the 42-yard line of Sun Devil Stadium. Participants from about 40 states attended the event last year.

The Tillman Foundation also will have a Pat's Run April 30 in Tillman's hometown of San Jose, Calif.

"As his brother-in-law, I want you to know how important the Sun Devil family was to Pat," says Alex Garwood, executive director of the Tillman Foundation. "He was very proud to be a Sun Devil, and proud to be a graduate of the fine W. P. Carey School of Business. I want to emphasize how encouraged I am by the generosity and pride ASU fans have shown to the foundation and family through the various activities in the athletic department. The Mike Sullivan mural and the display case in the new Sun Devil Hall of Fame are shining examples. I understand much better the Sun Devil family, and the way the ASU family takes care of its own."

Race coordinators are expecting about 10,000 runners and volunteers this year, according to Doug Tammaro of ASU's Sports Information Department.

Several major sponsors are stepping up in various ways for the second run. VIP Homes has become the main sponsor, and others are signing up as participants, Tammaro says.

Registration is available online at (www.patsrun.com). The event, organized by former ASU trainer Perry Edinger - who also helped train Tillman for a marathon - includes an improved Kid's Area and a silent auction. Participants can walk the 4.2 miles, and their children can run a 0.42-mile race, which also finishes in Sun Devil Stadium.

The event takes place on the same day as the annual spring football game. It is a wonderful day to be involved with ASU and Tillman's passionate legacy.

Keith Jennings, k.jennings@asu.edu
