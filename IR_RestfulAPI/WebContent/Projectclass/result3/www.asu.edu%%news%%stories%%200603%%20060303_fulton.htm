March 3, 2006

ASU rises to Fulton challenge

Fultons help university raise $1.4 million in 2005

A challenge from Phoenix-area home builder and philanthropist Ira A. Fulton and his wife, Mary Lou, resulted in ASU students, faculty and programs receiving more than $1.4 million in new charitable contributions in 2005.

Building on that experience, Ira Fulton recently announced a new "Fulton Challenge" that should result in even broader support for ASU.

In February 2005, Fulton and the ASU Foundation announced the terms of the 2005 Fulton Challenge. Seven ASU colleges participated, and each set a specific amount of contributions to raise from alumni, faculty, students and friends.

Under the terms of the challenge, if the colleges met their goals by the end of the year, the Fultons would match those amounts with an investment of their own.

The results were outstanding. Not only did the schools achieve their goals, but all surpassed them. In addition, the Fultons agreed to match the original goal and all funds raised by the colleges.

The final results included:

. Barrett Honors College - $25,000 challenge; $48,200 raised.

. College of Design - $25,000 challenge; $53,852 raised.

. College of Nursing - $25,000 challenge; $55,873 raised.

. College of Education - $25,000 challenge; $106,258 raised.

. Herberger College of Fine Arts - $50,000 challenge; $84,225 raised.

. College of Law - $50,000 challenge; $185,923 raised.

. College of Liberal Arts and Sciences - $100,000 challenge; $177,205 raised.

The total challenge amount was $300,000, and the seven colleges collected $711,536. The Fultons matched that amount. The total benefit to the university came to $1,423,072.

"Through the Fulton Challenge, Mary Lou and Ira Fulton once again demonstrated their affection for ASU and commitment to its students, faculty and staff," says Ira A. Jackson, the ASU Foundation's president and chief executive officer. "They are ASU's most generous financial supporters, having committed $162 million to the university during the past four years. What makes them unique is their desire to create a 'culture of giving' here, where everyone associated does his or her own part - large or small - to help the university grow and achieve its vision."

The success of last year's challenge has resulted in a new Fulton Challenge for this year, which was announced at a recent breakfast event honoring the couple. Of the seven colleges that participated in last year's program, all were encouraged to set a new goal and raise private contributions between Feb. 1 - June 30. The Fultons will again match their results.

This year's challenge also will include two important changes:

. Donations from parents will now be included in the fundraising totals and be eligible for a match.

. The 2006 Fulton Challenge will be expanded to include five more ASU units, making 12 in all. The new units are: the Polytechnic campus; the West campus; the College of Public Programs; the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication; and the Emeritus College.

Colleges and campuses participating in the new Fulton Challenge already have determined their fundraising goals. If all units achieve their goals, the result will be a minimum of $1.78 million for the university.

Fundraising for the new Fulton Challenge is already under way. The ASU Foundation will publish monthly results on its Web site (www.asufoundation.org).

The 2006 Fulton Challenge is the latest in a series of philanthropic investments by the Fultons at ASU. In December, Ira Fulton announced that he and his wife were investing $100 million in the university to name the College of Education and the ASU Foundation Building, and to start a strategic investment fund for use by ASU President Michael M. Crow.

In 2003, Ira Fulton made a $50 million pledge to the School of Engineering (now the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering) and a $5 million pledge to the College of Education where, in honor of Mary Lou, he established a Presidential Chair in remedial and early childhood reading and literacy, scholarships, graduate fellowships, and an educational research fund. Mary Lou and Ira also sponsored Fulton Challenges for the Engineering school and Intercollegiate Athletics (ICA) in 2004 - 2005.

For more information on the Fulton Challenge, please contact, Les Smith, associate vice president for university development in the ASU Foundation, at (480) 965-6380.
