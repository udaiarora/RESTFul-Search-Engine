CONSTITUTION

ARTICLE I. NAME
This organization shall be called the Library Staff Association of the Arizona State University Libraries.
ARTICLE II. PURPOSE
The purpose of this organization shall be to promote the interests of the personnel of University Libraries of the Tempe campus. This activity includes, but is not limited to, the fostering of camaraderie through social events, community outreach, and the maintenance of staff lounges.
ARTICLE III. MEMBERSHIP
  • Section 1. Membership shall be open to all staff members of the Arizona State University Libraries, i.e.., Classified Staff, Academic Professional, Service Professional, and Student.
  • Section 2. There shall be three classes of members: active, honorary and student.
    • (a) Active members are those whose dues have been paid.
    • (b) Honorary members are those persons the Executive Board chooses to honor. They shall enjoy all the rights and privileges of the active members unless otherwise designated by the Board.
    • (c) Student Library Aide members shall enjoy all rights and privileges of the active members except to vote and to hold elected office. Students are required to be employed at the Libraries and participation must occur outside working hours.
ARTICLE IV. OFFICERS
  • Section 1. The officers of the Association shall be President, President-elect, Secretary and Treasurer.
  • Section 2. The officers shall be elected by confidential electronic ballot within one month, prior to the annual meeting.
  • Section 3. All officers shall serve during the current fiscal year (7/1 - 6/30).
  • Section 4. All vacancies occurring among officers during the term, except the President, shall be filled by appointment by the Executive Board.
  • Section 5. The elected or appointed officers of the Association plus the chair of the Classified Staff Task Force shall constitute the Executive Board which shall meet each month. The immediate Past President shall be available to the Board for consultation and could serve on the Executive Board on a voluntary basis.
ARTICLE V. MEETINGS
  • Section 1. The Executive Board shall designate general meetings and special events, September through June.
  • Section 2. The June meeting shall be designated the Annual Meeting of the Association. The officers will be installed at this meeting.
  • Section 3. Special meetings may be called by the President or at the written request of fifteen active members. The purpose of the special meeting must be stated.
ARTICLE VI. AMENDMENT
Any proposed amendment to this Constitution may be submitted in writing at any regular meeting of the Association. Such proposed amendment must be signed by three active members before being submitted to the Association and read. After such notice, it shall be voted on at the next regular meeting, or electronically, and shall become a part of the Constitution only if approved by a two thirds majority of the electronic vote.

Accepted unanimously
6/21/04

BY-LAWS

I. DUES
  • Section 1. The annual dues shall be determined by a quorum of the membership.
    [Note: $5.00 currently]
  • Section 2. Annual dues are payable on or before the October general meeting.
  • Section 3. New personnel joining LSA after January 1 are eligible for membership in the Staff association upon payment of one half (1/2) of the annual dues.
II. DUTIES OF OFFICERS
  • Section 1. President: The President shall preside at all meetings and shall exercise general supervision over affairs and activities of the association. The following year, as immediate past president, he/she shall be available for consultation and conflict resolution and may serve on the Executive Board on a voluntary basis.
  • Section 2. President-elect: The President-elect shall assume the duties of the president during his/her absence and shall assume the office of President in the event the president is unable to serve. The President-elect shall also serve as Program Chairman. In the following year, the President-elect shall become President.
  • Section 3. Secretary: The Secretary shall keep the minutes of all meetings and handle the official correspondence of the Association.
  • Section 4. Treasurer: The Treasurer shall receive all Association funds and disburse on approval of the Executive Board or President.
III. TERMS OF OFFICE
No person may hold office for more than two consecutive years.
IV. EXECUTIVE BOARD
The Executive Board shall consist of Library Staff Association Officers plus the Chair of the Classified Staff Task Force. The Board shall formulate plans, purposes and procedures of the Association.
V. COMMITTEES
  • Section 1. Standing committees and special committees shall be appointed by the President.
  • Section 2. Classified Staff Task Force: (see CSTF Bylaws).
VI. QUORUM
The number of active members present at any general meeting shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.
VII. AUTHORITY
Where the Constitution does not specify rules of procedure, Robert's Rules of Order Revised shall govern.
VIII. AMENDMENT
Any amendment to these by-laws shall be proposed to the Executive Board who shall present the proposed amendment in written form to the members at the next general meeting. The amendment shall be adopted by a two-thirds majority vote of those members present at that meeting or the Board may elect to act electronically after the informational meeting. In the event of an electronic vote, a two-thirds majority shall also be required for adoption.

Accepted unanimously
6/21/04
