January 7, 2003

AZ Business Conditions Index continues climb in December

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index continued its steady climb to reach 50.9 in December, up from 48.3 the previous month.

The Index managed to exceed the critical 50-point level. A reading over 50 indicates that the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term. "This is the fourth consecutive month that the index has risen, albeit at a slow pace," said Dawn McLaren, an analyst with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center.

Driving the increase was a jump in the New Orders component, which rose to 55.7 in December from 50.5 in November. The Production sub-index fell slightly to 51.5 in December from 54.7, offsetting some of the gain contributed by New Orders. "It is a positive sign that Production remained above the 50-point mark," McLaren said. "The Employment sub-index has yet to break that level; it rose slightly to 40.9 in December from 38.1 the previous month. The Employment sub-index has not risen above 50 since December 2000.

"The Prices Index is showing some strength, rising to 55.1 in December from 50.5 the previous month. This is an indication that price weakness present during the recession has come to an end and that upward pressure on prices is evident."

Each December, the Arizona Business Conditions Survey inquires about plans for capital equipment expenditure. This type of expenditure is seen as essential to a return of healthy growth in the economy, both nationally and locally. Thirty-seven percent of respondents anticipate increasing their capital equipment expenditure in 2003, while 32 percent plan to cut expenditures. "This is a significant improvement over 2002, when just 26 percent of the respondents planned to increase their capital equipment expenditures," McLaren said. She noted that most of the planned expenditures for this year are for additional, rather than replacement, equipment.

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available online at www.cob.asu.edu/seid/eoc/azpmi/. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data available in prn format.
