February 16, 2005

Program builds educational experience across border

For the past year, Club ASU has invited young people from schools and organizations throughout Arizona to visit the university and get involved in the diverse campus life. On Feb. 17, Club ASU will welcome its first group of students from across the U.S.-Mexico border.

About 80 students from Eloy Junior High School and Escuela Secundaria Tecnica No. 2 from Obregon, Mexico, will be treated to an “eye-opening experience” at ASU.

“The students that participate will have the valuable opportunity to see how higher education fits into other students’ lives from the Latino community,” says Anna Wales, Club ASU’s program coordinator senior. “We hope Club ASU visits reduce anxiety regarding higher education and clear up misconceptions.”

Both visiting schools are participating in Hands Across the Border, a cross-cultural exchange program between students in the United States and Mexico dedicated to the improvement of cultural sensitivity and understanding among citizens of the border states of Mexico, Canada and the United States through educational school exchange programs.

Corey Brewer, school counseling chair for the Santa Cruz Valley Union High School , became involved in coordinating this visit, when her daughter, Katelynne, a seventh-grader at Eloy Junior High, told her about the HATB program.

Brewer says the HATB program has provided her daughter and fellow exchange students with a cultural view of education.

“It’s really given our visitors an opportunity to experience a different part of American culture – higher education,” says Brewer, of the HATB program, which was founded in 1981. “For many of these students from Obregon, this will be their only opportunity to step onto a college campus. Through HATB, students are not only able to learn about a culture different than their own; they are able to experience it firsthand.”

Students from Eloy Junior High visited Obregon Jan. 19 – 23 for their exchange. Next week, the students from Escuela Secundaria Tecnica No. 2 will visit Eloy Junior High and make their way to the ASU campus on Feb. 17. While at ASU, students from both schools will be recognized at a reception at 7 p.m., before they attend the ASU basketball game against Oregon State at Wells Fargo Arena.

“We are excited for this collaborative opportunity with schools outside of the metro area and ASU,” Wales says. “We hope this event will enlighten everyone who will be participating.”

For more information about Club ASU, visit the Web site (http://www.asu.edu/clubasu/).
