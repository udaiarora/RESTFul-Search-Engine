The ASU Libraries

There are 7 ASU Libraries within 4 organizational units:

  • The University Libraries are composed of 4 libraries on the Tempe campus: Hayden, Noble, Architecture and Music.
  • College of Law Library, also on the Tempe campus,
  • Polytechnic campus Library, and
  • Fletcher Library at the West campus

All libraries are open to the general public as well as to ASU students, faculty and staff. The Noble Science and Engineering Library is described in detail below. For more information about each of the other libraries see: Arizona State University Libraries (http://www.asu.edu/lib/help/asubrief.htm)

Daniel E. Noble Science and Engineering Library

Noble Library houses the collections for Engineering, Mathematics, Nursing and the Sciences (Astronomy, Biology, Chemistry, Geology, Physics) and includes the Maps Collection.

The library is located on the Tempe campus, on the southwest corner of Tyler and McAllister Malls, across the sidewalk from the Cancer Research Center and the Goldwater Building

Opened in 1983 and remodeled in 1999, the library encloses a four-story atrium that creates a spacious and pleasant atmosphere for study and research. Facilities include over 60 library workstations, wireless access to the campus network, plus open and enclosed study spaces for both groups and individuals.

The library has 4 service units with approximately 30 staff:

  • Access Services
  • Copy Services
  • Maps Reference
  • Science Reference

As Noble Science and Engineering Reference Librarians, the physics/astronomy and math librarians provide special services including:

  • Library or Information Literacy Instruction
    In your classroom or our instruction room, have demonstrations of specific databases and/or instruction for a specific assignment. The librarians will also prepare a web page for a specific class or assignment that provides the students with a list of appropriate resources and how to use them.

    To schedule library instruction for a class or group, please contact the appropriate librarian at least 2-3 weeks in advance so that the proper materials, demonstrations and database access privileges can be prepared. Requests for instructional services is especially intense during the first 4-5 weeks of the Fall and Spring semesters; when requesting instruction during this time, please have several dates available - we will do our best to accomodate your first priority.

  • Research Consultations
    You may also set up an appointment with the appropriate librarian for an individual consultation. Need help using specific databases or resources? Need a refresher on available library services and resources? Call or e-mail the appropriate librarian to set up a time.

  • Purchasing Requests
    Is there a book, journal, or database that you would like the ASU Libraries to purchase? Let us know! Please contact the appropriate librarian who will forward the request to our Collection Development Department.

How to Find ...

  • Books
    • To find books available in the ASU Libraries, use the ASU Libraries Online Catalog.

    • To identify published books (including those not available in the ASU Libraries) use WorldCat

  • Conference Proceedings

    • To find conference proceedings available in the ASU Libraries, use the ASU Libraries Online Catalog.
      Search Tip: Use the KEYWORD search and type in words from the conference's name.

    • To identify published conference proceedings (including those not available in the ASU Libraries) use Inspec

  • Dissertations

    • To obtain a copy of an ASU dissertation, use Current Research @ Arizona State University

    • To identify dissertations from other universities, use Dissertation Abstracts International

  • Journal Articles

    • To identify articles on a topic, use Inspec, MathSciNet, or Web of Science/Science Citation Index

    • To determine if the University Libraries have a specific journal, use the University Libraries' E-Journal list and then the ASU Libraries' Online Catalog.

  • Patents

    • U.S. Patents (1790 to the present) can be obtained from the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office web site at: http://www.uspto.gov/patft/index.html

    • Foreign Patents (coverage varies by country) can be obtained from the European Patent Office web site at: http://ep.espacenet.com/

  • Property Data (boiling point, density, etc.)

    • Start with the Index for Physical, Chemical, and Other Property Data at: http://www.asu.edu/lib/noble/chem/property.htm

Keeping Current

  • Research
    Keep up-to-date in your subject area by taking advantage of publishers' e-mail alerting services. Services vary but may include:

    • notification when a new journal issue is published (common),
    • notification when specific articles in forthcoming journal issues are available online, and
    • setting up a personal search profile that will identify individual articles of interest from multiple journals.

    Use the University Libraries' E-Journal list to find your favorite journals; look for instructions at the journal's home page.

Checking Out Material

  • How do I get a Library Card?
    Your SunCard (ASU ID card) is used to checkout materials. Forget your card? - Show us a picture ID.

  • How many items may I check out? ... For how long?
    There is no limit on the number of items a graduate student or faculty member may check out. Masters level graduate students may check out items for 30 days; teaching and research assistants, and doctoral level graduate students may check out items for 90 days; faculty, academic professionals and staff may check out items for 150 days. Items can be renewed until requested (i.e. "recalled") by another library user.

  • Are Fines Charged?
    For each item not returned or renewed within thirty (30) days of the due date, the borrower is assessed a $10 overdue billing fee and a replacement charge. Only the replacement charge is cancelled when the item is returned.

  • I'm a faculty member - I don't have to worry about Fines or Recalls, do I?
    Yes, you do. Every library user has the same responsibility to return materials promptly so that others may use them. Failure to return material on time results in fines. Long absences from campus (intersessions, summers, sabbaticals, etc.) do not release individuals from these responsibilites; please return materials prior to leaving or have someone on campus see that all due dates and recall notices are honored.

  • The book I need is checked out - how do I get it recalled?
    In the ASU Libraries Online Catalog, use the blue REQUEST button to have the item recalled. There is no charge for this service.

  • For more information see Access Services (http://www.asu.edu/lib/access/)

Document Delivery

  • The Item I need is in the ASU East or the ASU West Library - do I have to go there to get it?
    No, the University Libraries' will retrieve or photocopy items from other campus libraries.

    • For books:
      In the ASU Libraries Online Catalog, use the blue REQUEST button to have the item brought to any one of the main campus libraries' circulation desks. There is no charge for this service. Delivery Time: 2-3 business days.

    • For journals:
      Use the Library Express/Document Delivery photocopy request form. A photocopy of the article will be delivered to your mailbox on the Tempe Campus, or you may obtain the copy from the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. There is no cost for this service. Delivery Time: 2-3 business days.

  • The Item I need is in storage - where's that?
    The Libraries' operate a separate facility on the East Campus for overflow material and items that need special handling. The facility is not open to the public as the material is packaged and maintained in a special, controlled environment. To obtain material housed in this facility, you may use the REQUEST button on the catalog record if the volume you need is listed on the record OR stop by or call the Information/Reference Desk at 480/965-7607. We will have the item recalled. Delivery Time: 1-2 business days.

    Coming soon -- same or next day electronic delivery of journal articles housed in storage. We hope to have this service available sometime in 2004.

  • The Item I need is not available at any ASU Library - can the library get it for me?
    Yes, use the Interlibrary Loan Service. You will be notified by e-mail when the item is available. Most photocopies come directly via e-mail; books must be picked up at the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. Usually there is no charge for this service, however, if there will be a cost involved, you will be contacted for approval, prior to the request being ordered. Delivery Time: Journal articles average 1 week, while books average 2 weeks. Materials that are rare, in fragile condition, in high use, or non-circulating in most libraries, may take considerably longer or may not be able to be acquired. Rush service and renewals for books are generally not available.

  • I'm too busy to come to the Library; can some one deliver materials to me?
    Yes, we deliver books and photocopies to mailboxes on the Tempe Campus via our "Library Express" service. This service is free for faculty but graduate students are charged a fee. Cost for graduate students: Copy charges are $ 3.00 for the first 1-15 pages, plus $ 1.00 for each additional 1-10 pages per each individual article. Book charges are $ 1.00 each. Delivery Time: 2 business days.

    To use this service, set up an account with Library Express by going to the Interlibrary Loan Office in Hayden Library. Once the account is set up, to order books use the Library Express Book Form; to order journal articles use the Library Express Photocopy Form.

Copies

  • How much do Photocopies cost?
    Photocopies cost $0.10/page if using cash; $0.08/page if using a SunCard or Library Copy Card. .

  • Can you make copies of microfilm or microfiche?
    Yes, copies cost $0.15/page if using cash; $0.12/page if using a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

  • How much do computer prints cost?
    Computer prints cost $0.08/page and requires a SunCard or Library Copy Card.

Off-Campus Access to Electronic Resources

  • Can I use electronic journals and databases from my home or office?
    Yes, when accessing Library Resources from off-campus you will be prompted to sign in with your ASURITE ID and password.

  • Am I able to access Chemical Abstracts/SciFinder Scholar from my office? ... home?
    Access to the Chemical Abstracts database requires SciFinder Scholar software on the accessing computer and:

    • Office - any on-campus computer that is registered with ASU's Information Technology (thereby having an "asu.edu" domain name), should be able to access Chemical Abstracts once the SciFinder Scholar software is loaded. See downloading instructions below.

    • Home - only computers using an ASU PPP account or ASU VPN software along with the SciFinder Scholar software are able to have access to Chemical Abstracts. See downloading instructions below.

    Downloading Instructions:
    • Go to the Chemical Abstracts/SciFinder Scholar database description page (http://www.asu.edu/lib/resources/db/scifinder.htm)
    • Click on the link and enter your authorization information.
    • Click on "Download SFS Software and Documentation" link.
    • Follow instructions for either Windows or MAC
    • Once software is on your computer, access Chemical Abstracts/SFS from your desktop and not from the library web page.
