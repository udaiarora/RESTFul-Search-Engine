JANUARY
  • Spring Professional Development Mixer
    Thursday, January 25, 2007
    4:00-6:00 p.m.
    Location: Grilled Expedition

  • Understanding Your Graduate Financial Awards
    Wednesday, January 31, 2007
    9:00-10:00a.m
    Location: MU 222
    Speaker: Olivia Diaz

    This seminar will help students understand their financial aid awards (i.e. student loans, scholarships & outside resources) and how it correlates with their Cost of Attendance for the academic year.
    Audience: SFS seminars are intended for all ASU graduate and undergraduate juniors and seniors students.

  • How to Speak, Look and Act Your Way to the Top
    Wednesday, January 31, 2007
    3:30-5:30 p.m.
    Location: Neeb Hall
    Speaker: Ellen Kaye

    The difference between a mediocre and magnificent career usually hinges on one simple thing: how you present yourself to management, clients, coworkers, and the public.

    This seminar will be presented by Ellen A. Kaye who is an international leadership development consultant and the author of the best-selling business book Maximize Your Presentation Skills: How to Speak, Look and Act on Your Way to the Top.

    The event is co-sponsored by the Division of Graduate Studies, the Graduate and Professional Student Association (GPSA), and the ASU Memorial Union (MU).

    Ellen will share insights from her recent book about public speaking, business etiquette, and leadership image. She?ll explain how you can master these crucial career skills and stand out from the crowd, enabling you to communicate like a seasoned business leader.
    Audience: SFS seminars are intended for all ASU graduate and undergraduate juniors and seniors students.

FEBRUARY
  • Marketing Your Graduate Degree for Non-Academic Jobs
    Thursday, February 22, 2007
    2:00-3:30 p.m., Location: MU 223
    Speaker: Douglas Devoe

    Learn how to sell the skills set unique to graduate students to perspective employers. This presentation includes techniques that will help you showcase your graduate degree throughout all aspects of the job search (job fairs, résumé, interviews, etc).
    Audience: SFS seminars are intended for all ASU graduate and undergraduate juniors and seniors students.


  • Looking as Good in Person as You do on Paper
    Tuesday, February 27, 2007
    2:00-3:30 p.m., Location: MU 222
    Speaker: Douglas Devoe

    This presentation provides guidance in networking, interview preparation and general job search etiquette but is aimed more broadly at helping you re-think and re-cast your accomplishments and credentials while meeting employers in different formal and informal settings.
    Audience: SFS seminars are intended for all ASU graduate and undergraduate juniors and seniors students.




MARCH
  • Why Grad School?
    Wednesday, March 21, 2007
    12:00-1:00 p.m., Location: MU 208E
    Speakers: Chad Nash and John McKnight

    Is graduate school right for you? Going to graduate school affects the rest of your life. There are both pros and cons to continuing your education. Students choose graduate school for many reasons, including intellectual curiosity and professional advancement. Some choose grad school because they aren't sure what to do or don't feel ready for a job.

    In this seminar our speakers will help you with answers to some of the following questions:

    • Why do I want to go to graduate school? Is it for the right reasons?
    • What are my career goals?
    • Will graduate school assist me in meeting my career goals?
    • What will I specialize in? What are my interests?

    Audience: SFS seminars are intended for all ASU graduate and undergraduate juniors and seniors students.
