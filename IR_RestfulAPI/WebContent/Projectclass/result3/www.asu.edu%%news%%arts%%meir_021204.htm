February 12, 2004

Meir gives 'Valentine' performance, piano

Celebrating the inauguration of a new 9-foot Bösendorfer grand piano, faculty artist Baruch Meir presents a "great big Valentine to the public." Meir will perform "A Bösendorfer Valentine" at 2:30 p.m., Feb. 15, in Katzin Concert Hall. Tickets are $5 and available in advance by calling (480) 965-6447.

Meir, who joined the ASU piano faculty in 2000, was recently named "Bösendorfer Concert Artist" - an award conferred on only a small number of distinguished artists worldwide. With this recognition, Meir brings to the Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts School of Music a new piano directly from Bösendorfer's Vienna factory.

When asked to comment on the award, Meir explained, "Receiving this distinction from Bösendorfer is a true honor to me, and I believe that faculty and students will benefit a great deal from the possibilities of the new instrument."

Due to the inaugural occasion coinciding with Valentine's Day weekend, Meir has chosen a romantic theme for the evening's program. He will perform several solo works of Chopin as well as Scriabin's evocative Sonata-Fantasie. Colleagues and friends from the Herberger College School of Music will also join him for a variety of favorite duos. Performing with Meir are some of the School of Music's most outstanding, and newest faculty: baritone Robert Barefield; violist Nancy Buck; violinists Danwen Jiang and Jonathan Swartz; as well as guest pianist Hamilton Tescarollo. Meir and his colleagues will present "heartfelt and heartwarming" works by Rossini, Brahms, Godowsky/Heifetz, Quilter and selections of the most romantic of the famed Kreisler violin-piano duos.

Bell, with Herberger College of Fine Arts, can be reached at (480) 965-2817 or (rebecca.bell@asu.edu).

This article appeared in the February 6, 2004 issue of ASU Insight.
