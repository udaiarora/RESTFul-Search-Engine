ASU CARES tackles new public service project

ASU CARES, the annual spring public service project at ASU, is getting ready to take on its most ambitious project ever on March 6. ASU CARES volunteers are needed for it to be successful.

The two-part project will provide much needed supplies to the House of Refuge East and a full measure of volunteer labor for Sunshine Acres Children's Home in Mesa.

ASU CARES volunteers to improve the facilities of organizations, which serve members of our community who are the most in need of support.

"We have such a wonderful volunteer force that it is often hard to find large enough projects for the group to tackle," says Sheila Stokes, one of the ASU CARES coordinators. "Everyone works so hard and so fast we often finish in a few hours what others expect to take a few days. Our project this year provides challenges worthy of our volunteers."

At Sunshine Acres Children's Home, from 9 a.m. to 1 p.m., ASU CARES volunteers will paint and refurbish playground and BMX areas, landscape, plant trees and shrubs, and sort and organize a large warehouse that supports the thrift shop run by Sunshine Acres.

Faculty, staff and students are invited to take part. Volunteers will receive lunch and an ASU CARES T-shirt.

Volunteers are encouraged to wear sturdy shoes, work gloves, sunglasses, and/or safety glasses and bring sun screen. Volunteers are asked, if possible, to bring a rake and shovel, labeled with their name and address.

Sunshine Acres Children's Home is a residential facility for 65 children, ages 5 - 15 who are separated from their parents for one reason or another.

If you cannot volunteer to help March 6, but wish to contribute, ASU CARES also is accepting donations of disposable diapers for the House of Refuge East, located adjacent to the ASU East Campus. The House of Refuge East provides transitional housing and support services for local homeless individuals, families and victims of domestic violence.

In addition to diapers, ASU CARES will accept donations of non-perishable food, new or gently used clothing, personal hygiene items, cleaning supplies, etc. for Sunshine Acres Children Home and House of Refuge East.

To volunteer, register online at (www.asu.edu/mu/community/events.htm). Indicate whether or not you will need transportation. To donate items or for information, call Stokes or Cookie Ellis at (480) 965-3201.

Jennings, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-3504 or (k.jennings@asu.edu).
