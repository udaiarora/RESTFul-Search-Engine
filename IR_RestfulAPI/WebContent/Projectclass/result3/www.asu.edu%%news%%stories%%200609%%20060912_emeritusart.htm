September 12, 2006

College of Public Programs unveils ASU Emeritus College Art Exhibit

Retired faculty and staff from ASU are playing a special role in the emergence of the Downtown Phoenix campus, thanks to a unique partnership designed by the College of Public Programs that brings not just paint and color, but a major new art installation to Phoenix.

When Richard Jacob, the Emeritus College dean and a professor emeritus of physics, approached the College of Public Programs' dean, Debra Friedman, about how ASU's retired faculty could assist as she began her tenure at ASU, he was surprised by her response.

Friedman was hired in 2005 from the University of Washington to lead the ASU College of Public Programs in its role as a vanguard of the new ASU Downtown Phoenix campus. She has been responsible not only for refocusing the academic, research and community outreach programs, but also for transforming roughly 78,000 square feet of raw urban office space into a college and campus community.

She acknowledges that the emeriti group has vast academic and research expertise to offer. But she was more interested in harnessing direct involvement from the group ? and focusing on their passion.

Art was the answer. Three months after their initial conversation, Friedman and Jacob announced the opening of the ASU Emeritus College Art Exhibit on the fourth through ninth floors of the University Center at 411 N. Central Ave. Students, faculty and staff are not the only ones who can enjoy the display. The public is welcome to enjoy the exhibit and a reception from 6-11 p.m., Nov. 3, when it debuts as part of the city's free First Friday ArtWalk event.

Most artists will be present, and a printed guide to the gallery areas will be available.

?We are enormously grateful to the emeritus faculty artists for their generosity to the College of Public Programs,? Friedman says.

The more than 150 pieces, representing the work of 13 professional and avocational artists, include woodcuts, oils, lithographs, watercolors, fired clay, porcelain, ink washes, graphic, collages and sculpture. They range in size from ?Can I Have 5 Dollars Mister,? a 42-by-48-inch acrylic by retired art professor James Pile, to an 8-inch-square piece of mixed media on paper titled ?Sweet Shadows? by Louis Weschler, former dean of the College of Public Programs and professor emeritus of Public Affairs.

Professor emeritus of anthropology John Aguilar chaired the project team, which also included Mark Reader, professor emeritus of political science and Gene Valentine, professor emeritus of English. The committee also photographed 12 of the creations for a 2007 calendar that can be bought by contacting the ASU Emeritus College at (480) 965-0002.
