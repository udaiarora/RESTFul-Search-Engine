July 8, 2004

Brower to direct development for Fulton School of Engineering

Mark Brower has joined the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering as director of development.

Brower, who started at ASU June 14, served as director of annual giving at the University of Nevada Las Vegas Foundation since May 2003.

Prior to joining UNLV, Brower served as senior director of development and external relations for the College of Engineering at Michigan State University.

Brower has 13 years of broad-based development experience, much of it in higher education and engineering. He has experience in planning and implementing major capital campaigns and is accomplished in both major gift and annual fund raising. He has particular expertise in corporate and foundation giving, alumni giving and planned giving.

At MSU, Brower was responsible for a portfolio of 200 major gift and planned gift prospects and donors throughout the world. In addition to securing million dollar plus cash gifts, he closed estate and planned gifts in excess of $17 million and raised $11.5 million in annual gifts while at the college. He led an interdepartmental team that secured a university record $100 million gift from General Motors and EDS.

Brower also has served as director of development for the General Motors Engineering and Management Institute - now Kettering University - in Michigan; director of annual and planned giving for Villa Julie College in Maryland; and campaign coordinator for the American Public Works Association, a civil engineering non-profit trade organization in Washington D.C.
