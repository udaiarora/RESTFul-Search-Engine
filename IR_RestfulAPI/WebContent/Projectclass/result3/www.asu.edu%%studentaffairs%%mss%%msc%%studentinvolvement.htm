Student Involvement and Leadership

The Multicultural Student Center’s student coalitions and organizations provide cultural programming, academic support, and leadership development to multicultural students. The Student Coalitions are the umbrella groups for many student organizations designed to serve the African American, American Indian, Asian American/Pacific Islander, and Hispanic/Latino/Chicano student communities. The student coalitions and organizations sponsored by the MSC include:

  • African American Men of ASU (AAMASU)
  • Adelante supports incoming freshmen, specifically Latino, Chicano, and Hispanic students.
  • American Indian Council
  • Asian /Asian Pacific American Students Coalition
  • Black African Coalition
  • El Concilio
  • Native Americans Taking Initiative on Success (N.A.T.I.O.N.S.)  builds and nurtures the Native American freshman community at ASU.
  • Students Taking Action to Reach Success (STARS) supports incoming freshmen, specifically African American students, to have a successful first year at ASU by addressing the five points of the star:  academic enrichment, leadership development, career information, community service, and social interaction.
  • Students Toward Educational Progress (STEP), is a multicultural honor society committed to academic excellence and community service.

Affiliate student coalitions and organizations include:

  • Coalition of International Students 
  • Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, and Queer Coalition
  • Phoenix Union Devils
  • Women's Coalition
