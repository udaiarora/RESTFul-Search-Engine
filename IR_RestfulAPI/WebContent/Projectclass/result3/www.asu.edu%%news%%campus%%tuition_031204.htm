March 12, 2004

Regents Set ASU Tuition

Below is a message from ASU President Michael Crow

Today, the Arizona Board of Regents set tuition rates for Arizona State University that will enable us to improve undergraduate and graduate education and address critical student financial aid needs.

For the 2004-05 academic year, resident undergraduate students will pay $4,062 ($456 increase); non-resident undergraduate students will pay $12,917 ($804 increase); resident graduate students will pay $5,127 ($1,334 increase); and non-resident graduate students will pay $13,647 ($1,334 increase). For resident undergraduates, the tuition increase is $130 higher than the university requested, but is still less than the increases at the University of Arizona and Northern Arizona University. In addition, the Regents did not consider a proposed $80 ASU student health fee, however, some tuition revenue will be used to enhance student health services and reduce co-pay prices.

In making its decision for ASU's tuition increase, the Regents looked at the needs of our institution, the capacity of the university to meet those needs, as well as what the other universities were doing.

With increased revenue from tuition, we will, for the second year in a row, hold unharmed or improve the financial situation of all resident undergraduate Pell Grant recipients with unmet need. In addition, there will be a significant investment in undergraduate student success by reducing first-year English and Math class sizes.

If the Governor's budget recommendation for higher education holds up, we intend to implement full tuition remission for half-time teaching and research assistants. We also intend to increase library hours and hire additional personnel in the ASU Department of Public Safety.

I would like to express my thanks to the Arizona Board of Regents for recognizing our financial need and setting tuition at a level that keeps ASU affordable, but also enables us to improve the quality of both undergraduate and graduate education.
