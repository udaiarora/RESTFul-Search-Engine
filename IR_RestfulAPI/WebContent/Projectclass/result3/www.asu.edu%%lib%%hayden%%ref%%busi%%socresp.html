Social Responsibility, Ecology, and Sustainability

Businesses conduct themselves in socially responsible ways when it is required of them. Below are sources that are useful for finding information about a company's practices, as well as general information on sustainability and social responsibility. To find more information on this topic, try looking up a company name or industry and the terms "social responsibility", "green marketing", "ethics", "environmental auditing", or use an "environment" term in the article indexes .

For more non-company specific information about social responsibility, ecology, and sustainability look at the information found at Sustainability (ICAEW), National Strategies for Sustainable Development, its Links page, or Sustainable Development Information System. Yahoo! has several web pages that list various sites for business ethics and responsibility.

  1. ACCA Sustainability Reporting Awards. Association of Chartered Certified Accountants, 2004- .
      http://www.accaglobal.com/sustainability/awards/

    These awards are available by region. Some regions have awards going back to 1997. If the award winners are not listed on the main page of each region, they are listed in the Judge's Report.

  2. Climatebiz.com: The Business Resource for Climate Management.. Washington, DC : The National Environmental Education and Training Foundation, 2002- .
      http://www.climatebiz.com/

    Advice for businesses on how to manage the climate in environmentally responsible ways. One of the pages is Best Practices, which provides examples from companies and how they are doing responsible climate management. Another useful section is the Backgrounders, which are sections explaining what climate management is, why it is important, and some things that can be done.

  3. Graham, John W. "Corporate Environmental Information: A New Source of Competitor Intelligence." Business & Finance Bulletin. n113:39-43, Winter 2000.
      Hayden Reference Desk

    Graham, John W. and Wendy C. Havelick. Corporate Environmental Policies. London : Scarecrow Press, 1999.
      HD 30.255 .G72 1999 Hayden Reference
      HD 30.255 .G72 1999 West Reference

    The first item is a short article that summarizes some recommended places to look for information about a company's corporate social responsibility statements. It places a particular emphasis on the CAER (Corporate Annual Environmental Report) and related reports that are becoming a regular part of corporate disclosure. The book, by the same author as the article, provides the environmental policies of 237 companies along with basic directory information and the corporate web site. There is an alphabetical list of companies, a country list of the companies, and an industry list included. The list of contacts for environmental organizations is also helpful.

  4. Corporate Conscience Awards. Social Accountability International, 1986- .
      http://www.cepaa.org/programs/CorporateConscienceAwards.htm

    The Social Accountability International organization is focused primarily on workplace conditions throughout the supply chain. This award is given to companies that respect the rights of workers and the local community through responsible business practices.

  5. Corporate Watch. San Francisco: Corporate Watch, n.d.
      http://www.corpwatch.org/

    An electronic magazine that is activist in nature. It is the organization of the articles in categories of information that are useful for marketing projects that make this site worth mentioning. Also a great source for negative opinions of the companies included.

  6. DowJones Sustainability Indexes. SAM Indexes, 1999- .
      http://www.sustainability-index.com/

    "Launched in 1999, the Dow Jones Sustainability Indexes are the first global indexes tracking the financial performance of the leading sustainability-driven companies worldwide. Based on the cooperation of Dow Jones Indexes, STOXX Limited and SAM they provide asset managers with reliable and objective benchmarks to manage sustainability portfolios." The Monthly Update has the indexes with charts. To see how individual companies and industry sectors are doing, use the
      DJSI World - Market Sector Leaders
      DJSI World - Industry Group Overviews
      DJSI STOXX - Market Sector Leaders and
      DJSI STOXX - Market Sectors Overviews
    The company information is very good and does include charts and graphs.

  7. EPA Industry Profiles. EPA Office of Compliance Sector Notebook Project. Profile of the _____ industry. Washington, DC : Office of Compliance, Office of Enforcement and Compliance Assurance, U.S. Environmental Protection Agency : 1995- .
      http://es.epa.gov/oeca/sector/index.html
      EP 1.2:P 94/ HAYDEN US DOCUMENTS

    "The EPA's Office of Compliance has developed a series of profiles or notebooks containing information on selected major industries. These notebooks, which focus on key indicators that holistically present air, water, and land pollutant release data, have been thoroughly reviewed by experts from both inside and outside the EPA." These are very detailed and take time to review. "Each Notebook brings you comprehensive, well-researched details gathered for the first time in a single source and includ[ing] a comprehensive environmental profile, industrial process information, pollution prevention techniques, pollutant release data, regulatory requirements, compliance/enforcement history, government and industry partnerships, innovative programs, contact names, bibliographic references, and description of research methodology".

  8. Ethical Consumer. London: ECRA, Ethical Consumer Research Association, updated 5/7/99.
      http://www.ethicalconsumer.org/

    This interesting British site mostly refrains from excessive rhetoric on ethical issues. It is a good site to check your company name against their list of companies included in articles and research. The list includes many US companies. The Links page is also very well organized by ethical issue and includes links to many international organizations.

  9. GoodMoney Home Page: Social, Ethical and Environmental Investing and Consuming & Corporate Accountability. Ossipee, NH: GoodMoney, 1999- .
      http://www.goodmoney.com/

    This is an interesting site. It looks for good money, or those who are trying harder to improve. Everything on this site takes a positive approach to corporate social responsibility.

  10. Greenbiz.com: The Resource Center on Business, the Environment, and the Bottom Line. Washington, DC : The National Environmental Education and Training Foundation, 2000- .
      http://www.greenbiz.com/

    Generally a very good site to find out more about sustainable business. Two of the pages the are worth a more detailed mention:

    • Greenbiz.com Corporate Web Sites. Washington, DC : The National Environmental Education and Training Foundation, 2000- .
        http://www.greenbiz.com/reference/corporate_sites.cfm

      This is a web site that lists corporation web sites with information about their environmental page or environmental reports of corporations. Companies can be found by name, industry sector, or keyword search. The corporate web pages vary widely in what they include. Some have enviromental mission statements. In all cases, the corporate web page puts the company in its best light.

    • Greenbiz.com Awards Directory. Washington, DC : The National Environmental Education and Training Foundation, 2000- .
        http://www.greenbiz.com/reference/awards.cfm

      If you are looking for environmental awards, including sustainability and community service, this is the list to use. Some of the awards are for specific geographic areas, such as states, cities, and counties. Awards can be searched by topic, keyword and by award name or sponsor.
