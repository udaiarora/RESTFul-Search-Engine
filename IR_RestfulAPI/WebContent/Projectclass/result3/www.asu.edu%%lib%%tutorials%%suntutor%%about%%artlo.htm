Learning Outcomes: Find Articles

Why Articles?

  • The student can recognize when they should use articles.

Scholarly Journals vs. Popular Magazines

  • The student can distinguish between a scholarly journal article and a popular magazine article.

Using Indexes

  • The student can recognize that an index is the tool to use to find articles.

General Indexes vs. Subject Indexes

  • The student can distinguish the difference between a general index and subject-specific index.

Using Citations

  • The student will identify the elements they need from the citation to locate an article.
  • The student recognize elements from citation they need for their bibliography.

Breaking Questions into Keywords

  • The student will reduce research questions into concepts and then identify appropriate keywords for each concept.

Using Connectors (and, or)

  • The student will us 'and' to combine different concepts.
  • The student will use 'or' to combine synonyms.

Using Truncation

  • The student will use truncation to broaden their search.

Refining the Search Strategy

  • The student will recognize when it is necessary to refine their search.
  • The student demonstrates techniques to refine their search strategy.

Locating Articles

  • Student will determine if the library owns the journal they need.

Who Can Help?

  • Student will know to ask a librarian for help when needed.
