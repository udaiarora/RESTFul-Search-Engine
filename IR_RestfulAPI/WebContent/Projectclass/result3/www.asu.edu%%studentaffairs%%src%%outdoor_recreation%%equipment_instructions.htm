1 Person Eureka! Backcountry 1 tent

Floor: 8' x 3'
Height: 2' 2?
Packed: 5.5? x 14?
Weight: 2 lbs. 8 oz.
Stuff Sack Contents: Tent body, Rain-fly, 2 long poles, 1 short pole, stakes,
Tent Floor Protector.

Assembly Instructions

1

 Choose an appropriate site for the tent. Make sure the tent is not in a rain wash area, on a rocky surface or on an angle. Please keep the tent a good distance from any fire as even a hot ember will burn holes in tent fabric.

2

 Straighten all of the poles, making sure all of the pole sections are fully inserted.

3

 Spread the tent floor protector on the ground where you are going to erect the tent. **IMPORTANT** Failing to use the ground cloth may result in costly damage to the tent.

4

 Unroll the tent and remove the rain-fly on top of the tent floor protector. In the case of high winds, stake down a corner of the tent facing into the wind.

5

 Put the rain fly, the smaller green piece of fabric, aside and spread out the tent. Start at one corner and slide a long tent pole through the two sleeves in the center of the tent. Push the pole across the tent and through the sleeve on the opposite side. Repeat with the second long pole creating an ?X? pattern.

6

 Secure the poles by placing the end of each pole into one of the grommets (small metal rings) at the four corners. (You may have to pull the tent fabric to ease the poles through as they form arcs)

7

 Attach all clips to the tent poles.

8

 Spread out the rain-fly with the writing side down. Place the ends of the short rain-fly pole into the pockets on the long edges of the rain-fly.

9

 Turn the rain-fly right-side up and place it on top of the tent. Fasten buckles at corners of rain-fly to the four corners of the tent.

10

 Move the tent into the final position, tuck the excess tent floor protector beneath the tent, and use the provided stakes to stake down the four corners of the tent when possible. (If tent is resting on a rock area, rope tied to rocks will also serve the same purpose as the stakes.) **It is important that the tent floor protector is completely beneath the tent because in the event of rain, any portion sticking out will gather water and channel it under the middle of the tent.

11

 Stake out the ends of the fly using the webbing and buckles provided. Adjust the tension of fly at the four corners using the buckles.

12

 Take down the tent in the reverse order that it was put up. With the tent flat on the ground, fold the body into thirds, lay the rain-fly on top of the folded body, making it match the width of the tent. Fold the long rectangle in half the long way, place the poles at the creased end, and role tightly so that it will fit in the stuff sack.

Print Version

1 Person Eureka! Solitaire

Floor: 2'8? x 8'
Height: 2'2?
Packed: 5.5? x 14?
Weight: 2 lbs. 8 oz.

Stuff Sack Contents: Tent body, 2 short poles, stakes, Tent Floor Protector, rope lines

Assembly Instructions

1

 Choose an appropriate site for the tent. Make sure the tent is not in a rain wash area, on a rocky surface or on an angle. Please keep the tent a good distance from any fire as even a hot ember will burn holes in tent fabric.

2

 Straighten all of the poles, making sure all of the pole sections are fully inserted.

3

 Spread the tent floor protector on the ground where you are going to erect the tent. **IMPORTANT** Failing to use the ground cloth may result in costly damage to the tent.

4

 Unroll the tent on top of the tent floor protector. In the case of high winds, stake down a corner of the tent facing into the wind.

5

 Spread out the tent. Start at one corner and slide a tent pole through the sleeve, and do the same at the other end of the tent. The tent will still lay flat on the ground.

6

 Secure the poles by placing the corner pins into the each end of the tent poles. (You may have to pull the tent fabric to ease the poles through as they form arcs)

7

 Move the tent into the final position, tuck the excess tent floor protector beneath the tent, and use the provided stakes to stake down the four corners of the tent when possible. Stake down the door end first pulling the fly away from the tent. Next, stake down the cords pulling the opposite end of the tent, tight enough to make the tent hold its form without drooping. (If tent is resting on a rock area, rope tied to rocks will also serve the same purpose as the stakes.) **It is important that the tent floor protector is completely beneath the tent because in the event of rain, any portion sticking out will gather water and channel it under the middle of the tent.

8

 Use the slider to adjust the tension of the fly stake down lines. Attach the s-hooks on the fly's shock-cords to the ring and pin assemblies at the back of the tent, and finally stake down the cords at the sides of the fly.

9

 Take down the tent in the reverse order that it was put up. With the tent flat on the ground, fold the body into thirds. Fold the long rectangle in half the long way, place the poles at the creased end, and role tightly so that it will fit in the stuff sack.

4PT-B

Print Version

4 Person Eureka! Sunrise 11

Floor: 11' x 11'
Height: 7'
Packed: 8 ? x 33 ?
Weight: 6lbs. 21lbs

Tent body, 2 tent poles, rain fly, 2 fly rods, stake bag w/stakes, pole bag, tent floor protector, 4 mesh organizers.

Assembly Instructions

1

 Choose an appropriate site for the tent. Make sure the tent is not in a rain wash area, on a rocky surface or on an angle. PLEASE KEEP TENT AWAY FROM FIRE, tent is very flammable!

2

 Straighten all of the poles, making sure all of the pole sections are fully inserted.

3

 Spread the tent floor protector on the ground where you are going to erect the tent. **IMPORTANT** Failing to use the ground cloth may result in costly damage to the tent.

4

 Unroll the tent and remove the rain-fly on top of the tent floor protector. In the case of high winds, stake down a corner of the tent facing into the wind.

5

 Insert the two long poles into the rod sleeves on the tent body. They should run corner to corner crossing in the center.

6

 Place a corner pin into the end of one of the poles. At the opposite end, with the pole in one hand and a pin & ring in the other, feed the pole up, bending it evenly along its length to create an arc. Place the pin into the end of the pole to maintain tension. Repeat with the other pole to make the tent freestanding.

7

 Attach all the tent clips to the poles.

8

 Stake the tent down through the corner loops. Stake the opposite corners while applying tension to smooth the floor wrinkles. Locate the stake loops on the sides of the tent and stake these last.

9

 Spread the fly out upside down. Place the tips of the fly rods into the pockets at the edge of the fly. Fasten the hook and loop strap around the intersection of the two poles to secure them in place.

10

 Set the fly other the tent with the Eureka! Logo over the door. Wrap the internal hook and loop straps around the poles. Fasten the shock cord and J-hook at each of the corners of the fly to the corners of the tent.

11

 Tuck the excess tent floor protector beneath the tent. (If tent is resting on a rock area, rope tied to rocks will also serve the same purpose as the stakes.) **It is important that the tent floor protector is completely beneath the tent because in the event of rain, any portion sticking out will gather water and channel it under the middle of the tent.

12

 Take-down: Remove fly and open door. Remove clips from the tent poles, then pull the pins out from the pole ends. Fold the tent to the center and roll it towards the door to allow the air to escape.

13

 *This tent is equipped with hanging mesh pockets for small accessories and water bottles. Do not keep food in the tent. A hungry animal that smells the food will chew or claw through the fabric.

Print Version

3 Person Eureka! Timberlite XT

Floor: 7'6? x 6'4?
Height: 4'
Packed: 7? x 16.5?
Weight: 6 lbs.

Stuff Sack Contents: Tent body, Rain-fly, 2 longer front poles,
2 shorter rear poles, 1 spreader pole, stakes, Tent Floor Protector

Assembly Instructions

1

 Choose an appropriate site for the tent. Make sure the tent is not in a rain wash area, on a rocky surface or on an angle. Please keep the tent a good distance from any fire as even a hot ember will burn holes in tent fabric.

2

 Straighten all of the poles, making sure all of the pole sections are fully inserted.

3

 Spread the tent floor protector on the ground where you are going to erect the tent. **IMPORTANT** Failing to use the ground cloth may result in costly damage to the tent.

4

 Unroll the tent and remove the rain-fly on top of the tent floor protector. In the case of high winds, stake down a corner of the tent facing into the wind.

5

 Put the rain-fly, the single layered piece of fabric, aside and spread out the tent. Place the tapered end of a front leg section through diagonal hole in plastic junction tube marked ?Top Right Front?. Note: there is a ?Front? and ?Rear? plastic junction tube tied to the tent.

6

 Place the other end of the front leg section into the first grommet (small metal ring) on the web at the floor corner. Repeat this with other front leg.

7

 Repeat steps 5 and 6 with rear of tent. Spread the two triangle shapes apart with the spanner pole (identifiable by two tapered ends, the other poles have 1 tapered end and one grommet fitting end) inserting each end in the center holes of the plastic junction tubes.

8

 Connect the tent to the frame by pulling the black shock-cord loops at each peak over the plastic junction tube. Snap the tent clips to poles at front and back as well. Stake out four corners at this time with the provided stakes.

9

 Place the rain-fly over the tent lining up the rain-fly door with the tent door. Connect 4 corner buckles to the tent.

10

 Pull out the front vestibule and stake down using the two loops at the bottom of the fly. Also stake out the sides of the tent, but first connect the fly to the tent using the small loop and snap on the underside of the fly in the middle of the tent sides.

11

 Tuck the excess tent floor protector beneath the tent. (If tent is resting on a rock area, rope tied to rocks will also serve the same purpose as the stakes.) **It is important that the tent floor protector is completely beneath the tent because in the event of rain, any portion sticking out will gather water and channel it under the middle of the tent.

12

 Take down the tent in the reverse order that it was put up. With the tent flat on the ground, fold the body into quarters, lay the rain-fly on top of the folded body, making it match the width of the tent. Fold the long rectangle in half the long way, place the poles at the creased end, and role tightly so that it will fit in the stuff sack.
