A Celebration of the Human Spirit

The Art & Artifacts of Arizona's World War II Relocation Camps

(posted 5/22/06)

August 11, 2006 is the LAST DAY of the exhibit

 

“A Celebration of the Human Spirit: The Art & Artifacts of Arizona's World War II Relocation Camps" will run May 22nd through August 11th during normal library hours in the Charles T. Hayden Library Rotunda, Arizona State University, Tempe campus.


Featuring rarely seen art, photographic images and objects from the archives of the Arizona Historical Foundation and ASU Libraries Archives & Special Collections documenting the Japanese American experience at the Gila River and Poston relocation camps.


  • 1942 film of the construction of the Poston Camp
  • Exquisite watercolors by the artist Kakunen Tsuruoka
  • Photographic images depicting camp life
  • Diaries, scrapbooks, letters, newspapers
  • Special objects on loan from the Japanese American in Arizona Oral History Project (JAAZ)

This exhibit is inspired by the grace, beauty, and endurance of those interned in Arizona from 1942-1945.


Download the Library Channel Interview (MP3 Audio) and listen to Fred McIlvain interview Linda Whitaker, of the Arizona Historical Foundation, about the exibit.


Exhibits in ASU Libraries reflect the scholarly, historical, social, and cultural concerns of Arizona State University and its community. If you would like to propose an exhibit, please visit the Exhibits Policy page or contact Katherine O'Clair, Exhibits Committee Chair at 480-965-5964 or katherine.oclair@asu.edu.
