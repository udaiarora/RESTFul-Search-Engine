USI 601–05: Eating Disorders Program

Purpose

To provide appropriate services for ASU students with eating disorders

Source

University policy

Policy

The Eating Disorders Program at ASU consists of an interdisciplinary team of psychologists, counselors, social workers, psychiatrists, general physicians, and a nutritionist. The program is offered at the ASU Tempe campus and is coordinated by staff members from Counseling and Consultation, although cases may be referred from any ASU campus.

Counseling centers at all campuses provide outreach and prevention programming for eating disorders. Faculty members, Residential Life personnel, Intercollegiate Athletics, Greek Life, and other campus groups may request presentations on issues related to disordered eating, dieting, body image, and related stress. These presentations educate students and university groups about eating disorders, the referral process, healthy eating, and body image.

See the counseling centers’ Web pages for detailed information on services provided at Counseling and Consultation at the Tempe campus, the Student Health Service Counseling Center at the West campus, and Student Counseling Services at the Polytechnic campus. For information about counseling and other health services at the Downtown Phoenix campus, see the ASU Health Center Web site.
