Minutes of Meeting


February 19, 2003
Hayden C6A

Meeting was called to order at 3:05 pm.

A quorum was present.

Chair: Julie Tharp

Secretary: Sheila Young

I. Approval of Minutes (December 18, 2002)

 

The minutes of the December 18, 2002 Librarians’ Council were approved as written.

 

II. Chair & Executive Committee's Report- No report

 

III. Dean's Report

 

The library’s strategic planning retreat was held in January. The attendees worked on identifying goals, timelines, and measuring outcomes. Individual goals of the Academic Professionals will be linked to the strategic plan.

 

The Dean gave a brief update of the budget situation. There has been a loss of some funds for acquisitions and 22.4 vacant positions have been given back. It is unlikely there will be further reductions this year.

 

President Crow recently held several open sessions for faculty on campus. The Dean provided information on two items that were mentioned at one of the sessions. The first area relates to journals and a comment made by President Crow asking the faculty how many journals they need. There is no magic number and it is expected that there will not be a cancellation project unless there is no money. The second area relates to entrepreneurial behavior. The Dean explained that the library is a cost center.

 

The topic of the library being open 24/7 has resurfaced. In response to a previous request for 24/7 hours, the library had submitted an estimate that included adding an additional shift with services. More recently, in a survey related to the tuition increase, the students indicated that they wanted a 24/7 library hours. This request is on the students’ wish list.

 

A question was asked concerning the relationship of the International Digital Library for the Humanities. The Dean gave a brief background on this endeavor, which involves a proposal for an NSF grant. This effort, led by Gary Keller, of the Hispanic Research Center would have a focus on Colonial America, including Spain and Mexico. The part that the library would be involved with is: usability, organization of digital documents, XML, and a thesaurus of Spanish Colonial Times. The grants are due in April and the decision will be made in the summer.

 

Sherrie reported that she has been appointed to two new committees. The first is Responsibility Centered Budgeting. This committee determines how tuition and state money is divided and then determines what comes off the top. Funds for the library come off the top. The second committee is Partners with Industry, which will look at partnerships with business.

 

The Dean also reported that the Provost has been asked why there is a need for a University Librarian. The Provost responded that the President wanted someone responsible for what all the libraries do.  In addition, the libraries have always worked together and it makes sense that the University Librarian is on the main campus.

 

IV. Committee Reports

 

a. Personnel Committee – Rob Spindler

1.      Librarians’ Handbook Section G.1.2- The proposed changes will be presented under old business.

2.      Librarians’ Handbook Section G.1.2- The Personnel Committee met with the Collection Development Coordinators to review the mandatory supplemental evaluation for collection development and will draft changes based on that discussion.

3.      Librarians’ Handbook Section G.2.2- The Personnel Committee is reviewing the requirement for the Review Committee to notify an AP in the case of a positive recommendation for promotion.

4.      Marion Librarian Packet- The CV for the packet for promotion to full librarian is completed. The committee is working on the cover letter, which will be reviewed by the Executive Committee and the Review Committee.

5.      Librarians’ Handbook Sections J and K- The committee is in a fact-finding phase for improvement or revisions of these two sections.

b. Review Committee – Marcia Anderson

– No report

c. Research and Professional Development Committee – Lucy Lyons

There is a small amount of research money available. There has been one applicant.

d. Compensation Committee - Lois Schneberger

Proposed changes to the Librarians’ Handbook will be presented under old business.

e. Academic Senate – JoAnn Mulvihill

– No report

f. Committee on Academic Professional Status – Sue Hunter

The CAPS spring Spotlight Speaker will be Vice Provost Gail Hackett on May 7th.

g. Web Master – Yuwu Song (excused, Scott substituting)

There was a request to increase the size of the font on the Librarians’ Council web page. Scott will convey this to Song.

 

V. Old Business

 

a.   Personnel Committee:

Revision to Librarians’ Handbook Section G.1.2: Supplementary Evaluation: There was no discussion. A vote was cast and the proposed changes were accepted.

b. Research and Professional Development Committee

Revision to Librarians’ Handbook Section H.1.2: Travel Fund Procedures: There was no discussion. A vote was cast and the proposed changes were accepted.

c.   Compensation Committee

Revision to Librarians’ Handbook Section C.2.5: Compensation Committee and Revision to Librarians’ Handbook Section B: Bylaws of Librarians’ Council:

A question was asked as to why the committee recommended the reduction in number. The reply from the committee chair was that five members are not needed to carry on the work of the committee. A vote was cast, and the changes were accepted.

 

VI. New Business

 

There was no new business.

 

The meeting was adjourned at 3:40 pm.

 

Sheila Young

Secretary
