United States

Bad Environmental Policies, Hinder Poverty Reduction
IPP Media
World leaders have been urged to focus on the sustainable and friendly use of the environment and resources as a new alternative to reducing poverty.

 

Business Roundtable Sets Sustainable Growth Plan
Industry Week
Capitalizing on corporate social responsibility efforts already underway, the Business Roundtable on September 21 launched a sustainable growth initiative to further encourage U.S. companies and their executives to "measurably" improve society, the environment and the economy.

 

Katrina's Environment Threat Not Over, Greens Say
Environmental News Network

As the floodwaters recede along the Gulf Coast in Katrina's wake and initial environmental testing indicates less damage than feared, environmentalists warn that the worst effects may not yet be revealed.

 

More U.S. Corporations Weighing Climate Risk

GreenBiz.com

More U.S. corporations than ever before now factor climate change into the risks and opportunities faced by their businesses, according to a new report by the Carbon Disclosure Project, a coalition of institutional investors with more than $21 trillion in assets.

 

Survey Shows Voters Care about Environment, But Not Necessarily at the Ballot Box

Environmental News Network
Voters care about the environment in theory, but not necessarily in practice, suggests a survey released this week. Conducted for Duke University 's Nicholas Institute for Environmental Policy Solutions, the survey uncovered significant gaps between voters' general feelings toward protecting the environment and their election day behavior.

 

Top Ten Green Building Questions
Earth Vision
Introducing green-building guru Eric Corey Freed, whose "Ask the Green Architect" column will appear in this space each month. We're kicking things off by covering the basics -- Freed's top ten frequently asked questions. Next we'll need to hear from you.

 

U.S. Government Taps Cornell for Green Research with $8 Million Grant
Earth Vision
Cornell University has been awarded more than $8.2 million in federal funding over four years as one of five Sun Grant Centers of Excellence -- regional hubs that will take the lead in researching green technologies.

 

U.S. Green Building Council Targets Climate Emissions from School Buildings
Earth Vision

The U.S. Green Building Council (USGBC), along with the Turner Corporation and Haverford College, has announced a series of commitments aimed at transforming the way buildings in schools across the United States are designed and operated in order to reduce their energy consumption, environmental impact, and greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions.
