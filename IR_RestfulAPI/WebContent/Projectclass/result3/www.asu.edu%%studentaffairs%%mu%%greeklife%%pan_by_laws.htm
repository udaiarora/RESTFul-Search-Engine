Panhellenic Council - By-Laws

(Revised November 2002)

ARTICLE I. FINANCE

Section 1 - FISCAL YEAR
The fiscal year of the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University shall be from January 1 to December 31 inclusive.
Section 2 - CONTRACTS
All contracts require the signature of the President and Vice President Administration and must be acknowledged by the Panhellenic Advisor and the Executive Coordinator of Greek Life to bind the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University.
Section 3 - CHECKS
All checks issued on behalf of the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University shall be signed by either of the following: the Vice President of Administration or Panhellenic Advisor.
Section 4 - PAYMENTS
All payments due to the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University shall be made to the Vice President of Administration who shall record and deposit them. Checks for payments shall be made payable to the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University.
Section 5 - MEMBERSHIP DUES
  1. The dues of each Panhellenic member fraternity shall be an assessment per active and new member. The amount of such dues shall be five dollars per semester per initiated member and eight dollars and fifty cents per new member.
  2. The dues of each Panhellenic Council member fraternity shall be payable at or before the fifth regular meeting of each semester. Following this, chapters that have not paid will be given a one-week grace period. After this grace period, a one-time late fee of twenty dollars will be assessed for late payment.
Section 6 - EXPENDITURES
Prior notification of all expenditures of Panhellenic moneys shall be given to the Panhellenic Vice President of Administration.
Section 7 - BUDGET
The budget of the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State is open for the review of all Panhellenic members and chapters.

ARTICLE II. SELECTION OF OFFICERS

Section 1 - PROCESS
  • The five Executive officers shall be elected by a majority vote of the member chapters prior to the end of the fall semester. Officer transitions shall be completed no later than the first week of the spring semester.
  • In the case of a tie vote, the chapter delegates will reconvene to conduct a tie-breaking session. In the case of a further tie, the outgoing President will cast the deciding vote.
  • Applicants running for an Executive position must have held an office on Panhellenic or in their own chapter, been a Panhellenic Assistant, been a Panhellenic delegate, or been an Order of Alpha panel member for at least one year.
  • The candidates for Executive office who are not elected to their first preference have the option of running for other Executive positions. The order of elections shall be: President, Vice President of Judicial Affairs, Vice President of Membership, Vice President of Administration, and Vice President of Development

ARTICLE III. OFFICER DUTIES

Section 1 - THE PRESIDENT SHALL
  • Have overall responsibility of the operation of the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University.
  • Call and preside at all regular and special meetings of the Panhellenic Council and the Panhellenic Executive Board at Arizona State University.
  • Serve as ex-officio member of all Panhellenic Council Committees.
  • Maintain a complete and up-to-date President's file which will include current copies of the following items: the Panhellenic Council Constitution and Bylaws, Recruitment Rules, Panhellenic budget, National Panhellenic Conference Manual of Information and related materials, correspondence and material received from the National Panhellenic Conference Area Advisor, copies of the College Panhellenic Reports to the Area Advisor and other pertinent material.
  • Meet weekly with the Panhellenic Advisor.
  • Transition her successor.
  • Hold regular chapter Presidents meetings, no less than one (1) per month.
  • Be responsible for an all-chapter Presidents' retreat each semester.
  • Assist the Vice President of Membership as Recruitment Staff during Formal Recruitment.
  • Perform all other duties not otherwise restricted or prohibited by these Bylaws and/or the Constitution.
Section 2 - THE VICE PRESIDENT OF JUDICIAL AFFAIRS SHALL
  • Perform the duties of the President in her absence, inability to serve, or at her call.
  • Serve as chairperson of the Order of Alpha Judicial Board.
  • Have overall responsibility for her assistant.
  • Meet regularly with the Panhellenic Advisor.
  • Organize a bi-annual risk management seminar.
  • Transition her successor.
  • Assist the Vice President of Membership as Recruitment Staff during Formal Recruitment.
Section 3 - THE VICE PRESIDENT OF MEMBERSHIP SHALL
  • Be responsible for all Panhellenic membership activities, which include: Expansion, Fall Recruitment, Spring Recruitment, Continuous Open Bidding, and Orientation week in the Fall.
  • Have overall responsibility for her assistant
  • Select the Head Rho Chis and Rho Chis.
  • Be in charge of any expansion at ASU, such responsibilities include:
    • Coordinate all expansion activities including National Panhellenic Conference visits and presentations.
    • Be responsible for contact between the ASU Panhellenic and national sorority offices in such a way as to establish interaction that would offer support and assistance to any invited NPC group or associate member group.
  • Transition her successor.
Section 4 - VICE PRESIDENT OF ADMINISTRATION
  • Be responsible for the preparation of each semester's budget by the sixth meeting of each semester and following its approval by the Panhellenic Council, for providing a copy to each member fraternity, the Panhellenic Advisor and the Executive Coordinator of Greek Life.
  • Collect all amounts due to the Panhellenic Council, collect membership dues, and issue receipts.
  • Be responsible for the prompt payment of all debts incurred by the Panhellenic Council.
  • Maintain current financial records. Give a monthly financial report at regular meetings of the Panhellenic Council.
  • Keep an up-to-date roll of the members of Panhellenic Council and call on each at all Council meetings.
  • Keep full minutes of all meetings of the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University and distribute the minutes of these meetings to Panhellenic Delegates and Executives, Executive Coordinator of Greek Life, Fraternity Advisor, National Panhellenic Area Advisor, and Panhellenic Advisor.
  • Maintain a complete and up-to-date file which will include minutes of the meetings of the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University from the date of its organization; copies of all contracts made by the Panhellenic Council; and current correspondence for two full years.
  • Within a reasonable time frame, complete for the Panhellenic Council:
    • Phone lists of the executive members, delegates, cabinet members, chapter presidents, and chapter executive officers.
    • Secure all rooms for weekly Panhellenic meetings.
  • Have overall responsibility for her assistant.
  • Transition her successor.
  • Assist the Vice President of Membership as Recruitment Staff during Formal Recruitment.
Section 5 - THE VICE PRESIDENT OF DEVELOPMENT SHALL
  • Have overall responsibility for her assistant.
  • Oversee the transition of all newly elected and appointed officers.
  • Promote the Greek community to the campus and community which includes: publicizing events which promote Panhellenic, the Greek Community or any special event a sorority may be facilitating
  • Produce a Panhellenic and Cabinet officer description pamphlet.
  • Be responsible for the applications associated with all Panhellenic positions.
  • Transition her successor.
  • Assist the Vice President of Membership as Recruitment Staff during Formal Recruitment.

ARTICLE IV. THE EXECUTIVE BOARD

Section 1 - DUTIES
  • Administer routine business meetings of the Panhellenic Council as appropriate and such other business as has been previously approved for action by Panhellenic Council vote.
  • Meet once a week before the regular meeting of the Panhellenic Council to discuss the agenda and current activities of Panhellenic.
  • Maintain the standards under which they were elected, including a 2.50 grade point average semester and cumulative. If these standards are not fulfilled, an officer will automatically be removed from office with no probationary period.

ARTICLE VI. ASSISTANT OFFICERS

Section 1 - SELECTION
  • All assistant officers will be appointed by the incoming Panhellenic Executive Officers.
Section 2 - ASSISTANT TO VICE PRESIDENT OF JUDICIAL AFFAIRS SHALL
  • Attend all weekly Panhellenic Council meetings.
  • Hold meetings with the chapter Risk Management chairs.
  • Schedule one Risk Management seminar each semester.
  • Transition her successor.
  • Attend all Order of Alpha meetings, training sessions, and hearings.
  • Act as Secretary of Order of Alpha hearings.
  • Assist the Vice President of Judicial Affairs.
Section 3 - ASSISTANT TO VICE PRESIDENT OF MEMBERSHIP SHALL
  • Attend weekly Panhellenic Council meetings.
  • Assist the Vice President of Membership.
  • Transition her successor.
Section 4 - ASSISTANT TO VICE PRESIDENT OF ADMINISTRATION SHALL
  • Attend weekly Panhellenic Council meetings.
  • Assist the Vice President of Administration.
  • Transition her successor.
Section 5 - ASSISTANT TO VICE PRESIDENT OF DEVELOPMENT SHALL
  • Attend weekly Panhellenic Council meetings.
  • Assist the Vice President of Development.
  • Be in charge of educational and resource programs (i.e. speakers, workshops) for Panhellenic and the entire Greek community in conjunction with other ASU organizations and departments.
  • Provide information on and coordinate community service opportunities for Panhellenic and its members.
  • Be responsible for informing Panhellenic delegates of any opportunities for membership in campus organizations and the application dates that apply.
  • Inform Panhellenic Council of campus events and resources.
  • Transition her successor.

ARTICLE VII. RECRUITMENT STAFF

Section 1 - RECRUITMENT STAFF SHALL
  • Consist of Panhellenic Executive Officers.
  • Disaffiliate during Fall Recruitment.
Section 2 - HEAD RECRUITMENT COUNSELORS SHALL
  • Assist the Vice President of Membership, Recruitment Staff, and the Panhellenic Advisor during formal recruitment.
  • Disaffiliate during Fall Recruitment along with the rest of the Recruitment Counselors.

ARTICLE VIII. ORDER OF ALPHA

Section 1 - ORDER OF ALPHA JUDICIAL BOARD
MEMBERSHIP
  • The Judicial Board shall consist of twenty five members: The Panhellenic Vice President of Judicial Affairs (chairwoman), the Panhellenic Advisor (ex-officio member), the Order of Alpha Secretary/assistant, and eleven chapter representatives; consisting of one representative from each Panhellenic Chapter officially recognized on the Arizona State University campus.
  • The Vice President of Judicial Affairs will appoint a secretary of her choosing to record the official written and recorded minutes of the hearing. The secretary will have no voice or vote on the board. The secretary will not be present during deliberations, except at the request of the chairwoman to review minutes. All aspects of confidentiality the members of the board uphold also bind the secretary.
SELECTION
The Vice President of Judicial Affairs, the Panhellenic Advisor and Panhellenic President shall select the eleven delegates after receiving applications and conducting interviews.
REQUIREMENTS
The delegates must have a 2.00 cumulative grade point average at the time of selection and must maintain a 2.00 cumulative grade point average to remain on the panel. The delegates are required to be at every training session and all scheduled meetings to remain on the panel.
TERM OF OFFICE
A member shall serve on the board throughout her college years during which she is an active member, in good standing, of her chapter.
DUTIES
Meet when necessary to establish guidelines, settle disputes and infractions when in violation of State, University, Panhellenic Council Constitution, Bylaws, rules or regulations.
HEARINGS
  • A group of five (5) members shall be necessary to hold a hearing.
  • Any member of the Judicial Board whose respective chapter is involved in a case brought before Order of Alpha shall immediately remover herself as a member of the board for the duration of that particular case or hearing.
  • The Chair of the Board shall immediately remove herself from the Board for the duration of a case involving her respective chapter or in cases where she deems herself biased and shall yield her position to another voting member of the board when necessary.
  • The Panhellenic Advisor shall serve as an ex-officio, non-voting member of Order of Alpha.
Section 2 - CONDUCT POLICIES
  • Any action by a woman or women casting an unfavorable reflection on one sorority or the fraternity system as a whole shall be considered by the Panhellenic Council to be sufficient reasons for a conduct case. All conduct cases shall come under the heading of one, or any combinations of the following:
    • Violations of State or University regulations
    • Violations of National Panhellenic Conference resolutions
    • Violations of Arizona State University Panhellenic Council Constitution, Bylaws, rules or regulations
Individual members will hold chapters accountable for said violations. Any violation of the Arizona State University Code of Conduct will be referred to the Judicial Affairs Office of Arizona State University.
Section 3 - COMPLAINTS
Cases brought before Order of Alpha must be submitted in writing to the Chairwoman and can be referred by any student, faculty, or staff member of Arizona State University. Anyone filing a report should be prepared to present such evidence or testimony at the time of the official hearing, however, it is not required. The report (complaint) must be presented to the President of the Panhellenic Council within 24 hours or on the first school day after the alleged infraction is known and not more than 10 school days after it has occurred.
Section 4 - PROCEDURES
Order of Alpha shall adhere to the following procedures in hearing or reviewing cases:
PRE-CASE
Written notification for a sorority to appear before Order of Alpha shall be personally delivered to the Sorority President by the Vice President of Judicial Affairs or the designated person at least forty-eight hours prior to the scheduled hearing. The sorority shall be informed, in writing of the reason for their request to appear. A hearing shall take place when sufficient evidence is present to take definitive action and shall carry some penalty or warning stated in Section 5. The Panhellenic Vice President of Judicial Affairs will then contact the respective sorority advisor about the scheduled hearing. ANY INQUIRY SHALL TAKE PLACE WHEN INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE IS CURRENTLY AVAILABLE BUT STRONG ACCUSATIONS HAVE BEEN MADE. NO PENALTY MAY OCCUR; HOWEVER, WARNINGS AGAINST FURTHER INCIDENTS MAY BE MADE.
CASE
  • Anyone may attend the Order of Alpha hearing if she/he is directly or indirectly involved in the point of discussion with the reservation that they must leave if asked to do so by the Order of Alpha Chairwoman.
  • A secretary shall be appointed by the Order of Alpha Chairwoman to take official minutes.
  • A tape recorder will be present during the hearing in case of an appeal.
POST-CASE
The decision of Order of Alpha shall be rendered, in writing, within a period of twenty-four hours and shall be filed with the Panhellenic Advisor with copies of the hearing sent to the chapter's alumni advisor, NPC Delegate, National President and the NPC Area Advisor.
Section 5 - PENALTIES AND WARNINGS
The following are the penalties, which may be invoked by Order of Alpha, which do not exceed the limits as stated in the National Panhellenic Council Unanimous Agreements:
  • Warnings may be given to those sororities involved in a first offense violation within a one year time period.
  • Monetary fines, not in excess of $25.00 per member, to be paid within a period of sixty days from the original decision.
  • Community service, not in excess of three hours per member, to be completed within a period of sixty days from the official decision.
  • Educational programming to be planned, organized, and completed within a period of sixty days from the official decision to be approved and coordinated through the Panhellenic Council and Panhellenic Advisor.
  • Panhellenic Council probation for a period of one month to one year that may include any combination of the following:
    • Restricted social functions.
    • Denial of intramural points.
    • Limited involvement of members appointed to Panhellenic Boards or committees.
    • Recruitment limitations in accordance with the Panhellenic Recruitment Guidelines.
    • Denial of recognition for all sorority achievement awards and trophies.
    • Any other appropriate sanction that fits the violation.
  • Recommendation to the Office of Student Life that a chapter be put on University probation.
  • Recommendation to the Vice President for Student Affairs that University recognition of the chapter be rescinded.
Section 6 - APPEALS
Any individual and/or sorority can appeal a decision of Order of Alpha. The procedures are as follows:
PROCEDURE FOR APPEAL
  • All decisions may be appealed to the Panhellenic Appeals Committee, consisting of the Executive Coordinator of Greek Life and the Panhellenic President, in writing within a period of three class days from the time of notification of the decision of Order of Alpha.
  • The Executive Coordinator of Greek Life will notify the Chapter President and advisor at least forty-eight hours prior to the scheduled appeal hearing.
  • Penalties of Order of Alpha are not in effect during the appeal process.
CONDITIONS FOR APPEAL
Appeal of the Order of Alpha decision shall be accepted only if one or any combination of the following conditions exists:
  • Improper procedure was followed by Order of Alpha.
  • Proper due process was denied.
  • Further evidence has been found since the case was heard.
  • Sanctions do not fit the violation.
ACTION FOR APPEAL
An appeal can:
  • Void the decision rendered by Order of Alpha.
  • Modify or uphold the decision of Order of Alpha.
Section 7 - GRIEVANCES
Order of Alpha shall also be an advisory outlet for the Panhellenic Community. It may be used by any sorority to listen to concerns or as an arbitrator during an inter-sorority dispute, as recommended by representatives of all parties involved, members of Panhellenic Executive Council, or the Panhellenic Advisor.

ARTICLE IX. PHILANTHROPIC

Philanthropy and service are cornerstones of the fraternity and sorority experience. In order to highlight the positive aspects of philanthropy and service, they shall not include any events that show a lack of respect, degrade, or demean women, men or other chapters. Disrespectful, demeaning and degrading behavior will be defined by the respective judicial board upon receipt of a complaint from any individual and the appropriate follow-up and/or sanctions will occur.
Section 1
Fraternities and sororities must schedule and turn in a proposal including desired dates and locations for their philanthropic event by a date determined by the Council Executive Officers (i.e. Panhellenic, IFC, HGC, NPHC) the semester prior to the event occurring. Approval from and contact information for the Chapter Advisor and/or House Corporation Board President must be included in the proposal.
Section 2
Chapter philanthropic activities that include the involvement of other chapters, student groups, or teams are limited to 2 days.
Section 3
All Philanthropic team entry fees are set at a rate of $2 per person of participating chapter (based on chapter roster on file with Greek Life and/or SORC), not to exceed $100. All checks must be written directly to the designated charity.
Section 4
Teams representing Greek chapters must have participants reflective of their entire chapter membership and cannot be limited to new members.
Section 5
House decorating and/or banner hanging is not allowed given that it does not benefit the charity in any way.
Section 6
Absolutely NO alcohol is allowed at any philanthropic event and events may not be held at any establishment that serves alcohol.
Section 7
Philanthropy participants/hosts are expected to adhere to all chapter and council rules, bylaws and policies as well as those of ASU and the State of Arizona.
Section 8
Special consideration can be made if submitted in writing at least 30 days in advance to the involved chapters and/or councils.
Section 9
It is the responsibility of the entire Greek community to ensure that the philanthropic and other bylaws are being followed. Witnesses are responsible for submitting complaints to the appropriate judicial board and/or the Dean of Judicial Affairs within 10 days of the violation occurring.

ARTICLE XII. ADMINISTRATION OF MEMBERSHIP SELECTION

Section 1
The National Panhellenic Conference quota-total system shall be followed.
Section 2
The preferential bidding system shall be used.
Section 3
Except during formal Fall Recruitment, continuous open bidding shall be in effect during the academic year (fall thorough spring) for all eligible female students, in accordance with the National Panhellenic Council Unanimous Agreements.
Section 4
If a chapter has not filled quota during formal Fall Recruitment, they may do so in continuous open bidding even though it puts them over total.
Section 5
Every regularly enrolled new member, initiate, or affiliate of a chapter shall be counted in the Chapter total.
  • A list of new member, initiated, and affiliated members shall be filed with the Panhellenic Advisor two weeks following the completion of formal recruitment.
  • Any depledging, termination, or other change in membership shall be reported to the President of the Panhellenic Council and the Panhellenic Advisor no later than twenty-four hours after it has occurred.
Section 6
Final bid matching following preference shall be conducted and attended exclusively by alumnae members of the chapters and the Panhellenic Advisor.
Section 7
Refer to the Recruitment Rules for further legislation.
Section 8
Each chapter must turn their C.O.B. cards in to the Sorority Advisor within twenty-four hours of the bid acceptance.

ARTICLE XIII. NEW MEMBERSHIP AND INITIATION

Section 1
All forms of hazing are banned.
Section 2 - DEFINITION
Hazing is defined as any action taken or situation created, intentionally, whether on or off fraternity premises, by a group, organization, or member of that group or organization in which members or prospective members are subjected to activities which harass, intimidate, physically exhaust, impart pain, humiliate, cause undue mental fatigue or distress, or which may cause mutilation or laceration of the body or parts of the body.
Section 3 - EXAMPLES
Such activities or situations include but are not limited to: paddling of any form; advocating substance abuse; tests of endurance; submission of members or prospective members to potentially dangerous or hazardous circumstances; activities which have a foreseeable potential for resulting in personal injury; creation of excessive fatigue, mental distress, panic, human degradation or public embarrassment; physical and psychological shocks; wearing publicly, apparel which is conspicuous and not normally in good taste; engaging in public stunts and jokes; morally degrading or humiliating games and activities; late-night sessions which interfere with scholastic activities; and any other activities which are not consistent with the regulations and policies of Arizona State University.
Section 4
Recognized organizations and groups shall be permitted certain initiation ceremonies and activities, which when examined by the average University student, participation would seem reasonable under the circumstances and justified in the view of purpose for which they are conducted.
Section 5
New member activities must not interfere with academic, personal, or employment responsibilities.
Section 6
Each chapter must submit a copy of their new member program to the Panhellenic Advisor by the last week in September of the Fall semester.

ARTICLE XIV. VOTING

Section 1
New business may be voted on during the same Panhellenic Council meeting in which it was introduced, unless there is a motion that the issue be tabled and voted on at the next scheduled meeting.
  • Exception: Motions regarding philanthropic events, by-law revisions, or constitutional amendments must be tabled and voted on at the next scheduled meeting.
  • In the case where the timing of the business does not permit votes to wait until the next regularly scheduled meeting, the Panhellenic Council can vote to allow chapter votes to be called into the Panhellenic President within 24 hours of the Panhellenic Council meeting.
Section 2
Tiebreakers shall follow the procedures as set forth in Article II, Selection of Officers.

ARTICLE XV. RULES OF ORDER

Section 1
The Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University shall be governed by Robert's Rules of Order newly revised except in matters otherwise provided for in the Panhellenic Constitution, Bylaws, regulations and rules.

ARTICLE XVI. SCHOLARSHIP

Section 1
Each chapter must submit a roster of their members for GPA ranking in addition to the new/initiated member cards by the specified due date each semester.
Section 2
Each chapter must submit a copy of their scholarship/academic program to the Panhellenic Advisor by the last week in September.

ARTICLE XVII. CHAPTER ADMINISTRATION

Section 1
Panhellenic Chapters are required to have two delegates attend all Panhellenic Council general and special meetings.
Section 2
Panhellenic Chapters are required to:
  1. Register as student organizations with the Student Organization Resource Center. Failure to be a registered student organization will void the chapter from participation in Panhellenic sponsored events, including formal recruitment.
  2. Have new member cards on file within a week following Fall and Spring Recruitment.
  3. Attend at or participate in one campus programs with 80% attendance of their members.
  4. Attend one risk management program per semester with 80% attendance of their members.
  5. Attend one educational program with 80% attendance of their members.
Section 3
Submit a chapter officer list and a house roster to the Panhellenic Secretary by the specified due date of each semester.
Section 4
Each Chapter President must have at least one meeting with a member of the Greek Life Staff per semester.

ARTICLE XVIII. RISK MANAGEMENT

Section 1
Any intention of holding either a pre-party or post-party must not include the words "all sorority" in any fliers, publicity, or schedule of events. This is to avoid any conflict with sorority alcohol and insurance policies. Such activities shall not be mentioned in the general announcements at Panhellenic Council meetings, but instead invitations should be distributed at a later time.
Section 2
Chapters must submit a certificate of liability insurance to the Panhellenic Advisor no later than the second week of classes in the Fall semester.
