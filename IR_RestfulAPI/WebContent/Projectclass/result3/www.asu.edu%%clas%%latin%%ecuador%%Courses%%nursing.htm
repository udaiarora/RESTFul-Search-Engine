NUR 394/598: International Community / Public Health Nursing
Offered in association with the Center for Latin American Studies (CLAS)

 
FACULTY
Roxena Wotring, M.S., R.N.
Clinical Assistant Professor
 
Method of Instruction:
Guided study including classroom, clinical practicum, guest speakers, and discussion guided by the Community / Public Health Nursing course faculty.
  
Credit:
1-4 credit hours
Type & Course Placement:
An elective course that may satisfy elective credit in undergraduate and graduate nursing programs.
  
 
Semester Offered:
Summer, in association with the ASU Center for Latin American Studies (CLAS Field School) in Ecuador.
Catalog Description:
Theoretical and clinical application of community assessment, interventions, health education program planning and culturally competent nursing care in Ecuador.
  
Course Description:
Engage and analyze the health issues, priorities, systems (public and private), culture and disparities of the Ecuadorian Amazon communities and indigenous people. Work with master community/public health nurse faculty in the assessment, delivery and evaluation of selected health screenings, community programs and health education projects. In addition, students will have the opportunity to work with local health care providers in both private and/or public health care systems.
Course Objectives:
Upon completion of the course, students are expected to:
1. Analyze the impact of a country’s political, economic and social development on the health of its population and its health care system.
2. Identify the positive and negative impacts of ‘western allopathy’ on third world health care systems and indigenous people.
3. Describe Ecuador’s health care system in relation to local ‘folk’ beliefs and rituals, shamanism and gender/race/economic disparities.
4. Compare and Contrast the nursing education, professionalism and practices of US vs. Ecuadorian nurses and nursing.
5. Critically analyze the health needs of the Amazonian communities based on local, national, and international assessment data
6. Provide culturally competent health screenings and health education.
7. Explore the concepts of capacity-building and sustainability within the local health care context.
8. Evaluate personal and professional insights about health care practices, cultural competence, professional ethics, community/public health nursing practice and working with indigenous people and communities.
 
Content Outline:
1. International nursing
2. Cultural competence
3. Working with indigenous people and communities
4. Ecuadorian health care system and practices
5. Indigenous health beliefs, practices and practitioners
6. Community assessment theories and methods
7. Health education program planning
8. Ethics of international nursing practice
9. Methods of community outreach, capacity building, project sustainability and funding
10. Professional self reflection and renewal strategies
  
 
Course Requirements:
1. Meet all admission requirements as outlined by the Center for Latin American Studies (CLAS Field School).
2. Received all CDC recommended immunizations.
3. Co-registered for required CLAS language class.
4. Meet health and stamina requirements to live and work in a rainforest environment.
5. Fund personal travel, university and program fees
