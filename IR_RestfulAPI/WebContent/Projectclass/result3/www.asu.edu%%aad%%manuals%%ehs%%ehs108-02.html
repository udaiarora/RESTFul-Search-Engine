EHS 108–02: Defensive Driver Training

Purpose

To reduce the potential for injuries to occupants of university-owned and non-university-owned vehicles used for university business and to reduce property damage resulting from motor vehicle-related accidents

Source

Arizona Administrative Code R2–10–207

Applicability

Individuals who operate university-owned and non-university-owned motor vehicles for university business

Background

Motor vehicle accidents are among the leading causes of death on the job within the United States. Substantial losses can be sustained from medical costs, property damage, and lost productivity. By completing defensive driver training, an individual can improve driving skills and thus possibly reduce the occurrence of motor vehicle accidents.

Policy

Individuals who operate a passenger automobile while performing their duties or as approved by their departments and units are encouraged to attend a defensive driver training program. Departments and units are encouraged to require defensive driver training for individuals prior to the operation of a passenger vehicle.

Special training requirements for operators of high-occupancy vehicles used for university business can be found at EHS 108–03, “Training for Operators of High-Occupancy Vehicles.”

Procedure

To schedule a defensive driver training for individual departments and units, contact the Environmental Health & Safety at 480/965–1823. To register for training courses, please go to the Environmental Health & Safety Training Schedules and select the appropriate campus for class dates and times. If you encounter difficulties with the registration process, contact LPS@asu.edu.
