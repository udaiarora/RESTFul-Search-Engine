February 27, 2004

ASU continues building great communities, seeks volunteers

Some college students may choose to spend Spring Break soaking up the sun on a beach. Others may choose to spend an alternative Spring Break by soaking in a unique cultural experience.

ASU's Building Great Communities (BGC) is teaming up with the city of Nogales to offer an Alternative Spring Break for college students who want to volunteer March 17 - 19.

BGC, established in 1999, is based on the outcomes of a series of roundtable discussions from ASU's Morrison Institute for Public Policy's report, "What Matters in Metropolitan Phoenix." The discussions generated an effort to coordinate projects that promote university-community alliances and address complex issues such as the economy, the environment, social spending, communication and quality of life.

Most of BGC's focus has been directed toward the Phoenix area, but the program has expanded its efforts to work with border cities, including Yuma in 2003.

Barbara Shaw-Snyder, director of BGC community outreach, says, "The biggest rewards for BGC are watching students, faculty and community folks come together and utilize their own unique talents and experiences to address community needs."

This year, BGC's project will be working with the citizens on both sides of the U.S.-Mexico border, in Nogales, Ariz, March 17 - 19.

On March 18 volunteers will visit Casa de Miseriocordia, in Nogales, Sonora, which was a Catholic church that provided meals to children who lived in the neighborhood. The old church, which was purchased by Borderlinks, a not-for-profit organization, now serves as a community center that provides meals and includes a learning center and daycare center. Volunteers will work with college students from Instituto Tecnologico de Sonora to help paint the day care center, interact with the children and help prepare food for visitors.

On March 19, volunteers will work with Crossroads Nogales Mission, in Nogales. This shelter has been serving the community since 1995, providing food and shelter for the homeless. Volunteers will help prepare and serve food.

BGC is recruiting 30 volunteers, who are interested in experiencing a cultural and rewarding experience by doing community service during their Spring Break. Volunteers must be committed to making a difference and must demonstrate an interest in learning about the Mexican culture and people. This opportunity is open to ASU students of all ages, who are U.S. citizens or have their visas and documents available.

Volunteers will be provided accommodations along with three meals each day. Volunteers are asked to bring comfortable clothing, a sleeping bag, a pillow and their own toiletries.

"The alternative Spring Break in Nogales offers students a unique experience to gain cultural diversity and interact with college students from Nogales, Sonora," says Miguel Cano, BGC student worker. "Quite simply it offers a productive alternative during spring break at no cost."

Students interested in participating in ASU's Alternative Spring Break project, can contact Cano, student worker/coordinator, at (602) 696-8124, (swankroc@hotmail.com).

For information about Building Great Communities, located at ASU's Downtown Campus, visit the Web at (http://www.asu.edu/xed/asudt/community.html).
