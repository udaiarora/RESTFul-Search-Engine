Academic Professionals Task Force Final Report

The Academic Professionals Task Force was appointed in September 2003 to examine the AP ranks with an eye towards strengthening and improving the promotion and continuing status process as well as working conditions for academic professionals. The overall purpose of this Task Force is to review the ASU Personnel Policies and Procedures governing Academic Professionals and make recommendations about needed changes (see Charge).

The Task Force was composed of academic professionals, faculty members, academic senate representatives, and administrators from East, West, and Main campuses (see Roster).   The Task Force was charged by the University Provost. Task force members, along with academic professionals across the university, also met with President Crow to discuss issues related to its charge.   A draft report was developed and disseminated to the university community in March 2004; feedback was solicited and the Task Force revised and finalized its report, which is posted below.

The Task Force makes a series of recommendations that address performance expectations and evaluation procedures for academic professionals. The most substantive recommendations are the creation of a multi-year appointment option, the creation of an all-university promotion and continuing status committee that will evaluate AP’s from all ASU campuses, and the institution of a post-continuing status review. These recommendations have been made to clarify the status of AP’s at ASU and to provide more flexible employment options for both the institution and for academic professionals.

The Provost of the University would now like to solicit input from the university community before making his recommendations; ultimately the report and the Provost’s recommendations will be sent to the Academic Senates for review.   Academic professionals, as well as ASU faculty, administrators, and staff, are encouraged to review the final report of the AP Task Force, posted below, and submit comments and suggestions online, via email to the Provost at recommendations@asu.edu, or via hard copy to Office of the Executive Vice President & Provost of the University, Mail Code 2803. Please send your comments by September 1, 2004.

After university community feedback is received, the Provost will send his official recommendations to the Academic Senate in late September 2004.
