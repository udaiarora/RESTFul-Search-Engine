March 10, 2005

Sustainability workshop adds to ASU’s prestige in field

A recent workshop of the International Human Dimensions of Global Environmental Change Program (IHDP) brought some of the world’s leaders in sustainability to Tempe Feb. 8 – 12.

IHDP is a leading international science organization for social science research in the area of environmental studies, sustainability and global change.

Twenty-five people attended the meeting, which included researchers from Canada, the United Kingdom, Germany, the Netherlands, the Philippines, India , Chile and the United States. The meeting will help IHDP prepare a multiple-year “road map” for its efforts, and it brought international focus to ASU’s growing strength in the areas of local and global sustainability. It also helped ASU researchers intensify contact with leaders in this interdisciplinary field.

“This is a very distinguished group of international scientists who have been engaged in how humans are organizing themselves to address global climate change,” says Charles Redman, director of ASU’s International Institute for Sustainability and co-director of the Central Arizona-Phoenix Long Term Ecological Research Project. “This includes hundreds of researchers from around the world who are working in the trenches of sustainability.”

“The workshop is an important component of IHDP’s mid-term review process that will culminate in the Sixth Open Science Meeting, in October in Bonn, Germany,” says Sander van der Leeuw, chair of ASU’s anthropology department and organizer of the meeting. “The goal of the workshop is to identify key issues for research and priorities for the program’s development.”

This is the first time ASU’s new International Institute for Sustainability played host to such an eminent group of scholars from all over the world and from different disciplines.

“It is an important opportunity for ASU to start wider collaborations with an international program coordinating sustainability research on a global level,” adds van der Leeuw, who has been a member of the IHDP scientific committee since 2004.

Redman said that for ASU the meeting was essential in meeting key people who are working in similar and associated areas as those within ASU’s sustainability institute.

“We are building networks of collaborators to improve our own expertise and that of our students, and to contribute on large-scale initiatives,” Redman says.
