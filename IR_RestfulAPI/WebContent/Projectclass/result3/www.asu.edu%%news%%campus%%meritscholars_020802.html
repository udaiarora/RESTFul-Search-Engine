February 8, 2002

ASU has high number of Merit Scholars; admissions applications increase

ASU has the sixth highest number of freshmen 2001 merit scholars of all public universities in the United States, according to a report published by the National Merit Scholarship Corporation. Last fall 115 of these top scholars enrolled at ASU.

Students who receive merit scholarships are among the top scorers on the PSAT exam, representing about one-half of one percent of the nation's high school seniors. The 8,229 merit scholars in this year's class are enrolled in 371 colleges and universities.

The top five public universities chosen by the students were University of Texas at Austin with 233; University of California, Berkeley with 231; Texas A & M with 171; University of Florida with 158; and University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, with 145.

ASU is building a critical mass of these high-achieving students, who tend to excel academically, are involved in numerous extracurricular activities and usually prepare for demanding careers. As word gets around, ASU is seeing an increased number of freshman applications for next fall.

"We find that high school students are aware of our merit scholar numbers and of the Barrett Honors College," says Tim Desch, director of Undergraduate Admissions. "Our national recognition has been strong for the past several years, and we're building on that.

"Our in-state applications for next fall are up 18 percent and our out-of-state applicants have increased nine percent, as of Jan. 1. We believe our entering class of 2002 will likely exceed that of last fall, which was a record number, and our increased retention rate will contribute to a larger returning student population."

Though the sluggish economy and soft employment outlook may contribute to higher enrollment numbers, the phenomenon would be more evident in an increase in transfer or re-entry students than freshmen, Desch says.

He believes the increase in ASU's freshman applications reflects the growth in the college-age population in Arizona and top feeder states, the growing academic reputation of the university and the perceived value when comparing cost of attendance at ASU with competing institutions. Arizona students may also be choosing to stay closer to home this year.

Merit scholars are heavily recruited by colleges and universities across the country. Desch says ASU is able to offer them many of the same experiences as a small private college, through the Barrett Honors College, along with the benefits of a public research university.

More than 365 merit scholars have enrolled at ASU in the past three years, representing a concentrated effort to increase the academic quality of the student body. Ten years ago the ASU freshman class included six new merit scholars.
