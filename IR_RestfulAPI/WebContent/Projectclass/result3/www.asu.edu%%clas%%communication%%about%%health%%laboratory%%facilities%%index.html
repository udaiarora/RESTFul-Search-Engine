Personnel | Facilities and Equipment | Current Projects

The Communication Sciences Laboratory is equipped for behavioral and physiological research.

Behavioral studies make use of three Canon VC-C3 communication cameras mounted on the interior walls of the lab, and two Shure MX-202 microphones suspended from the ceiling.  The cameras and microphones allow for audio- and video-taping of participant behavior, sending their electronic signals to a control room adjacent to the laboratory.  In the control room, researchers can see and hear participant behavior via a bank of monitors and speakers, can reposition and refocus the cameras, and can record and duplicate the audio and video signals.  Two coding stations in an adjacent room, each equipped with a Toshiba TV/VCR combination, can be used for verbal and nonverbal coding of behavior.

The laboratory itself features comfortable swivel chairs, silk plants, and a coffee table around which participants can sit for dyadic interaction studies.  The suspension of the microphones from the ceiling eliminates the need for lavelier microphones that must attach to participants’ clothing and can restrict their natural movement.

The laboratory is also equipped to measure several physiological activities and markers.  The lab features a Coulbourn Instruments LabLinc V electrocardiograph (EKG) and a skin conductance response (SCR) monitor that is equipped to measure these signals from two people simultaneously.  Blood pressure can also be measured non-invasively, either using the auscultatory method or using one of several automated oscillometric monitors.

The lab also features sample collection and analysis equipment for measuring cholesterol, serum glucose, and glycosylated hemoglobin.  These tests are conducted using capillary blood drawn by pinprick from a finger.  Laboratory personnel have been trained in safety measures and the avoidance of bloodborne pathogens, and employ universal precautions when handling blood samples.

Finally, the laboratory features equipment for taking saliva samples for the analysis of several hormone markers.  Thus far, research in the laboratory has focused on cortisol, testosterone, dehydroepiantrosterone-sulfate, estradiol, and secretory immunoglobulin-A.  The laboratory does not have the facilities to analyze samples for these markers (unlike for cholesterol, glucose, and glycosylated hemoglobin); rather, saliva samples for these hormones are shipped to external service laboratories for analysis.
