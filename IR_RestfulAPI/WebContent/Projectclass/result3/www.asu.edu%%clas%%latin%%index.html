The Andes and Amazon Field School Initiates its Seventh Annual Gathering

Jan 25 , 2007

The seventh annual Andes and Amazon Field School kicks off next month! The summer 2006 group includes approximately 25 students and 8 expert faculty members from ASU and various other universities. Session one will be from June 6-30-07 and session two will be from July 4-28-07. During each session, students will be embarking on a truly rich and culturally diverse experience including a stay with the Santo Urcu Amazonian Quichua Community.read more...

Quichua Language Classes Available This Fall

Jan 25 , 2007

Luz María De La Torre was born and raised in the Otavalo community in Ecuador and has an impressive professional record of research, teaching and community involvement. Quichua language is a gateway into Quechua culture, which values community and mutual help (ayni, minga, randi-randi). Many aspects of modern Amerindian cultures and worldviews are encoded in the language, and there are many interesting folk tales and oral histories recorded in Quechua.read more...
