August 24, 2003

Nursing selects Sousa as associate dean

The College of Nursing announced the appointment of Professor Karen Sousa as associate dean for graduate programs and research. Sousa's research credentials and experience made her one of the college's leading candidates to assume the position of associate dean. She had been the interim associate dean since January.

Sousa has been involved in health care for 25 years. She has served in many positions, from clinical nurse providing direct care to patients, to a hospital director of inpatient services and a patient care coordinator.

Before coming to Arizona, she earned a Ph.D. from the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF), was a research coordinator for Kaiser Permanente Regional Offices in Oakland, and spent two years at UCSF establishing her research focus of nursing systems and health outcomes.

In 2000 Sousa was awarded a grant from the National Institutes of Health (NIH) for a multiyear research project, titled "Health-related Quality of Life in Arthritis and AIDS." Sousa's research examines six dimensions of health-related quality of life - biological variables, symptoms, functional health, general health perception and overall quality of life - in two chronic illnesses.

"By assessing both the unique contribution each dimension makes to the health of people living with arthritis or AIDS and the changes in each dimension over time, we expect to identify ways to improve the quality of life for persons with these and other chronic illnesses," says Sousa.

During 2002, NIH awarded her a supplement to the grant so that she could mentor a new College of Nursing faculty member.

Sousa has published numerous articles on health-related quality of life and presented her research at many national and international conferences. This fall she will deliver a paper, titled "Evaluating measurement equivalence: A concern for quality of life research," at the International Society of Quality of Life Researchers annual conference in Prague, Czech Republic.

In addition to teaching both undergraduate and graduate research courses in the College of Nursing, Sousa has been a research consultant to the Arizona Nurses Association, ASU Community Health Services Clinic, Health Services Advisory Group, Inc., and local health care systems.
