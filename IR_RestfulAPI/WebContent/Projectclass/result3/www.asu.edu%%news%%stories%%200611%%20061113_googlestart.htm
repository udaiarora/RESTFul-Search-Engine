Nov. 13, 2006

ASU and Google offer personalized start page for students

As part of its ongoing alliance with Google, ASU has announced that it will be providing students with a customizable start page based on technology delivered as part of the Google Apps for Education suite.

The new ASU start page, available at http://my.asu.edu/beta, is a richer and more dynamic version of ASU’s existing portal, myASU, providing students with interactive tools, announcements, ASU news, and access to course information and interactive services.

“Our new personal start page is the next step in the evolution of ASU’s student environment that our alliance with Google is helping to drive,” says Adrian Sannier, university technology officer. “Using Google’s infrastructure allows ASU to focus its efforts on creating content and services specific to ASU student needs. Today’s new start page will be the foundation for a more dynamic, more customizable online experience for members of the ASU community. This is just the beginning.”

In addition to the ASU specific tools, students can populate their my.asu.edu/beta Web page with interactive gadgets available from Google and registered third parties. The ASU start page lays the foundation for a personal space where ASU students can customize their online ASU experience.

“I like having all of my important information at my fingertips and available at a moment’s notice, and Google’s personalized page makes that easy to set up and maintain,” says Salman Ahmad, a junior majoring in computer systems engineering at ASU. “While my.asu.edu provides a wealth of information, it cannot be customized at the level Google’s page can be. With a service like the Google Personalized Homepage, relevant information would be easier to organize and more accessible.”

Like “Gmail for ASU,” the University Technology Office has worked with Google to make the my.asu.edu/beta Web page a secure online environment. The new my.asu.edu/beta Web page will use ASU “webauth” technology to maintain a high standard of security for uninterrupted, efficient service in line with the highest industry standards in the commercial technology field.

Alliances with industry leaders such as Google are central to ASU’s strategy for accelerating technology’s role in advancing the academic enterprise. ASU’s use of Google Apps for Higher Education gives students an educational edge – one that enhances their learning experience and increases the value of their time at ASU. Beginning Nov. 17, ASU students will be able to begin using the my.asu.edu/beta Web page as their personalized front page for their online ASU experiences.
