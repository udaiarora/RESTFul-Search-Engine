What is LSF and what are its advantages?

LSF or Load Sharing Facility, is a general purpose distributed computing system from Platform Computing Corporation. It unites a group of UNIX and NT computers into a single system to make better use of the resources on a network and in the process integrates hosts from various vendors into a seamless system.

LSF is designed to continue operating even if some of the hosts in the cluster are unavailable. One host in the cluster acts as the master, but if the master host becomes unavailable another host takes over. LSF services are available as long as any one of the host in the cluster is up. When a host crashes, all the jobs running on that host are lost and no other pending or running jobs are affected.

At ASU, LSF is available on the research.asu.edu, stats.asu.edu and medusa clusters for the purpose of batch processing of user jobs. Some of the advantages that users have with LSF are:

  1. Submit jobs from one cluster to another.
  2. Submit jobs and logoff. Then logon at a later time and check the status of their job or they can request for an email notification from LSF when the job completes.
  3. Better processing times because of larger system resources in terms of CPU, memory and directory-space for application specific temporary files on the batch machines because these have LSF.
  4. Better tracking of jobs.

For more information, please refer to the LSF User's Guide available in the Customer Assistance Center (formerly COMPASS) and on the main (previous) LSF web page.
