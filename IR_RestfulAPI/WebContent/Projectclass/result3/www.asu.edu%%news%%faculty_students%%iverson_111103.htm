November 11, 2003

Iverson receives outstanding mentor award

Regents Professor Peter Iverson has been selected as the ASU Outstanding Doctoral Mentor for 2003. The history professor is the twentieth recipient in the 16 years of the award presented by the Graduate College and the ASU Foundation. The award and its $5,000 prize recognize faculty members who demonstrate excellence and commitment to doctoral student mentoring.

Since arriving at ASU in 1986, Iverson has earned respect for his ability to recruit, mentor and place graduate students, and work with them following graduation. He drives his students with these four words: "You can do it." Thirty-one of his students have obliged him and earned Ph.D. degrees.

Professor Noel Stowe, chair of the History Department, says, "Iverson's work, taken as a whole, is that of an active scholar and dedicated teacher and mentor. His work with his students is far beyond the norm of others on this faculty or elsewhere."

In Iverson's view, "Teaching is taking people from where they are, rather than where you imagine they should be. When you enter the academy, you must come to terms with a particular and, especially to the uninitiated, a peculiar culture. Twenty-seven of my 31 Ph.D. graduates did not grow up in an academic environment. Many are the first person in their families to graduate from college, let alone finish a Ph.D. Part of my job as a mentor involves demystifying this culture," he says.

Several of Iverson's students have won prestigious awards and fellowships, such as the Spencer Fellowship, Smithsonian Fellowship, Woodrow Wilson Postdoctoral Fellowship, and American Philosophical Society award. During 2002 and 2003, Iverson helped place Ph.D. graduates in tenure-track positions at Creighton University, Southern Connecticut State University, University of Idaho, University of Montana, University of Nebraska-Lincoln and University of Texas at El Paso.

Iverson, who will be honored at a Nov. 13 reception, says, "I look forward to that special occasion. I have been honored to serve as the mentor for these students. Each day they remind me that I have the best job in the world.''
