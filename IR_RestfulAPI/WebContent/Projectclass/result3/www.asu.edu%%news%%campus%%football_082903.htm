September 29, 2003

Football team hopes for big year, fan support

At a recent news conference at Sun Devil Stadium, ASU starting quarterback Andrew Walter, a 6-foot-5 junior, towered over a group of sports reporters. Speaking into a cluster of microphones, he described how this season he and his teammates will be dealing with something new and unfamiliar-extremely high expectations.

"The only pressure you have is what you put on yourself," Walter says. "We've been looking forward to this and, it isn't just me, we are all ready to go."

The buzz surrounding this year's team has been fueled by impressive preseason rankings. The Sun Devils were recently ranked in the top 25 in both the USA Today/ESPN Top 25 Coaches Poll, and the AP (Associated Press) Preseason College Football Poll. Earlier this summer, the Sun Devils were picked to finish second, behind the University of Southern California, in the Pac-10 Conference in the Pac-10 Media Poll.

Adam Wald, assistant director of marketing and promotions for Intercollegiate Athletics, says preseason ticket sales haven't been so strong since 1997-the season following the Sun Devil's last Rose Bowl appearance.

"Coming off a good season last year, at 8-6 and the success of Terrell Suggs and Andrew Walter, fans are excited for the upcoming season," Wald says. "With many players returning from last year's squad, there is plenty of excitement. With the team being ranked in the Top 25, a great Pac-10 home schedule and the price of tickets this year, people should want to come and see exciting Division I College Football."

The Sun Devils will kick off the season Sept. 6 as they take on Northern Arizona University at Sun Devil Stadium. ASU Executive Athletic Director Gene Smith said he hopes many faculty and staff members will take advantage of discounted season tickets and come to support the team.

"We hope that these price levels can translate to additional season ticket holders. ASU has been a part of this community for a long time and we are simply responding to the feedback from that community," he says.

Faculty and staff members who work at least 20 hours per week are eligible to receive a 20 percent discount on 2 season tickets. End zone seating is $80 (regularly $100) and sideline seating is $124 (regularly $155 plus a gift).

To order season tickets or single game tickets, call (480) 965-2381. Tickets can be purchased online at (www.thesundevils.com). Fans can also buy tickets at the Sun Devil ticket office, located at Sun Devil Stadium, Monday through Friday, from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
