Quality first priority for ASU's KAET-TV

KAET-TV/Channel 8 has been bringing quality programming to Arizona since Jan. 30, 1961. With each year, KAET programs have kept viewers, young and old, entertained and educated.

Ratings have documented this success. In fact, for the month of June, TRAC Media Research ranked KAET as the No. 1 public broadcast station (PBS) in the country.

"These rankings are done on a per-capita basis, so all markets, large and small, are ranked equally," says Joe Campbell, program manager for KAET. "KAET ranks consistently in the top five most watched stations per capita."

KAET currently reaches more than 80 percent of Arizona households, with approximately 1.8 million viewers each week. In addition to airing programs distributed nationally by PBS, KAET has produced its own award-winning programs including "Horizon," now in its 22nd year; and "The Arizona Collection."

Campbell says there are many reasons for KAET's success and longevity.

"First and foremost, we respect the intelligence of our viewers, regardless of their age," Campbell says. "All of our programs assume that the viewers have a lively curiosity about the world they live in, and want to learn more about it. We also have an ongoing commitment to helping our viewers know more about Arizona."

KAET has demonstrated its commitment to children's education by providing programs like "Arthur," "Clifford the Big Red Dog," and "Sesame Street," with almost 500,000 Arizona children watching the children's line-up each week.

"The producers of these shows go far beyond the programs themselves to deliver solid educational content to children and parents," Campbell says. "Each program has an extensive interactive Web site, which allows children the ability to reinforce the lessons learned on the show. Adult areas give parents advice and suggestions for activities they can do with their kids."

As reality shows continue to pop up every week on many networks, in some cases mimicking the documentary style first introduced by public television, KAET has managed to keep up with the competition.

"Imitation is the sincerest form of flattery, so we are flattered by the programs produced by our commercial brethren," Campbell says. "While many of the reality shows on commercial television are designed to highlight the foibles of human nature, ours are designed to help viewers better understand a time, a place or a situation and how people today might react to those situations."

For information about KAET, visit the Web at (http://www.kaet.asu.edu/).
