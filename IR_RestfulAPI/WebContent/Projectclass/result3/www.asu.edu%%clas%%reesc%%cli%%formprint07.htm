Background Information

The Russian and East European Studies Center at Arizona State University, through the Critical Languages Institute, offers intensive summer language instruction in Albanian, Armenian (Eastern), Bosnian/Croatian/Serbian, Macedonian, Navajo, Polish, Russian, Tatar, and Uzbek.

With support from ASU Summer Sessions, the ASU College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, and funding from the Social Science Research Council and the American Council of Learned Societies, tuition is waived for all courses to enable more students to study these less commonly taught languages. This blanket waiver is unique to the ASU Critical Languages Institute, and results in annual savings to CLI students of thousands of dollars. The only charge for enrolling in a CLI language offering is the one-time $400 registration processing fee.  Payment can be made by credit card or check (CLI fee payable to the Critical Languages Institute). Click here for a credit card authorization form.

Eligibility

The CLI is open to all ASU and non-ASU undergraduate and graduate students and non-degree students. Advanced high school students can be eligible to apply. The instruction is very intensive - you will complete the equivalent of a full year of academic work in eight weeks. This program should be viewed as a full-time commitment.

Practicums

Students of Albanian, Armenian, Bosnian/Croatian/Serbian, Macedonian, Polish, Tatar, and Uzbek may register for an optional two-credit, three-week practicum in Tirana (Albania), Yerevan (Armenia), Sarajevo (Bosnia), Ohrid (Macedonia), Poznan (Poland, Tatarstan (Russia), and Tashkent (Uzbekistan). The practicums, July 30th -August 17, 2007, immediately follow the CLI. Estimated costs are approximately $950-$1,400 for tuition, program fees, housing, excursions, and local airport transit. Roundtrip airfare is not included. Please see our CLI Study Abroad page for additional information. Applicants for the CLI practicums will submit a practicum deposit in the amount of $275 when registering for the practicum.
