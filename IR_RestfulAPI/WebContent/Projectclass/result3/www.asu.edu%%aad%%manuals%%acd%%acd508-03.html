ACD 508–03: Dismissal for Cause—Academic Professionals

Purpose

To describe procedures for dismissing academic professionals for just cause

Sources

Arizona Board of Regents Policy Manual - 6–301
Personnel Policies and Procedures for Academic Professionals

Applicability

Academic professionals

Policy

A continuing academic professional may not be dismissed without a finding of just cause. A probationary or year-to-year academic professional may not be dismissed during an appointment period without a finding of just cause.

Grounds for Dismissal for Just Cause

Just cause shall include, but not be limited to:

  1. demonstrated incompetence or dishonesty in professional activities related to fulfillment of assigned duties and responsibilities associated with the position

  2. substantial neglect of properly assigned duties

  3. personal conduct that substantially impairs the individual’s fulfillment of properly assigned duties and responsibilities

  4. substantial incapacity (physical or mental) to perform properly assigned duties, with due consideration given to the nature and duration of the incapacity

    and

  5. violation of the Code of Conduct of a serious nature.

Investigations and Hearings

Investigations and hearings require the judicious consideration of facts, but they should neither partake of the form of courts of law nor be constrained by the limitations imposed upon such courts. They are academic hearings, the purpose of which is to safeguard and protect not only the individual rights of the academic professional but also the integrity of the university.

Limitations on Disability Status

When an individual has been on disability status for more than 18 months, there is no assurance of reemployment with the university. At this stage, the university may utilize the following procedures for termination.

Dismissal Process

Suspension

Suspension will be considered in the following cases:

  1. a continuing, probationary, or year-to-year academic professional may be suspended with pay pending an investigation into a case in which the president determines that continued presence of the academic professional on a campus constitutes a substantial interference with the orderly functioning of the university or of a substantial area, unit, college, or department of the university

    or

  2. during the investigation if, in the judgment of the president, immediate harm to the academic professional or to others is threatened by continuance or if continued performance of duties would result in extreme harm to the academic/research program.

Unless prohibited by law, any suspension shall be with pay.

Initial Dismissal Procedures

When reason arises to question the fitness of an academic professional who has continuing appointment or whose term of appointment has not expired, the appropriate administrative officers, as designated by the president on a case-by-case basis, should ordinarily discuss the matter with the academic professional in personal conference. The matter may be disposed of by mutual consent at this point.

If a settlement is not reached and the academic professional is recommended for suspension or dismissal, the president shall refer such recommendation to a conciliation committee.

Conciliation Procedures

This committee shall be a university ad hoc committee consisting of an academic professional ombudsperson appointed by the Committee on Academic Professional Status, one continuing academic professional appointed by the president, and one continuing academic professional appointed by the Committee on Academic Professional Status. The purpose of the conciliation committee will be to recommend one of the following to the president:

  1. that conciliation is not possible and the process should proceed

    or

  2. that conciliation is possible as an alternative to dismissal, with an explanation of the terms of the conciliation.

The committee shall meet on one or more occasions with only the academic professional and a representative of the administration, separately or together, at the option of the academic professional. The committee will report its findings, decisions, and sanctions, if any, and make a confidential recommendation to the president within 15 days of the committee’s appointment.

The president will notify the academic professional in writing of the conciliation committee’s recommendation within five working days of receipt of the report.

If the committee recommends that conciliation is possible and both the academic professional and the president concur with the terms of conciliation, the action will be discontinued.

Formal Appeal Process

The formal appeal process commences if:

  1. the academic professional refuses or fails to accept the recommendation

    or

  2. if the president, after due consideration of the committee’s recommendation, decides that the academic professional should be suspended or dismissed.

A statement with reasonable specificity of the grounds proposed for the dismissal or suspension shall be formulated by the president or designee. The appropriate dean or department chair shall provide the academic professional with a formal notice of dismissal or suspension, which shall include a statement of charges and notification of the right to a hearing.

Within 15 days after receipt of a notice of suspension or dismissal and statement of charges, the academic professional shall state in writing to the president whether a formal hearing on the charges is desired. Failure to request a hearing within the 15-day period shall act as a waiver of any right by such academic professional to a due-process hearing before any committee at the university.

If requested, a hearing to determine whether the academic professional should be removed from the academic professional position on the grounds stated will be conducted by the Academic Professional Grievance Committee.

Code of Conduct Violations

Should it be decided by the president, at any stage, that allegations involve matters prohibited by the Code of Conduct, then the procedures delineated in that document prevail.
