Try RefWorks - It Makes Research Easier!

 


(posted 8/15/05)


RefWorks is a user-friendly program that manages bibliographies and other resource lists, and it's now available free to all ASU faculty, staff, and students.


RefWorks provides convenient, searchable storage of bibliographic information about sources, and makes it possible to easily export references in a variety of standard citation styles for use in papers and publications. The program also includes RefShare, a tool that lets you share databases with others, including individuals outside of ASU.


As a web-based product, RefWorks functions on virtually all major computing platforms (Windows, Mac, Unix, etc.), and citation databases are available to users from any computer with an Internet connection. Citations can easily be exported directly to RefWorks from most databases available through the ASU libraries as well as the library’s online catalog.


This new service is available at no charge to ASU students, faculty and staff. You will need to sign up for an individual account the first time you use RefWorks. RefWorks integrates with Microsoft Word through a small downloadable utility program, “Write-N-Cite.”


To access RefWorks go to: http://www.asu.edu/refworks. You will be asked to login using your ASURITE ID before being referred to the RefWorks login page.


A “Quick Start Guide” to RefWorks is available at http://www.asu.edu/lib/refworks. RefWorks training will be available at ASU Libraries this Fall.

 

New This Fall: RefWorks Integrates with "Get It! @ ASU"!

 

“Get It @ ASU” provides other services useful to RefWorks users:

  • When you use databases or full text journals licensed by ASU that support SFX linking technology, you will see the “Get It @ ASU” graphic, either linking you to full text or to the “Get It @ ASU” services menu.

  • The “Get It @ ASU” service menu may include a link under “Save this Citation” to directly export citation data to RefWorks – this is especially handy when databases of journal publishers do not provide an “export to RefWorks” link.

  • “Get It @ ASU” also provides a tool for formatting citations and for creating a stable URL that can be used in reading lists, etc. Look for this tool under “Save this Citation.”

  • If you need to find the full text of an article you have cited but have not yet entered into RefWorks, the CitationLinker tool at http://www.asu.edu/lib/sfx/citation/ can help you find it fast!

  • You can browse a list of all journals available online to ASU users from http://sfx3.exlibrisgroup.com:3210/asu/a-z/default.


RefWorks is made available through the collaborative efforts of the ASU University Libraries, ASU IT’s Digital Media and Instructional Technologies, and the Fletcher Library, ASU at the West Campus.
