June 25, 2003

Gonzalez joins Student Affairs; Rund heads new initiative

ASU President Michael Crow announced the appointment of Juan Gonzalez as vice president for Student Affairs, following a successful national search. Crow also announced the creation the Office of University Undergraduate Initiatives and has appointed James Rund to lead the university-wide effort as vice president for University Undergraduate Initiatives.

Both appointments are effective Aug, 1, pending approval by the Arizona Board of Regents.

Gonzalez, currently vice president for Student Affairs at Georgetown University, will focus on campus life, residential life, student well-being and health and other areas that impact student success and overall experience. Rund, currently interim vice president for Student Affairs at ASU, will have cross-cutting responsibilities, working closely with all administrative areas to manage enrollment growth, selective admissions, diversity and achievement in a multi-campus setting. The changes are necessary as ASU continues to grow and get better at the same time.

"This is a critical time for ASU," said Crow. "We are growing the university to provide services to larger enrollments of highly qualified students, while continuing to enhance quality and remain open and accessible. To do this successfully, with student success being our highest priority, we need to be intensely focused."

Crow is making the changes without expanding his administrative staff.

Rund has been interim vice president for Student Affairs since last October, when Christine Wilkinson was named senior vice president and secretary of the university. Rund's previous position as associate vice president for Student Affairs and dean of Student Development, which he held from 1995 to 2002, is not being filled.

Gonzalez joins ASU from Georgetown, where he has been vice president for Student Affairs since August 2000. From 1994 to 2000, Gonzalez was vice president for student affairs at California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo. He was vice president for Student Services at California State University, San Bernardino, from 1990 to 1994.

Among his responsibilities, Gonzalez will oversee general campus welfare to promote a healthy campus environment. He was the principal investigator for a three-year grant from the Anheuser-Busch Foundation for a campaign to reduce high-risk behavior and encourage the health and safety of Georgetown University students.

Rund has been instrumental in improving the undergraduate experience at ASU. He created ASU's Freshman Year Experience program, which ranked 23rd in the 2003 U.S. News & World Report's "America's Best Colleges" ranking of first-year programs designed to help freshmen be successful.

Crow said, "Jim and Juan will provide the strong, focused leadership we need in those areas that are key to student success as we move quickly to advance the multiple goals associated with the Arizona Board of Regents' Changing Directions initiative, enrollment growth, student quality and achievement and improving campus life and the residential learning environment."

In his new role, Rund will design and build the enrollment and academic support infrastructure for the university to operate on multiple campuses. He will develop and implement university enrollment goals and strategies that will enhance university-wide student quality and diversity, while accommodating anticipated enrollment growth.

"Integrating the multi-campus efforts are a prodigious undertaking that is best done centrally and with the support of the entire university," said Milton Glick, executive vice president and provost of the university. "Both Jim and Juan have a tremendous opportunity ahead of them and I'm confident our team is stronger in every way with this new structure."

Rund will oversee student recruitment and admission efforts that support the academic program plan, including coordinating the selective admissions program and creating a seamless university experience for students within and between campuses and programs.

"The university has been re-energized since president Crow has come on board, and I marvel at his tireless commitment to making ASU not only better than it has ever been, but the best in every aspect," said Rund. "He has outlined a bold vision that will serve Arizona well. I'm pleased to continue serving ASU and advancing the President's agenda in this new role."

To accomplish the mission of his office, Rund will employ the tools of Undergraduate Admissions, Student Financial Assistance, University Registrar and Student Services Administrative Computing.

Gonzalez will focus on student life, well-being and success, including designing and directing programs and services that enhance the student experience and overall quality of campus life and the learner-centered environment.

Gonzalez will lead efforts to enhance the residential experience and develop additional campus communities, and the university's living and learning environment. He will oversee development of the new South campus academic village, which will unite students and faculty in a living and learning environment designed to encourage more interaction between students and faculty. Gonzalez also will work with the dean of the Barrett Honors College to expand the living-learning environment for ASU's premier undergraduate honors program.

"Under President Crow's leadership, ASU has tremendous potential to impact students' lives in ways that will forever transform them as individuals and contributing members of society," said Gonzalez. "Over the next 10 years we have the opportunity to redefine success and become a university that is directly engaged within the state, country and on a global basis," said Gonzalez.

Gonzalez says he has a great deal to learn, but is pleased with the solid foundation in Student Affairs at ASU.

"I believe the most effective model to reduce high-risk behavior is one that engages students in the learning process," Gonzalez said. "Currently, there is tremendous promise in the reduction of high-risk underage drinking with the social norms philosophy, and I'm pleased that ASU employs that program and is realizing impressive results."

He will have responsibility for Student Health and Wellness, Counseling and Consultation, Career Services, Recreational Sports, Residential Life, Memorial Union/Student Development, Student Media, and Student Life.

David Young, chairman of the search committee charged with hiring the vice president for Student Affairs, said, "I think Dr. Gonzalez' breadth of experience will serve him well at ASU, and make him an outstanding vice president for Student Affairs. I'm impressed by his commitment to work with the faculty to enhance the learning environment, as well as his enthusiasm for making higher education an exceptional experience for all students."

Gonzalez earned his bachelor's degree in Latin-American Studies from Texas Tech University. He has a master's degree in bilingual-bicultural education from the University of Texas, and a Ph.D. in educational psychology from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

Rund has a B.A. from Moorhead State University, an M.S. from North Dakota State University, and an Ed.D. in higher and adult education from ASU.
