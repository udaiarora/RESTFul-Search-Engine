Source: Shai Ginsburg, 480-965-9048

Israeli and Palestinian experts to discuss possibility of peace

Representing both sides of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, four participants in past peace negotiations will meet at Arizona State University on Sunday, February 9 and discuss future possibilities for restoring the peace in two public workshops.

Discussing issues that are key to a resolution of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict are Palestinian political analyst Manuel Hassassian, Executive Vice President of Bethlehem University; Palestinian history professor Rashid Khalidi, Director of the Center for International Studies at the University of Chicago; Israeli political science professor Dr. Menachem Klein, expert advisor to Israel's Minister of Internal Security and Foreign Affairs; and Israeli lawyer Daniel Seidmann, an authority on East Jerusalem issues.

"These are people who have all taken part in the previous peace negotiations, who all know each other and are still in communication," said Jess Schwartz Professor of Modern Hebrew Literature and ASU Jewish Studies faculty member Shai Ginsburg. "The purpose of the workshops is to show that people can still talk reasonably to each other, and are still working to resolve the conflict peacefully."

Entitled "After Oslo: New Strategies for Middle East Peace," the event begins at 10 a.m. with a two-hour public workshop on the subject of "Can a Peace Process be Reinstated?" and then continues at 2 p.m. with a session on "Overcoming Stumbling Blocks." The free workshops are in the ASU Education Lecture Hall, located just north of Gammage Auditorium. Free parking is available at Gammage.

The workshops are part of a national series of similar events scheduled to take place in Amherst, Massachusetts, Washington, D.C. and San Francisco. The ASU event is sponsored by the ASU Jewish Studies Program and ASU's Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict and was made possible in part by a gift from the Marshall Fund of Arizona. Additional support was also provided by the Jewish Federation-Israel Center and Hillel.

"Many of us have been concerned about developments on campuses nationwide regarding the Arab-Israeli conflict where rhetoric stands in place of knowledge and the complexities of a settlement are not understood," explained Jack Kugelmass, director of ASU's Jewish Studies Program. "I thought Jewish Studies should play a proactive role here by taking what is in the public mind and using it as an opportunity to teach--to make the public knowledgeable about something very few people understand. The best way to do this was to bring to campus people who are on the front line of negotiating a fair settlement. I'm particularly delighted with what Shai Ginsburg put together and I have a feeling that this will be emulated on other campuses."

"This workshop promises to be a very informative discussion on the current state of the Israeli/Palestinian conflict, and possibilities for its resolution," said Linell Cady, director of ASU's new Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict. "The topic is clearly among the most challenging and important in its ramifications for international affairs. The Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict is happy to co-sponsor an event that is so closely aligned with its own thematic interests."

For further information on the workshop leaders, please see the attached brief biographies. For more information on the workshops, please call 480-727-6906.

ASU

Manuel Hassassian is Executive Vice President, Bethlehem University and a political analyst. He also teaches at the Center for International Development and Conflict Management, in the Department of Government and Politics, University of Maryland at College Park, as well as in other American universities. His most recent book is Citizenship and the State in the Middle East: Approaches and Applications.

Rashid Khalidi is Professor of Middle East History and Director of the Center for International Studies at the University of Chicago. He is the President of the American Committee on Jerusalem, and was an advisor to the Palestinian delegation to the Madrid and Washington Arab-Israeli peace negotiations from October 1991 until June 1993. His most recent book is Palestinian Identity: Construction of National Consciousness.

Menachem Klein is Sr. Lecturer in the Department of Political Science at Bar-Ilan University, Israel, and Sr. Research Fellow at the Jerusalem Institute for Israel Studies. In 2000, Dr. Klein was an external expert adviser for Jerusalem Affairs and Israel-PLO Final Status Talks to the Minister of Interior Security and Minister of Foreign Affairs, Prof. S. Ben-Ami. He was also a member of the political advisory team operating in the office of the Prime Minister Ehud Barak. His latest book is Jerusalem: The Contested City.

Daniel Seidmann is a lawyer specializing in East Jerusalem issues and matters of public interest in East Jerusalem. He served, informally, as an advisor to participants in the final status negotiations from 2000 to 2002, and was a member of a committee of experts commissioned by Prime Minister Barak's office to generate sustainable arrangements geared toward implementing emerging political understandings with the Palestinians.
