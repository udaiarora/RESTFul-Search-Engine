What's New in GAPD?

Forms

In order to facilitate the uniform review of proposals, as well as to assist proposing units with the fullest possible development of their programmatic strategies, many forms, templates and other proposal instruments have been developed. Below, please find links to some of these documents. As always, you are encouraged to contact (www.asu.edu/graduate/gapd/contact) the Division of Graduate Studies at the earliest stages of proposal development.

  • Indication of Support (Program Development) Form
    www.asu.edu/provost/curriculum/ProgramDevelopmentForm.doc
    Prior to the development of a full-blown proposal, the Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University wants to insure that the unit chair/director and college dean indicate their support for the new initiative. If the proposal is interdisciplinary, all participating units and colleges also need to indicate support. Please note that this form must be submitted and be approved prior to the development of proposals for new degrees, certificates, or concentrations.

  • Changes to Existing Graduate Academic Programs
    www.asu.edu/graduate/gapd/forms/acaProgChange.doc
    This form should be used when a unit is proposing a change to an already-approved academic program. These changes might include changes to the minimum required hours for a degree, a change to the culminating experience option(s) available to students in a program, a change in delivery format for a degree program, or the establishment of a dual or concurrent degree program.

  • Establishing Graduate Certificates
    www.asu.edu/graduate/gapd/forms/estblGradCert.doc
    This form is for units proposing a graduate certificate. Please note that this form is for graduate certificates only.

  • New Graduate Concentration Proposals
    www.asu.edu/graduate/gapd/forms/GradConcProposalProcs.doc
    Use this form if you are proposing to create a new concentration under an existing degree. Please note that a "concentration" is a subspecialty within a degree and major which reflects that the student has fulfilled a designated, specialized course of study.

  • Joint Degree Program Guidelines for Bachelor's/Master's Joint Degree Programs
    (revised Jan. 25, 2005)
    www.asu.edu/graduate/gapd/forms/BachelorsMastersGuidelines012505.doc
    These guidelines describe the policies and guidelines for the development and implementation of a joint bachelor/master's program, when both the bachelor's and master's degrees are existing.

  • New Graduate Degrees: Planning and Implementation
    The proposal process for new degrees has been established by ABOR and consists of a two-prong approach whereby units must request planning authorization and subsequently implementation approval. Templates and other information may be found at www.asu.edu/provost/curriculum.
