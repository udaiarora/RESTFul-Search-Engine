May 12, 2003

ASU West expands accelerated teacher certification program

Five additional Valley school districts plan to collaborate with the ASU West College of Education during the 2003 - 04 school year to offer an accelerated program for people with a bachelor's degree in a field other than education who wish to pursue teaching careers.

ASU West implemented its accelerated postbaccalaureate teaching certificate program with the Peoria Unified School District (PUSD) as a pilot program at the start of the current school year. More than 20 students have spent significant amounts of time in one of four PUSD elementary schools since early August, while also taking the university-level education courses needed for teacher certification. The students will continue working in the Peoria schools through the conclusion of the school year in late May.

New districts planning to participate during 2003 - 04 include the Glendale Union High School District and the Alhambra, Cartwright, Litchfield and Washington Elementary School Districts. The Litchfield District's involvement is part of a partnership among DMB Corp., ASU West, and the school district to develop a range of educational opportunities in DMB's new Verrado residential community located in the West Valley.

"The ASU West students have the opportunity to develop real relationships with teachers and pupils in the schools in which they are placed, and to experience everything from parent-teacher conferences to standardized testing schedules," says Betty Porter, PUSD administrator for curriculum and instruction. "It also gives the school district the chance to get a first-hand look at potential prospective teachers."

"I would recommend this program to anyone," says ASU West student Tyson Boothe. "The classroom experience it has given me has prepared me extremely well for a career as an educator."

As many as 25 students are expected to enroll in the program in PUSD and each of the additional districts during the upcoming school year. ASU West offers this accelerated program in addition to its on-campus postbaccalaureate teacher certification programs in elementary and secondary education, which may be completed in approximately 18 months on a fulltime basis, or over a longer period on a part-time basis.

"Students need to make a major commitment of time and energy to participate in the accelerated program," says Michael Awender, dean of the ASU West College of Education. "Students willing to make that commitment experience a high-quality program that immerses them in the public school setting for a full school year."

Registration for the program at the six participating districts is open until July 1. For information, call the ASU West College of Education at (602) 543-6354.

Crum, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or (matthew.crum@asu.edu).
