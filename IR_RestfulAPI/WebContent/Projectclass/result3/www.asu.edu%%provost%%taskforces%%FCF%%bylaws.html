Provost’s Advisory Committee on Full-time Contract Faculty

[Committee Roster]

Mission Statement

A representative group, the Provost’s Advisory Committee on Full-Time Contract Faculty serves as a resource for and advises the Provost on issues concerning Full-Time Contract Faculty in order to enhance the quality of education at Arizona State University. Full-Time Contract Faculty’s charge includes but is not limited to assessing and making recommendations about policies and procedures, appointment options, professional development and working conditions/status of all Full-Time Contract Faculty at ASU. As part of this charge, the committee would assist in providing substantive advice to the Provost in regards to matters of concern brought forth to this body.

To accomplish these purposes, FCF will communicate with both the Provost and all constituencies represented by this group. The committee will, as necessary, gather information and call on the resources of other university units.

Bylaws

1. Members of the committee will be asked to serve three-year commitments. Approximately one-third of committee members will be replaced annually.

  • The FCF Committee Chair will serve a two-year commitment.
  • A committee recorder will serve a one-year commitment.

Membership

2. Members of this advisory committee are appointed by the University Provost.
Regular Members:

  • Members on the committee will normally number between 15 and 18.
  • More than half of the membership will come from the ranks of lecturers and senior lecturers.
  • At least two representatives will be appointed from the ranks of Instructor, Clinical Faculty, Research Faculty, and any other category of Full-Time Contract Faculty.
  • The committee members will be comprised predominantly of Full-Time Contract Faculty and include Track faculty or Administrators with relevant expertise.

The advisory committee will have representation from as many colleges on the Main campus as possible; the East and West campuses will have at least two representatives each.

Ex officio members:
University Provost designee, normally a vice provost for academic affairs and a Senate representative.

Meetings

3. Meetings will normally occur once a month.

  • A sub-committee may need to meet as often as necessary.
  • The advisory committee will meet at least once a year with the University Provost.

Voting

4. A quorum shall be at least 2/3 of all committee members. If a member is unable to attend he/she may select an alternate to represent him or her. A quorum is necessary for items to be voted upon.

  • A three-fourths majority vote is needed on actions to be suggested to the Provost
  • A simple majority vote of present members is required to elect the committee chair or approve the minutes.
  • The chair will determine how votes shall be cast, for example, whether secret ballots are necessary.
  • Any action taken by this committee will NOT be voted on at the meeting when the action is first introduced.

Reporting

5. An annual report of the committee work will be presented to the Provost as necessary. Suggestions for actions to be taken shall be supported with appropriate rationale.

Attendance

6. FCF members are expected to attend all regular and other meetings of the entire FCF committee. The minutes of each meeting will record the names of those members present, including via proxy or conference call. If a member accumulates three absences during a single academic year, his/her place will be considered vacated.
Changes

7. Changes to the Mission Statement or By-Laws

  • Changes to these documents may be proposed by any committee member.

Approved: Spring 2004
