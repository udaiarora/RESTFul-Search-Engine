Learning Planners

Listed under the table below are tools for planning learning. One is for you to use for yourself as an individual, the others are tools supervisors can use in identifying learning needs with individual team members, for a team as a whole or for use with new employees.

All ASU staff are entitled to 16 hours of release time (SPP 601) to support continuous learning by encouraging employees to participate in work-related or university-sponsored training, including professional development classes, workshops, and conferences. The key is "work related"! If you have an interest in taking advantage of some of the learning opportunities available at ASU, try using the planner to begin to identify which would support your performance or career goals. Then, meet with your supervisor, add in their ideas and put together a plan for the year.

  • My Learning Plan: A tool to help individuals plan their learning needs for the year.
  • My Development Plan: an online tool linked to the online course catalog
  • Learning Planner for Supervisors: A tool to help supervisors think through the learning needs of individual staff members. This tool examines not only needs for the current job, but learning that will support the employee's career goals as well.
  • Team Learning Planner for Supervisors: A tool to help supervisors plan learning needs for an entire team.
  • New Employee Learning Planner for Supervisors: A tool to help plan in advance the learning needs of a new employee.
