Secure Telnet Now Required

Following in the secure steps of the ASU IT department, the ASU Libraries will no longer support unsecure telnet access to the Online catalog and Integrated Library System (INNOPAC). The "Text Version (Telnet)" option will be removed from the ASU WebOPAC main menu screen on Sunday December 5th. Public users must use the WebOPAC to search the online catalog.

Staff who access the Integrated Library System from off-campus, including users running VPN software at home, will need to use a secure telnet client to connect to the system. Secure Shell telnet is available free from ASU IT: http://www.asu.edu/it/security/software/html/ssh/index.htm.

Once the off-campus access has been secured, on-campus users who still need to access staff functions via telnet will be moved to secure telnet. Unsecure telnet access will be deactivated entirely and all passwords will be changed at that time.

Millennium users will not be affected by these changes.

On Thursday, December 2, 2005 from 1:00-2:00 p.m. in Hayden C6A, Fred McIlvain and Keith Swartz will be meeting with affected staff members to demonstrate how to download and install the Secure Shell Client software and configure it for access to the Integrated Library System. All interested staff are invited to attend.

If you have any questions or need additional assistance, please contact Fred McIlvain (5-9427) or Keith Swartz (5-2854).

McIlvain Hosts Arizona Innovative Users Group (AZIUG) Meeting

The ASU Libraries, represented by ILS administrator Fred McIlvain, hosted the inaugural meeting of the Arizona Innovative Users Group on Wednesday October 27, 2004.

There were 22 participants, from ASU, ASU West, NAU, U of A, Pima Community College, Tempe Public Library and Scottsdale Public Library. Two representatives from Innovative Interfaces also attended.

Members of the group discussed their individual implementations of the INNOPAC/Millennium system, what modules were up and running and expected for the future. The Innovative representatives did a presentation on continuing development at Innovative Interfaces.

The group is going to hold meetings twice a year (Spring and Fall) with the next meeting tentatively scheduled for March 2005 in Tempe. There will be a presentation from Innovative staff and breakout groups to discuss implementation, processes and procedures for a variety of the Millennium Modules.

The agenda for the next meeting will be posted, and interested ASU staffers are invited to attend. For more information regarding the AZIUG, please contact Fred McIlvain (fred.mcilvain@asu.edu) or Betty Dong (betty.dong@asu.edu).
