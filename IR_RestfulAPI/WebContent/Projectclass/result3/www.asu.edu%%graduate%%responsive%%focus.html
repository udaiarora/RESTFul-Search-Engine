Principles Guiding Focus Groups

  1. Diversity of opinions and groups represented should be sought and documented.

  2. Strategies for change: each group should focus on the guiding questions as they pertain to the ASU Ph.D. educational process. In particular, each group should explore areas where specific change (e.g., in majors, departments, educational practices) might be changed.

  3. Each group will develop a 3–5 page report that reflects the scope of deliberations, issues, and strategies suggested. Note-takers will be assigned to each group. The note-taker will record the depth and quality of discussion. These notes will be shared with focus group leaders who will utilize them as the foundation of the final report.
