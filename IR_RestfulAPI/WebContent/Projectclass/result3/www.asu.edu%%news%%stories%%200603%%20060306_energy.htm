March 6, 2006

Lecturer focuses on energy solutions

No one can predict the future, but, according to Graham R. Fleming, "we can be reasonably confident that the world's demand for energy will double over the next 50 years or so."

Fleming, the Melvin Calvin Distinguished Professor of Chemistry at the University of California-Berkeley, will give a free lecture titled "Supplying the World's Energy Needs: Challenges and Potential Solutions" at 7:30 p.m., March 9 in room 150 of the Bateman Physical Sciences Center H Wing on the Tempe campus.

Fleming will present the annual Eyring Lecture sponsored by the Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry.

The lectures are named for LeRoy Eyring, Regents' Professor of Chemistry, "whose extraordinary instructional and research accomplishments and professional leadership at ASU helped to bring the Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry into international prominence."

Fleming says it's difficult to comprehend what it will require to generate twice the power that the world now uses.

He says he will use his lecture to "try to illustrate the scale of the challenge we face and talk about potential solutions."

"Fundamental chemistry, biology and physics will all play key roles in creating the solutions necessary for sustainable, CO2-neutral power generation," Fleming says. "Humankind has embarked on its biggest experiment yet on our planet: the increasing atmospheric CO2 levels - currently about 380 parts per million from their historical value of about 275 parts per million, and climbing - which are likely to produce significant climate changes unless drastic steps are taken to curtail emissions."

Fleming is a physical chemist who has made contributions to many areas of condensed phase chemical and biological dynamics. He is an expert in ultrafast spectroscopy and has developed several new experimental methods.

For more information about the lecture, call Linda Russett at (480) 965-2093.
