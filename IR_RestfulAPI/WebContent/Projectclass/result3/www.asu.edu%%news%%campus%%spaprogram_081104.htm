August 11, 2004

ASU offers new spa certificate program

Relaxation and rejuvenation has become big business.

According to a study by the International Spa Association, the spa industry has grown from a $10.7 billion business in 2001 to a $11.1 billion business in 2003. Between 2002 and 2004, the number of spas in the United States increased 25 percent.

To help prepare future spa industry leaders, Arizona State University's Department of Exercise and Wellness is introducing a new Spa Management Certificate program as part of its undergraduate curriculum this fall.  Students will prepare for careers in spa administration through courses specifically selected to meet core competencies identified by the spa industry and an advisory committee of spa directors.

ASU is the only university in the country offering a certificate in spa management, filling a niche that is virtually untapped. 

"The spa industry has identified a real need, not only for more spa directors, assistant directors and supervisors, but for management candidates with formal education and training in spa administration," said Larry Woodruff, professor of Exercise and Wellness.

"The certificate will help provide a solid foundation needed for leadership positions in the industry, reducing a lot of on-the-job training time," said Woodruff.

The certificate is recognized by the International Spa Association and should significantly enhance opportunities for placement and advancement of ASU certificate holders within the spa industry, according to Woodruff.

Students will be required to successfully complete courses in leadership, fitness, all aspects of business administration, holistic health, healthy cuisine, professional seminars on specific spa management issues, massage therapy, as well as complete an internship in a resort spa. Enrollment in the program will be limited, and acceptance will be based on a combination of higher education credits, grades, references and prior work experience.

"The courses that will be offered are a natural fit for the faculty in Exercise and Wellness and Business to teach," said Woodruff. "We hope the new certificate will help strengthen the business and wellness components of the spa industry."

For more information about the program, contact Larry Woodruff at (480) 727-1935.
