April 17, 2003

ASU poets sweep Arts & Letters awards

It was an ASU sweep at the National Society of Arts & Letters, Arizona chapter, April 6 awards luncheon. Regents Professor of English Alberto Rios won the 2003 Medallion of Merit, and three of his graduate students in creative writing won scholarships as Young Emerging Artists.

Rios, who was nominated this year for the National Book Award, was presented with a medallion created by David Pimentel, ASU art professor. All of this year's awards were given in the category of poetry.

Josh Rathkamp, an MFA candidate from Linwood, Mich., won first prize and a $3,000 scholarship. He will now compete in the national competition May 16 - 18 for awards ranging up to $15,000. Second place of a $2,000 award went to Anne Caroline Berry, MFA candidate from West Columbia, S.C., and third place of $1,000 went to Brian Leary of Page, Ariz.

The national awards competition provides an opportunity for young artists to be seen, heard and read by managers, producers, publishers, conductors, critics and gallery directors, as well as patrons of the arts. The Arizona chapter, founded in 1966, is one of 28 chapters encouraging young talent and promoting greater appreciation of the arts.
