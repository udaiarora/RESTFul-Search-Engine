Barrett Honors College

Named the Craig and Barbara Barrett Honors College, this is the program in which ASU's undergraduate scholars bloom. From classes designed for honors students to a special living community, the Honors College offers students a top-notch program. The Honors College moved this past year from McClintock Hall to Center Complex. Administrative offices are located on the first floor of Best Hall (shown).

 

Freshman Year Experience (FYE)

The residence halls at ASU have most likely changed since you were in school. Now, many of the halls are dedicated as Freshman Year Experience communities, and a majority of ASU's freshmen choose to live in these communities. Why? Because ASU offers academic advising, tutoring, academic classes, computer labs and other academic support programs in the halls. And because FYE works. The persistence of freshmen at ASU has dramatically improved in recent years, and this is directly attributed to the FYE program.

FYE communities are located at Palo Verde East (pictured), Palo Verde Main, Palo Verde West, Manzanita, Mariposa, McClintock, Ocotillo, Sahuaro and Sonora Halls. Manzanita houses special floors for business and engineering/applied science majors within its FYE community.

Sonora Center Residence Hall

A student pedals into the courtyard of Sonora Center, the youngest residence hall in ASU's housing system. The hall, opened in 1990, is located at the south part of campus near Adelphi Drive and Sahuaro Hall. In fall 2001, ASU plans to open a new residence hall on north campus to serve freshmen and a new residence hall on south campus to serve sororities. Palo Verde Main, the current sorority hall, will become an FYE hall.

 

Computer Labs on Campus

For some of the LSP alumni, your college experience involved plugging quarters into typewriters at Hayden Library and the Memorial Union. Today's college student enjoys state-of-the-art computing facilities throughout campus, including the Technology Center (pictured) located next to Sonora Center at south campus, the Computing Commons with its beautiful computing atrium, and academic computing facilities within colleges and departments.
