Guidelines for students and Tips for Success

 

What approach do we use to teach Spanish in the Lower Division Spanish Program?

·      We use a proficiency-based approach. That means we will develop students? ability to understand spoken and written Spanish and to communicate from the Beginning (SPA 101) to the Intermediate Mid or Intermediate High level (SPA 202). Students will gradually learn how to perform a series of functions: narrate (in the present and past), describe, compare/contrast, express likes and dislikes, invite, refuse an invitation, etc. etc.

·      We will develop your listening, speaking, reading and writing skills and will provide you with valuable cultural information.

 

What language will we use in class?

·      We use Spanish to communicate in class. Your instructor will accommodate his/her language to your level of proficiency. Students who do not listen or use Spanish frequently will not be able to develop their listening comprehension and/or oral skills.

 

What will the role of grammar be? Will grammar be taught in class?

·      The role of grammar for effective and accurate communication cannot be  underestimated. However, the student will be responsible for studying the grammar and  vocabulary at home, so that he/she will be able to apply this knowledge in class through a  variety of activities.  A rich variety of activities is provided in the Workbook and Lab Manual that will allow you  to control the grammatical structures and vocabulary within different types of activities.  In addition, you will also have a number of computer-based activities that we have designed  for you. You can access these activities on Blackboard. Your instructor will provide you with more information about them.

 

Why is homework important?

·       Homework is an integral part of the course.  It helps students to consolidate what they did in class and prepare future work

 

What type of activities will be done in class?

·          There will be a great variety of activities with varying degrees of complexity.  Your instructor will start with simple activities where you will directly apply, in short sentences, the grammar and vocabulary you have studied at home. These activities usually have only a right or wrong answer.  Then, your instructor will provide more complicated activities that will require you to express your own meanings. These are communicative activities and they involve role-plays, debates, discussions, etc.  Remember, the emphasis is on communication!

 

Will the students be required to participate?

·    Students will participate in a series of paired and group activities during the class period either  o develop their speaking skills or their reading skill.  Students are strongly encouraged to participate actively. Failure to do so will affect their grade.

 

Will the class be fun?

·        Learning a foreign language is always fun! You will have fun through the series of activities you will be asked to perform, the different roles you will be asked to play: a friend, a waiter, a professor, a boss, a father/mother-in law, etc.  You can make these roles more or less interesting or fun depending on your preference.

 

How can students succeed?

·     Student?s motivation to learn a foreign language and to communicate and understand people of a different cultural background is of great importance in order to succeed.

·     Class attendance is of primary importance. In class you will have the opportunity to interact and work cooperatively with your classmates, listen to tapes, read short stories, discuss cultural topics, plan your writing activities, get feedback from your instructor and fellow classmates.

 

Will the instructor help a student if he/she has problems with the class materials?

·     Spanish instructors are very willing to work with students on an individual basis during their office hours. However, if a student needs constant assistance, a tutor may be recommended.

 

 How are the exams designed?

·     Teaching and assessment are integrated. Oral and written exams will provide the students with the opportunity to answer short questions (either filling in the blanks or matching words or words and illustrations, choosing from a variety of options), to provide their own meanings (writing compositions, reacting to readings, etc.).  

The techniques used in the Lower Division Spanish Program will help you develop your Proficiency in Spanish gradually. Work hard, but above all enjoy this opportunity!

 

Tips

Undergraduate course work in Spanish will not only help you become proficient in a second language and culture, but also make you a more attractive candidate on the professional market after you graduate. We want your experience with Spanish study at ASU to be both positive and fruitful. The following are some tips for success:

·         Seek help from your instructor sooner rather than later. Tutoring is available at very reasonable cost provided by the Learning Resource Center, located in the Student Services Building, room A361 (965-6658). An additional list of tutors who charge hourly fees is available from the DLL (LL 440).

·         Make the decision to profit from the course you are taking, even if you are enrolled to fulfill a requirement. Success in language study requires time, energy and commitment, especially in terms of out-of-class preparation. Full preparation is essential for active participation in class activities.

·         Your instructor will be very specific regarding his or her expectations for your performance. If you have questions related to the course, address them immediately to your instructor during an office hour, or before/after class. Questions and feedback concerning your Spanish course can also be directed to section coordinators and/or the Director and Assistant Director of Lower-Division Spanish.

·         Seek out opportunities to listen to Spanish outside of class (Univisión = channel 33 and Telemundo = channel 64). In addition, many Spanish language newspapers, magazines and radio stations are readily accessible in the Phoenix metro area. The more you interact in Spanish, the more successful your acquisition process will be.
