January 19, 2004

Cardenas to lead undergraduate outreach efforts

Raul Cardenas, Jr., director of the Rodel Community Scholars program at ASU's W. P. Carey School of Business, will be joining the Office of Undergraduate Admissions as the department's associate dean and executive director, community outreach and partnerships. In this new position, Cardenas will be responsible for the development and execution of strategic outreach initiatives that will enhance ASU's reach to prospective students in Arizona. His role will include strengthening partnerships with key school districts in the state to increase access and success for students coming from those districts.

"The vision of the new American university requires mining the pool of talent and potential that is Arizona's youth," says Jim Rund, vice president for University Undergraduate Initiatives. "Raul's skills and experience will help amplify the efforts already underway to build a university of the highest academic rank that meets the needs of a rapidly growing state."

"We will be advancing ASU's goals through building meaningful partnerships with school districts as well as individuals," says Tim Desch, dean of Undergraduate Admissions. "Raul's leadership in the education community and his personal commitment to helping students achieve a college education will help us reach our goals."

Cardenas joined the W. P. Carey School in February 2001 to build the Rodel Community Scholars program. Rodel marshals the talents of some of the best students at the W. P. Carey School to study and address high school and university retention issues. Cardenas, who began his professional career with ASU's Project Prime, has also held various leadership roles in the charter schools system. He holds a B.A. from Pepperdine, master in educational leadership from NAU, and is completing his Ph.D. in educational leadership and policy studies at ASU.

Cardenas will assume his new responsibility in early February. The W. P. Carey School has named Brennan Forss interim director of the Rodel program, and will conduct a search for a new director this spring.

Sater, with Undergraduate Initiatives, can be reached at (480) 965-2244 or (gini.sater@asu.edu).
