January 30, 2004

Comet discoverer coming to ASU for "Star Party" and space lecture

With the country riveted on space exploration, the ASU Barrett Honors College will tap into the spirit of the times with a "Star Party" Feb. 11 and a lecture, "Space Policy and America's Future: Igniting the Imagination," on Feb. 18 by Parade science editor David Levy. Both events are free and open to the public.

Levy is one of the most successful comet discoverers in history, having discovered 21 comets, eight of them using his own backyard telescope. When Comet Shoemaker Levy-9 smashed into Jupiter in July 1994, Levy and his friends Eugene and Carolyn Shoemaker were the first to see it, witnessing one of the most spectacular explosions in the solar system.

Levy and others will set up telescopes on Hayden Lawn from 7 to 10 p.m., Feb. 11, giving people the opportunity to gaze at stars and planets in the night sky and hear a presentation by Levy.

He will present the Rhodes Lecture on space policy at 7 p.m., Feb. 18, in Katzin Concert Hall, in the School of Music. Tickets are required for the free event and are available at all ASU bookstores, Borders Books throughout the Valley and Changing Hands bookstore.

Levy is visiting ASU as the John J. Rhodes Chair in Public Policy and American Institutions. He is teaching a course to honors students this spring, "From Shakespeare to space: a continuum of expression in the arts and sciences."

His discovery of the Shoemaker-Levy 9 Comet 10 years ago fulfilled a lifelong dream and spurred him to share his passion for comet hunting with others. He has written 30 books on science and astronomy, including "Skywatching," "Impact Jupiter" and his latest, "Cosmic Discoveries: The Wonder of Astronomy." He has appeared frequently on national television, including "The Today Show" and "Good Morning America."

The John J. Rhodes Chair in Public Policy and American Institutions celebrates the public service career and contributions to civic life of the Arizona congressman who was one of the nation's most distinguished leaders. Other Rhodes lecturers have included Henry Kissinger, Sen. Alan Simpson, author Jonathan Weiner and historians Donald Fixico and Jean Strouse.
