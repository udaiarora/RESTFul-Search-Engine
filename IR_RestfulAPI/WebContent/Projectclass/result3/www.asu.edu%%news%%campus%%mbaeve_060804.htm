Classes Offered at ASU Research Park This Fall: Additional East Valley Location for W. P. Carey MBA - Evening Program

The W. P. Carey MBA will offer Evening Program classes at the ASML Training Center in the ASU Research Park in Tempe, beginning in fall 2004.

The W. P. Carey MBA - Evening Program, a part-time graduate program for working professionals, also offers classes at the Arizona State University (ASU) Tempe campus and at the ASU Downtown Center in Phoenix. The ASU Research Park was chosen as the program's third venue because it is in the heart of the area where Evening Program students live and work. The ASML Training Center is located at 2010 E. Centennial Circle.

"Students who choose to go to class at the ASU Research Park location will take the same courses from the same renowned W. P. Carey School faculty as students in Tempe and downtown Phoenix," said Dr. Nancy Stephens, faculty director of the program. "These students will also be permitted to take courses at the other two locations as long as seats are available."

Benefits of the Research Park location in addition to the convenience of the location include abundant, free parking close to the facility and less traffic congestion getting to class during the afternoon rush. Plans are being considered to open a fourth W. P. Carey MBA - Evening Program location near the Scottsdale Airpark in January (the W. P. Carey MBA has no connection to the ASU West MBA).

The W. P. Carey MBA - Evening Program was ranked 17 th in the nation among part-time programs, according to the April 2004 survey by U.S.News & World Report. For more information about the program and how to apply, see wpcareymba.asu.edu/eve or call 480-965-3332

W. P. Carey School of Business

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report , the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 14th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
