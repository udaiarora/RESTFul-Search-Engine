Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

October 7, 2003

Members Present:  Sue Hunter (Chair), Patty Feldman, Beth Luey and Barbara Trapido-Lurie

Excused:  Carol Behl, Paul Scowen, Victoria Trotta

Old business

CAPS Secretary:  Barbara Trapido-Lurie agreed to serve as secretary at least through December 2003.

CAPS Archives:  Following up on last month?s discussion, Sue Hunter contacted ASU?s University Archivist to find out whether the Archives would like copies of CAPS minutes.  The Archives does keep minutes of University committees such as CAPS, but at present has no minutes after 1997.  All missing years are available on the CAPS website; Sue will print these and send them to the University Archives.  In future years, the CAPS Webmaster should send printed copies of the year?s minutes to the Archives at the end of the year.  If Annual Reports are written, they should also be sent.

Spotlight Speaker:  Sue Hunter reported that President Crow?s calendar is full through the end of 2003, so the proposal that he be the Fall Spotlight speaker isn?t possible.  However, President Crow?s calendar does include a time slot in November when he will address the AP Task Force, and possibly also CAPS, PACAPS or APs as a whole.  CAPS recommends that this time be used to address APS as a whole.  Patty Feldman, chair of the Task Force, will pass this request on the Task Force.

CAPS discussed alternate Spotlight Speakers for the Fall.  Tentatively, the Fall Spotlight presentation will focus on the plan to review AP policies and procedures, with a panel representing the AP Task Force, CAPS and PACAPS.  Sue will try to schedule a date in November for this session.

New business

AP Task Force update:  Patty Feldman reported that the task force held an initial organizational meeting in September, and will meet again the week of October 13-17.    The Task Force?s mission is currently posted at http://www.asu.edu/provost/ap.  This website will serve as the group?s principal vehicle of communication, with minutes of each meeting to be posted there.

Presentation of revised ACD Section 508 to Academic Senate:  The revised text of this section, which contains all current AP policies and procedures, was distributed to the Academic Senate for a vote at the September meeting.  Corrections were submitted, but could not be voted on due to the Senate?s requirement for a 30-day lead time in distributing documents to be brought up to a vote, and the lack of a quorum.  The document will be voted upon at a future meeting.

Committee on Committees update:  As Chair of CAPS, Sue Hunter serves on the Committee on Committees and helps assemble a request for committee members to be distributed to faculty and APs.  Sue confirmed with CAPS that the position of ?Academic Professional Caucus Convener? no longer exists, and should not appear on the ballot.

Academic Associate representation:  Carol Behl, who was appointed by CAPS to represent the University?s Academic Associates on CAPS, has a regularly-scheduled departmental meeting this year that conflicts with CAPS meetings.  Carol will propose the names of several Academic Associates who could attend CAPS meetings in her place when she is unable to attend.  (Amendment as of 10/8/03: Carol Behl will be able to attend upcoming CAPS meetings, so it won?t be necessary to select an alternate Academic Associate to attend in her place.)

Welcome message:  Sue Hunter will draft a welcome message from the CAPS 2003-2004 committee to ASU academic professionals; this message will be distributed by email to all APs after approval by CAPS.

Meeting adjourned at 10:50 am.

Submitted by Barbara Trapido-Lurie

Minutes were approved 10/20/03
