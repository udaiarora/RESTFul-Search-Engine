Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

October 5, 2004 ? Called to order 10:00 am

Members Present:  Barbara Trapido-Lurie (Chair), Beth Luey, Elizabeth McNeil, Bill Sharp, Dan Stanton, and Victoria Trotta

 

Old business

Nancy Gutierrez will be the guest speaker at the fall Spotlight program on Tuesday, December 7, 2004, in the Turquoise Room, MU 208F. Barbara Trapido-Lurie will create a flier to be linked to the committee?s web page, distributed as a link to the electronic discussion list, and posted where appropriate. Barbara Kerr, president of the Academic Senate, will be asked to speak at the spring Spotlight session. 

New business

Victoria Trotta reported on the first meeting of PAC-20. The committee discussed post-tenure review for faculty; a similar process will probably be implemented for academic professionals. At the unit level, progress toward tenure or continuing status is to be addressed separately from annual performance reviews.

 

Nancy Gutierrez joined the meeting at 10:15. She reviewed the progress of the ACD revision process. The provost?s decisions should be announced by the end of the month, followed by writing of the ACD text and implementation. It is hoped that the new policy will be drafted by the end of the 2004-05 academic year. She also discussed possible changes in the composition of the Academic Senate. She explained that the task force on Academic Advising is one step behind the Academic Professional task force in the Provost?s queue and deals with the Academic Advisors as a personnel category rather than with regard to promotion and continuing status. If the decision is made to place all academic advisors in service professional status, they will no longer be part of CAPS.

 

In response to a question from Barbara Trapido-Lurie, Dr. Gutierrez said that last spring?s Continuing Status reviews have been resolved satisfactorily and that the task force recommendations should prevent similar problems from arising in the future.

 

Dr. Gutierrez also suggested that during the coming year CAPS will be dealing with the question of how to bring together academic professionals from all the campuses.

 

The committee thanked Dr. Gutierrez for attending the meeting. She left at 10:50.

The committee discussed the work of the academic advisors task force and the appropriateness of academic professional status for various positions.

Meeting adjourned at 11:25 am
