January 20, 2006

Alumni Association battles UA in scholarship competition

Everybody loves a little friendly competition, and the Phoenix Coyotes National Hockey League team has challenged ASU and the University of Arizona to a ticket-selling competition that will help build scholarship funds.

The competition is part of the team's "Pac-10 Night," slated for Jan. 28, when the Coyotes take on the San Jose Sharks. Five dollars from each ticket sold to an ASU-affiliated fan will go to the Alumni Association's Medallion Scholarship program. If the Sun Devils top the Wildcats in terms of numbers of tickets sold, the Medallion fund will receive an additional $500.

Karen Emery, senior program coordinator for the Medallion Scholarship, said the event is a great way to connect with other faculty and staff in a recreational environment, while also doing something that benefits ASU.

"The great thing about this competition is that Sun Devil fans can become donors to ASU's Medallion Scholarship fund - and have fun doing it," she says. "We need everyone to identify themselves as an ASU fan when they buy their Coyotes tickets. Let's ice those Wildcats!"

The Medallion Scholarship program is the association's primary scholarship and provides 20 incoming ASU students from Arizona with tuition assistance, volunteer opportunities with the association and one-on-one mentoring from alumni.

Each ticket comes with a free hot dog and soda voucher, and the first 10,000 fans in the door will receive a plush "Howler" doll. All ASU-affiliated fans are encouraged to wear gold to the hockey game. The game starts at 7 p.m. in Glendale Arena, located at 9400 W. Maryland Ave. in Glendale.

Ticket prices for the game range from $23 - $38. To have a ticket counted toward ASU's part in the contest, call Randy McClusky at (623) 463-8860, or fax the order form - available online at ( www.asu.edu/alumni/events/athletics/06icethewildcats.html ) - to the Phoenix Coyotes, c/o Randy McClusky, to (623) 463-8810.
