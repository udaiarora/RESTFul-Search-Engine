December 11, 2003

New ASU award honors social embeddedness

ASU's Employee Recognition Program has added a new award that will promote projects and programs that demonstrate excellence in the work ASU employees do in the community.

"The new 'President's Medal for Social Embeddedness' supports President Crow's initiative in recognizing institutional efforts that help support, advocate for and further develop the communities in which we live and work," says Christine Wilkinson, senior vice president and secretary of the university.

The President's Medal for Social Embeddedness recognizes ASU departmental, inter-departmental or multi-disciplinary teams that have identified community needs and issues and then developed partnerships with the community to provide solutions. Programs or projects that have been implemented since Aug. 1, 2002, and that have demonstrated positive results will be considered for the award. The project must be institutional work done in the name of ASU rather than individual or group community service.

This new award complements the President's Award for Innovation, established in 1996, which recognizes ASU individual employees or teams for innovations that improve educational, administrative or other organizational processes through creative approaches. The innovation should be an original program, project, initiative or technique that has been implemented and has demonstrated positive verifiable results.

Teams and individuals who meet the criteria for these awards are encouraged to apply. Submission deadline for both applications is Jan. 22, 2004. Teams that meet the criteria for these awards will be notified in early March 2004 and recognized at a President's Reception March 24.

Winning teams will receive an award, each team member will receive a certificate and the teams will be mentioned in Insight and on ASU's Web site.

"These awards help promote collaboration, creativity and innovation among departments and disciplines," says Linda Uhley, coordinator for university recognition programs. "They help recognize and celebrate a job well done."

Writer's briefings will be held for those interested in submitting an application. Tips on effectively organizing and writing applications will be provided. Sessions will be held from 2 to 3:30 p.m., Dec. 15, at ASU West, and from 10:30 a.m. to noon, Dec. 16, at ASU Main. To register, contact Linda Uhley at linda.uhley@asu.edu or (480) 965-5089 by Dec. 12.

For application forms for the President's Medal for Social Embeddedness and the President's Award for Innovation, visit the Web at (www.asu.edu/hr/programs/erp/employee_ rec_programs.html).

 

This article appeared in the December 5, 2003 issue of ASU Insight.
