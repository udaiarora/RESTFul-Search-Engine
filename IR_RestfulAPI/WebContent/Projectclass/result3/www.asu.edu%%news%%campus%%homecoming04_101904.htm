October 19, 2004

Bigger and Better Homecoming on Tap

Building on the huge success of last year’s inaugural Homecoming Block Party, ASU will welcome the public to a gala 2004 Homecoming FestDevil Block Party on Nov. 6 that promises to be bigger than ever.

The festive event will feature bands, food from local restaurants, sports games for children and adults, a raffle, an Art Walk and an author book-signing, with art and books for sale.

Everyone is welcome at the free public event, including students, faculty and staff, graduates and non-graduates alike.

Tempe will close down University Drive between College Drive and McAllister, while more than 30 campus organizations, colleges and units set up tents to welcome back ASU alumni and friends.

Preceded by a week of campus celebrations built around an “I love the ’80s” theme, the Block Party begins with the Art Walk and Kids Zone at 10 a.m., Homecoming Parade featuring the Anheuser-Busch Clydesdales at 11:30 a.m., and the Block Party from 12:30 p.m. to 4 p.m., ending in time for the ASU-Stanford football game at 4:30 p.m.

Three stages will feature entertainment from a half-dozen bands, including local rock band Cruz and the Boomers, ASU Mariachi Diablos del Sol and the Second Shift, a group from Salt River Project. Three FM radio stations will broadcast on-site: KMLE Country 108, KOOL 94.5 and 101.5 The Zone.

An extreme sports area will challenge fun-seekers with a climbing wall, bouncy boxing, pedestal joust, NFL obstacle course and fast-speed pitch. Younger children can watch fascinating “Science is Fun” presentations and have fun at the moonwalk, bungee run, obstacle course and Cameron the Caterpillar.

A variety of food will be available from several Tempe restaurants, including Waldo’s Barbecue, Rinaldi’s Deli, Macayo Depot Cantina, Silver Mine Subs, Chompie’s, Hooters, Ducks Restaurant/Holiday Inn and Popcorn Mill. The Hispanic Business Students Association will sell carne asada with rice and beans.

The second annual Herberger Art Walk from 10 a.m. until 4 p.m., a juried show by the Herberger College of Fine Arts, will bring together alumni and graduate student artists to show and sell their work. Media categories include painting, ceramics, watercolor, photography, jewelry, fiber, digital art, printing and mixed media. Patrons attending the game can store their purchases at the University Club.

An inaugural book-signing and sale from 12:30 to 3:30 will feature a large group of talented writers who are graduates of ASU’s nationally ranked creative writing program. The juried event will feature five prominent fiction authors, including Tayari Jones (“Leaving Atlanta”), Mike McNally (“Almost Home” and “Quick”), Rich Yanez (“El Paseo del Norte: Stories on the Border”), Ed Dee (“Nightbird” and “Bronx Angel”), and H. Lee Barnes (“Gunning for Ho” and “Dummy up and Deal”).

Also attending will be poets Irena Praitis, Jorn Ake, Nick Norwood and Frances Payne Adler; children’s book author Jean Ekman Adams; writers Sharon Geyer and Edward “Bud” Jones; and renowned cartoonist Gerald Dumas, creator of “Sam and Silo” and author of “An Afternoon in Waterloo Park” and “The Complete Cartoons of the New Yorker.”

All attendees are invited to celebrate the ’80s at the Alumni Village, a giant area with food, fun, games, giveaways and several ’80s icons roaming about, hosted by the ASU Alumni Association.

Homecoming events leading up to the Block Party include a campus Spirit and Cultural Day Nov. 5, climaxing with the traditional Lantern Walk up “A” Mountain at 7 p.m. For more information on the week’s celebration events, go to (www.asu.edu/homecoming/calendar.html).

Auffret, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-6991 or (sauffret@asu.edu).
