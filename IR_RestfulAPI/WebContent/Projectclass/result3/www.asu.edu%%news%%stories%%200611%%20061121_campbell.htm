November 21, 2006

Carol Campbell wins national business award

Carol N. Campbell, executive vice president and chief financial officer of ASU, has received the 2006 Distinguished Business Officer Award by the National Association of College and University Business Officers (NACUBO), the most prestigious award given by NACUBO.

Campbell earned the recognition for her outstanding contributions to business and financial management in higher education throughout her career.

NACUBO's membership consists of chief administrative and financial officers at universities and colleges across the nation. The association, located in Washington, D.C., provides its members with the best resources, advocacy and professional development programs in higher education business and financial administration.

Campbell has been with ASU for a year and a half, but she has been extensively involved with NACUBO during the course of her career. She has served as chair of a variety of committees, planned and served as faculty in NACUBO symposiums and workshops, and testified before a congressional committee on pension reform. She also was a consultant for the Department of Education.

Campbell accepted the award at NACUBO's annual meeting in Washington , D.C.

?I was just delighted,? she said on how she felt upon hearing of the honor. ?It's very nice to be recognized by your peers.?

In the brief time she has been at ASU, Campbell already has made a strong impression on her colleagues at the university.

?Carol is a pleasure to work for,? says Ray Jensen, associate vice president of university business services. ?She is knowledgeable, experienced, candid and very supportive.?

Campbell's responsibilities at ASU include financial accounting and reporting, construction and capital planning, facilities maintenance, asset and debt management and business services.
