FAQs  regarding health insurance policy issues at ASU

Q1:    Is the State's  plan for a self-insured health insurance to be effective October 2003 still a possiblity?

 

A1)The Legislature passed a bill which the Governor signed which leaves the door open for self insurance, but  eliminates the mandate to do so by October, 2003.  The ADOA still believes that Self Insurance offers many benefits and will proceed toward that goal, but at a slower pace.  This delay provides more time to work through the concerns we raised about the process and funding. 

 Q2:    How much are (When will know if) employee health insurance premiums going up for next year?

A2)     ADOA is currently negotiating the renewal of the fully insured CIGNA contract for the coming year.  University representatives(Tri-University Work Group), including Doug Johnson, Gaye Murphy, and Christine Cervantes from ASU, have been advocating for university employees with ADOA. We are exploring ways  to best meet the university employees needs given the constraints of this negotiations process.  We expect a decision on next year's premium rates by April, 2003 with final approval from the Legislature by May.

 

Q3:    Has the state support and contribution strategy  for employee health insurance premiums been determined for the coming year?

A3) The Governor and members of the Legislature have addressed the budget crisis in different ways.  The Governor set aside $46 million to offset the cost of health insurance premiums while the Legislative budget plan provides no additional funding.  It is unclear whether the Legislative plan would address premium increases by forcing state agencies absorb the increased costs from their base budgets or requiring state employees to pay higher rates.  

 

Q4:    Are there other ways, outside the Cigna contract, that the university can meet our need for quality health insurance at an affordable cost? 

A4)  The Tri-University Health Insurance Work Group and ADOA are exploring other alternatives to meet the  health insurance needs of university employees. Our Watson Wyatt consultants confirm that there are several promising new alternatives.  We  are currently working to evaluate and reach consensus on strategy.  We need support from UofA and NAU, before we can move forward with these possibilities. We are vigorously pursuing these alternatives.
