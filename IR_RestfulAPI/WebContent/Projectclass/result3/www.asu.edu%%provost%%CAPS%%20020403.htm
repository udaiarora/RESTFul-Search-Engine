Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

April 3, 2002

CAPS Spotlight Speaker: Barbara Mawhiney, Director of Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action Office

Topic: Rationality of the Job Classification System for Academic Professionals

Present: 27 people


The federal government requires ASU to categorize jobs into EEO categories. The Board of Regents uses 5 categories:

  • Administrative
  • Service Professional
  • Academic Professional
  • Faculty
  • Classified Staff

The Academic Professional category was created out of recognition that there are people in positions that require protection (i.e., academic freedom) similar to faculty.

Over time, ranks and position names have been codified. In the late 1980's/early 1990's the AP ranks were codified and put into policy under the leadership of Alleen Nilsen. In the mid-90's, the administrative and service professional categories were added.

The Academic Associate title was created for APs to have a job title comparable to Faculty Associate. Academic Associates, like Faculty Associates are a single rank title and were never meant to be "on track". Originally, the Faculty Associate title was for people who were teaching; the Faculty Research Associate title was for people who were doing research for faculty; and the Academic Associates were engaged in a variety of activities that were neither strictly teaching nor research. In recent years many academic advisors were moved to the AP category so that now, at least 50% of Academic Associates are advisors. Academic Associates who are advisors must have a minimum of a master's degree.

About 70% of APs are currently not on track (i.e., not eligible for continuing appointment); roughly 37% of these positions are funded from grants. Most APs that are on track are either in the libraries or in science/engineering subject areas.

There are 3 categories within the AP classification:

  1. Not on Track: these are year to year contracts that require 90 days notice of non-renewal; termination can be for "no-cause" including performance issues, lack of funding, reorganization, etc.

  2. On track: these are the contracts whereby individuals are given six years to achieve continuing appointment.

  3. 3. Continuing Status: this is the AP equivalent of tenure for faculty. Continuing status is the expectation of continually receiving a yearly contract. Continuing status also guarantees academic freedom. APs with continuing status may be terminated for "cause" or for budget constraints.


Question: Why don't all three Arizona universities use the same classifications and titles in the same way?
Answer: ASU titles are also ASU specific. UA derives their own titles; they have the medical school and agriculture extension agents, etc. that contribute to UA having 2000 more APs than ASU.

Question: Many academic advisors now teach, do research, etc. Will the difference between what was originally intended for Academic Associates and what exists today ever be reviewed?
Answer: Originally, academic advisors were made Academic Associates so it would be easier to recruit and to pay them more than the classified staff category. "It isn't working; recruiting and retention have not improved."

Question: If someone is unhappy with their classified title what should they do?
Answer: For individuals, go to your supervisor and ask for career progression outside classification or ask for re-class to AP (this last request must eventually have the Provost's concurrence). For a group, you need to take the request to your college's Dean.

Question: Are APs entitled to sabbatical?
Answer: Yes, this is covered under ACD-705. Academic Professionals who have achieved continuing status and completed six years of full-time service at ASU as probationary or continuing status academic professionals.
http://www.asu.edu/aad/manuals/acd/

Question: Is there a university policy that APs are automatically members of the college assembly?
Answer: No, there is no policy; colleges and departments vary.

The EEO office tracks gender/race statistics for the progress of APs through ranks. Ms.
Mawhiney's office will take complaints for any aspect of discrimination EXCEPT denial of promotion/continuing status (although you can consult with them.) Denial of promotion/continuing status complaints should go to the Academic Professional Grievance Committee.
