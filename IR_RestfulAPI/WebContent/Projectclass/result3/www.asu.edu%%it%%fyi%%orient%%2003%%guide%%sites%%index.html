Information Technology operates four computing sites on the ASU campus. The sites are available to all students, faculty, and staff who have active ASURITE UserIDs.

Computers

Up-to-date software is available in all the computing sites, along with high-speed Internet access. Depending on the site location, you can work on a Windows or Mac computer. Scanners and Zip drives are also available. Detailed information on the sites’ hardware and software is available.

Printing

  • Black-and-white laser printing is available for a charge of $0.08 per page side.
  • Color printing is also available in the Computing Commons for $1.00 per page side.
  • All students will need to use their Sun Cards to print in the computing sites.
  • For more information on printing in the sites, see the Computing Sites Printing page.

File Storage

There are a variety of ways in which you can store your files while using the computing sites, or from your home computer.

When you are working on a computer, it is best to save files on the local hard drive. This is faster than saving to the AFS space or the floppy drive. When you’re ready to leave, copy the files to your AFS space or diskette.

Note: The storage space on the local hard drive is only temporary. You should never store anything on the desktop, since this space is erased every time the computer is restarted.

AFS Space

You can save files to your AFS space from any of the Mac or Windows machines in the computing sites. It is recommended that you always save your files first to the local hard drive and then copy them to your AFS space.

Zip Disks

Many of the computers in the sites have Zip drives in which you can use your own storage media. You can purchase Zip disks from the ASU Computer Store, or from the ASU Bookstore.

Instructor Volume

Instructor Volumes are data storage spaces for students and faculty to share documents related to a particular class. Instructors may post software, sample test questions, assignments, or handouts on the Instructor Volumes, so that students can access them from any computing site. For more information, see FYI’s Instructor Volume pages.

CD-R / CD-RW

You can also store files on CD-R or CD-RW using the Mac workstations available in some computing sites.

Accessibility

Information Technology (IT) provides a variety of Adaptive Technology (AT) for student use in the computing sites and classrooms. The Computing Commons Atrium site is open longer hours and is centrally located, housing the majority of AT equipment. IT staff will make accommodations for students in any computing site or classroom with the assistance of the Disability Resources for Students office as requested. Disability Resources for Students can be reached at 480.965.1234. Students requiring AT resources have priority on the designated workstations.

In addition, all Dell GX110 PCs in the IT Sites and classrooms have the JAWS screen reader and Zoom Text magnification software installed.

Sites supporting equipment checkout are the Computing Commons Atrium, GWC 185, ECG 150, and BAC 16. Staff are available for assistance and items can be checked out from the help center counters. The Computing Commons Atrium also offers audible rocker buttons for customers who need assistance at their workstation. In addition, some reference material is available in Braille and large print.

Students using PC classrooms in the Computing Commons have access to the IT mobile cart. The cart is equipped with a Dell 667 MHz Pentium III computer. Special software or hardware accomodations can be arranged using the mobile PC. The cart is designed for easy access to the IT network in the Computing Commons classrooms. Arrangements for help with the IT mobile cart or any other AT equipment for computer classrooms can be made by calling 480.965.3342, stopping by room 215 in the Computing Commons, or e-mailing classroom.support@asu.edu.
