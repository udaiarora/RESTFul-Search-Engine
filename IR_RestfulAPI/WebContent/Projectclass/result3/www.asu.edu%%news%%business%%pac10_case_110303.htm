November 3, 2003

Pac-10 business schools face off for first time; W. P. Carey MBA students win Pac-10 Case competition

A team of W. P. Carey MBA students swept the first-ever business case competition among Pac-10 schools, winning the honor of leading the conference defense against teams from the Big Ten at the inaugural "Rose Bowl of case competitions" coming this January.

The W. P. Carey School organized and hosted the Pac-10 competition Oct. 23-25, bringing together teams of MBA students from prominent business schools within the Pac-10. The W. P. Carey School of Business is also staging the next level of challenge: the 2004 Pac-10/Big Ten MBA Case Challenge, an academic championship between the three top teams from each conference, to be staged at Arizona State University Jan. 15-17.

"We are extremely pleased with the strong interest we had in this event from across the Pac-10 conference, and, of course, with the fine showing of our W. P. Carey MBA students," said Associate Dean Dennis Hoffman. "The Big Ten conference has conducted a case competition for years. Now that the Pac-10 has a similar structure the two conferences can face-off, paralleling the annual football rivalry. The W. P. Carey School of Business is proud to host this new tradition."

Case competitions provide business students with a forum to exercise the knowledge and skills acquired in class by developing solutions to hypothetical business problems within a set time period. Typically, students work in teams and present their proposals to a panel of judges. Teams were given 24 hours to prepare for their Oct. 25 presentations.

Top finishers included first-place Arizona State University, second-place University of Washington and third-place University of Southern California. These teams will face Indiana University, Purdue and the University of Wisconsin from the Big Ten.

The W. P. Carey team, dubbed Objective Viewpoint, was coached by management Professors Amy Hillman and Gerry Keim, and included second-year W. P. Carey MBA students Talli Brown, Nathan Kennedy, Brian Martin, Chad Meyers and alternate Matt Disbrow. The team finished first overall, and collected honors for Best Presenter, Best Q&A and Most Innovative Solution.

Judges at the Pac-10 competition included:
· Honeywell: Jim Wojciehowski, vice president of aftermarket development in the engine systems business and previously the vice president of commercial marketing; and ASU alumnus Vince Trim, vice president of contracts for the aerospace business and former acting president of the engines business.
· Cap Gemini Ernst & Young US LLC: Saverio (Sal) Barbera, senior manager, manufacturing/supply chain.
· ON Semiconductor: Joe Neel, director of planning and sustaining engineering, high frequency products division.
· Phelps Dodge Corporation: Dave Pulatie, senior vice president of human resources.
· General Dynamics: Paula Adkins, director of strategic marketing for General Dynamics' Decision Systems
· IBM: Greg Sylvester, development engineer of IBM Storage Products; and David Sawin, director, Tucson Design Center.
The Masters Consulting Group, a student organization at the W. P. Carey MBA, helped organize the competition.

W. P. CAREY SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
