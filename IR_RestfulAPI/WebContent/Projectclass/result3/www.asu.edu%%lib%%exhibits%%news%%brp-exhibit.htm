Publishing on the Borders:
30 Years of Latina/o Publishing by the Bilingual Review Press


Hayden Library Rotunda, Tempe campus
January 17 – March 9, 2007

(originally posted 1/11/07)

 

The ASU Libraries are pleased to showcase a broad selection of books published by and artwork commissioned by the internationally renowned Bilingual Review Press (BRP) . The exhibit coincides with ASU Libraries’ hosting of the 2007 Transborder Library Forum/FORO Transfronterizo de Bibliotecas from February 20-24, 2007 on the Tempe campus.

Founded in 1973 and housed at the ASU Hispanic Research Center since 1986, the BRP publishes literature and scholarship primarily by or about U.S. Latinos and Latinas. In the last 33 years, the BRP has garnered international recognition through numerous important prizes and awards (eight American Book Awards, two Western States Book Awards, a PEN Oakland Josephine Miles Award, and a Carl Sandberg Literary Award).

Language use worldwide—in English, Spanish, Apache, Chinese, French, Italian, and Portuguese, among others—has been a major focus of the Press since its inception. The BRP has produced award-winning translations and bilingual editions of many literary works, usually with facing-page translations. The BRP’s journal, Bilingual Review/Revista Bilingüe, now in its 34th year, continues to bring forth quality articles in the areas of bilingualism, bilingual education, and ethnic scholarship, as well as the best creative literature by established and emerging Hispanic writers.

The independent press has given voice to important Latina writers, such as Ana Castillo, Alma Luz Villanueva, Marjorie Agosín, Alicia Gaspar de Alba, Demetria Martínez, Elva Treviño Hart, and local novelist Stella Pope Duarte from South Phoenix, who have been overlooked by mainstream publishers. The Chicano Classics/Clásicos Chicanos series is intended to ensure the long-term accessibility of deserving works of Chicano literature and culture and includes works by celebrated authors such as Ron Arias, Aristeo Brito, Rolando Hinojosa, Miguel Méndez, Alejandro Morales, Estela Portillo Trambley, and Tino Villanueva.

Since 1998, the BRP has embarked on major new projects that include commissioning and selling lithographs and serigraphs by Chicana/o and other Latina/o artists as well as compiling and producing an ambitious series of art books and electronic publications. The first component was an award-winning book, Contemporary Chicana and Chicano Art: Artists, Works, Culture, and Education (2002). A second book, Chicano Art for Our Millennium (2004), was developed in connection with an exhibition of the same title at the Mesa Southwest Museum in spring 2004. Triumph of Our Communities: Four Decades of Mexican American Art followed in 2005.
