October 1, 2003

National convention to share entrepreneurial spirit with Hispanic youth, business leaders

Future entrepreneurs will have the opportunity to learn and become inspired by the most prominent and successful businessmen and women in the United States at the U.S. Hispanic Chamber of Commerce (USHCC) 2003 convention and business expo, Oct. 1 - 4, in Phoenix. ASU is collaborating with USHCC to host the event.

George Herrera, USHCC president and CEO, says this year's convention, which is celebrating its 24th anniversary, will provide an opportunity for students, entrepreneurs and community members to learn about the growth established through Hispanic entrepreneurship.

"This event is an entrepreneurial culmination of how Hispanics are continuing to make a difference in the business community," he says. "Right now we are seeing that businesses owned by Latinas are having the biggest impact in the growth of the Hispanic business community. Historically, the Hispanic-owned business has evolved from the service and retail sector (i.e. beauty salons and travel agencies) to the non-traditional sector (i.e. telecommunications and construction businesses). It's truly an example of the diversification of business ownership and entrepreneurship."

The USHCC Foundation is committed to giving Latino youth alternatives for life preparation and life-long learning by developing educational campaigns that nurture the entrepreneurial spirit and Latino youth.

During the four-day event future entrepreneurs can attend Bizfest, USHCC Foundation's youth entrepreneurship program that provides youth participants with a variety of experiences through hands-on business training, mentoring and personal growth aimed at increasing self-esteem and preparation for the workforce. At Bizfest, aspiring entrepreneurs will learn how to form a business plan; study time management and leadership skills ; and improve their understanding of the financial issues related to starting a business and life in general.

ASU will have an information booth set up during the free Business Expo on Oct. 3 and 4 at the Phoenix Civic Plaza, which will host more than 200 exhibitors composed of local Hispanic chambers of commerce and a delegation from Mexico.

Nancy Jordan, assistant vice president of University Initiatives, says the event is a great opportunity for ASU students and the community at large to meet some of the key players in the Hispanic business arena.

"This event is part of the foundation's youth initiative to create awareness of the business community," Jordan says. "It's a way for them (youth) to gain a better understanding of business practices and networking. Students and the community will benefit from this first-hand experience."

At the convention, ASU President Michael Crow will be one of the speakers in a special Plenary Session on affirmative action. The session is scheduled from 10:15 to 11:45 a.m., Oct. 3.

Herrera says the convention serves as an ideal setting for those who attend and participate.

"This conference brings in a lot of the movers and shakers in Fortune 500 companies," he says, "and it really provides a forum for the exchange of ideas and a place to exchange dialogues."

For a complete list of scheduled workshops and events during the U.S. Hispanic Chamber of Commerce Convention, visit the Web at (www.ushcc.com).
