International students learn English as volunteers

For eight international students at ASU, learning about America didn’t happen with just a textbook and a professor; it happened through hands-on community service.

Students from Japan, Taiwan, Italy, Colombia and the United Arab Emirates spent the last two months learning English and American culture by volunteering at nonprofit organizations such as the Salvation Army, the Tempe Community Action Agency, the ARC of Tempe, Sunnyslope High School and Hacienda Inc.

“Before I came here, I thought all Americans were rich and had a swimming pool and yacht,” says Kuo Pin Lee, a student from Taiwan who helped stock canned goods for the Tempe Community Action Agency. “I didn’t know so many people needed help. I was very surprised.”

Their community involvement experience is part of a new course offered by the ASU American English and Culture Program designed to expose students to real-world situations and submerge them in American culture to foster their English skills.

“It takes the students outside of classroom situations and right into everyday English,” says class instructor Scott Wagy.

Wagy says that students quickly learn how to connect with people culturally and linguistically in these types of situations.

“I joined the class because I like to work with children,” says Salvatore Tagliaferri, a student from Italy. “Sometimes people just need to talk. If you just talk with people, maybe you can help.”

Soshi Takayama, a student from Japan, was impressed with accommodations made for people with disabilities in the United States.

“We seldom see disabled people in Japan because there are few accommodations for them,” he says.

The students volunteered more than 140 hours in the community, and some plan to continue their efforts.

The ASU American English and Culture Program provides intensive English language instruction and a variety of cultural activities for students from more than 60 countries per year.

For information about the program, call (480) 965-2376 or visit (www.asu.edu/esl).

Breese, with the College of Extended Education, can be reached at (480) 727-5314 or (shaun.breese@asu.edu).
