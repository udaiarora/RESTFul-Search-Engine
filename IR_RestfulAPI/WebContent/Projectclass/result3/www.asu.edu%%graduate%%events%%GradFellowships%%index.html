The Division of Graduate Studies, Office of National Scholarships in the Honors College, and the Office of the Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs are pleased to announce seven workshops for students interested in applying for the Javits, National Science Foundation, Ford, and Harriett Jenkins (NASA) 3-year graduate fellowships. These workshops are designed for graduating seniors, first-year graduate students, and second-year graduate students. Faculty mentors are also welcome to attend the workshops.

Graduate students who have successfully competed for these awards in the past and some of their faculty mentors will be available at these workshops to talk about the proposal process and answer questions. In addition, faculty who received these fellowships when they were in graduate school and who now serve on NSF and Ford review panels will also be participating in the workshops.  

Copies of successful proposals and reference letters written on behalf of successful students will be available for interested faculty or students to review. Contact Pat Saldivar, Division of Graduate Studies, or Dr. Janet Burke, Honors College Office of National Scholarships, for access to these files.  

Reservations are not required for these events.
