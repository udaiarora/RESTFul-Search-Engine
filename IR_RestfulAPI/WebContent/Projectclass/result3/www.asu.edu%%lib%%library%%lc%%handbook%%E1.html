Librarians' Council

E.1: Introduction

Effective 8/22/91 .. Revised 2/3/92

The policies and procedures in this section apply to all academic professionals eligible for continuing appointment without reference to specific job title or to percentage of time employed. The only exception is in the criteria for initial appointment (Librarians' Handbook, D.1), which necessarily address the educational requirements for librarians and archivists and learning resources specialists separately.


If changes in the criteria for continuing appointment occur during an academic professional's fourth or fifth probationary year, the academic professional has the option of using either set of criteria when applying for continuing appointment. Applications for promotion must be based on current criteria.


Persons holding year-to-year appointments are not eligible for continuing appointment but may apply for promotion.


The ASU Libraries operate under the jurisdiction of the Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs. The timing of certain personnel actions, and to some extent the policies and procedures used, are dependent on guidance from that office. Each year the Assistant Dean for Personnel issues a calendar of deadlines for personnel actions; appropriate university forms are also sent at the proper time to those who need them.


All personnel actions within the Libraries take place within the context of applicable university documents. (See ACD 500) By signing a contract, a person indicates willingness to work under the conditions imposed by these documents.
