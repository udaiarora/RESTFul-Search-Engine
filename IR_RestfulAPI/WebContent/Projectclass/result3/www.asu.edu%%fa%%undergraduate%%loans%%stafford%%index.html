Federal Direct Stafford Loan

All eligible students at Arizona State University may bor loans. ASU currently awards these loans throughrow Federal Stafford student the Federal William D. Ford Direct Student Loan Program.

To apply for Stafford loans, complete the appropriate academic year FAFSA.

There are two types of Stafford student loans; subsidized and unsubsidized. Academic level, dependency status, and credit load dictate award amount.

Repayment of Principal Balance Begins 6 months after you drop below half-time enrollment, graduate or leave school. The time between leaving school and repayment is referred to as your grace period.
One-Semester Only Students Due to federal delayed disbursement rules, ASU can release only half of your approved funds at the beginning of your loan period. The second half will disburse at the midpoint of your loan period.

If your loan period is for both the fall and spring semester, the first half of your funds will disburse in the fall semester. The second half will disburse in the spring semester.

If your loan period is for one semester (this applies to students attending one semester only), the first half of your funds will disburse at the beginning of that semester. The second half will disburse at the midpoint of that semester.
New Freshman Borrowers If you are enrolled in the first year of an undergraduate program of study and you have not previously borrowed a Federal FFEL or Direct Student Loan, ASU cannot release the first installment of these loans until 30 days after the first day of classes. Refer to the online calendar for disbursement information.

Loan Limits

You should monitor your borrowed loan amounts to prevent mid-year loan adjustments. You may check the amount of federal loans you have borrowed on the National Student Loan Data System (NSLDS) Web site.

How to Start Repayment

You will need to complete Exit Counseling once you graduate or leave college. Payments should be directed to the AFSA Data Corporation in Utica, New York, the servicer for the federal government. Although the Servicing Center will communicate with you mostly by mail, you may contact the Servicing Center online or call 1.800.848.0979 (toll-free) any time you have questions or concerns regarding repayment of your Stafford loans.

Loan Discharge Information

You will not qualify for a loan discharge if you are unable to meet the physical or legal requirements (age, physical or mental condition, criminal record, etc.) of your state to work in the career for which you were training.

Stafford Loan Forgiveness Program

On October 30, 2004, President Bush signed the Taxpayer-Teacher Protection Act of 2004, which changed the requirements for teacher loan forgiveness applicable to the previously authorized level of $5,000 and established provisions under which new Stafford Loan borrowers who meet certain criteria (detailed in this letter) may qualify for teacher loan forgiveness of up to $17,500 under sections 428J and 460(c) of the HEA.
