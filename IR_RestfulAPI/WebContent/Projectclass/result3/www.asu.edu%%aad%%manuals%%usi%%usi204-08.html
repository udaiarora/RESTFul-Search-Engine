USI 204–08: Declaration of Graduation

Purpose

To establish a Declaration of Graduation for undergraduate students relating to the student’s specific degree requirements for bachelor degree completion and graduation

Sources

University Registrar’s Office
DARS Project
Individual colleges within the university

Background

A system called DARS (Degree Audit Reporting System) creates a degree audit, which is an automated record that matches courses a student has completed with the requirements of a particular academic degree program. Eligible students file a Declaration of Graduation in place of the Undergraduate Program of Study.

Policy

Undergraduate students following the curriculum requirements of the 1996–98 or later General Catalog must file Declaration of Graduation forms no later than the semester in which they earn 87 semester hours for Tempe, Polytechnic, or Downtown Phoenix campus students or 81 semester hours for West campus students. Student-athletes must file a Declaration of Graduation during their first semester at ASU. A student who does not file a Declaration of Graduation on time is prevented from further registration.

ProcedureD

Responsibility
Action
Undergraduate student
  1. Meet with an academic advisor.
Advisor
  1. Process degree audit and review with student.
Student
  1. Obtain signatures of approval on the Declaration of Graduation form from the advisor and the dean’s representative.

  2. Give the completed, signed Declaration of Graduation form to the college.
College
  1. Forward the approved Declaration of Graduation to the Graduation Section or Records & Registration at the West campus.
Graduation Section or Records & Registration at the West campus
  1. Update the Student Information System (SIS) to show Declaration of Graduation has been approved.
