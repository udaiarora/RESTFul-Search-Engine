Meet Our First Student Carilloneurs

Tom Peterson and Michaela Stephens have been selected as Arizona State University¹s first Student Carillonneurs.

Peterson is the winner of the Arizona State Savings & Credit Union & Friends $1,000 scholarship, and Stephens, who will serve as Assistant Student Carilonneur, received $500 from the Carillon Society.

They will join ASU Carillonneurs William Swayze and Kevin Snow in performing on ASU's Symphonic Carillon, which was given to the university by Associated Students of ASU in 1966.

The carillon is a memorial to ASU students, faculty and staff who have given their lives in service to their country.

Student Carillonneurs

Tom Peterson, a Glendale, Ariz., native who is majoring in music composition, has played the piano for many years. "My father is a pianist and an organist," he said, "and as a result I have played the piano for as long as I can remember."

He has had several of his compositions premiered at Arizona Music Educators Association annual concerts, and his works have been performed by the Apollo High School Band, Desert Foothills New Horizons Band, University of Texas-San Antonio Concert Choir, and by several ASU student and ASU tuba professor Sam Pilafian.

Peterson plans to write music for the ASU Symphonic Carillon, as well.

Michaela Stephens, a native of Freeport, Ill., is majoring in electronics engineering technology.

She has played the piano since she was 6, and studied the carillon for five semesters at Brigham Young University (which has a tower and cast-bell carillon).

Stephens, who lives in Chandler, said she loves playing carillons.

"It doesn¹t matter to me that the ASU Carillon is electronic rather than mechanical," she said. "As long as I can share music with lots of people, I am happy."
