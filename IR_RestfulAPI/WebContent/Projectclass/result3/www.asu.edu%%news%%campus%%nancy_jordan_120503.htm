December 5, 2003

Jordan joins mayor's office to develop Downtown Campus

Phoenix Mayor-elect Phil Gordon and ASU President Michael Crow have announced an unprecedented partnership that will position ASU senior administrator Nancy Jordan as a loaned senior executive in the Mayor's office. Jordan's primary responsibility will be to work on a joint project to establish a 12,000-student ASU campus in downtown Phoenix.

Both Mayor-elect Gordon and President Crow agreed the loaned executive exchange was just the first step in a joint venture that would benefit both the state's largest city and largest university.

"Nancy is a strong, community oriented individual," says Mayor-elect Gordon. "Not only will she be our go-to person on the ASU Downtown Campus project, she brings a wealth of experience and good relationships with her to the mayor's office - including a background in education and outreach within the Hispanic community."

"ASU is eager to work with Mayor-elect Gordon and the city of Phoenix in true partnership," says Crow. "We want to repay and reinvest in the community's commitment to ASU by loaning our executive staff to the city - an idea whose time has come and one that makes sense for both institutions. Nancy Jordan, with her many skills and years of experience working with the city and at ASU, is the ideal person to serve as liaison between Mayor-elect Gordon and the university administration. With her help, we're truly breaking new ground in serving this community."

A Phoenix resident, Jordan earned a B.A. degree at the University of California, Riverside, and a master's of counseling from ASU.

At ASU, Jordan has been responsible for managing advocacy programs, constituent outreach, special events, special programs and community relations and outreach. She also served as associate executive director of the ASU Alumni Association from 1996 to 2000.

Prior to joining the university administration, Jordan was executive director of the Genesis Program, Inc., a non-profit educational and service agency for youth; dean of school and community relations for Phoenix College; and executive assistant to the chancellor of Maricopa Community Colleges.

"Nancy will be a full partner in my administration," says Gordon. "Her vast experience in K-12 education, community colleges, and her deep roots in the Hispanic community will make her role much broader than just the ASU Downtown Campus project. But we expect to use her there first."

The mayor-elect's vision of the ASU Downtown Campus is one where students can live and learn in an urban environment. It will bring an economic boost to many of the grocery, restaurant and retail shops in downtown, infill vacant areas with new construction and be a welcome environment to the incoming creative class of entrepreneurs, teachers, scientists and educators.

"I am excited to have this opportunity to work with both ASU and the city of Phoenix on such an innovative and ground-breaking project," says Jordan. "It is a great opportunity to combine experience with a truly innovative urban project."

"The mayor-elect's plan to help attract the new economy to our central city is visionary and bold," says Crow. "We are fortunate to have a partner who will work tirelessly in helping this venture succeed as well as in finding new and innovative ways to combine our expertise in the future."

Jordan was a teacher and counselor in the Roosevelt School District. She is a member of a number of community service organizations including the Hispanic Woman's Corporation, Heritage Commission, Arizona Hispanic Chamber of Commerce, East Valley Partnership, South Mountain College Advisory Board and Hispanic Leadership Institute Advisory Board.

She is a recipient of the Distinguished Citizens Award of the Phoenix Union High School District, the Leadership Award - Mayor's Partnership Championship Awards, and Innovator of the Year award of Maricopa Community Colleges and South Mountain Community College.

Jordan, whose salary will be paid by the university, will be a senior staff member of Gordon's administration. Her loaned position is a new administrative staff function, symbolic of the partnership between Gordon's administration, the city of Phoenix and ASU.

This article appeared in the December 5, 2003 issue of ASU Insight.
