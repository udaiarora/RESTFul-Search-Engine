The study of environmental issues in Latin American has a strong presence at Arizona State University. There are ten Faculty members working on environmental issues in Latin America in a range of themes. These themes include environmental planning, cultural ecology, ecosystems, conservation biology, and botany. ASU has three study abroad programs for the research and analysis of environmental topics in the region. In the Amazon and Andes Field School, students learn about plants and ecology conservation in the Ecuadorian Amazon of Ecuador. The School of Life Sciences offers a study abroad program in tropical biology in Costa Rica and a field program to study marine mammals and ecology in the Sea of Cortes, Baja California, Mexico. ASU is the home of the Global Institute for Sustainability and the Southwest Consortium for Environmental Research and Policy. These programs focus on the study and research of environmental issues on the US/Mexico border and South America.

  • The Amazon and the Andes Field School in Ecuador
    The Amazon and the Andes Field School is located in the Ecuadorian Amazon region and offers the opportunity to learn about Ethnobotany and Neo-tropical plant Biology within a conservation context. The courses offered include the central factors associated with people's past and present use of plants, and issues that have emerged from an increased search for pharmaceutical resources in foreign lands. Students gain an appreciation for the vast biodiversity found in the Amazon and become aware of the threats to sustainability and integrity of the neo-tropical ecosystem.
  • Mexico Field Ecology
    Students will learn the biology of marine mammals and ecology of the sea lions in the Gulf of California, México. This program is collaborative with the Mexican Institution Centro Interdisciplinario de Ciencias Marinas in La Paz.
  • Graduate Courses in Tropical Studies in Costa Rica
    The School of Life Sciences through the Organization of Tropical Studies, offers graduate courses in Tropical Biology and Ecology in Costa Rica.
