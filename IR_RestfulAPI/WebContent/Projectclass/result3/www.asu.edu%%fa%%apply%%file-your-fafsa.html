File Your Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)

For most types of financial aid, you will need to complete the FAFSA, available online or at any high school in your community. This form is very user-friendly and the online version has help features built right in to assist you with the more difficult questions.

The FAFSA may be filed beginning January 1 of each year. File early to receive consideration for grants, student loans, Federal Work-Study and some scholarships. There is no charge for submitting the FAFSA.

We encourage you to try your best to submit your FAFSA by ASU's priority filing date of March 1 each year. Eligible students who apply by this date have a greater chance of receiving exhaustible priority assistance. However, if the priority date has passed, you can still submit your FAFSA anytime throughout the academic year.

All students, and parents of dependent students, should apply for a U.S. Department of Education PIN online. PINs can be used to sign your FAFSA electronically, making the application process simpler and faster.

FAFSA Questions?

Call 1.800.4FED.AID, review the FAFSA FAQ's, or visit any of ASU's financial aid offices.

FAFSA Tips

  • Do not leave answers blank.
  • Answer all questions accurately.
  • List ASU as a college choice — ASU's Federal School Code is 001081 for all campuses.
  • Enter your name as it appears on your Social Security card.
  • Enter your Social Security Number and date of birth accurately and clearly.
  • Keep copies of all documents used to complete the FAFSA.
  • Register with Selective Service if required.
  • Send your FAFSA to the U.S. Dept. of Education, not to ASU.
  • If have a spam blocker on your email inbox, add cpsnotify@cpsemail.ed.gov to your "safe list" to ensure that you receive correspondence from the U.S. Dept. of Education about your FAFSA.

Eligibility Criteria

Along with submission of the FAFSA, you must meet minimum eligibility criteria to receive aid consideration.
