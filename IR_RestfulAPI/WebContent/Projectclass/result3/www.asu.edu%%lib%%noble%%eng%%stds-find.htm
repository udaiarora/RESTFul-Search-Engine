Technical Standards:
Finding Standards

 

Use the following search tools to find an unknown standard number or to do a subject search to see if a standard exists for a specific subject area.

 

  • Index and Directory of Industry Standards. Englewood, CO: IHS, 1988/89-
    T 59.2 .U6 I5 1989 Science Reference Desk
    This multi-volume set is divided in two parts, U.S. Standards and International Standards.  For both parts the index offers a subject index and a concordance grouping standards according to their sponsoring body.  ANSI approved standards are identified by asterisks in the U.S. concordance.

 

  • Annual Book of ASTM Standards Index Volume. Philadelphia, PA: American Society for Testing and Materials, 1970- . Annual.
    TA 401 .B6 Vol. 00.01 Science Reference (Standards Area)
    ASTM publishes its standards in this annual multi-volume set. The index volume references a standard by keyword or phrase and by standard number, and gives the volume number where it appears. Since the index is the last volume to be published, use the previous year's index to find what volume your standard is published in. Each volume of the set is also labeled by general subject categories.

FAQ

 

What is a standard?
"Standards and specifications are documents that stipulate or recommend 1) minimum levels of performance and quality of goods and services, and 2) optimal conditions and procedures for operations in science, industry, and commerce, including production, evaluation, distribution, and utilization of materials, products, and services."
Subramanyam, Krishna. Scientific and Technical Information Resources.
T10.7 .S93 Science Reference

 

Can standards be checked out?
The standards in Noble Library are non-circulating, meaning they cannot be taken out of the library or checked out. Some may be photocopied within the library.

 

How can I obtain a standard that is not owned by ASU?
In most instances the only option present to acquire a standard not owned by ASU is to purchase the item from the sponsoring agency or through document sellers. Order information can be found in the Index and Directory of Industry Standards or through the web sites of the sponsoring bodies. IHS's Global Engineering Documents (http://global.ihs.com/) and ILI (www.ili-info.com) are two companies who sell U.S. and international standards through their web sites. They offer mail, fax and electronic copies of the standards.
