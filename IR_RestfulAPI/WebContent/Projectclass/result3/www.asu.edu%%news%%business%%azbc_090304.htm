September 3, 2004

Arizona Business Conditions Index declines in August

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index fell to 62.0 in August from 67.2 the previous month, a 2 percent increase. An index reading over 50 indicates that the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

"In August, the index dipped out of its oscillation in the mid-60s that has held for almost a year," said Dawn McLaren, research economist for the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU's W. P. Carey School of Business. "This is the index's lowest level since September 2003. It should be kept in mind that this decline has not established a trend at this point, and the overall index remains well over the critical 50-point mark."

The employment component declined sharply in August, from 58.4 to 51.7. "This component has been struggling throughout the recovery, especially when compared to other components such as new orders and production," McLaren said. "Since the last recession, the employment component has reached beyond the 60-point level only five times compared to the overall index's 12 times. Other than those five times, employment has remained in the low 50s, close to neutral."

The Price Index, after having reached the third-highest level in its 42-year history, fell by 10.4 points in August to 63.6. This may be the result of a decline in overall demand, or a signal that supply has increased enough to meet demand. "It is not clear at this point which of these two factors we are observing in Arizona business conditions," McLaren said, "but the 8.8 point decline in the purchases component of the overall index suggests that it may be the former."

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available online at www.wpcarey.asu.edu/azbci . The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data available in prn format.
