First Batch of Staff Workstations Arrives

LIST has developed a three-year life cycle for all staff and patron computers in the Libraries. For the first year of this life cycle implementation, we've ordered over 170 new computers. The new PCs arrived early this week and have been inventoried, labeled, and sorted by department for distribution to staff throughout the Libraries. Since the placement process of staff workstations involves an interaction with each staff member to transfer personal work files to the new machines, the process may take 3 to 4 weeks to complete. In the past weeks, LIST has met with departmental representatives to explain the process, answer questions, and adjust the placement schedules to provide the least amount of disruption to the staff members involved in this first phase of the replacement cycle.

Reference Linking Vendor Demos

This week we were pleased to host a vendor presentation/demonstration of Article Linker, the link resolver provided by Serials Solutions. If you attended this session and haven’t turned in your evaluation of the product, please be sure to do so – your input is critical to us in deciding on the product that will best meet the Libraries’ users’ needs.

Next week (Thursday, May 5, 10:00-11:30 in C6) we will host Ex Libris, Inc. This vendor will demonstrate their reference linker, SFX. We hope you’ll attend this session and give us your input on the product.

Don’t forget you can also learn more about reference linking from the Reference Linking Task Group’s informative web page, available at http://www.asu.edu/lib/systems/openURL/.


Libraries Web Site Redesign Progress

After nearly two years of planning work, the new libraries web site is about to come online. You can see a preview of the home page at http://www.asu.edu/lib/. User testing has just been completed on the new home page and it’s time to start moving content! LIST will be migrating existing top-level and departmental content into the new site templates over the course of the next three weeks, and will have the new site ready for preview by library staff no later than May 23rd. If you have any questions, comments, or concerns about this process, please contact Heather Knowles.


University ID Update Project

Arizona State University has started a project to convert all affiliates who are using a Social Security number as their University ID number to a 993 number. The first phase is to convert benefits-eligible faculty and staff to their new numbers before June 30, 2005. Other populations will follow. Employees who are converted will be advised of the change before it is made. The first test batch will be converted the week of April 28, 2005. LIST has made some changes to the way patron records are loaded so that the targeted records can be overlaid with a minimum of disruption to the users’ library services. If you have any questions about this project, please contact Fred McIlvain.
