TRAVELING SCHOLARS

The Traveling Scholars Program of the Arizona University system enables a student to take advantage of programs or special resources available on another state university campus not available at their own institution. Traveling scholars are approved for a period of one semester, however, this may be extended by mutual agreement of both institutions. A degree-seeking student, enrolled full-time at one state university (Sponsoring University) may enroll for one or more courses at a second state university (Host University) and receive a waiver of admission requirements and application fee at the second university, and a guarantee of acceptance of credits earned by the sponsoring institution at which the student is seeking a degree. To be considered for the Traveling Scholar Program, a student must meet the following qualifications:

Good Standing; meet the minimum requirement for cumulative GPA:
                        2.5 for undergraduates
                        3.0 for graduate students

Full-time Enrollment at ASU, NAU, or U of  A  in the same semester that the student will be designated as a traveling scholar:
                        7 credit hours for students paying resident tuition
                        12 credit hours for students paying non-resident tuition

Degree-Seeking:
                        Enrolled full-time in a degree-seeking program at the sponsoring university.
 

As a participant in the traveling scholar program the student may receive:

Waivers from the Host University:
                        admission requirements
                        application fees
                        tuition fees

Guarantee from the Sponsoring University:
                        acceptance of earned credits
