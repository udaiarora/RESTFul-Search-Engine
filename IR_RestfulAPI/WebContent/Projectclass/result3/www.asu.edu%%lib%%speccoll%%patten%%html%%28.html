Colonna, Fabio

1567-1650.

Fabii Columnae Lyncei Minus cognitarum rariorumque
nostro coelo orientium stirpum [ekphrasis]: qua non pauce ab antiquioribus
Theophrasto, Dioscoride, Plinio, Galeno alijs[que] descriptae praeter illas
etiam in [Phytobasano] editas disquiruntur ac declarantur: item De aquatilibus
aliisque nonnullis animalibus libellus.

Omnia fideliter advivum delineata, atque aneis--typis expressa cum indice
in calce voluminis locupletissimo. Romae: Apud Jacobum Mascardum, 1616.
Purpura: & aliorum aquatilium observationes. Omnia ferme nunc primum
edita. The Aquatilium (p. i-lxxiii) has caption title: Aquatilium et
terrestrium aliquot animalium, aliarumq. Naturalium rerum observationes.
Part 2 has separate t.p.: Fabii Columnae Lyncei Minus cognitarum stirpum,
pars altera. Purpura ([8 ], 42 p. at end) has separate t.p.:
Fabii Columnae Lyncei Purpura: Hoc est de purpura ab animali testaceo
fusa. Artwork and engraving have been attributed to the author. References:
Pritzel 1821; Nissen, C. Botanische Buchillustration (2. Aufl) 385. Includes
indexes.

Colonna was both author and engraver for this and Phytobasanos.
His descriptions are very complete, giving information about location as
well as botanical details.  Colonna uses a variety of authorities
to give credence to his descriptions.  This work features 152 etchings
of plants and animals, showing fine detail for the first time.
