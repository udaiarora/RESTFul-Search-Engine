Standards for Firm Order Cancellations
 

Firm orders may be cancelled any time at the selector's request.  Vendors may also cancel firm orders if they are unable to supply the material. Acquisitions staff will take the initiative to cancel firm orders when they are claimable for the third time (180 days from order date for domestic orders; 260 days for foreign orders).  Firm orders may be redirected if acquisitions staff determines that the material may be available from another vendor or from the publisher. 


Guidelines for canceling firm orders

  1. Out-of-print and not available from other vendors

  2. Not yet published -- no due date promised 

  3. Publication abandoned

  4. Out of stock indefinitely

  5. Materials that go out of date quickly (e.g., computer software texts, directories)

  6. Sole source for materials, but vendor unresponsive


Guidelines for not canceling firm orders

  1. Not yet published, but due within six months

  2. Available from another vendor (redirect)

  3. Vendor history of supplying more slowly than the claim cycle

  4. Selector has indicated that he/she does not want the order cancelled

  5. Special order materials (e.g., pre-publication offers, Special Collections orders)

  6. Prepaid items


When orders are not appropriate for redirection or cancellation (see guidelines above), the order will remain open.


If materials arrive after the order has been cancelled and if the materials has not been received from another source, the order will be reinstated and the material will be received.


Selectors will be notified monthly of cancellations. Selectors will be sent a list of cancelled orders monthly, and may request that specific orders be reinstated or redirected if they feel it is appropriate.
