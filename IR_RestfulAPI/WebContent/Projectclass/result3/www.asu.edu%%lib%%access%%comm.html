University Libraries'
Community Card Loan Policy



The University Libraries are open to all users.  An individual not affiliated with ASU, through current enrollment or employment, who is an Arizona resident and meets certain age requirements (proof of residency and age required), may borrow library items by purchasing a Community Card.  The card must be applied for and renewed in person. There is an annual fee of $100. Borrowing privileges are not transferable. Interlibrary loan requests require the purchase of the $500 ILL Card.  Remote access is limited to viewing the ASU Library Catalog only, no online renewal or requests. (See Important Reminders).

Circulation Information

The Community Card entitles the borrower to a 14-day loan period for most items, as well as other circulation services, but no Interlibrary Loan (except for the Interlibrary Loan Card at $500). A borrower may check out up to fifteen (15) items. You must present picture identification each time you borrow an item.

 

Date Due

At the time of loan, the borrower will be notified of the due date for each item. The borrower is responsible for meeting the due date. Certain items, e.g., reserve, media, special permission items are short-term loans with different late fees. A courtesy overdue reminder will be sent. Failure to receive an overdue reminder, for whatever reason, does not remove the responsibility of the borrower for meeting each due date.

 

Overdues

For each regular item not returned or renewed within thirty  (30) days of the due date, the borrower is assessed a $10 overdue billing fee and a replacement charge. Only the replacement charge is cancelled when the item is returned. Once the fee has been paid, the item may be renewed.
A borrower with outstanding charges is denied borrowing privileges. An appeals process is available. and must be made within one year. Information may be obtained by contacting the appropriate Billing office. Contested library charges are only reviewed internally.

 

Renewals

Community card borrowers may make up two (2) renewals for each item.  Renewable Law Library items may be renewed two (2) times.  Items recalled by another borrower cannot be renewed.  Renewals may be done at any ASU Library by bringing the items to the Circulation Desk.  Renewals may also be processed over the phone at (480) 965-2595. Phone renewal will not inform you of your new due date(s). Call (480) 965-3605 after 48 hours to confirm renewal.  Certain items, e.g., reserve, media, microform are not renewable.

Please note: The library has transitioned to ASURITE login for all resources, including "My Account." As a result, Community Card users now need to call 24-hour telephone renewal at (480) 965-2595 to renew items. For other "My Account" functions, such as Request an Item or View checked out items, call the Circulation Desk at 480-965-3605. This number is available 24/5 Sunday- Friday and until midnight on Friday and Saturday.


NEW!
Registered Community Borrowers may now request books and renewals online without an ASURITE UserID by using the Renew/Reguest Materials form available online at http://library.west.asu.edu/services/borrowing/request/index.cfm

 

Recalls

Items that are checked out may be requested by other borrowers at any time.  All items are subject to recall.  Due to the strict enforcement of the recall process, failure to receive a recall notice does not absolve borrower from knowing an item is recalled.  It is important for all borrowers to regularly review the status of their library account, checking for recalls by contacting circulation.  Borrowers should also select the best method (print or email) for receiving notices, appropriately manage their email accounts and SPAM software, and/or keep mailing addresses valid, including checking departments if on payroll.  If there are other requests at the time of checkout, no notice is generated and the borrower is given an automatic 14-day, "no renewal, no fine free period" checkout.  This recall policy applies all year long: breaks, sabbaticals, vacations, etc.  Recall of an item may shorten the original loan period.  Items not returned by midnight on the recall due date are billed the replacement cost and a $10.00 fee per item.  Recall late fees are strictly enforced and only the replacement cost is cancelled when the item is returned.  If the library is closed, use the 24-hour bookdrop.

 

Abuse of Library Privileges

The library will suspend library privileges and block the account of Community Card borrowers who fail to adhere to library policies or who owe money. Failure to return library materials and pay overdue fees may result in further collection procedures, including the possibility of action by a Collection Agency.

 

Important Reminders

  • You are responsible for items checked out on your ID and any fees.
  • No proxies.
  • You must report a lost ID to the library.
  • You are responsible for keeping your mailing/e-mail address current with the library.
  • Certain library departments and library branches have individual loan and fee policies for various types of materials. Ask for this information when you use other library departments.
  • Community Card patrons may borrow media with shelf status by going to the owning library and checking the item out. Community Card patrons may not place a request on media that is checked out or have media paged from another ASU Library.
  • Items must be returned to an ASU library book return or Circulation Desk in order to be properly checked-in. Most items may be returned to or renewed at any ASU library branch. When the library is closed, use the 24-hour bookdrop (certain items such as Reserve and Media items cannot be returned through the 24 hour bookdrop).
  • Internet access is available for the Libraries' Online Catalog. While Community Card borrowers are not able to access proprietary licensed databases such as Encyclopedia Britannica or journal indexes remotely, access to these databases is available to all users in any library branch.
  • If you wish, you can have your notices sent to your  e-mail address by contacting any library Circulation desk.
