Librarians’ Council

Minutes

March 15, 2006

Hayden C6A


Meeting was called to order at 3:04 pm.


A quorum was present.


I. Approval of Minutes – (November 16, 2005) - Minutes were approved as amended.


 

II. Chair and Executive Committee Report

  • In January 2006, Executive Committee decided to remove the Marian the Librarian packets from the Librarians’ Council website. The packets were more hindrance than help. The copies of those documents will be sent to Archives.

III. Dean's report

  • Salary: Dean Schmidt talked about the across the board salary raise ($1650; retrospective to March 11th; will be in May15th pay check) and the merit based pay raise. She clarified the changes done to AP Merit Level form with advice from the Executive Committee of Librarians’ Council and Compensation Committee. PEs are advised to talked to APs about that.
  • Downtown campus library: Admin is moving forward with the search for the position of Director. Shelving will be ordered for the Downtown Campus library; money has been appropriated for books.
  • R2 recommendations: Administrative Council, invited APs and classified staff will work on the vision and mission statement at the planned retreat on March 22.

IV. Committee Reports

a. Personnel Committee - JoAnn Mulvihill

    • The committee is working on the Orientation Program (J.1) section of the Librarians’ Handbook. It will be ready for the review next month.


  • No information is available regarding the approval of ACD 507 by Provost's office.

b. Research and Professional Development Committee - Mark Scott -

  • No Report

c. Compensation Committee - Mimmo Bonanni


  • The committee met with Kurt in February to discuss salary comparison & equity. Committee also discussed things like housing market, etc. Handout was given on the ARL Salary comparison between peer institutions. The average level (not the entry level) salary at ASU libraries is at 12%.

d. Review Committee - Ginny Sylvester - No Report


e. Academic Senate - Deborah Koshinsky


  • Academic Assembly elections are coming up – March 24-31. Deborah will include a link to the election portion of the Academic Senate Web site when she will send out the recent minutes, so that you can read the candidate statements – which include those running for Academic Professionals Grievance Committee and for CAPS (Academic Professionals Status Committee).


Monday December 5:

Milton Glick:

  • He likes new faculty P & T proposal, esp. the one-university P & T Committee model it advocates. Further, he stated that by having a Senate-elected representative on the committee, it will be a considerable empowerment for the Faculty.


Marjorie Zatz:

  • Reported on the number and disposition of tenure and promotion cases from all the campuses (2003-2005). As we did not receive a handout and the totals were not summarized in the minutes, I cannot relay too many specifics. However, it was observed by one faculty member that the number of P & T cases dropped from 39 in 2003 to 25 in 2005. Marjorie Zatz indicated when asked if this is a result from depts. holding back cases that she did not think so. It could possibly be more a result of the numeric variances that occur in hiring cycles.

 


f. Committee on Academic Professional Status - Lydia LaFaro for Dan Stanton


  • CAPS Spotlight speaker series:
    Committee is looking for ideas, otherwise it will be cancelled.


  • CAPS Discussion list:
    All the APs are invited to join the discussion list so as to a forum for an AP dialogue.
  • A new task force has been formed to establish a University-wide Review Committee. Kurt Murphy is serving on this task force along with other members from the original AP Task Force.



g. Web Master - Yuwu Song - no report.


V. Old Business


VI. New Business

  • Community Service Opportunity - Linda Shackle
  • National History Day, April 8th: State Library needs volunteers to serve as a judge for different competition. Please contact Linda Shackle if interested.

Meeting adjourned 3:30 p.m.
