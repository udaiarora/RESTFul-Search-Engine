Archives exhibits take you on ‘Arizona Road Trip’

(posted 5/24/04)

Through July 23, Arizona State University’s Luhrs Gallery brings the Arizona road to life – as well as the magic of an old-time road trip – with four exhibits, grouped under the title “Arizona Road Trips.”



Click the image above for a video preview.
  • “Signs and Symbols: The Arizona Documentary Series” is a group of photographs taken throughout Arizona by the late Frank P. Hoy, former professor of photojournalism at the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication.
  • “On the road Again: Route 66” chronicles the mystical, mythical road that stretched 24 hundred miles between Chicago, Illinois and Santa Monica, California.
  • “Superior-Miami Highway, Route 60,” by ASU archivist Chris Marín, tells the story of a “road trip” from Globe to Phoenix in the summer of 1951.
  • “Arizona: By the Book” includes books by Arizona authors, a collection of pop-up books and books about Arizona by writers living elsewhere.

Three related videos are also available for viewing: “Arizona Crossroads: Historic Route 66" and "Arizona Crossroads: 66 Memories,” by John Craft and Fran Matera, from the Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication; and “Silver, Copper and Sweat: The Story of Superior,” by Paul John, providing a historic look at the mining town of Superior, Arizona.


Luhrs Gallery, located at ASU’s Tempe campus on the fourth floor of Hayden Library, is open from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday through Friday.
