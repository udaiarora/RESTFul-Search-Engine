Simmons Study of Media & Markets
Choices II Software
Instructions

These instructions are designed to help you set up a demographic profile of users of your product/services category and your brand & top competitors similar to what was available in the print edition of the Simmon's Study of Media & Markets. This is the simplest report to create and is called a CROSSTAB. A Media profile crosstab report can also be done when you understand how to set up a basic crosstab report.

The basics are:

1. CLICK the Choices icon:

2. Enter the workstation's ID: guest     then

3. CLICK on RUN CHOICES II.

When the software opens,

4. CLICK on STUDY on the top menu bar to

A. SELECT the year & edition you wish to use.

B. When the little window pops up with POP/HHLD,

1. CLICK on POP to have your information arranged by age groups. HHLD will arrange your information by households.

The next selection, by program default, is to pick the headings for your COLUMNS (columns run from the top to the bottom of the page).

5. In the upper right quadrant of the screen is a place to SELECT your DATA ACCESS information.

A. Type in a brief name for your product group (example: beer)

B. CLICK on the SEARCH button to the right.

From this point on, the computer processing slows the responses that you see on the screen. Do NOT be hasty in clicking on various buttons or you could wipe out your search!

A window will open in the lower right & one on the lower left.

6. From the left one, scroll until you find the right category for your product

example: under ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES (in black type), scroll until you see the correct type of beer in red type : draft beer, ice beer, dom(estic) beer, etc.

7. DOUBLE CLICK on the first category of the product you want to use.

example, the first category is the number of users. This will give you the universe of total users & non-users of your product group.

This has placed the number of users of your product in the window to the right.

8. Each product category has subcategories. You MUST select the subcategories you want displayed or the computer shows an empty set (no categories) to search & nothing happens. In the example, the Number of Users usually has 3 subcategories: YES, NO, DON'T KNOW.

A. To SELECT ALL subcategories:

1. CLICK & DRAG from the first one on the list to the last one & release the button. This is the one category that you really do want to select all of them.

2. CLICK on the SEND button just below the subcategories list on the right. (The coding for this product category & subcategories should appear in the upper left quadrant in the CODING section.)

9. To add more categories to your COLUMNS, you may make additional selections for your product (or change products completely) by scrolling up & down the category list. Within your product category are entries that say BRANDS... or say (Volumetrics... ). You may select these for additional COLUMNS, but you probably want the subcategories for your brand and only one or two top competitor brands for comparison. You may select these multiple subcategories by CLICKING individually on each subcategory you wish to included. (Example: Coors, Miller, Budweiser, Pabst).

10. After you select each category & desired subcategories,

A. CLICK on the SEND button just below the subcategories list on the right. When you have completed the list of COLUMNS you want to display,

B. CLICK on the OK button in that same window.

When you see the categories and subcategories listed on your WORKSHEET area (the lower ½ of the opening screen), you are ready to select your ROWS.

11. CLICK on the radio button to select ROWS/MEDIA

12. In the upper right quadrant of the screen is a place to SELECT your DATA ACCESS information.

A. Type in a brief name for your population group or type of media (example: age)

B. CLICK on the SEARCH button to the right.

A window will open in the lower right & one on the lower left.

13. From the left one, scroll until you find the right category for your product

example: under AGE, Head of Household Demographics (in black type), scroll until you see the correct age group in red type : Age - Household Head, etc.

14. DOUBLE CLICK on the age category you want to use.

example: Age - Household Head will give you the total age profile of Heads of Households.

This has placed the age groups of your demographic group in the window to the right.

15. If your previous work still shows,

A. CLICK on the X in the upper right corner of this window to close it & clear it.

B. DOUBLE CLICK again on the age category you want to use. (Carolyn & Lydia: The RESET button did not seem to work, or took forever to clear.)

This has cleared the previous information and placed the age grouping you selected in the window to the right.

16. Each demographic category has subcategories. You MUST select the subcategories you want displayed or the computer shows an empty set (no categories) to search & nothing happens.

A. To SELECT ALL age subcategories:

1. CLICK & DRAG from the first one on the list to the last one & release the button. This is the one category that you may really do want to select all of them.

2. CLICK on the SEND button just below the subcategories list on the right. (The coding for this age category & subcategories should appear in the upper left quadrant in the CODING section.)

17. To add more categories to your ROWS, you may make additional selections for your demographics (or change to MEDIA) by scrolling up & down the category list. You may select these for additional ROWS, but you probably want the subcategories for your target age group and only one or two other age groups for comparison. You may select these multiple subcategories by CLICKING individually on each subcategory you wish to included. (Example: ).

18. After you select each category & desired subcategories,

A. CLICK on the SEND button just below the subcategories list on the right. When you have completed the list of ROWS you want to display,

B. CLICK on the OK button in that same window.

When you see the categories and subcategories listed on your WORKSHEET area (the lower ½ of the opening screen), you are ready to send the coding request.

19. CLICK on the OK button in the CODING section in the upper left part of the main screen.

The computer prepares to send your command to do the search.

20. Next, CLICK on WORKSHEET on the TOP MENU BAR.

21. SELECT DISPLAY

22. SELECT DESCRIPTOR (This changes your column & row headings to descriptor words not codes)

23. Next, CLICK on FUNCTION on the TOP MENU BAR.

24. SELECT CROSSTAB and wait for the Excel spreadsheet to show.
