March 8, 2005

Memorial scholarship honors Craft’s achievements at ASU

The National University Telecommunications Network (NUTN) has established a memorial scholarship in honor of Betty Craft, the former director of Distance Learning and Technology in ASU’s College of Extended Education who died in 2004.

The Betty Craft Professional Development Scholarship will be given each year to an ASU graduate student or staff member who is new to the field of distance learning.

The scholarship will go toward paying registration fees for the organization’s annual national convention, and will allow those new to the discipline of distance learning to gain knowledge about the field. It will continue the long mentoring tradition that Craft established during her 20-plus years as an ASU administrator.

Craft was the founding director of Distance Learning and Technology at ASU, a department she established in the College of Extended Education. She began her career in the late 1970s as an adjunct faculty member in what is now the Hugh Downs School of Human Communication. Craft then was appointed the instructional television coordinator for ASU and later became director of the interactive instructional television program.

During her two-decade career at ASU, Craft developed a multichannel TV system that allowed the university to distribute courses to students located at high-tech manufacturing sites across the Valley. With the advent of the College of Extended Education , she used Internet technology to expand ASU’s educational offerings to a worldwide student body, which gave ASU an early position of prominence in distance learning.

Craft, an internationally recognized advocate of distance learning, was a longtime member of the NUTN’s advisory board. She served as an officer of that organization, and in May 2004 the association named her as the recipient of its J.O. Grantham Leadership Award for outstanding contribution to distance learning at the national and international levels. It is the association’s top honor.

The first Betty Craft Professional Development Scholarship will be awarded at the NUTN convention in San Francisco June 11 –13.

For more information and an application form, contact John Craft, the director of graduate studies in the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication, at (480) 965-2805 or (j.craft@asu.edu)
