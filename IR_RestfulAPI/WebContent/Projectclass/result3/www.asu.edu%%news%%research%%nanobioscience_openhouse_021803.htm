February 18, 2003

Center for Applied NanoBioscience to host open house

Members of the ASU community are invited to an upcoming open house for the Center for Applied NanoBioscience.

The open house is scheduled from 2 to 4 p.m., Feb. 20, in Engineering Center A-wing, room 300 (east end of the building across from the ASU Bookstore).

The event will also include a ribbon cutting at 11 a.m., acknowledging corporate contributions by Motorola and General Dynamics. Demonstrations to illustrate the center's capabilities will be given and refreshments will be available.

The Center for Applied NanoBioscience (ANBC) at ASU is part of the newly formed Arizona Biodesign Institute (AzBio). ANBC will provide a strong applied science and technology thrust as part of AzBio and its affiliation to the College of Engineering & Applied Sciences. The center will emphasize the application of advances in nanoscience, molecular biology and genomics to a new generation of enabling biological tools based on nano and micro scale technologies. The objective is to foster the potential of Nano-Bio-Science in breakthrough applications through integration of interdisciplinary research developments in biologics and technological devices.

A top-down approach will enable the development of integrated micro fluidic "lab-on-a-chip" platforms and advanced optical and electronic chip arrays for the detection and analysis of biological fluids. Presently, the activities are centered on the following main themes:

. Integrated micro/nanosystems design, modeling and prototyping (e.g. lab-on-a-chip, DNA & protein arrays);
. Development of fabrication and characterization techniques optimized for nanoscale applications (e.g. nanoimprinting);
. Molecular techniques for manipulating single bio-molecules and cells (e.g. electromagnetic field assisted manipulation in confined nanostructures); and
. Integrated systems for application-oriented products and information solutions (e.g. point-of-care diagnostics).

For information about the open house, call (480) 965-4808.
