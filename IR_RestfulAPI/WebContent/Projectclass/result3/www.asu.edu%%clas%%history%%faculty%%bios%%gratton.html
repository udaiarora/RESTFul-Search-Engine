Research and Teaching Interests

During 1996?97, Dr.  Gratton worked as a National Institutes of Health ( NIH ) Fellow at the Population Research Center of the University of Texas at Austin, developing a new series of representative samples of the Hispanic population in the United States between 1850?1970. In the spring of 1996, he was a Fulbright Fellow in Barcelona, Spain. Each of these fellowships contributed to his current research interest in Mexican-American and Latino immigration and migration history. In 1999, Dr.  Gratton received a two-year research grant from the NIH to fund the project ?Assimilation Trajectories in Mexican-American Families.? In 2002, he was a Fulbright Research and Lecturing Fellow in Ecuador, where he spoke on immigration conditions in the U.S. and worked with European and Ecuadorian researchers investigating emigration from that country. His previous work, summarized in Old Age and the Search for Security (Indiana, 1994), focused on social security, retirement, and the history of the elderly. He teaches courses in immigration and ethnicity, economic history, and twentieth-century social history.
