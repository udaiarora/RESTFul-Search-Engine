It's a Dry Heat - Biological Adaptations for Life in the Arizona Desert

(originally posted 8/8/06)


The ASU Libraries’ Exhibits Committee is pleased to announce the latest exhibit in Noble Library.


"It's a Dry Heat - Biological Adaptations for Life in the Arizona Desert" will run through December 2006.


The exhibit is a collaborative effort between the School of Life Sciences (SoLS), the Natural History Collections in SoLS, and ASU Libraries and includes some materials from the Arizona Game and Fish Department. Get to know Arizona's native living creatures and learn how they survive in the harsh desert climate. The exhibit features a Virtual Aviary, courtesy of Ask a Biologist, where you can view images of the birds of Arizona and listen to their calls and songs. Come into the library, cool off, and learn more about those amazing organisms that call the Arizona desert home.


The exhibit is viewable during library hours.


For more information on the exhibit partners, please visit the following sites:

If you would like to propose an exhibit, please visit the Exhibits Policy page or contact Katherine O'Clair, Exhibits Committee Chair at 480-965-5964 or katherine.oclair@asu.edu.
