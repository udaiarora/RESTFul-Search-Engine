January 23, 2006

ASU Scottsdale Innovation Center breaks ground

It was a day that many had waited years to celebrate. Partners of SkySong, the ASU Scottsdale Innovation Center on the former Los Arcos Mall site, broke ground on the project Jan. 20 in a ceremony attended by more than 200 people. 

Surrounded by plasma panels and color renderings of the project, Sharon Harper, president and CEO of The Plaza Companies, kicked off the ceremony with introductions of the development partners and local civic and business leaders on hand for the event.  At the close of the ceremony, Harper announced that SkySong had its first two tenants — eFunds and Wildfire Broadband Communications.  eFunds provides payment processing solutions to businesses and anticipates the move to the center will provide the opportunity to promote and accelerate its innovation processes as part of its solution strategy.  Wildfire Broadband, a provider of wireless solutions to the private and public sector, will provide wireless services at the center in addition to being a tenant.

eFunds and Wildfire Broadband will be joined at the center by the ASU Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative, Arizona Technology Enterprises (AzTE) and ASU Technopolis. ASU’s InnovationSpace and the Arts, Media & Engineering (AME) programs are also slated to expand or move to the center.  The university is also in discussion with the Scottsdale Unified School District and the Maricopa County Community College District regarding participation and expansion of education-related programs at SkySong.

Construction on the 1.2 million square foot project begins in March with occupancy scheduled for early 2007.  The project promises to be a hub for innovation, technology and business to stimulate new forms of global commerce, research, technology, art, education and economic development. 

During the ceremony, Harper read excerpts from congratulatory letters from Governor Janet Napolitano and U.S. Sens. John McCain and Jon Kyl. SkySong’s various development partners and economic development leaders followed with comments acknowledging the importance of the project and thanking those who made it happen. 

Among the comments shared by the speakers:

• City of Scottsdale Mayor Mary Manross said the project was “worth the wait…and the fight.”  She said the project would connect neighborhoods with the community and congratulated Scottsdale for creating its own future.

• ASU President Michael M. Crow proposed a new addition to the five Cs of Arizona, currently copper, cattle, citrus, cotton and climate, to which he would add “competitiveness.”  He said SkySong offered opportunities for Arizona to compete globally.  It also furthered the concept of ASU as “one university, many places” and reinforced the theme of ASU as “not a university but a force.”

• ASU Foundation Trustee Steve Evans said cities couldn’t make projects like SkySong happen on their own.  According to Evans, partnering on the project had resulted in a “dream team” to create SkySong, exemplified by the Higgins motto “think global, act local.”

• Barry Broome, president of the Greater Phoenix Economic Council, compared SkySong to Sky Harbor Airport in terms of its value to the entire Valley, calling it the “intellectual runway” of the region.  He also thanked the leaders of the city of Scottsdale for what he called a “learning moment” by not compromising their long-term vision to accept other projects that had been offered for the site but that would not have resulted in the future pay-offs for the region that SkySong promises to bring.

• Jack Higgins, chairman and CEO of Higgins Development Partners, said SkySong would soon be the “premier office and work environment” for the area and the outdoor design feature, for which the center is named, the “beacon” for the project. 

• Patrick Duncan, president and CEO of USAA Real Estate Company, said he couldn’t ask for a better opportunity in aligning capital with partners of the caliber of the city of Scottsdale, ASU and USAA.

Development and financial partners on the project include The Plaza Companies, Higgins Development Partners, the city of Scottsdale, ASU and the ASU Foundation, and USAA Real Estate Company.  CB Richard Ellis will handle marketing and leasing activities; Sundt Construction is the general contractor; and DMJM Design, a national architectural firm with a regional office in the Valley, will work in collaboration with the project’s architectural design firm, Pei Cobb Freid & Partners.

For more information on the project, visit www.SkySongCenter.com.
