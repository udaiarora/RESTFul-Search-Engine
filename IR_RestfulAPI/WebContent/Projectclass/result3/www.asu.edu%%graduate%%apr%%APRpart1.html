Part 1: Program Review Procedures

The Academic Program Review consists of four phases which are the preparatory phase, the development of the self-study report, the site visit, and the wrap-up phase.

Phase I. Preparatory Phase

  1. Notification
    One year in advance of the review, the UPRA Officer will notify the head of the academic unit and the academic dean that a review has been scheduled.

  2. Appointment of the Self-Study Committee
    Eight months prior to the self-study submission date, the head of the academic unit should forward committee nominations, including the name of the committee chair (the committee should not exceed six faculty members) to the Academic Program Review Office. In general, this committee should consist of a representative group of faculty from the unit. The UPRA Office will forward letters of appointment to the committee and committee chair. The self-study committee will be responsible for organizing and conducting the review process and for preparing the self-study report.


  3. Meet with Supervising Dean
    Once a committee is appointed, the academic unit chair and self-study committee are encouraged to meet with their supervising dean to discuss any requests for specific information/issues that the dean would like included in the self-study. Such information should be addressed in the “Unit Response to Supervisory Dean” section of the Self-Study Report.


  4. Unit Meetings with University Program Review Staff
    At least six months prior to the self-study submission deadline, each unit should schedule a meeting with the Division of Graduate Studies Associate Dean for Academic Programs who will work with the unit on the Program Review. The Assistant Dean for Accreditation will join this meeting if the programs in the unit are accredited by an external agency or have a center or institute component. In addition, a Program Review orientation meeting with the self-study committee of the unit will be held to provide an overview of the self-study process, to discuss the required format of the self-study report, to discuss relevant university data, and to provide direction. Questions regarding the assessment of the curricular offerings and student learning outcomes can be directed to the Office of University Evaluations.

  5. Nomination of Site Visitors
    (Please refer to appendices A2 & A3)
    The chair of the academic unit, in consultation with appropriate departmental committee and faculty and with supervising dean's approval, should submit a list of names and qualifications of twelve potential external site visitors including eight names of prominent discipline-based faculty and four local community members who are also preferably recent graduates. It is essential that units describe in some detail the nominees' areas of expertise, credentials, and prior professional relationship to the unit. The site visit is always scheduled for two full days (see Appendix A2).

  6. Site Visitor Selection
    Based on academic expertise and professional experience, three site visitors are selected by university officials (two faculty members, one community member who preferably is also a recent graduate). For accredited programs, it is advisable that at least one of the consultants be knowledgeable in, and experienced with, the procedures of the accrediting agency. All communication with site visitors should come from the Academic Program Review Office.



Phase II. Self-Study Report: Development and Preparation
  1. Document Preparation
    The Self-Study Report is an interpretive document that uses data to assess current program status and future directions (please refer to Appendix A1: Academic Program Review Self-Study Report Template for a detailed description of the Self-Study Document). As mentioned previously, the university provides a standard APR data set to the units. This data set is tightly integrated into the template for the self-study document. Data should be analyzed and discussed in relation to a unit's mission and goals. Although the report is compiled and written by the self-study committee, the unit director is responsible for the content, accuracy, and completeness of the work and should actively oversee the report preparation.


  2. Document Distribution
    The college dean and campus provost (where applicable) should be given the opportunity to review the Self-Study Document and executive summary (see Appendix A9, Word or pdf ), before it is forwarded to the Associate Dean for Graduate Academic Programs, who will review it further for content, completeness, and accuracy. When necessary, suggested changes/improvements will be returned to the self-study committee for revision. The document will only be distributed to site visitors when the unit, the UPRA Officer, and supervising dean all agree that the document is of sufficient quality.


  3. Number of Copies
    The UPRA Office requires nine copies of the final self-study document, including appendices, to distribute to site visitors and appropriate university administrators.

Phase III. The Site Visit and Site Visit Report

  1. Two-Day Site Visit
    During a two-day site visit with specific parameters (see Appendix A4: Information Regarding Site Visit Arrangements and Appendix A5: Sample Site Visit Activities and Interviews), the external site visit review team will analyze the program review document, collect additional relevant information, meet with appropriate faculty, administrators, student, and community groups, and prepare a report identifying program strengths, concerns, and recommendations. The site visit team is also asked to provide an assessment of the future direction and strategic initiatives of the unit as they relate to the unit's mission and vision for its programs.


  2. Site Visit Report
    Once the site visit is complete, the site visitors will be asked to submit a Site Visit Report within three to four weeks of their visit. This report is sent to the Academic Program Review Office for review and appropriate distribution.



Phase IV. Unit’s Response Report and Wrap-Up Session (Appendix A6)
  1. Unit’s Response Report to the Site Visit Report
    Once the Site Visit Report is received, it will be reviewed and shared with the academic unit chair, the supervising dean, and the Office of the Provost. The unit director should review and discuss the report with the faculty and prepare a Unit Response Report that addresses the program concerns and recommendations (e.g., program strengths and deficiencies, faculty and student quality, resource needs, future plans, strategic initiatives, etc.).

    This Unit Response Report should be submitted to the supervising dean and the UPRA Office within 6 weeks of receiving the Site Visitor's report. The Unit Response Report will also be shared with appropriate university officials (the Provost of the University, the supervising dean, the UPRA Officer, representatives from the Office of the Provost and when applicable, the Campus Provost).


  2. Wrap-Up Phase
    The wrap-up phase will include an assessment of the site visit team's findings and the unit's response by University officials. A wrap-up letter is forwarded to the unit head and the supervising dean to summarize this final assessment. This phase may also include a meeting of the program head with the Provost of the University, the UPRA officer and the supervising dean, if there are concerns or if further clarification is deemed necessary before the final wrap-up letter is sent forward to the unit head and the academic dean.


  3. Permanent Record of the Program Review
    The Self-Study Document, the Site Visit Report, and the Unit Response Report will be considered as the permanent record of the review. A summary report prepared by the UPRA Officer will be forwarded to the Office of the Provost and the Arizona Board of Regents.




FACULTY ACTIVITY REPORTS
All tenure/tenure track faculty must submit the following information for the Program Review process: (1) a five page (maximum) Activity Report listing his/her contributions to the success of the unit for the last five years. The Activity Reports submitted may be similar to those used for annual evaluation but must include, at a minimum:
Teaching: Courses taught, titles, year and semester; individualized instruction/mentor activities (i.e., dissertations, theses, honors projects, chair and committee responsibilities). On theses/dissertations, please specify student name, degree, thesis/dissertation title, and date of completion.

Research/Creative Activity: Publications, presentations, funding.

Service: University, professional, and community activity.
These Activity Reports must be included in Appendix F of the self-study report. A standard format for all faculty members is recommended.
