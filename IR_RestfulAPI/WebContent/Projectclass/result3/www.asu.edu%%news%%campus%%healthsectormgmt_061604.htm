June 16, 2004

School of Health Management and Policy Offers Master's Degree in Health Sector Management

A graduate degree that answers industry demand for health professionals who also possess sophisticated business skills will be offered at the School of Health Management and Policy at the W. P. Carey School of Business starting fall 2004.

Students in the two-year, part-time master of health sector management program will take core business courses from the 17 th ranked W. P. Carey MBA - Evening Program, and health courses from the nationally recognized research faculty of the School of Health Management and Policy. Three areas of specialization are being offered, including epidemiology, policy and management.

More information about the program and the specializations is available online at www.wpcarey.asu.edu/mhsm and at the following open houses:

June 15, 5:30 - 7 p.m.
ASU Downtown Center
Room C145
502 East Monroe, Phoenix

June 16, 5:30 - 7 p.m.
ASU Tempe Campus
Memorial Union, Alumni Lounge

"The health sector continues to undergo change, such as the development of new financing mechanisms, and the extraordinary growth in pharmaceuticals, bioinformatics and supply chain strategy," said Dr. Jeffrey Wilson, director of the School of Health Management and Policy. "This degree provides the deep knowledge of health issues and the business acumen that today's dynamic environment demands."

Graduates of the epidemiology specialization will be qualified to pursue careers in biotechnology, pharmaceutical and contract research organizations where clinical trial design and execution are critical components:

  • Policy specialization graduates may seek employment in consulting firms, advocacy organizations and trade associations where policy analysis and dissemination are important.
  • Management track graduates will be valuable in health care delivery organizations such as hospitals, physician group practices, community health centers, long-term care facilities and home health/hospice agencies.

The School of Health Management and Policy provides graduate level education preparing professionals for the convergence of business and health. In addition to the part-time Master of Health Sector Management, the school also supports the dual W. P. Carey MBA/Master of Health Sector Management degree. This full-time program features an intensive summer of health sector courses, an internship in the health industry, and a specialization in one of four business disciplines. For more information see www.wpcareymba.asu.edu/mhsm

W. P. Carey School of Business

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report , the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 14th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
