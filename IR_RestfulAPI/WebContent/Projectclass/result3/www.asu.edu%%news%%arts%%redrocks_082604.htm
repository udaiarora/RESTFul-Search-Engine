August 26, 2004

Red Rocks Music Festival, ASU present 'Love Themes'

The Red Rocks Music Festival is partnering with ASU to present "Love Themes," a free recital of Boccherini, Webern, Dvorak and Mendelssohn at 7:30 p.m., Sept. 1, at the School of Music's Katzin Concert Hall.

The Red Rocks Music Festival's "Love Themes" roster is impressive and includes: Magdalena Martinic-Jercic, associate concertmaster of the Phoenix Symphony; David Ehrlich, former first violinist with the Audubon String Quartet; Katie McLin, ASU violin professor; Nancy Buck, ASU viola professor; Annie Center, assistant principal viola, Phoenix Symphony; Peter Rosato, principal viola Phoenix Symphony; Ohad Bar-David, cello, Philadelphia Orchestra; Thomas Landschoot, ASU cello professor; and Jan Simiz, assistant principal cello, Phoenix Symphony.

The "Love Themes" program is just that - music composed to express emotions of love. From sensuality to a lover's rejection the program delves into love's complexities. The first piece on the program is Boccherini's String Quintet. With its provocative Spanish rhythms and soaring melodies, this quintet nicknamed "Fandango" evokes the spirit of a Spanish courtship dance.

Moving from dance to denial, Ehrlich, Martinic-Jeric, Buck and Simiz perform "Cypress." A young Dvorak,  recovering from a lover's rejection, composed this cathartic work initially as a song cycle and later adapted it for string quartet. Originally composed using the poetry of Gustav Pfleger-Moravsky, movements are titled "When Thy Sweet Glances On Me Fall" and "Never Will Love Lead Us To That Glad Goal." "Love Themes" also features Webern's soulful "Langsamer Satz." The piece, inspired by a hiking holiday taken with his bride to be, combines traditional romanticism coupled with Webern's developing austere style.

The final selection on the "Love Themes" program is Mendelssohn's "Quintet in B-flat Major, Op. 87." The lyrical quintet showcases the masterful playing of Ehrlich, McLin, Rosato, Buck and Bar-David. 

The Red Rocks Music Festival is dedicated not only to the performance of inspiring concerts, but also to education. Performers David Urlich and Ohad Bar-David will present master classes for the public at 2:40 p.m., Aug. 30 and Aug. 31 in ASU's Katzin Concert Hall. For more information, call (480) 965- 8863.

Bell, with the Herberger College of Fine Arts School of Music, can be reached at (480) 965-2817 or (rebecca.bell@asu.edu).
