The Valley National Bank was very anxious to have a branch bank located in the Luhrs Building on the southwest corner of Central Avenue and Jefferson Street. This corner location was, at that time, occupied by A.B. Robbs. The Valley National Bank made arrangements with A.B. Robbs to move to another location, which he did as of March, 1958. After remodeling, they opened up their 49th branch, known as the Valley National Bank, Luhrs Building Branch. Geo H.N. Luhrs had hoped, from the time the Luhrs Building was planned, that a bank would be located on the corner. That now was another dream come true.


In 1957, the Luhrs' decided to go ahead with the building of the Luhrs Parking Center, on the northeast corner of First Avenue and Madison Street, directly south across the alley from the Luhrs Tower.
