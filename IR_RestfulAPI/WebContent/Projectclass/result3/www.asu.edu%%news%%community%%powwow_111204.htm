November 12, 2004

Traditional Pow-Wow honors Native American veterans

Native American dancers and drum groups from throughout Arizona will participate in the fourth annual Traditional Pow-Wow at ASU’s West campus on Saturday, Nov. 13. The Veterans Day weekend event brings traditional Native American arts and crafts, food, music and dancing to the West Valley.

The pow-wow will be held from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. on the central lawn of ASU’s West campus, located at 4701 W. Thunderbird Road in Phoenix. It is free and open to the public. Attendees are encouraged to bring lawn chairs.

“This traditional pow-wow features gourd dancing, hoop dancing, Zuni dancers and Yaqui dancers,” says Dennis Eagleman, an ASU student and member of the campus pow-wow committee. Eagleman, a finance major at the West campus, was honored this month as the Outstanding Native American University Student by the Arizona Native American Recognition Days (NARD) planning committee.

Special dances are scheduled throughout the event, with two grand entries planned at 1 p.m. and 7 p.m. In addition to dancing and traditional foods, the pow-wow will honor the contribution of Native American military veterans.

This year’s participants include Roy Track of Mesa, master of ceremonies; John Murie of Tucson, arena director; Keith Tagaban of Albuquerque, head gourd dancer; Paul Rock Krech of Mesa, head man dancer; Karen Ramirez of Phoenix, head woman dancer; Avery Moore of Phoenix, head boy dancer; and Shanelle Lomakema of Phoenix, head girl dancer. The Northern Drum will be represented by Saste Takoja of Tucson and the Southern Drum will be represented by the Southern Nation of Tempe. The American Indian Veterans Memorial Organization will provide the Color Guard.

The pow-wow is sponsored by the Native American Events Committee, the Native American Student Organization and Public Events at ASU’s West campus. Additional information is available online at (www.west.asu.edu/naso) or at (602) 543-8130.
