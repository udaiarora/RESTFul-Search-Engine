Major Initiatives: Recommended Strategies
User Self-Sufficiency

Definition:
If they can do it themselves, and want to do it themselves, enable them to do it themselves.
Assumptions:
  • There is no such thing as total self-sufficiency;
  • The library will continue to offer the option of personal assistance;
  • The library will continue to serve a diverse population.
Current State:
To determine the present level of user self-sufficiency in the University Libraries, the Task Force conducted interviews to gather data for a situational analysis. From the data collected, as well as observations of the individual members of the Task Force, the following desired state evolved.
Desired State:
The University Libraries will be organized and designed, both physically and philosophically, based on customers' needs. The definition of customers will include both library staff in the internal environment and patrons in the greater community.

Services and materials will also be placed where customers would expect them respective to collections. Terms used to direct customers will be used consistently and will be easily understood by patrons outside of the library environment.

Finally, the technological interface will be easy to use and understand both on and off-campus, with screens, databases, and links effectively organized. The technological interface will include online help or brief tutorials for inexperienced users.

Recommended Strategies


I. Removal of Physical Barriers
  • Strategy: Implement a project to improve the user's ability to navigate the Libraries' buildings, to include the following components:


    • work with an outside consultant who will make recommendations on placement of service points relative to doors, elevators, stairs, etc. and on the logical placement of service points and collections (from the customer's viewpoint);


    • involvement of as much input as possible from customers through focus groups, interviews, surveys this can be coordinated with other customer service efforts;


    • possible site visits to other libraries and/or other service providers who have used space effectively in customer service planning;


    • analysis of ease and impact of recommended actions that result from the above;


    • development of priorities and implementation plan.


    • Rationale: For many users, the ability to be self-sufficient still means navigating library buildings successfully.


    • Priority 1: Begin developing plan immediately.


    • Performance Outcome Measure: Customers report that they have greater success in navigating the Libraries' buildings, finding what they are looking for faster.
II. Removal of Philosophical and Intellectual Barriers
  • Strategy: Implement a project to remove philosophical and intellectual barriers to self-sufficiency, to include the following:


    • examination of definitions, terminology, and jargon used by the Libraries in communicating to customers. Make changes as appropriate.


    • examination of how we point people toward physical locations, use of location codes, distinction between locations and collections;


    • study and evaluation of guides currently available in the Libraries;


    • recommendations for changes and implementation schedule.


    • Rationale: There is much that is difficult and/or illogical about how we interpret our collections and services to users; example, idiosyncratic location of call numbers. Clear interpretation needs to be consistently designed and implemented throughout the Libraries.


    • Priority 1: Begin working on this immediately.


    • Performance Outcome Measure: Customers report that they are more successful in using finding aids, online systems, etc., that the Libraries provide. They are more efficient in finding what they are looking for.
III. Improvement of the Libraries' Technological Interface
  • Strategy: Implement an ongoing capability for continuous improvement of the interface the user interacts with and should be able to use easily with minimal intervention from library staff. This capability should have the following outcomes:


    • examination of the content and location of all user work stations; reorganize as needed;


    • plan for making all on-line tools available from all work stations;


    • implementation of clear, readily available on-line instructions on all workstations.


    • Rationale: The customer's experience with the Libraries' technological interface has become as important as interactions with staff. This is a key point for developing self-sufficiency.


    • Priority 1: Begin working on this immediately.


    • Performance Outcome Measure: Customers report that they are able to navigate the Libraries' technological interface more effectively. Library staff receive fewer requests for assistance as a result, and such requests as are made are for help with truly complex problems.
