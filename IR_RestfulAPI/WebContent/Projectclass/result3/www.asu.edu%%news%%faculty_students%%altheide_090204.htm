September 2, 2004

Regents' Professor wins book award for second time

Regents' Professor David Altheide, has been awarded The Society for the Study of Symbolic Interaction's (SSSI) Charles Horton Cooley Award for Recent Book or Article, for the second time. He won this award in 1986 for his book Media Power, and is the 2004 winner for his book, Creating Fear. Professor Altheide is the first person to win the award for a second time.

Professor Altheide has been at Arizona State University for 30 years, spending 22 of those years with The School of Justice Studies. His research and teaching focuses on communication, social interaction and social organization. He teaches several undergraduate courses, including Introduction to Justice Studies, as well as two graduate seminars, Justice and the Mass Media and Qualitative Research Methods. Visit David's page to view his bio and a link to his vitae. 

The Society for the Study of Symbolic Interaction (SSSI) http://sun.soci.niu.edu/~sssi/ is an international social science professional organization of scholars interested in qualitative, especially interactionist, research. The society has its annual meetings in conjunction with the ASA annual meetings. SSSI also organizes panels and sessions at annual conferences such as the American Sociological Association, Pacific Sociological Association and the Midwest Sociology Society Annual Meetings, and each spring holds the Couch-Stone SSSI Symposium.
