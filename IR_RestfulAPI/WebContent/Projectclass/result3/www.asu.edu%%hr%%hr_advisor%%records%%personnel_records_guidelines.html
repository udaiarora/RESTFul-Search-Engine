Personnel Records
Frequently Asked Questions

 

The Personnel Records policy, SPP 1101, offers a consistent method to maintain and manage official and departmental personnel records.

It applies to classified, service professional and administrative employees and provides information on: type of file; content of file; record retention; accessibility; and maintenance responsibilities.

What does an employee's official personnel file include?
Hiring paperwork
Work performance documents

See SPP 1101 for a complete list

What records does the department maintain?
Each employee's departmental file must, at a minimum, contain:

  • Attendance records
  • Turnaround Personnel Action Forms (PAFS)
  • Documentation to substantiate employment-related changes for the employee such as changes to job classification or pay rate
  • Employee disciplinary records

Keep separate files for:
Medical records
ADA, FMLA, Worker's Compensation
Any documentation from a health care provider
Hiring records
The hiring unit must retain all records associated with a hire for at least three years.
MORE INFO: SPP 201-01 (Recruitment and Employment)

How long are records kept?
 Human Resources keeps official personnel files for five years after an employee's termination date. Departments keep files for three years after an employee retires, terminates or transfers to another department.

Who can view the employee's official personnel file?

  • The employee
  • Any person authorized by the employee in writing
  • Arizona Board of Regents and Regents' staff
  • External auditors
  • Office of General Counsel
  • Human Resources staff
  • President or President's designee
  • Other persons as authorized by the Director of Human Resources

Are there any viewing restrictions on the information?
Only employee conduct and work performance records such as performance evaluations can be viewed by:
Management directly responsible for the employee
Hiring officials considering an ASU employee as a finalist for hire, transfer, promotion or re-employment

What information is considered public record?

  • Employee Name
  • Titles or positions such as academic degrees and honors
  • Department
  • Campus telephone number
  • Employment dates
  • Salary or rate of pay
