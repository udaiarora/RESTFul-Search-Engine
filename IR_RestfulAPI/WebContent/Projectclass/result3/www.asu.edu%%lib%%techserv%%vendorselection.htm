Vendor Selection for Information Budget Orders

Technical Services Department, ASU University Libraries


“Vendor” for Library purchases refers to the entity we pay.  In most cases, vendors are jobbers who purchase and stock materials in bulk for library customers.  Jobbers focus on current imprints (last two to four years), usually published in one country or a group of countries sharing a common language or geographic area.


Monographs 

In areas of intensive collecting we generally establish approval or blanket order plans with particular vendors who are authorized to collect materials for ASU within written parameters or profiles, up to a certain amount of money per fiscal year.  Firm order selection is more widespread, based primarily on where the material is available.  Cost, speed of delivery, and other services (e.g. pre-processing of materials, electronic invoice availability, duplication avoidance, etc) are also considered.


Approval plan vendors supply more than half of all our monographic acquisitions purchases.  These vendors are selected by Collection Development and the Dean of the University Libraries based on sophistication of profiles, cost, delivery speed, publishers and materials covered, vendor size, and other services available.  There are few library vendors capable of servicing a library of ASU’s size and scope.  Vendors are notified each fall of their expenditure allowance for the year.  Our major vendors as of FY2003 are:

Firm order vendors are usually selected by Technical Services Department staff based on the established criteria below (“buyers” as per ASU Certificate made pursuant to ABOR 3-103).  Selectors and bibliographers (“buyers”) are encouraged to recommend vendor selection if special conditions apply.  Factors considered in vendor selection include:

  • Non-rush, current domestic and U.K. imprints: Blackwell Book Services is our vendor of choice due to price (domestic imprints receive 14% discount), duplication avoidance, invoicing facility, and our established service relationship. Many firm orders placed with Blackwell are selected from notification slips sent by Blackwell based on our approval plan profile, informing us of books available from Blackwell.
  • Current German or Western European imprints: Harrassowitz is our vendor of choice based on coordination with their approval plan. 
  • Other current foreign imprints: Country of origin is primary consideration, with vendor selection based on “Commonly Used Foreign Vendors” list (attached at end of this document). ASU Libraries has a developed business relationship with these companies based on years of experience, blanket order agreements, recommendations from other librarians, etc.
  • Out-of-print orders: Based on availability. If advertised in a vendor/publisher catalog or selector provides the vendor, we use that vendor. If not, Amazon Marketplace, which compiles stock lists from registered third-party sellers, is searched for available copies with condition and price suitable for Library purchase.
  • Special language imprints: Based on selector recommendation and/or “Commonly Used Foreign Vendors” list.
  • Vendor notifications: If vendor notifies us that they cannot supply an order they generally provide publisher or other distributor information as a possible source. In these cases we redirect orders based on vendor recommendation.

Serials 

Approximately 80% of our serial subscriptions are placed with two major serial agents, EBSCO Subscription Services in the US for domestic and UK publishers, and Otto Harrassowitz for German and other Western European publishers.  Vendor selection for the other 20% is based primarily on:

  • Party authorized to distribute the subscription (in some cases publishers require orders be placed direct)
  • Country of origin. If we do not have an established relationship with a vendor in that country, we check AcqWeb’s Directory of Publishers and Vendors, http://acqweb.library.vanderbilt.edu/pubr.html, an international directory of publishers and vendors used by libraries maintained for the benefit of the library community. 

List prices for non-electronic serials are generally non-negotiable and are therefore not a consideration unless differential pricing applies based on shipping from foreign countries (e.g. air mail versus ground mail delivery).   In such cases, we select the least expensive option unless speed of delivery is specifically noted as a requirement for Library users by the selector.


Electronic Resources 

Most electronic resources can only be purchased from one source, the publisher or aggregator.  In some cases publishers or aggregators allow serial agents to license on behalf of library customers.  Nearly all electronic resource purchases require signed license agreements negotiated between the Library and the vendor/publisher/aggregator.  Licenses are negotiated by the Collection Development Division, in cooperation with ASU’s General Counsel Office.  Copies of signed license agreements, usually including costs and terms, are kept on file in the Technical Services Department.  

 

In the relatively few cases in which an electronic resource is available from a variety of sources, Collection Development and Technical Services determine the most appropriate and expeditious vendor to use.


Authority for Vendor Selection 

Outside the Libraries:  Final authority for all vendor selection rests with the Department of Purchasing and Business Services.  ASU Libraries can only use vendors approved and input by Purchasing on Advantage.  Except for purchasing card purchases, Purchasing requires the Libraries to submit the following information for Purchasing to approve the vendor on Advantage:

  • ASU Substitute W-9 Form
  • Invoice
  • Vendor entry input information: Vendor name, Federal ID or Social Security number (domestic only), address, telephone, fax, e-mail, contact name if available

Purchasing and Business Services is responsible for questioning possible conflicts of interest.  Certain vendors (e.g. Standard & Poor’s, McGraw Hill) are flagged by Purchasing as possible problems.  In such cases, Accounts Payable requests that Technical Services staff send a copy of the invoice, which they forward to the Comptroller’s Office for review and signature. 


All invoices over $50,000 require Comptroller review and signature before Accounts Payable sends the check. 


Accounts Payable cuts and sends all checks.


Within the Libraries, approval and blanket orders vendors are selected by Collection Development and selectors, with final approval from the Dean of the University Libraries (“buyers” as per ASU Certificate made pursuant to ABOR 3-103).  The Dean also has final authority for selecting the Libraries’ major subscription vendors.  


For firm orders and subscription purchases (non electronic), Technical Services reserves the right to select vendors based on country of origin, availability, price, speed of delivery, year of publication, and other service considerations (e.g. availability of electronic invoices, approval plan duplication avoidance).  Consideration factors are weighted based on type of material being ordered.  Examples of weighting include:

  • For current domestic monographic imprints, price is primary. Blackwell extends a 14% discount on firm orders to ASU as a result of our approval plan purchase commitments.
  • For out-of-print monographs, availability and condition (suitability for long-term library use) are primary.
  • For rush orders, speed of delivery is primary.
  • For non-EBSCO/Harrassowitz subscriptions, country of origin, timely delivery of issues, invoicing, and renewal notifications at subscription expiration are primary.

Especially for foreign, non-English-language orders, selectors and bibliographers should recommend the vendor to use based on their knowledge of that vendor’s business and country.  In these cases Technical Services staff will utilize the recommended vendor unless Purchasing and Business Services requirements cannot be met. 

Checks and balances sufficient to meet financial audit requirements are in place throughout the process of acquiring materials for the Library collections:

  • Selectors and bibliographers select Library materials based on subject or area expertise, and faculty or other patron requests. 
  • Collection Development approves all firm orders and new subscriptions.
  • Technical Services has no selection authority. All orders are received from Collection Development. 
  • Bibliographic Search/Order Unit staff search orders to verify order information and check for duplication; select vendors; and key orders into the Libraries’ integrated library system, Innopac. Selector and/or requestor are noted on all firm order and subscription order entries on Innopac.
  • Monographic Receiving Unit or Serials Receiving Unit staff receive non-electronic orders, verifying receipt before approving invoices for payment processing. These staff are separate from staff authorized to place orders. Approved invoices are passed on to the Accounting Unit. Receiving staff are not password authorized to pay invoices.
  • Invoices for electronic resources are approved by a member of the Technical Services Management Team due to high pricing and access requirements.
  • Accounting Unit staff process invoice payments on the Libraries’ integrated library system and on Advantage. Only Accounting Unit staff have password authority to pay invoices. Only the Business Manager is authorized to approve payments on Advantage. 
  • Only vendors authorized by Purchasing and Business Services on Advantage may be used.
