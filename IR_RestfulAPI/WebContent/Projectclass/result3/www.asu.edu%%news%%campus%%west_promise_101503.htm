October 15, 2003

Campus makes 'promise' to youth

In 2000, ASU West earned the designation of "University of Promise" from the national organization "America's Promise: The Alliance for Youth." The mission of America's Promise is to mobilize people from every sector of American life to build the character and competence of our nation's youth by fulfilling five promises - caring adults, safe places, healthy start, marketable skills and opportunities to serve.

As the only university in the state designated as the University of Promise, ASU West is closely involved in the development and expansion of the America's Promise program in Arizona.

Last year, ASU West joined Arizona community leaders to launch the Arizona State Promise Station Internet site. The sites enable people in communities across the country to help youth where they live, work and worship using their own talents and interests.

Arizona was the first state in the nation to provide its residents with a statewide, Internet-based resource connecting youth-focused service organizations around the state. The site empowers individuals and organizations to initiate and participate in projects benefiting young people.

Most recently, ASU West was recognized for its involvement in America's Promise at the U.S. Hispanic Chamber of Commerce national conference during a press conference Oct. 2 in Phoenix.

America's Promise was founded after the Presidents' Summit for America's Future in 1997, when former living presidents challenged the country to make children and youth a national priority. Colin Powell is founding chairman.

For information visit (www.arizonastatepromise.org) or (www.americaspromise.org).

Did you know?
. Currently 27 percent of ASU West students identify themselves as members of an ethnic minority population, compared to 14 percent a decade ago.
. Approximately 67 percent of students are female - that percentage has remained substantially the same over the years.
. Approximately 49 percent of students are age 24 or younger - compared to 20 percent in 1990.
. ASU West is preparing to celebrate its 20th anniversary. On April 18, 1984, Gov. Bruce Babbitt signed legislation officially establishing the campus.
. The Classroom Laboratory Building, currently under construction and set to open in January, will be ASU West's first new classroom building since 1991.
. The inaugural freshman class in 2001 included 251 students; this fall, ASU West enrolled 357 first-time freshmen.
