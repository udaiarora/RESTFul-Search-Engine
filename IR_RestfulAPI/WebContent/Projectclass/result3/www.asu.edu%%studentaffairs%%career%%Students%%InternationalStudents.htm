Information for International Students

Writing resumes and looking for a career/internship opportunity is different in the U.S. from what you would do back home. So if you are planning on obtaining practical training in the U.S., prepare for the differences by using ASU Career Services events, on-campus recruiting, workshops, Career Education Center (CEC) resources in Student Services Building, Room 129 (SSV 129) and by meeting with a career advisor.

Requirements:

  • Apply only to those jobs that relate to your field of study and are of interest to you
  • You are only eligible for positions that do not require a U.S. national security clearance
  • Comply with U.S. regulations by using the International Student Office web site http://www.asu.edu/studentaffairs/ed/iso for Curricular (CPT) and Optional (OPT)) Practical Training

Ethics:

  • Do not misrepresent your status for employment in the U.S.; practical training is NOT work authorization
  • Once an offer has been accepted you are committed to the organization so withdraw from interviewing
  • You are responsible for knowing U.S. expectations and appropriate behaviors

Who Will Hire Me In the U.S.?

  • U.S. government positions that require national security clearance are not open to international students; areas of study that relate to those fields may restrict your possibilities in the U.S.
  • Generally, majors in the technical and some business areas offer more potential for practical training in the U.S.; however, your marketability depends on you and your accomplishments, including academic, participation in organizations, work experience and other related factors
  • More than one-third of the U.S. employers hire international students
  • Small, medium and large companies with special needs for highly developed skills and do not have enough U.S. citizens to fill positions
  • American firms operating in foreign countries and foreign firms operating in America; see resources in the CEC
  • Review ASU placement data as reported by ASU graduates and NACE Salary Survey located on the lower right side of ASU Career Services web site

What Do Employers Want?

  • Highly developed English communication skills, including reading, writing, speaking and interpersonal
  • Honesty, integrity, commitment. Once you have accepted a position you must quit the job search and follow through on your acceptance. Do not misrepresent your work status: practical training is NOT work authorization
  • Self-initiative; being able to see what needs to be done and willing to go beyond the job description
  • Being on time and mentally present; not down-loading music, talking on a cell phone and emailing family and friends while supposedly working

How Do I Prepare?

  • Concentrate on developing your communication skills in English
  • Connect with the University Writing Center (480 - 965 - 4272; http://www.asu.edu/duas/wcenter) in Language and Literature, room 340 for assistance with writing skills and/or with the American English and Language Program (480 - 965 - 3564; http://www.asu.edu/xed/aecp/index.html) in Mariposa, Room 113 for assistance in developing proficient English speaking skills
  • Attend ASU Career Services workshops on resume writing, networking, business etiquette, international student job search, interviewing and others
  • Watch networking, international student job search and interviewing videos in the CEC
  • Attend career events and employer information sessions
  • Practice answering behavioral interview questions
  • Start your job search two semesters before graduating

How Do I Market Myself?

  • Do not be the first to bring up your work status with an employer, but do be prepared to address the question of work status by knowing what you and the employer must do; review web sites listed on Helpful Web Sites
  • More than 70% of jobs in the U.S. are found through networking which is about relationships; networking is not about having a friend who has a friend who has a job opening in his company; attend networking workshops and review video Tapping the Hidden Job Market in the CEC
  • Develop an effective resume and prepare for interviews by attending workshops and reviewing videos on networking and interviewing in the CEC
  • Attend ASU Career Services recruiting events, but first prepare by attending a workshop on making the most of career events
  • Register with Sun Devil CareerLink and know how to effectively use the recruiting system
