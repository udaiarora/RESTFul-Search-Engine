Upcoming Free Workshop
Blackbelt Librarians: Every Librarian's Guide to a Safer Workplace

You are invited to register for a new free workshop to be held on January 8th (at Phoenix Public Library) or January 9 (at Scottsdale Civic Center Library): Blackbelt Librarians: Every Librarian's Guide to a Safer Workplace. This workshop is for front line librarians, paraprofessionals, security staff & management. During the session you will learn:

  • Essential elements of a security program
  • How to inform patrons of rules
  • How to recognize levels of emotion and strategies on how best to respond
  • Ten maxims of security no matter the size of your library

This workshop will be resented by Warren Graham, Security Manager for the Public Library of Charlotte and Mecklenburg County.

Register online at: http://www.lib.az.us/eventreg/session_registration.cfm?event=403

It's Time to Help Students Get un-LOST!

The Spring semester is upon us, the students are coming back from holiday break, and once again the libraries are taking an active leadership role in helping our customers start the year right! That means the return of our ?Lost Tables,? where we provide maps and smiles to those students in need of assistance. (Check out the Libraries' Flickr Site to see photos from last fall's tables). Once again, we'll be offering 30-minute volunteer slots. Many staff found that their hectic schedules precluded a full hour, so we want to make sure everyone feels included and has a chance to participate in this fun and exhilarating event.

Locations
Our Hayden tent will be set up in front of the MU fountain, and our Noble tent will be located on the north side of the building, in front of the entrance.

How to Sign Up
We have created Yahoo Calendars to sign up on the schedule. Here's how to grab your choice of times. Go to calendar.yahoo.com:

To sign up for Hayden:
Yahoo ID: asulosttables
Password: sparky

To sign up for Noble:
Yahoo ID: asulosttablesnoble
Password: sparky

Choose January 16 on the calendar on the left side of the screen, depending on when you wish to volunteer.

We are looking to have three people per half-hour segment at Hayden and two people per hour at Noble. Please click on the Name (i.e. Marc Mason) you see in the segment you wish to choose. If you see ?lost tables? instead of a name, no one has yet signed up at that time.



On the next page, in the ?title? field, please add your name after the last person to sign up. If you are the first to sign up for that half hour, please erase ?lost tables? and replace it with your name. Click the ?update? you see at the top of the screen, and then log out of Yahoo when you're done.



If you'd prefer to be signed up manually, send an e-mail to marc.mason@asu.edu with your preferred times, and we will check for availability and get back to you.

After you've signed up, please make a note of the times you chose, and we will look forward to seeing you when school starts. We are very grateful for your time and efforts!

Emerging Technology Update Workshop

If blog and wiki sound like characters from a children's book or you think aggregators are unruly teens, then this workshop is for you. The lingo will be defined and free technology tools will be explained, including blogs, wikis, RSS, podcasts, e-books, XML, and IM. Learn to use Bloglines to let the information come to help you on your job, find out the pros and cons, and be assured that you won't gain weight if you subscribe to a feed.

This workshop, presented by Stephanie Gerding, is for non-techy beginners, with a focus on making you more confident and comfortable with these new tools.

Offered January 17th from 1-4 p.m. or January 22nd from 9-12 noon in the Fourth Floor Staff Computer Training Room at Burton Barr Central Library, 1221 N. Central Avenue, Phoenix, AZ.

Register online at: www.lib.az.us/eventreg

Countdown to FORO

From February 20-24, 2007 the ASU Libraries, led by Dean and University Librarian Sherrie Schmidt, will be hosting the 2007 FORO Transborder Library Forum. This is the first of several planned updates about this exciting conference, which is being planned by a team of library staff volunteers. The co-chairs of this planning team are Rosa Gonzalez, Libraries Administration, and Jenny Mueller-Alexander, Social Sciences Information Services. Information about the conference can be found on the conference web site. We are anticipating up to 300 library professionals from around the world to attend the conference, many of whom will be from Mexico, Canada, the Caribbean, and even far away places such as Kenya and Germany.

What is FORO?
FORO, the Transborder Library Forum, is a volunteer organization that cultivates a venue for the cooperative exchange of ideas, and the discussion of experiences and efforts concerning the provision of library services in the border regions between the U.S., Mexico, and Canada.

FORO objectives include:

  • strengthening links among information professionals concerned with building information bridges across international borders;
  • planning and implementing cooperative projects between libraries across geographic borders;
  • facilitating the development of resource networks;
  • introducing librarians to current commercial library products and services;
  • sharing our cultural heritage while promoting literary and library services.

2007 Conference
The theme for the 2007 conference, Bridging the Digital Divide: Crossing All Borders, challenges us to explore and celebrate the ways in which information professionals in our border regions can further cooperate and solve common problems related to our digital information world. We cordially welcome your contributions and friendship as we construct bridges for information sharing through the use of collegial relationships and emerging innovative technologies. Special sub-themes for this conference are topics relating to:

  • Intellectual Property Rights;
    Copyright in the digital age; Global intellectual property issues, etc.
  • Information and Indigenous Peoples;
    Language & culture preservation; Technology & information in remote areas, etc.
  • Librarians: Professional Education and Preparation under NAFTA.
    Accreditation issues; Distance education, etc.

How Can You Help?
For this conference to be a success we will need many volunteers from the ASU Libraries. Even small contributions of time will be greatly appreciated. Look for future announcements in Soundoff for specific volunteer opportunities, or contact Rosa Gonzalez or Jenny Mueller-Alexander to indicate your interest.



--Rosa Gonzalez and Jenny Mueller-Alexander
FORO 2007 Conference Planning Co-Chairs

This Week In History

January 8th, 1790:
President George Washington delivers the first State of the Union address to the assembled Congress in New York City.

January 9th, 1493:
Italian explorer Christopher Columbus, sailing near the Dominican Republic, sees three "mermaids"--in reality manatees--and describes them as "not half as beautiful as they are painted."

January 10th, 1946:
The first General Assembly of the United Nations, comprising 51 nations, convenes at Westminster Central Hall in London, England.

January 11th, 1908:
U.S. President Theodore Roosevelt declares the massive Grand Canyon in northwestern Arizona a national monument.

January 12th, 1932:
Ophelia Wyatt Caraway, a Democrat from Arkansas, becomes the first woman to be elected to the U.S. Senate.

Recruitment

When applying for any position at ASU, please address work experience as it relates to each required and preferred qualification, and state complete employment dates (month/year). The Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) cannot give credit for qualifications not clearly documented on the resume.

Also, it is a good idea to (1) ask the person(s) you want to use as a reference for permission first; and (2) remember, when interviewing as an internal candidate, the SAC scores on answers to the questions not on what they think or assume you know.
