Athena in the College Of Design

Athena is a residential learning community located in McClintock Hall for students in the College Of Design. This unique program creates a community that fosters personal development both intellectually and socially while meeting students' academic and living needs. Athena combines a social community with programs designed to complement course work and student interest. In this community, students can

  • Attend academic programs with faculty in a relaxed environment to make co-curricular activities more convenient
  • Collaborate with faculty to guide program development
  • Participate in a mentoring program with faculty that will provide out-of-classroom time for informal discussions

 

Entrance Criteria for Athena LLC

  • Students must be admitted to the College of Design
  • Students must complete and submit the Residential Life application
  • Students who meet the academic requirements are invited to apply to the program. Requirements are generally, high school GPA of 3.5+ or, SAT score of 1250+, or ACT score of 25+ or top 5% of graduating class.
  • Applications are sent only to those students who meet the established criteria
  • Complete application and personal statement must be submitted
