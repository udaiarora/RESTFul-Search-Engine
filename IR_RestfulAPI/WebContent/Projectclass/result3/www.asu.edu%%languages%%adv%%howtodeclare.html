GENERAL INFORMATION -- WHAT TO DO, WHEN, WHERE

How to declare a MAJOR in Chinese, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Russian or Spanish:  If you are changing your major, pick up the "Degree-Major-Campus Change Request" form in the main office LL-440 or on the web from the Registrar forms site. Meet with one of the DLL Academic Advisors to go over the requirements for the major.  Complete the form then take it to the CLAS Office of Academic Programs in Foundation Building SS #1120 for approval, then to the Registrar for processing.

How to declare a CONCURRENT DEGREE (double major): If you would like to pursue a concurrent degree, follow the guidelines on this Concurrent Degree Checklist for the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.  You should keep in regular contact with the advisors for the majors that you would like to pursue. In the semester in which you are enrolled in 87 or more credits, you should file the Declaration of Graduation with your primary major advisor (the major that appears at the top of your transcripts).  Next, file the Declaration of Graduation with the advisor of your second major.  Note:  If you have out-of-state transfer credits and will be new to the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, you must have those credits evaluated by a CLAS Academic Advisor in Foundation Building, #1120, phone 480-965-6506.  At any time when you think you might want to pursue a concurrent degree in either Chinese, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Russian or Spanish, please make an appointment with an Academic Advisor in the Department of Languages and Literatures, 480-965-6281.

How to declare a MINOR in Chinese, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Russian or Spanish:  For any minor, you need a minimum of 18 credit hours, but each language requires specific courses. The check sheets are available at this web site or just outside of LL-440 (DLL Main Office). Make sure you know the classes you need for the minor. You must see a DLL advisor if you have transfer courses that may apply to the minor so that they may be evaluated.

To declare your language minor, see your MAJOR advisor.  Minors are attached to your DARS audit, and may be noted on your Declaration of Graduation form only by your major academic advisor.  In most departments, you will need to fill out and submit a DARS Minor Request Form.  If you are a language major and wish to declare your minor with the DLL Academic Advisor, please submit the Minor Request Form or your name, ASU ID number, major, the minor you wish to pursue, and your contact information to Beth Glessner-Calkins.  Her mailbox is in LL-440.
