GENERAL ENCYCLOPEDIAS/DICTIONARIES

 

Anderson, James. Dictionary of Opera. 3rd ed. London: Bloomsbury, 1998.
ML102.O6 A54 1998 MUSIC REF

A general single volume dictionary with brief entries for composers, performers, operas, characters, terms, etc.

American musical theatre: a chronicle. 2nd ed. New York: Oxford University Press, 1992. New York: Macmillan, c1992.
ML1711.B67 1992 MUSIC STACKS

A chronological account of American musical theater from its origin through the 1989/90 season.

Fath, Rolf. Reclams Opernlexicon. Stuttgart: Philipp Reclam, 1989.
ML102.O6 F38x 1989 MUSIC REF

A dictionary, in German, of operas, composers, performers, conductors, directors, characters, and opera houses.
Green, Stanley. Broadway musicals, show by show. 4th ed. Milwaukee, WI: H. Leonard, 1994.
ML1711.G735 1994 MUSIC REF

Includes basic information about major Broadway shows from 1866 through 1992.Arranged chronologically, with several indexes.

Hischak, Thomas S. The American musical theatre song encyclopedia. Westport, CN: Greenwood Press, 1995.
ML102.M88 H59 1995 MUSIC REF

A listing of ca. 1800 songs which attempts to explain what kind of song it is, its history, its purpose in the show, and who originally sang it. Selected from shows dating from 1866 through the 1993/94 season.

Hischak, Thomas S. Stage it with music: an encyclopedic guide to the American musical theatre. Westport, Conn.: Greenwood Press, 1993.
ML102.M88 H6 1993 MUSIC REF

Brief coverage of ca. 300 musical shows, plus entries on composers, directors, producers, designers, lyricists, actors, etc.

International dictionary of opera. Detroit: St. James Press, 1993. 2 v.
ML102.O6 I6 1993 MUSIC REF

A large 2-volume dictionary with substantial entries for individuals and operatic works. Includes bibliographies, where applicable, and indexes.

Lewine, Richard. Songs of the theater. New York: H.W. Wilson, 1984.
ML128.S3 L55 1984 MUSIC REF

A listing of ca. 17,000 song titles from the beginnings of American musical theater until the end of 1983. Also includes a major section listing the shows, opening date, composer/lyricist, and all songs included in the show at opening.

Loewenberg, Alfred. Annals of opera, 1597-1940. 3rd ed. rev. Totowa, NJ: Roman and Littlefield, 1978.
ML102.O6 L6 1978 MUSIC REF

A chronological listing with entries which include the name of the town and theater in which the first production took place. Sources of the plot, subsequent revivals in other cities, and translations into other languages also given.

Marco, Guy A. Opera: a research and information guide. New York: Garland, 1984.
ML128.O4 M28 1984 MUSIC REF

The Metropolitan Opera encyclopedia: a comprehensive guide to the world of opera. London:Thames and Hudson, 1987.
ML102.O6 M47 1987b MUSIC REF

A standard type of encyclopedia, but with details given about the Met-related aspects of the works. Highly illustrated.

The New Grove dictionary of opera. London: Macmillan Press, 1992. 4 v.
ML102.O6 N5 1992b MUSIC REF

A comprehensive international dictionary covering all aspects of opera. Includes indexes of role names and incipits of areas, ensembles, etc. Also includes detailed plot summaries.

Parsons, Charles H. The Mellen opera reference index. Lewiston, NY: Edwin Mellen Press, 1986-
ML102.O6 P25 1986 MUSIC REF

A series of indexes covering a range of opera topics, e.g. discographies, librettists, premieres, reviews, subjects, etc.

The Penguin opera guide. London: Viking, 1995.
ML102.O6 V55 1995 MUSIC REF

An abridged edition of the Viking Opera Guide, containing revisions and updates.

Suskin, Steven. Opening night on Broadway: a critical quotebook of the golden era of the musical theatre, Oklahoma! (1943) to Fiddler on the roof (1964). New York: Schirmer Books, 1990.
ML1711.8.N3 S9 1990 MUSIC REF

More opening nights on Broadway: a critical quotebook of the musical theatre, 1965 through 1981. New York: Schirmer Books, 1997.
ML1711.8.N3 M8 1997 MUSIC REF

Selected excerpts of major critical reviews of Broadway shows.

The Viking opera guide. New York: Viking, 1993.
ML102.O6 V45x 1993 MUSIC REF

A comprehensive guide to opera, operetta, singspiel and musical theater.

Warrack, John Hamilton. The Oxford dictionary of opera. New York: Oxford University Press, 1992.
ML102.O6 W37 1992 MUSIC REF

A dictionary of primarily brief entries for composers, singers, companies, characters, major arias, terms, etc. Also includes separate entries for cities and countries.

Who's who in opera: an international biographical directory of singers, conductors, directors, designers, and administrators, also including profiles of 101 opera companies. New York: Arno Press, 1976.
ML102.O6 W5 MUSIC REF


NATIONAL/TOPICAL

Borroff, Edith. American operas: a checklist. Warren, MI: Harmonie Park Press, 1992.
ML128.O4 B58 1992 MUSIC REF

A bibliography of operas by composers of the United States, containing over 4000 titles by over 2000 composers.

Griffel, Margaret Ross. Operas in German: a dictionary. New York: Greenwood Press, 1990.
ML102.O6 G75 1990 MUSIC REF


Martin, George Whitney. Twentieth century opera: a guide. New York: Limelight Editions, 1999.
MT95.M253 1999 MUSIC REF

Contains several introductory essays and plot synopses of ca. 90 operas.

Northhouse, Cameron. Twentieth century opera in England and the United States. Boston: G. K. Hall, 1976.
ML128.O4 N7 MUSIC REF

A chronological listing of performances of 20th century operas, giving composer, librettist, date and place. An appendix lists operas based on literary works and published operas.

Peterson, Bernard L. A century of musicals in black and white: an encyclopedia of musical stage works by, about, or involving African Americans. Westport, CT: Greenwood Press, 1993.
ML102.M88 P37 1993 MUSIC REF


Smith, Eric Ledell. Blacks in opera: an encyclopedia of people and companies, 1873-1993. Jefferson, NC: McFarland, 1995.
ML102.O6 S6 1995 MUSIC REF

Entries include dates and place of birth, education, repertoire, awards, and other career information.

Who's who in British opera. Brookfield, VT: Ashgate, 1993.
ML102.O6 A3 1993 MUSIC REF

A dictionary of individuals, both British and non-British, currently active in all aspects of opera performance or production. Based on questionnaires.

PLOT SUMMARIES/CHARACTERS

Bourne, Joyce. Who's who in opera: a guide to opera characters. New York: Oxford University Press, 1998.
ML102.O6 B68 1998 MUSIC REF

Drone, Jeanette Marie. Musical theater synopses: an index. Lanham, MD: Scarecrow Press, 1998.
ML128.O4 D76 suppl. MUSIC REF

Coverage expanded from original work to include ballets, oratorios, dramatic cantatas, films, TV, and several non-Western forms.

Ganzl, Kurt. Ganzl's book of the Broadway musical: 75 shows, from H.M.S. Pinafore to Sunset Boulevard. New York: Schirmer Books, 1995.
MT95.G19 1995 MUSIC REF

Ganzl, Kurt. Ganzl's book of the musical theatre. London: Bodley Head, 1988.
MT95.G2 1988 MUSIC REF

Contains synopses of ca. 300 works for the musical theater. Covers Great Britain, France, United States and Austria/Germany/Hungary.

Kobbe, Gustav. The definitive Kobbe's opera book. New York: Putnam, 1987.
MT95.K52 1987 MUSIC REF

PRODUCTION GUIDES

Eaton, Quaintance. Opera production: a handbook. Minneapolis: University of Minnesota Press,1961-1974. 2 v.
MT955.E25 MUSIC REF

Kornick, Rebecca Hodell. Recent American opera: a production guide. New York: Columbia University Press, 1990.
MT955.K82 1991 MUSIC REF

Provides brief plot summary and production requirements for over 200 operas.

Lucha-Burns, Carol. Musical notes: a practical guide to staffing and staging standards of the American musical theatre. New York: Greenwood Press, 1986.
MT955.L8 MUSIC REF

Martin, Nicholas Ivor. The Da Capo opera manual. New York: Da Capo Press, 1997.
ML102.O6 M35 1997 MUSIC REF

A practical production guide for opera.

Summers, W. Franklin. Operas in one act: a production guide. Lanham, MD: Scarecrow Press, 1997.
MT955.S86 1997 MUSIC REF

Most entries are for original English language works, or those which may be performed in English.

MISCELLANEOUS

Opera companies/houses, films/videos

Hotaling, Edward R. Shakespeare and the musical stage: a guide to sources, studies, and first performances. Boston, Mass.: G.K. Hall, 1990.
ML128.O4 H68 1990 MUSIC REF

Opera companies of the world: selected profiles. New York: Greenwood Press, 1992.
ML12.O63 1992 MUSIC REF

Opera on screen. Wien: IMZ, 1995. 2 vols.
ML128.O4 O647x 1995 MUSIC REF

A listing of ca. 4300 audiovisual recordings of opera and music theater from the earliest to most recent recordings. Definition of musical theater very broad to include oratorios, cantatas, ballets, etc.

Turnbull, Robert. The opera gazetteer. London: Trefoil, 1988.
ML12.T87x 1988 MUSIC REF

Brief histories of the major opera houses, theaters and festivals. International coverage.

Wlaschin, Ken. Opera on screen: a guide to 100 years of films and videos featuring operas, opera singers and operettas. Los Angeles: Beachwood Press, 1997.
ML102.O6 W55x 1997 MUSIC REF

In addition to the standard entries for individual operas, singers, conductors, etc., special subject entries are included.

Zietz, Karyl Lynn. Opera companies and houses of the United States: a comprehensive, illustrated reference. Jefferson, NC: McFarland, 1995.
ML13.Z54 1995 MUSIC REF
