December 17, 2003

ASU West graduates give new meaning to family togetherness

It took Ethel Short approximately a half-century longer than Leslie Short to earn a bachelor's degree - Ethel is 75; Leslie is 26. Considering that Ethel raised 10 children, worked evenings while her husband ran a business during the day, entered the real estate field, and successfully battled lymphoma a few years ago, one would be hard-pressed to criticize her for reaching this milestone on the same day as her granddaughter.

Though they did not take classes together at ASU West, the grandmother/granddaughter team will celebrate together as fall 2003 graduates. Ethel's bachelor's degree is in communication studies, while Leslie is completing her B.A. in sociology. Approximately 50 members of the family will gather to celebrate Ethel's and Leslie's accomplishments.

"My grandmother is amazing, and I'm proud of her," Leslie says. "She has been taking classes for as long as I can remember. It's wonderful that education is so important to her and she wants to keep learning."

"If you want to accomplish something, you can do it if you put your mind to it," Ethel says of her achievement.

Ethel spent many years taking community college courses. She earned an associate's degree from Phoenix College in 1975. Ethel continued to take what she describes as "fun" classes through the Maricopa Community Colleges until 2001, when she decided to pursue a bachelor's degree at ASU West.

"I felt accepted by the younger students in my classes, and there were times when I had the opportunity to talk to my classmates about what it was like living here when Phoenix was a much smaller town," Ethel says. She speaks with pride about having recently been installed as one of the founding members of ASU West's newly formed chapter of Lambda Pi Eta, the honor society of the National Communication Association.

Ethel and Leslie both plan to continue their education. Ethel has signed up for gerontology classes at ASU West. "Going to school keeps me young. But I'm not completely sure I want to study gerontology, since I'm not so old," she says.
Leslie, who lives in Peoria and graduated from Centennial High School, wants to help others and plans to attend graduate school in social work or public health.

The Short family has additional ties to ASU. Of Ethel's 10 children, 24 grandchildren, and 16 great-grandchildren (number 17 is on the way), two have received degrees from ASU.

The next ASU-related celebration will come in the spring, when Leslie's cousin, Christopher Short, graduates from ASU West. Christopher's sister, Ashley, is an ASU Main student. It's a safe bet the Short family's Sun Devil connections will only grow stronger in the years to come.
