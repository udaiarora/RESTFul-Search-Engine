W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University: Students win first place in logistics case competition

A team of undergraduate supply chain management students from the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University took first place among the seven undergraduate teams competing in the Denver Transportation Club's Logistics Case Contest on Feb. 6.

This competition has been held annually for the past seven years as part of the "Operations Stimulus" conference sponsored by Denver Transportation Club to raise money for education in logistics, transportation and supply chain.

Representing the W. P. Carey School of Business were students Olivia Bateman, Johnny Bray, Jami Coop, Teresa Hansen, Brett Johnson, and Maritza Tse. The team was coached by Dr. Sue Siferd, professor of supply chain management. Second place went to the U. S. Merchant Marine Academy, third place to the University of Arkansas, and fourth place to the University of Denver. Also competing were Michigan State University, Georgia Southern University, and the University of Houston.

The W. P. Carey School of Business is ranked 21 st for its undergraduate program, and fifth for its supply chain management program by U. S.News & World Report (2003). Current interest in supply chain management stems from the need for businesses to purchase, produce, move, and market goods and services on a global basis. Firms from throughout the country and in all sectors of the economy recruit W. P. Carey supply chain management graduates.

W. P. Carey School of Business

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report , the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
