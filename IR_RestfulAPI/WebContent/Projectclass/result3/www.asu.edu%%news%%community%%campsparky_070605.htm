July 6, 2005

Awards highlight success of Camp Sparky ’s ‘Big Camp’

During an excursion to Mount Lemmon in Tucson this month, nearly 70 fifth-graders from Flora Thew Elementary School took part in “Big Camp,” a three-day summer adventure for at-risk youngsters.

The annual program is coordinated by Camp Sparky, an ASU student-run organization sponsored by the College of Education. The goal of “Big Camp” is to promote learning as being fun and to encourage the students to attend college.

“We seek to inspire fifth-graders to love learning and have confidence in their future success,” says Mike Devenny, program chair. “Through unique learning experiences, we encourage them to pursue higher education. Camp Sparky members also develop commitment, awareness, leadership, and a sense of community.”

Camp Sparky was recognized this spring with the ASU Student Organization Resource Center’s (SORC) 2005 Outstanding Annual Program Award for its 2004 “Big Camp” excursion. The award honors student organizations and advisors who have made a difference in the areas of leadership, programming and service. This year’s awards were presented in April at the SORC’s annual Hall of Fame awards ceremony.

“Camp Sparky members have really set the bar high for student organizations on campus and within the community,” says Brian Goehner, coordinator of student involvement for SORC. “They have sustained great leadership year after year, which is somewhat unique considering the constant student turnover. In addition, they have also been able to maintain a level of excellence within their programming.”

Camp Sparky also received the 2000 and 2001 Outstanding Student Organization Award, which is considered SORC’s highest honor. The organization has received eight awards since the inception of the SORC Hall of Fame ceremony in 1997.

“It feels good to be recognized,” Devenny says. “It tells us what we’re doing matters, and it tells us we’re doing it well.”

Anel Mendez, a fifth-grade teacher from Thew Elementary School, is grateful for the dedication of Camp Sparky members.

“The volunteers are excellent role models for our kids,” Mendez says. “Our fifth-graders respect and look up to them. They have befriended our students, giving them an opportunity to see themselves as future ASU students.”

This year’s theme at “Big Camp” was “Exploring Nature,” with the goal of educating the youths on the effects humans have on their environment. To realize this goal, the students were taught how to use the “Three Rs” (reduce, reuse and recycle) around their home and school.

Activities to support this theme included making bird feeders out of water bottles; constructing kites from wooden dowels, cardboard, string and duct tape; learning basic first-aid skills; taking a nature hike; and discovering their natural surroundings through a scavenger hunt.

“While ‘Big Camp’ is only a few days long, a sense of community is developed by the end of the first day,” Devenny says. “Within that community, expectations are high that everyone will treat themselves and their peers with respect. They learn that they are more than the sum of their parts – and that together we can achieve great things.”

The fifth-graders that attend “Big Camp” are able to participate free of charge through the fundraising efforts of Camp Sparky.

In addition to the annual “Big Camp,” Camp Sparky hosts a series of “Day Camps” conducted on Fridays for fifth-graders at several elementary schools sites. The day camps feature four themed activity stations that are coordinated by Camp Sparky members.

Mendez says her students have benefited academically from Camp Sparky’s commitment to Thew Elementary School, and socially from their camp experiences.

“During the various day camps that precede ‘Big Camp,’ the students learn to work in groups by identifying and solving problems together teaching them life skills,” Mendez says. “During ‘Big Camp,’ they learn to rely on each other; they learn interdependence and other skills that are needed in life.”

Students interested in joining Camp Sparky can visit the Web site (www.campsparky.org) for more information.
