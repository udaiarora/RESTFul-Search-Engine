April 1, 2004

Business Conditions Index takes a tumble in March

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index declined to 62.8 in March from 67.5 the previous month. "All the components of the Index declined in strength over the month," said Dawn McLaren, an economic analyst with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center , which sponsors the index. "But the falloff in economic activity appears to be coming mostly from manufacturing sectors."

An index reading over 50 indicates that the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

The largest drop occurred in the production component, which fell by 8.6 points to 66.7. "In February, the production index had reached its highest level in the history of the index, so a retracement is not unexpected," McLaren said. "The new orders component fell slightly to reach 64.7, but that is still well into the range of growth."

As to the manufacturing decline, McLaren said: "Manufacturing has been suffering for a number of years, so this is not unusual to see during an economic recovery period. Even with the slight decline this month, manufacturing has gained a great deal of strength since mid-2003. This is reflected nationwide, according to the Institute for Supply Management's PMI."

The Prices Index remained well above neutral, at 68.3 in March. "Steel and fuel prices continue to be the impetus for this. The highest level reached by the Prices Index was 95.5 in December 1973, the time of the OPEC oil embargoes and subsequent gasoline crisis. In 1988, when an inflationary period caused monetary and fiscal policy problems, the Prices Index recorded levels in the low- to mid-70s."

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available online at www.wpcarey.asu.edu/azbci. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data available in prn format.
