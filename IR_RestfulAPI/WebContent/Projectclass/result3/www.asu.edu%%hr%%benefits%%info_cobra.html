COBRA
When your employment with ASU ends, you and your dependents (if they are covered under your plan) are entitled to continued insurance coverage according to the the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act of 1985 (COBRA) and all subsequent amendments.

Continued coverage is determined by the following criteria:

*The 18-month period may be extended to 29 months for qualified individuals who are considered to have been disabled under Title II or XVI of the Social Security Act. This must have occurred within 60 days of the date their plan coverage terminated due to a covered employee's termination of employment (for other than gross misconduct) or reduction in hours.

** A child is no longer considered a dependent at age 19, unless the child is a full-time student. As a full-time student, a child is considered a dependent until age 25 or until the child gets married, whichever comes first.

 

If a covered dependent has a second qualifying event while covered by the 18-month COBRA period, the individual will qualify for a total of 36 months of coverage, measured from the date of the original qualifying event.

If a child is born or adopted while you are under COBRA coverage, you must complete and return a COBRA form to add this dependent within 30 days of their birth, adoption or placement pending adoption to establish qualified beneficiary status for this new dependent under COBRA laws.

CONVERSION PRIVILEGE
 When coverage ends, it may be converted to an individual health insurance policy.

REMEMBER
You must notify the Human Resources Employee Service Center within 60 days of an event that affects COBRA.

PAYMENT
 If you choose COBRA, you are responsible for paying the entire monthly premium, with no State subsidy, in addition to an administrative fee of 2%.

The first payment is due within 45 days after you notify the plan administrator that you have elected COBRA coverage. You will be charged the full cost for any services or prescriptions before your premiums are paid; once premiums are paid, you will be reimbursed, minus any deductibles and co-payments.

COVERAGE TERMINATION
 The continuation of coverage under COBRA will end prior to the termination of the extended period for any of the following reasons:

  • The state ceases to provide a group health plan to any employee;
  • You fail to make the first payment within 45 days after you choose to continue coverage or any subsequent payment within 30 days of the established due date for payment, will result in the permanent cancellation of continuation coverage.
  • You or your qualified beneficiary become covered under any other health plan, unless that plan precludes coverage for pre-existing conditions;
  • You and/or your dependent become eligible to receive Medicare.
