January 30, 2006

Moore to lead Academic Affairs at West campus

Elsie Moore has been named vice provost for Academic Affairs at the West campus. Moore, who professional career includes an outstanding record of scholarship and leadership, begins her duties as vice provost July 1.

"Dr. Moore is a proven leader whose energy and experience will serve her well in her new position at the West campus," says Mark Searle, ASU vice president and provost at the West campus. "She will focus on continuous improvement of undergraduate education, facilitating efforts to provide students with exemplary educational opportunities both inside and outside the classroom."

Moore currently serves as director for the Division of Psychology in Education, College of Education, at ASU's Tempe campus. She has held this position since 1999 and has been an ASU faculty member since 1981. In her new position, Moore will be responsible for the oversight of academic program development, implementation and review.

Moore received her doctorate of philosophy and master's degree in human development from the University of Chicago, in 1980 and 1977, respectively. She completed her bachelor's degree at Elmhurst College in 1972, majoring in psychology and philosophy.

As an ASU psychology professor, Moore teaches classes in human diversity, personality development, and childhood and adolescence behavior. Her research interests include analyzing teaching strategies used by mothers in helping adopted children resolve cognitive tasks, in addition to classroom influences on ethnic minority children's mathematics achievement.

Moore has received several departmental, college and university awards for teaching excellence, including the ASU Alumni Teaching Award, the Student Council Outstanding Contributor to Students' Education award and the Student Services award. She also was honored by the city of Tempe for her outstanding community service. Moore has written numerous scholarly publications and papers relating to child development and social developments of the American youth.
