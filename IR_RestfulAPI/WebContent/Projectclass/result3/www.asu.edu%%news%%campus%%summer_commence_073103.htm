July 31, 2003

Commencement to honor 1,700 graduates

Almost 1,700 students will graduate this summer from ASU's three campuses.

ASU President Michael Crow will speak to the graduates from ASU's main and east campuses during summer commencement at 4 p.m., Aug. 8, in the Wells Fargo Arena. Almost 1,400 students will graduate from the two campuses.

ASU West will graduate 220 students at their summer commencement ceremony scheduled at 6:45 p.m., Aug. 12, in La Sala of the University Center Building.
Melissa Werner, program coordinator senior with ASU Summer Sessions & Commencement, said despite the smaller number of participants, summer commencement is a festive occasion for graduates and their families.

"We typically have about 200 students attending summer commencement," Werner said. "Many students complete coursework in first summer session and opt to participate in spring ceremonies, which reduces the number of participating students at the summer ceremony.

"Summer graduates do have the unique situation of being part of a small number of students graduating at a large institution," Werner said. "The ceremony itself is a bit shorter in the summer because of the smaller number of graduates."

Among this summer's graduates are two students who exemplify ASU's spirit and leadership.

Victoria Olson is graduating from ASU's Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communication, with a bachelor's degree in broadcasting. Frederic Leigh, associate director with the Cronkite School, said Olson has proven to be an outstanding student at ASU.

"Ms. Olson has vigorously pursued professional experience opportunities outside the classroom," he said. "She has completed an internship with Metro Source, a broadcast traffic report service in the Valley. She was part of the student news staff at the campus radio station over the past year and is currently doing an internship at KTAR News Radio in Phoenix. Victoria is an excellent student representative of the Cronkite School."

As an active ASU student, Olson was a member of the Order of Omega Honors Society, Society of Collegiate Scholars and a member of the Alpha Delta Pi sorority.
Steve Yazzie is graduating with a bachelor of science in environmental technology management. Phillip Huebner, director of American Indian Programs, says Yazzie's leadership skills have been beneficial to American Indian Programs and ASU.

Yazzie maintained a 3.4 GPA, is a Gates Millennium Scholar and a member of the Barrett Honors College. Yazzie also helped establish the American Indian Programs student organization, the One Nation Club.

"Mr. Yazzie is an accomplished student who is recognized by his peers, faculty, deans and the provost not only for his academic standing, but also for his leadership and commitment to the university community," Huebner said. "As the group's president and vice president, Yazzie recruited students, wrote the bylaws, initiated activities focusing on community outreach and also established the annual Student Recognition Banquet," Huebner said. "In 2001, the One Nation Club was recognized as ASU's Outstanding Student Organization of the year due in large part to Mr. Yazzie's efforts."
