Preamble

The Committee on Academic Professional Status shall act to improve the effectiveness and professionalism of all academic professionals at Arizona State University. Incorporating the tenets of academic freedom and respective professions, the Committee on Academic Professional Status shall endeavor to:

Foster and develop the role of the academic professional within the academic community emphasizing both the academic and the professional nature of this role;

Develop policies and procedures relating to academic professional service and representation that assures fair treatment to all members of this diverse class;

Provide leadership and governance of the academic professional community.

A. Authority and Functions

The Committee on Academic Professional Status (hereafter, referred to as the Committee) shall serve in a policy-forming and advisory capacity in the study, clarification, and formulation of policies and procedures affecting academic professionals at Arizona State University, including branch campuses, where appropriate. The Committee, although subject to the Provost's requests and to charges originating from that Office, shall also function autonomously in performing the organizational aspects of its duties. It shall independently gather appropriate data for issues which are ultimately sent forward to the Provost's Office as recommendations. The Committee is responsible for taking requests from individual academic professionals to the Provost's Advisory Committee and the Provost.

  1. Academic Professional Policies and Procedures

    The Committee shall develop and revise as needed academic professional policies and procedures for approval by the Provost's Office. Included policies and procedures related to annual evaluations, governance, salary adjustments, and other issues related to academic professionals. The Committee shall ensure that these policies and procedures are made known to new academic professionals through various activities including coordination of distribution via the Office of the Provost and presentation at new academic professional orientations.

  2. Liaison with Other Groups

    The Committee shall promote communication and understanding with other academic groups such as the Academic Professional Caucus, the Provost's Advisory Committee on Academic Professionals, and the Academic Professional Grievance Committee, and shall act as the official liaison between the Academic Assembly Senate and academic professionals.

    1. The Chair(s) of the Committee (or designate) shall attend each meeting of the Provost's Advisory Committee on Academic Professionals and the Academic Professional Caucus.

    2. At least one member of the Committee shall attend regularly scheduled Academic Senate meetings. The Committee representation and meeting attendance for other committees shall be addressed by the Chair(s) as appropriate.

    3. The Chair(s) of the Committee (or designate) shall attend each meeting of the Committee on Committees to provide input for future nominations to the Academic Professional Grievance Committee, and Committee on Academic Professionals.

  3. Reports

    The Chair(s) shall write an annual report on the Committee's activities for the year at the end of the fiscal year. Any unfinished business will be documented for the benefit of the new Chair.

B. Membership and Composition

  1. Terms of Office

    The Committee shall have six members with staggered three year terms beginning July 1, and two alternates who shall serve a one year term. The terms of vacated positions shall be completed so that the original pattern of terms is maintained.

  2. Diverse Membership of the Committee

    Composition of the Committee shall reflect the diversity of the academic professional community and the relative size of the various subunits. At any time, the Committee shall have only one member from any academic unit, except that the larger colleges/units may have up to two members on the Committee because of the large portion of academic professionals that such a unit comprises. Over an appropriate period of time, the Committee shall include members from all other colleges and other University units or off-campus units. As a guideline, those units with larger numbers of academic professionals shall be represented more often, but over a period of time, individuals from each unit shall be provided an opportunity to serve on the Committee.

  3. Replacement of Committee Members

    In the event of a vacancy on the Committee, alternates from the most recent election shall be asked to serve. In the event all alternates being unavailable CAPS will request the chair of the Committee on Committees to fill the vacant position(s).

  4. Eligibility Requirements

    1. To be eligible for election to the Committee, Academic Professionals must have at least three years of service at Arizona State University.

    2. Academic Professionals with year-to-year appointments, who otherwise meet the requirements, are eligible to serve. No more than one individual on a year-to-year appointment shall serve on the Committee at any one time.

    3. No person may serve two successive terms on the Committee. A minimum of a one-year hiatus in membership is required.

    4. No person may serve concurrently on the Committee and the Academic Professional Grievance Committee or other elected university level academic professional standing committees.

C. Meetings

  1. Meeting Schedule

    Regular Committee meetings shall be held bimonthly throughout the fiscal year.

  2. Special Meetings

    1. Special meetings may be convened by the Chair(s) or designate.

    2. A special meeting must be called within two weeks of written submission by any academic professional of a request for action regarding an issue within the purview of the Committee

  3. Quorum

    The presence of one half of the voting membership constitutes a quorum for the transaction of any business.

  4. Duties of Officers

    1. The Chair(s) shall prepare agendas, conduct meetings, serve as the contact with the Provost's Office, serve as liaisons with other campus organizations, serve ex-officio to the Provost's Advisory Committee on Academic Professionals, and prepare the annual report.

    2. The Secretary shall keep and distribute the minutes prior to the meetings, and reserve meeting rooms following the first meeting of the Committee.

    3. The Archivist shall collect and maintain a complete set of the records of the Committee's activities for the Archives to help the CAPS representative to the Committee on Committees. The outgoing Archivist will instruct the new Archivist in the procedure to maintain records.

    4. The "Web Master" shall keep the CAPS Web page up to date and administer the list server for the Committee. Also a manual shall be kept to allow a successor to continue keeping the system operational.

  5. Organizational Meeting

    1. The first meeting of each year shall be an organizational meeting during which the Committee shall:

      1. Review the Bylaws and previous annual report;

      2. Select Chair(s), Secretary, Archivist, and Web Master;

      3. Select regular meeting time and locations;

      4. Identify representatives to other committees as needed (i.e., Academic Senate, Provost's Advisory Committee on Academic Professionals, Legislative Task Force, etc.);

    2. A designated member from the previous year's Committee shall give all new members the latest annual report and the bylaws.

    3. The newly selected Chair(s) shall report the names of the new members of the Committee and the officers to the Provost's Office in writing before the second Committee meeting.

  6. Attendance

    Members who miss more than three meetings per year without prior approval of the Chair(s) shall be replaced for the balance of the term of service. See Section B.3 of the CAPS bylaws.

D. Amendments

Amendments to the bylaws submitted by academic professionals for review and action, or those initiated by the Committee, shall be reviewed annually or as deemed necessary by the Committee. Amendments recommended by the Committee shall be submitted to an open forum of academic professionals for discussion. The Committee shall distribute a ballot to all academic professionals within one month of the open forum. Acceptance by two thirds of those voting will implement the amendment. The Chair of the Provost's Advisory Committee on Academic Professionals (or their designate, who is not a member of the Committee on Academic Professional Status, if the Chair of the Provost's Committee is also a member of CAPS) shall be responsible for tallying the ballots and reporting the results directly to the Committee and to the membership at large via Insight.
