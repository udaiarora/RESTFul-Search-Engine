April 17, 2003

Nursing students earn scholarship

Three ASU College of Nursing students are recipients of the prestigious Barbara Browne Connors Nursing Scholarship for the spring 2003 semester.

Scholarship recipients Lisa DiMenna of Mesa, Sharon Holmes of Gilbert, and Karen "Beth" Saltzman of Phoenix, have their sights on earning advanced nursing degrees to further benefit their local communities.

The Barbara Browne Connors Nursing Scholarships award funds to working nurses returning to school to earn their bachelor's or master's degrees.

DiMenna graduated from ASU's College of Nursing undergraduate program in December 1998. She currently works in the newborn intensive care unit at Banner Desert Samaritan Medical Center and plans to earn a master's degree to become a neonatal nurse practitioner where she can expand her scope of practice within her specialty area of sick neonates.

Holmes believes there are two ways to make a difference in nursing. One is working in administration; the other is working in education. After completing a master's degree in nursing at ASU, she hopes to work in a hospital in-service program, either as an administrator or educator.

Saltzman recently returned to ASU to complete a master's degree in women's health nursing, ultimately becoming a nurse practitioner. After completing that milestone, she plans to further her education and become a certified nurse midwife. Saltzman is interested in serving a population that has been underserved by the conventional medical delivery system, either as a nurse practitioner or as a midwife.

The nursing scholarship was established in fall of 1998 to honor Barbara Browne Connors, a registered nurse whose volunteer efforts benefited scouting groups, schools, the arts, the Arizona Heart Association and many other organizations. The scholarships, awarded each fall and spring semester, are funded by the Connors Family Trust in memory of Connors, who died in 1996 after a yearlong battle with cancer.
