October 27, 2003

Diplomat to speak on economic growth, diplomacy in Taiwan

A Yale-educated diplomat who has long been an influential link between the United States and the Republic of China will give a public lecture at 10:40 a.m., Oct. 29, in the Nursing Building, room 110, as part of the new Chiang Ching-kou Chair in the Center for Asian Studies.

Fredrick E. Chien, president of the Control Yuan of the Republic of China (ROC), will speak on "The Economic Growth and Pragmatic Diplomacy of the Republic of China in the 21st Century." His visit is sponsored by the Barry Goldwater Chair of American Institutions.

As director of the ROC government information office in the 1970s, Chien made several speaking tours of the United States to promote a better understanding and a closer cooperation between the two countries. His tours led him to publish two books, "Speaking as a Friend" (1975) and "More Views of a Friend" (1976).

While living in Washington, D.C., during the 1980s as a representative of the Coordination Council for North American Affairs, Chien was known as an eloquent speaker and a statesman of keen intelligence, extraordinary tact and administrative ability. He was invited to speak in 41 of the 50 states.

Later he returned home to serve as the minister of foreign affairs. He led a strenuous campaign in pursuit of the ROC's participation in the United Nations, an effort that was unsuccessful. In 1996 he was elected the speaker of the assembly, making him third in line of governance after the president and vice president.

Chien came to America as a special envoy after the terrorist attacks of Sept. 11, 2001, to convey a message of condolence and support, and to deliver a donation of $1 million to the Twin Towers Fund. He also announced the donation of humanitarian supplies to assist Afghan refugees.

For information on Chien's visit, contact the ASU Center for Asian Studies at (480) 965-7184.
