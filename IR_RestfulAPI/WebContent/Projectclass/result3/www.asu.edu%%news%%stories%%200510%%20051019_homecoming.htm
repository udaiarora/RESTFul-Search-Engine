October 19, 2005

Homecoming Block Party offers a bit of everything

Some of the events at this year’s Sparky’s Reunion FestDevil Homecoming Block Party Oct. 29 will be loud and raucous, and some will be full of giggles.

For the more up-tempo attractions, we’re talking about the Battle of the Bands and the Kids Zone, of course.

But there will be several areas that will be more quiet and contemplative, too: the Alumni Art Walk, for one, and the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Alumni Book Fair for another.

College of Liberal Arts and Sciences graduates who have published books, and more than a dozen of them will sign or read from their books during the Block Party.

Authors this year will be coming from Florida, New York, North Dakota and other far-flung points to participate in the block party writing event, said Linda Raish, associate director of alumni relations for CLAS, who organizes the book fair.

“Even though they have to pay their own way, they are pleased to come,” Raish says. “It could grow to be a large event, but we have to limit it to 18 or so authors.”

The fair is juried by a commitee of CLAS faculty, staff and students who read the works and decide which authors will be invited to participate, with an eye toward a good mix of genres.

This year’s Block Party fair is the second sponsored by CLAS.

“Last year, we only asked people we knew who had published books to participate,” Raish says. “This year, we sent out a call for authors through the newsletter.”

This year, the fair also will include books available for purchase by authors not able to attend the block party, and tours of the Piper Writers House, which is part of CLAS.

The schedule includes readings at the Piper Writers House. The times for the readings are:

• 12:30 p.m. – Jorn Ake, author of “Asleep in the Lightning Fields,” and Richard Yanez, “El Paso Del Norte: Stories on the Border.”

• 2 p.m., Kevin Haworth, “The Discontinuity of Small Things,” and Ruth Ellen Kocher, “One Girl Babylon.”

Signing their books in the CLAS tent will be:

• 12:30 p.m. – Ronald Vossler, “Dakota Kraut”; Robert Brown, “The Power of Handshaking.”

• 1 p.m. – Wendy Marshall, “William Beaudine: From Silents to Television”; Marty Schupk, “Youth Baseball Drills.”

• 1:30 p.m. – T.M. McNally, “The Goat Bridge”; Arthur Edwards, “Stuck Outside of Phoenix.”

• 2 p.m. – Irena Pratis, “Touch”; Bonny Sanders, “Touching Shadows.”

• 2:30 p.m.: Alan Tongret, “The Inn of a Thousand Days: A Memoir”; D. Franklin Schultz, “A Language of the Heart.”

Signed books will be available for purchase at the CLAS tent, including Amanda Brown’s “Legally Blond”; Allison Dubois’ “Don’t Kiss Them Goodbye”; Rosemary King’s “Border Confluences: Borderland Narratives”; and Phil Mickelson’s “One Magical Sunday (But Winning Isn’t Everything).”

Tours of the Piper Writers House will be offered from 11:30 a.m. – 12:15 p.m. and 1:15 – 1:45 p.m.
