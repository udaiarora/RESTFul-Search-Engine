October 30, 2003

Tile painting to help support college's preschool

ASU Homecoming revelers are invited to join College of Education (CoE) faculty, staff, family and friends under their tent at the ASU Homecoming FestDevil Block Party for an activity that promises to delight kids and adults of all ages while supporting a worthy cause.

Guests will have the opportunity to create colorful, one-of-kind hand-painted tiles for purchase as family keepsakes or to donate as decorative pieces for display on the CoE Preschool playground.

"The tiles are fun to paint, and they make great gifts for families," says Charlotte Woodward, director of the CoE Preschool.

An ASU ceramics student and representatives of the preschool will lead the tile-painting. The tiles will be available for purchase in two sizes: the cost for one small tile is $20 or three for $50. Large tiles will sell for $50. Proceeds from the event will be deposited with the ASU Foundation to benefit the preschool playground fund.
The CoE tent will be on Alumni Lawn just west of Old Main.

For several years, youngsters from the CoE Preschool have joined in the annual ASU homecoming festivities as participants in the parade. The tradition will continue this year, as the children will be carried atop a horse-drawn wagon in the parade. The tile-painting activity will begin immediately following the parade and will continue until 12:30 p.m.

Woodward says the funds raised from the event will support a redesign project that is planned for the preschool playground.

"Our goal is to create a natural habitat playground that will feature trails, paths, conversation spaces and a shrub maze that the children can wander through," she says.

Housed within the Farmer Education Building, the CoE Preschool serves three- through five-year-olds in a culturally sensitive environment. A unique feature of the program is an afternoon "Two-Way-Immersion in Spanish Time" (TWIST) program.

The emergent curriculum includes activities that help develop children's appreciation for diversity. The preschool encourages the enrollment of children with special needs, and these children are integrated into the program.

Sherwood, with the College of Education, can be reached at (480) 965-2114 or (joan.sherwood@asu.edu).
