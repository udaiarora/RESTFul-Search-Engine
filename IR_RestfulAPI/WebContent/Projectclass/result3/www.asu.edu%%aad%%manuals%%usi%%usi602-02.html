USI 602–02: Eligibility for Services

Purpose

To define eligibility for services from ASU counseling centers

Source

University policy

Policy

Full- and Part-Time Students

All enrolled ASU students are eligible for a needs assessment and a range of ongoing services as approporiate.

Summer Students

During the summer sessions, a student enrolled in any class is eligible for services (as described above — see USI 601–01, “Clinical Services”).

A continuing student (i.e., one who has completed spring semester and is registered for fall semester) is eligible for services in the summer, whether enrolled in summer school or not.

Spouses and Significant Others of Eligible Students

Spouses and significant others of eligible students may receive couples’ counseling with the enrolled ASU student when staffing permits. Services are not available to a student’s spouse or significant other if his or her difficulties are the primary focus of therapy. However, the non-ASU affiliated spouse or significant other is eligible for career testing and test preparation courses on a fee basis.

Students Withdrawing from the University

Students who are withdrawing or have withdrawn from the university may receive one or two sessions of exit counseling.

Students Entering the University

Students entering the university may receive one or two sessions of preenrollment counseling if they present written confirmation of their acceptance into the university.

Faculty and Staff

Faculty and staff are not eligible for clinical services. However, they may be eligible for consultation services regarding ASU students.

Spouses and Children of Faculty and Staff

Spouses and children of faculty and staff are not eligible for clinical services. However, at the discretion of the center, they may be seen for purposes of referral to appropriate agencies.

Nonuniversity Persons

Persons not associated with ASU are not eligible for clinical services. Any nonuniversity individual requesting clinical services will be referred to resources in the community.

See the counseling centers’ Web pages for detailed information on services provided at Counseling and Consultation at the Tempe campus, Student Counseling & Health Services at the West campus, and Student Counseling Services at the Polytechnic campus.
