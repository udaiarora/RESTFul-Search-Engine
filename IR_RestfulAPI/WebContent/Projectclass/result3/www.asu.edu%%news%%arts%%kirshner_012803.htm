January 28, 2003

Event highlights refugee struggles

Russian actress Mia Kirshner will share her experiences from a recent visit to Russia interviewing women and children refugees displaced as a result of the Chechen war, 7 p.m., Jan. 28, at ASU Memorial Union (MU), room 218. The event, sponsored jointly by University Career Women (UCW) and Amnesty International, is open to the public at no charge.

Kirshner, who starred in such films as "Not Another Teen Movie" and "Anna Karenina" and the television series "24," recently returned from Nazran, Ingushetia, where she saw firsthand the human condition of the Chechen people - primarily women and children - and how the war is affecting the families caught in the crisis. Their stories, and the lives of other women and children refugees from around the world, will be documented in a forthcoming book "I Live Here" by Kirshner, in cooperation with Amnesty International.

During the program, Kirshner will feature a multimedia presentation to illustrate the plight of the Chechen people. Diane Post of Arizona Coalition Against Domestic Violence will be on hand to answer questions about local advocacy efforts and opportunities and speak of her experiences working on domestic violence issues in Russia.

Stories and photographs gathered on the trip are displayed in the basement of the MU through January.

For information, call Markus Roznowski at (480) 213-0194.

Summerill, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-4823 or (lsummer@asu.edu).
