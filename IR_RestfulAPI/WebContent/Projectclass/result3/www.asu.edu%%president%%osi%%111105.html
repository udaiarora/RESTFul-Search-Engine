American Universities Strive to Become More Energy Efficient
Daily Vidette
Across the country, more colleges and universities are fighting rising fuel prices by becoming "greener," including using hybrid vehicles, wind turbines and energy-efficient buildings.

 

Campaign Pledges to Conserve Energy
The Harvard Crimson
The Harvard Green Campus Initiative (GCI) has announced a new pledge campaign that asks students, staff, and faculty members to commit to a series of energy-saving measures.

 

New York State to Cut Greenhouse Gases from Cars

ENN
Cars sold or registered in New York State must cut carbon dioxide emissions beginning in 2009.

 

Technology Created at Colorado State Honored with Environmental Award
Newswise
EnviroFit International Ltd., a two-year-old non-profit company created with Colorado State University technology, was named one of the top five environmental laureates by Silicon Valley 's Tech Museum of Innovation Wednesday night. The Tech Museum Awards selected 25 companies and organizations from around the world as laureates for technological advancements that improve the human condition in the areas of environment, economic development, education, equality and health.

The U.S. Green Building Council (USGBC) has announced the 2005 recipients of the Fourth Annual Leadership Awards.

Green Fuel Obligation Announced
BBC News
Oil suppliers will have to ensure 5% of fuel sold in the UK comes from renewable sources by 2010, the government says.

 

Key points of Sustainability Report for Torino Winter Olympics
People's Daily Online

The Torino Olympic Organizing Committee (TOROC) unveiled its new sustainability report aimed at making next year's Winter Olympics the greenest ever at the Sixth World Conference on Sport and Environment, which opened in Nairobi Wednesday.

 

Renewable Energy Investment at Record High - Report
Planet Ark
Global investment in renewable energy hit a record $30 billion last year, accounting for 20-25 percent of all investment in the power industry, and with solar power the fastest-growing energy technology, a Worldwatch Institute report released on Sunday said.

 

UNDP, Arcelor Agree to Promote Sustainable Development in China
Forbes
The United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) and Arcelor, the world's second largest steel producer, have signed an initial pact to strengthen public-private partnerships (PPPs) and to promote sustainable development in China .
