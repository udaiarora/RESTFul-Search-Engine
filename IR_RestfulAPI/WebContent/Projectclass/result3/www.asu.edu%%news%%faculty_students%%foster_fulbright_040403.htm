April 4, 2003

Foster earns award recognition for sixth time

ASU Regents' Professor David Foster is no stranger to the prestigious Fulbright Scholars program - he's just received his sixth award and will be going to Argentina this summer as a Senior Specialist.

The Fulbright Scholar Program is recognized as the U.S. government's flagship program in international educational exchange. Fulbright's new Senior Specialist program offers two- to six-week grants to leading U.S. academics and professionals to support curricular and faculty development and institutional planning at academic institutions in 140 countries around the world. The new program is designed to complement the traditional Fulbright Scholars program that offers assignments of two to six months.

Foster will be a Fulbright Senior Specialist of U.S. Studies & Literature in the School of Languages at the National University of Córdoba/National University of Rio Cuarto in Argentina. Argentina's educational system is considered one of the world's leaders in the teaching of English as a second language.

While in Argentina in August, Foster will also continue his work on urban Latino literature.

Foster was first awarded a Fulbright in 1967 when he taught courses in Argentina on the structure of English at the Universidad Nacional de la Plata and the Instituto Nacional de Lenguas Vivas. He returned in 1973 to teach similar courses at the Instituto Nacional de Lenguas Vivas, the Universidad de Buenos Aires, and Profesorado Superior.

In 1985 Foster taught American literary theory and discourse analysis at the Universidade Nacional do Parána in Curitiba, Brazil, and in 1989 he taught American literature at the Universidad Católica in Montevideo, Uruguay.

He was also awarded a Fulbright to India in the early 1990s to conduct research on the subaltern studies group, but was unable to take the assignment due to administrative commitments at ASU.
