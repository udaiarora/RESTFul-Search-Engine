ASU College of Business launches policy forum: Arizona Campus Examines State and Local Fiscal Policy

The Arizona State University (ASU) College of Business brings together researchers, policy-makers, and leaders from business and the community for an examination of state and local fiscal policy at the inaugural Arizona Campus forum Wednesday, Jan. 22 on the ASU main campus.

Speakers include economics professor Dennis Hoffman and management professor Gerry Keim, presenting research that compares Arizona with other rapidly growing areas of the U.S. Panelists representing a range of views will include Glendale Mayor Elaine Scruggs, former House Appropriations Chair Laura Knaperek, former Senate Appropriation Chair Ruth Solomon; China Mist Tea Company CEO Dan Schweiker and others.

Arizona Campus is designed to be a recurring event presented by the College of Business. It is modeled after the successful Washington Campus program, which ASU helped to found. Arizona Campus will provide neutral ground upon which participants can examine public policy issues affecting 21st century economic development.

"The role of Arizona campus is to be an honest broker of research and information," said Dr. Keim, director of the program. "Although a range of ideological views will be expressed by participants during discussions, Arizona Campus will not endorse any advocacy position. Think of it as the Switzerland of policy debate."

The morning session will be held in BA 199 at the College of Business on the ASU main campus, with continental breakfast at 7:30 a.m. and the welcome by Dean Larry E. Penley at 8. Lunch (included with registration), afternoon sessions and the evening reception (concluding at 6:45 p.m.) will be at the University Club on campus.

Seating is limited to 50 at $595 each. Participants from the public and non-profit sectors may attend for $195. Members of the Arizona House & Senate will be admitted free. For more information call Jill Kipnes in the College of Business Executive Education office at 480-965-1253, or see http://www.cob.asu.edu/exec and click on Arizona Campus.

Media seating is available by contacting Liz Farquhar at 480-965-7774.
