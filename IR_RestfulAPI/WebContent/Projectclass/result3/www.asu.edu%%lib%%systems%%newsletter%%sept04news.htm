After-hours System Support

Now that the Libraries are open 24 hours a day, five days a week, technical issues such as problems connecting to databases may get noticed at all hours of the night. Since LIST was not funded to provide 24-hour support to coincide with the new library hours, and is not staffed to do so, we would appreciate it if everyone would continue to report system problems of this kind to the SOS desk at 5-9093. After the hours that the desk is staffed, please leave a message and we’ll address the problem as soon as possible -- which most often will be first thing in the morning. True systems emergencies are very rare, and when they do occur they generate automatic notifications to the appropriate staff member via pagers 24/7.

System Outage to Affect Libraries

On Saturday, October 16, 2004, IT will be upgrading equipment in the ECA and ECB computer rooms. The ASU Library Catalog and the Video Streaming servers are housed in the ECA computer room. These systems will go down at 6:00PM on Saturday October 16, and are expected to be back online by midnight.

The following functions will be offline from 6:00PM to midnight:
· Online Catalog
· Electronic Journals and Indexes
· My Account
· All Staff functions (Circulation, Tech Services, etc.)
· Video Courses Online
· Streaming Audio Files
· Electronic Course Reserves
· ASU AFS servers (campus-wide internet access may be down)

New Databases Available

The following new databases have been put online (http://www.asu.edu/lib/top-menus/new.html):

Statistical Theory And Method Abstracts (STMA) (Subscribed)
Statistical Theory & Method Abstracts (STMA) provides worldwide coverage of published articles on mathematical statistics and probability. These abstracts provide valuable information on new developments in all fields of probability theory, estimation, linear models, stochastic processes, operations research, testing of hypotheses, experimental design and time series analysis. Each year approximately 6,000 abstracts are published summarizing articles from journals worldwide.

Oxford Dictionary of National Biography (On Trial until 10/26)
Oxford Dictionary of National Biography includes 50,000 biographies of people who shaped the history of the British Isles and beyond, from the earliest times to the year 2000.
