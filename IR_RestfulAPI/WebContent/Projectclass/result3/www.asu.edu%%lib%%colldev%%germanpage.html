Arizona university resources

German Internet Resources This site by professor John Alexander, Arizona State University, features general information, search engines and specific subjects related to German Studies.

German Language Resources in Greater Phoenix Area Some subjects of interest listed by professor John Alexander, Arizona State University,  are  Phoenix companies in Germany, local German restaurants, and German tutors and translators in the Phoenix area. This site is currently under costruction but additonal subjects are planned.

German Section and Graduate Program, Dept. of Languages & Literatures, Arizona State University  Provides information on  faculty,  teaching Aasistants, class materials, textbooks, courses, major/minor requirements, the Regensburg Summer Program, and Study Abroad programs.

German Studies Pathfinder A guide to basic reference and other important works in German Studies held at the University of Arizona Libraries. Could be useful in most academic libraries as most of these titles are often available in many academic libraries.

Internet Resources for German Studies In addition to providing links to German Studies resources on the Internet, this page serves as a guide to major titles held at the University of Arizona Libraries.

Department of German Studies, University of Arizona  Includes information on faculty, academic programs, course descriptions, study abroad, campus clubs, and internet resources.

Dictionaries

The Alternative German Dictionary  Chiefly a dictionary of slang. Includes 150 entries.

LEO English-German Dictionary  Includes over 170,000 entries.

Langenscheidt's New College German Dictionary   Contains around 220,000 words and phrases used in modern English  and German.


Literature

Deutsche Literatur im Internet  Lists only German literary texts that originally appeared in print.

Feuchtwanger Memorial Library A site devoted to German exile literature.

German Studies Web: Literature Resources  Includes extensive links to electronic journals, literature in electronic form, links to manuscript collection, and anthologies of poetry and prose. Designed by the Western European Specialists Section of the Association of College and Research Libraries.

Middle High German Conceptual DataBase  Claims to be the largest electronic text archive of medieval German literature (appr. 130 texts, 1 million text lines and 8 million individual words).

19th Century German Stories  Selected  tales from  The Brothers Grimm, Heinrich Hoffman, Wilhelm Busch and Jeremias Gotthelf. Maintained by the Department of Foreign Languages of Virginnia Commonwealth University. Texts in German and English.

Projekt Gutenberg-DE  Online texts by more than 200 classic German writers.

International Dada Archive (University of Iowa)     A resource on the Dada movement and individual Dada writers and artists.

Electronic Text Collections

The German Collection at the University of Virginia  Contains collection of online German-language texts.  Some texts are restricted to University of Virginia users only.

The Kassandra project: Visionary German Women around 1800
A series of web pages linking to German women writers, artists, and thinkers during the second half of the eighteenth century through the first decades of the nineteenth.

Project Wittenberg  "Home to works by and about Martin Luther and other Lutherans," this website was conceived to be the "first step towards an international electronic library of Lutheranism." Includes hymns, theological treatises, commentaries, etc, in German and in translation where available.
