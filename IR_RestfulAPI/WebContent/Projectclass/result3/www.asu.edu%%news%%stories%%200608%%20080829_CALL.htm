August 29, 2006

Center calms public speaking fears

ASU's Communication Assessment and Learning Lab (CALL) is an innovative facility designed to help students and professionals overcome their public speaking anxieties.

The CALL Center on ASU's West campus provides such invaluable services as speech review, mentoring and interviewing techniques.

The center was established in 2004 to help ASU students improve their written and verbal communication skills. Its success has resulted in its expansion to offering sessions to the local corporate community, public agencies and the general public.

?We're here to help people sell their organization, persuade an audience, organize presentations, develop strong and effective delivery techniques and visual aids and, or course, get over their speech anxiety,? says Meg McConnaughy, CALL's director and a 1994 West campus graduate. ?Preparation and knowledge are the prerequisites for a successful presentation, and confidence and control are equally important. Participants leave with a solid understanding of their presentation, giving them the credibility and confidence to connect with their audience.?

More than two dozen CALL mentors are working with a growing number of groups and individuals to improve communication skills that ultimately lead to success in the boardroom, in the workday world and in life.

McConnaughy attributes much of the program's success to its corps of mentors, which consists of qualified ASU students and alumni who have been selected based on faculty and staff referrals, as well as their maturity and proven ability to work well in a classroom situation.

All the mentors also have completed a public speaking course and have a history of overcoming speaking challenges.

?Our mentors did not start out as polished, confident speakers,? McConnaughy says. ?In fact, it's quite the opposite in most cases ? they each have overcome their own public speaking issues. This is what makes the CALL mentors such excellent and successful teachers, because they can empathize with participants who are learning to conquer their own public speaking limitations.?

Mentors also are prepared to help participants select and create effective visual aids, including Microsoft PowerPoint, that enable comprehension and enhance retention of their message. Mentors share ideas and proven methods that put real ?sizzle? into presentations via visual images, charts, graphs and more.

?When you walk out of the lab, you know if your joke works and if your story is captivating,? McConnaughy says. ?You know how you will be received by your audience, which is valuable feedback your mirror cannot provide.?

The CALL Center, Located in the Faculty Administration Building on the West campus, 4701 W. Thunderbird Road, Phoenix, can be reached by calling (602) 543-5766. Additional information is available by visiting the Web site (www.west.asu.edu/call), or by sending an e-mail to (call@asu.edu).
