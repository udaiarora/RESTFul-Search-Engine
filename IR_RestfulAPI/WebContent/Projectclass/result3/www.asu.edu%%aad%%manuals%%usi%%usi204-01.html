USI 204–01: Undergraduate Program of Study

Purpose

To establish a program of study for undergraduate students relating to the student’s specific degree requirements for bachelor degree completion and graduation

Sources

University Registrar’s Office
Individual colleges within the university

Policy

Undergraduate students following the curriculum requirements of the 1994–96 or earlier catalog editions must file an official Undergraduate Program of Study form within the semester in which the 87th semester hour of credit is earned. A student who does not file an Undergraduate Program of Study on time is prevented from further registration.

To continue to meet athletic eligibility requirements, an undergraduate student-athlete must file an Undergraduate Program of Study prior to the beginning of the fifth semester of full-time collegiate enrollment.

Courses and requirements on the program of study must be approved by:

  1. the undergraduate student’s advisor

  2. the dean’s designated representative within each college

    and

  3. the University Registrar’s Office.

The undergraduate student must file a Petition for Changes to Undergraduate Program of Study form if any changes to the program are necessary.

ProceduresD

Filing an Undergraduate Program of Study

Responsibility
Action
Undergraduate student
  1. Obtain an Undergraduate Program of Study form online from the University Registrar’s Office.

  2. Obtain two unofficial transcript copies at the University Registrar’s Office or any registrar site (the Records Information counter at the Tempe campus, Records & Registration at the West campus, or Registration Services at the Polytechnic or Downtown Phoenix campus) by presenting an ASU student ID card.

  3. Obtain and photocopy all complete, up-to-date transfer transcripts from the departmental file, if a transfer student.

  4. Obtain a Curriculum Check Sheet from the college of the student’s major.

  5. Take to the advisor:

    1. the program of study

    2. unofficial transcript copies

    3. transfer transcript photocopies, if any,

      and

    4. a Curriculum Check Sheet.
  1. Complete the program of study form with the advisor’s assistance.

  2. Obtain signatures of approval on the program of study form from the advisor and the dean’s representative.

  3. Give the completed, signed program of study form to the college.
College
  1. Review the program of study form for departmental, college, general studies, and university requirements.
If approved:
  1. Forward the approved form to the Graduation Section, the Records Information Counter at the Tempe campus, Records & Registration at the West campus, or Registration Services at the Polytechnic or Downtown Phoenix campus.
If not approved:
  1. Make appropriate additions or changes in consultation with the student. Forward the approved form to the Graduation Section, the Records Information Counter at the Tempe campus, Records & Registration at the West campus, or Registration Services at the Polytechnic or Downtown Phoenix campus.
Graduation Section, the Records Information counter at the Tempe campus, Records & Registration at the West campus, or Registration Services at the Polytechnic or Downtown Phoenix campus
  1. Update the Student Information System (SIS) computer files to reflect the program of study.

  2. Verify that the program of study meets requirements for:

    1. catalog determination

    2. the minimum number of semester hours for the degree

    3. the minimum number of upper-division semester hours

    4. First-Year Composition

    5. residency credit

      and

    6. the minimum cumulative grade point average (GPA).
  1. Approve or disapprove the program of study.
If the program of study is approved:
  1. Send a copy of the approved program of study to the college advising unit.

  2. Mail a copy of the approved program of study to the undergraduate.
If the program of study is not approved:
  1. Notify the undergraduate by mail that the program of study has not been approved.

  2. Return the disapproved program of study to the college advising unit.
College advising unit
  1. Contact the undergraduate to make an appointment for further advisement.

Filing a Petition for Changes to an Undergraduate Program of Study

Responsibility
Action
Student
  1. Obtain a Petition for Changes to Undergraduate Program of Study form online from the University Registrar’s Office Web site.

  2. Take the petition form to the advisor.

  3. Complete the petition with the advisor’s assistance.

  4. Obtain the advisor’s signature of approval on the petition.

  5. Give the completed, signed petition to the college.
College
  1. Review the petition for departmental, college, general studies, and university requirements.
If approved:
  1. Forward the petition to the Graduation Section, the Records Information counter at the Tempe campus, Records & Registration at the West campus, or Registration Services at the Polytechnic or Downtown Phoenix campus.
If not approved:
  1. Make appropriate additions or changes in consultation with the student. Forward the approved form to the Graduation Section, the Records Information counter at the Tempe campus, Records & Registration at the West campus, or Registration Services at the Polytechnic or Downtown Phoenix campus.
Graduation Section, the Records Information counter at the Tempe campus, Records & Registration at the West campus, or Registration Services at the Polytechnic or Downtown Phoenix campus
  1. Verify that the petition adjusts the program to meet the requirements for:

    1. the minimum number of semester hours for the degree

    2. the minimum number of upper-division semester hours

    3. First-Year Composition

    4. residency credit

      and

    5. the minimum cumulative GPA.

  1. Approve or disapprove the petition.
If the petition is approved:
  1. Update the Student Information System (SIS) computer files to reflect the petition adjusting the program of study.
If the petition is not approved:
  1. Return the disapproved petition to the college advising unit.
College advising unit
  1. Notify the undergraduate by mail that the petition has not been approved. Suggest that the student make an appointment for further advisement.
