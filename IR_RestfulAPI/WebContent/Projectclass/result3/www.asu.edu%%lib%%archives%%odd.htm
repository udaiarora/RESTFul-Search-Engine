Dawn of a New Day

Odd S. Halseth Photograph Collection


Prior to Odd Halseth's work in Phoenix he was Curator of Archaeology at the Museum of New Mexico. Photographs from the period document the archaeological sites he visited as staff of the School of American Research. Photographs taken in Arizona and New Mexico on his many field trips indicate his interest in the contemporary Native Americans and their culture. He also ventured into the contemporary Native American community invited by Silas Johns, a founder of a religious group, to photograph their "sweat lodge." Another photograph shows a camp site with parked cars at the foot of the White House Ruins in Canyon de Chelly.
