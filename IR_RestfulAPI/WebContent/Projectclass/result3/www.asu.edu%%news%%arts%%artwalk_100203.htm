October 2, 2003

Homecoming Fall FestDevil takes artistic flair

The Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts is taking 2003 Homecoming FestDevil up a notch. When alumni, students and football fans converge on ASU Saturday, Nov. 1, they'll enjoy the university's first Homecoming Block Party.

But it will be more than just a chance to visit old haunts and old friends before the big game. This year, as part of the block party festivities, Herberger College will bring together more than 30 artists and a host of student performers for the inaugural Herberger Art Walk.

"Coming home to ASU is designed to be rich and rewarding, as well as a lot of fun," says Herberger College Dean J. Robert Wills. "We want to celebrate our alumni - and our soon-to-be alumni - and enable them to share their talents with the rest of the campus."

The artists who will show and sell their works are ASU alums and School of Art graduate students.

"This is a juried exhibition and the selection panel has worked to guarantee a diverse selection for the show," says Deanna DePass Stulgaitis, associate director of development for the college and the organizer of the Art Walk.

The Art Walk will be located along the sidewalk adjacent to the Alumni Lawn in front of Old Main at University Drive and College Avenue. It will begin at 10 a.m. and go until sunset.

"While most folks will be attending the 1:30 p.m. homecoming game, there are some campus visitors who may find a leisurely stroll through the Art Walk and around the campus and Tempe their choice for the afternoon, so we're going to keep the art tents open throughout the day," Stulgaitis says.

During the morning block party, Herberger College dancers, musicians and singers are scheduled to provide entertainment on stage in front of Old Main. Expect to hear a mix of Mariachi, jazz and African drums.

"Our academic units are all nationally ranked, so you're going to experience great performance with a light, sidewalk festival atmosphere," Wills says. "It's our goal to create a day-long experience that celebrates all of the arts. Homecoming is a wonderful opportunity to launch that endeavor, and we hope that over the years the Herberger Art Walk will grow and flourish."

Shaw, with the Herberger College of Fine Arts, can be reached at (480) 727-7664 or (stacey.shaw@asu.edu).
