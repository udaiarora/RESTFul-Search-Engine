September 21, 2004

Morrison Institute Names scholarship winners

Through an initiative to engage Arizona’s emerging young leaders, ASU’s Morrison Institute for Public Policy, in partnership with The Arizona Republic and Tucson Citizen, gives Arizona high school seniors the opportunity to earn a scholarship award to attend ASU.

This prestigious scholarship is designed to encourage Arizona high school seniors to think about public policy issues in Arizona and to promote the concept of “stewardship” – public leadership committed to doing what is best for the state and its residents, regardless of political philosophy or personal gain.

Selected through a highly competitive “blind review” process, the 2004 – 05 award recipients are: Dennis Mitchell from Greenway High School in Phoenix – who received the first-place award for his essay on “Bilingual Education” – and Joaquin Rios from Corona del Sol High School in Tempe, who received the second-place award for his essay titled “Campaign Reform.” Mitchell and Rios are freshmen attending ASU’s Barrett Honors College.

Elaine and Richard Morrison of Gilbert established this unique program to commemorate the Morrison Institute’s 20 years of policy research and to continue to expand the institute’s public service as evidence of their devotion to Arizona’s future.

The institute’s board of advisers is committed to continuing this special program in 2005 and beyond under the leadership of chairwoman Cathy McKee, senior vice president with General Dynamics C4 Systems, and board member Ed Fox, vice president of communications, environment and safety with APS. Sponsors for the next cycle of the Young Steward of Public Policy Scholarship Program are Salt River Project, General Dynamics and APS.

Mitchell received a $1,500 scholarship to ASU, while Rios received a $1,000 scholarship. Young Steward awards are not based on financial need, academic achievement or program of study. They are based on an outstanding essay about a public issue of critical importance to the state of Arizona and the specific policy recommendations on how the issue should be addressed. The awards are given only to students who attend ASU.

To read the essays, or for further information about how to apply to the 2005-2006 Young Steward program, visit (www.morrisoninstitute.org) or call the institute at (480) 965-4525.

The Morrison Institute for Public Policy is an ASU resource for provocative, objective public policy research and analysis. A part of the School of Public Affairs in ASU’s College of Public Programs, the institute brings university scholarship and public policy development together for the benefit of Arizonans.

Leland, with the Morrison Institute, can be reached at (480) 965-4525.
