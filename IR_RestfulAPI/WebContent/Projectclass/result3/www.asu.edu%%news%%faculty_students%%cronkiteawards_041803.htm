April 18, 2003

Cronkite School students win top awards in national contests

Students from ASU's Walter Cronkite School of Journalism & Mass Communication have continued their winning ways in several major journalism contests. The school's students were honored by the Broadcast Education Association, the William Randolph Hearst Journalism Awards and the Society for Professional Journalists.

Cronkite students scored four first-place finishes in the 2002 national student news competition for the Broadcast Education Association (BEA). In all, the Cronkite School won six of the 18 possible Student News awards. Junior William Pitts won the top award for Radio News reporting for a story he produced on a mock terrorism drill at Sun Devil Stadium. Junior Josh Davis was a first-place winner for Radio Feature reporting on a story about local auditions for Fox's "American Idol."

ASU swept the Radio Feature category with Josh Davis finishing second on a profile of legendary television journalist Walter Cronkite and William Pitts receiving a third-place award for a story about trumpeter Jesse MacGuire. Senior Holly Naylor snagged a first-place award for TV Feature reporting on a story about radio and television announcer Charlie Van Dyke and his ordination as a Catholic minister.

Former student Dan Bubany won first place for Sports Reporting for the story of last year's three-way quarterback battle for the ASU Sun Devil football team.

Davis received special recognition for his "American Idol" radio story. It was judged to be the "best of the best" radio story of the BEA Festival, which included entries from around the world. Davis walked away with another award - a prestigious National Association of Broadcasters (NAB) scholarship.

"The BEA competition is one of the emerging competitions in the country," said Joe Foote, director of the Cronkite School. "For ASU to do so well is another mark of excellence for the School. For Josh Davis to win an NAB scholarship and 'Best of Festival' award is remarkable."

Three students in the Walter Cronkite School made it to the semi-final competition of the William Randolph Hearst Journalism Awards. The Hearst Journalism Awards program is described as the "Pulitzer Prize" of collegiate writing competitions.

Finishing third nationally were students Joe Watson for Newspaper Feature reporting; Holly Naylor for TV Feature Reporting; and William Pitts for Radio News Reporting. Students who score a top-five finish nationally move into the semi-final round.
Other ASU students were recognized by the Hearst Awards: Josh Davis placed 7th in Radio Feature Reporting, Sara Thorson finished in 9th place for Column/Opinion Writing, Katie Orr placed 14th in Radio News Reporting and Cameron Eickmeyer scored a 15th place tie for Sports Writing.

ASU finished number six nationally in the Intercollegiate Broadcast News Competition. The final rankings for Intercollegiate Broadcast News Competition were based on the results from two radio and television writing competitions.

For the third straight year and fifth time in six years, the Cronkite School finished first in the Society For Professional Journalists' (SPJ) regional writing contest. ASU is in region eleven which includes California, Nevada, Hawaii and the Pacific Islands. The Sweepstakes Award is given to the school with the most accumulated points in print, television, radio and web reporting competitions.

ASU had 51 total points, more than twice that of its nearest competitor, including nine first-place finishes, 10 second places, seven third places and two honorable mentions.

The nine first-place winning students from ASU will compete nationally with other regional winners from across the country. Results for the second phase of the SPJ National Mark of Excellence competition will be announced this summer.
