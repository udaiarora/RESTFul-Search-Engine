Spring Distinguished Lecture Series Concludes with Playwright Joseph Megel

The Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing concludes the popular Spring 2004 Distinguished Lecture Series with playwright Joseph Megel, on Monday,  April 19, 2004 at 7:00 p.m. in  the Lyceum Theatre at Arizona State University. The event is free and open to the public.

Joseph Megel is visiting guest director for the Herberger College of Fine Arts Theatre Department's New Plays Marathon, a residency co-sponsored by the Piper Center and the Theatre Department. Megel was co-founder and artistic director of "A Renegade Theatre Ensemble and Words Across Cultures," where he directed over forty productions including his own adaptations of works by Isaac Bashevis Singer and Richard Brautigan. He will speak and take questions about "Developing New Work-The Director, Playwright, & Actor Collaboration."

The Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing at Arizona State University aims to foster the development of  a vibrant community of writers-for the university, the Phoenix metropolitan area and all of Arizona. Building on the existing strengths of ASU's graduate program in creative writing while helping to transform it into one of the nation's very best, the center offers programs that bring many of the country's best writing talents to the Phoenix area.
