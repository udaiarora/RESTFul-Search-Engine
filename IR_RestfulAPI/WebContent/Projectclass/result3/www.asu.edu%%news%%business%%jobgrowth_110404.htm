November 5, 2004

Lowest job growth estimates hit closest to mark

Over the last year, the more pessimistic forecasts of the nation’s employment growth proved to be the most accurate, according to the latest issue of the Blue Chip Job Growth Update, a monthly publication of the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business.

The nationwide economic panel’s forecasts are summarized quarterly in the National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity.

“The Bank One Economic Outlook Center wishes to commend our three most accurate forecasters for last year: Robert Eggert, Yasuo Nishiyama, Edward Leamer’s team at the UCLA Anderson Forecast and William Dewald,” according to Dawn McLaren, research economist with the center.

Among the highlights of the current Job Growth Update:

  • The U.S. economy grew by 1.8 million nonfarm jobs in September 2004 over September 2003, a 1.4 percent increase.
  • Nevada continues to hold the number one rank in total nonfarm job growth, with a 4.6 percent increase representing 51,100 jobs. The lowest rate was in Michigan , with a 1.2 percent decrease (51,500 jobs).
  • California had the largest number of jobs gained (66,400) in the professional and business services sector.
  • In metropolitan markets with under 1 million workers, the Las Vegas, Nevada metropolitan

area ranked first in nonfarm job growth for September 2004 over September 2003, posting a 5.1 percent gain, which represented 42,500 jobs.

The forecasts by the nationwide panel of experts are critical in assessing the state of the economy, said McLaren. The Bank One Center publishes the monthly Job Growth Update and its quarterly companion report, the National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity.

The Blue Chip Job Growth Update has been the first-to-user source of complete federal employment data since its inception in 1992. It presents data compiled from the U.S. Bureau of Labor in

an easy-to-read format, permitting state-to-state comparisons within 24 hours of the release of the data. The National Consensus Forecast of Labor Employment, Compensation and Productivity, which appears quarterly in the Job Growth Update, contains information from a panel of leading economists around the country. Consensus forecasting is the hallmark of the Blue Chip publications produced by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business.

The Blue Chip Job Growth Update is available to subscribers either by mail or on the Web. Internet subscribers may sign up for a free list serve, which automatically sends them an email when the data are posted.

A sample issue and information on subscriptions is available on the Web at the following address: wpcarey.asu.edu/seid/eoc/publication.cfm.
