Behavior Research Center Conducts Study for W. P. Carey School of Business:

40 percent of family owned businesses not prepared for sudden change in leadership

Four in 10 family business owners believe their companies would face serious leadership problems in the event of an unexpected death or serious injury to a family member in management, according to a study conducted for the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University by Behavior Research Center, Inc.

The Small Business Issues Survey, released today by the Family Business Forum at the Center for the Advancement of Small Business, was designed to evaluate how family-owned businesses plan for the future transition of their companies

"This research is part of our work to help strengthen family businesses and help them prosper across generations," said Mary Lou Bessette, center director and business owner on campus.

Over half (56 percent) of family business owners have a transition plan in place to ensure that the business remains in the family. The survey also shows that businesses in the middle range of "age" (in operation for six to 10 years) and revenue ($250,000 to $499,999 annually) are more likely to have a transition plan in place than companies at either end of the age or revenue spectrum.

"These findings indicate a substantial need for programs to assist family businesses in this type of planning," Bessette noted. "Our future seminars and symposia will be shaped to address the leadership and transition issues."

A founding partner of the ASU center's Family Business Forum, Behavior Research Center began investigating the small business sector in 1990 with its semi-annual BusinessTRACK tm study series.

BusinessTRACKT has demonstrated that companies with three to 100 employees are a vital segment of the economic picture, constituting roughly 95 percent of all commercial enterprises. Among these small businesses, from 75 to 80 percent are either wholly owned or controlled by family.

W. P. Carey School of Business

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report , the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
