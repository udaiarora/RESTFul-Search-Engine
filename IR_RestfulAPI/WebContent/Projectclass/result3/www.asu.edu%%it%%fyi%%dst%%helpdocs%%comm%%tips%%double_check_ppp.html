Double Checking those PPP dial-up numbers

It has been brought to my attention (Thanks to Kurt Johnson!!), that a gentleman out in Mesa has the phone number of 972-7003! If this looks somewhat familiar it's because our own PPP dial-in number is 727-0030. Unfortunately this gentleman has received 105 calls, from modems, in the past few days!!! Fortunately he is a nice person, has a lot of time on his hands, and has caller id - so he's been calling people back and telling them they have the wrong number!

I suppose you're now asking - "what does this have to do with me?" As consultants we often have our customers bring in that laptop or home machine to have PPP set up for them. In doing so (since we are thorough consultants) we need to test the dial in to make sure it works properly - in which case we may be using 9-727-0030. (I wasn't aware that a 9 would work to get an outside line until this morning). If we do not change that number back to just 727-0030, very often our customers don't either and this results in misdialed calls to 972-7003. This will also happen to 872-7003 (8-727-0030)!

What can we as consultants do to rectify this situation? There are a couple of options - first whenever you set up a laptop or home machine be sure to re-set the phone number, minus the leading 8 or 9, before you return it to your customer. Another option would be to use 7-0030 for your on-campus testing - which would not work at all when taken off campus. And lastly, this condition will also occur if, in the Microsoft Networking section, the "needing to get an outside line through a PBX" selection is checked so we need to insure that this option is not selected.

Thank you for your attention to this matter - especially from the gentleman in Mesa!

If you have any questions, comments, or concerns about this issue, please send an e-mail note to comm-q@asu.edu

Martha Ellis
