April 14, 2003

Hispanic Research Center hosts major Chicana/o art event

It has a rich history and is one of the hottest areas of contemporary art, and yet to many art collectors Chicana/Chicano art is undiscovered territory.

"Chicana/Chicano art is the some of the most vibrant, sexy contemporary art around," said Gary Keller, director of Arizona State University's Hispanic Research Center. "People like it and know that they should be collecting it, but some of them don't know enough to feel comfortable."

To solve this problem, the ASU Hispanic Research Center has developed a major art event that combines a public auction of more than 250 new pieces of art with critical and practical symposia on art scholarship and collecting, and an opportunity to personally meet 50 prominent Chicana/Chicano artists. The visiting artists' works will be among the pieces auctioned. Celebrities, important collectors and Chicano media icons are expected to be in attendance.

"Latina/o Art: Auction and Symposia," begins on May 2 with a symposium aimed at educating both the beginning and the serious collector. Beginning at 1:00 pm, the activities start with an afternoon-long scholarly session on Latina/o art history, criticism and education, entitled "Chicana/o Art: Scholars Look at Four Decades of Achievement."

Later that evening, event registrants are invited to a reception with 50 major Chicana and Chicano artists. The artists, many of whose work has been featured in Contemporary Chicana and Chicano Art (a two-volume work recently published by ASU's Bilingual Review Press) will all have pieces in the auction. The reception will also include a silent auction of artwork and various items for sale by artists.

On Saturday May 3 educational events continue at 9:00 am with a workshop presented by prominent collectors and museum and gallery professionals, entitled "Chicana/o Art: A How-To for Collectors, Beginning and Advanced." A luncheon follows at noon.

The main auction begins at 2:00 pm in room 60 of the Architecture and Environmental Design Building, and will feature approximately 250 recent works by the visiting artists and others.

The pieces to be sold will be previewed the morning before the auction. In addition to live bidding on the ASU campus, the auction will also allow Internet bidding via the Live Auction feature on Ebay.

The event ends May 4 with a tour of the Hispanic Research Center's gallery and art archives and special workshops on researching Chicana/o art and using the internet for art purposes.

"We expect this event to draw some major names from the entertainment industry," said Keller. "Some very well known people are major collectors of this art - Cheech Marín, Nicholas Cage, Madonna... We've got people coming from LA, Dallas, Chicago.

"We want to establish this as an annual Cinco de Mayo art event in Arizona. Chicano art is growing in prestige, and this could become one of the Valley's premier art attractions," he said.

Participants in the auction should register in advance. A registration form can be downloaded from the web at www.latinoartcommunity.org <http://www.latinoartcommunity.org>.
