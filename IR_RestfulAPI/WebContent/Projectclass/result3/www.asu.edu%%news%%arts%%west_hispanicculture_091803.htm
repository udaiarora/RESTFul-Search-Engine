September 18, 2003

Events bring taste, sounds of Hispanic culture to ASU West

The ASU West community is expressing its appreciation for the Hispanic culture by hosting a variety of events celebrating Hispanic Heritage Month. The events include:

. "Leandro Soto: A Retrospective of a Cuban Artist," 7:30 p.m., Sept. 15 - Oct. 16, at the Pauline Miller Gallery, 817 N. First St., Phoenix. For information, call (602) 307-9643. This free exhibition includes paintings, drawings, performance art, theater designs, and interdisciplinary art expressions by Soto, an ASU West professor.
. "Arte Latino en La Ciudad," Sept. 15 - 30, at the Fletcher Library. Latino artists will display their work in a variety of mediums that reflect their personal interpretations of identity, diversity and community. A reception is scheduled at 6 p.m., Sept. 23.
. "Pre-Columbian Danza Azteca," noon, Sept. 16, in the Delph Courtyard. Huehuecoytzin will play traditional ceremonial songs and perform dances honoring the plants, animals and elements.
. "Rumba," noon, Sept. 23, in the Delph Courtyard. "Meferefun Cuba" is a music and dance folkloric group of Cuban artists that will cover diverse music and dance expressions.
. Café III: The Journeys of Cuban Artists," reception at 6 p.m., Sept. 23, in the Student Lounge, second floor, UCB. Opening reception for this art installation curated by Leandro Soto, in the IAP Gallery.
. "Re-Membering Cuba," noon, Oct. 3, Sands 203. Andrea Herrera, chairwoman of Ethnic Studies at University of Colorado, Colorado Springs, will present her new novel.
. "Clout: The (Still) Growing Size and Influence of the Latina/o Electorate," 5 - 7 p.m., Oct. 6, in La Sala A. This roundtable discussion of elected officials and issue experts analyzes the targeting of Latina/o voters by both major parties of the 2004 Presidential race.
. Poetry Reading: Bilingual Latina Poetry from Spain, noon - 1 p.m., Oct. 10, in the Kiva.
. Día De Los Muertos art exhibit, 11:30 a.m. - 1:30 p.m., Oct. 30, in the Delph Courtyard. The exhibit will include a display of "cajitas" (memorial boxes) from the library exhibition.

For information, call Nancy McKenna, business manager, senior of the ASU West Library and chairwoman of the Latino Events committee at ASU West, at (602) 543-8530.

Romero, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 727-3116 or (mlromero@asu.edu).
