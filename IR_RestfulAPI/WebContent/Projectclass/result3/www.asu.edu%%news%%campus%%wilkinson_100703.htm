October 7, 2003

College gains resident diplomat to guide students

Though students hope a university education will lead them to interesting and fulfilling jobs, few imagine it might open the door to something as exotic and exciting as a career in international affairs.

This year, thanks to a program sponsored by the U.S. Department of State, ASU students will have access to Ambassador Sharon P. Wilkinson who will share her experiences as a career diplomat and give guidance to anyone interested in a life in the foreign service.

Wilkinson, who was U.S. Ambassador to Burkina Faso and Mozambique during the Clinton and Bush administrations, will be in official residence at ASU's College of Liberal Arts and Sciences during the 2003 - 04 academic year. She will be counseling and recruiting students for a wide variety of career opportunities in the State Department as well as guest lecturing in a variety of departments. She will also be available to speak to interested groups in the community about foreign service life.

"I am delighted to be here as the first diplomat in residence at ASU. The foreign service is an exciting, challenging, dynamic career option whose members come from all over the United States and from a surprising array of backgrounds, disciplines and special skill areas. ASU promises to be a rich source of candidates, and I am very much looking forward to meeting the students and professors who will work with me to make that happen."

"We had to work really hard to get her assigned here," said Academic Associate Barbara Colby, who is coordinating the residency. "The Department of State only assigns 16 Diplomats in Residence nationally, and she's one of the top placements. We got her here because we really think the Department of State has much to offer Liberal Arts and Sciences students, and we have quality candidates to offer them."

Wilkinson was educated at Brown University and the University of Chicago. She speaks Portuguese, Spanish and French and is a Career Member of the Senior Foreign Service, Class of Minister Counselor and has held numerous foreign posts.

She began her career as vice consul in Sao Paulo, Brazil and later was assigned as consul in Accra, Ghana. Returning to Washington, she served as program officer for Africa in the Bureau of Cultural Affairs, staff assistant to the assistant secretary for Inter-American Affairs, desk officer for Portugal, and management analyst for the Office of Management Operations.

She also spent one year as director of the Face-To-Face Program at the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace. Next, she was assigned as deputy principal officer in Tijuana, Mexico. She returned to Washington to direct the Office of Diplomatic and Public Liaison in the Bureau of Consular Affairs, followed by an assignment as consul general in Curacao. She participated as a member of the 35th Senior Seminar Class before departing for Lisbon, where she served as charge d'affairs before completing her tour as deputy chief of mission. In 1995, President Bill Clinton nominated her as the U.S. ambassador to Burkina Faso. In 2000, he nominated her as ambassador to the Republic of Mozambique.

"Ambassador Wilkinson's extensive experiences in foreign affairs and her history of serving all over the globe will be a major asset for our students to take advantage of," says Dan Bivona, associate dean of the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences. The State Department is looking for bright and talented individuals interested in representing our country abroad and in other kinds of service, and her presence here will open a real door to those careers."
Wilkinson can be reached at (480) 965-8089.
