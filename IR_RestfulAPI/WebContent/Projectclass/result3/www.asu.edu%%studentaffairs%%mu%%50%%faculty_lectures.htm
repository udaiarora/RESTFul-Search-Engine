Faculty Lectures

For 50 years, the Memorial Union has been the center of campus life and a reflection of our society and culture. Join ASU faculty experts in the areas of film, popular music, television and popular culture as we remember the past and look forward to the next 50 years.

All are welcome to bring their lunch to the MU and attend the Brown Bag Lectures. This event is coordinated by ASU's Memorial Union Programs & Activities.

Monday, February 20
"Hollywood U. - 50 Years of College Life in the Movies"
Ron Newcomer, Theatre
11:30 am - 1:00 pm
Memorial Union, Room 080 Fiesta
Ron Newcomer is a Faculty Associate in Film Studies. He attended the University of Arizona, the University of California, Los Angeles, and Arizona State University, where he received his Bachelor of Art in arts management and directing. Newcomer has been involved with more than 1,700 stage, television or film productions in his professional career. Recently, his screenplay Full Moon Lover won top honors in the Arizona Screenwriting Competition, and was a finalist for his screenplay New West. At Arizona State University he has received numerous honors, including the Distinguished Teacher of the Year Award from the Herberger College of Fine Arts.
Tuesday, February 21
"50 Years of Popular Music"
Richard Mook, School of Music
11:30 am - 1:00 pm
Memorial Union, Room 219 Navajo
Richard Mook is a Senior Lecturer in Music. He holds a Bachelor of Arts degree with high research honors in music and religion from the University of Rochester and a Ph.D. in music history from the University of Pennsylvania. He was awarded the Lise Waxer Prize from the Society for Ethnomusicology for his research on the history of barbershop quartet singing. Mook's current research projects include a book-length study of quartet singing on both sides of the Atlantic. His research interests include race and gender in American popular and vernacular music. He is currently developing and teaching music courses for non-majors.
Wednesday, February 22
"From Home Entertainment to Cultural Phenomenon: The Impact of Television in Public Spaces"
Greg Wise, Communication Studies
11:30 am - 1:00 pm
Memorial Union, Room 224 Coconino
J. Macgregor (Greg) Wise is an Associate Professor and Chair of Communication Studies at ASU at the West campus. Wise earned a B.A. at Trinity University and an M.A. and Ph.D. at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. His research is based in cultural studies and the philosophy and sociology of technology. He is interested in issues of culture and technology, new media, and theories of everyday life and globalization. Wise is on the editorial boards of Cultural Studies, The Journal of Communication, Communication Review, and Communication Theory. He has authored several books on technology and culture, and is currently working on a book on cultural globalization.
Thursday, February 23
"50 Years of College Life and College Memorabilia"
Chris Smith, History
11:30 am - 1:00 pm
Memorial Union, Room 224 Coconino
Chris Smith is an Associate Professor of History. He earned a B.A. from Union College and an M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Illinois. He joined the ASU faculty in 1971. Smith's area of specialization is U.S. History, particularly the American culture. He has received numerous awards for his teaching and public service, including the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (CLAS) Outstanding Teaching Award, Last Lecture Series Award and the CLAS Alumni Award for Distinguished Teaching. He has received the William Mura Award for Lifetime Achievements in Social Studies Education from the Arizona Council on Social Studies.
