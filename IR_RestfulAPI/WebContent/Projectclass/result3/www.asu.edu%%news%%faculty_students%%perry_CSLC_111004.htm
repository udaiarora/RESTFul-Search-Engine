November 09, 2004

ASU’s Perry takes leadership role on charter school issues

Eleanor Perry, associate professor at ASU’s College of Teacher Education and Leadership at the West campus and founder of the Leadership for Educational Entrepreneurs (LEE) program, has been named to the board of directors of the Charter School Leadership Council.

The Charter School Leadership Council (CSLC) is a coalition of diverse national organizations committed to expanding and improving the charter school movement in the United States. The eight organizations comprising the council have vast experience in state and federal education policy and have supporters, members and partners across the nation, including grassroots advocates, local school board members and superintendents, and elected legislators and state education executives.

“Dr. Perry has developed strong relationships with charter school leaders and operators throughout Arizona, which continues to be at the forefront of charter school development and quality,” says Howard Fuller, chairman of the CSLC. “Her enormous experience in the charter school movement, particularly her deep understanding of school leadership and teacher preparation, will be a great asset to the council.”

ASU introduced LEE in 2001 as the nation’s first structured university master’s program to bridge education and business, evolving from Perry’s six years of research focused on charter school leadership.

“National data show that charter school operators are often educators-turned-business people or business people-turned-educators,” Perry says. “The LEE program provides a unique academic blend for those who serve as instructional leaders and market-based entrepreneurs.”

The Leadership Council works to identify and build consensus on major issues facing the charter school community.

“The appointment of Dr. Perry to this important national council is both an honor for ASU’s College of Teacher Education and Leadership and a recognition of the groundbreaking work we’ve done in the area of school choice,” says Joseph Ryan, interim dean of the college. “With Arizona being one of the leading states in the charter school movement, it’s particularly fitting that one of our faculty members should be chosen to help direct the nationwide efforts of the council.”

Charter schools are public schools of choice, providing choices for families and greater accountability for student success. They do not charge tuition and are publicly funded. As such, they are accountable for academic and financial results to parents, sponsoring organizations and the public that funds them.

Since the first charter school opened in Minnesota in 1992, the charter school movement has grown to nearly 3,000 schools operating nationwide, serving more than 685,000 students. In Arizona, charter schools were created through legislation in 1994. Arizona now leads the nation with nearly 500 charter schools, serving more than 73,000 students.
