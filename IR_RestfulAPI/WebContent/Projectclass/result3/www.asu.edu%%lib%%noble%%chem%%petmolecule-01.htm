Research Tools and Strategies

 

Combined Chemical Dictionary (CCD)

This handbook is a quick source for finding the synonyms, molecular formula, chemical composition, structure diagram, basic physical properties and toxicology information, uses and selected literature references for the preparation, spectra and review articles/books of chemical compounds. Look for the References section located at the bottom of the record. Copy the references for synthesis (synth), reviews (rev), bibliographies (bibl), uses (use) and any others that cover the requested information. The Dictionary may have a brief statement about sales/ranking of very highly-prescribed drugs or chemicals produced in bulk.


 

 

Search Strategy:

  1. Click on the database name above and from the next screen, click the title again.

  2. Click on the Enter the Database link near the bottom right side of the page.

  3. Scroll the Searching screen down until the Chemical Name search box appears. Type in your compound name in the Chemical Name box. Scroll the page back up to the top and click on the Submit Search on the left hand side of the page.

  4. On the results page, click on the link for your compound.

  5. To see the structure, click on the benzene ring to the left of the compound name .

  6. Names for the compound are found under "entry name" and "synonyms" . Names followed by the designations "9CI", "12CI", etc. are the names used in Chemical Abstracts and can be used as the "IUPAC name".
  7. Literature references are located at the bottom of the record. Look especially for references labeled synth (synthesis) and rev (review).

  8. To print the structure of the main compound and all of the information page, in the upper left corner click on the "Print Preview Text & Struct" link.   Note: structures of derivatives will not print with this command. To print a derivative structure, "right click" the benzene ring to the left of the compound name and once on the structure window "right click" and select "print".

To find the referenced items see section How to Find Your References in the ASU Libraries.

 

 

 


Merck Index

This handbook is a quick source for finding the synonyms, molecular formula, chemical composition, structure diagram, uses and selected literature references (located mid-way down the record) for the preparation, pharmacology and reviews of drugs, biologicals and organic compounds. Copy the references for preparation (prepn), review articles/books and any other references that cover the requested information.

Search Strategy:

  1. Click on the database name above and from the next screen, click the title again.

  2. Click on Search Compound Monographs (no ChemDraw plugin) within the Compound Search box.

  3. In the All Names box enter an equal sign immediately followed by the compound name (e.g. =aspartame).    If the search retrieves nothing, use the Full Text box and just enter the name (e.g. aspartame).

  4. In the search results menu click on the "Show Details" button for the appropriate compound.

  5. Names for the compound are found under "Title", "CAS Name", and "Additional Names" . The CAS Name can be used as the IUPAC name.
  6. Look in the "Literature References" paragraph after the "prepn" abbreviation for references to synthesis articles and patents.

  7. The structure diagram is located underneath the "Literature References" paragraph .

  8. To print, use the browser's print command in the file menu.

To find the referenced items see section How to Find Your References in the ASU Libraries.
