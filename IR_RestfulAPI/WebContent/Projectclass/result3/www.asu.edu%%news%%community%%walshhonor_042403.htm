April 24, 2003

College honors Walsh for service, Achievement

James P. Walsh, Arizona attorney general special counsel and Antitrust Unit chief, has been selected to receive the annual ASU Distinguished Achievement Award from the College of Public Programs for his outstanding service to the college and for his distinguished record of public service.

The award is the highest honor presented by the College of Public Programs. Honorees must be citizens of Arizona who have contributed specifically to the college and who have a record of exceptional service or achievement.

Walsh, an attorney, specializes in government relations and alternative dispute resolution. His legal expertise ranges from environmental, health and education areas, to commercial litigation, antitrust and bankruptcy law. He was in private practice from 1993 - 2002 and was counsel to the firm of Lewis and Roca, LLP for government relations and special projects from 1989 - 93. He served as special assistant to Phoenix Mayor Terry Goddard for intergovernmental affairs.

Walsh earned his J.D. degree from the University of Chicago and has completed his coursework for a Ph.D. in the School of Justice Studies. His undergraduate degree is from St. Louis University.

His pro bono work and community service extends to the Arizona State Senate, and the Arizona Humanities Council, among others.

Walsh has been instrumental in raising funds to endow the John P. Frank Lecture Series for the ASU School of Justice Studies, and he continues to provide leadership to the lecture's steering committee.

He has been, says Marie Provine, director of the School of Justice Studies, "our most essential player in developing and maintaining the School's relationship with members of the socially concerned bar. With his help, we have been able to offer a series of nationally acclaimed lectures on law and social change each spring."
Walsh has also served on the College of Public Programs Dean's Community Resource Council since 1994.

For information, call the College of Public Programs at (480) 727-6238.

Palka, with the College of Public Programs, can be reached at (480) 727-6238 or (debra.palka@asu.edu).
