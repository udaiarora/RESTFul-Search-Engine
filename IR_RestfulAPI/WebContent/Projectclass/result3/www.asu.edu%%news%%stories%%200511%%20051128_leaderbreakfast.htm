November 28, 2005

Leadership Breakfast honors community efforts

Arizona State University has undertaken a broad-based approach to community outreach and social engagement. A wide variety of outreach programs and other efforts have made ASU a more dynamic institution as it works to diffuse the boundaries between the university and the community.

This semester’s Leadership Breakfast Series event, scheduled for Tuesday, Nov. 29, 2005, will recognize some of the many community outreach programs and activities as well as the faculty and staff coordinators who make those programs happen.

The Leadership Breakfast Series traditionally brings together a diverse group of leaders from both the university and the community, working to engage the two in a mutually beneficial forum for the open exchange of ideas.

The attendees of this semester’s breakfast are the true enablers of social embeddedness, representing mutually supportive partnerships between ASU and the community with which it engages. Without the efforts of these individuals and their outreach programs, many important relationships would not exist.

Among the key speakers at the event:

• Ruth Jones, vice provost at ASU, will speak about the long tradition of community outreach and social engagement at the university. Jones believes that engagement between the university and the community is integral to the success of each and is proud to see how ASU has continued to build upon its tradition of community outreach.

• Kent Scribner, superintendent of the Isaac School District #5, has led his district with a focus on initiatives including urban school improvement and parental engagement. Scribner will discuss the mutually supportive partnership between his district and ASU. Scribner has also been named an ASU Community Fellow by the Morrison Institute for Public Policy for his demonstrated ability to raise awareness of local community issues and promote faculty, student and community linkages.

• Kimberly Loui, special adviser to President Michael Crow and the executive director of the Office of University Initiatives, will speak about the many ASU outreach programs that have made the partnership between the university and the community possible. Without the dedicated efforts of the individuals who lead such programs and activities, ASU’s goal of true social embeddedness would remain out of reach.

The Leadership Breakfast Series, a partnership between the Office of University Initiatives and the Learning and Performance Solutions group, provides a dynamic forum for the open exchange of ideas. Each breakfast attracts a diverse group of leaders from the university and the community. Breakfasts are held throughout the year, usually in conjunction with an established college lecture series.
