February 16, 2003

Alumni association invites supporters to Statehood celebration with legislators

For the past two decades, thousands of dedicated ASU supporters - many of them linked to the university in multiple ways as alumni, students, parents, employees and retirees - have participated as volunteers in what has become an important annual tradition celebrating Arizona's statehood.

This year, as Arizona turns 91, university supporters will once again gather at the State Capitol, Feb. 17, for a festive noontime barbeque picnic with Arizona Legislators and their staff. The event is being held at the Wesley Bolin Park, located directly across the street from the Capitol rotunda.

Marla Giarratano, constituent relations director, ASU Alumni Association, says her organization and its Phoenix chapter appreciate the generosity of volunteers who have helped to make the event successful over the last two decades.

"Many, many people have taken time off from work to unite in support of our university, our state and our elected leaders. It is extremely satisfying to know that the alumni association and their many volunteers from throughout the Valley have worked so hard to retain this strong and valued ASU tradition," she said.

Last year's picnic set an all-time attendance record, says Wilma Mathews, director of ASU public relations.

"We had 450 people last year, and while it was a terrific turnout, we are anticipating we will top it this year. We have been very successful in sharing the ASU vision, and the statehood picnic is helping to change attitudes about the importance and value of higher education in Arizona," Mathews says.

One of the big draws for state legislators is the opportunity to mingle with ASU student researchers. Several student projects - from space exploration to measuring the density of human bones - will be demonstrated for state leaders and their staff.

"The event is a great opportunity to showcase the wonderful research being done at ASU and to let our legislators see how the university community supports the greater vision of Arizona's future," Giarratano says.

For information about the event or to volunteer, call the alumni association at (480) 965-5041 or visit the Web at (www.sundeviladvocates.org).

Summerill, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-4823 or (lsummer@asu.edu).
