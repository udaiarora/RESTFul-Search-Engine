CLI Newsletter: Summer 2006

Welcome from CLI Assistant Director

At ASU, summer is for language learning! We are delighted to welcome all new and returning CLI students to a challenging nine-week period of intensive training in less commonly taught languages of Eastern Europe and Eurasia.  As you will note below, we have assembled a wonderfully talented group of instructors. Our Russian and East European Studies Center and CLI staff look forward to making your CLI experience as rewarding as possible.  Please do not hesitate to stop by the REESC offices if we can be of help to you.

As many of you know, the CLI prides itself on a record of generating the most National Security Education Program (NSEP) and Fulbright Fellowships of any unit on the ASU campus.  These fellowships offer funding for extended periods of overseas study, and in the case of the NSEP awards specifically recognize those who are intending to enter international careers such as those in the U.S. Foreign Service.  This summer, we are pleased to report that we are able to continue what we hope is but one in a growing list of special student fellowships. Last summer, George E. Paulsen, professor emeritus of diplomatic history at Arizona State University, inaugurated the International Distinguished Engagement Award (IDEA)â??a fellowship rewarding the CLI student who best combines outstanding language achievement with commitment to international service or vocation. A further description of the Paulsen IDEA is provided below. 

 Happily, the renewal applications for funding the 2007 CLI have yielded some significant increases in support, including full funding for Albanian, Tatar, first- and second-year Armenian, first- and second-year BCS, and first- and second-year Macedonian.  Those completing two years of training in a less commonly taught East European or Eurasian language have a distinct advantage in international fellowship competitions.  It is not too early to begin to think about next summer!

We are also proud to report that we are among a handful of institutions now offering Advanced Mastery language training thanks to support from the American Council of Learned Societies (ACLS).  Last summer, ACLSâ??s Dr. Andrzej Tymowski came to discuss the potential for this new program with us and led several rousing discussions on the topic. Building on his enthusiasm for the program and our own interest in developing the CLI, we successfully applied and are now piloting an Advanced Mastery course for Albanian native and heritage speakers as part of the CLI.  Welcome to all Albanian Advanced Mastery students!

This summer, I have created an organization Blackboard site for all CLI students, which includes a calendar of events, syllabi and instructor bios, staff and faculty contact information, information about the Valley of the Sun, practicum information, ASU parking information, and more.  You can access the site through my.asu.edu under the tab â??Courses & Orgs.â??  The site will be updated on a regular basis to reflect the most current information we have on all topics.  If you would like to suggest a category of information to add, please tell me at your earliest convenience.

Finally, I want to encourage everyone to attend all the cultural events that accompany the CLI language classes.  We have assembled a particularly interesting and varied program of cultural events this summer to enrich your language learning experience, including Wednesday afternoon brown bag sessions and Tuesday evening movie nights. One of the important markers that accompany the Inter-Agency Language Roundtable (ILR) and American Council of Teachers of Foreign Language (ACTFL) standards for language proficiency has to do with mastery of the historical and cultural context within which languages are spoken. It is in that sense that the noontime brown bag cultural events and movie screenings sponsored by the CLI this summer are integral parts of your language learning experience.  I look forward to seeing you at these events, and getting to know you. To all the over sixty students in the CLI and our wonderful CLI instructors and staff, I wish you a most productive summer.

Ariann Stern
CLI Assistant Director

Funding Opportunity for Summer 2006 CLI Students
The George E. Paulsen International Distinguished Engagement Award (IDEA)

The George E. Paulsen International Distinguished Engagement Award (IDEA) honors students in the Arizona State University Critical Languages Institute who most effectively distinguish themselves 1) by academic achievement in intensive study of a less commonly taught CLI language; and 2) by their demonstrated commitment to a career in the Foreign Service or other international public sector service.  Preference will be given to those with demonstrated financial need. IDEA holders will typically have demonstrated their commitment to international foreign service by application for National Security Education Program (NSEP), Fulbright, or other study abroad fellowship, by passing the U. S. Foreign Service examination, or by commitment to Peace Corps or other overseas service opportunity. Michael McIntyre, our first recipient, returns to the CLI this summer to pursue his Macedonian language studies in preparation for a year in Skopje, where he will focus on continued acquisition of both Albanian and Macedonian.

CLI Registration

CLI registration is a complicated process.  CLI students receive full tuition waivers.  Because of the waiver, CLI registration requires multiple steps. Units involved in processing registrations and meeting the needs of students in the Critical Languages Institute include: the Department of Languages and Literatures, Summer Sessions, the Registrar, Residential Life, the Office of Sponsored Projects, the International Programs Office, the Graduate College, Undergraduate Admissions, ASU Health Services, Computing Commons, the Dean of Students Office, Student Financial Aid, and the ASU Bookstore.  The process is time consuming and bureaucratic. Inevitably there are some delays and inconveniences. We continually review procedures in an effort to streamline the process for future CLI programs.  We thank you for your patience.

REESC Baccalaureate Certificate and the New SGS REESC Baccalaureate Track
