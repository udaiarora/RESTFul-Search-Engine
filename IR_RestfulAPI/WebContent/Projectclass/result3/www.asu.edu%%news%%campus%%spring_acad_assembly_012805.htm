January 28, 2009

Crow: ASU bucks trends, handles changes with ‘calmness, focus’

ASU in 2004 educated the largest number of students in its history while embracing dynamic changes in the university. President Michael Crow, during his speech at the Spring Academic Assembly Breakfast Jan. 25, thanked the faculty for “a tremendous year, a great year … for taking on a huge task with such calmness and focus.”

Contrary to national trends, the university has accelerated its graduation rate and freshman retention rate despite economic and sociological factors, Crow said. New hires are as good as those at any top university in the world, and newly tenured faculty members are extremely accomplished. And while continuing its educational mission, ASU is simultaneously building six new facilities.

The coming year will be financially challenging, however, bringing the need for a somewhat slower pace of growth – and a tuition increase. Crow said he will recommend that ASU commit 15 percent of all external funds raised to financial aid, to continue to expand access.

“Our need outstrips our resources,” he said. “Gov. Janet Napolitano has given us about a 6.5 percent increase in base allocation in her budget, which is generous – but, at the same time, leaves us unable to keep up with the demands for student services. This is a dilemma, but this governor supports this university – dramatically so – and we’ll continue to work with her. The legislative numbers are less generous.”

ASU’s main goal by 2012 is to reach the highest level of academic achievement, while at the same time increasing access for students – a goal no other institution has achieved.

Crow wants to establish a position in the top 5 percent of colleges and schools in every field. ASU is on the road to becoming a national comprehensive university, while enhancing local impact and social embeddedness.

“This is a 10-year undertaking,” he said. “We’re making tremendous progress, and are 27 percent or 28 percent of the way along the path. It is through your aspirations for greatness that you achieve the most. This is an opportunity we have in time to build a university with this mission.”
