The Spanish classes offered in our Ecuador program will use the same texts, standards, and format as the corresponding courses offered on the Arizona State University campus.  Our instructors have gone through the same training as that given to Spanish instructors on campus.  There will not however be language labs since we will not have that equipment available to us in the Amazonian setting.  Here are the course descriptions for the standard campus courses which we will use as models for our courses in Ecuador:

The Amazonian context will of course make some important differences.  You will be practicing your Spanish with Amazonian Quichua people who speak Spanish as their national language but as a second language.  This is analogous to practicing English with people native to Arizona whose first language is Navajo.  Many of our students have found this a helpful environment for practicing their Spanish.  Because the Quichua people of this community speak Spanish as a second language they tend to speak a little more slowly and use a little less complicated vocabulary than do native speakers.  It is a very nonthreatening environment in which to risk expressing thoughts in a new language.  Because your Quichua hosts have gone through the process of learning Spanish themselves and have parents or grandparents who do not speak Spanish they are very understanding of the mistakes that are made in the process of learning a language and they are eager to help students improve.
