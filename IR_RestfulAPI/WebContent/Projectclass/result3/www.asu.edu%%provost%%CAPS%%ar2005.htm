Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)
Annual Report 2005

Members:

Beth Luey

Elizabeth McNeil (September - December 2004)

William Sharp

Dan Stanton

Barbara Trapido-Lurie

Victoria Trotta

John Wheatley (February ? June 2005)

 

For the 2004-2005 year, Beth Luey served as secretary; Dan Stanton served as archivist and webmaster; and Victoria Trotta served as CAPS representative to the Provost?s Advisory Committee (PAC-20).

The work of the committee this year focused on revision of ACD policies relating to Academic Professionals.  In October, Provost Glick released his response to the 2003-2004 AP Task Force Report, which had addressed a series of questions concerning academic professionals at Arizona State University. 

In December, CAPS sponsored a Spotlight Speaker Session in which Nancy Gutierrez, Vice Provost, summarized the Provost?s response. 

Provost Glick?s response mandated that a University-wide working group be created to operationalize the recommendations of the Task Force.  Beth Luey and William Sharp served as representatives of CAPS on the working group.  Other members were Nancy Gutierrez (Vice Provost and chair of the working group), Charles Brownson (Director, ASU East Library) Deg Farrelly (Librarian, Fletcher Library at West Campus), Kurt Murphy, (Associate Dean for Personnel, University Libraries), and Mary Stevens (Deputy General Counsel).  In June the working group completed its draft of ACD 507, Academic Professional Personnel Actions, and CAPS reviewed the document and transmitted questions and proposed revisions to Mary Stevens.

In April, CAPS hosted a Spotlight Speaker Session featuring ASU Academic Senate President Barbara Kerr and President-Elect Susan Mattson.  Discussion revolved around the role of the Senate in University governance, as well as roles of APs in governance.

Since 2000, CAPS has included one member as a representative of the Academic Associates who are academic professionals; a group primarily composed of academic advisors.  Following the report of the 2003-3004 Task Force on the Status of Academic Advisors, the university administration in 2005 implemented a plan to convert all academic advisor positions to classified staff or service professional positions.  In response to this decision, Elizabeth McNeil resigned her position on CAPS.  John Wheatley, an alternate member, filled the vacancy and served on CAPS from February through June.

Respectfully submitted,

Barbara Trapido-Lurie
CAPS Chair, 2004-2005
