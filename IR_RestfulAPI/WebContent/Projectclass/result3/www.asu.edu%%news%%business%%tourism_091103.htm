September 11, 2003

Tourism Barometer turns upward in war aftermath

The Arizona Tourism Barometer, a measurement of industry health produced by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at Arizona State University, rose to 84.4 in June from 82.3 the previous month.

"With the fading worry of backlash from the war in Iraq, which was at its height in April, recovery in tourism has begun again," said Dawn McLaren, research analyst. "A small tick upward was seen in the June reading of the Arizona Tourism Barometer as all the components rose. National parks visitation showed the most strength.

"The number of air carrier landings showed the weakest recovery of the barometer components. As tensions rose in Iraq early this year, overseas visitation to the United States was adversely affected. With the majority of the tension eased, this component of the barometer should begin to gain some strength once again. This, along with an apparent improvement in the economy overall, should provide some vigor to the tourism industry."

The Arizona Tourism Barometer is an index that provides a monthly look at the overall health of demand in the Arizona travel and tourism industry, which represents about $13 billion a year in direct visitor spending in the state, according to the Arizona Office of Tourism. The barometer incorporates both visitor and dollar components, and is seasonally adjusted.

For further information on the Arizona Tourism Barometer, call Dawn McLaren at (480) 965-7265, or visit the Web site, http://wpcarey.asu.edu/seid/eoc/atb/.
