H

Half-Life

  • Definition: The time needed to reduce a sample to 50% of it's original amount.
  • Symbol:
  • Units: seconds, minutes, hours, days, months, years
  • Resources:
    • Nuclear Physics and Chemistry
      Half-life is the time needed to reduce an unstable element's radioactive intensity by 50%. The activity could be alpha, beta, or gamma radiation.
      • Other Terminology: Unstable elements may also be described as isotopes, radioisotopes, nuclides or radionuclides..
      • Resources:
        • Analytical Chemistry Handbook
          ASU: QD78 .D43 Science Library 1st floor, shelved in Reference Section
          Other libraries
        • CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics
          ASU: library database
          ASU:           QD 65 .H3 Science Library 1st floor, shelved in Reference Section and at the Reserve Desk; also available at the West Campus Library in the Reference Section.
          Other libraries
          See Chapter 11
        • Radionuclide Half-Life Measurements Made at NIST
        • Visual Elements (Royal Society of Chemistry)
    • Biological and Pharmaceutical
      The time needed to eliminate 50% of a substance (drug, enzyme, protein, pharmaceutical, etc.) from the body; usually concerned with concentration in blood, plasma, serum, and/or urine.
      • Other Teminology: pharmacokinetics or absorption, fate in the body, elimination, especially if the fate does not need to be measured in 50% increments.
      • Resources:
        • Geigy Scientific Tables
          ASU: QP33.5 .G4513 1981 v.3 Science Library 1st floor, shelved in Reference Section
          Other libraries
        • Goodman & Gilman's Pharmacological Basis of Therapeutics
          Available online via Stat!Ref
          To access only this title, click on Stat!Ref's "List of Titles", select Goodman & Gilman's (de-select others if necessary), then fill in the search box at top of page.
          Other libraries
        • Human Protein Data
          ASU: QP551 H845 Science Library 1st floor, shelved in Reference Section
          Other libraries
    • Environmental
      The time needed to reduce a substance in the environment to 50% if its original amount. Environmental half-life is specific to the type of environment (air, soil, water, plant, or animal), and can be effected by environmental conditions (temperature, pH, depth within the soil or water, etc.) as well as the substance's inherent properties (solubility in water, volatility in air, chemical reactivity with other substances, etc.)
      • Other Terminology: Half-life in water may be referred to as hydrolysis half-life, and half-life in air may be referred to as photolysis half-life or photo-oxidation half life. May be listed with the substance's properties or under environmental fate/residual activity paragraphs.
      • See Also: Decomposition Rate, Degradation, Environmental Fate, especially if the decomposition does not have to be measured in 50% increments.
      • Resources:
        • Agrochemicals Desk Reference
          ASU: TD196.A34 M66 Science Library 1st floor, shelved in Reference Section
          Other libraries
        • Illustrated Handbook Of Physical-Chemical Properties and Environmental Fate for Organic Chemicals
          ASU: TD196.O73 M32 Science Library 1st floor, shelved in Reference Section
          Other libraries
