Class of 2006: Stay in tune with ASU through the ASU Alumni Association

Members of the Class of 2006 can stay in tune and stay in touch with their alma mater by joining the ASU Alumni Association as life members. Spring 2006 graduates who purchase a lifetime membership in the Association by May 31 not only show a lifetime commitment to ASU but also receive a free iPod Nano as a graduation gift.

The ASU Alumni Association – a non-profit organization comprised of alumni and friends of ASU – provides support to ASU and its members through its dues program. The financial support helps the association and ASU provide services and benefits to alumni while also helping future generations of Sun Devils through scholarships and other programs.

Lifetime membership allows a graduate to support ASU in one payment, as opposed to the Association’s annual dues program. Lifetime dues, normally $500, are discounted to $300. Annual dues are normally $45, but are discounted for new graduates to $25. New annual members from the Spring 2006 class who support ASU through membership will receive a $15 iTunes gift card.

Membership in the Alumni Association is one of the simplest ways for alumni to support their alma mater. Alumni financial support is an important factor in university rankings, such as in U.S. News and World Report. Increased support can help ASU boost its rankings. In return for the financial support, the Association provides benefits and services to its alumni members.

Here are a few of the membership benefits available:

• Sun Devil Career Advantage. New alumni can get that all-important first professional job through the association’s online career center. Alumni Association members have exclusive access to job postings that are updated daily, and can sign up for one-on-one career advice from alumni in their field.

• A travel program for adventurous adults. Sun Devil Destinations features itineraries for alums of all ages, including tours in locations such as Costa Rica and Australia with featured activities such as white-water rafting and scuba diving.

• A vibrant chapter and club network. Whether one’s goal is to stay in touch with classmates from a school or college, meet Sun Devils living nearby, or get together with alums who share one’s ethnicity or culture, the Alumni Association has a chapter related to it. The association also helps alumni form new chapters to cover locations and interests not previously serviced by the association.

• ASU Magazine. This quarterly publication covers ASU and its alumni. The magazine’s class notes section is the place to report a marriage, a new job or a new baby – and to find out what old friends have been doing.

All Spring 2006 graduates are encouraged to send their updated contact information to the Alumni Association. Those who stop by the association by May 31 with their contact information will receive a $5 Starbucks card.

For more information on the iPod/iTunes promotion for new graduates, or general membership information, visit www.asu.edu/alumni or call (480) 965-2586.
