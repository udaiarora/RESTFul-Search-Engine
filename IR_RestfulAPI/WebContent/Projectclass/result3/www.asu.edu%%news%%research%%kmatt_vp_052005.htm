May 20, 2005

ASU taps Matt as vice president for research

Kathleen Matt has been appointed an assistant vice president for research at ASU.

Matt will oversee research and development in the area of biomedicine, as well as assist in the development of the joint venture between ASU and the University of Arizona for a medical school in downtown Phoenix.

In addition to her new duties, Matt will continue as director of clinical partnerships at ASU. Her office establishes and maintains relationships between ASU and clinical medical centers such as the Mayo Clinic, Barrow Neurological Institute, Banner Health, Carl Hayden VA Medical Center and others.

Matt sees the partnership program as an avenue for enhancing the research prowess of ASU.

“If we use the opportunity correctly, we can lead the nation in connecting the intellectual capital of our university with the rapid application and transfer of knowledge,” Matt says. “By partnering with the academic institutions, hospitals, and businesses in Phoenix , we can create a climate that favors discovery, design, development and delivery.”

Matt is a professor in the department of kinesiology and has been with ASU for 17 years. She has used her interest in interdisciplinary and translational research in her administrative positions to set up collaborations that benefit all those involved. Matt will continue her studies on linkages between stress and disease. As an administrator, she will be able to combine her training in neural and hormone physiology with her motivation to give something back to society.

Price, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 965-9690 or (mbprice@asu.edu).
