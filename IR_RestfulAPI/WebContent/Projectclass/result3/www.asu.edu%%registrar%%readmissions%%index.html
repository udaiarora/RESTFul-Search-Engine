Returning to ASU

Students missing one or more semesters must be readmitted.

Application Timeline

To ensure that your application is reviewed, your application and fee (currently $50 for non-residents and $25 for residents) should be received by the priority application dates. However, Recording/Readmissions will continue to accept completed applications and fees through the first week of classes. Earlier due dates may apply to students who do not meet the minimum GPA Requirement.

• FEE: Applicants must submit a non-refundable application fee before the application can be processed. The non-resident application fee is $50, and the resident application fee is $25. You may pay by Visa or MasterCard when you apply online, or you can mail a check or money order made payable to Arizona State University.
• OFFICIAL TRANSCRIPTS: A current, official transcript sent directly from each institution attended must be on file at ASU before the application can be processed.
• INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS: All applicants on F1 or J1 visas must provide a Financial Guarantee to show evidence of sufficient funds available for their studies before the application can be processed. In addition, the US Bureau of Citizenship and Immigration Services (BCIS) requires ASU to obtain additional information prior to processing your visa application. You must go to www.asu.edu/interactive and login, and then select Immigration (INS) Records Entry for F1 and J1 VISAS. Failure to complete the required immigration procedure may cause you to lose student visa status. Contact the International Student Office at ASU for immigration information at (480) 965-7451 or ISO@asu.edu.

GPA Requirement

An applicant for readmission must have an ASU GPA of 2.0 or higher, depending on the college. Students who do not meet this GPA requirement are below good standing and may need to undergo an appeal process. In these cases, the completed application for readmission may be subject to a due date that is earlier than the priority application date. Students who are disqualified and students whose GPA is below a 2.0 and are applying for a major in a different college than the one last attended must submit an Application for Reinstatement.

Registration Restrictions

Go to www.asu.edu/interactive and login, select Class Registration Services, select the semester of registration, and then select Check Your Registration Eligibility Status to check the following requirements which may restrict your registration:

• IMMUNIZATION: Students born after December 31, 1956 will not be permitted to register without proof of measles immunity or of two immunizations (MMR), one of which must have been given after 1979.
• ACADEMIC ADVISEMENT: Some students are required to meet with an academic advisor before registering for classes. Contact the college of your major for an appointment or further information.
• DECLARATION OF GRADUATION/PROGRAM OF STUDY: Seniors (students with 87 or more earned hours) and post-baccalaureate students must have a current college-approved Declaration of Graduation or Program of Study on file with the University Registrar’s Graduation section before registering for classes. Contact an academic advisor in the college of your major for more information.
Exception: Students with pre-professional status in the colleges of Design, W. P. Carey School of Business, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, Education, Nursing and those students in the Social Work major are not required to file a Declaration of Graduation or Program of Study.
• UNIVERSITY TESTING REQUIREMENT for English: All new and returning undergraduates who register for seven hours or more need to comply with the University Testing Requirement as explained in the Schedule of Classes. Students who fail to meet this requirement will be placed on administrative hold and withheld from future registration. Contact the Registrar’s Recording/Readmission section for more information.

Degree/Program Requirements

The catalog year that determines your degree/program requirements for graduation may have changed because you have not maintained continuous enrollment at ASU. The current ASU catalog can be viewed at www.asu.edu/aad/catalogs. Contact an academic advisor in the college of your major for further information.
