Students Encouraged to Apply for DGS Fellowships

DGS is accepting applications for three internal fellowships the division launched last year to recruit and retain top graduate students and increase diversity. Doctoral students are encouraged to apply for the DGS Doctoral Enrichment Fellowship, a $15,000 award given to outstanding Ph.D. candidates who contribute to the diversity of ASU's graduate student population. The deadline to apply is March 1, 2006. Another funding opportunity available to students in the final stages of their post-candidacy doctoral work is the DGS Dissertation Fellowship, which provides them with $17,000 for one year plus resident tuition remission. The application deadline is March 31, 2006.

ASU undergraduates admitted for ASU master's degree programs can apply for the Reach for the Stars Fellowship, which provides a $12,000 stipend and full tuition remission to ASU's most promising juniors and seniors who are underrepresented in their disciplines. The last day to submit the application and corresponding materials is March 15, 2006. For more information on these and other fellowships available, go to www.asu.edu/graduate/financial/DGSopportunities.htm.

Changes to Online Graduate Applications
The Division of Graduate Studies has merged the degree and nondegree online applications into one website. The new application has a design that is consistent with the old online application, but now allows nondegree applicants to save their application and return at a later date to complete the process. Please refer all prospective students to www.asu.edu/gradapp to complete either graduate application. If you have links on your website, please update them. If you have questions, contact the DGS at grad-q@asu.edu.

Doctoral Student Update. Effective Spring 2006 semester, doctoral students are no longer required to register for 12 hours of research and/or dissertation after the semester in which they were advanced to candidacy, as long as they have met the required number of research and dissertation hours for their degree program. Note that any students who were advanced to candidacy prior to the Spring 2006 semester must still petition the Division of Graduate Studies for the policy to be waived. Please contact MacKenzie Case at 5-1364 or Roxanne Treece at 5-6326 if you have questions or need additional information.
