November 4, 2003

Summit to kindle connections among scholars, professional conflict practitioners

To foster more collaborations that connect academic experts in conflict transformation with those in the community, the Hugh Downs School of Human Communication will host the first all-day summit "Conflict Transformation in Arizona: Academics and Practitioners in Dialogue" Nov. 7, at ASU's Main Campus.

"By weaving theory and the practice of conflict transformation together in unique ways, we hope to foster further collaborations between researchers and practitioners in an effort to enhance the study and practice of conflict in a variety of contexts," says Jess Alberts, director of the Hugh Downs School of Human Communication and founder of the Conflict Transformation Project.

Research faculty from Arizona universities will be meeting with conflict professionals from across the state in what the planners hope will become an annual event, according to Alberts.

"We see this meeting as the first of many opportunities for the universities and local communities to come together to address conflict in private and public life," she says.

One of the first such collaborations happened last fall when Osborn Middle School partnered with the Hugh Downs School to provide interactive theater training, called Playback Theatre, for selected at-risk students with the goal of promoting empathetic listening, helping develop self-esteem and increasing communication skills.

"This project offered education and training in conflict skills that would not otherwise have been available to the community," says Alberts.

Workshops will be offered on organization/workplace conflict, religion and conflict, indigenous peacemaking, court mediation, family/domestic violence, restorative justice, cross-cultural conflict, ethics and power, experiential conflict transformation, and community conflict. The plenary speaker is Jeffrie Murphy, author of "Getting Even: Forgiveness and Its Limits," and an ASU College of Law professor.

The event is open to the public. Details are available at (http://conflict.asu.edu) or by calling Suzanne Wurster at (480) 965-2851. The fee for the program is $25 and includes a continental breakfast and lunch in Carson Ballroom, Old Main. Limited reserved parking is available and passes will be sent with confirmation materials.

The event is sponsored by the Conflict Transformation Project in the College of Public Programs Hugh Downs School of Human Communication and the School of Justice Studies, the Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict, the Lodestar Mediation Clinic in the College of Law, and the Maricopa Alternative Dispute Resolution Association.

Palka, with the College of Public Programs, can be reached at (480) 727-6238 or (debra.palka@asu.edu).
