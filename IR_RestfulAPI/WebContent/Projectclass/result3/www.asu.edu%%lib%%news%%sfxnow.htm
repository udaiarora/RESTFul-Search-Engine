"Get It! @ ASU" with SFX

New citation linking service now available

(posted 8/15/05)

 

 

 

It's now easier than ever to link from the ASU catalog and reference databases to online full text and even our document delivery services. ASU Libraries has implemented a new technology called “Get It @ ASU,” based on a document locator technology called “SFX.” (See http://www.asu.edu/lib/sfx/ for more information.) This technology uses bibliographic information from citations – author, journal title, ISSN or ISBN, etc. – to identify online full text of articles and books licensed by the ASU Libraries. Wherever you see the “Get It @ ASU” graphic:

there is a link to the SFX document locator service. If ASU has licensed a Web-based version of the resource in your citation, this link will either bring you directly to the full text or to a services menu that will let you select your preferred online view of the publication.

 

If you have linked to a published book or article to which ASU does not have access, you will be offered choices for how to obtain the text. Choices may include a link to search the ASU Libraries catalog for the item, or a link to our services for borrowing materials from other libraries.

 

Please note that if you have linked to articles or Web pages that do not appear in a published journal or book, the “Get It @ ASU” service will not be able to locate the item. In such cases, you should record the URL of the resource in your citation, and use that link to connect to it.

 

For additional information see the help page at http://www.asu.edu/lib/sfx/ and the Frequently Asked Questions list at http://www.asu.edu/lib/sfx/sfx-faq.htm. You can browse a list of all journals available online to ASU users from http://sfx3.exlibrisgroup.com:3210/asu/a-z/default.
