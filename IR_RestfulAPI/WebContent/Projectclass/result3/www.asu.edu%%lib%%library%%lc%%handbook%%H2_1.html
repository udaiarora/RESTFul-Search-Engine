H.2.1: Research Fund Policy and Procedures

Effective 5/12/92 .. Revised 5/16/2001

PURPOSE

The Librarians' Research Fund exists to stimulate and encourage research by providing support for investigations requiring financial resources. Proposals will be reviewed by the Research and Professional Development Committee which will forward its recommendations to the Dean of University Libraries and University Librarian.


All research proposals are subject to ASU guidelines. See the ACD 118 for specific guidelines. For additional research assistance, contact the Office of Research and Creative Activities.


POLICY

Although most academic professionals may apply for these funds, those with a Visiting appointment are not eligible, nor are those in the University Libraries Video Resources and on the ASU West campus, because they have separate budget allocations.


Because of limited funds and the need to serve many deserving librarians:

  1. The amount requested must not exceed $1,000.

  2. Eligible librarians may generally receive no more than 2 grants in a five year period.

  3. The same project may be funded twice; however, grants will generally not be given in consecutive years.

PROCEDURES

Applications are made by submitting an Application Form (PDF version)(MS Word version) and an Itemized Budget Form (PDF version)(MS Word version) to the Research and Professional Development Committee on or before the date designated by the Committee. The application deadline will be in May or June of the preceding fiscal year. If monies are still available, a second application deadline will be set no later than the following January. Two copies of both the Application Form and the Itemized Budget Form are required. One copy will be retained by the Committee; the other by Library Administration.


Applications will be evaluated according to the criteria listed on the Evaluation Form (PDF version)(MS Word version):

  1. Value of importance of research

  2. Feasibility of obtaining objectives within grant period

  3. Realistic budget

Grants will be given only for the current fiscal year (July 1 - June 30). Grants may be extended for up to six months into the next fiscal year if the funding is based on non-state resources, and if the Committee is notified two months prior to the end of the current year (i.e., April 30) that an extension is needed. Only one extension per grant will be given.


Non-state funds not expended by the end of the grant period (i.e., either June 30 or extension date, if given) will revert to the Librarians' Research Fund.


Expenses that research funds may cover include, but are not limited to:

  1. Reprographics

  2. Data Input/Capture

  3. Student wages and contract personnel

  4. Travel for research purposes

  5. Supplies/postage

  6. Computer searching

  7. Library compatible software (copyrighted software must be loaded on Library computers)

  8. Equipment not already available on campus

  9. Interlibrary loan

All supplies, software, materials or equipment purchased with grant funds are the property of the University Libraries and unexpended/reusable items are expected to be returned at the end of the project.


Upon approval of the application, the recipient should make arrangements for expenditure of funds with the Senior Business Operations Manager in Library Administration.


A one page progress report plus a final itemized budget (form for budget may be obtained from the Senior Business Operations Manager in Library Administration) must be submitted to the Committee within three months after the end of the grant period. A copy of any printed research must be deposited with University Archives.


Release time for research activity is not addressed by these procedures. Policies regarding this issue are included in the Librarians' Handbook, H.3.1


At the end of each fiscal year the Research and Professional Development Committee will submit to the Librarians' Council the total number of requests and a list of those persons who received research funds from the Committee. The Committee will keep a record of all requests, whether approved or denied.
