February 21, 2003

Beauty and Politics the subject of 10th annual Elaine Horwitch Lecture at ASU

Columbia University philosophy professor Arthur C. Danto will discuss Beauty and Politics at 7:30 p.m., March 26, in ASU's Neeb Hall. Danto is the guest speaker at the 10th annual Elaine Horwitch Lecture in Contemporary American Art Criticism, presented by the Herberger College of Fine Arts' School of Art and the ASU Art Museum.

The lecture will be followed by a public reception with Danto in the School of Art's Harry Wood Gallery. Neeb Hall and the Art Building are both located on the west side of the intersection of Forest and Tyler Malls at ASU's main campus in Tempe. No tickets are necessary for these free events.

In his lecture, Beauty and Politics, Danto will talk about the compatibility between political art and aesthetics, and explore the prospects for a return to beauty in art.

Jon Sharer, interim director of the School of Art, said that Arthur Danto was invited as this year's Horwitch lecturer because of his long and distinguished career as a philosopher and critic in the arts, and his role in addressing the changing directions of contemporary art over the years.

Danto has been a professor at Columbia University since 1966. He has been the recipient of many fellowships and grants, including two Guggenheims, an American Council of Learned Societies, and a Fulbright. He also has served as vice president and president of the American Philosophical Association, and president of the American Society for Aesthetics.

Danto has authored numerous books, including Nietzsche as Philosopher, Mysticism and Morality, Narration and Knowledge and Encounters and Reflections: Art in the Historical Present, a collection of art criticism that won the National Book Critics Circle Prize for Criticism, 1990. His most recent book is Embodied Meanings: Critical Essays and Aesthetic Meditations.

An art critic for The Nation, he has also published numerous articles in other journals. In addition, he is an editor of the Journal of Philosophy and consulting editor for various other publications.

The annual Horwitch lecture series is sponsored by the Horwitch Family and the Elaine Horwitch Cultural Fund in memory of the distinguished art gallery owner and art collector.

The Horwitch Lecture will commence at 7:30 p.m., March 26 in Neeb Hall. Parking is free after 7 p.m. in Structure 3 on Myrtle Ave. For more information, members of the public should call (480) 965-6303.
