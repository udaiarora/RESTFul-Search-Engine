Allenby selected as Lincoln Professor of Engineering Ethics

Brad Allenby, a professor of civil and environmental engineering at the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, has been selected as the Lincoln Professor of Engineering Ethics. He joins a notable association of Lincoln professors from various colleges at ASU that work closely with the Joan and David Lincoln Center for Applied Ethics on campus. Allenby’s appointment began Aug. 15.

“Engineering as a profession has nearly always had an ethics component,” Allenby says. “Many engineering disciplines are changing rapidly as science continues to evolve, and ethicists need to be aware of these changes just as engineers need to be aware of the ethical implications of their work.”

Allenby believes that his appointment as a Lincoln professor indicates a need for fresh thinking for ethics in engineering, especially since technical systems are becoming more significant in society.

“With the increasing power and importance of areas like nanotechnology and biotechnology, it is critical to have ethical dialog,” Allenby says.

The Lincoln Center was established to support ethical education and each of its namesake professors serve as liaisons between their colleges and the center. Allenby will be developing courses to integrate ethics and the various disciplines from the School of Engineering. He also will have the opportunity to use Lincoln funds for ethics programming in his field, and to support his research as well.

Allenby has a notable academic background in environmental sciences and ethics. He received his bachelor’s degree in environmental studies from Yale University , a law degree and a master’s degree in economics from the University of Virginia, and a master’s and doctorate degree in environmental sciences from Rutgers University.

Blankenship, with the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, can be reached at (480) 965-0123 or (mblanken@asu.edu).
