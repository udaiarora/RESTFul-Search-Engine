Caroline Leland Ruiz

Use of GIS in a roads analysis project through the US Forest Service

ABSTRACT

The topic of this presentation will be the use of GIS in a roads analysis project through the US Forest Service. Data was collected and used to create maps which convey the proposed uses of previously unauthorized roads and trails within an area of the Tonto National Forest. These maps will ultimately be used as part of the public scoping process required to comply with the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA). ArcView 3.2, ArcGIS 8.3, and ArcInfo were used in this process. Data was collected through GPS on so-called “wildcat” road segments created through unauthorized off-highway vehicle (OHV) use in the Desert Vista area of the Cave Creek Ranger District. This data was converted to shapefile format. Attributes were added to identify road segments and usage recommendations outlined by Forest Service personnel.
Data was then cleaned using ArcInfo and converted to coverage format. Spreadsheets with route information were joined as tables to geographic data. Spatial Analyst was also used to identify critical areas for natural and cultural resource protection. The presenter will outline how a route system was set up in preparation for the dynamic segmentation process using ArcGIS 8.3. Due to budget constraints, this system was not fully put into place, but is a possibility for further applications of this study.
