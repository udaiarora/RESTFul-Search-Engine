April 17, 2003

Community leader, students earn recognition at Cesar Chavez event

ASU hosted the 7th annual ASU Cesar E. Chavez Memorial Breakfast in the Memorial Union, on April 4. The breakfast recognized community leaders and students who have emulated Chavez' leadership.

Julian Nabozny received the Cesar E. Chavez Legacy Award. The Legacy Award is presented to an individual who has made significant contributions through community advocacy. Nabozny is a McDonald's owner/operator, dedicated to serving the South Phoenix community. For years, Nabozny has opened his McDonald's Restaurant on Thanksgiving Day and donated free meals to the underprivileged families of the South Phoenix area. He has advocated for many non-profit agencies and supported efforts such as voter registration and immigration education.

ASU students Christopher Tijerina of Casa Grande and Janey Pearl of Mesa were honored with the Cesar E. Chavez "Sí Se Puede" awards. Both have volunteered endlessly with programs statewide and at the same time, have demonstrated academic excellence.

Tijerina is currently a sophomore majoring in math education, a Flinn Scholar and co-founded the G.O.L.D. Scholars Program, a successful education program in his hometown of Casa Grande. The G.O.L.D. Scholars Program creates successful learning environments for high-potential but high-risk students.

Pearl is a senior majoring in organizational studies and with a certificate in non-profit management. Her passion for changing lives and educating others about diversity, equality and respect is showcased in her leadership with organizations such as ASU's Allies in Action, Camp Anytown, Axe Capoeira Association of Arizona and ASU's Community Service Program. Pearl is Bill Gates Millennium Scholar and an alumnae of the Cesar E. Chavez Leadership Institute. Most recently, Pearl was selected to represent ASU and the state of Arizona at the National Hispana Leadership Institute's Latinas Learning to Lead Program in Washington D.C. - a premier leadership training institute for Latinas in the country. Pearl is currently a finalist for the Harry S. Truman Fellowship Program.

Proceeds from the memorial breakfast benefited the ASU Cesar E. Chavez Leadership Institute for high school students.

Coronado, with the Visitor Information Center, can be reached at (480) 965-8889 or (marilena@asu.edu).
