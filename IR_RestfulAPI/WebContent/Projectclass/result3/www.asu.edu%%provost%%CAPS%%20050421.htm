Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting (Spotlight Series)

Thursday, April 21, 2005, MU La Paz Room, 10 -11 AM

Speakers:  Academic Senate President Barbara Kerr and President-elect Susan Mattson

Topic: Current and future steps in revising the university?s policies and procedures relating to Academic Professionals

Present: Approximately 40 people

The forum included informal presentations by Barbara Kerr, outgoing president of the Academic Senate, and Susan Mattson, the incoming president. George Watson, a former Senate president, joined them later in the meeting. 

Susan Mattson began by describing the Senate?s current agenda, which includes new provisions for the representation of nontenure-track faculty as well as academic professionals; the implementation of the anticipated multiple-year contracts; and the implementation of the state-required background checks for new hires. She noted that academic professionals are full voting members of the Senate and are eligible for Senate office. She suggested that the chair of CAPS could sit on the executive committee of the Senate and that if this proposal requires a change in the bylaws she would introduce such a change. 

Barbara Kerr reported on the work of the healthcare task force. The task force has proposed creating a walk-in clinic on campus for faculty, academic professionals, and staff, including a dispensary for low-cost prescription drugs. President Crow has appointed an implementation task force to proceed with this proposal. It will include both research and education components. She also reported that proposals are being prepared for the legislature that would allow a one-time opportunity to change retirement plans, the delay of a decision on retirement plans, and the creation of a voluntary employee benefit association that would allow retirees to receive accumulated sick leave to buy health insurance or pay for medical care with pretax dollars, rather than a cash payment. 

A lively discussion ensued about the conversion of lines that had provided the opportunity for continuing appointment to one-year and multiple-year contracts. The need for better explanations and continuing discussion of the university?s operation on a business model was also raised. 

In concluding, Barbara Kerr noted that the university administration and the Academic Senate have a long and fruitful record of cooperation but reminded us that the university is not a democracy.

Approved 4/22/05
