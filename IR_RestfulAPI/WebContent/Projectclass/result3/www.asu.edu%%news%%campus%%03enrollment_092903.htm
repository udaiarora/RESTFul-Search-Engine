September 29, 2003

Enrollment booming at ASU campuses

Enrollment continues to climb at ASU, with a record 57,543 students enrolled on all three campuses this fall, including the largest class of freshmen in history. This represents a 3.7 percent increase over last year's 55,491.

All three campuses registered a jump, despite a tuition increase that went into effect at Arizona's state universities. Total enrollment at the Tempe campus is 48,901, up 3.3 percent.

ASU East boomed almost 14 percent, to 3,551. The East campus is bursting at the seams and has just launched construction of its first new building since it opened in 1996, a student union.

ASU West grew 7 percent, to 7,105. Formerly an upper-division campus, the West campus began enrolling freshmen two years ago and this fall welcomed 509 freshmen and 734 sophomores, 30 percent more than last year.

"An ASU education is a terrific value, and students recognize this," says Milton Glick, executive vice president and provost. "Even with this year's tuition increase, ASU tuition is still among the lowest 10 states in the nation.

"We also have increased the amount of financial aid available, to make sure students who need help to attend ASU can get the assistance they need. We've doubled the number of scholarships for first-generation or low-income Arizona students, and increased other types of financial aid."

Main campus enrolled 7,126 first-time freshmen, an extraordinary increase of nearly 800 over last year and almost twice the number of freshmen enrolled 10 years ago.
In the last 12 years ASU has added stronger freshman support services such as the FYE program, added more class sections and residence hall spaces, and focused more on recruiting top students right out of high school.

"The growing reputation of ASU helps us tremendously in recruiting the best students," says Tim Desch, director of undergraduate admissions. "We've also been able to enroll more freshmen because we've built up great relationships with Arizona high school counselors over the last few years. Students who enroll here as freshmen get a fuller university experience."

Growth at ASU East was led by high-demand programs such as business, education and computer technology, and by nationally attractive specialty programs like professional golf management and aeronautical management technology, says David Schwalm, vice provost.

Elaine Maimon, provost at ASU West, says full-time enrollment at West has increased 77 percent in the last seven years, and the growth in the sophomore class is a particularly good sign.

"It shows last year's freshmen were pleased with their first-year experience at ASU West and have returned in record numbers," she says. "Not only is ASU West continuing to experience robust enrollment growth, but the percentage of students taking a full class load is at its highest level ever."

More students are attending school full-time on all three campuses. A record 69 percent are enrolled full-time, up from 62.9 percent in 1996, which should lead to a higher graduation rate. Undergraduates now make up almost 80 percent of enrollment, compared to less than 75 percent seven years ago.
