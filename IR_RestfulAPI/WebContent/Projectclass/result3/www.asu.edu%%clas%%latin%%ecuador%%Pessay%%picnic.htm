Immersion-based instruction in the Quichua language as it is currently spoken in the Napo River area the Ecuadorian Amazon forms the backbone of ASU's Amazona and Andes Field School in Ecuador. The primary goal is to have students conversing as quickly as possible. Through Quichua language study, students gain a greater insight into the thought patterns and classification systems used by the native people. Studying Quichua is also a good "icebreaker." Even learning a little Quichua goes a long way toward breaking down cultural barriers. Participants are presented with many opportunities to interact directly with their Quichua hosts both in small groups and one-on-one as you see some students doing here during and Andean Mountain picnic.
