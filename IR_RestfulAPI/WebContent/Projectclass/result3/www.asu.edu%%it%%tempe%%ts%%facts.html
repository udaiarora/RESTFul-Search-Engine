Facts & Figures

The ASU Telephone switch network consists of four switches, plus various adjunct equipment at remote sites. Each of the four campuses has an Avaya Definity ECS switch, the largest of which is on Main campus in Tempe. Here are a few highlights about these systems:

  • There are nearly 14,000 active extensions in the Main switch alone, and another 1,600 in the West, East, and Downtown Center switches
  • The Main switch consists of 17 port networks, or cabinets, which manage roughly 800 trunk circuits to the “outside world”
  • An additional 200 trunks run between the main switch and the West, East, and Downtown Center campus switches
  • On the first day of classes, the switch handles typically in excess of 390,200 call attempts
  • During the first month of the Fall semester, the main switch handles more than seven million calls, split almost 50-50 between on- and off-campus
