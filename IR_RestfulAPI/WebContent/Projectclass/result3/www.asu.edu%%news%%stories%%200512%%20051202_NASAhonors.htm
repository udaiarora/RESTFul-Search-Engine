December 2, 2005

Doggett, Greeley honored by NASA

Ron Greeley and his student, Thomas Doggett, have been recognized by NASA for their part in a project that could help give a spacecraft the ability to think on its own.

NASA is honoring a team of researchers – including ASU’s Greeley and Doggett, who worked with members from NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Interface & Control Systems, Microtel, Mitretek Systems, Honeywell and the University of Arizona – with the Software of the Year Award. The team members were honored for their development of the Autonomous Spacecraft Experiment software.

ASE is a new approach to spacecraft control. Rather than rely only on ground-based operators, ASE lets the spacecraft respond on its own to specific scientific events, giving it greater autonomy and allowing it to respond quickly to those events. ASU’s contribution is the development of algorithms to detect changes in ice on Earth, such as the freeze and thaw of lakes, snowfall on land, and the formation and retreat of polar sea ice. Other ASE algorithms monitor volcanic activity and flooding.

The overall goal of the project is to make a spacecraft more self-reliant for discerning interesting sites for scientists on Earth. That way, it can focus on the areas of interest and immediately respond to dynamic events, eliminating time delays that arise from transmitting data back to Earth, processing and analyzing the data, and only then sending new instructions to the spacecraft. As spacecraft visit ever-distant planets, this time delay grows significantly.

ASE is in development for use on the NASA/JPL Mars Odyssey Mission and is under consideration for numerous other NASA projects.

Doggett, Greeley and other ASE team members went to Washington, D.C., to receive their award from NASA in September.
