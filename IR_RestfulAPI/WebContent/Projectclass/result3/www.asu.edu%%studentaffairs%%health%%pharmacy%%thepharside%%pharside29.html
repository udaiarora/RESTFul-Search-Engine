An LA company called Allerca is taking $250 deposits for the genetically engineered kittens it hopes to start delivering in 2007.  They take embryos from British Shorthairs and use gene-silencing techniques to drastically reduce allergens.  The altered embryos are then implanted into female cats and the new hypoallergenic kittens are born shortly thereafter.  New owners claiming their kitties must pay the remaining $3,250 before they can take them home.

 

 

Did You Say 4 Periods a Year?

 

There is currently a lot of advertising about having four periods a year. For many women this is good news.  Besides a decrease in the frequency of periods, continuous oral contraception decreases symptoms that are often exacerbated during menses such as migraine headaches.  The new FDA approved product is called SeasonaleÒ.  It is packaged as 91 tablets (84 of active and 7 inert).  Another option many women choose is to use their regular birth control pills and take the three weeks of active pills, discard the placebo pills and start a new pack immediately.  This can be done, but it is only recommended with a monophasic product.  A monophasic product has the same dose of estrogen and progestin the entire month.  Other contraceptive products are called biphasic or triphasic and provide varying amounts of hormones throughout the month.  If you are not using SeasonaleÒ and are interested in continuous oral contraception check with your doctor or pharmacist to make sure your birth control pills are monophasic and are appropriate for continuous use. Many women are skeptical about the safety of four periods a year; however, since there is no build up of the uterine lining with continuous oral contraception, there is no requirement for a period to shed it.  - Allison Dell, PharmD candidate

Another Breakout?


Acne is a problem for almost everyone at one point in his or her life. It is estimated 85% of people ages 12 to 24 will have some acne. So if you are one of the 17 million people affected by acne you are familiar with the endless amount of products available for treatment. 

Let me simplify.  There are only 4 pharmacological ingredients available over the counter for treatment.  Many of the products being advertised are the same thing in different packages.

Acne is a result of increased sebum production, bacteria, and changes in cell growth of the follicle.  Sebum and bacteria mix with loose cells within the follicle and cause a plug or comedone.

It is also possible for acne to be worsened by certain medications. These include androgens, contraceptives that contain a high androgen activity progestin, and lithium salts. 

Acne defined as mild to moderate (less than 10 comedones and no inflammatory lesions) can be treated with over the counter products.  Severe acne is described as nodules, cysts, or large inflammatory lesions.  Severe acne cannot be treated by over the counter products. If severe acne is present a prescription medication such as antibiotics or retinoids may be required.

Treatment begins with a cleanser.  Cleansers are used to remove sebum and cause some drying of the skin.  The skin should be gently washed twice daily. 

The four nonprescription topical acne agents available are benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, sulfur, and sulfur-resorcinol combinations. 

Benzoyl peroxide (OXY, Clearasil) is available in strengths ranging from 2.5 to 10%.  Benzoyl peroxide will help to increase the turnover rate of cells lining the follicular duct to promote resolution of acne.  Benzoyl peroxide is also considered bactericidal because it has oxidizing potential.  Benzoyl peroxide should be used once to twice daily.  Common side effects are dryness, redness, and peeling. If this occurs a lower strength should be used

Salicylic acid is available in strengths ranging from 0.5 to 2% and acts as a keratolytic.  The term keratolytic refers to the softening and dissolution or peeling of the outermost layer of the epidermis. Salicylic acid is considered a less effective, alternative to tretinoin (Retin-A), which requires a prescription.

Sulfur is available in strengths ranging for 3 to 10%.  It also has keratolytic action.  Sulfur products are used once to three times daily.  Sulfur products have an odor and a color, qualities than can be inconvenient to the user.

Resorcinol in 2% strength may be added to sulfur products.  The resorcinol also helps with cell turnover.  Resorcinol may cause dark brown scales on the skin of darker-skinned patients.

Tips to remember

  • Acne is not caused by diet.
  • Treatment requires consistent, long-term use with results taking up to 6 to 8 weeks.
  • Do not use sulfur, salicylic acid, or benzoyl peroxide products at the same time unless instructed by a physician.
  • Benzoyl peroxide can bleach hair, clothing, or bed sheets.
  • Lotion and cream preparations are good for dry or sensitive skin.
  • Gel preparations are generally more effective because they stay on the skin the longest.  However, many contain an alcohol base that increases irritation.

When purchasing OTC acne products, look at the active ingredients listed on the back of the package, not the claims made on the front.  This will ensure you get the proper agent without any duplication. - Allison Dell, PharmD candidate

 

TARA

 

A recent Lancet reported the results of TARA, Trial of Atorvastatin in Rheumatoid Arthritis.  At six months, patients on 40mg of Lipitor daily showed improvement in swollen joint count, C-reactive protein, and sed rate.  There was no difference from the control group regarding tender joint count, early morning stiffness, visual analogue score, health assessment, or patient global assessment.   As expected, the atorvastatin group had lowered LDL, triglycerides and total cholesterol.  More studies are planned.

SPECIAL DELIVERY

 

Access Pharmaceuticals received approval for OraDisc A a new delivery system for amlexanox, which you may know as Aphthasol.  Indicated for aphthous ulcers, aka “canker sores” the new product is a mucoadhesive patch that dissolves steadily over the lesion, bathing it in medication.  Access Pharmaceuticals is also involved with products utilizing its oral delivery, vitamin mediated and nanoparticle delivery systems.

 

The Proof is NOT in the Pudding

 

Most of know that when we talk about booze, that the “proof” is simply the percentage of alcohol times two.  But consider these items:

·        Most beer in the US is about 10 proof.

·        Listerine is 53.8 proof.

·        Sam Adams Utopia MMII beer is 48 proof.

·        It is impossible to get a 200 proof (or 100%) alcoholic beverage because as soon as you expose it to air it draws moisture into itself until its 98% alcohol or 196 proof.

·        Maximum Strength Robitussin is 2.8 proof.

·        Non-alcoholic beer is allowed to be up to 1 proof and most states require ID to purchase it.

 

Not-so-sweet Drug Interaction

 

An 89-year-old man being treated for H. pylori with clarithromycin, amoxicillin and omeprazole was admitted through the ER in a comatose state after 2 days of therapy.  Other meds the man was taking are leuprolide, atenolol, glyburide, citalopram, doxazosin, and hydrochlorthiazide/amiloride.  Labs upon admission showed a glucose of 20, hyponatremia and leukocytosis (23,130 cells/uL). 

What happened?

It is most likely that clarithromycin displaces glyburide from protein binding sites resulting in more free drug and a precipitous drop in plasma glucose. 

Here is a case where IV glucose 50% was used.  The patient recovered nicely and presumably his H. Pylori continues to flourish.

 

What Happened To My Face?

 

What has happened is not uncommon to the 20 percent of Americans who experience outbreaks of herpes simplex labialis (HSV-1) each year.  This condition is more commonly referred to as cold sores or fever blisters, and it generally resides in oral regions, but can possibly infect other areas such as the eye.  While this type of herpes is generally not responsible for genital herpes, transmission of HSV-1 to genital areas is possible.  The usual duration of HSV-1 is 7 to 10 days, during which discomfort and disfigurement are the main symptoms, but in immuno-compromised patients (such as those with HIV), outbreaks may last much longer.  In any immune state, these outbreaks may become recurrent, requiring long-term therapy.           

                       

You’re probably wondering what you can do to fix your face, and get on with your life without feeling self-conscious.  Well, if you already have the cold sores or fever blisters, you may want to try an over the counter medication.  Currently there are a couple of options.  The first is to simply dull the pain with an analgesic medication such as Campho-Phenique which can be found in any pharmacy department at a reasonable price.  If you don’t have any time to waste, and you have a few more dollars to spend, you can buy Abreva (docosanol 10 %).  This medication is unlike the many other cold sore preparations in that it actually has antiviral activity to help prevent the proliferation of the cold sore.  While this medication can dramatically decrease healing time, it is most effective when you catch the cold sore very early, before it has fully appeared.  This can be difficult for those who do not have recurrent episodes of cold sores and don’t know what to look for.  Typical symptoms of an early developing cold sore include redness, tingling, burning, and numbness.  There is also some evidence to suggest that anti-inflammatory agents such as ibuprofen, may decrease outbreak duration as well as prevent recurrences.  More research must be done to strengthen these claims, but this could be another useful OTC therapy.  If you are immuno-compromised, or if you have a problem of recurrent HSV-1 outbreaks, it may be time to see your doctor.  There are prescription medications in pill or cream form that may be able to help.  These include acyclovir, foscavir, valacyclovir, famciclovir, and penciclovir.  These medications can help improve your current outbreak, and prevent others in the future.

Medications can only provide part of the answer.  The best way to avoid the hassle of cold sores is to prevent them from forming in the first place.  There are environmental triggers that can bring on HSV-1 outbreaks.  The best place to start is to avoid extremes.  Limit your exposure to both extreme hot or cold temperatures.  With the heat can come increased sun exposure.  This is a very common trigger of cold sores.  Apply sunscreen to your face and it is also beneficial to protect your lips with lip balms with SPF protection.  Don’t think that you’re not at risk of this in the winter.  If you plan on hitting the slopes, remember that snow is an excellent reflector of sunlight, and there may be less atmospheric protection at such high altitudes.  Colds, fevers, and influenza can also bring on a cold sore attack.  Finally, try not to stress out on your next test or final because that too can cause a break out.  Take care of yourself, and you may be able to avoid this possibly embarrassing nuisance. – Robert Kobleski Pharm. D. candidate
