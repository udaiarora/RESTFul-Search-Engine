Patron:

This facility needs more machines. I pay for it so I deserve what I pay for.

Response:

Thanks for your comment. I, as well as many of the other members of the SRC, agree that we need more machine/workout equipment. Unfortunately, our operational budget does not allow for us to purchase additional equipment or replace old equipment as often as we should.

As for the $25 fee that students pay to use the facility each semester, I must point out that that fee does not go to the operational budget of the SRC. That fee go directly to pay the debt of the facility, or the cost of construction. The operational budget comes from local collections. That is money distributed from the university, money from program fees, money from optional memberships, etc.

Unfortunately, our operational budget has dedicated purposes throughout the year. At the end of each year, usually July 1, if we have money left over, we will allocate it to replacement of equipment, capital improvements, and repairs of large cost items. Often there is not much money left therefore, our spending power is limited.

Last summer, our Director dedicated spending money out of our reserve accounts to pay for the additional treadmills and the electrical project that was necessary to operate the treadmills. The choice of purchasing treadmills was primarily because of feedback from the users, and because the summer before we purchased new stationary bikes.

The SRC has a little over $2 million in deferred maintenance that needs to be addressed. However, with limited revenue streams, this costly item will not be addressed immediately. Instead, we have to make priority decisions. Deferred maintenance items would be replacement of the carpet upstairs, which is still original from 1989, replacement of old cardio and strength equipment, that is still original from 1989, repairs to the balcony tiles, still original from 1989, and countless other projects.

It very difficult to share the extent of our budgetary concerns via email. If you are interested, we could set up a meeting and I can share more information with you.

With the addition of the McAllister Village across the street from the SRC, our user numbers will increase. This increase in users, and the increasing population of on- Campus students, may have a dramatic impact on our ability to provide space and equipment. We are being proactive in our efforts to continue to provide quality services and programs. Maybe, in our discussion, you may have some ideas that will help.
