Other Services

Automated Attendants

Automated attendants (also referred to as “nests”) provide effective routing of calls through simple menus. (For example, a menu might say, “For customer service, press 1. For sales, press 2 . . . etc.”) Providing a simple menu like this can increase a department’s overall call-handling ability by off-loading a large percentage of calls from a single receptionist. Routine questions can be answered via the automated system, freeing up employees to answer more detailed questions.

The Automated Attendant feature starts at $20 per month, plus installation charges that typically run less than $100. Telephone Services can consult with you to design and implement an efficient solution for your specific needs. For more information on Automated Attendants, contact us at (480) 965-2011.

Calling Cards

Calling cards are used to bill long distance calls to your ASU departments when traveling, for example, and are available to ASU employees only. To request a calling card, submit a Telephone Services Request (TSR) with the name to whom it will be issued and the appropriate Area/Org for billing.

Conference Calls

There are three types of conference calling available to ASU employees:

  • Conference Call

    • Most department phones are equipped and programmed to handle conference calling. However, if you would like assistance, the switchboard operators can facilitate conferencing for you at no additional charge. If you have a digital phone, a maximum of six parties can be conferenced simultaneously. If you have an analog phone, you may conference up to three parties on your own, or six if using the ASU operators.

    To place a conference call from a department phone without operator assistance:

    1. Dial the number of the first party to be conferenced.
    2. Press the Conference button on your phone, or, if you have an analog phone, press the Flash button.
    3. Dial the number of the next party you want to add to the call.
    4. Press the Conference (or Flash) button again, and all parties will be connected.
    5. Repeat steps 2 through 4 for each additional party you wish to add.

  • Meet-Me Conference Call

    • Meet-Me Conference calling allows participants to call a designated phone number at a prearranged time to join in on a conference call. A maximum of six parties can participate in the call. More details . . .

  • State-Capitol Assisted Conference Call

    • If you require more than six participants, you may contact the Arizona State Capitol operator at (602) 542-4900 and have him/her facilitate the call for you. The State Capitol charges $1.60 per conferenced party for this service, which will be billed back to your department, in addition to any applicable long distance or toll charges.

Fax Service

Fax services are available at our front counter (Old Main, Rm. 100) between 8 A.M. and 5 P.M. for ASU departments only. There is no charge for the service; however, long distance or other toll charges for the calls are billed back to your department, under the ASU phone number you designate.

Interactive Voice Response (IVR)

With interactive voice response, callers can access information around-the-clock, seven days a week. By automating everything from simple information requests to complete transactions (such as registration, schedule information, ordering, etc.), your department can improve employee productivity and provide better service to your callers.

Telephone Services utilizes an Avaya Conversant voice information system, and can customize an application specifically suited to your needs. For questions or more information about interactive voice response applications, contact us at (480) 965-2011.

TTY/TDD

Switchboard Services provides TTY/TDD assistance for hearing-impaired callers. The general TTY/TDD number is (480) 727-6225.
