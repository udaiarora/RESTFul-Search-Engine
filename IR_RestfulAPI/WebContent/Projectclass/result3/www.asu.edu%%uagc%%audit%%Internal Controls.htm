What Are Internal Controls?

Internal Controls comprise of the plan of organization and all methods and measures designed to:

  1. provide reasonable assurance that assets are safeguards, information (financial and other) is timely and reliable, and errors and irregularities are discovered and corrected promptly: promote operational efficiency and effectiveness; and

  2. encourage compliance with managerial policies, laws, regulations and sound fiduciary principles.1

Examples of Internal Controls

Cash Disbursements
 Return to Top

¨      A written request is submitted and approved before merchandise can be ordered.

¨      Accounts payable (A/P) supporting documentation should be noted when payment is requested to prevent duplicate payments.

¨      All vendor (A/P) invoices are to be double checked for accuracy.

¨      Invoices and supporting documentation are to be furnished with all advantage documents.

¨      Appropriate safeguards are in effect for all petty cash funds.

¨      Reconciliation is performed on all accounts, using supporting documentation and Advantage reports.

Personnel and Payroll
Return to Top

¨      Written departmental personnel policies that are in addition to university personnel policies are maintained in the department.

¨      Written personnel policies and procedures contain a code of conduct and provisions for conflict of interest.

¨      Performance reviews are performed on all employees on a periodic basis.

¨      All employees have adequate training.

¨      All employees are made aware of their responsibilities and expectations.

¨      Appropriate remedial actions are taken in response to departures from approved policies and procedures or violations of the code of conduct.  All actions comply with university policy.¨      References should be checked on all potential new hires.

¨      Employees have adequate supervision.

¨      Adequate staffing is maintained.

¨      Employee job descriptions are maintained.

¨      An “Open Door” policy is maintained.

¨      All changes in employment salary, wage rates, and payroll deductions are properly authorized, approved and documented.

¨      Appropriate payroll records are maintained for vacation leave, sick leave, etc.

¨      Records and procedures exist for timekeeping and attendance.

¨      Procedures exist for authorizing, approving and recording vacations, holidays and sick leave.  Compensatory time is controlled and approved.

¨      Controls exist over payroll preparation, including deductions, and distribution.

Cash
Return to Top

¨      There is limited access to petty cash funds.

¨      The responsibility for collection and deposit preparation functions is segregated from those responsible for recording cash receipts and general ledger entries.

¨      The responsibilities for cash receipt functions are segregated from those for cash disbursements.

¨      The responsibilities for disbursement preparation and disbursement approval function are segregated from those for recording or entering cash disbursement information on the general ledger.

¨      The responsibility for the disbursement approval function is segregated from those for the disbursement, voucher preparation, and purchasing functions.

¨      The responsibility for entries in the cash receipts and cash disbursement records are segregated from those for general ledger entries.

¨      General ledger control exists over all bank accounts.

¨      Depositories are periodically reviewed and formally re-authorized.

Purchasing
Return to Top

¨      All expenditures are approved by the appropriate levels of management.

¨      A written and uniform purchasing policy and procedure policy is in place.

¨      There is a policy statement with respect to conflicts of interest.

¨      Confirm that adequate funds are verified, taking into account all encumbrances, before purchases are made.

¨      Competitive bidding procedures are used.

Receiving
Return to Top

¨      A receiver document (when necessary) is promptly prepared upon receiving merchandise.

¨      Merchandise is inspected for condition and counted by the department.

Accounts Payable
Return to Top

¨      Duplicate copies of invoices are clearly marked, immediately, upon receipt to avoid duplicate payment.

¨      Invoices are reviewed for mathematical accuracy.

¨      Vendor credit memos are resolved promptly by cash refund or taken against subsequent purchases.

¨      Returned purchases are controlled in a manner which ensures that the refund or credit will be received.

¨      The encumbrance record is periodically reconciled to open purchase orders and commitments.

¨      Adequate records are maintained for unmatched purchase orders, receiving documents, and unpaid vendors’ invoices.  These records are periodically reviewed and investigated.

¨      Monthly statements from vendors are reconciled to open vouchers.

¨      Procedures are in effect to ensure proper expenditure cut-off.

¨      Past due balances are properly reviewed and followed up on.

¨      Original invoices are necessary to issue payment.

¨      All partial deliveries are followed up on.

¨      Procedures exist for ensuring the accurate account distribution of all entries resulting from invoice processing.

¨      Procedures exist for submission and approval of reimbursement to employees for travel or other expenses.

¨      Cash discounts are taken, and if applicable, exemptions from sales, federal excise and other taxes are claimed.

¨      Differences in invoice and purchase order prices, terms, shipping arrangements, or quantities are referred to purchasing for review and approval.

Accounts Receivable
Return to Top

¨      Credit and collection policies are in writing and are properly authorized.

¨      The accounts receivable function is maintained independently of cash receipt and disbursement functions.

¨      Noncash credits, bad debt write-offs, credit memos, and allowances are approved independently of processing, recording and collection.

¨      Accounts are aged periodically and are reviewed by a responsible official.

¨      All billings are controlled and properly accounted for.

¨      All valid receivable are promptly recorded.

¨      Adequate procedures exist for follow up and collection of delinquent accounts.

¨      Credit balances are reviewed periodically

.¨      Adequate control exists over the mailing of statements to prevent interception prior to the mailing.

¨      Disputed billings are handled by persons other than those responsible for the receivable recordkeeping procedures.

¨      Numerical or batch-processing controls exist over billings.

¨      Procedures exist that ensure that interest and penalties are properly charged on delinquent accounts.

¨      If payments are made in person, receipts are accounted for and balanced to collections.

¨      Individual receivable records are posted only from authorized source documents.

¨      Trial balances of the individual receivable accounts are prepared on a timely basis.

¨      Trial balances are reconciled with the general ledger control account and reconciling items are investigated by someone other than the accounts receivable clerk.

General Fixed Assets
Return to Top

¨      A disposal of assets form is done whenever assets are lost, stolen, transferred or sent to surplus property.

¨      There is a formal policy of authorization in effect for additions, replacements, and disposals.

¨      Written approval is required for the approval to sell, transfer, scrap or dismantle any equipment.

¨      Written procedures exist for the safeguarding equipment against improper or unauthorized use.

¨      Adequate records are maintained of the property under the custody of, but not owned by the department.

¨      All items are adequately safeguarded from loss from fire, theft, or misplacement.

¨      Grant funded acquisitions are subjected to the same controls as internally funded acquisitions.

¨      Supplemental authorizations are required, including, if applicable, those of the grantor agency, for expenditures in excess of the originally approved amounts.

¨      The accounting distributions are reviewed to ensure the proper allocation of charges to fixed assets and expenditure projects.

¨      For construction work performed by contractors, procedures exist to provide for and maintain control over construction projects and progress billings.

¨      Accountability for each asset is established.

¨      Procedures exist that ensure purchased materials and services for capital expenditures and repair projects are subjected to the same level of control that exist for all other procurements.

¨      Difference between records and physical counts are investigated and records are adjusted to reflect shortages.

¨      Lease transactions are subjected to the same control procedures as those required for other capital expenditures.

¨      Procedures exist for monitoring the appropriate disposition of property acquired with grant funds.

Federal Compliance
Return to Top

¨      The responsibility for monitoring grant activities is properly fixed.

¨      Grant activities are monitored from a centralized location.

¨      Procedures exist to monitor compliance with:

a.       Financial reporting requirements,

b.      The use of funds and other conditions, and

c.       Timely billing of amounts due under grants.

¨      Grant activity is accounted for so that it can be separated from the accounting for locally funded activities.

¨      There is a system for obtaining grantor approval before incurring expenditures in excess of budgeting amounts or for unbudgeted expenditures.

¨      Grant revenues and disbursements are processed under the same degree of controls applicable to the organization’s other transactions.

¨      Requirements are included in subgrantee agreements that the subgrantee comply with the primary grant agreement conditions as well as the grantee’s standards.

¨      Reasonable procedures and controls exist to provide assurances of compliance with recipient eligibility requirements established by grants.

¨      Indirect cost plans are approved by all grantor agencies.

¨      Rates generated by the plan are approved by the appropriate agent.

¨      Procedures exist to ensure that funds received are spent in accordance with legal requirements and spending restrictions.

¨      Procedures are instituted to identify, before order entry, costs and expenditures not allowed under grant programs.

¨      Procedures exist to monitor compliance with grant terms.

¨      Recipients are monitored sufficiently and on a timely basis to permit curtailment of any abuse before funds are disbursed.

¨      Controls are established to ensure that payroll costs charged to grants are in compliance with the grant agreement.

¨      Payroll and personnel policies governing compensation are in accordance with the requirements of grant agreements.

Cash Receipts
Return to Top

¨      Cash receipts are serially pre-numbered.

¨      Cash receipts are reconciled to the deposit on a daily basis.

¨      Cash receipts are reconciled to supporting documentation on a daily basis.

¨      Cash receipts are deposited on a timely basis.

¨      A restrictive endorsement is placed on each incoming check upon receipt.

¨      Undeoposited cash receipts are stored in a secured area.

¨      The validated deposit slip agrees with the department prepared deposit slip.

¨      Procedures exist for follow-up on “non sufficient funds” checks.

¨      The responsibilities for the cash receipt function are segregated from those for cash disbursements.

¨      Procedures exist to ensure that collections are recorded accurately and promptly.

¨      Unused cash receipts are adequately safeguarded.

¨      A cash receipt is issued for all monies received.

¨      Voided cash receipts are controlled.

¨      “Non-sufficient funds” checks are delivered to someone independent of processing and recording cash receipts.

¨      Daily reported cash receipts are compared on a test basis to bank statement to verify timeliness of deposits.

¨      During bank reconciliation procedures, deposit amounts are agreed with the cash receipt entries.

    1.  Definition according to NBD Bancorp's Auditing Divisions Training Program, revised 6/1/95
