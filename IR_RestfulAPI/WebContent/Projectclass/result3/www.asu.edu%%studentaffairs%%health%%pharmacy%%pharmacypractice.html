Student Health Pharmacy Practice

Pharmacy Hours:

M-F: 9:00AM to 5:30PM

(Summer 8:00AM to 4:30PM)

  • Average Rx Volume:230 per day. Tech supported, both Rx and OTC meds are entered in the pharmacy software database (ProPharm One). Highly oriented towards patient counseling.
  • Drug Information Center: The pharmacy serves as DIC for the entire Student Health Center, providing "on demand" drug information to doctors, nurse practitioners, nurses, mental health practitioners, other support personnel, and, of course, students.
  • Committee involvement: Pharmacy students are encouraged to participate in one of the Center's committees, particularly Pharmacy & Therapeutics and Continuous Improvement. The P&T Committee is an e-committee, with materials and discussion accomplished via electronic communications.

Preceptor's Scope of Practice

  1. Pharmacy Manager: Responsible for daily operation of the pharmacy, including personnel, inventory, and routine functions, coordinates clinical support functions by pharmacy staff, including drug information.
  2. Committees:
    1. Chair of P&T Committee, is responsible for maintaining the formulary, performing Drug Utilization Evaluation (DUE), Drug Utilization Review (DUR) and developing Policy and Procedure in all clinical areas.
    2. Chair of CI (Continuous Improvement), is responsible for data accumulation, preparation of audit summaries and reports, develop and improve audit designs, and disseminate findings to appropriate sections and committees.
  3. Professional Writing :
    1. "The Phar Side" Pharmacy Newsletter, published regularly, has been excerpted by the American College Health Association (ACHA) for use in their Nursing Newsletter and is distributed at ASU, other colleges, and is available on the Internet at www.asu.edu\health\pharlink.html.
    2. Editorial Advisory Board of Pharmacy Today, an official publication of the APhA.
    3. Published articles in Pharmacy Times, Pharmacy Today, JAPhA, and local media. See “In Print” ( link)
  4. Professional Presentations: Some recent presentations include:
    1. "The Use of Carbon Monoxide Testing in Smoking Programs," a podium presentation at the American Pharmaceutical Association annual meeting, March 2000, Washington, D.C.
    2. “Prescription Writing Rules and Regulations” at the Arizona School of Professional Psychology, Phoenix, AZ, May 9, 2001
    3. Co-presenter, “Show Some Skin – A Visual Journey Through College Health Dermatology,” at American College Health Association Annual Meeting, May 31, 2001, Las Vegas, NV.
    4. Co-presenter, “Mind Over Matter: The Brain’s Response to Nicotine,” for the Coalition for a Tobacco-Free Arizona, Halle Heart Center, Tempe, AZ, October 2, 2002
    5. “Collaborative Pharmacy Practice in College Health,” at American College Health Association Annual Meeting, June 9, 2004, New Orleans, LA.
    6. “Tobacco: Cardiac Risk Considerations,” at the Native American Community Health Center, Phoenix, AZ, February 22, 2005.
    7. “Emergency Contraception: Patterns, Perceptions and Paradigms,” presented at American College Health Association Annual Meeting, June 2, 2005, San Diego, CA.
    8. Co-presenter, “Smoking Cessation,” an eight-hour continuing education program at the first annual meeting of the Arizona Pharmacy Alliance at Wild Horse Pass Resort in Chandler, AZ on June 16, 2005.
  5. Educational Involvement:
    1. Co-evaluate case study presentations for ASU College of Nursing Graduate Program: Management and Maintenance of Adults with Chronic Health (NUR580).
