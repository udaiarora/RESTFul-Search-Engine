General SRC FAQ

How do I reserve space in the SRC?

Send an email to src.reservation@asu.edu indicating what space you are interested in, what dates and times your are requesting, Name of the organization, purpose of the event activities, equipment needs

How do I become a member?

All ASU students (Main Campus in Tempe) are automatically members though their ASU registration. Other memberships to the Student Recreation Complex are available to students, faculty/staff, alumni, and spouses of members. Memberships can be purchased with cash, check, debit and credit card during Administration hours. When the Admin. office is closed, memberships can be purchased at the SRC front desk with cash or check only.
Please visit the membership page for more information

Sun Devil Fit and Massage FAQ

Fitness & Yoga Home | Wellness & Therapy Home

How can I sample a Sun Devil Fit Group Fitness Class without having a semester pass?

Purchase a daily guest pass for $5 at the administration desk or equipment room.
Free classes are offered during beginning of each semester, during breaks, and special events.

What is the difference between an express class and regular class?

Express classes are 45 minutes in length. Most other classes will be one hour with the exception of our 30-minute abs class.

How do you say Pilates? (pull-ah-teez). What is it?

A combination of yoga and core training based on some dance and ballet moves.

How do I become a Sun Devil Fit Group Fitness Instructor?

Attend our Learn To Teach Group Fitness Workshop or if you have experience fill out an application and plan for an audition. Learn more here.

Is Water Fitness included with my group fitness class?

Yes, go to the equipment room for a stamp and meet the instructor at the pool.

What is a nutrition analysis?

For a nutrition analysis, individuals write down what they eat for one or three days and then a nutritionist enters their food intake into a computer program. The computer program provides a printout of total calories, fats, protein, carbohydrates, vitamins, minerals, etc. Our nutritionists will review the nutrition analysis with clients and then based upon your goals, provide personalized suggestions about your eating program.

Does the SRC offer massage?

Swedish, Therapeutic, and Sports Massages are offered by certified massage therapists at the SRC through Performance Massage. Learn more here.

Fitness & Personal Training

Fitness & Personal Training Home

How do I hire a personal trainer?

Should you choose to continue with your personal trainer after your initial 30-minute consultation, pre-pay at the SRC administration office by choosing any of our personal training payment packages. You may also pre-purchase sessions online. You and your trainer will then set up further training sessions according to both your daily schedules.

View Personal Trainers Biographies

What do I get after I purchase a personal training session?

  • Proper warm up, stretch, and cool down.
  • An exercise program according to your specific goals.
  • Answers to your cardiovascular exercise, resistance training, & flexibility questions.
  • Specific program design written on workout cards.
  • Periodic training to decrease boredom, increase motivation, decrease risk of injury, & train at peak performance all the time!

What else can a trainer offer?

  • Stretching & Flexibility
  • Abs and Back Training
  • Cardiovascular (Aerobic) Exercise
  • Program Creativity
  • Exercises for Home & Travel
  • Resistance Training

What else do I need to know about personal training?

Wear comfortable clothing including a t-shirt with full sleeves, athletic shoes, and non-jean apparel, to comply with weight room policies.

Rescheduling or canceling sessions must be done 24 hours prior to your session to avoid being charged for that session.

When meeting with your personal trainer, be sure to read and sign our personal training information sheet thoroughly. Pre-purchased sessions are non-refundable.

Why can't I wear a tank top or a cut off shirt (shirt with no sleeves or cut in mid-drift) when working our in the weight room, circuit training room, or on the weight machines on the second floor?

You are required to wear a full t-shirt with proper sleeves in order to maintain a clean and non-intimidating environment (the goal of the SRC is to target the average weight trainer and provide a comfortable environment), for sanitary issues (to decrease the spread of disease, bacteria, and viruses through body fluids and perspiration left on pads is discourteous to the next user), and to preserve the equipment (excess body oils and sweat destroys the pads on the equipment).

Where can I perform Olympic type or quick overhead movements in the weight room?

For the safety of all patrons, these exercises can only be performed INSIDE the power racks located on the South wall of the weight room.

Team Challenge FAQ

Team Challenge Home

What is Team Challenge?

Team Challenge is a safe, fun interactive approach to education. We bring you (and your group, organization, club, etc.) together as a "team" and we "challenge" you. Its that simple! Through games and initiatives, the group solves problems and works together as a team. Goals for having a team engage in a challenge may be (but is not limited to) working on communication, leadership, group process, roles within a group, conflict management, compromise, trust, risk-taking and of course.team building! We also do Team Challenges as icebreakers and to help groups get to know each other.

What is the cost?

Team Challenge's are free to all ASU student organizations and faculty/staff groups on campus. Minimal charges may be incurred if a Challenge is occurring off campus and we need to transport facilitators and equipment. There are several scholarship opportunities on campus to help offset those costs.

What is the Retreat Packaging Service?

The SRC offers a service that may or may not include Team Challenge. Many groups like to have retreats at different times of the year. The SRC is available to help plan and coordinate the retreat, including catering, if necessary. We can also include a Team Challenge component to your retreat schedule. The service for this is free. Some rental facilities and all catering are charged to the group. Facilitation is free, unless there is a charge for transporting equipment and facilitators to a location off campus. There are several scholarship opportunities on campus to help offset those costs.

Aquatics FAQ

Aquatics Home

What time is the pool open?

The pool closes one hour prior to the close of the building. Click here for building hours.

What time is lap swimming available?

There are always eight lanes open for lap swimming during the time that the pool is open.

Is there equipment available to use?

The SRC pool offers the use of kick boards, pull buoys, aqua joggers, and life jackets free of charge to all users. The items can be found stored on the pool deck, just outside of the lifeguard office. The lifeguards can not offer swim goggles to SRC users, due to the potential spreading of communicable diseases (i.e. pink eye)

When can I bring my children to swim?

Please view the pool schedule to see when you can bring your children. Parents must accompany their children in the water at all times. Children are to swim in the shallow area of the pool only, unless they are swimming laps with the parents. Children must share a lane with the parents. Life jackets are available upon request. Please ask the lifeguards on duty to assist you.

Do you offer swimming lessons at the SRC Pool?

Both adult and children swimming lessons are offered at the SRC Pool. Times and sessions may vary each semester. Please check the aquatics link on the website for the most current classes offered. Learn more here.

Do you offer Lifeguard Training and Water Safety Instructor Classes at the SRC.

The SRC pool provide instruction for both the American Red Cross Lifeguard Training and Water Safety Instructor Courses. Times and sessions may vary each semester. Please Check the aquatics link on the website for the most current classes offered. The SRC also offers training in American Red Cross First Aid and CPR for the Professional Rescuer. Learn more here.


If you have any further questions to be answered about the Aquatics or Safety Education Programs offered at the Student Recreation Complex, please call Justin Kern at (480)965-8924.

Intramurals FAQ

Intramurals Home

How do I sign up for Intramurals?

1. Pick up a individual flyer for each sport that consists of deadlines, sport
information, and team entry form. More information is available on the
intramural web page.
2. Fill the team entry form out completely. These forms are also available
online here.
3. Proceed to the Administration Desk for payment of the Entry Fee. Checks are
made payable to Arizona State University. Visa/MasterCard is accepted. A receipt
will be issued to the Team Captain and one will be kept on file. Please keep
this receipt for proof of payment.
4. For leagues only, proceed to the IM Desk with receipt to instant schedule
your team. Each event will have a scheduling folder with a team menu listing all
leagues available sorted by day and time. The intramural staff will assist you
in selecting the league, day and time of your choice.
5. Attend MANDATORY Captain's Meeting for that sport to review the rules and
regulations, and to verify league assignments.

What about tournaments?

1. Follow steps 1-3 from above
2. Proceed to IM Office, submit entry form, and check to see when schedules will
be available. Schedules will be posted on the IM Bulletin Board on the 1st
floor. There will also be copies available for you to pick up at the intramural
desk.
3. Attend MANDATORY Captain's Meeting if one is scheduled for your sport.

What is a Free Agent?

There are many individuals on campus who want to participate in Intramural
Sports activities but are unaware about how to get involved. Scheduled Free
Agent meetings are designed to assist individuals in forming teams and to
encourage participation for everyone. Free agents need to attend the free agent
meeting and we will do our best to put you on a team. Learn more here.

Sports Clubs FAQ

What are Sport Clubs?

Sport Clubs provide opportunities for students, faculty, and staff with similar interests to participate in various sport and recreational activities. Sport Clubs bridge the gap between intramural and intercollegiate athletics by providing competition at specialized levels, participation in tournaments and opportunities to practice. Although some clubs remain solely recreational, most are highly competitive.

Club privileges include the use of the Arizona State name, a small budget allotted by the Sport Club Association and the Department of Recreational Sports, and athletic facilities when available. Most clubs supplement their budget through membership fees and fund raising.

What can Sport Clubs do for me?

Arizona State University’s Sport Club program gives the student an opportunity to take part in the leadership, responsibility and decision making process of club activities. The success of the Sport Club is dependent on the professionalism and leadership shown by the club officers, advisors and coaches. It is created for students, by students, under the supervision of the Department of Recreational Sports-Sport Clubs Director and staff.
