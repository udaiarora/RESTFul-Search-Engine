Libraries at ASU
www.asu.edu/lib

 



Through a combination of print and electronic sources, the Arizona State University Libraries support the information needs of the various people who make up a Research I institution. The ASU Libraries on the Tempe campus are comprised of the following:
  • Hayden Library
    Located on Cady Mall, Hayden houses materials in the humanities and social sciences, including business and education. It also has a number of specialized collections including Government Documents (federal, state, local, and UN), Arizona Collection, Benedict Visual Literacy Collection, Special Collections, University Archives, East Asian Collection, Curriculum Collection, Chicano Research Collection, Child Drama Collection, Labriola National American Indian Data Center, and the Arizona Historical Foundation. There are coin-operated typewriters, photocopy services, and study rooms.




  • Daniel E. Noble Science and Engineering Library
    3 floors house the collections for astronomy, biology, chemistry, engineering, geography, geology, industry standards, maps, mathematics, medicine, nursing, and physics. These collections include approximately 425,000 volumes, 1600 print journal subscriptions, 210,000 sheet maps, and 20,500 aerial photographs. In addition, approximately 6,000 electronic resources (journals, reference databases, and indexing services) and the Internet are accessible via over 60 in-house workstations or by personal laptops equipped with wireless modems.

  • Music Library
    Holdings include books on music, printed music, recorded music in a variety of formats, journals, and music related microforms. There are listening facilities within the library. Materials may be used within the library by anyone and circulate to ASU and community borrowers.

  • Architecture and Environmental Design Library
    Supporting the teaching and research missions of the College of Architecture and Environmental Design, The Architecture and Environmental Design Library is a part of the Arizona State University Libraries system, which has close to three million volumes. The AED Library holds over 38,000 items including books, periodicals, CD-ROMs, video recordings, and microforms. Subject coverage focuses on design, including: architecture, interior design, graphic design, industrial design, housing, planning and landscape architecture.

  • Ross-Blakely Law Library
    The John J. Ross-William C. Blakley Law Library has a student lab with 22 computers and two computer-assisted research labs for Lexis and Westlaw and 27 study rooms for the exclusive use of ASU College of Law faculty, students, staff, members of the local bar, and people studying for the bar exam. The library also has more than 150 study carrels and table seating for more than 200 individuals. It is also recognized as an architecturally-significant building and has received numerous honors.



  • ASU Library at the Polytechnic campus
    Located in the Academic Center Building on the Polytechnic campus, this library provides a full range of services to ASU Polytechnic and Chandler-Gilbert Community College students, supplemented by access to the library holdings of the other ASU campuses. Numerous specialized online research databases and Internet services are available for student and faculty use. Within the ASU online catalog, books and other materials that are located on the east campus are identified as EAST.


  • Fletcher Library at the West campus
    The Fletcher Library at the West campus supports the campus curriculum and features an extensive media collection—an information access center designed to take full advantage of electronic technology. Compact discs, online databases, Internet, telecommunication networks and other systems are used to provide efficient and speedy delivery of information and materials. Book/photocopy/video delivery is available from other campus libraries. Within the online catalog, materials that are located on the west campus are identified as WEST. The Fletcher Library building also houses Technopolis, the student computing access center, on its lower level.
