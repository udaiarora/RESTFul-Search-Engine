Division of Graduate Studies (www.asu.edu/graduate)
The Division of Graduate Studies offers a series of professional development workshops, series, and programs for graduate students, including:
  • Strategies for Success (www.asu.edu/graduate/sfs)
    The Strategies for Success professional development includes teaching and instructional workshops, career development, and other programs designed to enrich the graduate experience. These workshops are open to all registered graduate students.
  • Preparing Future Faculty (www.asu.edu/graduate/pff)
    Preparing Future Faculty (PFF) is a program coordinated by the Division of Graduate Studies for doctoral students who are seeking careers in the professoriate. PFF encourages fresh thinking and planning in faculty preparation, identifies strategies to improve the quality of teaching and learning and orients doctoral students to different types of higher education institutions.
  • Preparing Future Professionals (www.asu.edu/graduate/pfp)
    The Preparing Future Professionals program administered by the Division of Graduate Studies assists doctoral students interested in pursuing careers in government, nonprofit, and business sectors. PFP parallels the well-established and successful PFF program. Through a series of activities, PFP familiarizes doctoral students with various nonacademic career tracks to develop skills to successfully pursue a wide range of professional opportunities.


Center for Learning and Teaching Excellence (clte.asu.edu)
The Center for Learning and Teaching Excellence (CLTE) is dedicated to enhancing the teaching and learning possibilities at ASU. To support this mission, the center provides a variety of training, support, and professional development programs for members of the university community, including graduate students who have teaching responsibilities.


Instruction Support (is.asu.edu)
Instruction Support (IT/IS) serves as a development center for the use of technology in the design and delivery of instruction. Staffed with students, faculty, and researchers skilled in the areas of system design, graphics, interactive software, networked delivery, and digital video, the innovation-driven group pushes the development of instruction to the limits of available technology.
Distance Learning and Technology, Online Teaching and Learning Group (asuonline.asu.edu/facultysupport)
The Online Teaching and Learning Group in Distance Learning and Technology supports Arizona State University faculty and students in the creation of effective online learning environments, including assistance with course design and development, face-to-face and online skill building, and effective online teaching.


Career Services (career.asu.edu)
Career Services provides advising for individual career planning and employment options.
Intergroup Relations Center (www.asu.edu/provost/intergroup)
The Intergroup Relations Center (IRC) promotes positive intergroup relations, prepares students for living in a diverse democracy, creates greater understanding between different groups that exist at ASU, and provides faculty, staff, and students opportunities to explore the rich diversity that is part of the ASU campus community.
International Student Office (www.asu.edu/studentlife/iso)
The International Student Office (ISO) is responsible for the administration and coordination of ASU’s international student program. The ISO’s principal responsibilities and services include initial orientation, counseling and advising, administrative support, and campus and community activities which promote international awareness and enrich the educational experiences of students. ISO monitors SEVIS requirements for all international students once they have arrived on campus.
