Interactive Program of Study (iPOS) - Live!
Beginning February 14, graduate students can file their Program of Study using a new, secure online process called the Interactive Program of Study (iPOS). To use this interactive tool, students must log in to the myASU portal (my.asu.edu), click on the Graduate Studies tab, and select their Interactive Program of Study. This electronic process will guide them through a step-by-step process and present a list of eligible courses for students to choose from. A number of edits are built in to ensure that the student has met university requirements prior to submitting their iPOS. A screen in the gPortal system will alert the academic unit that a Program of Study is pending their approval. Once approved by the academic unit, the iPOSs will be electronically routed to the Graduation Office and finally to the Division of Graduate Studies.

As of March 1, the Division of Graduate Studies will no longer accept paper Program of Study forms. To facilitate this online process, we are requesting that academic units review and submit all paper POS forms by March 1.

To ensure a successful implementation, training sessions have been established. It is critical that all support staff involved in the review and approval of graduate Programs of Study attend one of these sessions:
  • Tuesday, Feb. 7, 9-10:30 a.m., Computing Commons, Room 120
  • Wednesday, Feb. 8, 2-3:30 p.m., UCB 265 at the West Campus
  • Thursday, Feb. 9, 1:30-3:00 p.m., Student Union Ballroom B at the Polytechnic Campus
ASU and CONACYT
Arizona State University (ASU) is proud to present its partnership with El Consejo Nacional de Ciencia y Tecnología (CONACYT) in Mexico. This historic agreement grants 75 fellowships for Mexican graduate students to study at ASU, and provides for other types of research and exchange funding. For more information, go to www.asu.edu/opi/conacyt.

Clarification: DGS Internal Fellowships
Students interested in receiving the DGS Doctoral Enrichment and the Reach for the Stars fellowships must be nominated by their academic units. Students and/or academic units may apply for the DGS Dissertation Fellowship. For more information and how to apply, go to www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo.
