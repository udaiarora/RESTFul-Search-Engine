ASU East to host cancer society's Relay for Life fund-raiser

ASU East will host Relay for Life, the American Cancer Society's signature event, April 11 - 12 at the campus's track and field.

The all-night fund-raiser brings together volunteers, cancer survivors and their friends and families in a continuous trek around the track. Teams of people will camp out and take turns walking or running. Each team is asked to have a representative on the track at all times during the event.

Anna Wales, of the ASU Main Office of Public Affairs, is coordinating the event at East Campus. Relay for Life has special meaning to Wales, whose daughter, Meghan, is a cancer survivor.

"The power of Relay for Life is that it allows a community to grieve for those lost to cancer and to celebrate the lives of those who have survived," said Wales. "For the newly diagnosed, it offers a chance to meet someone who has survived the same type of cancer.

"For the cancer patient in treatment, it provides an opportunity to share with others. For the long-term survivor, Relay for Life brings a community's recognition of the struggle and closure to a trying time in life."

Because Relay For Life is a community gathering rather than an athletic event, anyone can participate. Teams come from businesses, clubs, families, friends, hospitals, churches, schools and service organizations. Team members share a common purpose - to support the American Cancer Society's mission.
Relay For Life also raises funds. Teams solicit donations, have garage sales, hold car washes and more.

At ASU East, special activities will include free food, live music, entertainment for children, a ride on the Saturn blimp for cancer survivors and the Relay for Life luminaria celebration.

The luminaria celebration, starting at 9 p.m., will include lighted luminarias around the track with the names of cancer victims and survivors. Each name on the luminarias will be read aloud.

Other special events include cancer survivors taking the first lap to kick off the events at 6 p.m.

ASU East's Relay for Life event will have 15 teams and include up to 500 participants.
Volunteers and participants are still welcome. For information, call Eva Antal at (480) 727-1665.

Holeman, with ASU East, can be reached at (480) 727-1173 or (j.holeman@asu.edu).
