Study Abroad at
The University of Tirana

Where

Visit Tirana (pronounced: Tih-rana) the capital and the largest city (1991 est. pop. 300,000) of Albania. It is the administrative, cultural, economic, and industrial center of the Republic of Albania. Located at the crossroads of the Adriatic and eastern Albania, the area around Tirana has been inhabited since the Neolithic period. Tirana was founded by the Ottomans in the 17th century and was originally named Teheran to commemorate an Ottoman victory in Persia. The University of Tirana has seven faculties that include humanities and science programs.
When

A semester or year-long program, October 1 - January 31 and/or February 15 - May 31
The Program

Students take 8 credits per semester of intensive language study as well as courses in the history and culture of Albania and the Balkans.
Eligibility

The program is open to all upper division and graduate students who have maintained a 3.0 GPA or higher. Students must have language proficiency in Albanian at the ILR Level 2 in reading and listening. Non-ASU students are welcome to apply.
Living Accommodations

Students are placed in private or shared housing in university dormitories, city flats, or with families. Meals are taken in the university cafeteria or in local cafes and restaurants.
Estimated Cost

Total costs, including tuition, room and board, travel, and personal expenses, are estimated to be $3,500 per semester for tuition, fees, and board. Additional expenses will include passport, visa, insurance, and personal expenses. Students who are classified as non-Arizona residents for tuition purposes are subject to a surcharge of approximately $1,100 per semester. Transportation to and from Tirana is not included. In addition to the one or two semester program, students should plan to participate in the Critical Languages Institute Albanian summer language study at ASU, or an equivalent language study program, prior to attending the University of Tirana.
Application Process

Interested students should fill out an International Program Qualifying Application Packet, which is available through REESC or at the ASU Office of International Programs. Applicants must meet the 3.00 GPA eligibility requirement set by ASU. Application packets are due March 1st for the fall semester program and October 1st for the spring semester program. A $350 non-refundable deposit is due at the time of application.
Financial Aid

Most ASU scholarships, grants, and loans can be used to help pay program costs. Students should consult with financial aid counselors. The International Programs Office has scholarships available, on a competitive basis, for students registered for study abroad or exchange programs. Students applying should consider joint application to the National Security Education Program.
ASU Credit

During the semester abroad, students are registered at ASU and qualify for up to 14 hours per semester of ASU residence credit. Upon completion of the semester, the ASU course credit and grade equivalency of each course taken in Tirana will be assigned to the student's ASU transcript.
