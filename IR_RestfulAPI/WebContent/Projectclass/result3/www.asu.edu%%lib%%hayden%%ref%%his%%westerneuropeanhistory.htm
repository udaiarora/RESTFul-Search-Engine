Western European History: Modern Period

INTRODUCTION

This guide describes the most useful indexes, bibliographies and other reference works required for research in modern Western European History. The final section includes selected internet sites for European primary sources. The period covered encompasses the Eighteenth through the Twentieth Century. 

Online Indexes

Arts and Humanities Citation Index    (Web of Science)
(1975+) Provides access to current bibliographic information and cited references. Covers more than 1,150 of the world's leading arts and humanities journals in a broad range of disciplines. 

British Humanities Index (BHI): 1962-Current
The database is an online index to (1962+) humanities journals and weekly magazines published in Great Britain and other English speaking nations including newspapers in UK

CRLCatalog    (Catalog of the Center for Research Libraries)
Includes cataloged monographs, newspapers,serials, archival materials in microform, microform sets and title analytics, CRL's cataloging for holdings that appear in OCLC and RLIN.

Eighteen Century Collections Online
Eighteenth Century Collections Online provides access to the digital images of every page of more than 150,000 books published during the 18th Century. With full-text searching of approximately 33 million pages, the product allows researchers new methods of access to critical information in the fields of history, literature, religion, law, fine arts, science and more.

Historical Abstracts
(1954+) Covers the world's scholarly literature in history. Includes article abstracts and bibliographic citations of books and dissertations on the history of the world from 1450 to the present, except for the United States and Canada.

Humanities Full Text    (Wilson Humanities Abstracts Full Text)
(2/1984+) Indexes articles in the fields of archaeology and classical studies, film, folklore, gender studies, history, journalism, communications, language, literature, literary and political criticism, performing arts, philosophy, religion, etc 

Internationale Bibliographie der Zeitschriftenliteratur    (International Bibliography of Periodical Literature)
(1987+) Covers basic research from all fields of knowledge, and is particularly strong in the humanities and the social sciences. Indexes a significant proportion of the periodicals published on the European continent. 

 PAIS International    (Public Affairs Information Service)
(1972+) Index to political, economic, & social issues in current debate. Covers public & social policy literature of business, economics, finance, law, international relations, public administration, government, political science, & other social sciences.

PCI Full Text    (Periodicals Contents Index (Selected Titles))
(1770-1995) PCI Web is an electronic index to the contents of thousands of periodicals in the humanities and social sciences from their first issues to 1995. Every article is indexed.

Social Sciences Full Text
(1983+) Covers concepts, theories, and methodologies in all areas of the social sciences. Subjects covered include anthropology, criminology, economics, law, geography, policy studies, psychology, sociology, social work, and urban studies. 

PRINT INDEXES TO OLDER LITERATURE

 Bibliographie der Fremdsprachigen Zeitschriftenliteratur. International Bibliographie der Zeitschriftenliteratur. Osnabruch: Dietrich, 1911-1964. AI 9 .B7 HAYDEN SERIALS

Indexes 1,400 periodicals in the principal non-German languages. Very useful for finding English, French, Italian and other European publications. Because of the lack of French periodical indexes, it is particularly important for French articles.  Merged with Bibliographie der Deutschen Zeitschriftenliteratur to form: Internationale Bibliographie der Zeitschriftenliteratur aus allen Gebieten des Wissens: International Bibliography of Periodical Literature Covering All Fields of Knowledge. 

Bibliographie Annuelle de l'Histoire de France du Cinquieme Siecle a 1958. Paris: Editions du Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique, 1953/54- . Annual. DC 38 .B54 HAYDEN REF

A classified bibliography of books and articles, with author and subject indexes. The most comprehensive work in the field of French history, it cites articles in over 2,000 journals, essays in collective works and conference proceedings.

C.R.I.S.: Combined Retrospective Index to Journals in History, 1838-1974. 11 vols. Washington: Carrollton, 1977-78. D 1 .C7x HAYDEN REF (located in Case 4A)

Indexes all articles from the earliest volumes through 1974 for 243 English language periodicals covering all periods and all areas in the field of history. The articles are arranged by country in Vols. 1-4. Europe in general is in Vol. 3.

International Index. New York: Wilson, 1907-65. AI 3 .R49 HAYDEN REF  

Predecessor of the Social Sciences & Humanities Index  

PAIS International in Print. New York: Public Affairs Information Service, 1915- . Monthly. H 1 .P85x HAYDEN REF  

Until 1991, entitled Public Affairs Information Service Bulletin, this publication includes foreign language material formerly included in PAIS Foreign Language Index. An alphabetical subject listing of books, pamphlets, periodical articles, and government documents in the field of public affairs, international relations, political science and economics. PAIS is selective in that it lists only the most significant materials in English.

 Times (London, England). Index to the Times. London: Times, 1790- . Quarterly. AI 21 .T46 HAYDEN REF

A subject index to the London Times. The index for 1906-1966 is on microfilm (AI 21 .T46 Microfilm).

BIBLIOGRAPHICAL GUIDES TO THE LITERATURE

The term "Bibliographical guide" refers to a bibliography which surveys an entire field by identifying the most important reference works, including other bibliographies. It serves to open up the literature for the beginning student and points the way to further research.

American Historical Association's Guide to Historical Literature. New York: Oxford University Press, 1995. 3rd ed. D 20. A55x 1995 HAYDEN REF Lists, with brief notes, the most important monographs and articles in every field of history.

Fritze, Ronald. Reference Sources in History: An Introductory Guide. Santa Barbara, CA: ABC-Clio, 1990. D 20 .F75x 1990 HAYDEN REF

An excellent source of information on the availability of library catalogs, bibliographies of special topics, periodical and newspaper indexes, oral history, biography, national archives, government publications, databases and much more. Essential for all graduate students.

Iggers, Georg G. International Handbook of Historical Studies. Westport, CT: Greenwood, 1979. D 13.162 HAYDEN STACKS

BIOGRAPHY

These works are used to locate biographies of important people from all countries.

Biography Index A Cumulative Index to Biographical Material in Books and Magazines. New York: Wilson, 1949- . Quarterly. CT 100 .B54x HAYDEN REF (located on Biography Table)

A valuable index to biographical material. Includes an index by profession.

Dictionary of National Biography. 22 vols. London; New York: Oxford University Press, 1885-1901. Decenniel supplements. DA 28 .D47 HAYDEN REF

The standard biographical work for Great Britain. The latest volume includes persons who died 1971-1980.

Lobies, Jean-Pierre. IBN: Index Bio-bibliographicus Notorum Hominum. Osnabruck: Biblio Verlag, 1972- . Irreg. CT 104 .L62x HAYDEN REF

An index to bio-bibliographical information in some two thousand collective works covering all periods and countries. Being issued in parts. By 1994, it had reached the end of the letter E.: use it to locate information on the most obscure individuals as well as the better known.

Michaud, J. F. Biographie Universelle Ancienne et Moderne. 82 vols. Paris: Michaud freres, 1811-62. CT 143 .M5 HAYDEN STACKS The most important of the large dictionaries of universal biography. Still useful because of the high standard of scholarship.

PRIMARY SOURCES ON THE INTERNET

Eurodocs: Primary Historical Documents from Western Europe
This site (Harold Lee Library, Brigham Young University) has pointers to documents and full text works for Medieval and Renaissance Europe, Europe as a region, and over twenty countries.The time frame is Medieval to the present. The section for Germany includes a broad range of documents, from the 95 theses of Martin Luther to the 1990 treaty on German unification and some contemporary reports on racism.

Documents in Military History
This collection is currently focused primarily on European military history before the twentieth century, although it intends to incorporate the American and non-Western military heritages, as well as contemporary warfare. 

Cold War International History Project   "The Cold War International History Project features new evidence from Central and Eastern European archives of the Cold War in Asia.   The CWIHP Bulletin contains recently released and translated documents from former Communist-world archives, along with brief introductions by leading Cold War historians and archivists.

The History of the European Union  "This website presents the chronology of important accomplishments of the EU and its institutions. Topics include: Robert Schuman's declaration of 1950 to the first enlargement waves in the 70's and the 80's, the establishment of the Single Market in 1993 to the introduction of the euro notes and coins on January 1st, 2002, and the opening of enlargement negotiations with the countries of Eastern and Central Europe."

History of European Integration Site  "On this site you will find all sorts of information regarding the history of the European Union.  The historical documents section contains nearly all information about European Integration from 1945 - present available online. It includes (references to) a unique collection of audiovisual fragments, documents, pictures and a dedicated collection about specific themes."

Internet Modern History Sourcebook
Presents a diversity of texts available on the Internet in the area of modern European history and modern Western Civilization. Designed to serve the interests of teachers and students in college survey courses in subjects such as: the Reformation; Ancien Regime; Industrial Revolution; American and French Revolutions; Scientific Revolution and Enlightenment; The World Since 1945.

The Medici Archive Project  "The Medici Archive Project aims to provide worldwide public access to the historical data in the Medici Granducal Archive by way of a fully searchable on-line database. The Project hopes to place many of its almost three million letters online. The creators have already begun placing select holdings online, some supported by an essay explaining the importance of the document. A valuable site for researchers."

Victoria Research Web   " The Victoria Research Web is a wide-ranging guide to research for students, teachers, and scholars pursuing interests in 19th-Century British history and culture.   The archives feature over nine years' worth of scholarly discussion by Victorianists around the world, while other features include a portal to dozens of reviews of books of 19th-century interest and tips for planning a trip to Britain."
