Karl S. Seitz

GIS from the Ground Up:
Development and Implementation of a
GIS for the Town of Queen Creek, Arizona

ABSTRACT

Geographic Information Systems have already found their niche within a broad range of application domains pertaining to both the private and public sectors. Municipalities are beginning to discover the potential value of GIS as a total data management system that fosters an efficient decision-making process. The town of Queen Creek, Arizona is one such municipality recognizing the advantage to approaching total systems management through GIS in the face of rapid growth. As a result, the town council has voted to incorporate a GIS approach into the town plan. This paper (presentation) will outline the cooperative effort between ASU and Queen Creek for implementing a functional GIS for the town. Beginning with a year-long pilot phase grant, ASU has developed a base-level GIS with town data, executed training for town staff, and is now in the process of implementing the turn-over of the GIS to Queen Creek while continuing necessary support.
