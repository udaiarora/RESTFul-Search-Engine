February 11, 2003

ASU conference examines 'Ethics When Cultures Clash'

Ethics, politics, religion and cultural clashes - some of today's thorniest topics - will be on the agenda at a Feb. 27 - 28 conference, "Ethics When Cultures Clash."

Sponsored by ASU's Lincoln Center for Applied Ethics, the conference will feature panelists and speakers from across the United States. It is free to the public on a first-come, first-served basis, although guaranteed seating is available for a small reservation fee by Feb. 12.

Sessions will be held 9 a.m. - 3:30 p.m. each day in Old Main, the University Club and in the KAET television studios.

The first morning's sessions include "Do Religion and Universal Moral Values Mix?" with David Little, director of the Harvard Center for Religion and a leading scholar on religion and human rights, and Richard Wentz, ASU professor emeritus of religious studies whose books include "Why People Do Bad Things in the Name of Religion." The two will discuss whether religion promotes or complicates achieving common moral values.

Other sessions include "Our War/Just War?/Iraq War," with representatives from Rutgers and Emory universities, the U.S. Naval Academy and the Rand Corp.; and "Flying from Fear: Ethics, Politics, Asylees, Refugees and Culture Collision," with panelists from Sierra Leone, Burundi and Sudan.

Keynote presentation at 11:30 a.m., Feb. 27, will be a talk by producer-director Christopher McLeod and a screening of his award-winning documentary, "In the Light of Reverence."

Afternoon sessions center on the clash of values on university campuses, the Bush doctrine of peace and the rule of law when cultures clash. The following day's sessions focus on ethics at the border, the cultural impact of globalization, indigenous peoples and environmental conflict, terrorism and the Global Sullivan Principles in corporate culture.

For information, program listings or registration, call (480) 727-7691 or visit the Web at (www.asu.edu/clas/lincolncenter).

Auffret, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-6991 or (sauffret@asu.edu).
