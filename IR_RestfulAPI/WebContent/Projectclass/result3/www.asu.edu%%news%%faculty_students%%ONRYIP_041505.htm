April 15, 2005

Standout Fulton researchers tackle prestigious Navy projects

Two Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering faculty members, Raja Ayyanar and Junshan Zhang, have been named recipients of a prestigious Office of Naval Research Young Investigator Program (ONR YIP) award, given to outstanding new faculty to support naval research.

The projects both professors are working on will further understanding of naval research in priority areas that include electrical power systems and wireless sensor networks.

Ayyanar’s proposal will help the Navy reach its goal of producing an all-electrical propulsion system by streamlining the design process for high-voltage power converters. Current systems are limited by a variety of designs, and Ayyanar’s approach offers a new intelligent control design that is adaptable and reconfigurable to many different megawatt power loads.

Zhang’s project is wide-ranging, focusing on developing an energy-efficient cross-layer optimization framework for ad-hoc/sensor networks to enhance naval capabilities of autonomous operations. Zhang will investigate fundamental limits and network protocol design for three network models: many-to-one, multiple-hop and wideband sensory-relay networks, to improve and optimize the energy efficiency of sensor communications and data transport.

Zhang also was the recipient of a 2003 National Science Foundation CAREER award, given to young faculty deemed to be among the most promising new researchers in the nation.

The ONR YIP program is extremely competitive, with less than 10 percent of all proposals resulting in YIP awards. Nationwide, there were 24 awards given for the fiscal year 2005 to eligible recipients who have received a doctorate after Nov. 1, 1999.

Not only does the significance of the award speak volumes about the talent of young faculty at the Fulton School – ASU was one of four universities in the nation that had dual award winners – but it also showcases the Department of Electrical Engineering, since it was the only department in the nation to have two winners.

“The EE department is very proud of this outstanding accomplishment by Junshan and Raja,” says EE chair Steve Goodnick. “It’s phenomenal that both occurred in the same year.”

Selection criteria were based on factors that included past performance, the strength and creativity of the individual proposal, as well as strong support from the applicant’s institution.

The awards can provide a maximum support of up to $100,000 per year for three years, with the possibility of additional support for scientific equipment or collaborative research with a Navy laboratory.
