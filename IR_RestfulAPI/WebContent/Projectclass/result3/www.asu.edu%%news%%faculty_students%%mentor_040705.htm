ASU students mentor children throughout Valley

From south Phoenix to west Mesa, at least 300 ASU students each semester provide tutoring and mentoring to children throughout the Valley.

They travel several times a week to 19 different locations, to give math and reading help, science and art enrichment, and homework tutoring to youngsters in struggling communities.

As part of ASU’s Academic Community Engagement Services (ACES) program, ASU students provided more than 17,000 hours last semester to help children learn. Over the last 11 years, 5,600 ASU students have worked with more than 58,000 youngsters. They have given 260,000 hours of service, many of them in credit-bearing service learning internships.

One of the newest partnerships is about a mile from the Tempe campus, at Gilliland Middle School, where students perform after-school tutoring three times a week. It’s a school with high absenteeism and family mobility, where two-thirds of children qualify for free and reduced lunch, and test scores are well below average.

The project is part of a Title V incentive grant for juvenile delinquency prevention, given to the City of Tempe and administered by the Governor’s Office for Children, Youth and Families. The two-year grant pays for a graduate student supervisor.

The ASU students encourage and motivate, working with structured lesson plans and with the children’s teachers. They write about their experiences, and invariably they say they learn more from the children than vice versa. Many develop an understanding of social justice issues and acquire a life-long commitment to volunteerism.

The ACES program, now a part of ASU’s University College, was developed to enhance ASU students’ academic skills through community engagement, while at the same time increasing the chances of success for at-risk children.

Students teach plant biology through gardening, devise science experiments, plan field trips and even teach English as a second language. They also organize after-school clubs for space science, garden science and creative writing.
