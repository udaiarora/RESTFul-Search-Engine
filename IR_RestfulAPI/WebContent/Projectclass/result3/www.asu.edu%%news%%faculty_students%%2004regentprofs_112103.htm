November 21, 2003

Arizona Board of Regents Designates 8 New Regents Professors

The Arizona Board of Regents has approved the appointment of Charles Arntzen, Ronald Carlson, Philip Christensen, Gene Glass, Luis Gomez-Mejia, Stephen Pyne, Irwin Sandler and Mary Lee Smith as Regents Professors, to take effect on Aug. 15, 2004. The action took place Nov. 21 at the board meeting at the University of Arizona.

This is the highest number of Regents Professors to be approved at one time since the selection process was converted to a two-year cycle in 1993 - 94, bringing to 46 the number of ASU faculty accorded the honor since 1988. Jeffrey Coles, chairman of the nominating committee, says this is one of the highest honors the university can bestow on faculty members.

"This prestigious honor includes an increase of $5,000 to the faculty member's base salary as well as an annual grant of $5,000 to support their scholarly endeavors," says Coles, finance professor and the Francis J. and Mary B. Labriola Endowed Chair in Competitive Business in the W. P. Carey School of Business. "In addition to their regular teaching and research activities, Regents Professors may be called on to serve the state's universities by lecturing, consulting on curriculum and research, advising administrative officers and performing other similar activities."

Nominations for Regents Professorships are made by faculty members and are submitted to a nominating committee by Oct. 1 of the academic year preceding appointment. The nominating committee consists of nine to 12 tenured faculty members, appointed by the university president, who are noted for their scholarly achievements and represent a variety of academic disciplines. The nominating committee makes its recommendations to the president, who approves the final selections.

"The committee was impressed with the accomplishments and qualities of the entire set of nominees," Coles says. "While this made the committee's deliberations more difficult and lengthy, it is evidence of the outstanding quality of faculty across the campuses of this university."

This year's newly appointed Regents Professors are:

· Charles Arntzen was appointed the Florence Ely Nelson Presidential Endowed Chair at ASU in 2000 and also served as the founding director of the Arizona BioDesign Institute. Arntzen, a professor with the School of Life Sciences, has led ASU's research efforts in plant molecular biology and protein engineering.

· Ronald Carlson has been a professor in ASU's English department since 1986. In 2000, Carlson was the only writer in America with stories in all three major literary anthologies of the year: "The Best American Short Stories," "The O'Henry Prize Stories" and "The Pushcart Anthology."

· Philip Christensen is Korrick Professor of Geological Sciences and has been pioneering ASU's exploration of Mars. Christensen is the principal investigator for Mars Global Surveyor Thermal Emission Spectrometer (TES), Mars 2001 Odyssey Orbiter Thermal Emission Imaging System (THEMIS) and co-investigator on the Mars Exploration Rover missions Athena Rovers, providing the Mini-TES instrument.

· Gene Glass is a professor in the Division of Psychology in Education and Educational Leadership and Policy Studies, in ASU's College of Education. Glass is responsible for pioneering work in the field of quantitative research methods with the publication of "Design & Analysis of Timeseries Experiments," in 1975. He has published more than 200 literary works including books, chapters, reviews and articles.

· Luis Gomez-Mejia is the Horace Steele Arizona Heritage Chair in the department of management in the W.P. Carey School of Business at ASU. He is one of the leading scholars internationally in the field of management and has published more than 115 scholarly works. He is on the Dean's Council of 100 Distinguished Scholars, and he founded and is president of the Iberoamerican Management Association. The Minnesota Board of Regents has awarded him the Distinguished Achievement Award from the University of Minnesota.

· Stephen Pyne is a professor in the ASU School of Life Sciences and a respected authority on the cultural history of fire and fire management. Pyne has received numerous fellowships including a MacArthur Fellowship, a Fulbright Fellowship and two NEH Fellowships, and has twice held fellowships at the National Humanities Center.

· Irwin Sandler is professor of psychology and the director of the Program for Prevention Research in the ASU College of Liberal Arts & Sciences. Sandler's expertise has been sought by prevention researchers worldwide including the National Institute on Drug Abuse. He was also a consultant to the New York City Fire Department following the terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001.

· Mary Lee Smith is a professor in the Division of Educational Leadership and Policy Studies in the ASU College of Education. Smith has made major contributions to the development and refinement of meta-analysis by adding such nuances as examining the relationships between effect size and the quality of methodology and source of publication.
