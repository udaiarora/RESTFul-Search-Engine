Medieval History

INTRODUCTION

Although there is no single place to look for books and articles on medieval history, the library has made available a number of online indexes, bibliographies, internet sites and other reference works which will help you in researching a topic in this field. A selection of the most useful of these is described in this Subject Guide.

ONLINE INDEXES

Arts and Humanities Citation Index    (Web of Science)
(1975+) Provides access to current bibliographic information and cited references. Covers more than 1,150 of the world's leading arts and humanities journals in a broad range of disciplines.

ATLA Religion Database + ATLAS    (ATLAS Full Text Plus 1949-)
(1949+) American Theological Association Database covering such topics as Biblical studies, world religions, Church history and religious perspectives on social issues. Includes citations from journals, essays, and book reviews.

British Humanities Index (BHI): 1962-Current
The database is an online index to (1962+) humanities journals and weekly magazines published in Great Britain and other English speaking nations including newspapers in UK

Historical Abstracts
(1954+) Covers the world's scholarly literature in history. Includes article abstracts and bibliographic citations of books and dissertations on the history of the world from 1450 to the present, except for the United States and Canada

Humanities Full Text    (Wilson Humanities Abstracts Full Text)
(2/1984+) Indexes articles in the fields of archaeology and classical studies, film, folklore, gender studies, history, journalism, communications, language, literature, literary and political criticism, performing arts, philosophy, religion, etc

International Medieval Bibliography Online
(1967+) Index to journal articles and miscellaneous publications (conference proceedings, essay collections) on the Middle Ages published worldwide starting in 1967. Includes citations on over 300,000 articles on the Middle Ages in Europe, the Middle East and North Africa.

Iter: Gateway to the Middle Ages and Renaissance
A newly incorporated, non-profit research project to increase access to all published materials pertaining to the Renaissance (1300-1700) and, eventually, to the Middle Ages (400-1500), by creating online bibliographies.

JSTOR    (Electronic Journals Collection)
Includes long runs of backfiles of scholarly journals. Subjects covered include Anthropology, Asian Studies, Ecology, Economics, Education, Finance, History, Mathematics, Philosophy, Political Science, Population Studies, and Sociology

BOOKS

Books in the ASU Libraries may be located by using the ASU Libraries Online Catalog. Check the Library of Congress Subject Headings for proper subject terms. For example, Medieval History is searched by "Middle Ages--History" while many aspects of the Medieval period are searched by the specific topic modified by "Medieval"; for example, "Manuscripts, Medieval" and "Ships, Medieval."

SUMMARIES OF INFORMATION

Dahmus J. Dictionary of Medieval Civilization. New York: Macmillan, 1984.
CB 351 .D24 1984 REF
Brief entries containing information on people, events, books, monasteries, universities and cities. Includes the entire Middle Ages.

Dictionary of the Middle Ages . 12 vols. New York: Scribner, 1982-1989.
D 114 .D5 1982 REF
An encyclopedic work with major articles of approximately 10,000 words and brief definitions and identifications. Bibliographies refer the reader to both English and foreign language works. Scope includes the Latin West, the Slavic world, Asia minor, and the Muslim-Christian world of North Africa.

Langer, William L. Encyclopedia of World History: Ancient, Medieval, and Modern. Boston: Houghton Mifflin, 1972.
D 21 .L27 REF
A handbook of historical information arranged chronologically with an index to specific facts. Contains maps and genealogical charts for royal families. Serves as a summary or outline of world history.

STANDARD MEDIEVAL BIBLIOGRAPHIES

Boyce, Gray C. Literature of Medieval History, 1930-1975. 5 vols. Millwood, NY: Kraus International, 1981.
D 117 .P322x REF
This exhaustive guide for advanced students supplements Paetow's Guide to the Study of Medieval History (see entry #4 in this section).

Conference on British Studies. Bibliographical Handbooks. (series) London: Cambridge University Press [dates vary]
  These volumes are designed to serve as basic, convenient reference guides for advanced students.

  • Anglo-Norman England, 1066-1154. 1969.
    Z 2017 .A43 HAYDEN STKS
    High Middle Ages, 1154-1377. 1978.
    DA 205 .W5x HAYDEN STKS
    Late-Medieval England, 1377-1485. 1976.
    Z 2017 .G87 HAYDEN STKS
Crosby, Everett U. Medieval Studies: A Bibliographical Guide. New York: Garland, 1983.
CB 351 .C7x 1983 REF
An annotated guide to the major collections of sources and secondary literature on the history and culture of the West European middle ages, Byzantium, and medieval Islamic civilization.

Graves, Edgar B. Bibliography of English History to 1485. Oxford: Clarendon, 1975.
DA 130 .B5 REF
The standard bibliographical guide for medieval English history, a revision of the older work by Charles Gross. A systematic and critical bibliography of both primary and secondary works.

Paetow, L. J. Guide to the Study of Medieval History. Millwood, NY: Kraus Reprint, 1980.
D 117 .P32x 1980 REF
Note that the Paetow guide is essentially the 1931 edition, newly reissued in 1980 with errata.

Rouse, Richard H. Serial Bibliographies for Medieval Studies. Berkeley: University of California Press, 1969.
Z 6203 .R66 STKS
Describes 280 continuing bibliographies which pertain wholly or in part to medieval studies.

Van Caenegem, R. C. Guide to the Sources of Medieval History. New York: North-Holland, 1978.
D 117 .C2213 REF
Contains essays on medieval historiography and descriptions of collections, reference works, periodicals and important series.

INTERNET SITES RELATING TO MEDIEVAL HISTORY

These are a few sites selected from the large (and growing) number now available. Most of them will lead you to still more sources of information.

If you have questions concerning these sources, please contact Ed Oetting at edding@asu.edu

Eurodocs: Primary Historical Documents from Western Europe
This site (Harold Lee Library, Brigham Young University) has pointers to documents and full text works for Medieval and Renaissance Europe, Europe as a region, and over twenty countries.The time frame is Medieval to the present. The section for Germany includes a broad range of documents, from the 95 theses of Martin Luther to the 1990 treaty on German unification and some contemporary reports on racism.

Internet Medieval Sourcebook
Provides links to the full text of primary sources in medieval history

The Labyrinth: A World Wide Web Server for Medieval Studies
The Labyrinth is a global information network providing free access to electronic sources in medieval studies through a WWW server at Georgetown University. It provides connections to databases, services and electronic texts and images, as well as bibliographies and directories of medieval studies organizations.

ACMRS (Arizona Center for Medieval and Renaissance Studies)
Contains complete information on all ACMRS programs and activities.
