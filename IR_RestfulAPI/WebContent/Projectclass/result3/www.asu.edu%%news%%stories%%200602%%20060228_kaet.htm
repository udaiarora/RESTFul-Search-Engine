February 28, 2006

KAET-TV unveils new station identity

KAET, Arizona's award-winning public television station, has announced the launch of a new station identity - Eight - providing a more flexible, all-encompassing reference to the media outlet's content.

"The new identity will allow us to better represent all the ways in which we serve the state - our television broadcasts, analog, digital and high-definition; the online components on azpbs.org; KAET Magazine; and our education and community outreach initiatives," says the station's general manager, Greg Giczi. "The timing was right, and we were fortunate to be able to use the talents of one of the best names in the media branding business: John Ridgway."

The new logo comprises the word "eight," spelled out, with the number "8" replacing the letter "g." The word "channel" is eliminated, with the call letters KAET taking a less significant role. Different design variations will accommodate specific content areas, such as childrens' or public affairs programming.

Historically, station call letters and channel numbers were used to direct audiences to a specific location on the television dial. Both identifiers are becoming obsolete, as cable and satellite systems have made the references less of a recognizable factor. "Our audiences have many ways of accessing our content," Giczi says. "It is no longer limited to sitting in front of a TV screen."

Podcasts, RSS feeds (updated content sent to computers via the Internet), and video online offer access to station content on demand. The station's education outreach initiatives reach tens of thousands of Arizona students in the classroom - and assist thousands of teachers with professional development online.

After an initial meeting with Giczi, John Ridgway, a former station employee, ASU alumnus and now the chief executive officer of Via Worldwide, expressed an interest in helping with the rebranding.

"Coming back to the station, offering my talents and insights for their new direction, I've come full circle," he says. "KAET gave me my first opportunity to work in television. From that job came a position at the Los Angeles PBS station and opportunities to work with "Entertainment Tonight," the "CBS Evening News," "NBC Nightly News," "Dateline," CNBC, ESPN, and networks in Europe, Asia, the Middle East and Africa."

It is exciting and gratifying to be part of the process of creating a new identity for KAET, he says.

"As Eight continues to adapt to new technologies and delivery systems, our audiences can continue to look to us for quality content as they have for the past 45 years," Giczi says.

Eight specializes in the education of children, in-depth news and public affairs, lifelong learning, and the celebration of arts and culture, using the power of noncommercial television, the Internet, educational outreach services and community-based initiatives. More than 80 percent of Arizonans receive the signal through a network of translators, cable and satellite systems. With more than 1.7 million viewers each week, Eight consistently ranks among the most-viewed public television stations per capita in the country.
