Preserving Authentic Electronic Records
A Report on the InterPARES Project

US-InterPARES Project
Philip B. Eppard
ECURE 2001

1

Purpose of the InterPARES Project

  • To develop the theoretical and methodological knowledge essential for the permanent preservation of records generated electronically.
  • To formulate model policies, strategies, and standards capable of ensuring their preservation.
US-InterPARES Project

2

Previous UBC Project

Born out of previous UBC research

  • “The Preservation of the Integrity of Electronic Records” (“The UBC Project”).
  • Defined the requirements for creating, handling and preserving reliable and authentic electronic records in active recordkeeping systems.

With U.S. Department of Defense

  • Developed DoD Records Management Standard 5015.2.

Authenticity Findings

  • Authenticity often expressed implicitly, rather than explicitly.
  • Medium is not significant in assessing authenticity of records.
  • Records are inextricably embedded in their contexts.

  • Identification of digital components of electronic records
  • Systematic & dynamic controls for preservation

  • ‘Original’ electronic records vs. copies of electronic records
  • Preservation means reproduction
