Demographic, Economic & Statistical Information

This page is for finding sources that provide primary data and statistics on population and economics. Also included are industry statistic not covered in the Company & Industry Research - Industry Information Sources - Overview Sources. Few of the sources listed provide any detailed analysis. If you don't find the statistical sources you need in this listing, try also the Consumers and Market Data.

  1. Almanac of the 50 States. Burlington, VT: Information Publications, 1985- . Annual. (ASU owns 1985- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
      HA 203 .A5 HAYDEN REFERENCE
      HA 203 .A5 WEST REFERENCE

    This handy one volume source is a compilation of many different statistics collected by the U.S. government agencies and various business sources. Organized by state, each entry covers basic geographic and demographic data and statistics in the following areas: "Geography and Environment; Demographics & Characteristics of the Population; Vital Statistics & Health; Education; Social Insurance & Welfare Programs; Housing & Construction; Governments & Elections; Governmental Finance; Crime, Law Enforcement & Courts; Labor & Income; Economy, Business, Industry & Agriculture; and Communication, Energy & Transportation."

  2. American Demographics. [Ithaca, N.Y., American Demographics Inc.], 1979- . Monthly. (ASU owns 1979- .)

      K1 .M5935 LAW PERIODICALS
      HB 881 .A54x WEST JOURNALS
      http://demographics.com/publications/ad/index.htm

    This journal has always been a good source for demographic statistics with plenty of charts, graphs, and tables. The web site is an index to the journal that will help locate the needed statistics faster than using other indexes.

  4. Demographics USA. County ed. [New York]: S&MM Magazine, Sales & Marketing Management. 1976- . Annual. (ASU owns 1989- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
      HF 5415.1 .D46x HAYDEN REFERENCE

    Published by Sales & Marketing Management magazine and formerly called Survey of Buying Power (HC 106.7 .S8x HAYDEN JOURNALS), this service gives income levels; buying power; retail sales, by 12 store groups and 10 merchandise lines; age, sex, and household demographics; market growth; projections; and much more information for every county, metropolitan area, and television market in the country. Divided into 21 sections: sections 1-7 give demographic and economic data; sections 8-12, state and regional summaries, rankings, and totals in terms of retail trade; and sections 18-21, rankings and projections, and breakdowns of population, buying power, and retail sales in TV markets. Numerous tabs aid in locating specific sections. The same information is published in four parts in issues of Sales & Marketing Magazine.

  5. Development Report Card for the States. Washington, D.C.: Corporation for Enterprise Development, 1987- . Annual. (ASU owns 1987-.)

      HC 106.8 .D48x WEST REFERENCE
      HA 214 .S788x FICHE WEST JOURNALS (SRI #R4225-1)

    This book reports on the economic health of each of the 50 states over the past year. While essentially a tool for economic development and facilities location decisions, this is useful to marketers to determine if potential customers are likely to be in a given state.

  8. Editor and Publisher Market Guide. New York: Editor & Publisher, 1927- . Annual. (ASU owns 1964- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
      HF 5905 .E38 HAYDEN REFERENCE
      HF 5905 .E38 WEST REFERENCE

    Analyzes over 1,500 U.S. and Canadian communities where a daily newspaper is published. Marketing data given includes location, principal industries, climate, retail outlets, and daily newspaper circulation. Also gives market rankings of U.S. cities, future projections for a variety of data, and figures on population growth, disposable personal income, and retail sales by type of product.

  11. REPS: Regional Economic Projections Series. Washington: National Planning Association, 1962- . Annual. (ASU owns 1968, 1970/71, 1984- . Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
      HC 101 .N38x HAYDEN REFERENCE

    This valuable work has three volumes to the set: "Analysis of Growth Trends," "Employment and Income Detail," and "Households and Population, by Age, Sex and Race." Each volume contains related information for the nation, regions, states, and metropolitan statistical areas. The growth trends also include analysis for economic areas and counties. The statistics are given for the current projection year, with historical statistics given at decade intervals for three decades and projections at five year intervals for the next fifteen or so years.

  12. Sourcebook of Zip Code Demographics. Arlington, VA: CACI Marketing Systems, 1984- . Annual. (ASU owns 1985, 1989, 1990, 1995, 1997. Latest year in Reference; earlier years in Journals.)
      HA 203 .S66 HAYDEN REFERENCE
      HA 203 .S66 WEST REFERENCE

    This hefty volume gives detailed demographic information on every ZIP code, as the title indicates. Data given include: age distribution, median age, race, population, income data, and calculated "spending potential indexes" for each area. The second section is part of the ACORN report showing the top consumer type of each Zip Code. The definition of these consumer types is in Appendix 1: ACORN User's Guide. The third part is a Zip Code list showing the number of firms and total employees and the top industry. The Census Edition also includes some employment and housing information.

  13. Statistical Abstract of the United States. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Department of Commerce, Bureau of the Census, 1878- . Annual. (ASU owns 1894- . Latest year in Reference; all years in US DOCUMENTS.)

    A handy compilation of statistics covering most areas of the U.S. economy, including demographics, labor, business enterprises, transportation, manufacturers, and communications. Sources for the tables are given if more up-to-date or related information is needed. Some tables have historical series. This item is included fulltext in the Statistical Universe.

  15. Trends & Projections. New York: Standard & Poor's. Monthly. (ASU owns latest 2 years.)
      HC 106.6 .S75x HAYDEN REFERENCE (Located on Table 9B)

    This statistical source, which is a supplement to the Standard & Poor's Industry Surveys, has well-written analyses of the economic and demographic conditions of the United States. Each section first describes the recent trends and then includes a forecast. It also has graphs and tables of leading economic indicators.

  16. Vital Signs. New York: W. W. Norton, 1992- . Annual. (ASU owns 1992- . Latest edition in Reference; earlier editions in Journals.)
      HC 10 .V53 HAYDEN REFERENCE

    Covers trends in the major industries and other issues of economic importance. The work is divided into two sections: Key Indicators and Special Features. Each trend topic has a page of written analysis and a page of statistics.
