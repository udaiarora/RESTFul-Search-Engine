G.1.2: Supplementary Evaluation

Effective 5/16/90 .. Revised 2/1/94

PURPOSE

The purpose of a supplementary evaluation is to provide the academic professional and the primary evaluator with an additional assessment of the academic professional's job performance, professional development and contribution, or service. There are two three situations when a supplementary evaluation may occur:

Supplementary evaluations are required for all probationary academic professionals who have library instruction and/or collection development responsibilities. Academic professionals with who have continuing appointment who have collection development responsibilities are required to have written supplementary evaluations every three years, although collection development goals need to be approved annually by the Collection Development Coordinator.

Academic professionals who work in more than one department may have a supplementary evaluation. This evaluation is not always necessary. The evaluation must be part of an initial agreement between the department heads and the academic professional.

An academic professional or primary evaluator may request a supplementary evaluation from any person who can provide additional information to the primary evaluator regarding job performance, professional development and contribution, or service.

It is the responsibility of a supplementary evaluator to provide a constructive assessment of the academic professional's performance in areas under the evaluator's coordination or direction. When appropriate, a supplementary evaluator may evaluate the academic professional's professional development and contribution or service efforts relevant to the evaluator's area of responsibility.

The supplementary evaluation process follows the Schedule of Personnel Actions issued by the Assistant Dean for Personnel.

LIBRARY INSTRUCTION AND COLLECTION DEVELOPMENT SUPPLEMENTARY EVALUATIONS

  1. By the second Friday in February, Tthe primary evaluator solicits the supplementary evaluations from the Head of Instructional Services and the appropriate Collection Development Coordinator for academic professionals with collection development responsibilities. Written evaluations are solicited for all probationary academic professionals and for just those academic professionals with continuing appointment who have not had a written supplementary evaluation in the last two years. It is the mutual responsibility of the primary evaluator and the academic professional to determine if this is the year when supplementary evaluations are required. The Assistant Dean for Personnel will keep track of who requires a supplementary evaluation each year and communicate that information in the written reminder sent to academic professionals in early January concerning annual evaluation submissions.

  2. By the second Friday in February, Tthe academic professional furnishes the supplementary evaluator with a copy of the collection development section of the self-evaluation and goals statement or the appropriate sections. This is done even if a written supplementary evaluation is not necessary that year.

  3. The supplementary evaluator evaluates the submitted materials and approves the new goals. If the goals need to be changed or negotiated in any way, the evaluator will request a meeting with the academic professional to resolve any differences.

  4. The supplementary evaluator notifies both the academic professional and the primary evaluator when the goals have been approved. This is done even if a written evaluation is not necessary.

  5. The supplementary evaluator writes an evaluation if one was solicited (probationary academic professionals and every third year for academic professionals with continuing appointment). The evaluation is sent to both the primary evaluator and the academic professional by the first working day in March.

  6. After all supplementary evaluations have been received, the primary evaluator reviews the academic professional's performance, following the "Procedures for Annual Performance Evaluations" in Librarians' Handbook, G.1.1.

  7. Supplementary evaluations are included in the annual evaluation packet.

OTHER SUPPLEMENTARY EVALUATIONS

  1. The aAcademic professionals desiring a supplementary evaluation of any area of their performance should submits names of appropriate supplementary evaluators to the primary evaluator by the first working day in February.

  2. Academic professionals should send the appropriate section(s) of the self-review and goal statement to the supplementary evaluator by the second Friday in February.

  3. Also by the second Friday in February, Tthe primary evaluator solicits the supplementary evaluations, clearly specifying what the evaluator is being asked to evaluate.

  4. The supplementary evaluator writes the evaluation, sends it to the primary evaluator, and sends a copy to the academic professional by the first working day in March.

  5. After all supplementary evaluations have been received, the primary evaluator reviews the academic professional's performance, following the "Procedures for Annual Performance Evaluations" in Librarians' Handbook, G.1.1.

  6. Supplementary evaluations are included in the annual evaluation packet.
