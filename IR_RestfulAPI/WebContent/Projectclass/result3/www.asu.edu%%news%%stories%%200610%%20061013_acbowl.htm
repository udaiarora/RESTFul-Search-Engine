October 13, 2006

ASU kicks off Academic Bowl

Four teams advance to second round

The 2006 ASU Academic Bowl kicked off in grand fashion Oct. 12, with head-to-head competition between eight college teams.

Students answered a variety of questions Oct. 12, during the opening round of the ASU Academic Bowl.

The Group A first-round matches, in front of a cheering audience in the Memorial Union’s Pima Room on the Tempe campus, included eight of the 16 teams in the event. The Group B first-round matches, with the other eight squads, will take place Oct. 19.

The competition, sponsored by the offices of Public Affairs and Student Affairs, is modeled after the national College Bowl. It features four-member teams squaring off in a question-and-answer format on a wide range of subjects, including current events, history, science, sports and culture.

ASU Vice President for Public Affairs Virgil Renzulli says the students took the matches very seriously and were impressive in their display of knowledge.

“There were some funny moments when questions dealt with TV shows that aired long before the contestants were born,” he says. “But, generally, observers were impressed with the knowledge all the teams demonstrated. There were no poor teams – only good teams and better teams.”

The first round opened with the Herberger College of Fine Arts, with students Mathew Wadsworth, Robin Bariel, Stephen Cummings and Tim Witucky, taking on the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, with students Karl Sturm, Amy Teegarden, Andrew Holle and Marko Manojlovic. Fulton took the match, 270-75

From left, Benjamin Goodrich, Robert Gaytan and Lamarcus Boyd of the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences confer on an answer as their team beat East College 120 to 95 in the closest match of the night.

The second match of the night featured University College, with students Kristina Woodbury, Travis Henry, Tara Egnatios and Vince Rollins, facing off against the College of Science and Technology, with students Joe Martin, Sarah Roux and Adam Lessell. University College took the match, 385-60.

The third match pitted the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences, with students Benjamin Goodrich, Robert Gaytan III, Michele Lefevre and Lamarcus Boyd, against East College, with students Elvie Krieger, Carrie Durward, Brian McMullin and John Palino. In a seesaw contest, New College came out ahead, 120-95.

The final match of the evening featured the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Team A, with students Eric Cox, Chris Ray, Carlos Ross and McKay Jones, against the College of Teacher Education and Leadership, with Michael Varela, Daniel Goman, Melissa Porter and Nicole Piemonte. CLAS came away victorious, 340-140.

The Group B matches will take place in the Pima Room of the MU beginning at 7 p.m., Oct. 19.

The matchups include:

The Herberger team had a cheering section during its first round match with the Fulton School. Tawny Kerr holds the sign to cheer on her team.

• 7 p.m. – Match 5: School of Global Management and Leadership versus Mary Lou Fulton College of Education.

• 7:40 p.m. – Match 6: College of Human Services versus Morrison School of Management and Agribusiness.

• 8:20 p.m. – Match 7: College of Design versus the W. P. Carey School of Business.

• 9 p.m. – Match 8: College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Team B versus Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communications.

A complete listing of the teams involved, as well as the dates for the Academic Bowl competition, can be found online at (www.asu.edu/news/academicbowl/about.html).
