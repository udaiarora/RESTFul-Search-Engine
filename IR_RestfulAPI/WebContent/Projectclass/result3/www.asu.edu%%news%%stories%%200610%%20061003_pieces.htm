October 3, 2006

New schools at ASU ? Putting the pieces in place

Over the last few years, ASU has made major strides putting the pieces in place to realize the goal of becoming a New American University . A key part of that development has been the formation of new schools that foster teaching and research across traditional boundaries. These new schools are leveraging the university's intellectual capital and making the university stronger as a whole. Here is a brief look at schools that have been approved by the Arizona Board of Regents and launched at ASU.


School of Community Resources & Development
Established: 2006
Director: Randy J. Virden
College: College of Public Programs

The School of Community Resources & Development targets the social, economic, environmental and cultural well-being of local and global communities through instruction, research and service. The school, including the former Department of Recreation Management and Tourism, provides nationally recognized interdisciplinary research expertise and innovative academic programs in nonprofit leadership and management, parks and recreation management, tourism development and management. The highly regarded Center for Nonprofit Leadership and Management is a critical component of the school.

School of Computing and Informatics
Established: 2006
Director: Sethuraman “Panch” Panchanathan
College: Fulton School of Engineering

The School of Computing and Informatics (SCI) is charting a bold course advancing the frontiers of computing and informatics – areas that will have a tremendous impact on almost every aspect of society. SCI incorporates the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, and the new Department of Biomedical Informatics in collaboration with the University of Arizona College of Medicine – Phoenix . In addition, the school will develop new transdisciplinary computing and informatics research, and academic programs (such as cognitive informatics, sustainability informatics, ecoinformatics, geoinformatics, social informatics, business informatics and policy informatics) through collaboration with entities inside and outside ASU.

School of Criminology & Criminal Justice
Established: 2006
Director: Scott Decker
College: College of Human Services

At ASU, criminology and criminal justice is a broad discipline, encompassing the scientific study of crime, criminals, the lawmaking process, the criminal justice system and the treatment of offenders. The School of Criminology & Criminal Justice, an expansion of the nationally ranked department of Criminal Justice and Criminology, draws upon many different disciplines, including sociology, psychology, political science, economics, history, mathematics and law. The school enjoys collaborative relationships with several academic units within ASU, including the School of Social Work , the School of Public Affairs , and the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences.

School of Earth and Space Exploration
Established : 2006
Director: Kip Hodges
College: College of Liberal Arts and Sciences

The School of Earth and Space Exploration fuses earth sciences, planetary sciences, astrophysics and engineering in an unprecedented way to enable new explorations of Earth and the universe beyond. The school's aim is to produce a new generation of explorers who are part scientist and part engineer, capable of asking the fundamental questions that lead to great discoveries and of designing the instruments to answer those questions. Important research themes include monitoring the physical, chemical and biological processes that define evolution of the Earth system – and, ultimately, its habitability. Beyond Earth, research targets range from the early evolution of the universe to the development and natural history of planets and planetary systems.

School of Educational Innovation and Teacher Preparation
Established: 2006
Director: Bette Bergeron
College: East College

The School of Educational Innovation and Teacher Preparation at ASU's Polytechnic campus in Mesa takes a decidedly hands-on approach to teacher education. In addition to the existing education degree programs at the Polytechnic campus, the school is home to new bachelor's degrees in education in early childhood education, special education and selected studies. Also, master's degrees in education in special education, and educational administration and supervision, have been added to the school's offerings. All programs have a distinctly applied focus and close partnerships with schools and districts in the surrounding communities.

School of Geographical Sciences
Established: 2006
Interim Director: Anthony Brazel
College: College of Liberal Arts and Sciences

With its transition from the former ASU geography department, the School of Geographical Sciencesis the first school of its kind in the western United States.

The reorganization is expected to enhance the flexibility of the geography program and lead to increased collaboration with other academic and research units at ASU. The school's research activities will be organized around four key themes: social and cultural dynamics of space and place; complex land-use systems; climate and environmental change; and geographic information science (GIS).

As a school, the geographical sciences program aims to become a local and regional force in problem-solving, helping to energize the economy, spearheading geographic education and literacy, and contributing to community health and well-being.

School of Global Studies
Established : 2005
Director: David Jacobson
College: College of Liberal Arts and Sciences

Combining scholars from across the sciences and humanities, the School of Global Studies aims to encourage rethinking of how people understand the world –institutionally, socially and politically – and to find ways to more effectively apply academic knowledge to address global challenges.

The school is organized around seven intersecting areas of study, with “quality of life” as the overarching theme: governance, migration, natural resources, urban systems and development, health and well-being, circulation of culture, art and intellectual property, information technologies, markets and global communications, and diffusion and intervention in violence.

In addition to the thematic areas of emphasis, the school offers regional emphasis areas, such as Asian studies, Latin-American studies, and Russian and East European studies.

School of Human Evolution & Social Change
Established: 2005
Director: Sander van der Leeuw
College: College of Liberal Arts and Sciences

ASU's highly ranked Anthropology Department steps into the 21st century as a new interdisciplinary school – the School of Human Evolution & Social Change. The more than 50 faculty – including two National Academy of Science members – specialize in sociology, economics, mathematics and geography along with the school's core focus of anthropology. The school, organized around six dynamic research themes, addresses the most compelling issues facing society through collaborative, policy-relevant research that uses theories and tools from across the sciences. Faculty and students – engaged with scholars across the globe – study the complexities of human societies over the very long term, often from their origins to the present, to identify long-term trends, more recent variations and myriad possibilities.

School of Life Sciences
Established: 2003
Director: Robert Page
College: College of Liberal Arts and Sciences

The merger of the departments of biology, microbiology and plant biology has changed life sciences at ASU and created a new and model for life sciences research and education. The School of Life Sciences has crafted a learning environment that promotes a wide range of skills for undergraduate and graduate students and a platform to engage with the community in education and public policy arenas. The structure allows the school, in collaboration with on- and off-campus partners, to become an international leader in variety of multidisciplinary fields, including urban ecology, neuroscience, cellular and molecular biology, genomics and proteomics, social complexity, biogeochemistry, bioethics,emerging infectious disease, sustainability science and biotechnology.

School of Materials
Established: 2006
Director: Subhash Mahajan
College: College of Liberal Arts and Sciences and Fulton School of Engineering

ASU's drive to accelerate its advance to the forefront of technology innovation is taking a major stride with the creation of the School of Materials. Studies in the science and engineering of metals, ceramics, polymers, composites and functional materials will provide students the knowledge required for a broad array of pursuits in the field. Students will be trained in the materials aspects of microelectronics, nanotechnology and other technologies critical to biomedical, communications and energy industries. The school combines faculty, resources and the oversight of the deans from ASU's College of Liberal Arts and Sciences and Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering.

School of Social and Family Dynamics
Established: 2006
Director: Rick Fabes
College: College of Liberal Arts and Sciences

The School of Social and Family Dynamics merges faculty and programs from two previously separate departments: family and human development and sociology. The aim of the new school is to become a world-class leader in the study and advancement of the most important and valued aspects of human life: children, youth, families and their social worlds. The school will continue to offer the same degree programs previously offered through the two departments, and new educational opportunities will be developed for students who seek an interdisciplinary path.

School of Sustainability
Established: 2006
Director: Chuck Redman

In the fall of 2007, the first group of undergraduate and graduate students at the School of Sustainability will embark on a novel educational odyssey. These students will become leaders in the struggle to achieve sustainability in the face of challenging environmental, economic and societal needs. The school builds upon the extensive research portfolio of the Global Institute of Sustainability to educate students through collaborative learning, interdisciplinary approaches, and problem-oriented training to address the environmental, economic, and social challenges of the 21st century. Challenges the school will address include urbanization, energy and materials, water, economic development and social transformation, biodiversity and socio-ecological resilience.

School of Theatre and Film
Established: 2006
Director: Linda Essig
College: Herberger College of Fine Arts

The School of Theatre and Film comprises leading-edge programs that make it stand apart: a nationally recognized Theatre for Youth graduate program, a first-of-its-kind theater and performance of the Americas doctorate, and performance design education that integrates media and live performance – one of just two U.S. theater programs to do so. The school's new bachelor's degree in film, with concentration in film and media production, includes a special focus on ethics in entertainment.

University College
Established: 2004
Dean: Gail Hackett

Exploration is the central feature of the University College. Students have the opportunity to explore possible majors, degree completion programs, interdisciplinary studies or opportunities to become involved in the community. University College is also the home to the School of Interdisciplinary Studies, allowing students to combine study areas for a personalized education. University College also offers students choices of traditional classes on a campus or locations throughout the Valley, as well as online or televised courses.
