Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

December 2, 2003

Members Present:  Sue Hunter (Chair), Carol Behl, Beth Luey, Paul Scowen and Barbara Trapido-Lurie

 

Excused:  Patty Feldman, Victoria Trotta

Old business

Revisions to ACD 508-05 and 509-03:  A 1999 decision by the Supreme Court of Arizona, dealing with the right of a university grievant to be represented by legal counsel, provides the impetus for revisions to ASU?s AP grievance policies.  ASU faculty and staff policies have already been changed in accordance with this Supreme Court ruling.

 

At today?s meeting, CAPS discussed proposed changes to ACD sent to the Academic Professional Grievance Committee in 2001 by Mary Stevens (ASU Deputy General Counsel), and forwarded from the AP Grievance Committee to CAPS in April 2003.

 

CAPS proposed that wording in item 4 in Section I, Grievance Policies, be revised slightly for consistency with wording in the Procedures Hearing section, items 7 and 8.  Otherwise, the committee approved the proposed revisions as forwarded from the AP Grievance Committee.  Sue Hunter will forward the proposed revisions to the AP listserv for all academic professionals? review and approval.

Spring Spotlight Speaker:  The committee discussed options for a Spring Spotlight program.  It was decided that a report from the AP Task Force would probably be appropriate, but that no firm plans should be made until the Task Force has progressed further in its work.

New business

Report from the Academic Senate:  Sue Hunter reported that, in its last meeting, the Senate discussed the move to create a University-wide senate, with representation from all 4 ASU campuses, and from a broader mix of individuals, perhaps including staff and students.  The Senate Executive Committee is putting together a task force to look at this proposal.  A member of the senate inquired about Academic Professional representation on the task force; the response was that the make-up of the task force is still in development.

Report from PACAPS:  Sue Hunter reported on some recent and upcoming work of PACAPS:

­       The survey sent to all Academic Professionals in November received a strong initial response, but more responses are still desired.

­        PACAPS is working on finalizing its bylaws.

­        Current PACAPS members were given copies of a recommendation made in 1999 by PACAPS, for a post-continuing status review process at ASU.

 

CAPS membership:  When CAPS next meets in February, Victoria Trotta?s sabbatical will be complete and she will again participate in CAPS meetings.  Paul Scowen?s service as an alternate is complete, unless another CAPS member becomes unable to attend meetings in the spring semester.  Sue Hunter thanked Paul for his participation during the fall semester.

 

 

Meeting adjourned at 11:00 am.

 

Submitted by Barbara Trapido-Lurie
