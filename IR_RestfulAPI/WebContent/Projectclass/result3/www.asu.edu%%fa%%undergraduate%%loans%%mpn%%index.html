Master Promissory Notes

A Master Promissory Note (MPN) is a promissory note that does not specify the amount the borrower must repay. When a borrower signs an MPN, the borrower is agreeing to repay all loans awarded under that MPN. In some cases this means multiple loans over multiple academic years (as with Federal Stafford loans). For Federal Perkins loans, ASU has chosen to use the Perkins MPN as a "one-year" note. As such, you will need to sign a new Perkins MPN each academic year.

Before the Student Financial Assistance Office is able to disburse loan funds, it must have record that you have completed an MPN for each type of loan you accepted. If you have already completed an MPN at another school, you must still complete one at ASU.

Federal Direct Stafford Loan Federal Perkins Loan

You will need the following items in order to complete your Federal Direct Stafford Loan MPN:

  • Social Security Number
  • Driver's License Number
  • E-mail Address
  • Current Address
  • References (2 with complete address and phone number)
  • US Department of Education PIN
