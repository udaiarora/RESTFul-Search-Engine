March 23, 2005

Student retention expert to lead community scholars program

Gregory P. Hickman, a leader in developing initiatives to address high school retention issues, will become the new director of the W. P. Carey School’s innovative Rodel Community Scholars program April 1.

Hickman is the director of ASU’s Arizona Dropout Initiative at the Center for the Future of Arizona, where he has been responsible for building research, consensus and statewide collaboration to reduce the dropout rate and increase the high school graduation rate in Arizona. He has been instrumental in organizing the 2005 Effective Strategies Institute Southwest, to be held March 13 – 15, where more than 300 administrators, teachers and policy makers will gather around the theme “Discover Proven and Promising Practices in Dropout Prevention.”

The Rodel Community Scholars program brings together talented students from the W. P. Carey School and across ASU to use their new knowledge in addressing retention and graduation issues in area high schools. The program, launched in 2001, is designed to develop civic leadership in ASU students and to encourage high potential high school students to stay in class and receive a diploma, then go on to college.

This year, 25 Rodel Scholars are working with 340 high school students in six high schools.

“The Rodel Program challenges our students – future business leaders – to arrive at creative solutions to aspects of a problem that profoundly affects our community: the high school dropout rate,” says Philip Regier, deputy dean of the W. P. Carey Schoo . “Dr. Hickman, with his close working relationships throughout the education community, will expand what is already an important outreach program.”

Hickman earned a doctorate in human ecology from Ohio State University.

As director of the Arizona Dropout Initiative, Hickman has conducted research into the factors affecting high school attendance, including the impact of compulsory attendance laws and the AIMS test. He is also conducting a 10-year study evaluating the effectiveness of mentoring programs.
