September 16, 2003

Web application replaces old paper process for tracking, grading students' progress

Today's college students are able to take advantage of the latest technology when completing their assignments.and, now, how they receive their grades.

The Registrar's Office, Information Technology and representatives from all ASU campuses have developed a new online system that allows for faculty and teaching staff to use the Web to access class and grade rosters, input grades, and post grades to the student system, without using a scrap of paper. Faculty can also save their class rosters to an Excel spreadsheet for their own use. Even department chairs and directors can use the application to track the progress of grade reporting for their areas.

"We used to print out at least 30,000 rosters per year," says Bonnie Wilcox, associate registrar. "Now faculty can go online to access the rosters, using their ASURITE IDs and passwords. Students access their grades the same way at ASU Interactive or through SunDial."

Faculty can access the Rostergrades application by visiting (www.asu.edu/workingatasu/online/). "The new system provides less chance of error, it's more efficient and students can get their grades literally the day after professors submit the rosters," she says.

Looking into processing rosters a different way was precipitated by the fact that only one company in the Valley still offers the manual data entry service needed to process the old rosters.

"We wanted to be ahead of the game and eliminate the middle work before they decided to eliminate their service," says Wilcox.

The team worked on the project for just over a year and ran a pilot during winter session 2002, then on a limited basis during spring 2003. The system went university-wide during summer sessions.

Eliminating the printing of the rosters will allow the Registrar's Office to focus more on its clients - students and faculty - as well as save money.

"The system is still being evaluated, and we are looking for faculty input to help us with this process," says Wilcox.

Faculty can provide feedback through their roster contacts, which are individuals within each academic unit who have been trained on the new application and who are the first line of support as faculty learn the new application.

"The roster contacts will provide feedback to the work team so that we will be able to enhance the application," Wilcox says.

For information on the Rostergrades application, visit (http://www.asu.edu/provost/roster/instructions.html).
