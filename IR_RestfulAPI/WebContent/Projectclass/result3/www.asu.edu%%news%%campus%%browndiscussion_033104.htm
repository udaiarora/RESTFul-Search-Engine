March 31, 2004

Commemorative "THE 50th ANNIVERSARY OF BROWN V. TOPEKA BOARD OF EDUCATION : THE HISTORY AND LEGACY OF A LANDMARK DESEGREGATION DECISION"

Phoenix, AZ - May 16, 2004 - 6:00pm
Phoenix Prep Academy, 735 E. Fillmore
Phoenix, AZ 85006 

What is the history and legacy of Brown v. Topeka Board of Education ? What should have the Brown opinion called for? How can we, at the dawn of the 21st century, better understand the spirit of Brown, and the ways in which it can be used to dismantle de facto segregation in our schools, and our entire society? THE 50th ANNIVERSARY OF BROWN program will address these questions through video, a round table discussion, interpretive performances and more.

The program's participants will include: Carole Coles Henry, EOD, City of Phoenix; Governor Janet Napolitano (via video); Professor Thomas J. Davis (ASU); Judge Elizabeth Finn (Glendale Municipal Court), Stella Pope-Duarte (Cesar Chavez High School); Dr. Betty Greathouse (Professor Emeritus, ASU); Coy Payne (Former Mayor of Chandler); Peterson Zah (former President of the Navajo Nation); Professor Matthew Whitaker (ASU); Rory Gilbert, Chair, City of Phoenix Human Relations Commission).
