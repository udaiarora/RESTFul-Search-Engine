March 9, 2006

Del E. Webb School of Construction: Student chapter lends expertise to help Hunkapi Horse Program

After 15 months of phone calls, presentations and meetings, two ASU groups celebrated a groundbreaking Nov. 10 for a $1.5 million project that will house the Alternative Intervention Research Clinic (AIRC) Hunkapi Horse Program at the Phoenix Zoo.

The Hunkapi Horse Program is operated by ASU's Department of Kinesiology. The other ASU group involved in the project is the Associated General Contractors Student Chapter (AGCSC) at the Del E. Webb School of Construction.

The Hunkapi Horse Program works with more than 300 full-functioning - as well as mentally and physically challenged - children per week through equine therapy and has had phenomenal results.

In November 2002, the program was notified that the land on which it was operating might be sold. Officials with the Phoenix Zoo were made aware of the situation, and they decided to provide 5 acres of ground at the zoo for a permanent home for the program.

Since the Hunkapi Program operates solely from donations, it had been unable to make any progress on financing or building a new home. Given the support the AGCSC members receive from their industry, they felt they could help the Hunkapi Program achieve its goal.

After meeting with program officials in August 2004, the AGCSC members immediately began their efforts to get the support to complete the project. Brady-Aulerich and Associates helped the AGCSC get the campaign started by donating their efforts to perform the necessary surveying of the area at the zoo, and for an architect to begin the drawings of the facility.

In addition, Sergio Martinez and Michael Candio of the Orcutt/Winslow Partnership Architecture firm donated their time to create the design and the drawings for the project. As the drawings are completed, the students will use what they have learned at the Del E. Webb School of Construction and perform all of the quantity take-offs, an overall project estimate and a project schedule.

Once completed, the AGCSC members will ask industry officials for help in completing the project.

With guidance from volunteers working in the industry, the majority of the construction will be performed by the students. It will allow them to gain valuable field experience to complement their classroom instruction.

The AGCSC can be contacted through its faculty adviser, Christine Fiori, at (480) 965-3615. The group's mailing address is: AGC Student Chapter, Del E. Webb School of Construction, P.O. Box 870204, Tempe, AZ 85287-0204.
