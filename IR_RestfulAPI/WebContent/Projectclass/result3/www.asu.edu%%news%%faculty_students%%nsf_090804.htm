September 8, 2004

Graduate students receive NSF fellowships

Eight ASU graduate students have been honored by the National Science Foundation as new recipients of the foundation's prestigious Graduate Research Fellowships.

In all, the NSF paid tribute to 33 ASU graduate students for the 2004-05 academic year.

"These are some of the most highly sought-after fellowships," says Paul Johnson, associate vice president for research at ASU. "Because of the financial support the fellowships provide, they allow students to play a larger role in setting the direction of their research. They also offer students continued recognition in the future for having been selected as a recipient. I would like to congratulate all of the recipients and those that received honorable mentions in this year's competition."

The fellowships offer three years of support for advanced study to about 900 outstanding graduate students in the math, physical, biological, engineering and behavioral and social sciences fields. Recipients receive an annual stipend of $30,000 and an education allowance of $10,500 paid to their institution in lieu of tuition and fees.

The winners of the 2004 - 05 National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellows are Michael Teague O'Mara, anthropology; Lubna Munir Ahmad and Teresa Ann Murray, bioengineering; Visar Shahin Berisha and Jennifer Nisha Desai, electrical engineering; Mayra Y. Bámaca, family and human development; and Jonathan Roy Davis and Brigitte Wieshofer Hogan, life sciences.

The honorable mentions for ASU are Jamie Melichar Hodgkins, Emily Kathryn Landis, Thierra Kennec Nalley and Lydia Virginia Pyne, anthropology; Aaron W. Conovaloff, Andrea Lorraine Downing and Jacob Alan Goble, bioengineering; Dawn Angela Lantero, kinesiology; Sydella Anne Blatch, Susannah Smith French, Andrea Jutta Jani, Gwynne Irene Helen Johnston, Nathan Isaiah Morehouse and Shade Timothy Shutters, life sciences; Elizabeth Jennie Green, mathematics; Amber Nicole Straughn, physics; and Jenessa Rachel Shapiro, psychology.

Continuing fellows are Jessica Thompson, anthropology; Robin Roth, chemical engineering; Karen Chancellor, computer science; Nicholas Schmerr, geological sciences; Cinnamon Hayes and James Heffernan, life sciences; and Noah Goldstein and Vladas Griskevicius, psychology.

Two former ASU undergraduates, bioengineering student Merrill Jay Birdno and economics student Christopher Aspen Gorry, also received fellowships but went on to other universities for their graduate studies.

To learn more about this year's fellowship recipients and their research, go to (www.asu.edu/graduate/news_publications/News/index.htm).

Coronado, with the Division of Graduate Studies, can be reached at (480) 965-5995 or (nora.coronado@asu.edu).
