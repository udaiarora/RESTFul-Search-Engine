January 10, 2003

Federal funds increase for charter school program

An ASU West graduate program for charter school administrators, the first such program in the nation, has received second-year funding from the U.S. Department of Education.

The $412,000 in funding, an increase from the first-year appropriation of $348,000, ensures that the first cohort of students will be able to complete ASU West's Leadership for Educational Entrepreneurs (LEE) program.

Twenty-four charter school operators from 11 states gathered at ASU West last June to begin their studies through the LEE program, which bridges the College of Education's master's degree in educational administration and the School of Management's MBA degree. Federal funding is covering 80 percent of the students' tuition expenses.

The students have continued their studies through online instruction and now are finishing their fourth class. They met in person again in late December to begin work on applied research projects.

All 24 students have remained in the program, with graduation expected in spring 2004.

"It is gratifying to see the students already applying their learning to increase student achievement," says Eleanor Perry, graduate studies department chairperson in the ASU West College of Education. "One example is Mario Ybarra, founder of Harvest Academy in Yuma, who is using LEE's action research course to shape long-term literacy goals for his students - many of whom are from migrant worker families. Mario is working with parents to identify issues, collect and analyze data, and make program changes."

LEE is the first structured university program aimed at the growing charter school industry.

"Many charter school leaders are either business people turned educators or educators turned business people," Perry says. "This program is designed to give participants both the business and education skills required to successfully operate a charter school."

Recent statistics compiled by the Center for Education Reform show continuing growth in the number of charter schools operating in Arizona and around the United States. Arizona now has 468 charter schools - the most of any state. The number of charter schools has increased 12 percent in Arizona and 14 percent nationally in one year.

"As the number of charter schools continue to increase, the essential elements of successful charter schools are being recognized," says Dean Kern, director of public charter school programs for the U.S. Department of Education. "The LEE program has examined the research and responded to the need for entrepreneurial leadership to further advance school reform and choice."

Pending availability of funding, a second LEE cohort will begin working toward their master's degrees in the summer of 2003. For information visit the Web at (www.west.asu.edu/coe/graduate/LEE.htm).
