January 4, 2005

Arizona Business Conditions Index rises in December

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index rose to 64.6 in December from 61.4 the previous month, a 5.1 percent increase. An index reading over 50 indicates the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

The 3.2-point rise marks the 20 th consecutive month of readings above the critical 50-point level and indicates continued short-term growth in the local economy, according to Dawn McLaren, economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business.

The production component of the overall index recorded the largest gain in December, reaching 67.5, a 14.3 percent increase over the previous month. Employment, which has lagged in improvement post-recession, gained 8.5 percent to reach 65.2, boding well for the job market. The only component to falter slightly was new orders, which slipped from 64.3 in November to 64.2 in December.

The Price Index rose by 6.8 percent to reach 73.6. The Price Index has remained in the 70s since April. The last time this index recorded extended levels in the 70s was in late 1994 and early 1995.

Each December, the Arizona Business Conditions Survey asks a question about capital equipment expenditure. This type of expenditure is seen as essential to a return of healthy growth in the economy, both nationally and locally. Of the respondents to this year’s survey, 51 percent anticipate increasing their capital equipment expenditure in 2005, and 23 percent plan to decrease such expenditures. (In comparison, last year 52 percent of the respondents planned to increase their capital equipment expenditures and 14 percent planned a decrease.) In manufacturing, the 2005 planned expenditures are evenly divided between new and replacement equipment.

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available on the Web at wpcarey.asu.edu/azbci. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data.
