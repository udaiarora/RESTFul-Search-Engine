Arizona State System for Information on Student Transfer (ASSIST)
A Status Report as of April 30, 1999
John D. Porter

Overview

ASSIST (formerly known as the Transfer Student Data Warehouse or TSDW) is a relational database containing student enrollment records from Arizona?s universities and community colleges. The purpose of ASSIST is to provide community colleges and universities information for (1) completing the IPEDS Graduation Rate Survey and (2) assessing transfer articulation effectiveness. ASU signed a Memorandum of Agreement to create ASSIST with data warehousing technology as part of the Arizona Transfer Articulation Support System (ATASS). The project was funded as a proof of concept for three years beginning July 1, 1998. The ultimate success of the project depends on institutions providing accurate and complete data. ASU?s goal is to create the technical environment in the first year, then focus on qualitative improvements over the remaining two years (e.g., data quality and completeness).

Current Status

The three universities contributed four years of data (NAU is missing data for 94-95).
The following community colleges contributed five years of data: Coconino, Maricopa, Mohave, Pima, and Yavapai. (Coconino data are missing some data elements, but the institution is working on providing the data.) Central, Cochise, Eastern, Northland Pioneer, and Western are missing two or more years of data (Eastern withdrew from the project pending resolution of FERPA concerns).
The 1998-99 data exchange occurs in December 1999, and for the first time will include course data from the community colleges (the universities provided course data beginning in 1994-95).
ASU developed a computer program to test the accuracy and completeness of the data. The usefulness of ASSIST depends on the quality of the data. Experience has shown that all institutions need to review their data very carefully before submission.
ASU created a matching algorithm to match student records between institutions. The algorithm has 8 steps using combinations of student ID (SSN if available), last name, first name, and birth date. The program performs phonetic matching and adjusts for transposition errors in student ID. ASU is satisfied this program provides reliable enrollment matches between institutions.
ASU created a relational database containing over 700,000 enrollment records from the three universities and five community colleges on the CAS 2 server. Since this database contains personally identifiable information on students, it resides behind a firewall and is accessible only to the ASSIST development team. An identical relational database excluding personally identifiable information has been created on the CAS 3 server. The CAS 3 server is outside the firewall and accessible to participating institutions over the web. The architecture of the relational database on CAS 3 is such that an institution only has access to their former students and not the students of another institution. CAS 3 will not be released for use until completion of the prototype test.
ASU created a prototype of ASSIST with data from the five community colleges and three universities, a web site for disseminating information about ASSIST, and demonstrated the prototype and web site at the Steering Committee meeting on March 25th and the Arizona AIR meeting on March 26th.
ASU assisted community colleges in completing the graduation rate survey (due for the first time on March 1st).
ASU completed a selection process for web-based reporting software. Brio Technology, Inc was the successful vendor. The new tool allows ASU to develop web-based reports from ASSIST and broadcast them to institutions over the web.

 

Future Plans


During the summer, ASU will organize and train a team from the five community colleges and University of Arizona to (1) validate the ASSIST data model, (2) verify the accuracy of the data by comparing to institutional data, and (3) identify data tables requiring indexing to meet performance standards. Another major effort is to work with ABOR and the State Board?s legal counsels to ensure the ASSIST security plan safeguards student privacy. Pending resolution of the FERPA issues and the outcome of the prototype test, ASU plans to release ASSIST for use by the community colleges and universities in the fall. Future goals involve using the Brio tools to develop reports from ASSIST, and encouraging the remaining five community colleges to provide missing data.
