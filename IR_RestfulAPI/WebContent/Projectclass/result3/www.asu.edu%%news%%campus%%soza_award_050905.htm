Chicanos Por La Causa pays tribute to Soza

Chicanos Por La Causa (CPLC) has honored ASU Dean of Students Bob Soza by presenting him with its annual Professional Development Award.

The award is presented to an individual who has reached a level of professionalism beyond others in addition to surpassing personal and professional achievements.

Soza, who has been at ASU for the past 16 years, has been an educator for twice that long. He was the assistant vice president for Student Affairs for 12 years; for the past six years he has been the dean of students for ASU. He has been involved with Chicanos Por La Causa for quite a while.

“With the support of Student Affairs, for many years I have done outreach to many school districts in Arizona,” Soza says. “I have always enjoyed working with community-based organizations, and the people from CPLC are great people to work with.”

Soza says he feels extremely fortunate to be at the Tempe campus, because he is able to help students who want to come to this campus. He also enjoys assisting them and helping them to be successful once they are attending ASU.

He says ASU always has been known as a good place for all students, especially those in the Latino community.

Soza, who attended ASU in the 1960s, has a great appreciation for ASU’s diverse student population, just like his daughter experienced in the 1980s. He says ASU continues to provide support for all diverse groups today.

“We continue to find and develop even more ways to improve our programs, and that is what I think is important,” says Soza, who was very surprised and pleased to receive the award.

He says he will continue to work hard within the community in his role as the dean of students.

“ASU is a great place today more than ever with all of the supportive programs, faculty and staff,” Soza says. “There really is no better place for students than ASU.”

Huck, with Marketing and Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480)-965-9690 or (colleen.huck@asu.edu).
