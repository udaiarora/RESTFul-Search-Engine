July 8, 2003

Mason becomes artistic director for college's Mainstage Season

In a progressive move that presents exciting opportunities for Valley audiences, the Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts at ASU has named renowned director Marshall W. Mason as the artistic director for theater for its upcoming Mainstage Season.

In turn, Mason, an ASU theater professor, is expanding the 2003 - 04 theater offerings by presenting the season in rotating repertory. The juxtaposition of alternating shows allows productions to "speak" to each other in unexpected and provocative ways, enhancing the theater-going experience.

"Repertory has been the keystone of great theatre throughout the history of theater," Mason says. "This is how Shakespeare, Chekhov and Molière presented their plays. This is a unique program not only here in the Valley, but in universities across the nation."

The theater schedule for the Mainstage Season includes the following performances:

. The season-opening pairing of Paul Vogels' startling Pulitzer Prize-winning play "How I Learned to Drive with the powerful docudrama, "The Laramie Project," by Moisés Kaufman. Both plays are hard-hitting, emotional journeys through dark and narrow paths of dysfunction, sexuality and morality.

. The pairing of the classic "Our Town" by Thornton Wilder and the scathing study of rootless American youth "suburbia" by Eric Bogosian. Both are studies of what it means to be human in small-town America.

. The pairing of Anton Chekhov's "The Cherry Orchard" with "Fifth of July" by Lanford Wilson. Both plays are portraits of a family and a civilization teetering on the brink of permanent change; both were hits in the 2003 Broadway season, as well.

. The eighth annual New Plays Festival, an exciting celebration has developed from a marathon of staged readings to three full productions in repertory. The plays are written by students in the Master of Fine Arts in playwriting program and developed in a collaborative process. Audiences will have the unique opportunity to participate in the process through post-performance discussions with the playwrights and the artistic teams.

ASU's Mainstage Season's dance productions begin with an exciting fusion of Asian cultures and modern dance in "September Concert," featuring the choreography of students Ying Yang, Siew Kong Wong, Yeong Wen Lee and Min Kim. They combine their talents and traditions into a synthesis of beautiful movement. The college's "New Danceworks II" will be offered in November.

The Herberger College Mainstage Season also includes six organ concerts and four Lyric Opera Theatre shows - "The Impresario/Riders to the Sea," "Caterina's Son - World Premiere," "Once Upon a Mattress" and "La Finta Giardiniera."
