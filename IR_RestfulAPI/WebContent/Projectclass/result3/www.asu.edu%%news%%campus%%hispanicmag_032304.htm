March 23, 2004

ASU a top spot for latinos

ASU is listed among the "Top 25 Colleges for Latinos" in the March 2004 issue of Hispanic magazine. Academic excellence and Hispanic achievement are the main qualities considered, along with freshman retention rate, minority enrollment and number of degrees awarded to Latinos.

"ASU is known for having a caring faculty with several renowned programs," the magazine reported, citing business and fine arts as among ASU's nationally recognized programs. ASU awards a high number of bachelor's degrees to Latinos and has a beautiful campus with good food and easy access to recreational destinations, editors said.

The undergraduate student body on ASU's Main Campus is 11 percent Hispanic, and this year's freshman class is 16 percent Hispanic. Major student support programs include the Hispanic Mother Daughter Program, the Minority Engineering Program and the Multicultural Student Center.

This article appeared in the March 12, 2004 issue of ASU Insight.
