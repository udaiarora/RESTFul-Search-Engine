Catalog Council Policy for Cataloging Electronic Resources


This document is intended to supplement the general cataloging policy of the Arizona State University Libraries and to provide more detailed information about cataloging policy decisions unique to electronic resources. Supplemental and background documentation informing this policy is available from the Electronic Resources Cataloger.

 

Role of the Catalog

Catalog Council holds that the online catalog should provide an authoritative and primary source for access to electronic resources. We agree that secondary points of access are desirable, but we will maintain the catalog as a central point of access to information in any format.

 

Sources for Records

At the current time, cataloging of electronic resources into the III system is performed using OCLC’s Worldcat and RLIN as sources for copy cataloging records, and as the databases to which we contribute original cataloging. We may consider broadening our participation to include the Cooperative Online Resource Catalog (CORC) in the future. We may also choose to purchase sets of records, or to outsource cataloging for specific electronic collections.

 

Single vs. Multiple records

For in-house cataloging of single serial publications and e-journal packages, we will prefer a single record approach, which allows us to consolidate our print and electronic access onto one record. For in-house cataloging of individual monographs or small sets of monographs, we will prefer separate records in accordance with current cataloging standards for monographic reproductions.

 

There may be cases where we purchase cataloging which does not exactly conform to our local practices for large sets of material. While we believe that maintaining consistency in the catalog is important, and we will make every effort to ensure that purchased cataloging is consistent with our local practices, we will not refuse to purchase a set of records based on a single vs. multiple record approach conflict.

 

Classification

Electronic resources will be assigned LC classification numbers according to their subject matter. Remote access materials and those direct access materials (CD-ROMs) which are loaded on the library’s LAN will have a location code in the catalog that indicates Online Access in the public display. Direct access electronic resources with a physical carrier will be assigned to the appropriate location in the Libraries.

 

Categories of material (purchased, free, free with print, etc)

We are committed to cataloging any electronic resource for which the University Libraries have paid. These materials are of first importance among electronic resources for cataloging.

 

We will also provide links to remote access electronic resources that come free with their print counterpart as requested by Selectors or Collection Development Coordinators, or librarians at other ASU campus libraries.

 

At this time, we do not catalog free web resources. As we work with Collection Development, Reference, and librarians at other ASU campuses, we expect to develop policies for the selection and cataloging of free web resources.

 

URL Maintenance

Creating links to remote access electronic resources means that we are introducing dynamic and rapidly changing information into the system. Routine systematic maintenance of these links is a time consuming and labor intensive process, and at the current time, we do not have such a routine in place. We repair broken links as they are reported to us, and will continue with that policy until we can automate some of the procedures for finding and repairing broken links.

 

Resources Unique to East and West Campus

We will catalog any electronic resource on a physical carrier that resides at East or West campus libraries. These items will be assigned appropriate locations and classifications in the online catalog. It is our policy to provide links in the catalog to remote access resources that provide the greatest benefit to our users. We will carefully consider the consequences before adding a link to any resource that is not available to the majority of our users.
