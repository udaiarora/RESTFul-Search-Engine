October 5, 2004

Gail Tsukiyama opens Piper Center’s visiting writers season

The Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing’s 2004 – 05 Distinguished Visiting Writers season begins with the distinguished Asian-American novelist Gail Tsukiyama, who will read from her works at Scottsdale Center for the Arts at 7:30 p.m., Oct. 8.

Tsukiyama’s first novel, “Women of the Silk,” was published by St. Martin ’s Press in 1991. Her second novel, “The Samurai’s Garden” was published in 1995. Her most recent novels include “Night of Many Dreams,” “The Language of Threads,” and “Dreaming Water” (published in 2002).

Tsukiyama began her writing career as a poet and has been a winner of the Academy of American Poets poetry award. She was born in San Francisco to a Chinese mother from Hong Kong and a Japanese father from Hawaii .

As resident of the San Francisco Bay Area, she has been a part-time lecturer in creative writing at San Francisco State University , as well as a freelance book reviewer for the San Francisco Chronicle.

She has been a judge for the Kiriyama Book Prize and is book review editor for the online magazine Pacific Rim Voices. In September 2001 she was one of 50 authors chosen by the Library of Congress to participate in the first National Book Festival in Washington D.C., and has been a guest speaker at the Hong Kong International Literary Festival and the Sydney Writers’ Festival.

Tickets for Tsukiyama’s reading are $10, available at the Scottsdale Center for the Arts, (480) 994-2787.

Future visiting writers in the 2004 – 05 series will include Robert Pinsky, Oct. 16; Charles Baxter, Oct. 28; Edward Albee, Nov. 16 and 17; and Alexander McCall Smith, Nov. 19.

For more information on this series, visit the Piper Center Web site at (www.asu.edu/pipercwcenter), e-mail (pipercenter.info@asu.edu) or call (480) 965-6018.

Hathaway, with the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, can be reached at (480) 965-6375 or (jim.hathaway@asu.edu).
