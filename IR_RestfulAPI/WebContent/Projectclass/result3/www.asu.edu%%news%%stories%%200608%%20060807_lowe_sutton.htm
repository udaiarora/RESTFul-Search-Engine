August 7, 2006

ASU Police Department bids farewell to Lowe, Sutton

ASU Police is recognizing two veteran officers as they bid farewell to their posts at the university.

After nearly 30 years in law enforcement, Cmdr. Floyd Lowe and Cmdr. John Sutton will be exploring new opportunities ? and spending more time with their families.

At ASU, Lowe was chief of police for the West campus and oversaw the department until ASU consolidated its police operations in 2003. Lowe continued to manage the West campus and was promoted to commander of administrative services for the entire department this year.

Before joining ASU, Lowe served in different capacities for several institutions, including the Maricopa County Sheriff's Office, the Glendale Police Department, Estrella Community College and the Avondale Police Department. For the past 37 years, he devoted himself to protecting the community while raising two generations of family at home.

Because his children ?are only young once,? Lowe wanted to shift his focus to raising his 7-year-old twin daughters. His plans involve taking long vacations, including camping trips in northern Arizona .

?Although I reminisce every time I see a patrol car, nothing compares to having the time to bond with my wife and children,? Lowe says.

Sutton, meanwhile, will be pursuing another career path. He hopes to have more time to focus on his passion for music ? and to achieve his long-standing goal of becoming a professional musician.

He will teach a music class once a week at Central Arizona College this fall.

Sutton started at ASU in 1998 as a lieutenant and moved up the ranks to commander. After serving in the Army during Vietnam , Sutton joined a law enforcement agency in Washington and served their for 20 years before moving to Arizona .

?My past law enforcement experience aptly prepared me for my job at ASU, but I remember I still had to learn the ins and outs of working at a university,? he says.

For Sutton, his most memorable experience at ASU was managing the police detail for the 2004 presidential debate at ASU. Campus Law Enforcement Journal highlighted his work for the debate in its April issue.

?Commanders Lowe and Sutton are valued leaders of the ASU Police Department, and the force is sad to see their departure,? says Chief John Pickens. ?However, we wish them the best of luck in their future endeavors.?
