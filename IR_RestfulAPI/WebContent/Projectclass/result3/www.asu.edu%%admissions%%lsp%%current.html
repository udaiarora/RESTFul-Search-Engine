Selection Process

Arizona Residents:
Each year ASU announces the Leadership Scholarship Program to high school counselors throughout the state. High schools will usually conduct a selection process and then submit a single student nomination to ASU. Students do not contact ASU directly. If you are interested in learning more about the program's selection process, talk with your high school counselor.

Nonresident Students:
Four scholarships are available each year to nonresident students. Students must be members of and involved with Key Club International.

Program Components

Leadership Scholars enjoy a range of benefits, including:

  • personal leadership development class specifically designed for the Leadership Scholarship Program
  • monthly meetings with all current ASU Leadership Scholarship Program students
  • one-on-one mentoring by an ASU staff member
  • "Big Buddy" program in which new scholars are matched with an upperclassman scholar
  • leadership development conferences and workshops
  • networking opportunities with Leadership Scholarship alumni
  • social activities throughout the year such as fall retreat and holiday events
  • community service opportunities

Program Requirements

Leadership Scholars are expected to:

  • maintain a 3.0 cumulative GPA
  • complete 24 credit hours within the nine month academic calendar
  • actively participate in LSP
  • participate in service with the university or community

    • All students must:

    • attend all monthly general meetings scheduled for the first Tuesday of each month
    • serve on at least one LSP committee: fall retreat, alumni homecoming reception, holiday party, senior banquet, or LSP outreach

Freshmen must additionally:

  • attend the LSP fall retreat
  • enroll in the Leadership Development course during the fall semester and spring semester
  • meet with assigned advisor once each week in the fall and twice a month in the spring

Sophomores, juniors and seniors must :

  • participate in any co-curricular involvement that will benefit ASU and/or the community
  • participate in a long or short term community service project
  • participate in any on or off campus job or internship
  • offer support to Leadership Scholarship Program freshman during their class service project in the spring
