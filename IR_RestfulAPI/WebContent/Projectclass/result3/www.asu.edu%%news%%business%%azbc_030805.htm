March 8, 2005

Arizona Business Conditions Index rises in February

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index rose to 66.6 in February from 64.0 the previous month, a 4.1 percent increase. An index reading over 50 indicates the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

“February marks the 18 th consecutive month that the index continues to register within a range in the mid-60s, indicating continued growth in the local economy,” said Dawn McLaren, economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business.

All components of the index recorded healthy levels in February, with production emerging as the strongest. Among the weaker components was employment, but even that remained in the growth range. “Since the recession of 2001, Arizona has remained a leader in job growth in the United States, but actual job growth has not met the expectations of the forecasts,” McLaren said. “The employment component of the Arizona Business Conditions Index confirms this, with growth in the short-term, but at a pace somewhat slower than output.”

The Price Index also remains above the critical 50-point benchmark, indicating continued upward pressure on prices in the near term. “This is a trend that began in the beginning of 2004 with a jump in steel prices and continued as other commodities became involved,” McLaren noted.

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available on the Web at wpcarey.asu.edu/azbci. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data.
