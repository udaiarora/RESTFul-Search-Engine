Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

March 7, 2001

Present: Victor Becerra, Maggie Mangini, Stefan Myhajlenko, Helen Seaton, Linda Shackle, and Santos Vega, Chair.

The CAPS meeting was held at 10:00 a.m. at the Hispanic Research Center Conference Room.

Victor Becerra announced that he is leaving. Victor moved that Carol Behl replace him on CAPS. Linda Shackle seconded the motion. Motion carried.

Melvin Pratt has been nominated and voted in as a member of the Grievance Committee to fill a vacancy left by Dr. Tom Keating when he became a faculty member. Dr. Keating was no longer eligible to serve as an AP on the Grievance Committee. Stefan Myhajlenko moved that CAPS approve Melvin Pratt's selection. Linda Shackle seconded the motion. Motion carried.

The Committee on Committees was informed about the action CAPS would take and it was agreed upon at their regular meeting. Each person serves until the end of the semester. Both names will be placed on the ballot for the up-coming election.

Ballots will be mailed out to all APs with continuing, or hope of continuing, appointment, Senior Lecturers, and Faculty. Santos asked about Academic Associates. Those Academic Associates who are continuing or are in continuing status as reported by Human Resources will get a ballot. If AAs know of someone who is continuing but did not get a ballot, please compare lists with Darby Shaw. Human Resources is the department that sends the list to Darby.

Santos asked for names of possible candidates for CAPS that he can give to the Committee on Committees. He has already given them some names for CAPS plus Mel Pratt for Grievance Committee. Maggie Mangini and Helen Seaton will be rotating off the committee this year.

Worked on plans for the next brown bag presentation on May 2. The business meeting will be at 11:00 a.m. followed by the presentation from 11:30 to 12:30. A notice will be put in Insight plus sent out on our AP distribution list.

Victor Becerra and Carol Behl will draft a letter to Dr. Gail Hackett concerning voting rights and representation for Academic Associates. After the committee approves the letter, Santos will forward it to Dr. Hackett.


Helen Seaton
Secretary
