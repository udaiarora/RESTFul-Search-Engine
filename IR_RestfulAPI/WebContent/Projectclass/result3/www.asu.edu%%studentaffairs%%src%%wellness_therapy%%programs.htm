LivWeLL Assessment Center
(Physiological Assessment)

Fitness testing provides a measure of your cardiovascular (aerobic) and muscular fitness, flexibility and body composition. Knowing these parameters of fitness is key in determining effective exercise program design and can be a great motivational tool as you track your progress toward your fitness goals. At the LivWELL Center (located in the Student Recreation Complex), qualified staff lead you through the assessment protocols and educate you on what the results mean. Let us assist you in developing and implementing your fitness goals!


Services offered Fall 2006


Quick PA (Physiological Assessment)
The “Quick” Physiological Assessment is a full fitness assessment measuring heart rate, blood pressure, aerobic fitness, body composition, muscular endurance, and flexibility. Aerobic fitness is assessed through a submaximal exercise test (bicycle ergometer or step test). Therefore, the resulting aerobic fitness values will be somewhat less accurate than if a maximal exercise stress test was performed. Body composition is estimated via a 3-site skinfold caliper measurement. Muscular endurance is measured via a sit-up and/or push-up test.
Flexibility is measured via a sit-and-reach test. The time requirement for this option is only 45-60 minutes.
Cost: Student $20           Non-student $40

Body Composition only
A three-site skinfold measurement is used to predict relative body fat. Skinfold measurement sites differ between the sexes: chest, abdomen, and thigh for men; triceps, suprailiac, and thigh for women. This body composition technique is not as accurate as is a hydrostatic weighing, but still estimates body fat with reasonable accuracy (+/- 3.5%). Therefore, it is a technique which is appropriate for those interested in a general understanding and reasonable estimation of their body fat percentage. It is also a good technique to use to monitor changes over time.
Cost: Student $10          Non-student $20

Flexibility (sit and reach) only
Flexibility is the ability to move a joint through its complete range of motion. A sit-and-reach protocol will be conducted to measure hamstring flexibility. The protocol involves slow, static stretching of the muscles. Proper warm-up will be performed prior to the test to minimize the risk of soreness or injury.
Cost: Student $5          Non-student $10

Muscular endurance (sit-up, push-up) only
Muscular endurance is the ability of a muscle group to execute repeated contractions over a period of time sufficient to cause muscular fatigue, or to maintain a voluntary contraction for a prolonged period of time. Sit-up and/or push-up protocols will be used to evaluate the endurance of the abdominal muscle groups and upper body muscles, respectively.

Registration Information
1. Register and pay for any assessments at the SRC Administration Desk during regular business hours.

2. To schedule your assessment appointment, bring your receipt to the SRC Weight Room Supervisor on duty. The Weight Room Supervisor cannot schedule your appointment without proof of payment.

3. The Weight Room Supervisor will give you an Assessment Information Packet
which includes the following forms:

         • Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire (PAR-Q)
         • Pre-participation Screening Questionnaire
         • Pre-testing Guidelines
         • Informed Consent

4. Complete the PAR-Q and Pre-participation Screening Questionnaire forms and return packet to Weight Room Supervisor to review. Weight Room Supervisor cannot schedule appointment without these completed forms.

5. Based on information on the PAR-Q and Pre-participation Screening Questionnaire, medical clearance may be needed before you can participate in certain assessments. If so, the Weight Room Supervisor will give you a Medical Clearance form.

6. If required, the Medical Clearance form will need to be completed by your health care provider and returned to the SRC Administration Desk. Once received, the Assistant Director of Fitness and Wellness Programs will call you to
schedule your assessment(s).

7. If no medical clearance is required, the Weight Room Supervisor will schedule your appointment based on Assessment Center Specialist availability and the time needed to complete the assessment(s).

8. The Weight Room Supervisor will give you an Appointment Reminder form. Please note that you will be assessed a $10 cancellation fee if you do not call 480.965.8920 within 12 hours of your scheduled time to cancel your appointment.

9. The Weight Room Supervisor will give you the Pre-test Guidelines and the second page of the PAR-Q; the rest of the packet will be accessed by the Assessment Center Specialist for use during your scheduled assessment.

10. Follow Pre-test Guidelines prior to your assessment.
