March 9, 2004

Manufacturers look forward to increasing job growth, business spending

The 2004 Greater Phoenix Manufacturing Survey indicates a level of optimism that has not been seen since the boom in the mid-1990s, according to the current issue of the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

Approximately 78 percent of those responding to this year's survey believe that the U.S. economy is moderately or substantially better than it was 12 months ago, and 65 percent believe the Greater Phoenix economy is ahead of last year's, according to Elliott D. Pollack, contributing editor. "Looking ahead 12 months, this year's results are the most optimistic on record, with approximately 81 percent believing that the economy will be better," Pollack said. "The highest level of optimism, however, was reserved for local conditions: Eighty-two percent of those surveyed believe that conditions will be better in the Greater Phoenix economy this year."

Perhaps the best news of all, according to the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip report, is that 43 percent of manufacturers responding to the survey foresee an increase in employment over the next year, compared to only 16 percent in last year's survey. Business spending, too, is expected to increase in the next 12 months.

The Greater Phoenix Manufacturing Survey has been conducted annually since 1994, and is sponsored by Arizona State University's Bank One Economic Outlook Center and Center for Business Research.

Elsewhere in the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip , economic analyst Dawn McLaren writes that the forecast panel sees a significant increase in job growth for the Greater Phoenix area in 2004 and 2005. "The range of growth expected is between 2.1 and 4.0 percent," McLaren reported. "It's been a long time coming, but the recovery is finally here."

The Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast is published four times a year by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. To order, call the center at (480) 965-7858.  For economic information, contact Tracy Clark at (480) 965-5543, or by e-mail at Tracy.Clark@asu.edu .
