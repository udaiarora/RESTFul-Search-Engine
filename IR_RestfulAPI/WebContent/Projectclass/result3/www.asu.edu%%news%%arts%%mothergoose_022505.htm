February 25, 2005

Duo to perform ‘Mother Goose Suite’

The Transcontinental Duo, Jan Meyer Thompson and Elaine Greenfield, will perform a program of Schubert, Dvorak, Barber, Stravinsky and Ravel at 2:30 p.m. , Feb. 27, in ASU’s Katzin Concert Hall.

Thompson, a professor of piano at ASU, has enjoyed a diverse career as a soloist, collaborative artist, lecture-recitalist and master teacher on three continents. Chamber music, duo piano performances and master classes have taken her across North America, Europe, Russia and Asia. As pianist with the Kent Camerata, Thompson recently toured China, presenting recitals and master classes in the premier conservatories of Beijing, Shanghai and Tianjin. The Chicago Sun Times has described Thompson’s playing as possessing “beautiful sound, easy versatility of style, and remarkable sensitivity to phrasing and nuance.”

Thompson’s collaborative partner, Greenfield , has performed nationwide, including noted appearances at Carnegie Hall, Merkin Hall, Steinway Hall , Washington ’s Phillips Collection, Boston’s Gardner Museum and numerous college campuses coast to coast, including Harvard. The year 1996 marked her first of several performances at the Frederick Collection in Ashburnham, Mass., where her recent CD release, “Debussy Preludes, BKS 1&2” (Centaur Records), was recorded on a 1907 Blüthner identical to Debussy’s piano.

Greenfield has been televised and broadcast over national and international radio, including the BBC, NPR, WGBH and WNYC. The New York Times praised her playing as embodying “crystalline clarity, tonal warmth, and graceful airiness” and “a formidable technique, which she uses for expressive purposes rather than flashy surface effect.”

The evening’s performance of four-hand piano works will feature Maurice Ravel’s intimate “Mother Goose Suite”; his great duo-piano transcription of the orchestral work “La Valse” will conclude the evening.

Tickets to the event are $5. For more information, call (480) 965-8863.

Bell, with the Herberger College of Fine Arts, can be reached at (480) 965-2817 or (rebecca.bell@asu.edu).
