Dancing Across Borders

Celebrating Dance Cultures at ASU Libraries

(originally posted 11/03/06)

Hayden Library Rotunda - November 1st through the end of the Fall 2006 Semester

Dance is a human universal that knows no boundaries, physical or intellectual. This exhibit, created in coordination with Cross-Cultural Dance Resources and the ASU Department of Dance to complement the annual meeting of the Congress on Research in Dance, is designed to celebrate the diversity and interconnectedness of dance cultures, from the global to the local. Masked dance in Africa; Yaqui spirituality; intricate hand mudras in Cambodia; American modern dance; puppetry in Burma; the university tango class; and the dance scholar’s field notes, this exhibit presents the broad spectrum of dance experience from movement to enquiry.

Cross-Cultural Dance Resources (CCDR) is an organization dedicated to a global understanding of cultures through dance and about dance through cultures. A core asset of the organization is an interdisciplinary research library drawing from primary monographic and serial publications in the fields of dance, folklore, ethnomusicology, anthropology, religious studies, and the performing and visual arts. Unique to the library are the personal and archival collections of three prominent figures in the field of dance ethnography: Joann W. Keal‘iinohomoku, Eleanor King, and Gertrude Prokosch Kurath. ASU Libraries is proud to exhibit here key works of those scholars from the CCDR collections in addition to select publications of Elsie Ivancich Dunin, CCDR Vice-President. A valuable Arizona-based cultural resource, CCDR is supported by funding from the National Endowment for the Arts, the Arizona Commission on the Arts, the Flagstaff Arts and Science Commission, and contributions from its membership. For more information visit the Cross-Cultural Dance Resources website.

The Congress on Research in Dance (CORD) will convene for its 38th annual conference on the Tempe campus of Arizona State University, November 2 - 5, 2006. Founded in 1965 and currently comprised of a broad international membership, CORD’s mission is to: encourage research in all aspects of dance, including related fields; foster the exchange of ideas, resources, and methodology, through publication, international and regional conferences, and workshops; and promote the accessibility of dance research materials.

Exhibits in ASU Libraries reflect the scholarly, historical, social, and cultural concerns of Arizona State University and its community. If you would like to propose an exhibit, please visit the Exhibits Policy page or contact Katherine O'Clair, Exhibits Committee Chair at 480-965-5964 or katherine.oclair@asu.edu.
