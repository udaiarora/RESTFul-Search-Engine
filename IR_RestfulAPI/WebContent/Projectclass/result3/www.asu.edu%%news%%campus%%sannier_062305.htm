Sannier to strengthen university’s information technology infrastructure

Expert in human/computer interaction joins ASU as senior technology officer

Adrian Sannier, an expert in human/computer interaction and three-dimensional visualization, has been named deputy senior vice president and university technology officer at ASU.

He begins his new job at ASU the last week of July.

Sannier, the Stanley Professor of Interdisciplinary Engineering at Iowa State University’s Department of Industrial and Manufacturing Systems Engineering, sports 17 years of experience in the public and private sectors.

“Adrian brings a breadth and depth of experience to ASU that will be critical to our need to use information technology in new and creative ways to advance our student’s success, our research productivity, our ability to innovate in real world technologies, and system support to improve our administrative efficiency and student support,” says Richard Stanley, ASU senior vice president and university planner.

Sannier will develop and implement a five-year strategic plan for the application and use of information systems and technology. His objectives:

• Enhance student access and student success.

• Empower faculty research, knowledge production and technology transfer.

• Enhance university operations and efficiency.

• Integrate technology to connect ASU campuses, as well as to better connect the university to the community and the world.

“I believe in President Michael Crow’s vision for a New American University, and I’m excited at the prospect of working with people from all corners of the ASU community to harness the latest technology in support of that vision”, Sannier says.

In addition to research and teaching, Sannier serves as associate director of Iowa State ’s Virtual Reality Applications Center. Research at VRAC focuses on the applications of immersive visualization and next generation human/computer interfaces to challenges in science, technology and the humanities.

Before joining the Iowa State University faculty in 2001, Sannier was vice president and general manager of Engineering Animation Inc., a leading provider of 3D computer graphics software. Sannier led a group of 200 programmers and artists who created products for a diverse group of companies, from Mattel and Disney to Ford and General Motors.

Sannier did his undergraduate work at Michigan State University. He began his graduate studies at Cornell University, and returned to Michigan State to earn his doctorate in systems science.
