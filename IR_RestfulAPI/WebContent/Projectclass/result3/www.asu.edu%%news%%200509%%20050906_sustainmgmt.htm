ASU offers certification in sustainable management

Sustainable management component at ASU merges business, engineering capacities

Today’s business world is engaging competitors on a global scale more than ever, thanks to burgeoning technologies and foreign outsourcing.

As Phoenix continues its evolution into a sprawling metropolis, sustainability issues in the context of globalization and urbanization are becoming bigger priorities for business executives and engineering professionals.

A joint venture between the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, ASU’s International Institute for Sustainability (IIS) and the W.P. Carey School of Business is offering industry leaders the chance to earn professional certification in sustainable management to assist them in evaluating the impact their companies have on the environment, society and the economy, and ways to make their decisions accordingly.

Although several prestigious U.S. universities – including the University of Pennsylvania and the University of California system schools – offer sustainable management education in business or engineering capacities, the unique aim of the sustainable management component at ASU is to merge the two.

“We work with managers and engineers to identify opportunities to reduce operation costs and environmental impact while increasing competitive advantage through new technology,” says Jay Golden, director of the Sustainable Materials and Renewable Technologies (SMART) program at IIS.

Golden, who will be teaching Introduction to Sustainable Development this fall, joins a cast of talented faculty members from the W. P. Carey School and the Fulton School who are teaching the four courses required for the certificate.

These classes will equip professionals with the practical information necessary to understand sustainability as well as global and urban issues, while exposing them to speakers from major international companies.

At the end of the program, students have the opportunity to put their newfound knowledge to work by developing a sustainable business plan for their own companies.

The certification is offered at the ASU campus in Tempe and online through the Center for Professional Development in the Fulton School.

For more information about the certification program, contact Liz Marquez via e-mail at (liz.marquez@asu.edu).

Blankenship, with the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, can be reached at (480) 965-0123 or (mblanken@asu.edu).
