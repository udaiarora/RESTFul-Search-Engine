December 2, 2003

Public Events connects community with arts

ASU Public Events' Cultural Participation programs take the performing arts off the stage and into the lives of children and adults, through more than 50 outreach activities a year. This November alone, ASU Public Events' Cultural Participation programs will reach more than 3,000 school children from elementary to high school age.

On Nov. 13, more than 40 high school students came to ASU's Gammage Auditorium to learn about professions in the performing arts. The three-hour School to Work program is designed to give high school students the opportunity to plan a mock-theater performance in a professional working theatre environment. The students work in small groups with ASU Public Events' programming, marketing, operational and outreach departments before getting a backstage tour of a Broadway production, this year "42nd Street."

The week of Nov. 17, an Asian American group of drummers, recognized as TaikoProject, performed their world premiere at ASU's Gammage Auditorium, but for several days prior to their performance they were in the community conducting workshops. A three-hour session with ASU's Hispanic Mother Daughter Program reached more than 60 high school students and their mothers. The workshop, guided by members of TaikoProject, increased the participants' awareness of other cultures through demonstration and interactive dialogue about the traditions of taiko drumming and the importance of generations within a cultural context.

On Nov. 21, more than 2,000 school children from 25 elementary schools will converge on Gammage for a special TaikoProject school matinee. School matinees are offered to school groups at various times throughout the year.

For information on any of Public Events' outreach programs, contact ASU Public Events at (480) 965-5062.

Medina, with Public Events, can be reached at (480) 965-1884 or (kariann.medina@asu.edu).
