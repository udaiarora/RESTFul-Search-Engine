GMAT

Amanda V. Brown: Quantitative and Verbal
Ms. Brown graduated from Drew University in 2004 summa cum laude with a Bachelors degree in English and Music. She achieved specialized honors status for her undergraduate degree through the completion and defense of a thesis on flute playing in the nineteenth century, and was initiated into Phi Beta Kappa in 2004. She is currently enrolled as a graduate student at Arizona State University, seeking entrance into a Secondary Education M.Ed. program.

She scored an 800 on the quantitative section and scored in the 99th percentile on the verbal section of the GRE.  On the GMAT, she scored a 770, with a verbal score in the 99th percentile and a quantitative score in the 95th percentile. Her teaching experience includes tutoring of students in various subjects, including Mathematics, Spanish, English as a Second Language, and flute.

John Carter McKnight: Verbal
Mr. McKnight is Program Development Specialist and Prelaw Services Coordinator for PEP. He has taught LSAT courses for ASU for over three years and also teaches the verbal sections of the GRE and GMAT. He is co-author of PEP’s LSAT, GRE and GMAT curricula.

For over ten years, he practiced corporate finance law in New York and California, specializing in high-technology Initial Public Offerings. He has been an officer or director of several nonprofit corporations active in science education and policy advocacy, taught GRE and SAT preparatory courses for Kaplan, Inc., served as editor of several business publications, worked as a freelance writer and consultant on space exploration topics, and was CEO of an engineering design firm.

He holds a J.D. from New York University School of Law, a Masters in International Affairs from Columbia University and a B.A. (honors) in Political Science from the University of California, Santa Barbara. He scored in the top percentile on the LSAT as well as on the verbal and analytical sections of both the GRE and GMAT.

Sudheendra Somashekara: Verbal
Mr. Somashekara is currently a full time M.B.A. student at the W.P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. He graduated in 2003 from the Vishveswariah Technological University in India, with a Bachelors degree in Information Science. Prior to joining the M.B.A. program at ASU, he worked as a Software Engineer at IBM Global Services in Bangalore, India. He has 3 years of experience in the IT industry. At IBM, his responsibilities included software development and maintenance and mentoring and training new recruits in the company in technical areas as well as organizational Quality Processes.

Sudheendra has scored a 90th percentile in the GMAT with a 93rd percentile in the Verbal section of the test. He is passionate about teaching and enjoys doing it. He has experience teaching mathematics to high school students.

Margaretha Warnicke: Quantitative and Verbal
Ms. Warnicke is Curriculum Coordinator and Program Development Specialist for Professional Enhancement Programs. She is the co-author of PEP’s GMAT and GRE course curricula, and has taught its GMAT course.

She was Manager of a Kaplan, Inc. test preparation center in addition to serving as a GRE instructor for the company. She has strong management experience, having worked for an online media company as a Product Manager for several years and more recently as Director of Administration for an automotive import company.

She earned her B.A. in History with Distinction from Carleton College and her M.A. in History with a Certificate in Museum Studies from the University of Delaware. She is a member of Phi Beta Kappa and was a National Merit Scholar. She scored in the 98th percentile on the GMAT, the 92nd percentile on the verbal section of the GRE, and in the 90th percentile on the quantitative section of the GRE.

Alethea Kimmel-Guy: Verbal
Ms. Kimmel-Guy earned a B.S. in Geography with an Environmental Studies Certificate from the Ohio University, graduating summa cum laude in 2005.  She was inducted into Phi Beta Kappa in 2005, and received an Award of Excellence from Phi Kappa Phi and the Outstanding Student in Geography award the same year.  Winner of a nationally competitive Jack Kent Cooke Foundation Graduate Fellowship, Ms. Kimmel-Guy read for a Masters in Science of Geography: Environment, Science, and Society, at the University College London.  She is currently in her first year of pursuing a Ph.D. in Geography from Arizona State University, where she is an Associate with the National Science Foundation’s IGERT program in Urban Ecology.  She scored in the 97th percentile on the verbal portion of the GRE.
