HRMS Decentralized Entry Documentation Policy

Each department is to maintain a personnel file for each of their employees. The file should contain minimally:

  1. Internal departmental authorization for each personnel action as defined and required by each department. Only with department head approval may a department choose not to require copies of internal authorization. Required prior to data entry.
  2. Documentation of approval for actions requires approval outside the department e.g., reclassification special duty assignment, etc, as specified in the SPP/ACD manual. Required prior to data entry.
  3. For Research Release: Documentation of PI/authorized account signer and approved Sponsored Projects form in retroaction. Required prior to data entry.
  4. The turnaround PAF with an indication that the action has been reviewed (initials). If possible, the PAF should be reviewed by someone other that the person who did the entry.
  5. For classified/administrative/academic and service professional staff and faculty the file should contain records as indicated in SPP 1102.

This policy for documentation required in the department for HRMS decentralization is based on the need for audit trails, compliance with applicable federal, state and university regulations, accountability, and the need to trace actions to their source. The policy assumes that:

  • security for online data entry is restricted to the department or agreed upon common departments
  • the individual who is entering data online is unable to affect their own record
  • New hires, promotions, transfers, and termination of benefits-eligible staff continue to be submitted on paper, not online.
  • Items included in official personnel records file in Human Resources
    Each staff member's official personnel records file will be maintained in Human Resources shall contain:
  1. The application for employment and/or resume including job related supplemental sheets for employment physical(s) and criminal history check(s), as applicable
  2. The original Personal Data Form including name, ASU ID number, social security number, date of birth, home address and telephone number, citizenship, sex and ethnic origin
  3. The loyalty oath
  4. Standards for professional conduct for faculty
  5. Income tax withholding forms
  6. Original Personnel Action Form
  7. Performance Evaluations
  8. Letters of commendation or recommendation
  9. Employee written reprimands. The department head may request that these be purged from the records at any time. A staff member may request in writing to the Assistant Vice President for Human Resources or designee that a written reprimand that is inactive for one year be purged.
  10. Exit interview information.

ITEMS MAINTAINED IN SEPARATE RECORDS
The following records will be maintained separately:

  1. Paycheck direct deposit forms
  2. US Immigration and Naturalization Service Employment Eligibility Verification (Form I-9)
  3. Wage Assignment, garnishment and levy orders
  4. Payroll deduction authorizations for insurance plans, retirement, tax sheltered agreements, athletic event tickets, parking decals and the like
  5. Subpoenas
  6. Grievance information
  7. Allegations or investigations regarding the staff member
  8. Arrest records
  9. Medical records

 

RECORDS MAINTAINED IN THE EMPLOYEE'S JOB DEPARTMENT
Departmental files should, at a minimum, contain:

  1. Turnaround PAF's
  2. Attendance records
  3. Documentation to substantiate compensation changes for the employee. Compensation change examples include but are not limited to:

Employee Status
Employee Type
Pay Status
Position Number
Job Classification
Pay rate
% Full Time
Account Number
Earning Type
Rate/Amount
Account %
Start and Stop Dates
