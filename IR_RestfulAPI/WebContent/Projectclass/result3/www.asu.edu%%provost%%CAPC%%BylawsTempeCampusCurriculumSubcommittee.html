Tempe Campus Curriculum Subcommittee (TCCS)
Bylaws

Policies and Procedures Governing Course Approval
Arizona State University– Tempe Campus

 

I. General Policies Regarding Approval of Courses

A. Scope and Emphasis of Courses: The Main Campus Curriculum Subcommittee of
the Academic Senate’s Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee will have the
following criteria in mind when evaluating a course request.

1. The course should have a clear and essential place in the existing program
offerings of the department or similar academic unit, either filling a gap in
the existing course structure (without duplication or needless overlapping).

2. The course should not be an instance of unnecessary or undue
proliferation, that is, of the division of a body of knowledge or a field of
study into parts too small to warrant separate treatment.

3. The content of the course should represent a unified and integrated body of
subject matter.

4. The course should not meet demonstrated student educational needs.

B. Duplication of Courses: As a rule, duplication of courses normally held to be within
the range of another department (or similar academic unit) shall not be approved.
When the Main Campus Curriculum Subcommittee is asked to rule upon a new
course proposal which contains material normally held to be within the coverage of
another department, yet not offered in exactly the same form, the subcommittee will
consult with the relevant departments, and will evaluate such matters as:

1. The extent of duplication.

2. The criteria set forth above in Section 1 A.

3. The stated justifications for the new course.

4. The level of instruction.

5. Predicted enrollment.

6. The extent to which the new course is central to the core curriculum of the
program in which it is offered.

7. The availability of the potentially duplicated course to students outside the
department in which it is offered.

8. The effects of the new course on other affected departments.

 

II. Procedures Regarding Approval of Courses

A. Main Campus Curriculum Subcommittee of the Academic Senate’s Curriculum and
Academic Programs Committee

1. Membership: The subcommittee shall consist of a chairperson and four
representatives from a variety of colleges. The CAPC chair and a
representative from the Provost’s Office serve in an ex officio capacity. The
chairperson of the subcommittee shall be appointed annually by the
Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee chair. Members shall serve
two years, on a rotating basis, with half the subcommittee selected each
year.

2. Duties

(a) The subcommittee shall, subject to the approval of the vice provost for
academic programs, grant or deny final approval of all proposals for
new courses.

(b) The subcommittee may delegate to the vice provost for academic
programs the authority to grant or deny final approval of proposals
deleting existing course listings from the University Catalog.

(c) The subcommittee may delegate to the vice provost for academic
programs the authority to grant or deny final approval of proposals
changing existing course listings in the University Catalog. Such
changes may concern, among other things, course numbers, titles,
descriptions, credit hours, and prerequisites.

B. New Course Proposals

1. Stages of Review

(a) Each proposal for a new course shall be reviewed and approved at
the department and college levels before review by the Main Campus
Curriculum Subcommittee.

(b) If a new course is to be included in an upcoming University Catalog, it
shall be submitted to the Main Campus Curriculum Subcommittee by
the date established by the catalog coordinator.

(c) The vice provost for academic programs and the Main Campus
Curriculum Subcommittee chair shall review all courses to be
presented to the subcommittee and shall prepare an agenda to
include: courses that appear to meet the criteria for approval (consent
agenda) and courses that should be considered by the subcommittee
(courses for actions). Any member for the subcommittee may, at the
beginning of the scheduled meeting, request that a course consent
agenda and be considered by the subcommittee. Proposers of
courses on the action agenda will be invited to appear before the
subcommittee.

(d) All decisions of the Main Campus Curriculum Subcommittee
concerning new course proposals shall be final and binding, except
that any department objecting to a decision of the committee
approving or denying a new course may appeal that decision to the
vice provost for academic programs within five days after receiving
notification of the committee’s decision.

2. Subcommittee Administrative Procedures

(a) Before reviewing new course proposals, the subcommittee shall:

(1) Publish the title and course description of each new course
proposal in ASU Insight.

 

(2) Make all reasonable efforts to identify all other departments or
similar academic units offering courses on the same or
substantially similar subject matter as that covered by the new
course, and to circulate the new course proposal to such
departments or similar academic units.

(b) When an objection is made concerning the approval of a course,
either by an interested faculty member of department, or by a member
of the subcommittee, the subcommittee shall receive all further
information it deems pertinent concerning the new course proposal.
The burden of establishing that a new course complies with university
policies regarding approval of courses shall be placed upon the
proponent of the course in question.

 

(c) The Main Campus Curriculum Subcommittee shall promptly notify all
affected departments of its decisions concerning the approval of
courses.

 

3. Information to be Submitted to the Subcommittee

Each proposal for a new course to be included in the University Catalog
shall include, on an appropriate form provided by the subcommittee:

(a) A concise course title, not to exceed four or five words in most
instances.

(b) A catalog description, limited to 20 words of less, excluding title,
prerequisites and credit hours.

(c) An indication in the appropriate box of the form relative to the method
of instruction (e.g., lecture, lab studio).

(d) An indication in the appropriate box on the form if a course is to be
cross-listed.

(e) An indication in the appropriate box on the form if a course is going to
be considered for General Studies credit.

(f) An indication in the appropriate box on the form if the course is going
to be required for a degree program.

(g) A statement of prerequisites, credit hours, and projected enrollment.

(h) A detailed statement of need and justification for the course, including
an indication of how the proposed course fits into the core of the
overall departmental curriculum.

(i) An identification of all other departments or similar academic units
offering courses which cover the same of substantially similar subject
matter as that contemplated for inclusion in the new course proposal.

(j) A course syllabus.

(k) An indication if there are sufficient library holdings to accommodate
the course.

(l) An indication that the course has been offered at least twice under an
omnibus number and how many students were enrolled (unless the
course is required for a degree program or by an accrediting body).

(m) For upper-division courses, an indication of current faculty qualified to
teach the course.

(n) For upper-division courses, prerequisites are to be included.

(o) For an upper-division course without a prerequisite, the rationale for
offering the course at the upper-division level.

(p) For a 400-level course, an indication of whether the course should be
listed in the Graduate Catalog.

 

C. Proposals for Course Changes

 

1. Stages of Review

 

(a) Each proposal for the course change shall be reviewed and approved
at the department and college levels before review by the Main
Campus Curriculum Subcommittee.

(b) If a course change is to be included in an upcoming University
Catalog, it shall be submitted to the Main Campus Curriculum
Subcommittee by the date established by the catalog coordinator.

(c) Subject to the approval of the subcommittee chair, his/her designee
shall review and approve all course changes.

2. Information to be Submitted to the Subcommittee

a) Each proposal for a change in an existing course in the University
Catalog shall include, on an appropriate form provided by the
subcommittee:

(1) A statement of the action requested, such as a change in a
course number, title description or credit hours.

(2) A statement of the need and justification for the proposed
change in the catalog.

(3) An identification of all other departments or similar academic
units offering courses which cover the same of substantially
the similar subject matter as that contemplated for inclusion in
the course change proposal.

III. Experimental Courses

Nothing contained in these procedures shall be interpreted as a prohibition or limitation
of the opportunity for faculty members to offer new courses under one of the university’s
omnibus numbers. All review and approval or courses offered with an omnibus number
shall be conducted on a departmental or college level, rather than by the Main Campus
Curriculum Subcommittee. It is expected, however, that the policies set forth in Section 1
above will be adhered to by all departments and similar academic units when reviewing
courses proposed under omnibus number.
