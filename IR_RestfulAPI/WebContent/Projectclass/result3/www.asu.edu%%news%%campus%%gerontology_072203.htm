ASU consolidates Gerontology Program

As society faces changes brought on by an aging population, ASU has consolidated the administration of its Gerontology Program to better serve students and the community.

While the program will be housed at ASU West, students at all ASU campuses will have access to classes helping them learn about the fastest-growing segment of the U.S. population.

From 1990 to 2000, Arizona's 65-and-older population increased by almost 40 percent. Projections indicate that nearly 1 million people 65 and older will live in the state by 2015.

"The aging of America demands that we pay attention to issues associated with this demographic phenomenon - everything from health, recreation and sexuality to Social Security and economic and work force issues," says Richard Gitelson, director of ASU West's Gerontology Program, who will oversee the university-wide program.

"This consolidation provides an opportunity for outstanding faculty across the Main, East and West campuses to engage in dialogue about how best to work together to enhance the teaching, research and service mission of the Gerontology Program," says Maria T. Allison, interim dean of the ASU Graduate College, which has housed the Main Campus Gerontology Program. "The Gerontology Certificate will continue to be available to students on all campuses."

Plans call for core courses in the certificate program to be available online. Elective courses will make use of faculty expertise in a variety of disciplines, such as psychology, sociology, business, social work and recreation and tourism management, at each campus.

"The fact that the campuses of ASU offer a wide-ranging and interdisciplinary curriculum made it logical for us to connect all three campuses into one program," Gitelson says. "In addition to enabling students to learn about the variety of topics associated with aging, we are committed to making the university's expertise available to the community."

Community members will have the opportunity to interact with gerontology faculty when ASU West hosts a half-day conference on aging, Jan. 24, 2004. Gerontology faculty member Kathleen Waldron is working with university and community representatives to plan the event. Workshops are expected to address topics including care-giving, financial planning, health and wellness, Alzheimer's disease and more.

For information about the gerontology program at ASU, call (602) 543-6642.

Crum, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or (matthew.crum@asu.edu).
