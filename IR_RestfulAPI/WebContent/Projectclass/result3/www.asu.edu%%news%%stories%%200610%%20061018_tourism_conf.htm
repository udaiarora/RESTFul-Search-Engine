October 18, 2006

Conference addresses tourism safety, security issues

Hundreds of tourism professionals and public safety officials will gather Nov. 3 in Phoenix for Arizona's first-ever Arizona Tourism Safety & Security Conference, presented by ASU's School of Community Development and Resources, Arizona Hotel & Lodging Association, and the Arizona Tourism Alliance.

Tourism is more sensitive to concerns over safety and security than most industries, and recent incidents of crime, terrorism, civil disorder and natural disasters make it clear how vulnerable the industry is to such concerns. These incidents also demonstrate some of the challenges police and security professionals face in protecting and serving visitor communities.

Many of Arizona 's regional economies depend on tourism, which makes protecting this industry a vital issue. Last year, 31 million domestic and international overnight travelers visited Arizona and spent $17.5 billion, which equates to nearly $48 million pumped daily into the state's economy.

The conference, to be held at the Phoenix Convention Center, will offer educational and networking opportunities for tourism industry professionals and public safety officials.

Edward J. Gabriel, director of crisis management for Walt Disney Co., will present the opening address, ?Preparedness is the Key,? at 8:30 a.m. Gabriel was the deputy director of New York's Office of Emergency Management during the 9/11 terrorist attacks, which caused business communities, government agencies and the tourism industry to confront the threats of terrorism and large-scale disasters. He will share how he helped manage the catastrophic aftermath of 9/11, and also provide insight into how to recover from crisis situations ? and how to avoid them altogether through preparation.

Other highlights of the conference include presentations from Frank Navarrete, director, Arizona Department of Homeland Security; John E. Lewis, special agent in charge, FBI, Phoenix Division; Robert E. Mittelstaedt Jr., dean, W. P. Carey School of Business; and Joan L. Eisenstodt, chief strategist, Eisenstodt & Associates LLC. A panel session will feature Phil Black, director of safety and security, Westin Kierland Resort & Spa; Paul Blue, deputy aviation director, Phoenix Aviation Department; Commander Jeff Halstead, Phoenix Police Department Homeland Defense Bureau; and Detective C. J. Wren, Arizona Counter Terrorism Information Center .

?Only a few other states have offered similar conferences, so we expect some cutting-edge ideas to emerge as a result of this project,? says ASU economist and tourism professor Tim Tyrrell, who will chair the closing session.

To register, or to become a sponsor or exhibitor, visit the Web site (www.AZTourismSafety.com) or call (602) 265-2252. The registration deadline is Oct. 20.
