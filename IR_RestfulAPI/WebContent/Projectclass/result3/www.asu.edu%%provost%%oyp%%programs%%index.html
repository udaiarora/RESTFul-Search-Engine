Programs


Astronomy Fair

The Astronomy Fair is a science-based program for students in kindergarten through fourth grade that is conducted in conjunction with the STARLAB portable planetarium. The program’s curriculum establishes connections between astronomy and a variety of subject areas including mathematics, language arts, and social studies. In May, the program was piloted in partnership with Programs for Talented Youth and the NASA/Mars Education Program.

 

Financial Aid and Academic Planning

Financial Aid and Academic Planning is a workshop designed for 7th and 8th grade students and their parents. This workshop provides participants with information regarding high school courses needed for higher education, strategies to explore careers and colleges, and details about financial assistance for college.

 

Junior Law

Junior Law is an outreach program offered by the College of Law and the Office of Youth Preparation at Arizona State University. The program is designed to introduce middle-school students to logical problem solving, the benefits of academic achievement, and the requirements for becoming an attorney. During in-class, fifty-minute presentations, students from the College of Law at Arizona State University guide middle school students through the facts, the arguments, and the decisions of cases which were brought before the United States Supreme Court.


Kaleidoscope

Kaleidoscope is an academic enrichment experience offered to schools which links the performing arts and the educational community by providing a theatrical experience for Arizona middle and high school students. The program recognizes, encourages, and motivates students who do not have the opportunity to experience cultural events such as Broadway productions at ASU’s Gammage Auditorium. Kaleidoscope is a collaborative effort between the Office of Youth Preparation and ASU Public Events.


Leaps & Bounds: A Kindergarten Readiness Program

Children who enter kindergarten without readiness skills tend to lag behind other students throughout the foundational years of school. Approximately one hundred elementary-grade teachers were asked the question, “What are the minimum skills that a child should know or be able to perform upon entering kindergarten?” This presentation provides the top ten responses to this question along with a variety of activities that parents can do at home to prepare their child for kindergarten. Parents learn ways to help the child build skills that will prepare him or her for a successful kindergarten experience.

 

Options for Excellence

Potentially outstanding high school students are given special classes that require them to do college level work while still in high school. Advanced placement exams are offered by the College Board at the completion of the course work. Students who demonstrate by examination that they are qualified will receive college credit for the work they are accomplishing in high school.

 

Programs for Talented Youth

Programs for Talented Youth is a program at Arizona State University’s Main Campus that offers talented fourth through seventh grade students an opportunity to experience challenging courses from a variety of academic disciplines. Utilizing the University’s resources, the program is designed to provide students with courses that are interdisciplinary, enriching, and stimulating. These educational opportunities are provided on a year round basis: a three-week program in the summer, and a six-week Saturday program in the fall and spring.
www.asu.edu/talentedyouth


Young Writers Program

The Young Writers Program provides opportunities for the K-12 community to work with ASU poets and writers in order to build student confidence through creative writing. The goals of the program are to increase the number of students who have access to a meaningful experience in creative writing, to promote creative writing as an important means of motivating student writing, and to create a system for the long-term support of creative writing in a given setting.
