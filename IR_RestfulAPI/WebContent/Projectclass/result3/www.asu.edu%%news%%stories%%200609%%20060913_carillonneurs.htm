September 13, 2006

Double the music: ASU welcomes 2 carillonneurs

It's been a long time coming ? about 40 years, to be exact ? but ASU finally has an official university carillonneur.

Two carillonneurs, actually.

William (Bill) Swayze and Kevin Snow have been named the first university carillonneurs, and they will perform on the refurbished, re-dedicated ASU Symphonic Carillon.

Swayze and Snow will give public performances and play for special occasions, such as commencement, on the 148-bell carillon that was a gift to the university from Associated Students of ASU in 1966.

The carillon has ?tubular? cast bells, which hang in glass cases on the wall, as opposed to the large cast bells found on many Eastern university campuses. It is played with an organ-like keyboard.

Swayze, who was the carillonneur at Kirk in the Hills Presbyterian Church in Bloomfield Hills, Mich., said the carillon is unlike any other keyboard instrument.

?It's not an organ and not a piano,? he says. ?It's a percussive, classical sound that has been heard for centuries ? that now, in its present form, is capable of supporting musical virtuosity. Aside from that, it's fun!?

Maas-Rowe, the manufacturer, which is located in Escondido, Calif., made just 100 carillons with large keyboards similar to ASU's.

ASASU bought the carillon at the suggestion of then-university President Homer Durham.

Durham described how the bells would enrich special campus events and add an important musical dimension to the great university that he and the students were endeavoring to build.

The carillon, which is housed in the Zuni Room of the Memorial Union on the Tempe campus, was used a few years, then put into storage. It remained forgotten until 2002, when it was rediscovered.

The carillon was re-dedicated in October 2005 following a fund-raising campaign.

Swayze and Snow bring different talents ? and experiences ? to the carillon keyboard.

Swayze is an expert in improvisation, and he studied organ and carillon with Frederick L. Marriott, who was a student of noted organist Marcel Dupre.

Swayze, the senior staff accompanist for the Herberger College of Fine Arts Dance Department and manager of the department's recording studio, has had a long career as a jazz pianist and bassist, percussionist, composer and arranger. He also is a composer and arranger for DanceLife USA . He has his own recording studio, as well as a composing and arranging business.

Snow grew up in a musical family and was exposed from a young age to a variety of musical styles, including classical, American folk and sacred choral.

He plays organ, French horn and baritone saxophone, and by high school he was writing and arranging music for solo piano, small instrumental ensembles and choir.

Snow studied music theory and composition at ASU, where he also was a student of classical piano and baroque harpsichord. As a student, he also played with Marimba Maderas de Comitan and the South Indian Karnatika Music Ensemble.

He is a piano soloist, church organist, electronic music recording artist, and composer and arranger.

The Carillon Society is raising funds to buy an additional 100 bells, at a cost of just under $25,000. With the new bells ? 49 Flemish bells and a 61-bell harp/celeste instrument ? the university's carillonneurs will be able to use the instrument's full range of voicing and instrumentation.
