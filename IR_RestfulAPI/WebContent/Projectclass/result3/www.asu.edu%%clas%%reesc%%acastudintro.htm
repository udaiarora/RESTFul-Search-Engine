Academic Programs

The ASU Russian and East European Studies Center administers a comprehensive array of academic programs designed to meet the needs of undergraduate and graduate students engaged in study of Eastern Europe and Eurasia.  The Centerâ??s nationally recognized Critical Languages Institute offers intensive summer training in several of the less commonly taught languages of the region. CLI language training programs are closely linked to overseas August on-site practicums and academic-year study abroad programs. The August practicums and academic-year study abroad programs build upon well established inter-university agreements that assure quality of instruction for all overseas REESC programming. REESC overseas programs, which are administered in cooperation with the ASU International Programs Office or ASU Summer Sessions, are open to both ASU and non-ASU students.

In addition to its own CLI and overseas programming, the Center collaborates with ASU schools and departments in offering coordinated programs of undergraduate and graduate training.  At the undergraduate level, REESC administers a baccalaureate certificate program designed to reward students who combine advanced area-related language study with trans-disciplinary coursework on Eastern Europe and Eurasia.  Beginning in the fall of 2005, the Center will offer its own Russian/East European track within the new trans-disciplinary baccalaureate degree program in Global Studies.

At the graduate level, the Center cooperates with more than a dozen ASU schools and departments that offer M.A. and Ph.D. training with concentrations involving the East European/Eurasian area.  Beginning in the fall of 2006, the Center will be offering a Russian/East European track within the new interdisciplinary Global Studies masterâ??s degree program. 

The University Libraries has become one of the richest repositories in the American southwest for English and foreign language materials on Eastern Europe and Eurasia. A guide to the Slavic holdings of the ASU Libraries, including its rich serial holdings, can be found on-line and in the accompanying web page.
