Meet our Staff

Director

DR. DELIA S. SAENZ

Director and Associate Professor of Psychology

Dr. Saenz was hired as Director in July, 2004. In addition to holding this administrative post, Dr. Saenz is a member of the faculty in the Department of Psychology, and a Research professor in the ASU Hispanic Research Center. Her doctorate, in Social Psychology, was awarded from Princeton University in 1987, and she served previously as an assistant professor of Psychology at the University of Notre Dame before moving to ASU. During her tenure at ASU, Dr. Saenz has served administratively as Director of the Graduate Program in Social Psychology, and as interim associate dean of the Graduate College. She has also held numerous leadership positions in a number of campus organizations.

Her research over the past 15 years has focused on tokenism, intergroup processes, acculturation, social identity and family dynamics, and has included both laboratory and field research. Her journal articles on tokenism are often cited for their innovation and contribution to the understanding of diversity in work groups. Dr. Saenz is currently the principal investigator of Diversification and the Academy Post-Grutter, a project that focuses on experiences of women of color faculty in higher education. This research is supported by a grant from the Ford Foundation. Her previous work has been supported by the National Science Foundation, and the National Institutes of Mental Health.

Beyond her contributions to scholarship and administration, Dr. Saenz has been recognized at both institutional and national levels for outstanding contributions to the teaching and mentoring of undergraduate students and graduate students of color.

Assistant Director

THOMAS WALKER

Thomas has been involved in the diversity field for sixteen years, working with NCCJ-Anytown, the National Coalition- Building Institute, the Southern Institute for Education and Research, and C. Harris Companies, Inc. consulting. At ASU, he has been active with Classified Staff Council, Ubiquity, the Leadership Development Team and the Maroon & Gold Scholarship Program, and currently advises ASU's LGBTQ student Coalition and Phi Eta Sigma/ Alpha Lambda Delta honor society. He joined the Center in 1999, and became Asst. Director in November of 2005. He also teaches as a Faculty Associate in the Hugh Downs School of Human Communication.

Areas of responsibility: Oversees Center personnel and student/staff programs, coordinates organizational developmental efforts, and coordinates local and national collaborative relationships.

Subject Interests: language and prejudice; issues around ability, age and sexual orientation; and "whiteness."

Recent works: Anijar, Karen; Chen, Hsueh-hua Vivian; and Walker, Thomas E. (2005). Poofs-Cheesy and Other: Identity Politics as Commodity in South Park, in Mary M Dalton and Laura R. Linder, Eds., The Sitcom Reader: America Viewed and Skewed. SUNY Press: New York.

Education: working on doctorate; M.A., Intercultural Communication (ASU, 1997); B.A., Interpersonal and Group Communication (Tulane, 1995); year abroad at the Victoria University of Manchester (1993-94).

TERI MURPHY

Teri has worked in the field of conflict transformation for over 20 years. She is focused especially on identity based conflicts with an emphasis on the roles religion and religious identity play in the creation and cessation of conflict and violence. As adjunct faculty, she also teaches classes in community and social justice, globalization of human rights, transitional justice, conflict transformation and peacebuilding. Teri is the co-director of the South Africa Community Fund out of which she has been directing academic and service learning programs in Cape Town for the last several years.

Teri's professional career has been primarily nested in the arena of Higher Education Administration. She served as an Episcopal chaplain at ASU and an Assistant Dean of Chapel at Gordon College at which time she created and administrated international service learning programs under the auspices of local or indigenous NGO's. Through these experiences abroad, her interest in peace building was heightened. A trained mediator, she now serves as a conflict transformation facilitator and consultant in a variety of international and domestic contexts.

Areas of responsibility: Focusing on student leadership programs, including the new InVision retreat!

Education: M.A., Educational Ministry (Wheaton, 1983); B.A., Elementary Education (Taylor, 1980)
