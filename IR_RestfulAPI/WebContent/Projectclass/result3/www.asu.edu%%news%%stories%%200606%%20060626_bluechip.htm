June 26, 2006

Arizona economy going strong

Reports for the first quarter show that Arizona's economy is firing on all cylinders so far in 2006, according to an analysis in the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Economic Outlook.

"A few months ago, there were expectations that, as building activity peaked, construction jobs soon would dry up, pulling down the rest of the economy," says Lee McPheters, contributing editor.  "But during the first quarter, construction averaged 231,000 jobs, a gain of 13.5 percent over the first quarter of 2005 and an all-time construction employment record for any quarter in Arizona history."

So far, there is no slowdown in sight, according to McPheters.

"The Arizona economy is continuing to perform at the strong growth pace of 2005," he says.

 For all of last year, employment grew by 5.3 percent, second-fastest in the nation, behind Nevada. Arizona's 2006 first quarter job growth actually accelerated, with an average gain over the first three months of the year at 5.5 percent. In 2005, the Arizona economy added 125,600 new jobs and construction accounted for 21 percent.  

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip, editor Tracy Clark reported that the forecast panel is keeping an eye on the gradual economic slowdown at the national level.  "This gradual slowing of the national economy is not a bad thing," said Clark.  "If economic growth had continued for very long at the heated 2004 pace of 4.2 percent, the Federal Reserve would have had to raise interest rates much higher than it has already done, in order to keep a lid on inflation."

The Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L.
