January 22, 2003

University to honor educators with MLK leadership Award

Two women who have made an indelible mark on the landscape of black education have been named to receive the 2003 Martin Luther King Jr. Servant Leadership Award from ASU.

Sisters Betty and Jean Fairfax of Phoenix will be honored at ASU's annual MLK celebration breakfast at 7 a.m., Jan. 29, in the Memorial Union. Arizona Gov. Janet Napolitano and ASU President Michael Crow will also speak at the event.

Also receiving this year's MLK Servant Leadership award will be ASU social work senior Andrea Chavez, who has dedicated her time outside class to helping the homeless and hungry. A leader in campus service groups, she prepares food bags for distribution on Mill Avenue, serves 700 meals a week at a Phoenix shelter and mentors underprivileged children.

Betty and Jean Fairfax have been influential educators, reformers, civil rights activists and philanthropists for 60 years. Jean has been a college dean, an administrator of civil rights programs for the American Friends Service Committee and a division director for the NAACP Legal Defense and Educational Fund. Betty came to Phoenix in 1950 to teach at the segregated all-black Carver High School, later becoming a counselor and dean of students at Central High.

The two caused a stir in 1987 when they adopted the 8th grade class at the Mary McLeod Bethune School and promised scholarships of $1,000 a year to all of the 92 graduates of the inner-city school who finished high school and went to college.
This will be the 18th year for ASU to pay tribute to Martin Luther King Jr. with free exhibits and events at all three campuses. Schoolchildren from throughout the state participate in ASU's annual MLK poster-essay contest, with the winners attending the celebration breakfast with their parents, teachers and principals. This year's contest had 1,508 entries.

Alonzo Jones, executive coordinator of the ASU Multicultural Center, will kick off the celebration with a presentation at 3 p.m., Jan. 23, in the MU Fiesta Room on "Echoes of a King: a Reflection Upon the Words of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr."

Several hundred students will perform a day of service Jan. 25 in Guadalupe, doing clean-up projects with the Boys & Girls Club, a senior center and other agencies. Andrea Chavez will speak at a student rally at noon Jan. 29, urging students to find ways to serve others in the spirit of King's ideals.

For information on events and contest winners, visit the Web site (www.asu.edu/mlk). The winning posters also will be displayed on the main ASU Web site during January at (www.asu.edu/photogallery).
