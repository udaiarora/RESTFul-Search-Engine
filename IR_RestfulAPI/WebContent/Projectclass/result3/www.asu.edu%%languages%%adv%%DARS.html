DARS AUDITS AND DECLARATIONS OF GRADUATION FOR CHINESE, FRENCH, GERMAN, ITALIAN, JAPANESE, RUSSIAN and SPANISH MAJORS

ALL STUDENTS MUST FILE A DECLARATION OF GRADUATION FORM BEFORE THEY COMPLETE 87 CREDIT HOURS.

WHAT IS A DARS AUDIT?
DARS stands for DEGREE AUDIT REPORTING SYSTEM. A DARS Degree Audit is a computer program that compares your transcripts, including any Arizona transfer credits and previously evaluated out-of-state transfer credits, to your university, college and major requirements.  It checks each category and indicates what requirements have been filled and which ones need to be completed before graduation.  An audit can be run at any time to check your progress toward graduation in your major, just go to Request/View Your Degree Audit at ASU Interactive. To run an accurate report, you should specify your catalog year.  For help on determining your catalog year, contact the DLL advisor or the ASU General Catalog for more information. You must have a DARS Audit run in order to complete the Declaration of Graduation (DOG) form.

WHAT IS THE DECLARATION OF GRADUATION (DOG) FORM?
The DOG form records your academic major and catalog year.  It also allows you to declare a minor and/or indicate that you are pursuing a university-approved certificate.  When you are pursuing concurrent degrees, you must file Declaration of Graduation forms for both majors.  By signing the form, you certify that you understand the requirements needed to graduate from ASU.

WHEN DO YOU NEED TO FILE A DECLARATION OF GRADUATION (DOG) FORM?
You will need to file a Declaration of Graduation form during or before the semester that you will complete 87 credits.  The Declaration of Graduation does not mean that you plan to graduate in that semester, but rather it is a checkpoint so that we review your DARS report and fix a catalog year under which you are completing graduation requirements.  The Declaration of Graduation form is very important to complete in a timely manner because you will not be able to register for classes for the following semester until the form is processed. You should receive a letter in the mail from the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Graduation Office that lets you know the deadline for turning it in.  To file the DOG form, you must make an appointment with one of the DLL Academic Advisors.  It is recommended that you make the appointment within the first month and a half of the semester in which you will arrive at 87 credits so as to comfortably meet the deadline. 

WHERE DO YOU TURN IN THE DECLARATION OF GRADUATION FORM?
After your appointment, take the completed and signed Declaration of Graduation form to Foundation Building #1120, CLAS Academic Programs Graduation Office.  The Foundation Building is on the northeast corner of University and College Avenues, just across University Ave from the Languages & Literatures building.

ONLY ATHLETES MUST FILE A PROGRAM OF STUDY.

WHAT IS A PROGRAM OF STUDY?
The Program of Study is a form that indicates the courses you are taking during the current semester and the courses you intend to take to complete your Bachelor of Arts degree. This method of verifying graduation requirements has been phased out at ASU, and is only used by athletes so that they are able to meet eligibility requirements in their sports.  If you are not an athlete and have previously submitted your Program of Study for your major and need to change any of the classes on your program, please contact a DLL Academic Advisor for how to proceed.
