May 8, 2006

ASU readies for 6,000 graduates

More than 6,000 students will graduate May 11 from ASU, most entering a robust job market that is hungry for their skills. The new graduates will receive their degrees from ASU President Michael Crow at a 10 a.m. ceremony in Wells Fargo Arena.

Among them will be about 190 nurses, 460 teachers, 620 engineers and 1,400 business graduates, almost half of these with MBAs. There are more than 300 architects, designers and planners, 320 fine arts graduates and almost 550 natural scientists.

Outstanding seniors receive top honors

Nineteen ASU graduating seniors will receive Outstanding Graduate awards from the ASU Alumni Association at commencement May 11, after having been nominated by their individual colleges. These students combine high academic achievement with leadership and service to the community. Read more

Among the most popular degrees: biology, psychology, communication and justice studies.

Employers expect to hire 14 percent more new college graduates this year than they did last year, according to a study released in April by the National Association of Colleges and Employers. Heavy recruiters at ASU include the financial services industry - including investment companies, banks and insurance - as well as school districts, construction firms and retail.

ASU graduates will include a woman who has been blind since birth and is now receiving her doctorate, an award-winning writer who is a decorated Iraq War veteran, an immigrant who went from picking strawberries five years ago to graduating in speech and hearing science, and a mother and daughter who kept each other going through many years of struggle.

ASU will award honorary degrees to two higher education leaders. Wu Qidi, vice minister of education of the People's Republic of China, will be honored for achievements as a leader in higher education and exceptional work in academic research. Frank H.T. Rhodes, educa-

tion policy expert and former president of Cornell University , will receive the award for his vision and leadership on the role of higher education in America .

A free breakfast for graduates and their guests will take place from 8:30 - 9:30 a.m. on Frank Kush Field at Sun Devil Stadium, with a no-host luncheon at noon.

Parking is free throughout campus that day, except in visitor lots and metered spaces. All roads are expected to be open, though lane restrictions are in effect for light rail construction nearby. Guests should arrive early to avoid delays.

More information on the spring graduation schedule can be found at the Web site www.asu.edu/graduation.
