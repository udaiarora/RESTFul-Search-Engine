Frequently Asked Admission Questions for Undergraduate International Students

The answers to all of these admissions-related questions can be found at www.asu.edu/admissions/international.

What are the admission requirements for undergraduate international students?
Read the International Undergraduate Student Admission Requirements and Application Procedures at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html.

How can I apply to ASU as an undergraduate international student?
Know the entire process by reviewing the guidelines at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html and complete International Undergraduate Student Admission Requirements and Application Procedures.

What documents must I send with my undergraduate admission application?
Read step number 2 of the International Undergraduate Student Admission Requirements and Application Procedures at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html.

How much does it cost to attend ASU as an undergraduate?
Read current estimated expenses at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/expenses.html.

Does ASU have a financial guarantee form for undergraduate admission?
Read step number 4 of the International Undergraduate Student Admission Requirements and Application Procedures at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html.

How can I pay the U.S. $50 application fee?
Read step number 1 of the International Student Admission Requirements and Application Procedures at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html for information about paying the application fee.

Is taking the SAT a requirement for undergraduate admission to ASU?
No. Read step number 3 at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html for more information about English language proficiency requirements (TOEFL/IELTS scores) and the exceptions.

Must I take the TOEFL, if I have been studying in the United States?
Read step number 3 at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html for more information about English language proficiency requirements (TOEFL/IELTS scores) and the exceptions.

What English language proficiency tests are accepted by ASU for undergraduate international admission?
You can submit either a TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) or an IELTS (International English Language Testing System) score. Read step number 3 at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html for more information about English language proficiency requirements (TOEFL/IELTS scores) and the exceptions.

Where do I send my application materials if I am applying for a program offered at ASU's Polytechnic campus or ASU's West campus?
All application materials must be sent to the address provided at ASU's Tempe campus regardless of where your program is offered. Read step number 1 at www.asu.edu/admissions/international/apply.html to learn where to send application materials.

I only have official /original credential papers. How do I submit acceptable copies to ASU?
You can submit a photocopy of your credentials that have been verified as true copies of the original by a responsible official of the institution that you attended or by an educational representative of the your home country's government or embassy. Each paper of the credentials must bear the verifying official's signature and stamp.

When do I get my I-20 form?
After your file has been completed for admissions, ASU's undergraduate admissions reviews your file to determine if you are eligible for admission to ASU. If you are admitted, then we will issue your I-20 and send it along with other important information to you in the mail.

How can I apply to live on campus in the university residence halls?
Living on campus is the best way to experience life as a student at ASU. Space is very limited. Therefore, after you have been admitted, you need to submit your residence hall application as soon as possible.
For more information about living on campus, visit www.asu.edu/viewbook/onCampus.
For information about off-campus housing near ASU, visit asasu.net/usg/services/off-campus-housing.php.

How do I know that my undergraduate admission application is complete?
You will receive a letter from ASU's undergraduate admissions office that lists and requests the missing documents. You may also check your admissions status online. In order to check your admission status, you must activate your ASURITE UserID and choose a password. You ASURITE UserID, and a temporary password, are assigned to you when you apply to ASU. Once you have activated your ASURITE UserID (which includes choosing a new password), you are able to check your admission status through ASU Interactive (ASU's online student service.) For more information about activating your ASURITE UserID and using ASU Interactive, visit www.asu.edu/admissions/steps/computer.html.

As an international student, do I qualify for financial aid or scholarships?
Because ASU is a publicly funded institution, ASU is not able to provide funds in the form of loans or financial aid for international students. ASU does not offer scholarships to international undergraduate students.
