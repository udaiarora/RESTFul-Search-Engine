April 16, 2003

Two-Day Forum explores disappearances in Mexican border town

On April 17, ASU West will host a special screening of "Señorita Extraviada/Missing Young Woman," a documentary film about the tragic disappearances and murders of more than 300 young women from Ciudad Juarez, Mexico during the past decade.

The screening will begin at 6 p.m. in the La Sala Ballroom at 4701 W. Thunderbird Road and will be followed by a discussion with the filmmaker, Lourdes Portillo, and a number of activists from Juarez. Ms. magazine recently selected Portillo as one of its 2002 Women of the Year.

Portillo's appearance and the presentation of her film will round out the two-day event "Gender, Justice and the Border," April 16 and 17 at ASU West.

"We are seeking to raise awareness about the terrifying situation in Ciudad Juarez and to help in making a change in the conditions for women there," says Bill

Simmons, assistant professor of political science in Social and Behavioral Sciences at ASU West. "The ASU West community is bringing together the major players for discussion, gathering thousands of signatures on petitions, collecting food donations for the border area, raising money to send to Juarez and putting together a public art project that will travel to many other venues."

The schedule of events will include a candlelight vigil for the women of Juarez at 9 p.m., April 16, in front of Fletcher Library and panel discussions on "Gender, Justice and the Border" - including activists, politicians, government officials, law enforcement personnel, civic leaders and scholars, from 1 to 4 p.m., April 17 (University Center Building, La Sala).

According to Simmons, the "Gender, Justice and the Border" forum was developed to apply university expertise to help the public understand and act on the horrific incidents in Juarez, which tie together issues of violence against women, cross-border globalization, immigration policy and the effects of NAFTA.

"This has truly been a grassroots effort among those on campus and in the community," Simmons says. "Several of us saw the film 'Señorita Extraviada,' and we knew we had to do something about the situation in Juarez."

This Gender, Justice and the Border Forum will be the first of what is planned as an annual event focusing on border justice issues. Visit (www.west.asu.edu/borderjustice/juarez) for information and for a complete schedule of campus events. Information is also available at (602) 543-6089.

Fuller, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5308 or (barbara.fuller@asu.edu).
