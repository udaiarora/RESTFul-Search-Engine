Career Development for Language Majors

On-line Fall 2005, 3 credits, sln# 92567

 

 

The class will cover both the theoretical and practical aspects of career planning and development with a research focus on language-related careers, so that you may:

 
  • Learn how to research occupations in which you may use foreign language skills
  • Identify your skills, talents, and lifestyle and work preferences
  • Learn about national and international job-search resources
  • Learn how to create?and begin to build?a job-search network
  • Identify and understand aspects of organizational culture in both foreign and domestic companies or organizations
  • Learn job-search etiquette in US and international (country specific) settings
  • Produce key job-search tools/documents and learn how to adapt them for use on both the US and international (non-English-speaking) job market
  • Learn interviewing procedures and practices in the US and abroad
  • Explore ways to enhance your academic plan that match your identified career goals

·   Through course readings and by developing your own career narrative, come to understand various career-related continuities within your life-story to project wisely into your future
