Arizona Archives Month - October, 2005

(posted 9/30/05)


In celebration of the past as part of the present and future, October has been designated Arizona Archives Month. The following programs will be presented by Archives and Special Collections, ASU Libraries.


Time: 12:00- 1:30 p.m.
Location: Room 133, 1st Floor, Hayden Library
480-965-3145   www.asu.edu/lib/archives
Brown Bag Lunch

October 7

Guy Gabaldon, American Hero written and produced by Alfredo Lugo; in association with KOCE TV Foundation; directed by George Brennerc,1998. Interview with Guy Galbaldon, World War II Marine who was awarded the Navy Cross for capturing 1,500 Japanese soldiers and civilians on Saipan in 1944. 28 minutes.

Ballad of An Unsung Hero KPBS TV and Cinewest Production; producer, Paul Espinosa; 1983. Uses archival footage, photographs, and recordings, as well as interviews, to chronicle the life of Pedro J. Gonzalez, who served as telegrapher for Pancho Villa, became a radio star and recording artist in the United States, championed the cause of Mexican-Americans. Introduced by Chris Marin, Curator of the Chicano Research Collection.

October 14

Tales of Transformation, told by Don Doyle Professional storyteller Don Doyle tells 3 tales, My World in a Cornfield (growing up in Iowa), The Story of Crooked Mountain (Pima Indian legend explaining the formation of the Superstition Mountains) and Beauty and the Beast (set in a canyon in Sedona). 41 minutes. Introduced by Katherine Krzys, Curator of the Child Drama Collection.

October 21

In whose honor? written and produced by Jay Rosenstein. Discussion of Chief Illiniwek as the University of Illinois mascot, and the effect the mascot has on Native American peoples. Graduate student Charlene Teters shares the impact of the Chief on her family. Interviewees include members of the Board of Regents, students, alumni, current and former "Chiefs" and members of the community. 46 minutes. Introduced by Patricia Etter, Curator of Labriola Center.

October 28   SunDevil Short Films

ASU Generations: Homecoming
See and hear the history of Homecoming at ASU as University Archivist Rob Spindler narrates this digital video presentation of archival film and photography. 15 minutes.

Homecoming 1954
View student-built parade floats and see the Sun Devil Band march on Central Avenue in this amateur silent film from the golden age of Homecoming! 15 minutes.

ASU Generations
Remember the history of ASU in this collage of historic films, photos and music presented by the ASU Libraries! 15 minutes. Introduced by Robert Spindler, Head of Archives and Special Collections.
