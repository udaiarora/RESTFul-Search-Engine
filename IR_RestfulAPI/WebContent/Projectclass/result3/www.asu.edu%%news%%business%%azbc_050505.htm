May 05, 2005

Arizona Business Conditions Index falls in April

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index fell by 5.2 percent in April, from 70.4 to 66.7. An index reading over 50 indicates the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

The April number, although a decline from last month, shows continuing good news for the local economy, according to Dawn McLaren, economic analyst with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU's W. P. Carey School of Business. "Since May 2003, the index has held to a level above the critical 50-point benchmark and has oscillated around a level in the mid-60s, which means continued economic growth."

Employment, at 74.2, was the strongest component of the index. Another positive component was production, which recorded a level of 69.6. "Strong readings in production, employment and new orders spell good news for the local economy in the near term," McLaren said.

The Price Index remains in a range well above the neutral 50-mark. "Local price increases continue, with the Price Index continuing to show upward pressure," McLaren said. "The Arizona Price Index recorded a level of 67.7 in April."

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available on the Web at wpcarey.asu.edu/azbci. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data.
