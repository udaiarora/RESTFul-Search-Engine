January 17, 2006

ASU senior receives math scholarship

ASU mathematics senior Yukiko Kozakai has been chosen as one of eight students nationwide to receive a $3,000 Trjitzinsky Scholarship from the American Mathematical Society.

Yukiko was born in Japan and began college as a business major. She became aware of the beauty of mathematics through her courses in business math and was accepted at ASU to begin work on a mathematics degree in August 2003.

Her interest is in probability and statistics, and she plans to take the first professional actuarial exam this academic year.

The Trjitzinsiky award is made possible by a bequest from the estate of Barbara and Waldemar J. Trjitzinsky.

Waldemar Trjitzinsky, born in Russia in 1901, taught mathematics at the University of Illinois and was the author of about 60 mathematics papers. He showed particular concern for math students who had financial need and made personal efforts to ensure that financial considerations would not hinder their studies.

For the 2005 Trjitzinsky awards, the AMS chose seven geographically distributed schools to receive one-time awards of $3,000 each. The mathematics departments at those schools then chose students to receive the funds.
