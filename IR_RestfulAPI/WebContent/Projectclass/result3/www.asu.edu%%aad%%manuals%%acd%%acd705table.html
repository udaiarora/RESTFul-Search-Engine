Application for Sabbatical Leave: Colleges with Departments or Divisions

The applicant submits the proposal, curriculum vitae, and required supporting data to the chair, director, or supervisor.

The chair, director, or supervisor:

  1. obtains letters evaluating the worth of the proposal from three persons (inside or outside the department or division) competent to judge the proposal

  2. in counsel with appropriate committees, reviews the material and makes a recommendation

  3. writes a letter indicating how the integrity of the programs within the department or division will be maintained during the applicant’s absence

    and

  4. forwards to the dean of the applicant’s college all materials.

The dean receives advice from the appropriate college committee and makes the decision to approve or deny the request.

If the recommendation or decision at the department, division, or college level is adverse, the chair, director, or supervisor informs the applicant of the reasons for the recommendation or decision. The applicant may respond if desired. If the applicant responds, the chair, director, or supervisor reconsiders the decision and communicates the decision and the reasons to the applicant.

The dean sends a list of the college’s applicants for sabbatical leave to the executive vice president and provost of the university (including each applicant’s department affiliation, approval or disapproval of the application, and the time period for the sabbatical).

Application for Sabbatical Leave: Colleges without Departments or Divisions

The applicant submits the proposal, curriculum vitae, and required supporting data to the dean.

The dean obtains letters evaluating the worth of the proposal from three persons competent to judge the proposal, and with counsel of the appropriate committee, reviews the material, and makes the decision to approve or deny the request.

If the decision is to deny the request, the dean informs the applicant of reasons for the decision. The applicant may respond if desired. If the applicant responds, the dean reconsiders the decision and communicates the decision and the reasons to the applicant.

The dean sends a list of the college’s applicants for sabbatical leave to the executive vice president and provost of the university (including each applicant’s department affiliation, approval or disapproval of the application, and the time period for the sabbatical).
