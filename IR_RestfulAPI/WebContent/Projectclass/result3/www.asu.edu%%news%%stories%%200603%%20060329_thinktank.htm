March 29, 2006

Group to review "think tank" reports

Education reports released by private think tanks are now being scrutinized by expert, academic reviewers associated thanks to the just-launched Think Tank Review Project.

The Think Tank Review Project is a collaboration of ASU's Education Policy Studies Laboratory and the University of Colorado's Education and Public Interest Center.

Within two weeks of a report's release the project provides reviews to policy makers, the media, and the public. Reviews are posted on the ASU Education Policy Studies Laboratory Web site at: http://thinktankreview.org .

According to the Education Policy Studies Laboratory Director, Alex Molnar, the project is much needed.

"Despite garnering media attention and their influence with many legislators, reports released by private think tanks are often of very poor quality," Molnar says. "Many think tank reports are little more than ideological argumentation dressed up as research. We believe that policy makers, reporters and the public will greatly benefit from having qualified social scientists provide reviews of these documents in a timely fashion."

According to Molnar, "Project reviews will help the public separate the wheat from the chaff."

 The project is launching with the reviews of three reports released by private think tanks since January. The reports being reviewed include:

. The Cato Institute and the Friedman Foundation's report on Washington D.C. 's voucher program. The review can be found at ( http://www.asu.edu/educ/epsl/EPRU/ttreviews/EPSL-0602-118-EPRU.pdf )
. Review of the Manhattan Institute For Policy Studies and the Hoover Institution's report on retention and social promotion. The review can be found at (http://www.asu.edu/educ/epsl/EPRU/ttreviews/EPSL-0602-119-EPRU.pdf )
 .  The Wisconsin Policy Research Institute's report on school funding and academic achievement. The review can be found at
( http://www.asu.edu/educ/epsl/EPRU/ttreviews/EPSL-0602-117-EPRU.pdf )

Think Tank Review Project reviews and links to reports being reviewed can be found online at ( http://thinktankreview.org ). Biographies of the Think Tank Project reviewers are online at ( http://www.asu.edu/educ/epsl/EPRU/ttreviewpanel.htm ).
The Think Tank Review Project is funded by the Great Lakes Center for Education Research and Practice.
