GRADUATE STUDIES ADVISORY COUNCIL (GSAC)
September 8, 2004
FAB N 303 F

The council met in FAB N 303 F at 1:00 p.m. In attendance were: Joan F. Brett (co-chair), Ronald Davidson (co-chair), Gaylene Armstrong, Candice Bredbenner, Dottie Broaddus, Ray Buss (represented Ida Malian) Mohan Gopalakrishnan, Dennis Isbell, Jeffrey Kassing, Majia Nadesan, Eleanor Perry, Marge Williams, and Paz Zorita. Also in attendance were Deans Maria Allison, Filiz Ozel, and Marjorie Zatz from the Division of Graduate Studies ASU Tempe campus. Excused were: Ida Malian and Julie Ramsden.

The minutes from the April 14, 2004 meeting were approved.

Since there were so many new members this year, the first few minutes of the meeting were spent introducing ourselves and stating the area that we each represented.

The Graduate Studies Advisory Council (GSAC) serves in an advisory role to the Associate Vice provost of Academic Programs and Graduate Studies. As part of its duties, GSAC reviews proposals for new graduate degree programs and concentrations and sets campus specific policies and standards for admissions.


INFORMATION ITEMS

There are three new graduate programs that are being developed. They should be ready for review by the GSAC members soon. The programs are Master of Arts Metropolitan Studies, Master of Arts Justice, Conflict, and Human Rights, and our first doctoral program an Ed.D. from the College of Teacher Education and Leadership. It is anticipated that the Ed.D. program will go to ABOR later this academic year. It must first be reviewed by an external advisor, which is now a requirement for all new doctoral programs.

The accelerated process for submitting new programs is now in place. This process will shorten the approval process by allowing the request for planning and implementation to be processed simultaneously. Deadlines to request planning/implementation authority for new programs for inclusion in the ASU West 2005-2006 catalog was distributed to the members.

The Indication of Support form the Dean for Proposal Development form was distributed to the members of GSAC. This new form is to be used in the curriculum process.

The proposal for the Center for Violence and Public Safety is not ready for committee review; therefore, it will be emailed to the members for review and comments within the next two weeks. This way, any concerns may be addressed prior to the October meeting, which will allow the proposal to be on ABOR's November agenda.

The discussion regarding the role of the committee took many directions, from clarifying consistency of graduate programs (that all graduate programs possess a level of difficulty) to the quality of the content of the graduate program. Some questions were raised such as, how do we scrutinize programs without impeding academic freedom? It was suggested that we formulize a set of criteria to get the discussion going, keeping in mind that not all colleges are at the same level.

It has been recommended that the Travel Grant for graduate students be reestablished. The old form was distributed to the members with the recommendation that the new Travel Grant be considered a "matching fund" up $500.00, which could be used by the student to offset the expense of a job search or the student may attend a conference or seminar in their field of study. The members of the committee were asked to think about imposing a rotating deadline; therefore, not all the funds would be award at the beginning of the academic year. Further discussion will be held at our next GSAC meeting.


DISCUSSION/DECISION ITEMS

The nomination of Najah Williams as Graduate Student Representative was accepted and approved for the 2004-2005 academic year. Marge Williams will contact the student.

Ronald Davidson volunteered to serve as co-chair of GSAC for one more academic year. The committee unanimously approved.


CROSS-CAMPUSES COORDINATION

The members of GSAC were given copies of the appeals policies and procedures for Division of Graduate Studies ASU at the Tempe campus and Graduate Studies ASU at the West campus and asked to review both documents. At our next schedule meeting, the committee will be asked to consider adopting the Tempe policy on which the West policy was originally modeled.


SHARED TOPICS

It was suggested that at each month a specific topic would be discussed to help each other. Some of the topics are: 1) best practices in recruiting; 2) community outreach; 3) survey alumni/contact.

The meeting adjourned at 2:35p.m.
