November 7, 2005

ASU's White to give lecture in defense of attorneys

Tell Patricia White a lawyer joke, and she’ll probably respond with a few of her own. But she’s dead serious about the value of her profession – and she’ll share that message with others, too.

White, dean of the ASU College of Law, will talk about “Why Do We Need Lawyers Anyway?” in the second ASU Honors Lecture of the year, at 5 p.m., Nov. 9, in the Agriculture Building, room 150.

The talk is free and open to the public.

White has a broad background in the law, having practiced with the Washington law firms of Steptoe & Johnson and Caplin & Drysdale, where she specialized in tax. She has been on the faculty at several law schools including Georgetown University, the University of Michigan and the University of Utah.

A fellow of the American College of Tax Counsel, she has published extensively on topics in the areas of taxation and bioethics.

In addition to being on the ASU law faculty, she is an affiliated professor in the Department of Philosophy.

The lecture series is sponsored by the Barrett Honors College, to bring to students and the community a wide range of intellectually interesting scholarly work and issues.

All lectures in the series are free and open to the public.
