ASU graduate student team wins Urban Land Institute competition

Once they decided to enter the competition, they only had 10 days to prepare an entry.

But their teamwork and knowledge paid off, and an interdisciplinary team of Arizona State University's master's students has won Urban Land Institute (ULI) Gerald D. Hines Student Urban Design Competition.

The students beat Harvard University, Massachusetts  Institute of Technology and a combined team from Stanford University and the University of California, Berkeley, winning a $50,000 prize. The sponsoring college receives 10 percent.

And, the ULI team, which includes Prasoon Kumar, Parul Mittal and Timothy Parke, environmental planning; Matthew Muller, architecture; and Mohan Sankrit, business, has been asked to help design a new Arizona Capitol, along with other ASU students, to be completed in time for the state's centennial in 2012.

For the ULI competition, the teams were asked to submit designs for a 57-acre parcel of "underutilized" land adjacent to the Allegheny River, near Pittsburgh's Strip District, that would revitalize the area and reconnect it to the greater community.

ASU's design team, which was assembled by Kumar, named its concept "Destination Allegheny." It includes a plaza that offers a connection from downtown Pittsburgh to the waterfront district plus a place to have festivals or open-air theatrical productions.

Around the plaza, as the team envisions it, would be cafes, night spots and offices. An old railroad shed on the property would be converted to retail, and the students included row houses so there would be a mixture of uses.
