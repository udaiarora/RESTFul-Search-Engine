Learning Resource Center here to help you!

Okay, so school has started again and you have an assignment to write a paper and you are not sure exactly how to begin. Or perhaps a math problem made perfect sense in class and now you are looking at the homework and it looks like gibberish.

Good news! The Learning Resource Center staff is back and ready to assist you with your studies. Writing Tutors can help you get that paper started and our Subject Tutors can help you work on each of your assignments. We also have Software Specialists available to show you how to create a great Power Point presentation and Academic Coaches that can help you with strategies to manage your projects and your time.

The LRC is opening during the day and in the evening in various locations throughout campus, including the Memorial Union, Palo Verde West, Manzanita, Sonora, Hassayampa Academic Village and Student Services building. You don’t have to handle everything alone. We are here to help.

Please contact us at: 480-965-6254 if you have any questions or you can visit our webpage at www.asu.edu/lrc.

Isaac Card Access

The University has adopted a new door access technology that is being deployed campus-wide, including all residence halls on campus. It’s called ISAAC (Integrated System at ASU for Access Control) and uses the latest iClass proximity/smart card technology. This technology is integrated into the new ASU Sun Cards (the ones with a large gold starburst of the left side of the card).

If card is missing or stolen, you MUST 1) Report it to your hall front desk immediately; AND 2) Call 480-965-CARD or report it online at http://www.suncard1.com. Missing or stolen cards will be deactivated and students must obtain a new Sun Card at the Sun Card office. When you receive a replacement card the new card will be enabled with the same access as the old card.

  • Students are responsible for all replacement card fees.
  • Do not punch holes in your Sun Card .
  • No “tailgating.” As you enter a secure area using your Sun Card, DO NOT let people enter the building with you. If you do, you become responsible for those individuals.
  • Do not open the door for others. People who do not have access privileges should contact a Residential Life representative to gain entry.
  • Do not prop doors. Any door left open for an extended amount of time will trigger an alarm. Alarms will not silence until the door is closed.
  • Emergency exit doors are for emergencies ONLY . Alarms will sound when doors are opened.
  • Tampering with security equipment may result in Residential Life License Agreement termination. There is a minimum $500 charge for any damage, vandalism or disabling of fire/emergency equipment.
  • Report all problems with door access to your front desk immediately.
