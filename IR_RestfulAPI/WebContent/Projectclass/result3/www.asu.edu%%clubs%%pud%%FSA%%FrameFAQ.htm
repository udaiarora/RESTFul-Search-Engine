Here are some frequently asked questions about FSA:

 

 
  • What is FSA?
    • A free conference for Phoenix Union High School students which teaches the importance of continuing education after high school and what students should do to succeed.

 

  • How do I apply for FSA?
    • Sign-up during your school visitation day.  
    • Look out for the Phoenix Union Devils table during lunch. 
    • Get applications from your teacher.  
    • Print the application from our website. 

 

  • What will I be doing there?
    • Interacting with fellow Phoenix Union High School students and learning about the importance of college and what it takes to get there through a variety of speakers, interactive activities, and workshops. 

 

  • What important information will I learn?
    • Specific information regarding steps to take during high school to get to college, financial aid info, scholarship info, personal stories of people who are in college who graduated from the PUHSD, and what its like in college.

 

  • I'm not sure if I want to continue schooling after high school, will FSA help me decide what to do?
    • Future Scholars of Arizona will help you better understand how to overcome obstacles stopping you from attending college.  
    • FSA will also give you many tips on what colleges are looking for in order to get into college. 

 

  • I don't want to go to ASU, should I still go to FSA?
    • Yes, FSA is open to all PUHSD students regardless of future educational interest. 

 

  • Who sponsor's FSA?
    • FSA is coordinated by the Phoenix Union Devils student organization and sponsored by the Phoenix Union High School District/Arizona State University Partnership.

 

  • Where is FSA held?
    • FSA is held at Arizona State University Main campus in Tempe, Arizona.  Transportation is provided. 

 

  • What should I take with me to the conference?
    • An eagerness to learn, motivation, a willingness to interact, and the quest to have fun!

 

  • How long will the conference take? 
    • The conference will take place Saturday, February 18, 2006 from 10:00 am until 3:00 pm.
