NEW MILLENNIUM FELLOWSHIPS ESTABLISHED FOR GRADUATE STUDENTS AT ARIZONA STATE UNIVERSITY

The first in a series of Millennium Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowships to aid graduate students completing dissertations have been awarded to four ASU doctoral students. Each of the Millennium Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowships in the series will be named for its donor.

Dana Perry, of Mesa, and Ken Voglesonger, of Tempe, have been selected as the first recipients of George C. Beakley, Jr. Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowships. Peggy Lindauer, of Tempe, is the first recipient of the Nelson Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowship and Larry Ellis, of Phoenix, also receives a Millennium Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowship. Each of the winners is awarded $15,000 to support their groundbreaking, interdisciplinary research.

Perry plans to document which pollutants are dispersing across the valley and discern their origin. To accomplish this, she will identify particles deposited on tree leaves from throughout the Phoenix area. Her interdisciplinary applications of geology, chemistry, engineering, plant biology and statistics represent an innovative approach to the study. Perry's mentors are Regents Professor Peter Buseck and Assistant Professor Karl Booksh in chemistry and biochemistry and James Anderson, an associate research scientist in mechanical and aerospace engineering.

Voglesonger assists John Holloway, professor of chemistry and biochemistry, and Peggy O'Day, assistant professor of geology, in researching a hypothesis that life might have originated in volcanic ocean vents. This work has profound implications for defining favorable planetary environments where conditions may have allowed life to begin.

Voglesonger said, "I am very appreciative that the Graduate College and the sponsors of this award recognize the need for interdisciplinary research, in order to begin to answer some of the big-picture questions in science today — life on other planets and origin of life on earth, for example. In interdisciplinary research, the lines between traditional fields are blurred in order to get a better look at the whole picture."

O'Day said, "Ken is a scientist and educator for the new millennium. He is firmly grounded in interdisciplinary, quantitative, physical sciences — chemistry, geology and physics — and critical analysis. Combined with his growing knowledge and experience in biochemistry and microbiology, he will lead in the emerging fields of astrobiology and geologic biocomplexity."

Lindauer is writing a critical analysis of the Heard Museum exhibition, "Inventing the Southwest: The Fred Harvey Company and Native American Art." Her interdisciplinary work combines education, museum studies, literary analysis, sociological theory and Native American studies. Lindauer works with curriculum and instruction professors Donald Blumenfeld-Jones and Tom Barone, Assistant Professor Laura Tohe in English and Associate Professor Peg Bortner in Justice Studies.

"Being awarded the Nelson Millennium Fellowship is a great honor, representing a tremendous vote of confidence in my ability to carry out interdisciplinary research," Lindauer said. "The award grants me the privilege of concentrating full-time on my work, which in turn will allow me to complete my dissertation in the next 12 months."

Ellis is among the first persons attempting to document and record the storytelling traditions of Southeastern Creek Indians, a highly-acculturated tribe now attempting to restore its native customs. He is mentored by anthropology professor Donald Bahr and English professor Kathleen Sands. "The Millennium Dissertation Fellowship will help to assure a thorough study of the oral traditions of the re-emerging Creek tribes in Florida and Alabama, and an increased academic and public recognition of these seldom-noticed Native American communities," Ellis said.

Applicants for the Millennium Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowship must be engaged in interdisciplinary research that is co-directed by two doctoral mentors from different disciplines. The students must present a well-articulated problem and approach with a clear explanation of why one disciplinary approach will not suffice, and they must have already defended their dissertation proposal.

The Millennium Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowship series originated with Bill Beakley, the son of the late ASU professor George C. Beakley Jr. Bill Beakley and representatives of the Graduate College and the ASU Development Office created the concept of providing fellowships to doctoral students working on innovative, interdisciplinary research.

George C. Beakley Jr. was an engineering professor and associate dean in the engineering college at ASU, where he served from 1956 to 1992. He died in 1998. The award was established in his memory.

The Nelson award is named for Florence and Jerry Nelson, long-time supporters of graduate education at ASU. Florence Nelson is chair of the Dean's Advisory Council, which assists the Graduate College in its efforts to enrich, expand and support graduate education at ASU.

The Graduate College serves as an advocate for all advanced studies at ASU in the state, nationally and internationally.

It coordinates and supports graduate programs on the Main and East campuses and is a liaison with advanced studies at ASU West and other universities. ASU is a Research I university that offers a full range of professional and academic programs for more than 10,000 graduate students. ASU's 91 master's and 48 doctoral degree programs attract graduate students of diverse ethnic and cultural backgrounds. U.S. News & World Report currently ranks 35 ASU programs among national leaders.
