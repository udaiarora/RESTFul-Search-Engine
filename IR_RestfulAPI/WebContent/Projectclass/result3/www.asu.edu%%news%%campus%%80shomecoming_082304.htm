August 23, 2004

ASU moonwalks back to 80s for Homecoming

Slip on your checkerboard Vans, dig out those pastel polo shirts, and pop in a Cyndi Lauper tape - the 80s are back! At least they will be for ASU's 80th annual Homecoming, which will feature the second annual Homecoming FestDevil Block Party on Saturday, Nov. 6 along University Drive in front of Old Main.

Virgil Renzulli, vice president for Public Affairs, says last year's first-ever ASU Homecoming Block Party was a huge success and the goal is to make this year's even better.

The Block Party, which will follow the parade, is the Homecoming FestDevil's signature event for the entire community. The celebration is a particularly special time for alumni to return to their alma mater to reconnect with fellow alums.

Like last year, this year's Block Party will feature a Kid's Zone; an Art Walk, sponsored by the Herberger College of Fine Arts; a book signing by ASU alumni authors; an Extreme Zone for the daring at heart; food booths; live music; and tents hosted by colleges, schools, alumni chapters, clubs, etc.

In addition to the Block Party, multiple other events are being planned throughout the week. As planning continues, updated information, and registration forms for departments and other organizations ordering tents, will be at (www.asu.edu/homecoming).

Monday, Nov. 1

. Night of A Thousand Laughs, 7 p.m., Tempe Improv. ASU will host the 10-year anniversary reunion of the Barren Mind comedy troupe. At least 50 alumni of the group will be in attendance to watch and perform with Barren Mind.

Tuesday, Nov. 2

. Election Day Event, 10 a.m. - 4 p.m. An all-day event to encourage registered voters to get out and vote. Live music, free food, and activities.
. Night Out in Tempe, TBD, Downtown Tempe. All ASU students, faculty, staff and alumni are invited to spend the evening exploring Tempe. Enjoy dinner and drinks in a variety of restaurants with Sun Devil Discounts. Take in a free classic 80s movie screening as well.

Wednesday, Nov. 3

. Sparky's Concert, 4 - 8 p.m., location TBD. Join Sparky as ASU kicks off the weekend with a big name concert. Enjoy a free concert on campus while experiencing free food, contests and games. Additionally, sign up to participate in the annual sand volleyball tournament.

Thursday, Nov. 4

. Homecoming Masquerade Ball, 9 p.m. - midnight, Location TBD. A festive night of dancing, featuring live music, food and drink.

Friday, Nov. 5

. Spirit & Cultural Day, 10 a.m. - 2 p.m., Hayden Lawn. Join student organizations, Sparky, the ASU band, and ASU cheerleaders on Hayden Lawn in numerous spirit activities and cultural performances.
. Royalty Coronation, 12:30 p.m., Hayden Lawn.
. Homecoming Gala, 6:30 p.m., downtown Phoenix Bank One Building Grand Lobby. Celebrate the growing partnership between ASU and Arizona business. Sponsored by Bank One. 
. Lantern Walk, 7 - 8 p.m., the base of "A" Mountain. Come participate in the annual tradition as students, faculty/staff, alumni, and members of the community hike up "A" Mountain to light up the A.

Saturday, Nov. 6

. Homecoming Block Party, 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Alumni Lawn, University Drive & Palo Verde Beach.
. Homecoming Parade, 11:30 a.m., University Drive, from McAllister Avenue to Myrtle Avenue. Watch the Sun Devil Marching Band, student organization floats, Hall of Fame inductees, and the Homecoming King and Queen  make their way down University Drive.
. Band leads march to stadium, 3:45 p.m.
. ASU vs. Stanford Game, 4:30 p.m., Sun Devil Stadium.
For information about ASU Homecoming FestDevil, including the second annual Block Party, go to (www.asu.edu/homecoming).
