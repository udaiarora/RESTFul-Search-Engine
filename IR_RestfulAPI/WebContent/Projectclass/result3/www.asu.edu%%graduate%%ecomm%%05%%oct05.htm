Graduate Academic Program Development Unveils New Website

The graduate academic program development area (GAPD) has developed a new website to help clarify the process of program development and assist academic units in strategically managing their graduate academic programs. Academic program development is a critical component of the self-governance of ASU and clarity of each program's mission, curriculum, policies, and procedures helps faculty plan for the implementation of their programs and makes it easier for students to understand the program's requirements for completion of their program of study. The website is located at www.asu.edu/graduate/gapd. If you have any questions, please contact Gabe Goltz at gabriel.goltz@asu.edu or 5-3542.


Call for Nominations: PEO Scholar Awards

P.E.O. Scholar Awards are one-time competitive awards for women of the United States or Canada who are either pursuing a graduate degree or engaged in advanced study or research. In addition to recognizing and encouraging excellence in higher education, these awards provide partial support for study and research for women who will make significant contributions in their varied fields of endeavor. Priority is given to women who are well established in their graduate programs or advanced study or research. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/peo

ASU Health Insurance. The deadline to register for ASU Health Insurance via SunDial or the Interactive website is February 3 for the spring semester. For more information, go to: www.asu.edu/health/insurance.

TA/RA Benefits. TA or RAs hired after the week of October 24 (the 10th week of the fall semester) will not be eligible for TA/RA tuition remission and health insurance. www.asu.edu/graduate/TARAhandbook/TARApart2.html

Social and Academic Mentoring Program (SAM). There are still research assistantships (8%) available for advanced graduate students who are able to mentor new incoming graduate students during their first year of graduate school. For more information, go to: www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/SAM.

Survey of Earned Doctorates. Please remind your doctoral students to submit a Survey of Earned Doctorates (www.asu.edu/graduate/forms) when they schedule their defense. The survey is funded by the National Science Foundation and results are used to improve graduate education. All information provided by students is confidential and is used only for the purposes of the survey. If you have any questions, please contact Roxanne Treece at Roxanne.Treece@asu.edu.

TA Orientation. October 1st was the deadline for students to attend graduate professional development program as a make-up option for missing Fall 2005 TA Orientation and Training. All newly hired TAs that were unable to take advantage of this option will need to make arrangements to attend Spring 2006 TA Orientation in January. More information at www.asu.edu/graduate/tara/TAorientation.html

UGEM Research Assistantships. UGEM research assistantships are still available for first-year competitive, underrepresented graduate students. UGEM grants are nomination-based and DGS matches the academic unit's contribution to make it a 50%-time research assistantship. For more information, go to: www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/ugem.

The Nonprofit Sector Research Fund is offering this scholarship to introduce students to issues relating to philanthropy, voluntarism, and nonprofit organizations.
