September 14, 2004

Forum reaches out to future leaders

A photograph Craig Kielburger saw when he was 12 changed his life forever.

The photo, on the front page of the Toronto Star, was of a young Pakistani boy who had been sold into bondage as a carpet weaver before escaping from his plight. The headline on the story read, “Boy, 12, murdered for speaking out against child labor.”

The picture and story motivated the young Canadian to help found Free the Children, an organization geared to help children worldwide fight abuse and exploitation.

Kielburger, now 21 and a college student, will be the keynote speaker for the fourth annual Fall Leadership Forum, which will take place from 6 p.m. to 9 p.m. , Sept. 14, in the Arizona Ballroom of the Memorial Union, on ASU’s Tempe campus. The theme for this year’s free event is “The Future is Now … Seize the Moment.”

Kielburger’s speech from 6:30 p.m. to 8 p.m. will include a half-hour of fielding questions from the audience. A book signing, student networking event and reception will follow from 8 p.m. to 9 p.m.

A school supply drive for needy children in local elementary schools also will be held at the event, and a half-dozen community agencies will have tables at the reception to highlight student volunteer opportunities that focus on working with children and children’s issues.

ASU students, faculty and staff members – and a select group of local high school students – are invited to listen to Kielburger’s message. An online registration form can be found at (www.asu.edu/mu/slp).

Up to 20 tickets for the forum will be given out to each of the following Valley high schools: Corona del Sol, Marcos de Niza, McClintock and Tempe. The hope is that Kielburger’s inspirational speech will encourage these youths to assume leadership roles in the community as well.

Becky Hornack, communications coordinator for Student Development and Memorial Union, says this is the first year that area high school students will be invited to the event.

“They’ll get to learn what leadership is,” Hornack said. “The forum is about defining what it is to be a leader, and what you can do to make an impact.”

The forum brings together current and emerging student leaders to build a stronger community among individuals who want to have a positive effect on the ASU campus throughout the academic year and beyond. As Kielburger himself says, “Often assumed to be the leaders of tomorrow, our generation must be the leaders of today.”

Marisel Herrera, ASU’s coordinator of Student Leadership Programs for Student Development and Memorial Union, saw Kielburger speak at Arizona Collegiate Leadership Conference at the University of Arizona in February and was impressed by what she heard.

“He had a compelling message, and he showed how one person could make a significant difference and motivate others,” Herrera says. “He moved people immediately, but what struck me at the same time was his humility.”

The four Valley high schools invited to the event have been very receptive to ASU’s offer, Herrera says, and the University Undergraduate Initiatives division is using the opportunity to distribute ASU material to the high school students.

The event is sponsored by Student Leadership Programs, PAB/ASASU, the Campus Environment Team, Student Health, Student Life, the Intergroup Relations Center and University Undergraduate Initiatives.
