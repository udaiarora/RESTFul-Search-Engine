Charge to the NewCu Task Force

December 10, 2004

FROM: Milton Glick, Executive Vice President and Provost of the University
Barbara Kerr, Tempe Campus Academic Senate President
Paul Patterson, East Campus Academic Assembly President
William Simmons, West Campus Academic Senate President
TO:

Task Force on the Curriculum of the New American University
SUBJECT:

Task Force Charge

ASU is developing a new model for the American research university, creating an institution that measures its academic quality by the education that its graduates have received rather than the academic credentials of its incoming freshman class; one at which researchers, while pursuing their scholarly interests, also consider the public good; one that does not just engage in community service, but rather takes on major responsibility for the economic, social, and cultural vitality of its community.

While remarkable programs in the undergraduate curriculum have been widely developed across the ASU campuses in recent years that reflect this mission, a comprehensive review of student learning outcomes and competencies has not occurred. Consequently, in order to address this need, this task force has been formed as a joint effort of the Office of the University Provost and the academic senates from the three campuses. It is the global charge of this task force to identify learning outcomes that we expect from all ASU graduates from all the campuses. Another way of framing this charge is to ask the question, “What should an ASU graduating senior look like?”

More specifically, the charge to this task force is composed of the following elements:

  • Survey the three campuses for quality undergraduate programs that include both learning goals and quality assessment strategies;
  • Become familiar with the conversation regarding undergraduate education, including model programs and assessment practices, occurring at the national level;
  • Identify learning outcomes for the university overall that can be applied to all four campuses;
  • Articulate the relationship between foundational (i.e., general studies) and advanced curricula (i.e., the major) in a school-centric model;
  • Affirm the cooperative relationship between academic and student affairs in educating the whole individual;
  • Suggest next steps in embedding these outcomes throughout the university.

In responding to this charge, the task force must take into account ASU’s transformation into a school-centric university, in which schools and colleges will be responsible for the quality of their programs. The task force should also keep in mind ASU’s important role as a transfer institution for many of its students. And finally, while this aspect is not the immediate focus to this task force’s work, the importance of continuous assessment should be an essential element of all recommendations.

This task force is leading the conversation on one of the most critical, if not the most critical, aspects of ASU’s mission. We ask you to develop a communication plan to keep pertinent campus constituencies informed about your progress by means of regular reports. We ask to have your final report completed by May 15, 2005. This report should be sent to each of the academic senate presidents, with a copy to the University Provost and campus provosts.
