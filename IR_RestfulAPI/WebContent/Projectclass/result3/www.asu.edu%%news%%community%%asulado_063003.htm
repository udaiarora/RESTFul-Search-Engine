June 30, 2003

Reaching out - Program, volunteers offer ASU information to Hispanic community

Univision 33 got a strong dose of "maroon and gold" June 16 - 19, as part of their television program "33 A Su Lado" or "33 On Your Side." In a collaborative effort with the City of Phoenix and KTVW-Univision Channel 33, members of the university family helped build awareness about ASU by appearing on short informational segments that aired during the 5 p.m. news broadcasts June 16 - 19. The segments featured information on a variety of topics, including financial assistance, undergraduate admissions, Hispanic Mother-Daughter Program and the Cesar Chavez Leadership Institute.

On June 19, "33 A Su Lado" was broadcast live and provided viewers the opportunity to call in with questions about ASU. More than 20 Spanish-speaking volunteers answered 739 live calls and 209 voicemails during the June 19 broadcast. As of June 24, there were an additional 210 voicemails from viewers who watched the show, making the outreach effort a success for the community and ASU.

Maria Elena Coronado, program coordinator senior with the Visitor Information Center, says there was a certain level of hope and empowerment when she spoke with many of the callers.

"Whether it was prospective students or parents, I sensed a small bit of relief from them as they felt encouraged that there are people available at this institution who speak the language, understand the culture and showed sensitivity and sincerity to the issues they are facing," she says. "There is an obvious interest from the Spanish-speaking immigrant community to pursue a college education. All we can really do is provide them with the information to successfully achieve that goal. They have to feel empowered to do the rest."

In addition to Coronado, the other Spanish-speaking volunteers were: Carlos Ortega, outreach counselor senior, Equal Opportunity Center (EOC); Bob Mena, post-secondary outreach coordinator, EOC; Frank Granillo, post-secondary outreach coordinator, EOC; Jo Ann Lopez, graduate research assistant, Hispanic Mother-Daughter Program (HMDP); Antonia Franco, executive coordinator, Multicultural Student Center (MSC); Angelica Sanchez, student recruitment/retention specialist senior, HMDP; Maria Moreno, student recruitment/retention specialist senior, HMDP; Rose Mary Coronado, office specialist senior, HMDP; Olivia Diaz, financial aid manager, Student Financial Assistance Office (SFAO); Iliana Garcia, financial aid specialist, SFAO; Rosa Corrales, support systems analyst, SFAO; Gabriela Jimenez, financial aid specialist, SFAO; Jose Dagnino, financial aid specialist, SFAO; Maria Symington, office supervisor, Undergraduate Admissions (UGA); Dave Morales, program coordinator senior, UGA; Regina Matos, academic associate, Honors Program, W.P. Carey School of Business; Veronica Hernandez, student academic specialist, Upward Bound; Anita Verdugo, coordinator, Student Development Office; Manny Romero, editor/publisher, Media Relations & Public Information; Bob Soza, assistant vice president and dean of students; Nancy Jordan, assistant vice president, Public Affairs; and Jose Martinez, alumnus, Los Diablos Alumni.

Non-Spanish speaking volunteers were: Gini Sater, assistant to the Vice President of Student Affairs; Jennifer Stults, assistant to the dean of students; Diane Stemper, director of the Student Financial Assistance Office (SFAO); Craig Fennell, associate director, SFAO; and Tim Trumble, university photographer.
