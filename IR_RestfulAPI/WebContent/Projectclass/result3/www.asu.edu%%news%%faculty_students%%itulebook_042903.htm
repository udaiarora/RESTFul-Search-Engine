ASU's Bruce Itule releases first novel

Hand-picked art conservators from Europe discover a cache of Spanish gold in the walls of San Xavier del Bac Mission while they are working on the magnificent frescoes and statuary of the old church south of Tucson.

They deliver the ingots, coins - and a priceless gem-studded crucifix - to the very popular parish priest who lives in a modest home next to the church.

A day later, the priest is murdered, the gold is gone - and the art conservators seem to be the most likely suspects. But how could such trusted, respected, renowned professionals have done such an unspeakable thing?

Enter New York freelance journalist Nick Genoa, who sets out to solve the mystery in Bruce Itule's debut novel, "The Gold of San Xavier."

The book is a mystery set in Tucson and at San Xavier del Bac, inspired by a story that Itule, retired director of ASU student media and current journalism faculty member, wrote about San Xavier del Bac for Arizona Highways in the early 1990s.

"'The Gold of San Xavier' has been in and out of my computer for the past 10 years, and it's gone through six or eight major revisions," Itule said.

In writing "The Gold of San Xavier," Itule said he learned that "reading and enjoying a mystery isn't the same as crafting one, and that writing the book was the easy part, compared to getting it published."

After several agent/rewrite misadventures, Itule and two colleagues established their own press, Thunder Mountain Publishing Company, and "The Gold of San Xavier" is their first title.

It's an appropriate beginning for a press that hopes to focus on regional work. The Tucson setting for the novel draws in those who have lived in or visited Tucson, and have marveled at the beauty of San Xavier del Bac.

Itule's next book tells the story of a young Apache who learns about life as he leaves the reservation to track down a killer and several thieves, and to recover a stolen religious artifact.

To learn more about Itule's works, visit the Web at (www.thundermp.com).
