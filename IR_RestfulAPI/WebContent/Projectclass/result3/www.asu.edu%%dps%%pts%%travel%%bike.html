Bicycling is a very popular method of transportation at Arizona State University - over 15,000 people bicycle to campus each day. Not only is bicycling a reliable, timely and convenient way to get around Tempe and ASU, but it is good exercise and great for the environment! Bicycle lanes and paths can be found throughout Tempe and the ASU campus, as well as numerous bike racks for you to secure your bicycle. For a copy of the latest Metro area or City of Tempe bike map, call (480) 965-1072 or e-mail the Travel Reduction Program including your name, address, and phone number. Also, Bike-on-Bus makes it possible to use the bus system and bring your bike along.

With the convenience and flexibility a bike provides, it is highly recommended that you license your bicycle to protect it. Remember... lock your bike through the frame and both wheels to secure it when it is unattended.

BIKE SAFETY

Did You Know?

  • 96% of all car-bicycle accidents result in injury or death to the bicyclist
  • wearing a helmet will reduce your chances of serious injury by 85%
  • in 69% of car-bicycle accidents, the bicyclist has committed a violation
  • 44% of all car-bicycle accidents are a result of bicyclists riding the wrong way

Bicyclists are required by law to:

  • ride with the flow of traffic while on the street or sidewalk
  • yield to traffic before entering the street from a sidewalk
  • obey all traffic control signals including red lights and stop signs
  • use a headlight and a rear reflector at night


ON-CAMPUS BIKE REPAIR

Need to fix your brakes? Got a flat? Need to air up your tire? Drop by the Bike Repair Co-op. Located on the south side of the Student Recreation Center (SRC), the Bike Repair Co-op provides students with tools, used parts, new parts at a reduced price, and assistance on how to repair bikes. The Bike Repair Co-op is open Mon. - Sat., 10:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m. To receive assistance or volunteer, please call (480) 965-4748.
