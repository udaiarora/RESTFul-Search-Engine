December 22, 2005

ASU steps forward for Katrina relief

When Hurricane Katrina hit the Gulf Coast with its destructive force Aug. 29, the ASU community quickly stepped forward to help the survivors recover.

Whether it was enrolling new students, aiding existing students, collecting money and items for the survivors or reaching out to other universities, ASU answered the call for help.

“We have an unprecedented opportunity to reach out to those whose lives have been devastated by Hurricane Katrina,” said ASU President Michael Crow in a September message to the ASU community.

Over several weeks, ASU enrolled 96 students from the various universities in the area using a streamlined enrollment process and assistance for academic offices across the university. The students quickly were placed in classes and were provided housing, parking or whatever else was needed.

“We had one student come into the office at 9 a.m., and by 11:30 that morning he was sitting in class,” said Lou Ann Denny, ASU’s registrar.

Another 55 enrolled in free online courses. While most are heading back to the region, at least 19 are enrolled for the spring semester.

ASU’s College of Design also worked to relocate an entire class of architecture students and their professors to ASU for the semester. The students worked on a variety of projects to help rebuild their ravaged city.

Other employees put together smaller efforts to collect food and clothes for friends and family members in the region. Human Resources helped coordinate additional efforts, including offers of housing for hurricane survivors.

The largest effort for the university came on the football field. The university, in conjunction with Louisiana State University, moved its Sept. 10 gridiron contest from Baton Rouge to ASU. The game, which was moved less than a week before kickoff, served as a community fundraiser. More than $1 million was raised for hurricane relief and to support students affected by the disaster.
