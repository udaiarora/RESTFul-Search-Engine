May 23, 2003

Grogan helps others attend ASU, earns degree

DeDe Grogan has worked for ASU's Child & Family Services Office as an administrative assistant since 1996. She not only provides information and assistance to student parents, but also shares with them words of encouragement and friendship.

Her co-workers agree Grogan is exceptionally good at cheering on student parents as they deal with the ups and downs of the college experience. She reminds them that with enough determination anything is possible, and she serves as the perfect example.

For the past 11 years, Grogan has juggled a full time job, college course work and raising her son Seth. This week she is graduating from ASU cum laude with a bachelor of arts in English.

Grogan says she really enjoys her job at the Child & Family Services Office because she is able to help parents find the resources they need to make their dreams of graduating come true.

"Many times I get parents who are upset or frustrated at not being able to find the resources they need for their families," Grogan says. "I have been given the wonderful opportunity of mentoring and even assisting families in finding those resources to make attending school and even graduating a reality. What more could someone ask for within a job?"

After graduation she plans to continue working at ASU, but will take a break from school so she can spend time with her family before continuing her education.

"After my small break I plan to enroll in the College of Education and work on a post-bac. This would certify me as a teacher," Grogan says. "Then I plan to obtain my masters in early childhood Education with an emphasis on special needs."

From there, Grogan wants to create special-need child care centers for various ages which would encompass all aspects needed by a child, teen, young adult, adults and families.

Though she feels good about her latest accomplishment, Grogan says she owes much to her supervisors and her co-workers.

"I am very lucky and grateful to have such a wonderful support system; everyone should be so lucky," she says. "I believe that support from family, friends and co-workers is the key to my graduating this May 2003."

Linda Sullivan, executive coordinator for Co-Curricular Programs, says Grogan is far too modest.

"What sets DeDe apart from others is her unwavering commitment to achieving her goals, even when faced with personal and professional challenges," Sullivan says. "As a full-time employee and mother, DeDe has had to make many difficult decisions and sacrifices over the years. She has done so with the knowledge that some day it would pay off for her and her family. Thanks to her persistence and dedication, that time has finally arrived."

Maureen Duane works as a program coordinator, senior, for the Child & Family Services Office, and says she loves working with Grogan because she "gets it."

"She has a very strong work ethic and puts her heart and soul into her work," Duane says. "This is evident from her direct interactions with families to her advocacy efforts on behalf of all families."

Duane says she is thrilled Grogan has decided to stay on with the department indefinitely.

"Having worked with ASU families for 14 years now, I see DeDe as the perfect example of why student parents are here - they do it for their children, both as a way to improve their lives economically, but also to serve as a model of the importance of education," she says.
