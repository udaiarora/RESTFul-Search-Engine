Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)

Minutes of Meeting

May 14, 2003

Members Present: Patricia Feldman, Carol Behl, Sue Hunter, Stefan Myhajlenko, Barbara Trapido-Lurie 

Absent: Victoria Trotta

1. Minutes from April 28 Spotlight Series featuring Dr. Gail Hackett as the speaker have been approved (via e-mail) and posted to Web.

2. OLD BUSINESS

1. In regards to setting up the Task Force: As far we know, nothing has been decided yet.  Patty will follow up with Dr. Gail Hackett on this matter and her planned web response to Q&A pertaining to the Spot Light Speaker forum last month.

2. In regards to the Academic Senate meeting of May 5th: action on motion No.11 and motion No.16 (ACD-508) will be delayed till the Fall semester.

3. Carol pointed out that the AP list circulated by the VP?s office appeared incomplete and that there was no way to discern the employment status of each AP i.e. whether they were Year-to-Year, Continuing, etc.  CAPS thought this information would be important as discussions on AP status via Task Force unfold.  Sue suggested we also check with Barb Mawhiney (Director EO/AA) who had circulated AP statistics at an earlier Spot Light meeting.

4. The on-going question of whether or not Academic Associate can get voting privileges on the Academic Senate has not been resolved: though George Watson is being made aware of the matter.

5. CAPS request to President Crow to respond to recent AP concerns is still being addressed, per Gail Hackett. 

 3. NEW BUSINESS

(i) Gary Hembree (chair of AP Grievance Committee) forwarded amendments to CAPS regarding ACD-509-03.   This excerpt from the cover letter suggests CAPS will be involved: 

?According to my reading of ACD 509-03, the amendment of our committee's rules and procedures has a clearly defined set of steps. We will suggest the changes that are required to the policies affected by the Supreme Court ruling to the Committee on Academic Professional Status. From that point CAPS will carry the revision process forward.?

(ii) It was suggested that CAPS might consider taking a pro-active role in assisting the Task Force by calling an AP Caucus to discuss recent and future developments.  The Caucus would provide APs at large an opportunity to be informed and provide feedback.  The proposed Caucus meeting date would be dependent upon the Task Force timeline i.e. if the latter is several months away, it would be beneficial for the Caucus to meet sooner than later. 

4. Plan Next Business Meeting

 (i) In light of the Task Force and other AP developments it was suggested that we accept nominations and elect a new CAPS chair before the Fall meeting.  This was agreed upon.

5. Meeting was adjourned at 10:45AM

Respectfully submitted, Stefan Myhajlenko

Minutes were approved 6/16/2003
