Arizona State University Thanks Widespread Corporate Support

ARIZONA STATE UNIVERSITY THANKS WIDESPREAD CORPORATE SUPPORT

Athletic Department still looking for ways to involve corporate community in supporting ASU’s effort to
raise money for Hurricane Katrina victims.

TEMPE, Ariz. (www.thesundevils.com) -- Arizona State University wishes to thank several corporate sponsors who have donated funds and services to aid in the staging of the ASU-LSU football game that will be played Saturday at 6:15 p.m. at Sun Devil Stadium.

ASU, in conjunction with the Sun Devil Athletic Department and Viacom, has announced that to date over $245,000 has been raised to offset the operating expenses of the game. All net proceeds from Saturday’s college football game vs. LSU will help support the Bush-Clinton Katrina Relief Fund and the Hurricane Katrina Student Relief Fund (www.KatrinaSRF.com).

The Phoenix Thunderbirds have stepped up and donated $100,000 and All State Insurance has contributed $50,000. Chase Bank, Wells Fargo Bank, Bank of America and APS have also contributed funds to help in the relief efforts.

In addition to monetary support, the City of Tempe has donated all of its services to help stage the
monumental event, including traffic management and security.

The Fiesta Bowl has also provided the LSU traveling party with hotel accommodations and paid for all of
their meals.

In addition to looking for additional corporate help, ASU will also engage in a massive volunteer effort to
collect cash donations from ASU students and fans attending Saturday’s game. There are still opportunities for corporate sponsors to get involved to help secure donations to assist in the relief effort.

A main goal of the ASU Athletic Department is to underwrite and reduce the university’s cost relating to the staging of the game, therefore increasing the total contribution ASU will be able to make to the Bush-Clinton Katrina Relief Fund and other agencies.

Any private or public entities that wish to help with this fundraising effort should contact David Benedict in the Sun Angel Foundation at 480-965-6972 (office)/480-363-9785 (cell)/david.benedict@asu.edu (e-mail) if you are interested in helping with this memorable and supportive event.
