SUBJECT: Spring TA Orientation and Professional Development Forums
DATE: November 22, 2004
The Division of Graduate Studies will offer an orientation and diversity training program for new teaching assistants/associates on Monday, January 10, 2005. Please note the requirements and options available to academic units and new TAs.
  1. Standard Orientation
    The TA in the University Setting
    Date: Monday, January 10, 2005
    Place: Lattie Coor 170
    Time: 8:30 a.m. - 3:00 p.m. Registration begins at 8:15 a.m.
    (For Spring 2005, the 2-day orientation has been condensed into one day only)

  2. The TA Orientation is an Arizona Board of Regents requirement for both new TAs and TAs new to ASU, even if they have taught elsewhere. Registration begins at 8:15 a.m. Departments are responsible for informing their new TAs of this required orientation. In cases where TAs fail to attend, or arrive unduly late, chairs and directors will be contacted, and re-appointed or continuing TAs will be required to attend Strategies for Success: Professional Development Seminars to make up the sessions missed.

  3. Professional Development Seminars: The Division of Graduate Studies will offer a variety of professional development seminars for graduate students as part of its Strategies for Success Series. Several of these seminars will be aimed at new and continuing TAs. We encourage departments to promote the participation in these forums to their TAs. Dates, locations, and times will be distributed during the orientaion session and are available at www.asu.edu/graduate/sfs.

    Please advise students to bring their ASU ID or know their ID number in order to register.

  4. Please send the Department New Teaching Assistant/Associate List (www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/TAdptlst) of participating students to gradpd@asu.edu by Friday, December 10, 2004 or contact (480) 965-8969.

Feel free to contact gradpd@asu.edu with any further questions. We appreciate your continuing support of the Division of GraduateStudies TA Training program.
