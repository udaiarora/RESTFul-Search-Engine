ACADEMIC PATHS THAT ENHANCE CAREER INTERESTS:

All language majors are required to complete 30 credit hours in their major (Chinese, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Russian or Spanish) and 15 additional credit hours in a Related Field. The choice of a Related Field should complement your major and reflect your career interests.

Here are some suggestions:

If you are interested in working as a TRANSLATOR / INTERPRETER, you should not only hone your translation skills, but also have a broad knowledge of the languages and cultures with which you will be dealing. You should take courses in language, literature and culture in your major, then focus on advanced grammar, conversation & composition courses, and any course that may expand your vocabulary in specialized areas. For Spanish speakers, the Spanish section offers the Certificado de Traducción Certificate Program in Translation (Spanish-English). Come to the Department of Languages and Literatures for more information.

If you would like to become a HIGH SCHOOL FOREIGN LANGUAGE TEACHER, you should pursue a Bachelor of Arts in Education, with an academic specialization in your chosen language.  You would follow the requirements of the College of Education and course work in your language.  In addition to specific language, literature and culture classes, 'Methods in Teaching Foreign Languages' courses are required (FLA 479 Introduction to Teaching Languages and FLA 480 Methods of Teaching Languages).  You will need to apply to the College of Education's Teacher Preparation Program (ITC) to attain your goal of student teaching and Secondary Education certification.

If you are interested in working as a PROFESSOR OF LANGUAGES, you should take all language, literature and culture classes in your language. You should supplement your major with courses that develop public speaking, critical thinking and academic writing skills. After the Bachelor of Arts degree, you would then enter Graduate School and complete a Master of Arts and a Ph.D. in your language to be able to teach on the university-level.

If you are interested in working as a REGIONAL OR AREA SPECIALIST, it is a good idea to take as many civilization and contemporary culture courses in your major as possible. You should also supplement your major with geography, political science, and history courses that focus on the country or region of interest. 

If you are interested in working in INTERNATIONAL BUSINESS, you can supplement your degree in languages with an International Business Certificate. The certificate consists of International Business, Language and Area Studies courses and many courses in your major may apply towards the certificate. You should also take any available courses in your major that focus on business, such as: Business French (FRE 319), German Business Correspondence and Communication (GER 319), or Italian for Business (ITA 315), or Spanish Business Correspondence and Communication (SPA 319).
