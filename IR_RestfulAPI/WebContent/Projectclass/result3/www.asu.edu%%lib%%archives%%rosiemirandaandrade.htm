Rosie Miranda Andrade


Outstanding fielder, starred at three infield positions during a 22-year career which included 8 national tournaments. Rosie earned All-America second team honors at 3rd base while playing for the famous Phoenix PBSW Ramblers in 1957. Following a 7-year stint with the Poston Tillage Sweethearts of Glendale, where she earned All-State honors 5 times as a shortstop, she joined the legendary Phoenix A-1 Queens and starred as their second baseman in 1956. While with the Ramblers, in 1957-1960, she helped lead the team to the runner-up spot nationally in 1957 and was voted the most colorful and popular player in the regional tournament. Blessed with a strong, accurate arm, good speed and hitting prowess, Rosie Miranda Andrade ended a 5-year retirement to join the Sun City Saints in 1966. During three seasons with the Sun City Saints, she twice was chosen on the Pacific Coast Women's Softball League All-Star Team in 1967 and 1968. Rosie Miranda Andrade's softball career spanned the period from 1947 to 1969. She was inducted into the Arizona Foundation Softball Hall of Fame in 1979.
