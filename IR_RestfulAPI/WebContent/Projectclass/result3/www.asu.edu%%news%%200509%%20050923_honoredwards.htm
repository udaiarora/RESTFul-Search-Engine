September 26, 2005

ASU, community come together to celebrate Blake Edwards

The creative force behind such memorable films as “Breakfast at Tiffany’s,” “The Pink Panther,” and “Victor/Victoria” was presented with an honorary doctoral degree Friday following intermission at a special ASU Symphony Orchestra concert.

ASU President Michael Crow awarded filmmaker Blake Edwards the degree for extraordinary achievement and contribution to modern cinema, television and radio.

To accept the honor, Edwards crossed the Gammage Auditorium stage on a motorized scooter to the accompaniment of the “Pink Panther” theme, as he had done to the amusement of millions of viewers at the 2004 Academy Awards, where he was presented with a rare special lifetime achievement award.

“A Renaissance Man in the truest sense of the phrase, Blake Edwards’ prolific talent, artistic advocacy, social conscience and sophisticated sense of humor have earned him a significant and lasting place in the history of media entertainment,” said David Young, vice president and dean of the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, who read the formal award citation during the honorary degree presentation.

The evening concert featured soprano Monica Mancini, the daughter of composer Henry Mancini, who composed many of the songs from Edwards’ work.

Highlights included the themes from Peter Gunn and The Pink Panther, as well as classics like Moon River, Days of Wine and Roses and Bolero.

The concert and degree awarding ceremony were the culmination of two days of events honoring Edwards and celebrating the planned opening this fall of ASU’s Center for Film and Media Research.

The previous evening, Edwards was guest of honor at a gala reception and film screening of “Victor/Victoria” at Harkins Centerpoint Theatre in Tempe. The gala was a fundraiser for ASU’s proposed Center for Film and Media Research, which the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences will launch this fall pending Arizona Board of Regents’ approval.

The center will provide a prominent research environment for transdisciplinary study of the significant cultural, social and economic role film and media play in shaping human values and global communities.

Edwards will also teach a course on film production as part of the university’s recently launched bachelor’s degree in film program. This fall, the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences began offering an undergraduate film degree with a concentration in film and media studies. In fall 2006, the Herberger College of Fine Arts will begin offering the same degree with a concentration in film and media production.
