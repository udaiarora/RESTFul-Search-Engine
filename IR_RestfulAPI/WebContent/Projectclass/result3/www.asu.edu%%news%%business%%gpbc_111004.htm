November 9, 2004

Single-family setting records; commercial market improving

Commercial real estate markets throughout the metro area are expected to improve over the next couple of years, while the single-family sector will see only a moderate decline from record heights, according to an analysis in the current issue of the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip Economic Forecast. The fourth-quarter issue examines the outlook for the real estate market through 2006.

“The single-family housing market will set new records this year,” according to Elliott D. Pollack, contributing editor. “Only minor declines are anticipated in 2006 and 2006. Overall, the commercial markets are improving as absorptions exceed new supply, thus allowing vacancy rates to decline. Single-family housing is expected to remain strong, albeit not as strong as this year.”

Pollack said the industrial market will be the first to get back to normal levels of vacancy. “The consensus is that the retail market will be relatively healthy, with construction and absorption balancing out over the next three years.” The apartment market is improving as well. As interest rates and median home prices move up, affordability for single-family housing could become more of an issue. Such a trend may not be welcome news to potential homeowners, but as Pollack noted, “it would help return the multi-family market to normal.”

Elsewhere in the Greater Phoenix Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark notes that the national economic doldrums have the potential to slow an otherwise healthy local economy. “In the face of uncertain economic performance at the national level, the local economy continues to hold its own. However, those nationwide uncertainties mean the Greater Phoenix area is not faring as well as it normally would at this point in the economic cycle.” In a nationwide field of sluggish job growth nationwide in August, the Phoenix metro area ranked third of all the states, with a rate of 2.5 percent.
