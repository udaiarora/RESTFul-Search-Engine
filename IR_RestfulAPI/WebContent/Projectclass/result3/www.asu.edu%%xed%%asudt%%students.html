ASU Bookstore  

During the first week of the fall and spring semesters (Monday – Thursday), textbooks for Mercado classes are available for purchase from 4:30 to 6:30pm in Building C, check the marquee on the first floor for the exact location.

For spring semester, because of the holiday (Martin Luther King, Jr.), the bookstore sells books from Tuesday to Thursday and on the following Monday.

 

  
Classrooms

The Mercado is a multipurpose facility where many downtown businesses hold conferences and workshops. We appreciate your cooperation in leaving classrooms arranged as you find them. If furniture rearranging is necessary, please return the room to its original setting when your class is over. Table legs are easily damaged by improper moving; please avoid moving tables whenever possible.

Please check the marquee on the first floor for exact classroom assignments.

 
Computer Lab and Library Access

Students and faculty can order books or copy materials from the libraries using the forms in the computer lab. Please complete the form and leave it with the lab assistant. We will contact you when your copied materials and/or books have arrived (allow three to four days). Please have correct change available to pay the receptionist (Building C, second floor) when picking up your copied materials. Students and faculty can also access the library and order books through the ASU Internet site. The Web site to refer to is www.asu.edu/library

Building C and A are wireless. Students need an ASUrite ID and a network card to access the wireless network.

 
Copy Machine

The copy machine is located on the second floor of Building C. The machine requires 10 cents per copy. Please see the receptionist (Building C, second floor) if you have problems, or leave a message with the ASU Campus Security aides.

 
Emergency Evacuation Procedures
ASU has a strong commitment to the safety and welfare of its employees, students and visitors. Since it is impossible to predict what types of situations may develop into an emergency, these instructions are intended to provide guidance in evacuating the building.

If the alarm sounds:
1. Stop what you are doing.
2. Assist anyone needing help exiting.
3. Leave the building using the nearest and safest exit, preferably the end stairwells, which empty onto the sidewalk outside of the building. The center stairwell empties into the lobby, the point at which emergency personnel would be entering. Do not use elevators.
4. Do not reenter the building if the alarm stops; wait until you have received an "all clear" signal from a supervisory authority or the fire department.

Any emergency warrants a call to 911, even if you think another person has called. Specify whether you need police, medical or fire assistance.

When calling 911, have the following information available:
1. Name and address:

Arizona State University
Mercado
502 E. Monroe
Phoenix

2. Building name (Building C), floor and room number (if applicable), elevator and cab number
3. Type of emergency, i.e., injury, fire, gas odor, etc.

If time permits during evacuation, secure your area and take personal items with you. If fire conditions exist, evacuate immediately, leaving personal belongings behind.

Equipment for Presentations

All classrooms are equipped with dry erase boards and markers. Televisions, VCRs, overhead and slide projectors, flip charts and tabletop podiums (with optional microphones) are available. Please notify faculty immediately of your audiovisual needs since the equipment must be reserved in advance.

Most classrooms are equipped with computer display units these must be reserved in advanced. PC laptops are available by instructor request only and must be reserved one week in advance. Due to the limited availability of these laptops, students are expected to provide their own computer for electronic presentations. Be aware that your computer may require a setting change to be compatible with the projectors. If staff assistance is required, please call at least two working days in advance to schedule an appointment. Technical support is not available before or during class time.

 
Lounge Areas

Student lounges are located on the second and third floors of Building C. The lounges have vending machines and pay telephones. In Building A, vending machines are located near the classrooms and a pay phone is located to the right of the elevator on the second floor. The Building C second floor lounge has a microwave for your use.

Security

Security for the Mercado is provided by AKAL Security. A guard is on site 24 hours a day. In addition, ASU Campus Security aides provide building security during evening class times. The area is also monitored by the City of Phoenix Bike Patrol, along with Downtown Security Guides provided by the Downtown Phoenix Partnership.

 
Parking Information
 
Parking Cards

Students are expected to purchase a parking card for $105 per session for the Heritage and Science Park garage (HSPG) at Fifth and Monroe streets. Cards are programmed to accommodate the number of class hours and study time required for the class in which a student is enrolled. The cards are monitored and not intended for personal use.

Students who have paid $480 or higher for parking on the Tempe campus can park in the reciprocal parking lot at 320 N. 4th Ave.

Replacement of lost or damaged cards is $5 with proof of purchase, or $35 without it.

Parking cards remain the property of ASU and may not be sold or assigned to another individual.

 
Parking Entrance and Areas

Students should enter the garage from Monroe Street. Preferred parking for ASU users is on the second and third floors of the garage. If the Monroe Street entrance is closed, students may use the Fifth Street entrance.

Click here for general information, driving directions and a map of the Mercado.

 
Replacement Fees  

With proof of purchase, replacement of lost or damaged cards is $5. Without proof of original purchase, replacement cards are $35.

 

  
Entrance/Exit  

The parking cards are programmed for entry/exit usage. If the card is used to enter the garage, it must be used to exit. There are times when the exit gate may be open. The card must be used at exit time or reentry may not function. Cards are programmed to continually delete hours if it is not used at exit. This may necessitate the purchase of a new card at the replacement cost.

 

  
ASU Responsibility  

The Heritage and Science Park Garage is property of the City of Phoenix. ASU assumes no responsibility for the care and protection of any vehicle or its contents at any time the vehicle is operated or parked in the garage. Please read the guidelines available from Standard Parking.

 
Disabled Vehicles  

In the event a vehicle breaks down and must be parked in the garage overnight, the vehicle's driver should notify the parking attendant on duty in the garage. Disabled vehicles must be removed within 24 hours.
