Graduate Division Wins Award for Diversity Initiative, Sweeps National Competition

ASU's Division of Graduate Studies (DGS) has won a national award for Pathways to Success, an initiative it launched this year in partnership with the Barrett Honors College and the Office of the Vice President for Undergraduate Initiatives to promote diversity in graduate education. On behalf of ASU and the DGS, Vice Provost and Dean of Graduate Studies Maria T. Allison accepted the 2004 Peterson’s Award for Innovation in Promoting an Inclusive Graduate Community from the Council of Graduate Schools at its annual meeting in Washington, DC, in December.

ASU is the only university to receive the Peterson’s Award, and the only university in the country to receive all three grant awards given this year by the council, the only national association dedicated to representing and advancing graduate education. ASU effectively swept the competition, winning two other grant awards for projects that focus on the responsible conduct of research and enhancing doctoral completion rates. The proposals were written by Allison and Associate Deans Marjorie Zatz and Filiz Ozel. www.asu.edu/graduate/aboutus/news/2005/011805.htm.

— UPDATES/ANNOUNCEMENTS —

Clinic: Preparing for Graduate School on Feb. 3 has been canceled.

The DGS will now accept the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) examination results for demonstration of English proficiency. The minimum acceptable scores are 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0. For more information about IELTS go to www.ielts.org. In addition, students who have completed at least 90 semester credit hours with a cumulative grade point average of 3.00 in the U.S. at a regionally accredited post-secondary institution will be exempt from the English proficiency requirement. Both of these options are effective immediately, and are in addition to the current options students have as noted in the Graduate Catalog (page 86). For questions, email michael.dickson@asu.edu.

Graduation Deadlines and Procedures have been updated for the Spring 2005 semester. To review the changes, visit www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns.

Student Academic Services (SAS) will begin emailing all oral defense paperwork for theses and dissertations defenses. Detailed information on how the process will change is forthcoming.

The DGS has upgraded GPortal. The new version includes changes that make GPortal easier to navigate and make it possible to save student queries. The most notable addition to GPortal v2 is the added capability to obtain a list of students who have a financial award and view their individual information from the SIGMA Financial Aid system, as well as from Human Resources. For questions or assistance with the new features, email grad-it@asu.edu. www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/egad/gPortal2.htm.
