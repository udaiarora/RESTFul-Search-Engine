April 7, 2003

ASU library employee coordinates collection effort

For the past two months, ASU Library Assistant Senior Demetria Elms has been writing daily to her brother, Corporal Daniel Bratton, 20, a Marine serving in Iraq. She has also been sending him care packages full of items to help make his days there a little more bearable.

In his letters home he has requested things like books, foot powder and eye drops. Realizing other soldiers would also enjoy receiving goodies from home, Elms has set up a collection site in the Hayden Library Access Services department and welcomes donations from the ASU community.

Elms says receiving packages from home helps troops keep a strong, supportive connection with their families and friends.

"For those donating supplies and sending packages, it is rewarding to know that we can do something helpful for our loved ones while we wait for them to safely return," she says.

Because the military has strict restrictions on the food items, Elms is only collecting a limited list of donation items. They include things such as: combs, brushes, shaving cream, pocket sized shampoo, conditioner, soap, lotion, hand sanitizer, toothpaste, baby wipes, eye drops, eye wash, foot powder and batteries (especially AA).

Elms says troops are also requesting items to help pass the time during slow periods such as: books and magazines, decks of cards and travel-sized games.

"Receiving these goodies provides a welcoming distraction from the isolation created by the distance and activities of war," she says.

Elms says she hopes Americans will support their troops no matter how they feel about the war itself.

"In all honesty, I have done little questioning of the actual war," Elms says. "I know that I have a responsibility to stand by my brother and my friends over there. They need our support and I will do my best on this side."

Those interested in getting involved with Elms project, whether by sending letters of support or donating supplies, can e-mail Elms at (elms@asu.edu).

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
