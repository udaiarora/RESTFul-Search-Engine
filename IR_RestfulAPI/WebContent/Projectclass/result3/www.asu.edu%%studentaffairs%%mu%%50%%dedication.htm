MU Dedication & Statement of Purpose

The Dedication

"This Union Building Is a Living Memorial.Commemorated here are the valor and deep loyalty of students, faculty and alumni who served in defense of our country. Many gave their lives on distant seas, in far off lands, in foreign skies - This building is dedicated to them in humble gratitude so that their sacrifice shall not be forgotten. May it inspire the living to preserve the democratic traditions they cherished."

Statement of Purpose

"This building is a living memorial to the men and women from this campus who gave their lives for the purpose of defending and preserving the heritage they cherished. This heritage - the American way of life - is founded on the individual citizenship which seeks a balance between freedom and responsibility. Therefore, this Memorial Union is designed as a laboratory in democratic living, and dedicated to the education which students receive in the informal process of working, playing, and living outside the classroom. It is intended to supplement the academic factors of campus life so that the student's training as a whole person will be well-rounded and complete.

The Memorial Union is the living room, reception room, dining room, and recreation room for the College. It is for the use of students, faculty, and alumni, and to the extent feasible, the community of Arizona. It is intended to promote, by a coordinated program, harmonious and pleasant relationships within and between these various groups.

The Memorial Union is the focal point of the political, business and social processes of student life. It is dedicated to the belief that activities carried on with these walls should bring to students those aesthetic, religious, and social values essential to our culture and our times. Plato, commenting on the standards of culture, said 'What is honored in a country is cultivated there.'"

Official statement of purpose of Union
From Dean Weldon P. Shoftstall and Memorial Union Committee
