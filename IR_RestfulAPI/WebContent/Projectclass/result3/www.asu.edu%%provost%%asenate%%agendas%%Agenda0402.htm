Title of Motion: Request from the College of Business to Change the Name of
Degree Program
 

The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal submitted by the College of Business to change the name of a degree program from MS in Information Management to MS in E-Business and Information Systems.

Rationale:

The reasons for the name change are that advances in technology have changed and continue to markedly change the global business landscape. Terms such as E-Commerce and E-Business are now very much part of the vocabulary. In response, to market their programs, universities have increasingly responded by re-branding their technology rich programs with names such as Master of E-Commerce or Master of E-Business. Prospective employers and prospective students, in their search processes, now look for these names.

Title of Motion: Request from the College of Fine Arts for the Disestablishment
of a Degree Program
 

The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal submitted by the College of Fine Arts for the disestablishment of a Degree Program, BFA, major in Theatre Education.

Rationale:

Previously, the request to disestablish the Theatre Education concentration was approved. As a follow-up, it is now requested that the BFA degree in the Theatre Department be eliminated, as well. The Theatre Department no longer will have any BFA degree or concentration.

Title of Motion: Request from the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences for the
Establishment of an Undergraduate Certificate in Classical Studies

The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal submitted by the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences for the establishment of an Undergraduate Certificate in Classical Studies.

Rationale:

The certificate program in Classical Studies will benefit both the students and the university in a number of ways. By means of the certificate program in Classical Studies, students will be able to augment and enrich their degree programs in related fields with an organized and consistent curriculum which will expose them to many aspects of ancient language and culture, and they will also be prepared to pursue advanced studies in Classical Studies or in related fields in Medieval and Renaissance Studies, Ancient History, English, Art History, or Philosophy with the strength of an approved program behind them.

Report on the March 22, 2001 meeting of the Personnel Committee

a) United Way- The Committee?s consensus was that there is no workable way to ensure that an individual?s choices of where he or she wants his or her contributions to go will have an effect on the funds that different agencies ultimately will receive from the United Way. In brief, people have two realistic choices: to contribute or not. On another matter, there was consensus that it would be better if less pressure were exerted on people to contribute during the annual fund-raising campaign.

b) Whistleblower Policy- The Whistleblower Policy bill was defeated in the House. In light of the fact that it included provisions that would adversely affect the tenure and promotion procedure, the news was received with relief.

c) Married Faculty Occupying Administrative or Other Significant Positions in the Same Department. There have been concerns about husbands and wives concurrently occupying positions such as Department Chairs and Chairs of significant committees. The Committee?s unanimous view was that there was no feasible way to prevent such situations.
