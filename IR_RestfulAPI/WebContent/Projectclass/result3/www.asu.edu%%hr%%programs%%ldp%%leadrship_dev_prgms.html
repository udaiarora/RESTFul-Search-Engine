The Leadership Development Program

Purpose

The Leadership Development Program recognizes that effective leadership/management at all levels is important to achievement of the University's goals. Its purpose is to help administrators at ASU:

  • develop functional, interpersonal, and conceptual skills necessary for their current positions
  • network and share best practices with their peers
  • contribute to the development and implementation of University policy
  • prepare to take on broader and more demanding leadership responsibilities in the future, and
  • acquire knowledge necessary for involvement in internal and community partnerships in behalf of the University

The program also helps the University to identify qualified and competent individuals for promotion.

The Leadership Development Program is formally administered through Human Resources Programs by a designated HR coordinator. The Program additionally receives support from the Office of the President and the Office of the Provost. There is significant collaboration for programming with the Center for Learning and Teaching Excellence and the Learning and Performance Solutions Group.

Components

  • The Leadership Breakfast Series
  • The Associate and Assistant Deans Council
  • The Academic Chairs and Directors Council
  • The Academic Administrators Exchange
  • The Leadership Academy
