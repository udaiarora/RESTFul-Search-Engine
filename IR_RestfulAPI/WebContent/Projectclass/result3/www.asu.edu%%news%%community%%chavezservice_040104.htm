April 1, 2004

ASU continues tradition of Chávez Day of Service

ASU students and staff will reach out to the community as they participate in the third annual César E. Chávez Day of Service April 3, at Brooks Academy, a public elementary school in south Phoenix.

"This event is a day of service, a cultural affirmation and celebration that acknowledges and intentionally promotes the Chávez legacy of social justice, education and servant-leadership," says Marisel Herrera, program coordinator for the Student Development Office and the event's coordinator.

Chavez's work and leadership was officially recognized in 2001, through the dedication of former Arizona State Senator Joe Eddie López and his colleagues who declared March 31, César E. Chávez Recognition Day. The U.S. Postal Service commemorated Chávez's life with a stamp in his honor. The release of the stamp coincided with the 10th anniversary of Chávez's passing, April 23, 1993.

During the day of service, more than 100 ASU volunteers will travel to Brooks Academy to participate in a mock parade, a variety of programs, and break-out components for students ranging in grades K-2, 3-5 and 6-8.

"This year's theme is 'Si Se Puede Through Expression,' and offers a curriculum designed to empower youth through various cultural art forms, including mosaic tiles, meditation for leadership, mural making, career and college knowledge circles and community guests," Herrera says. "This is also the first year that a parental component will be included in the program."

This year's speakers are Eugene Garcia, vice president and dean of the College of Education; Juan Gonzalez, vice president of Student Affairs; and Cordelia Candelaria, chairwoman of ASU's Chicana/o Studies Department.

Terry Orozco, a third grade teacher at Brooks Academy, says recognizing Chávez's legacy is vital for encouraging today's youth to succeed in school and life.

"He sacrificed his life and personal comforts to help a group of workers that had no voice and no political power," says Orozco, whose father was a farm worker. "He thought of others first before himself and he chose a very noble journey of life to help others. If we could duplicate even a small amount of what he did, we'll create better communities and continue building upon his dream."

Brooks Academy students who participate in the César E. Chávez Day of Service will receive a backpack filled with school supplies along with other gifts and prizes.

Romero, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 727-3116 or (mlromero@asu.edu).
