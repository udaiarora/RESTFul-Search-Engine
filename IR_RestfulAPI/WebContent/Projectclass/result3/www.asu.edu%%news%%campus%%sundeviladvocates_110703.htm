November 7, 2003

Advocates play crucial role in university's future

Wanted: individuals interested in making a difference by getting involved and influencing those who make policies affecting ASU. No political experience is necessary, just an enthusiastic attitude and the desire to see ASU become a great American university.

The Sun Devil Advocates is a growing group of volunteers who want to contribute to ASU's future success. The advocates are alumni, faculty, staff, retirees, students, parents and friends who are willing to participate in the legislative process on behalf of ASU.

Wilma Mathews, director of ASU Public Relations, is an active member of Sun Devil Advocates. She says new members are always welcome to join the group, which is an important component to ASU's overall advocacy mission.

"ASU faces many challenges and opportunities in the next decade and beyond," Mathews says. "The Sun Devil Advocates help inform and educate our elected and appointed officials about the best ways for ASU to meet the challenges and take advantage of the opportunities."

Mathews says Sun Devil Advocates have been responsible for bringing much-needed attention to issues important to ASU.

"The advocates can be counted among the many individuals and organizations that helped the research infrastructure bill get passed this year," she says. "Also, the advocates and the Alumni Association continue to thank Arizona legislators each year at the Statehood Day picnic, a well-attended event."

The next ASU Alumni Association Statehood Picnic is scheduled for Feb. 16, 2004, at the State Capital. This annual event provides an important opportunity for ASU advocates to meet and thank Arizona legislators for their continued support, and to discuss with them issues affecting ASU.

Mathews encourages those who support ASU's continued growth and success to become Sun Devil Advocates. Even those who may not have much time to contribute can make a difference.

"As a Sun Devil Advocate, it isn't the amount of time you spend in advocacy that's important, it's what you do with that time," Mathews says. "Whether it's writing a letter, making a telephone call, or attending a legislative thank-you reception, you are making an impression on legislators and that's the important part."

For more information about how to get involved with Sun Devil Advocates, go to (www.sundeviladvocates.org).

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
