May 8, 2006

ASU offers tips for attending spring graduation ceremonies

With an expected attendance of 10,000 students, faculty, family and friends, ASU offers the following tips and reminders for attending the university's spring graduation ceremonies.

ASU hosts a university-wide commencement for all graduation students and their families on Thursday, May 11, 2006 at 10 a.m. at Wells Fargo Arena. During the commencement, students are seated with their respective colleges and ASU President Michael Crow gives his graduation remarks and confers degrees. Following, each individual college holds their own convocation ceremony, where each student walks and receives recognition from the college dean.

For more information on the spring graduation schedule, go to www.asu.edu/graduation.

•  All graduates and their guests are invited to attend the President's Breakfast from 8:30 a.m. to 9:30 a.m. on May 11 before the university commencement. At this event, held at the Sun Devil Stadium, students and their families are welcome to enjoy a free continental breakfast and a family photo opportunity. Please RSVP at www.asu.edu/graduation .

•  Doors open one hour in advance for all convocation ceremonies and open seating is common for most events, including the university commencement. ASU suggests arriving early for favorable seating. Contact your specific college to see if assigned seating is given at its convocation.

•  Tickets are not required for the university commencement at Wells Fargo Arena on May 11. However, tickets are required for convocations taking place at Gammage Auditorium. Please contact your specific college regarding convocation information. For special interest convocations, please contact the University Ceremonies Office at (480) 965-6620.

•  Tempe is experiencing road construction on several streets near the ASU Tempe campus, including:

•  Rural Road between University Drive and Terrace Road is restricted to one lane each direction from 7 a.m. to 4 p.m.

•  Rio Salado Parkway between McClintock Drive and Price Road is restricted to one lane in each direction.

•  Apache Boulevard between Rural and Price Roads is restricted to one to two lanes in each direction.

•  University Drive between Rural Road and Mill Avenue is restricted to one lane in each direction.

ASU recommends visiting the City of Tempe Web site at www.tempe.gov/traffic/construction.htm to learn alternate routes to campus.

•  Parking is free on May 11, the day of university commencement. Visitor parking lots and metered parking spaces will continue as paid parking areas during the ceremonies.

•  ASU West graduates and guests can take a free shuttle bus from the West campus to the Tempe campus to attend the university-wide commencement event. To sign up for the shuttle bus, go to www.asu.edu/graduation .

•  Guests may bring still or video cameras to the graduation ceremonies, however access to certain areas is restricted.

•  Large signs, banners or balloons are not recommended at the graduation ceremonies as these items could obstruct another attendee's view of the ceremony.

•  For those friends and family who cannot attend in person, the university-wide commencement will be broadcast live via Web cast at www.asu.edu .

•  After the university-wide commencement, President Crow is hosting a celebration lunch for graduates to celebrate their individual achievements. Lunch is at Sun Devil Stadium from noon to 1:30 p.m. and is $15 per person. For more information, visit www.asu.edu/graduation .
