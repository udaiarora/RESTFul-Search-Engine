Changes with the ASU Advantage Program

On Monday, November 27, President Crow announced a change in the ASU Advantage program.  Previously, the qualifying income was $18,850 in order to have direct costs covered by the institution.  On Monday, effective for Arizona  freshmen entering Fall 2007, that income bar will be raised to $25,000.  All other aspects of the program will remain the same ( http://www.asu.edu/fa/advantage/ )

This change will allow ASU to impact the state of Arizona by serving more students and broadening access to a quality education at Arizona State University .  This further strengthens our position as one of the nation's most accessible and highest quality institutions.

11th annual College Goal Sunday

When: Sunday, Feb. 11, 2-4 p.m.
Where: Polytechnic, Tempe and West campuses

On Sunday, February 11, 2007 from 2-4 p.m. the Arizona Commission for Postsecondary Education will host the 11th annual College Goal Sunday. College Goal Sunday is the first step toward finding money for college. ASU staff will be available at the Polytechnic, Tempe and West campuses to help students and families through the financial aid process. Attending College Goal Sunday allows students to:

  • Complete the financial aid form ( FAFSA );
  • Speak with financial aid professionals; plus
  • Have a chance to win scholarships or one of ten Dell computers (provided by Waddell & Reed).

What do I bring?

  • If you would like to complete your FAFSA on site, visit www.pin.ed.gov to receive your PIN number before the day of event.
  • Bring your 2006 tax information or your last paycheck stub that you receive at the end of December and your Social Security number. If you are under 24, also bring your parent(s) or guardian and their 2006 tax information.

Visit www.asu.edu/fa/cgs for locations and other information.

Spring 2007

When: Lecture, Tuesdays & Thursdays, 12:15 - 1:30 p.m. Lab, Wednesdays, 12:15 - 1:30 p.m.

Opportunities for Hispanic Students

  • The Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities (HACU) and Laureate have come together to establish one of the largest international scholarship programs dedicated to encouraging US Hispanic students to participate in study abroad programs.
  • The program offers study abroad tuition scholarships that can be applied to more than 20 campus locations within the Laureate International University network. Schools are located in many different countries in Europe and Latin America, including: Spain, France, Switzerland, Costa Rica, Honduras, Chile, Panama, Peru, Ecuador and Mexico.
  • Depending on the study abroad location, coursework takes place in English, Spanish, or French. Together, Laureate schools offer a wide range of courses in numerous academic areas.
  • HACU member institutions in the U.S., including Puerto Rico, have exclusive access to the scholarship program.

Arizona State University is a HACU member institution - ASU students are eligible for this scholarship.

THE ASU PARENTS ASSOCIATION SCHOLARSHIP AWARDS

APPLICATION DEADLINE:  MARCH 1, 2007

The Parents Association at Arizona State University supports students and the university through the Parents Association Scholarships.

With the generous support of ASU parents across the nation, the Parents Association has established a fund to make multiple one-time awards of $4,000 for the 2007 ? 2008 academic year. Funds are distributed to scholars in 50 percent increments for the fall and spring semesters during financial aid disbursement. In addition to the financial award, scholars have the opportunity to get involved in the ASU community via the Parents Association and participation in such events as Welcome Week and Family Weekend. Strong consideration is given for financial need, community service and/or university involvement and to students who are the first generation in their immediate family planning to graduate from a four year college or university.  Awards will also be based on demonstrated evidence of strong commitment to academic success and achievement of personal and educational goals.

Applications can be found on the Parents Association website at: www.asu.edu/parents .

Harry T. and Leone Goulding Scholarship

Harry T. and Leone Goulding Scholarships are merit-based awards of up
to $10,000 per year for first-year students who are Navajo or from the
Four Corners region of Utah , Colorado , New Mexico and Arizona .
Candidates for these scholarships have taken challenging courses,
performed well, and contributed to their schools and communities.
Several Goulding Scholars are selected each year on the basis of their
application for admission.

The Goulding Scholarship was established in honor of Harry T. and Leone
Goulding, long-time friends of Knox College and founders of the historic
Goulding Trading Post in the Monument Valley area of Utah . During the
early 1950's, the Gouldings donated land for the establishment of
medical services for the valley?s Navajo residents. Monument Valley
Hospital was constructed near the Trading Post and Lodge in 1961 and
still today serves the people of the area.

The Scholars Program

The Scholars Program brings talented African American, Latino, American Indian/Alaska Native, and Asian/Pacific Islander college seniors and recent graduates to Washington , D.C. , where they are placed in Congressional offices and learn about health policy. Through the nine-week program (May 21- July 28, 2007), Scholars gain knowledge about federal legislative procedure and health policy issues, while further developing their critical thinking and leadership skills. In addition to gaining experience in a Congressional office, Scholars participate in seminars and site visits to augment their knowledge of health care issues, and write and present a health policy research memo.

All materials are available at the website: http://www.kff.org/about/jordanscholars.cfm

The Washington Center 's American Indian/Alaska Native Initiative

Summer 2007 Internship ? Deadline, March 15

The Washington Center for Internships and Academic Seminars has fully-funded internships available for American Indian students and recent graduates to intern in DC while earning academic credit and participating in career-enhancing structured programming.

STUDENTS RECEIVE:

  • An all-paid-for internship through a federal agency-internship sponsor that provides a travel stipend to and from Washington , D.C. , fully-furnished TWC housing, fully-covered TWC program fee, and a $250 weekly stipend
  • Internships with challenging job responsibilities in a major government agency;
  • Practical job training workshops with an emphasis on writing and enhancing overall communication skills, guidance and career counseling, and social activities and events

For further information about the American Indian/Alaska Native Initiative, please visit http://www.twc.edu/students/financial_federal.shtml#american , or contact Machell Louis , Program Coordinator at 202-513-7529 or via email at MachellK@twc.edu .
