March 30, 2006

Long earns Distinguished Achievement Award

Robert F. Long, vice president for programs in the philanthropy and volunteerism program area at the W.K. Kellogg Foundation, received the Distinguished Achievement Award from ASU's College of Public Programs at a private reception in his honor March 20.

It is the highest award the college bestows on a non-graduate.

Long has been supportive of ASU's Center for Nonprofit Leadership and Management (CNLM), contributing to its design of academic and practitioner partnerships for effective nonprofit capacity building. He has advocated for Kellogg Foundation investments and has encouraged other philanthropic donors, resulting in nearly $8 million in grants to CNLM since its launch in 1999. From 2004 - 2005, in his role as senior fellow at CNLM, he developed and taught a graduate course on nonprofit sector trends and issues; participated in program and resource development activities; and contributed to strategic organizational development planning.

Long has been with the W. K. Kellogg Foundation since 1993, and he manages more than 300 projects and $100 million annually. He previously served as an endowed professor of youth leadership studies at the University of Northern Iowa.

Long's commitment to improving society through new approaches to philanthropy, nonprofit management education and community-university relationships is evident through the broad acclaim he has received. He was selected three times as one of the Nonprofit Times' "Top 50" most powerful and influential leaders of the nonprofit sector in the United States.

"We have a champion in Bob Long," says Debra Friedman, dean of the College of Public Programs. "His enduring support and guidance have propelled our center to national prominence and multiplied its capacity to serve the thousands of nonprofit organizations across the state."

"Bob Long has not only helped shape our thinking about capacity building, but he has contributed in countless ways to the development of nonprofit academic centers nationwide," adds Robert Ashcraft, director of the Center for Nonprofit Leadership and Management. "He has always focused on that which matters most given his fundamental commitment to student development, community engagement, and use-inspired research. His intellect and energy know no limits when it comes to pushing higher education institutions to realize their promise as forces for positive social change in communities."

Previous recipients of this award include Terry Goddard, Brad Nielsen, Walter Cronkite, Betsey Bayless, John P. Frank, Janet Napolitano and Hugh Downs.
