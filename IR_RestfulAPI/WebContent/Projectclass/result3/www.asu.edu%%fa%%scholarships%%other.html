Army Reserve Officer Training Corps (ROTC) Two, Three, and Four Year Scholarships
Award:
  • Total Potential Award
    • Full tuition
    • Books
    • Labs and fees
    • Plus a monthly stipend of $225.00
Eligibility:
  • US Citizen
  • Under age 27 on June 30th on the year you graduate college
  • Minimum 920 SAT, 19 ACT
  • Pursue an Army-approved academic major
  • Agree to accept a commission as either a Regular Army or US Army Reserve Officer
Application:
  • Applications are available from the ASU ROTC department at 965-0782, can be mailed upon request.
  • Also available online at armyrotc.com.
Conditions of Award:
  • Admissible to ASU and full-time enrollment at ASU (completion of 24 ASU credits per academic year).
Contact:
  • Scholarship Admissions Office at (480) 965-0782
  • (800) 858-ROTC
  • www.asu.edu/clas/military
Asian American & Pacific Islander Student Scholarship
Award:
  • Award for outstanding individuals who demonstrate academic excellence combined with leadership and service.
Eligibility:
  • Will attend or are currently attending ASU in Tempe
  • Minimum 3.0 GPA (on a 4.0 scale) for high school or university applicants
  • Demonstration of strong leadership in co-curricular activities and community service
  • Full-time, undergraduate student (minimum 12 credits per semester)
  • Preference given to United States citizens or permanent residents
  • Arizona residency
  • Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid by March 1
Application:
  • Asian American & Pacific Student Scholarship Application
Phelps Dodge/Una Promesa Community (PD/UPC) Scholars
Award:
  • The Phelps Dodge/Una Promesa Community Scholars are one-year competitive tuition scholarships.
  • The organizations that participate in the Phelps Dodge/Una Promesa Community Scholars program will use the criteria listed below as the minimum criteria for selection of recipients.
Minimum Criteria for New Recipients:

Note: The minimum criteria listed on the right does not apply to Los Abogados scholarship applicants. Los Abogados will coordinate their selection process with the ASU College of Law. The contact at the College of Law is Joseph Lindsay.
  • Admitted to Arizona State University.
  • Must enroll full time (12 credit hours).
  • Resident of the state of Arizona.
  • It is strongly recommended that students file a Free Application For Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) at www.fafsa.ed.gov by the ASU priority filing date to be considered for other types of aid.
  • Selection Categories for undergraduate students.
    • Entering freshmen Top 15% of their class.
    • High school GPA 3.0 and above.
    • ASU Sophomores ASU Grade Point Average 2.50 and above.
    • ASU Juniors: ASU Grade Point Average 3.0 and above.
    • ASU Seniors: ASU Grade Point Average 3.0 and above.
    • Community College Transfer GPA of 3.0 and above.
Specific Organization Criteria & Applications:
  • Hispanic Business Alumni
    • Recipients must be Business majors.
  • Hispanic Women's Corporation
    • Applicants from all 3 ASU campuses are encouraged to apply.
    • Special consideration given to nursing and health sciences students.
  • Chicanos Faculty and Staff Association - Laura Rendon Scholarship
  • Chicanos Por La Causa
    • Special consideration: students from rural areas and outside of Maricopa County.
  • Phelps Dodge Copper Scholars
  • Los Diablos Alumni
  • Cesar Chavez Leadership Institute
    • Preference given to alumni of the Cesar Chavez Leadership Institute.
  • Los Abogados
    • Students admitted to the ASU College of Law.
    • Specific selection criteria and process determined by Los Abogados and the College of Law.
    • The scholarship is open to any law students who demonstrate a commitment to serving the Hispanic community and who strive to become leaders in the Hispanic community.
    • Nine students are selected annually (3 per class year) based on their past and present service to the Hispanic community (including participation in student organizations and programs), their potential for serving the Hispanic community in the future, and their financial need.
    • The contact at the College of Law is Joseph Lindsay.
    • The website is www.losabogados.net.
Current PD/UPC Scholars Recipients?? Reapplication Minimum Criteria:
  • 2.5 GPA in ASU coursework.
  • Satisfactory progress towards the completion of a degree, or a minimum of 24 earned credits in the academic year of award.
  • Other criteria as required by the donor organization.
