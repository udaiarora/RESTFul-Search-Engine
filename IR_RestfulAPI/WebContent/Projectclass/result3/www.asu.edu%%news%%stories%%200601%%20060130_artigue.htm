January 30, 2006

Valley sports business leader joins ASU

Ray Artigue, one of the most recognized leaders in Valley sports business, has joined the W. P. Carey School of Business at ASU as executive director of its MBA Sports Business program. He brings three decades of marketing knowledge and executive management capabilities to his new role.

"I'm very excited to be joining the faculty of the W. P. Carey School and its emerging MBA program," Artigue says. "My new position will allow me to share the practical knowledge that I have to help students develop into outstanding sports business professionals."

Artigue joins the W. P. Carey School after 15 years as the senior vice president of marketing and communications for the Phoenix Suns. As such, he was responsible for all of the team's marketing efforts, including the advertising, public relations, community relations, ticket sales, fan loyalty, branding and research functions. Under his leadership, many of the Suns' marketing programs have become "best practices" throughout the NBA and sports business.

As executive director, Artigue will have a wide range of responsibilities including classroom teaching, directing sophisticated consultative projects for the industry, managing internship and career development activities, and promoting the program and its distinctive events and projects. He also will assist in the strategic marketing of the W. P. Carey School.

"We are very excited to have Ray join our team," says Michael Mokwa, founding director of the sports business program and the Tillman Foundation Distinguished Professor. "Because of his extensive experience, he has the ability to work well with a wide range of groups."

The W. P. Carey MBA Sports Business program integrates conventional MBA coursework with advanced knowledge and skills specific to the sports industry. The graduates bring value to franchises and companies through their high-level understanding of revenue generation and marketing, revenue and financial management, sports market behaviors, fan satisfaction measurements, and sports law and politics. Students also build and demonstrate skills through comprehensive applied projects for industry partners throughout their course of studies.

According to Steve Hank, a graduate of the sports business program and the ASU assistant athletic director for revenue generation and marketing, there was no better choice than Artigue to take the helm.

"Ray Artigue is one of the consummate professionals in our business," Hank says. "He has exceptional people skills and business acumen and is a true educator. His vision and passion will help carry the MBA program to new heights."

Artigue graduated from ASU in 1976 with a bachelor's degree from the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication. In 1994, he received the Hall of Fame Award from the ASU College of Public Programs and three years later, the Hall of Fame Award from the Cronkite School. In 2002, he received the first "Distinguished Champion Award" from the W. P. Carey School of Business MBA Sports Business program.

As a member of numerous civic and community organizations, Artigue serves or has served on a variety of boards, including the Maricopa County Sports Commission, Walter Cronkite Endowment Board, Phoenix Suns Charities, Home Base Youth Services, Sun Angel Foundation, Phoenix Museum of History, and the Phoenix Thunderbirds. In 1999, Artigue served as general chairman of the PGA Tour Phoenix Open and as the organization's president in 2000.
