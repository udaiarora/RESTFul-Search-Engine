March 16, 2006

Lecturer takes critical look at EU woes

Sir Neil MacCormick, Regius Professor of Law at Scotland's University of Edinburgh, will present the 11th Annual Willard H. Pedrick Lecture at 4 p.m., March 29, in the College of Law's Pedrick Great Hall.

The topic of his lecture is "When Founding Fathers Fail: The European Union's Unadopted Constitution."

MacCormick began his distinguished academic career in law in 1965 and was one of the youngest professors ever appointed by the University of Edinburgh . He is one of the world's leading philosophers of law and has been Regius Professor of Law at the University of Edinburgh since 1972. He holds a Leverhulme Personal Research Chair for a project on "Law, State and Practical Reason."

At the University of Edinburgh, MacCormick also served as dean of law faculty (1973 - 1976, 1985 - 1988); provost faculty group of law and social science, and dean of faculty of social science (1994 - 1997); and vice principal for international affairs (1997 - 1999).

MacCormick is a graduate of Glasgow, Oxford and Edinburgh universities, and he also holds honorary law doctorates from universities in several countries. He is a foreign member of the Finnish Academy; a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh and of the British Academy; a member of the Academia Europaea; and an honorary Queen's Counsel. In 2001, he was knighted "for services to scholarship in law."

From 1999 - 2004, he served as a member of the European Parliament for Scotland. In that position, he took part in the Convention on the Future of Europe that drafted the "Treaty Establishing a Constitution for Europe," whose ratification is being debated.

MacCormick is a former member of European Parliament committees on legal affairs and the single market (coordinator for the Greens/European Free Alliance Group); its constitutional affairs committee; and vice president of the temporary committee (2000 - 2001) on the echelon interception system. He also has served as a substitute member on the Convention on the Future of Europe, as well as vice chairman of the Scottish National Party.

His most recent publications include "Rhetoric and the Rule of Law: A Theory of Legal Reasoning" (2005) and "Who's Afraid of a European Constitution?" (2005). He is the author of many articles and books on legal and political theory, including "Questioning Sovereignty: Law, State and Nation in the European Commonwealth" (1999); "Legal Right and Social Democracy" (1982); and "Legal Reasoning and Legal Theory" (1978).

The Willard H. Pedrick Lecture was established in 1997 by the Pedrick family in memory of the founding dean of the College of Law . The annual lecture brings to the law school outstanding legal scholars, jurists or practitioners to enrich the intellectual life of the college and the community.

A reception will be held immediately following the lecture. The lecture is free and open to the public. To R.S.V.P., or for more information, send an e-mail to (amanda.breaux@asu.edu) or call (480) 965-6405.
