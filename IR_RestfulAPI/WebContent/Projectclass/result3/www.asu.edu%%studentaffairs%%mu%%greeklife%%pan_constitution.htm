Panhellenic Council - Constitution

PREAMBLE

In order to promote the best interest of the University; to assist and strengthen fraternities in the achievement of their worthy purposes; to encourage superior scholarship and academic excellence; to provide a medium for exercising self-government and the regulation of fraternity affairs; to advance the quality and standing of fraternity life on campus; to act as a liaison between fraternities and the University administration; to strive for effective communication and cooperation among fraternities in a forum of common interest and activity; and to foster fellowship and good will among the fraternities and the student body; we, the members of Panhellenic do ordain and establish this constitution.

ARTICLE I. NAME AND PURPOSE

Section 1
The name of this organization shall be the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University.
Section 2
The purpose of the Panhellenic Council shall be to develop and maintain fraternity life and inter-fraternity relations at a high level of service and achievement consistent with the Unanimous Agreements of the National Panhellenic Conference and the policies of Arizona State University.

ARTICLE II. MEMBERSHIP

Section 1
The membership of the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University shall be of two types: regular and associate.
  • The regular membership of the Panhellenic Council shall be composed of all chapter members in good standing of National Panhellenic Conference Fraternities at Arizona State University.
  • The associate membership shall consist of all members in good standing of National Panhellenic Conference colonies at Arizona State University, as well as of all initiated members of non-National Panhellenic Conference women's fraternities at Arizona State University which have been voted into associate membership by a majority of the voting members on Panhellenic Council.
Section 2
The Panhellenic Council shall establish through the bylaws of the Panhellenic Council such other reasonable procedures, standards, and requirements for granting regular and associate memberships, as it deems necessary and consistent with this constitution, the Unanimous Agreements of the National Panhellenic Conference and the policies of Arizona State University.
Section 3
Regular and associate members of the Panhellenic Council shall be entitled to all rights and privileges separately granted to each herein. Regular and associate membership shall involve support of this constitution, its bylaws, and the Unanimous Agreements of the National Panhellenic Conference.

ARTICLE III. OFFICERS AND STRUCTURE

Section 1
The executive officers of the Panhellenic Council shall be President, Vice President of Judicial Affairs, Vice President of Membership, Vice President of Administration, and Vice President of Development. Duties of the officers shall be set forth in the bylaws.
  • The executive officers shall be delegates from fraternities holding regular membership in the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University. Delegates from fraternities holding associate memberships shall not be eligible to hold executive office.
  • The officers shall serve for a term of one year, the term of office to begin the day of transitioning, which follows a week after elections.
Section 2
The Panhellenic Council shall function through the following:
  • Executive Officers
  • Panhellenic Cabinet
  • Panhellenic Council
  • Order of Alpha

ARTICLE IV. ELECTIONS

Section 1
Executive officers shall be elected annually by a majority of the regular members of the Panhellenic Council in accordance with the bylaws.
  • Candidates for executive office shall be initiated members in good standing of a fraternity holding regular membership in the Panhellenic Council.
  • Candidates for office shall have a minimum 2.50 grade point average.
  • Candidates for executive office must have served a minimum of one full year as an officer of Panhellenic or in their own chapter, Panhellenic Council Assistant, Panhellenic delegate, and/or Order of Alpha representative.
Section 2
Delegates to Panhellenic Council from fraternities holding associate membership shall not be eligible to hold an executive office.
Section 3
No more than two fraternity members of the same fraternity shall serve as executive officers concurrently.

ARTICLE V. SUCCESSION OF OFFICES

Section 1
In the event an executive officer cannot complete her term in office, the vacancy shall be filled as follows:
  • A vacancy in the Presidency shall be filled by the Vice President of Judicial Affairs. The resultant vacancy in the Vice President's position shall be filled by a regular member who has met all requirements for holding executive office as enumerated herein; and who has been elected by a majority of the regular members of the Panhellenic Council in accordance with the bylaws.
  • A vacancy in any other executive office shall be filled by a regular member who has met all requirements for holding executive office as enumerated herein; and who has been elected by a majority of the regular members of the Panhellenic Council in accordance with the bylaws.

ARTICLE VI. EXECUTIVE BOARD

Section 1
The Executive Board shall consist of the five executive officers: President, Vice President of Judicial Affairs, Vice President of Membership, Vice President of Administration, and Vice President of Development.
Section 2
The Executive Board shall coordinate the functions and activities of the Panhellenic Council.
Section 3
Standing Committees may be established and appointed as necessary:
  • Members shall serve at the pleasure of the Panhellenic Council for a period not to exceed the council members' term in office.
  • Regular and associate members shall be eligible to serve on standing committees.
  • The standing committees shall have such duties and responsibilities prescribed in the bylaws and consistent with this constitution.
Section 4
The President shall preside over the Executive Board.
Section 5
The Panhellenic Cabinet is composed of the assistants to the four vice presidents.

ARTICLE VII. PANHELLENIC COUNCIL

Section 1
The administrative body of the Panhellenic Council shall be the Panhellenic Council. It shall be the duty of the Panhellenic Council to administer all business related to the overall welfare of the Panhellenic Council and to compile rules governing the Panhellenic Council including recruitment and new members, which do not violate the sovereignty, rights and privileges of member fraternities.
Section 2
The Panhellenic Council shall be composed of one delegate and one alternate delegate from each National Panhellenic Conference fraternity chapter at Arizona State University and from such National Panhellenic Conference fraternity colonies, or other sororities at Arizona State University as the Council may approve for membership in the Panhellenic Council. Each chapter holding regular or associate membership is allowed one vote in business matters coming before the Panhellenic Council.
Section 3
Delegates to Panhellenic Council shall be selected by the respective fraternity chapters to serve for a term of one year. Whenever possible the alternate delegate in one year shall be the delegate for the succeeding year.
Section 4
When a delegate vacancy occurs, it shall be the responsibility of the fraternity concerned to select a replacement within two weeks and to notify the Panhellenic Vice President of Administration of the replacement delegate's name and contact information.
Section 5
The executive officers of the Panhellenic Council shall serve as the officers of the Panhellenic Council.
  • Officers of the Panhellenic Council shall have such duties and responsibilities as are prescribed in the bylaws and consistent with the constitution.
  • Officers of the Panhellenic Council shall have voice but no vote in the Panhellenic Council.
  • The President of the Panhellenic Council shall preside over the meetings of the Panhellenic Council.
Section 6
Meetings of the Panhellenic Council shall be called at a time and frequency to be decided at the first meeting of each semester by the Panhellenic President.
  • The first meetings of each semester shall be called by the President no later than Friday of the third full week of each semester.
  • Special meetings of the Panhellenic Council shall be called by the President, or as a result of a written request presented to the President by any voting delegate to the Panhellenic Council, or the Panhellenic Advisor.
Section 7
Two-thirds of the total regular member fraternities shall constitute the quorum number required for the transaction of business at a meeting of the Panhellenic Council.
Section 8
The Panhellenic Council shall have the following responsibilities:
  • To enact and maintain the bylaws of the Panhellenic Council consistent with this constitution.
  • To establish and enforce policies and procedures for recruitment and colonization.
  • To review and take appropriate action upon the annual budget recommended by the executive officers.
  • To require reports from the President on any activity of the executive officers.
  • To take any action on any matter within the jurisdiction of the Panhellenic Council which is not otherwise prohibited by this constitution, its bylaws, the Unanimous Agreements of the National Panhellenic Conference, or the policies of Arizona State University.

ARTICLE VIII. ORDER OF ALPHA

Section 1
There shall be a Judicial Board, called Order of Alpha, of the Panhellenic Council vested with authority to resolve any dispute arising out of the administration, interpretation, and/or actual violation of this constitution, the bylaws, or other rules and regulations of the Panhellenic Council.
Section 2
If the decision of the Judicial Board is not acceptable, the manner may be appealed to the NPC College Panhellenics Committee. Notice of intention to appeal shall be given to the Panhellenic Council President. All requirements as stated in the NPC Manual of Information shall be met.
Section 3
Delegates to Panhellenic Council shall be selected by the respective fraternity chapters to serve for a term of one year. Whenever possible the alternate delegate in one year shall be the delegate for the succeeding year.
Section 4
Penalties imposed by Order of Alpha shall be in conformity with those recommended by the National Panhellenic Conference.

ARTICLE IX. UNANIMOUS AGREEMENTS AND POLICIES

Section 1
All members of the Panhellenic Council at Arizona State University shall act in accordance with fundamental Panhellenic rules and policies established by National Panhellenic Conference in the Unanimous Agreements.
Section 2
All Panhellenic Council rules and policies shall be in harmony with those established by the National Panhellenic Conference.

ARTICLE X. IMPEACHMENT

Section 1
Articles of impeachment may be brought against any executive officer of the Panhellenic Council.
Section 2
The Panhellenic Council shall have the sole power to try all impeachments.
Section 3
At least one half of the regular member delegates to the Panhellenic Council shall be required to call for the impeachment of an executive officer.
Section 4
An affirmative vote by at least two-thirds of the regular member delegates to the Panhellenic Council shall be required to remove an executive officer from office.

ARTICLE XI. AMENDMENT

This constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of the regular member delegates to the Panhellenic Council, provided notice of the proposed amendment has been given in writing at the preceding regular meeting.

ARTICLE XII. RATIFICATION

This constitution shall become effective immediately upon ratification by two-thirds majority vote of the regular member delegates to the Panhellenic Council.
