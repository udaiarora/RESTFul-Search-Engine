Hayden Library To Host C-SPAN Exhibit, American Presidents: Life Portraits

Traveling Exhibit Co-sponsored by the White House Historical Association Opens October 13


(posted 10/4/04)

Wednesday, October 13 through Thursday, November 4, American Presidents: Life Portraits, a presidential portrait exhibit presented by C-SPAN and the White House Historical Association, will be on display at the Hayden Library at Arizona State University. With the final presidential debate in Arizona this month, the exhibit will highlight presidential history as the nation prepares for the 2004 presidential contest.


American Presidents: Life Portraits features the only complete collection of American presidential oil portraits by one artist.


Commissioned by C-SPAN, artist Chas Fagan painted the presidential portraits to complement C-SPAN’s Peabody Award-winning television series, American Presidents: Life Portraits. The public affairs network devoted 1999 to America’s chief executives, exploring the personal stories of the American presidents, from George Washington to George W. Bush.


Accompanying the Fagan portraits are biographical sketches of all 42 presidents, photographs contributed by the White House Historical Association that capture each president’s time in the White House, and historic front pages from the Atlanta Journal-Constitution. Each front page, beginning in 1876, dates back to the year the President was elected and offers context into the time-period in which the President served.

The American Presidents: Life Portraits exhibit has traveled to many venues around the country including the White House Visitor Center, the Gerald R. Ford Library and Museum in Ann Arbor, MI and the George Bush Presidential Library and Museum in College Station, TX. The exhibit has also been displayed at a number of Campaign sites including the Republican and Democratic National Conventions in Philadelphia and Los Angeles in 2000, the Prudential Center in Boston during the Democratic National Convention in July of 2004, and at the South Street Seaport in NYC during the Republican National Convention in August of this year.


C-SPAN, the political network of record, was created in 1979 by America’s cable companies as a public service. C-SPAN is currently available in 88 million households, C-SPAN2 in over 73 million households, and C-SPAN3 in 8 million households nationwide. For more information about C-SPAN, visit its web site at C-SPAN.org.


The White House Historical Association, a non-profit organization, was created in 1961 to enhance understanding, appreciation and enjoyment of the White House. The association sponsors lectures, exhibits and other outreach programs. Schools, universities and libraries receive free educational materials about the White House. Traveling exhibits and videos are circulated to the presidential libraries and museums around the country.
