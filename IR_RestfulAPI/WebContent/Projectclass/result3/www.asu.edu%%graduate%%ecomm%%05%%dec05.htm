Division of Graduate Studies (DGS) moves to Administration Building, B-wing
at the Tempe campus

The Division of Graduate Studies has moved to the Administration Building, B-wing. The Graduate Admissions office is located on the first floor, room 170. The Vice Provost/Dean's office, Graduate Academic Programs, Academic Program Review (APR) and Accreditation and Graduate Student Programs are all located on second floor of Administration Building, B-wing, along with business operations, IT support, and Publications. Our mail code, telephone numbers, and hours of operation remain the same. Student Academic Services Office and Assistant Dean Mike Dickson remain in Wilson Hall (access through the middle lobby) with plans to move after the first of the year. They will be on the first floor.
— REMINDERS —

ARCS Fellowship Application. December 9 is the deadline for eligible departments to be submitting ARCS applications to Catherine Lebihan at DGS, Mail Code 1003. For more information, visit www.asu.edu/graduate/financial/DGSopportunities.htm

December 2005 Graduation. The University-wide Commencement ceremony is Thursday, December 15 at 10 a.m. Doctoral students must have sent an RSVP form to the DGS in order to participate. Please note that doctoral students may not participate until they have completed all degree requirements (oral defense, going to the bookstore, internships, etc.). www.asu.edu/ssc/commence

Degree Program Start Date. As a reminder, the first day of the semester/year a student is admitted to and enrolls in a degree program determines the initial start date of their program; therefore, any coursework before the first day of that semester/year is nondegree course work. This policy change was effective for students admitted to ASU starting fall 2004 and is effective for all students admitted for a semester after fall 2004. www.asu.edu/graduate/current/sas.htm

Spring 2006 New TA Orientation & Training is being held on Monday, January 9, 2006 and is mandated by ABOR for all new TAs. All TAs hired after August 15 and those TAs that were not able to attend the Fall 2005 TA Orientation must make arrangements to attend. www.asu.edu/graduate/tara/TAorientation.html

TA/RA Benefits. Assistantship benefits' information for spring 2006 must be entered into gPortal by academic units before December 13 tuition payment deadline.
  • New and continuing TAs and RAs for 2005-2006 AY must be entered through our gPortal system to receive their tuition remission and health insurance (if applicable).

  • If you are offering an assistantship to a student enrolled in an academic unit other than your own, you will need to contact his/her "home" department to enter the assistantship information.

  • Remember to fill out the Hiring Process Report (HPR), available at www.asu.edu/hr/forms for any new TAs and RAs that you are hiring.

  • If you do not currently have access to manage TA and RA benefits, please have your direct supervisor send an email request to grad-access@asu.edu.

Winter Session Thesis Defenses. Students must be registered for fall 2005 to defend a thesis or dissertation during the winter session/interim period. All students defending during this period will be May 2006 graduates and should follow the 2006 Graduation Deadlines and Procedures. Please note that students scheduling a defense after January 13, 2006 must be registered for the spring 2006 semester. www.asu.edu/graduate/format
