May 20, 2003

Colorado State University names new president: W. P. Carey School Dean Larry Penley accepts top job

Larry Edward Penley, dean of the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University (ASU), has been named president of Colorado State University and chancellor of the Colorado State University system. The formal announcement will be made tomorrow in Fort Collins, Colo., at a 2 p.m. press conference.

Penley will assume his new post sometime this summer.

"We are grateful for Larry Penley's passionate leadership as dean of the W. P. Carey School of Business for the past 12 years, and for his academic excellence and commitment to quality education at ASU since 1985," says ASU President Michael M. Crow. "Larry has positioned the business school on a steep upward trajectory, and we are committed to continuing on this path with the same energy and passion."

Colorado State University, based in Fort Collins, serves almost 24,000 students at its main campus and three satellite campuses. The system also includes the University of Southern Colorado, which becomes Colorado State University - Pueblo on July 1, 2003.

"We have worked very hard to come up with the right person to lead the institution and the system," said Don Hamstra, president of the board of governors of the Colorado State University system. "In Larry Penley we have selected a leader with vision, experience, integrity and with a real passion for moving Colorado State and the university system forward. We're thrilled to make this announcement tomorrow and excited about what the future holds."

ASU Senior Vice President and Provost Milton Glick said an interim dean will be appointed, and a national search will begin soon to find a top leader to succeed Penley.

"This is an attractive position," Glick said. "The progress of this school over the last decade is extraordinary. We have a strong faculty, and the Carey name and gift gives us an opportunity to bring in a strong leader who can take off from the solid base that Larry Penley built."

Penley has been on the faculty of ASU's business school since 1985, when he was hired as chair of the management department. In 1990, he was named interim dean, and appointed to the permanent position in 1991. His crowning achievement was the January 2003 W. P. Carey School of Business naming, spurred by the second largest gift ever given to a U.S. business school -- $50 million from New York City real estate investment banker Wm. Polk Carey.

During his tenure as dean, the W. P. Carey School has emerged as a top-ranked MBA and undergraduate program led by a strong research faculty and supported with the latest in information technology.

Penley developed deep collaborative ties with the business community regionally and nationally, leading to the program improvements and funding resources that fueled the W. P. Carey School's rapid rise to prominence. The L. William Seidman Research Institute grew to include eight prominent research centers providing business data and new knowledge essential to companies and the community. Under his leadership, the school added a dozen endowed chaired and 15 professorships, greatly improving incentives for the best faculty to join and remain at the W. P. Carey School.

"Larry Penley's leadership brought ASU into the ranks of the leading public business schools," said Lee McPheters, associate dean for executive and professional programs. "The most notable program accomplishments were in the national recognition of the MBA program and the growth in size and quality of the Business Honors program, but faculty research productivity also continuously improved. The Dean emphasized technology and this was evident in the success of the online MBA program as well as the growth in technology support for faculty staff and students."

Penley is past chair of AACSB International - The Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business. At ASU, he is an affiliated faculty member with the Center for Latin American Studies. He serves on the boards of several community organizations, including the Greater Phoenix Economic Council. He is the past chair of the K-12 Education Task Force of the Valley Citizens League and was honored by INROADS Arizona with the Frank C. Carr award.

Raised in Tennessee, Penley received a B. A. in psychology and M. S. in communication from Wake Forest University, and a Ph. D. in management at The University of Georgia. He studied Spanish at Centro de Artes y Lenguas, Mexico, and Universidad Autonoma de Mexico. He has taught strategic and human resource management. His executive training experience and his consulting have brought him into contact with industries ranging from manufacturing to health care. In addition to his service at ASU, Penley's academic career included 10 years on the faculty at University of Texas, San Antonio, and was a visiting faculty member at Universidad de Carbobo, Venezuala and ITESM, Mexico.
