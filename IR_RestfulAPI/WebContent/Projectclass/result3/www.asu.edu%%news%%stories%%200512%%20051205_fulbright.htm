December 5, 2005

Fellner, Prorok earn Fulbright honors

Dan H. Fellner, a faculty associate in the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication, has received a Fulbright Scholar grant to teach journalism and public relations in Moldova. He will teach at Moldova State University in Chisinau, Moldova, for five months beginning in January.

Like many of the countries belonging to the former Soviet Union, Moldova is building a free press. It became an independent country in 1991.

This will be Fellner’s fourth teaching stint in Eastern Europe. In 2001 – 2002, he spent three semesters teaching journalism and public relations at universities in Riga, Latvia, as a Fulbright Scholar. In 2004 and 2005, he received grants through the Fulbright Senior Specialists Program and taught at universities in Kaunas, Lithuania, and Daugavpils, Latvia.

Also named to receive a Fulbright grant was Mandy Prorok, program coordinator in the Office of International Programs, who attended a three-week seminar in Germany in October for international education administrators.

Prorok learned about major educational changes that are under way in the European Union, how they are affecting European higher education and how they will affect U.S. colleges and universities.

They are among about 850 U.S. faculty and professionals who will travel abroad to some 150 countries this academic year to teach and do research. The program was established in 1946 by the U.S. Department of State to building mutual understanding between the American people and those of other countries.
