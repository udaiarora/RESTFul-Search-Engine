LIST's Newsletter
August, 2002

Major changes are happening in LIST in August! Read on for the good news about WAM and SerialSSolutions, and a farewell to one of our favorite staff members, Jennifer Garcia.

Two new products to improve access to Electronic Resources

SerialSSolutions for Electronic Journal Access

On Monday, August 19, 2002, ASU Library patrons will have a new and improved way to access full text electronic journals. To keep up with the ever-growing selection of electronic journals, the ASU Library has contracted with SerialSSolutions to manage the holdings for these resources. SS contacts the vendors, publishers and aggregators and produces a list of updated holdings and URLs every other month. Library staff can see the current list, accurate as of July 1, 2002 at: http://www.asu.edu/lib/techserv/ejh/geninfo.html

This address is distributed to library staff only for testing and review. Comments on the list can be sent to ejournalcomments@mainex1.asu.edu. On Monday, August 19, 2002 the address for this page will be: http://www.asu.edu/lib/resources/ejournals.htm

The list will be updated every other month, and the next update will be in early September.

In addition to the updated Electronic Journals List, SerialSSolutions has been working with Innovative Interfaces to provide information that can be directly downloaded into our catalog records. Working through the III Millennium Serials interface, we will be able to load the holdings information and current URLs directly into the catalog records for each title. This product is still in development, but look forward to more information in the future.

Web Access Management (WAM) provides access to electronic resources for remote users

LIST is currently implementing a new product, Web Access Management, which will enable the ASU Library to better serve our remote patrons. We are working to increase the number of electronic resources available to remote patrons without an ASU PPP account. Web Access Management will allow off-site users to authenticate via the On-Line Catalog patron database, and then will pass them through with an ASU IP address.

Initially users will need to configure their browsers to use the proxy service, but after the simple setup they will be able to use many of the resources available only to on-site or ASU PPP users. Look forward to additional information on this product as development continues.


Jennifer Garcia Moves Onward and Upward!

L.I.S.T. is sad to say goodbye this month to Jennifer Garcia, who recently completed law school, took (and, we are sure, passed!) the bar exam, and accepted a clerkship with a judge in the Arizona Court of Appeals. Jen took over so capably when our administrative secretary relocated two and a half years ago, and went to such lengths to fit her library job in with her school schedule and her law-related job, that it?s hard to believe she was only a student worker all that time. We will miss her dedication, cheerfulness, hard work, and fashion sense. Congratulations and good luck, Jen!
