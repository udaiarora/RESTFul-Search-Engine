Memorial Union 50th Opening Ceremony: A Rededication

Thursday, February 16
11:20 am - 1:00 pm
North Entrance, Memorial Union

The Memorial Union 50th Anniversary Opening Ceremony: A Rededication is the official kick-off event for the MU's 50th anniversary celebration. This opening event begins with a rededication of the memorial status of the facility and encompasses a "birthday" celebration immediately afterwards.

The rededication ceremony will include:
  • Presentation of Color by Air Force ROTC
  • National Anthem Performed by "Pitchforks" - all-female a cappella ASU student organization
  • Brief Speeches from:
    • Sally Ramage, Interim Vice President, Student Affairs
    • Brett Perozzi, Director, Memorial Union
    • Corinne Widmer, President, Undergraduate Student Government - Associated Students of ASU
    • Jess Koldoff, President, Graduate and Professional Student Association - Associated Students of ASU
    • Andrew Moe, President, Programming and Activities Board - Associated Students of ASU
    • Milton Glick, Executive Vice President and Provost of the University
    • Grady Gammage Jr.
  • Poem Reading by Alberto Rios*
  • Introduction of New Memorial Union Advisory Board
  • Moment of Silence
  • Ribbon Cutting
  • MU 50th Birthday Celebration

Birthday cupcakes and punch will be served at the conclusion of the ceremony.

*Alberto Ríos is the author of eight books and chapbooks of poetry, three collections of short stories, and a memoir. Ríos is the recipient of the Arizona Governor’s Arts Award, fellowships from the Guggenheim Foundation and the National Endowment for the Arts, the Walt Whitman Award, the Western States Book Award for Fiction, five Pushcart Prizes in both poetry and fiction, and inclusion in The Norton Anthology of Modern Poetry, as well as over 150 other national and international literary anthologies. His work is regularly taught and translated, and has been adapted to dance and both classical and popular music. Ríos is presently Regents’ Professor of English at ASU.
