Reference Department Policy and Guidelines
Covering Work at Home

Approved by Dept. Consensus 7/13/95

 

I. Definition:

 

Work at Home is defined as ASU Library related work/activities accomplished at home in lieu of the work place, with specific, advance authorization by one's supervisor. This definition and policy does not include or apply to work "taken home" beyond the 8 hour workday, or other professional activities pursued at home which extend beyond the regular 8 hour workday.

 

II. Guiding Principles for requesting/approving Work at Home:

 

1. Permission to work at home is not an individual's right; it is granted for a specific amount of time, and for a specific purpose. Permission to work at home will be granted based on the demonstrated need and the condition that it will have a minimal negative impact on departmental and team operations. Scheduled reference desk responsibilities (as well as other Departmental and Team responsibilities and commitments) take priority over an individual's need to work at home. It is an individual's responsibility to ensure that reference desk shifts for which s/he is scheduled (or other job commitments) will be covered before requesting permission to work at home (i.e., it is the individual's responsibility to "clear" his/her schedule to work at home).

 

2. There is a defined limit on the length of time for which a request to work at home will be considered/approved; approval to work at home will be given for no more than eight hours/day, for no more than two consecutive days, without approval by team consensus. Team approval (i.e., consensus) will be required for requests for longer term work at home schedules, or for work at home schedules which otherwise fall outside of the "two consecutive days" guideline (e.g., work at home one day every week or every other week for a month, semester, etc.). For such cases, refer to III/4, below.

 

3. Hours worked at home must be accountable and task or project specific.

 

4. Nothing in this policy will be interpreted to supersede or contradict current or future policies regarding work at home schedules developed by the ASU Libraries or by the University.

 

III. Process for requesting/approving permission to Work at Home

 

1. Submit to your immediate supervisor/PE a specific proposal to work at home for a specific time period (it is understood that requests to work at home might necessarily be made with short notice--however, every effort should be made to anticipate the need, and seek approval as far in advance as is practical). Address need/purpose, impact on personal and departmental workload(s), whether you will be accessible by phone or email, etc.; "make your case", considering department-approved guidelines and criteria for evaluating work at home requests (see below). Requests may be made in writing, or conveyed verbally.

 

2. If the requestor's supervisor/PE is someone other than the Team Leader, the supervisor/PE will forward request to the Team Leader with recommendation of support or non-support.

 

3. The Team Leader will approve/deny request based upon the criteria/guidelines developed and approved by the Department.

 

4. If the request requires Team approval by consensus, the Team Leader will coordinate this process and communicate the Team's decision to the requestor; if consensus cannot be reached, the Team Leader will approve/deny the request based upon the criteria/guidelines developed and approved by the Department.

 

IV. Criteria for evaluating requests to Work at Home:

 

The following criteria are listed in descending order of importance/weight.

 

1. Demonstrated need: How and why the specific task(s)/ project(s) would be best completed at home rather than in the work place.

 

Examples: (1) work needs to be done on a home computer due to specific software/hardware needs not met at work; (2) block of time free of interruptions needed to complete a specific project or meet a work-related deadline such as personnel reviews, report deadline, etc.

 

2. The number of persons approved to work at home on any specific day(s): The potential impact working at home will have on the department increases with the number of persons working at home; though there is no specific "cutoff" on the number of requests/requestors, multiple requests will be considered accordingly and Departmental needs will always take precedence.

 

3. Personal needs of requestor (e.g., the need to be home to attend to or to accommodate personal/family commitments, schedules, or responsibilities): In our current Team environment, it has become increasingly important that Team members be onsite during working hours, even when not scheduled on the reference desk. Therefore, requests to work at home based on "personal" circumstances are strongly discouraged and such circumstances are more appropriately addressed through the use of sick time, compensatory time, or vacation time.
