Membership in the National Academies

NATIONAL ACADEMY OF ENGINEERING

Ronald J. Adrian, Professor, Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: measurement techniques to characterize single and multiphase turbulent flows; interpretation of turbulence measurements. Elected NAE 1996.

John C. Crittenden, Richard Snell Presidential Chair of Civil and Environmental Engineering, Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: removing toxic organic compounds from air and drinking water. Elected NAE 2002.

Gerald T. Heydt, Director, Center for the Advanced Control of Energy and Power Systems, Department of Electrical Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: technology of electric power quality. Elected NAE 1997.

Subhash Mahajan, Chair, Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: structure-property relationships in semiconductors; magnetic materials; light-wave communication. Elected NAE 2005.

James W. Mayer, Regents’ Professor and Galvin Professor of Science and Engineering, Center for Solid State Science. Research areas: ion implantation, Rutherford backscattering spectrometry, and other major aspects of solid-state engineering research and education. Elected NAE 1984.

Bruce E. Rittman, Professor, Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: biofilm fundamentals; cleanup of contaminated waters, soils, and ecosystems. Elected NAE 2004.

John M. Rowell, Distinguished Research Professor, Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: superconductivity; Josephson effect; electron-phonon interaction and conventional superconductivity; low and high-Tc superconductors. Elected NAS 1994. Elected NAE 1995.

Rustum Roy, Research Professor, Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: non-metallic materials. Elected NAE 1973.

Milton C. Shaw, Emeritus Professor of Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: chemical synthesis, lubrication and bearing design, and machine tool design and performance. Elected NAE 1968.

Vijay Vittal, Murray and Ruth Harpole Professor, Department of Electrical Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: improvements in real-time control and dynamic security assessment for electric power systems. Elected NAE 2004.

 

NATIONAL ACADEMY OF SCIENCES

Charles J. Arntzen, Florence Ely Nelson Presidential Chair, School of Life Sciences. Research areas: basic research in plant molecular biology and protein engineering, as well as the utilization of plant biotechnology for enhancement of food quality and value; expression of pharmacologically active products in transgenic plants; and overcoming health and agricultural constraints in the developing world. Elected NAS 1983.

Jane Buikstra, Professor, School of Human Evolution and Social Change, College of Liberal Arts and Sciences. Research areas: paleopathology; paleonutrition; health and demographic characteristics of prehistoric populations. Elected NAS 1987.

Roy Curtiss, Professor, School of Life Sciences. Research areas: genetic and biochemical bases of pathogenicity of various bacteria, such as Salmonella and E. coli; recombinant vaccines against agricultural and human pathogens; edible vaccines; biologic containment for genetically engineered organisms. Elected NAS 2001.

Bert Hoelldobler, Professor, School of Life Sciences (joint appointment, University of Würzburg). Research areas: evolutionary biology; insect behavior and the behavioral ecology of invertebrates. Elected NAS (Foreign Associate) 1998.

John M. Rowell, Distinguished Visiting Professor, Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. Research areas: superconductivity; Josephson effect; electron-phonon interaction and conventional superconductivity; low and high-Tc superconductors. Elected NAS 1994. Elected NAE 1995.
