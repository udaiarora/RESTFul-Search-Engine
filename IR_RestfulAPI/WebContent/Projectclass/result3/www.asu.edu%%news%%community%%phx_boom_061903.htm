June 19, 2003

Urban expert says Phoenix heading for boom or bust

Like Paris in the 1860s, New York City in the 1910s, or Los Angeles in the 1950s, Phoenix is humming with the energy and excitement of a city on the verge of blossoming.

Nan Ellin, ASU associate professor in the College of Architecture & Environmental Design, says Phoenix is at a tipping point and the choices citizens make will determine whether or not Phoenix tips toward becoming a more vibrant metropolitan area.

Currently the sixth largest city in the United States, Phoenix is growing the fastest of the 15 largest cities and expected to be the third largest city by 2020.

"This rare combination of human ingenuity and urban growth presents tremendous opportunities for bringing Phoenix into the hallowed realm of most livable and vibrant cities." But Ellin warns, "We will not avail ourselves of these opportunities unless certain questions pertinent to this particular place and this particular time are adequately addressed."

These questions include:

"What will it take for Phoenix to offer a truly urban experience in addition to the other lifestyles currently available?"

"How can our city become pedestrian-friendly as well as auto-friendly?"

"How can it be authentic, true to its roots, have a sense of place, or a 'there' there?"

"How can we gracefully accommodate people of different ethnicities, races, social classes, age groups, and abilities?"

"These questions are both logistical and existential," she says. "Answering them requires skill as well as imagination. And the future success of our city hinges upon how we choose to respond."

Ellin will present her perspective on these issues at the free, public presentation, "Slash City," from noon to 1 p.m., June 25, at the ASU Downtown Center, 502 E. Monroe St.

The presentation is part of the ASU College of Extended Education's summer lecture series and should be of particular interest to urban planners, city and corporate leaders and citizens interested in the potential for Phoenix growth.

Ellin was a Fulbright Scholar in France where she carried out research for her dissertation on the European New Urbanism. She is the author of "Postmodern Urbanism" which offers a survey and critique of contemporary urban design.
Reservations for this free event are required and may be made by calling (480) 965-3046 or online at (www.asu.edu/xed/lectures/downtown revitalization.htm). Free parking will be available for attendees.

This presentation is part of the summer lecture series produced by the College of Extended Education.
