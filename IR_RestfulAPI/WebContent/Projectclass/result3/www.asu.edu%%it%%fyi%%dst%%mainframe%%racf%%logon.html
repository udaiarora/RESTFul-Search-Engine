LOGON

The logon password, which is also known as the TSO password or the system access password, is used when you logon to TSO or submit a batch job.

Logon passwords will need to be changed at least every 90 days. Any new logon passwords must be at least 6 characters in length; the maxiumum length is 8 characters. Passwords may contain only uppercase letters, digits, and national characters. (National characters are the dollar sign $, the at sign @, and the pound sign #.) When you change your logon password, you cannot change it to the current password or to the previous password.

Whenever you logon to TSO, the system will automatically prompt you to change your password if your password has expired, i.e., if one of the following conditions is true:

  • the first time you logon to your account
  • your password is more the 90 days old
  • the first time you logon after the Computer Accounts Office resets your password (this would occur if you had forgotten the password and requested the Computer Accounts Office to reset it)

It is not necessary to wait for one of the preceding events to occur before changing your password.

Once a logon password has expired, you cannot successfully logon to TSO or submit a batch job until the password is changed. If you logon to TSO, the system will automatically inform you when the password has expired and will prompt you for a new password. If you often submit batch jobs to the academic MVS system from another system and do not regularly logon to TSO, you should be aware that batch jobs will fail if the password is expired at the time the batch jobs are submitted. (There is a way to change the logon password from a batch job even if the current logon password has expired. Refer to the RACF BATCH topic in this help facility for information on changing your password with a batch job.)
