ACD 517–03: English Language Competence and Employment of Teaching Assistants

Purpose

To implement the Board of Regents’ policy to assure the English language competence of international teaching or graduate assistants

Sources

Arizona Board of Regents Policy Manual - 2–407
Office of the Executive Vice President and Provost of the University

Applicability

International teaching or graduate assistants whose primary language is not English

Policy

Certification

All international teaching or graduate assistants whose primary language is not English are required to be certified in their English language abilities. Those individuals whose mother tongue is not English will be tested with the Test of Spoken English (TSE). For this group, no Personnel Action form (PAF) will be signed until this examination has been passed.

The American English and Culture Program (AECP) will certify all teaching assistants (TAs) who take the TSE test. International students whose primary language is other than English will not be permitted to serve as TAs unless they have AECP certification.

Classroom Observation

The performance of all international TAs whose primary language is not English will be assessed at least once a term (by midterm) in the classroom by a tenured faculty member. Each semester or summer session, a report on the class observation and an overall evaluation of the student’s teaching performance and English language ability will be filed in the college.

Procedures

Testing of International Teaching Assistants

  1. All prospective international teaching or graduate assistants whose primary language is not English will be required to take the TSE regardless of the type of instructional responsibility they will be assigned.

  2. International graduate students whose primary language is other than English and who apply for a teaching assistantship at ASU will be encouraged to take the TSE before arriving at ASU.

  3. ASU’s AECP will provide appropriately scheduled opportunities to take the TSE on campus.

  4. Test scores will be provided to deans, chairs, and the Division of Graduate Studies.

  5. Students scoring 230 or above on the TSE will be permitted to assume primary teaching responsibilities, e.g., serving as the instructor of a class or leading discussion groups. Students scoring 210 or above will be permitted to assume secondary teaching responsibilities, e.g., as a laboratory teaching assistant, reader of tests, etc. Students scoring below 210 will not be given any teaching responsibilities, until the student is certified by the AECP.

  6. Students scoring below 190 on the TSE will be encouraged to enroll in intensive English classes to prepare to retake the examination.

Seminar Implementation

  1. Students scoring 190 or above, but below 230, on the TSE will be required to take a noncredit seminar offered by the AECP. The seminar will address three issues: pronunciation, cultural differences in classroom behavior and educational expectations, and instructional methodology.

  2. To pass the seminar and be eligible for an assistantship with primary teaching responsibility, the student will be asked to make at least a 15-minute teaching presentation before a panel of language and/or instructional experts who will judge the student’s ability on the basis of the student’s overall ability to communicate. Departmental representatives will be invited to observe, but will not participate in the actual assessment.
