Mesa artist casts bells to benefit ASU Carillon

Metalsmith Barbara Atkinson, an Arizona State University student and business owner, has cast a series of six sterling silver miniature bells to benefit the ASU Carillon restoration fund.

The bells range in size from 1-1/8 inch to 1/2-inch, and most come with a jump ring so they can be worn as necklaces or on a charm bracelet. One is a pin, suitable for either men or women. The bells are priced from $15 to $25, plus an additional $2 for shipping.

Each tiny bell has a clapper, hand-soldered by Atkinson. The bells are exclusive to the ASU Carillon Society.

Net proceeds will be deposited with the ASU Foundation, a separate nonprofit organization that exists for the benefit of ASU.

Atkinson, a Mesa resident, began working with metals when she was in high school. She has taken classes at Mesa Art Center for 15 years, and for the past two years studied metals at ASU with David Pimentel, until his death Oct. 1.

This summer, Atkinson studied with master wax carver Kate Wolfe in Portland, Maine, where she learned new techniques for raised surfaces and intricate designs.

Each bell begins with a solid wax tube, which is carved and hollowed out on a lathe. This process takes about two hours. Then, the wax is polished, the model placed in a plaster-like material called an “investment,” then the wax model is burnt out, leaving a hollow cavity.

The cavity is filled with molten metal, which then becomes the cast. The metal cast model is then cleaned up, and a rubber mold is made. Once the rubber mold is made, multiples can be created.

Atkinson owns her own residential property appraisal business, and she credits her husband, Dick, for helping her with her business and giving her time to attend ASU.

The bells may be purchased through the Carillon Society web site, www.asu.edu/carillon, or by calling ASU Public Relations, (480) 965-0456, or by e-mailing Judith Smith at jps@asu.edu.

The ASU Carillon Society is raising funds to refurbish ASU’s 148-bell Symphonic Carillon, which was purchased by Associated Students of ASU in 1966. After the carillon was played a few years, it was put in storage, where it remained, forgotten, for nearly 35 years. The initial fund goal is $50,000.
