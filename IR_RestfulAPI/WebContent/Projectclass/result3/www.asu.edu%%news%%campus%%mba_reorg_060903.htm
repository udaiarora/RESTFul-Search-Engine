W. P. Carey MBA at Arizona State University Reorganizes Leadership: Dr. Carl H. Harris Adds Executive Director of Recruiting and Admissions Duties

Dr. Carl H. Harris of the W. P. Carey MBA at Arizona State University is heading up a new initiative designed to refocus staff and streamline services to more effectively compete for the best graduate business students regionally and nationally.

The new position - executive director of recruiting and admissions - is an extension of his current responsibilities. For the past three years he has been serving as assistant dean for graduate programs at the W. P. Carey School, with responsibility for the W. P. Carey MBA online application system, alumni services and other support functions.

Dr. Harris came to the W. P. Carey School from the nationally ranked University of Texas at Austin McCombs School of Business, where he was director of admissions.

"With Carl Harris in this new leadership position, the W. P. Carey MBA is ready to implement strategies that come directly from the naming, thereby moving us toward being a top 25 school" said Dean Larry Edward Penley. "This reorganization -with Carl's appointment -will allow us to enhance the make-up and prestige of our cornerstone W. P. Carey MBA - Full-time Program and it will aid us in providing the finest programs for working professional programs in the Greater Phoenix and Arizona business community."

Dr. Harris is known nationally in his field as an effective manager with outstanding strategic perspectives, and for pioneering the utilization of emerging technologies in the areas of applicant recruitment and on-line application processing, alumni services, and other electronic applications. Under his leadership, the W. P. Carey School Graduate Alumni Community was launched. This web-based tool allows alumni of the W. P. Carey MBA, masters and doctoral programs to stay abreast of news and network.

Dr. Harris is also an elected member of the Board of Directors of the Graduate Management Admissions Council (GMAC). This Board is charged with developing the GMAC global strategic vision, new initiatives, and delivery of services to over 1,500 graduate management programs from 170 countries.

Before coming to ASU, Dr. Harris served for 10 years in various roles at the University of Texas McCombs School of Business. Immediately before that, he was assistant to the vice president for student affairs at Arizona State University from 1984 to 1989. During that time he was a Regents' Executive-in-Residence at the Arizona Board of Regents. His background also includes two years as associate dean for student development at the University of South Carolina and 11 years in various administrative posts at Southern Illinois University at Carbondale.

Dr. Nancy Stephens, who had served as interim executive director this spring, will continue as director of the W. P. Carey MBA - Evening Program.

W. P. Carey School of Business
The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 25th in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
