October 24, 2005

Art Walk spotlights alumni artists

Wearable art, hanging art and table art are all part of the third annual Herberger Art Walk as part of the ASU Homecoming Block Party Oct. 29.

The artwork of more than 25 alumni artists from the Herberger College of Fine Arts will be up for sale from 10 a.m. – 4 p.m. The result is a diverse array of top-quality art: ceramics, fiber, jewelry, mixed media, painting, photography and more, created by veterans as well as emerging artists.

For buyers or browsers, Art Walk offers a fun, festive atmosphere. One of this year’s artists, Daniell LaMaide, will sell her hand-painted scarves that are worn by rock star Lenny Kravtiz. And ceramicist Sandra Luehrsen, featured on Home and Garden Television’s “Crafters Coast to Coast,” will display her ceramic gems. There also will be live vignettes from the Herberger College Mainstage Theater production of the whimsical 18th century fable, “The King Stag.”

The Art Walk is in the white tented booths along University and Normal streets, on the left side of the University Club. The public is invited to shop before or after the homecoming parade, which starts as 11:30 a.m., or before the football game, which has a 3:30 p.m. kickoff. Buyers going to the game can arrange with the artists to pick up their purchases.

“The Art Walk lets us celebrate with the Valley community the talent and legacy of the Herberger College of Fine Arts,” says Deanna DePass Stulgaitis, Herberger College associate director of development and alumni relations. “Now in its third year, we hope the Art Walk has become an annual tradition – a ‘can’t miss’ event.”

For more information or the full roster of Art Walk artists, go to the Web site (http://herbergercollege.asu.edu/alumni/index.html), or contact Stulgaitis at (480) 965-8985 or (deanna.stulgaitis@asu.edu).
