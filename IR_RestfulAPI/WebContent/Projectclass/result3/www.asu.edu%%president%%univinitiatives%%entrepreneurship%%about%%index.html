How will we know when we are an entrepreneurial university?

  • ASU has developed an innovative entrepreneurship model for the Phoenix metropolitan area.
  • Faculty are empowered to be more entrepreneurial through increased flexibility and knowledge.
  • ASU and community college students, as well as the external community, have increased access to and visibility for entrepreneurial work.
  • ASU units have generated unique and interdisciplinary entrepreneurship.
  • ASU is a leader in developing models for taking ideas to the marketplace and to society.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


Becoming an Entrepreneurial University

 

At ASU, we are committed to embedding entrepreneurship as a way of thinking into the culture of our institution and in our partnerships with our community. Through enterprising leadership and resources, we seek to inspire our students and faculty by equipping them with the skills to turn their innovative ideas into reality.


To bring about this vision, the university requires an environment that is responsive to quick-paced risk-taking and encourages sustainable and collaborative innovations that span across colleges, academic disciplines, and campuses. To embed entrepreneurship within the fiber of our institution, social networks, processes, and even the physical locations of entrepreneurial activity must be creatively approached in order to have the strongest impact in sparking innovations in all arenas.


We are defining entrepreneurship as the spirit and process of creative risk-taking and innovation that leverages university knowledge to spur social development and economic competitiveness. Rather than developing a collection of programs that address entrepreneurship on a smaller scale, our vision is to establish Arizona State University as an entrepreneurial university that uses its knowledge and resources in their entirety to positively impact society.
