ASU offers summer fun, enrichment for children

Summer is fast approaching, and parents soon will be scrambling to find activities for their children that keep them active and engaged. ASU offers more than 50 creative programs for children ages 5 through the teen years.

From archaeology to computers, from guitar and piano playing to basketball and swimming, ASU departments and schools are reaching into the community to give children a chance to learn and have fun at the same time. For some it will be their first visit to a university campus, sparking their interest in a college-educated future.

A description of ASU summer programs for kids and teens is online at (www.asu.edu/youth). Included are dates, costs and program goals.

Programs for Talented Youth offers talented third- through seventh-grade students the chance to experience challenging academic courses, for instance, and the Johns Hopkins University Center for Talented Youth provides enrichment courses for gifted seventh- through 10th-graders.

Intercollegiate Athletics offers camps in wrestling, golf, basketball, baseball and swimming, and the Herberger College of Fine Arts is launching its first full summer schedule of music, theater, dance and art classes for children, teens and adults.

The ASU Student Recreation Complex once again will play host to the Sun Devil Kids’ Camp from May 31 through Aug. 5, an all-day program of sports and arts and crafts for children between the ages of 5 and 11. Older children up to age 15 can enroll in Kids’ Quest, which includes bowling, billiards, horseback riding, rock climbing and other activities. Others may become junior counselors.

Various summer bridge programs ease the transition from high school to college for older youth, often in specific academic fields such as engineering.
