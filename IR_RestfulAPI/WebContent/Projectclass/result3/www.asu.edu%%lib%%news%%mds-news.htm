Multi-Database Search

The ASU Libraries have partnered with the Association of Research Libraries and six major university libraries (including the University of Southern California, University of California - San Diego, Dartmouth College, University of Arizona, Iowa State University, and the University of Utah) to implement a Scholar’s Portal using software developed by Fretwell Downing, Inc. ASU calls its portal "Multi-Database Search."


ASU’s Multi-Database Search will eventually become the centerpiece of the ASU Libraries’ web site, providing a single point of access on the Web to find high-quality information resources and, to the greatest extent possible, to deliver the information and related services directly to the user’s desktop. It will deliver cross-domain searching of licensed and openly available content in a range of subject fields and from multiple institutions. The portal will aggregate and integrate the results of the search, and support delivery of the content to the user's desktop.


You will need to click on "Sign In" in the left-hand menu bar to get started using MDS. ASURITE IDs are used to authenticate users into the portal, using the “standard” ASURITE authentication web interface.




Once you have successfully signed in, choose a "Profile" to search.




The initial set of databases searchable through this interface is limited, and users are encouraged to provide feedback evaluating this interface. As more and more electronic resources become MDS-accessible, additional collections will be created and added to the interface. You can send your comments or suggestions regarding the new Multi-Database Search tool to ScholarsPortal@asu.edu.
