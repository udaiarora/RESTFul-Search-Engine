October 1, 2004

Freshmen class includes record number of scholars

ASU’s largest freshman class in history – 7,719 first-time freshmen – also includes a record number of scholars.

The class has 162 freshman National Merit Scholars, and a record 482 National Merit Scholars are enrolled at the university overall. ASU was third in the nation last year among public schools in enrolling these top students.

There are 69 National Hispanic Scholars in this year’s class, an impressive 72 percent increase over last fall. The National Merit and National Hispanic scholarship winners represent approximately the top one-half of 1 percent of this year’s graduating high school seniors.

More freshmen were in the top 10 percent of their high school class, with higher test scores, than ever before.

Two-thirds of the 2004 freshman class are from Arizona , a 6 percent growth from last year, with high levels of academic achievement. They include nine Flinn scholars, the most ever for ASU. This is the most competitive award for Arizona students, providing full funding from the Flinn Foundation.

Arizona freshmen also include 1,367 President’s and Provost’s Scholars, a 25 percent higher number than last year. These awards for top Arizona students are based on an index of GPA, class rank and test scores and provide a range of scholarships.

The Barrett Honors College has a freshman class of 759, with a record average SAT score of 1,310 and high school GPA of 3.8. Test scores were up for all ASU freshmen, reaching an average 1,102 SAT and 23.3 ACT.

“The rising quality of our freshman class is testimony to the caliber of our academic programs and the growing reputation of ASU,” says James Rund, vice president for university undergraduate initiatives. “We are proud of the level of accomplishment these students bring to the university.”
