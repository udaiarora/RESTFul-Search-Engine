March 31, 2003

Kingston reflects on years as faculty representative

ASU professor Jerry Kingston has announced he will leave his position as faculty athletics representative at the close of the 2003 - 04 fiscal year. Kingston will return to teaching full time in the economics department within the W.P. Carey School of Business at ASU.

A replacement will be chosen within the next few months. Kingston says this timeline will provide ample time for him to train the new representative ensuring a smooth transition.

ASU President Michael Crow says Kingston has been a true asset to the university, and has made an impressive impact on the Pac-10 conference and the NCAA.

"Jerry Kingston has provided steadfast leadership in representing ASU's intercollegiate athletic interests not only on campus, but also in the Pac-10 and through the NCAA governance structure," Crow says. "We appreciate his long tenure in serving in this role and know that he will provide strong guidance and support to his successor."
Kingston has held a variety of positions within the NCAA and the Pac-10 conference. He has served on each of the three major governance units of the NCAA: the Academic Testing and Requirements Committee, forerunner of the current Academic Cabinet (1991 - 95); the NCAA Council (1993 - 95) and the Championships and Competition Cabinet (1999 - 2003). Kingston is also completing a four-year term of service on the NCAA Certification Committee, the group that evaluates and extends or withholds accreditation to the athletics programs of NCAA Division I member institutions.

"I have observed first-hand, the many positive benefits that intercollegiate athletics provides for student-athletes," says Kingston, who has held the position since 1987. "I am pleased to have been a part of ensuring that such benefits are provided within both the spirit and letter of all NCAA and Pac-10 Conference regulations."

Kingston was also a founding member of the Faculty Athletics Representatives Association, serving as president in 1995 and 1996. He first served as president of the Pac-10 Conference Council during the 1993 - 94 academic year. This coming year he will take on the position once more.

Kingston was involved in the formation of NCAA Proposition 16, implemented in 1995, which raised initial academic eligibility standards for student-athletes and has ultimately led to increased student-athlete graduation rates. He has also received distinguished service awards from the NCAA Council and the Faculty Athletics Representatives Association.

Kingston says he will miss interacting with the many friends he has made in the intercollegiate athletics community.

"The individuals who work in intercollegiate athletics are among the most dedicated, hardworking, optimistic people with whom I have ever had the pleasure of working," Kingston says.

ASU's Executive Director of Athletics Gene Smith says he appreciates the important strides Kingston made during his service as representative.

"Jerry has served our university and all of intercollegiate athletics at the highest level," Smith says. "We are indebted to him and will be forever grateful."
