Find Information: Online Resources (Video DEMO)

Compendex (1970+) Links to some full text 
Engineering Index. Articles, conference proceedings, and technical reports in all areas of engineering. 

 

Inspec (1969+) Links to some full text 
Produced by the Institute of Electrical Engineers (IEE). Over 5 million citations and abstracts to journal articles and conference papers in physics, electrical engineering, electronics, telecommunications, computers, control technology, and information technology.



IEEE Xplore (1988+) Full Text
Contains full-text of journal articles, conference papers, and technical standards from the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) and Institution of Electrical Engineers (IEE).

 

ACM Digital Library (1985+) Full Text
Citations and full-text of articles from Association for Computing Machinery journals, magazines, and conference proceedings.

 

ACM Guide to Computing Literature  Links to some full text
The Guide is a collection of bibliographic citations and abstracts of works published by ACM and other publishers. Citations for all ACM published works are included in the Guide as a small subset of the total bibliographic space. More than 750,000 citations from 3,000+ publishers, including ACM, covering: books, journal articles, conference proceedings, doctoral dissertations, master's theses, and technical reports.

 

Knovel Full Text
Collection of online full text handbooks, databases. Search the full text of individual books or all books simultaneously.  Special features include and advanced search which searches interactive tables for ranges of numeric data. Some interactive tables have one or more of the following "Productivity Tools:"  Equation Plotter, Graph Plotter, or a Graph Digitizer. 

 

Safari Tech Books Online Full Text
Full text books on computer programming and information technology. Includes a large number of the books published by O"Reilly and Associates. In addition to the usual search capabilities, one can search by code fragments.

 

Lecture Notes in Computer Science   Full Text
Conference Proceedings in Computer Science and Information Technology



ABI/Inform Global Edition Some full text
Covers business and topics related to management and production.



Web of Science Links to some full text
 In addition searching by topic, you can also go forward in time to find out who has cited a particular work. To use this feature, use the "Cited Reference Search."

Get Library Materials

Books

  1. WorldCat  (Video DEMO) is a combined catalog from many libraries and contains citations to a large number of books and other items.
  2. Check "knovel" and "Safari" to see if the book that you want available in one of these collections.
  3. If the book you want is not online through ASU, contact the Interlibrary Loan department of your local Public Library to obtain books from the ASU libraries, or other libraries.

 

Articles  from journals and conference proceedings(Video DEMO)

 

When you have the citation, follow these steps to obtain the article.

  1. Electronic full text online:
    Journals and conference proceedings and standards published by IEEE are in IEEE Xplore  
    Journals and conference proceedings published by ACM are in the  ACM Digital Library  
    For other journals, search by Journal Title in the EJournals list. (Video DEMO)

  2. Search the Library Catalog to see if ASU owns the journal or conference proceedings in paper. 


    If ASU owns a paper copy of the article:

    a. You may order a photocopy through Document Delivery/Library Express (Video DEMO), a fee based library service. You must set up a deposit account in order to have the item photocopied and mailed to you.
    Contact the Library Express office to set up this account at 480/965-2466 or e-mail libraryexpress@mainex1.asu.edu



    OR


    b. You may contact your local public library to see if you can obtain the article from the journal or conference proceeding through their Interlibrary Loan service. Public libraries frequently offer this service for free or for a very minimal charge per item.

  3. Interlibrary Loan (Video DEMO)
    If Arizona State University does not own the article, either electronically or in paper, you may order it free of charge through Interlibrary Loan. Most of these will be sent to you electronically as a link in your email.
