Morphological Changes in the Verde River

ABSTRACT

The purpose of this project is to use GIS to detect morphological changes, at three different locations within the Mazatzal Wilderness Area of the Verde River. The morphology of a river is important to understand in order to determine potential habitat for vegetation and wildlife. Detecting ways in which the morphology of the river is changing is important to determine if there is a net loss or gain in vital habitat. The Verde River is one of the few perennial flowing rivers in central Arizona. Eleven of the original sixteen native fishes are still found in the Verde River watershed. Several plants and animals that depend on the Verde River and its tributaries are listed as endangered or threatened.
