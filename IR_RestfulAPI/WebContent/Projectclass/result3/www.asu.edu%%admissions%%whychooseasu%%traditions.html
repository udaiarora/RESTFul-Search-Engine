ASU Traditions - Make it your Tradition

Arizona State University is steeped in traditions dating back more than a century, traditions that have evolved with our changing institution and that will continue to develop as we expand across our multiple campuses.

Every Arizona State University tradition is open to all students regardless of campus affiliation or year. Here are some of our longest standing traditions ...

Sun Devil 101
Whitewash the "A" is the annual tradition when the freshman class hikes "A" Mountain and whitewashes the coveted gold "A" during Welcome Week, indicating the commencement of another year at Arizona State University.

Homecoming Festivities

The Homecoming Parade is a popular, time-honored event that draws large crowds to downtown Tempe where hundreds of campus clubs, organizations, and local businesses display their floats to members of the community and student body, as well as alumni.

The Lantern Walk is considered ASU's most prominent tradition, dating back to 1917, the Lantern Walk unites all students and alumni when they gather at the base of "A" Mountain for a night of Sun Devil pride culminating in a fireworks display at the top of the mountain.

Gold RUSH
The Gold RUSH can be found at all ASU athletic events when the student section becomes a sea of solid gold due to the long-established tradition that all students wear gold to show their school spirit and support.
