October 10, 2004

ASU and the Council on Foreign Relations Present Symposium on Foreign Policy Issues That Face Next President

Arizona State University and the Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) will jointly sponsor a symposium on October 13th, the day of the third presidential debate in Tempe at Arizona State University's campus. The conference will take place at the Tempe Mission Palms hotel in Tempe, Arizona from 7:45 a.m. until 3:30 p.m.

In an age of globalization, there is increasingly a connection between foreign and domestic policy. The symposium serves as a bridge between the Miami presidential debate on foreign policy on September 30 and the Tempe presidential debate on domestic and economic policy on October 13. It is designed to bring together community leaders, ASU faculty and student leaders, and journalists to reflect upon the pressing foreign policy issues that will face the next administration.

Senator Jon Kyl (R-AZ) will give the breakfast address and Arizona Governor Janet Napolitano has been invited to deliver the luncheon address.  The first panel will explore “International Challenges Facing the Next Administration”; the second panel will focus on “The United States and the Global Economy”; and the third panel will address “The Americas and the United States.”  Panelists are leading experts in their respective fields, and members of the national press will serve as moderators. 

Confirmed participants in the first panel include: former Middle East Peace Negotiator Dennis Ross, CFR Vice President and Director of Studies James Lindsay, CFR Senior Fellow Walter Mead and ASU Professor of Law Orde F. Kittrie.

Confirmed participants in the second panel include: CFR Fellow and Columbia University Professor Jagdish Bhagwati, Brookings Senior Fellow and former Deputy National Economic Adviser Lael Brainard, and ASU Professor Carolyn Warner.

Confirmed participants in the third panel include: Pew Hispanic Research Center Director Roberto Suro, former Arizona-Mexico Commission President Jose Cardenas,  and American University V.P. for International Affairs Robert Pastor.

The President of Arizona State University, Michael Crow, and Council on Foreign Relations Executive Vice President Michael P. Peters will make introductory remarks.

Attendance at this conference is by invitation only. 

More information and panelist biographies are available at <http://www.asu.edu/clas/globalstudies/events/index.html>.

The conference’s ASU sponsorship comes from the School of Global Studies, a strategic initiative of ASU’s College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, currently pending approval from the Arizona Board of Regents.

The Council on Foreign Relations is an independent, national membership organization and a nonpartisan center for scholars dedicated to producing and disseminating ideas so that individual and corporate members, as well as policymakers, journalists, students, and interested citizens in the United States and other countries, can better understand the world and the foreign policy choices facing the United States and other governments. The Council is headquartered in New York with an office in Washington, DC.

Contacts:  Erin Mote, External and Strategic Relations, School of Global Studies (480) 965-8682; Irina Faskianos, Vice President, National Program and Academic Outreach, Council on Foreign Relations (212) 434-9465.
