May 9, 2003

Almost 5,000 will graduate from ASU Main Campus on May 15

Nearly 5,000 students will receive their sheepskins from ASU President Michael Crow on May 15, in the first spring commencement for the energetic president who is cutting a wide swath with his vision for the future of the university. The Main Campus ceremony will be at 4 p.m. in Wells Fargo Arena.

Commencement for ASU West will be at 6:45 p.m., May 16, in the Sundome Center for the Performing Arts in Sun City West. ASU East's 365 graduates will participate in the Main Campus ceremony.

First in the minds of many graduates is the job search, as the sluggish economy begins to warm up. Many companies, particularly accounting and computer software development firms, are hiring, keeping the talent pipeline open to meet the need for well-educated workers in the future.

A record 325 ASU students are receiving bachelor's degrees in interdisciplinary studies (BIS), a relatively new degree program that allows students to combine two subject areas, preparing them for careers in a world that increasingly bridges academic disciplines - such as philosophy and religious studies or English literature and urban planning.

The next most popular degree program is elementary education, with psychology and communication coming in next. Marketing, management and finance all rank high, with justice studies rounding out majors with the largest number of graduates.
Ten students have been named the outstanding graduates from their colleges, including Katie Peterson, outstanding graduate of the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, who has a 4.0 grade-point average and a double major, English and biology. She is the only undergraduate ever to hold the position of associate editor of the literary magazine "Hayden's Ferry Review," and is an opinion columnist and reporter for the State Press.

Lisa Prout, the outstanding graduate from the College of Engineering & Applied Sciences, makes presentations in high school science classes and was a presenter at the recent Sally Ride science festival. Both Lisa and her brother Brian are graduating with honors. Brian is one year older and is getting a degree in architecture.

Jonas Zygas, named outstanding graduate from the W. P. Carey School of Business, has already been hired by Citigroup, where he interned, as an analyst in the investment banking division. While studying in Lithuania last year, he participated in a debate with the country's former president about public and private education.

Other outstanding graduates include Joseph E.V. Benesh, Katherine K. Herberger College of Fine Arts; Wendy Coursen, College of Public Programs; Andrew Jacob, College of Law; Meredith Banasiak, College of Architecture & Environmental Design; Collin Raymond, Barrett Honors College; Luis Cortez, College of Nursing; and Marina Gair, College of Education.

Sixteen students will receive the Moeur Award, ASU's oldest continuing honor presented by the Alumni Association, for having 4.0 GPAs and all their coursework completed at ASU. They are Brooke Bagwell, Sandra Bensley, Marie Blanchard, Rebekah Bratrud, Mhairi Brown, Kate Buenau, Kortney Gray, Kurt Hinterbichler, Lauren Kientz, Jonathan Land, Melissa Ludens, Sara McKenzie, Joanna Pearson, Donya Pettit, Sulema Sutton and Jack Zeal.

The commencement will be Webcast live on the Internet at (http://is.asu.edu/live).
Parking is free in all lots across campus on that day. The closest parking for guests is in lot 59 and parking structure 5.
