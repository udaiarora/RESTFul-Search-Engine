February 25, 2005

Bioethicists to Tackle “Genetic Modification”

As part of the Western Regional Bioethics Conference being hosted by Arizona State University, the Arizona Consortium for Medicine, Society and Values is co-sponsoring a public discussion on the controversial topic of "Genetic Modification," featuring a panel of prominent bioethicists and scientists.

The panel discussion is scheduled for Friday, February 25 at 7:00 P.M. in the Great Hall of the College of Law, Armstrong Hall. The event is free and open to the public.

The panel will include Adrienne Asch, Professor in Biology, Ethics, and the Politics of Human Reproduction at Wellesley College;  Mark S. Frankel, Director of the Scientific Freedom, Responsibility and Law Program, American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS); Sarah Iden, Genetic Counselor at the University of Arizona; and Lee Silver, Professor of Molecular Biology and Public Affairs at Princeton University. The panel will be moderated by David Young, Vice President and Dean of ASU’s College of Liberal Arts and Sciences.

For more information on the Western Regional Bioethics Conference, go to <http://www.asu.edu/news/research/bioethics_conference_020805.htm> .                

The Arizona Consortium for Medicine, Society and Values is a partnership between Arizona State University (the Center for Biology and Society and the Barrett Honors College), the Flinn Foundation, Mayo Clinic Scottsdale, Translational Genomics Research Center (TGen) and the University of Arizona Health Sciences/Phoenix Campus. The Consortium plans to offer three to four events a year across the Valley, including large public lectures, panel discussions, policy roundtables, conferences, symposia and workshops.

The Consortium has further events planned for 2005, including the visit of National Book Award winner, physician-writer Sherwin Nuland, who will be in residence in March in conjunction with the Barrett Honors College’s Staging of Illness course. Nuland will give a talk entitled "The Artist Examines the Doctor: Five Hundred Years of Observation" on Wednesday, March 9 at 6:00 p.m. at the Mayo Clinic’s Ashton B. Taylor Auditorium.
