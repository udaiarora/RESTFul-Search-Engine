September 24, 2004

Sponsors make debate a reality

Generous community support through in-kind and cash donations to the ASU Foundation is making it possible for the university to play host to the upcoming presidential debate, which is scheduled to be televised live from ASU’s Gammage Auditorium at 6 p.m., Oct. 13.

The university sought underwriting from local communities, businesses and individuals to cover the costs of conducting the event, estimated at about $2.5 million.

As the only public university playing host to a 2004 presidential debate, ASU has relied on private support to fund debate preparation and implementation. Sponsor donations will cover costs associated with the event, including construction and infrastructure upgrades.

“It is a privilege for ASU to host the final 2004 presidential debate, and with that privilege comes great responsibility,” says ASU President Michael M. Crow. “The community has stepped forward in support of our endeavor to make this an historic event for ASU, metropolitan Phoenix and all of Arizona. We owe a great debt of gratitude to our sponsors, whose generosity and commitment are making this event a reality.”

Debate planning at ASU has been under way for some time and is right on course. Most of the seating area at Gammage Auditorium is being temporarily converted to camera platforms that will accommodate pool feed cameras.

These cameras will provide live television coverage of the presidential debate to a worldwide audience.

Outside the auditorium, facilities are being built to accommodate hundreds of journalists, providing phone lines, Internet access, power and other services needed to broadcast and report on the debate.
