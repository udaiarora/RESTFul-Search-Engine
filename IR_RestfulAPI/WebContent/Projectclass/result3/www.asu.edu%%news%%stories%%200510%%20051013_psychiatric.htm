October 13, 2005

Study: State's psychiatric physician work force lags

A study just completed by ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business looks at the psychiatric physician-to-population ratio in Arizona . The state ranks below the national average of 16.5 psychiatrists to every 100,000 people.

The study – conducted by research professor Mary E. Rimsza and economics professor William G. Johnson, of the Center for Health Information and Policy at the Seidman Research Institute – found that the psychiatric physician-to-population in Arizona lags behind the national average.

Only one Arizona county, Pima, has a ratio that is higher than the national average.

According to the study, there were 691 psychiatrists in active practice in Arizona in 2004, comprising 5.7 percent of the state’s physician work force, at a physician-to-population ratio of 12.14 psychiatrists to 100,000 residents.

The highest percentage of psychiatrists practice in Pima County, with 22.12 for every 100,000 residents. About 60 percent of the psychiatrists practice in Maricopa County , but four counties – Graham, Greenlee, La Paz and Santa Cruz – have no practicing psychiatrists.

The subspecialty of child psychiatry has grown nationally, exceeding the growth in general psychiatry from 1990 – 2000.

In 2004, there were 134 child psychiatrists in active practice in Arizona. About 58 percent practiced in Maricopa County and 28 percent in Pima County. Five counties are without a practicing child psychiatrist: Gila, Graham, Greenlee, La Paz and Santa Cruz.

Psychiatry is the fourth largest medical specialty in the United States.

There are large disparities in the distribution of psychiatrists throughout the country, with the highest ratio of 31.1 in Massachusetts and the lowest at 4.6 in Idaho.
