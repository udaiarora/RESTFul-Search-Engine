Vice President of Student Affairs Gonzalez to leave ASU

Arizona State University Vice President of Student Affairs Juan Gonzalez is leaving the university to become vice president of student affairs for the University of Texas at Austin.

Gonzalez joined ASU in 2003 and has served the university during one of the most dynamic periods in its history. Gonzalez’ focus during his tenure has been on campus life, the quality and operation of student residence facilities, and student health and well being. He came to ASU from Georgetown University, where he also served as vice president for student affairs.

“My service at ASU has been the high point of my career so far,” said Gonzalez. “The energy, pace and goals that President Michael Crow has set for the university have given me the opportunity to grow, both personally and professionally. I’m excited about my new position at UTA, but I am also honored to have been part of ASU and to have served under someone with the vision that Michael Crow has. It was wonderful to work with ASU’s dedicated, hard-working student affairs staff and to advocate for student needs.”

One of the highlights of Gonzalez’ work at ASU was the definition and groundbreaking for the McAllister Academic Village, an integrated living and learning community that is the first to be built on the Tempe campus since 2001. McAllister Academic Village will open for operation in fall 2006 and will provide classrooms, dining and retail facilities within minutes of student rooms.

“Juan has been an instrumental member of the team as we moved the university forward over the last two years,” said Crow. “I wish him well in his new position in Texas and thank him for his service to our students and their parents.”
