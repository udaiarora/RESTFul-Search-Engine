Herberger College now enrolling adults, youths for fall community arts classes

If you’ve always wanted to learn how to play the piano, perfect your dance moves or create a computer animation just for fun, the professionals at ASU can help.

The Herberger College of Fine Arts, one of the top fine arts schools in the nation, is enrolling for its first fall semester of community arts classes in dance, music, theater and visual art for adults, teens and younger children. Most classes in the Herberger College at Large (for adults and older teens) and Herberger College for Kids (for toddlers to teens) start after Labor Day, with sessions available through December.

Herberger College for Kids classes for budding artists aged 6 weeks old to 14 years old are held after school and on Saturdays. The roster includes 25 class types, from classics like Ballet, Kindermusik, Music Theory and Hip-Hop to truly unique offerings only available at ASU – Javanese Gamelan, Southeast Asian Dance, Children’s Art Workshop and Imagination Corp., as well as private music instruction in piano, wind and stringed instruments by ASU music majors and professors.

By popular demand, the Herberger College at Large has increased the number of classes it offers for adults and teens aged 15 and up, from six last summer to 25 this fall. Most of the classes are interactive and no experience is necessary. They include 3-D Imaging, Latin Salsa, Broadway Dance, Life Drawing, Latin Jazz Workshop, Music Composition, PhotoShop and more. Native American music and public art appreciation classes offer cultural enrichment through field trips and discussion.

Adults and teens also can schedule private music instruction in piano, wind and stringed instruments.

The Herberger College launched its two community schools in June 2005 and taught 84 classes to 700 children, teens and adults – offering excellent teacher-to-student ratios for targeted, personalized instruction. Surveys of parents whose children attended the Herberger College for Kids revealed that the classes helped improve their children’s’ creativity, enthusiasm and desire to continue arts activities.

“Only the Herberger College of Fine Arts can offer the Valley a community arts education program developed by nationally ranked academic programs in art, dance, music and theater,” says director Cathy Fletcher. “Our teachers are either ASU faculty or experienced arts education majors who apply the most current teaching methods to their classes. The depth of our classes and university location gives the atmosphere of college prep for kids.”

Fletcher, who moved to the Valley this year to start up the Herberger College community schools, is considered a visionary in the field of community school development and will present the topic of pedagogy (teacher education) at this year’s National Guild of Community Schools of the Arts conference. The Herberger College model has become a national example of a successful community arts school, prompting the guild to hold its conference in the Valley next year.

ASU has designated the Herberger College for Kids and Herberger College at Large exemplary university initiatives and featured the programs in its recently released New American University report.

For registration information and applications, go to (http://herbergercollegeforkids.asu.edu) and (http://herbergercollegeatlarge.asu.edu) or call (480) 727-0700. Registration will be accepted until each session is full. All classes except Kindermusik are held on ASU’s Tempe campus. A limited number of financial aid awards are available for the fall semester. ASU employees, full-time students and their children receive a 10 percent discount on all Herberger College for Kids and Herberger College at Large classes, except Private Music Instruction, String Project or Piano Prep/Conservatory programs.

Tanguay, with the Herberger College of Fine Arts, can be reached at (480) 965-7144 or (denise.tanguay@asu.edu).
