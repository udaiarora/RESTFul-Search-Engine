Librarians' Council

 

B: Bylaws of Librarians' Council

Effective 12/20/1989 .. Revised 5/16/2001

NAME

The name of this organization is: Librarians' Council of the Arizona State University Libraries. It is hereinafter referred to as the Council.


PURPOSES

The purposes of the Council are to:

  1. Address itself to the professional interests and concerns of its members and be a collective voice for them on issues related to the conditions under which they practice their professions;

  2. Be the collegial body through which its members participate as professionals in the governance of the University Libraries;

  3. Provide for the participation of its members in the governance of the University by the election of Academic Senators and representatives of the Libraries to elected university committees, and in other appropriate ways; and

  4. Provide for peer participation in personnel decisions related to its members, including appointment, retention, continuing status, promotion, salary adjustments, and so forth.


MEMBERSHIP

The membership of the council consists of all persons holding continuing, probationary, or year-to-year appointments to academic professional positions (including part-time positions) in the Arizona State University Libraries. Persons holding year-to-year appointments with a visiting designation to such positions are affiliate members with all rights of membership except to vote and to hold office. Membership in the Council is neither restricted nor privileged in any way by any concurrent appointment to an administrative or faculty position.


YEAR

The Council will operate on the basis of a year beginning July first and ending June thirtieth.


OFFICES

  1. The Chair.

    1. On July first of each year the Chair-Elect succeeds to the Chair.

    2. The duties of the Chair are to:

      1. Preside over all meetings of the Council;

      2. Exercise general supervision over the business of the Council;

      3. Communicate for the Council with the Administration of the Libraries, and with other organizations as required; and

      4. Fulfill such other responsibilities as are given to this office elsewhere in these Bylaws or by action of the Council.


    3. If the Chair becomes vacant for any reason, the Chair-Elect immediately succeeds to it for the balance of the year as well as for the following year.

    4. The Chair will appoint a member Parliamentarian to advise in the planning and conduct of meetings. The appointed individual will be called the Kenneth B. Knepp Parliamentarian.

    5. The Chair will appoint a Librarians' Council Page Master.


  2. The Chair-Elect.

    1. A Chair-Elect is elected annually from the membership to serve in that office for one year.

    2. The duties of the Chair-Elect are to:

      1. Assume the duties of the Chair in the absence of that officer;

      2. Succeed to the office of Chair for the following year;

      3. Maintain the Librarians' Handbook by the distribution of additional Council documents, revisions to existing documents, and current personnel policies and procedures for professionals in the Libraries; and,

      4. Fulfill such other responsibilities as are given to the office elsewhere in these Bylaws or by action of the Council.


    3. If the office of Chair-Elect becomes vacant because the incumbent succeeds to the Chair, or for any other reason, an election will be held to choose a new Chair-Elect to serve that office until taking the Chair when the term of its incumbent ends.


  3. The Secretary.

  1. A secretary is elected annually from the membership to serve for one year.

  2. The duties of the secretary are to:

    1. Take notes of the proceedings and prepare and present the minutes of the meetings;

    2. Maintain and act as custodian of the records and membership rolls of the Council;

    3. Prepare and distribute the required notices of meetings and the proposals to be considered;

    4. Assist the Chair with the official communications and correspondence of the Council;

    5. Maintain the roster of Commencement participation by librarians and notify librarians when their participation is needed; and,

    6. Perform such other duties as are assigned in these Bylaws or by the Council, or as are usually assigned to secretaries of organizations.


MEETINGS

  1. The Council meets:

    1. At least four times each year.

    2. As needed, on call of the Chair.


  2. The Chair must call a meeting within two weeks of the receipt of a request signed by five members, or from a Standing Committee.

  3. QUORUM. One third of the voting membership must be present to constitute a quorum for the transaction of any business.


COMMITTEES

  1. The Standing Committees of the Council are: The Executive Committee, The Personnel Committee, The Review Committee for Personnel Action, The Research and Professional Development Committee, and The Compensation Committee.

  2. Executive Committee.

    1. The Executive Committee consists of the Chair, Chair-Elect, Past Chair, Secretary of the Council, the Chair of the Personnel Committee, the Chair of the Review Committee for Personnel Action, the Chair of the Research and Professional Development Committee, the Chair of the Compensation Committee and the Academic Senator-at-Large from the University Libraries, the Committee on Academic Professional Status representative from the University Libraries (if any), the Kenneth B. Knepp Parliamentarian (non-voting member), and the Council Page Master (non-voting member).

    2. The Chair and Secretary of the Council hold the same offices in the Executive Committee.

    3. The duties and functions of the Executive Committee are to:

      1. Assist the Chair in planning agendas for meetings of the Council;

      2. When urgent matters arise between meetings of the Council, act for the Council subject to review and approval at its next meeting;

      3. Report and make recommendations to the Council on matters of general concern or policy, either on its own initiative, or in response to questions or proposals referred to it.

      4. Provide for the maintenance of a Librarians' Handbook to include the basic documents of the Council and current personnel policies and procedures for professionals in the Libraries.


  3. Personnel Committee.

    1. Personnel Committee consists of five members elected for terms of two years, plus the Chair of the Council. The terms of the elected members are staggered with two or three members to be elected each year. The Chair of the Council serves as a non-voting advisory member.

    2. The duties and functions of the Personnel Committee are to:

      1. Develop and recommend to the Council statements of personnel policy and the procedures and criteria for its implementation; and,

      2. Consider individual grievances which may be brought to it, and offer advice to the grievant, the Administration of the Libraries, and/or the Council concerning them.


  4. Review Committee for Personnel Action (hereinafter referred to as the Review Committee).

    1. The Review Committee consists of:

      1. Five members elected for terms of two years. At least one member shall be an Academic Professional with full status. The terms are staggered with two or three members elected each year. These members must have continuing appointment status or have at least six years of service with year-to-year appointment.

      2. One non-voting advisory member with probationary status and assistant rank, elected for a term of one year.

      3. A representative from the Personnel Committee as a non-voting advisory member.


    2. The duties and functions of the Review Committee are to serve as the committee of peers in personnel actions which call for participation of such a committee.


  5. Research and Professional Development Committee.

    1. The Research and Professional Development Committee consists of three members elected for terms of two years. The terms are staggered with one or two members elected each year.

    2. The duties and functions of the Research and Professional Development Committee are to:

      1. Develop and recommend to the Council policies and procedures for distributing the funds available for professional development activities.

      2. Receive and evaluate requests for professional development activities, and allocate funds among those requesting them on the basis of policies approved by the Council.

      3. Develop and recommend policies and programs for continued support for professional development activities.


  6. Compensation Committee.

    1. The Compensation Committee consists of five members elected for terms of two years. The terms of the elected members are staggered with two or three members to be elected each year.

    2. The duties and functions of the Compensation Committee are:

      1. To develop and recommend to the Council policies and procedures on compensation issues such as merit plans.

      2. To conduct salary surveys and monitor pay equity and market adjustment issues and recommend appropriate adjustments for consideration by the Dean of University Libraries.

      3. To address any compensation matters as appropriate.


  7. General Provisions Relating to Standing Committees.

    1. Each Standing Committee, except the Executive Committee, organizes itself annually by the election of a chair and any other officers it deems necessary.

    2. A majority of the voting members of any committee must be present to constitute a quorum.

    3. Each Standing Committee is responsible for developing a detailed statement of the policies and procedures to be followed in its activities. These statements and any subsequent revisions will be submitted to Council for approval and will be included in the Librarians' Handbook.

    4. Each Standing Committee is to submit to the Council a report of its activities by June 30 of each year.

    5. Any Standing Committee may have questions or proposals on any subject referred to it by the Council or the Executive Committee, and is empowered to make recommendations to the Council on any such matter or any other matter arising out of its activities.

    6. When, for any reason, a vacancy occurs on a standing committee, the Executive Committee in consultation with the Chair of the standing committee involved, will decide how best to fill the vacancy; e.g., by appointment by the Executive Committee, by election by the Council. Standing Committee members will submit a notice of resignation to the Secretary of the Librarians' Council at least two weeks prior to the resignation date.


  8. The Council may create special committees as they are needed for any purpose.


APPOINTED POSITIONS

  1. Parliamentarian.

    1. A member parliamentarian is appointed by the Chair. The appointed individual will be called the Kenneth B. Knepp Parliamentarian. The term of service is one year and is renewable.

    2. The duties are to advise the Chair in the planning and conduct of meetings and to serve as a source of information on parliamentary procedure.


  2. Librarians' Council Page Master

    1. A Librarians' Council page master is appointed by the Chair. The term of service is one year and is renewable.

    2. The duties are to:

      1. Assist Council secretary in placing agendas, minutes, annual reports, and other Librarians’ Council documents on the web site;

      2. Assist Council committees in placing documents on the web site;

      3. Update Librarians’ Handbook on the web by:

        • Changing pages when notified by the Chair-Elect;

        • Maintaining Revision Notices page;

        • Printing out an archival copy of the new/revised Handbook pages


      4. Maintain a web page of Handbook changes that are awaiting administrative approval.

      5. Consult with University Libraries’ web master(s) to ensure that Librarians’ Council site complies with University and Library web policies.

NOMINATIONS AND ELECTIONS

  1. There will be a Committee on Nominations and Elections consisting of three members: two will be elected annually at the first regular meeting of the Council. The immediate past chair of Council will serve as the third member and chair of the Nominations and Elections Committee. Nominations for the elected members will be made from the floor. Members of the Committee on Nominations and Elections will serve a one-year term.

  2. The duties and functions of the Committee on Nominations and Elections are to:

    1. Report at the appropriate times slates of nominees for all offices and committee memberships for which elections are to be held during the year, except when other provisions are made in these Bylaws.

    2. Prepare, distribute, collect and count ballots, and report the results to the Council.


  3. The Council may adopt rules and/or policies to provide for representation of various constituencies of the membership on the Committee on Nominations and Elections, or on slates of nominees.

  4. Candidates shall be elected to office by receiving a plurality of the legal votes cast for that office.

  5. Ballots shall be counted by a meeting of the Committee on Nominations and Elections with a quorum (majority) present. (See Section B, Meetings)


PARLIAMENTARY AUTHORITY

The authority on all procedural questions not covered by the Bylaws or rules of the Council is the latest edition of Sturgis, Alice, Sturgis Standard Code of Parliamentary Procedure.


AMENDMENTS

  1. Amendments to these Bylaws may be proposed at any meeting of the Council.

  2. Amendments are not to be adopted in the meeting at which they are first proposed. They may be discussed for clarification and/or referred to a committee at that meeting.

  3. Proposed amendments must be distributed to the membership, through the minutes or otherwise, at least eight days before the meeting which is to vote on their adoption.

  4. Adoption of amendments to these Bylaws requires two-thirds of the legal votes cast.


POLICIES

  1. Standing rules and policy and procedure statements may be proposed at any meeting of Council. These actions may not be adopted at the same meeting at which they are proposed.

  2. Standing rules and policy and procedure statements shall be adopted or amended by a majority of the legal votes cast.
