April 17, 2003

V Foundation honors Allen with comeback award

ASU junior and cancer survivor Justin Allen, a forward on the ASU men's basketball team, has been named the recipient of the 2003 V Foundation Comeback of the Year Award. The award, presented by the V Foundation for Cancer Research and ESPN, is given annually to a collegiate basketball student-athlete who embodies the spirit and courage represented by the late coach and broadcaster Jim Valvano.

V Foundation CEO Nick Valvano presented the award to Allen at the ASU basketball banquet April 8 in Scottsdale. Allen was selected from more than 30 nominees nationwide.

Allen was diagnosed with Hodgkin's disease in September 2000 following his freshman year at ASU. He returned to the court at ASU in 2001 - 02 while still recovering from the effects of the disease, following chemotherapy and radiation treatments. He played in 19 games in 2002 - 03 and helped the team reach its first NCAA appearance since 1995.

"Justin Allen is the perfect example of someone not giving up and is someone who will make the V Foundation and the Valvano family proud," said ASU Head Coach Rob Evans. "He has been a champion in his commitment to fighting the disease and has been a leader in our program. Most importantly, Justin is someone we can all learn from on how to deal with adversity."

Valvano coached North Carolina State University to the NCAA national championship in 1983. After leaving the school, he worked as a broadcaster for ESPN. In 1993, while dying from cancer, he gave an emotional speech telling people "don't give up. Don't ever give up." The words became the motto for the foundation, which was founded that same year.

"I am honored to receive this award and to be associated with the V Foundation and the Valvano family," Allen said. "I was born in 1981, just two years before Coach Valvano's team won the national title, but I am an example of how his inspiration and name can help make a difference in the fight against cancer. Coach Valvano's words of never giving up were spoken by my family many times. I remembered those words both on the court and in the classroom, in the hospital and walking around campus."
Allen will graduate this spring with a degree in justice studies, but plans to return to the team for his senior year of athletic eligibility while pursuing an advanced degree.

The V Foundation Comeback Award is presented annually and is open to men and women collegiate basketball student-athletes in all NCAA division levels.
