TRiO continues to make college dreams come true

Since 1965, more than 10.5 million Americans (67 percent from poor and working families) have benefited from the services of TRiO pre-college and college programs.

In Arizona, 31 TRiO programs have given 11,835 students the opportunity to experience higher education first-hand and have brought in $9,426,448 annually. In the Phoenix area, programs at ASU (Tempe and West campuses), Gateway Community College, Phoenix College and Friendly House Inc. have assisted 5,232 students with federal funding totaling $2,820,281.

To honor the students that succeeded in college with the support of our Nation’s TRiO programs, TRiO students, college graduates and administrators celebrated National TRiO Day Feb. 26 on the Tempe campus. Events included keynote speakers and award presentations to Upward Bound students, who read their award-winning essays.

"I am proof that these programs work,” says Lydia Perez, assistant director of ASU’s Upward Bound Program.

"I am confident that my family would have done whatever they could to make a university education possible, but I knew there had to be a way that I could ease the responsibility,” says Perez, an alumna of TRiO’s Upward Bound Program at ASU who earned her bachelor’s degree in psychology from ASU in 1995. “I know that my family is the primary reason that I excelled in high school and completed a postsecondary education.”

Since 1966, the Upward Bound Program at ASU has assisted thousands of low-income and first-generation high school students reach their dreams of a postsecondary education. The program provides its participants with an intensive six-week summer residential program that offers students an opportunity to live in a college residence hall and take academically challenging courses to prepare for college admission and enrollment.

ASU’s Upward Bound Program has had every one of its participants graduate from high school, and each of these graduates has been accepted to post-secondary institutions.

For more information about ASU’s Upward Bound Program, visit the Web at (http://www.asu.edu/studentlife/ub/home.html).

For more information about TRiO Student Support Programs, visit (http://www.asu.edu/drs/trio/trio_homepage.html).
