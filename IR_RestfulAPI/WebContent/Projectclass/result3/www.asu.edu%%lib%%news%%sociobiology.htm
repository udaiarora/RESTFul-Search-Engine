Social Insects Invade Noble Library!

(posted 2/15/06)

 

 

A Triumph of Sociobiology:

An Exhibit Highlighting Social Insect Biology and Research at ASU

 

Noble Science and Engineering Library

Spring Semester 2006

 

Once denounced as a “pseudo-science” that provided justifications for social evils and inequalities, sociobiology now is recognized for what it is, a powerful branch of evolutionary biology. In the 30 years after sociobiology was named and brought to public view by E.O. Wilson, the sociobiological approach has successfully demonstrated its value to those of us interested in the evolutionary basis of animal behavior, including that of our own species. The many triumphs of sociobiology have advanced our understanding of such things as how the sterile castes of the social insects have evolved, why self-sacrificing altruism is widespread in many social animal species, and how natural selection has shaped the psychological mechanisms present in the human brain. - John Alcock, Regents’ Professor, ASU School of Life Sciences

 

This exhibit shows examples of social insects, including honeybees and ants, and how they live in the world around us. Included are several colonies of live ants, all of which are native to Arizona. The exhibit was prepared by the Visualization Laboratory and Social Insect Research Group at the School of Life Sciences to highlight their research, and will run through the end of the Spring semester at Noble Library.

 

Don’t miss a special night with Harvard University Emeritus Professor Edward O. Wilson, named by Time Magazine as “one of the 25 Most Influential People” (1995). He will speak on The Future of Life Thursday, April 13, 2006 at 7pm at the Galvin Playhouse, ASU Tempe Campus.

 

 

Exhibits in ASU Libraries reflect the scholarly, historical, social, and cultural concerns of Arizona State University and its community. If you would like to propose an exhibit, please visit http://www.asu.edu/lib/about/policies/exhibits.htm or contact Katherine O'Clair, Exhibits Committee Chair at 480-965-5964 or katherine.oclair@asu.edu.
