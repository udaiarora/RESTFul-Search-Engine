December 8, 2003

Task force to coordinate ASU interactions with City of Phoenix

ASU's effort to develop a 12,000-student downtown campus is one component of the university's overall master planning process to build its capacity to serve 92,000 students by the year 2020.

The downtown effort, because of the many diverse constituencies that will be involved, will require a high level of coordination to ensure those representing ASU are kept up to date on the planning process and can respond accurately to inquiries or provide appropriate input.

Nancy Jordan's new position as a loaned executive with the city of Phoenix is one of the key drivers on the downtown campus effort. As part of her role, ASU President Michael Crow asked Jordan to serve on a task force he is chairing to coordinate all of ASU's connections with the city of Phoenix related to the development of the Downtown campus. The task force is currently made up of four subcommittees as follows:

. Facilities and Planning, headed by Deputy Executive Vice President of University Services Scott Cole and University Architect Ron McCoy;
. Interactions with City Government, headed by Associate Vice President Nancy Jordan;
. Downtown Campus Area Conceptualization, headed by College of Architecture and Environmental Design Dean Wellington Reiter; and
. Academic Programs, headed by Executive Vice President and University Provost Milton Glick.

"As the downtown campus begins to develop as a biomedical center of town, we want to complement what the city and private developers have planned for the area, while making it fit ASU's needs," says Crow. "We also want to make sure that our messages to the city are consistent."

Faculty, students and staff are encouraged to get involved in the planning processes by attending open forums and providing feedback on all phases of the development plan.

This article appeared in the December 5, 2003 issue of ASU Insight.
