December 12, 2005

'Tis the season for graduates

About 4,000 ASU students will receive a much-awaited holiday present Dec. 15: their college degrees. The ASU fall graduation ceremony will take place at 10 a.m. at Wells Fargo Arena.

The distinction of graduating from the country’s largest university won’t be lost on the graduates, many of whom now have an extra advantage in the job market. ASU’s boom in quality and growth and its research initiatives have been featured in recent national publications, including the Chronicle of Higher Education.

Graduates from the West and Polytechnic campuses will join those from the Tempe campus in the university-wide commencement, with ASU President Michael Crow addressing the students and their families.

Two exceptional individuals will receive honorary doctoral degrees at the event. They are Lord John Browne, group chief executive of British Petroleum, one of the world’s largest energy companies, and the Right Honourable Kim Campbell, former prime minister of Canada and the first woman to hold that position. Brown also will present a keynote address on long-term sustainability through energy production and use.

Two students will receive Moeur Awards from the ASU Alumni Association for grad-

uating with perfect 4.0 grade-point averages and having completed all their coursework at ASU.

Rachel Gemmill will graduate with a bachelor’s in special education, and Shannon Zollinger will receive a bachelor’s in political science and history. Both are from Phoenix.

For the first time, three sisters will sing the national anthem and the alma mater as a trio, giving a special twist to the music for the event. Lynette Kidman is graduating in musical theater, and her sisters Jennifer Kidman and Lynda Kidman Nolan are receiving bachelor’s degrees in choral music.

Another family group graduating together includes Lee Ann Dumas, receiving her doctorate in higher education, and her two sons, who are receiving their bachelor’s degrees: Shannon Cook in journalism and Nash Cook in interdisciplinary studies.

Individual colleges and cultural groups will hold smaller convocation ceremonies Dec. 14 – 17. A convocation schedule is at (www.asu.edu/ssc/commence/fall).

The fall commencement ceremony will be broadcast live on the Web, at (http://is.asu.edu/live).
