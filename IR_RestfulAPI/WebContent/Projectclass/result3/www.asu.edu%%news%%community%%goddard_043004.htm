April 30, 2004

College of Public Programs honors attorney general

Terry Goddard, Arizona State Attorney General, has been selected to receive the 2004 Distinguished Achievement Award from the ASU College of Public Programs for his outstanding service to the college and for his distinguished record of public service.

The award was presented April 29 at the University Club on ASU's Tempe campus.

"I am humbled to be recognized by the college, which has done so much to raise the level of public service in Arizona," Goddard said. "I share this award with the hard-working men and women I've had the great privilege of working with in city state and federal government. The College of Public Programs has made a huge contribution to promoting public service as a career choice, and creating a new generation of public servants dedicated to making Arizona a great place live, work and raise our families."

The award is the highest honor presented by the College of Public Programs. Prior recipients include Janet Napolitano, Tom Chauncey Sr., Frank L. Snell, Walter Cronkite, Betsey Bayless, John P. Frank and Hugh Downs. Honorees must be citizens of Arizona who have contributed specifically to the college and who have a record of exceptional service or achievement in a professional field or in public service.

Goddard has a long history of serving the public, including four terms as mayor of Phoenix and seven years as the Arizona state director for the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). He was elected attorney general in 2002.

Goddard's career has exemplified a commitment to improving the lives of Arizo nans. Since becoming attorney general, he has fought for consumer justice by:

. Fighting Internet crime, especially identity theft and crimes against children;

. Expanding consumer protection by the AG's Office, resulting in millions of dollars for the state and consumers; and,

. Drafting and urging passage of legislation to tackle predatory lending and price gouging.

Goddard has championed the College of Public Programs as the chair of the Dean's Community Resource Council, working closely with Dean Anne L. Schneider on various projects in support of the College.

"Terry brought new people into the Community Resource Council and helped open doors for faculty partnerships with people in the community, " Schneider said. "He is truly one of Arizona's real pioneers in public service and university-community partnerships."

Goddard received his law degree from ASU and served in the Navy, retiring as a Commander after 27 years of service in the Reserves.

For information contact the College of Public Programs at (480) 727-6238.
