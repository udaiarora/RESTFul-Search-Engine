ORSPA names ASU’s Conover as new director

Cheryl Conover has been appointed as director of the Office for Research and Sponsored Projects Administration (ORSPA). Conover takes on the responsibility of managing several departments within the ORSPA, which assist students, faculty and staff with obtaining outside support for research and public service projects.

The office also is responsible for making sure those projects comply with federal, state, university and sponsor requirements.

“I’m going to be involved in a lot of outreach because the university is growing so fast,” Conover says. “I would like to see the grant infrastructure in the colleges grow with it. That will be necessary to support the handling of proposals and grants that bring in research money.”

Conover became director March 16 after serving as interim director since September. She moves up from her capacity as director of research administration, where she helped develop and implement the research information system used by ORSPA.

Conover came to ASU in 1987 from Iowa as an accountant in the Office of Sponsored Projects while earning her master’s degree in public administration from ASU. She has worked in various administrative capacities as an analyst, manager, assistant director and director.
