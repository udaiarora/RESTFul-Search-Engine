November 6, 2003

Crawdad Technologies LLC, ASU win Tech Transfer Grant

Crawdad Technologies, LLC of Chandler announced today that it has received a $100,000 Small Business Technology Transfer Research (STTR) award from the United States Air Force Office of Scientific Research to analyze text streams under storage constraints. The grant is in conjunction with Arizona State University (ASU).

Crawdad Technologies, a software spinout from ASU, helps organizations derive business value from unstructured text such as reports, memos and news streams. The company uses its patent-pending text analysis method, developed at ASU, to transform text into networks that represent author intent. The technology has applications in national security, knowledge management, and competitive business intelligence.

Steve Corman, a professor at ASU and CTO for Crawdad Technologies, says, "We're very happy that the United States Air Force sees potential in our technology. The product we're developing will help intelligence and business analysts find information and patterns in large volumes of streaming text."

STTR funds are dedicated to collaborations between universities and small businesses engaged in technology transfer. The intent of the program is to promote the development of academic technologies into practical commercial applications. The award will help Crawdad extend its existing product offerings into a new area.

"The technology developed by Professors Steve Corman and Kevin Dooley incorporates a unique methodology that can be used in numerous business applications," stated Anil Jain, Vice President and Managing Director of Technology Ventures at Arizona Technology Enterprises, LLC. "Arizona Technology Enterprises and Crawdad Technologies are collaborating to take this technology to market." Arizona Technology Enterprises, LLC is the technology commercialization firm that represents ASU and Northern Arizona University (NAU).

The ASU Software Factory will play and important role in the project. Part of the newly formed Institute for Computer and Information Sciences Engineering (InCISE), the Software Factory is an on-campus unit that provides software engineering support for research and technology transfer projects.

The Phase I grant will be shared with ASU, and will lead to an architectural design of a system to analyze streaming texts like news articles and emails. It will also assess storage and computational requirements of the system, and the potential performance of the system in identifying texts of interest to users.

Success in Phase I will allow for Crawdad to apply for additional funding. Phase II would support development of a prototype system and proof-of-concept. Phase III would fund commercial development and deployment of the system. The Federal Government retains the right to use any technologies developed under the STTR award free of charge.

For more information please contact Kevin Dooley at dooley@crawdadtech.com or by phone at 480.236.9588, or Randi Bergo at randi.bergo@asu.edu or by phone at 480.236.1333.
