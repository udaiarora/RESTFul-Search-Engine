Preparing for Format Review, the Oral Defense,
Approval of Your Document, and Binding

Overview
When your chair believes that you have produced a complete, defense-ready draft of your document (i.e., it has been proofread for grammar and punctuation and includes the text and all tables, figures, references, and appendices), you must submit an unbound, one-sided, standard print quality document to the Division of Graduate Studies for format review.

The Submission Process
Before your complete thesis, dissertation, or equivalent may be accepted by a Division of Graduate Studies representative for format review, the following requirements must be met:
  • Your approved Program of Study is on file.*


  • Your committee has been approved by the Division of Graduate Studies. If you need to change your committee, you must fill out a Graduate Supervisory Committee Change Form (www.asu.edu/graduate/forms), obtain the appropriate signatures, and submit it to the Division of Graduate Studies.


  • You have passed all required examinations (e.g., foreign language or comprehensives), and the department has sent the results to the Division of Graduate Studies.


  • You must be enrolled for at least one credit hour that appears on the Program of Study, or one hour of appropriate graduate level credit, during the semester or summer session in which you defend and/or meet special requirements if defending during the summer or interim period (for further information, please refer to the handout Graduation Deadlines and Procedures available in Administration Building, B-wing, room 170 or at www.asu.edu/graduate/generalinfo/GradDdlns).


  • You have a completed Format Approval Sheet signed by your chair indicating that the Format Manual and your selected style guide have been followed. Your document cannot be reviewed until you indicate on the Format Approval Sheet which standard style guide or journal article you have used (to format your headings, in-text citations, references, and tables and figures) and provide a sample article with examples of all these elements, if necessary.


  • If you are a doctoral student, you have been advanced to candidacy.
* You may check your status in relation to these requirements at any time during your graduate career by contacting a Division of Graduate Studies staff member at grad-sas@asu.edu or (480) 965-3521.

Please note: All required forms mentioned in the Format Manual are available online at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms. When submitting for format review and scheduling your defense, you must turn in all forms and your document at least 10 working days before the anticipated date of your defense. A ten working-day calendar for submitting theses/dissertations can be found on our website.

At the time of submission, doctoral students should submit a Survey of Earned Doctorates (available at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms or from the Division of Graduate Studies). Students who opt to delay the publication of their theses/dissertations (confidential status) must submit a petition at the time their document is submitted for format review (see Special Instructions). After these items have been collected and all necessary requirements have been checked by a Division of Graduate Studies staff member, your document will be released to a format advisor for review.



The Format Review Process
A format advisor checks your work against the Division of Graduate Studies Format Manual requirements and the requirements of your selected style guide or journal. He/she also spot-checks for misspellings, inconsistencies, typographical errors, and grammatical problems, but a thorough review of the entire document for these errors is the responsibility of the student and the committee chair.

Turnaround time for review fluctuates depending on volume and increases as the semester deadlines approach. Your document will be reviewed as quickly as possible, and you will be contacted via email upon completion of the review. Most students need to make some corrections or changes, as indicated on the Format Approval Sheet and the actual document pages, and are asked to resubmit either a full document or selected pages.

Regardless of the outcome of the initial format review, the approval page must be corrected and approved for format prior to your defense. Although your format may be approved at any time (before or after your defense), you must have final format approval before the dean?s signature can be obtained on your approval page.



Holding the Oral Defense
You are responsible for bringing a clean copy of the correctly formatted approval page to your defense for your committee to sign. Your Report for Master?s Thesis/Practicum Defense or Announcement and Report for Doctoral Dissertation Defense (?pass/fail form?) will have already been sent to your chair from the Division of Graduate Studies prior to the defense.

In the event that one of your original committee members must be absent from the defense, please contact a Division of Graduate Studies staff member as soon as possible so that you may be referred to the specific absent committee member procedures set by the Graduate Council.

Although defense procedures vary by department or degree program, after a successful defense, you will typically receive the signed approval page and a signed, completed pass/fail form. You will then need to take these pages to the head of your academic unit to be signed. Although your committee may recommend changes to your content following your defense, there is no need to submit a full revised copy of your document to format unless indicated as part of your previous format review. However, if your previously approved title and/or abstract changes based on committee recommendation after your defense, you will need to resubmit revised title and approval pages and/or the abstract for an additional format review before the dean?s signature can be obtained



Obtaining Format Approval and the Dean’s Signature
Once all formatting corrections have been made satisfactorily, the format advisor gives final format approval to the document, as indicated on the Format Approval Sheet and by email. After you have final format approval, have passed the oral defense, and have made any committee-requested changes, take the original, signed approval page and the pass/fail report to Administration Building B-wing, Room 170 to obtain the Division of Graduate Studies dean?s signature before the semester deadline.

If your department/unit forwards the pass/fail report to us after the head of the academic unit has signed, we will retain it with your files. Upon completion of the dean?s signature, we will provide four copies of the signed approval page for you to take to the ASU Bookstore.

Please note: There is a minimum 24-hour turnaround time for dean?s signatures. Please consult with a Student Academic Services staff member for more information.




Students with Internships after their Oral Defense
Students completing internships after the semester of their oral defense will not be eligible to receive the dean?s signature until the semester in which they intend to graduate. After the oral defense, students with internships should deliver their signed approval page and pass/fail form to Student Academic Services (SAS) in Administration Building B-wing, room 170 for safekeeping. Please include an attached note stating the intended semester and year of graduation. When SAS is notified by the committee chair or internship director that the internship has been completed and the student has applied for graduation, the student will be eligible for the dean?s signature.




Submitting Your Thesis/Dissertation to the Bookstore for Binding
Two bound copies of your document are required by the university; please check with your graduate support staff person to find out if you are required to have a copy bound for your department. Print and/or copy the number of theses/dissertations you will need to have bound on 20 lb.-minimum, acid-free paper. In your thesis/dissertation copies, place the photocopies of the approval page signed by the dean of the Division of Graduate Studies in the appropriate place in your document, and submit them as indicated by the ASU Bookstore?s instructions. The Bookstore will not accept your copies without a Division of Graduate Studies dean?s signature on your approval page.
