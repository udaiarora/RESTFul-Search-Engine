September 16, 2004

Workshops help scholars boost fellowship applications

This fall, the ASU Division of Graduate Studies will offer a series of workshops designed to help students strengthen their applications for national fellowship programs, such as the National Science Foundation, Ford Foundation and Jacob F. Javits Foundation.

The workshops are guided by students who are fellowship recipients and faculty members who have served on fellowship review panels. They provide tips on how to write successful applications.

Competitions for national fellowships are open to ASU seniors, first-year graduate students, and in some cases second-year graduate students. All eligible students and their faculty mentors are encouraged to attend the workshops.

The workshops are a central feature of the initiative launched jointly by the Division of Graduate Studies, the Office of National Scholarships in the Barrett Honors College, and the Office of the Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs to boost the number of ASU students receiving national-level fellowships.

As part of the initiative, the Office of the Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs and the Division of Graduate Studies also have teamed together and created an incentive program that rewards faculty for their mentoring role in the development of successful fellowship applications.

Workshops are scheduled as follows: Sept. 7, 9 – 11 a.m., Memorial Union, Pima Room 218; Sept. 8, 10 a.m. – noon, West campus’ Sands Building, Room 323; Sept. 13, 2 – 4 p.m., Memorial Union, Pima Room 218; Sept. 22, 2 – 4 p.m., Memorial Union, Pima Room 218; Sept. 30, 2 – 4 p.m., Memorial Union, Pima Room 218.

Copies of successful proposals and reference letters are available for review by faculty and students by contacting the office of Associate Dean Marjorie Zatz in the Division of Graduate Studies or Associate Dean Burke in the Barrett Honors College Office of National Scholarships.

Coronado, with the Division of Graduate Studies, can be reached at (480) 965-5995 or (nora.coronado@asu.edu).
