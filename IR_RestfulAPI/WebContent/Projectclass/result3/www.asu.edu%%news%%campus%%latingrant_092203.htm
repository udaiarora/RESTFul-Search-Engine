Arizona Latin American Studies Consortium receives $1.5 million grant

The Arizona Consortium of Latin American Studies, comprised of programs from Arizona State University and the University of Arizona, has been awarded a grant from the U.S. Department of Education for $1.5 million dollars to enhance the study of Latin America at both universities and in their respective communities.

The three-year grant designates both Arizona state universities as "National Resource Centers" for Latin American Studies and enhances the Arizona consortium's status as one of the top centers for Latin American Studies in the nation.

Among other things, funds from the grant will be used to provide graduate fellowships that will help ASU recruit top tier graduate students in the Latin American area. The graduate fellowships will also support the teaching of Portuguese and Quichua languages at ASU.

In addition, funds have been allocated to conduct an outreach effort targeting K-12 schools throughout the state to enhance the quality of education on Latin America in the schools. As a part of this outreach there will be continuing education scholarships for area teachers to participate in ASU's Ecuador Field School where they will develop curriculum units on the Amazonian rainforest as well as on the Andean region.

Another project funded through the grant will be the development of a Mexican diploma course for the general community on Mexican history, art and literature jointly sponsored by ASU's Center for Latin American Studies and the National Autonomous University in Mexico City (UNAM).

For more information on these programs and the grant, contact the Center for Latin American Studies at 480-965-5127.
