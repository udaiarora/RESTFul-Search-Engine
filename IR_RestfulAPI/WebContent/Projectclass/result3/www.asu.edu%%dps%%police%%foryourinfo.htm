For Your Information: 
Weapons, Drugs and Alcohol Policies at ASU

ASU is a Weapons Free Zone
    Possession, use, or storage of weapons in on-campus student housing is prohibited. Possession or storage of weapons on campus, on University property, or at a University-sponsored activity is also prohibited, except in very limited circumstances. To avoid creating undue concerns, the use or possession of non-operational or model weapons that have the appearance of actual weapons or firearms is subject to the approval of the ASU Police. Participants or attendees at public or special events held on campus or at University facilities may be required to submit to security checks as a condition of entry at the discretion of ASU Police. [A.R.S. 13-2911, DPS 201-05, ABOR 5-303.3]

Alcohol and Drug Policies
    ASU takes seriously its responsibility for creating a community conducive to living, learning and working. In keeping with this responsibility is the establishment of clear rules and regulations regarding the use of controlled substances.
    In 1994, ASU adopted a comprehensive policy regarding alcohol and other drugs. Copies of the policy are available from the Office of the Vice President for Student Affairs (965-7293). Detailed information regarding possession, use, and distribution of drugs and alcohol is set forth in ASU's Drug-Free Work Place Statement and Drug-Free Schools and Campuses Act Statement, which is printed in the class schedule. ASU maintains zero tolerance toward the possession, use, distribution and manufacture of illegal drugs. Consumption of alcohol and alcohol advertising, sponsorship and promotions are also addressed in ASU's policy regarding Permits to Serve Alcohol Beverages on Campus. (STA 106-03, DPS 202-03, RSK 304)
        It is a violation of the law to possess and or consume alcohol anywhere if you are under the age of 21. It is a violation of the law to consume alcohol in public even if you are 21. State Law requires permission must be given for extended premises consumption. It is a felony to posses any useable amount of illegal drug or drug paraphernalia.
    Student Health educator David Bower (965-1132) teaches a course on how to overcome Marijuana and Alcohol use.
