Meal Plan Payment Process

There has been a slight change in your meal plan payment process for the spring 2007 semester. For fall 2006, meal plan charges were billed to your ASU Student Account and paid directly to the university. For spring 2007, your meal plan payment is due by December 20th, 2006 and must be paid directly to ASU Campus Dining. Please use one of the convenient payment methods. If you receive financial aid, your money will not automatically be disbursed to cover the cost of your meal plan. You will need to pay ASU Campus Dining separately. If you have any questions, please contact ASU Campus Dining directly at 480.965.3464.

Spirit contest

This fall, each residence hall was given the challenge to work together and show their ASU spirit through a variety of hall programs and initiatives. The new hall decorating contest takes place the week before each home football game and is judged by a panel of staff and students. Each week the winning hall receives $5,000 to be used for hall improvements. The first three contest winners have been Cholla hall (September 30), Sonora hall (October 21) and Hassayampa Academic Village (November 9). Points are cumulative, and at the conclusion of the season, the hall with the most points overall will win $25,000 to use to improve its community. Get involved and help show your Sun Devil pride!

Renters Insurance

Renters, including students living on campus, should seriously consider renters insurance, especially if you have a computer, bike, television, stereo and other electronics and valuables in your room. Renters insurance can provide peace of mind and protection against tragedies such as fires, theft, and floods caused by plumbing. In many cases students can be covered by their family’s homeowner’s policy. Now is a great time to call your insurance agent and find out what the rates are for students living on campus. According to a recent article on youngmoney.com, prices are affordable and can vary from $15 to $30 per month depending on location, square footage and possessions.

Residence Hall Association

The Residence Hall Association took a delegation of 23 residents and staff to the Intermountain Affiliate of College and University Residence Halls 2006 Conference at the University of Idaho this past weekend.

The IACURH conference is held every year at a different host school from the Rocky Mountain region and is a chance for RHA student leaders to trade ideas on programming, advocacy, leadership development and regional involvement.

RHA was awarded the highest regional honor in being named the IACURH School of the Year. This award recognizes outstanding contributions on the campus, regional and national level by a residence hall organization.

Wellness and Health Update

At Wellness and Health Promotion, we know that students who practice healthier behaviors are more successful academically, personally, and professionally. Therefore, we provide information and education to give students the skills to make healthy decisions while at ASU and throughout their lives. We provide information and education on Alcohol, Tobacco and Other Drugs, Body Image and Eating Disorders, Depression and Suicide Prevention, Healthy Eating and Physical Activity, Sexual Assault and Relationship Violence Prevention, Sexual Health, and Stress Management. We offer a variety of programs and services to disseminate this wellness information. These programs and services include presentations to classes or organizations, special awareness events, information tables around campus, and posters promoting wellness behaviors and ways to “live well.”
