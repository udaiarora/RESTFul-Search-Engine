March 9, 2005

‘Desert Nights, Rising Stars’ writing conference finds home at ASU

In its third year, ASU’s “Desert Nights, Rising Stars” is coming home, staging what has become one of the nation’s most comprehensive writing conferences on campus for the first time.

Of course, it helps to have a place to come home to: the recently inaugurated Virginia G. Piper Writers House, which houses the Virginia G. Piper Center for Creative Writing. Created and hosted by the Piper Center, the ASU Writers Conference will be held March 9 – 12 at the beautifully restored historic house and at a number of other nearby facilities.

The conference, timed to coincide with the magic of the orange blossom and wildflower season in the spectacular Sonoran Desert, offers aspiring and professional writers exposure to some of the best talent in the writing. An expanded faculty of 62 includes prominent novelists, poets and screenwriters, as well as biographers, children’s book writers, playwrights, journalists, travel writers, essayists, literary agents, editors, publishers, booksellers and literary critics.

The conference’s literary stars include Booker Prize winning fiction writer A. S. Byatt; American Book Award winning novelist Denise Chavez; former U.S. poet laureate Billy Collins; four-time Pushcart Prize winning poet and children’s literature author Naomi Shihab Nye; acclaimed poet and editor Dave Smith; radio personality, voice artist and novelist Sarah Vowell; and the former poet laureate of Rhode Island, C.D. Wright.

Also teaching at the conference are the full faculty of ASU’s Creative Writing Program, which is ranked as one of the nation’s top master of fine arts degrees by U.S. News & World Report.

Conference organizer and Piper Center Artistic Director Jewell Parker Rhodes emphasizes that the goal of the event is to provide an intimate setting for the study of the craft of writing.

“Participants will have a chance to hone their craft in the classroom and also rub elbows with distinguished writers, sharing dialogue during classes, lunch, and strolls on our garden campus,” she says.

For more information on the conference, go to (www.asu.edu/pipercwcenter/conference) or call (480) 965-0051.

Hathaway, with the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, can be reached at (480) 965-6375 or (hathaway@asu.edu).
