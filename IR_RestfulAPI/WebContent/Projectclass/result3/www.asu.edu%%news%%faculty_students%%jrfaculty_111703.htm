November 17, 2003

ASU to launch development program for junior faculty

The Office of the University Provost, in partnership with the East and West campus provosts, is creating a comprehensive university-wide faculty development program.

Vice Provost Nancy Gutierrez, who will be coordinating the innovative new program, says faculty retention is crucial to ASU's future success.

"ASU will only be able to transform itself into the new American university by retaining its best faculty overall," Gutierrez says. "Junior faculty are the future of ASU, since they will be the creators of knowledge in the generations to come."
Although initially targeted for junior faculty members, the program's scope will be widened in the next academic year.

Gutierrez says the objective of the program is to provide both the information and the professional support necessary for junior faculty to be successful at ASU in all aspects of their work - instruction, scholarship, professional service and outreach.

"The program will be a new structural piece to an already vibrant scholarly community at ASU," Gutierrez says.

The development of this program is the result of feedback from a series of discussions an outside consultant had last spring with faculty and administrators.

Gutierrez says the administration hopes to enhance communication with faculty regarding university practices, particularly procedures related to promotion and tenure.

"We recognize the ongoing need to provide consistent information for junior faculty members across the campuses regarding evaluation procedures, in order that these faculty members might be successful," she says.

Gutierrez stresses that this is a voluntary program, and not a replacement for department or college programs. Instead, it is intended to augment current activities. Units and colleges will maintain their responsibility to orient new faculty into their cultures and to provide them with the necessary support to allow them to be successful.

"While we will initiate a full-scale program in the fall of the next academic year, we will pilot a smaller program this spring, focused on the fourth- and fifth-year faculty members," she says.

For information, call (480) 965-7405.
