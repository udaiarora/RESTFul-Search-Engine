Update Files from PC

These instructions include copying files to or from an Instructor Volume from a PC in an IT computing site or from your office PC.

From an IT Computing Site

  1. Go to any ASU IT PC computing site.
  2. Turn on the PC, if it is not already on.
  3. After performing some setup on the PC the Windows logon screen will be displayed.
  4. Enter your ASURITE UserID and password.
  5. Access your Instructor Volume; see the Access from PC page
  6. Proceed with the copying of file(s).
  7. Do not forget to log out when you've finished. Write access to the Instructor Volume will not be disconnected until you log out.

From Your Office PC

To copy files from your office to an Instructor Volume you must have a PC with an ethernet card and be able to run a SFTP application (available from http://www.asu.edu/it/fyi/comm/sw/serveraccess/ssh.html or Information Technology's Customer Assistance Center) and connect to the ASU General Purpose server. This will enable you to SFTP the file(s) into your Instructor Volume disk space.

Note: Students may copy files from your Instructor Volume using this same procedure.

  1. SFTP to the ASU General purpose server (general.asu.edu).
  2. Log on to the ASU General purpose server using your ASURITE UserID and password.
  3. After getting in, you'll need to change directory to the Instructor Volume space. The path is
    /afs/asu.edu/class/x/y/z/xyz123
    where xyz123 is the name of the Instructor Volume you want to access. See the documentation or on-line help of your SFTP program regarding how to change directories.
  4. Proceed with the SFTPing (copying) of file(s).

Note: Be sure to use the ASCII transfer mode for plain text files and binary for all other files.
