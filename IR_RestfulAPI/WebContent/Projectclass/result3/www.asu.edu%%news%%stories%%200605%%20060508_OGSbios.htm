May 8, 2006

Outstanding Graduating Seniors

Tinsae Babo
Tinsae Babo is graduating with her master of science in agribusiness degree from the Morrison School of Agribusiness and Resource Management. She earned her B.A. in accounting and served on the faculty of business and economics at Addis Ababa University in Ethiopia. She was a recipient of the James J. Sweitzer Memorial Scholarship for Graduate Education. She has been a graduate research assistant for agribusiness faculty, working with both undergraduate students and particularly with faculty associates who come to give special courses at the Polytechnic campus. Since 1997 she has successfully managed the Café Lalibela in Tempe, seeing it grow consistently in both sales and profits over an eight-year period. Tinsae also finds time to mentor a group of young adults from very diverse backgrounds at a local church.

Tiffani Borcherding
Tiffani is graduating from the College of Public Programs with a bachelor of science in recreation tourism. She was a member of the Recreation and Tourism Student Association as well as a research assistant in the ASU School of Community Resources and Development. While working there she compiled data derived from surveys conducted at Yosemite National Park and produced information for the National Park Service. She also conducted on-site surveys with Yosemite visitors to gather data. In addition, Tiffani was an Arizona Parks and Recreation Association Student Member and an Arizona Wilderness Coalition member.

Brian Clapp
Brian Clapp is graduating with a bachelor of science in interdisciplinary studies from the University College. Brian has been a member of Alpha Phi Omega service fraternity and the Supreme Court of Associated Students of Arizona State University (one year he served as the chief justice), and he is the opinion columnist for the State Press. He has led alternative spring breaks during which he volunteered to help the homeless in California. In addition, he was invited to attend Gordon Research Conference on Science and Technology Policy where he presented a poster on efficacy of science as a tool for public relations with the Muslim World. He has held an internship with the Department of State in which we researched the Muslim science community and its relationship to foreign policy. He is also an assistant Scoutmaster.

Colleen Cox
Colleen Cox is graduating with a doctorate (Ph.D.) of law from the College of Law. Colleen was a member of the College of Law Journal, the Moot Court team and she was the leader of a government and public interest law organization. In addition, she was part of the advocacy program for battered women and she was a volunteer with Maricopa County’s Attorney’s office. She also completed a crime victims’ legal assistance project and 196 volunteer legal hours.

Miriam Grill
Miriam is graduating from the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences with a bachelor of arts in English. In addition, Miriam will receive a bachelor of arts in theater and a minor in women and gender studies. Miriam was the first ASU student to study at the University of Hyderbad in India. She also traveled to Burma to work at a school that lacked the basics, while working there she wrote a grant for a new school. The grant was approved and a new school was built. When she returned she interned with the UN Population Fund. In her position there she helped bring the UN photography exhibit “World of Women” to Gammage. She has also appeared in several ASU School of Theatre and Film Mainstage and Student Production plays and for her capstone project Miriam is writing and performing a solo piece, Spinning at the Temple with Durga.

Antonio Gutierrez
Tony is graduating with a bachelor of science in electronics engineering technology from the College Technology and Applied Sciences. He has held summer and part-time positions at Lockheed Martin Space Company in Sunnyvale, Calif., and at Arizona Public Service in Phoenix while completing his degree. He was a research apprentice on juice sterilization research using electrical pulses. His work was presented in the FEMLAB international conference, and he gave a presentation in ASU’s Western Alliance to Expand Student Opportunities workshop. Tony is an ASU Presidential Scholarship award recipient and a Hispanic College Fund Scholar. In addition, he has made the dean’s list from 2002 to 2005. He also is a student member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers and a member of the Tau Alpha Pi, the national honor society for engineering technology.

Steven W. Hare
Steven, a curriculum and instruction/instructional media major, will receive his master of education (M.Ed.) from the School of Educational Innovation and Teacher Preparation. This is his second M.Ed. – he received his first one in curriculum and instruction/English as a second language. For Steven, teaching is his second career; he worked in industry for 25 years. Steven is very involved with his church, having served four missions to Mexico. He also has served in his church’s “Serve Chandler Campaign” to provide service where it is needed in Chandler. Steven plans to teach, using technology and other non-traditional strategies to help start students off on a lifelong journey of learning that is not in their native language.

Jerrod Hofferth
Jerrod, an aerospace engineering major, will receive his bachelor of science in aerospace engineering from the Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering. He has been an engineering tutor, a volunteer Webmaster, an academic tutor for Sigma Gamma Tau and a member of AIAA ASU student branch. In addition, Jerrod is active in his church and with Camp Sparky, a community outreach program that introduces underprivileged children to Astronautics and Aeronautics. He is also a liaison to the Phoenix professional section of AIAA.

Allyson Horgan
Allyson will graduate will a bachelor of science in psychology from the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Sciences. She has been a research assistant in the Legal Psychology Research Laboratory, a volunteer at the Rocky Mountain Psychological Association Annual Conference and a research assistant at Nana’s Children. She also presented a poster at the New College of Interdisciplinary Arts and Science Student Poster Session as well as posters and papers at many regional and national psychology conferences. Allyson also mentors Somali-Bantu refugees.

Michiko Howlett
Michiko will receive her bachelor of science in journalism and mass communication from Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication. Michiko has worked for The Blaze 1260 AM, ASU’s campus radio, since 2003. She is a William Randolph Hearst Journalism Competition winner and a SPJ Student Journalism Competition regional winner. In addition, Michiko has made the Dean’s List from 2002-2006. She is also a member of Phi Eta Sigma honors society, Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication Professional Program and Radio Television News Director’s Association.

Aaron John Kulik
Aaron will be receiving his bachelor of science in interior design from the College of Design. Aaron was a member of the International Interior Design Association-ASU Campus Center; he served as president, president-elect, vice president of publicity, studio liaison and as a mentor. He was also a member of Gamma Beta Phi honor society, AIAS, the College of Design Council; in which he served as a mentor, and he was an undergraduate teaching assistant for Computer Aided Design and Computer Modeling. In addition, Aaron has been a volunteer for Habitat for Humanity and he juried projects for ASU lower division and Phoenix College students.

Debbiesiu Lee
Debbiesiu, a counseling psychology major, received her doctorate (Ph.D.) from the College of Education in August 2005. Debbiesiu was very active while at ASU. She started a local branch of Students Association for Minority Mental Health and she was the co-coordinator of Race 2000, a conference that focuses on issues of assessment and culture in education and psychology. She is also involved in several anger management programs in local schools and she has served as a group facilitator in an intervention program for first time juvenile offenders. She has also co-taught many courses at ASU including Counseling Pre-Practicum, Group Counseling and Multicultural Counseling. In addition, she worked with Native American educators to acquire grant funding so Native American youth could attend the yearly Sundance ceremony in Minnesota.

Cory Jordan Nikolaus
Cory is graduating from the School of Global Management and Leadership with a bachelor of science in global leadership and management. He is a member of the Golden Key International Honor Society, Boy Scouts of America and La Circle Francaise, the ASU French Club. He is a Service Warehouse volunteer where he participates in disaster relief projects and works in grocery distribution to help those who are less fortunate. Cory has also been a volunteer piano player at a senior center and has participated in Habitat for Humanity. He also has completed a two-year mission in France and is active in his church.

Nyssa Packard
Nyssa is graduating from the W.P. Carey School of Business with a bachelor of science in marketing. In addition, Nyssa is earning a bachelor of arts in French, a minor in psychology and an International Business Certificate. She has gained experiences through internships with Noodles & Company and Citigroup. She has also been working as a marketing coordinator for the Office of University Initiatives at ASU. In this position she created a product launch campaign for ASU’s community outreach Web site. Nyssa has been a student leader in the W.P. Carey School of Business; she was the marketing director and a consultant with Honors Consulting as well as the vice president of marketing and the new member president for Pi Sigma Epsilon.

Dena Pollock
Dena is graduating with a bachelor of science in elementary education from the College of Teacher Education and Leadership. She has organized workshops with mentor teachers; she has helped with class field trips and played a key role in organizing a physical education field day. Dena is also involved with her church and she is a volunteer for the young students’ activities. In addition, she often volunteers to chaperone her daughter’s club volleyball and softball activities.

Michael Sample
Michael Sample is graduating from the Herberger College of Fine Arts with a Bachelor of Music, in music education and performance (voice), with a minor in theatre. Sample has achieved high academic standards – accumulating a 3.90 GPA – and was involved with more than eight collegiate extracurricular activities and several community activities. He has participated in ASU Choral Ensemble Sun Devil Singers; ASU University Choir; Study abroad programs in Ecuador, England, France, Guatemala, Italy, Peru and Switzerland; Lyric Opera Theatre; and guest Speaker for ASU School of Music freshman orientation. Sample has also been a musical director for a youth theatre program and volunteered for a youth education program in South America.

Patricia Selvey
Patricia will graduate with a bachelor of science in social work from the College of Human Services. She was the representative to the West Point Ethics Institute for Human Services and ASU at the West Campus. She served as co-president of the Parent Council at a community school and she attended and presented at numerous conferences. She also worked on an independent study, Strengths Perspectives, in which she collected oral histories from older adults and learned how they contribute to their communities.

Patricia O’Donnell
Patricia is graduating with a bachelor of science in nursing from the College of Nursing. Patricia is a Sigma Theta Tau International member, a member Gamma Beta Phi honor society, a member of the Student Nurses Association, a senior two graduation co-representative, part of the Curriculum Committee for the College of Nursing and she was selected by her peers as a Main Campus Commencement speaker. In addition, she was a member of the National Students Nurses Association and she was an appointee to the University of Notre Dame Advisory Council. She also found time to volunteer as a crisis counselor for the National Child Abuse Hotline.

Tiffany Wear
Tiffany is will receive her bachelor of science in management from Barrett Honors College. Tiffany was a member of Alpha Kappa Psi business fraternity and president of the Chi Omega sorority. She was a student ambassador for Barrett Honors College and co-chairman of the Dean’s Advisory Board. She was part of International Mission on Business where she participated in case studies with international companies to explore the difference in American and Chinese corporate cultures. Tiffany was also part of the International Business Seminar where she conducted case studies on European companies to learn about marketing, business processes, supply chain and corporate cultures. In addition, she has completed over 745 community service hours and has been honored with numerous awards including the Arizona Public Service award and the Phoenix Economic Club scholarship and award.
