Classified Staff Council

The Council advises the President of Arizona State University and directs itself to the following tasks:

Improving the employment environment, policies and procedures affecting classified staff, and the general welfare and equal opportunity for classified staff;

Enhancing internal communication among classified staff, other campuses, universities, and local communities;

Promoting organizational development.

 

Sam Wheeler of Auxiliary Business Services explains a map detailing where ASU students and staff live in the metropolitan area during the September Classified Staff Council meeting.

The Council’s September meeting featured a presentation by Parking and Transit Services’ spokesman Sam Wheeler, Executive Director, Auxiliary Business Services. He was assisted by his colleagues Linda Riegel and Theresa Fletcher. He explained the realities that drove the recent parking rate increases and the steps that are being taken to improve the FLASH bus service, some new and improved shuttle services to other campuses, a dedicated ride-sharing program and other initiatives to reduce traffic immediately around the Tempe campus. As a result of a question following the presentation by CSC member Danielle True, Parking and Transit has extended the FLASH bus service to 1:00 a.m., Monday through Thursday.

Message from the CSC President 

On Thursday, January 18, 2007, over 100 people attended a very informative session by Paul Matson, Director of the Arizona State Retirement System (ASRS).  I received numerous compliments from attendees on the topics discussed by Paul.  With over 500,000 members and $26.5 billion in assets, we were given a better understanding of how our current contribution rates are calculated, to asset allocation at ASRS, to strategic planning at ASRS.  We learned a great deal with numerous questions answered for attendees. 

 

I would like to thank and acknowledge Robin Cook, chair of our Comp & Benefits committee, for coordinating this event for the Classified Staff Council. 

Please check out the ASRS website at www.azasrs.gov
