Response to Report of the Child & Dependent Care Task Force and Recommendations regarding Family Friendly Policies

Provost’s Response to Child and Dependent Care Task Force (May 2004)

 

Recommendation

Response

Priority

Move Child and Family Services (CFS) to the Office of the Provost.

We will consider the best reporting line and organizational structure as we move forward in planning expanded child care at ASU.

2004

Establish a standing Work/Life Council on each campus.

We have requested that the Director of EAP/Wellness/Worklife Balance oversee setting up such councils on each campus, and also consider how the campuses can best coordinate university-wide.

2004

Create a stronger sense of community by maximizing community partnerships and strengthening collaboration among on-campus providers.

We will assign this responsibility to the worklife councils for implementation.

2004

Enhance and publicize the existing Elder Care Support Group

Assigned to the work/life councils for implementation.

2004

Enhance and publicize the existing on-line and community – based referral services for dependent care.

We will integrate such information more fully into the Working@ASU web site and alert deans, departments.

2004

Conduct a graduate student study to determine the best options for expanded campus child care.

The survey was conducted in Fall 2003; results are available on request.

Done

Embed family support in all levels and aspects of university planning, e.g., private spaces for lactation.

We are referring this task to the University Planners and Facilities Management for incorporation into the planning process.

2004-2006

Emphasize family supportive climate and policy issues for tenure track faculty and for staff.

 

Partner assistance procedures have been implemented and we are sending a proposed alternate duty assignment policy forward to the Academic Senates and Classified Staff Counsils for approval..  See also the Working@ASU web site. 

The Worklife Councils can assist with implementation issues.

2004

Expand employee benefits, e.g. move forward on partner benefits.

A University priority; we will continue working toward this goal.

2005-2008

Expand on/near campus full-time child care options for children 3-5.

 

Consultant hired to evaluate current needs and recommend priorities re: child care facilities.  Will move forward as soon as possible on expanding child care offerings and will evaluate possibility of adding child care facilities in new complexes.

2004-2008

Expand summer child and youth care options.

We agree that this should be a priority as we discuss new child care options.

2005-2008

Expand accessibility and affordability of child and dependent care services for students, staff  and faculty, e.g. through use of vouchers and subsidies.

Yes, a long term priority

2005-2008

Enhance out-reach through programs and information targeting specific campus audiences about the availability of referral services, support groups, Employee Assistance Program (EAP), and applicable HR benefits.

We would like to refer this to the worklife councils for immediate implementation.

2005

Provide in-depth information about adult dependent care housing options.

Yes, we refer this to the work/life councils.

2005

Expand infant and toddler child care options on Tempe, West and East campuses.

A long term priority for all campuses.

2005-2010

Maintain family housing on the East campus and explore the development of family housing on the Tempe and West campuses, to include on-site or near-site child care.

Feasibility of graduate student housing and child care center being investigated on main south campus site.

2004-2010
