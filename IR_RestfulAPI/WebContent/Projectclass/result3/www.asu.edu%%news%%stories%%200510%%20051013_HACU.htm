October 13, 2005

9 ASU students to take part in Hispanic education conference

Nine ASU students will join top undergraduate, graduate and professional school students from colleges and universities across the United States as participants at the 19th annual conference of the Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities (HACU) Oct. 15 – 18 at Phoenix Civic Plaza.

The conference will expose the participating students to comprehensive leadership training, career exploration activities and networking opportunities through a special student program track designed specifically for aspiring leaders.

Nora Coronado, who is pursuing a master’s degree in public administration through the School of Public Affairs , is one of ASU’s nine student ambassadors to the conference.

“I am a member of the Hispanic community and, in general, very interested in issues affecting Hispanics,” Coronado says. “I hope that, by attending HACU’s conference, I will gain new knowledge and a better understanding of effective initiatives, programs and practices that help Hispanics make gains in higher education, and of policies that affect the education opportunities of Hispanics.”

Phoenix Mayor Phil Gordon, U.S. Rep. Raul Grijalva and U.S. Secretary of Education Margaret Spellings also will deliver keynote addresses during the conference proceedings.

Zoila G. Tovar-Gamero, a doctoral candidate in counseling psychology with the College of Education , also will represent ASU at the HACU conference.

“Current statistics inform us that, nationally, Latinos have a dismal rate of high school graduation and college-going behaviors,” she says. “Here in Arizona , these numbers do not fare any better. A national organization such as HACU can provide the influential backing to push for continued support and possible reform in how we can get Latinos into higher education. Most importantly, HACU provides the networking and guidance that is so often lacking at institutions where the Latino population is small or ignored.”

Also representing ASU as student ambassadors at the 2005 conference are: Rafael Carranza, journalism and Spanish; Yolanda Chavez-Cappellini, Spanish literature and cultural studies; Delis Cuellar Klitzke, educational leadership and policy studies; Viola Fuentes, public administration; Vanessa Garcia, communication; Mary Delores Guerra, educational leadership and policy studies; and Irene Vega, higher and postsecondary education.

ASU is an associate member of HACU and is participating as a lead sponsor of the organization’s 19th annual conference.

“With the conference taking place in our community for the first time in the organization’s history, I’m delighted that ASU is playing a prominent role in bringing this important forum to Phoenix, and providing students and professionals the opportunity to discuss critical issues and share information,” says Eugene E. Garcia, vice president for university-school partnerships and dean of ASU’s College of Education.
