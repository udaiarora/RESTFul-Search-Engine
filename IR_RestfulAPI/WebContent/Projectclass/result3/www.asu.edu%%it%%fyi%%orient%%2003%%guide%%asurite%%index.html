About Your ASURITE UserID

Every person at ASU is assigned an ASURITE UserID. This UserID is unique, and is yours to use as long as you are associated with the university and you follow the appropriate policies and guidelines.

The ASURITE UserID is used to access various services at ASU. Your eligibility for services will depend on your affiliation with ASU. New applicants, for example, may have access to certain Web pages to check the status of their application or to register on-line once they have been admitted. Enrolled students, faculty, and staff will be able to add other computing services as needed to their UserIDs.

Because the ASURITE UserID is used for such a wide variety of access, including sensitive information such as your grades, it is important to protect your password. Do not share it with others. Change your password frequently. Pick passwords that are difficult to guess. Contact Computer Accounts if you suspect your access has been compromised.

ASURITE Activation

If you have not already, activate your ASURITE UserID by visiting the ASURITE Activation and Service Management Web site. This Web site also allows to you add services to your UserID.

Adding Services

Once you have activated your ASURITE UserID, you can add a variety of services, including:

  • AFS Fileservice
  • General Computing
  • Student E-Mail
  • Personal Webpage Hosting

AFS File Service

You can add the AFS file service to your ASURITE UserID, which provides five megabytes of disk space for storing your personal files. These files are stored on a large file server so that you can access them from almost anywhere. This service is also required to access the computing sites.

General Computing

The general computing service gives access to your AFS space from the computing sites and UNIX servers. It also lets you log in to the servers and transfer files from home over the Internet. For this, you will need the Secure Shell Client software available on the Communications CD.

Student E-Mail

If you need an e-mail account, ASU will provide you with an e-mail mailbox (called an IMAP account) if you would like one. To access your IMAP e-mail account, go to EMMA.

Personal Web Page Hosting

Activate your Web space by adding this service to your UserID. Once your space is registered with ASU’s public server, you can then upload your pages into a www folder in your AFS space and make them available on the Web. If you need help creating your Web pages, you can go to the Jukebox Web pages.

Computer Accounts Office

The Computer Accounts office takes care of all computer accounts for students, faculty, and staff. They are located in the Computing Commons building, on the second floor, in the Customer Assistance Center. If you have questions, or encounter problems with your account or your ASURITE UserID, come visit the Computer Accounts office.
