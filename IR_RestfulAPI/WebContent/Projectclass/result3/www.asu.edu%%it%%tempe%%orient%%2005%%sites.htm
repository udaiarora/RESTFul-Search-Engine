Computers

Up-to-date software is available in all the computing sites, along with high-speed Internet access. Depending on the site location, you can work on a Windows or Mac computer. Scanners and CD burners are also available. Detailed information on the sites’ hardware and software is available.

Printing

  • Black-and-white laser printing is available for a charge of $0.08 per printed side.
  • Color printing is available in the Computing Commons and Coor Computing Commons for $1.00 per page side.
  • All students will need to use their Sun Cards to print in the computing sites.
  • For more information on printing in the sites, see the Computing Sites Printing page.

File Storage

There are a variety of ways in which you can store your files while using the computing sites, or from your home computer.

When you are working on a computer, it is best to save files on the local hard drive. This is faster than saving to the AFS space or the floppy drive. When you’re ready to leave, copy the files to your AFS space or a disk.

Note: The storage space on the local hard drive is only temporary. You should never store anything on the desktop, since this space is erased every time a users logs off.

AFS Space

You can save files to your AFS space from any of the Mac or Windows machines in the computing sites. It is recommended that you always save your files first to the local hard drive and then copy them to your AFS space. To access your AFS space from any of the computing sites, go to the Start Menu and select Access Personal Space (M) (or on a Mac select AFS from the Dock). This 5 MB of file space is accessible from any browser, using the AFS Manager. To access the AFS File Manager log into my.asu.edu, click on emma, then AFS Manager.

For more information on AFS at ASU, see the AFS Web site.

CD-R / CD-RW

You can store files on CD-R or CD-RW using workstations available in all computing sites.

Instructor Volumes

Instructor Volumes are data storage spaces for faculty to share documents related to a particular class. Instructors may post software, sample test questions, assignments, or handouts on the Instructor Volumes, so that students can access them from any computing site. For more information, see the Instructor Volume pages.

Accessibility

Information Technology (IT) provides a variety of Adaptive Technology (AT) for student use in the computing sites and classrooms. The Computing Commons 102 site is open longer hours and is centrally located, housing the majority of AT equipment. IT staff will make accommodations for students in any computing site or classroom with the assistance of the Disability Resource Center office as requested. Disability Resource Center can be reached at 480.965.1234. Students requiring AT resources have priority on the designated workstations.

All Windows PCs in the computing sites and classrooms have screen reading and magnification software installed.

Sites supporting equipment checkout are CPCOM 102, COOR 150, GWC 185, ECG 150, and BAC 016. Staff are available for assistance and items can be checked out from the help center counters.

More information can be found at the IT Adaptive Technology Web page.
