October 15, 2003

W. P. Carey MBA: NACDA and ASU to fund scholarship for sports business students

The W. P. Carey School of Business has announced that the National Association of Collegiate Directors of Athletics (NACDA) will provide a $10,000 donation to help fund a scholarship for underrepresented students in the sports business specialization in the W. P. Carey MBA at Arizona State University (ASU). The contribution from NACDA will be matched by $10,000 in scholarship funds raised through the Mike Haynes Golf Classic.

The $20,000 McLendon/Haynes scholarship will be awarded to an underrepresented student enrolled in the W. P. Carey MBA sports business specialization. The selected scholarship recipient will also receive a two-year graduate assistantship with the ASU Athletic Department.

"We are very excited about the opportunity to provide such a substantial award to incoming graduate business students," commented Gene Smith, director of athletics at Arizona State University. "By opening up the door to those who may not otherwise be able to afford the cost of a postgraduate education, ASU is able to draw the best and brightest into our program. We truly appreciate the opportunity to work with NACDA and look forward to a rewarding partnership."

NACDA supports educational opportunities for underrepresented students through the John McLendon Memorial Minority Postgraduate Scholarship Awards, which provide $10,000 to minority senior students who intend to pursue a graduate degree in athletics administration. NACDA has agreed to commit one of these scholarship awards to Arizona State University with the agreement that matching funds will be provided by the school. The NACDA Foundation has awarded more than $8.2 million in postgraduate scholarships since 1982.

"We are pleased to be working with the W. P. Carey School to provide this outstanding scholarship opportunity," said Mike Cleary, executive director of NACDA.

"The recipients of the McLendon Haynes scholarship will have the opportunity to study at the highest ranking MBA program offering a sports business specialization. The agreement between ASU and NACDA sets an example for other schools to follow in their pursuit of top-notch, yet underrepresented students."

For more information about the W P. Carey MBA sports business specialization, please contact Jim Kahler at (480) 965-5533.

About the W.P. Carey School of Business
The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 25th in the nation by U.S. News & World Report, the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
