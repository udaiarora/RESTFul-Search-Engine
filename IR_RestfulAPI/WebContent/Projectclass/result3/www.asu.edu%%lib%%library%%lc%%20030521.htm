Minutes of Meeting


May 21, 2003
Hayden C6A


Meeting was called to order at 3:04 pm.

 

A quorum was present.

 

I. Approval of Minutes (April 23, 2003)

 

Approval of the minutes of the April 23, 2003 Librarians’ Council meeting was postponed until the next Librarians’ Council meeting.

 

II. Chair & Executive Committee's Report—no report

 

III. Dean's Report

 

The Dean congratulated Sue Hunter and Tom Grieves on attaining continuing appointment and welcomed them into the ranks of those with continuing status.

 

The Academic Senate has tabled action on the ACD manual that would have removed references to APs from the manual without replacement of sections addressing APs. Sherrie thanked the senators from the library for their work in this matter.

 

Sherrie has requested that President Crow meet with the librarians, including the librarians from the West and East Campuses and the Law librarians during the Fall 2003.

 

The Dean’s office has gathered material to present to Vice Provost Gail Hackett in support of continuing status for librarians. This information includes a comparison of peer institutions, an article from the literature, and a document containing excerpts from the literature and a bulleted list, “Elements of Professional Librarianship Requiring Academic Freedom,” that frames the issues regarding the need for academic freedom for librarians. Members of Librarians’ Council contributed comments and suggestions.

 

Kurt reported that he, along with the Patty Feldman, Chair of CAPS and Tory Trotta, Director of the Law Library, were asked to meet with Vice Provost Gail Hackett to discuss the composition of the AP task force, which will consider matters related to the category of Academic Professionals.

 

IV. Committee Reports

a. Personnel Committee –Rob Spindler

–no report

b. Review Committee –Marcia Anderson

–no report

c. Research and Professional Development Committee –Lucy Lyons

—no report

d. Compensation Committee - Lois Schneberger

–no report

e. Academic Senate – JoAnn Mulvihill

There was a second reading of Motion 11, which deals with the section of the ACD manual related to promotion and tenure for faculty. There was a first reading of Motion 18 which captures the sections in the current ACD Manual related to Academic Professionals and consolidates these sections into a new section, section 508. Both motions were tabled until fall semester 2003. JoAnn commended the Academic Senate leadership for their work related to the ACD manual and Academic Professionals.

f. Committee on Academic Professional Status – Sue Hunter

CAPS has asked the chair of CAPS to follow up on the request to president Crow for a formal statement regarding academic professionals.

In response to a request by Vice Provost Gail Hackett, CAPS has forwarded a list of names for consideration for selection to serve on a committee that will review the category of Academic Professionals.

g. Nominations and Elections Committee - Dan Stanton

Dan thanked all who participated in the recent elections. The results are as follows:

Chair-Elect: Deborah Koshinsky

Secretary: Brian Doherty

Compensation: Dan Stanton

Personnel: Leping He, Betsy Redman, Brad Vogus

Research & Professional Development: Jennie Duvernay

Review Committee - Continuing Appointment: George Bell, Julie Tharp

Review Committee - Probationary: Smita Parkhe

h. Web Master – Yuwu Song

—no report

V. Old Business—none

 

VI. New Business—none 

 

VII. Announcements

 

The chair extended a warm welcome to Sandy Chen who recently joined the Arizona State University Libraries in Original Cataloging.

 

Meeting adjourned at 4:20.

 

Sheila Young

Secretary
