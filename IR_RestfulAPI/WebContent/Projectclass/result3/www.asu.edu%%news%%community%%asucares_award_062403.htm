ASU Cares program earns community service recognition

The 2003 ASU Cares program has been recognized for its service to the community in the Phoenix area.

The program was the recipient of a special award during the first Corporate Volunteerism Awards program sponsored by The Business Journal.

"The Volunteer Center would like to thank you for Arizona State University's nomination for the first annual awards for excellence in corporate volunteerism," the award letter from the judging committee said. "While the objective of this awards program is to recognize corporations engaged in outstanding volunteer activities, we would like to recognize Arizona State University for its commitment to volunteerism in the community."

ASU Cares also received recognition from the Citrus Olympics sponsored by the Westside Food Bank.

ASU provided more volunteers than any other organization and picked the most fruit during the Citrus Olympics, which was scheduled throughout the spring. The citrus gleaning also benefited the Arcadia neighborhood in Phoenix that needed to get the fruit removed from the trees.

"In all, 738,957 pounds of citrus was gleaned from Arcadia by more than 1,100 people from about 30 different groups," said David J. Seigler, director of the West Side Food Bank. "Donations resulting from the activity were worth about $18,500. Our clients received between five to 10 pounds of fruit.

Seigler also said that fruit from the 2,655 trees picked was distributed across Arizona and three other States

"Our 365 volunteers picked 56,000 pounds of oranges and grapefruit during 2 1/2 hours of work," said Sheila Stokes, coordinator of the ASU Cares program. "ASU's students, faculty and staff care about our community and show it every year by donating their time and the labor to our projects."

The first ASU Cares project was in 2000 at the Tempe School District's Flora Thew Elementary School, where more than 200 ASU students and employees volunteered their time and services to enhance the appearance of the school.

In 2001, more than 300 people donated a day of work at the St. Mary's Food Bank, to paint, landscape and participate in some renovation projects at the west Phoenix site as well as sorting donated food items.

In 2002, more than 300 volunteers painted, landscaped, created murals and sorted donations at the Thomas J. Pappas School in Phoenix.
