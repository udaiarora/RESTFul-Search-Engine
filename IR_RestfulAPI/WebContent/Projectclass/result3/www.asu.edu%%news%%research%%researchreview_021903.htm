February 19, 2003

KAET presents 'ASU Research Review,' Feb. 19

Laurie Leshin, associate professor of geological sciences and director of the ASU Center for Meteorite Studies, is leading a team of scientists to design a proposal for SCIM (Sample Collection for Investigation of Mars), the first Mars mission that will gather Martian dust and atmosphere and return the samples to Earth. The proposal has been selected by NASA as one of four finalists for the Mars Scout mission.

Leshin's story will be included in KAET's "ASU Research Review," which airs at 8 p.m., Feb. 19, and 11:30 p.m. and 5 p.m., Feb. 23 on Channel 8.

Other featured profiles include:

. Kathleen S. Matt, professor of neuroendocrinology, the associate director of the Arizona Biodesign Institute and the director of the Exercise & Sports Research Institute, studies stress in order to understand its role in disease development. Her research is exploring the effects of physical and psychological stress on humans and animals, including how males and females respond to stress differently.

. Jiping He is a professor of bioengineering and the director of the IGERT (Integrative Graduate Education in Research and Training) Program that emphasizes neural and musculoskeletal adaptation in form and function. He is working with Dr. Richard Herman at Good Samaritan Regional Medical Center to help victims of spinal cord injuries to walk again. They have made medical history by implanting an electrical stimulation device on the spinal cord of a quadriplegic patient and "teaching" the patient to walk again. With help from the implanted device, the patient can now walk more than 1,000 feet. Similar success has also been achieved with a second spinal cord injury victim and there are plans for a third patient. He is now leading a group of researchers in the newly established Arizona Biodesign Institute for more advanced therapeutic approaches to treat neurological trauma or diseases.

For information about KAET-TV/Channel 8, visit the Web at (www.kaet.asu.edu).
