Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)
“Who Are Academic Professionals and What Do They Do?”

Who Are Academic Professionals?
"Academic professionals, including librarians and researchers, are nonclassified employees who are involved with research or teaching programs, have advanced degrees and/or specialized training, require professional and intellectual freedom, and report to a person below the level of a senior vice president and provost or vice president." (from ACD 505-03)
Academic professionals are involved integrally in the research and instructional components of the University's mission and are collaborators with the faculty in the University's educational process.

Academic professionals are eligible for stability of employment (continuing status) after a multiple year probationary period. In order to gain this status they must excel in the performance of their professional responsibilities, demonstrate excellence through their professional development, and participate in service to their profession, the University and the community that surrounds them.

What Do Academic Professionals Do?

Academic professionals are a diverse group with a variety of responsibilities and job titles. In addition to the specific activities associated with the position descriptions listed below, academic professionals may also conduct research, teach formal classes, give seminars, and offer brief courses as well as mentor, advise, and direct the research of undergraduate and graduate students.

Academic professionals often seek funding from the private sector and governmental agencies to support research programs or purchase research equipment. A major responsibility of many academic professionals is the instruction of students in the use of University resources required in their educational pursuits.

  • Librarians, Archivists, Learning Resource Specialists and Museum Professionals are responsible for the University collections of instructional and research material. They acquire, organize, manage, preserve, and make accessible library materials, historical documents, instructional media and equipment, and museum collections, respectively.
  • Research Engineers and Research Scientists hold doctoral degrees and work independently or collaboratively with faculty, students and post-doctoral researchers on significant research projects. Research Professionals, Research Technologists and Research Administrators support the University research mission through their operation and management of significant research equipment or research facilities.
  • Instructional Professionals teach and mentor students for the majority of their responsibilities. Clinical Professionals instruct and supervise students working through clinical practice. Fine Arts Specialists collaborate with student and faculty artists and designers, enabling them to realize their visions. Administrative Professionals have significant supervisory and managerial responsibilities for University programs.

Document Dated: May 29, 1997
