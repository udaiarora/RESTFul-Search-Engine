ASU offers children many artistic options this fall

As parents start sending their children back to school, it's an ideal time to start thinking about weekend activities to keep little ones entertained and stimulated this fall.

Whether it's drama, dance or art, ASU offers a wide variety of artistic classes designed to promote confidence and inspire creativity. There are a variety of offerings for toddlers as young as 18 months and school-aged kids as old as 15.
See the course listing below for specific time, date, prices and class descriptions.

Imagination Corporation
Saturday, Sept. 27 through Nov. 1, 9 - 10 a.m. for ages 5-7 or 10:15 - 11:15 a.m. for ages 8-11. Cost is $45 per child for six sessions. Information: (480) 727-6114
Drama classes for children tap into their natural instincts of pretend play to develop communication skills, creativity, self-esteem, and to help solve real-life problems.

The Imagination Corporation is not geared to teach a child to act on stage, but these classes are helpful to children should they have a desire to perform as they grow older.

Discover Dance
Saturday, Sept. 27 through Nov. 1, 9 - 10 a.m. for ages 8 - 11 or 10:15 - 11:15 a.m. for ages 5 - 7. Cost is $45 per child for six sessions. Information: (480) 965-0412.

Discover Dance offers a unique approach to dance called Creative Dance. Rather than focusing on a particular kind of dance, Creative Dance lays a foundation for understanding, moving and choreographing in a variety of dance styles. Because the emphasis is placed on individual interpretation and response, children create and perform their own distinctive dances. Previous dance experience is welcomed but not required.

Children's Art Workshop
Saturday, Sept. 13 through Nov. 15, 9 - 10:30 a.m. or 11 a.m. - 12:30 p.m. for ages 5 - 15. Tuition of $35 covers materials for all 10 sessions. Information: (480) 965-6981.

The art education program at ASU offers classes in the visual arts that encourage creativity and personal expression, provide instruction in the use of materials and techniques for rendering images, and introduce children to art history and culture.

For children ages 5 through 9, there will be instruction in more than one process: drawing, painting, ceramics, sculpture and printmaking; and for children ages 10 to 15 there will be more in-depth instruction in one or more of these processes. Each process will be related to the art work of an artist, culture, style or time period.

Music for Tots
Saturday, Aug. 25 through Dec. 18, tuition for Growing With Music for ages 3 - 4 or 4 - 6 1/2 years is $212; Music for Tots for ages 1 - 2 is $295. Also offered is Music for Tiny Tots for ages 18 months - 3 years and their parents/caregivers. Late registration for all programs will be accepted. Classes take place at Mitchell Park School, 900 S. Mitchell, Tempe. Information: (480) 517-9765.

The School of Music offers Music for Tots using the Kindermusik series. The series offers classes by three age groupings with age appropriate home materials, 15-week sessions and a class size of eight children. Music is for every child but not every child is expected to be a musician. The purpose is to allow the whole child to develop through the introduction of musical concepts that will enhance the child's daily life by focusing on beat, listening skills, singing, speech patterns, purposeful movement, imagination and social skills.

Zajac, with ASU Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
