The Format Manual: Online Updates


The following changes to the online Format Manual are effective February 1, 2007.

In December 2006, UMI/ProQuest announced its acquisition by Cambridge Publications. Because of this buyout, UMI/ProQuest required the Division of Graduate Studies to make substantial changes to the Format Manual. The new requirements take immediate effect. Below appear the revised requirements along with the pages in the current manual of the requirements they replace.

SUBMITTING YOUR THESIS/DISSERTATION TO THE BOOKSTORE FOR BINDING (Format Manual Page 9)

Two bound copies of your document are required by the university; please check with your graduate support staff person to find out if you are required to have a copy bound for your department. Print and/or copy the number of theses/dissertations you will need to have bound on 24 lb.-minimum, acid-free paper. The Bookstore will not accept your copies without a Division of Graduate Studies dean?s signature on your pass/fail form.

FONT AND SIZE OF TEXT (Format Manual Page 16)

The Division of Graduate Studies requires that students use one of the following fonts in 10, 11, or 12 points when composing their documents: Arial, Century, Garamond, Georgia, Lucida Bright (10pt only), Sans Serif, Tahoma, Times New Roman, Trebuchet MS (10 pt only), or Verdana.

You should retain the same font and font size throughout your document. However, you may deviate from this requirement if your style guide indicates the need for differing font and font size.

APPROVAL PAGE (Format Manual Page 23-27)

This page is an official record of your committee?s approval and the Division of Graduate Studies? acceptance of your thesis or dissertation. Your title on this page should match the one on your title page exactly in terms of wording and punctuation; if you change one title, please make the other title consistent. If your title changes based on committee recommendation after your defense, you will need to resubmit revised title and approval pages for an additional format review, even if you already have final format approval, before the dean?s signature can be obtained.

To create a correctly formatted approval page, reproduce precisely the sample in Figure 3, which indicates the proper spacing and arrangement and refer to the general directions to create this page.

General Directions for the Approval Page:

  1. Using the same font and size as the title page, type the title in ALL CAPITAL LETTERS and double-spaced, centered between left and right margins with the exact wording and punctuation as the title page.

  2. On a new line (double-spaced and centered), type ?by? in lowercase letters.

  3. On a new line (double-spaced and centered), type your full name as it appears on your transcript as listed with the Registrar?s Office in title-cased letters.

  4. Add two double-spaced lines (four single-spaced lines) after your name and type ?has been approved? in all lowercase letters and hit return to add one double space.

  5. Type the month and year of your oral defense (not graduation) formatted as follows:
  • Month (title-cased).
  • No comma between month and year.
  1. Type ?Graduate Supervisory Committee? followed by a colon (:) and hit enter.

  2. Add one double-spaced line.

  3. Add the Graduate Supervisory Committee members? names as follows:
  • If you have two chairs, type ?Co-Chair? (title-cased) on the first and second line.
  • If you have one chair, type the chair?s name, comma (,), one space, then ?Chair? (not ?Chairman? or ?Chairperson?).

  1. Type ?ACCEPTED BY THE DIVISION OF GRADUATE STUDIES? in all capitals, beginning at center (at 4.5 inches) between margins.

  2. Ensure the font and size for all text on the page are consistent and match the title page.

  3. Double check that your approval page looks like the sample (Figure 3).
