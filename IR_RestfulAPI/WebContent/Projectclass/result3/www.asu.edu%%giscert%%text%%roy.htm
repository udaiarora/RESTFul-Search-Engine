Shouraseni Sen Roy

Analysis of the spatial patterns in the diurnal character of the winter precipitation in Arizona using surface interpolation techniques and DEMs

ABSTRACT

In Arizona, a substantial amount of precipitation is received during the winters characterized by showers in the low-lying areas and snowfall in the highland areas.

In the present study the hourly precipitation data from 1987 to 2003 have been analyzed for twenty-one weather stations spread across the state of Arizona limited to a region south of Flagstaff.

The analysis consists of two sections. The first section is mainly the statistical analysis of the hourly precipitation data using harmonic analysis. Harmonic analysis has been used in several earlier studies to find out the time of maximum for the precipitation occurring over a region. The second part consisted of the study consists of the use of spatial interpolation techniques such as kriging and spline to find out the spatial patterns. The final analysis is done by overlaying the krigged maps on a DEM of Arizona to find the effect of elevation on the resulting patterns.
