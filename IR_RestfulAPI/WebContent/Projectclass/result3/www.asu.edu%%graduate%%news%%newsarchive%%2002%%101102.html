Dennis Durband

October 11 , 2002


ARIZONA STATE UNIVERSITY GRADUATE STUDENT IS TOP ENDOWED P.E.O. SCHOLAR IN U.S., CANADA

Christine Mari Yoshikawa, a piano performance doctoral candidate and faculty associate of piano at Arizona State University, has been chosen the top endowed award winner among 85 Philanthropical Educational Organization (P.E.O.) Scholars for 2002. She received the Mary Louise Remy Endowed P.E.O. Scholar Award. Just six of the 85 scholars — selected earlier this year from 6,000 nominees — were declared endowed scholars.

As an endowed scholar, Yoshikawa will perform at the P.E.O State Convention in May, and is likely to perform at the P.E.O. International Convention in Oklahoma City next year. Yoshikawa has performed on several continents, is preparing for fall performances in South America and the United Kingdom, and is also preparing to record an album. She was the second-prize winner this summer at the 2002 Jacob Flier International Piano Competition in New York.

The P.E.O. Scholar Award provides $8,000 prizes to American and Canadian women engaged in advanced study and research. There are nearly 6,000 chapters in the U.S. and Canada with more than 250,000 active members. ASU doctoral students Jennifer Rytting and Kristin Pruden also received the P.E.O. Scholar award earlier this year.

Professor Robert Hamilton, as Steinway Artist and professor of piano at ASU, is Yoshikawa's academic adviser. He said, "Christine Yoshikawa is brilliant both as a concert performer on the piano and as a scholar. Seldom do we see a doctoral candidate take the initiative in establishing a concert career to the extent Christine has. Her accomplishments and busy concert schedule continue to grow and amaze. She is an exemplar in each of the many tasks she accepts, and is among
the most promising students I have taught in 35 years."
