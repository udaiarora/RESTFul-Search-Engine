May 8, 2003

ASU project wins 2003 Phoenix Mayor's Partnership Award

An ASU project that educates both children and their parents in an inner city Phoenix neighborhood has won a 2003 Mayor's Partnership Award from the city of Phoenix. The "Let's Go to School Together" project at Sunland School, part of ASU's Academic Community Engagement Services (ACES), received the award at a May 1 ceremony at the Phoenix Civic Center.

The project includes after-school tutoring and science activities for children and support groups and adult literacy classes for their parents. A social worker assists families with counseling and needs such as food. Participants can earn a 35-pound box of food each week from the United Food Bank for perfect attendance.

"Our goal is to facilitate enhanced academic, social and life skills for both children and parents through tutoring, social skills development, science internships, ESL and GED classes and parenting workshops," said Carmela Zega, ACES assistant director, in her nomination for the award.

The project also tries to increase participants' perception about the value and purpose of education, increase school attendance and parent involvement and develop higher expectations about college attendance.

Phoenix supplies a school-based social worker, the Arizona Community Foundation and American Express fund adult education instruction and the DeMuro Family Fund provides student support. Other partners include AmeriCorps VISTA and Sunland School.

The progress of one sixth-grade class of participants is being tracked until they graduate from high school in 2007. Results from the first two years show a 34 percent increase in reading scores, a 36 percent increase in math, a 12 percent rise in language, a 97 percent promotion rate and an 11 percent reduction in discipline referrals.

"So many lives have been so positively affected by this complex and many-faceted project," said Gay Brack, ACES founding director. "Very few projects could better demonstrate just how effectively ASU is embedded in our community."

ACES received the award in the leadership category. This is the second year in a row that ACES has won a Mayor's award.
