April 7, 2003

ASU Collegiate Job Fair connects employers with job-seekers

About 2,000 students and alumni from colleges throughout the state are expected to come to ASU Main from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m., April 12, for the 10th annual AZ Collegiate Job Fair. The event is in the Wells Fargo Arena and is free for job-seekers.

Students or graduates from any four-year school may attend. They can connect with at least 100 employers in the span of six hours, making contacts for jobs, internships and future employment.

For the first time this year, ASU Career Services is also sponsoring a golf tournament to raise money for scholarships the following day, April 13, at Karsten Golf Course. The scholarships will be need-based, for students encountering critical financial situations during the academic year.

"Many employers will conduct interviews and make job offers, while others will collect resumes to pre-screen candidates," says Elaine Stover, associate director of Career Services. "We're seeing more small employers, government and nonprofit agencies and companies from the service sector.

"That's where the opportunities are, in smaller firms. In a small company you can wear more hats, learn a lot of different skills, and become more marketable later. People who can succeed in a small organization not only get good experience, but learn more about what type of work they want to pursue."

Retail and restaurant companies are looking for managers, and city police departments are looking for officers and dispatchers. The FBI is recruiting accountants, computer scientists and people who can speak foreign languages. For those with will power, Godiva Chocolatier is seeking store managers.

"I have hired quite a few ASU graduates," says Janet Woo, senior recruiter for Panda Restaurant Group who has attended the job fair for several years. "They do well, because they are knowledgeable and hardworking. We have seven ASU graduates in management right now, and I hope to find at least two more, because of the growth of our company."

Students and alumni in any field are encouraged to attend the event, network and find out what companies are looking for, Stover says. Participants should dress professionally and bring multiple resume copies.

A list of participating employers and their opportunities is posted on the Web, at (www.asu.edu/career) under upcoming events. The list is updated daily. For more information on the job fair, call (480) 965-2350. For information on the golf tournament, call (480) 965-5125.

Auffret, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-6991 or (sauffret@asu.edu).
