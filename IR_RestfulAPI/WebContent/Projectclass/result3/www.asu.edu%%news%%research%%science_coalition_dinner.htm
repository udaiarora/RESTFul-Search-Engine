April 8, 2005

Crow, university chiefs discuss science issues with media

ASU President Michael Crow was the moderator at the Science Coalition’s sixth annual media roundtable, held April 4 at the Penn Club of New York.

Eight university presidents and chancellors from public and private institutions were on hand at the event, including: Karen Holbrook of Ohio State University, who co-hosted the roundtable with Crow; Don Randle from the University of Chicago; Dennis Berkey of the Worcester Polytechnic Institute; Shirley Kenny from SUNY Stony Brook; Larry Faulkner of the University of Texas; Lee Todd from the University of Kentucky; and James Wagner of Emory University.

Two dozen members of the national media also attended the discussion, among them: Catherine Arnst, Business Week; Sam Dillon, New York Times; Andy Fisher, CNBC; Robert Bazell, NBC; Alexis Gelber, Newsweek; Scott Jaschik, Inside Higher Ed; Jeff Brainerd and Goldie Blumenstyk, Chronicle of Higher Education; John Rennie, Scientific American; Corey Powell, Discover magazine; Sharon Begley, Wall Street Journal; Ron Scherer, Christian Science Monitor; Stephen Cass, IEEE Spectrum; Liz Willen, Bloomberg; Gabe Pressman, WNBC; Marcella Bombardieri, Boston Globe; Ira Flatow, National Public Radio; and Max Jarmon, Arizona Republic.

Special guest Craig Barrett, president and chief executive officer of Intel, also addressed the group, which focused its discussion on how the United States can hope to maintain its global competitive advantage if it does not invest significantly more in science programs. Other policy issues for science and higher education also were discussed.
