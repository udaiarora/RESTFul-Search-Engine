March 2, 2005

ASU Cares sets sights on Phoenix’s Grant Park

When residents of the Grant Park neighborhood in Phoenix look out their windows on the morning of March 5, they will experience the same sensation that the children at Sunshine Acres had last year: Waves and waves of ASU students, faculty, staff and alumni converging on their neighborhood, rakes and other tools in hand.

The Grant Park neighborhood, just south of where ASU’s Downtown Campus will be located, now is home to many elderly and low-income people.

Bounded by Grant Street and Buckeye Road on the north and south and Central and 7th Avenues on the east and west, Grant Park is one of Phoenix ’s older neighborhoods.

As their first neighborly act, the ASU community will spend four hours on March 5 helping revitalize the older homes and gardens.

Last year, more than 700 ASU volunteers showed up to work on Sunshine Acres. This year, coordinator Sheila Stokes expects more than 800.

ASU Cares, now in its sixth year, is the university’s largest volunteer community service project.

Each year, volunteers receive an ASU Cares T-shirt and a pizza lunch, and Luis Flores, a University Services Plumbing Shop employee, serves as disc jockey to create a party-like atmosphere for the day of work.

But few volunteers consider the event a day of work. It’s a chance to help someone else.

For many, participating in ASU Cares – and its predecessor, Christmas in April – has become a yearly commitment.

“I think you should be giving back to the community that gives to ASU,” says Polly Pinney, associate director of Facilities Management, who volunteers along with many of her staff. “It feels good to be a part of it.”

Cookie Ellis, who does “a little bit of everything” at ASU Cares each year, makes sure that everything is working and that “all the pieces are fitting together.”

Even ASU President Michael Crow pitches in. Pinney noted that some years ago, when some volunteers on the project were harvesting citrus, Crow “showed up to work – and proceeded to do so.”

To register for ASU Cares, go to the Web site (www.asu.edu/asucares). Volunteers will receive electronic information of their registration, along with a link to a map and directions to the event.

Volunteers should bring their ASU Sun Cards on March 5, and a signed copy of the liability waiver form located on the registration site.

Those who cannot volunteer on March 5 still can contribute to ASU Cares by buying infant or adult disposable diapers for the Valley of the Sun Community Diaper Bank.

Drop off diapers at Administration Building A-207 in Tempe, Administration Building reception desk at East campus, or the Faculty/Administration Building lobby on West campus.

For more information, send an e-mail to (asucares@asu.edu).

Smith, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 965-4821 or (jps@asu.edu).
