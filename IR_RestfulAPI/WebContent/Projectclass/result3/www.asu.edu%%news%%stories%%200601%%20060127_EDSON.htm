January 27, 2006

Edson Initiative funds student business efforts

Eighteen ASU student-led ventures will be launched this year as part of the second annual Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative selection process. Student teams competed for award money to explore their innovative ideas for business products and services or social good. Submissions came from 86 teams across ASU.

The initiative, made possible by a $5.4 million donation in October 2004 from Orin Edson to the ASU Foundation, supports student led ventures at ASU. Awards totaling $200,000 are given out annually, including up to $20,000 in seed funding, office space at the Brickyard in downtown Tempe, and training and networking opportunities with ASU faculty, researchers and entrepreneurs.

The Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative will expand operations with a presence at the newly opened ASU Scottsdale Innovation Center, which aims to become a force and focal point for excellence in technological innovation, cross-disciplinary collaborations and entrepreneurial ventures.

"It is an honor to congratulate the student awardees of the 2006 Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative," says ASU President Michael M. Crow. "They represent the very best of ASU's competitive spirit, entrepreneurial dynamism and creative innovation."

Team members of the 18 Edson ventures come from across the university, including the Polytechnic campus, West campus, W.P. Carey School of Business, Ira A. Fulton School of Engineering, Herberger College of Fine Arts, College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, University College, College of Public Programs, College of Education, College of Design and Barrett Honors College.

The 18 Edson venture awardees for 2006 are:

. Angels With Attitude - An education-based company dedicated to stopping violence and sexual assault against women through professional speaking presentations and role-playing self-defense training.

. The Brightest Crayon - A nonprofit organization that provides relief and gift time to caretakers and socialization opportunities for local youth with special needs through respite care social activities.

. FITTmaxx - Develops and markets a line of straightforward, convenient and affordable exercise programs to elicit greater physical activity adherence.

. Grid Records LLC - Commercially develops local hip-hop music and culture with strategic plans to globally brand the Phoenix metropolitan area as the next prospering region of the music industry.

. Hydro Hat - Manufactures and markets a soft, fabric headwear that uses water-absorbing crystals to reduce body temperature, acting as a personal evaporative cooler.

. I&E Electronics - Develops and markets wireless technology solutions using the latest design theory to access and transfer crucial energy data efficiently and economically.

. Ideal Capacity Plasmid Preparation Machine - Develops and markets a machine to efficiently and automatically produce high-yield, pure samples of plasmid for biotechnology research and development.

. Integral Science Supply - Produces and markets specially designed, prefabricated "science learning/exploration kits" that offer an innovative new interdisciplinary approach for teaching science to elementary and high school students.

. Integral Shelter - A nonprofit organization dedicated to developing sustainable solutions for inadequate living conditions in poverty-stricken areas throughout the world.

. Max Power Motors - Develops and markets a light, compact, portable, energy-efficient and permanent magnetic electric motor for bicycle transportation.

. Mobile Powered - An event-based service that provides user interaction and feedback for large groups via cell phone interface and specialized software.

. Onto IP - Develops and markets intellectual property analysis software.

. Organic: Inside & Out - Develops and produces an innovative television show and companion Web site that inspires viewers to embrace sustainable green living through daily lifestyle choices and home improvement projects.

. Project Literacy - A nonprofit organization that promotes and develops reading fluency among local disadvantaged elementary school children through curricula and student tutoring delivered weekly by ASU volunteers. This model has proven successful in Guadalupe schools.

. Strategic Engineering Group - Manufactures and markets a line of cost-effective products for people with disabilities, enabling greater access and mobility

. Three Space Technologies - Develops and markets an alternative to using a computer mouse.

. Visudi/Sufficiently Advanced Technologies - Researches and develops an environmentally friendly robust water purification and fluid-processing solution.

. Virtue Touch - Develops and markets software solutions for the restaurant industry.

In addition to the 18 newly awarded ventures, eight teams from the Edson 2005 portfolio were granted second-year funds and space in the Edson facility. They are: BioEnginuity; Community Outreach and Advocacy for Refugee; ETEKA LLC; Flowchips; Halo Cups Inc.; MedFrame; Off-Road Direct; and Youth Re:Action Corps.

"The ventures of our Edson students are high-caliber and real," says Terree Parlett Wasley, director of ASU's Entrepreneurial Initiatives. "Through mentoring by ASU Technopolis' seasoned entrepreneurs, these ventures will further excel and move closer to market."

Teams of students started organizing in the early fall after attending an Edson Initiative training session. Teams had to submit brief proposals describing the business ideas, marketing strategies and venture budgets. Select ventures provided presentations to a panel of judges.

A panel of private-sector entrepreneurs and service providers, plus university administrators, judged the submissions based on business concept, marketing strategy, team qualifications, proposed budget, and potential for financial return or social impact.

The Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative is a program administered by ASU's Office of Economic Affairs within the Office of the Vice President for Research and Economic Affairs. For further information about the Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative, visit (www.studentventures.asu.edu).
