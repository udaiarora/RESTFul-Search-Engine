Assessment of the Internal Environment:
Strengths and Challenges

During the fall of 1995, all library staff were invited to participate in an assessment of the internal environment of the library through town meetings, a survey, and a special session of the Strategic Planning Council. Both strengths and areas for improvement were examined.

There were many strengths noted in the assessment (please also see the section below on organizational values, which highlights many organizational strengths). Knowledgeable, capable, productive, and committed staff are strongly service-oriented. Several positive moves to improve services are already in place. The Libraries' Administration has a clear vision. Current technology, while not at the level desired, is used expertly. Collections are strong in many areas, with some distinction in special collections. The library has a strong commitment to staff development. A special strength was noted in relation to the strategic planning process, which has established a means for examining the organization critically and designing plans for improvement.

At the same time, several areas were identified for improvement. Decision-making processes are sometimes slow and lack adequate and appropriate input. Communication--top-down, bottom-up, intra- and inter-departmental--is not always as effective as it might be. Teamwork is still emerging as an organizational mode; especially at the inter-unit level, competing interests sometimes undermine collaborative efforts to solve problems. In the area of technology, we don't have all we need because we cannot afford it; we don't always fully exploit the technology we have. Training and technical assistance in the area of technology are not always satisfactory. There are stresses associated with reduced resources, including staff reductions. Reward and incentive systems need to be rethought in relation to the emergence of teams, shifting job responsibilities.
