Music Library


The Music Library is located on the third floor of the School of Music. The Music Library houses Arizona State University?s principal music collections. Holdings include books on music, printed music, recorded music, and videos in a variety of formats, journals, and music related microforms. There are listening facilities, computers, and wireless internet access within the library. Materials may be used within the library by anyone and circulate to ASU and community borrowers.



What?s New at the Music Library


Our CDs have moved! Over the Winter Break, we set up new shelving and moved the CD collection to the East side of the library, near the SLPs. This will allow us more room to spread out in additional recordings and books!


Finding the Music in Government Information is our new exhibit running from January 16-March 19, 2007.  Discover the music that can be found in Government Documents!


Dr. Christopher Mehrens will join the ASU Libraries on March 1, 2007 as the new Head of the Music Library.  Christopher currently works at Indiana State University-Terra Haute, Cunningham Memorial Library.  We are very excited to have him join our team!



Spotlight


It’s the beginning of a New Year as well as a new semester! To celebrate, we’d like to draw your attention to a few library services that may interest you:


Request Materials: Tips on how to obtain materials from other ASU campuses, as well as other libraries!
