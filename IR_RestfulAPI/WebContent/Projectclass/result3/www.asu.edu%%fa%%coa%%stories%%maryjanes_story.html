Mary Jane's Story

Mary Jane is a nontraditional undergraduate student who has provided foster care for animals for the past 6 years. Her animal shelter is affectionately named "Fur Love FurEver." She currently cares for 4 dogs, one wolf and 7 cats!

Mary Jane transferred from a local community college and plans to eventually receive professional degrees that would allow her to work as a High School Counselor and Teacher.

When asked about the costs of her education, she replied, " I had to make a choice. Doing without a little today will pay off in the long run. I choose to lower my expenses by not buying new clothes, not having cable and clipping coupons. I remind myself what I am working for and try to keep my choices in perspective. And, I know that research shows that a college educated person makes more than a non-educated person."

Mary Jane advises other students to:

  • Apply for absolutely everything you think you might qualify for and be aware of important deadlines like the FAFSA and the General Scholarship Application
  • Obtain employment on campus if possible because it provides professional experience and is convenient
  • Realize that what you are doing right now will affect your future
  • Avoid credit card debt

How Mary Jane financed her 2005-06 academic year.
