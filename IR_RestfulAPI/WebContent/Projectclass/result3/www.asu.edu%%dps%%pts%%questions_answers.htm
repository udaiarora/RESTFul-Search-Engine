Why are parking rates being increased?

Parking and Transit Services (PTS) is an auxiliary business service of ASU and funds its own expenses, including parking structure debt service. This means all services that PTS provides are paid for by revenue generated by the sale of parking decals, visitor parking and citation revenue. As the Tempe campus continues to develop, more and more surface parking spaces will be lost to academic, research and residential buildings. The only way to financially address parking and transportation issues, as the parking inventory is reduced, is through a parking rate increase. Current parking rates simply can no longer support the many needs of the ASU Tempe campus.

Whose decision was it to raise parking rates?

The Parking and Transit Task Force recommended the new rates, which will fund all the improvements and new programs described on the Changes and Improvements Ahead and Planning for the Future web pages. The recommendations were then approved by university administration.

Why is ASU going to a tiered parking rate system?

In order to fund the necessary improvements and changes identified by the Parking and Transit Task Force, ASU parking rates were raised to market value. Rates were compared to other Pac-10 and ABOR approved peer universities, as well as local and regional business rates. However, the task force wanted to provide faculty, staff and students a menu of prices to chose from. Therefore, a tiered parking rate system was developed based on the historical demand of each parking location. Parking rates for 2006-07 range from $150-$540. The end result is a parking rate system that provides options for everyone while also generating adequate revenue to sustain a comprehensive program.

If I have a PS-1, PS-3, ASU Fulton Center, Brickyard or Disabled decal, why do I have to pay a higher rate?

Parking Structures 1, 3, Brickyard and the ASU Fulton Center parking structure are currently the highest demanded parking locations on the Tempe campus. Currently, parking supply can not meet the demand for spaces in these areas. Therefore, the Parking and Transit Task Force concluded that the price for a parking decal in PS-1, PS-3, Brickyard and the Fulton Center parking structure should reflect the convenience and current demand of each space. The new rates for these locations were determined by the current market rate for structure parking on a metropolitan campus like ASU, and the historical demand of each parking decal.

An ASU Disabled All-Access decal allows the decal holder to park in any ASU disabled parking space on the Tempe campus. Since the decal holder is granted access to many parking locations, the rate of the decal must coincide with the rest of the decal rates.

Why are you raising the parking rates so much? I can’t afford it.

For year's ASU has charged below market rates for parking on the Tempe campus. The Campus Master Plan calls for a 25% reduction in current parking on the Tempe campus. As parking spaces continue to be lost to the development of research, academic and residential buildings, the discounted rates could no longer be sustained, let alone support the increased needs of faculty, staff and students. The only way to fund necessary parking and transportation improvements and programs is by raising parking rates to their market value.

The Parking and Transit Task Force concluded that a tiered parking rate system would provide financial options for everyone. Parking rates for 2006-07 range from $150-$540, which are quite reasonable when examined more closely.

What other options do I have?

Approximately 15,000 students and 2,400 faculty and staff live within a five mile radius of the Tempe campus. Living close to campus has many benefits as alternative transportation modes are very effective when your destination is within five miles. There are many transportation options for you to choose from… the choice is yours.

Carpooling - PTS has launched a free ridematching service dedicated to ASU. Powered by Alternet Rides, the ASU Rideshare Service provides ASU faculty, staff and students the ability to locate others who want to carpool to work, class, events and more. Create an account today and start saving money on the cost of parking, gas, vehicle maintenance, insurance and more.

Carpoolers can apply for a free ASU carpool permit which grants access to reserved carpooling stalls in parking structures and surface lots. An ASU parking decal is required before applying for a carpool permit. Click here for more information on obtaining a carpool permit.

Biking – Approximately 15,000 students and 2,400 faculty and staff live within a five mile radius of the ASU Tempe campus. Tempe is a nationally recognized bicycle friendly community, earning Silver-Level status from the League of American Bicyclists in 2005. There are several Tempe roadways that have designated bike lanes and more work is on the way to make them even more bicycle friendly. For more information on biking in Tempe visit http://www.tempe.gov/bikeprogram/.

To help address bike theft on the ASU Tempe campus, bike corrals will be built that feature security technology so that entry and exit of the corral will be monitored. Site locations of the bike corrals will be determined in coordination with the ASU Architect..

Tempe Bike Week is April 15 – 23, so dust off that bike and give it a try. If your bike is in need of some minor repairs hand it over to the great staff at the ASU Bike Co-op. Also, if you don't have a bike and would like to cruise around the Tempe campus in an awesome Phat Sea Breeze bike for FREE visit the USG Community Bikes in the SRC.

Parking and Transit Services is also currently offering for sale high-quality bicycle helmets and headlights at 50 percent off the retail price as part of their year-long “Helmets and Headlights” safety campaign. To purchase both the bicycle helmet and headlight (complete with tail light and batteries) is $25. Helmets are sold separately for $15 and the headlight/tail light for $20.

The “Helmets and Headlights” campaign is part of a nation-wide effort to prevent bicycle injuries. According to recent statistics, 85 percent of all injuries from bicycle accidents could be reduced by simply wearing a helmet. Also, 10 percent of all bicycle ridership is at night, yet evening accidents comprise 50 percent of all bicycle accidents.

To take advantage of this offer, visit the PTS Decal Sales office Monday through Friday, 7:30.m. - 4:30 p.m.

How will I get to class on time if I have to park so far away?

To improve frequency and accommodate peak boarding times, the FLASH Forward shuttle service will be improved by the addition of one bus and service hours will be extended to midnight.

Also, to help alleviate demand from the FLASH shuttles and provide another on campus transit option, ASU will launch a dedicated McAllister shuttle beginning Fall 2006. The McAllister shuttle will service Lot 59/59N and the south campus via McAllister Avenue. Service hours will be from 7 a.m. to 10 p.m., Monday through Friday.

If ASU is losing parking spaces, why not just build more parking structures?

Parking structures are expensive to build and maintain. The average parking structure (above ground) costs approximately $14,000 per space to build. It is only logical that ASU invest in fiscally responsible transportation alternatives while also investigating other parking and transit arrangements for the future.

Will ASU Polytechnic and West experience the same rate increases?

All of ASU campuses will experience parking rate increases. However, the increases on each campus will differ based on the needs of each specific campus.

For more information on ASU Polytechnic rates visit http://www.poly.asu.edu/admin/pts/

For more information on ASU West rates visit http://westcgi.west.asu.edu/adaff/auxs/parking

Parking rates for the ASU Downtown Phoenix campus (including the DTC and Mercado) will mirror the rates at the ASU Tempe campus. More information on parking locations and rates can be found at: http://www.asu.edu/downtownphoenix/

If I purchase a parking decal for another campus (Downtown Phoenix, Polytechnic or West) can I park on the Tempe campus?

Yes, but only if the decal you purchased is at least the value of $150, which is the lowest value of any Tempe campus decal. This reciprocal agreement has been made with ASU Downtown Phoenix, ASU Polytechnic and ASU West. At the ASU Tempe campus, all decals from ASU Downtown Phoenix, Polytechnic and West campus that are at least in value of $150 will be valid in Lot 59E. After 4 p.m. any decals from any ASU campus may cross park in designated areas.

Reciprocal parking locations (decal must be at least $150 in value):

  • ASU Tempe: Lot 59E
  • ASU Downtown Phoenix: perimeter locations
  • ASU Polytechnic: uncovered parking areas
  • ASU West: student parking lots
