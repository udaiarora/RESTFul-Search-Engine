Self Guided Tour of the Gallery of Design
8:00 a.m. - 5:00 p.m.
1st Floor, College of Design, South Building
Unintended Consequences Exhibit
The exhibit explores the role that regulations, restrictions, laws, zoning, codes, and other enforceable mandates have on our surroundings. Contributors to the exhibition include college faculty and PhD students and members of the Phoenix community who seek to suggest creative alternatives and explore the value of design thinking on the physical, social, and cultural landscape. Unintended Consequences is a companion exhibition with the ASU Art Museum's New American City exhibition.
Sneaker Tour of ASU's Tempe Campus
9:00 a.m-10:30 a.m.
Meet at 8:45 a.m. at Old Main by the steps and fountain
This sneaker tour offers limited walking and will include a visit to:
  • The Herbarium, Vascular Plant, School of Life Sciences
    Reminiscent of the natural history collections of Darwin and other 19th Century naturalists, the ASU Vascular Plant Herbarium contains more than 260,000 plant specimens, about 40 percent from Arizona. The ASU Natural History Collections contains more than just vascular plants - trees, shrubs and herbs. There are also collections of paleo-botanical fossils, one of the largest lichen collections in the country, and various kinds of zoological specimens including hundreds of thousands of insects, shells, snakes and fish.
  • ASU Glassblowing Facility, Chemistry and Biochemistry Department - College of Liberal Arts and Sciences
    The Glassblowing Facility provides highly specialized support for teaching and research operations within the Chemistry & Biochemistry Department as well as the entire university. Two full time professionals design, construct and maintain sophisticated glass apparatus and glass high vacuum systems for use by university research personnel. The skilled craftsmen are particularly adept at developing prototype glass apparatus using state of the art materials and techniques.

Sneaker Tour of ASU's Tempe Campus
3:30 p.m.-5:00 p.m.
Meet at 3:15 p.m. at Old Main by the steps and fountain
This sneaker tour offers limited walking and will include a visit to:
  • Institute of Human Origins - College of Liberal Arts and Sciences
    The Institute of Human Origins is a multi-disciplinary research organization dedicated to the recovery and analysis of the fossil evidence for human evolution. IHO seeks to provide a singular research environment for leading investigators of paleoanthropology and, through the coordinated interaction of scientists, strives to allocate its resources strategically to research on the most important questions yet to be answered regarding the course, cause and timing of biological and behavioral events in human evolution. Education and training are among the Institute's major commitments.
  • Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict - College of Liberal Arts and Sciences
    The Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict at Arizona State University promotes cutting edge research and education on the causes and dynamics of religious conflicts with the aim of contributing to their containment or resolution. By serving as a research hub that fosters exchange and collaboration across the university as well as with its broader publics-local, national, and global-the Center fosters innovative and engaged thinking on matters of enormous importance to us all.

On Campus Religious Services
8:15 a.m. - 9:00 a.m.

Join us for one of the following faith-based services:
Grace Community Church,
Vital Impact University Bible Fellowship and
Quo Vadis Christian Bookstore
Alumni Lounge, Room 202
Grace Community Church, Vital Impact, University Bible Fellowship and the Quo Vadis Christian Bookstore join together for a simple service of worship including: praise, songs, prayers, communion and a short message from the Bible. All are welcome.
Tempe United Methodist and the Wesley Foundation
Gold Room, Room 203
Join the Wesley Campus Ministry and Tempe First United Methodist Church for worship. There will be readings from Scripture, a brief sermon, singing with live musicians, and a celebration of communion. Our communion table is open to all people, regardless of age, religious background or church membership. Our worship brings together a sense of reverence and a sense of warmth, new ideas and ancient prayers, comforting words and a challenging message.
The Faithful City
Yavapai, Room 209
The Faithful City provides a Christian worship and prayer service, inviting the presence of God and His Spirit to those who devote their heart to God. During the service people are encouraged to encounter God in silence, corporate praise and prayer. The service also includes a devotional message and intercessory prayer for the community.
Episcopal Campus Ministry
Yuma, Room 211
Episcopal Campus Ministry will host Morning Prayers from the Book of Common Prayer. Morning Prayers is a 30 minute service that has been practiced for over 500 years. The service is open to all and very ecumenically friendly. If you have never been to a Morning Prayer service you will find this very inviting and our BCP user friendly.
