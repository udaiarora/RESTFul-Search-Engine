LIST's Newsletter
May, 2004


ERR Meeting Summary

Fred opened the meeting with an announcement that III will be upgrading to Millennium Silver in June. He also said we will be installing advanced keyword searching on the webOPAC during the summer intersession. This will require at least a day to create the new indexes, during which time keyword searching in the catalog will be unavailable. Then Marc Mason briefly discussed Illiad, the new ILL system that will replace CLIO and WebZap.

Regarding the web site redesign, Heather reported that the Web Development and Management Team has divided into two working groups, the Technology group and the User Interface group. The Technology group is working to identify or develop a suitable content management system (CMS) for the new site, while the User Interface group is planning the organization, navigation, and look of the site. Both are hoping to finish their initial efforts by the end of June, at which point the Technology group will begin implementing the new CMS to create a test site.

Steve Tanner gave a brief presentation explaining what content management is and the benefits of changing from our current static site to a dynamic site. (View the presentation at http://www.asu.edu/lib/list/lib-web-management.ppt.) Jennie Duvernay then talked about the User Interface group’s efforts, and showed the UC Berkeley library site as an example of some design/navigation ideas they are considering. She also suggested that pagemasters go through their directories and delete whatever files are no longer needed, to facilitate the migration of existing content to the new content management system in the future.

Finally, Heather discussed the Western Waters Digital Library, a grant-funded project managed by the Greater Western Libraries Alliance. ASU Libraries will be purchasing ContentDM software to participate in the Western Waters project, and LIST plans to harness the software’s exciting features for other digitization projects as well. You can browse other libraries’ ContentDM projects at http://www.contentdm.com/customers/index.html.

Ask LIST

Please send questions on any library technology topic to Heather Knowles to be answered in future columns.

Q. "Will the upgrade to advanced keyword searching require a catalog outage? What system features will be affected?"

A. Here’s what you need to know about the outage required to perform the upgrade:

ALL staff must be logged out of ALL functions by 1:50PM on Friday May 14, 2004. The INNOPAC/Millennium system will be unavailable from 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM on Friday May 14, 2004. During this time, Innovative Interfaces will be loading additional software and removing the keyword indexes from the system. When the system comes back up at 4:00PM, all functions except keyword searching will be available. The keyword indexes will be rebuilt over the weekend (May 14-16) and Advanced Keyword searching will be implemented on the online catalog on Monday 5/17/04. The new search feature will have the same properties as U of A’s advanced search page.

From 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM Friday May 14, 2004 there will be no access to any functions on the INNOPAC/Millennium system:
1. Circulation activity must be handled manually.
2. Records cannot be added, accessed, updated or deleted from the system (Acquisitions, Cataloging, Circulation, Serials).
3. Any electronic resources directed through the Web Access Management system (proxy server) will be unavailable. This includes off-campus access to all electronic resources, as well as on campus use of electronic course reserve, video courses and streaming audio.

Any questions regarding the system outage or Advanced Searching can be directed to: fred.mcilvain@asu.edu
