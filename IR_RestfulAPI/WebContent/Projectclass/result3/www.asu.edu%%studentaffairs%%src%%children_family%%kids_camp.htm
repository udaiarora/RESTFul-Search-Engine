General Information:

  • Campers must be ages 5 (and having completed kindergarten)-11 years of age.
  • Parent(s) or Guardian(s) need to provide a lunch for their children.
  • A mid-morning and afternoon snack will be provided (children with special diets need to provide their own snack).
  • Campers need to bring appropriate attire for swimming and sporting activities, including sunscreen and tennis shoes.
  • Campers are divided into groups according to age and participate in age appropriate activities.
  • Enrollment is limited to eighty (80) children. Each session is on a first come first serve basis. To reserve a spot, please pay the deposit or full payment for each session.
  • Sessions 1 and 6 are shortened due to Memorial Day and the 4th of July holiday. Camp will start on Tuesday, May 30th for Session 1 and camp will be closed on Tuesday, July 4th in Session 6.
  • CAMP TIMES: A weekly information newsletter available for the parents and campers each week. The newsletter will cover Quest Programs, Days for Dress-Up, Friday Fun-Days, and pertinent information needed for each parent/guardian for that week.
  • A Parent Guide is available for each family on the day of Registration. This Guide will help answer any and all questions you might have about parking, what to bring to camp, policies, and important phone numbers.
  • Camp Hours: Monday-Friday, 7:30am-5:30pm, *Camp Programming Hours are from 9:00am-4:30pm
    Camper Drop Off: (7:30am-9:00am) South end of the SRC. Parent/Guardian must sign in each child they drop off.
  • Camper Pick Up: (4:30-5:30pm) Campers must be picked up and signed out by a parent or authorized person by 5:30pm. All parents/guardians who do not pick up their child(ren) by 5:30pm will be charged $2 per minute that they are late.

Camp Registration:

  • Tuesday, Apr. 11, 2006 for only returning campers and families.
  • We will be taking registrations for only returning families at the Administration Desk of the Student Recreation Complex starting at 7am on this day only.
  • All other registrations will be taken at the Administration Desk of the Student Recreation Complex, starting April 12, 2006. Hours: Mon-Thurs. 8am-6pm & Fri. 8am-5pm

All registration materials must be complete before a camper can be registered for any session. No birth certificate needed. If a session is filled, your child may be placed on a waiting list, but must have a completed registration form on file. No deposit is necessary to be placed on the waiting list. Registration continues until a camp session is full or begins.

Registration Checklist

(what to have completed to make the registration process run smooth).

  • Registration and Health Information Form
  • Release and Assumption of Risk Form
  • Session and Quest Registration Form
  • Behavior Guildance Form
  • Copy of Insurance Card

Session Schedule:

Week 1: Great Outdoors Week (May 30-June 2 *No camp on Memorial Day)
During Great Outdoor Week we'll hike “A” Mountain, observe insects, learn about wild animals, and improve our knowledge of fire safety. You don't want to miss this great adventure!
Dress-Up Day: Dress like you are going to the jungle!

Week 2: Superhero Week (June 5-June 9)
Nothing is better than a good superhero. Everyone knows that Superman and Spiderman are the coolest. During this week we will have the chance to learn about these fictional superheroes - and meet some of our local superheroes. The Fire Department and ASU police department will make a visit to camp during this week.
Dress-Up Day: Dress up as your favorite hero

Week 3: Why? Week (June 12-June 16)
Why? Week returns with more great activities that are designed to answer the questions in all of us. Why? Week will bring out the scientist in all of us with hands-on experiments and demonstrations that will facilitate problem solving skills.
Dress-Up Day: Backwards Day

Week 4: Splish-Splash Week (June 19-June 23)
Cool off during the “dog-days of summer” during our week of wild, wonderful water activities. Each child needs to bring a t-shirt to tie-dye.
Dress-Up Day: Beach/Hawaiian Shirts and Grass Skirts

Week 5: Tribal Quest Week (June 26-June 30)
Test your brainpower and problem solving and teamwork skills through a series of tribal challenges in your quest for the camp treasure.
Dress-Up Day: Crazy Sock Day

Week 6: Freedom Week (July 3-July 7 *No camp on 4th of July)
Lets celebrate our freedom this week as we learn how our independence day came about! We will celebrate all week for the 4th of July holiday.
Dress-Up Day: Wear your best Red-White-Blue

Week 7: World Carnival Week (July 10-July 14)
Celebrate the cultures of every camper through crafts, dance, food, games and more.
Dress-Up Day: Crazy Hair Day

Week 8: It’s Showtime! (July 17-July 21)
The spotlight is on you this week as we prepare for our camp talent show. If you’ve always wanted to be center stage, this week is for you!
Dress-Up Day: Halloween in the Summer Costumes

Week 9: Sun Devil’s in Training Week (July 24-July 28)
This is a week of learning and celebrating the traditions of Arizona State University and the Sun Devils!
Dress-Up Day: Jersey Day

Week 10: Camp Olympics (July 31-Aug. 4)
The Week of the Olympians promises to be a great week. Campers will experience the games by creating their own countries and participating in popular and unfamiliar sporting events known throughout the world. We will also learn the benefits of staying active and the value of sportsmanship and teamwork.
Dress-Up Day: Wear your team colors all-week!

Camp Fees :

Camp fees are per session per child. Cash, check, or VISA may be used (no payroll deduction).

SRC Member $115 ($25 deposit included)

Non SRC Member $135 ($25 deposit included)

Session 1 and 6 (4 days of camp)

SRC Member $95 ($25 deposit included)

Non SRC Member $110 ($25 deposit included)

*A deposit of $25 per child is required for each session that a camper has signed up for. Deposits are non-refundable and non-transferable. The balance is due the Friday prior to the start of each upcoming session. A $10 late fee will be assessed if payment is NOT made the Friday before the next session. Balance for your child(ren)'s very first week of camp is due on the first day at camp.

Pay by Blocks

SRC Member

•  Sessions 1-5 $440 (savings of $115)

•  Sessions 6-10 $440 (savings of $115)

Non SRC Member

•  Sessions 1-5 $515 (savings of $135)

•  Sessions 6-10 $515 (savings of $135)

•  Payment required in full at time of registration. No deposit required. Cash, Check, or VISA accepted. Payments are NOT transferable to any other session .

You need not be an ASU alumnus to join. For more information, please call the ASU Alumni Association (480) 965-2586.

Staff

•  Students attending Arizona State University

•  CPR/First Aid/AED Certified

•  Background Checks

•  Driving Record Check

•  All student employees go through an extensive interview process, and extensive training which entails safety responsibilities, human relations and interpersonal skills with children.

Refunds:

•  Deposits are non-refundable and non-transferable.

•  All refund requests must be received by 8:30am on the third date of the camp session.

•  Upon approval by the Kids Camp Coordinator, participants may receive a 75% refund of the unused days for the camp session. Please remember that this excludes the $25 deposit.

•  If Sun Devil Kids Camp cancels a session, a full refund or transfer will be issued.

•  Camp fees paid by credit card will be credited immediately upon approval by Sun Devil Kids Camp. Purchases made by check or cash will be refunded by voucher within one week after approval by Sun Devil Kids Camp. Refunds will be made only to the original payee or credit card holder.

Absences:

•  Refunds are not available for vacations, special events, short-term illnesses of four days or fewer, or other personal commitments that prevent attendance.

•  Extended Illnesses - A refund may be available for an extended illness of five or more consecutive program days. A doctor's note and written note from the parent or guardian explaining the situation must be received within eight working days from the first day of absence. Upon review, participants may receive a prorated refund for the unused days.

Camper Dismissal:

There are times when the camp must dismiss a child due to psychological or emotional problems that precludes the child from participating safely or effectively in a group. Dismissal will take effect only after consultation among the parents, camper, and camp director. Dismissal for the aforementioned reasons will result in a complete refund for the unused days.

On occasion, dismissal may be necessary for disciplinary reasons. This action will take effect only after consultation among the parents, camper, and the camp director. If a camper is dismissed for disciplinary reasons, there will be NO REFUND for the unused days.

Junior Counselor Program

Objective:

  • To provide Junior Counselor members with work experience where they can develop leadership qualities, self confidence, communication skills, responsibility, a strong work ethic, and teamwork.

Goals:

  • To assist Camp Supervisors in their day to day camp programs and activities.
  • Be a positive role model for ASU Kids Camp participants.
  • To participate in weekly meetings providing and receiving feedback to aid JuCo members in developing their abilities and enhancing their experience.

•  To gain counselor skills through team participation and observation

Attendance:

  • Training will be provided for all JuCo members and will be done as a group to encourage unity and participate in team building activities. T raining will be done during the first week of their three-week session.

Length and Cost:

  • Must be available for the full three-week program
  • Session A: Sessions 2-4 (June 5-June 23) and/or
  • Session B: Sessions 5-7 (June 26-July 14) and/or
  • Session C: Sessions 8-10 (July 17-Aug. 4) and/or
  • SRC Member: $115/week, Sessions 1 and 5 are only $95.
  • Non SRC Member: $135/week, Session 1 and 5 are only $110
    • Prices are subject to change

Requirements:

  • Male or Female, 12-15 years of age;
  • Have experience, high energy levels and possess a willingness and desire to lead and care for youngsters;
  • Can work with children from all ?walks of life?

How to apply :

  • Applications will be accepted for Block 1 and Block 2: Tuesday, April 11, 2006 to Friday, May 5, 2006.
  • Once accepted, there will be a JuCo AND parent/guardian meeting. Time of meeting will be given at the time of notification of exceptance.
  • Complete application form and submit to the Kids Camp Coordinator, by email or postal mail.
  • More information about the program is given online at www.asu.edu/src

Scholarship Program

The Sun Devil Kids Camp is committed to serving families from all cultural and economic backgrounds. We reserve space in certain camp sessions for campers on scholarships. Financial assistance is available for both low and middle income families. In keeping with our mission, scholarship awards are kept confidential and camp staff is not aware of any camper's financial situations.

The application deadline is May 26, 2006 for all sessions where scholarships are available. Applications are only accepted through the mail, so please make sure to mail your application and registration forms well before the deadline. After the deadline date, we will continue to process applications. Applications are processed in the order they are received.

Requirements:

  • Applicants must be 5 (and finished kindergarten)-11 years old
  • Applicants must participate in the USA Tennis 1-2-3 over the course of the session
  • Applications must be submitted by May 26, 2006

Application Process: Please submit the following

  • Scholarship Application Form
  • Income Verification
  • Sun Devil Kids Camp Registration Form
  • Health History Form
