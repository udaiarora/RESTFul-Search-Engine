The Academic Chairs and Directors Council

Purpose

The Academic Chairs & Directors Council is for academic program heads. The program was developed after a series of focus groups composed of chairs and deans identified critical skills for academic chairs. A chairs steering committee format was established in 1988 that provides continuing guidance and support for the program. This committee has become an important indicator of participant ownership of the program. Chairs meet in luncheon sessions, afternoon discussions, and a once-a-year retreat to hear speakers and refine skills. Chairs have used these occasions to build a collegial network that provides general support as well as, answers to specific questions. Invited speakers have included both outside experts and experienced ASU administrators. Presentations have included external experts speaking on current issues and experienced ASU administrators speaking about academic leadership and management skills.

Activities of the Academic Chairs and Directors Council are planned by a ten member steering committee with support from the Office of the Provost and are coordinated by a designated Human Resources coordinator through HR Programs.
