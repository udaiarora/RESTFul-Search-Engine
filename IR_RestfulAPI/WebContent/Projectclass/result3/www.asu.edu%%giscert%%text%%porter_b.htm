Bethany Porter

Discrepancies between Mapping Techniques for
Alhagi maurorum (camel thorn) in Northern Arizona

ABSTRACT

The focus of my project will be looking at Alhagi maurorum (camel thorn). Alhagi is an aggressive plant that reproduces by extensive rhizomes and seeds. The roots have been known to reach water tables as deep as 15 feet. Due to its aggressive nature, Alhagi has become of great concern to natural resource managers. My goal is to complete a broad vegetation classification of an area north of Flagstaff while attempting to identify Alhagi from a remotely sensed image. I am then going to import the remotely sensed image into ARCGIS to compare with the point data from the Southwest Exotic Mapping Program (SWEMP) to investigate discrepancies between the two.
