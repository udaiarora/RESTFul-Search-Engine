February 2, 2005

Arizona Business Conditions Index slips in January

The seasonally adjusted Arizona Business Conditions Index slipped to 64.0 in January from 64.6 the previous month, a .09 percent decline. An index reading over 50 indicates the local economy is growing; below 50 suggests a slowdown in the overall level of economic activity in the near term.

Despite the incremental decline, the January index marks the 21 st consecutive month of readings above the critical 50-point level and indicates continued short-term growth in the local economy, according to Dawn McLaren, economist with the Bank One Economic Outlook Center at ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business.

The good news is reflected in the employment sub index, McLaren noted. “The employment component of the Business Conditions Index showed improvement of 2.1 points, bringing it to 67.3, its highest level since May 2004.”

“Both the purchases component and the purchased material inventory level component increased, a signal that local businesses continue to receive a steady stream of work. This is confirmed by the January level of 66.3 in the production sub index, albeit this component fell slightly.” New orders, like production, fell slightly in January to 62.9. Still, McLaren noted, “This is above the 50-point mark, indicating that the Arizona economy should continue along a growth trend in the short term.”

Productivity, measured by a ratio of production to employment, has fallen in Arizona since mid-2003. A high level of output per worker nationwide had been blamed for the country’s lag in employment recovery since the 2001 recession.

The Arizona Business Conditions Index is updated monthly and is available on the Web at www.wpcarey.asu.edu/azbci. The site contains current and past press releases, graphs and historical data.
