F.1: Position Descriptions

Effective 5/16/90 .. Revised 9/10/99

INTRODUCTION


A position description is a formal statement of the essential functions, responsibilities and qualifications of the position. The position description serves as the basis for advertising/recruiting a position as well as the basis for the job performance section of the annual review. It is a concise, accurate and complete description of what a particular position requires and how the person holding it spends his or her time. It is the description of the job itself, and not of the individual academic professional who holds the position. The position description outlines the essential functions and ideally pinpoints the critical elements that spell the difference between successful and unsuccessful job performance.


The description should differentiate it from other jobs and provide the detail necessary to understand the requirements of the position, as well as provide a listing of functions and responsibilities that the academic professional and primary evaluator can discuss objectively.


CONTENTS


The position description may contain the following basic elements:

1. TITLE [required]

A description of the academic professional's main functions that sets the position apart from all others. This may require a subtitle.

2. GENERAL SUMMARY [required]

A summary of the primary purpose of the position and its essential functions.   A description of the academic professional's working relationships up and down the line of authority as well as outside the line of authority. (To whom responsible, supervisory responsibilities, liaison and regular contacts outside the department or library.)

3. ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS [required]

The essential functions of a position must be determined before advertising that position. The essential functions are the core or required duties of a position as opposed to the marginal responsibilities of a position. The eight factors found in ACD 505-04 should be used in determining essential functions.

4. NON-ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS & OTHER DUTIES [optional]

Marginal responsibilities of a position as opposed to core or required duties.

5. KNOWLEDGE& SKILLS [required]

Knowledge and skills necessary to perform the essential functions. These are not necessarily the position qualifications (e.g. MLS) but rather what is needed to perform one's core duties at full capacity.

6. QUALIFICATIONS [required]

Required:
A brief listing of the minimum education, experience and skills required to obtain/hold the position.

Preferred:
A brief listing of additional education, experience and skills that would enhance performance of the essential functions.
