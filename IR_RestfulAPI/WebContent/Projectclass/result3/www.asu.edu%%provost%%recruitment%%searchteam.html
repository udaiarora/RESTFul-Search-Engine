INTRODUCTION

As Arizona State University enters a period of aggressive growth and change, it will see the continual addition of new faculty and administrators in strategic areas. The University is committed to the pursuit of excellence in its new hires and in the increased diversification of its faculty, and is creating new approaches to ensure the most dynamic candidate pools. Success depends upon moving from the traditional, passive search model to one that is more proactive and aggressive.

A multi-dimensional plan will support the dual goals of pursuing dynamic searches for new faculty and achieving greater diversity in the faculty and administration. Components of the plan include:

  • The establishment of an internal Academic Leadership Search Team, designed to support high level academic searches (selected department heads in pilot phase) in ways similar to those used by external executive search firms.
  • The development of a Search Blueprint to be used in all academic searches, containing a checklist of necessary steps and benchmarks aimed at securing the most highly qualified and diverse candidate pools. The process will include series of review and certification points during executive search processes, and will coordinate closely with the ASU Recruitment Handbook.
  • The creation and implementation of supporting educational programs (workshops, materials) tailored to the specific strategic needs of ASU, targeted at search committee members and chairs.

This document describes the design and roles of the internal Academic Leadership Search Team, an important component in the multi-part plan to support successful searches and hires in this exciting new era of growth at Arizona State University.


Academic Leadership Search Team: Membership

Members of the Provost’s team have considered the broad expertise available from all University ranks and areas, to assemble a team of individuals who will serve as a major resource to both search committees and hiring authorities. The number of individuals on the team is determined by the need for certain expertise, and by projecting the number of searches that will be supported by the team. Membership represents a considerable time commitment when active in ongoing searches.

Academic Leadership Search Team: Focus

Initially, the Academic Leadership Search Team’s primary focus will be on positions of department head. Working with the Office of Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action and with the deans, the Office of the Provost will determine which searches to use as “pilot programs” during the academic year 2004-05. The processes and best practices used in the pilot phase will eventually serve as models for all searches.

Academic Leadership Search Team: Roles

The Academic Leadership Search Team offers a broad array of support services. At the beginning of each search in which the Team is to be used, the Team and the Hiring Authority will determine the level of support needed for that particular search. Normally, one or two members of the Academic Leadership Search Team will serve on the search committee as ex officio members, and as the “point people” for that particular search. From their vantage point as committee members, and with access to the search chairs and hiring authorities, the point persons will regularly consult with other Team members as the search progresses, in order to utilize the full wisdom of the Team.

As a search committee provides the Hiring Authority with regular progress reports, the Academic Leadership Search Team will analyze each step in the search process to help the Hiring Authority determine the advisability of moving forward. In searches using the Academic Leadership Search Team, it is essential that they be involved in the very first stages of the process.

Some of the functions of the Team may include:

For the Hiring Authority, to

  • help design the composition of the search committee (and search advisory team, when used)
  • review the search design and timeline
  • provide advice on the position announcement, to send the right message
  • assesss the quality and diversity of the initial candidate pool
  • assess the quality and diversity of candidates invited to interview
  • assist in creating an agenda for an effective introduction to campus and the community
  • assist in evaluating the information produced during the reference and background check processes

For the search committee, to

  • provide advice on search plan design and timeline
  • assist in utilizing the campus and other resources in identifying qualified women and minority candidates
  • assist in identifying and contacting highly sought-after individuals
  • offer suggestions during meetings on ways to work more effectively
  • assist in interview process
  • assist in due diligence and referencing
  • assist in communications with the Hiring Authority

 

USING THE “SEARCH BLUEPRINT” TO CREATE A COMPREHENSIVE SEARCH PLAN

The key to an effective search lies in design of a comprehensive search plan containing all the elements of the process from beginning to a successful conclusion. The traditional, “passive” model simply lets the search unfold, with each next step taken in reaction to the previous outcome. An aggressive, proactive search lays out a design for the entire search ahead of time, and then carries out each stage according to an overall strategy.

The ASU Search Blueprint is a search plan template to support the search committee in conducting an effective search. Using the Search Blueprint as a guide, the Academic Leadership Search Team will work with the Hiring Authority and the search committee chair to create a comprehensive plan unique to the particular search.

Elements of the comprehensive search plan will include:

  • The timeline and proposed approach for soliciting nominations from internal and external sources
  • The proposed position announcement(s)
  • The proposed venues for announcing the position (to include journals, meetings, listserv messages, direct mailings, professional association, caucus contacts, website postings, etc.)
  • The proposed plan for ensuring diversity of the candidate pool
  • The proposed timeline for receiving and reviewing application materials
  • Ways in which senior administrators (President, Provost, Vice Presidents, Deans) or key faculty might help to persuade outstanding candidates to become applicants
  • The proposed timeline for phone and campus interviews
  • The proposed plan for scheduling campus visits (to include interview schedules, presentations, meetings with community members, relocation officials, etc.)
  • Benchmarks during the process where Hiring Authority, search chair, and Academic Leadership Search Team assess search to see if it should go forward or temporarily stop to correct deficiencies

In addition to assisting in the framing of the Search Plan, the Academic Leadership Search Team, working with the point person assigned to the particular search, will monitor the development of the plan as the search progresses.

THE ACADEMIC LEADERSHIP SEARCH TEAM TASK ASSISTANCE

Once the decision has been made to use the Academic Leadership Search Team, the Team will meet with the hiring authority to determine the extent of the Team’s participation in the search. The Team can offer assistance in any or all of the four main components of the search:

  1. Preparation of the Search
  2. Creation of the Pool
  3. The Selection process
  4. Completion of a Successful Search

The worksheet at the end of this document provides a detailed list of ways that the Team can assist in searches, and a format determining the Team’s role in a particular search.

NEXT STEPS

The establishment of an internal Academic Leadership Search Team will improve executive searches as soon as it is deployed. During the 2004/2005 academic year, this new model will be used to support selected searches as “pilot programs.” Lessons learned from this pilot effort will refine the design and future use of ASU’s internal Academic Leadership Search Team. The best practices developed eventually will expand to broader usage in all searches throughout the University.

THE ACADEMIC LEADERSHIP SEARCH TEAM TASK WORKSHEET

Download Task Worksheet (PDF)

The Academic Leadership Search Team can assist in any or all of the following actions to support Search Committees. During the initial meeting between the Team and the Hiring Authority, the following worksheet can assist in determining which tasks the Team will perform and in creating approximate deadlines for the assignments.

 

Preparation of the Search

Assistance:

Development of the draft position description, criteria

 

Selection of the search chair

 

Creation of search process timeline, advertising plan

 

Providing advice on desired minimum and preferred qualifications

 

Preparation of announcement

 

Creation recruitment strategy

 

Creation of advertisements

 Creation of the Pool

Assistance:

 

Obtaining names of potential candidates identified by current college and department employees

 

Obtaining names of people connected to others in the field, who might be called upon to offer nominations

 

Obtaining names of on-campus employees from self-identified minority and underrepresented groups, who may be able to help in providing names of potential candidates or contacts

 

Obtaining names of on- and off-campus experts who are publishing important research, giving national presentations in the field, etc.

 

Determining departmental websites on which the announcement may be placed

 

Identifying professional or community organizations, caucuses, etc., relevant to the position, that represent the issues of women, people of color, people with disabilities, the LGBT community, and other groups

 

Identifying those locations where the most outstanding people in the discipline/profession are employed (excellent departments at other universities, the top institutions in the private sector, etc.)

 

Determining which professional associations serve people in the discipline/profession

 

Determining which journals are most heavily used by people in the field

 

Determining which websites are visited by people in the discipline/profession

 

Identifying venues where people in the discipline/profession congregate (professional meetings, concerts, lecture series, etc.)

 

Identifying those relevant professional meetings, conferences, etc. that will occur during the recruitment period

 

Identifying alumni who might be potential candidates or referral sources

 

Creating other strategies to obtain nominations from members of the University and Phoenix community

 

The Selection Process

Assistance:

 

Creating effective plan for telephone or airport screening – what core questions to ask, what areas to cover

 

Creating initial finalist list, or direct search committee to redouble efforts to create a diverse finalist list

 

Evaluating finalists and assist in writing recommendations for Hiring Authority

 

Conducting reference checks

 

Assist Hiring Authority in assessing strengths and weaknesses of finalists against the needs of the institution

 

Completion of a Successful Search

Assistance:

 

Creating a plan for bringing finalists to campus and the community – which meetings are essential, how to create an agenda that does not overwhelm candidate, accommodating both institution’s and candidate’s needs

 

Communicating with the finalist

 

Advice on final stages of the search – the decision-making process, the issues around spousal and family, dual career considerations, the final announcement, the welcome
