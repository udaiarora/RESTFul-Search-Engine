September 1, 2003

ASU Center hosting "Sustainable Technologies" conference

Arizona State University's Center for Rapidly Urbanizing Regions is hosting an organizing conference on Sustainable Technologies on Thursday, September 4, from 3 to 4:30 p.m. in the Pima Room of ASU's Memorial Union.

The center is involved in researching "available and emerging sustainable technologies to effectively manage the impacts of rapid urbanization in metro Phoenix." In order to do this, the center's Sustainable Technologies Program is in the process of building extensive business-academic collaborations that will try to find ways to address environmental issues while maintaining a strong economy.

The conference will involve an audience of several hundred business leaders, government officials, policy experts, and growth and development authorities. ASU will introduce the Sustainable Technologies Program and solicit input, advice and participation.

Invited speakers at the meeting will include Steven Owens, director of the Arizona Department of Environmental Quality, David Kimball, chairman of the Environmental Committee for both the Arizona State Chamber of Commerce and the Greater Phoenix Chamber of Commerce; and Jonathan Fink, ASU vice president for research and economic affairs.

The conference is open to any members of the media who would like to attend. For more information, contact Jay Golden, coordinator of the Sustainable Technologies Program, at 480-965-2975.
