Admission to the Program
Applicants may be regularly or provisionally admitted to a program. Regular admission means that a student may proceed with the completion of a degree in the standard manner indicated below and in accordance with any other procedures established by the academic unit offering the degree program. Provisional admission is granted when additional evidence of an applicant’s qualification for admission with regular status is required. If the student has been granted provisional admission, a status change must be recommended in writing to the dean of the Division of Graduate Studies by the head of the academic unit after the provisions for regular admission have been fulfilled.



Residence
The period spent in residence after admission to a doctoral program provides an opportunity for students to avail themselves of university resources and to interact fully with faculty and fellow students. The residency requirements for the specific degree programs are as follows:
Ph.D., D.M.A.:
Enroll full time at ASU for two consecutive semesters, not including summer sessions.
Ed.D.:
Complete 30 hours of course work at ASU within 18 consecutive months. Not more than 10 hours of applied project, research, and dissertation credit may be included in the 30-hour total.



Continuous Enrollment
Ph.D., D.M.A. Once admitted, Ph.D. and D.M.A. students must be enrolled continuously, excluding summer sessions, until all requirements have been fulfilled. If a program of study must be interrupted, the student may apply for leave status, not to exceed one year. The approved petition must be filed no later than the last day to register for classes in the semester for which the student is requesting a leave. A student who interrupts a program without obtaining leave status may be removed automatically from the program.

Ed.D. If a program of study must be interrupted for one or more semesters, the Ed.D. student must apply to the supervisory committee and the division director for leave status, not to exceed one calendar year. Those who have not been continuously enrolled must apply to the Division of Graduate Studies for re-entry, and following approval of the application, must register for a minimum of one semester hour of graduate credit in the degree area during each of the following semesters. Applications for re-entry are considered along with all other new applications to the degree program.



Program of Study
In consultation with a faculty advisor and upon regular admission, the student files an official Program of Study to outline the planned course of study for the degree. The forms are available online at all campus computer sites, at the ASU POS Forms Online website (www.asu.edu/registrar/forms/pos.html). The Program of Study must be completed and signed by the student, the supervisory committee, and the head of the academic unit. Students return the completed and signed form to the Graduation Office. Course changes to the approved Program of Study may be requested by submitting the Petition for Course Changes to the Graduate Program of Study (www.asu.edu/registrar/forms/pos.html) to the Graduation Section of the Registrar’s Office. All other changes to the Program of Study require the use of the Petition to the Division of Graduate Studies (www.asu.edu/graduate/forms), submitted to Student Academic Services (SAS).



Foreign Language Requirement
If a foreign language is required, students must demonstrate at least a reading knowledge prior to taking the comprehensive examinations. Competency is certified by the Department of Languages and Literatures only upon satisfactory performance of a Foreign Language Examination specific to the particular graduate program in which the student is enrolled. The examinations are administered three times each year. Students planning to take the examination must register by completing the Foreign Language Examination form (available in Word or pdf at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms) and submitting it to Languages & Literatures at least one month in advance of the examination date. The students must pass the examination in no more than three attempts. Students must petition the Division of Graduate Studies for a third reexamination.



Comprehensive Examinations
When students have essentially completed the course work in an approved Program of Study, they should make arrangements with the supervisory committee chair and the academic unit to take the comprehensive examinations. Application for Comprehensive Examination forms are available on the web at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms or from the Division of Graduate Studies.



Maximum Time Limit
A candidate must have the final oral examination in defense of the dissertation within five years after passing the comprehensive examinations.



Candidacy
The dean of the Division of Graduate Studies admits students to candidacy. Letters are sent when the Division of Graduate Studies receives the Comprehensive Examination forms indicating when students completed the examinations. Ph.D. students must pass the examinations and defend the dissertation prospectus before admission to candidacy. Following the semester in which they are admitted to candidacy, Ph.D. students must enroll for a minimum of 12 hours of either of the following courses: 792 research credit, 799 dissertation credit, or a combination of both in subsequent semesters.



Applying for Graduation
Application for graduation should be made no later than the date specified in the Graduate Catalog calendar. All fees are payable at this time. The student applies for graduation by
  • paying the graduation fee at the Cashier’s Office in the Student Services Building,
  • taking the receipt to the Graduation Office, SSV 140, and
  • completing the Application for Graduation form provided by the Graduation Office.

An additional late fee will be assessed if these procedures are completed after the date specified in the Graduate Catalog calendar. A student may withdraw an Application for Graduation by contacting the Graduation Office. If the student wishes to apply the filing fee to a subsequent semester, he or she must withdraw the application no later than the application deadline. During the summer, the graduation application must be withdrawn by July 1. If a student does not complete all degree requirements by the date of graduation for which he or she has applied and has not withdrawn the application by the designated time, the graduation fee must again be paid to reapply.



Dissertation
A copy of the Format Manual is available online at www.asu.edu/graduate/formatmanual or from the Division of Graduate Studies. Because it outlines all relevant procedures, a careful review of this document well in advance of the preparation of the final copy of the dissertation is strongly recommended.



Registration
  • Whenever using university facilities or faculty services, graduate students must be registered for a minimum of one hour of graduate-level credit. The credit hour(s) must be an appropriate course in the academic unit in which they are pursuing their degree program. This requirement also applies to registration during any semester or summer session in which written or oral examinations are taken.

  • All graduate teaching and research assistants and associates must register for a minimum of six hours of course work each regular semester they are employed.

  • A student who does not enroll for three calendar years is considered withdrawn and must reapply for admission to a degree program.

  • These enrollment requirements may not be waived.



Dates and Deadlines
The university calendar, found in the current Graduate Catalog, lists deadlines for applying for graduation, submitting dissertations to the Division of Graduate Studies, holding an oral defense of a dissertation, and submitting the dissertation to the ASU Bookstore for binding. See also the Division of Graduate Studies site, Graduation Deadlines and Procedures.
