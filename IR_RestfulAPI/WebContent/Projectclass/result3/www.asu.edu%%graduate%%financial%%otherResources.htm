Other Helpful Resources

  • AfterCollege Post-Doc Opportunities is a free service with information regarding post-graduate opportunities.
    www.aftercollege.com/jobseekers/phd


  • The Chronicle of Higher Education Grants and Fellowships website is a great source for information and links to many federal funding sites. chronicle.com/free/grants/


  • College Next provides links to financial aid information. www.collegenext.org/financial_aid/links.html


  • COS (Community of Science) Funding Opportunities
    A comprehensive source of funding information, representing over 400,000 funding opportunities. fundingopps.cos.com


  • FinAid is a comprehensive, annotated collection of information about student financial aid. www.finaid.org


  • Forecast of Funding Opportunities
    This site lists virtually all programs and competitions under which the U.S. Department of Education has invited or expects to invite applications for new awards and provides actual or estimated deadline dates for the transmittal of applications under these programs. www.ed.gov/fund/grant/find/edlite-forecast.html


  • Fundsnet provides information on funding and scholarship opportunities. www.fundsnetservices.com/finaid06.htm


  • GrantLink is a comprehensive source of information on grant support for social science and public policy researchers. www.grantlink.org


  • GrantsNet is a searchable, continuously-updated database of funding opportunities in biomedical research and science education. www.grantsnet.org


  • The Illinois Researcher Information Services (IRIS), a unit of the University of Illinois, compiles databases of funding opportunities and maintains a library of publications from over 7,000 funding agencies. These databases contain records on federal and nonfederal funding opportunities in the sciences, social sciences, arts, and humanities. ASU is a subscriber and anyone can sign on to the server, as long as they do so with an asu.edu email address. www.library.uiuc.edu/iris

  • National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH) is an independent grant-making agency of the United States government dedicated to supporting research, education, preservation, and public programs in the humanities. www.neh.gov

  • SallieMae CollegeAnswer Free Scholarship Search provides access to an award database containing more than 2.4 million scholarships. salliemae.collegeanswer.com/paying/scholarship_search/pay_scholarship_search.jsp


  • Studentjobs.gov is a joint project between the United States Office of Personnel Management (OPM) and the U.S. Department of Education's Student Financial Assistance office. This website provides information you need to find the job you want in the Federal Government and access to OPM's USAJOBS database of opportunities. www.studentjobs.gov


  • U.S. Department of Education website lists links to a variety of scholarship programs and financial aid resources. www.ed.gov

  • UC Davis: Institute of Governmental Affairs lists many current funding opportunities and resource tools. www.iga.ucdavis.edu/gfund.html
