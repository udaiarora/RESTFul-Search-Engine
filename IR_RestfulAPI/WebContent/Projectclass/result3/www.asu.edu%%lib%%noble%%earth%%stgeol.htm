Finding Books in the ASU Libraries

To find books on the Geology of a state, use the ASU Libraries' online catalog (http://library.lib.asu.edu).

Search Tips:

  • Do a subject search for geology state (ex. geology oklahoma)
  • Note the call number, library and location for each item.


Finding Journal Articles in the ASU Libraries

To find journal articles in the ASU Libraries, use the GeoRef database. Georef is available via "Find -->Articles" on the ASU Libraries' Home Page (http://www.asu.edu/lib/).

Search Tips:

  • In the first row of search boxes type the state name and change the "field" box from "Keywords, KW=" to "Descriptors, DE="; enter subject term(s) in the 2nd and 3rd row of boxes.
    (Locations should always be searched in the "descriptor" field; this will ensure that the location is a topic of the article and not the location where the author works.)
  • The asterisk (*) may to used to find variant forms of a word.
  • The search will include USGS, AzGS and AGS publications, proceedings of conferences, Master thesis and PhD dissertations.
  • To limit the search to journal articles:
    • In the next row (use the "add row" link in the upper right if necessary), put serial in the search box and change "field" box from "Keywords, KW=" to "Publication Type, PT=";
    • In the next row, change the boolean operator from "and" to "not"; in the search boxes put conference or abstract or report and change "field" box from "Keywords, KW=" to "Publication Type, PT=".
  • To find articles online and/or in the ASU Libraries, use the "Full Text" link or click on the yellow "Get It @ ASU" butoon at the bottom of the record.


Finding Maps

  • Maps in the ASU Libraries
    Sheetmaps are located in the Map Collection on the 3rd floor of the Noble Science and Engineering Library.
    • To find geological maps of the states in the ASU Map Collection, use the ASU Map Index (http://www.asu.edu/lib/resources/db/mapindex.htm).
    • In the Term box, type city/region state geology
      For example:
      • Tempe Arizona geology
      • Pikes Peak Colorado geology
    • In the Field box, select All

    • To find USGS maps in the ASU Map Collection, use the ASU Map Index (http://www.asu.edu/lib/resources/db/mapindex.htm).
    • In the Term box, type city/region state USGS geology
      For example:
      • Mesa Arizona USGS geology
    • In the Field box, select All

    • Some maps may also be located by using the ASU Libraries Online Catalog (http://catalog.lib.asu.edu)
    • Do a Keyword search for City/region state (map or maps) geology
      For example:
      • Mesa Arizona (map or maps) geology
  • Online Maps
    • National Geologic Map Database (NGMDB)
      http://ngmdb.usgs.gov
      Includes"information about maps and related data for: geology, hazards, earth resources, geophysics, geochemistry, geochronology, paleontology, and marine geology." This is an ongoing project of the United States Geological Survey and the Association of American State Geologists. To identify maps on a specific state or county use the "Geologic Maps" database. After the maps have been identified, search the ASU Map Index (http://www.asu.edu/lib/resources/db/mapindex.htm) and the ASU Libraries' Online Catalog (http://library.lib.asu.edu) to determine if the map is available in the libraries.
    • Geology.com
      http://www.geology.com
      Types of maps available vary with each state.


Identifying United States Geological Survey (USGS) Publications

USGS publications, except for sheet maps, are located in Hayden Library in the Government Documents collection on the 3rd floor. Sheet maps are located in the Map Collection on the 3rd floor of the Noble Science and Engineering Library.

  • USGS Home Page (http://www.usgs.gov)

  • USGS State Information Available on the Internet
    Go to the USGS Home Page, click on the appropriate state in the US map under "Science in Your Backyard" in the right-hand column.

  • Identifying USGS Publications via GeoRef
    To find USGS publications on a specific state/region/city via the GeoRef database:
    • In the first row of search boxes type the state name and change the "field" box from "Keywords, KW=" to "Descriptors, DE=";
      (Locations should always be searched in the "descriptor" field; this will ensure that the location is a topic of the article and not the location where the author works.)
    • In the 2nd row of search boxes, type usgs and change the "field" box from "Keywords, KW=" to "Descriptors, DE=";
    • In the 3rd search box, type in the subject.


    Georef is available via the "Find -->Articles" category on the ASU Libraries' Home Page (http://www.asu.edu/lib/).

  • Finding USGS Publications Available Online
    Go to the USGS Home Page, click on the "Maps, Products & Publications" button near the top of the page, and select on "Publications Warehouse" from the list. Once a search is completed, click on the "More info" link to the left of a document to get the complete information, and, if available, a link to the document online. Note that the search engine in the Publications Warehouse is not as sopishicated as the one in GeoRef, so fewer and/or less accurate results may be obtained. In addition to the Publications Warehouse, this section also provides links to the National Biological Information Infrastructure (NBII), National Water Data (NWISWeb), The (topographic) National Map, National Atlas of the United States®, Geodata.gov (Geospatial-One-Stop), and links to publications by product type.
