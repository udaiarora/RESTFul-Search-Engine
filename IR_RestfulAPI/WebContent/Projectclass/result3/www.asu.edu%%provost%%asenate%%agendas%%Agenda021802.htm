5.A   Personnel Committee (Doug Johnson).

5.A.1   Resolution #3 (2001-2002).

Regarding the Senate Personnel Committee review of the recommendations of the Task Force on Promotion and Tenure.

             ?Whereas, the Senate agrees that the Arizona State University Promotion and Tenure Policy (ACD 501-513) should be updated to improve clarity, coherence, and consistency and to promote high quality decision and review processes.

The Academic Senate recommends the acceptance of the Promotion and Tenure Task Force Recommendations (see attachment 1) subject to the clarifications and changes recommended by the Academic Senate Personnel Committee (see attachment 2). The Academic Senate recommends the appointment of a university taskforce to investigate best practices for evaluating the quality of teaching/learning and recommend models of evaluation to facilitate implementation by academic units.

Furthermore, the Senate applauds the formation of the Provost?s Personnel Advisory Committee (PAC20) as a mechanism for insuring campus wide input into the process of implementing and maintaining university personnel policies (ACD Manual). And the Senate looks forward to reviewing the updated ACD Manual including the updated statement of policy on Promotion and Tenure.

The Senate commends the Promotion and Tenure Task Force for thoughtful and thorough deliberations and open hearings which have allowed campus wide issues and concerns to be incorporated into this important process.?

6.C.1   Senate Motion #4 (2001-2002) (Second Reading). 

            ?The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal submitted by the College Law for the establishment of a Graduate Certificate in Law, Science and Technology.  Rationale: The certificate program will enable the College of Law to recruit prospective students more effectively, especially those with science degrees and a high quantitative aptitude.  The Program will help the College of Law place its graduates with the law firms, government agencies, private companies, and public interest groups that focus upon law, science, and technology subject areas.  The Program will enrich the educational experience of its students by recognizing their special interests and needs and creating networks of encouragement and cooperation.?

6.C.2    Senate Motion #5 (2001-2002) (Second Reading).

            ?The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal submitted by the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences for the name change of an Undergraduate Certificate from Enriched College Degree Certificate to College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (CLAS) Enriched Certificate (CLASEC).  Rationale: The name change of this certificate program is requested in order to bring the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Enriched Certificate into a match with its acronym of CLASEC used on the CLAS web site and in other communications.?

6.C.3   Senate Motion #6 (2001-2002).

            This motion was approved by CAPC 2/12/02 with a request to suspend the rules at the Senate meeting on 2/18/02. 

            ?The Curriculum and Academic Programs Committee recommends Academic Senate approval of a proposal from the College of Business to establish an Undergraduate Certificate in Dealership Management.  Rationale: There is shortage of well-trained candidates to become dealer/owners and operators in the automotive industry.  During the past two decades, the complexity of dealerships has increased because of industry consolidation, advances in information technology, and the emergence of supply chain management issues.  As a result, dealer/owners now need skills typically provided by university business degrees to effectively manage their operations.

           This certificate is supported by grants from the Ford Motor Company and is part of Ford?s Dealership Education Program for Minorities. This certificate presents opportunities for ASU students to pursue careers in the automotive industry at both the corporate and dealership levels.  Other industries will also find the entrepreneurial retail background attractive.  Within Arizona, there are no other competing programs.?
