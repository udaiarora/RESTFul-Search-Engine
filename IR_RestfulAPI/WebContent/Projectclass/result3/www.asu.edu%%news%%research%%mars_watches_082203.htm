August 22, 2003

ASU offers up-close view of Mars

The close approach of Mars this year provides a historic opportunity for Earthlings to see the Red Planet up close.

To help local Earth dwellers view this once-in-a-lifetime astronomical event, the ASU Physics and Astronomy Department will hold three free Mars Watches, on consecutive Friday evenings, beginning Aug. 22.

The watches will be from 9 to 11 p.m. Aug. 22, Aug. 29 and Sept. 5, on the roof (fifth floor) of the Physical Sciences Center H-wing, on the ASU Main Campus.
Mars' closest approach to Earth will be Aug. 27, and it will slowly recede during the fall, said Kevin Healy, a graduate student in physics and astronomy.

"We also will continue to view the Red Planet during our regularly scheduled, free Astronomy Open Houses on Sept. 26, Oct. 31 and Nov. 21, from 8 to 10 p.m., also on the H-wing roof."

For parking information and directions to the H-wing, visit the Astronomy Open House Web site at (http://eagle.la.asu.edu/openhouse).

The Mars Watches will be cancelled in case of cloudy weather. The regular Open Houses will be held regardless of weather.
