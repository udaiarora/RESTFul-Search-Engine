February 11, 2004

Black History Month at ASU main campus

During Black History Month, several events on ASU's main campus will focus on the campus' rich diversity and examine the experience of African-Americans in higher education.

A dinner and panel discussion on "Contemporary Black Male Issues" will be presented at 6 p.m., Feb. 17, in the Memorial Union La Paz room, sponsored by African American Men of ASU. Panelists are Myles Link, College of Law; Mark Strickling, W. P. Carey School of Business, Jean Boyd, Intercollegiate Athletics; and Michael Johnson, Counseling and Consultation. For information, call Ordell Jones,
(480) 965-4415.

Mernoy Harrison, executive vice president of administration and finance, will speak at the third annual African American Scholarship Dinner at 5:45 p.m., Feb. 18, in Old Main. Sponsors are the ASU African American Alumni and General Dynamics. For information, call Carlos Pastor, (480) 965-5357.

Joe Trotter, History Department chair and director of the Carnegie Mellon University Center for African American Urban Studies and the Economy, and author of "The African American Experience, Vols. I and II," will present two seminars on Feb. 19 and 20 as part of the inaugural seminar series for the Committee on African and African American Research (CAAAR). The seminars will be 3 - 6 p.m. in the Coor Building 4403, the first on "Conceptualizing the African American Experience: Research and Teaching" and the second on "African American Urban History: Past, Present and Future."

CAAAR seminars on March 8 - 9 will deal with philanthropy and its impact on the development of ethnic cultural institutions, and seminars March 25 - 26 will examine African Christianity.

The fourth annual Women of Color Conference, "Desert Rain: Women of Color Transforming the Arizona Academy," will be 8:30 a.m. - 6:30 p.m., Feb. 20, in the Memorial Union. Panelists will be faculty from ASU, the University of Arizona and Northern Arizona University. The conference is free and open to everyone. For information, visit (www.public.asu.edu/~dejesus/rain.htm).

Students from African-American Studies are holding an art sale to benefit their new student resource center, at 3:30 p.m., Feb 18, in Cowden 224. A representative also will be available every Wednesday during February from noon to 2 p.m. The art may be viewed at (http://esimpson56301.ppiart.com). A portion of proceeds will purchase books, videos and other resources for students.

This article appeared in the February 6, 2004 issue of ASU Insight.
