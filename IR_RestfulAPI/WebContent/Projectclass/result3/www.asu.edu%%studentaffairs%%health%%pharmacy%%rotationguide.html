Pharm. D. Rotation Guidelines

Primary Preceptor: Carl Labbe
Other Preceptors: Philip Leavitt, Betty Novack

Goals

  • To develop the capability to comfortably and confidently provide drug information to individuals and groups in an ambulatory care setting.
  • To attain proficiency in medication therapy management.
  • To positively contribute to the mission of the healthcare team on a professional level.

Objectives

  • Demonstrate the ability to research drug information independently using multiple sources in a timely manner.
  • Complete and deliver a substantial presentation on an appropriate topic to an audience of health center staff.
  • Complete thorough and accurate assessments of therapeutic end-points and recommend pharmacologic and non-pharmacologic interventions.
  • Effectively counsel patients, demonstrating listening skills, asking open-ended questions, and verifying patient understanding of information provided.
  • Utilize the pharmacy software to process new and refill prescriptions, complete prescription transfers, and access pricing and inventory data.
  • Demonstrate initiative.

Activities

  • Assume most of the daily activities of a staff pharmacist (albeit in training).
    • Receive new prescription orders in person, via telephone, fax or other electronic means.
    • Counsel patients regarding disease states, prescription and non-prescription medications and complimentary therapies.
    • Manage total committed time of 40 hours to meet responsibilities and complete projects.
    • Meet or exceed the minimum requirements of the college of pharmacy.
    • Proactively communicate with primary preceptor on a weekly basis to review priorities, consider unmet objectives, and discuss any items brought forth by student or preceptor.
    • Attendance and participation in special events such as residents’ conferences, journal club, educational presentations and other meetings as suggested by a preceptor.

Hours of Operation

8/16 to 5/15 9AM to 6PM
5/16 to 8/15 8AM to 5PM

A 40-hour schedule must be established and approved by the primary preceptor at the beginning of the rotation.
