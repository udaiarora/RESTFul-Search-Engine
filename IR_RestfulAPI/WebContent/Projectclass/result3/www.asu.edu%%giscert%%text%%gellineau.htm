Cherisse Gellineau

Developing a Predictive Model for the
Avian Influenza in the United States

ABSTRACT

The perilous scare we are all faced with today as humans is the threat of a global pandemic caused by the Avian Influenza A (flu virus) if it morphs into a strain that could be transmitted by humans. Avian Influenza A (H5N1) epizootic (animal outbreak) in Asia has been increasing significantly, giving rise to human cases (CDC, 2006). Thus, technological advancements, particularly geographic information systems (GIS) is increasingly being employed to provide real-time monitoring of this virus.

The scope of this project involves the effective use of GIS in developing a predictive model for the Avian Influenza in the United States, based on the epidemiological factors that led to its spread in Asia. The model will be based on the spatial correlation of fatalities and cases in Asia with poultry farms, produce markets, annual temperature and the migratory patterns of the waterfowl. This model will be analyzed using two geostatistical analyst tools: geographically weighted regression (GWR) and nearest neighbor.

This type of spatial data can be used by environmental professionals to assess current conditions of outbreaks and make risk management decisions. A GIS will be able to enhance a nation?s preparedness and mitigation plans in response to any infectious disease threat, whether natural or intentional.
