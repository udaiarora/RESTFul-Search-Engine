September 15, 2004

Web site bolsters ASU’s community outreach

From tutoring schoolchildren to providing health and legal clinics for low-income families, from giving music performances to free lectures on key public issues – ASU is deeply involved in serving the needs of its community. For the first time, the university has created an online listing of its community outreach programs, presented on a searchable Web site.

“ASU in Your Community” is a resource for ASU faculty and staff, superintendents, legislators, journalists, policymakers and other community members. The Web site (www.asu.edu/community) lists more than 240 programs at more than 400 locations, reflecting ASU’s commitment to social embeddedness, to its becoming a force, not just a place.

Faculty and staff who coordinate outreach programs are encouraged to visit the site, add their programs if not listed, and maintain and update their information. Coordinators can use the site to learn about other programs and possibly to work together, making their mission more complementary to other programs that exist at the same location.

It also will be a way for others to learn about ASU’s outreach efforts, and for these programs to be counted and recognized. Participation in the project will make it a resource faculty and staff can use for planning outreach activities.

“Universities must meet the needs of their local communities,” said President Michael Crow in discussing his design imperatives for a New American University. “As we seek to cultivate the excellence of our academic programs, I believe we must structure our programs to not only advance knowledge, but wherever practicable, directly serve the needs of the people of Arizona, as well as the national and international communities.

“ASU must be a community of scholars concerned with the impact of their work, giving thought to its possible role in a better society. We must integrate the advancement of knowledge with the transformation of society.”

To help further this goal, the Office of University Initiatives developed the project in partnership with Student Affairs and University Undergraduate Initiatives’ Technology Services.

Community outreach includes traditional service activities in which ASU students, faculty and staff provide time and labor to the community, or teach nontraditional students. Teaching includes noncredit seminars, workshops, consultations or technical assistance. Outreach also can involve providing medical, educational or therapeutic services, or providing volunteer or professional expertise.

Users can search for programs by keyword, program name, location, college or department, and one of nine impact areas: arts and culture, business and economic development, education, environment, public policy, health and wellness, human rights, technology and other.

A GIS mapping tool is being developed that will enable users to see where ASU’s programs are located throughout the Phoenix area and the state. When it is operational, users will be able to zoom into areas for a detailed view, then click on the location and read program details. Eventually users can add different layers of information, such as municipal boundaries, school districts, legislative districts, and census data such as age, population density, race and income.

For more information, contact (yourcommunity@asu.edu).
