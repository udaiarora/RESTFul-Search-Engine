October 19, 2006

Sun Lakes Watercolor Club brushes up on multiple techniques in new exhibit

For the past 13 years, they have been meeting to paint, critique each other's work and learn new techniques. Now, members of the Sun Lakes Watercolor Club will exhibit their artwork at ASU Gammage through Nov. 19.

The exhibit, titled ?Symphony in Color,? will be a celebration of color, light, design and texture, according to club member Diane Hitt.

Subject matter includes the Southwest, landscapes, architecture, flowers, people, animals and abstracts, in watercolor, pen, and ink and acrylics.

?The artists have drawn inspiration from their lifelong experiences and eclectic interests,? Hitt says.

The Sun Lakes Watercolor Club was founded in 1993 to bring artists together in their quest to learn more about painting with watercolor.

Members also experiment with other water media, combinations of the different mediums, and methods of applying water media in new ways.

Exhibit hours at ASU Gammage are 1-4 p.m. Mondays, or by appointment. Because of rehearsals, event setups, performances, special events and holidays, it is advisable to call either (480) 965-6912 or (480) 965-0458 on the day of attendance to ensure viewing hours.

Parking is available at meters around the perimeter of ASU Gammage, and entrance is through the east lobby doors at the box office.

For more information about the exhibit, call Lesley Davis at (480) 965-6912.

Judith Smith, jps@asu.edu
