April 22, 2004

Staff, supervisors trade places during Frontlines Week

The Classified Staff Council (CSC) is urging ASU managers to join in an annual eye-opening experience known as Frontlines Week, being held April 18 - 24.

Geraldine Thiele serves as business manager for ASU's Center for Solid State Electronics Research and is coordinating this year's Frontlines Week activities. Thiele says some managers will be piloting their department's front desk for a few hours, while others will spend a day directly serving customers.

She describes Frontlines Week as an opportunity for management to show support of their frontline staff.

"We tend to overlook the people at the front lines, the staff that are always there with a smile on their faces and a sparkle in their eyes," Thiele says. "They know where the supplies are kept, and what needs to be ordered. They know where to direct lost students, how to schedule a conference room, how to direct someone on where to park. They know the ins and outs of the university."

Thiele says getting involved in Frontlines Week improves communication between staff and management, promotes an understanding of how to better meet the needs of the university community, and ultimately results in enhanced staff morale and improved service.

Thiele says she has been that person on the front lines. For many years she worked at the front desk in various departments, helping the customers along with all the other responsibilities that come with that position.

"That's why I would ask all management to spend a little time at the front desk in their department," she says. "Get a feel for the type of work that has to be done and get a feel for all the interruptions that happen during the course of the day.

"Even spending a couple of hours at the front desk in your department can give you a better idea of what your frontline personnel can do," she says.

Participants are being encouraged to share their Frontlines Week experiences by visiting (www.asu.edu/csc/frontlines) or by faxing them to (480) 727-6273. CSC will be recognizing those involved by entering their names in a drawing for a prize.

For more information, call the CSC office at (480) 727-8233.

Zajac, with Media Relations & Public Information, can be reached at (480) 965-8145 or (izajac@asu.edu).
