December 12, 2005

Pioneering leaders to receive honorary degrees

Lord John Browne, group chief executive of BP, one of the world’s largest energy companies, and the Right Honourable Kim Campbell, former prime minister of Canada, will be awarded honorary degrees at ASU’s fall commencement Dec. 15.

ASU will confer upon Browne the degree of Doctor of Humane Letters in recognition of his contributions to the fields of engineering and business, his leadership in global energy issues and his commitment to environmental responsibility.

He has been a strong advocate for environmental and sustainability issues and has countered his own industry, which by and large maintains that there is little or no proof that human activity is responsible for an observable rise in temperatures around the world.

Under Browne’s leadership, BP (formerly known as British Petroleum) limits its greenhouse gas emissions and has invested $1 billion in solar energy technology, becoming the world’s largest photovoltaic manufacturer.

Most recently, it announced its plans to double its investment in alternative and renewable energies and will manage an investment program in solar, wind, hydrogen and combined-cycle-gas-turbine power generation, which could amount to $8 billion over the next 10 years.

ASU will confer upon Campbell the degree of Doctor of Humane Letters in recognition of her lifelong commitment to public service, the advancement of women and the promotion of democracy throughout the world.

She is a Senior Fellow at the John F. Kennedy School of Government’s Center for Public Leadership and secretary-general of the Club Madrid, an organization of former heads of state dedicated to strengthening democracy worldwide.

Campbell served as Canada’s 19th – and first female – prime minister in 1993. She also held cabinet portfolios as minister of state for Indian affairs and northern development, minister of justice and attorney general, and minister of national defense and veterans’ affairs.

Campbell was the first woman to hold the justice and defense portfolios, as well as the first woman to be defense minister of a NATO country. During her tenure, she tackled significant – and sometimes controversial – issues relative to gun control, women’s reproductive rights and victim’s rights.

ASU’s fall commencement takes place at 10 a.m. at Wells Fargo Arena in Tempe. The ceremony is open to the public, and no tickets are required. For more information, contact the University Ceremonies Office at (480) 965-3565 or via e-mail at (commence@asu.edu).
