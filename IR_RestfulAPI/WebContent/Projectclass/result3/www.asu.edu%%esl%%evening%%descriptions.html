Evening and Weekend Course Descriptions

BUSINESS

Communicating in Business English
Learn business English for common business situations such as business telephone calls, giving presentations, participating in meetings, socializing with business contacts, negotiating deals, and writing business letters
and documents.

Giving Professional Business Presentations
In this course, students will observe and make presentations related to international business. The skills to effectively evaluate and provide feedback will be developed.

Business Communication and International Case Studies
This course is for international business students. This course uses the case-study method to develop general communication, business vocabulary, and business knowledge.

ACADEMIC

Building Academic Vocabulary
This course is designed for advanced students or professionals who want to develop lexical precision in such often-exercised modes as cause-effect, general description, description of process, or comparison/contrast.

Idioms for Academic and Social Success
Close the gap between classroom language and authentic language. Learn common and useful English idioms and expressions.

General and Academic Words
This course makes learning essential vocabulary fun, practical, and useful for daily or academic
life.

Return to top

PROFESSIONAL

Medical English and Communication
This course meets the communication needs of medical doctors, nurses, and practitioners. It covers doctor-to-patient conversation and medical terminology, including surgical and medical equipment.

Infotech - English for Computer Users
Infotech is a comprehensive course for being able to use the English of computing for study and work. The course is up-to-date in the fast moving world of computers and multimedia but does not require a specialist knowledge of computers. Great course for improving all English skill areas while learning English for computer users!

The Culture of American Business: Understanding Americans at Work
Learn essential tips for succeeding in the American work environment. Explore straight talk, American style, and the basics of work-related etiquette.

LANGUAGE TEST PREPARATION

TOEFL Test Preparation
Improve test-taking strategies for specific areas of the TOEFL. This course covers listening for the TOEFL, special TOEFL grammar, error correction practice, and actual test-taking procedures and techniques.

The AECP offers the TOEFL test five times a year. Please call for dates and times.

TSE/SPEAK Test Preparation
This course presents communication techniques and test strategies, as well as practice exercises and questions, for students preparing to take the TSE or SPEAK Tests. Students will practice and improve their spoken English so that they sound more fluent and communicate more clearly.

TOEIC Preparation
TOEIC is the international standard for measuring English language proficiency and is designed specifically to test abilities in English as it is used in real-life situations and business.

The AECP offers the TOEIC test five times a year. Please call for dates and times.

ENGLISH SKILLS

Pronunciation and Accent Reduction, Part 1
This course is designed for nonnative speakers of English and focuses on improving rhythm, stress, and intonation.

Pronunciation and Accent Reduction, Part 2
This course provides rules, learning strategies, and contextualized practice in the stress, rhythm, and melody of English words and discourse -- those features of English pronunciation that affect intelligibility the most.

Intermediate English Grammar
Learn and use grammar more effectively by connecting grammar with American cultural context.

Advanced English Grammar
This course explains what students in universities and colleges need to understand to succeed in academic work. It is designed for students who know a lot of English, but who may not feel confident in a grammar class.

Intermediate and Advanced English
This course develops communicative competence in English through focusing on the four primary communication skills: speaking, listening, reading, and writing; it also covers vocabulary. Course emphasis will be placed on communication.
