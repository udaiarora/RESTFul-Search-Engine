Registered cohort participants may access the PFF 2007 Schedule of Events through Blackboard.

Open PFF Seminars
All graduate students interested in preparing for and pursuing careers in the professoriate are welcome to attend the following PFF seminars:
  • Preparing Your CV I
    Monday, February 12, 2007
    2:00-4:00 p.m.
    Location: MU 211
    Speaker: Dr. Duane Roen

    In this session, Dr. Roen will discuss the genre and conventions of curriculum vitae (CVs) and cover letters for applying to academic and some research positions. Dr. Roen will bring examples of letters to discuss in detail the requirements of the documents. He will also analyze the various examples, pointing out strengths and weaknesses specific to the individual, their discipline, and the context for the document


  • PFF Informational Fair
    Wednesday, February 14, 2007
    11:30 a.m.?1:00 p.m. .
    Location: Memorial Union (MU) Turquoise Room 208

    In the spring, we will be holding a PFF Informational Fair. The fair will include additional information about the program, a panel of current PFF students and staff, and the opportunity for you to ask any questions you may have about the program or the application procedures. Email either of the PFF coordinators, Charlene Roach charlene.roach@asu.edu or Paula Stice pstice@asu.edu with any questions you may have.


  • New Faculty Panel II
    Friday, February 16, 2007
    2:00-5:00 p.m.
    Location: John W. Schwada Classroom Office Building (SCOB), Room 250
    Panel:
    • Dr. Zane Olina, Instructional Systems Program, Department of Educational Psychology and Learning Systems, Florida State University
    • Dr. Cecilia Mafla, Department of Languages and Cultures, Minnesota State University, Moorhead
    • Dr. David Baker, Department of Public Administration, California State University, San Bernardino
    • Dr. Luis Fernandez, Department of Criminal Justice, Northern Arizona University

    A panel of new faculty members and PFF alumni from across the country will discuss their hiring experiences, as well as their first-year teaching experiences. A variety of university models will be represented in this discussion. There will also be opportunities for questions from the audience.

  • Preparing Your CV II
    Wednesday, March 7, 2007
    3:00-5:00 p.m.
    Location: MU 211
    Speaker: Dr. Duane Roen

    In this session which is a follow up to session I, Dr. Roen will discuss the genre and conventions of curriculum vitae (CVs) and cover letters for applying to academic and some research positions. Dr. Roen will bring examples of letters to discuss in detail the requirements of the documents. He will also analyze the various examples, pointing out strengths and weaknesses specific to the individual, their discipline, and the context for the document.


  • Academic Job Search
    Thursday, March 29, 2007
    3:00-5:00 p.m.
    Location: MU 211
    Speaker: Dr. Duane Roen

    This session will provide an overview of the how to prepare for the academic job market. Duane Roen will address the following questions:

    • What can I do as a graduate student to enhance my marketability?
    • What is the process for applying for academic jobs?
    • How do I set up a dossier?
    • How can I get the best possible letters of recommendation?
    • What materials are part of the job application packet?
    • What can I expect in campus interviews?
    • How can I effectively negotiate a contract for an academic job?

    In addition to addressing these questions, Duane will encourage participants to raise other questions.


Note: Registration for these open PFF seminars is available at www.asu.edu/interactive. Open the ?Career Enhancement Programs? menu under ?Current Students.? Click on the link "Sign up for Graduate Workshops" and log in to ASU Interactive with your ASURITE ID.
All graduate students are welcome to attend! Email pstice@asu.edu with questions.
