Technical Services Department


Large gift or purchased collections of print material


The Technical Services Department proposes a few Technical Services/Collection Development interface procedures that would make TS handling of large gift or purchased collections of print material more efficient. ("Large" is defined by TS as more than 500 titles all arriving at once; "print" includes microforms but not electronic acquisitions). The ABC Group, a TS standing committee, and the TS Management Team, suggest the following: 


Advance notice

TS would like advance notice of large gifts or collections. It would be helpful if, on completing negotiations for such acquisitions, CD would send a note to the TS Management Team giving the general subject area, predominant language(s) and projected arrival date. Advance notice gives TS time to plan redistribution of resources-- time, staff, language skills-- to accommodate bulk receipts. TS would also like to be notified when the material actually arrives.


Priorities

As noted in the Technical Services Priorities policy, gift material of any kind can be flagged by selectors on the same basis as other receipts as rush, first priority, etc. Unflagged gifts are made part of the regular workflow whenever possible but may have a lower priority in some areas of Tech Services. Large collections, also addressed in the priorities policy, pose special problems that may need to be negotiated among all those involved. Any particular priority requirements of a large collection need to be discussed with the Tech Services Management Team.


Delivery and storage

Tech Services has some space available for storage of collections after CD processing has been completed. Having collections in one place and easily available contributes considerably to efficient TS routines. Delivery to TS in installments is a possible option. Delivery and storage issues should be discussed along with priorities.


Special processing requirements

The feasibility and mechanics of special processing, e.g. bookplates, alterations in the normal added copy/duplicate policy, etc., should be discussed with the Tech Services Management Team before TS work on a collection begins. Unusual or serious condition/preservation problems should be settled with the Preservation Dept. before TS work begins.
