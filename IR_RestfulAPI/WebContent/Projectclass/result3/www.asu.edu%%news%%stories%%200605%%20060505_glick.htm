May 5, 2006

ASU Provost Glick named president of University of Nevada at Reno

Milton Glick, executive vice president and provost of Arizona State University, was unanimously appointed president of the University of Nevada at Reno by the Nevada Board of Regents May 5.

Glick was appointed provost of ASU in 1991 and in 2002 was named executive vice president. His responsibilities at ASU include academic planning, budgeting and management for all university programs on all four of ASU’s campuses.

“I am honored to have received the unanimous support, first of the selection committee and now of the Nevada Board of Regents,” Glick said. “I was not planning to leave ASU. It has been an extraordinary place to work. However, the opportunity to lead a high caliber university in a fast-growing state like Nevada was not one I could pass up.”

Glick, who has been ASU’s provost or chief academic officer for the last 15 years, is continuing an ASU tradition of producing top executives for institutions of higher education. In March 2006 ASU’s Polytechnic campus provost, Gerald Jakubowski, was named president of the Rose Hulman Institute, one of the nation’s preeminent engineering schools. Lawrence Penley, former dean of ASU’s W. P. Carey School of Business, is now president of Colorado State University. And Charles Bantz, who served as vice provost of ASU, is now chancellor of Indiana University Purdue University Indianapolis.

“ASU is proving itself as a producer, not only of talented, entrepreneurial students, but also of strong executives with leadership skills that are valued by other colleges and universities,” said ASU President Michael Crow. “As the academic leader of this institution over the last decade and a half, Milt has been indispensable to the success of ASU. I am looking forward to a continued relationship with him in his new position.”

During Glick’s tenure at Arizona State University, there has been progress on numerous fronts: the university has made significant gains in student retention and student graduation; the size and quality of the freshman class has substantially increased; the number of minority students has increased and their retention is equal to that of the overall freshman class; and a number of interdisciplinary strategic research areas have been developed.

“I am well acquainted with ASU’s growth and tremendous accomplishments in last 15 years, especially under Provost Glick’s leadership,” said James Rogers, chancellor of the Nevada System of Higher Education. “That is the kind of leadership we strongly believe our system needs. That is what the search committee and the Regents felt. I am honored and delighted that he would accept our offer and join the university.”

Prior to his ASU appointment, Glick was provost at Iowa State University for three years, also serving the Iowa campus as interim president for eight months. He has actively promoted the careers of minorities and women in academia and encourages the innovative and appropriate use of computers and programs to support both learning and research.

In 2004 Glick was named senior fellow for the EDUCAUSE Center for Applied Research for his expertise in the integration of technology into learning environments. Glick advises on ongoing ECAR research, particularly on the relationship between institutional process performance and information technology investment, leadership and culture.

In April 2006 Glick announced he would step down as ASU provost. He was inducted as University Professor, the first person ever to hold that title at Arizona State University. University Professor is the highest academic rank at ASU and is awarded in recognition for lifetime achievement.

A search for Glick’s replacement as ASU provost is in progress. Three finalists for the position were announced earlier this month.
