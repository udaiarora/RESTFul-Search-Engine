SUBJECT TO CHANGE WITHOUT NOTICE

Students who have applied for admission to ASU and are classified as nonresidents must petition for a review of residency through the Residency Classification Office. Petition forms will be available two days prior to the beginning of the petition period. The Arizona Board of Regents guidelines require clear and convincing evidence of residency for tuition purposes.

Residency Classification is a complex issue. Each petition is individually reviewed. Not all circumstances can be addressed in this brief summary. If you wish to discuss your individual case, contact the Residency Classification Section. Noncitizens who wish to petition are encouraged to contact the Residency Classification Office, since not all visa categories qualify.

The term "Arizona resident" for tuition purposes is independent from other definitions of Arizona residency. A person who has an Arizona driver's license or who is an Arizona resident for tax, voting, state aid purposes, etc. may not necessarily be considered an Arizona resident for tuition purposes. For tuition purposes, a resident is someone who meets the requirements set forth by the Arizona Board of Regents, which are reflective of state law.

It is the responsibility of the student to initiate the petition process. This is to include submitting a petition and all supporting documents by the deadline date.

The deadline to submit a petition for reclassification is firm. Failure to do so constitutes a waiver of your right to petition for that semester. In addition, residency reclassifications are not retroactive.

Students may petition for residency according to the following schedule.

Students who petition for reclassification subscribe and swear to the following statement: "

All statements, information, and evidence presented are true and complete. I understand that if I provide false information, if I make a material misrepresentation or mission in connection with my Petition for Change of Residency, or if I make any effort to fraudulently claim Arizona as my state of legal domicile, it will jeopardize my case for residency and subject me to disciplinary action, dismissal for the University...and may result in civil and criminal liability. I hereby grant permission for ASU representatives to verify any supporting evidence submitted with this petition."

Any petitioner found to have intentionally provided false or misleading information will be referred to Student Judicial Affairs for disciplinary action. Student Judicial Affairs

FAQ's regarding residency

Independent Students

You must couple twelve months of physical presence in Arizona with two tax years of financial independence and twelve months of objective evidence of intent to make Arizona your permanent home. All of the evidence is weighed under the presumption that a non-resident student's presence in Arizona is primarily for the purpose of education and not to establish domicile, and that decisions of an individual as to the intent to establish domicile are generally made after the completion of an education and not before. 

1. Physical Presence (12 Months)(You must be at least 18 at the beginning of the domicile year).

To be eligible for classification as a resident for tuition purposes, you must provide clear and convincing evidence of continuous physical presence in the state of Arizona for the 12 months which precede the semester in question. The 12 month period is referred to as a domicile year. The domicile year for future semesters is as follows:

2. Intent to Establish a Domicile (12 months)

Your evidence of intent to be a resident of this state is demonstrated by the absence of ties to your former state of residence. Actions used to indicate intent must be accomplished at the beginning of and maintained throughout the domicile year. If implementation of these steps is delayed, the start of your one year domicile period will be delayed. All requirements must be demonstrated for one full year before resident status may be granted. Samples of indicia are: 

  1. Arizona driver's license general information
  2. Arizona motor vehicle registration
  3. Employment history
  4. Transfer of major banking services to Arizona
  5. Eligible immigration (visa) status if you are a non-citizen*
  6. Arizona voter's registration ( Voter Registration Request Form )
  7. Change in permanent address on all pertinent records
  8. Filing of Arizona state income taxes
  9. Additional documents, as requested

* Visa holders will need an I - 485 receipt - dated on or before the beginning of the domicile year.

3. Financial Independence/Source of Support (24 months)

You need to be self-supporting (financially independent) and not claimed as a dependent by your parents or anyone else for two tax years. Financial independence means that you met your own expenses (minimum of $8,000 per year) and any tuition costs incurred during the two years immediately proceeding the semester from self generated funds under your control. Generally a student is considered dependent on parents while in high school regardless of how taxes are filed. Loans from friends, family, etc..., PLUS loans, cosigned loans, gifts and cash earnings are NOT acceptable. Auto insurance policy must be separate from parents' policy. Provide explanations if health insurance is not separate from parents. Typical documentation to be considered should include: 

  1. Paystubs
  2. W-2s
  3. Financial Aid
  4. Documented Inheritance
  5. Veteran's or Social Security Benefits
  6. Trust Funds (established prior to high school)
  7. Accident/Insurance Settlements
  8. Federal/Arizona State Income Tax
     
Exceptions to the Twelve Month Rule

All of these exceptions MUST be verified through the Residency Classification Office. There are only six approved exceptions to the twelve-month rule. You may be eligible for resident status without waiting twelve months, if one of the following applies on or before the last day of regular registration:

TOP

Dependent

If you are a dependent of parents who are permanently domiciled in Arizona, you may possibly qualify for reclassification without waiting 12 months. Both parents (unless divorced) must claim Arizona as their legal and permanent domicile. Students who are over 18 years of age when the semester begins must be claimed as a dependent for the most recent tax year. For students under 18 years, the domicile of the parent with legal custody will determine residency.

TOP

Company Transfer

If you are domiciled in Arizona and are an employee or spouse of an employee transferred to Arizona by his/her employer for employment purposes, you may possibly qualify for reclassification without waiting 12 months. This exception does NOT apply in the following situations: 

  1. New Hire
  2. Self-Employment
  3. Family Owned/Operated Business
  4. Recruited by an Arizona employer
  5. Employee-Initiated Transfer
This Company Transfer provision exempts the qualifying applicant from the 12 months physical presence requirement, but not the intent to make Arizona your permanent home, and the two years financial independence requirements. If financial independence is in question, the applicant will be asked to document two tax years of independence. In addition, the employee transferred needs to be a continuous full-time benefit-eligible employee of same company out-of-state to the same company in Arizona.
TOP

Military

If you are not domiciled in Arizona, but are a member of the U.S. Armed Forces stationed in Arizona pursuant to active duty military orders, including AZ National Guard and Reserves, or you are a member's spouse or dependent child as defined in A.R.S. Section 43-1001, you may possibly qualify for reclassification without waiting twelve months. As a full-time student, you do not lose resident status while in continuous attendance towards the degree for which currently enrolled, if military service is discontinued. In addition, if you are domiciled in Arizona immediately prior to becoming a member of the U. S. Armed Forces, you will not necessarily lose resident status because of your absence while a member of the U. S. Armed Forces. However, Arizona law requires filing state income taxes while on active duty military to retain eligibility for resident status upon your return. For honorably discharged military personnel who have changed their legal residence to Arizona one year prior to their discharge, please contact the Residency Classification Office directly.

TOP

Spouse of an AZ Resident

The person's spouse has established domicile in this state for at least one year and has demonstrated intent and financial independence and is entitled to claim the student as an exemption for state and federal tax purposes, but maintained a domicile in this state. If the person is a non citizen, the person must be in an eligible visa status pursuant to federal law to classify as an in-state student for tuition purposes. Contact the Residency Classification Office for further assistance.

 Native American

If you are not domiciled in Arizona, but are a member of a Native American tribe whose reservation land lies partially in Arizona and another state, and you are a resident of such a reservation, you may qualify for reclassification without waiting 12 months.

To qualify under this exception, the student must meet both stipulations:

    1. is an enrolled member of an American Indian tribe whose contiguous reservation land lies partially in Arizona and an adjoining state and

    2. is residing on that reservation land.

This exception does not apply to tribal members who are currently living off their reservation land base. This exception is a geographical rather than a cultural exception based on Indian national sovereignty laws.

TOP

Teachers Under Contract / Full-Time Non-Certified Classroom Aides

If you are employed by an Arizona school district and either on contract to teach full-time or are a full-time non-certified classroom aide, you may be eligible to pay resident tuition for courses required for certification.

The teacher exception is a waiver. We do not actually change your residency. It is the only residency exception that must be renewed every semester. To qualify for the teacher exception, you must be employed by an Arizona school district and either on contract as a full-time teacher or are a full-time, non-certified classroom aide. The wavier only applies to courses that are required for an initial Arizona Teacher Certification. You should contact Residency Classification Office to discuss your situation.
