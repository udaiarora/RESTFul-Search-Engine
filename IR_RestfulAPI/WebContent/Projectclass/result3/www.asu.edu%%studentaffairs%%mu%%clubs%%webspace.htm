Web and Email

Club E-mails

To create an email account for your club, go to http://www.asu.edu/askit/. Request a mailbox name that is 5-8 characters long and an alternate name just in case your first choice is not available. (i.e. ASIA@asu.edu or AsiaStudies@asu.edu) The Email request also needs to contain the names, email, ASU ID number or ASURITE of both the person who will act as the primary contact or owner of the mailbox and a secondary contact. This is usually the Club Advisor and a student. ASU Information Technology will contact the owner of the mailbox after it has been created.

Club Listserv

To create a listserv, submit a Request for Computer Access form. The form is available at the Computer Accounts Services website, the SORC in the MU, or the second floor of the Computer Commons.
Section 1
Should be completed, signed and dated by the person who will own the list ID. This person can be faculty, staff, or a student.
Section 2
Check the box marked NEW LISTSERV. Create a listserv ID (5-8 characters long), an alternate listserv name, and write out the purpose of the list.
Section 3
Leave blank
Section 4
Your club advisor MUST sign this section as a secondary contact for the Listserv. This person will be contacted if the owner of the list leaves the University so that we can continue uninterrupted service to the listserv members. Information on the administration and use of Listserv lists is available at http://lists.asu.edu/. If you need additional assistant with the administration or use of this listserv, please refer your questions to the Electronic Messaging Team.

myASU Group

To create a discussion group using Blackboard and MyASU, submit a request to myASU-q@asu.edu. Create a group name that is easy to remember and a password. The Email request needs to contain the names, email, ASU ID number or ASURITE of the person who will act as the primary contact. You will receive an email from MyASU After the discussion group has been created.

Getting Web Space

A student organization must first be registered with SORC to be eligible for web space. If the organization is NOT registered, please visit the SORC office located on the 3rd floor of the Memorial Union and fill out a registration form.

If your organization is registered for the current school year, please complete the Web Space Request Form (MS Word). You will be notified by the SORC webmaster within 2 weeks to confirm your web space.

Registered student organizations will be assigned 10 MB of space. If this limit is exceeded a warning will be sent along with a deadline to remove the excessive files. SORC will delete any unused web space, web space belonging to unregistered student organizations, and/or any files that violate the ASU Web Use Policy.

Please read ASU's Computer, Internet, and Electronic Communications Policy (ACD 125). All organizations using web space through SORC must abide by this policy as well as any other related policies regarding student organizations.

Changing Webmaster

New webmasters and other persons responsible for updating web pages can be granted access rights to an organization's space by completing the Web Space Request Form (MS Word) and submitting it to the SORC office located on the 3rd floor of the Memorial Union. After submitting a request, you will be notified by the SORC webmaster within 2 weeks confirming your new webmaster.

How to Transfer Files

Choose one of the following methods to upload your files using either an FTP client or the ASU Emma E-mail system. A downloadable version of an FTP client is available for your use if you use it for educational purposes.
  • How to Use an SSH- FTP (Secure Shell Client) to Transfer Files
    (For use on and off campus with an FTP program)
  • How to Use Emma to Transfer Files
    (For use on and off campus with any internet connection)

SSH to transfer Files

Are you using FTP to transfer your files?
Some of the common FTP programs like WS_FTP won't work any more with ASU server, because of security regulations. One of the alternative options is to use SSH - Secure File Transfer. Remember that this copy is free and can be downloaded from the ASU website at http://www.asu.edu/security/software/html/ssh/index.htm.

HOW TO DOWNLOAD AND INSTALL SSH : Secure Shell File Transfer Client

  1. Go to http://www.asu.edu/security/software/html/ssh/index.htm.
  2. Click on the "Download" tab at the top of the table. You will be asked to login with your ASURITE UserID and password before downloading software.
  3. Click on the version of the SSH software you will need (depending on operating system).
  4. Click "Save" and select your desktop as the location to save the file.
  5. Double-click on the installation file. Follow the instructions and install the SSH client.

HOW TO ACCESS AND TRANSFER FILES from your student organization webspace

  1. Open Secure File Transfer Client from the start menu, and you will get this window displayed.
  2. Click on Quick Connect (at the top left on your screen). Enter the host name as general.asu.edu and username will be your ASURITE id
  3. Now click on connect and enter your password and you will see the contents of your M drive space.
  4. Click on Operation tab (in the top) and click on Go to Folder
  5. Change the folder name to your organization AFS space location, and click OK
  6. Now you can see your files in your student organization webspace. You can upload and download multiple files using the arrow keys on the top. You can even drag and drop files on to your server space.

To Transfer Files Using Emma

You can also use emma, ASU's e-mail system to transfer your files and update your organization's webpage.
  1. Log on to Emma, and then enter your ASURITE ID and password.
  2. Once inside look on the left hand display and choose the "AFS Manager" option.
  3. Under the heading "Current Directory" should appear your personal space on the ASU public server. Change that "Current Directory" to the following...
    /afs/asu.edu/www/studentprgms/orgs/your_organization_folder

    Then click the open folder button located on the right. Then you should be in your groups designated folder.

    Note: Only persons using authorized ASURITE ID's can access a groups folder. Access is granted through the SORC office if requests are made in person or via the online Webmaster Access Form on this site.
  4. Once inside your group's directory, you can upload files by clicking the "Upload" icon, then all you have to do is click the "browse" button, find the file from your computer that you wish to upload, and then press the "upload" button on the left.

    You can also delete or move files by checking the box next to the file's name and then clicking the "Delete" or "Move" icons respectively.
Your web address (URL) will be...
http://www.asu.edu/clubs/your_organization_folder

Your directory (location on ASU's web server) will be...
/afs/asu.edu/www/studentprgms/orgs/your_organization_folder

This should get you through the rest of the process. If you have any other questions please contact the SORC office.
