December 4, 2003

Arizona's outlook for 2004: time to break out the sunglasses

A panel of Arizona's leading business and economics professionals expect that 2004 will bring the best economic news the state has seen in four years, according to the current issue of the Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast.

"The consensus forecast from the Blue Chip panel of Arizona economy-watchers calls for personal income in 2004 to increase by 6.5 percent," said Lee McPheters, contributing editor. "This will be the best performance since 2000, and a significant improvement over the sluggish 4.0 percent gain recorded in 2002."

Employment is projected to rise by 3.4 percent in 2004, translating into 78,000 new jobs in the state, McPheters reported. "Although the rate of growth will be nearly double that expected for 2003, the labor market expansion will be below the average 4.8 percent of the years 1995-2000," he noted.

The Arizona Blue Chip forecasters are projecting that retail sales will increase by 5.8 percent in the year ahead, according to McPheters. And two stalwart Arizona indicators - population growth and housing permits - are expected to continue to be robust in 2004.

Elsewhere in the Arizona Blue Chip, Editor Tracy Clark surveyed the consensus panel on its first look at 2005. The signals for both 2004 and 2005 economic performance are mostly encouraging, Clark said. "There will be two major forces working on consumer spending in the months to come," he noted. "The positive force will be greater consumer confidence as the job market improves, along with increased income as the unemployment rate declines. The negative force will come from the high debt levels consumers are carrying."

The Bank One Arizona Blue Chip Economic Forecast includes the consensus forecast of economists from 19 groups and institutions. It is published by the Bank One Economic Outlook Center, an affiliate of the L. William Seidman Research Institute in the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. For information, call (480) 965-7858.
