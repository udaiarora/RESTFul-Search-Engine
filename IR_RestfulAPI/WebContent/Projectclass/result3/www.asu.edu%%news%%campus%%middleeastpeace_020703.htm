February 7, 2003

Israeli, Palestinian experts to discuss possibility of peace

Representing both sides of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, four participants in past peace negotiations will meet at ASU on Feb. 9 and discuss future possibilities for restoring the peace in two public workshops.

Discussing issues that are key to a resolution of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict are Manuel Hassassian, executive vice president of Bethlehem University; Rashid Khalidi, director of the Center for International Studies at the University of Chicago; Menachem Klein, expert adviser to Israel's Minister of Internal Security and Foreign Affairs; and Daniel Seidmann, an authority on East Jerusalem issues.

"These are people who have all taken part in the previous peace negotiations, who all know each other and are still in communication," said Jess Schwartz, professor of modern Hebrew literature. "The purpose of the workshops is to show that people can still talk reasonably to each other, and are still working to resolve the conflict peacefully."

The meeting, "After Oslo: New Strategies for Middle East Peace," begins at 10 a.m. with a two-hour public workshop on the subject of "Can a Peace Process be Reinstated?" and continues at 2 p.m. with a session on "Overcoming Stumbling Blocks." The free workshops are in the ASU Education Lecture Hall, located just north of Gammage Auditorium.

The workshops are part of a national series of similar events scheduled to take place in Amherst, Mass., Washington, D.C. and San Francisco. The ASU event is sponsored by the ASU Jewish Studies Program and ASU's Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict, the Jewish Federation-Israel Center and Hillel; and was also made possible in part by a gift from the Marshall Fund of Arizona.

"Many of us have been concerned about developments on campuses nationwide regarding the Arab-Israeli conflict where rhetoric stands in place of knowledge and the complexities of a settlement are not understood," said Jack Kugelmass, director of ASU's Jewish Studies Program. "I thought Jewish Studies should play a proactive role here by taking what is in the public mind and using it as an opportunity to teach - to make the public knowledgeable about something very few people understand."
"This workshop promises to be a very informative discussion on the current state of the Israeli/Palestinian conflict, and possibilities for its resolution," said Linell Cady, director of ASU's Center for the Study of Religion and Conflict.

For information on the workshop, call (480) 727-6906.

Hathaway, with the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, can be reached at (480) 965-6375 or (Hathaway@asu.edu).
