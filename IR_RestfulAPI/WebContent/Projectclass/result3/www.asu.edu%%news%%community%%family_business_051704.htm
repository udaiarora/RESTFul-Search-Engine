Arizona State University's Center for the Advancement of Small Business Presents "Families in Business Together: How to Have a Productive Family Argument"

"How to Have a Productive Family Argument," - a seminar presented by the Family Business Forum at the W. P. Carey School of Business on Wednesday, May 26 - will feature Earl and Suzanne de Berge of Phoenix-based Behavior Research.

The seminar was designed exclusively for family members who are in business together in order to help them strengthen and improve their working relationships. It will be facilitated by Dr. Robert Cottor and Sharon Cottor, M.S.W., of Cottor Associates, P.C., who are among the nation's leading experts in family business.

The Family Business Forum is a program of the Center for the Advancement of Small Business (CASB) in the

W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. It was designed to bring together family businesses across generations to share ideas, explore new possibilities and plan for their success and survival.

Behavior Research Center began investigating the small business sector in 1990 with its semi-annual BusinessTRACKT study series. BusinessTRACKT has demonstrated that companies with three to 100 employees are a vital segment of the economic picture, constituting roughly 95 percent of all commercial enterprises. Among these small businesses, from 75 to 80 percent are either wholly owned or controlled by family.

Earl de Berge, the chairman and director of research, is known nationally for his public opinion research. De Berge pioneered the development of regional, non-partisan, public information polls. He has been director of research since 1969 and editor and director of the Rocky Mountain Poll since 1976. Suzanne de Berge is chief financial officer.

The seminar will be from 7:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. at the Wells Fargo Conference Center in Phoenix. Tickets are $49 for the first family member and $25 for each additional member. Continental breakfast and refreshments will be served. Reservations can be made by calling 480-965-3962. Media seating is available. Contact Liz Farquhar at 480-965-7774.

W. P. Carey School of Business

The W. P. Carey School of Business provides quality undergraduate, masters and doctoral programs that draw upon a dynamic learning environment to prepare students at all levels for the future. Internationally recognized faculty enrich the curriculum with their latest research findings, and deliver this knowledge in a technology-rich environment. Ranked 21st in the nation by U.S. News & World Report , the undergraduate program features Business Honors comprised of some of the best students in the country. The MBA, ranked 17th in the nation among public programs, combines business fundamentals with marketplace issues and trends, giving graduates the knowledge and experience to excel in a global economy. W. P. Carey School of Business alumni are lifelong learners connected to the school and each other through an active alumni community and executive education opportunities.
