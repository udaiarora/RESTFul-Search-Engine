1. Submit MMR Immunization Records Form
You must have verification of two MMR (measles [rubeola] /mumps/rubella) immunizations on record at ASU, or you cannot register for classes. Freshmen should complete this step before attending ASU Orientation. Use the ASU Immunization Records Form that ASU sends to you with your admission certificate or print the ASU Immunization Form.

To verify that ASU has received and recorded your immunization documentation before you attend ASU Orientation, go to ASU Interactive, log in, and view your ASU Orientation Summary. Please be aware that it takes approximately two days from the time ASU receives your documentation to record it in the system. Therefore, you must send/fax your MMR documentation well in advance of your scheduled Orientation program.

2. Get Connected
You must activate your ASURITE User ID at www.asu.edu/asurite.

The ASURITE User ID allows you to access:

ASU Interactive www.asu.edu/interactive
You will need to access ASU Interactive during Orientation to register for classes. In the future, you will use ASU Interactive to register, pay tuition/fees, check grades, order transcripts and more.

myASU my.asu.edu
Customizable content, courses and online communities are available to students through an online portal, much like "my Yahoo!" only for ASU.

E-mail Service
Students should activate their @asu.edu e-mail account to have fully supported ASU e-mail service and access to their e-mail on the Web through my.asu.edu. You can add ASU e-mail service to your ASURITE User ID at www.asu.edu/asurite. More information is available at www.asu.edu/mailbox.

New students will have access to the following services at the start of their first semester:

  • University Technology Office (UTO, ASU's IT department) Computing Sites for Windows and Mac workstations.
  • A file storage allotment of 5 megabytes on the computing network.
  • General computing for accessing ASU servers from off campus.

Students may choose to add more services to their ASURITE User ID through www.asu.edu/asurite anytime after the beginning of their first semester, including Web Hosting, where students create and maintain their own ASU Web page. www.asu.edu/jukebox

3. Placement Exams

English Placement
Request that your official ACT or SAT test scores be sent to ASU. If you did not take the ACT or SAT, you may take the English Placement exam to determine the appropriate First-Year Composition course. (Contact University Testing Services at www.asu.edu/uts or 480.965.7146.)

You will be advised to register for a First-Year Composition course to meet the First-Year Composition Requirement based on your ACT English or SAT Verbal score.

If you have an ACT English score of 26 or higher, or an SAT verbal score of 620 or higher, you may take ENG 105 to fulfill your First-Year Composition requirement. Students may also qualify for ENG 105 by achieving appropriate scores on the CLEP General Examination in English Composition with Essay or the CLEP Subject Examination in College Composition with Essay. For CLEP Examination information, contact University Testing Services at www.asu.edu/uts or 480.965.7146.

If you have an ACT English score of 19-26, or an SAT verbal score of 470-620, you will take a two-semester sequence to fulfill your First-Year Composition requirement. The courses in that sequence are ENG 101 and ENG 102.

If you have an ACT English score of 18 or lower, or an SAT verbal score of 460 or lower, you will take a three-semester sequence to fulfill ASU's First-Year Composition requirement. The courses in that sequence are WAC 101, ENG 101 and ENG 102.

Advanced Placement (AP) credit and Transfer English Credit
For Advanced Placement (AP) credit you must submit a request to the College Board to send your test scores to ASU.

For transfer credit you must have each college you have attended send your official transcripts directly to ASU.

If you have AP credit or college credit for an English class you took at another college or university, ask your advisor about how that course will transfer to ASU.

Mathematics Placement

Unified Math Placement Exam
All students are required to complete the Unified Math Placement Exam. You must take the Unified Math Placement Exam online 72 hours before your ASU Orientation Program. Your math class will be determined by your performance on the mandatory Unified Math Placement Exam. Remember to bring your score printout to ASU Orientation. Note the practice exam will not be accepted as an official score. Visit https://fym.la.asu.edu:8443/placement/servlet/getASUCookie2 to take the exam.

Based on your exam score, your academic advisor will provide information about math requirements for your major at ASU Orientation.

Calculus Placement Exam
If your major is in:

Biochemistry, Biological Sciences, Chemistry, Earth and Space Exploration, Economics, Engineering, Geological Sciences, Mathematics, Meteorology-Climatology, Physics, Psychology (B.S. degree), Science and Technology, Secondary Education Mathematics, Secondary Education Physics, Secondary Education Chemistry,

or if you plan to register for Calculus with Analytic Geometry I (MAT 270), Calculus for Life Sciences (MAT 251) or Calculus for Engineers I (MAT 265), you must arrive at ASU Orientation 45 minutes early to take the mandatory Calculus Placement Exam. You will not be able to register for Calculus I without an acceptable score on the Calculus Placement Exam. Calculators are prohibited; be sure you arrive on time to take advantage of the full time allotted for the exam.

Advanced Placement (AP) credit and Transfer Math credit
For Advanced Placement (AP) credit you must submit a request to the College Board to send your test scores to ASU.

For transfer credit you must have each college you have attended send your official transcripts directly to ASU.

If you have AP credit or college credit for a math class you took at another college or university, ask your advisor about how that course will transfer to ASU.

Foreign Language Placement
If you plan to register for French, German or Spanish at ASU, you must determine your level by taking an online placement test at www.asu.edu/languages/lpinfo.html.

For all other languages: if you have never studied the language before, you should enroll in a 101 class. If you have some experience with the language, consult with an Academic Advisor and/or an instructor in the language who can administer a mini-placement exam and offer advice. For a list of faculty members, go to www.asu.edu/languages.

4. Critical Tracking and Academic Roadmaps
As a member of the fall 2007 freshman class, you will experience a new way of managing your academic experience. ASU is introducing Critical Tracking, which will give you the tools to successfully follow your academic roadmap to graduation. Critical Tracking will help you:

  • Learn more about graduation requirements for your major.
  • Plan for and schedule appropriate courses in the correct sequence to maximize your success.
  • Monitor progress toward your degree.
  • Find out how your courses may fit into other majors if you decide to change your major.

Your college or school will provide you with more information at Orientation.

5. Special Needs
If you have special needs contact New Student Programs at 480.965.2880 or orientation@asu.edu at least 10 business days before your scheduled program. Special needs include: disabled parking, assistance with tours, language interpretation and/or a special meal.
