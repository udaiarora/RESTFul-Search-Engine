November 13, 2006

W. P. Carey School adds Kauffman

The W. P. Carey School of Business is proud to welcome Rob Kauffman, one of the leading information systems research professors in the world, as the fifth W. P. Carey Chair.

With his selection, Kauffman joins the ranks of Edward Prescott, Ruth Bolton, James Ohlson and Robert Hoskisson as one of the W. P. Carey Chairs. According to Philip Regier, deputy dean of the W. P. Carey School of Business, there is no more than one W. P. Carey Chair per department, and it is the pre-eminent chair in those departments.

The W. P. Carey Chair is reserved for leading scholars who demonstrate impact in their respective fields, and who make continuing and significant contributions in their fields of study, Regier says.

Kauffman joins the W. P. Carey School faculty following a distinguished career at the University of Minnesota , where he directed the MIS Research Center at the Carlson School of Management, and served as department chair (from 2000 to 2006) and professor of information systems. He also has an outstanding teaching and mentoring record with doctoral and master's degree students at the Carlson School of Management.

Kauffman has been ranked as one of the top five IS faculty worldwide, according to the Journal of the Association for Information Systems.

?Rob Kauffman is one of the top five information systems professors in the world, and arguably the top professor in the world in terms of interdisciplinary research,? says Robert St. Louis, chair of the information systems department at the W. P. Carey School .

Kauffman's research interests extend from information systems to other areas of business ? including marketing, supply chain management and finance, among others.

?He is joining the school with a mandate to broaden the reach of information systems, by partnering with supply chain management, finance, health care and bioinformatics,? Regier says.

Before joining the University of Minnesota , Kauffman served as a visiting scholar for the Department of Economic Research at the Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia , and served as an associate professor at the Stern School of Business, New York University. Kauffman earned his doctorate at Carnegie Mellon University , and holds degrees from Cornell University and the University of Colorado .

?In teaching, Rob Kauffman was the adviser for more than half of the Ph.D. students in the Carlson School of Management,? St. Louis says. ?In research, he has published more than 40 journal papers during the last seven years, and he won the ?best paper' award at every major information systems conference.?

?The Information Systems department is incredibly fortunate to have been able to recruit him. Our department is currently ranked 11th among all IS departments in the United States by U.S. News & World Report, and the addition of Rob to our program is almost certain to put our program into the top 10,? St. Louis says.
