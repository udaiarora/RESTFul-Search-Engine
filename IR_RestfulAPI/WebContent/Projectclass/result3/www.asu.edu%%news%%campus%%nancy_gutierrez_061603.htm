June 16, 2003

Gutierrez accepts ASU vice provost position

Effective Aug. 1, Nancy Gutierrez will serve as vice provost in the Office of Executive Vice President and Provost of the University Milton Glick.

Gutierrez, associate dean for academic personnel in the College of Liberal Arts & Sciences (CLAS), will have a varied role, with duties including personnel issues and academic issues, such as a refinement of the university's general education requirements. Gutierrez joins vice provosts Ruth Jones, Gail Hackett and Kathleen Church in Glick's office.

"I am looking forward to joining the provost's team," says Gutierrez. "I have worked with the people in the provost's office for the last 10 years in various capacities and have been extremely impressed with their efforts. I am eager to help move the university in new and exciting directions under President Crow and Provost Glick."

For the past three years, Gutierrez has served as associate dean with CLAS. During her tenure, Gutierrez developed a new faculty seminar series and a series of professional development workshops. In conjunction with the Provost's office, she helped develop a new university-wide procedure for assisting new hires find university positions for their partners and a new policy covering alternate duty assignments for family leave. Gutierrez also served as co-chairperson of the Chapter Four Committee for the university's Accreditation Self Study and as a member on the Provost's Personnel Advisory Committee.

"Nancy has shown an ability to excel in complicated situations and provide strong leadership," says Glick. "Her work with various personnel issues has made ASU a better place for all of us. I know she will continue to help move us forward as a university."

Prior to her time as associate dean, Gutierrez served as English Department chairwoman from 1994 to 2000. Previously she served as the department's associate, acting and interim chair.

Gutierrez has served on a number of college and university committees, including the Task Force for the University for the Next Century, the Task Force on Full-Time Non-Permanent Faculty, the NCAA Program Review Committee, the Intellectual Property Committee, the CLAS Interdisciplinary Studies Committee, the CLAS Strategic Planning & Resource Allocation Committee, and numerous university hiring committees.

An active literary scholar and faculty member in the English Department, Gutierrez's area of academic expertise is Renaissance English literature, including Shakespeare and women in early modern culture. She is past president of the Rocky Mountain Medieval and Renaissance Association and former chairwoman of the awards committee for the international Society for the Study of Early Modern Women.

Her new book "'Shall She Famish Then?' Female Food Refusal in Early Modern England," will be released this fall.
