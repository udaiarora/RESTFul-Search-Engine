Facilities

Question: Do you feel that the library facilities are conducive to your needs?

Noble study rooms do not have enough insulation between you can hear groups in the next room, they are also stuffy. There are no places to sleep anywhere on campus, both the student union and the library have removed spaces. When you're on campus for many hours straight you need a place to rest.

 

Library is so overwhelming, I needed a guide before I got the guided tour. I asked other students about the lilbrary and they don't know much about it either. It's so overbearing and overpowering; staff is helpful but they're not there to give tours (like we to locate a couch!) Students don't come to the library because it's overwhelming.

 

I found it easy to use the library but obviously not everyone has had that experience. But I do find it appalling to see people sleeping in the library. Is this the appropriate place to sleep?

 

Where else can we sleep?

 

I'm concerned that I don't see more recycling in the library; there are no recycling places. Can printers do double-sided copying to save paper? Also, there's no microsoft software on the computers.

 

Yeah, we need WORD .

 

Carrels are nice in Noble, but not those fabric carrels in Hayden; in the future buy carrels that can be cleaned.

 

I like ILL here; we get pdfs (electronic delivery) and it's free. It's not the same at other universities (told story about friends at U of South Dakota who have to go to Nebraska to get stuff ?because USD shut down their ILL ?)

 

Can we get dissertations thru ILLI heard on East campus you can keep the dissertation and not pay (as we do here)

Ginny Sylvester explained more about the Interlibrary Loan service, which obtains copies of articles, books, dissertations, etc for students.

 

ILL is on par here. Most libraries have paper request forms. I like to see more library hours especially during holidays, intersessions, and spring break. The hours need to be longer - like one library open 24/7 ? you could have a different library each time, it wouldn't have to be the same one. Or at least extend the library hours during those times.

 

Ditto ? lot's of grad students are studying then. Does it take lots of staff and $$ to do this? ASU West has self-checkout.

 

Dean Sherrie Schmidt explained about the costs involved with moving to 24/5 for Hayden and the security issue.

 

Library: For these extended hours, do you need to get to specific print resources, use a computer or need a quiet place to study?

 

I need books.

 

I need a place to study

 

Even if you only had one library open, it would be OK if items from the other libraries could be retrieved during those times. The group study rooms in Noble need signs that state the policy of groups having priority over singles ? it helps when groups are trying to get an individual out of the room. (Another student ditto-ed) Bathrooms on the 1 st floor not cleaned enough ? the mess is a regular occurrence There was water on the floor in the 3 rd floor men's room. Need more extension cords for laptops.

 

I requested a book was listed overdue 3/16 and I got a call that item was missing but couldn't put in system ? shouldn't the system state when a book is missing?

 

Ginny Sylvester explained that usually does have indication but should say ?lost and billed?, but perhaps since 3/16 was simply overdue. But it is true that the catalog should indicate status more clearly.

 

Need a cafeteria or vending machines in Noble, too. Why was café put in Hayden? Is there more traffic there? Lot 's of buildings around Noble ? should be able to generate business.

Dean Sherrie Schmidt explained that more traffic in Hayden, president wishes and vendor requires a certain amount of traffic.
