Arizona State System for Information on Student Transfer (ASSIST)
Status Report as of December 31, 1999
John D. Porter, Project Director

Background

ASSIST (formerly known as the Transfer Student Data Warehouse or TSDW) is a relational database containing enrollment records from Arizona?s universities and community colleges. The purpose of ASSIST is to provide community colleges and universities information for (1) completing the Graduation Rate Survey and (2) assessing transfer articulation effectiveness in Arizona. ASU agreed to create ASSIST using data warehousing technology as part of the Arizona Transfer Articulation Support System (ATASS). The project was funded as a proof of concept for three years beginning July 1, 1998. The ultimate success of the project depends on institutions providing accurate and complete data. ASU?s goals are to create the technical environment in the first year, resolve security and data issues in the second year, and focus on reporting and training issues in the third year.

There are two committees involved in the ASSIST project. The Steering Committee is a policy group responsible to the Academic Program Articulation Steering Committee (APASC) for the ASSIST project. This Committee makes decisions concerning the use and content of ASSIST. The second group is the Technical Committee. They are a working group responsible for providing quality data and developing standard reports from ASSIST. The Steering committee meets semi-annually in Spring and Fall. The Technical Committee meets as often as needed.

Current Status

The last meeting of the ASSIST Steering/Technical committee was September 24, 1999. At this meeting the two committees approved the Security Plan developed by ASU and recommended the plan be forwarded to APASC for approval. ASU also asked the Steering committee to ask each community college to submit individual course and grade data to ASSIST. The Steering committee remanded that decision to APASC with a recommendation for approval.

On October 14, 1999, APASC met and approved the ASSIST Security Plan and recommended the plan be forwarded to the Board of Regents and the State Community College Board of Directors with a recommendation for approval. APASC also approved the Steering committee?s recommendation that each community college contribute course and grade information to ASSIST. (The universities currently provide this information.)

On November 19, 1999 the Arizona Board of Regents and the State Board of Directors for Community Colleges of Arizona in separate meetings approved the ASSIST Security Plan and adopted the Joint Resolution making ASSIST an official reporting requirement for community colleges and universities in Arizona. Since separate district boards govern the community colleges, the Security Plan and Joint Resolution will be presented to each district board for ratification in the future. The State Board of Directors will coordinate getting the plan on the agenda of each district board. The Security Plan ensures that the privacy rights of students whose records are included in the database are protected.

The date for submitting another year of data to ASSIST was December 1999. As of the end of the month, ASU had received data from Cochise and Central Arizona Community Colleges, and the University of Arizona. Cochise and Central are providing data to ASSIST for the first time. In the coming year, ASSIST will contain data from the three universities and seven of the ten community colleges. This represents approximately ninety five percent of the available data. We anticipate receiving the remaining data when the district boards approve the Security Plan and adopt the Joint Resolution.

Unfortunately, Pao-seh Lin, the technical analyst who wrote the matching program and implemented the ASSIST prototype has resigned her position due to a terminal illness. Of course, this saddens us, but the project will continue to move on. Because of Pao-Seh ?s efforts over the last year and a half, the ASSIST database contains more than 750,000 student records and 125,000 transfer matches. ASSIST will be her legacy to the State of Arizona.

Plans for the Next Three Months

  1. Meet on February 4, 2000 with the Technical Committee to begin designing standard reports from ASSIST.
  2. Meet with the Steering Committee on March 30, 2000 in Bullhead City to review the status of the project and gain approval of the standard report formats.
  3. Coordinate presenting the ASSIST Security Plan and Joint Resolution to the ten community college district boards.
  4. Hire a new technical analyst.
  5. Complete the data exchange for the last academic year.
  6. Load Cochise and Central Arizona Community College data into ASSIST.
  7. Revise the web site and release for customer use.
