Reference Department Policy and Guidelines
Covering Flexible Workweek Schedules

Approved by Ref. Dept. Consensus 6/8/95


I. Definition:

Flexible workweek schedules are any work-schedules significantly outside of a "standard" 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM or 9:00 AM -6:00 PM 8 hour/day, 40 hour/week work schedule. They are defined as a formal arrangement/agreement to alter a workweek schedule for a specified period of time. This definition is not meant to include telecommuting, or such flex-time as is necessary to accommodate regularly scheduled evening or weekend reference desk schedules, evening or weekend instructional sessions, etc., (covered by current "comp time policy"). Also excluded are flexible schedules requested to accommodate "special" personal or organizational circumstances which might require alternative/ flexible scheduling for a brief and limited period of time.

II. Guiding Principles for requesting/approving flexible workweek schedules:

1. Flexible workweeks are not an individual's right; they should offer a positive benefit to the department as well as to the individual requestor and should have a minimal negative impact on departmental and team operations.

2. There is a defined limit on the length of time for which a flexible workweek schedule will be considered/approved; flexible schedules will be approved for no more than a semester's time. When approval has expired, requesters must "re-apply", following the procedures for application outlined in section III, below.

3. 4/40 workweeks (i.e., 10 hour days) are discouraged, but will be considered to the extent that they satisfy the first "principle" above; 9/80 work-schedules (9 hour days) are a better alternative.

4. Flexible schedules that regularly require a Friday or a Monday off are discouraged, but will be considered to the extent that they satisfy the first "principle" (above).

5. Hours worked "outside" the 8-5 workday on a flexible schedule must be accountable (e.g., hours worked on the Reference Desk, hours spent on specified tasks, duties, responsibilities). A simple log of activities should be kept for these hours.

6. Requests must be submitted and approved as far in advance as is reasonable to accommodate scheduling.

7. An approved flexible schedule may be canceled at any time, by consensus of the reference Department, to meet emergency staffing needs.

8. Nothing in this policy will be interpreted to supersede or contradict current or future policies regarding flexible work- week schedules developed by the ASU Libraries or by the University.

III. Process for requesting/approving flexible schedule:

1. Submit to your immediate supervisor/PE a specific proposal for a flexible schedule for a specific time period. Address need/purpose, impact on personal and departmental workload(s); i.e., "make your case", considering department-approved guidelines and criteria for evaluating flexible schedule requests (see below).

2. If the requestor's supervisor/PE is someone other than the Team Leader, the supervisor/PE will forward request to the Team Leader with recommendation of support or non-support. The Team Leader and Scheduling Team will evaluate the request based on the written, approved criteria. Input will be provided to the requestor if necessary.

3. The Team Leader will present the request to the reference team for decision by consensus. The requestor will have an opportunity to answer questions from the team members, and then will be excused for the team discussion and decision.

4. Should consensus not be achieved, the Team Leader will approve/deny request.

IV. Criteria for evaluating requests for flexible workweeks:

1. How a specific flexible schedule potentially impacts the Dept. as a whole, and impacts team efforts: (e.g., needs of the department, potential negative/positive impact on department scheduling and team planning and analysis efforts).

2. The number of requesters and/or the number of persons currently on approved flexible workweek. The impact of flexible workweek schedules on the department increases with the number of persons on flexible schedules; though there is no specific "cutoff" on the number of requests/requesters, multiple requests will be considered accordingly.

3. The time of the year; because individual and departmental workloads will vary during the year, these factors will be taken into consideration.

4. Personal needs of requestor.
