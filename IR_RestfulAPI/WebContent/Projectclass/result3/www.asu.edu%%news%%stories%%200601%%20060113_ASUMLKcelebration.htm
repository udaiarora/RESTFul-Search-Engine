Gomez, Pfister earn MLK Servant Leadership Awards

Community leader and public servant Jack Pfister will receive the 2006 Martin Luther King Jr. Servant Leadership Award from ASU at the university’s annual MLK celebration breakfast at 7 a.m., Jan. 31 in the Memorial Union (MU) on the Tempe campus.

Pfister has been a quiet builder and shaper of Arizona, establishing a tradition of community involvement for employees when he was general manager of Salt River Project. He was a driving force behind the passage of an MLK holiday in Arizona. Pfister also has been deeply involved in many community and civic projects, serving as an ASU professor and vice president and as a member of the Arizona Board of Regents.

This is the 21st year for ASU to celebrate the birthday of the great civil rights leader through a statewide poetry-essay contest for schoolchildren and a series of events. Seven years ago, ASU began giving an annual Servant Leadership Award to outstanding community members and students who demonstrate leadership through service.

This year’s student Servant Leadership Award winner is Bridgette Gomez, a dynamic secondary education major who first came to ASU as a participant in the Math-Science Honors Program for under-represented high school students. With her high aptitude and love of math, she works with younger children in the public schools, volunteers with homeless children in the Salvation Army Mind Wizards, and has developed a math anxiety workshop for ASU students.

The MLK poster-essay contest has grown in scope each year, with about 1,500 schoolchildren from across Arizona describing people they know who lead through service. Two dozen winners will attend the breakfast along with their parents, teachers and principals, and four will read their first-place essays. Winning posters are on display on the MU Union stage, and on the Web at (www.asu.edu/ia/photogallery).

Offices and classrooms on the campuses will be closed Jan. 16 to observe the MLK holiday. More than 100 students will participate in an MLK Day of Service Jan. 21 at Hacienda Inc., reading to disabled and chronically ill children at the facility. In addition to the annual celebration breakfast Jan. 31, students will stage a rally for diversity that day from 11:30 a.m. – 12:30 p.m. in front of the MU.

The West campus will have a Jan. 18 re-enactment by schoolchildren of the march on Washington, and a performance and poetry jam that week. Events at the Polytechnic and Downtown Phoenix campuses are in February, to coincide with Black History Month. For more event information, visit the Web page (www.asu.edu/mlk).
