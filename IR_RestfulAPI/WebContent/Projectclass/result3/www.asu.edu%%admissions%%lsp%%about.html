Letter from Dr. Christine Wilkinson

Greetings:

I am very pleased to welcome you to the Leadership Scholarship Program?s web site. Since inception in 1977, the Leadership Scholarship Program has been an integral part of the development of students first as leaders on campus and then, after graduation, as leaders in the community.

Within the site, you will find information about the history of the program, more about the current students who are in the program, and news from LSP alumni. We have also included a section about the Campaign for Leadership, our efforts to establish long-term support for the Leadership Scholarship Program.

To the current LSP students using this site, I hope this provides an excellent mechanism for you to connect with your peers, ASU resources, and LSP alumni. You are at the very heart of the program, and we are here to support you in your academic and personal goals.

To our alumni, you are very important to the university, the program and to those of us who worked with you as undergraduates. We continue to improve ways to stay in touch with you. We hope that you will stay in touch with us by visiting the site often and contributing to the site by sharing memories of your years with LSP and sending us photos to include on the site.

Best wishes for continued success in all of your endeavors.

Sincerely,

Christine Wilkinson, Ph.D.
Senior Vice President and Secretary of the University

Overview

When Dr. Christine Wilkinson first proposed a scholarship program for high school students with outstanding leadership potential, she had several goals in mind. It was 1976 and Arizona State was still a fledgling institution. Wilkinson, then director of high school-college relations, was trying to establish a strong network of student organizations on campus. The key to success was finding a way to attract some of Arizona's most promising high school leaders--students who could help build ASU while developing their own special skills for community leadership in the future.

The Leadership Scholarship Program received enthusiastic support from both the university and the Board of Regents. In 1977, the first seven Leadership Scholars were able to enter ASU with tuition waivers and small cash stipends. Over the next four years, these students would participate in an intensive leadership development program that included academic enrichment classes and peer and faculty/staff mentoring. In return, these future leaders were required to participate in campus organizations and co-curricular activities while maintaining a 3.0 grade point average.

Today, the Leadership Scholarship Program attracts 21 of Arizona's brightest young leaders each year, along with four students from other states. In addition to tuition waivers, the students receive $1,500 in cash support annually as well as four years of leadership development training. Over 400 students have now graduated from ASU as Leadership Scholars. One is an award-winning television anchor in Los Angeles and another is a city manager in Pennsylvania. Most are living and working in Arizona--as outstanding teachers, attorneys, financial analysts and college administrators.
