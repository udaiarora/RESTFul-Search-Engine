Admission to the Program
Applicants may be regularly or provisionally admitted to a graduate degree program. Regular admission means that a student may proceed with the completion of a degree in the standard manner indicated below and in accordance with any other procedures established by the academic unit offering the degree program. If a student has been granted provisional admission, a status change must be recommended to the dean of the Division of Graduate Studies in writing by the head of the academic unit after the provisions for regular admission have been fulfilled.



Program of Study
In consultation with a faculty advisor and after regular admission, the student must file an official Program of Study to outline the planned course of study for the degree. The faculty advisor should be consulted at the earliest possible date after regular admission about planning a Program of Study.
  • A Program of Study may include a maximum of nine hours of course work completed prior to admission to a graduate degree program at Arizona State University. These are identified as “nondegree” hours.

  • The number of hours transferred from other institutions, including ASU West, may not exceed 20 percent of the total minimum semester hours required for a master’s degree unless stated otherwise for a specific degree program. Transfer credit taken prior to admission is included in the total number of nondegree hours.

Program of Study forms are available online at all campus computer sites, at the ASU POS Forms Online website www.asu.edu/registrar/forms/pos.html. The Program of Study must be completed and signed by the student, the supervisory committee, and the head of the academic unit. Students return the completed and signed form to the Graduation Office. Course changes in the approved Program of Study may be requested by submitting the Petition for Course Changes to the Graduate Program of Study (www.asu.edu/registrar/forms/pos.html) to the Graduation Section of the Registrar’s Office. All other changes to the Program of Study require the use of the Petition to the Division of Graduate Studies (www.asu.edu/graduate/forms), submitted to Student Academic Services (SAS). A Program of Study, approved by the Division of Graduate Studies dean, must be on file before exams (foreign language, comprehensives) can be taken or a thesis can be submitted for defense.



Foreign Language Requirement
If a foreign language is required, students must demonstrate at least a reading knowledge of that language. Competency is certified by the Department of Languages and Literatures only upon satisfactory performance of a Foreign Language Examination specific to the particular graduate program in which the student is enrolled. The examinations are administered three times each year. Students planning to take the examination must register by completing the Foreign Language Examination form (available on the web at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms) and submitting it to Languages & Literatures at least one month in advance of the examination date. The students must pass the examination in no more than three attempts. Students must petition the Division of Graduate Studies for a third reexamination.



Comprehensive Examinations
Written and/or oral comprehensive examination forms are available in the Division of Graduate Studies or on the web at www.asu.edu/graduate/forms. The Report of Final Master’s Written or Oral Examination Form is to be held in the academic unit until the examination has been taken. It should then be completed and returned to the Division of Graduate Studies. After the Division of Graduate Studies approves the Program of Study, the academic unit can submit the form.



Maximum Time Limit
Unless stated otherwise in the Graduate Catalog, www.asu.edu/aad/catalogs/graduate, all requirements listed on the Program of Study, available at www.asu.edu/registrar/forms/pos.html, must be completed within six consecutive years. The six-year period begins with the earliest course in the Program of Study.



Applying for Graduation
Students should apply for graduation no later than the date specified in the Graduate Catalog calendar. Any deficiencies or foreign language requirements must have been completed, and all fees are payable at this time. A student applies for graduation by (1) paying the graduation fee at Cashiering Services in the Student Services Building, Room 244, (2) taking the receipt to the Graduation Section, Student Services Building, Room 140, and (3) completing the Application for Graduation form provided by the Graduation Section. An additional late fee will be assessed if the student does not complete these procedures by the date specified in the Graduate Catalog calendar. A student may withdraw an Application for Graduation by contacting the Graduation Section in person or in writing. If the student wishes to apply the filing fee to a subsequent semester, he/she must withdraw the application no later than the deadline to apply for graduation. During the summer, the application must be withdrawn by July 1. If a student does not complete all degree requirements by the date of graduation for which he/she has applied and has not withdrawn the application by the designated time, the graduation fee must again be paid to reapply.



Thesis
A copy of the Format Manual is available on the web at www.asu.edu/graduate/formatmanual or from the Division of Graduate Studies. It outlines all relevant procedures. A careful review of this document prior to the preparation of the final copy of the thesis is strongly recommended.



Grade Point Average
To be eligible for a degree in the Division of Graduate Studies, a student must achieve a grade point average of “B” (3.0) or better in all courses numbered 500 and above and in all work specifically included in the Program of Study. Two different grade point averages are computed: (1) the overall graduate grade point average in all courses numbered 500 or higher that appear on the transcript and (2) the grade point average in all courses that appear on the Program of Study. Classes taken in the College of Law and transfer work are not included in these two grade point averages.



Important Reminders
Registration
Whenever university facilities or faculty services are utilized, graduate students must be registered for a minimum of one hour of graduate-level credit that appears on the Program of Study or that is an appropriate graduate-level course in the academic unit in which they are pursuing their degree program. This includes registration during any semester or summer session in which written or oral examinations are taken, even if graduation occurs in a later semester. All graduate assistants must register for a minimum of six hours of course work each regular semester they are employed. A student who does not enroll for three calendar years is considered withdrawn and must reapply for admission to the degree program. These enrollment requirements may not be waived.



Dates and Deadlines
The university calendar found in the current Graduate Catalog lists deadlines for applying for graduation. Deadlines for the last date to hold an oral defense of a thesis, submitting a thesis to the Division of Graduate Studies, and submitting the thesis to the ASU Bookstore for binding are also found in the Graduate Catalog calendar. See also the Division of Graduate Studies site, Graduation Deadlines and Procedures.

Questions? Check the Graduate Catalog (www.asu.edu/aad/catalogs/graduate) or email the Division of Graduate Studies at grad-q@asu.edu. If you have questions about the information on this page, please contact Student Academic Services (SAS) at (480) 965-3521.
