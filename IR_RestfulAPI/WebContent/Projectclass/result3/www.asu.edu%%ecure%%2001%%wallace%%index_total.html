4

Recordkeeping frame ...1

  • Rapid and dramatic increase in email use
  • Contemporaneous with:
    • new and emerging organizational forms
    • new and emerging communication forms (aka document genres)
  • Shared meaning & practices absent
  • Enduser desktop autonomy
  • Email a software utility that creates many different forms of “records”

5

Recordkeeping frame ...2

  • When is email a “record”?
    • university record, public record, non-record...
  • How should email records be managed throughout their lifecycle?
    • Creation (initiation, receipt)
    • Use (filing, access permissions, retrieval)
    • Disposition (appraisal, scheduling, preservation, destruction)

6

Recordkeeping frame - 3

  • Form / function of message more important than its identification as an “email message”
  • Policy implementation / evaluation vacuum
  • Lifecycle management is essential
  • Traditional ARM practices will not scale
  • Electronic recordkeeping systems needed

Strategies for capturing and preserving record email ...1

  • Considerations
    • Existing culture(s) of use & management
    • Evolving legal environment re: recordness and discovery -- legal risks associated with unmanaged email
    • Policies are created, but are they implemented and evaluated?

13

Strategies for capturing and preserving record email ...2

  • Strategies
    • Clear and definitive records policy
    • Implement and evaluate on pilot basis, edit as necessary
    • Education and training
    • Appraise and retain based on “type” of email, not on “format” of email
    • Recognize applicability of FOIA
    • Recognize discovery risks
    • Adopt electronic recordkeeping
