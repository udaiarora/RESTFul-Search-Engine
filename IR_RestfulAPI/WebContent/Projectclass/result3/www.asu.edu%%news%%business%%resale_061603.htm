Arizona Real Estate Center: Greater Phoenix resale market shows signs of slowing

In a possible response to continuing international and economic concerns, the greater Phoenix resale housing market showed some signs of slowing in May. Even if the slowing continues, however, other signs in the market point to underlying strength, according to data released by the Arizona Real Estate Center today.

Since hitting 6,725 recorded sales in March, the resale market slowed to 5,890 recorded sales in May 2003. Recorded sales activity in May 2003 represented .7 percent of the single family inventory, however, while the typical monthly share is .6 percent. Further, May 2003 was well above the 4,510 sales recorded a year ago. That brings the 2003 year-to-date to 26,990 recorded sales, compared to 23,955 sales for the same period in 2002.

The median home price in May continued at a record pace with $154,000, in contrast to $152,400 for April 2003 and $145,000 for a year ago. Since the Greater Phoenix area is so large, the median price can range significantly from $362,500 in North Scottsdale to $89,750 in the central western areas of the city of Phoenix.

"This recording activity represents the end point of a decision-making process that probably began in late 2002, when there was mounting concerns about the war in Iraq," said Dr. Jay Q. Butler, director of the center. "These concerns combined with continuing economic weakness probably contributed to the slowdown in the market activity. Since the activity is still relatively strong, it appears that people have not lost their belief in the housing market."

Following a pattern comparable to the single-family market, the resale townhouse/condominium housing sector slowed from 1,460 recordings in March 2003 to 1,240 for May 2003, but is well ahead of the 1,010 units recorded sold a year ago. For 2003 year-to-date, the activity stands at 5,880 units, in contrast to 5,480 homes for 2002 year-to-date.

As a source of affordable housing and investment opportunities, the median price in the townhouse/condominium sector has strongly increased from last year's $95,600 to the current $104,990. This market can also have significant range in median prices from $163,650 in North Scottsdale to $86,650 in North Mesa.

The median square footage for a single-family home recorded sold in May 2003 was 1,665 square feet, which is larger than the 1,650 square feet reported a year ago. In the townhouse/condominium sector, the median square footage was 1,175 square feet, which is larger than the 1,170 square feet reported a year ago.

· In contrast to May 2002, recorded sales in the City of Phoenix improved from 1,320 to 1,635 sales, while the median sales price increased to $126,000 from $119,000. Since Phoenix is a geographically large city, the median prices can range significantly such as $102,450 in the Maryvale area to $182,500 in the Union Hills area. The townhouse/condominium sector increased slightly form 270 to 285 sales, while the median price increased from $84,500 to $90,000.

· The Scottsdale resale home market increased from last year's 510 recorded sales to 600 recorded sales. The median sales price continued its upward movement by increasing from $292,750 to $330,000. The median resale home price is $362,500 in North Scottsdale and $171,000 in South Scottsdale. The townhouse/condominium sector in Scottsdale improved from 200 to 295 sales, while the median sales price increased from $150,000 to $152,000.

· The Mesa resale housing market grew strongly from 555 to 700 sales, while the median price increased from $133,500 to $140,250. The townhouse/condominium sector improved slightly from 155 to 170 sales and the median home price increased from $88,975 to $92,000.

· Glendale moved from 390 to 495 recorded sales and the median sales price increased from $137,000 to $142,500. The townhouse/condominium sector remained at 45 sales, while the median sales price increased from $69,990 to $79,900.

· In comparison to a year ago, the Sun City resale market increased from 120 to 155 sales, with the median sales price increasing from $98,000 to $117,000. Resale activity in Sun City West remained at 90 recorded sales, but the median sales price increased from $134,900 to $138,000. The townhouse/condominium market in Sun City improved from 95 to 100 sales and the median sales price increased from $74,500 to $77,500. In Sun City West, sales increased from 3 to 35 sales and the median sales price increased from $90,750 to $107,535.

· The resale market in Gilbert grew from 230 to 370 sales, while the median sales price increased from $164,650 to $175,000. The townhouse/condominium market was also stable at 15 sales, but the median sales price increased from $104,000 to $107,500.

· For the City of Chandler, the resale market improved from 325 to 500 recorded sales, while the median sales price increased from $156,000 to $159,900. The townhouse/condominium market also improved from 50 to 75 sales and the median sales price increased from $99,000 to $110,000.

· The resale market in Tempe moved from 150 to 190 sales, with the median sales price increasing to $164,900 from $153,000. The townhouse/condominium sector improved from 75 to 105 sales, while the median sales price increased from $103,000 to $107,500.

· The highest median sales price was in Paradise Valley at $750,000 with a median square foot house of 3,425 square feet.

· In the West Valley, the following communities represent 6 percent of the resale market, while it stood at 5 percent for a year ago:
- Avondale improved from 65 to 85 sales with the median price going from $136,900 to $148,000.
- El Mirage grew from 10 sales ($100,500) to 40 sales ($110,100).
- Goodyear improved from 55 sales at $155,000 to 65 sales at $180,000.
- Surprise expanded from 100 sales ($134,900) to 165 sales ($139,900).
