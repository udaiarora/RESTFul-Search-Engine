June 24, 2003

Two Bilingual Review/Press books receive awards

Two books published by ASU's Bilingual Review/Press were honored with awards on June 6 at BookExpo America in Los Angeles, the largest annual book trade show held in the United States.

"Contemporary Chicana and Chicano Art," compiled and written by ASU professors Gary D. Keller and Mary Erickson in addition to other scholars, won the Fine Art category in the 2003 Independent Publisher Book Awards. The two-volume set is a comprehensive compendium of Chicana and Chicano artists that has been characterized as "the absolute bible of Chicana and Chicana art" by actor and noted Chicano art collector Cheech Marín. Gilberto Cárdenas, executive director of the Inter-University Program for Latino Research, has called it "the most comprehensive and lavish that has been conceived."

"Moving Target: A Memoir of Pursuit" won Best Biography in the Latino Literary Hall of Fame 2003 book awards. In this memoir, People magazine correspondent Ron Arias recounts his father's emotional distance after spending time as a prisoner of war in World War II and Korea, his eventual disappearance from their home, and Arias's 14-year search for him, during which he learned that his father might have been a spy.
Bilingual Review/Press has given a start to authors who have later signed with major publishing houses or have won prestigious awards. Phoenix author Stella Pope Duarte, who eventually signed with publishing giant Harper Collins, had her first short story collection published by Bilingual/Review Press. Poet and novelist Demetria Martínez also got her start at the Bilingual Review/Press before signing with the large book publisher Ballantine Books.

While the Bilingual Review/Press has earned a number of awards over the years, it is a major event to win two at one time. Currently affiliated with ASU under the umbrella of the Hispanic Research Center, the Bilingual Review/Press has been publishing the works of Hispanic writers since 1974.

Bilingual Review/Press has more than 150 titles in its backlist and will publish eight to 10 titles a year. Most of the books are by or about U.S. Hispanics and most are written in English, though the press does feature bilingual and Spanish-only titles as well. Since its founding 30 years ago, the press has pledged to forego the world of purely commercial entertainment and to focus instead on works of weight and significance.

In addition to new works, classics of Chicana and Chicano fiction are being kept alive and available through the Press's Clásicos Chicanos/Chicano Classics imprint. As the Bilingual Review/Press begins its fourth decade of business, it solidifies its mission to make available the wealth that is to be mined from Hispanic writing.
