Discussion Questions: What are the strengths of the ways in which Schultz combines formal, statistical, and case study methods? In what order did Schultz combine these methods, and what implications does this have for what information or data had heuristic or "use" novelty at different points of his research? Does Schultz do a good job of selecting cases for individual study? Does he pay sufficient attention to outlier or deviant cases? How might he have done an even better job of combining methods?
