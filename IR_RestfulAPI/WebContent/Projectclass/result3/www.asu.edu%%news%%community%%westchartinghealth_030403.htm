March 4, 2003

ASU West hosts Health Care Decisions forum

"Whose Life Is This, Anyway? Charting Your Health Care Destiny." is a free lecture and discussion presented by Health Care Decisions, a non-profit institute of Hospice of the Valley, in collaboration with ASU West's gerontology department.

ASU West will host the forum from 1:30 to 3:30 p.m., March 14, in La Sala Ballroom at 4701 W. Thunderbird Road in Phoenix.

Health Care Decisions, a project of the Hospice of the Valley, encourages members of the community to talk about and write down their wishes for end-of-life care. Fewer than 25 percent of Americans have expressed their thoughts about how they want to be cared for when the end of their life nears.

"When people make these difficult decisions ahead of time, they ease the stress for themselves and their families later on," says Barbara Volk-Craft, co-director of Health Care Decisions and a registered nurse who will present the March 14 forum. "We want families to discuss the issues and the choices involved. We want them to put the final decisions in writing."

Kathleen Waldron, lecturer in gerontology at ASU West, says it's never too early to think about health care treatment and advance care directives.

"Too many people avoid thinking about health care issues and the repercussions involved, and family members are left with no idea what to do if dad has a stroke or mom needs a feeding tube," says Waldron. "It is vitally important for family members to know what the others desire in case of a medical emergency."

Health Care Decisions will distribute free living will forms. They will answer questions about how to fill out the forms and how to have the conversations with loved ones.
For information and to R.S.V.P, call the gerontology department at ASU West at (602) 543-6742.

Crum, with ASU West, can be reached at (602) 543-5209 or (matthew.crum@asu.edu).
