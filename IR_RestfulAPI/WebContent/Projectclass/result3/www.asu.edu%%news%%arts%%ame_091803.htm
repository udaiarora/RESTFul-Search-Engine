September 18, 2003

ASU's Arts, Media and Engineering (AME) 2003-04 season features performances, multimedia presentations and film screenings

ASU's Arts, Media and Engineering (AME) graduate research and education program announces its 2003-04 digital arts season featuring performances, film and multimedia presentations by national artists. AME is one of the nation's top programs for arts research and education activities that focus on digital media, and is part of ASU's Institute for Studies in the Arts (ISA).

iChamber - Computer Music International, Oct. 23 and 24, 2003, Digital Arts Ranch
Current aesthetic and technical developments in electronic music converge on AME/ISA as iChamber presents a program of compositions for musicians and computer-generated sounds from the world's major electronic music centers.

An Evening with Gary Hill, Oct. 28, 2003, Neeb Hall
Gary Hill's work in video and installation art has opened new possibilities for the use of developing media. Internationally exhibited, his work has been studied for its contributions to art as language and thinking as well as for its innovative uses of technology. Hill's lecture complements his exhibition, Language Willing, at the ASU Art Museum.

motione with Bill T. Jones, Nov. 8, 2003 and April 24, 2004, Intelligent Stage
Dancer/choreographer Bill T. Jones joins composer Roger Reynolds, lighting designer Robert Wierzel, visual artists Paul Kaiser, Shelley Eshkar and Marc Downie and AME/ISA artists and engineers to present ongoing research in motion capture and analysis, gesture recognition, media flow architecture, interactive environments and collaborative multimedia works. The final work will be presented in the world premiere of motione in April 2005.

motione with Bebe Miller, Dec. 6, 2003 and March 23, 2004, Intelligent Stage
Dancer/choreographer Bebe Miller joins composers Todd Ingalls and David Birchfield, visual artists Paul Kaiser, Shelley Eshkar and Marc Downie, and AME/ISA artists and engineers in this presentation of motione research. The team is developing a semi-improvised, multimedia, interactive duet driven by the movement gestures of Miller and a percussionist. The gestures and their relationships will create and control the accompanying digital sound and visual environments. motione will include this work in its world premiere in April 2005.

motione with Trisha Brown, Jan. 10, 2004 and July 9, 2004, Intelligent Stage
Choreographer Trisha Brown, in her first residency for motione, joins visual artists Paul Kaiser, Shelley Eshkar and Marc Downie, and AME/ISA artists and engineers to present ongoing motion capture and analysis research for the extraction of movement vocabulary and the creation of collaborative multimedia works. The work will be presented in the world premiere of motione in April 2005.

House [raw], Jan. 26, 2004, Digital Arts Ranch
Choreographer Ralph Lemon explores the digitally enabled transmission of history, connections, details and personal experience in House[raw], the Internet component of Geography III: House, that will premiere in 2004 at the Krannert Center for the Arts. Lemon has collaborated with interactive performance designer John D. Mitchell, computer scientist Hari Sundaram, and new media artist John Klima to develop a Web-based space for viewing, observing, browsing, searching and interacting with the artifacts of human memory and the creative process.

Miranda July, Jan. 29, 2004, Digital Arts Ranch
Miranda July's multimedia performances have been presented globally in spaces such as The Kitchen in New York and the Institute of Contemporary Art in London. Her radio performances can be heard regularly on NPR's The Next Big Thing. The Paris Review and Harvard Review have published her short stories and she is currently working on a feature-length film supported by the Sundance Institute. Join the artist at the Ranch for a screening of her films and a discussion of her work.

Peggy Ahwesh, Feb. 12, 2004, Digital Arts Ranch
Filmmaker Peggy Ahwesh bases her new film Certain Women on the novel of the
same name by Southern genre writer Erskine Caldwell. Caldwell's pulp storytelling,
proto-feminist stance and unabashed social dramatization inspired her work.
Join Ahwesh for a post-screening discussion of her distinguished career in film.

Digitally Enhanced Percussion and Conducting, April 1, 2004, Intelligent Stage
Join composers/performers J.B. Smith, Todd Ingalls and David Birchfield, and ASU Bands director Gary Hill in this presentation of motion capture and motion analysis research in applications for digitally enhanced percussion and conducting performance and pedagogy.

Haunted When it Rains, April 15, 2004, Digital Arts Ranch
Miranda Zent, John Tang, David Birchfield, Loren Olson and AME/ISA artists and engineers working on sensing, feedback, indexing and media flow architecture present a mediated theatre event, exploring the possibilities technology presents to the dramatization of myth. Set in the legendary Birdcage Theater in Tombstone, Arizona, the piece examines the technology-enabled transformations of familiar performance forms that echo the mythology of the Old West.

Shelter Against Violence, April 29, 2004, Digital Arts Ranch
Muriel Magenta, Frances McMahon-Ward and Hari Sundaram present research engaging art, media and engineering for a social cause. This community partnership between Arizona State University and the YWCA of Maricopa County's Haven House shelter serves as a vehicle for the empowerment of domestic violence survivors as they learn interdisciplinary art skills and increase computer competency. The research explores a storytelling-centered learning experience in which shelter residents contribute images, text and sound bites toward the development of an interactive Web site and DVD on the subject of breaking the cycle of domestic violence and homelessness.

All AME/ISA events are held on the ASU campus at 7:30 p.m. and are free and open to the public. Digital Arts Ranch is located on the Southwest corner of University Drive and Myrtle Street. The Intelligent Stage is located in Matthews Center, at the intersection of Cady and Tyler Malls. Neeb Hall is located at the intersections of Forest and Tyler Malls. Seating for all performances is limited and reservations are encouraged. For more information, the public may call 480-965-9438.

The Arts, Media and Engineering graduate research and education program was developed within the Institute for Studies in the Arts, an interdisciplinary research and education center in the Herberger College of Fine Arts at Arizona State University. AME/ISA are co-sponsored by the Herberger College of Fine Arts and the Fulton School of Engineering at ASU. For more information about the AME/ISA, visit isa.asu.edu.
