Should I buy a Dell OptiPlex or a Dimension?

Question:

The ASU Computer Store sells only the Optiplex line, however the Dimension line looks like it has a much better price/performance ratio.

Response:

I asked a Dell representative about the difference between the OptiPlex and Dimension lines. Most of the major differences can be placed in two categories: certification and engineering.

Certification:

The Dimension is more leading edge technology, whereas OptiPlex is more conservative "tried-and-true". The OptiPlex line with its various components are certified to work for a variety of operating environments whereas the Dimension line is not. For example, the following descriptions are for Dell's OptiPlex GXa and Dimension XPS. 
 
OptiPlex GXa                        Dimension XPS
------------                        -------------
Factory Installed                   Factory Installed
  Operating Systems                   Operating Systems
- Windows 95                        - Windows 95
- Windows NT 4.0                    - Windows NT 4.0
 
Certified
  Operating Systems
- Windows NT 4.0
- Windows 95
- Novell Netware Client 32 for
    DOS/Windows and Client for
    Windows 95
- IBM OS/2 Warp 4.x
- SCO UNIX 5.0.2
- Sunsoft Solaris 2.5.1
- Banyan 7.0

Note the lack of a certification list for the Dimension, including no mention of certification for 95 or NT. This is not an oversight. It means the Dimension is expected to work in the 95 and NT environments but has not been through the certification process that the OptiPlex line has undergone. (If the components were all certified, considering the elapsed time required and the cost of certification, then the system wouldn't be leading edge and it wouldn't be as price attractive as it is.)

No mention of the "other" operating systems for the Dimension line doesn't mean they won't work on Dimension, but you may be stuck with back level drivers or have other struggles getting everything up and running the way you want it.

Engineering:

If you've ever upgraded a PC, inserted/removed memory, drives, cards, or otherwise worked around inside a PC, you've probably had a few scraped knuckles and had plenty of use for that tool kit (and wished you had a better one). Dell has done a lot on engineering on the OptiPlex line which is not available on the Dimension line. You push a button here, a button there, and everything opens up real nice for convenient working inside the PC.

Bottom line is that you pay for that comfort and security of the certification process and for the extra engineering that went into the OptiPlex.

Eldon Norton
