April 13, 2004

'Frog Pod' leaps to design contest win

ASU design student Rebecca Finell has won the grand prize in the inaugural Juvenile Products Manufacturers Association (JPMA) Student Design Competition. Rochelle Letourneau, a junior in industrial design, won third prize.

Randy Jackson, a junior from Wichita Falls, Texas, won an honorable mention in the International Housewares Association (IHA) Student Design Contest.

Finell, a junior from Tempe, won $2,500 for her bath toy scoop, drain and storage unit called "Frog Pod." Letourneau, of Scottsdale, was honored with $1,000 for her design of "Tiko," a dental center for children that combines all their dental hygiene products.

Jackson's entry was "icetube," a tube that freezes water into ice cubes that can be twisted out of the tube. Jackson won $200 for the honorable mention.

The JPMA competition was open to industrial design students enrolled in degree programs at any of the Industrial Designers Society of America (IDSA)-affiliated schools. The judges, all IDSA member designers, critiqued each submission for design, design research and ergonomics.

The International Housewares Association recognizes and rewards young design talent and encourages career opportunities in the housewares industry.

ASU has participated in the IHA competition 10 of the last 11 years. ASU students have won prizes each year, and first place twice.

Smith, with Public Relations, can be reached at (480) 965-4821 or (jps@asu.edu).
