Committee on Academic Professional Status (CAPS)
Annual Report 2006

Members:

Beth DiFelice

Judy Grace

Lydia LaFaro

Brenda Shears

Dan Stanton

Barbara Trapido-Lurie

 

For the 2005-2006 year, Beth DiFelice served as secretary and Lydia LaFaro served as archivist and webmaster. Dan Stanton represented CAPS at the Academic Senate, Judy Grace served as CAPS representative to the Committee on Committees, and Brenda Shears CAPS represented CAPS at the Provost?s Advisory Committee (PAC-20).

Continuing its work from the previous year, the committee assisted with final revisions to ACD 507, Academic Professional Personnel Actions, distributing new drafts to all APs, gathering input, and passing on recommendations to Vice Provost Marjorie Zatz and the working group. The new policy was approved, and became effective January 9, 2006. 

In November, CAPS sponsored a Spotlight Forum in which Vice Provost Marjorie Zatz discussed implementation of ACD 507, as well as a new initiative to develop personnel policies for postdoctoral scholars. 

In the Spring, CAPS undertook several projects:

  • Discussed ACD language about voting rights for APs. A proposal for revised language was sent to Vice Provost Zatz, but it was agreed that this change would best be advanced at a future time, together with a number of other changes needed to make policy consistent at all ASU campuses, and make older portions of ACD consistent with the newly-approved ACD 507.
  • Created the APForum listserv, as a vehicle for interactive discussion among academic professionals.
  • Advised on implementation of ACD 507 regarding creation of a University Promotion and Continuing Appointment Review Committee (UPCARC): Vice Provost Zatz formed an ad hoc group (consisting of Marjorie Zatz, Victoria Trotta, deg farrelly, Ellen Welty, Judy Grace, Kurt Murphy, Lise Hawkos, and Barbara Trapido-Lurie) to develop a proposal for the new university-wide review committee. CAPS advised on the proposal, and in May, hosted a Spotlight Forum to get input on the proposal from the academic professional community.


Respectfully submitted,

Barbara Trapido-Lurie
CAPS Chair, 2005-2006
