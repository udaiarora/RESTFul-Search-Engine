ASU Fall Commencement 2006
Honorary Degree Recipient

Jane Dee Hull
Doctor of Humane Letters, honoris causa

Jane Dee Hull, a native of Kansas City, Missouri, graduated from the University of Kansas with a bachelor’s degree in Elementary Education, and undertook postgraduate coursework in Political Science and Economics at Arizona State University.  She is also a graduate of the Josephson Ethics Institute.  A dedicated educator, she taught elementary school in her home state and on the Navajo Nation in Chinle, Arizona, while raising four children with her husband, Terrance Hull.

Active in Arizona politics since 1965, Governor Hull was first elected to the Arizona House of Representatives in 1979, where served until 1993.  During that time, she acted as both the GOP Whip (1987-1988) and the Speaker of the House (1989-1992).  In 1995, she was elected Secretary of State and distinguished her administration by transitioning the office into a new technological age.  She succeeded to the governorship of Arizona in 1997, making her the second female in state history to hold the office.

Governor Hull became the first woman elected to the state’s governorship in 1999 and advanced the issues of education, healthcare, the economy and environmental preservation as priorities during her term. 
Ever the advocate for the youth of Arizona, Governor Hull’s Students FIRST program helped to ensure funding equity for school facilities throughout the state.  Under her leadership, the KidsCare program provided healthcare coverage for over 130,000 children of the working poor, and Arizona became one of the first states in the nation to provide Internet access to all of its public schools.  Arizona made significant headway in providing classroom and teacher funding and expanding accountability the state’s education system during her administration.

Governor Hull is also credited with making important strides in strengthening Arizona’s relationship with Mexico, especially in the areas of commerce, trade and tourism.  Working closely with other western governors and then Mexican President, Vicente Fox, Governor Hull’s leadership established Arizona as a leader in bi-national relations.  

In addition to her education and public service careers, Governor Hull has served on the boards of many community service and political organizations including the Western Governors Association, Bush-Cheney 2004 Steering Committee, Women for Bush, 2004, the Advisory Council on Historic Preservation, the Arizona Women’s Forum, Race for the Cure, Save-A-Life Alliance, Developing Older Adult Resources, the Morrison Institute of Public Policy, and many others.  An elementary school in Chandler, Arizona has been named in her honor.  Governor Hull is also the published author of multiple newspaper and magazine articles including, The Power of Character; The Challenge of Public Service.

Among her numerous awards and acknowledgments, she was named National Legislator of the Year in 1989 by the Republican Legislators Association.  She also received the Distinguished Public Service Award from the Arizona Regional Board of Directors of the Anti-Defamation League; the Freedom of Information Award from the Arizona Newspaper Association; the Good Neighbor Award from United States and Mexico Chambers of Commerce; and the Condecoracion Aguila Azteca from the government of Mexico.

Arizona State University is honored to confer upon Governor Jane Dee Hull the degree of Doctor of Humane Letters, honoris causa, in recognition of her contributions to education, healthcare, global engagement, and public service, and her enduring commitment to the youth of Arizona.
