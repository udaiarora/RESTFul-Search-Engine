ASU Yesteryear Carriage provides riders therapy, joy

Debbie Crews knows that animals have the power to heal. As a faculty research associate in ASU’s Kinesiology Department and the developer of ASU Yesteryear Carriage, she has seen the horse-and-carriage program bring joy – and provide therapy – to young and old alike for the past two years.

ASU Yesteryear Carriage is a program within the Alternative Intervention Research Clinic at ASU that provides therapy to people via rides in a horse-drawn carriage. It also caters to the public by providing carriage rides on Mill Avenue and for Glendale Glitter and Glow.

The horse-and-carriage rides begin Sept. 16 and will run every Friday and Saturday night from 7:30 – 10 p.m., weather permitting.

The ever-growing program recently set up a partnership with the Phoenix Zoo called ASUZOO. This partnership has benefited ASU Yesteryear Carriage by allowing the two groups to share equipment and animals.

There are six carriage horses in the program, including four Clydesdales and two Percherons. For now, the horses are housed in Crews’ neighborhood, but she says the program plans to move the horses to a permanent home at the Phoenix Zoo.

ASU Yesteryear Carriage will begin working soon with Mesa’s Fellowship Village Healthcare Center to conduct research, and to study the bond between the riders and horses.

Working with the elderly is particularly rewarding, Crew says, because many of them say that they always wanted to be around a horse or other animal.

“You get to hear stories about when they were younger,” she says.

Although the primary animals used in therapy sessions are horses, the program also provides animal-assisted therapy with a number of other animals, including dogs, cats, sheep, goats, tortoises, wallabies and birds.

The program also hopes to branch out into the community; to this end, the horses and carriages can be rented for special events such as weddings and homecomings.

ASU Yesteryear Carriage charges $150 per hour for a standard event, but the program offers discount rates for ASU events. It also accepts sponsors, and a donation of $5,000 allows the program to visit centers such has Fellowship Village once a week during a fall and spring season.

Crews says that the vision of ASU Yesteryear Carriage is to grow as Tempe grows. To keep up with that growth, the program is seeking additional part-time carriage drivers.

Persons with horse experience can apply, Crews says, and they will be trained as drivers. The drivers who work during the day will be involved in therapy driving, while those who work nights and weekends will drive for the public. Anyone interested can contact Crissy Arrington at (602) 361-5355.

Those interested in donating to ASU Yesteryear Carriage can contact Crews at (602) 361-2964.

Campbell, with Marketing & Strategic Communications, can be reached at (480) 965-9370 or (lisa.campbell@asu.edu).
