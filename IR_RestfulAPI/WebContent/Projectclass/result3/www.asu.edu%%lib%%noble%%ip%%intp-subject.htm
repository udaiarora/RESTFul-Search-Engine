Searching By Subject/Keyword

 

General Search Tip:


Patents may be searched by both subject (also know as the International Patent Classification) and by keyword.


Although keyword searching appears to be easier than searching by classification, keyword searching has liabilities that can only be overcome by a subject/classification search. In keyword searching, the searcher must be able to anticipate every possible way that an invention can be described, however, patents do not use standard nor consistent terminology. This is true even when considering patents issued in just one specific language - it is especially true when searching an international database that has translated titles and abstracts from languages worldwide. Additionally, some foreign patents do not have their titles/abstracts translated into English. Searching by classification will retrieve all patents in an area regardless of the words used to describe the invention or if no English translation of the title is available.


In summary:

  • a classification search is best for subject searching because of its comprehensiveness
  • a keyword search can be used to supplement a classification search or to help identify the correct classification by finding similiar patents.

There are over 100 countries and/or agencies worldwide that grant patents. The following pages are a list of intellectual property offices with links to their web sites. For each entity, the availability and the searchability of their patent grants varies.

  • PIPERS: Directory of National and Regional Intellectual Property Offices
    http://www.piperpat.co.nz/ipoff/

  • WIPO: Web site Addresses of Industrial Property Offices
    http://www.wipo.org/news/en/links/ipo_web.htm

European Patent Office's Worldwide Database

The European Patent Office maintains a worldwide database that covers over 80 countries and/or patenting agencies, however, the dates of coverage and the type of coverage varies from entity to entity.

To determine the coverage of titles, abstracts and classification information for each patent entity see

  • Worldwide Database - Facsimile and Classification Coverage
    http://ep.espacenet.com/espacenet/ep/en/help/tab1.html

    The column labeled "Bibliographic Data" indicates the availability of patent titles, the "Abstracts" columns indicate if abstracts are available and if so for what dates and the "IPC" column indicates during what time frame International Patent Classification codes are available. For more details about the coverage of this database see:
    esp@cenet helpfiles - Coverage (http://ep.espacenet.com/espacenet/ep/en/help/15350.htm)

To do an international patent search:


Esp@cenet: Europe's Network of Patent Databases
http://ep.espacenet.com/

Enter the database by selecting "Advanced Search" on the left-hand side of the screen under "Issued Patents".

To search by International Patent Classification:

  1. Scroll the search page down to the "Publication Number" search box and enter the country or agency code (ex., US, GB, AU, EP, WO, etc.) with no spaces or punctuation.
    If you want to search the complete database without restricting to country or agency, leave this box blank.

  2. Scroll the search page down to the "IPC Classification" search box and enter the IPC code.
    To find an IPC code see the instructions below.

To search by keyword:

  1. Scroll the search page down to the "Publication Number" search box and enter the country or agency code (ex., US, GB, AU, EP, WO, etc.) with no spaces or punctuation.
    If you want to search the complete database without restricting to country or agency, leave this box blank.

  2. Scroll the search page up to the "Keyword(s) in Title or Abstract" search box and enter the word (or words).
    Notes:
    • A search limited to a specific country will process up to 5 words in the "Title or Abstract" search box.
    • A worldwide search (i.e., not limited to a specific country) will only process the first 2 words entered in the search box.
    • Two or more words entered in the search box will be automatically "AND"ed by the system.
    • To search words as a phrase, put the words in quotes ("")
To view a patent:
  1. Click on the patent title in the results screen
  2. Click on the original document tab on the bibliographic data page

Notes:

  • To view a patent requires the Adobe Acrobat Reader.














International Patent Classification

To determine the International Patent Classification of an invention, go to:

International Patent Classification (IPC) (7th edition)
http://www.wipo.org/classifications/en/ipc/


OR


If the U.S. Patent Class/subclass is known, the US/IPC Concordance can be used:


US to IPC Concordance
http://www.uspto.gov/go/classification/
